# Spellcraft Overview

**Spellcraft** is the particular art and theory of magic as implemented in the Crucible system. Proficiecy with spellcraft advances through Talents that are learned as part of character progression. The Crucible spellcraft system is designed to be dynamic and generative, allowing players who wish to focus their character on spellcasting to improvise and discover effective combinations and applications for their magical abilities.

## Masterful Composition

Spellcraft allows characters to manifest magic by composing a spell using a [[Rune]], [[Gesture]], and optionally an [[Inflection]].

- **Runes** represent the base magical essence (such as Spirit or Flame), metaphysical concepts that combine to form the laws of reality.
- **Gestures** represent the forming of the rune into a spell, using physical motion as a somatic component which shape (such as Create or Arrow) a Rune's power, causing it to act in a specific way.
- **Inflections** represent a further change or modification (such as Quicken or Pull), and use vocalization to alter how a spell is formed.

As your character learns Talents which provide Spellcraft components, these components will appear on the Grimoire tab of your character sheet and become usable through the **Cast a Spell** action. The process of learning components and discovering new spell combinations is intended to provide a rewarding experience of magical experimentation and narrative development for characters which invest into Spellcraft.

It is useful to think of the rules and structure of the Spellcraft system linguistically. If you think of a spell as a sentence, each spell must have a Rune (noun) and a Gesture (verb), and may optionally include an Inflection (adjective/adverb) that modifies either.

Spells which attack an opponent follow the rules of [[Attack Rolls]], with changes to the Ability Bonus and Base Damage of the attack depending on the combination of Rune and Gesture used.

#### Example: Zarajah the Pyromancer

Zarajah is an ambitious Level 6 sorcerer who strives to master the arts of elemental magic. Zarajah knows the runes for **Flame**, **Kinesis**, and **Lightning** as well as gestures for **Touch** (automatically learned), **Influence**, and **Arrow**. Additionally, Zarajah has also learned the **Quicken** inflection. Using these components, Zarajah has access to 18 different combinations which form Spells.

Zarajah's favorite spell is **Quickened Arrow of Flame** which allows her to hurl a projectile of arcane fire at a single target up to 10 spaces distant at the cost of **1 Action** and **2 Focus** points. The Flame rune targets **Reflex** defense and deals **Fire Damage** to **Health**. Arrow and Flame both scale using **Intellect**, so the effectiveness of this spell scales using only Intellect as a single ability score.

When Zarajah casts a **Quickened Arrow of Flame** towards a swooping Manticore, she rolls a **28** on her spell attack roll, beating the Manticore's Reflex defense of **21**. This **7 Overflow Damage** is increased by the **10 Base Damage** of the Arrow gesture, resulting in a total of **17 Fire Damage** dealt to the foe.

Since this attack is also a Critical Hit, the Manticore bursts into flame due to Zarajah's **Pyromancer** talent which inflicts the **Burning** condition causing additional damage-over-time.

## The Cost of Magic

Every magic spell has a cost- whether used within a combat situation or for out-of-combat benefits. Generally, this cost is paid in the form of [[Resources]], though in some situations the cost may use other resources or have other requirements.

As you construct your spells, the cost of the spell in Action Points and Focus Points may change based on your choice of [[Rune]], [[Gesture]] and [[Inflection]].
