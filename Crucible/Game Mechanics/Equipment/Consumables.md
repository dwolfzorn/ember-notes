# Consumables

**Consumables** are single-use or limited-use items that are expended when activated. Consumables can be browsed in the **Crucible > Items > Equipment** compendium pack.

Unlike weapons, armor, and accessories, consumable items do not have an enchantment tier. Their effectiveness scales based on **Quality** alone. Higher quality consumables are more potent but also more expensive.

Characters have 3 toolbelt slots by default. Consumable items and tools must be equipped to a toolbelt slot to be readily available for use during combat.

## Consumable Categories

Consumables are organized into the following categories:

**Ammunition**

Projectiles and other expendable materials used by ranged weapons. Ammunition is consumed automatically when the associated weapon is used.

**Bomb**

Explosive or volatile substances that can be thrown at a target area. Bombs typically deal area damage and may apply status effects.

**Flask**

Drinkable liquids including healing elixirs, tonics, and other alchemical preparations. Flasks provide immediate effects when consumed.

**Kit**

Collections of tools or supplies that enable specific activities such as crafting, healing, or field work.

**Scroll**

Enchanted parchments containing spellcraft components. See the **Spell Scrolls** section below for detailed rules.

**Trap**

Mechanical or magical devices that can be placed to trigger when a creature enters their area.

**Other**

Consumables that do not fit neatly into other categories.

## Consumable Properties

Consumables may have the following special properties:

#### Thrown

This consumable can be used at range, typically by throwing it at a target or target area. The effective range depends on the specific item.

## Spell Scrolls

A **Spell Scroll** is a specialized piece of vellum or parchment upon which are carefully inlaid the glyphs of one or more spellcraft components. Activating a spell scroll temporarily imbues its reader with familiarity with those components as if they have known them for years.

The number of spellcraft components a scroll can contain depends on the quality of its construction:

| Quality | Components |
| --- | --- |
| [[Spell Scroll (Fine)]] | 1 |
| [[Spell Scroll (Superior)]] | 2 |
| [[Spell Scroll (Masterwork)]] | 3 |

Spellcraft components include **Runes** (which determine the elemental school of a spell), **Gestures** (which determine the physical form of a spell), and **Inflections** (which modify how a spell behaves). A scroll may contain any combination of these component types up to its capacity.

Scrolls can be activated with the **Read Scroll** Action, which grants the reader with temporary knowledge of its components. Only one spell scroll effect may be active at a time; reading a different scroll replaces any current effect. The scroll is consumed upon use.
