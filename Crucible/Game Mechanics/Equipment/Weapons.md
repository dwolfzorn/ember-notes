# Weapons

**Weapons** are a specific Item type which represents items that are wielded in a character's hands and used in combat. The Crucible system provides the basic rules for creating and classifying weapons, and also provides an array of pre-defined weapon types which are typically observed in a fantasy role-playing game. These weapons can be browsed in the **Weapons** Compendium pack.

## Weapon Properties

Weapons all have the following attributes, in addition to those shared by all items:

#### Category

The category of a weapon establishes certain parameters about how that weapon may be used. How many hands does it require to wield? Is it a melee weapon or a ranged weapon? Does it require ammunition? Which ability scores modify its usage? And more. The possible weapon categories are listed in the table below.

#### Damage Type

Each weapon deals a specific damage type when it inflicts damage in combat.

#### Weapon Properties

Weapons may have a number of special properties which instill that item with special rules functionality described in the table below.

## Quality Tier

**Quality** of a Weapon describes its craftsmanship, materials, and condition. Quality tiers provide an additional bonus to the base damage of the weapon and contribute towards its Rarity score.

| Quality Tier | Damage Bonus | Rarity Modifier |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Shoddy | -2 | -1 |
| Standard | 0 | 0 |
| Fine | +1 | +1 |
| Superior | +2 | +2 |
| Masterwork | +3 | +4 |

The quality tier of a weapon is established at its time of initial creation and can never be increased after that point.

#### Broken Weapons

During the course of use, or over the passage of time, weapons may become **Broken**. Weapons which are Broken deal half their normal damage and cannot contribute to Block or Parry defenses. Repairing a Broken weapon restores it to its original quality tier or a lesser quality tier depending on the result of the **Tradecraft** skill check to repair the weapon.

## Enchantment Tier

**Enchantment** of a Weapon describes its level of magical imbuement. Enchantment tiers provide an additional bonus to attack rolls of the weapon and contribute towards its Rarity score.

| Enchantment Tier | Attack Bonus | Rarity Modifier |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mundane | 0 | 0 |
| Minor | +1 | +2 |
| Major | +2 | +4 |
| Legendary | +3 | +6 |

## Special Properties

Some weapons may have one or multiple of the following special properties. Each property describes some physical characteristic of the weapon. While all properties provide some benefit, some also come with a tradeoff to ensure that they are not strictly superior to weapons which lack that property.

#### Ambush

This weapon can be easily concealed and retrieved quickly. This weapon may be drawn for free during your own turn or as part of a Reaction. Its compact size reduces its effective range by 1 foot for melee weapons or 10 feet for ranged weapons.

#### Blocking

This weapon provides +1 to Block defense while equipped and not Broken. The weapon's defensive design reduces its base damage by 1 for one-handed weapons or 2 for two-handed weapons.

#### Engaging

This weapon is designed to better allow you to combat multiple foes, increasing your Engagement by +1. The weapon's defensive design reduces its base damage by 1 for one-handed weapons or 2 for two-handed weapons.

#### Intuitive

This weapon is intuitive enough to be used untrained. The Skill penalty when using this weapon untrained is only -1 instead of -4.

#### Oversized

This weapon is large for its category and is unwieldy in combat without special training. The weapon gains +1 base damage for one-handed weapons or +2 for two-handed weapons, but is slow and provides +2 Banes to Initiative rolls.

#### Parrying

This weapon provides +1 to Parry defense while equipped and not Broken. The weapon's defensive balance increases the critical hit threshold for Strikes made with this weapon by +1, making critical hits harder to achieve.

#### Versatile

This normally one-handed weapon can be used effectively with two hands. While equipped without any item in the off-hand its base damage is increased by +2 and Action cost is increased by +1.

#### Thrown

This weapon can be used to Strike at range up to a distance of 20 feet. When used to Strike a target that is not within melee range, the Weapon becomes unequipped and must be retrieved before it can be used again.
