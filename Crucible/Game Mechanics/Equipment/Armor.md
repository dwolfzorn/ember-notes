# Armor

**Armor** are items which can be worn to provide [[Defenses]] against attacks or environmental hazards. The Crucible system provides a framework and rules for creating and classifying Armor, and also provides an assortment of predefined Armor types which are typical to a fantasy role-playing setting. These Armors can be browsed in the Armor Compendium pack.

## Armor Categories

The category of armor establishes certain parameters about how that armor is used. What is it's weight category? How much physical protection does it afford? How capable of nimble movement is someone while wearing it? Supported Armor categories are listed in a table below. The following table lists the categories of Armor supported in the Crucible game system.

| Armor Category | Base Armor | Base Dodge | Scaling Threshold |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Unarmored | 0 | 8 | 0 Dex |
| Light | 4-8 | 6-8 | 4 Dex |
| Medium | 9-13 | 3-5 | 6 Dex |
| Heavy | 14-18 | 0-2 | 8 Dex |
| Natural | 0-18 | 0-8 | 0 Dex |

The total **Physical Defense** contributed by the piece of Armor is equal to the sum of Armor and Dodge components.

Physical Defense

=

Base Armor + Bonus Armor + Base Dodge + Bonus Dodge

[[Scale Mail]] is Medium Armor which offers **13 Armor Defense** and **3 Base Dodge Defense**. As Medium Armor, the effectiveness of Scale Mail will continue to improve if the wearer has more than **6 Dexterity**, it's **Scaling Threshold**. When Jex, a nimble fighter with **8 Dexterity**, dons Scale Mail armor they benefit from a total **Physical Defense** of **18 (13 Armor + 5 Dodge)**.

When Jex returns to the city from adventuring, they purchase a **Fine Quality** set of Scale Mail with a **Minor Enchantment**. Jex' total **Physical Defense** improves to **20 (14 Armor + 6 Dodge)**.

Wearing **Heavy Armor** does not permit a free **Move** action each turn as other armor types do unless the character has a specific Talent which negates this penalty.

## Armor Defense

Each piece of Armor provides a certain amount of Base Armor defense, bounded by the range allowed by its category. This Armor defense contributes directly towards Physical Defense when the item is equipped. In addition to Base Armor, the piece of armor may offer Bonus Armor according to its Quality tier which is described below.

## Dodge Defense

Each piece of Armor provides a baseline amount of Dodge defense. The amount of base dodge defense provided by the armor is defined according to a formula specific to its category.

Base Dodge (Light)

=

10 - (Base Armor / 2)

Base Dodge (Medium)

=

10 - ((Base Armor-1) / 2)

Base Dodge (Heavy)

=

10 - ((Base Armor-2) / 2)

Base Dodge (Natural)

=

10 - (Base Armor / 2)

In addition to Base Dodge defense, each piece of Armor can yield additional Bonus Dodge defense depending on the wearer's Dexterity ability score exceeds a certain Dodge Scaling Threshold. Bonus Dodge is also increased by the item's Enchantment Tier which is described below.

Bonus Dodge

=

Max(Dexterity - Scaling Threshold, 0) + Enchantment Bonus

## Quality Tier

**Quality** of Armor describes its craftsmanship, materials, and condition. Quality tiers provide an additional bonus to Armor defense and increase item rarity. The quality tier of Armor is established at its time of initial creation and can never be increased after that point.

| Quality Tier | Bonus Armor | Rarity Modifier |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Shoddy | -2 | -1 |
| Standard | 0 | 0 |
| Fine | +1 | +1 |
| Superior | +2 | +2 |
| Masterwork | +3 | +4 |

## Enchantment Tier

**Enchantment** of Armor describes its level of magical imbuement. Enchantment provides further increases to Bonus Dodge defense and increases item rarity. Armor can be enchanted after its original creation or have its enchantment improved.

| Enchantment Tier | Bonus Dodge | Rarity Modifier |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mundane | 0 | 0 |
| Minor | +1 | +2 |
| Major | +2 | +4 |
| Legendary | +3 | +6 |

## Broken Armor

During the course of use, or over the passage of time, Armor may become **Broken**. Armor which is Broken only benefits from half its normal Armor Defense.

## Armor Properties

Some Armor may have one or multiple of the following special properties. Each property describes some physical characteristic of the armor. While all properties provide some benefit, some also come with a tradeoff to ensure that they are not strictly superior to armor which lacks that property.

#### Bulky

Armor that is **Bulky** is cumbersome to wield and conveys a penalty of +2 Banes to all Initiative rolls.
