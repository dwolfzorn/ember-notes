# Heroism

Actions taken during Combat generate **Heroism** which accumulates until it reaches a threshold at which point every combatant in the encounter gains a **Heroism Point**. Heroism is a resource that allows combatants to perform legendary or iconic feats of power or resilience.

Heroism is accumulated by any Action which applies healing, damage, or effects to some other character. Actions which only target yourself, like **Move** or **Defend** do not contribute towards Heroism. The actions of both allies and adversaries count towards Heroism accumulation. The amount of Heroism accumulated by each Action is equal to its **Action** cost.

The progress of Heroism accumulation is visualized at the top of the Combat Tracker. A customizable game setting exists to configure whether this progress bar is visible to all players or only to Gamemasters. If you are new to the Crucible system, we encourage you to allow this progress bar to be visible to everyone, but once you are used to the rules of the system concealing it from players can help reduce metagaming that may occur from players trying to precisely anticipate the award of their next Heroism point.

The number of **Action Points** that need to be spent in order to yield a **Heroism Point** depends on the number of Hero characters in the Combat encounter:

Action per Heroism

=

Number of Heroes × 12

#### Example: Ray of Life

Suppose Kagura, a powerful Healer, casts a spell **Composed Ray of Life** which heals two of her allies and costs **5 Action** to cast. Since this spell heals other characters, this action contributes 5 Action towards Heroism accumulation. If this accumulation were enough to fill the Heroism meter, then Kagura and each of her allies would immediately gain **+1 Heroism**.

With her remaining Action for the turn, Kagura uses **Defend** which costs **1 Action**. Since this Action only affects herself, it does not contribute towards Heroism accumulation.
