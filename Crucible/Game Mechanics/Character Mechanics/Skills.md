# Skills

**Skills** represent the education or degree of professional training your character has received. These Skills might have been learned as part of their [[Background and Ability Scores]] or some other aspect of their life experience. Skills progress when a character selects a Training Talent. During [[Character Creation]], some Training Talents are awarded during the selection of a Background.

More information on Training Talents can be found in [[Talents]].

## Exploration Skills

**Athletics**

The **Athletics** skill represents the ability to undertake and surmount physical challenges. These could involve traversal of difficult terrain like climbing, running, or swimming, or could involve feats of strength, endurance, or flexibility.

**Awareness**

**Awareness** is a measure of your skill in translating sensory input into useful information. A character with high Awareness is able to notice and interpret details that others might miss, and is able to quickly identify and react to threats.

**Stealth**

**Stealth** covers the ability to hide effectively, to move without making a sound, to disguise oneself, and to otherwise use your environment to hide your presence. Those trained in Stealth focus on finding ways to remain unnoticed, whether sneaking in solitude through a dark alley at night, or blending in at a celebration at a crowded ballroom.

**Wilderness**

Those skilled in **Wilderness** learn to create shelter where there is none, identify which water is safe to drink and which is not, and locate sources of food in even the most inhospitable places, and to otherwise survive in harsh environments.

## Knowledge Skills

**Arcana**

Whether the focus of the education is magical or religious in nature, **Arcana** encompasses the study of esoteric knowledge. Students of Arcana learn fundamental principles of metaphysics, study spiritual beings and deities, runic theory and ritual, the nature of chaos and order, and the history of how magical and spiritual forces impact societies and cultures.

**Medicine**

The study of **Medicine** is a practical one, with a focus on studying the mechanical, biological components that make up living creatures, the ailments that can afflict them, and the ways to best treat injury and illness alike.

**Science**

**Science** is concerned with physical phenomena and examining the world through the lens of logic, reason, mathematics, and the use of structured observation. A student of science learns to posit and confirm theories, analyze the construction of physical objects and machines, and apply mathematical thinking.

**Society**

The **Society** skill is most often used for the purposes of research or understanding into the nuanced aspects of a social situation. Those educated in the workings of Society study the factors which contribute to social structures. Knowledge of Society is used to establish historical or socio-political context, to identify the social rank of particular individuals, or to otherwise draw connections between a person or place and its most likely contributors.

## Social Skills

**Deception**

**Deception** most commonly comes into play when a character is attempting to lie or mislead others, forge documents, or pierce the veil of lies others construct. Those who pursue an education in Deception are often politicians, diplomats, spies, thieves, or other professions for whom flexibility in the truth is a necessity.

**Diplomacy**

**Diplomacy** is rolled most often to persuade others to see things your way. A skill most commonly found in the politically savvy or influential orators, Diplomacy allows for the use of rhetoric and emotional appeals to inspire and convince others. Diplomats focus their efforts on the use of words, rather than deeds, to change opinions and inspire emotional reactions.

**Intimidation**

**Intimidation** is used to force others to do your bidding, whether through displays of aggression or threats of violence. Those trained in the art of intimidation are capable of using their words and actions to inspire fear in others, to demonstrate dominance or press others into subservience. Those who train their skill in intimidation need not necessarily be physically imposing in terms of stature, for words and deeds can be equally capable of inspiring fear.

**Performance**

Those who study **Performance** learn, above all else, how to use their talents to inspire and entertain others whether through music, dance, storytelling, or other forms of artistic expression.. Performers learn verse and music, use of pigment and light, poetry and story, dance and even physical feats to evoke emotional reactions in others.
