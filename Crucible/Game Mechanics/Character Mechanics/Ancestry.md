# Ancestry

**Ancestry** classifies the biology and physiology of a character in Crucible. Fantasy settings may feature a spectrum of species which are playable as characters. Crucible supports defining Ancestries for each of these options which are narratively and mechanically appropriate for your own game.

## Creating Ancestries

Crucible is not designed for a specific game setting. Gamemasters are encouraged to create or modify Ancestries to fit the lore of their own worlds. To configure an Ancestry in Crucible, create a new **Item** with the **Ancestry** type. Fill out the Ancestry configuration sheet to select a primary Ability score, a secondary Ability score, and optionally Resistance and Vulnerability to two different damage types. Every Ancestry may either have both a Resistance and a Vulnerability, or neither.

Each Ancestry includes:

- A **Primary Ability** Score (which begins at 3)
- A **Secondary Ability** Score (which begins at 2)
- A **Size** value in grid spaces (typically 4)
- A **Stride** value in grid spaces that determines the starting movement speed (typically 10)
- Optional: A **Damage Resistance** to a certain type of damage (+3 resistance) — If a Damage Resistance is selected, a Damage Vulnerability MUST be selected.
- Optional: A **Damage Vulnerability** to a certain type of damage (-3 vulnerability) — If a Damage Vulnerability is selected, a Damage Resistance MUST be selected.
- An **Ancestral Talent** which grants a specific special ability or knowledge to a character of that ancestry.

## Flexible Ancestry

Crucible allows use of custom ancestries by providing a way for GMs to configure their own compendium packs to use as a source during character creation. To add your compendium as a source, go to **Configure Settings ➤ Crucible ➤ Configure Compendium Sources**.

Role-playing games inspire and thrill us with tales of heroes who are exceptional and unique. Biological traits do not guarantee physical attributes, nor are species meant to be homogenous or monolithic (unless narratively intended). Gamemasters are empowered and encouraged to modify or customize Ancestries to suit the stories they and their players wish to tell. Consider Ancestral traits as suggestions rather than requirements.

Alex (a player) intends to roleplay as an Orc who, at the beginning of their journey, is not particularly tough but instead unusually wise. In such cases the initial Ability Scores of **3 Strength** and **2 Toughness** that the example Orcish Ancestry provides are at odds with the character development Alex desires. The Gamemaster can work with Alex to create a variation of the Orcish ancestry which offers different starting Ability Scores, for example by swapping the Primary Ability from Strength to Wisdom.
