# Ability Scores

**Ability Scores** approximate facets of a character's physical and mental capability. All dice checks in the Crucible system are modified by at least one ability score of the character performing the test. Ability Scores are also used to calculate a number of secondary attributes for your character, such as [[Defenses]] and [[Resources]] pools.

The ability scores used in the Crucible system are described below, following a designed symmetry that is reflected both on the character sheet as well as in the talent tree. The six ability scores are further divided into physical attributes which describe bodily capabilities and mental attributes which describe cognitive or spiritual capabilities. The three ability scores within each division are classified as power, resilience, or quickness attributes.

**Power Attributes**

Strength (physical) and Wisdom (mental) are power attributes which describe capability to exert force.

**Resilience Attributes**

Toughness (physical) and Presence (mental) are resilience attributes which describe capability to withstand strain.

**Quickness Attributes**

Dexterity (physical) and Intellect (mental) are quickness attributes which describe capability to act swiftly.

Ability scores generally range from 1 to 12. A score of 2 or 3 would be typical for the median person inhabiting the game setting. A score of 1 indicates an area of weakness compared to the median, while a score of 12 or higher is reserved for supernatural entities or peerless heroes.

Some creatures may rarely have a score of zero in a particular ability score, denoting cases where a certain ability is not relevant for a particular creature. This alters the rules for that creature in a meaningful way, as described below.

## Strength

Strength is a physical power attribute describing a creature's potency, brawn and might. Strength affects **Fortitude** defense, carrying **Capacity**, and the use of weaponry which scales with Strength. Each point of Strength increases base **Health** by 2.

Characters with high Strength are typically capable of feats of physical prowess. They may wield oversized or heavy weaponry, wear heavy suits of armor, or use their brawn to physically subdue their opponents.

Creatures with zero Strength are incapable of exerting physical influence on the world around them. They may not possess equipment nor can they physically interact with objects. They may not perform any Action which scales only using the physical ability scores of Strength, Toughness, and Dexterity.

## Toughness

Toughness is a physical resilience attribute describing physical hardiness and tenacity. Toughness affects the **Fortitude** defense. Each point of Toughness increases base **Health** by 4.

Characters with high Toughness can typically withstand considerable physical strain, demonstrating both grit and endurance in demanding situations. They may thrive amidst the center of danger, using their tenacity to hold or break the frontlines of combat.

Creatures with zero Toughness are incorporeal or otherwise have no meaningful physical form. They have no **Health** pool and can become neither **Weakened** nor **Dead**. Half of the Health pool they would have possessed instead contributes as a bonus to their **Morale** pool. The creature becomes **Incapacitated** if their Morale reaches zero.

## Dexterity

Dexterity is a physical quickness attribute that reflects agility and precision of movement. Dexterity affects **Initiative** in taking action, the **Dodge** component of Physical Defense, **Reflex** defense, and the use of weaponry which scales with Dexterity.

Characters with high Dexterity typically act swiftly, favoring quickness and precision of movement over pure power. They may wield weapons that allow for nimble movement or offer ranged capabilities, seeking to outmaneuver their foes before they even get a chance to act.

Creatures with zero Dexterity are inherently sluggish or ponderous. Their **Initiative** is always zero and they may not perform any actions with the **Reaction** tag.

## Wisdom

Wisdom is a mental power attribute describing a creature's force of conviction, depth of knowledge, and patience. Wisdom affects **Willpower** defense and size of **Focus** pool. Each point of Wisdom increases base **Morale** by 2.

Characters with high Wisdom typically act deliberately, demonstrating patience and judgment to intervene at the ideal moment where leverage is greatest. When they do act, they do so with conviction and commitment to their chosen path.

Creatures with zero Wisdom are non-sapient and incapable of exerting mental or spiritual influence on the world around them. They can neither obtain nor generate **Heroism** and they may not perform any Action which scales only using the mental ability scores of Wisdom, Presence, and Intellect.

## Presence

Presence is a mental resilience attribute describing a creature's force of personality, resolve, and self-belief. Presence affects **Willpower** defense and the size of **Focus** pool. Each point of Presence increases base **Morale** by 4.

Characters with high Presence can inspire themselves and others to feats of great heroism, overcoming obstacles, in part, through the sheer belief that things are possible. The aura of such heroes can instill similar belief in allies or foment doubts in the minds of adversaries.

Creatures with zero Presence have no sense of self or meaningful cognitive form. They can neither influence the thoughts of others nor have their own behavior influenced. They have no **Morale** pool and can become neither **Broken** nor **Insane**. Half of the Morale pool they would have possessed instead contributes as a bonus to their **Health** pool.

## Intellect

Intellect is a mental quickness attribute that reflects speed of thought and decisiveness of action. Intellect affects **Initiative** in taking action, the **Reflex** defense, and the size of **Focus** pool.

Characters with high Intellect are highly adaptable to various circumstances. They apply excellent problem solving and decision making skills to think on-the-fly and synthesize learned knowledge to guide decisive action. By anticipating and outwitting their opponent they can gain the upper hand in any conflict.

Creatures with zero Intellect lack the ability to concentrate or make rapid decisions. They have no **Focus** and cannot perform any action which requires it.
