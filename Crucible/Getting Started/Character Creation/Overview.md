# Overview

This chapter of the rules guides Gamemasters and Players through the process of creating their characters and preparing for play.

## Creating a Party

Begin setting up a game in Crucible by creating a **Party** which the player characters can all belong to by creating an Actor with the **Group** type. As players create their heroes, add them to the Party by dragging-and-dropping them onto the Party sheet.

Once you have created a Party, you should also configure this group as the "Primary Adventuring Party" under **Game Settings ➤ Crucible ➤ Primary Adventuring Party**. Taking this step allows a large number of mechanics to be automated which depend on knowing which group is the party of active Heroes.

## Creating Heroes

**Heroes** are the characters in Crucible which are controlled and portrayed by players, also known as "player characters". All Heroes, even those at Level 1, are capable adventurers. They are smart, charismatic, athletic, and skillful - but not yet to a degree that is wondrous. As characters advance in level they attain tiers of power where their physical, mental, and social capabilities are truly legendary. Character advancement in Crucible is level-based. Increases in level grant additional Ability and Talent points to spend which increase a character's power and capabilities. The maximum character level in Crucible is anticipated to be **18**.

Character creation is an easy process which begins by creating a **New Actor** of the **Hero** type. Creating a character is a guided process with a unique interface that walks you through the process step-by-step. You may reset the character creation process at any time by clicking the "restart" button located at the top right of the character creation screen.

## Starting Level

If organizing a gameplay experience that seeks to tell the origin story of heroes, starting your game at Level 1 is the recommended choice. If, however, you want to jump into an adventure with characters who are already established champions, beginning at a higher level may be more suitable.

> If starting at a higher level than 1, we recommend each player prepare a set of short anecdotes which explain the skill and talent advancements that their character has unlocked prior to the start of the game. This process helps to solidify their character backstory through past events that explain their heroic capabilities.
