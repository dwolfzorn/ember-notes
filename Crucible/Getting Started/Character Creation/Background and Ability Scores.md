# Background and Ability Scores

Each character chooses a [[Background]] which reflects a hero's cultural upbringing, professional training, career to date, or other aspects of their past before the advent of the campaign's storyline. Your choice of Background further augments your character.

## Background Features

Each Background includes:

- Novice Skills (typically 2)
- Knowledge Areas (typically 2)
- Known Languages
- Starting Talents (typically 2, one of which is training talent)

While Crucible provides a number of example backgrounds, it also allows use of custom backgrounds by providing a way for GMs to configure their own compendium packs to use as a source during character creation. To add your compendium as a source, go to **Configure Settings ➤ Crucible ➤ Configure Compendium Sources**.

## Ability Scores

After you have selected your background, it is time to allocate Ability Points to determine your character's starting [[Ability Scores]].

Each Ancestry provides a Primary Ability Score which begins at **3**, and a Secondary Ability Score which begins at **2**. All other ability scores begin at **1**. The values of each Ability Score range from **1** to **12**, where the typical value of this attribute for a non-remarkable Human is **2**.

#### Flexible Ancestry Abilities

By default, the base Ability score bonuses provided by each Ancestry are pre-configured, however should your Gamemaster desire a more flexible character creation experience, you may enable the **Flexible Ancestry Ability Bonuses** optional setting under **Configure Settings ➤ Crucible**.

When this option is active, players will be provided with a choice for their Primary (begins at 3) and Secondary (begins at 2) ability bonuses during the Ancestry step of character creation.

In addition to starting Ability Score values provided by your Ancestry, each character begins with **9 Ability Points** which can be allocated towards any of the six Ability Scores. To spend Ability Points click the up or down arrows to the right of the desired Ability.

You may only increase a single Ability Score a maximum of 3 times during character creation. A counter at the top of the Ability Scores section displays how many points you have remaining to spend.

Once you have completed character creation and advanced your character to **Level 1** the starting Ability Scores which you purchased can no longer be changed, but these ability scores may still be increased over time as you continue to level up.
