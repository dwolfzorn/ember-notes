# System Cornerstones

Crucible is built with the following cornerstones and design principles in mind.

## Consequential Character Progression

A primary goal for Crucible is to provide systems for meaningful, exciting, and impactful character progression. Creating and advancing a character in Crucible should be enjoyable and impactful as you gain access to new capabilities that tangibly evolve your playstyle and character persona.

## Strategic Grid-Based Combat

Game mechanics in Crucible are designed for application to encounter or area maps with a structured grid which defines distances and positions of characters to create tactical choices about movement during combat. Movement actions and costs, engagement, flanking, cover, area-of-effect abilities, and more are all ways to add tactical nuance and dynamism to combat.

## Consistently Automated Mechanics

A key goal for Crucible is to design and implement mechanics which are seamlessly automated or theoretically capable of seamless automation. We don't want automation that distracts from gameplay with the need for bookkeeping - but we also want the underlying game mechanics which are automated to be understandable to players at the table. We don't want mechanics which are inscrutable simply because we have a layer of automation in front of them. In Crucible you will find game mechanics which are elegantly and intuitively designed, but our approach to full automation of those mechanics means you can stay focused on the fun of roleplaying.

## Bring Your Own Story

Crucible is optimized and designed with extended-form narrative in mind. While it can be used for one-shot adventures, the system's mechanics are intended to play out over months or years of game sessions.

Crucible provides relatively little rule-based guidance around narrative, personal character story, or the development of intrapersonal relationships - deliberately leaving these narrative pillars for the Game Master and players to explore using whatever setting or storytelling style they most prefer.

Crucible intentionally does not define a canonical setting. The system provides Gamemasters with tools to apply the system to a variety of settings. As a general rule of thumb, Crucible will work best in a gritty fantasy setting but it can be adapted for other genres and tones.

## Ease of Powerful Use

Crucible is designed to be a system which is easy to learn and easy to use. Gamemasters should feel empowered to tell the stories they want to tell, and players to play the characters they want to play. We want game mechanics in Crucible to be crunchy and strategic while being presented to players in a way that is intuitive and fun to engage with.
