# Missing Scouts

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This exploration and social event invites the characters to explore the [[Mycelian Expanse]], where they'll discover a makeshift camp used by a group of [[Mutagist Scout]]. Although unoccupied, this camp shows signs of recent use. In this event, the party can:
>
> - Investigate the [[Mutagists]] camp for details about the scouts that occupy it.
> - Find a map detailing other key events they could encounter in this area.
> - Encounter a group of Mutagist Scouts posing as members of the [[Anachraenum]].
> - Inadvertently alert the Mutagists to their investigation.

### Investigating the Camp

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Cold Camp
>
> A successful **Stealth (DC 14)** check leads you to believe that this camp was very specifically laid out to be hard to spot while traveling nearby trails. You're not even sure you could spot it from above unless you knew what you were looking for.
>
> A successful use of the **Wilderness (DC 13)** skill notes the firepit still holds a few smoldering coals buried under the ash and burned fuel. While it's impossible to tell precisely how long ago the fire was used, it wasn't more than a day or two if the pit is still warm.
>
> Examining the camp area with a successful **Wilderness (DC 13)**check finds numerous footprints belonging to a group that recently left.
>
> - Critical Success You can tell that there were 3-4 people in this camp, given the distinct gaits, boot shapes, and impressions they made.
>
> A successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check notes that the camp has collections of fungal wood, plant fronds, bags of mushrooms and other edibles on offer.
>
> - **Critical Success**The amount of useful and edible materials here is far more than the camp occupants would need. They are likely collecting resources meant for another location.
>
> #### Unattended Bags
>
> The following items can be found in the camp:
>
> - [[Rations]]
> - [[Cook's Toolkit]]
> - [[Expanded Toolbelt]]
> - [[Fletcher's Toolkit]]
> - [[Travel Jug]]
> - [[Rope]]
> - Two backpacks.
>
> #### A Useful Map
>
> Attached to one of the backpacks, sealed up in a protective case is a fairly detailed [[Pathways Scout Map]] of the immediate area. Along with the camp itself, this map details numerous locations in the area, and paths to or from them.
>
> The scout map item description contains a number of locations that can be explored if the party choose to use it as a reference point.

Once the party has finished exploring the camp, or once some time has passed, continue to Returning Scouts below.

>

### Avoiding the scouts

> [!quote] Read Aloud

>
> > Well that was a bust, we'll rest here for a bit, then go check the other areas.
>
> Another adventurer speaks up, glancing back at their comrade:
>
> > I'm telling you, the Jurtak probably got them. They've been really active around here lately, and I think the last couple teams managed to completely piss them off by poaching their hunting grounds. We'll be lucky if we find any trace of them. We should just load up and head back to the lab.
>
> Though none of the others say it, there's a ripple of grudging agreement that moves through the rest of the adventurers.

### Addressing the Scouts

If the party decides against hiding and instead chooses to meet the scouts directly, or if they are found by the scouts when they search the camp, use this description:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> One of the adventurers goes into full alert, and calls out:
>
> > We've got company!
>
> Hands go to weapons, and the adventurers turn to face you, taking defensive stances. However, they don't immediately enter combat, instead choosing to parley.
>
> > Mind explaining why you're skulking around our camp? We're not expecting visitors, and not keen on raiders. So talk or walk, strangers.

The scouts are not immediately hostile, but are very suspicious at the beginning of this exchange. They won't immediately resort to violence without a reason, giving the party a chance to talk their way out of the situation, but combat remains a real possibility depending on party actions.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Who Are These People?
>
> The scouts are clandestine agents that belong to the [[Mutagists]], and are out in the Pathways collecting resources for the nearby [[Repurposed Quarry]] which houses a secret Mutagist lab.
>
> They are wary of strangers in the area, and will be careful not to let slip their true nature or mission in the Pathways, instead they style themselves as Anachraenum adventurers on commission to pull resources out of the Pathways. This isn't untrue, as most of them are active members of that guild, and that is their job. The lies are in the omissions.
>
> Refer to the [[Mutagist Scout]] biography page for further details on these scouts.

### Speaking with the Scouts

If the party has chosen to address the scouts peacefully, they will have questions for the party, and the party will be able to ask them questions as well. However, the wariness of the scouts needs to be managed to avoid causing a fight to break out.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Scout Wariness
>
> As they interact with a party their wariness will increase. Failing a skill check for Persuasion, Deception, and Intimidation increases wariness by 1. If they become too wary of the party, they may attempt to warn their base of operations ahead of time.
>
> The scout's wariness and bearing to the party is rated as follows:
>
> - 0-1 Wariness: The party is viewed as weird but harmless. The scouts do their best to get send them on their way quickly as possible.
> - 2-3 Wariness: The party is viewed as suspicious, but doesn't warrant direct action unless they refuse to leave. The scouts will plant to report them once done with their work here.
> - 4-5 Wariness: The party is now highly suspect, and the Scouts will begin scrutinizing anything they say, and pick at any holes in their story so far. They will plan to leave in the next hour or two report back to their superiors immediately.
> - 6+ Wariness: The party is clearly a threat to the Mutagist operation and needs to be reported quickly. Four of the scouts break off without explanation, and leave the camp immediately, heading for the [[Repurposed Quarry]].
>
> #### Lying to the Scouts
>
> A successful **Diplomacy (DC 12)** or **Deception (DC 12)** check can convince the scouts that the party is just a bunch of fellow explorers that found the camp by mistake, or that the party doesn't want any trouble or mean any harm.
>
> #### Intimidating the Scouts
>
> Only a successful **Intimidation (DC 20)** will effectively intimidate the scouts and make them grudgingly accept the presence of the party.

> [!info] Social
> #### Questions for the Party
>
> The scouts want to know the following things:
>
> - **Who are you?** The Scouts are almost guaranteed to have no idea who the party is, so any answer is viable here. However, if Ankarist outs himself or is introduced as a Veiled Chain investigator, that will increase their wariness by 1.
> - **What are you doing in the Pathways?** Almost any answer will work. However, mention of investigating the Drakes, chasing strange bandits, or hunting down leads on the origins of either, or similar, will increase their wariness by 1.
>   - **How'd you get down here?** There are only so many ways down into the Mycelian Expanse, and the scouts know them. If they are honest about using the Mythspire lift, the scouts will acknowledge they knew of the route, but not how to get in. Attempting to lie about how they got into the Pathways will raise wariness by 1.
>   - **Did you run into an adventuring team around Skybrush?** These scouts are aware that a team went topside to find a way down, but don't know what happened to them. Getting information on their fate could help reduce wariness by 1.
>   - **Is the way up open now?** This is purely information collection, and has no impact on bearing on their wariness.
> - **Have you seen any other travelers down here?** The scout team is aware they've lost people down here. If the party mentions the bodies found in Cruel Traps, the scout team will note that's who they're looking for, and will need to get those bodies buried.

The party may also ask questions of the scout team, but need to be careful as the wrong questions could raise suspicions.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What are you doing down here?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We are down here collecting rare reagents, plants, animals, artifacts and the like for contacts in Ordain. It's not really a formal expedition so much as a bunch of Anachraenum just trying to make some coin and build reputations.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Are you with the Anachraenum?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We sure are, actually.
>
> The scouts present their personal Anachraenum badges and insignia, allowing you a moment to look at them up close.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Part of the Club
>
> A successful **Awareness (DC 12)** check confirms that these are authentic Anachraenum seals, badges, and insignia proving they are members in good standing.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What's this camp for?
>
> **A:**
>
> > There's several teams. We all mostly work together and share knowledge. That's what this camp is for. We stockpile things, talk about finds, so forth. Sure, we might make less than if we hoarded our finds, but, this way involves way less squabbling over rights to territory and finds.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why are there multiple Anachraenum teams in the area?
>
> **A:**

> > We're all working for the same patron on the same scouting job.

> A successful **Society (DC 12)** may lead one to wonder why there are multiple teams of adventurers down here. Characters with the **Culture: Ordani** know that usually Anachraenum only work in teams of 3-5 people unless it's an official/sponsored expedition.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** A question?
>
> **A:**
>
> > A narrated quote answer.
>
> Additional out-of-character information.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Have you seen anyone out of place or suspicious down here?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Aside from you? Hard to say.
> >
> > There's all sorts of weird things down here, and weird people coming through the Pathways. We're not aware of anyone particularly, but it's a big space. You might try heading down south into the fog-shrouded areas of the Pathways, there's plenty of ways topside there, and places to hide out.

> [!info] Social
> #### Sensitive Matters
>
> If the party asks about any of the following, they will immediately increase the scout team's suspicion of the party:
>
> - The presence of Drakes in the upper region, and their attack on Helkas.
> - People utilizing poison or alchemical weapons, such as those in the Mythspire ruins.
>
> Players with **Diplomacy (DC 14, Passive)**or making a successful **Diplomacy (DC 16)** check recognize that there was a negative reaction to the topic, and that they know more than they are letting on. Pursuing this topic increases their wariness quickly (see above) and will cause further tension to form in the group of adventurers.

### Fighting the Scouts

If the party manages to provoke the scouts during a social confrontation, ambushes them, or is caught while investigating the camp, a fight will break out. The scouting team consists of four scouts.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Scout Tactics
>
> The Mutagist scouts are coordinated skirmishers used to working together to take down larger prey. They rely heavily [[Precision Shot]] on specific targets and focused fire directed by [[Unknown]]. A single scout will shout out a target to focus, and the others will rally to attack it to maximize the benefit of the Tactician's call.
>
> They are vicious and unforgiving, and will use [[Paralytic Vial]] to add a paralyzing effect to their crossbow shots in order to disable a target so that they can all focus on the weak link.
>
> The Mutagists are not fearless zealots that will fight to the death, however. If they are **Broken** or seems they will not be victorious in the fight, they will attempt to flee, regroup, and set up to ambush the party later. See "Following the Scouts" below.

#### Ragen Attunement: Mutagist Scouts Attacked

If the party chose to attack the Mutagist scouting party, each character advances their **Attunement: Ragen (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Following the Scouts

It is possible for the party to trail the team from their camp if they want. Unless they are made wary and suspicious enough to immediately report back to their superiors, they will travel to the location of [[Cruel Traps]], then eventually head to the Mythspire Overlook, and up into the observatory.

If the team was made to feel especially wary and suspicious they instead will begin heading for the [[Empty Laboratory]]. However, it's not possible to follow them all the way there, as the party will likely encounter other events that will distract them, causing them to lose sight of the scouts.

In either case, when the party follows the scouts, mark this event complete, and then guide the party token toward the next relevant location.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Staying Hidden
>
> The group needs to succeed on a **Stealth (DC 14)** group check with at least half the party succeeding to avoid being spotted as they travel. If successful, the team remains unaware of the party. They only need to make this check once per day.
>
> If the party fails at any point, the scout team becomes aware they are being followed. In response, the scout team scatters, causing the party to lose their trail. At your discretion, the team is not encountered again, or, the party is ambushed by four Mutagist Scouts while the remainder continue on to inform the lab.

#### Primordis Attunement: Mutagist Scouts Avoided

If the party stays hidden from the Mutagist scouting party, each character advances their **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Concluding the Event

Once the party has finished with the camp and the scouts, they can continue exploring.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> These scouts are connected to the larger Mutagist group operating in the region. The party might try to follow the scouts, or they might have guessed based on what they've learned that they need to find the nearby [[Repurposed Quarry]] in order to conclude their investigation.
>
> If the party have not encountered [[Cruel Traps]] , [[Crackpot in the Caves]] or [[Impossible Skin]] they may need to gather a little more information first.
