# A Strange New World

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event occurs at the [[Mythspire Overlook]], where the party begins to explore the mysterious [[Pathways]] in service of [[Ankarist]] investigation for the [[Veiled Chain]]. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Scout the area for some early details about Ankarist's investigation.
> - Discover evidence of a recently used camp at the [[A Strange New World]].
> - Speak with Ankarist to discuss the party's next steps.
> - If desired, take advantage of the area's relative shelter to make camp and rest.
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Overlook" Level of the [[Vista: Mycelian Expanse]] Vista.

### Abandoned Campsite

Characters may want to take stock of their immediate surroundings, but their respective familiarity with the Pathways is highly dependent on their individual skill and knowledge in areas of nature and subterranea.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring the Pathways
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 14)** check while observing the biome is able to confirm a few details about their surroundings: the [[Mycelian Expanse]] is lively, filled with numerous species of bioluminescent plants and fungi that have developed the ability to survive without sunlight; in addition to countless creatures of unknown origin, the biome is inhabited by a large array of fungi, molds, and slimes, alongside strange phosphorescent grasses, plants, and trees.
>
> - **Knowledge: Subterranea**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character has heard rumors of certain malicious creatures that inhabit the biome, including the reptilian [[Jurtak]]. Seasoned explorers are wary of these nefarious marauders.

After the party sets out into the wilderness of the Pathways on their investigation with Ankarist, they'll soon discover an abandoned campsite, where another group of adventurers appears to have come through somewhat recently. This discovery will provide scant clues about what's to come.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> After making your way through the shaded depths of the Pathways for a short while, you come upon an interesting sight amidst the primeval savagery of this place. A small circle of stones marks the remnants of a fire pit, situated at the center of what must have been a camp some recent time ago.
>
> Ankarist steps lively to scrutinize the area, and briefly searches the grounds before unearthing a small refuse pit, haphazardly buried and barely hidden.
>
> > Looks like this camp was used pretty recently. A week or so, at most.

The party can inspect the abandoned camp for themselves, and the procedure allows ample time for a chat with Ankarist about what's in store for the investigation ahead.

> [!info] Social
> #### Ankarist's Plan
>
> As the party lingers or begins to investigate the area for themselves, Ankarist chimes in with some recommendations about how they proceed with the investigation. Various points of Ankarist's plan include:
>
> - Scouting the immediate area first for initial leads or trails worth following.
> - Get a feel for surroundings in the Pathways to avoid unwanted hazards or encounters.
> - Make camp to rest before proceeding too much further, so the group can be as hardy as possible in the journey to come.
>
> If the characters mention the possibility of leaving the Pathways to return to the surface of Ember, Ankarist urges against this course of action — immediacy is an investigator's best friend, and while Ankarist would love the party's help, he also doesn't want the trail to grow cold.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating the Abandoned Campsite
>
> The campsite here is large enough to accommodate a group of four to eight travelers, with the remnants of four tents scattered around the fire pit.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check is able to spot a weather-logged [[Muddy Logbook]] that appears to have been left behind in the nearby foliage, next to some dry rations that seem to have been raided by local wildlife.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character also locates a random [[Arcturian Trinkets]] along with `[[/roll 1d2]]` random results from the [[Corpse Loot]] Table.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Science (DC 12, Passive)** check can readily deduce that the camp was carefully cleared when the previous occupants left: the tents were secured, the fire pit was safely smothered, and most refuse was collected. No signs of violence or distress appear to mark this place, which was likely abandoned a couple of weeks ago by what appears to have been a competent scouting party. And signs of subterranean wildlife abound, wiping away any trace of lootable foodstuffs.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Subterranea**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character is able to locate the clawed marks of some bipedal creatures with prodigious climbing skills, which appear to have scaled some of the taller foliage nearby for a better vantage point on the campsite. A character with **Knowledge: Monsters** can identify these markings as the clawed tracks of a [[Jurtak]] with the [[Spider Climb]] ability.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 11)** check is able to locate the presence of tracks in the area. Roughly two weeks old, these booted footprints are presumably those of the humanoid adventurers who camped here, and appear casual in their movement and intention.
>
> - **Knowledge: Tracking**: The character automatically succeeds on this check and gains the benefit of a Critical Success.
> - **Knowledge: Monsters**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Subterranea**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character can locate a second, newer set of tracks, only a week old and located farther afield. Made by a quartet of bipedal reptilians, these clawed tracks appear to circle the perimeter of the camp in a clandestine fashion, as if they were part of a coordinated scouting or combat maneuver.
>
> Both sets of tracks — those of the humanoid adventurers and the Jurtak marauders — set out deeper into the Pathways, and can be tracked for a short distance before they become swallowed by the primeval expanse and its incessant mycelial growth.

### Concluding the Event

The party must now head deeper into the Pathways in search of answers about the [[Afflicted Pallid Drakeling]] from [[Helkas]].

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> If the party decides to camp here, this is a reasonable moment for a social encounter as part of the [[Traveling with Ankarist]] suite of encounters.
>
> As the party continues their exploration deeper into the Pathways with Ankarist, they'll eventually encounter more of the clues they're looking for during [[Cruel Traps]], [[Missing Scouts]], and [[Crackpot in the Caves]].
