# Traveling with Ankarist

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event can occur multiple times while traveling with [[Ankarist]], and provides the party with an opportunity to learn about the investigator, his story, and his opinions on the current situation.
>
> This Event can occur during a rest at any point during Chapter 2 if Ankarist is a member of the [[Party]]. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Discuss the current situation with Ankarist and learn his thoughts.
> - Get to know Ankarist better by understanding his story.
>
> This Event is depicted using the current Scene.

### Thoughts on Events

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Skybrush and Liestra
>
> **A:**
>
> > I have only visited Skybrush once before. It is a remote settlement, and the conditions there are demanding. The community is cordial, though I would not choose such an isolated environment for myself; I learned early in life that I prefer less remote surroundings.
> >
> > In Skybrush, my contact is Liestra Grann. She deals in antiquities, and her business with Shent and Aedir artifacts has earned her connections among collectors and the Anachraenum alike. She is well informed about local excavation sites and ruins. If there is an entrance to the Pathways nearby, she is likely to be aware of it.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Liestra's Story[[Mapping the Myth]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > It is largely accurate.
> >
> > We, the Chain, identified a gang of thieves planning to steal a nobleman's private collection, and identified anyone we could. Somehow word got out and most of the crew abandoned their associates, leaving Liestra and a few others to get caught red handed during the robbery.
> >
> > The nobleman did try to bribe me to look the other way. I'd seen his kind before, thinking his power and influence and money meant he could have and do whatever he wanted.
> >
> > I decided to deny him what he sought, since I couldn't otherwise confront him. So, I personally delivered the burglars to the courts and did what I could to ensure their cases received all due fairness — no more, but no less than justice required.
> >
> > Liestra took that as a sign I liked her, and made a point of tracking me down to thank me for saving her life. After that she just sort of lingered, and ended up helping me track down the people who betrayed her to the Chain. Somewhere along the line there we became friends.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Mythspire Observatory[[Guardian of Myth]] or [[Reuniting with Ankarist]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > It's an impressive place, I can only imagine what it was like in its heyday. I do wonder where some of the upper framework of the orrery went. There wasn't enough debris on the lower levels to account for all the overhead collapses.
> >
> > I'm also always astounded by how much of the old Shent and Aedir ruins still stand after how long it's been. Something about the magic used to make the stone, I suspect, since they use the same sort of transmuted stone as seen in Aedir ruins.
> >
> > It's a different stone, yes, but similar striation in the exposed stonework means they used similar magical processes. It makes me wonder if they shared their techniques at some point, or developed the same methods independently.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Guardian[[Guardian of Myth]] or [[Reuniting with Ankarist]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > I've never seen anything like it before, and I find it strange the creature was abandoned for so long in Mythspire, and seemingly forgotten. I respect it though, a timeless defender, always vigilant, always dedicated. It deserved better.
> >
> > I wish there was something we could have done for it. I think it might have been a great ally and source of knowledge. However, that acid it was afflicted with goes beyond anything I've seen before. It's clearly toxic to living beings beyond simply necrotizing the flesh.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Pathways[[A Strange New World]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > I read quite a lot about them before, they are broken up into various regions: Mycelia Expanse, Fogbound Caverns, the Sinkhole Depths, so on. They're all a little different, but broadly the same: overrun with fungal life, filled with strange creatures, and home to countless dangers.
> >
> > It's a thrill getting to see them for myself, though I don't really have the freedom to just wander around and sightsee, much as I'd like to. There's still an an investigation to conduct, after all.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Dragon Skin[[Impossible Skin]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > That was a Vile Dragon's skin. If you want a good point of reference, they are like the Drakes we fought but many magnitudes worse, and much larger. Since we encountered the Drakes I've been wondering if they were related. Finding the shed skin feels like a confirmation my hunch was right.
> >
> > Given the age of skin and the state of things around it, I think that the creature moved on some time ago, so I doubt we're in any real danger. If it is in the area however, well, we'll do our best to avoid it.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Mutagist Laboratory[[Empty Laboratory]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > That site dates back to when Ordain was first being built. They cut deep into Ember to find a good source of the white stone the city uses. While the quarry was sizable, I doubt it was the main site the city's stone came from.
> >
> > If I recall, the construction of Ordain required multiple quarry sites working in tandem, this one may have been a latter era site, or it might have been abandoned when it flooded.
> >
> > Regardless, repurposing it into a hidden base was very smart, it's large enough to allow for expansion, and very well hidden. Once the location gets reported to the Chain I'm sure they'll go tear the place apart and extract every last secret the quarry has.

### Thoughts on Characters

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Agraband
>
> **A:**
>
> > He is a much better bard than a merchant, and clearly prefers leading people in song to leading wagons through dusty back roads. However, the realities of commerce are often more profitable than music, and everyone has debts.
> >
> > I cannot claim to know him well, but he has a reputation for reliability. There are certainly less trustworthy choices when it comes to reaching your destination. When I was preparing to return home and investigating caravan routes, Strayhearth’s company came highly recommended.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Clipper
>
> **A:**
>
> > From my observations, Agraband appears to be gradually entrusting Clipper with much of his network — contacts, routes, and even having them undertake certain responsibilities while in transit. It resembles a deliberate succession.
> >
> > I would not be surprised to see Strayhearth’s caravan someday operating under Clipper’s leadership. Whether that aligns with Clipper's wanderlust, I couldn't say.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Lyla
>
> **A:**
>
> > She has always been regarded as a significant figure in Ordain, even during her absences to administer far-flung operations. As the daughter of House Cevher’s patriarch, her position is nearly equivalent to nobility, a status shared by her siblings as well. However I believe she chafes against that, and the yoke of expectation placed on her as a result.
> >
> > She's unlike other emissaries of her station. Her demeanor is less formal, her conduct less restrained, and her willingness to do things herself isn't what is considered customary among her peers. I prefer her approach, and can respect someone who wants to get their hands dirty doing the work, rather than delegating from afar.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Sin
>
> **A:**
>
> > Sin strikes me as someone guided by optimism, with perhaps a certain naivete regarding the realities of organizations and their motives. Placing absolute trust in a group one knows little about rarely ends well.
> >
> > Sins intentions are utterly sincere, and the desire to do good should not be underestimated, but such openness can leave one vulnerable — to exploitation or to commitments they may later come to question.
> >
> > It is a disposition I respect, and one that more people could benefit from having.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Liestra[[Mapping the Myth]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > Liestra’s instincts tend toward opportunism, though I have never doubted the sincerity of her intentions when she states them plainly. She is resourceful and always seeking an advantage — a habit born of hardship, I suspect, rather than malice.
> >
> > Her decisions are sometimes questionable, and her preference for discretion can create complications. Even so, she has made a consistent effort to improve, and her actions are often motivated by genuine goodwill.
> >
> > That willingness to do better, in my estimation, deserves recognition.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Kern[[Crackpot in the Caves]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > The idea of a solitary druid surviving within the Pathways for an indeterminate length of time is unsettling in itself, and requires cunning, power, or secrets that far outstrip our own. I have no desire to challenge someone capable of such endurance.
> >
> > Kern is unpredictable and undoubtedly dangerous; on that much, I have no doubts. I find much of what he says difficult to verify, and I am certain he does not require our assistance at all. However, I do not believe he poses a threat to us unless provoked — and I have no intention of testing that theory. Discretion, in this case, seems the wiser course.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Kryz[[Rocky Rescue]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > If you’re asking for my opinion on “Kryz,” I’ll be clear: I do not ascribe personhood to a large inanimate crystal, painted face or otherwise.
> >
> > That said, it is worth noting that the Jurtak were unusually invested in recovering this particular stone. That alone raises questions about its significance and properties. My unease about Kern’s request was not unfounded, clearly. There were signs that something was amiss, even if I could not articulate them at the time.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Kalasak[[Empty Laboratory]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > If I am being as generous, Kalasak was a danger that needed to be contained, permanently and without exception. Those who conduct experiments on living beings without regard for suffering or dignity, forfeit any claim to sympathy. I have encountered others of his kind — methodical, remorseless, treating sentient life as little more than raw material.
> >
> > Death might be too good for them, and no justice would feel truly sufficient otherwise.
> >
> > Bringing an end to his work cannot undo his crimes, but it does ensure he cannot inflict further harm. For that, at least, there is some small satisfaction. His death leaves us with unanswered questions, though the records and notes he left behind may reveal more in time.

### About Yourself

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your story?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I spent my youth in the Tayan Empire, getting mired in politics and scheming. Ultimately that life was not for me and I left in search of something different. I found a different calling and a different purpose that led me to Ordain where I have lived now for many years. It turned out that the Veiled Chain had need of my particular mixture of skills and I found myself well suited to the work.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Veiled Chain?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The Veiled Chain is one of the branches of government in Ordain, it's the investigative arm responsible for resolving some of the most severe issues that the city faces. We get involved in cases where community enforcement of laws fails or is incapable of handling the situation.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your interest in dragons?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Enough to know they are bad news. I saw the carnage they are capable of first-hand during my time in the Tayan Empire. I'm not sure which are more dangerous, wild ones or those bound in service to the Empress. In either case, wherever dragons appear, bloodshed swiftly follows.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About your magic?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I suppose you could say I have a knack for sorcery, and I've picked up a few tricks here and there. Mostly things to help with my work. Most non-magical crime can be solved by the Ordani community without getting the Veiled Chain involved, but when there's a dangerous spellcaster in play it's important to understand a bit of arcana. I've put one or two outlaw mages in Spellbreaker Tower myself.

> [!info] Social
> #### Ankarist's Clandestine Pact
>
> [[Ankarist]] keeps his Warlock pact with the elder goddess [[Alar]] a secret to everyone but his closest intimates (of which he has very few).
>
> Any character who makes either a successful **Deception (DC 16)** or **`[[/skillCheck 16 arcana]]`** check sense that Ankarist is not being entirely forthcoming about the nature of his magical capabilities.
>
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **If the character is a Warlock**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character can reasonably suspect that Ankarist's arcane magic is the result of a Warlock's pact with some supernatural (if not otherworldly) patron.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The true source of your power?
>
> **A:**
>
> Ankarist scowls and his expression darkens slightly.
>
> > I suppose my explanation didn't fully satisfy you.
>
> He shakes his head wistfully before a vaguely approving smile softens his expression.
>
> > You show the insticts of an investigator!
> >
> > This is a private matter, and it's nothing that need concern you or that will affect our time together. I will share with you, however, that I have a patron who has been valuable in cultivating my capabilities. I'll speak no more of it now, but perhaps if we journey together I will have opportunity to confide in you further.
