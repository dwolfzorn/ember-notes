# Mapping the Myth

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This social event focuses on an encounter with the pawnbroker [[Liestra Grann]], who is a long-time contact and associate of Ankarist. In this event, the party can:
>
> - Learn about current events in [[Skybrush]], including its local quest [[A Brush With Death]].
> - Learn about a passage into the [[Pathways]] via the [[Mythspire Observatory]].
> - Learn a bit about how Liestra and Ankarist know each other.

### Meeting Liestra

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Leaned against the wall of the consignment shop is a towering Kivahr woman who immediately notes your presence. She tucks a jeweler's loupe and a trinket into a pocket, and pushes off the wall, coming up to her full height.
>
> > Well, well, well, look who it is! You Veiled Chain really do know about everything, don't you?
>
> Ankarist looks to be at a loss momentarily, which Liestra catches. She grunts, and gestures to her shop, pushing the door open.
>
> > Let's chat inside.

Liestra is not without manners, and will go around learning everyone's name, showing them around the shop, and asking a few questions of them and Ankarist. In return she offers a bit about herself.

> [!abstract] Liestra Grann
> **[[Liestra Grann]]**
>
> Level 4 · Kivahr Thief
>
> ![Liestra Grann](../../../assets/image150.webp)
>
> > [!quote] Read Aloud
> > A muscular Kivahr femme clad in in loose layers of cloth and leather leans upon the wall with casual composure. A side-parted bob of tawny hair hangs just below her chin as she scrutinizes some curious trinket using a small eyepiece. A wry, expressive grin rises to meet you moments before she sizes you up with amber-colored eyes, and you can't help but spot a stony ersatz mace strapped to her side. It's quite evident she means business.

> [!info] Social
> #### Polite Company
>
> Liestra is keen to find out where everyone is from, and how they came to meet their mutual pal Ankarist. She's also happy to share details about herself, and the story about how she met the investigator. Ankarist, however, would like to focus on the task at hand.
>
> #### Reading Liestra
>
> A successfull **Awareness (DC 15)** check or a character with the **Knowledge: Politics** or **Knowledge: Crime** knowledge suspects that while Liestra is large of frame there is a certain deviousness to her. She is more capable of subtlety than she appears, and hides it behind plenty of bluster.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** How did you meet Ankarist?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I met the good inspector in Ordain, got into a bit of trouble and needed a hand. He was right there to save the day and get me out of a tight spot. Turns out I do not have the lithe frame of a burglar!
>
> Ankarist clarifies:
>
> > You were attempting to steal items from the Lorando Collection and got caught. Not exactly the most auspicious beginning for a friendship. All I did was make sure you got a fair hearing.
>
> Liestra takes the story a bit further:
>
> > Lot more than that, Ankarist, and you know it. If you weren't a good man, you'd have taken that wannabe merchant-lord's offer to look the other way while he killed me for breaking into his manor. I'm actually glad you caught me and took me to the Hallows. I wouldn't bet on most in the Chain being so upright as you.
>
> Ankarist offers a faint, dissenting grumble but says nothing as Liestra wraps up:
>
> > Anyway, after that I got out of Ordain, and ended up here in Skybrush. Been running this pawnbroker for years now, and mostly deal in local artifacts found by Anachraenum passing through. Specialty is Shent artifacts, which I ship off to a couple collectors in the region. Those are my real money source these days, everything else is small time.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Scanning the Long Haul
>
> A successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check or having the **Knowledge: Artifacts** knowledge reveals that most of the free-standing items around the Long Haul seem like unremarkable, but practical, wares. A mix of salvaged and some newer-looking items. You do spot, however, several items artifacts around the store, but a subset of them which are secured away behind a locked display case.
>
> - A further **Society (DC 15)** confirms that these items are of Shent origin, and their people populated this region in ancient times. Magically and technologically sophisticated, their relics and artifacts could certainly be valuable to collectors or researchers.

### Getting Liestra's Help

Ankarist wants to know about a way into the [[Pathways]], and suspects that Liestra knows of one. She absolutely does, and is happy to share that information, though it does come with a request first.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Ankarist looks between you and Liestra, then clears his throat before speaking.
>
> > I really would like to find the way to the Pathways, and I suspect that's the sort of thing you have information on. Would I be correct in that suspicion?
>
> Liestra flashes a quick smile at that.
>
> > You would be, yes, but I'm not going to make getting the info easy.
>
> Ankarist's expression sours ever so slightly, though he doesn't look surprised. Liestra continues:
>
> > You know me Ankarist, I'm happy to provide you information, always am. But this time I've got a condition: I want you to help Skybrush resolve its current problems. It's why I thought you were here in the first place.
>
> Ankarist's brows rise at that, not quite surprised, but certainly not expecting that answer. He goes to open his mouth, but Liestra doesn't let him speak, instead she seems to predict his response:
>
> > Look, I just don't like how messy things have gotten in town. The current chaos is bad for business, and, besides, this is my home now, Ankarist. I don't like seeing the locals turning on each other. They're good people up here, and they might end up doing things they regret. I want things to go back to normal.
>
> Ankarist lifts his hands, half in defeat, half in a soothing gesture to Liestra. His tone is softened a touch, betraying some genuine care for a friend.
>
> > I understand. You don't need to convince me, Liestra. Besides, on the way in I noticed things felt off. What can you tell us about the situation?
>
> Liestra's shoulder relax, her relief palpable.
>
> > Well, the short and long of it is that there's been some murders in the town. Nasty ones too. One of the victims was pregnant, which is just… awful. So, naturally everybody's up in arms and wanting justice.
> >
> > You know, Ankarist, I'd wager that Constable Valaston could use the help of an actual Veiled Chain investigator. Poor bastard's barely barely holding things together right now.

> [!info] Social
> #### Local Law
>
> Characters with the path of **Knowledge: Politics** suspect that the constable in a frontier town like this is capable of enforcing local law and dispensing justice, but perhaps out of their depth when it comes to a complex murder investigation.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Where should we start looking?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I recommend talking to some of the other locals who probably know the latest news. I've been keeping busy here lately, so I don't know everything going on. Hit up The Roost and talk to the regulars there, guaranteed well of gossip those bunch.
> >
> > In fact, if you're sticking around, why don't we all head over to The Roost together? We can throw a few back and you can "interrogate" the locals!

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Changing Quests
>
> Once the party has met with Liestra, the party may choose to investigate the events of [[A Brush With Death]] first, at her request. This is a side quest that is set primarily within Skybrush.
>
> If the party chooses to do this, you can mark this event complete and begin the [[A Brush With Death]] events. Once that quest is complete, you'll want to return to this one to continue with **Liestra's Directions** below.
>
> #### Ankarist's Interest
>
> Generally speaking, Ankarist favors doing this first, as he does not like the idea of leaving a murder unsolved, especially if the locals need help which he can offer.
>
> If the party is not yet at least level 3, you may wish to strongly suggest the party investigate this side quest before continuing onward.

### Liestra's Directions

> [!info] Social
> #### Where There Is A Way
>
> If the party helped Skybrush resolve its issues ([[Delivered from Evil]]) or someone makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)**, **Intimidation (DC 15)** or **Deception (DC 17)** check to convince or coerce Liestra into coughing up the location, she will reveal the presence of Mythspire Observatory. Though if the party does use intimidation, Ankarist will intervene if they are particularly aggressive or threatening.
>
> #### Coercion and Consequence
>
> If the Party uses intimidation, but were not explicitly threatening of violence, after they have left the shop Ankarist will take them aside for a gruff expression of distaste for their choice of approach:
>
> > Are you proud of how you handled that? Not only was that unnecessary, you risked the investigation. Did it occur to you that your act of coercion might have just made her shut down?
>
> Ankarist won't belabor the issue, though, merely express his disappointment before trudging on toward their destination.
>
> If they were particularly threatening, Ankarist intervenes — but doesn't hesitate to take advantage of the opportunity to exploit the coercion.
>
> > "**That's not necessary**. I want to pursue this as much as you, but this isn't the way to do it."
> >
> > Turning his face toward Liestra, Ankarist proffers a simple but direct: "Sorry, Liestra," before turning back toward the party and adding "She doesn't need to be threatened— she knows as well as anyone that **some people** might construe her refusal to assist us as **impeding our investigation**. I am sure she doesn't want to end up on the **wrong side of this**."
>
> While Ankarist wouldn't have chosen intimidation, successfully pursuing his quarry is foremost in his mind, and if his connection to Liestra is already hurt by the act of coercion he may as well be sure to get the information he's after by redirecting the party's successful threat.

Liestra says the following about the Mythspire:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Liestra moves to a shelf, and shifts a few scrolls aside before grabbing a larger roll of parchment.
>
> > You know… it's passing into peculiar that you aren't the first group I've provided these directions to recently. What is the sudden fascination everyone has with Mythspire?
>
> Liestra lays out the parchment, unfurling a map of the local area as Ankarist cocks his head slightly at her question.
>
> > I'm investing something that caught my attention. Why, have others been asking about it?
>
> Liestra weighs the map down with a couple items nearby, and then looks at Ankarist.
>
> > Yeah, I recently helped a group of Anachraenum explorers find the location… that was only a few weeks ago, in fact. Said they were trying to map entrances or something. Weird bunch, never did come back. Not that I was expecting them to. But yeah, two groups in as many weeks.
>
> She taps the map.
>
> > Anyway, it's right around here, six miles northeast of here. Aim for the highest peak in that direction, you can't miss it. Be prepared though, a creature inhabits that ancient ruin, an ancient guardian of sorts.
> >
> > It's not aggressive, just weird. It asked me lots of questions about myself and the outside world, why I was poking around the ruins, so on. I don't think it's dangerous, but it's very old, and was not used to visitors. So my advice would be to tread cautiously when you get there.

Hearing about the Mythspire, the party may know a thing or two about it.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Unraveling the Myth
>
> Characters who succeed on a **Society (DC 15)** check have heard legend of the Mythspire, an ancient Shent ruin somewhere in the Wedgelands. However, its location was never confirmed, and nobody looking for it ever came back.
>
> An **Arcana (DC 16)** check suggests that if the location was Observatory it would be built somewhere with an unobstructed view of the night sky and that it would likely be upon a summit that stands solitary from, or higher than, other neighboring peaks.

If the party wants to ask about the previous group of adventurers, they can. Though Liestra doesn't know it, these were a group of scouts attached to the very group that Ankarist and the party are out here chasing.

> [!info] Social
> #### Previous Party
>
> If asking Liestra about the party that came through town, or asking locals, it's possible to glean some small pieces of information. A successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check reveals the following information. Liestra recalls more clues the better the party rolls on this check:
>
> - **Success.** They smelled ununsual like a tannery. The odor was noticeably unpleasant to be around.They were curt but not rude, it was clear they were in a hurry and focused on getting up into the mountains fast.
> - **Critical Success.** They were traveling light for an Anachraenum expedition, and didn't really go into detail about what they were doing. Liestra offered them to resupply before their journey and they declined, despite seeming to have few supplies. One of their company had badly stained and damaged hands that looked as if they had been burned or scarred with pock-marked flesh. A character with **Path: Crown Apothecary** realizes that Liestra's description sounds like a sort of chemical burn.

#### Luxarum Attunement: Directions to Mythspire

If the party manages to obtain directions to Mythspire Observatory via their dealings with Liestra Grann, each character advances their **Attunement: Luxarum (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.
