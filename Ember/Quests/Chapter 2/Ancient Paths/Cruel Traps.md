# Cruel Traps

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event occurs as [[Ankarist]] and the party explore the dim terrain of the [[Mycelian Expanse]], where they happen upon an array of iron foothold traps (along with their most recent victims). In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Discover an assortment of large iron foothold traps, some of which have been triggered and contain the corpses of their various victims.
> - Find the remains of the scouting party from the[[A Strange New World]] in [[A Strange New World]], who met a gruesome end here.
> - Locate the [[Scout Journal]] among the carnage, providing additional clues about Ankarist's the ongoing investigation.
> - Examine the surrounding area for additional clues.
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Traps" Level of the [[Mycelian Expanse]] Vista.

### Hazardous Terrain

The area ahead presents some dangerous hazards for the party in the form of several large iron foothold traps, which also contain the various corpses of their latest catches. Some of these remains will provide clues for the party, assuming they can avoid the deadly traps.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Drawing closer, you see a huge ten-legged reptile trapped within the massive iron jaws of some horrible trap. Its body is limp and beginning to rot. All round it are other signs of death, rotting carcasses now more bone than beast, and the remnants of traps sprung, the victims missing.
>
> Close by lay two dead humanoids, their bodies badly wounded and stripped of their equipment, which is strewn about them both. Their blood has been spilled, the deep red a stark contrast against the otherwise blue tones of the Pathways.
>
> Each of the bodies is held in place by the teeth of steel traps previously hidden in the brush. Ankarist utters a grim sound as he surveys the scene.
>
> > Best not to assume there aren't more traps, I don't want to get caught in whatever killed those two.
>
> He gestures to the two humanoids.
>
> > But I do want to get a look at them.

If the characters decide to take a closer look at the traps and their gruesome contents, they'll need to survive the hazards associated with the dangerous task.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Foothold Traps
>
> The area here is littered with large iron foothold traps, which are maliciously designed to catch would-be poachers, large creatures, and other humanoids in their unforgiving serrated jaws.
>
> For every hour they actively search or move throughout this area, each character must make a successful **Awareness (DC 16)** check to avoid triggering a trap.
>
> Characters who fail to spot the traps as they navigate the area must make a successful `[[/save dexterity 14]]` save to avoid taking `[[/damage 2d4 piercing]]` damage or half as much damage on a success. A character damaged this way is also **Restrained**.
>
> Whether the trap is armed or if it has been triggered, a foothold trap can be disarmed by any character with a successful **Athletics (DC 15)** check. Alternately, the trap can be disarmed with a successful **Stealth (DC 15)** or **Wilderness (DC 15)** check made using a [[Disarming Toolkit]]. A character who is Restrained by the trap takes **Sharp Teeth (Hazard 2, Fortitude, Health, Piercing)** damage for each failed check.

### Exploring the Area

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Caught in the Traps
>
> Any character who takes at least 10 minutes to search the area is able to locate a few details of interest. A good portion of belongings remain piled nearby, including some vials and the [[Scout Journal]].
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)**check is able to determine that the two humanoid corpses appear as though they were violently attacked and stripped of their equipment upon deafeat.
>
> Investigating the bodies yields the following information:
>
> With a Successful a **Wilderness (DC 14)**check, it is apparent that the lizard creature was killed by the trap. It looks like it has been dead for quite some time, and aside from struggling in vain to extract itself from the rusty teeth of the trap, was otherwise left alone.
>
> With a Successful a **Medicine (DC 14)**check, The bones of numerous unidentifiable creatures lay scattered around this area, some of them showing signs of being scavenged, others butchered by crude weapons.
>
> With a Successful a **Awareness (DC 14)**check,The two humanoid corpses look like they were stripped of their equipment, and violently attacked. However, a good portion of their belongings remain piled nearby, including some vials and a [[Scout Journal]].
>
> No matter the method, successfully clearing all the traps yields four [[Sharp Hunting Traps]]. These traps are much heavier, and far more dangerous than typical hunting traps.

Characters will want to examine the journal, as will Ankarist. It will yield them useful information about the two dead men, and what they were doing, confirming the presence of some sort of base or working area in the Pathways. This is a major clue that Ankarist is looking for.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Journal
>
> The journal belongs to one of the dead men, and suffered some damage due to the natural moisture in the environment, causing much of the ink to run. However, many passages remain legible.
>
> The last page is scribbled with shaky writing and reads:
>
> > Damn the jurtak. They came and moved our traps, set them specially for us. I told Kalasak that they wouldn't tolerate us poaching their kills and messing with their hunting grounds. We could've just let them alone, but no, Kalasak had to be right, didn't he? Tell Kalasak I told him so.
> >
> > Well now Erd's dead, and I can't stand on my leg. I'm sure those accursed lizard men will be along soon, and if the bleeding doesn't get me, they will.
> >
> > I hope they choke on my guts.
>
> #### Tracking the Poachers
>
> The journals detail this particular team and their patterns. There is one immediate takeaway anyone reading the journal can immediately pick up on:
>
> - The journal gives a basic rundown of the direction the pair will take, a map of trap locations that this particular team would rotate through. Using this, it's possibly to work out where the scout teams were coming from: They have a camp somewhere several miles southeast of here, on the other side of the bridge.
>
> With this, the party can figure out the path the pair of dead men took to get here, which in turn lets them figure out how to backtrack to where they came from. It's not a precise location, but it will get them close!

No roll is required to decipher the path the poachers took. The party or Ankarist simply needs to take a few minutes examining the journal to get a feel for their travel patterns and work out where to go next.

### Journal in Detail

The party may want to dig into the journal further, and they are free to do so, though it will take some dedicated reading time to parse through all the entries and pull out anything of value. It's not something they can do with a single skill check or action, rather, it should be done during downtime.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Journal Deep Dive
>
> If a character spends some time poring over the journal during a Rest, they can glean some additional information from the pages:
>
> - The scout teams are coming out and setting traps to catch local animals, then taking them back to a camp somewhere beyond the "great bridge." Why isn't explicitly stated, but they've been doing it for quite some time, and there's some indication it was for food.
> - Some of the teams were actively poaching Jurtak hunting grounds and trapped spaces as well, making sure to avoid taking game that died from the Jurtak poison. There was some fear that poisoned carcasses were making the drakes sick.
> - Sporadic entries detail work in a lab of some sort, meetings with someone named Kalasak. It references pens, creatures, feeding schedules, alchemical handling notes, and more, all centered around developing and testing some formula called "the mutagen."
> - There are numerous entries present that complain about being sent to collect materials and animals by the lab director. It seems that the author strongly objected to sending lab personnel out to collect animals, and argued for the scout teams to take that work on themselves to avoid anyone dying unnecessarily.

The party may also want to examine the dead and their belongings, they can do this if they like, collecting a few insights for their time.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating the Remains
>
> A successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check to survey the area yields the presence of an [[Alchemical Grenade]], a ,[[Rallying Elixir]] and an assortment of coins. Also, if not already examined, a [[Scout Journal]] lies nearby.
>
> - The [[Unknown]] matches remnants of glass and brass spotted in the Mythspire Observatory, and was possibly what was used to badly wound the guardian there. It seems to contain a viscous chemical under pressure.
> - The [[Rallying Elixir]] is only notable because its maker's mark shows it was made in Ordain. There appear to have been two other potions as well, but they were smashed at some point and it's impossible to learn more about them.
> - The coin is spread out across two coin purses, amounting to  **5 128 35**
> - If the party now examines the journal, see the above exploration entry of the same name to find out what it contains.
>
> A successful **Wilderness (DC 14)** while examining the scene leads one to believe that whoever raided the corpses, these Jurtak, were only interested in simple, useful items: rations, material, armor, weapons. They left behind money and potions, perhaps having no use for them, or not understanding their value.
>
> A successful **Medicine (DC 13)** check while examining the bodies reveals that the deer-like creature was undisturbed, the fungal snake was carved up after death, as were both men.
>
> In particular, the postmortem wounds on the men were to remove large chunks of muscle and several organs. The cutting was fast and crude, and makes it look like they were butchered for food.
>
> A successful **Wilderness (DC 13)** examination against the traps reveals that they would have been nearly impossible to spot if you didn't have some warning of their presence.

The party may confer with Ankarist if they like, he has the following to say about the traps and dead scouts:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Ankarist grimaces at the scene.
>
> > I don't want to be too glib about this, but, these people agitating the Jurtak ended up being a boon for us. With the journal we should be able to figure out where these scouts came from. This will either give us more leads to follow, or if we're lucky, lead us to the source of these scouts.
> >
> > Either way, we should get moving, if only to avoid being here when the Jurtak come to check on their traps.

### Concluding the Event

Once the party has finished examining the camp, they can continue exploring. The party simply needs to continue on their way through the tunnels, now guided by the journal of the dead harvester. Ankarist remains with them, eager to get to the bottom of this mystery.
