# Where Evil Lurks

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration and Combat Event occurs northwest of [[Skybrush]] on the Region Map, and challenges the party to explore the shadow-haunted interior of [[The Bleak Archive]], an aged [[Shent]] repository of Abyssal relics and pre-Shattering antiquities. By exploring this terrible reliquary, the characters can:
>
> - Identify the source of [[Unknown]] curse that afflicts Skybrush.
> - Overcome the area's hazards, including various mechanical and magical traps, along with the evil minions of the wraithlike warlock [[Tethra Shùl]].
> - Combat Tethra Shùl herself in an attempt to put an end to the Bewilderment and the Abyssal scourge that has come to Skybrush.
> - Recover a forbidden Abyssal artifact from Tethra Shùl known as a [[Finger of Nethehepticas]].
> - Entreat with a spectral memory of the Shent Sage known as [[Mioroth]].
>
> #### Area Walkthrough
>
> Exploration of this Event takes place on [[The Bleak Archive]] Area Map. A complete description of its contents can be found in the Area Walkthrough, [[The Bleak Archive]].

### Exploring the Bleak Archive

Once the party reaches the Bleak Archive, they must find a way inside and explore its tenebrous reaches for signs of the Bewilderment's furtive origin. A full description of the Bleak Archive Location can be found in [[The Bleak Archive]] Area Walkthrough.

#### Signara Attunement: Mirror Puzzle Solved

If the party manages to reach the Bleak Archive's [[True Vault]] by successfully solving the mirror array's puzzle of sealed chambers, each character advances their **Attunement: Signara (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Ragen Attunement: True Vault Breached

Although the Bleak Archive's True Vault can't normally be reached by any way other than solving the mirror array puzzle, this is a game of exceptions.

If the party manages to reach the True Vault by some method other than solving the mirror puzzle (including the use of Spells or Spell-like abilities), each character advances their **Attunement: Ragen (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### The Warlock's Legacy

Once the party reaches the Bleak Archive's True Vault, they must contend with the wraithlike warlock Tethra Shùl in mortal combat. After Tethra Shùl has been defeated, the characters must destroy or consecrate her worldly remains to loosen the Bewilderment's hold on Skybrush.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Cleansing the Wraith's Remains
>
> Once Tethra Shùl has been defeated in combat, her worldly remains must be destroyed or consecrated via one of the following methods:
>
> Tethra Shùl's worldly remains are imbued with a persistent enchantment from @Spell[spell.death.aura.influence].
>
> - **Destroying the Remains:** The remains can be destroyed by dealing 33 points of magical damage to the remains. If the remains are destroyed, all creatures within 30 feet experience **Necrotic Wave (Hazard 4, Fortitude, Health, Corruption)** and **Abyssal Screams (Hazard 4, Willpower, Morale, Void)**. Characters who take damage this way are subject to the effects of a [[Minor Abyssal Madness]].
> - **Dispelling the Remains:** The remains can be dispelled using a ritual to counterspell the effect, through **Counterspell (Death, Influence, Negate, DC 16)**. Dispelling the remains effectively consecrates them for the purposes of the Consecration outcome.
>   - The nature of the enchantment can be recognized with **Talent: Recognize Spellcraft**.
>   - Attempts to dispel using the **Life** rune gain +2 Boons.
>   - Attempts to dispel using the **Death** rune suffer +2 Banes.

#### Heart Attunement: Warlock's Remains Consecrated

If the characters cleanse the Bleak Archive by consecrating Tethra Shùl's remains, each character advances their **Attunement: Heart of Ember (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Cora Attunement: Warlock's Remains Destroyed

If the characters cleanse the Bleak Archive by destroying Tethra Shùl's remains, each character advances their **Attunement: Cora (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

`[[/outcome cleansed]]`

Once the worldly remains of Tethra Shùl have been cleansed — via consecration or destruction — read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The physical remains of Tethra Shùl begin to shudder and crack as beams of light shoot forth from tiny fissures in the wraithlike warlock's malevolent skull. After a brief crescendo, the remains disintegrate in a blinding flash, and the undying spellcaster is no more.
>
> You can't help but think of the treetop hamlet and the curse that has afflicted it. The people of Skybrush will no doubt wish to know about your triumph here this day.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Inert Relics
>
> Once Tethra Shùl's remains have been properly dealt with, both the curse and the enchantments laid upon the five Varùn Relics become inert and the items lose their potency. These items are still of interest to collectors, but are now non-magical and only worth 25% of their original stated value:
>
> - [[Rune-Marked Arrowhead]]
> - [[Rune-Marked Band]]
> - [[Rune-Marked Knife]]
> - [[Rune-Marked Sash]]
> - [[Rune-Marked Vial]]

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### The Shadow of Tethra Shùl
>
> If the party fails to destroy or consecrate Tethra Shùl's worldly remains, the effects of the Bewilderment continue to plague the citizens of Skybrush and anyone who comes into contact with the cursed Varùn artifacts.
>
> Once 100 days have passed, Tethra Shùl will rise from the grave to inhabit the Bleak Archive once again.

### Seizing the Finger of Nethehepticas

Whether or not anyone the party takes possession of the [[Finger of Nethehepticas]] while looting the [[True Vault]] of the Bleak Archive affects both any individual who chooses to take possession, and the story that the party will face ahead.

#### Abyss Attunement: Strange Relic

The first character to take possession of the [[Finger of Nethehepticas]] advances their **Attunement: The Abyss (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### A Departing Word

As the party exits the Bleak Archive via the [[Main Gallery]], the arcane spirit of the Shent Sage [[Mioroth]] appears to the characters to share a word of warning.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Final Visit from Mioroth
>
> Whether or not a character interacts with one of the Crystal Relics or the Alloyed Clockwork here, the spectral memory of Mioroth appears near one of these esoteric items.

Mioroth addresses the characters directly as soon as he arrives. Read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you make your way through the Main Gallery, the semi-translucent memory of the Shent Sage Mioroth appears once more, appearing close to a nearby gemstone in a fluttering brilliance of arcane light.
>
> > Ebbok Zhùr is no place for the faint of heart, and whatever wounds you wear — be they scars of the body or mind — shall serve in remembrance.
> >
> > As you depart, remember what you take with you. The Abyss and its minions leave a stain that, once marked, may forever remain.
>
> He looks to you with the calm interest of a scholastic mentor, as if waiting for an inquiry or the solicitation of rare advice.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Parting Conversation with Mioroth
>
> The characters have a few moments to beseech the Sage's memory before they depart. Mioroth can readily offer the party information on the following topics:
>
> - The Abyss is an infinite realm beyond the Weave that contains otherworldly creatures of terrible power.
> - The Abyss can be a source of power for the peoples upon Ember, the kind of power that comes at a price. The Varùn warlock named Tethra Shùl was a servant of these dark powers via her eldritch otherworldly patron.
> - The dark wand known as the Finger of Nethehepticas is an accursed artifact of unknown power, with direct ties to the Abyssal Horror of its namesake.
> - [[Nethehepticas]] is an Abyssal Horror whose insidious schemes have troubled the people of Ember for centuries, and will continue to do so if allowed to go unchecked.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 16)** check can convince Mioroth of the veracity of their cause, and may elicit some additional help from the Sage (at the Gamemaster's discretion).
>
> Additionally, any character who succeeds on a **Diplomacy (DC 10)** check easily determines that Miorith is a wizened sage speaking earnestly on topics in which he has expertise.
>
> Once the characters have completed their exchange with Mioroth, he bids them farewell before his apparition dissipates with a fluttering of arcane light.
>
> > Our fates are one. Moons guide you; and may the waxing light of the Six lead us to brighter futures. I will call upon you yet again, when the time and need are right.
