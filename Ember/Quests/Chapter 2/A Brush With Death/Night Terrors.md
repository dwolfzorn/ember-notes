# Night Terrors

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event can occur (and recur) during Rests anywhere in the world after the party has visited the town of [[Skybrush]], and reinforces the mysterious curse affecting both the town and its inhabitants. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Experience one of four random dreams riddled with nightmarish portents and visions of doom.
> - Uncover thinly-veiled allusions to Skybrush in these dreams, including vague details about a few of the town's inhabitants.

### The Stuff Dreams Are Made Of

During a Rest, one or more unsuspecting party members are beset by a strange and supernatural nightmare. This surrealistic jaunt is actually a remote manifestation of the psychic malevolence of an [[Abyssal Horrors]] known as [[Nethehepticas]], who slumbers in a forgotten tomb somewhere in the [[Wedgelands]].

The existence of Nethehepticas is entirely unknown; these nightmares are merely the first signs of a gradual awakening that might lead to the Horror's return. Unbeknownst to both the party and the citizens of Skybrush, the awakening presence of Nethehepticas is responsible for the outbreak of a great many undead manifestations in the region.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Whom Doth Dream?
>
> The character with the highest **Awareness (DC 0, Passive)** skill is the first to experience the nightmare. During further Rests to come, this event may recur — each time experienced by a different party member in descending order of **Awareness (DC 0, Passive)** skill.
>
> #### A Solitary Experience?
>
> Consider whether you wish to take the affected player aside and convey the material of the dream to them in private. This is not required, but can produce interesting roleplay moments.
>
> Characters will naturally be confused by their nightmarish experiences, and may attempt to seek answers. A few of these details may be closer at hand than they realize, however vague they might seem.

### 1. The Crawling Catacomb

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Torchlight kept you safe as you wandered the surreal subterranean landscape of some shadow-haunted catacomb, lined with endless ossuaries and scuttling darkness. The skulls of countless skeletons there seemed to glare at you with ill intent, yet the allure of some unknown treasure kept pushing you further and further into the shaded corners of this macabre and meandering place. The sinister whispering of some evil voice taunted you as you navigated the winding corridors, and the last thing you remember before waking is the gaze of two luminous crimson eyes and the rank smell of sepulcher rot.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Analyzing the Nightmare
>
> The dreaming character can use any number of the methods below to interpret the nightmare.
>
> If the character makes a successful **Arcana (DC 13)** check, they recognize that the surreal expanse of the subterranean landscape was adorned with telltale signs and symbology of illusion and divination magic — in a nightmare more vivid than any they have experienced before.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character also recalls the vile signs of otherworldly eldritch magic lurking among those tenebrous dead-end halls, in both the menacing taunts of the skeletons and the baubles they clutched in their bony hands.
>
> With a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check, the character feels in their very soul that this was no mere dream, and that the places depicted in that nightmare landscape are somehow real — and are inspired to seek this place out.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The dream feels to the character like, on some level, a warning, as much as it may be an overt call-to-action.
>
> By succeeding on a **Society (DC 15)** check, the character can determine that the burial rites in the dream appeared archaic and old-fashioned, and resembled certain consecration rites performed by the disciples of a legendary [[Shent]] sage known as Heroth, the Harrow-Hearted.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The characters knows that in addition to Heroth's arcane prowess, the Shent sage was known to be a war priest of the Elder God [[Sockets]], and stood proudly and successfully against many Abyssal incursions of the ancient past. This proximity to The Abyss came with rumors about Heroth's corruption — rumors staunchly denied by his disciples and allies.
>
> Additionally, characters with particular features gain information automatically:
>
> - **Culture: Kithil**: The character recognizes that the architecture of this place shares common elements with centuries-old Shent designs.
> - **Language: Abyssal**: Though the character can't make out very many specifics, they can identify some of the dark, foreboding speech from the dream to be Abyssal in origin. One phrase in particular stands out amidst the noise:
>   > Away to Ebbok Zhùr.

### 2. The Ruined Village

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You dreamt of climbing an impossibly tall tree in a mountaintop forest. The bark scraped the flesh of your hands raw as if it was petrified stone. Perched high above, you looked down upon the rocky ruins of some ancient village — its crumbling foundations splayed out in the shape of a gruesome humanoid skull. An impossibly sinister voice in the deepest baritone rang through your ears as you struggled to hold on for dear life; the words, while unclear, were remarkably menacing.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Analyzing the Nightmare
>
> If the character makes a successful **Arcana (DC 13)** check, they notice that in addition to the central skull shape, some of the ruins below them were arranged into images that resembled the arcane symbols for illusion and divination magic — in a nightmare more vivid than any they have experienced before.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character vaguely remembers the repugnant signs of otherworldly eldritch magic scrawled upon the massive tree they climbed.
>
> With a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check, the character feels in their very soul that this was no mere dream, and that the places depicted in that nightmare landscape are somehow real — and are inspired to seek this place out.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The dream feels to the character like, on some level, a warning, as much as it may be an overt call-to-action.
>
> By succeeding on a **Society (DC 13)** check, the character associates the imagery of the mountainside treetop village with a storied [[Rustvar Valleys]] settlement known as [[Skybrush]], which is indicated as a Point of Interest on the Region Map. The character is familiar with Skybrush and its role in [[Arctus Plateau]] commerce as a source of gravelbark pine and rare giantkin artifacts of the ancient [[Shent]] and [[Varún]].
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character recognizes a phrase from the sinister whispering: *"Ebbok Zhùr,"* which they know to be a Shent landmark of some kind.
>
> Additionally, characters with particular features gain information automatically:
>
> - **Culture: Kithil**: The peculiar alignment of the moons reminds the character of an ancient fable about an ill-fated Keth who became obsessed with a giant's wardrobe.
> - **Language: Abyssal**: Although the character can't make out very many specifics, they can identify some of the dark, foreboding speech from the dream to be Abyssal in origin. One phrase in particular stands out amidst the noise:
>   > The Night Terror beckons the brave.

### 3. The Giant's Footsteps

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The silhouette of some shade-haunted giant ambled on the horizon, and you felt compelled to follow. Yet, no matter how fast you ran, you couldn't keep pace with the plodding giant, whose massive strides carried you both to a mountainside forest at the edge of the world. Eventually, you caught up with your mysterious quarry; and as the giant turned to face you, its expression was no more than a pitch black nothingness. A damnable voice whispered from within, muttering unspeakable truths in an otherworldly tongue.

### 4. The Cerulean Bloom

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You recall endless hillsides of luminous blue flowers that resembled small anemones. Their pale cerulean glow cast a pall on everything around you, including the faces of dozens of nameless commoners who seemed to appear from nowhere. As you beheld their forlorn expressions, each countenance transformed into a grim specter of skeletal rot. Doomed whispers of doleful fates crawled forth from their hollow mouths, alongside a cryptic utterance like the voice of the netherworld itself.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Analyzing the Nightmare
>
> If the character makes a successful **Arcana (DC 13)** check, the character notices that the flowers seemed to grow in patterns that unmistakably resembled the symbols for both illusion and divination magic — in a nightmare more vivid than any they have experienced before.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character also recognizes the loathsome signs of otherworldly eldritch magic in some of the nightmare's imagery, from the sight of spectral undead to the sound of cosmic incantation.
>
> With a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check, the character feels in their very soul that this was no mere dream, and that the places depicted in that nightmare landscape are somehow real — and are inspired to seek this place out.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The dream feels to the character like, on some level, a warning, as much as it may be an overt call-to-action.
>
> By succeeding on a **Wilderness (DC 15)** check, the character recognizes the lambent blue anemones as [[Moon Blossom]], which are native to certain areas where the magical leylines of Ember's moons run deep.
>
> Additionally, characters with particular features gain information automatically:
>
> - **Culture: Kithil**: The peculiar alignment of the moons reminds the character of an ancient fable about an ill-fated Keth who became obsessed with a giant's wardrobe.
> - **Language: Abyssal**: Although the character can't make out very many specifics, they can identify some of the dark, foreboding speech from the dream to be Abyssal in origin. One phrase in particular stands out amidst the noise:
>   > The Bleak Seed has been sewn.

### Concluding the Event

The lack of finite information should compel the party to seek additional details about the place featured in the recurring nightmares, taking them closer and closer to Skybrush. Precisely how and where they find this information is up to them.

With the rest of the Arctus Plateau at their disposal, it's only a matter of time before the characters are able to identify the location of their nightmare dreamscape.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> This Event may recur when the party takes another Rest. Continue repeating this event until each party member has experienced one of the dreams.
