# The House on Lonely Hill

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This is an Exploration Event occurs on the Region Map just southeast of Skybrush, and invites the characters to survey the home of a third suspect in the recent [[Skybrush]] murders. This Event can only occur after the [[The Situation in Skybrush]] evidence has been acquired in [[The Situation in Skybrush]]. By exploring the home of the artist Gedron Tath, the characters can:
>
> - Scour Tath's house clues about the recent Skybrush murders.
> - Witness Tath's horrific self-immolation during the onset of the ancient eldritch curse known as [[Unknown]].
> - Discover five macabre artworks illustrated by Gedron Tath that ominously depict the various murders in Skybrush.
> - Acquire one of the accursed Varùn artifacts, potentially becoming afflicted by the curse itself.
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Snowy Ledge" Level of the [[Vista: Wedgelands]] Vista.

Clues gained during the party's first trip to Skybrush should inevitably lead them a mile south from town to investigate the remote domicile of **Gedron Tath** (Chaotic Neutral, Arcturian Kivahr, he/him) — an eccentric illustrator and sculptor who purchased a cursed Varùn ring from Liestra Grann. Here, the partyencounter more than they bargained for, and gather telling evidence about the grim forces motivating the situation in Skybrush.

### Approaching the Homestead

While limited information can be gained from examining the exterior of Gedron Tath's home studio, this initial moment also serves to slow down the party's approach and nurture an air of suspense as they ponder what could be happening inside.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Inspecting the Mural
>
> If the party takes a closer look at the mural that decorates the exterior wall, they can easily discern that it was painted by Gedron Tath himself — who apparently signs his last name upon his work using esoteric, stylized strokes.
>
> Observant or knowledgeable characters may be able to discern more:
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 15)** check is somewhat familiar with the artistic works of Gedron Tath, having seen one of his originals on display during their travels. The esoteric autograph on the mural is unmistakably Tath's signature.
>
> With a successful **Science (DC 13)** check, any character can reason from the faded tones of the pigments and the weathered erosion at the edges that this expressionistic mural was likely painted at least several months ago.
>
> By succeeding on a **Wilderness (DC 15)** check, any character can recognize the expressionistic elements of the mural as depictions of Ember's six moons as well as the three Inner Realms of [[The Cosmos]]. Despite the abstruse, almost mythical nature of the imagery featured here, you can identify the creatures as various entities associated with positive themes of wisdom and protection.
>
> #### Where the Moon Blossoms Grow
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 14, Passive)** readily notices scattered [[Moon Blossom]] blooming outside Tath's home.

### Tath's Immolation

The party has arrived at Gedron Tath's isolated home studio just in time to witness his baleful metamorphosis into a [[Horrendor]] under the evil dominion of the wraith [[Tethra Shùl]]. However, in anticipation of his hideous change, Gedron Tath has decided to take his own life to spare himself a gruesome undying fate. Once the party's investigation of the exterior is complete and they begin to survey what's going on inside, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you draw nearer to the painted Arcturian edifice, you're able to discern someone inside while peeking through an open window. A hairless Kivahr male adorned in a loose paint-spattered tunic kneels on the floor. Facing away from you, this figure — who you assume to be Gedron Tath — appears to be fidgeting with something in his unseen hands. A low-burning candle flickers nearby.
>
> After a brief moment, the man slowly turns his head to look at you, revealing an expression of abject fear upon his face. His skin is remarkably pale, and you can almost see him stifle a macabre smile as he gazes intensely in your direction.
>
> The Kivahr holds the candle in a trembling grasp, and illuminated beneath its subtle glow you perceive the reflected sheen of some liquid that coats the artist like a veneer of oil. His smile lingers for a moment as he presses the candle to his chest — resulting in a sudden and horrible conflagration of flame and black smoke as the man immolates, burning himself alive in front of your very eyes.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### You Are Too Late
>
> The party has arrived in the very moment that Gedron Tath has taken his life, and is effectively powerless to stop him from burning alive. If they rush in to save the immolated artist, his unconscious form slumps to the ground in a dismal blaze.
>
> Since Tath's body has already succumbed to the baleful transformation of the Bewilderment, no means of healing mundane nor magical will return him to consciousness. The characters were too late to save him, and all that remains is Tath's smoldering corpse, an ancient [[Varún]] artifact worn like a bracelet on his left arm, and a studio full of curious and macabre artworks.
>
> #### The Troublesome Trinket
>
> The solitary item remaining on Tath's immolated corpse is a strange stone-and-metal bracelet worn upon the artist's left arm. This item is initially unidentified, but is the [[Rune-Marked Band]] which was obtained in trade from Liestra Grann.
>
> This Varùn ring is not only a curious magic item, but also the party's most substantial evidence to date regarding what is actually happening in Skybrush. Naturally, the characters may be compelled to acquire and attune to it — a process which will unavoidably afflict that character with [[Unknown]].

> [!info] Social
> #### A Conversation with Tath's Corpse
>
> Because Tath was able to take his life before the transformation into a Horrendor was complete, his charred remains are an eligible target for magical methods of communication with the dead. If the party attempts to communicate with Gedron Tath in this way, the deceased artist is able to croak out a few pointed answers from the sooty confines of his burnt throat. Tath's corpse is able to relate aspects of the following information, should the characters ask suitably related questions:
>
> Tath is a loner who generally kept to himself. He only journeyed into town for supplies or to peddle his art.
>
> > Kept to myself … only went to town for supplies or to sell pieces.
>
> He had nothing to do with the deaths of Branos Erekos or Veera Hult, and knew Qory Hult by reputation only.
>
> > Nothing to do with the deaths. Knew Branos not. Cared for Veera.
>
> The "inspiration" for the macabre paintings on display in the studio came to Tath in dreadful nightmares. These dreams portrayed an ill-omened alignment of the moons, alongside a doomed Skybrush and a mysterious otherworldly voice full of portent.
>
> > Saw it in dreams … the doom that comes … for me too now.
>
> The dreams came to Tath after he started wearing the ancient Varùn ring as a bracelet, which he purchased from Liestra Grann.
>
> > Started once I wore the ring. The giant's bauble. A pretty thing.
>
> Tath killed himself to spare the other citizens of Skybrush from the dangers of his horrible transformation into a Horrendor — something he anticipated via the grim self-portrait in his studio.
>
> > I knew what was coming. Painted it. I chose a different path.

### Body of Work

Once the literal and figurative smoke clears, the party can survey the interior of Gedron Tath's home studio for additional clues related to the incident at hand.

Tath's home also serves as his studio, and is decorated with an array of recent artworks of a macabre and disturbing nature. Painted in a surrealistic style, Tath's art depicts eerie scenes and images that apparently presaged the recent atrocities in Skybrush and foreshadow the encounters of Quest Events to come.

Five notably gruesome art pieces are on display here:

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Prisoner
>
> > A masculine figure clutches the bars of a jail cell. You recognize the face of the man as Qory Hult, whom you met in Skybrush, but his unhinged countenance is corpselike and hideous.
>
> This portrait depicts **Qory Hult** whose affliction by the Bewilderment progresses even as the party investigates this very scene. It is clear to all characters that this depiction of Qory is far more horrific than the visage of the man you spoke to previously.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Science (DC 12)** check concludes from the fresh tones of the pigments and the relative lack of dust on the parchment that these images are likely somewhat new compared to other artworks in the studio. Surely, this illustration can be no more than a week old.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character determines that Tath used his own blood for one of the pigments used in these paintings, which has dried to a dark shade of crimson-brown.
>
> #### The Palanquin
>
> > A mid-forties Kiska woman languishes proudly atop a grotesque litter of humanoid bones, her finger curled around a trailing strand of sinew as if a practiced diversion.
>
> This is a future image of **Kel Kornan**, who is currently experiencing early onset of the Bewilderment.
>
> If the party is seeking Kornan on behalf of Kadrick Pond from [[Locating Kel Kornan]], any character with **Awareness (DC 13, Passive)** recognizes the Kiska from the description they were given of Kornan.
>
> #### The Artist
>
> > You behold a portrait of a Kivahr with a sinister and rotted disposition taking over one half of his face. Notes surround the portrait scrawled in blood with maddened haste.
>
> This is a self-portrait of **Gedron Tath**, the scrawled notes demonstrate the artist's rapid descent into lunacy and the intrusive thoughts of an unnamed "Master."
>
> Any character who knows **Language: Abyssal** recognizes some of the lunatic scrawlings on the portrait as Abyssal script. These characters appear to refer to the aforementioned "Master" as an entity known as "The Night Terror."
>
> #### The Conflagration
>
> > A diorama of a small cottage, its furniture in disarray and walls blacked by scorch marks and peeling. A slumped figure lies in the center of the room, horribly burned and surrounded by a mound of ash and dust.
> >
> > The scene depicted in this portrait matches exactly your current vantage point of the home before you — including details you are certain would have been impossible to predict.
>
> This painting is the result of [[Nethehepticas]] dream that showed to Gedron Tath one of the possible paths which lay before him — the path which the artist ultimately chose to take.
>
> #### The Archive
>
> > The final painting depicts a set of double doors to some mysterious stone edifice. The entrance is flanked by two statues of celestial hounds carved from moonstone. Two luminous eyes of a tenebrous entity loom ominously overhead, as if watching you approach.
>
> This painting depicts the entrance to [[The Bleak Archive]]. Gedron Tath has not visited this place himself, but was shown a vision of it by Nethehepticas, urging him to go there and join the other Horrendor in service to Tethra Shùl.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 15)** check can determine from the craftsmanship that the double doors and the surrounding moonstone decoration appear to be ancient [[Shent]] or [[Varún]] in origin.
>
> Meanwhile, any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 15)** check notices that the landscape surrounding the mysterious stone doors and their stone edifice is reminiscent of the neighboring [[Wedgelands]].

Should the party take the paintings with them, the artworks may prove valuable as clues. However, relatively few citizens of Skybrush will respond to these perverse images with open minds.

### Concluding the Event

Whatever the outcome of the party's encounter with Gedron Tath, the most reasonable course of action is a continuation of the investigation in Skybrush.
