# Checkmate for Chessmen

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This combat event pits the party against the four Downsiders in their camp at Aberin's Folly. In this event, the characters can:
>
> - Survey the construct camp at Aberin's Folly to devise a plan.
> - Infiltrate the Downsiders encampment and either capture or kill [[Hew]], the Renegade Construct.
> - Repel a counterattack from the awakened Chessmen known as [[Chamberlain]], [[Lucent]], and [[Rider]] (with his [[Jobri]]), along with a small cadre of four regular [[Woven Construct]] allies.

### Raiding the Camp

Even if the party manages to convince Hew to come willingly, this confrontation will inevitably lead to a combat sequence, during which the Downsiders will attempt to flee while fighting defensively.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Avoiding Detection
>
> The party rolls initiative as soon as they arrive at the camp.
>
> Characters can covertly approach the Chessmen or the [[Jobri]] by succeeding on a **Stealth (DC 13)** check to avoid detection. If each party member succeeds on this check, the character with the highest initiative order can take their turn during a surprise round, after which the Downsiders and their allies also roll for initiative.
>
> - Failure results in all four Downsiders immediately jumping into initiative order by grabbing their weapons and taking defensive positions. Rider moves toward and mounts the Jobri.
> - Characters with **Knowledge: Subterranea** have advantage on this check.

> [!info] Social
> #### Apprehending the Renegade
>
> The party can attempt to subdue Hew through parley, whether via intimidation or otherwise.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Diplomacy (DC 18)**, **Deception (DC 18)**, or **Intimidation (DC 18)** check is able to convince Hew and the other Downsiders to stand down willingly. Characters with **Knowledge: Crime**, **Knowledge: Machines**, or **Knowledge: Souls** have **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> - Hew agrees to join the party as they escort him back to Arcturel to face justice, while the other Downsiders agree to stay behind.
> - **Critical Success**: Hew, Lucent, and Rider lay down their weapons in an overt display of fealty to the party and their Silver Beam patrons.

Even if the party manages to convince Hew to come with them, the Chessman known as Rider will protest his ally's capture once the characters begin to restrain him. Read the following text after Hew crosses the map to join the party:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Renegade Construct approaches your party, shoulders stooped in resignation, as the other three Chessmen look on with nervous expectancy.
>
> You take the Renegade into your custody when, out of nowhere, the sleek construct made of blue steel stands up in defiance and shouts:
>
> > You have no right! Hew is innocent, and those cads at Silver Beam know it! If your hearts be as cold as your intentions suppose, you have a bit of a problem on your hands …
>
> The steel construct produces a spear tipped in a nimbus of lightning and immediately moves towards the reptilian steed nearby. A battle is at hand!

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Chessmen Tactics
>
> If the party decides to attack the Downsiders here and now, they won't hesitate to defend themselves.
>
> [[Rider]] will mount his [[Jobri]] and target any ranged attackers with his Crossbow before closing in for melee with his Shock Spear or War Pick.
>
> [[Hew]] attempts to flee the conflict immediately. If cornered or blocked from leaving, he'll arm himself with a War Pick to keep attackers at bay, otherwise using defensive actions to try to avoid any harm.
>
> [[Lucent]] will utilize a Crossbow in battle, fighting defensively while placing himself directly between Hew and the party on the battlefield.
>
> [[Chamberlain]] will summon forth a supplemental force of allied constructs to aid the Downsiders in battle (see below), and will also use his Net to entangle attackers that close distance to the other constructs. When confronted with melee combat, Chamberlain will utilize his personal [[Rapier]].
>
> #### Automated Reinforcements
>
> During the first round of combat, Chamberlain issues a command to call forth a supplemental force of 4 [[Woven Construct]], who dredge themselves out of the detritus strewn throughout the camp.
>
> > As the battle begins, the silver-plated Chessman stands erect and emits a strange inhuman sound, as if speaking in some bizarre language made of mechanical blare and electrostatic fuzz. All of a sudden, four aged constructs emerge from the timeworn detritus of the camp to stand beside the other four defenders, with verdigris-crusted chassis covered in mushroom growths and the soot of several decades.
>
> The Woven Constructs attack with no regard for their own safety, using their [[Rusty Scythe]] and [[Shovel]].
>
> #### Nothing To Lose
>
> The Downsiders and their construct allies will ultimately fight to the death, unless the characters convince one of them to surrender during the course of battle. If they become **Broken**, skill checks featured above in "Apprehending the Renegade" can be made again with **+2 Boons**.

#### Abyss Attunement: Downsiders Destroyed

If the party raids the Chessmen camp and destroys the Renegade Construct, each character advances their **Attunement: The Abyss (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

#### Akon Attunement: Downsiders Captured

If the party raids the Chessmen camp and chooses to capture the Renegade Construct "alive" for additional scrutiny, each character advances their **Attunement: Akon (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Collecting Evidence

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Proof for Larissa Toth
>
> Once the battle is over, the party needs to collect some kind of evidence that proves their victory here at Aberin's Folly. This can include (but is not limited to) the following:
>
> - [[Renegade Construct Scraps]] that identify the construct's model number.
> - Hew, the Renegade Construct, can accompany the party alive as a willing or unwilling hostage.
>
> #### Looting the Camp
>
> If the party examines the table where the constructs were gathered, they find a crude map of the Sinkhole Depths scrawled upon it — with Rock Bottom, the Scrapyard, and the Inkaro Pools locations noted in somewhat fresh ink. They also find a set of [[Scrivener's Toolkit]] specifically set up for the task of cartography nearby.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Awareness (DC 12)** check while searching the camp is also able to locate the following:
>
> - A [[Repair Kit (smith)]].
> - A leather purse containing  **3 3**.

Check the Outcome to conclude the event, once the players have collected their evidence for Larissa Toth and had ample time to search the camp.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster

>
> Completing this event earns the party a [[Milestone Progression]], potentially advancing them in level. This milestone is mutually exclusive with the [[Storming the Consortium]] event which cannot be earned.
>
> #### Next Steps
>
