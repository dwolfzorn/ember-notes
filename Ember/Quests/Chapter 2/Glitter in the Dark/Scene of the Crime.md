# Scene of the Crime

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This social and exploration event allows the party to continue their investigation into the Renegade Construct's so-called murder of Kellan Lorde by visiting the scene of the crime in Lower Arcturel. In this event, the characters can:
>
> - Speak with a House Cevher miner named Eolas Hathwick, who was a secondhand witness to the altercation between Kellan Lorde and the Renegade Construct.
> - Meet an inkaro lamplighter named **Calandra**, who was the key witness when the accident occurred, and learn about the time-honored duties of the lamplighters.
> - Assist Calandra with her current task by helping her locate a misplaced inkaro pearl nearby.
> - Investigate the scene of the crime itself for evidence of the accident.
> - Hear rumors of a new accident that has recently occurred at the Inkaro Pools of the Sinkhole Depths.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Off Limits: The Mines
>
> This event takes place in Lower Arcturel, near a mine that plays an important role in the [[An Old Friend]] main quest. Unless the party is allied with Lyla Cevher and currently engaged in the events of [[Unhappy Accidents]], the mine is closed and narratively off limits. Don't allow the characters to enter the mine at this time, citing the following readaloud should you need it:
>
> > The entrance to the mine is sealed with a barricade and a sign that reads: "Off Limits. Mine Closed for Refurbishment. See Zodi Trask for credentials." By all accounts, you can't get in. And the mundane silence that awaits you on the other side of the threshold does very little to hold your attention.

### The Secondhand Witness

The miner exiting the edifice is a middle-aged quarryman named Eolas Hathwick, who is seen leaving [[Wrestful Repairs]] with a refurbished pickaxe — since his was lost during the conflict with the Renegade Construct. The party can stop Eolas to speak with him if they wish; if not, he will relocate himself to the [[Level 3 Mine Office]] to continue his work shift.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Conversation with Eolas Hathwick
>
> The characters are free to approach **Eolas Hathwick** (LN, Arcturian Wirrun, he/him) as they see fit, whether they stop him outside Wrestful Repairs or meet him at the Mine Office at a later time. Once the party asks Eolas about the renegade construct or the incident with Kellan Lorde, he has the following to say:
>
> > "I already told Trask and those stiff-shirts from Silver Beam everything I know, but I suppose it was only a matter of time before someone else came around asking questions about what happened. It's a damned shame … Kellan Lorde deserved better than a long fall off the company ledge. But tell me, how exactly do I know this investigation of yours is legitimate? The last thing I need is a heap of trouble for answering questions to the wrong folks. We try to keep things clean around here."
>
> If the characters want additional information from Eolas, they must convince him to give it up via charismatic speech or bribery.
>
> - Any character who makes a successful**Diplomacy (DC 13)**, **Deception (DC 13)**, or **Intimidation (DC 15)** check is able to convince Eolas to reveal more information about the accident. Characters with **Knowledge: Crime** and **Knowledge: Forensics** have **+2 Boons** on these checks.
> - Alternately, a bribe of 15 sp is enough to compel Eolas to reveal more information.
>
> If adequately convinced or bribed, Eolas offers up the following details:
>
> - Kellan Lorde had only been working in the mines for six months or so. He kept to himself a lot, and was a bit of a rolling stone, but he seemed like a decent guy. Rumor had it that he was offered a higher paying gig with Silver Beam shortly before the accident happened, but didn't have the guts to put in his notice with Trask.
> - Eolas was working in the Mine Office when the accident happened. Kellan Lorde exited the Mine a few moments before the incident, so Eolas heard more than he saw. Kellan could be heard shouting — something like "Put that down!" — and by the time Eolas ran outside to investigate, Kellan and the "Renegade" Chessman were already tumbling off the ledge.
> - According to those in the know, when the search party went down to investigate Rock Bottom, all they found was a liquified splatter of Kellan Lorde's corpse, with no sign of the Chessman.
> - A lamplighter named Calandra was the closest eye witness to the crime, and she's conveniently lighting lamps in the district at this very moment. If the characters speak with Eolas outside, he'll point directly to Calandra, who can be seen making her rounds (see The Lamplighter's Plight below).
> - Apparently, yet another accident involving a Chessman occurred in the past day or two. Word on the street is that a Silver Beam worker was swallowed alive by a Mootap after a construct pushed him into an [[Inkaro Pools]]. Ask for the foreman Zirca if you make it down there.
>
> If a character succeeds on one of these Charisma skill checks by more than 5 (or offers to double the previously offered bribe , Eolas also offers the following details:
>
> - House Cevher's operations in Arcturel have suffered a great deal of late, whether the result of faulty equipment, natural and supernatural disaster, or aggressive Silver Beam buy outs.
> - Zodi Trask is very competent foreman, and is a gregarious leader of the local community. He seems to be under a lot of stress lately, even more than usual.

### The Lamplighter's Plight

The party has arrived in Lower Arcturel at a convenient time for their investigation, as the empathic lamplighter **Calandra** (Neutral Good, Arcturian Nir'ae, she/her) is currently making her daily rounds. According to locals, Calandra was the only eye-witness to Kellan Lorde's death.

When the party decides to approach the thoughtful lamplighter or catch her attention, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You regard the attentive Nir'ae woman near the catwalk railing, clad in loose robes marked by the dirt and grime of the Dives. She places something at the end of her staff — a small nacreous orb that alights with enchanted luminance as she extends it towards a sconce on the catwalk railing. Just then, an untimely incident occurs as you approach …
>
> An erratic swarm of tatterwing moths flutters up from the shadows below to swirl chaotically around the sconce and the woman herself. The lamplighter loses her balance, and the newly-lit inkaro pearl tumbles from the lamp fixture into the steel grating below the catwalk. She drops to her knees and starts frantically searching for the orb beneath the steel grating.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Aiding the Lamplighter
>
> The characters are free to approach Calandra as they see fit, but they have an opportunity here and now to help her out an ill-timed predicament. The lamplighter is willing to provide some resources and additional information if the party comes to her aid.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a**Awareness (DC 14)** check is able to help Calandra locate the fallen inkaro pearl.
>
> - Alternately, a character who uses the [[Detect Magic]] spell can locate the inkaro pearl more directly.
>
> A successful **Stealth (DC 13)** check is necessary to retrieve the inkaro pearl from the crisscrossed underbelly of the steel grating.
>
> - Alternately, a character can use[[Rune: Kinesis]] or Spell[kinesis.influence] to retrieve the pearl can grab it with relative ease, avoiding a skill check as long as they do the activity unhindered.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Conversation with Calandra
>
> As the characters help her search for the missing inkaro pearl, Calandra eagerly shares a bit of lore about this arcane commercial resource and its importance in Arcturel's livelihood.
>
> > I must admit, it's been quite some time since a swarm of tatterwings caused me to lose my balance. Those fussy little things love the light of inkaro pearls more than anything, and tend to swarm lamplighters who work the lower reaches of the sinkhole.
> >
> > Losing a pearl is a little tougher on us these days … supplies are short lately, and I'd wager the accident has something to do with it as well. It's a bit hard to trust constructs with pearl farming if they can't be trusted at all …
>
> If the characters assist Calandra, she gifts the party an [[Inkaro Pearl, White]] of their own. Either way, she shares the following observations and rumors about the matters at hand:
>
> - She was the only one on the catwalk other than Kellan Lorde and the Chessman when the accident happened. The construct was apparently fiddling with the inkaro lantern outside the Mine Office near Hob Korell's, and Kellan tried to stop it. As soon as the miner put his hands on the construct, the Chessman erupted into a fit. She heard Kellan yell: "Put that down. Do your job." And after a short struggle, they both went over the ledge.
> - She's quick to point out the specific location of the accident to the players, an inkaro lantern outside [[Hob Korell's Unconventional Steeds]] along the southern section of the catwalk. (The details of this area are featured below in "Surveying the Crime Scene").
> - Calandra and a few of her lamplighter associates have seen Silver Beam workers toiling in their headquarters in Upper Arcturel during odd hours. It seems innovation never sleeps for that group.
> - According to rumors, yet another accident involving a Chessman occurred in the past day or two, a fatality involving a Mootap in the [[Inkaro Pools]] down in the Sinkhole Depths. The poolside foreman Zirca Bronzebellow is a reasonable person to speak with about the incident.
>
> Although she teeters on the edge of hesitation, a successful **Diplomacy (DC 15)** **Diplomacy (DC 15)** check (or a small bribe of 5 sp or more) compels Calandra to relate anecdotal evidence suggesting that the Silver Beam Consortium has also been withholding some of their inkaro pearls from public consumption.

### Surveying The Crime Scene

The party can inspect the scene of the crime itself: the catwalk outside Hob Korell's Unconventional Steeds, where a solitary inkaro lantern sits opposite the door to Korell's stables.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Searching the Catwalk
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Awareness (DC 15)** check is able to spot the following (characters with **Knowledge: Subterranea** have **+2 Boons** on this check):
>
> - Crude gouging and scratches on the inkaro lantern.
> - The railing is bent and buckled beneath the weight of something heavy that leaned off the edge.
> - A modest blood spatter stains the grating near the inkaro lantern, no larger than a waterskin.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Science (DC 16)** or **Wilderness (DC 16)** check is able to determine the following (characters with **Knowledge: Crime**, **Knowledge: Forensics**, or **Knowledge: Machines** have **+2 Boons** on this check):
>
> - The gouging and scratches on the inkaro lantern were likely caused by the hands of a Chessman construct.
> - The construct was heavy enough to cause the buckling on the rail.
> - The blood splatter could have easily been the result of the physical conflict between Kellen Lorde and the construct. Perhaps a wrist, head, or collarbone injury, where the bone is close to the skin's surface.

### Investigating the Dives

The party can investigate other areas in Lower Arcturel for clues, although efforts to gather additional information inevitably point the characters in the same direction: towards whatever evidence might remain at the bottom of the sinkhole, where the construct and the deceased victim presumably fell.

> [!info] Social
> #### Other Denizens of the Dives
>
> Rumors abound in Lower Arcturel, if the characters want to seek them. Most of the local superstition is fruitless, but a few citizens have some adequate information to share. Helpful locations and individuals include:
>
> [[Hob Korell's Unconventional Steeds]]. The friendly lizard trader **Hob Korell** (Chaotic Good, Waerd Keth, he/him) can also be a source of information.
>
> - Hob's most trusted Chessman, a construct named Rider, disappeared a few weeks ago along with one of the [[Jobri]]. All accounts suggest that Rider stole the reptilian mount and rode off into the Depths, but that kind of behavior deviates from his normal commands and protocol.
> - Although the accident with Kellan Lorde happened right in front of Hob's stables, nobody from Unconventional Steeds saw the accident. He and others rushed outside to the catwalk, but it was too late.
> - Hob provides exotic steeds like Jobri and giant frogs for all of Arcturel, including itinerant adventurers and established businesses like the Silver Beam Consortium.
> - Sales have been up recently, and Hob’s supply of Jobri is relatively short. The Silver Beam Consortium is responsible for a lot of new purchases (although, somewhat suspiciously, there hasn’t been a matching influx of mine and pool workers to necessitate them).
>
> [[Arvoda's Elixirs]]. The erudite alchemist **Emelyn Arvoda** (Neutral Good, Arcturian Ashka, she/her) didn't witness anything, but the following rumors have been on the lips of her customers of late:
>
> - Silver Beam recruiters have recently been trying to headhunt various miners and other laborers throughout Arcturel, and a lot of them seem to be getting caught up in accidents or unsavory assignments.
> - House Cevher seems to be under a lot of pressure. Emelyn sees Zodi Trask more than most, since his apartment is right next door to her shop, and it seems like the whole world's on his shoulders lately.
> - Larissa Toth of the Silver Beam consortium stops by from time to time in search of rare ingredients and alchemical reagents, and she overshares when she's feeling more confident. It sounds like production of Silver Beam constructs is on the rise in the wake of the accidents with Chessman models.

#### Primordis Attunement: Gathered All Three Clues

If the party manages to find all three clues during the course of this event (Hathwick's Account, Calandra's Account, and The Damaged Lantern), each character advances their **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.
