# Concerned Merchants

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This social event introduces the party to a group of merchants that have recently visited the sinkhole city of [[Arcturel]], where a "renegade construct" is purported to have murdered a human being. In this event, the characters can:
>
> - Meet the merchants Doran Styme and Nevel Rux, the gregarious leaders of the "Rux Brothers" caravan, and join them for a free meal.
> - Learn about the caravan's recent visit to Arcturel, where rumors abound about a murderous Chessman construct on the loose.
> - Purchase various goods and services from the merchants, if desired.

### Approaching the Caravan

If the characters approach the caravan with any amount of caution, propriety, or stealth, they can easily overhear a couple of the merchants engaged in conversation — who earnestly discuss their most recent venture to the infamous sinkhole city of Arcturel. These are **Doran Styme** (Lawful Neutral, Arcturian Human, he/him), a squat leather trader in his late forties, and his older "brother" **Nevel Rux** (Chaotic Good, Arcturian Human, he/him), a reedy jewel merchant in his early fifties.

As the characters approach, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Nearing the encampment, your attention is drawn to the cook, a squat human merchant dressed in Arcturian fineries who sprinkles some kind of aromatic herbs from a small jar onto the spit-roasted meat. A taller man clad in similar garb accented by countless rings, bracelets, and necklaces joins the shorter fellow with a pouch full of his own seasoning — which he sprinkles on the meat despite protestation from his associate.
>
> > Moons help us, Nevel, you're going to ruin the recipe!
>
> The taller fellow retorts:
>
> > Pish posh, Doran. You'll never form a proper bark without enough pepper. Now stand aside and let a real artist cook.
>
> Doran keeps a close eye on his tall friend, who offers a liberal dusting of spices to the meat. The short merchant continues:
>
> > I know you better than that. You're just in a hurry to use those spices you picked up in Arcturel. So be it! But if you ruin that roast, you will never hear the end of it, Nevel. I promise you!
>
> Just then, these two would-be gourmands notice your group. They greet you with a polite smile and a hurried wave that beckons you to join them. Stepping forward, you notice a banner on the wagon behind them that reads: "Rux Brothers, Est. 2495."

### Meeting the Merchants

As the rest of the caravan members go about their business in the background, Doran and Nevel are eager to become acquainted with the party — not only for the company, but for the timely chance of an unexpected sale.

> [!info] Social
> #### An Invitation to Supper
>
> The merchants quickly introduce themselves as the **Rux Brothers**, although they are brothers in name alone. Treat them both as [[Arcturian]].
>
> - **Doran Styme** (Lawful Neutral, Arcturian Human, he/him), a squat leather trader in his mid-fifties. Weathered and well-traveled, Doran greets people with a hearty laugh and warm voice. The gregarious merchant is pragmatic and patient, always willing to find the best solution after weighing options.
> - **Nevel Rux** (Chaotic Good, Arcturian Human, he/him), a reedy jewel merchant in his early sixties. A few years Doran's senior, Nevel acts as the assertive and pontifical "big brother" in the relationship and is quick to offer his so-called expert opinion on nearly everything.
>
> After introductions, the Rux Brothers invite the party to join them for a complimentary meal, which consists of the seasoned meat from a large Nothrin shank and a salad of mixed greens, dried fruits, and candied nuts. Doran and Nevel offer up a variety of anecdotal information during the meal, including:
>
> - Their history together as entrepreneurs of the Arctus Plateau.
> - Their recent visit to Arcturel, along with rumors about a haywire Chessman accused of murder and the failing trade practices of House Cevher.
> - Tantalizing descriptions of the origin of their wares, which include various weapons, armor, tools, rations, and trade goods. And while they've recently collected a fair amount of Inkaro Pearls from Arcturel, that inventory is spoken for, and the nacreous arcane orbs are not for sale.
> - Advice for the journey to Arcturel, including some basic resources available to visitors.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Deception (DC 12)** check is able to confirm the validity of Doran and Nevel's claims, and have no reason to mistrust their account of the sinkhole city and its plight.

#### The "Rux Brothers"

Doran

> I met this old buzzard at the schoolhouse in Nain when we were just boys. Those days, my diminutive stature earned me more than a few taunts from the other children; so you can imagine my surprise and appreciation when the tallest kid in school started standing up for me.

Nevel

> Indeed. I saw a little brother in Doran back then. And so, my brother he became. As the years went by and we stumbled from adolescence into adulthood, we decided to chase our fortunes and glory together on the open road as merchants of the wide Plateau. And it's been a fun ride ever since. Though we are brothers from different mothers, we are siblings all the same.

Doran

> Still, I remain convinced: the banner would look better if it read "Styme Brothers" instead!

Nevel dismisses this claim with a cheeky grin before sipping a draught of wine.

> Yes, Doran. I'm sure you do …

#### Arcturel

Doran

> Ah, yes. The Glittering City. Those of you unfamiliar with Arcturel should know that it is built among the walls of a massive sinkhole that stretches from the surface village of Brimtown to the rough-and-tumble planks of Rock Bottom in the Pathways below. There's no other city in the world quite like it.

Nevel

> It is truly an intriguing place — that is, if you can manage the incessant dust and rust that comes along with it. Perhaps your intrepid troupe would have some insights to offer the troubled locals regarding their so-called Renegade Construct.

#### Renegade Chessman

Doran

> Certainly, you've seen a Chessman in your travels. They happen to be the most prized automatons on the Arcturian market these days. An ingenious Hulg'run fellow named Vartholomew Chess is responsible for their exceptional design. This is the first time I've heard of a Chessman falling short of its expectations; they're rather remarkable machines, if you ask me.

Nevel

> Truly, the modern Inkaro trade thrives because of the workforce these tireless constructs provide. And as you can imagine, a complication for the pearl industry is a complication for the entire Arctus Plataeau. Hopefully, this haywire Chessman is an isolated incident. House Cevher has enough problems on their hands these days without faulty automatons killing innocent citizens when they least expect it.

#### House Cevher

Nevel

> In all my decades, I've known House Cevher to be a competent and conscientious partner. As one of the Plateau's most esteemed jewel merchants, I consider my alliance with the Trading House to be a rather necessary one. I grow concerned whenever I hear of troubles within House Cevher's operations. And it seems they're having a little trouble in Arcturel these days.

Doran

> No doubt. Rallyhome was full of rumors about impending issues, from the case of the Renegade Construct to a mounting unrest among the House Cevher miners. It seems like they're having a bad season. But I don't grow too concerned for the Trading Houses — they have all the resources they need to bounce back from a little slump. I'm sure they'll be just fine …

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### What the Party Already Knows
>
> One or more of the party members may already be familiar with the sinkhole city of Arcturel. If the players ask or the characters would otherwise know, consider the following skill checks:
>
> **Society (DC 12)** The character is familiar with the general history of Arcturel and its location near the southern edge of the Golden Flats. Among various details, the character understands Arcturel to be governed by a syndicate of business owners, they know of Arcturel's relatively lawless nature, and its status as the region's only source of Inkaro Pearls. Characters with **Knowledge: Subterranea** or **Knowledge: Trade** have advantage on this check.
>
> - Success - Other than Inkaro Pearls, Arcturel is a source of a wide variety of rare resources and raw materials (both magical and mundane), and House Cevher has maintained a foothold on this industry for most of Arcturel's storied history. Furthermore, the inventor of the Chessmen constructs — a gifted artificer named Vartholomew Chess — lives in Upper Arcturel's "Balconies" district.
> - Critical Success: The character is familiar with Arcturel as a haven for adventurers and would-be profiteers, and knows of a talented Vrjnhar bard named Gullivhar Paras who frequents a tavern in Upper Arcturel known as Rallyhome. They say the Oaken bard knows anything and everything about what happens in the sinkhole city, so he may serve as a potential point of contact.
>
> **Arcana (DC 15)** The character is aware of the importance of Inkaro Pearls to the Arctus Plateau, and knows that these nacreous orbs are harvested from dangerous creatures called Mootaps, which lair in pools of arcane water within the Sinkhole Depths.
>
> - Characters with **Knowledge: Monsters**, **Knowledge: Subterranea**, or **Knowledge: Trade** have advantage on this check.

### Trade with the Rux Brothers

If the characters are interested in buying or selling any goods, Doran and Nevel are eager to trade with the party on behalf of the rest of the caravan.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Goods & Services
>
> The Rux Brothers caravan is currently stocked with ample goods gathered from all over the Arctus Plateau, and can supply the characters with the following in exchange for their standard prices:
>
> - Any light or medium armor, up to a quantity of 4 each.
> - Any simple or ranged weapon, up to a quantity of 4 each.
> - Any martial weapon, up to a quantity of 2 each — excluding glaives, greatswords, halberds, lances, pikes, tridents, blowguns, and hand crossbows.
> - A wide variety of Adventuring Gear, Tools, and Trade Goods.
> - Most spell components that cost 1 gold piece or less (at your discretion).
>
> Doran and Nevel are also willing to spend up to 400 gp to the party in exchange for any used goods the characters wish to sell them, but will only offer half of each item's original purchase price.
>
> - Each character can convince the Rux Brothers to pay full price for their own used goods with either a successful **Deception (DC 15)** or **Diplomacy (DC 15)** check. Characters with **Knowledge: Trade** have advantage on these checks.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> Following their encounter with the concerned merchants, the party can journey to Arcturel to find out more about the so-called Renegade Construct that is rumored to be on the loose. Once the characters reach the surface-level Arcturel suburb known as Brimtown, they'll encounter the events of [[Surface Matters]].
