# Presenting the Evidence

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This social event tasks the party with presenting the evidence they've gathered to Zodi Trask and Larissa Toth, and provides a branching-out point for the conclusion of the quest's narrative. In this event, the party can:
>
> - Journey to Lower Arcturel for a semi-public meeting with Zodi Trask and Larissa Toth, along with Lucent (if the characters decided to absolve the Downsiders of their erroneous crimes) or Hew (if they decided to condemn the Downsiders and side with Silver Beam).
> - Present the evidence they've gathered throughout Arcturel and the Sinkhole Depths, whether in support for or against the Downsiders.
> - Convince Zodi Trask about the veracity of their claims, one way or another.
> - Decide upon their next steps and determine the overall event outcome in the process.

### Arranging a Meeting

"Presenting the Evidence" presumes the party has gathered enough information to support an argument for either exonerating or condemning the Renegade Construct known as Hew. With evidence in hand, the characters journey back to Lower Arcturel to meet with Zodi Trask, who has been patiently awaiting an update.

The characters conveniently reunite Zodi during a moment of contemplation in Lower Arcturel, a stone's throw from both his apartment in the Dives and the scene of the so-called crime.

> [!info] Social
> #### Prelude to an Inquest
>
> The party must approach Zodi Trask with their intentions of presenting the evidence and formalizing an inquest about the case of the Renegade Construct. Trask explains the following to the characters:
>
> - A fair inquest will require both parties to meet on neutral ground. Trask nominates this very spot as a location for the arbitration.
> - During the inquest, Trask will hear the party's argument and review their evidence, which will be weighed against testimony from the plaintiff Larissa Toth (as corroborated by other locals of Arcturel).
> - The Chessman dubbed Hew will either be released or decommissioned based on Trask's ruling.
>
> Once the party members have agreed to Trask's terms, he'll ask them to gather here again at this spot in two hours, along with Larissa Toth and other key witnesses.
>
> - If the party plans to exonerate Hew and the Downsiders, Lucent will join them as a key witness. The other Downsiders will remain behind in Rock Bottom, well out of sight of their Silver Beam enemies.
> - If the party plans to condemn Hew and the Downsiders, any surviving Chessmen will remain in (or escape to) Aberin's Folly to begin fortifying their camp.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Timely Preparations
>
> The characters now have two hours to prepare for the inquest. Advance Time by 2 Hours once the party's preparations are complete.

### The Inquest Begins

Two hours later, the party gathers once more with Zodi Trask in Lower Arcturel, this time with Larissa Toth in attendance. Since Silver Beam has their own missing Chessman and a vested interest in the entire ordeal, Larissa is insistent upon hearing the so-called evidence for herself.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The air is thick with anticipation as you make your way to the catwalk outside Hob Korell's stables, where Zodi Trask and Larissa Toth survey the scene of the crime — the precise spot where Kellan Lorde and the Renegade Construct tumbled to their doom. Beyond these two officials, the twinkling lights of lower Arcturel scintillate in the darkness as far as the eye can see, and the clamorous din of the sinkhole city echoes with a relaxing ceaselessness.
>
> Larissa Toth glances up as you approach, surveying your party with measured aplomb.
>
> > They're here.
>
> Zodi Trask turns to greet you.
>
> > Welcome back. I assume you've met the esteemed Larissa Toth.
>
> The Railen executive nods in your direction as Trask continues:
>
> > It seems the time has come to stand and deliver. Tell us, what information have you found about the Renegade Construct?
>
> Larissa Toth speaks once more:
>
> > Indeed, the people of Arcturel have a right to know if a killer lurks among them. What news, friends?

> [!info] Social
> #### The Party's Evidence
>
> The party must present their evidence to Zodi Trask and Larissa Toth, whether in favor of exonerating the Downsiders or condemning them to an unfortunate fate. As part of this presentation, the characters will also be tasked with various skill checks, as described below.
>
> #### Exonerating the Downsiders
>
> In order to convince Zodi Trask of Hew's innocence, the party must present at least 3 pieces of the following evidence in support of Hew and the Downsiders' exoneration:
>
> - One or more [[Inkaro Pearl, Entropic]], particularly those removed from the Chessmen by Ifton Shepp.
> - A piece of [[Chessman Scrap Metal]] from the Scrapyard bearing the Vartholomew Chess trademark.
> - Celestial Remains from the encounter with the Vorg and Skither at the Scrapyard.
> - The [[Inkaro Pool Mandate]] obtained from the Inkaro Pools.
> - [[Silver Beam Schematics]] obtained from either the Scrapyard or the Silver Beam [[Side Offices]].
> - Information gained from personnel at Silver Beam HQ via the [[Telecognition]] spell.
> - Verbal testimony from Lucent.
>
> The characters must also succeed on 1 or more of the following skill checks:
>
> - **Diplomacy (DC 13)** To convince Trask that Hew and the Downsiders aren't responsible for their malfunctions.
> - **Intimidation (DC 18)** To convince Trask that Silver Beam currently poses the greatest threat to Arcturel.
> - **Deception (DC 18)** To trick Trask into believing that a piece of irrelevant evidence is pertinent to Hew's absolution.
> - **Arcana (DC 15)** To impress upon Trask the extent to which the Inkaro Pearls must have been tampered with by a spellcaster with intent.
> - **Performance (DC 15)** To rousingly convince Trask about the sheer weight of the evidence that's been presented.
>
> #### Condemning the Downsiders
>
> Alternately, in order to convince Zodi Trask of Hew's guilt, the party must present at least 1 piece of the following evidence in support of Hew and the Downsiders' condemnation:
>
> - An account of the Downsiders camp at Aberin's Folly, with physical evidence of the discarded [[Used Chessman Parts]] found in Rock Bottom.
> - The large Riding Lizard that was stolen by Rider during his escape, which can be found at Aberin's Folly and returned to Hob Korell (who promptly recognizes the missing steed).
> - An account of the disastrous Jurtak raid that occurred at the Inkaro Pools, with physical evidence of the encounter (such as a Jurtak item or bodily remains).
> - The erroneous and misleading testimony of a false witness.
>
> The characters must also succeed on 2 or more of the following skill checks:
>
> - **Diplomacy (DC 13)** To convince Trask that Hew and the Downsiders are responsible for their malfunctions.
> - **Intimidation (DC 18)** To convince Trask that the Renegade and other Chessmen currently poses a great threat to Arcturel.
> - **Deception (DC 18)** To trick Trask into believing that a piece of irrelevant evidence is pertinent to Hew's castigation.
> - **Arcana (DC 15)**To adequately impress upon Trask the inscrutable nature of Inkaro Pearls.
> - **Performance (DC 15)** To rousingly convince Trask about the sheer weight of the crime as it was witnessed.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Orchestrating the Inquest
>
> Since several NPCs are directly involved in this social encounter, here's some advice to keep the conversation going. If you find the parley is drifting too far from the matter at hand, use the personal motivations of each of the key NPCs as pivot points for the conversation:
>
> - Don't waste time. Time is money, and Larissa Toth has very little to waste with antics in Lower Arcturel.
> - Give everyone a chance to speak. Zodi Trask is a representative of the people's will, and the people of Arcturel believe in fair justice.
> - Remember the real victims. Lucent is quick to offer words of confirmation and support for any of the party's arguments. Alternately, a Lower Arcturel bystander could have something to say that paints a clearer picture of the accident.

One of two scripted moments will occur once the criteria for "Exonerating the Downsiders" or "Condemning the Downsiders" has been fulfilled, based on the party's actions.

#### Downsiders Exonerated:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Larissa Toth's eyes narrow with derision as you provide your closing statements, as if she's well aware of the unsavory shadow this evidence has cast upon her precious Silver Beam Consortium.
>
> After a moment of protracted silence, Zodi Trask speaks with unfaltering authority:
>
> > It seems to me that the evidence is quite clear: these tainted inkaro pearls have something to do with the malfunctions, rather than the construct's programming or a fault of their maker's. The bad pearls are to blame. Furthermore, it stands to reason we have a lot to learn about these awakened Chessmen. They're not mere automatons any more. I dare say, this evidence also suggests that Silver Beam hasn't been entirely forthcoming about certain operations …
>
> Larissa Toth shoots the quarryman a scornful gaze and offers him a disrespectful sneer:
>
> > I dare say the evidence is threadbare at best. These vigilantes you've hired are as reckless as Chess and his constructs. Silver Beam has nothing to do with the malfunction of these automatons, and this slander against my company simply won't stand. I deny these feckless allegations, and plan on launching a new investigation of my own.
>
> She looks over your group with disdain.
>
> > Our business here is done. Good luck cleaning up the mess, Trask. I have my own problems to deal with. Now, if you'll excuse me …
>
> Toth snaps to attention and storms off, leaving you in the gregarious company of Zodi Trask, whose skeptical gaze follows the Railen executive as she skulks away to the elevator. Once she's out of earshot, Trask speaks to you under his breath:
>
> > If I didn't know any better, I'd say that arrogant was hiding something. Care to help me find out?

#### Akon Attunement: Against the Consortium

If the party chooses to exonerate the Downsiders, each character advances their **Attunement: Akon (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

#### Downsiders Condemned:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> After a moment of silence, Larissa Toth speaks up:
>
> > I think we've seen enough. It's quite evident that the Renegade Construct acted of its own volition, and that these Chessmen can't be trusted to maintain the safety of Arcturel's people. Silver Beam is suspending all activity of our Chessian models, effective immediately. And we'll be recommending that the rest of Arcturel do the same. Our town is in your debt, friends.
>
> She offers you a respectful nod. Meanwhile, Zodi Trask scratches his chin as equal measures of doubt and relief cross his furry face:
>
> > If only we'd have seen it coming sooner, we could have pulled the bad constructs from active duty and spared Kellan Lorde his untimely fate. At least the city can find some closure in all this. But ol' Varth is going to have a hell of a time trying to move his product from now on.
>
> Toth quickly offers a word of caution:
>
> > If things don't change, Master Trask, I fear Vartholomew Chess is only going to have more problems on his hands, as more Chessmen continue to malfunction and more accidents continue to happen.
>
> Finally, Trask looks to you with unmistakable appreciation:
>
> > You've nearly earned your reward for a job well done. Now, are there any loose ends we should worry about?

#### Akon Attunement: Against the Constructs

If the party chooses to condemn the Downsiders, each character advances their **Attunement: Cora (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Aftermath

In the wake of the inquest, one of two downstream events will take place: [[Storming the Consortium]] or [[Checkmate for Chessmen]]. Before those events begin, the characters have a moment to gather their thoughts and discern the intentions of their allied NPCs.

> [!info] Social
> #### Trask's Plan
>
> In order for Zodi Trask and House Cevher to launch a formal complaint against Silver Beam on behalf of all of Arcturel, the party must infiltrate Silver Beam HQ to gather one last piece of irrefutable evidence that connects Silver Beam to the Entropic Pearls and the ongoing malfunctions of Chessmen.
>
> #### Toth's Vengeance
>
> If the party has yet to do so, Larissa Toth tasks them with raiding the Downsiders encampment at Aberin's Folly and either the capture or execution of the Renegade Construct. Toth would prefer a clean slate, and is willing to offer a bonus if all four of the Downsiders constructs are executed or dismantled.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> If the party decided to exonerate Hew and the Downsiders, the characters will proceed to the events of [[Storming the Consortium]]. We recommend not starting the 'Storming the Consortium' event until the party is ready to go. At the top of that event, the characters will find themselves downstairs at the [[Glimmer Lounge]] in Upper Arcturel, where Chamberlain and the other Chessmen will help the party scrutinize their plans in the moments before the final assault against Silver Beam.
>
> Alternately, if the party decided to condemn the Downsiders, the characters continue the saga in [[Checkmate for Chessmen]], where they'll be poised to assault the hideout at Aberin's Folly from the camp's outskirts.
