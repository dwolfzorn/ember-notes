# Surface Matters

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This social and combat event introduces the party to the local tensions between Chessmen constructs and humanoids in Arcturel. In this event, the characters can:
>
> - Choose whether or not to intervene during a hate crime against a Chessman construct and its owner, a young Arcturian forager named Torra.
> - Listen to various rumors, including stories about the missing Renegade Construct and Arcturel's current economic troubles.
> - Learn about a couple of persons of interest that reside in Arcturel's Upper and Lower districts, who might provide key insights into the rumors at hand.

**Torra** (Chaotic Good, Arcturian Human, she/her) is a scrappy forager who collects herbs, spell components, and random salvage from the upper reaches of Arcturel and the wilderness beyond the sinkhole brim. Her [[Chessman]] companion — a refurbished model she's lovingly named "Rusty" — serves her dutifully and without malfunction.

**Arcos** (Chaotic Neutral, Arcturian Human, he/him) is a lanky but handsome scoundrel who loves to stir up trouble when he isn't drowning his misdirected woes in ale at the local tavern. Armed with a bucket of paint, he's joined by 6 other unarmed mob members during this loosely-coordinated effort to harass Torra and her Chessman construct.

Treat Torra, Arcos, and the other mob members as [[Arcturian]]. One of the unnamed mob members is armed with a knife, while another carries a second bucket of red paint.

### If the Party Intervenes

If they party decides to intervene, they must coerce Arcos and the assembled mob into standing down from their assault, which is readily accomplished via the skill checks featured below.

> [!info] Social
> #### Defusing the Mob
>
> Any character who makes a successful**Diplomacy (DC 15)**, **Deception (DC 15)**, or **Intimidation (DC 15)** check is able to convince the mob to stand down.
>
> - Characters with **Knowledge: Crime**, **Knowledge: Machines**, or **Knowledge: Warfare** have advantage on these checks.
> - Characters who brandish lethal weapons also have advantage on Intimidation checks against the mob.
>
> Alternatively, characters capable with **Talent: Rune: Control** may be able to use it creatively to calm the crowd individually or as a group.

If the party manages to defuse the situation, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Having been robbed of their pointless ire, the various members of the mob begin to slowly back away from the scene. Some of the ne'er-do-wells look almost ashamed of themselves, while others remain notably suspicious. Nevertheless, Arcos and the thugs begin to retreat. Propriety has won this day, and the teenage girl wastes no time before cleaning the crimson stains from her beloved construct before addressing you:
>
> > Thanks. That got a little scary there for a minute. I really appreciate you showing up when you did … While you're here, care to take a look at my wares? I have trinkets for sale, with fair prices for altruists like yourselves. And I could use the coin to have my Chessman detailed.
>
> She continues wiping the red paint away from the nooks and crannies of the construct's chassis. Meanwhile, you take note of the market stall behind her, stocked with a curious array of fresh herbs and Arcturian trinkets.

#### Ragen Attunement: Protected Torra's Construct

If the party manages to successfully protect Torra's construct Rusty from the mob, each character advances their **Attunement: Ragen (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

`[[/outcome protected]]`

> [!info] Social
> #### Speaking with Torra
>
> The characters are now free to chat with Torra, who is happy to provide the following rumors and information:
>
> - The reason for the mob's confrontation and the red paint. A Chessman construct owned by House Cevher is said to have gone haywire and killed a miner in the Dives a few weeks back. The incident has rattled many of the locals, particularly those who already had a tenuous hold on their emotional state — like the down-and-out or the displaced.
> - A brief history of her time as a young forager. 17 years young and orphaned at an early age, Torra has made a good go of her career as a forager and scavenger of Arcturel's surrounding environs for a couple of years now. She's well-known among frequent travelers for her rotating collection of esoteric trinkets for sale.
> - The basics about her Chessman companion named Rusty. Torra found Rusty's damaged body in a garbage heap two years ago during one of her early outings, partially dismembered and fried from lightning damage. After dragging him out of the mess and gathering suitable replacement parts, she managed to put the Chessman back together with the help of a few locals. They've been inseparable since.
> - A job lead. Zodi Trask of House Cevher is looking to hire investigators for an official inquiry regarding the cause of the fatal Chessman incident. He can be found in Rallyhome when he isn't attending the mines in Lower Arcturel.
>
> A successful **Diplomacy (DC 11)** check validates the honesty of Torra's claims from her perspective, which by all accounts seems to be a measured and educated one (especially compared to Arcos' mob).
>
> - **Critical Success**: Torra obviously has something to prove, both to others and herself. Her history as an orphan has contributed to her standoffish nature as a loner; she'd never consider diplomacy as a way to deal with the mob, favoring a display of strength instead.
>
> #### Torra's Trinkets
>
> If the party wants to offer Torra their patronage, each character can spend 5 gp for one of Torra's Trinkets, which can be randomly selected via the [[Arcturian Trinkets]] table.
>
> Additionally, Torra has a selection of herbs for sale, many of which can be used for spell components. Her prices are firm, and a lot of the funds go towards repairing Rusty on a regular basis.

### If the Party Stands By

If they party decides to stand idly by, they will witness the following exchange:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You stand back to watch the scene unfold without intervening. The teenage girl and the greasy-haired thug exchange a few more hushed words before the girl spits in the assailant's face. Before you can blink, the young man pushes her to the ground as the rest of the mob rushes in with tools and clubs to pummel the Chessman.
>
> A moment later, the mob staggers back, sated. As the dust clears, you notice the Chessman has been battered enough to be broken. The girl sits alone, sobbing at the senseless violence, powerless to do anything about it. As the mob disperses, the greasy-haired youth approaches you with a look of smug satisfaction on his face.
>
> > She should have listened to reason. Everyone else in Arcturel knows those Chessmen can't be trusted.

#### Orbis Attunement: Didn't Protect Torra's Construct

If the party chose to stand by while Torra's construct Rusty was attacked by the mob, each character advances their **Attunement: Orbis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

`[[/outcome destroyed]]`

> [!info] Social
> #### Speaking with Arcos
>
> The characters are free to chat with Arcos (or another member of the mob), who is willing to provide the following rumors and information:
>
> - The reason for the mob's confrontation and the red paint. A Chessman construct owned by House Cevher is said to have gone haywire and killed a miner in the Dives a few weeks back. The incident has rattled many of the locals, particularly those who work in and around the constructs, like lower Arcturel miners and inkaro pearl farmers of the Sinkhole Depths.
> - The basics about Chessman constructs, from a very biased perspective. The famed constructs were designed by the artificer Vartholomew Chess, who lives in Upper Arcturel. Despite their ubiquity, Chessmen are becoming more and more dangerous and unpredictable. Arcos and other locals view the Chessmen as liabilities in terms of the safety and livelihood of the citizens of Arcturel.
> - A job lead. Zodi Trask of House Cevher is looking to hire investigators to track down the missing Renegade Construct to ensure it doesn't kill any other citizens of Arcturel. Trask can be found in Rallyhome when he isn't attending the mines in Lower Arcturel.
> - A brief history of his time in Brimtown. Arcos moved to the surface after being displaced from mining work by a Chessman in Lower Arcturel. Instead of digging for ore, he now spends his days as a cut-rate guide or sellsword. If the party wants to hire him for 5 gp a day, he's willing to join up with them (but his promises far outweigh his efforts).
>
> A successful**Diplomacy (DC 13)** check confirms the honesty inherent in Arcos' perspective, but does hint at the ignorance and bias that drives his actions. He doesn't seem to be an outright bad person, but bad circumstances have probably led him to make bad decisions.
>
> - **Critical Success**: There is likely some incident from Arcos' past that has already corrupted his pride and sense of self, and his aggression against Rusty and other Chessmen is probably the transference of that previous pain.
>
> Based on how friendly the characters are to Arcos, he can potentially be a point of contact in Brimtown for the party moving forward — although his rather bohemian and chaotic lifestyle means his appearances are less than consistent, and his haughty mindset exceeds his work ethic at every turn.

### Brimtown Rumors

After their encounter with Torra and the mob, the characters can optionally spend time gathering additional rumors in Brimtown before proceeding to Upper Arcturel.

> [!info] Social
> #### Gathering Additional Information
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Diplomacy (DC 13)** check is able to track down one of the following rumors via conversation with locals:
>
> - A few locals speak of inkaro pearl shortages, opting to use oil lanterns, candles, or torchlight to illuminate their dwellings at night while the local resource grows notably scarce for the first time in recent memory.
> - Rumor has it that the Renegade Construct in Lower Arcturel wasn't the only Chessman to start acting up. There isn't any hard evidence, but stories are floating through town about other constructs that have gone missing rather than malfunction. Maybe it's just thievery or hearsay. Times can be tough, after all.
> - They say the Sinkhole Depths are crawling with all sorts of strange, carnivorous creatures. If you journey too far from Rock Bottom, be sure to steer clear of the bone effigies left by the jurtak, or you may never see the light of Lantyr again.
> - Rumors abound regarding the Silver Beam Consortium, a group of Railen engineers and businessfolk who have made some impressive strides over the past months when it comes to their industry and holdings. Members of the Brimtown Watch claim that shipments to and from the Silver Beam headquarters tend to come at night, when the rest of the city is at rest.
> - Caravaners who've camped at Brimtown in recent weeks have spoken in hushed voices of a growing concern for House Cevher's ability to consistently supply certain goods like inkaro pearls, shadestone, adamantine, silver, and precious gemstones.
