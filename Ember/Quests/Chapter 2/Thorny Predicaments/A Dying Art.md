# A Dying Art

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> In this Exploration Event the party makes their way through [[Steed's Point]]. While traveling through this abandoned the town characters will:
>
> - Encounter [[Rattletrap, the Rickety Man]] and learn about them and Steed's Point.
> - Encounter [[Kali Andrella]] and her ensorcelled home.
> - Get [[Edivel Sprout]] the training they need to become a wizard.
>
> #### Area Walkthrough
>
> The party begins in the [[Steed's Point]] Scene, where the central gameplay of this Event transpires. A complete room-by-room description of the underground environment and the gameplay that occurs there is detailed in the [[Steed's Point]] Area Walkthrough.

### Encountering Rattletrap

If the party speaks to Rattletrap in Steed's Point Fields, he will engage in general conversation with the party, as noted in the area walkthrough. He can also share a few extra pieces of information specific to this quest in response to Edivel, as follows:

> [!info] Social
> #### A Conversation with Rattletrap
>
> Rattletrap is willing to share the following in response to additional questions from the party or Edivel:
>
> - Rattletrap believes that Kali Andrella is in the cottage in the center of the town and that she knows he is there — her traps do not trigger when he passes. Still, he has not seen or spoken to her since the town's fall and isn't sure how to get her attention.
> - Rattletrap thinks Kali has remained in Steed's Point because she also has a connection to his creator, Bertron Steed, who Kali saw as a father figure. He believes she has a strong drive to protect this place, even with so little of it left.
> - Rattletrap knows Kali's daughter, Moriah Foxhaven, but Kali and Moriah parted on somewhat bad terms. She hasn't been seen since before the fall of Steed's Point.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Kali's Location?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I remember Kali. Hard woman to forget. Used to go all over the Plateau solving problems with a handful of seeds, a newly carved wand, and a smile. Pretty sure she's still here, same as me, tucked into that house wrapped in thorns in the middle of town. Can't prove it or anything, but this whole place is full of the kind of nasty hedge bits that I think I remember she was good at, and they always leave me alone. Maybe that's her way of saying hello?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Contacting Kali?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Maybe bring her something she wants? Or make your way to her door and ask nice? There's some winding hedges to the north that I think lead right to her. If you're trying to hurt Steed's Point, she'll cut you down where you stand, but if not — she might welcome the company.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why is Kali still here?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Maybe she's like me, feels some kind of tie to the place? Or to Bertron? He's who this whole town was named after, Bertron Steed. Feels like if we all leave, we lose the last piece of him. Whatever her reason is, I'm sure she's doing good - those hedges come out with their thorns out whenever the gore birds or jurtak get too close to the middle of town, like she's protecting it.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Kali's Daughter?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Kali likes to think she's all alone in the world, but I know her daughter's out there somewhere. Moriah Foxhaven. Always with the hundred questions. Why is the grass golden? How come I can talk but the rattle man can't? Guess back then, I couldn't. I'd forgotten about that. Pretty sure she left before everything went wrong with Steed's Point - she and her ma had different ideas about how she should be living her life.

### Meeting Kali

> [!abstract] Kali Andrella
> **[[Kali Andrella]]**
>
> Level 1 · Unknown Unknown
>
> ![Kali Andrella](../../../assets/image084.webp)

Once the party has reached Kali, they may speak to her generally about Steed's Point as noted in the [[Kali's Cottage]] area walkthrough. When there is a pause in the conversation or party questions, Edivel interrupts:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> While Edivel appears to listen at least somewhat attentively to Kali's answers, they begin squirming as she continues to speak, finally blurting out, in a rush of words:
>
> > I'm sorry Kali Andrella I don't mean to interrupt your talk but I'm Edivel and I really need your help with vineweaving or else I think Brevin could end up just like Steed's Point. We're growing a bridge of vines to go across the canyons but I think the recent shaking of the earth might throw off what the druids have done so far - from everything I've heard, they have to be very precise to make the bridge grow properly.
>
> Kali looks the young agrimage up and down, and smiles slightly, the vines at her fingertips reaching toward them as she does, then slowly nods.
>
> > Brevin, you say? I don't know it, but what you're saying about the bridge sounds right - some vineweaving might be good to have in case of trouble. And when the earth shakes, it often means trouble. It was the shaking earth that gave the jurtak a way out of the depths to attack Steed's Point, destroy most of my home. And now I have to figure out what to do about it. Once I've done that, I'd be happy to help you out.

> [!info] Social
> #### The Situation At Hand
>
> Kali is focused on deciding what to do next about her situation in Steed's Point — until she does, she can't be of any help to Edivel. She can share the following, but always comes back to her big decision. With a `[[/check insight 15]]` check, characters know that Kali legitimately wants to help them, but can't focus until she figures her own situation out.
>
> - The vineweaving technique is a tricky one, hard to master on your own. Edivel will need training from her or another agrimage to learn it. They will also need a colorful flower found in Bramble Gully, Everbloom.
> - The [[Handcarved Vine Wand]] is indeed something she made. She used to make several magical items and trade them to those who visited Steed's Point.
> - Kali is willing to train Edivel, but only after she decides whether she wants to stay in Steed's Point or leave it. Until then, she's focused on her decision.
>
> Full answers below.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Vineweaving Technique?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Not surprised the vineweaving isn't going well, it took me a while to master it myself. I'm sure I could give you a few pointers, though. Something tells me you'd pick it up pretty fast. I just don't really have the time to teach you yet. Big decisions to make.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Vine Wand?
>
> **A:**
>
> > That definitely looks like one of mine, though it's been a few years since I carved for someone else. Used to make things like this for other agrimages or wizards looking to hone their craft, but with no one to trade with, I've almost forgotten a few of these techniques. I'll hold on to this, see if I can learn something from it one way or the other. No matter what I decide, this could come in handy.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Helping Edivel?
>
> **A:**
>
> > It's not that I don't want to help — I absolutely do. But it's hard to focus and teach when you're still figuring things out for yourself. Stay here in Steed's Point or leave it behind? I just can't quite figure out which would be right.

### Helping Kali Decide

With the party waiting for her to decide, Kali asks for help deciding what to do next.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > You seem to be a pretty determined group. Remind me of my baby girl, Moriah, wherever she is these days. And since I know you want me helping this young agrimage, maybe you can help me in return. I've been going back and forth for a while now about whether I should stick things out here or go back into the world, and sometimes it's good to get out of your own head. I'll lay it out for you and then you tell me: what do you think I should do?

> [!info] Social
> #### Reading Kali
>
> A character that makes a successful `[[/check insight 17]]` check recognizing the following about Kali:
>
> - She can be pushed toward staying by emphasizing the legacy of Steed's Point and the value of maintaining and or even rebuilding the settlement.
> - She can be pushed toward leaving by giving her somewhere else to focus her energy, such as settling down somewhere else, seeking out her daughter, or by convincing her that Steed's Point is fine without her.
>
> #### Influencing Kali
>
> The party can convince Kali to take a specific course with three successful checks to convince Kali. The party can use any of the following skill checks to achieve this:
>
> - A successful `[[/check performance 18]]` check to talk up the wonders of the Arctus Plateau outside of Steed’s Point.
> - A successful `[[/check arcana 18]]` check to convince Kali that Steed’s Point will survive without her if she sets up the right agrimagical items before she leaves.
> - A successful `[[/check persuasion 16]]` check to suggest a place to resettle, or to push a character's specific agenda for Kali's future.
> - A successful `[[/check intimidation 16]]` check to emphasize the danger of the Jurtak and wildlife in the area, either to push Kali to secure the settlement better, or to abandon it.
> - A successful `[[/check history 18]]` check to emphasize the importance of Steed's Point as an Arcturian settlement and hub for local Agrimages can push Kali toward rebuilding the town.
>
> - **Moriah's Note**: Giving Kali the [[A Farewell Note]] grants an automatic success, and pushes Kali toward abandoning Steed's Point to seek out her daughter.
> - **Knowledge: Trade** : Knowledge of local trade hubs and activity can be used to push Kali toward rebuilding or abandoning Steed's Point.
> - **Knowledge: Politics**: Speaking on the social and political climate of the region can be used to push Kali toward rebuilding or abandoning Steed's Point.
> - **Knowledge: Crafts**: Insight into the work needed can be used to push Kali toward rebuilding or abandoning Steed's Point.
> - **Knowledge: Monsters**: Understanding the dangers one might face in the area can be used to push Kali toward rebuilding or abandoning Steed's Point.
>
> #### Kali's Decision
>
> Once the party has finished attempting to influence Kali, she will take some time to consider their words, and what she wants to do. If the party didn't gather enough successful checks to push her toward a specific outcome, she will remain uncertain, and remain in Steed's Point until she decides.
>
> - **Kali is Convinced to Stay and Rebuild:** Kali decides to rebuild Steed's Point, with the party bidding goodbye in [[A Dying Art]].
> - **Kali is Convinced to Leave:** Kali decides to go to Nain, where she has a good friend, and settle there, leaving Steed's Point to fall but giving her a chance to live her life and possibly gather resources to rebuild it. The party can head out as she gathers her things in [[Helping Kali Pack]].
> - **Kali Can't Decide:** Kali decides to focus on the here and now and dig into her life in her cottage, staying in the safety of its walls while she decides what's best. The party can leave her to it in [[A Dying Art]].

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About leaving?
>
> **A:**
>
> > My baby girl's out there, like I said, and she has no idea her mama is holed up here. If I leave, I could go find her, maybe make up for lost time. And while I get along fine here for now, it might be nice to hear some voices other than my own, see the sights I used to when I traveled all over the plateau. Until you all got here, I don't think I realized just how much I missed that. Only problem is, if I leave, who will keep this place from being destroyed once and for all?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About staying?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I know it seems like I've already decided to stay, seeing as I've been here for a while now, but I still feel like I did right after the battle, when Bertron was on the ground and folks were running for the hills, and all I could do was call down gore birds to fight the jurtak and protect what was left with the hedges in the ground. If I was really staying, I'd do it differently. Rebuild, add security, try to keep the jurtak away. I just don't know if it's better to try to build back or to leave it all behind.

### Staying In Steed's Point

Kali thanks the party for their help in getting her to decide to stay in Steed's Point:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > You're right. Steed's Point needs me. But not like this, like a scared child cowering and waiting to make up their mind. If I ever want Steed's Point to be more than this, I need to come out of hiding and start fixing this mess It'll take some time, but it's worth the doing.
>
> Kali reaches into her pocket and retrieves the handcrafted wand that Edivel gave her, presenting it to them with a flourish.
>
> > And Edivel Sprout, that town of yours, Brevin, it's also worth the saving, Edivel. So I'll teach you a little of what I know, and you can hang onto this wand. Just in case you need it. Now let's get some vineweaving in that spellbook of yours.

In addition to giving Edivel Sprout the [[Handcarved Vine Wand]], she offers the party a gift: a [[Reaching Vine]] which she has crafted over her time in Steed's Point.

> [!info] Social
> #### Taking a Stand
>
> If asked, Kali shares the following about her plans:
>
> - She plans to spend more time in and around Steed's Point outside of her cottage, working to fix the place up.
> - She looks forward to talking with Rattletrap more and thinks that together they might be able to fight back the jurtak and gore birds, but at the least she will invite him to her cottage.
> - She wants to get the word out to Moriah that she can be found in Steed's Point and asks the party to tell others, though she wants Moriah only to visit if she can do it safely.
>
> Expanded answers follow.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Kali's Plans?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I'll protect Steed's Point, like I have been, but I can't just protect it for me. I want to drive these creatures out, build the place back to what it used to be. Think I got a little too comfortable just being within these walls. And don't worry, Edivel - if the jurtak or those gore birds try to mess with me, they'll learn some of the new agrimagic tricks I've been developing with all my free time. Can you say exploding roots? In a year or so, I'll have that one perfected, can teach it to you.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Relationship with Rattletrap?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I knew Rattletrap was out there, but the way you talk about him, like he was actually speaking back to you? That's something new. I'll admit, I'm curious to know what happened there, and even more than that, it would be nice to have someone else to talk to, maybe reminisce a bit about the way Steed's Point used to be.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Relationship with Moriah?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I wonder what Moriah thinks happened to me. I tried to get word out when Steed's Point fell, was hoping she might come in and turn the tide, but the message never got out. I wonder if you might spread the word a bit, up in Brevin or wherever else you go in the Arctus Plateau, let her know I'm here and she's always welcome back. I'd say as long as she can make it here safely, but Moriah always knew how to take care of herself.

Kali remains in Steed's Point, but can be found in varying locations around the settlement.

#### Mayis Attunement: Kali Rebuilds

If Kali is convinced to stay and rebuild Steed's Point, each character advances their **Attunement: Mayis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Leaving Steed's Point

Kali thanks the party for their help in getting her to actually make the move to Nain and leave Steed's Point behind.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > You're right. I've been living in the past. And staying here doesn't bring Bertron Steed back, or make my daughter come home, or fix any of the broken homes around this place. I have a friend in Nain — think I might head there for a bit, maybe get some folks to help me come back and build something new. That's what Bertron would've done.
>
> Kali reaches into her pocket and retrieves the handcrafted wand that Edivel gave her, presenting it to them with a flourish.
>
> > And since I'm starting fresh, I'll leave this old wand with you, Edivel, hope that it does you a bit of good. And don't you worry, I'll teach you a bit of the vineweaving to go along with it. If the rest of you don't mind waiting for just a moment, I'll get Edivel here up to speed.

In addition to giving Edivel the [[Handcarved Vine Wand]], Kali also offers the party a gift — a [[Reaching Vine]], which she won't need in Nain.

> [!info] Social
> #### A New Beginning
>
> If asked, Kali shares the following on her plans going forward:
>
> - She plans to reconnect with an old friend in Nain and possibly get others in Nain to come back with her to Steed's Point to revitalize it in the future.
> - She worries what will happen to Steed's Point without her there, but is cheered by the idea that at least Rattletrap will be around to let her know if she is needed.
> - She wants to get the word out to Moriah that she can be found in Nain and asks the party to share the info with those they see during their travels.
>
> Full answers below.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Kali's Plans?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I used to get around the Plateau a lot, so I should still have a few friends in Nain. Gonna look up my old pal Zenodora — see if she's still running her General Store. And if she isn't…let's just say I got a long list of folks who'll probably be happy to see me. I'm gonna need 'em all if I want to get a group together to come back this way and reclaim Steed's Point for the future.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What Happens to Steed's Point?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I'm not sure. I've been keeping an eye on things, but then so has Rattletrap, I suppose, so maybe nothing changes at all. Or maybe the jurtak run the whole thing over. My guess is that either way Rattletrap stays around here, so if things get too bad, he can let me know.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Relationship with Moriah?
>
> **A:**
>
> > It would be nice to see Moriah after all this time. I tried to get word out when Steed's Point fell, was hoping she might come in and turn the tide, but the message never got out. I wonder if you might spread the word a bit, up in Brevin or wherever else you go in the Arctus Plateau, let her know I'm heading to Nain and she might want to come visit.

Kali relocates to Nain.

#### Cora Attunement: Kali Resettles

If Kali is convinced to relocate to Nain, each character advances their **Attunement: Cora (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Kali Undecided

Kali thanks the party for trying to help her make a decision, even if it wasn't a successful effort:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > Harder decision than it seems like, I know. But you've given me a few new things to think about, so I appreciate that. And as a thank you, I suppose I can take a little time to teach Edivel a few tricks of the vineweaving trade. Just talking it all out a bit helped me get a bit of focus, so don't worry, you're getting my best.
>
> Kali beckons an exhilarated Edivel over and opens their spellbook.
>
> > Don't worry — it won't take too long. And if you want to come back and visit, you're welcome anytime.

> [!info] Social
> #### Staying Put
>
> If asked, Kali shares the following about her plans:
>
> - She will continue weighing whether to stay or go, but will remain in the cottage in the meantime. With her new supplies, she may be able to turn the tide against the jurtak one day.
> - It would be great to see Moriah, but it isn't safe for her to visit. She asks that if the party sees Moriah, they let her know that Kali is okay, but to stay away.
>
> Full answers below.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Kali's Plans?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I'm not quite sure what I'll do, but with these new supplies, I won't need to figure it out for a while, can stay here and weigh things out. Who knows, maybe I'll get the better of the jurtak one of these days.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Relationship with Moriah?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I tried to get word out to Moriah when Steed's Point fell, but it never got to her. I'd like her to know I'm okay. Not going anywhere, at least not for now. Doubt she was planning on coming by, but you tell her to stay back until this place is a little safer. I can take care of myself.

Kali remains permanently in Steed's Point.

#### Orbis Attunement: Kali Undecided

If Kali remains undecided about her fate, each character advances their **Attunement: Orbis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.
