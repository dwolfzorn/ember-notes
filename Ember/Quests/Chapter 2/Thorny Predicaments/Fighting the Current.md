# Fighting the Current

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> In this Exploration Event, the party makes their way through the [[Splinter Canyons]], either heading north toward [[Bramble Gully]] or south in search of Edivel's spellbook. Whichever way they go, they face the increasingly dangerous rapids of the Splinter Canyons — which they can face head on or try to avoid by climbing above them through the Canyons' rocky ledges.

#### Region Map Update

In the future, the region map will change to show flowing water that matches the event text noted here.

### Seeking Safety

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Edivel surveys the surroundings, then turns to face you, worry evident in he features, despite his best efforts to remain positive.
>
> > Okay, so, I'm not an expert adventurer or anything, so if you've got a better idea, say so, but I think we're either going to need to pick our way across the cliffside, or, cross the rapids. I don't really like either option, but staying here isn't any better.
> >
> > Thoughts?

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Surveying the Options
>
> Characters with **Wilderness (DC 16, Passive)** or making a successful **Wilderness (DC 14)** check note that there are dangers to both routes: if they make their way across the cliffs they will be at the mercy of falling rocks, unstable ground, and possibly territorial creatures riled up by recent events. If they decide to brave the rapids the threats are fewer, but no less dire, as debris in the water and a general drowning risk will be constant threats.

### Climbing the Cliffs

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Planning an Approach
>
> Before setting off, members of the party can use their knowledge to make the group's progress easier:
>
> - With a successful `[[/check perception 18]]`, any character can note places where the ground is uneven or slippery and avoid them. The entire party has advantage on the survival check to traverse the cliffs.
> - With a successful `[[/check nature 17]]`, any character can note the location of vines and grasses on the cliffs that provide sturdy handholds. The entire party has advantages on dexterity checks and saves.

If the party chooses this path, they find the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You walk behind each other, weaving along the narrow path as it takes you above the canyon floor. The ground is slippery and the height makes every step a precarious one, but the true danger is less from what is ahead of you than what hangs from above, as showers of pebbles make it clear that pieces of the ledges overhead could fall at any moment.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Traversing the Ledges
>
> To make their way through the ledges, the party must succeed on a a sequence of four challenges listed on the Climbing Hazards table below. These are group challenges - for all checks and saves, the entire party makes a roll. If at least half the group succeeds, the whole group succeeds. If not, the whole group fails.
>
> Roll on the table below four times and challenge all members of the party to deal with each encountered obstacle.

|  | #### Group Climbing Hazards |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | #### Narrow Ledges  The ground of the ledge is hard to step on without falling. The party must make a `[[/save dexterity 17]]` saving throw; if the save is failed, they fall into the waters below and must face the rapids. |
| 2 | #### Desperate Leap  The path runs out; the party must a navigate a 20 foot jump across to the next ledge. The jump is into difficult terrain; the party must succeed on a `[[/check dexterity 15]]` check or they fall into the waters below and must face the rapids. |
| 3 | #### Plummeting Boulders  A set of boulders fall from above, right into the path of the party. If the save is failed, the party must also succeed on a `[[/save dexterity 15]]` saving throw or they fall into the rapids. |

#### Cora Attunement: Climbing the Cliffs

Any character who successfully navigates the Splinter Canyons by climbing the cliffs advances their **Attunement: Cora (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Facing the Rapids

If the party chooses this path, or ends up in the rapids as the result of a fall, they find the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The water picks up speed, tossing and turning its way through the canyons as a rushing, surging river. Rocks and pieces of fallen carts caught in the water's path are dashed against the sides of the canyon as if they were nothing more than twigs and pebbles, and the echoing water almost roars as it slams its way between the canyon walls, as if it had a voice of its own.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Braving the Currents
>
> To make their way through the currents, the party must make movement checks, which differ based on whether the party is going north (moving against the current) or south (moving with the current).
>
> These are **group challenges** — for all checks and saves, the entire party makes a roll. If at least half the group succeeds, the whole group succeeds. If not, the whole group fails.
>
> Any party member with a swim speed (whether innate or granted by an object or spell) has advantage on all checks while their swim speed is active.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Swimming Against the Current (North)
>
> If the party is heading north, against the current, they must succeed on two `[[/check athletics 16]]` movement checks to summon the strength to move forward against the powerful rapids.
>
> - If they succeed on a check, they stay afloat, but must deal with one of the dangers from the Canyons Rapids Peril table below.
> - If they fail on a check, they must succeed on a `[[/save strength 15]]` saving throw or they are pulled fully into the water, taking `[[/damage 1d8 bludgeoning]]` from its pressure against their bodies. On a successful save, they take half damage. Whether or not the save was successful, they must reattempt the movement check.
>
> If the party fails a movement check twice in a row, or at the GM's discretion, they are unable to make their way through the currents and must try going above instead, via [[Fighting the Current]].

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Swimming With The Current (South)
>
> If the party is heading south, against the current, they must succeed on two successive `[[/check acrobatics 16]]` checks to summon the strength to move forward against the powerful rapids.
>
> - If they succeed on a check, they stay afloat, but must deal with one of the dangers from the Canyons Rapids Peril table below.
> - If they fail on a check, they must succeed on a `[[/save dexterity 15]]` saving throw or they are tossed by the rapids into the side of the canyons, taking `[[/damage 1d8 bludgeoning]]` from the impact. On a successful save, they take half damage. Whether or not the save was successful, they must reattempt the movement check.
>
> If the party fails a movement check twice in a row, or at the GM's discretion, they are unable to make their way through the currents and must try going above instead, via [[Fighting the Current]].
