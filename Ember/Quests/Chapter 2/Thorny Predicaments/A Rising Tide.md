# A Rising Tide

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> In this Exploration Event, the party encounters [[Edivel Sprout]] who is trapped in the [[Splinter Canyons]] as water begins to pour into the area, flooding it. the water rises. By making use of tools and the environment the characters can:
>
> - Locate Edivel, and figure out how to reach him, and assist him in escaping the flooding canyon.
> - Decide if they want to assist Edivel in recovering his lost spell book.

#### Canyons Vista

The Canyons Vista for this scene has a more dramatic water surge than noted in the text — it will likely change in future.

### Finding Edivel

Characters can't see Edivel from their position in the Canyons, but they can narrow down where the Thornling is.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Edivel's Location
>
> Characters can locate Edivel's location by searching the canyons with a successful `[[/check investigation 20]]` check and 30 minutes of work. Failure means the party has not narrowed down Edivel's location, but still spent the time. The party will automatically find Edivel after three failed searches, but suffers an effect on the Canyon Catastrophes table below.
>
> Doing any of the following checks beforehand reduces the DC of the above investigation check by 2. Each check be done only once, for a maximum DC reduction of 6. The actions take 10 minutes each and are:
>
> - A `[[/check perception 18]]` check made to narrow down the origin of Edivel's echoing voice. If characters speak to Edivel again and/or get the thornling to call out to locate the echo, they have advantage on this check.
> - A `[[/check nature 17]]` check to look for signs of Edivel's fall and use it to figure out their likely trajectory after falling. If the party saw Edivel fall in [[The Fall's Gonna Kill You]], they have advantage on this check.
> - A `[[/check survival 16]]` check to narrow down what is around Edivel, working with the thornling to identify any landmarks they can see compared to what the party sees.

### Freeing the Agrimage

Once the party reaches the aspiring [[Agrimage Circle]], they find that Edivel is, in fact, stuck in place.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Edivel comes into sight as you round one of the Splinter Canyons' many winding corners, their foot wedged deeply into a narrow crack in the middle of the rock. "My heroes," the agrimage says, waving at you all and gesturing to their trapped foot.
>
> > Thank you so much for coming down to help! Usually, I can get myself out of trouble, but this time I was actually starting to worry about ending up under water.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### The Waters Rise
>
> From this point on, the party faces the rising water levels of Splinter Canyons. Each time the party does a check, roll a `[[/roll d4]]` and consult[[Flash Flood]], going no higher than level 5.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Freeing Edivel
>
> Characters can remove Edivel's foot with a successful `[[/ability str 14]]` check, freeing them to move. Failure still free Edivel but deals `[[/damage 1d6 bludgeoning]]` to them and reduces their movement speed to half for the next 24 hours. While Edivel's leg is injured they also suffer disadvantage on actions that would require their leg.
>
> - **Medicine**: Assessing Edivel's leg with a successful `[[/check medicine 17]]` check allows the party to avoid injuring them, granting **+2 Boons**.
> - **Investigation**: Examining the way that Edivel is trapped with a successful `[[/check investigation 17]]` check grants **+2 Boons**.

### Getting to Safety

Edivel may be free, but the waters have continued to rise, blocking the path that the party took down to the canyons.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > Thank you for coming to my recue, friends! Now maybe I can help you. I 'm not sure how you got down here, but if it's back that way, there's no good way through. But don't worry - I've got a way out of here that keeps all of our feet dry.
>
> Edivel gestures to a rock at the edge of the still-rising waters.
>
> > If we can get up there, we should be safe from the water for now - it never gets up that high. Once we're above the water, I can thank you properly, and maybe you can help me figure out what to do next?

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Climbing the Rocks
>
> To ascend the cliffs, characters must make a **Athletics (DC 15)** check. On success they are able to climb up to half their movement speed toward safety before repeating the skill check. On a failure, they lose their grip and fall back to their starting point.
>
> - **Climb Speed**: Characters with a climbing speed automatically succeed and can ascend at their full climbing speed toward safety.
> - **Equipment**: Characters with a [[Climber's Kit]] automatically succeed.
> - **Flight & Teleportation**: Characters with a fly speed, or using Spells such to fly, levitate, or teleport automatically succeed and can ascend as appropriate to the solution they used.
> - **Wilderness (DC 16)**: A competent survivalist notices roots jutting out from the freshly exposed canyon wall and gains **+2 Boons** on this check until their ascent is complete.
> - **Critical Success**: The character can ascend at their full movement speed until the next time they make this check.

### Edivel's Ask

Once the party has safely made it out of the canyon waters, read or paraphrase:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You clamber up onto a slightly higher section of the canyons - still far from the Golden Flats above, but safely away from the water that has rushed into the bottom section of the canyons. No sooner have your feet touched the ground than Edivel grins.
>
> > You were amazing back there! Real adventurers, like in the stories. And just when I need help - if I don't figure out how to make my vine weaving spell work, Brevin could be in serious trouble.

> [!info] Social
> #### Edivel's Request
>
> Edivel wants the party to accompany them as they attempt to master their vine weaving technique. They can share the following information:
>
> - They lost their spellbook, which drifted downstream. It is enchanted so that they can locate it, but they want to make sure they stay safe in the process.
> - They need a specific flower from Bramble Gully, an upstream section of the canyons that used to hold the Thornling community that moved to Brevin.
> - They need to master the vine weaving technique now because they believe the earthquakes may have weakened the still-growing bridge that Brevin is opening in an upcoming celebration - their technique might help shore up any damage.
> - They believe that Brevin's Steward, Triss Carpel, will reward the party if they help Edivel - Triss has a soft spot for them.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What do you need?
>
> **A:**
>
> > As you can maybe tell, I have a bit of a habit of getting myself into trouble… and usually I get back out again! Eventually, anyway.
> >
> > Usually I'm not in any hurry, but now? I need to figure out my vine weaving technique before Brevin unveils the bridge, and that means getting my spellbook back from downstream and the flower I need for the spell from upstream.
> >
> > If you're with me, I might have a shot to get it done in time.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** How does this help?
>
> **A:**
>
> > If you'd asked me before I fell down here, I would've said I wanted to impress Aythorn, show off how good an agrimage I can be.
> >
> > Now though? I'm worried that these earthquakes might cause trouble with Brevin's new bridge, and maybe even Brevin itself. Maybe I'm wrong, but if I'm not, I need to do something right?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Bramble Gully?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I used the wrong flower for the spell the last time I tried it - it's one of the reasons it went all wrong. Luckily, Bramble Gully's got tons of different flowers - the Thornlings who used to live there were big on flower displays and they left most of them when they moved on to Brevin. I know what I need will be there.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Spellbook?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The spellbook got away from me when I fell. I've got about 10 enchantments on it, so it should be safe and I can track it, but I still have to head downstream to pick it up and hope no gumtoad's stuck to it or anything like that.
