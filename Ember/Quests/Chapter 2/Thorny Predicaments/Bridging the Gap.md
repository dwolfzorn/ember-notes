# Bridging the Gap

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> In this exploration event, Brevin is being pulled by its waterlogged bridge into the Splinter Canyons. While Edivel goes to help the druids, the party must provide support for the town by helping with a series of emergencies.
>
> Once the final fate of the bridge is known, Aythorn appears, beginning a conversation that continues in the next event.

Edivel immediately pinpoints the shouting as coming from The Root, Brevin's central town square. They are concerned that Brevin may be under attack and take off running for the center of town, as rumors have spread about the attack on Helkas and they fear the worst. When characters arrive, the place is in chaos.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> In the center of town, a crowd has gathered on the large trunk at the heart of Brevin, standing on it as they point off in the direction of something you can't yet see. Suddenly, the platform shifts, creaking and cracking as it jolts closer to the edge of the canyons and throws several Thornlings to the ground.
>
> A voice calls out, cutting through the crowd.
>
> > This is what I mean! If we don't cut the bridge loose now, we're going to lose the whole town!
>
> The voice belongs to a stout little thornling who quickly pushes through the crowd, and takes a direct line to you.

> [!abstract] Triss Carpel
> **[[Triss Carpel]]**
>
> Level 1 · Unknown Unknown
>
> ![Triss Carpel](../../../assets/image154.webp)

If the party has already met Triss in [[Natural Wonders]] her response is familiar, and grateful.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Wonderful, you're back! We could use some good news right now. If we don't do something soon, the whole town is going to plunge into the canyons! I have no idea what you can do about it, but maybe you can work with Edivel and figure something out.

Otherwise, she introduces herself first.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Hello! Welcome! Hate to say you've come at a terrible time. I'm Triss Carpel, Steward of Brevin, which is currently sinking into the canyon thanks to our new bridge. If you've got any tricks or help you can lend, that would be superb!

### Talking to Triss

> [!info] Social
> #### Trouble in Town
>
> Triss updates the party on what is currently happening to Brevin:
>
> - Brevin was getting ready to unveil their new vine bridge when the entire town began sliding into the canyons. The slide is being caused by the vines of the bridge, which were dislodged by the earthquake. They are hanging down into the canyon and weighing down the city.
> - Because Brevin is a seed city, the entire city is interconnected at its roots - the vines are a real danger.
> - The druids are attempting to coax the vines back into place, but are running out of time. They are attuned to the vines, so what they need is support.
>
> Characters may have some knowledge of the situation as well.
>
> A character that succeeds on a `[[/check history 16]]` check know that the seed cities are completely interlinked. If the vines cannot be fixed, the only way to stop them from dragging the city into the canyons is to cut them loose.
>
> A character that succeeds on a `[[/check nature 16]]` check know that if the druids can get the vines to reach across the canyons, it will fix the problem.
>
> A character that succeeds on a `[[/check insight 16]]` check can confirm that Triss is telling the truth, but is somewhat understating the problem — the city is in definite danger of being destroyed if the vines are not fixed soon.
>
> Full answers below.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Situation at Hand?
>
> **A:**
>
> > All those recent earthquakes shook the vines loose from the formation that the druids coaxed them into and now they're all just hanging down into the canyons below, weighing down the rest of the town. I don't want to lose the bridge, but it's pulling all of Brevin down into the canyons, and we can't lose everything we've nurtured here!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Seed City Interconnection?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Seed cities are amazing because the whole place grows together and everything is interconnected, but there's one big issue. What affects one part of the city affects us all. Those vines are linked into the roots of this town and it's dragging all of us into the canyons below.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Fixing the Problem/The Druids?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The druids are connected to those vines and they can get the bridge to stretch back across, but they need to do it fast and not get distracted from their mission. Otherwise it won't matter what the bridge does - we'll need to cut it free or all be smashed to pieces below!

Once Triss has finished explaining the situation, Edivel decides to take action.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Edivel immediately straightens up to their full height and gestures at their spellbook with the hand holding their wand.
>
> > Triss, I can help! I met Kali Andrella – ***the*** Kali Andrella -and she taught me a new vineweaving technique. I think I can use it to reinforce the vines that have fallen.
> >
> > I'm sure the druids are working on getting the vines into a better position again, but this can buy them some more time. Please, let me try! And maybe my new friends can help you with things here in the meantime? They’ve already gotten me out of a lot of trouble!
>
> Triss looks a bit skeptical, but slowly nods.
>
> > Edivel, check in with the druids at the bridge. The rest of you, you're with me. We need to get everyone out of danger!

### Brevin Emergencies

As Edivel heads off to try to help with the vine weaving, Triss asks the party to keep Brevin safe – if there are any emergencies, it can take time and energy away from those trying to save the bridge.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Emergency Situations
>
> Roll `[[/gmroll 1d6 # Brevin Emergencies]]` on the **Brevin Emergencies** table below to find the issue that the party must attempt to solve. Once it has either been successfully dealt with or failed, roll again. Each time the party finishes their attempt at a task, Brevin slides a bit closer to the canyons.
>
> - If the party successfully convinced Carmin to change their ways and head back to Brevin, the former brigand can successfully complete one task as the Canyon Crusader, giving the party an automatic success.
> - If the party helped the revelers on the road find their way to Brevin, they get the help of Leini and Karao on one check, giving them advantage.
>
> #### The Final Outcome
>
> - If the party succeeds on three emergencies, Edivel and the druids pull off a final fix and the bridge is saved!
> - If the party fails to complete three total emergencies, Brevin's efforts fail and the bridge is lost!

### Frozen in Fear

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A small group of thornlings stands on the far side of the trunk platform in the center of the Root. Each time the town shifts, they are knocked over and pulled closer to the edge of the canyon. Scramble as they might, they aren't moving quickly enough, and are on the verge of going over the edge. Around them other townsfolk call out and offer hands, but none are willing to get close enough to actually help them.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Helping the Townsfolk
>
> The party needs to accrue five successful checks to help the locals before they accrue three failures. Each success saves a stranded townsperson, and if they reach the target number of successes they will have saved all the stranded townsfolk. Three failures results in the remaining townsfolk going over the edge of the cliff with the platform, almost certainly meeting their end at the bottom of the canyon.
>
> Saving the locals can be done with any of the following checks:
>
> - A successful `[[/check athletics 14]]` check allows a character to climb the uneven and broken terrain to carry someone to safety.
> - A successful `[[/check acrobatics 14]]` check allows a character to deftly navigate the unsteady terrain and bring someone to safety.
> - A successful `[[/check intimidation 14]]` check lets a character can spur the other townsfolk into actually helping someone.
>
> - **Useful Gear**: A character with [[Unknown]] or a [[Climber's Kit]] automatically succeeds.
> - **Climb Speed**: A character with a climb speed can easily save people and automatically succeeds. Applying a climbing speed to the trapped townsfolk would also suffice here.
> - Fly Speed: A character with a fly speed can easily retrieve someone, and automatically succeeds.
>
> There are many possible solutions a party may employ to save the trapped townsfolk here, and the above list is not exhaustive.
>
> #### Outside Help
>
> Brevin has a large number of Druids among its citizenry, and for a less lethal outcome to this challenge, the townsfolk can be saved by nearby Druids who divert from their current crisis to save the townsfolk before the fall to their demise. This allows the party to fail without anyone dying as a result.

### Dangling at the Edge

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A scream comes from the edge of the Splinter Canyons. One of the druids who was working on stabilizing the bridge has fallen and is now dangling over the edge of the canyons, slowly losing their grip on the small bit of vine that they are currently holding on to.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Saving the Druid
>
> The party can pull the dangling druid up with a successful `[[/check athletics 14]]` check, or by using a spell capable of supporting their weight. If the check fails, the druid begins to slip. After three failures they lose their grip and tumble into the canyon below.

### Toxic Tonics

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A powerfully bitter scent wafts through the air, accompanied by the sound of coughing and retching in the distance. A cart with a sign reading Temi’s Tonics has overturned and the contents within it are creating a large, noxious cloud that has enveloped a few Thornlings who are doubled-over and unable to move.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Saving the Townsfolk
>
> There are 4 townsfolk within the cloud of fumes have begun to suffer from &reference[suffocation] and will expire once a few minutes have passed without being rescued. Characters can brave the noxious fog and drag a townsperson out with a successful `[[/save con 16]]` saving throw, but a failed save deals `[[/damage 1d6 poison]]` damage to the character and they are forced to fall back to safety without saving anyone.
>
> - **Safety Gear**: A character with a [[Protective Mask]] automatically succeeds.
> - **Immunity**: A character with immunity to poison damage automatically succeeds.
> - **Resistance**: A character with resistance to poison gains **+2 Boons**.
> - **Critical Success**: Two townsfolk are rescued with a single success.
>
> Characters can also disperse the toxic fumes immediately with the [[Gust of Wind]] Spell (or similar), which allows for all of the townsfolk to be rescued automatically.

### Staking the Ground

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Triss yells from across the trunk in the center of Brevin, where she has collected a pile of large wooden stakes whose roots are growing and curling even as they lie in front of you.
>
> > We use these root posts to reinforce trees as they grow — they may be able to counteract the weight of the vines. Can you drive these two in at the edge of the trunk here? I’ll handle the other side. Just pick a spot that feels right!

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Securing the Roots
>
> Characters can find the best place to drive the stakes into the ground with a successful `[[/ability str 16]]` check. A total of three spikes need to be successfully set to stabilize the tree. If the party's attempt fails, the stakes break and cannot be used. Triss runs to bring them to the druids, who are able to mend the stakes and give clearer directions about where they should go.
>
> - **Triss' Example**: A character that watches Triss set a stake and makes a successful `[[/check 18 perception]]` check gains **+2 Boons**.
> - **Study the Tree**: A character that studies the tree and makes a successful `[[/check 18 nature]]` check gains **+2 Boons**.
>
> `[[/check 18 nature]]` check to tell where the trunk needs to be stabilized or `[[/check perception]]` to observe what Triss is doing on the other side and follow her lead.
>
> If characters know where the stakes should be placed, they successfully drive them into the ground with a `[[/check 18 athletics]]` check for each stake, with advantage. If they do not know where to place the stakes, each stake requires a `[[/check 19 athletics]]` check.

If the party succeeds, the tree stabilizes and Triss shouts their thanks:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Triss gives you a thumbs up and says:
>
> > Great! That'll do it. I'll handle my side, you go see who else needs help!

If the party's attempt fails, Triss sends the party to do something else.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Triss grimaces as the tree begins to shudder and lean, the bracing stakes snapping under the pressure.
>
> > No good! Thanks for the attempt, but go see who else needs help. I'll fetch one of the druids to brace things!

### Animal Stampede

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> At the end of another jolt toward the canyons, the sound of a commotion and animal calls can be heard in the distance.
>
> > They're loose!"
>
> Someone calls, just as a small herd of pavelins run into the center of town. The shaggy four-legged creatures are small, but their crowns of horns and diamond-sharp hooves can be dangerous if they run into or over a bystander.
>
> Already, the group has begun galloping around the outskirts of the town center, causing those in their path to jump back or step closer to the canyons edge.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Wrangling the Animals
>
> The herd can be wrangled by the party, and three are successes are required to calm down the herd leaders and move the animals to somewhere they can be contained. The party can achieve this through several different methods:
>
> - A successful `[[/check animalhandling 17]]` to read, direct, and wrangle the animals peacefully.
> - A successful `[[/check intimdation 16]]` check to scare or coerce the animals into cooperating.
> - A successful `[[/check athletics 18]]` check to physically grapple and push the animals around.
>
> - **Useful Equipment**: A character with [[Unknown]] gains **+2 Boons**
> - **Appropriate Magic**: A character that casts [[Animal Friendship]] or [[Speak with Animals]] gains **+2 Boons**.
> - **Critical Success**: The check counts as two successes against this challenge.

### Panic Attack

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Even in the middle of the chaos, Edivel's voice cuts through the clamor:
>
> > Friends, can you help? We've got a situation here!
>
> Edivel leads you to an older Thornling holding a sharp blade and alternating between brandishing it over their head and hacking at parts of the ground around them with no apparent rhyme or reason.
>
> > You heard Triss — we need to cut it all free now! Or we're all going to die! Just like the people of Helkas!
>
> It clear this man is in a complete panic and will not be easily reasoned with, and the other townsfolk around him are unwilling to place themselves in harm's way to try. Even Edivel seems unwilling to get too close, instead looking to you to do something.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Disarming the Thornling
>
> Any character with a `[[/skill insight 17 format=long]]` or making a successful `[[/check insight 14]]` check recognizes that the older Thornling is in a state of irrational panic and can not be easily reasoned with at the moment.
>
> - A character that approaches the older Thornling with intent to disarm them must succeed on a `[[/check athletics 18]]` check. Failing by 5 or more inflicts `[[/damage 1d6 slicing]]` damage as the old Thornling lashes out with their weapon.
> - Any character can attempt to reason with the old Thornling and get them to relinquish their weapon with a successful `[[/check persuasion 18]]` check.
> - Any character can attempt to scare the old Thornling into dropping their weapon with a successful `[[/check intimidation 18]]` check.
>
> - **Appropriate Spells**: A character that casts a spell to charm or calm the Old Thornling, such as using [[Charm Person]], [[Command]], [[Hideous Laughter]], or [[Sleep]], automatically succeeds.
> - Distraction: Any character that distracts the old Thornling with a successful `[[/check peformance 18]]` or `[[/check deception 18]]` check provides **+2 Boons** to another character.
>
> Once the Thornling has been disarmed and talked down they are no longer an issue, and the party can move on.

### Brevin Bridge Outcomes

Depending on the party's actions, Edivel may or may not have been able to work with the town's druids to save the bridge.

If the bridge was successfully saved:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A cheer echoes through the air from the canyons edge, and Edivel quickly comes running back, high-fiving and celebrating with other locals around them.
>
> > We did it! Mostly the druids here — they know the vines better than anyone — but the vine weaving that Kali taught me? It helped, took some of the pressure of them so they could get the vines back in line and we could fix them back to the ground the way they should be. And all you did here around town? It meant we could focus, do our work together as one. And there you have it — the Brevin Bridge, just the way we promised. I only wish Aythorn was here to see it.

`[[/outcome saved]]`

#### Akon Attunement: Bridge Saved

If the party manages save the bridge from destruction, each character advances their **Attunement: Akon (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

Alternatively, if the bridge was lost:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Triss' voice cuts through the noise again:
>
> > We're doing the best we can, but we have to cut it loose!
>
> Her words are followed by a sawing, ripping noise, as if something has been wrenched out of the dirt. The ground around you shudders one more time and then stops, followed by a distant echoing splash. Soon, Edivel reappears, walking toward you with slumped shoulders.
>
> > We did everything we could. The druids were amazing, getting the vines to work to form the bridge, and my vine weaving really helped, but the earth shaking has just done too much damage and we weren't able to stay focused on the work with so many things going on.
> >
> > The town is safe, though, and that's something.

`[[/outcome falls]]`

#### Abyss Attunement: Bridge Lost

If the bridge is ultimately destroyed, each character advances their **Attunement: The Abyss (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.
