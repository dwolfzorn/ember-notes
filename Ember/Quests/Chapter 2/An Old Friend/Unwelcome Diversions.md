# Unwelcome Diversions

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Combat and Exploration Event brings the party to Lower Arcturel, where they must investigate the leads they discovered in [[Unhappy Accidents]]. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Break into the distillery and get a sample of the brew that has been sickening the locals.
> - Investigate the repair shop and figure out what has been making the repairs fail.
> - Visit Hob Korell's stables and figure out what is wrong with his lizard steeds.
> - Confront the Tyraphem [[Aburyx]] Kilner in the locked area.
>
> #### Area Walkthrough
>
> The party begins in the [[Arcturel Lower]] Scene, where the central gameplay of this Event transpires. A complete room-by-room description of the area and the gameplay that occurs there is detailed in the [[Arcturel Lower]] Area Walkthrough.
>
> In addition to the details provided in the Area Walkthrough, there are five key moments during exploration where Event-specific gameplay should occur:
>
> 1. [[Unwelcome Diversions]].
> 2. [[Unwelcome Diversions]].
> 3. [[Unwelcome Diversions]].
> 4. [[Unwelcome Diversions]].

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Glimpsed Cloak
>
> While the party is walking through the Area Map, they can catch glimpses of the Tyraphem [[Aburyx]] Kilner watching their progress. Any time a character makes a successful **Awareness (DC 16)** check outside of the mine, they see a cloak out of the corner of their eye.
>
> > Just at the edge of your vision, you see a piece of dark fabric sweeping around a nearby corner, as if someone just moved out of your field of view.
>
> If characters make a successful **Wilderness (DC 18)** check, they can identify where the figure disappeared and follow the direction back to the door of the mine. This may mean they skip some of the other clues and investigations and jump straight into the confrontation with Kilner.

### Investigating the Distillery

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The metal door to the distillery is closed, and there is no sign of life or activity through the one window in the center of its frame. A sign taped to that window with an impressive amount of adhesive reads, "Closed for Internal Processing." Beneath these words, in smaller handwriting, are the words, "If you believe our karal is to blame for your current illness, please see Apothecary Arvoda for treatment."
>
> A final scrawled note, which is accompanied by a skull and crossbones, as well as a lightning bolt, is taped up next to the keyhole above a simple metal knob.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Entering the Distillery
>
> The distillery door is locked. It can be unlocked with a successful**Stealth (DC 14)** check requiring [[Lockpicks]] , but opening the door triggers a trap (see [[Unwelcome Diversions]] below).
>
> Alternatively, characters can smash through the glass with a successful **Athletics (DC 22)** check. On a failure, a piece of glass flies off of the window — the character who made the attempt must make a successful **Athletics (DC 14)** to leap out of the way or suffer **Shattered Glass Fragments (Hazard 6, Fortitude, Health, Piercing)**.
>
> - Characters who make a successful **Wilderness (DC 14)** check or have **Knowledge: Crafts** can identify the window material as double-spun glass, which is extremely difficult to break unless hit at a weak point and sprays pieces of glass at anyone who tries.
> - Characters who make a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check to examine the window can see minute cracks underneath the adhesive that is attaching the "Closed for Internal Processing" sign to the window.
> - Characters who target the window's weak point can smash through the glass with a successful **Wilderness (DC 14)** check with **+2 Boons**. Due to this targeting, no glass sprays if they fail the check.
>
> - **Convinced Felisa:** The character gains **+2 Boons** on these checks if the party convinced Felisa to reveal what she knows about the Waterborne locks (see [[Unhappy Accidents]] in the [[Unhappy Accidents]] Event.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Do Not Enter
>
> The door to the distillery has been rigged with an electricity trap, triggered by anyone who attempts to pull on the metal handle. If characters try to use the knob to open the door, they must make a successful **Athletics (DC 15)** to leap out of the way or suffer **Electrical Discharge (Hazard 6, Fortitude, Health, Electricity)** . The trap can be triggered multiple times, with no reset time required.
>
> Characters who make a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check, or who use a spell or talent to locate traps, spot a tiny sliver of metal in the gap between the edge of the door and the door frame, which seems out of place. This is the trigger to the trap.
>
> To disable the trap, a character must make a successful **Stealth (DC 14)** check, a [[Disarming Toolkit]] grants +2 Boons for this check. . On a success, the trap is disabled. On a failure, the trap is disabled, but **triggers one final time in the process**.

Once inside [[Waterborne Distillery]], characters can easily take a sample of the brew aging in the barrels without a check using one of the vials or glasses located on the tables in the distillery.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The karal pours into the container with a pleasant fizzy sound and a rich earthy smell, but there is something else in the air as the liquid fills the glass — something ever so slightly off.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Tainted Brew
>
> Characters who take a sample from the barrels acquire [[Suspicious Karal]].
>
> Characters who make a successful **Wilderness (DC 14)** check can identify the toxin in the brew as [[Concentrated Ore Dust]]. Unlike regular mine dust, this has been alchemically altered to be more toxic.
>
> - **Knowledge: Alchemy**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Machines**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> If characters cannot identify the substance, or wish to know more about it, they can ask Apothecary Arvoda of [[Arvoda's Elixirs]].
>
> #### Tampering
>
> Any character who examines the barrels and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check identifies a small section of the wood at the top of the barrel with different grain than the wood around it.
>
> Characters can remove this wood either with any number of tools, such as [[Crowbar]], [[Hammer]], or some of the tools typically contained in a [[Fletcher's Toolkit]] or [[Smith's Toolkit]], revealing a large hole in the top of the barrel with perfectly smooth edges, as if the section was surgically removed by a metal tool.
>
> Any character who searches the room and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 15)** check spots wood dust on the ground. They can follow it to the barrels at the back of the room, which hide the entrance to the mine that leads to [[The Storage Room]].

### Investigating the Stables

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A wave of heat washes over you as you step into the stables, muggy and thick, and somehow everywhere, with no clear origin point other than the large glowing lamps that beat down onto a group of reclining lizards. The young stablehand seems not to feel the heat, barely looking up from the rhythmic work of scooping and tossing straw in the middle of the enclosure as you enter the shop.
>
> > If you're here for a Jobri ride, you may have to wait a little bit. They're resting. And if you're here to try to get a free drink out of Hob by fixing whatever's wrong with 'em, go for it. You won't be the first.

The young stablehand **Kaol** (Chaotic Neutral, Arcturian Human, he/him) returns to his absentminded work. If asked any questions, he shrugs — he doesn't know what's wrong with the lizards, and he isn't in the mood to guess.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Jobri Pen
>
> The 3 [[Jobri]] here appear to be fine, if sleeping soundly. Any attempts to rouse them have no effect. If the party attempts to get them to wake up, the stablehand just chuckles.
>
> > They've been sleeping a while — don't think they're getting up any time soon. At least this time they aren't falling asleep mid-climb, but still, something about them is just off.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 14)** check knows that something is bothering the Jobri that is external to them — something in their environment is causing them to sleep.
>
> - **Knowledge: Beasts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who searches for the source of this imposed slumber and makes a successful **Arcana (DC 14)** check discovers that there is a spell originating in the heat lamp that is causing the Jobri to sleep. Two of the heat lamps are, in fact, [[Lazing Lamp]].
>
> - **Talent: Recognize Spellcraft**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> The Lazing Lamps can only be disabled magically — this can be done using **Talent: Counterspell** against a Control rune with an Aura gesture and a willpower defense of 16.
>
> The lamps can also be physically removed from the room, though they are extremely hot to the touch. Any character who attempts to remove a lamp without using gloves, [[Smith's Toolkit]], or magical means must make a successful **Wilderness (DC 15)** when touching the lamps or suffer **Mild Burns (Hazard fortitude, Health, Fire)**.
>
> Characters who use magic capable of locating an object can locate a Lazing Lamp found within [[The Storage Room]] in the mine.
>
> If characters remove or disable the [[Lazing Lamp]], the stablehand Kaol asks what is happening or what they are doing, but makes no effort to stop the party.
>
> - Kaol will freely tell the characters that the heat lamps are new, procured from a merchant recently after the heat lamps all malfunctioned and the replacements went missing.
> - Kaol suspected that someone from the mine took the heat lamps but has no idea why.

### Investigating the Repair Shop

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Pieces of metal are scattered across one of the repair benches alongside a number of tools and jars of unknown substances. Above them is a sign that reads "Cevher Repairs." Notes in and around the metal may have once been in some kind of order but have been scattered around the room. As you enter, a clank echoes through the room, followed by the distant sound of whirring, humming through the floor. A moment later, the sounds disappear, leaving only silence.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Mechanical Echo
>
> The sound of clanking and whirring can be heard intermittently in Wrestful Repairs.
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 15, Passive)** can tell that the noise is coming from the eastern wall (which is the wall closest to the mine).
>
> - **Standing in Adjacent Room:** The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Characters who hear the sound and have **Knowledge: Machines** can tell that it is being caused by a machine of some type.
>
> #### The Odd Metal
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 16, Passive)** of the repair shop can see dust between the pieces of metal, of the same gray color as the metal.
>
> If any character picks up a piece of metal from the bench, a flaky dust falls from it onto the floor.
>
> > Where your hands touch the metal, small bits of flaky gray dust float down to the ground. They're almost too tiny to see, but where they glint slightly in the light, their silvery color matches the metal in front of you.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Alchemy** or **Knowledge: Machines**, or who have the **Talent: Smithing Novice** or **Talent: Alchemy Novice** talent know that this kind of effect is caused either by something physical that has been applied to the metal or a natural weakness in the material, as opposed to a spell. They also have **+2 Boons** on further checks on the metal.
>
> - Any character who makes a successful **Science (DC 14)** check or uses a tool on the metal find that the bulk of the metal is strong, but the edges are flaky.
> - Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check can see residue on the edge of the metal pieces and on the edges of several tools nearby. It has a distinctive acrid smell to it.
> - Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 14)** check can identify the substance as the secretions of the boreworm, a metal-burrowing bug. The substance can also be brought to the apothecary, Arvoda, for identification.
>
> Once characters have identified the substance, they can detect the same acrid smell elsewhere in Wrestful Repairs with a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check. It is primarily found in a small vat that has the word "glue" written on it in blocky handwriting — this substance is, in fact, [[Metal Crumbler]]. The handwriting does not resemble any other handwriting found within the repair shop.
>
> #### On The Record
>
> Rennerie's records have been scattered all around the bench. Characters who take the time to go through the papers and make a successful **Science (DC 12)** check find one paper that seems like it might be relevant.
>
> > I know people are saying that the mine is odd these days, and Zodi has pulled everyone back to the front, but I'm excited to get in there and get the excavator working! Maybe I'll try that new sample glue I got. Like that merchant said, you can never have enough supplies!
>
> Characters can locate the vat of glue referenced in the note without a check. Any character with**Awareness (DC 15, Passive)** can smell an acrid smell in it that seems out of place. As above, the vat of [[Metal Crumbler]] can be identified as a metal-weakening agent with a successful **Science (DC 14)** check.

#### Mayis Attunement: Leads Investigated

If the party manages to investigate all three of the primary leads in Lower Arcturel, each character advances their **Attunement: Mayis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### Asking the Apothecary

> [!info] Social
> #### Haggling with Emelyn Arvoda
>
> Arvoda will answer questions for the party about poisons, potions, and alchemical substances, but she wants to be paid for her expertise. Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check intuits that while she is very concerned with her bottom line, she is knowledgeable and confident in her answers.
>
> > Don't tell me. You drank the bad karal. Or got thrown from some ornery lizard? Although, now that I think about it, you look less like locals than adventurers. Adventurers with a lot of questions on their tongues.
> >
> > Happy to help with anything you need, though you will need to make a purchase first. Or donate to the Dives Merchant Association, if you'd prefer. Nothing under 5 gold per question, please, or it will be difficult to make time for anything but small talk.
>
> For each substance that the party wants information about, they must either purchase an item or donate  **-1 each** to the apothecary. Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check can convince her to lower the cost of getting an answer to  **1**.
>
> - **Knowledge: Trade**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Arvoda's Identifications
>
> Arvoda can help with any of the following:
>
> - The sample of [[Suspicious Karal]] from Waterborne Distillery, which she identifies as being contaminated with [[Concentrated Ore Dust]]. She notes that this kind of concentrated ore dust must be created. On its own, a mine would not generate enough dust to create this effect.
> - A thick grayish gloop that resembles congealing paste, found in Wrestful Repairs, which is [[Metal Crumbler]].

As the party leaves Arvoda's for the first time, she stops them in their tracks:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> "Wait," the apothecary says, as you turn to leave. "Now, I'm not in the business of giving away free information, as you know, but these are odd times, and I certainly don't want any unnecessary harm to come to any of my customers. So, between us, an odd man came here not too long ago, asking about whether I thought the mine was cursed and if I was worried about working so close to it. Decided then and there to stay put. Seemed like he was trying to warn me off, and I don't like being managed. I didn't get anything else from him, but last I saw, he was headed toward the mine, cloak trailing behind him."

### Abandoned Mine

With all roads leading to the abandoned mine, the party can keep investigating the area further, though they will have to unlock the door first.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Entering the Abandoned Mine
>
> The party will have to unlock the mine's gate in order to enter. The lock can be only be unlocked with a successful **Stealth (DC 16)** check requiring [[Lockpicks]] .. However, the nearby mine door is weaker — it can be unlocked with a successful **Stealth (DC 14)** using a [[Lockpicks]] or pried open with a successful **Athletics (DC 14)** check using a crowbar or other prying tool.
>
> - [[Metal Crumbler]]: The character gains **+2 Boons** on these checks.
>
> If the party cannot find a way to break into the mine, they may visit Zodi Trask, who has relocated to [[Zodi Trask's Apartment]] and ask for the key. He is reluctant, but can be convinced to give it up with a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Area Walkthrough
>
> Once they enter the mine area, the party's progress can be tracked using the [[Arcturel Lower]] Area Walkthrough. The party's time in the mines ends with their battle against the Tyraphem [[Aburyx]] Kilner and a search of his belongings for information about the issues occurring in Arcturel.

If the characters search Kilner's belongings thoroughly, they may come across his notes, [[Kilner Notes]], which mention a way to decode the [[Coded Bandit Instructions]] found on the bandits in Helkas.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Decoding the Bandit's Message
>
> As mentioned in [[Kilner Notes]], to decode the message left by the bandits, characters must hold the paper in their hands and say the words "For Other Fortunes." If they do this, they receive [[Decoded Bandit Instructions]] in place of [[Coded Bandit Instructions]].
>
> If parties successfully decode the message, mark the "Decoded the Bandit Instructions" outcome in [[A Friendly Reunion]] as complete.

#### Cora Attunement: Disable the Device

If the party manages to disable the device found in [[The Hub]], each character advances their **Attunement: Cora (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Signara Attunement: Discover the Hidden Room

The first character who manages to discover the hidden room in [[The Last Pit]] advances their **Attunement: Signara (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Signara Attunement: Defeat the Aburyx

If the party manages to defeat Kilner in combat, each character advances their **Attunement: Signara (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### After the Mines

After the [[Aburyx]] Kilner has been defeated, Lyla will mention they should check back in with Zodi to tell them of what they have uncovered. When the party reaches Zodi's apartment, the foreman is anxiously pacing back and forth and immediately gestures for them to sit.

> [!info] Social
> #### Updating Zodi Trask
>
> Zodi immediately wants to know what the group has discovered and if it can help right things for House Cevher and the residents of Arcturel. He is both shocked and relieved to find out that it appears that some creature was behind the recent troubles in the city.
>
> > So all of the things going wrong? They were all on purpose? The bad karal? Hob's lizards nodding off? The machines breaking? I guess I'm glad to know that I'm not somehow the worst liaison that House Cevher has ever had here, but it feels like there was some kind of organized campaign to ruin what we've built here.
>
> Lyla (if present) agrees wholeheartedly.
>
> > Between this and the attack on Helkas, someone appears to have House Cevher in their sights. But my friends and I stopped them here, and we'll stop them again. I just need to figure out who's behind it all, and why. At least I know where to go next, though. There's clear evidence of House Wandren's influence here. I hear that my old friend Juro Wandren is in the village of Nain. I'm sure he's not involved in this, but his house appears to be connected. Hopefully, he can shed some light on things.
>
> Zodi wishes the party luck and invites them to stay in Arcturel as long as they'd like. If desired, this can provide an opportunity for an onramp to the Event [[Glitter in the Dark]], which also takes place in Arcturel.
>
> > You do what you need to do. We'll be okay here. Now that that creature has been dealt with, we here in Arcturel can pick up the pieces, and we'll keep an eye out for any other odd happenings. We're sturdy folks, and we don't mind labor. Not that you have to go, of course. Feel free to stay here and rest — my home is your home.
