# Unhappy Accidents

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> In this Social Event, the party investigates the troubles disrupting Arcturel and the rumors turning local opinion against House Cevher. They begin by speaking with Zodi Trask, House Cevher's liaison in the settlement. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Question Felisa about the contaminated local brew in [[Unhappy Accidents]].
> - Question Rennerie about the recent machinery failures in [[Unhappy Accidents]].
> - Question Hob Korell about his slugging riding lizards in [[Unhappy Accidents]].
> - Question Tantrin about the rumors spreading through Rallyhome in [[Unhappy Accidents]].
>
> The party only needs to investigate one of these leads to proceed to the next Event, where they can uncover direct evidence of sabotage in Lower Arcturel. Lyla separates from the party after the initial conversation with Zodi and can summarize one or more leads the characters did not pursue.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### What's Wrong In Arcturel
>
> Arcturel has been the victim of sabotage by a Tyraphem [[Aburyx]] named Kilner, who was sent to the sinkhole city with the goal of disrupting trade and discrediting House Cevher, using his [[Cloak of Kindly Visage]] to appear to be a friendly traveler or merchant.
>
> All of Kilner's attempts at sabotage have had the side benefit of clearing the area around the Level 3 Mine, a mine in Lower Arcturel that Kilner is using as a home base — the nearby distillery, lizard stables, and repair shop have all been successfully taken out of commission, and the mine has been shut down. Kilner has been building a device within the abandoned mine to create and amplify ground shakes, though its purpose is unclear to the party at this time.

### Entering Rallyhome

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Under the cap of what might be one of the largest mushrooms to ever exist, a large seating area is filled with tables and chairs, many made of unexpected materials, like discarded pipes and interlocking pickaxes. Above, in the remaining gray petrified flesh of the mushroom cap, a colony of smaller luminescent mushrooms has taken up residence, shedding a faint glowing light on the locals eating, drinking, and sharing news below. As you enter, Lyla points across the room at a tall Kiska man in work clothing sitting in front of a pile of maps and diagrams.
>
> > There's Zodi! From what I've heard, he's a real leader of the people, coming up through the ranks to become the foreman and main House Cevher contact, which isn't easy, given all of our regulations about who can represent us in our mining interests.
> >
> > You're welcome to come with me to meet him. Or you can gather information on your own — either way, I know we'll get to the bottom of what's going on.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Note: Zodi's Current State
>
> If the party has begun the quest [[The Book Of Tales]], but has not yet met Zodi Trask, Amalthea has told them that he is beginning to turn to crystal. While this is true, the condition is currently in its early stages and hidden by his clothing. He will not mention it in front of Lyla and shrugs off any questions about it as less important than the current situation.
>
> If the party completed [[The Crystal Quarryman]] in The Book of Tales, Zodi is either fully crystal or fully human. In either case, he greets the party warmly but focuses his attention on questions from Lyla.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Conversation with Zodi
>
> Though Zodi is putting a brave face on for Lyla, the settlement is obviously overwhelmed, and Lyla's showing up is unexpected. He greets her hesitantly.
>
> > Lyla? Lyla Cevher? I’d heard you were off somewhere doing your own thing, but I see you’re back on the Plateau and checking in on our little settlement. I know that the last shipments have been a little behind schedule, but I’m sure we can catch up once we get a little bit of luck on our side and get the new mine open. No need to worry about anything, whatever you've heard.
>
> Zodi claims that everything fine, but any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 14)** check intuits that he is deeply worried and needs help. Any character who presses further and makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check will get him to reveal the following:
>
> - There have been a few small issues going on in Arcturel, which Zodi believes that people are making too much of a big deal about - a few more machines have broken down than normal, there's something wrong with Hob Korell's lizards, and the town's famous local brew, karal, is making people sick.
> - The most recent issue is the closing of the mine on Level 3 - the ground started shaking there recently, among other strange incidents, and Zodi closed it for safety. He's trying to figure out a new location in that level of Arcturel, which has unusually rich iron deposits.
> - Individually, the issues are nothing, but there are whispers that Cevher is to blame for all of it, many of which are being spread by Tantrin, a local “reporter” who is little more than a glorified gossip. Zodi isn’t sure where Tantrin is getting his information.
> - If the party wants to learn more, they can:
>   - Speak to one of his former workers, Felisa, who now works in Rallyhome as a server and whose adopted family makes the brew ([[Unhappy Accidents]]).
>   - Check in with his most trusted repair person, Rennerie, in the corner of Rallyhome to figure out what's been going wrong with the machines ([[Unhappy Accidents]])
>   - Track down Hob Korell, who is currently drowning his sorrows, about his issues with his steeds ([[Unhappy Accidents]])
>   - Talk to Tantrin, whose "news of the day" appears to put the blame on the Cevhers ([[Unhappy Accidents]])
>
> If the party is with Lyla at the end of this conversation, she chooses to pursue one of the leads they are not following and asks them to check in with her to compare notes when they are done. If the party plans to pursue all leads, she offers to work with Zodi on trying to repair House Cevher's image and asks for them to update her when they've learned more.
>
> > We can probably get more done if we split up a bit. I want to talk to Zodi about some business details, but I'll try to follow up on one of the leads when I'm done. Let's meet back here in a few hours, hopefully with notes to swap.

As for the repairs, you'll find my name on all the repair slips, but I really rely on Rennerie over there in the corner to let me know when the machines are out of whack. Just avoid Tantrin — I don't know what I ever did to him, but to hear him tell it, if you sneeze, it's because House Cevher gave you a cold.

### Rallyhome Rumors

In lieu of speaking to Zodi, the party can attempt to gather information on their own by keeping their collective ears to the ground. As they make their way through Rallyhome, they are greeted by Tantrin, who shares what he knows about the settlement.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> One moment, you are walking through Rallyhome alone. The next, a young man appears in front of you as if out of nowhere, a patchwork cap on his head and a proffered drink in his hands. At first glance, he appears to be little older than a teenager, awkwardly blocking your path forward, but when he speaks, his voice is deep and his words pour out in a practiced flow that might make a bard jealous.
>
> > Hello, friend. It's always good to have newcomers in the area. These days, with everything House Cevher's put us through, we're lucky to get more than a merchant. But I'm sure you wouldn't want to know about our troubles. Unless, of course, friend, you want to keep yourself safe with information from reliable sources.

> [!info] Social
> #### Tantrin's Tidbits
>
> Tantrin is eager to talk about what he knows and will share the following:
>
> - The karal has not been safe to drink ever since House Cevher got mine dust inside of the distillery barrels. The Waterborne family is furious about it. Felisa Waterborne, who is currently clearing drinks, knows all about it.
> - House Cevher machines have been breaking apart and injuring people, likely because they don't put any effort toward real repairs. Rennerie, in the corner of the bar, can confirm.
> - House Cevher may have poisoned Hob's riding lizards so that they can force everyone to use the elevators and charge for them. Hob might know more.
> - It's good that the mine is shut down — if Cevher kept it open, it was likely to collapse and kill someone. It still could, given how badly it was constructed, though Zodi won't admit it.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 14)** check can tell that there's something Tantrin is holding back about where he gets his information.
>
> Any character who presses further and makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** or `[[/skillcheck intimidation 14]]` check can get him to confess that he's getting all of his information from an inside source — someone who used to work in the mines. They're fairly new to town, but they know a lot about what's going on. Tantrin usually meets this source by going to the Dives and waiting for them to arrive with information — they're tall and always wear a distinctive black cloak.

And I heard that when the parts get old, they take from other machines — like the elevators — to repair them. Rennerie over there in the corner says she doesn't know about it, but I'm not sure.

> [!question] Q&A

I'm not sure if this is true, but I heard that Hob thinks Cevher is poisoning them so they can force everyone to the elevators and charge for them. Any way to make money from us everyday folk, I guess.

The party can find him at the bar to start [[Unhappy Accidents]].

### Investigating the Bad Brew

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A middle-aged woman with dusty hands passes through Rallyhome on a regular rotation, collecting glasses from tables and picking up any pieces of errant trash from the floor. Her steps are heavy against the floor, and her smile does not reach her eyes. As she stomps by, you can hear the distinct sound of muttering, though you can't make any specific words out other than "mine" and "level three."

> [!info] Social
> #### Waterborne Illnesses
>
> If asked about her frustrations or about the mine, Felisa takes a seat at the party’s table.
>
> > Are you sure you wanna know? I saw you walked in here with one of the Cevhers. I can tell by the way Zodi sat straight up and paid attention. I know he's the people's foreman, but no one wants to look like they're slacking off in front of the boss. Probably won't even tell her about what's going on with the karal, but I will. You just be sure to pass it on.
>
> Felisa is all too eager to talk about the problems with karal, a popular and common beverage in Arcturel that is said to raise the overall well-being of those who drink it, and shares the following:
>
> - Everyone loves karal in Arcturel, which is primarily made by the Waterborne family.
> - She suspects that the karal was intentionally poisoned and thinks that House Cevher was somehow responsible. Tantrin, at least, seemed sure of it.
> - There are still large kegs of karal in the distillery that could be contaminated, but Sevali, leader of the Waterbornes, has locked it up while she decides what to do with it. If someone could get past her locks and get a sample, it could be used to find what really happened.
> - If asked, Felisa can read some of the shifting ink message, making out the words "drakes," "cloaked man," and "Arcturel." She notes that a merchant with a cloak came through town recently.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 14)**check knows that Felisa is telling the truth, and that she is likely going to quit her job very soon.
>
> Any character who presses further and makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check can convince her to share what she knows about the Waterborne locks — the handle to the distillery is rigged so that it shocks anyone who tries to open it.
>
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What Karal Is
>
> **A:**
>
> > It was the Waterbornes who came up with the idea of using some of the water from deep down in the sinkhole to make a drink, and even they didn't realize how popular it would be. Until it started making people sick, anyway. And I mean really sick. Pale faces, coughing up their guts, shaking and shivering. Luckily, it wore off pretty fast. Which is what made me think it was less illness and more poison. I heard someone say something from the mine could do that.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who Is To Blame
>
> **A:**
>
> > Folks are saying that something from the closed-down mine leaked into the karal barrels, something about House Cevher providing old rusty pipes, but if you ask me, someone working for the Cevhers poisoned the brew on purpose. They want to keep us in the mines, away from any work they can't control. No offense to your friend there, but that's what people are saying. Tantrin has a source and everything, so it's what I'm going with unless there's some proof that's wrong.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Where to Get More Information
>
> **A:**
>
> > There's still karal in the distillery, aging in barrels, and Sevali still hasn't decided what to do with it. Does she throw it away just to make everyone feel better? Do the Waterbornes try to figure out what made everyone sick? For now, Sevali's got the whole place locked up tight. Probably hoping everyone forgets about it. Get past her locks, though, and you can test it for yourself, maybe find out the truth.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Clues Found at Helkas
>
> **A:**
>
> > No fancy leather or phrases about fortunes here. Mostly just folks working hard. But that ink? The Waterbornes use it sometimes to protect the karal formula, though they'd never admit it. I'm no expert, but I think I can make out a few words: drakes, cloaked man, and Arcturel. There was actually a man in a cloak here recently — a merchant — but I can't see how he would be involved in anything needing a code.

To find out more about the karal brew, characters can get a sample from the locked up distillery in Lower Arcturel. But first, they are approached by Lyla for an update in [[Unhappy Accidents]].

### Investigating the Sluggish Steeds

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Keth merchant, Hob, sits in front of Rallyhome's bar, ordering up drinks for anyone who passes by and can "put some vigor back into the hearts of my lazing lizards!" He is surrounded by eager advice-givers, whose remedies range from playing fast-paced music in the lizards' stable area to sending one of the Lamplighters who keep the local Inkaro pearls glowing to commune with their spirit. He does not appear to be cheered by their help, though it does not stop him from paying the tab.

> [!info] Social
> #### Hob's Despair
>
> Hob immediately offers the party a drink if they help him figure out what is wrong with his steeds, though he warns them not to try the karal, which has made several people in the area sick.
>
> > Hello there, friend! You don't happen to be an expert in the lethargy of lizards, do you? I need some sort of help, or Arcturel is cursed and all is truly lost! Hob, hobbled by fate! Korell corralled! I'm here for any answers, and if you figure it out, your next drink's on me.
>
> Hob is happy to tell his story of woe, sharing the following:
>
> - Hob Korell has always known that there was more to life in a stable than horses, and coming to Arcturel has given him the opportunity to expand. The riding lizards, which he brought from miles and miles away, are one of his finest achievements, but now they keep falling asleep mid-task.
> - Several people have almost plummeted to their deaths because the lizards have gone to sleep while in the midst of climbing up the sinkhole walls.
> - Hob has tried everything to try to get the lizards to pep up, but nothing has worked, and it is stressing them out. Some have blamed House Cevher for the problem, believing that they've targeted the lizards for some reason, but Hob doesn't know and doesn't care. All he wants is for someone to check on his riding lizards and figure out what to do. He will give the party **1** each if they can get the lizards fixed.
> - If asked about the clues that the party has, the only one he has information about is the leather pattern, from his days traveling and procuring unusual steeds — he's fairly sure it's from a port city, like Seawall or Ordain.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 14)** check can tell that Hob feels guilty and partially responsible for the state of his lizards.
>
> Any character who presses further and makes a successful **Intimidation (DC 14)** or **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check can get Hob to confide that he somehow misplaced some of his normal lizard lamps shortly before things started going wrong and couldn't replace a set that unexpectedly broke. He was able to get some new ones from a visiting merchant in a very dapper cloak, but worries that they aren't quite the same.
>
> - **Knowledge: Beasts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who Hob Is
>
> **A:**
>
> > But wait, I should probably introduce myself! I'm Hob Korell, procurer of unconventional steeds. Or at least I was! And good at it, too. My riding lizards have been the talk of Arcturel since I brought them here as hatchlings. They can climb in and around the sinkhole carrying goods, ferrying tourists, and bringing joy to the lives of all who lay eyes on their beautiful green fins. Or they could. Now they just sleep. Been like that for half a season!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What's Wrong with the Lizards
>
> **A:**
>
> > You might think sleeping lizards isn't a bad thing. After all, everything alive sleeps eventually. But these lazeabout laggards are falling asleep on the job! Mid-crawl! One dropped an entire haul of ore, and another one almost dropped a family on its head! I can't have that.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Fixing the Lizards
>
> **A:**
>
> > I've tried everything, but after being poked and prodded and fed this and told that, the lizards are starting to get stressed out at the very sight of me! Me, their favorite person in the whole world! Maybe you could go down and see how they're doing, figure out if there's any way to fix this? I've got a stablehand down there, but she doesn't know any more than I do.
> >
> > This all sounds silly to be worked up about, I know, but with all the machines breaking around here, this was one way folks knew they could move things around, and now it's stopped in its tracks. Maybe it is something about the Cevher mine upsetting the balance of things in Arcturel. Or Zodi's poisoning them? That doesn't seem likely, though I do hear whispers. I don't know. I just know things need to change. Soon.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Existing Clues From Helkas
>
> **A:**
>
> > Fancy leather with a pattern? Yeah. I've seen that before, when I was off trying to bring my lizards across the ocean, not that they seem to appreciate it now. There was a leatherworker in one of the port cities who used it, but I can't remember which one.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Hob's Guilt (After a Successful Check)
>
> **A:**
>
> > I keep thinking it's all my fault. If I hadn't misplaced all the lamps and had to replace them — the lizards loved those lamps. Kept everything just the right temperature. I was able to get new ones from that merchant who came by a little while back in the very warm-looking cloak, but I think the lizards know the difference, and it's completely thrown them off.

To find out more about what happened to Hob's lizards (and check in on his behalf), the party will need to go to Lower Arcturel to visit his stables. But first, they are approached by Lyla for an update in [[Unhappy Accidents]].

### Investigating the Mechanical Breakdowns

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Keth woman sits in a corner of Rallyhome, focused on the papers in front of her, her grip on the piece of charcoal in her hand so tight that it seems like it might snap at any second. She writes notes and makes small drawings, then crosses them out, or crumples up the nearest parchment and tosses it on the table into a growing pile of papers.

> [!info] Social
> #### Rennerie's Failings
>
> Rennerie is trying desperately to figure out what has gone wrong with the machines she's worked on and tries to keep things brief.
>
> > If you need a repair, I'm not the person to ask right now. Everything I touch seems to fall apart. I'm surprised this chair hasn't collapsed underneath me.
>
> She explains that she does most of the repairs for the elevators and mines two and three for House Cevher, but recently, all of her repairs are coming apart.
>
> > I've been working repairs for House Cevher mines since I was old enough to pick up a wrench, and I've never had so many disasters. Maybe I'm losing my touch? Or spent too much time working on my art and not enough time staying up to speed? I did get sick from a bad batch of karal, but the problems started before then, about a season ago. If I don't figure out what's going wrong, someone is gonna get hurt, and I'll be stuck making chairs and tables.
>
> Rennerie is reluctant to share anything more. Any character with **Knowledge: Crafts** can see that the chair she is on is handcrafted from metal pieces. If characters compliment the work (either sincerely or with a successful **Deception (DC 13)** check), make a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check, or have **Knowledge: Machines**, she will share the following:
>
> - There have been three bad accidents so far - a digger had pieces fly off of it and cut people nearby, an elevator plummeted halfway down the sinkhole before someone stopped it with magic, and a walkway nearly collapsed. She had done some sort of repair on each of them a few days before the issue, but she can find nothing wrong with the pieces.
> - Any rumors about House Cevher slacking on repairs are just that — rumors.
> - Rennerie has noticed some weakness in the metal pieces that she used after the accident, but she could swear they were fine before she used them. She'd appreciate if the party went and looked at her tools and materials to see if she'd done anything wrong.
> - Rennerie has been hearing odd metallic sounds down in the Dives — whirring and clacking. Normally, she would dismiss it as the signs of work, but it has happened even when no one was in the mine, and usually just before something fell apart.
> - If asked about the clues the party has, Rennerie has never seen the ink or leather markings found at Helkas, but she does recognize the phrase "For Other Fortunes" — the merchant who sold her some of her new metal glue used the phrase. He tried to get her to say a phrase back, but she doesn't remember it other than that it involved using her hands.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What's Happening
>
> **A:**
>
> > First the digger comes apart, and pieces cut up everyone nearby, then an elevator nearly drops to the floor of the sinkhole, and then a walkway starts to come apart at the hinges. And if that wasn't bad enough, I'd done repairs on each of them not long beforehand. No one blames me yet — they say the tools sent by House Cevher just aren't up to standard — but I'm sure that'll change soon enough. Plus, it doesn't explain the odd metal. Or the sounds.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Odd Metal
>
> **A:**
>
> > I took a look at the metal bits that were left behind after each accident and the metal did seem sub-par. Chalky, almost. I left it down at my shop, Wrestful Repairs, because I'm not sure what to make of it all. You're not from around here, so maybe you'll see something I missed before I have to give up on repairs altogether!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Creepy Sounds
>
> **A:**
>
> > I've been hearing things down in the Dives. Clicking. Whirring. At first, I thought it was the Waterbornes, but then the distillery shut down, and then I thought maybe the mine, but I'd hear it even when I was in there doing repairs after hours. Maybe it was some kind of warning sign? Every time I'd hear it, something would break soon after.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** House Cevher
>
> **A:**
>
> > Tantrin running his mouth again? He's convinced Cevher's to blame for everything, but I do good work, and when things got weird in the mine, Zodi cleared out. I don't know why he's still spreading those lies.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Party's Clues
>
> **A:**
>
> > I don't know anything about weird ink or leather. That phrase, though — "For Other Fortunes?" There was a merchant here a while back, before things started going wrong around here - sold me metal glue and a few other bits and bobs. He used that phrase. Struck me as kinda odd, especially because he kept insisting that I say something about my own hands back, like some kind of code. I don't remember anything else about him, though, or what the rest of that code was. Sorry.

To find out more about what might have caused the mechanical breakdowns, the party will need to check out the chalky metal left behind in the repair shop in Lower Arcturel. But first, they are approached by Lyla for an update in [[Unhappy Accidents]].

### Determining Next Steps

Back at Rallyhome, Lyla is eager to share her updates and to hear what the party has learned. She has either followed one of the leads that the party didn't pursue or (if the party chose to split up and pursue all three) is working with Zodi to find a way to repair House Cevher's reputation and wants an update.

> [!info] Social
> #### Lyla's Updates
>
> Lyla can update the party on any of the paths they did not pursue:
>
> - **The Bad Brew:** Most or all of the supply of karal in Arcturel has gotten contaminated and has been making people sick. No one knows why, and the Waterborne family has been reluctant to open the casks left in the distillery to find out how deep the problem goes. The party could try to discover the truth.
> - **The Sluggish Steeds:** Hob Korell wants someone to check in on his sluggish lizards — Lyla has heard that their tiredness is somehow House Cevher's fault (though no one knows why) and would like the party to figure out if there might be another cause.
> - **The Mechanical Breakdowns:** The recent failures of equipment provided by or maintained through House Cevher resources are upsetting. According to the main maintenance person, Rennerie, all protocols were followed, but the metal they used did seem a bit brittle after the accidents, and there have been odd sounds coming from the area. They'd love someone to go down and see if there's something they did wrong.
