# Traveling with Lyla

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event can occur during a **Rest** at any point during Chapter 2. It can occur multiple times while traveling with Lyla and provides the party with an opportunity to learn about the House Cevher heiress, her story, and her opinions on the current situation. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Discuss the current situation with Lyla and learn her thoughts.
> - Ask Lyla for her opinion about characters the party has met along the way.
> - Get to know Lyla better by learning more about her story.
>
> #### Prerequisites
>
> [[Lyla Cevher]] must be a member of the [[Party]] for this Event to occur.

### Thoughts on Events

Lyla can share her thoughts on some of the recent things that the party has encountered while on this Main Quest.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Oldcraft Lodge[[A Friendly Reunion]]
>
> **A:**
>
> It's wonderful to be back at the Lodge — I remember spending long days in Sigil's hidden arcane library while my father visited the mines in Arcturel. It's a shame he's closed those doors, but I suppose he's trying to avoid losing himself in reading for a few years while so much is going on outside.
>
> Sigil never fails to put a smile on my face, and he's always been such a sweetheart. He's always been buried in his books, though, so I was pleasantly surprised to see him actively helping those refugees! He has a lot of potential to help those around him, so I'm glad he's putting his powers to good use.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Arcturel Investigation[[Unhappy Accidents]]
>
> **A:** I shudder to think about what might have happened in Arcturel if I hadn't been there to learn what was going on. More sabotage? What would people think of my house and our reputation? This is what I worry about most. I need to take charge, but there's so much going on that I can't possibly control it all. I was just lucky you were here with me this time.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Kilner[[Unwelcome Diversions]]
>
> **A:** That thing … in the bottom of the mine? It was honestly terrifying, like something from an old story brought to life. Why would it be trying to harm my house? I'm deeply afraid there is even more at stake than I thought, and it makes me terrified that my house is being deliberately targeted. There is more going on here than some simple trade dispute or local act of sabotage.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The "Otherhood"[[A Smuggler Extraordinaire]]
>
> **A:** Desperate times make people do horrible things. I could never have imagined that bandits and outlaws on this scale would be terrorizing the Plateau. Their leaders are surely guiding them toward a bad end, but I can't help but wonder whether we in Ordain bear some responsibility for the chaos out here and could help more. But for the Otherhood to become this much of a movement in just a few short years? Everywhere I look, something else seems to deepen this web, and I don't like where it is leading.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Seawall[[Simmering Discontent]]
>
> **A:** Everything seems to come back to Seawall. How can a place so close to Ordain be so antagonistic to the Trading Houses, the Ordinate, and normal Ordani? It seems like someone is working very hard to cause chaos. It's also strange that no one seems to have noticed. Something needs to be done soon to improve relations between the Trading Houses and Seawall, or there will be more violence on the Arctus Plateau. After fleeing Seawall, though … and seeing how angry everyone was, I don't think I'd ever be able to get through to them.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Sea Serpent[[Strange Depths]]
>
> **A:** I think that creature was a Leviathan, or something similar. You hear about them, but this is the first time I've ever seen one. I thought people always exaggerated their size ... I believe it might actually be helping the Otherhood, or somehow under their control, which is another scary thought. I would do everything I could to avoid running into it again, and I'll try to warn others to stay away from the waters around Seawall.

### Thoughts on Characters

Though Lyla is not a gossip, she's happy to share her assessment of significant NPCs the party has encountered.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Agraband
>
> **A:**
>
> Who doesn't love a good story? Which means loving a good storyteller as well, of course. While I was away, it was storytellers like Agraband — and even Agraband himself, once or twice — who kept me feeling like I knew what was happening in Ordain. Though he's been telling big stories while on this road, gossip and storytelling often go hand in hand.
>
> If I'm being honest, part of me wants to ask him if he has any stories of House Cevher back in the day. Maybe my father got up to some hijinks in his youth that he never had a chance to tell me about? I'd love to hear those stories with Agraband's flair for the dramatic.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Clipper
>
> **A:** A fun spirit, and a useful merchant to find while traveling. I hope to run across them again. There is never a lack of need for important items, especially in these troubling times.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Ankarist
>
> **A:** I keep wondering if I remember Ankarist from Ordain. In truth, he might have escaped my notice, despite his distinctive looks. Then again, it's sometimes hard to spot investigators of the Veiled Chain; they seem to blend into the background. He appears to be a dour yet dependable person, and I wish him luck in his own investigation. Truly.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Sin
>
> **A:** A charming little companion. I don't think I've ever met someone from the Striders before; they seem quite interesting! She appears amazed by everything around her, and I think it's lovely that she's enamored with the Cindaric Sages. It's a good reminder of the Cindaric Sages' important to the people. Too often, I forget all they do, and how they can be a comfort to those seeking their way.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Sigil[[A Friendly Reunion]]
>
> **A:** I can't think of a better mentor and friend. I have so many fond memories of him teaching me random things from his vast library; he was always so patient. A little scatterbrained, though. I still have the first book he ever gave me in my pack, *Trade in the Early Days of Ordain & the Fall of House Komric*. It was one of many he patiently read to me, and seeing him again was a blessing.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Zodi Trask[[Unhappy Accidents]]
>
> **A:** He has tried his best, and he seems to be well loved in Arcturel, but I worry about everything that has happened there. I hope that he is able to right the ship now, but I'll need to check in with him often.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Juro Wandren[[A Smuggler Extraordinaire]]
>
> **A:** Juro and I were close once. I was born into one Trading House, and he stumbled his way into another. When my father found out we were friends, he was angrier than I had ever seen him. Still, Juro and I were young, and we both liked creative solutions to problems. I hadn't spoken to him in many years, but I hoped he could help, and I hope I can trust him now. He does like to hide in the shadows, though, so I hope I'm not proved wrong in the future.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Sticks[[Simmering Discontent]]
>
> **A:** I don't know where that villain came from, but he seems to hate the Trading Houses and Ordain. He was accusing House Cevher of causing earthquakes and hurting people! Nonsense. And all while he seems to be the one riling up people in the Arctus Plateau and sowing chaos. His silver tongue is dangerous, though. A lot of innocent people are caught up with the bad, and he could easily cause a crisis in the future.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Salara`[[/eventState otherhood1Ship]]`
>
> **A:** Truthfully, despite her otherwise obvious flaws … talking to Salara felt a little like home. That insistence on rules being rules is classic House Cevher, but so is applying the rules with a bit of heart. If you're clever, you can leave just enough of a loophole in a regulation for those with good intentions to squeeze through, and those with bad ones to trip on. And something tells me the Queen of Scales is plenty clever. I just hope she's clever enough to stay clear of this Otherhood nonsense.

### About Yourself

If the characters would like to know more about Lyla, she's happy to share some of her past.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your story?
>
> **A:**
>
> I suppose I'm not that important in the grand scheme of things. I've never felt like I belong in this position, but if you must know ... In Ordain, I'm Lyla Cevher, the eldest daughter of Kalton Cevher, who was the head of House Cevher, one of Ordain's famous Trading Houses. I'm on my way back to the city because ... well, my father is dead and ...
>
> > She pauses and takes a long, deep breath.
>
> That means I'm now the head of House Cevher. At least, I will be once I get back to the city and assume the title and all the responsibilities. I used to have big dreams about one day finding new paths for House Cevher, paths that would make us stronger than ever before with my father's help after he retired. Now I'm going to have to work it out on my own.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** House Cevher?
>
> **A:** House Cevher is a Trading House focused on ores, mining, and stonecraft, a bit of metal refinement, and the like. To the outside world, they're one of the great Trading Houses of Ordain, a vital link between Ordain and the Arctus Plateau beyond. And that's all true. Not to mention that they're my family. Literally. The problem for me is that my father always led the house with one foot in the past. I've always wanted to shake things up a bit, but I trusted him to keep the ship steady.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why you were away?
>
> **A:** I felt that I needed experiences that were hard to come by in Ordain. My father suggested I go and stay with my distant great-aunt Jeramia in Casla Brava. I've been there for about four years now, and I was planning to come back next year ... but then everything changed.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your father's death?
>
> **A:** Death can happen at any moment … yet I never expected it to happen this way. He wasn't old or sick; we only spoke through magic a month ago. When I heard he died, I was … devastated. We occasionally argued about trying new mining techniques and changing traditional methods, but we always stayed close. I feel immense guilt for not being with him in his final moments. I hope he knew how much I loved him.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your plans?
>
> **A:** I need to return to the city, officially assume the position of head of the house, and ensure my brother, Funar, is well. I also owe it to my father to protect House Cevher's interests, regardless of our current troubles or future challenges. He may not be here to see it, but I will make my father proud. I'm confident of that.
