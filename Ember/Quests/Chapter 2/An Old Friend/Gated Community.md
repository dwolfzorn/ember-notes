# Gated Community

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration and Social Event occurs as the party reaches the gates of Seawall, which are well guarded. In this Event, the party can:
>
> - Either talk or sneak their way past the Seawall guards.
> - Gather resources or information from those waiting in line.
> - Create a distraction.
>
> The party's choices determine whether they gain Full Access, Limited Access, or manage to sneak into the city, which will affect the party's choices in the following Event. Refer to the Outcomes section to select the correct Outcome.

### Talking Your Way In

Seawall is a large city, and unlike the nearby Ordain, it is well fortified with strong gates and high walls. Its walls cannot be scaled without attracting attention from the guards and other waiting travelers, and the two entrances to the city pass by guard stations. The guards are strict, questioning each traveler who seeks to enter the city, but they rotate regularly, so the party may make the attempt more than once.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you draw closer to the front of the line, Lyla begins shifting her weight from one foot to the other, and once again, her hand rests idly on her rapier's guard. She turns to whisper to you.
>
> > Listen … getting into Seawall is going to be no easy matter. The place has always been well fortified, but do you see how many travelers the guards are simply turning away? Maybe we should talk to some of them, see if they have any tips that will help us through?

> [!info] Social
> #### Talking Your Way In
>
> If the party decides to proceed to the front of the line, the guards question their reasons for entering Seawall.
>
> > Again, I ask, are you here to rail at fortune, or do you come to find your own?
>
> The party can convince the guards that they should be allowed into Seawall with a successful **Diplomacy (DC 20)** or **Intimidation (DC 20)** group check (if at least half of the group succeeds, the entire group succeeds). If the party is with Lyla and she is not in disguise, they automatically fail the check.
>
> > There's something about you, miss … what did you say your name was again? I'm sorry, but you just don't strike me as the Seawall type.
>
> The DC of the Persuasion and Intimidation checks can be affected by the party's actions, dropping by 2 for meeting any of the following conditions:
>
> - The party has [[Forged Recruitment Papers]] from Juro Wandren.
>   - If the party has a Pristine Forgery, the check to enter Seawall is also made with **+2 Boons**.
>   - If the party has a Rudimentary Forgery, someone in the party must make a successful **Deception (DC 15)** check to explain its quality. On a failure, the guards do not believe the forgery and confiscate the forged papers.
> - The guards are distracted (see "Creating a Distraction" below).
> - A member of the party is wearing an article of clothing associated with the Otherhood (see "Gathering Intel & Looking the Part" below).
> - The party hides among the various members of the crowd as they enter (see "Hiding in the Crowd" below).
> - A member of the party uses the phrase "For Other Fortunes." (see "Gathering Intel" below).
>
> On a successful group check, the party gains full access to the city. While the party enters Seawall using this method, it doesn't mean they have passed the gates entirely unnoticed; either a guard, the people entering alongside the party, or locals inside the gates see them and start spreading the word.
>
> If the party fails the check, they are denied access to Seawall. They may attempt the check again, but unless they convince the guard that they are newcomers to the gate (via the use of a disguise via a Disguise Kit, borrowed clothing, or a spell or magic item that changes their appearance), they roll with **-2 Banes**. If the party succeeds on a repeat trip to the gates, they are given only limited access to the city.

#### Luxarum Attunement: Full Access

If the party manages to gain full access to Seawall, each character advances their **Attunement: Luxarum (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

`[[/outcome fullAccess]]`

#### Mayis Attunement: Limited Access

If the party tries repeatedly and eventually gains access to Seawall, each character advances their **Attunement: Mayis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

`[[/outcome limitedAccess]]`

### Sneaking Your Way In

If the party instead attempts to sneak past the guards, they can try to sneak around the sides of the busy line and slip inside unseen. However, this does have consequences in the following Events.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Sneaking Into Seawall
>
> The party can sneak past the guards with a **Stealth (DC 20)** group check (if at least half of the group succeeds, the entire group succeeds).
>
> - [[Gated Community]]: The characters gain **+2 Boons** on this group check.
> - [[Gated Community]]: The characters gain **+2 Boons** on this group check.
>
> If the party fails the check, they are denied access to Seawall and may not reattempt the Stealth check. They may reattempt the Stealth check only if they take efforts to disguise themselves (via borrowed clothing or a spell or magic item that changes their appearance).
>
> While the party can sneak into Seawall using this method, this does not mean they have passed the gates completely unnoticed, and either a guard, the people entering alongside the party, or locals inside the gates might spot the party entering and start spreading the word.

#### Primordis Attunement: Sneaking In

If the party manages to successfully sneak into Seawall, each character advances their **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

`[[/outcome seawallSneak]]`

### Access Options

While the party might succeed on their first try without planning, they can also pause to discuss the best way to get inside. Some of the following options make both [[Gated Community]] and [[Gated Community]] easier.

> [!info] Social
> #### Gathering Intel
>
> The party can attempt to use the line of travelers waiting to get into Seawall as a resource that increases their chances of making it past the guards and into the city. To learn some ways of getting past the guards, the party can chat with those waiting in line.
>
> Any character who looks at the line of travelers and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check can identify a family of four who have made their way to the front before and might have information to share about the guards.
>
> > You've seen these four before. At least once. Being turned away at the gate. That hasn't stopped them, though, as they are back in line, wearing slightly odd-fitting clothes. Maybe they know something you don't.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check spots a confident young person who stands out as being confident about their chances of getting into the city.
>
> > Others may look nervous, shifting back and forth from one leg to another, but not the wiry human with the bow on their back and a gleam in their eye. If there's a secret to getting past the guards, it seems like they know it.
>
> If characters attempt to ask other travelers in line about the guards, they receive no useful information.
>
> #### The Family of Four
>
> The matriarch of the family, Isei Velz, has noticed that those who are wearing leaf leather like the Otherhood does are getting through. Her family traded some of their belongings for a pair of leaf leather gloves and suggests the party do the same.
>
> > This isn't our first time near the front of the line, but we've never actually spoken to the guards. Just done a bit of observation over the last day or so. See these gloves? Leaf leather. Very pricey, really. But it's what the Otherhood wears. Every time I see someone who has gone right to the front and been let in without question, they've got on something like this. There are some folks who have it in line, if you can spot them and have something to trade.
>
> #### The Confident Traveler
>
> The young traveler, Garrius, is convinced that saying "For Other Fortunes" when answering the guards will make a difference.
>
> > It's all about saying the right thing, if you know what I mean. Use the words that they use and they'll think you already belong. A whisper of "For Other Fortunes" and I'll be inside the walls. I'm sure of it.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Looking the Part
>
> If the party has the [[Cloak of Kindly Visage]], Lyla will request to wear it, as her face may be known by those familiar with House Cevher. If the party declines, or does not have the item, Lyla will need alternate clothes before entering the city. In addition, the party can use any clothes they procure to create a disguise of their own.
>
> - **Stealing an Outfit:** Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check sees clothes on a wagon whose driver isn't paying much attention to them. They can sneak up on the wagon with a successful **Stealth (DC 14)** check and steal a set of clothing from the wagon with a successful **`[[/skillCheck stealth14]]`** check. If they are caught stealing, they only gain limited access to Seawall, even on a success at the gates.
>
> - **Knowledge: Crime**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> - **Buying an Outfit:** Characters can offer to purchase a spare outfit from one of the travelers waiting in line. The going cost is 4gp for a simple outfit and 10gp if the outfit includes some item made with leaf leather. Any character who makes a successful**Diplomacy (DC 14)** or **Intimidation (DC 14)** check convinces the traveler to drop the cost to 3gp and 8gp .
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character convinces the traveler to drop the cost to 2gp and 6gp .
>
> - **Convincing Someone to Give Up An Outfit:** Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check convinces someone to give up their outfit.
>
> - **Knowledge: Crafts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Trade**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Hiding In The Crowd
>
> The party can attempt to blend in with one of the larger groups of travelers to avoid catching the eyes of the guards completely by finding a large enough group with a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check and slipping into the crowd with a successful **Stealth (DC 14)** group check (if at least half of the characters succeed, the entire group succeeds).
>
> - [[Gated Community]]: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Creating a Distraction
>
> The party can attempt to create a distraction in order to sneak past the guards more readily. This could be a magical effect that would catch the guards' attention, starting a fight as noted below, or any other improvised distraction by the party.
>
> - **Starting a Physical Fight:** Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 16)** check can spot groups that are standing close enough together that a good shove might knock them out of alignment and cause those waiting to crash into each other. To pull off the maneuver, they must make a successful **Athletics (DC 14)** check, or the ensuing tangle of carts and travelers fails to cause a chain reaction.
> - **Starting an Argument:** Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check can spot Higel, a traveler who is not taking the wait well and could be easily drawn into a fight.
>   > They see all of us waiting out here, but do they care? Bad enough that bandits are driving folks out of their homes, but now everyone is acting like we don't belong? I was a mayor, I'll have you know. Of a very small village, but still. Why should I have to wait at the back of the line?
>   Higel can be drawn into an argument with those around him in line with a successful **Deception (DC 14)** check to make some loud declarations about mayors and lines that will enrage him.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Suspicion Rating
>
> At the conclusion of this Event, the party gains a Suspicion Rating of either 0 or 2. A higher Suspicion Rating will limit what they can access in the following event of [[Simmering Discontent]].
>
> - If the party succeeds the first time in [[Gated Community]] they can enter Seawall with a Suspicion Rating of 0.
> - If the party succeeds any other time in [[Gated Community]] they enter Seawall with a Suspicion Rating of 2.
> - If the party decides to sneak into the city instead of [[Gated Community]], they enter Seawall with a Suspicion Rating of 0, they are penalized for failing checks in the following Events.
>
