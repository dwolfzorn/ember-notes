# Strange Depths

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Combat Event occurs as the party flees Seawall via ship and are forced to defend itself against an unleashed [[Leviathans]] that is being controlled by the Otherhood.
>
> During this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Experience ocean combat and repair their commandeered ship as they flee.
> - Evade the pursuing [[Leviathans]] Jerracksis and fight it off as it attacks their ship
> - Learn more about Leviathans and discuss the impact of their investigation in Seawall.

### Leviathan Attack

**Work in Progress!** This Event uses the [[Ocean]] Scene. This is temporary. In later updates, the Event will feature a full Leviathan combat Scene. For the time being, this Event can be run on the Region Map.

As the party flees the docks of Seawall, they are quickly overtaken by the Leviathan Jerracksis unleashed by Sticks. Jerracksis, a youthful, serpent-like Leviathan, is mostly aquatic but is an extremely fast and lethal creature, not to mention larger in size than the ship the party is on. Sticks is manipulating it using strange magic, leading the Otherhood members to believe he possesses godlike powers, and this is one of the main reasons they follow him without question.

As soon as the party takes a breath, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The shouts of the Otherhood members echo behind you as your ship sets sail. Strangely, no other ships appear to be casting off or trying to follow. Along the docks, you see the Otherhood cheer and raise their fists. Across the water, they start chanting the same name repeatedly, accompanied by a rising, deep thump of drums.
>
> > JER-RACK-SIS, JER-RACK-SIS, JER-RACK-SIS!
>
> Even as the chants fade and the docks recede into the distance, the strange drumming remains clearly audible, along with the whimpers of several terrified crew members. One of the three on the boat grips the gunwale so tightly that his knuckles turn bone white, while another has his head in his hands. The third, without hesitation, lets out a scream and jumps over the side into the awaiting waves.
>
> > You don't understand! We … we have to get out of here, he's unleashed it!
>
> The captain you convinced to come along, whose name you still don't know, lets out a long, hearty laugh and doesn't look behind him as he maintains the steady course. As you look back, you see a shape in the water rapidly approaching.
>
> A very … very large shape.
>
> > LOOKS LIKE WE'VE DONE IT NOW!
>
> As the captain finishes his bellow, a titanic crash lurches the ship to one side as a massive beast explodes out of the water next to you. It is easily twice as long as your vessel, and its movements displace water in such great volumes that it nearly capsizes you all at once. Only the skillful efforts of the captain and the natural buoyancy of the ship keep you from utter disaster. Water rains down from the creature's large scales as its baleful gaze twists in the air, staring at you with golden, burning eyes. You cling to the rigging and rails to avoid toppling into the heaving waves.
>
> The captain bellows over the chaos:
>
> > TO ARMS! YA POX-RIDDEN BALLAST! Fight the thing off while I keep us steady! OH! And try NOT TO GET EATEN!
>
> The captain's voice continues to boom out commands as he laughs over and over again.

#### WIP: Early Access

The combat section below is for an Event recently revised to reflect the intended design for this encounter, but it is not yet prepared for play. Expect future changes to introduce the mechanics of this combat encounter in a fully functional manner!

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### The Escape from Jerracksis
>
> While the party has escaped from the Otherhood in Seawall, they are being relentlessly pursued by Jerracksis across the bay outside the city. They have little hope of killing the creature as it currently stands and must continue to flee, rolling initiative at the conclusion of the above read-aloud.
>
> Thankfully, the unnamed captain keeps the ship steady and maintains its course. During the combat with Jerracksis, the captain cannot take any damage, and the ship continues in a straight line. The terrified crew takes no action as they cling to the ship.
>
> As Jerracksis swims closer to the ship, it can take several actions that the party needs to defend against or attempt to repair as a consequence with their own actions during their turn. The party will also need to actively attack Jerracksis, as while the damage is not enough to kill the creature, it will drive it off and end the chase.
>
> #### Jerracksis Actions
>
> The Leviathan Jerracksis makes three initiative rolls, representing attacks by its head, body, and tail. This means it takes three actions each round, following the sequence of the initiative rolls in between the rolls made by the party. On its turns, roll on the table below to decide which action it performs for each of the three initiative rolls.
>
> | 1d3 | Jerracksis Actions |
> | --- | --- |
> | 1 | Smash - Jerracksis slams his body into the side of the ship, dealing 1 Ship Damage, and every character must make a**Athletics (DC 14)** check or they are knocked prone |
> | 2 | Venomous Spray - Jerracksis rears its head out of the water and spits acidic bile onto the deck. It deals no ship damage, but sets a small fire as the bile ignites upon contact with the wood. Creates a 10ft circle of fire that deals `[[/damage 1d6 fire]]` damage to anyone in that space and anyone who ends their turn in it. |
> | 3 | Bite - Jerracksis lunges forward and bites a massive hole in the side of the ship, allowing water to rush in and dealing 1 Ship Damage |
>
> #### Ship Health
>
> The ship has a maximum of 10 Health Points. Due to the actions taken by Jerracksis, if the ship's health drops to zero, it will be shattered, causing the party, captain, and frightened crew members to be thrown into the water. They will then need to find their own way to land as Jerracksis swims away, satisfied.
>
> #### Ship Actions
>
> A player character can perform one of these actions on their turn, replacing their usual combat options. As the combat begins, inform the party that these actions can be taken on their turn instead of attacking the Leviathan. Many of these actions are also commands that the captain yells amid the chaos as the ship desperately tries to leave the bay.
>
> - **Plug That Hole!** The character runs over to the hole in the ship made by Jerracksis and stuffs it with tar and canvas, then tries to secure it with wood and nails lying on the deck. Make a**Athletics (DC 14)** check.
>   **Result:** On a success, heal one Ship Damage; on a failure, the hole was too big to repair, and the damage remains. The ship cannot be repaired above its maximum health of 10.
>
> - **Clear the Deck!** The character runs to put out the fires made by the Venomous Spray. Make a **Stealth (DC 13)** check.
>   **Result:** On a success, the fire is removed from the deck.
> - **Where's it heading?!** The character must move around the ship using their entire movement speed on their turn, attempting to spot the creature's heading. Make a**Awareness (DC 16)** check.
>   **Result:** On success, the character works out where the creature is heading next, and the next two attacks made against it gain **+2 Boons**. On a failure, Jerracksis slips beneath the water and disappears for a moment.
>
> #### Escaping the Leviathan
>
> As they attempt to escape, the party must attack Jerracksis to drive it away. To end the combat, Jerracksis has 90 Hit Points. When the party deals this amount of damage, the creature retreats, slipping underneath the ship and returning to the waters around Seawall.

### A narrow escape

Once the party is clear of danger, they may want to discuss what just occurred and after a few tense moments after the combat ends, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Looking back, the Leviathan's long form recedes into the distance as it appears to swim back toward Seawall. It seems your efforts were worth little, and most of your attacks didn't seem to bother it much—perhaps it experienced the pain like a sting from some insect and decided you were far enough from the city it safeguards, but it's hard to tell.
>
> The captain on the tiller doesn't move from his post but turns to regard you with a grin.
>
> > A grand old escape, and you all seemed to acquit yourselves well, eh! What's the heading, onward to Ordain?

> [!info] Social
> #### What Was That?
>
> After the combat ends, the captain reveals himself as Captain Razor Blackwood, captaining the Flywind. He is a former member of House Bastilla who is now retired. He maintains his own ship to transport rare goods between the Waerd city of Wick and Ordain. He stopped in Seawall to smuggle a recent shipment of weapons, for which he was promised a substantial purse. Unfortunately, the Otherhood kept delaying payment, causing him increasing frustration.
>
> > I think I won't be dealing with those "Otherhood" types anymore; they seem unfriendly, and not in a good way. Plus, they swindled me out of a pretty penny for my last haul…
>
> #### Speaking with Lyla
>
> During the combat, Lyla tried to help as much as she could, but she is now lost in thought. The party can try to talk to her, but she only responds with the following:
>
> > It's all quite overwhelming. This Otherhood is gathering near the city, and they appear to hold a grudge or vendetta against my house. I will need to seek help from the other Trading Houses, but I can't shake the feeling that I am in over my head. As soon as we get to Ordain, I'm heading to the Ordinate to tell them what has happened.
>
> Although Lyla is not in the mood to talk, during the journey, Captain Blackwood is happy to chat with the party about all sorts of topics and has a lot of knowledge about the oceans of Ember and ships.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Was that a Leviathan?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Yeah, it was … though I’m not sure why my reckless crew was so worried; it was just a young one. If you don’t know, though, Leviathans are massive creatures said to be as old as the world itself. They are generally reclusive creatures, choosing to avoid settlements, but many are territorial, aggressive, and violent. It is believed that Leviathans are intelligent and immortal, and they wield power on the same level as the Shard Gods in many cases.
>
> He stops to think for a moment and rubs his short beard.
>
> > What's more important is how these Otherhood folks are controlling it …

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What will you do when we get to Ordain?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Hah! I'll have to go retrieve some of my savings from my old estate and get this old ship back in shape. I'll probably stick around for a while and try to wrangle a new crew... this one seems a little useless... but if you need my services in the future, you can come and find me.
>
> He winks at the party, and his toothy grin is somehow infectious.
