# A Smuggler Extraordinaire

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration and Social Event occurs as the party searches [[Nain]] for [[Lyla Cevher]] old friend, [[Juro Wandren]]. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Ask the locals about the bandits and where to find Lyla's old friend Juro Wandren.
> - Talk to [[Juro Wandren]] on the [[Vista: Golden Flats]] to get information about the current situation with the bandits and their connection to the city of Seawall, as well as House Wandren's involvement in the Arcturel scheme.
> - Obtain forged documents from Juro to help them gain entry into the nearby city of Seawall.

### Lyla's Mood

Before attempting to ask about Juro Wandren by talking to the local villagers, the party can stop and ask Lyla if there is anything wrong, and if the party is paying close attention to her, you can read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Lyla appears visibly flustered, and her nervousness is obvious. She has her hand once more on the guard of her rapier at her side and has her mouth tightly pressed together.

> [!info] Social
> #### Lyla's Uncertainty
>
> The party might be curious about Lyla's uncertainty, which is clearly evident. If they wish to understand more, they can ask her directly or try to gauge her mood. When asked why she's nervous or if she's excited to see Juro Wandren again, she says the following:
>
> > I … well … Look, we haven't seen each other in a long time — years, in fact, and I'm sure he's a different person now. I remember him being vibrant and curious about the world, and he also had a mischievous streak that I always found refreshing and, honestly, very funny. I hope he's glad to see me and able to help.
>
> Any character who observes Lyla's face and mannerisms and makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 12)** check intuits that she's not exactly worried about the meeting with her old friend because she's afraid or fearful; instead, she seems to be flustered, and her cheeks have turned slightly pink.

### Asking about The Locals

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Nain is a very typical Arcturian village; its streets are quiet and almost tranquil, and there is a softness and a sense of relaxed atmosphere everywhere you look. However, the villagers don't seem very welcoming. None of the villagers are openly hostile, but you do get the sense that the few nearby are silently judging you.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Nearby Locals
>
> There are several locals near the party that they can walk over and attempt to talk to. It's entirely possible that a few bad rolls will make the villagers defensive, and they will refuse to tell the party any information that will reveal where Juro is.
>
> The first person the party can ask is Elowen. An older woman is sitting on a nearby porch, mending a thick, woolen coat. She doesn't look up at first as the party approaches, but she stops her needle mid-stitch. She looks at the party's armor, then at the weapons on their hips, her eyes narrowing with a weary sort of defiance.
>
> > Juro? We don't have many by that name here. Just folk who mind their own business.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 18)** check can lean into their good intentions or mention helping refugees which mollifies Elowen. She'll suggest trying the Kapen Street back to the main road, Juro has a little overhang there. Only one character can attempt to persuade Elowen, and any other attempt fails. If anyone tries intimidation tactics, she immediately rises and heads inside her house without another word.
>
> The second person the party can ask is Rorphen, a burly man hauling a crate of salted fish. He drops the crate with a heavy thud as the party approaches. Rorphen's voice is like grinding gravel. He wipes sweat from a scarred brow.
>
> > This isn't a town for sightseeing, and we've had enough bandits passing through lately. If you're looking for someone to rob, you’ve picked the wrong time and the wrong village.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 19)** check can lean into their good intentions or mention helping refugees. which mollifies Rorphen. He'll suggest trying the Kapen Street back to the main road, Juro has a little overhang there. Only one character can attempt to persuade Rorphen, and any other attempt fails. If anyone tries intimidation tactics, he immediately rises and heads down the wharf without looking at the party again.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Following a Lead
>
> If the characters say the wrong thing to any of the local Nain villagers, which is very easy to do, they may be at a loss for how to proceed, but as they wait, a child named Merinda Chip, who had been listening to the party and Lyla nearby, very obviously begins to sneak away. She crouches low to the ground and "sneaks" around some crates near the wharf.
>
> The party must make a successful **Awareness (DC 15)** check. If successful, they realize that Merinda seems to be sneaking away from them and likely attempting to warn Juro. Even on a failure, the party senses that the little girl is acting oddly, and it might be interesting to follow her.
>
> - **Knowledge: Crime**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> A character can visually track Merinda with a successful **Awareness (DC 12)** or **Wilderness (DC 12)** check. On a failure, the character loses sight of her among the crates, barrels, and houses.
>
> If the party succeeds, they slowly follow Merinda through Nain and eventually reach a side street and an overhang where Juro has set up a few small market stalls in the shadows. Merinda then whispers to Juro for a moment before running off. Juro, meanwhile, doesn't seem alarmed and waits calmly for the party.
>
> If the party completely loses track of Merinda and no one can follow her through the streets, she eventually makes her way to Juro on her own and tells him that there are people looking for him. At that point, Juro peeks out from a side street and sees Lyla, waving excitedly for her to come over.
>
> > Lyla! Is that you? Over here!

### A Talk With Juro

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you approach the tarp stretched between several posts beside one of the village buildings, you are taken aback by what appears to be a vast array of trinkets. Some items glint in the light, while others resemble exotic weapons or tools. Among the beads, charms, and necklaces, there are also several ancient relics, books, pieces of clothing, and mismatched boots. The person responsible for the collection stands with hands on hips, grinning widely so that his Wirrun features are stretched into a jubilant expression. He looks directly at Lyla with bright eyes, clearly unable to hide his excitement.
>
> > Ly! What on earth are you doing here? I thought you would be in Ordain by now!
>
> Lyla, meanwhile, seems to relax as she realizes Juro *is* pleased to see her and begins chatting with Juro as if the two have never been apart.
>
> > I would have been, but with all this bandit absurdity, I had to investigate myself. I think the old itch to get things done came over me again. How have you been, though? You're looking well!

> [!abstract] Juro Wandren
> **[[Juro Wandren]]**
>
> Level 4 · Wirrun Operator
>
> ![Juro Wandren](../../../assets/image139.webp)
>
> Though he maintains a pleasant smile, with an expression that appears calculated to seem warm, the tall lanky wirrun is unmistakeably sizing up everyone and everything nearby, letting his gaze brush past any potential items of value before focusing on whatever is directly in front of him. As he finishes his appraisal, he smiles more brightly, though whether that's because it's met his expectations or not is hard to tell.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Happy Reunion
>
> Lyla and Juro start talking among themselves before introducing the party. Since Lyla is with the party, Juro is more than happy to chat with the party about any topic they want to ask about.
>
> Lyla and the party can fill Juro in on what they have learned so far in their search for the bandits who attacked Helkas. The conversation can either be between Juro and Lyla, or led by the party, but Juro is willing to share the following. Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 18)** check understands that Juro isn't saying everything, but he isn't willing to reveal any more of what he knows. This is not because of any malicious intent, but rather because of his own caution in saying too much.
>
> - The bandits, who call themselves the Otherhood, are based in Seawall.
> - The Trading Houses and Ordain itself are unpopular in Seawall. These days, Juro limits his time in Seawall as a known member of House Wandren.
> - Juro is willing to forge a letter that will help the group get into the city, but he can't help with any kind of disguise. Depending on what the party has done so far, the letter's quality will vary, as noted in [[A Smuggler Extraordinaire]].
> - Juro had heard that trade with Arcturel was bad, but didn't have any details, and wasn't involved enough with House Wandren at the moment to know why their name was found on papers held by the creature in the Arcturel mine. He suggests asking Hephiss Wandren in Ordain, the leader of his house.
> - If asked about the clues that the party has found so far, Juro suggests that they all point to Seawall.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Juro's Shop
>
> Juro has a few items to sell, which at this time are not officially listed. His stock changes frequently, but if the party asks, he has a few select items for them to purchase.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Otherhood Bandits
>
> **A:**
>
> > Oh, I've heard of the Otherhood, though it seems like they came out of nowhere. One day, it's a rumor, then a few isolated incidents, and now it feels like it's all building up to something. I try to keep my nose down, whatever you may think of my relationship with the law …
>
> He stops to wink at Lyla, who smiles softly.
>
> > I don't support anyone who goes around burning down farms and kicking puppies, though; that's just evil. I do know where you should go to find out more, though. Seawall. Though you may not make it past the front gate without my help.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Getting Into Seawall
>
> **A:**
>
> > The Trading Houses are currently less than welcome in Seawall, though I have no idea what it is they think we've — they've — done. Unfortunately, Ly, you scream House Cevher. Even if you changed your name, there's a chance someone might know you. I'd suggest going incognito. I'm not great with disguises, but if you need paperwork that makes you seem like you are Otherhood affiliated, I can help.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Forged Papers
>
> **A:**
>
> > To be honest, I want to know more about what's going on in Seawall as much as anyone else! Plus, I know better than to get in front of Lyla Cevher when she's made up her mind to do something. All I ask is that you let me know whatever you find out. And maybe owe me a favor in the future. I will say that despite my skill, how good this will be is going to depend on materials. What do you have on hand?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Arcturel and House Wandren
>
> **A:**
>
> > As a trader of all kinds of items, I do keep tabs on what's happening in the Plateau, and I'd heard there were some troubles in Arcturel, but that's all I know. I really don't see House Wandren getting involved in small local issues, but if they are, you should look into it in Ordain. Hephiss Wandren is still in charge, last I heard from the Wandrens I trade with in the city — she would know more. Though with this Otherhood everywhere, I'm sure she has her hands busy.
>
> Juro's tone and mannerisms make it clear that he doesn't have a very high opinion of Hephiss.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Clues So Far
>
> **A:**
>
> > Everything you've found has the mark of Seawall on it. That leather marking? There's a leatherworker in Seawall who uses that. And the ink that you mentioned, the shifting ink? I'm running low right now, but I get it delivered to me here via a contact in Seawall's Gray Market. I don't know if the place will have all the answers you need, but I'm sure some of them are there.

### Forging Otherhood Papers

While Juro is more than willing to forge documents for Lyla and the party, he wants their help in gathering the right materials — paper, shifting ink, and knowledge of the bandits' catchphrase. The more they have, the better the forgery will be.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Juro takes a quill out from somewhere you can't quite see, as if he made it appear just by thinking about it. As he speaks, he gestures to a stack of papers on a nearby table.
>
> > A good forgery doesn't just come out of thin air. You want to start with the right materials. Ideally, we'll need some paper used by the bandits, a pot of shifting ink, and some language that sounds realistic. You got any of that?

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Proper Ink
>
> To forge the message, Juro can supply [[Shifting Ink]]. Characters can provide him with the ink if they have any. If they do not have ink, Juro will grin and say he would be willing to part with some of his own in return for a little gold, or perhaps something they have found on the journey.
>
> > I know! I should be generous, but this stuff is hard to come by, I'll use my own supply for 200 gold, a pretty fair price, to be honest, or perhaps you have an item you can trade for instead?
>
> The party can hand over the gold to Juro, or they can attempt to give him a magic item they no longer need.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 12)** check convinces Juro to take an uncommon item.
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 16)** check convinces Juro to take a common item.
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> #### Scraps of Paper
>
> Juro needs a sample of the type of paper used by the bandits to recreate it. If the party has any of the following, they can use it, but they will lose the item, which Juro uses to make new paper: [[Recruitment Flyer]], [[Coded Bandit Instructions]], [[A Message To Tantrin]], or [[Decoded Bandit Instructions]].
>
> If characters don't have paper that works, or don't want to give up what they have, Juro suggests finding a recruit in Nain and taking their recruitment letter.
>
> > The villagers here have been trying to run potential bandits out of Nain, but the Otherhood still tries to sneak through. I'm sure you can find one if you look hard enough. Maybe back by the wharf where you came in. If you can find them and pick their pockets, you might find a recruitment flyer.
>
> Characters can acquire the paper in the wharf by first identifying a potential bandit with a successful **Awareness (DC 15)** check to spot the tattoo or leather armor of the bandit in question.
>
> - **Knowledge: Crafts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Crime**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Once the bandit is identified, the party can:
>
> - Pickpocket the target with a successful **Stealth (DC 15)** check.
>
> - **Accused By Crowd:** The character gains **-2 Banes** on this check if they weren't able to shrug off the crowd's earlier accusations.
>
> - Convince the target to give them a flyer with a successful **Diplomacy (DC 15)** check.
>
> - **Accused By Crowd:** The character gains **-2 Banes** on this check if they weren't able to shrug off the crowd's earlier accusations.
>
> On a success, characters acquire a [[Recruitment Flyer]]. On a failure, the target spots them and calls them out, agitating the crowd and preventing a second attempt.
>
> #### A Catchy Phrase
>
> If characters share the phrase "For Other Fortunes," Juro tells them that there is more to it, but adds it to the document.
>
> > For Other Fortunes, yeah. It's half of it. But it's a call-and-response kind of thing. I think the forgery works a lot better if you have both the call *and* the response. I wish I knew more, but we can get by without it. All I'm sure about is that it has something to do with hands. Keep your ears open in Seawall — if you can figure it out, you might fit in better.
>
> #### Putting It All Together
>
> The quality of Juro's forgery depends on how many items the party was able to acquire.
>
> - If they acquired both paper and ink, it is a pristine forgery.
> - If they acquired only one of the two, it is a quality forgery.
> - If they did not provide Juro with anything, it is a poor forgery.
>
> Use the Outcome boxes below to indicate the forgery quality.

#### Mayis Attunement: Pristine Forgery

If the party manages to obtain a pristine forgery from Juro, each character advances their **Attunement: Mayis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Cora Attunement: Decent Forgery

If the party manages to obtain a decent forgery from Juro, each character advances their **Attunement: Cora (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Ragen Attunement: Rudimentary Forgery

If the party manages to obtain a rudimentary forgery from Juro, each character advances their **Attunement: Ragen (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

`[[/outcome pristineForgery]]`

`[[/outcome qualityForgery]]`

`[[/outcome poorForgery]]`

Whatever the precise quality of the document (be it pristine, decent, or rudimentary), Juro hands over the [[Forged Recruitment Papers]].

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Juro waggles an eyebrow as he hands you the forged document. He shares a look with Lyla as he does, and his expression suggests no small measure of concern.
>
> > It really is nice to see you again, Ly. It looks like your time away has been good for you, and you're definitely in good company. I'll be packing things up here soon. I'm needed back in Ordain. If you need any help, come and find me, and good luck in Seawall.
>
> Lyla nods in return and smiles.
>
> > Of course, thank you so much for helping us and the refugees with supplies. You have a heart of gold under all that thievery.
>
> Juro looks shocked for a moment, his hand flying to his chest, but he then smiles and winks at her.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> It is clear that the source of information about the Otherhood is somewhere in Seawall. Armed with the information they need, the party and Lyla head for the port city, reaching the gates in [[Gated Community]].
