# Fallen House

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs as the party returns to Ordain following their investigation into the Otherhood. It can also occur if the party left Lyla to investigate on her own at any point in the Main Quest.
>
> In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Discover that the situation in Ordain for House Cevher has gone from bad to worse.
> - Ask nearby officials about what's happening, gaining insight into the proceedings happening ahead.
> - Meet important figures in the city of Ordain, including every member of the [[Ordinate]].
> - Discover that [[House Cevher]] seat on the [[Ordinate]] held by Ralton Cevher has been stripped away from Lyla, and House Cevher is under investigation for the major earthquake that hit the Arctus Plateau twenty years ago.
> - Receive a reward for their efforts from Lyla.
>
> This Event is depicted using the [[Vista: Ordain Overview]] Vista.

### Arriving at the Ordinate

Depending on the party's choices, they may have arrived at the Ordinate on their own or with Lyla.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Music: Lyla's Theme
>
> When the party reunites with Lyla, play  **Music: Lyla Theme**

If the party arrives with Lyla:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The clerks and officials rushing through the Ordinate don't pay you or Lyla any mind, but several members of the Trading Houses and Hallows glance at Lyla with their eyes widening in recognition. They then give Lyla a wide berth, going out of their way to avoid eye contact. One word among the murmured oddities is repeated over and over in whispers as people step out of Lyla's path: "A full session." Something is happening within the Council Atrium. Soon.
>
> As you make your way onward, Lyla is quiet, her eyes are wide, speaking softly only to say:
>
> > Something is terribly wrong; everyone is treating me like some kind of ghost. Half of me wants to turn around and run. I'm glad you're here with me.

If the party arrives alone/without Lyla:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> No sooner have you stepped a few feet onto the steps of the Ordinate than a cracking voice can be heard behind you as someone says, "You're here?. You turn, and you see Lyla Cevher staring at you with wide eyes.
>
> > If you came for me, thank you. I'm not sure what is going on, but something is terribly wrong; everyone is treating me like some kind of ghost. Half of me wants to turn around and run. No one will speak with me — about the bandits, about Arcturel, about anything — and I can't reach anyone in my family. There's been talk of a full session, and I need to take my seat on the Ordinate Council. Will you ... accompany me inside?

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Music: Default
>
> Once the party has finished this dialogue with Lyla, return to the default music:  **Music: Reset**.

### An Oncoming Storm

If the party wants to stop for a moment to question some of the people around them, they can ask Lyla to wait while they quickly try to figure out what is happening.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Furtive Rumors
>
> The party can attempt to pull aside a clerk, scribe, low-ranking member of one of the other Trading Houses, someone representing the Hallows, or a Cindaric Sage. They will not be pleased at first, but can be calmed with a successful **Diplomacy (DC 15)** check.
>
> Any character who speaks with the clerk and makes a successful **Intimidation (DC 18)** or **Diplomacy (DC 16)** check learns that something major was uncovered recently about the origins and culprit of the massive earthquake that ripped across the Arctus Plateau twenty years ago.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Intrigue**, or **Knowledge: Politics** will automatically be able to see that the people rushing toward the building are as confused as they are about what is going on, but it seems that this "Full Session" everyone is whispering about means that the Atrium is filled with over a thousand people, and a major surprise announcement is about to happen.

### The Main Council Atrium

As the party makes their way inside the massive Council Atrium, they push past the crowds gathered and make their way toward the central dais where the Ordinate Council sits in session.

Load the Ordinate Atrium Vista and then read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The large chamber is filled with people, with hundreds congregating on the main floor around the dais and more in the alcoves above. Members of the Veiled Chain, dressed in their rarely seen distinctive black leathers and attire, are maintaining a barrier between the crowd and the council members sitting at the curved table at the far end of the dais.
>
> Lyla attempts to sit in the empty seat at the far end of the table, but the Veiled Chain members block her. She attempts to speak, but a loud clang of a staff striking the flagstones echoes through the chamber, silencing all the murmuring voices and conversations.
>
> > SILENCE.
>
> The speaker, a member of the Ordinate, stands holding a long, ornate staff and dressed in long white and gold robes, while all other members remain seated.
>
> > There has been much discussion and deliberation about the topic we are about to address today. I thank everyone here for their patience. The issue today concerns the devastating earthquake that occurred twenty years ago and that I'm sure everyone here was affected by. For many years, we had assumed the earthquake was a natural, unavoidable phenomenon, and no one person or group was responsible.
> >
> > With great regret, I must inform the city that the Council has recently learned that this is not true.
>
> Murmurs and whispers spread among the people gathered around the chamber, and a few loud gasps can be heard echoing throughout, before the speaker slams her staff onto the stone once more.
>
> > SILENCE … I will have order!
>
> She pauses as her firm voice reverberates around the now silent room once again.
>
> > Due to the nature of our discovery, I regret to inform you that there is a change in the Ordinate Council. By unanimous vote, House Cevher has been removed from its position and is under formal investigation for its egregious actions that have endangered our great city. While the investigation is underway, the House's funds will be seized and held by the Ordinate, and all members of House Cevher with authority will be questioned to determine their culpability in a number of egregious actions.
>
> Suddenly, Lyla's voice rings out in response.
>
> > What are you talking about!? Are you claiming House Cevher is responsible for the earthquake twenty years ago? That's ridiculous! Where's your proof that our House has done anything wrong? I am Lyla Cevher, and I demand some answers!
>
> A wave of collective gasps and shock seems to push back the crowd as people step away from you and Lyla. The woman in gold and white only turns her head slightly toward Lyla and then signals with her free hand to one of the Veiled Chain members. He quickly walks forward and hands Lyla a sealed letter.
>
> Lyla shakes her head desperately, attempting to remain standing as she rips open the seal and quickly reads the letter. After only a moment, she gasps, dropping it to the ground. And the Ordinate speaker speaks in a ringing voice once again.
>
> > You ask for proof? You already have it! Our city is peaceful, yet your uncle, Darius Cevher, has been working with dark forces to bring about our ruin. He appears to have been betrayed and murdered for his foolishness, likely by the same corrupt forces he was trying to summon into our city! And your brother is missing. Could he be the murderer? Or just another victim of your family's vices? What do you say to that?
>
> But Lyla has nothing to say. The personal shock is too overwhelming for her as she gazes dimly at the letter on the floor. Though she nearly crumples with it for a moment, she steadies herself — as if she has steel in her spine — and, holding her head high, with tears streaming down her cheeks, she speaks to the gathered people and the council.
>
> > I say that it is time for me to mourn, not answer questions.
>
> With that, Lyla storms from the chamber.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Dark Place
>
> The party can stay in the chamber, but doing so only reveals that the council is leaving and the crowd seems to be melting away. Several clerks are tripping over themselves to head towards the antechambers with notes, and several people can be seen running for the doors to spread the news.
>
> The party can follow Lyla to the steps of the Ordinate, where she stands with a breeze that has whipped up her hair, and she is wiping her tears from her face.
>
> > I'm afraid that was not what I expected at all. I suppose the plots against my house are not just bandits attacking our mines.
>
> #### Reading Lyla
>
> Any character who makes a successful **`[[/skill diplomacy 12]]`** check sees clearly that Lyla is deeply hurt and on the verge of breaking down, but she is holding it together.
>
> > Thank you for your help. I need to take a breather. I will fight this and clear my house's name, but I need to find my brother and figure out what's going on. I'll return to my office for the time being, but I suspect I'll need your help in the days and weeks to come.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Can we do anything to help?
>
> **A:**
>
> Lyla doesn't smile, and tears well up in the corners of her eyes.
>
> > Probably, yeah. I need to try to take a breath, so please, I need some space. I'll reach out soon, and I would be glad to have your help.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What about our compensation?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Oh, of course, give me a moment. I have some with me right now.

If the party achieved [[Unwelcome Diversions]] they receive a  **10**.

If the party achieved [[Strange Depths]] they receive a  **25**.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Lyla remains on the Ordinate steps after you finish speaking, as people keep rushing out of the building. They avoid her as if she were cursed or ill, some sneering or giving her angry looks.
>
> > I will head back to Quarry Run to think. My house has — or rather, had — some issues, so I'll try to reorient myself. Thank you for all you've done for me.
>
> With that, she slowly walks away and disappears into the crowd after a few moments.
