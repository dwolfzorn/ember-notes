# The Turn of a Friendly Page

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social and Exploration Event takes the party back to [[Nain]] in an effort to help [[Amalthea Stonecraft]] remember who she was prior to her reincarnation in [[The Moon Child of Lake Jinro]], and serves as the finale for "The Book of Tales." In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Search the confines of the Stonecraft Estate for 3 or more "keepsakes" that will help young Amalthea recollect her memories.
> - Listen to Amalthea's final contribution to The Book of Tales: the story of her reincarnation as the so-called Moon Child of [[Lake Jinro]].
>
> This Event is depicted using the [[The Arctus Plateau]] Region Map.

### Amalthea's Homecoming

Whether or not the characters escort young Amalthea home from Lake Jinro, the Book of Tales only concludes once the party returns to Nain and visits the Stonecraft estate. Here, they must explore the contents of the house in search of three or more keepsakes that will help jog Amalthea's hazy, spell-addled memory.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Skill Challenge: Locating Amalthea's Keepsakes
>
> The lifetimes of information that Amalthea once knew will slowly return to her; but before they do, the party must help the Moon Child discover a few of her most treasured possessions — keepsakes that serve as psychic anchors to the many existences she has previously known.
>
> The party must succeed on at least 3 of the following checks as a part of this Skill Challenge. As the characters present each keepsake to Amalthea, her eyes alight with greater recognition, and she has a pointed anecdote or reaction to share.
>
> - **Arcana (DC 13)** The character is drawn to a wondrous item on a cluttered and unassuming bookshelf, a wooden mask that Amalthea once used as an arcane focus during her earlier days as a bard. The face of this lightly-enchanted mask can alter slightly according to the wearer's mood or performance.
> - **Society (DC 13)** The character recognizes the title of one of Amalthea's earliest works, a collection of epic [[Arcturian]] poems titled "Songs of the Windswept Reach."
> - **Science (DC 13)** The character is able to identify precisely which pigments Amalthea favored among her collection of inks, which can be corroborated in the handwriting of several of her most recent tomes and manuscripts.
> - **Awareness (DC 13)** The character spots a pair of [[Signara]] reading spectacles that Amalthea has used over various centuries, particularly during her elderly years.
> - **Diplomacy (DC 13)** The character is able to draw forth a clue from Amalthea herself to locate one of her most prized possessions. If persuaded to talk, Amalthea speaks of a stitched-up raggedy doll made in her own likeness, which can be found on a windowsill in her bedroom. This tiny effigy was a keepsake from Amalthea's first generation on Ember, and was gifted to her by a dear friend.
> - **Stealth (DC 13)** The character is able to amuse young Amalthea with card tricks using a deck of her own oracle cards, which is easily found nearby. The moment of frivolity conjures up countless memories of the joy she's given to other young ones throughout the years.
>
> When making the above checks:
>
> - **Culture: Arcturian**: The character gains **+2 Boons** when making a History or Persuasion check.
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** when making an Investigation or Perception check.
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** when making an Arcana or Sleight of Hand check.

### The Moon Child's Tale

Once three keepsakes have been presented to Amalthea, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A curious wind sweeps through the room, causing flames to flicker and parchment to flutter. The timeless assortment of curiosities that decorate this place seem to nearly come alive as they stir in the breeze and swell with arcane lambency. As this magical gust subsides, a familiar light returns to young Amalthea's eyes, along with the confidence of recognition.
>
> > My dear friends. What a road we've travelled together. The generosity of your guidance is immeasurable, but I'll begin by thanking you for helping me find myself amongst the detritus of my cluttered home. Each time I take the journey to Lake Jinro, I must try a bit harder to recollect what came before.
> >
> > Like all Aster, I was born to Ember with strange and unique blessings. My feathers and bardic talents are only part of my boons, as you've seen with your own eyes. I live to be reborn, every eight decades, and have lived on Ember for several generations. I am not invincible; my body is still a fragile and mortal vessel. But thanks to the power of Ember's moons, the ritual shrine at Lake Jinro, and my own precautions, I have lived to see many tales of the Golden Flats be told — including your own.
> >
> > A new day dawns, and another page is turned. Along with the stories you've collected, "The Moon Child of Lake Jinro" marks the final story in **The Book of Tales**. Well done, my dears. Now … care for a cup of tea?

### Concluding the Event

And with that, The Book of Tales is complete. Amalthea Stonecraft, newly reacquainted with her personal history and surroundings, is poised to once again assume her role as the Arctus Plateau's preeminent storyteller.
