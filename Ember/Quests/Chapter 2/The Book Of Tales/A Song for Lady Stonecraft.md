# A Song for Lady Stonecraft

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs while the party traverses either the [[Golden Flats]] or [[Rustvar Valleys]], and introduces the characters to the legend of the famed [[Arcturian]] storyteller [[Amalthea Stonecraft]], and serves as an adventure hook for "The Book of Tales." In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Encounter a roving band of bohemian vagabonds known as the Grass Road Revelers, whose resident bard Oona Tilelle sings a most peculiar song.
> - Listen to Oona's ballad about the storyteller Amalthea Stonecraft and the enchanted nature of her tales.
> - Chat with Oona and her allies to gain more information about Amalthea Stonecraft and her home town, the riverside trade hub known as [[Nain]].
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Ridge Valley Road" Level of the [[Vista: Golden Flats]] Vista.

### The Vagabond Camp

This small encampment of free-spirited vagabonds is openly inviting to the party if the characters approach with a friendly disposition themselves. Calling themselves the Grass Road Revelers, they're comprised of a motley but charming crew of foreign travelers and [[Ordani]] expatriates, led by a bard named Oona Tilelle.

**Oona Tilelle (Chaotic Good, Waerd Human, she/they)**

Thanks to her relentless charisma and far-traveled sensibilities, this winsome twenty-something raconteur serves as the de facto leader of the Grass Road Revelers, a roving troupe of vagabonds, misfits, and musicians who wander the [[Arctus Plateau]].

As the characters approach, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you make your way into the camp, the woman tuning the kithara looks up and greets you with a curious and welcoming smile. Her light gray eyes suggest a Lowlands heritage, a detail all but confirmed by the Wyrdic stitching and trinkets that decorate her loosely-fitted garb. She strums a chord and begins singing an enchanting song …
>
> > The tales of Lady Stonecraft, believe them if you may,
> > believe them if you care to find your own along the way.
> > Believe them if you feel a certain stirring in your heart.
> > Believe them 'cause the Lady is a master of her art.
> >
> > The tales of Lady Stonecraft are said to ring with truth,
> > From wedding bells to coffin smells and recklessness of youth.
> > From waking dreams and wanderlust of childhood fantasy,
> > to Ember shards to broken hearts and all that's in between.
> >
> > We're on the road to Nain again to hear the Lady tell!
> > We're on the road to Nain my friend, so join us for a spell.
>
> The rest of the troupe joins in for a second round of the chorus, which they conclude with a triumphant cheer. A wry grin crosses the bard's mouth before she speaks.
>
> > They've been practicing that bit for weeks. My name is Oona Tilelle, these are the Grass Road Revelers, and we appreciate your patronage.
>
> With a wink, the young raconteur motions to an open kithara case at her feet, where a few loose copper and silver coins are scattered for inspiration.

One or more of the characters may already be familiar with the legends and tales of Lady [[Amalthea Stonecraft]], who is famed throughout the Arctus Plateau for her epic, life-affecting stories.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Bard's Tale
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Society (DC 13)** check knows of Amalthea Stonecraft, the famous storyteller of Nain. She is understood to be a wise [[Aster]] decorated with the feathers of a bird, and her stories are said to inspire thousands the world over.
>
> - **Knowledge: Legends**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Culture: Arcturian**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character is well-read when it comes to Amalthea Stonecraft's fantastic tales, or has been exposed to some of them via oral storytelling. Indeed, they probably have a favorite story from her vast body of work that occasionally comes to mind.
>
> Any character who succeeds on an **Arcana (DC 13)** check can readily confirm that there is nothing magical about Oona's song, but can also reasonably speculate that Amalthea Stonecraft is capable of arcane spellcasting in her own right (if the legends about her stories are true).
>
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

The Grass Road Revelers have a few mundane provisions for trade and plenty of warm company for those who wish to keep it.

> [!info] Social
> #### Friendly Conversation
>
> If one or more party members agree to tell the raconteurs a brief tale of their own, Oona and the rest of her troupe have the following information to share:
>
> - The brief history of the "Grass Road Revelers" and how they came to be the adventuring troupe they are. The first three met each other about five weeks ago in a tavern in Casla-Brava, and they haven't looked back ever since. A hopeful but aimless group of raconteurs, the six of them roam the Arctus Plateau in search of stories, songs, and experiences to add to their collection.
> - The Revelers are slowly making their way to visit Amalthea Stonecraft, the legendary storyteller from Nain — a wordsmith whose stories are said to be so powerful, they change the lives of those who read or hear them.
> - If the party was unaware, Nain is built upon the banks of an enchanted river and serves as an important trade hub for the Arctus Plateau.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Deception (DC 13)** check is confident that Oona's account of Lady Stonecraft is one that she earnestly believes in. The character feels as though Oona has very little reason to mislead the party about her enthusiasm for the famed Amalthea Stonecraft and her celebrated storytelling acumen.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who are you?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Me? I'm just a kithara player from Casla-Brava with a few songs to sing. Long story short, I left my rather war-torn family behind to myself find a new one.
>
> She gestures toward her companions.
>
> > And these mad lads will certainly do. The first three of us met each other one night about five weeks ago over several flagons of ale, and we haven't looked back since. Now, we simply wander the plateau in search of stories and songs and friendly hearts.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Amalthea?
>
> **A:**
>
> > My whole life I've been told that the world has never known a greater storyteller than Amalthea Stonecraft. And with each new story I hear, I tend to agree. Her stories are said to be so powerful, they actually change the lives of those who hear or read them. But, like most things in life, you have to see it to believe it — so we're headed to Nain to visit Lady Stonecraft and find out for ourselves. Eventually.
>
> She smiles as the Revelers all chuckle with acute understanding of their shared wanderlust.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Nain?
>
> **A:**
>
> > They say the waters are never calm in Nain, which is built on the enchanted shores of the River Elenain at the edge of a gorgeous waterfall. Nain is even said to be adorned with talking statues that orate Amalthea Stonecraft's stories to passers by on the street. A sight to see, if ever there was one.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** A favorite tale?
>
> **A:**
>
> > When I was six years old, my dear old nan read me a book I've treasured ever since: "The Fourteen Fables of Fortigard." There isn't a day that goes by where I don't remember one of those silly fables. And they always seem to come to mind at the right time. Isn't it funny how childhood stories can stick with you for so long?

### Concluding the Event

The Grass Road Revelers are happy to host the party for a day and an evening, after which time they pack up camp and set off on their own — towards Nain, of course, but with a few unknown stops along the way. They politely refuse any offers from the party to join up for travel, citing a need to tie up loose ends before reaching Amalthea's hometown themselves.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> The party must decide whether or not to investigate the veracity of Oona Tilelle's song by visiting the famed riverside town of [[Nain]] for themselves. If the characters have yet to hear of Nain, this may strike them as an interesting reason to explore the peculiar waterfall settlement, where they'll encounter the Event known as [[The Storyteller of Nain]].
