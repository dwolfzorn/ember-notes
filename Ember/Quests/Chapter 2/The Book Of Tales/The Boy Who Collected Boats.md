# The Boy Who Collected Boats

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social and Exploration Event occurs four miles north of [[Nain]], on the eastern bank of the River Elenain, and allows the party to pursue the story of young **Eb Hargrove** — a boy who has a proclivity for finding parchment boats riddled with archaic messages on the nearby riverbank. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Visit the Hargrove Farmstead, where Eb lives with his parents **Gabi** and **Brandt** — only to discover that the boy has recently gone missing.
> - Inspect the farmstead for clues about Eb's whereabouts, focusing on: toys scattered in the front yard, the location of Eb's favorite fishing spot, and the confines of Eb's bedroom.
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Ridge Valley Road" Level of the [[Vista: Golden Flats]] Vista.

### The Hargrove Farmstead

Once the characters arrive at the Hargrove Farmstead, they'll quickly discover that young Eb is missing, and the boy's parents could use some timely help finding him. No matter what time of day the party arrives, the Hargroves — two hard working [[Arcturian]] — can be found in the yard woefully lamenting the disappearance of their son.

**Brandt Hargrove (Lawful Good, Arcturian Human, he/him)**

This stoic, hard-working farmer of the [[Golden Flats]] started a family with his childhood sweetheart, Gabi. Brandt tends to remain speechless these days, overcome with anxiety and grief about the helplessness of his son Eb's disappearance.

**Gabi Hargrove (Chaotic Good, Arcturian Ashka, she/her)**

This no-nonsense agriculturalist has a heart of solid gold. Gabi is eager for answers about her son Eb's disappearance, and will welcome household visitors in the hopes that they can help find the missing boy.

When the characters decide to approach the farmstead, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you survey the farmstead, the frocked woman takes note of your arrival and assuredly makes her way towards you. Drawing near, she looks back for a brief second to the sullen man hoeing the field before mustering a flimsy smile.
>
> > Well, you look like a capable group. And thirsty, perhaps. We've got plenty of clean water in the well, and hospitality for those that deserve it. The name's Gabi Hargrove. So, what brings you all the way out here?
>
> A glint of hope drifts to the woman's tired eyes as she awaits your introduction.

Once the party explains their reason for visiting, Gabi continues:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As soon as you mention the boy's name, Gabi's eyes alight with a spark of hope.
>
> > Ebdan Hargrove is my everything, friend. His little eight year-old smile can melt the hardest heart, and his silver eyes are brighter than the dawn. You've never met a child with more enthusiasm about these changeful fates of ours. Eb's been the light of our lives ever since he came into this world, and it will be a cold day on Ragen before I give up on him.
> >
> > You'll recognize my Eb by his light green scales and his silvery-gray eyes. And he loves the color red. He was wearing a red tunic the day before he disappeared. You see … five days ago, we woke up to find Eb gone, missing along with a few of his things and an old rucksack. Someone had pillaged the pantry as well. His little footsteps led out to the river and … that was it.
>
> The young mother is overcome with emotion, which she stifles a bit before continuing.
>
> > He's always been a good swimmer … News must travel quickly for you all to have arrived so soon. And for that we are very grateful.

> [!info] Social
> #### Meeting the Hargroves
>
> Gabi offers the party a round of water to quench their thirst, and invites them to join her on the porch or in the front room to discuss the plight of young Eb's disappearance.
>
> Gabi and Brandt are both relatively ignorant about the cause of Eb's vanishing, but they're both anxious to see their boy return home safely. While Brandt remains overtly silent, Gabi has the following information to share and recapitulate:
>
> - Eb has been missing for 5 days.
> - Life on the farm is simple, but easy. Eb has never wanted or needed for anything.
> - Eb's favorite pastime included folding parchment boats that he'd send downriver, and collecting the parchment boats of other children that live upstream — from places like [[Storsa's Strand]] and various homesteads along the river.
> - If the party wants to explore the Hargrove Farmstead for [[The Boy Who Collected Boats]], the characters should focus their investigative efforts on three locations in particular: the Toys in the Yard, Eb's Fishing Spot, and Eb's Bedroom.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Diplomacy (DC 12)** check or uses magic to read Gabi's mind is able to confirm the veracity of Gabi's claims.
>
> Once conversation has been exhausted, Gabi officially solicits the party for their help with locating Eb. In exchange for the party's services, Gabi is willing to offer them the only meager wealth the family has:
>
> - A [[Fluorite Bracelet]] heirloom
> - As many crops as they can reasonably carry (treat as 3 [[Rations]] each).
>
> Some specific dialogue options for Gabi are presented below.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Eb's paper boats?
>
> **A:**
>
> > As long as I can remember, children on the river have sent parchment boats scrawled with wishes and other messages downstream towards Nain. My own nan taught me how to fold boats when I was about five; and she was my age when she learned the pastime.
> >
> > Eb loved collecting boats that didn't find their way all the way to the falls in Nain, and he loved sending his own downstream. I wish he was out there putting one in the water right now …

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Hargroves?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Brandt and I settled here on the farm about ten years ago, after we inherited this plot of land from my family. He's a good man, a good provider; even if he doesn't have much to say at a time like this.
> >
> > Neither one of us has any extended family to speak of, none that we keep in touch with, so Eb brought a lot of life to this little house. And it's too damn easy to notice when it's gone.

### Gathering Clues

If the party decides to help the Hargroves locate Eb, Gabi invites them to take a look at anything around the farmstead they might consider useful, including: the **Toys in the Yard**, Eb's favorite riverside **Fishing Spot**, and the contents of **Eb's Bedroom**.

The scattered array of toys in the farmstead yard invites scrutiny, but offers very little conclusive information about Eb's disappearance.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Toys in the Yard
>
> Characters who take the time to examine the yard will locate a variety of rustic toys and playthings befitting an adolescent boy of eight: a collection of stone and glass marbles, a small army of tiny wooden soldiers, and a weather-beaten hobby horse.
>
> Any character who succeeds on an **Awareness (DC 12)** check comes to the firm conclusion that these toys have simply been neglected or dismissed, but don't point towards any other evidence or information.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

Eb's favorite fishing spot down near the water's edge offers some vague indications about the series of events that led to Eb's vanishing.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Eb's Fishing Spot
>
> Characters who take the time to examine the fishing spot will be able to find some rustic, child-sized fishing gear that's been cast aside: a rod and a tangled reel with no hook or lure, a small wooden bucket full of driftwood snags and polished river stones, a fishing net that's been patched a few times too many.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Wilderness (DC 13)** check is able to notice a few sets of weeks-old Eb-sized tracks that travel to and from the waterfront towards the farmstead's front door. The newest set of tracks, however, journeys to the river's edge and disappears — as if Eb was either pulled into the current, or waded directly into the water of his own volition.
>
> - **Knowledge: Tracking**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character notes the relative cleanliness of the tracks, and is unable locate any signs of struggle or conflict, suggesting that Eb waded into the river himself.
>
> Any character who succeeds on an **Awareness (DC 13)** check is able to locate a [[Tiny Copper Key]] in a hollowed out rock near the fishing gear. A character that is a sufficiently talented **Talent: Smithing Novice** , or that makes a successful **Science (DC 14)** check can deduce that the key isn't big enough for anything other than a small lockbox, and that perhaps the boy may have hid one in his Bedroom (see below).

Eb's bedroom is the location of the definitive clues that will point the party in the right direction to find the missing boy.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Eb's Bedroom
>
> Characters who take the time to examine Eb's bedroom will discover (alongside a standard array of adolescent furniture and toys) an overstuffed satchel full of parchment boats of various sizes and make. Most of them are blank and a few are scrawled with famous Arcturian nursery rhymes or expressions of well-wishes.
>
> Any character with a**Awareness (DC 13, Passive)** check is able to locate a loose floorboard in Eb's room, which can be easily pulled away to reveal a hidden compartment containing Eb's Lockbox. Alternately, any character who succeeds on a **Science (DC 13)** check can locate the loose floorboard while knocking on the hollow compartment.
>
> #### Eb's Lockbox
>
> Eb's Lockbox can be opened using the [[Tiny Copper Key]] found at Eb's Fishing Spot. Alternately, a character can open the lockbox by making either a successful **Stealth (DC 13)** check using a [[Lockpicks]] or forced open with a successful **Athletics (DC 18)** check.
>
> The Lockbox contains a secret stash of three parchment boats, each of them marked with crudely-scrawled writing. [[Parchment Boat 1]], [[Parchment Boat 2]], and [[Parchment Boat 3]] appear to be crafted from handwritten letters, featuring the same handwriting, and detail a curious story:
>
> - Closer inspection of these three hand-scrawled missives reveals a narrative. Via these three notes, a young girl named "Jo" claims to have found a cave upriver where a society of children has formed. Hidden away from the scrutiny of adults, this "Calloway Cove" is a paradise for the lonely and forgotten children of the [[Arctus Plateau]], who lack the friends or siblings to provide true companionship at a young age. Furthermore, Jo claims that the cave halts the aging process, and that the children who live there will never grow old.
> - By all accounts, it seems these letters compelled Eb to follow their guidance to locate this so-called cave a few miles upstream.
>
> These letters were actually written by the wily and contemptible kidnapper Jodessa Cort herself — a clandestine detail that will elude the party for now. The text of these three special parchment boats from the secret stash of Eb's Lockbox is presented below for your convenience.
>
> **[[Parchment Boat 1]]**
>
> *Hello. My name is Jo. I'm ten years old. I grew up in Storsa's Strand. But now I live in Calloway Cove. It's special here. There are no adults to tell us when to wake up or when to go to sleep. Nobody makes us wash our ears, and nobody tells us what to eat. My new brothers and sisters take care of me here. I hope you have a special place, too!*
>
> **[[Parchment Boat 2]]**
>
> *Hello! My name is Jo and I live in a special cave by the river, which we call Calloway Cove. I've only been here for a few weeks. Some of the older kids say that they've been here for years and never get older. I don't know if I believe them, but everybody else seems to. There are lots of kids here and we all take care of each other. We don't need adults. They don't understand.*
>
> **[[Parchment Boat 3]]**
>
> *Hello! My name is Jo and I'm looking for friends to join us in Calloway Cove. I'm ten years old and we have our own city here, where we make all the rules and nobody ever tells us no. Baxon says the water here is magic, and that we'll never get sick or old if we keep drinking it. Bax is my friend, so I believe him. But I miss my family sometimes. I guess it's okay to make a new family, isn't it?*

`[[/outcome found]]`

Following the party's preliminary investigations of the homestead, Gabi invites the characters to return as soon as they have any information (or if they're simply in need of rest and sanctuary).

### Concluding the Event

Once the characters have located the three parchment boat letters in Eb's bedroom and are satisfied with their investigation, they can proceed northwards along the River Elenain to find this so-called "Calloway Cove" and the missing boy himself.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> Once the party has discovered the parchment boats in Eb's Lockbox, they must continue their investigation by exploring the region two miles northward, where they can locate the hidden riverside lair of the kidnapper Jodessa Cort during the [[Down by the River]] Event.
