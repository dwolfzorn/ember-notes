# The Storyteller of Nain

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event takes place in [[Nain]], where the party will meet the famed [[Aster]] storyteller [[Amalthea Stonecraft]], who will set them off on an adventure of mythic proportions. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Learn more about Amalthea from one of many enchanted storytelling statues that are installed throughout her hometown, the riverside settlement of Nain.
> - Visit Amalthea's home. Here, the party will meet the renowned storyteller and learn about her forthcoming manuscript, her history as a fabulist, and a her life as an Aster.
> - Take on a Quest from Amalthea to track down four different [[Arcturian]] citizens and transcribe their personal stories in the manuscript.
>
> This Event is depicted using the [[The Arctus Plateau]] Region Map.

### Nain's Orating Statues

While traversing the streets of Nain, the party discovers one of many enchanted storytelling statues that feature the likeness of a local legend — Amalthea Stonecraft, the region's foremost author on folklore and myth.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The statue proceeds to orate a most fantastic tale for you, involving the whimsical story of a local pauper, a helpless romantic who found a curious bottle while fishing one day. After uncorking this wayside cruet, it seems the fisherman was magically pulled inside, and found himself living blissfully on the beaches of a miniature island. They say he lives there still, awaiting the fateful day when his one true love will find the same bottle washed upon her own lonely shore. The statue concludes by addressing you directly once more …
>
> > So be careful with precisely whose bottles you open, my friend, and remember this lovelorn tale as you float amidst life's tumultuous seas.
>
> And with that, the statue returns to resolute stillness.

After they listen to the statue's tale, the characters can inspect the stone effigy for additional information.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating the Statue
>
> Any character who inspects the statue will notice an inscription etched in **Language: Common** at the base of the pedestal it sits upon:
>
> > The Stonecraft Estate calls, if you have stories to share.
>
> Any character with a `[[/check perception 15 passive format=long]]` or who succeeds on a **Awareness (DC 13)** check is able to notice that there are actually some words inscribed in the stone book held by the statue:
>
> > Find your fate in the stories of Lady Stonecraft.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 13)** check is somewhat familiar with Lady [[Amalthea Stonecraft]], the famous storyteller of Nain depicted by the statue. She is known to be a wise Aster decorated with the feathers of a bird, and her stories are said to inspire thousands the world over.
>
> - **Knowledge: Legends**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Culture: Arcturian**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character is well-read when it comes to Amalthea Stonecraft's fantastic tales, or has been exposed to some of them via oral storytelling. Indeed, they probably have a favorite story from her vast body of work that occasionally comes to mind.
>
> A successful **Arcana (DC 15)** check is required for a character to accurately speculate the magical practices needed to accomplish the statue's enchantment (which include **Talent: Enchanting Journeyman**{Journeyman Enchanting} and at least some amount of capability with the **Talent: Rune: Illusion**{Illusion Rune}), and can reasonably speculate that Amalthea Stonecraft has some magical prowess in her own right (if the legends about her stories have any truth to them).
>
> - **Talent: Enchanting Journeyman**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Legends**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> After relating a brief tale, the magical statue issues another proclamation: intrepid adventurers are encouraged to visit Amalthea's nearby estate to meet with the author herself.

Once they've finished examining the statue, the party can choose whether or not to track down the so-called Stonecraft Estate where the famed storyteller of Nain resides among a library of her works.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Locating the Stonecraft Estate
>
> Any character who succeeds on an **Awareness (DC 13)** check over the course of a 30-minute search is able to track down the address of or directions to the Stonecraft Estate, an aged two-story house near the center of town, perpetually cluttered with countless books and historical records. This check can be repeated as many times as necessary, with a duration of 30 minutes each time.
>
> - **Culture: Arcturian**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character is able to locate the Stonecraft Estate within 10 minutes instead of a half hour.
>
> If any of the characters has an explicit history with Nain (as mentioned in a written backstory or conversation with the Gamemaster), they already know where the Stonecraft Estate is located, and can direct the party towards Amalthea's book-strewn residence themselves.

### The Stonecraft Estate

Once the characters successfully reach the Stonecraft Estate, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> An impressive two-story house of historical Arcturian design is nestled here at the end of a cul-de-sac in the middle of town. A handsome bronze placard mounted on the outer wall labels this sagging edifice as Stonecraft Estate.
>
> Past the wall's rusty gate, you can see piles of books through the home's dust-clouded windows, and weeds crowd the overgrown flower bushes that decorate the front lawn boxes. Driftwood wind chimes sing their inviting songs from the estate's front porch, and a lantern at the front steps is set to shine a warm, welcoming light upon the yard.
>
> As you regard the unmistakable charm of this place, the front door swings open slowly by itself, as if it were calling you to enter.

If the characters decide to enter:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Lit by candle and lamplight, the front room of the Stonecraft Estate is an amalgam of library and museum, instead of what many might consider to be a proper foyer. Various maps, trinkets, and contraptions are surrounded by countless books, diagrams, and scrolls. The place smells of apple cider, and you could spend your idle time here forever.

A moment later, the lady of the house appears:

> [!abstract] Amalthea Stonecraft
> **[[Amalthea Stonecraft]]**
>
> Level 10 (Elite) · Human Bard
>
> ![Amalthea Stonecraft](../../../assets/image163.webp)
>
> A wizened woman dressed in loose-fitting Arcturian garb stands before you, her elderly hands perched upon a knotted walking stick. You spy a pair of feathered wings on her stooped back, as brilliant and violet as any amythest, draped beneath the folds of a stitched brown apron. Similar quills line her forearms, and a ruffle of smaller feathers around her neck serves as an ersatz collar.
>
>  > [!quote] Read Aloud
> > A plume of purple feathers sits upon her aged head like a posh hat, capturing a fashionable kind of folk sensibility, which she wears with grace and aplomb. Her smile is as bright as the dawn, and her wrinkled eyes regard the world with a warm and perpetual interest.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This must be Lady Stonecraft, the legendary storyteller of Nain. She addresses you with the sincere familiarity of an elderly auntie.
>
> > Welcome home, my young friends. I've been waiting for you for quite some time. And here you are! My name is Amalthea Stonecraft, and I invite you to consider a friendly solicitation … a job, if you will. With payment, of course, should it benefit you. But please, make yourselves comfortable.
>
> As you settle in and begin to consider her words, Amalthea continues.
>
> > The time has come for my latest manuscript, the Book of Tales, featuring a collection of local legends — narratives as unique as the Arcturians who were born to tell them. And I need your help collecting these rather special stories.
> >
> > Like divinations themselves, my reputation as an oracle often proceeds me. However, my narratives must be gathered without the benefit of such prophecy. And as you can see, I have quite a few years behind me; I need a group of able raconteurs to travel a bit faster and a bit farther than these old bones might allow. Truth be told, I require fresh perspectives and fleet travelers to bring this new manuscript to life.
> >
> > But first, tell me a tale about yourselves! I have my suspicions you'll make a fine group of storytellers.

> [!info] Social
> #### The Storyteller's Quest
>
> Following a round of introductions and pleasantries, Amalthea petitions the party to visit four citizens of the [[Golden Flats]] to transcribe their tales for her latest book of Arcturian folklore. Each of these citizens harbors a story they have yet to tell, and the characters must coax these stories out of each of them. The proposed stories include:
>
> **[[The Rickety Man]]**
>
> Journey eastward to [[Steed's Point]], where a sentient construct made of wood and vine named [[Rattletrap, the Rickety Man]] keeps watch over a doomed village. Ask him how he came to be.
>
> **[[The Boy Who Collected Boats]]**
>
> Head to the **Hargrove Farmstead** north of Nain, where a young boy named **Eb Hargrove** has mysteriously disappeared from his front yard. Find the boy and learn his tale of lost and found.
>
> **[[The Signborn's Secret]]**
>
> Travel southward to [[Vortest Tower]], where a [[Signborn]] mage named [[Corvana Vortest]] navigates a clandestine situation. Learn the secrets of her solitude.
>
> **[[The Crystal Quarryman]]**
>
> Visit [[Arcturel]] and the [[Kiska]] quarryman known as [[Zodi Trask]], who is rumored to be undergoing a most strange transformation — one that is turning him to solid crystal. Find out how his perilous situation came to be.
>
> As a reward for the party's efforts, Amalthea promises to immortalize the characters in one of her tales and offers a choice of one of the following Items:
>
> - [[Unknown]]
> - [[Unknown]]
>
> Any character with a **Diplomacy (DC 13, Passive)** check is able to confirm the veracity of Amalthea's intentions.
>
> Amalthea is willing to share pertinent information and her perspective on a variety of subjects that she deems appropriate, including:
>
> - More about herself and Aster in general.
> - General details about Nain and its surrounding environs.
> - The precise location of each of the stories they must gather (indicated on the Arctus Plateau Region Map).
> - A brief history of her time as a professional storyteller, and her various accomplishments throughout the years.
> - Specific and general advice for the party in a mentorship capacity.
> - At least one story from her collection of legends and tall tales.
>
> Some specific dialogue options for Amalthea are presented below.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** More about yourself?
>
> **A:**
>
> > In my youth, I'd wander the Plateau gathering myths and fables from Casla-Brava to Caste, speaking to any outlander I could spend a free moment with. And if I couldn't journey far enough to find the adventure I was looking for, fate often brought it to my own doorstep in the form of a rare book or a far-off traveler. Everyone has a yarn to spin, my dears, and my life has been spent weaving a most colorful blanket.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Aster?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I see you've noticed my plumage. These violet and indigo feathers of mine are a resplendent gift from the Heart of Ember. As you can imagine, life as an Aster can bring with it many unexpected wonders and revelations; and I'd wager you or someone you know has been touched by the grace of Ember themselves.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why not gather the stories yourself?
>
> **A:**
>
> > As you can see, I have more years behind me than ahead. And these old bones aren't as spry as they once were. Fate simply whispered your names in my ear, and then you arrived. I'd be a fool to not seek your timely assistance and nurture the fabulist within each of you. After all, who will tell these tales when I'm gone?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Where should we begin?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Go where your hearts lead you. You're the storytellers now. Make them your own. But I can certainly set you on the path of whatever tale you fancy the most.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Tell us another tale?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Have you ever heard the story of the Wintermaned Huntsman and the Hearthfire Grove? Long ago, there lived a Wirrun Huntsman whose deft arrow could pierce the center of a copper piece from six-hundred feet. No quarry could outpace him, and no prey could escape.
> >
> > One day, this Wintermaned warrior spied the most intriguing game he'd ever seen on the hunt, an elegant four-horned stag with a brilliant coat of crimson fur. The Huntsman followed the stag for many a mile, until the beast retreated into a curious grove of burning trees. But rather than relent in the face of mortal peril, the Wintermaned Huntsman betrayed his own body to keep up the chase. They say he hunts that stag among smoke and cinders to this very day, immolated but alive, and that the grove can only be seen when desperation runs high on a moonlit night.
> >
> > It seems the price we pay to serve our passions can be rather steep.

> [!info] Social
> #### Accepting the Call to Action
>
> If the party agrees to take up Amalthea's task, she presents them with a pouch containing a [[Pen]], 2 Bottles of [[Ink]], and a blank leather-bound tome full of empty pages: the [[Stonecraft Manuscript]].
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 15)** check is able to discern something vaguely magical about the manuscript, but the actual properties are abstruse and obscure.
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character suitably talented with **Talent: Enchanting Journeyman** or who inspects the manuscript using **Talent: Recognize Spellcraft** is able to detect the telltale signs of the **Talent: Rune: Control**{Control Rune}, and that the book has indeed been enchanted, but beyond that the manuscript's nature is inscrutable.

### Concluding the Event

Amalthea will entertain the party as long as the characters have questions to ask or time to listen to her tales, but she eventually presses a small amount of urgency upon the adventurers if they linger for too long.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> The party has four primary options from here, corresponding to each story proposed by Amalthea Stonecraft for her manuscript:
>
> - Travel north for a few miles along the eastern riverbank to encounter [[The Boy Who Collected Boats]].
> - Journey northeast to [[Steed's Point]] for [[The Rickety Man]].
> - Go south to find [[Vortest Tower]] and [[The Signborn's Secret]].
> - Head southwest to locate [[Arcturel]] and [[The Crystal Quarryman]].
>
> No matter what order the party pursues these stories in, the clue that leads to the climax featured in [[The Moon Child of Lake Jinro]] is presented after completion of the fourth story Event, via [[The Manuscript Awakens]].
