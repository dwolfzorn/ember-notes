# Down by the River

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social and Exploration Event takes place at the unmarked location known as Calloway Cove, a few miles north of the Hargrove Farmstead. Here, the party must rescue young **Eb Hargrove** from the clutches of the kidnapper **Jodessa Cort**, who has lured Eb and a handful of other children into a life of servitude. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Discover the secluded riverside cave referred to in Eb's [[The Boy Who Collected Boats]], a remote cavern known as Calloway Cove.
> - Confront Jodessa Cort and loosen her malicious hold on the kidnapped [[Arcturian]] children of Calloway Cove.
> - Escort a rescued Eb Hargrove back to the Hargrove Farmstead and into the welcoming arms of his worried parents.
>
> This Event is depicted using the [[The Arctus Plateau]] Region Map.

### The Riverside Cave

On the heels of the clues mentioned in Eb's secret Parchment Boats, the party can locate the source of these curious letters: a kidnapper who lairs in a cave north of Nain. Here, at the so-called Calloway Cove, the characters can unravel the plot of the kidnapper Jodessa Cort and convince her to release the runaway children.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The closer you get to the dark hollow you noticed in the eastern rock face, the surer you become that it is indeed the mouth of some small cave. Vines and ivy are draped across this shaded opening, which is only a stone's throw away from the water's edge.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Outside the Cave
>
> This 15-foot-wide cave mouth is indeed the threshold of the lair of Jodessa Cort, the kidnapper who sent the parchment boats downstream for little Eb Hargrove (and others) to find. An inspection of the area can confirm the party's suspicions. For more information about Jodessa, see "Confronting Jodessa Cort" below.
>
> Any character with a successful **Awareness (DC 12, Passive)** check will notice that the concealment provided by the ivy and vines draped across the cave mouth has been enhanced with a blanket of man-made camouflage netting.
>
> - **Knowledge: Tracking**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Plants**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character can detect the faint sound of animal noises or young voices echoing from within the cave, along with the slightest smell of a campfire or torch.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Wilderness (DC 14)** check is able to locate several distinct sets of humanoid footprints leading in and out of the cave to the waterfront. The size of these footprints are diminutive enough to be those of small-sized adult humanoids or younger medium-sized humanoids.
>
> - **Knowledge: Tracking**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character also notices that a few sets of these tracks appear to come and go from the cave mouth with regularity, and can be dated as recently as yesterday or as far back as a couple of weeks ago.
>
> The 15-foot-wide cave entrance is dark, and narrows into a 10-foot-wide natural passage that winds roughly 200 feet down into the hillside before opening up into a larger aqueous cavern — the so-called Calloway Cove.

Once the characters journey into the cave, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A narrow passage behind the unsuspecting cave mouth leads further into the earth, and winds its way steeply downward towards the faint sound of muffled chatter and crackling firelight. Eventually, you draw nearer to some large underground cavern, where you spy the distinct decoration of a camp or makeshift hideaway. A half-dozen bedrolls are circled around a central fire pit, occupied by several adolescent children — their faces dirty and full of hardship. These kids appear to be sorting through a variety of stolen goods as they inspect a purloined stack of purses, satchels, and rucksacks.
>
> A thirty-something Arcturian woman stands above these youths, gesticulating in protest and admonishing the children with a stern reprimand. Clad in a tight gray jerkin and a collection of colorful scarves, sashes, and bands, this overseer bears a distinctly roguish affectation. Her words are as harsh and biting as the rocky stalactites that decorate this musty cavern.
>
> > I told you to stick together, didn't I? I told you to avoid strangers, didn't I? Then why have you been talking to strangers, my loves!? Do you seek to destroy the sanctity of this place we call home, where your hateful parents can never hurt you? The Cove is our sanctuary. You are safe here. You are wanted here. The outside world doesn't want you or love you! You know this! Now, who really loves you?
>
> There is a brief moment of uncomfortable silence before a few children mutter in reluctant response:
>
> > Jodessa loves us.
>
> The woman grows angry, and replies with a singsong threat.
>
> > I caaan't hearrr youuu! … **Who loves you**!?
>
> The children reply, adamant with faux enthusiasm.
>
> > Jodessa loves us!
>
> Satisfied, the woman responds with an air of dismissal.
>
> > That's right, my loves. Jodessa is the only one who truly cares about you …

The party has arrived just in time to hear Jodessa Cort address 4 [[Arcturian]] and young Eb Hargrove within their secret riverside lair.

**Jodessa Cort (Neutral Evil, Arcturian Human, she/her)**

Jodessa is a cruel but charismatic leader of Calloway Cove's youthful gang of misfits and runaways. A would-be footsoldier of the [[Otherhood of Fortune]], Jodessa is both athletic and streetwise — but likely outmatched by the party and their resources. Treat Jodessa as an [[Arcturian Sea Captain]].

**Eb Hargrove (Chaotic Good, Arcturian Ashka-Human, he/him)**

Normally, this eight-year-old dreamer is full of whimsy and wanderlust, but the trials and tribulations of latter-day life in the [[Golden Flats]] have already begun to harden his young heart. Green-scaled and silver-eyed, Eb's [[Ashka]] features are prominent, and he's known for liking the color red.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Inside the Cave
>
> Characters who take a moment to survey the area will notice the following:
>
> - The woman is armed with a rapier, which she keeps a ready palm on as she paces about the fireside, as if to subtly threaten the onlookers.
> - 5 adolescent Arcturians are gathered here in fealty to this woman, and look upon her with a measure of fear and misplaced respect in their eyes. Characters who previously succeeded on the Wisdom (Survival) check made to notice the footprints outside of the cave mouth can correlate them to the small size of these children's feet.
>
> Any character with a successful **Awareness (DC 13, Passive)** check notices one particular child among the adolescents: an Ashka-featured boy with light green scales, silver eyes, and a red tunic. Naturally, this is Eb Hargrove.
>
> #### Closing the Distance
>
> Any character who makes a successful**Stealth (DC 14)** check is able to avoid detection while they venture closer towards Jodessa's camp. If half or more of the party members succeed on this check, the group's overall attempt to sneak up on Jodessa's camp is successful.
>
> If they manage to catch the kidnapper by surprise, the party will gain an upper hand for their negotiations (see "Confronting Jodessa Cort" below).

If the party succeeds on their attempt to surprise Jodessa, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You stealthily make your way closer and closer to the makeshift camp at the bottom of the cave. By the time the adolescent thieves turn their eyes to greet you, it's too late for the woman to notice you for herself. This roguish Arcturian gang leader spins around with a look of utter surprise on her panicked face.

If the party fails their attempt to surprise Jodessa, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The woman pauses for a moment and raises her right hand. With a snap of her fingers, this roguish Arcturian gang leader sends the children scurrying for weapons and cover in various nooks and crannies around the camp. She raises her voice in mockery before turning slowly to greet you.
>
> > Well, well, well. It looks like we have company, my loves! These awful people mean to hurt you or take you away.
>
> A snide grin crosses the woman's face as she draws a wicked rapier from its sheathe and addresses you directly.
>
> > You wouldn't want to hurt the children, would you?

> [!info] Social
> #### Confronting Jodessa Cort
>
> Jodessa has been grooming the cove's coterie of adolescent Arcturian thieves, and the parchment boats are simply one of her odious recruitment methods.
>
> If the party wishes to save Eb Hargrove and the other children from Jodessa's devious influence, they must succeed on a Skill Challenge that includes success on their choice 3 or more of the 6 checks listed below.
>
> - **Athletics (DC 15)** The character is able to effectively flex their physical superiority over Jodessa, and she begins to question the prospects of her victory.
> - **Deception (DC 14)** In one way or another, the character is able to convince Jodessa that harboring the adolescents will result in her demise.
> - **Intimidation (DC 14)** The character is able to bully or dishearten Jodessa enough for her to question the likelihood of her survival.
> - **Medicine (DC 13)** The character is able to identify a small outbreak of a common caravan sickness known as the Red Cough among two of the Adolescent Arcturians, which adds an element of anxiety to Jodessa's temperament.
> - **Diplomacy (DC 14)** In one way or another, the character is able to convince Jodessa that giving up this kidnapping scheme is more profitable than maintaining it.
> - **Stealth (DC 15)** The character is able to reposition themself to a more tactically advantageous spot in the cave, which undermines Jodessa's standard security methods.
>
> When making the above checks:
>
> - **Knowledge: Crime** : The character gains **+2 Boons** when making a Deception, Persuasion, or Sleight of Hand check.
> - **Knowledge: Warfare**: The character gains **+2 Boons** when making an Athletics, Intimidation, or Medicine check.
> - If the party managed to avoid detection and surprise Jodessa (see "Inside the Cave" above): the character gains **+2 Boons** when making an Athletics, Deception, Intimidation, or Sleight of Hand check.
>
> Once the characters have succeeded on 3 of these skill checks, Jodessa will drop to her knees and lay down her arms as she surrenders to the party. A mere thug in service to the crime syndicates of Nain and [[Seawall]], Jodessa is willing to offer a small amount of information in exchange for her prompt freedom.
>
> If the characters fail, Eb Hargrove himself will join the attempt to confront Jodessa — adding 1 or more successes as needed by the Gamemaster.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Avoiding Combat
>
> Naturally, some characters will be eager to dispense vigilante justice during the encounter with Jodessa Cort. However, this encounter is designed with a lack of combat in mind, and the party should be encouraged to achieve their goals via the Skill Challenge presented above.
>
> Reasons the characters might/should want to avoid combat include (but are not limited to) the following:
>
> - A desire to shield the adolescent victims from witnessing the violent act.
> - A concern that Eb Hargrove might get caught in the crossfire.
> - Fear of repercussion from Jodessa's allies in the Otherhood of Fortune.
>
> If the party is determined to fight Jodessa Cort in mortal combat, she either submits to their mercies or is quickly slain in a swift victory — no combat will actually take place during the encounter. Nevertheless, this display of savagery will not go unnoticed: each party member is subject to [[The Abyss]] Attunement if they take this unsavory route to resolution.

#### Ragen Attunement: Jodessa Bullied

Each character who uses their skills of Athletics or Intimidation to force Jodessa Cort's surrender advances their **Attunement: Ragen (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Primordis Attunement: Jodessa Deceived

Each character who uses their skills of Deception or Sleight of Hand to force Jodessa Cort's surrender advances their **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Cora Attunement: Jodessa Convinced

Each character who uses their skills of Persuasion or Medicine to force Jodessa Cort's surrender advances their **Attunement: Cora (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Abyss Attunement: Jodessa Slain

If the party chooses to defeat Jodessa Cort via instantaneously fatal combat, each character advances their **Attunement: The Abyss (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### The Kidnapper Surrenders

When Jodessa Cort finally submits to the party's confrontation, she drops to her knees and offers a confession, along with a limited amount of information about her criminal operation here in Calloway Cove.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Jodessa's confession:
>
> **A:**
>
> > Wait, wait! I surrender! Please … Whatever you think of me, it took a lot of hard times in low places to wind up where I am today. I'm not proud of who I am sometimes, but I manage to survive. And I gave these kids some leadership when they needed it most. So please. Let me live another day.
> >
> > You can take the kids, send 'em home, whatever; I'll just crawl back to the rock I was hiding under and we can forget this ever happened. You obviously have everything under control.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About your operation?
>
> **A:**
>
> > If I tell you anything about who I usually work with, they'll kill us both. But I can tell you're not going to take "no" for an answer, so I'll just let you know that Nain and Seawall have plenty of fences for people with hot merchandise and scarcely any scruples.
> >
> > Working out of this cave seemed like a great way to avoid the hustle and bustle while still making ends meet. Once or twice a week, we'd head down to Nain and liberate a few purses from their owners. It worked so far; but I'm done.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Regarding the adolescent Arcturians?
>
> **A:**
>
> > These dumb runts were just looking for a place to belong. I can relate. It's a cruel world for all of us born between a rock and a hard place, you know? They didn't know any better. But maybe they learned a thing or two about living while they were here.
> >
> > If I had someone like me when I was their age, somebody to look up to and learn from, I could have been somebody special. I was good to these kids. Mark my words.

> [!info] Social
> #### Rescuing Eb and the Others
>
> Once the party has successfully subdued Jodessa via the Skill Challenge in "Confronting Jodessa Cort" above, they can liberate the gang of 4 Adolescent Arcturians from their misguided servitude.
>
> Furthermore, the party can influence the gang's decision regarding their next steps: the Adolescent Arcturians can either stay here in Calloway Cove, or they can disperse and return to their various settlements or homesteads of origin.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Deception (DC 13)** check or a **Diplomacy (DC 13)** check can sway the gang's collective decision one way or the other.
>
> Eb Hargrove joins the party no matter what, and is eager to return to the Hargrove Farmstead and into the waiting arms of his parents. The characters must escort him safely home in order to transcribe his tale for the Stonecraft Manuscript.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Looting the Hideout
>
> If the characters decide to loot the hideout after defeating Jodessa, they can search tent that served as the gang leader's private quarters. A footlocker inside contains  **25**, a bottle of [[Seawall Brandy]], and a random [[Arcturian Trinkets]].
>
> Additionally, any character who decides to search the stolen goods the Adolescent Arcturians were sorting when the party arrived can roll `[[/roll 1d2+1]]` times on the [[Corpse Loot]] table and `[[/roll 1d2]]` times on the [[Arcturian Trinkets]] table to determine what they find, along with  **1 2** in a soft leather pouch.

#### Orbis Attunement: Runaways United

If the party manages to convince the runaways to stay united, each character advances their **Attunement: Orbis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Akon Attunement: Runaways Disbanded

If the party manages to convince the runaways to disband, each character advances their **Attunement: Akon (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### Eb Hargrove's Tale

After the characters safely escort Eb Hargrove home, he’s able to relate the full tale of his recruitment by Jodessa Cort and a first-person account of his liberation at the hands of the party. As Eb relates his story, one of the characters must transcribe the tale in the Stonecraft Manuscript (if not recall it from memory later).

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Before you even reach the Hargrove Farmstead's sprawling front yard, you notice Gabi and Brandt Hargrove rushing out the front door to greet you. Eb breaks into a run and closes the distance, before Gabi reaches down and grabs the boy in a longing embrace, tears of joy streaming down her rosy cheeks.
>
> As you join the family, young Eb is already spinning a yarn for his folks about what really happened out there in Calloway Cove …
>
> > I'm sorry for leaving without saying goodbye, mama. Really, I am. I didn't mean to make you cry. I just had to find out if the story on the boats was true. This girl named Jo said there was a city of kids who needed friends like me, and promised us that we'd live forever, but it was all a bunch of lies. She was just a nasty liar. And she made us lie for her, and steal for her, and do all sorts of nasty things. Am I a bad person, mama?
>
> The Hargrove boy bursts into tears of guilt as his mother comforts him.
>
> > No, baby. You're not a bad person. You simply got taken advantage of by someone who knew better. But you promise me right now Ebdan Hargrove. Promise me you'll never leave my side again without first saying goodbye.
>
> Eb looks up with determination.
>
> > I promise, mama. Dada. I won't let you down. But I have to tell you about the other kids I met! I don't think they were bad people either.
>
> The boy continues his tale as his family looks on with newfound contentment and renewed appreciation for the simple life.

`[[/outcome collected]]`

### Concluding the Event

Once Eb Hargrove's tale has been written into the Stonecraft Manuscript, the characters can hear the soft music of Amalthea's driftwood wind chimes stirring on the breeze.
