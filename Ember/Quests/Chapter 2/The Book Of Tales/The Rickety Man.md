# The Rickety Man

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration and Combat Event takes the party to the abandoned village of [[Steed's Point]], where they can solicit [[Rattletrap, the Rickety Man]] for his personal story to add to the [[Stonecraft Manuscript]]. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Be tasked with a special errand from the absent-minded Rattletrap after prompting him with a special question from [[Amalthea Stonecraft]].
> - Gain Rattletrap's favor by fetching an out-of-reach heirloom from the abandoned ruins of Steed's Point.
> - Survive combat with a variety of enemies, including [[Gore Bird Ravage]], [[Jurtak Warrior]], [[Jurtak Hunter]], and a [[Jurtak Geomancer]].
> - Learn about Rattletrap's true origins and the source of his curious sentience.
>
> #### Area Walkthrough
>
> This Event begins with the party in the [[Steed's Point Fields]] section of the [[Steed's Point]] Area Map. Rattletrap paces these fields methodically, weeding and performing basic maintenance in a diligent, if seemingly futile, effort to keep the fields productive. Much of this event is handled through exploration of Steed's Point, and the Steed's Point Fields entry in particular contains additional social interaction and an expanded Q&A for Rattletrap.

### Rattletrap's Mission

The party should encounter Rattletrap in the [[Steed's Point Fields]], where they'll be able to ask him the question mentioned by Amalthea Stonecraft: "how he came to be." This will lead to the following social interaction:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You notice a shift in the Rickety Man's body language as you finish your question. The construct grows silent for a brief moment before turning to you with an arresting sense of curiosity. Despite his static countenance, Rattletrap's newfound enthusiasm is unmistakable.
>
> > What's this? You want to know how Rattletrap came to be? Then you must learn about the greatest smithy the Plateau has ever seen…
>
> The Rickety Man appears lost in thought for a second, then addresses you once more.
>
> > But sometimes, this dusty ol' brain of mine has trouble rememberin'. Maybe if you fetch me something peculiar that might help me recall the way things were… a special keepsake. If only I could remember where it was…

> [!info] Social
> #### Rattletrap's Quest
>
> The party will need to help [[Rattletrap, the Rickety Man]] recall the basic elements of this "keepsake" he mentioned, including its current location: [[Steed's Smithy]] in the northwest corner of town.
>
> After his initial remarks, Rattletrap can be steered towards the following topics of conversation:
>
> - Exactly who the statement "the greatest smithy the Plateau has ever seen" refers to.
> - Precisely what this "special keepsake" mentioned could be.
> - The hazards of Steed's Point and the invasive species that have brought it to ruin.
>
> After issuing the call to find the keepsake he can't quite remember — a magical tool known as the [[Shardsteel Hammer]] — Rattletrap will ultimately advise the party to steer clear of the cruel Jurtak who prowl the overgrown streets of Steed's Point, testifying to the efficacy of their deadly poison.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 13)** check is convinced of the veracity regarding Rattletrap's account of Steed's Point and its ongoing threats.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 16)** check is familiar with the basic qualities of [[Jurtak]], including their extensive use of poison, their subterranean origins, and the savagery of their hunting tactics.
>
> - **Knowledge: Abyssals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Subterranea**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 12)** check is able to readily confirm the fact that Rattletrap is no ordinary Woven Construct, as reflected by the expert quality of the craftsmanship and/or sapient wit on display.
>
> - **Culture: Arcturian** : The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Crafts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Souls**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Rattletrap can also point the characters towards the grassland artifice of [[Moriah Foxhaven]] — an agrimage that roams the plains west of the River Elenain. She is rumored to have developed a potent ward against the Jurtak poison, and may be able to provide the party with material aid in the form of her so-called "Grassland Talisman."
>
> Additionally, any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 15)** check is well-versed in the history of Steed's Point and its inhabitants. They know about the founder, Bertron Steed, and his accolades as a talented smithy. They're also well-aware of the scourge of Gore Birds and Jurtak that were part of the village's downfall.
>
> - **Culture: Arcturian**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the smithy?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Yours truly was created by the great Bertron Steed, the intrepid founder of this village and the most talented smithy to ever swing a hammer. Steed can work wood and steel in perfect tandem, you see. A craftsman like no other, full of grit and ambition. The kind of fella you want to be around, to learn from, and to impress. A leader, with a good heart. I often wonder where the old man is now … it's been a terribly long time. Terribly long, indeed.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the keepsake?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Now, that's a good question. My memory ain't quite what it used to be, you see … I suppose it has something to do with Bertron? I suppose it's special enough to recognize when you see it.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why not fetch the keepsake yourself?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I learned long ago to keep to my field. The Jurtak won't bother messing with me out here in the field, but they'll tear these old wooden limbs up faster than a summer wildfire if I roam too far into their territory. You all look to be made of sterner stuff than ol' Rattletrap, and you came this far for my story, so I reckon' you'll do the job better than anyone else.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Jurtak?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Those scaly, six-eyed monsters are good at eatin' meat and causin' a fuss, and not much else. I'm lucky I don't have to worry about the poison they seem to pour on everything, but you might not be so fortunate, my friends. You may want to consider arming yourselves against the venom with some kind of protection. Have you heard of a Grassland Talisman?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Grassland Talisman?
>
> **A:**
>
> > There's an agrimage who roams the Golden Flats west of here, a beast rider who travels with a group of like-minded drifters. Her name is Moriah Foxhaven, and they say she can forge a charm that protects its wearer from the Jurtak's poison. Seek her out before you dive headlong in to the bramble and get yourself roasted on a Jurtak spit.
> >
> > Find Moriah, and tell her Rattletrap sent you. That might be your best bet for surviving a trip to Steed's Smithy. I'd hate to lose more friends; it's awful lonely here sometimes.

Once the characters have gathered enough information, they must make their way east and then north through Steed's Point to reach their destination: [[Steed's Smithy]].

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Exploring Steed's Smithy
>
> Characters familiar with Steed's Point will be well aware of its dangerous denizens, including the [[Gore Bird Ravage]] and [[Jurtak]] hunting party that prowls the ruined village's overgrown grounds. The presence of these predatory species makes it difficult for live humanoids to traverse the village without the furtive cover of stealth or magic.
>
> The party may or may not choose to leave Steed's Point in search of the [[Grassland Talisman]] from Moriah Foxhaven, which will introduce them to the [[Grassland Artifice]] Event. Whether or not they make this side trek, the characters must explore the grounds about Steed's Smithy and locate Rattletrap's special keepsake known as the [[Shardsteel Hammer]]. Originally owned by Bertron Steed himself, this hammer is the key to activating Rattletrap's reluctant memory — and the secret tale of his curious origin.

#### Mayis Attunement: The Grassland Talisman

If the party chooses to leave Steed's Point in order to retrieve a Grassland Talisman before they continue, each character advances their **Attunement: Mayis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Heart Attunement: Jurtak Eliminated

If the party manages to successfully rid Steed's Point of the invasive Jurtak, each character advances their **Attunement: Heart of Ember (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### Rattletrap's Tale

Once the characters return to the Steed's Point Fields with the Shardsteel Hammer in hand, they can offer it to Rattletrap to magically jog his memory and hear the secret tale of his creation. As the Rickety Man relates his story, one of the characters must transcribe the tale in the Stonecraft Manuscript (if not recall it from memory later).

Rattletrap reacts to seeing the hammer as soon as the party presents it:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > Well, isn't this a sight for sore eyes.
>
> Rattletrap's statuesque countenance appears fixated on the hammer as you produce it for him. He reaches forward to grab it, and once the keepsake is in his wooden hands, you see it glow with a scintillating arcane brilliance that lights up the construct's face.
>
> > This, my friends, is the Shardsteel Hammer, an invaluable tool used by the late Bertron Steed.
>
> The Rickety Man looks off to an overgrown patch of dirt in the northwest corner of the field, a tract you've previously overlooked until now. There, a human skeleton barely peeks out from beneath the tall grass, and a realization comes to you that Bertron Steed met his untimely end right here.
>
> > I can tell you my story, now that I remember it. You know me as Rattletrap, but I was once a man. And Bertron Steed was my name.
> >
> > When I first settled this place, I found something in the bedrock below the land my smithy still stands on — a crystal shard of impossible brilliance, a magical treasure I kept hidden away, unable to decipher its secrets.
> >
> > This construct body you see before you now was my dearest companion; a tireless assistant I forged to help me work the shop. I named him Rattletrap, and our accomplishments were many. That is, until the gore birds and the jurtak came.
> >
> > Making our final stand here in the field against a band of jurtak hunters, I held that crystal shard aloft in the hopes that its magic would somehow turn the tide. And in the aftermath of a great arcane blast, I discovered that my soul — the soul of Bertron Steed — had been somehow grafted to the fabricated body of my dear friend Rattletrap.
> >
> > I believe the sheer impossibility of that moment is what led me to forget who I really was. Who I really am.
>
> Bertron looks over to his skeletal remains with a distinct air of remorse.
>
> > I suppose it isn't easy to admit our own mortality, even if we manage to escape it.
>
> He looks to you once more.
>
> > So, that's the story of The Rickety Man of Steed's Point. And I must admit, I'm glad to have heard it myself.

`[[/outcome collected]]`

#### Aura Attunement: Rattletrap's Tale

If the party manages to successfully record Rattletrap's Tale in Amalthea's Manuscript, each character advances their **Attunement: Aura (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### Concluding the Event

Once Rattletrap's tale has been written into the Stonecraft Manuscript, the characters can hear the soft music of Amalthea's driftwood wind chimes stirring on the breeze.
