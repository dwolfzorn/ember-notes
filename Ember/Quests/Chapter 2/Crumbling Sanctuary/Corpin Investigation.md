# Corpin Investigation

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event challenges the party with an investigation at [[Corpin Sanctuary]], where they'll search for evidence regarding the accusations found in the mysterious note left for [[Sin Marmot]] the night before. In this event, the characters can:
>
> - Attempt to intuit who authored the [[Note to Sin]].
>   - Search the sanctuary for evidence of suspicious activity or wrongdoing.
>   - Find evidence that [[Mira Wavehorn]] has been turning away refugees.
>   - Find evidence that [[Avwynn Taol]] is not a simple visitor, but is actually a [[Sanguinaries]].
>   - Find evidence that [[Evesso]] is involved in eldritch rituals utilizing forbidden arcana.
> - Witness the enclave's revelation that the central dais of the [[Sanctorum]] is, in fact, an ancient elevator that descends into the [[Pathways]] below.
>
> #### Area Walkthrough
>
> This event takes place on the [[Corpin Sanctuary]] Scene. Unlike the previous event, the characters now have the freedom to explore the area on their own. Consult the [[Corpin Sanctuary]] Area Walkthrough for full details about the areas they can explore and the evidence they can find.

### Planning an Approach

Before commencing the investigation, the party must confer with Sin and plan the next course of action. This could happen anywhere, but is most likely to occur in one of the [[Empty Dormitory]].

> [!info] Social
> #### Sin's Suspicions
>
> Sin will admit to a lack of experience in clandestine investigations such as this, and volunteers to follow the party's lead. However, Sin is rather concerned that this investigation could damage their prospects with the Cindaric Sages — a group Sin is still very intent on joining.
>
> The characters must align with Sin on a plan. To do so, they must succeed on one of the following skill checks to assuage Sin's trepidations and concerns for the Cindaric order:
>
> - **Arcana (DC 12)** Sin's emergent understanding of necromancy is guided by the character's own acumen regarding the magical arts. Characters with **Knowledge: Undeath** have advantage on this check.
> - **Deception (DC 12)** The character uses a "little white lie" or some other method to trick Sin into believing that the necromancer poses no threat.
> - **Diplomacy (DC 12)** The character senses the earnestness of Sin's concern, and is able to offer the proper words of encouragement. Sin's resolve is bolstered by the character's persuasive call-to-action.
> - **Society (DC 12)**Sin is adequately convinced of her personal duty to take up the task, lest the Cindarics fall into ruin. Characters with **Knowledge: Souls** have advantage on this check.

Once Sin has been duly motivated, the party members can start their investigations in earnest (in any order they please). Sin will accompany the first character to succeed on their skill check unless otherwise instructed or compelled.

### Investigating Mira

If the party has any suspicions about [[Mira Wavehorn]], they may investigate her activities. The crucial clues for this investigation are found in [[Mira's Office]] and [[Mira's Chamber]].

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Mira's Mandate
>
> Once they identify the location of Mira's office and private quarters, they can search each location for clues about the Cindaric leader's current activities.
>
> - [[Mira's Office]] contains a [[Letter from Sigil]], which makes it look as if Mira is independently turning away refugee aid.
> - [[Mira's Chamber]] contains a [[Letter from Lilla Arien]], which clarifies that Mira is reluctantly following instruction provided to her by a superior in Ordain.

> [!info] Social
> #### Interrogating Mira
>
> If the characters wish to question Mira more directly, they can find her in the Refectory during the morning, in the Sanctorum during midday, in her Office during the afternoon, in the Entry Courtyard during the early evening, and in her Chamber during the late evening.
>
> As the designated leader of Corpin Sanctuary, Mira will only entertain polite questioning that probes into Cindaric matters up to a certain point. She remains protective of the Sanctuary and its residents, as well as the Cindaric order itself. Mira can, however, elucidate the following:
>
> - Her history as the leader of Corpin Sanctuary for the past decade, and a scant few details about her personal life.
> - How Corpin Sanctuary's mission to harbor refugees of the Arctus Plateau has been compromised by unstable politics and edicts from Ordain.
> - The basic details Avwynn's visitation to the Sanctuary as a scholar of note.
> - Evesso's appointment to the Sanctuary nearly six months ago by the Holy Speaker himself.
>
> Additionally, any character that casts [[Telecognition]] is able to read Mira's thoughts.
>
> - Mira's surface thoughts include her duties for the day, which include sending a letter to the Holy Speaker.
> - Mira's emotional state is tinged with well-measured anxiety, and the plight of Arcturian refugees looms large in her mind.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Event Outcomes
>
> If the party discovers that Mira has been following instruction to deter refugees from remaining at Corpin Sanctuary, mark the **Evidence on Mira** outcome as completed.

### Investigating Avwynn

If the party has any suspicions about [[Avwynn Taol]], they may investigate her activities. Investigating Avwynn requires the party to shadow her movements from an unobserved position, taking note of what she does during the day.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Shadowing Avwynn
>
> After early breakfast in the [[Refectory]] and morning tea in the [[Entry Courtyard]], Avwynn will return to [[Avwynn's Chamber]] to retrieve a satchel full of writing supplies and [[Avwynn's Journal]].
>
> Avwynn subsequently carries her journal to the [[Meditation Pavilion]], where she spends time discretely transcribing her thoughts. After an hour, she hides the journal in her room before joining other sages in the [[Sanctorum]] for a sacred reading.
>
> #### Reading Avwynn's Journal
>
> Once they know what to look for, the party can break into [[Avwynn's Chamber]] and read the contents of [[Avwynn's Journal]]. Any character who spends at least 5 minutes studying this journal is able to learn about its contents — which reveal Avwynn's identity as a [[Sanguinaries]], posing as a scholar of renown to investigate the Sanctuary's practices firsthand.
>
> Avwynn's Chamber also contains a [[Black Aspen Wand]], which savvy characters may recognize as an arcane focus often used by Sanguinaries.

> [!info] Social
> #### Interrogating Avwynn
>
> If the characters wish to question Avwynn more directly, they can find her in the Refectory during the morning, in the Entry Courtyard during midday, in the Meditation Pavilion during the afternoon, in the Refectory during the early evening, and in her Chamber during the late evening.
>
> As an outsider with suspicions of her own, Avwynn is fairly nonplussed by the party's investigation as long as they remain cordial and/or complimentary to her ego and her cause. She does, however, keep her identity as the leader of the Sanguinaries a close-guarded secret. Avwynn can elucidate the following:
>
> - The basic details of her visitation to the Sanctuary as a scholar of note.
> - Her history as an affluent Maziran scholar and her extensive travels throughout the continent of Aterica.
> - A casual mistrust of the pomp and circumstance affecting the Cindaric Temple in Ordain and a condemnation of the bureaucracy that restricts them.
>
> Additionally, characters that use [[Telecognition]] to penetrate Avwynn's mind may learn the following:
>
> - Avwynn's surface thoughts include apprehension about Evesso's pyrefly ritual, which she was able to observe from a clandestine spot near the Sanctorum Entrance.
> - Avwynn's emotional state is a mix of curiosity and contempt, and the questionable agenda of the Cindaric order (as a whole) looms large in her mind.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Event Outcomes
>
> If the party discovers that Avwynn is a Sanguinary, rather than the traveling scholar that she claims to be, mark the **Evidence on Avwynn** outcome as completed.

`[[/outcome evidenceAvwynn]]`

### Investigating Evesso

If the party has suspicions about [[Evesso]], they can investigate the sage's activities. The crucial clues to Evesso's true malignant nature are found in the [[Library]], in [[Evesso's Chamber]], and in the hidden [[Profane Altar]].

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Evesso's Darkness
>
> To fuel their speculation regarding Evesso's dark nature, the party can first discover the [[Letter of Introduction]]: a poignant document in [[Mira's Office]] that describes how Evesso came to join the ranks of the Corpin Sanctuary sages under unusual circumstances.
>
> If the characters don't come to the notion on their own, Sin suggests they search Evesso's Chamber for evidence that points towards necromantic activity (or other suspicious behavior).
>
> #### Signs of Necromancy
>
> Evesso's Chamber seems extremely normal at first glance. However, it contains two important clues that point towards Evesso's true intentions:
>
> - [[Evesso's Note]], scrawled with an arcane sigil and a mysterious sequence of numbers.
> - A secret door to the [[Profane Altar]], which can only be opened if the characters unravel the mystery of Evesso's Note.
>
> To unravel the mystery of Evesso's Note, the characters can do the following:
>
> 1. Confirm the arcane sigil on the note is the same as the arcane sigil on the secret door.
> 2. Investigate the [[Library]] for [[Nineteen Nights in Haxim]], a book alluded to via the first number written in Evesso's Note.
> 3. After finding the book, consult the page, line, and word that respectively correspond to the next three numbers mentioned in Evesso's Note. The 5th word on the 16th line of the 53rd page — "malediction" — is the password to the secret door in Evesso's Chamber.
> 4. Speak the password at the secret door in Evesso's Chamber to open it and reveal the Profane Altar.
>
> The Profane Altar unambiguously identifies Evesso as the Necromancer, as a thorough examination will confirm. And the presence of other suspicious accoutrements (including the secret door) only further supports whatever argument the party is prepared to make.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Destroying the Profane Altar
>
> The [[Profane Altar]] can be destroyed or consecrated via one of the methods indicated in the area walkthrough.
>
> If the characters manage to destroy the Profane Altar before their conflict with Evesso in the [[Sanctuary of Death]] event, the necromancer loses his [[Profane Blessing]] feature.

> [!info] Social
> #### Interrogating Evesso
>
> If the characters wish to question Evesso more directly, they can find him in the Refectory during the morning, in the Library during midday, in the Entry Courtyard during the afternoon, in the Sanctorum during the early evening, and in his Chamber during the late evening.
>
> Evesso is, in fact, the villain the characters seek, and will attempt to maintain his deception at all costs. The information he offers is elaborate, truthful, and full of misdirection. Evesso will elucidate the following:
>
> - His history as an esteemed ritualist and a student of arcana, which has been a great boon to the Cindarics since his induction many years ago.
> - How Corpin Sanctuary's mission to harbor refugees of the Arctus Plateau has been compromised by unstable politics and edicts from Ordain.
> - His appointment to the remote Sanctuary nearly six months ago by the Holy Speaker himself.
>
> Additionally, characters that use magic to penetrate Avwynn's mind may learn the following:
>
> - Evesso's surface thoughts include the Sanctorum, and the architecture of Corpin Sanctuary.
> - Evesso's emotional state is calmness colored with apprehension, and a heightened suspicion about the party's motives looms large in his mind.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Event Outcomes
>
> If the party discovers that Evesso has been practicing forbidden necromantic rites using the Profane Altar, mark the **Evidence on Evesso** outcome as completed.
>
> Additionally, if the party chooses to destroy the Profane Altar, mark the **Profane Altar Destroyed** outcome as completed.

`[[/outcome effigyDestroyed]]`

#### Heart Attunement: Profane Altar Destroyed

Any character who participates in the destruction or consecration of the Profane Altar advances their **Attunement: Heart of Ember (+2)** at the conclusion of the event.

#### Abyss Attunement: Attuned to the Altar

Any character who attunes to and uses the Profane Altar advances their **Attunement: The Abyss (+1)** at the conclusion of the event. This advancement only occurs once.

#### Signara Attunement: Evesso's Truth

If the party manages to discover the truth of Evesso's identity as a necromancer, advance each character's **Attunement: Signara (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### The Sanctuary Elevator

Once the party has had ample time to pursue the leads they deem worthy for investigation, the narrative is driven forward by Evesso himself — who opens an ancient elevator to the Pathways beneath the [[Sanctorum]] floor. Created millennia ago during the foundation of Corpin Sanctuary, this clandestine elevator opens offscreen during a dramatic moment of *deus ex machina* guided by you, the Gamemaster.

When the time is right, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Just then, the entire Sanctuary begins to shudder as a low, stony rumble reverberates throughout each chamber and shakes the very mountainside. Dust and grit falls from the ceiling as this calamity persists for about ten seconds, after which time the whole place grows remarkably silent and still. Moments later, the panicked voices of Cindaric sages rise to greet the uncomfortable calm.

At Sin's insistence, the party rushes to the Sanctorum to investigate what happened. Here, they discover the gaping aperture of the elevator standing open wide as the platform descends to fathomless depths. [[Mira Wavehorn]] is also here, attempting to maintain order among the chaos. However, there is currently no sign of either [[Evesso]] or [[Avwynn Taol]] — who both seem to have disappeared.

> [!info] Social
> #### Mira's Confession
>
> Mira confesses that she knew of this elevator, but that it was a closely held secret for Cindaric leadership and that she thought no other sage at Corpin Sanctuary knew of its existence.
>
> - When pressed for more information, Mira engages a hidden control gem and causes the elevator to rise back to the surface, its platform empty.
> - Mira and Sin both agree that there is only one course of action: the party must descend the elevator to ascertain what exactly is below and what has happened.

### Concluding the Event

When the party is ready, the elevator to the Pathways — and a further investigation into Evesso's machinations — awaits.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Event Outcomes
>
> Once the Sanctuary Elevator has been opened and returned to the surface using Mira's control gem, mark the **Pathways Opened** outcome as complete, which will allow the next events in the quest to proceed.
>
> #### Next Steps
>
> After the Sanctuary Elevator is unlocked, this event can be marked as completed and the party can use the elevator to descend into the Pathways. Upon arriving in the Pathways, the subsequent event [[Shadowed Depths]] will begin.
>
> Alternately, if the party finds some other route down into the Pathways, they may advance indirectly to the [[A Sage Welcome]] event.
