# The Blockaded Bridge

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> In this Social Event, the party is halted upon arriving at the [[Ordani Skywalk]] by a squadron of [[Burnished Hand]] who rebuff their progress. By interacting with the blockade the characters can:
>
> - Learn why travel across the bridge has been recently restricted, keeping people from moving between [[Ordain Gazetteer]] and the rest of the [[Arctus Plateau Gazetteer]].
> - Encounter a company of [[Lumek]] warriors, captained by [[Luna Karrowrath]] who have been denied access to the city.

### Halted Passage

The main bridge leading into Ordain is restricted by order of the [[Ordinate]] in response to heightening unrest from bandit raids, mysterious creature attacks, and rumors of undead in the [[Wedgelands]]. The guards here are stopping travelers to question them and ensure that they pose no risk to the safety of Ordani citizens.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you size up the crowds, you identify three groups butting up against the blockade — a collection of refugees arguing with a bunch of Sages and Ordinate personnel, a checkpoint of Hallows and Veiled Chain inspectors holding up a train of merchants, and lastly two squads of soldiers locked in a heated argument as a few exasperated city officials watch things unfold.
>
> It looks like you won't be able to just walk into Ordain today.

The party will need to investigate these groups if they want to gather more information and get past the blockage.

### The Refugee Problem

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A Cindaric Sage speaks up toward a crowd of murmuring refugees, the lot of them growing quiet and attentive.
>
> > Good folk, hear me! We have done all we could today to admit your number. We have reached the limit of what the city can bear —
>
> The crowd's discontent immediately rises, the murmur returning, with a few frustrated shouts raising above the din. The Sage holds out their hands, attempting to placate them.
>
> > Please understand that we are working as hard as we can to admit everyone, and allow them to pass, but right now the city is focused on merchants and keeping the supply chains running. We beg you understand, and show us patience in these most trying times!
>
> The crowd's displeasure turns into open, angry disdain, but it never grows past yelling and complaints, as a handful of Trade House guards step forward to shield the Sage, and dissuade the refugees from getting too upset.
>
> Nearby you a short human man throws his hands up, and begins to complain, eventually turning to you.
>
> > Unbelievable! Least they could do is bring us some bedrolls, or food. Bad enough sitting here another day, but sleeping on stone too? Can you believe this?

> [!info] Social
> #### Fed Up Farmer
>
> If the party interacts with the fellow arguing with the ordinate, he'll introduce himself as Stohb Krunder (Neutral Good, Arcturian Human, he/him), a generally well meaning and hard working farmer whose family has fallen on hard times and now seeks refuge in Ordain.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who are you?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Oh, hey, where are my manners! I'm Stohb, Stohb Krunder. This is my wife Kiona, and these are my children Vinda and Jidds. Looks like we're all in the same boat. Sorry to say you're gonna be stuck waiting alongside us.

If the party lets Stohb keep chatting, or asks for his story, he'll happily share it.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Krunders?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Used to run Krunderfield Farms in the Golden Flats, at least until the damned raiders came through and burned it to the ground, twice! First time the got the and mill, second time they got the fields, which eventually got the house…
> >
> > Anyway! We're headed up to Redrak Fields to meet up with my brother Sterhb. He runs Krunderfield Ranch with the rest of the family there. I figure we can fold into his operations until we're back on our feet.

If the party asks about the backed up refugees and why everyone's stuck, Stohb can only offer his view of things.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the bridge blockade?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Can't rightly say. We've been here two days and they keep telling us that they'll let us in once things calm down, but there's nobody leaving the city, just coming in. Don't really know how anything's going to change. Frankly, if things don't get moving fast, I'm not sure I'll be able to afford to get us to Redrak.

If the party offers Stohb some money, or gets his family through the blockade, he thanks them profusely.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Offering Help.
>
> **A:**
>
> > You're too kind! I'm afraid I don't have any way to repay you, but know you've got my eternal gratitude! If you're ever out round Krunderfield Ranch past Redrak, stop by, I'll at least see that you get a nice meal.

> [!info] Social
> #### Cindaric Stoppage
>
> The Cindaric Sage addressing the crowd is Klobin Gates (Lawful Good, Ordani Human, he/him), a well meaning and well-spoken member of his order chosen to handle the crowds. He doesn't relish turning people away, but he has his orders, and earnestly believes they are for the greater good of Ordain.
>
> If the party speaks with him, they can glean some insights into the situation:

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the blockade.
>
> **A:**
>
> > Ah, most sorry, perhaps you missed the announcement: the way into the city is closed until tomorrow. However if you wait until the next dawn, we will be admitting more refugees in. We presently are prioritizing those with skills that can directly impact food production, magic healing, and the like. If you or anyone you know fits that measure, speak with the Trade House representative when they are here tomorrow. They arrive midday and leave be early afternoon.

> [!info] Social
> #### Considering the Situation
>
> Any character with **Culture: Arcturian** or **Culture: Ordani** knows that the city should be more than capable of handling the influx of people into the city, and this blockage is highly unusual.
>
> Meanwhile, characters with **Knowledge: Politics** or **Knowledge: Trade** know that while the Temple's resources are not infinite, it absolutely has the clout and resources to make sure all these people are fed and comfortable if it wants to.

If the party points out this discrepancy to Sage Klobin, he attempts to offer a deflection:

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Ordain has the capacity.
>
> **A:**
>
> > Not quite so any longer! Things have become difficult in Ordain. Much of the Golden Flats has begun flooding into the City, either passing through to Redrak, or cramming the city's inns and streets with refugees. Not only that, but with the falling production in the Flats, food is only coming in now by ship or from Redrak, and grain only grows so fast.
> >
> > So no, the city can't support all the refugees right now.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About limited access.
>
> **A:**
>
> > I know that it seems unnecessarily restrictive, but the Trade Houses are very busy, they can only spare so many people for so long. They are doing the best they can, I assure you.

> [!info] Social
> #### Crossing the Bridge
>
> If the party would like to convince the sage to let them cross the bridge, a successful **`[[/skill diplomacy 16]]`**check is necessary.
>
> - **Path: Cindaric Initiate**: A member of the Cindaric Sages automatically succeeds.
> - **Culture: Ordani**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
> - **Critical Success**: The Sage will agree to let the party through, and a small number of additional refugees, including Stohb and his family.

### The Ordinate Checkpoint

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Trade House guards form a checkpoint as Hallows inspectors move down lines of wagons, looking through goods and items. Alongside them, Veiled Chain investigators speak to merchants, and collect reports. The entire scene feels less chaotic than anywhere else along the bridge, but there is an air of agitation among the waiting caravans used to moving freely across the grand Skywalk.
>
> A Trade Guard walks down the rows of carts and people, calling out:
>
> > Clear the central lane! Clear the central lane! Travelers on the right, merchants on the left. Be prepared with manifests, Trading House badges, and other merchant identification! If you are are shipping food or perishables, speak up!
>
> She moves past you, barely sparing a glance. The line trundles along slowly, foot traffic and the odd mounted traveler moving past you on the right. Every now and then a caravan gets cleared to move forward, prompting carts to take the central space and head on down the bridge.

> [!info] Social
> #### Orderly Processes
>
> If the party chooses to wait in line and access the city via the traveler's entry line, it takes a while to be seen, but eventually they are met with a Veiled Chain investigator and watched by a Burnished Hand protector. The questions begin mundane, asking if anyone in the group is ill, injured, diseased, or similar.
>
> Eventually, the questions expand to ask if they have had any run-ins with bandits, noticed any identifying marks or symbols on them, and where they were encountered. This is further expanded to cover monsters, and then the undead.
>
> They are also asked to turn over any clearly corrupted, abyssal, or necromantic artifacts they have, as they are being confiscated by Ordinate law to help protect the people of the plateau.
>
> If the party wishes to lie their way though the checkpoint either to avoid giving accurate info, or to hide items that would be taken, the group must must succeed on a group **Deception (DC 14)** check to fool the investigator. On success the party is cleared through and given no further questions. Failure tips the investigator off that there's something amiss, and may result in their packs being searched.

However, if the party answers truthfully about their encounters, and hands over any items, their names will be told the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > Sounds as though you've had an eventful time of it. I'll be entering your names in a record for the Veiled Chain in case we need to follow up with more questions. You're not in any trouble, but we might need more details as we continue looking into things.

They'll then be allowed to cross the bridge and enter the city.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Smuggled Inside
>
> It is possible to hide in a caravan about to move through the blockade, which requires the party to make a group **Stealth (DC 14)**check. If at least half the party succeed, they are not discovered, and can ride the wagon into the city. Otherwise they are found, and kicked out.

### The Detained Lumek

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you approach the group of armored warriors their voices become clearer over the general din and chaos around the blockade. A burly Kivahr man throws his hands up in response to something a Lumek woman said just before you arrived.
>
> > You can't just go stomping across the plateau looking for the undead. You need to take your soldiers to the Ordinate, meet with the council, and work with everyone else. I don't know what part of that is unclear!
>
> The Lumek woman sneers, eyes flaring with fire.
>
> > Unacceptable! I can't believe the Sages, Ordinate or you Brass cowards can stand by and debate how and where and why we should be acting while the undead are growing in the region. I knew I shouldn't have come to this city.
>
> The Burnished Hand officer bristles.
>
> > First of all, Flameguard, we're the Burnished Hand, second of all we're not cowards, and finally —
>
> The pair devolve into arguing once more as a nearby Ordinate official tries to interject to no avail. Eventually the Burnished hand falls silent, crossing his burly arms over his chest, and the Lumek woman stomps back to her waiting soldiers while radiating frustration.
>
> It's only then that a nearby Ordinate official takes note of you.
>
> > Sorry, the Skywalk is presently restricting traffic through this area, if you're with the refugees, you want to find the Sages, and if you're a merchant or general traveler, the Ordinate have a checkpoint that will sort you out. This is a separate issue.

On the Ordain side of the bridge, the party encounters a company of Lumek discussing with a squad of Burnished Hand. The Lumek wish to venture out into the Wedgelands and confront the rumors of undead head-on. The Burnished Hand are insisting that they return to Cindarin temple and discuss their plans with the Speaker.

She carries herself with an uncharacteristic grace for someone so heavily armored. Even the immense mace strapped to her back doesn't seem to slow her down.

> [!info] Social
> #### Burnished Officer
>
> The Burnished Hand squad here is lead by Faro Jekkagg (Neutral, Ordani Kivahr, he/him) a stern and short-tempered officer with no love for wasting time or breath in debate. He has orders, and he intends to follow them unless countermanded by someone with sufficient authority.
>
> #### Hallows Official
>
> The Hallows Official trying to settle the situation here is Jeala Hailf (Lawful Good, Ordani Human, she/her) who is a low-level functionary and clerk within the city's governmental structure. She actually lacks any real authority in this situation, and is more of a witness than a problem solver.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Burnished Hand Officer: Reason for blockage?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Look, the help is welcome, but these Flameguard come in here, stomping around, causing more chaos, it doesn't improve things. I understand they have probably killed more undead than us, but this isn't their war, it's ours. They can work with us, or they can get lost.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Hallows Official: Reason for blockage?
>
> **A:**
>
> > This whole blockade is a massive joint venture to address everything going on in the region by first collecting information, and making sure that the city doesn't take on more refugees than it can handle. It's heartbreaking to hold up people on the skywalk, but the spike in people coming over the bridge lately due to monster and bandit attacks has begun to strain the city.

> [!info] Social
> #### The Flameguard Company
>
> **Path: Flameguard Militia** characters suspect that this may be Luna Karrowrath, the champion of Lumé, a legendary warrior that was responsible for slaying a powerful necromancer who laid siege to her home city. The fact she's here could be a very bad sign, but there's probably nobody better qualified for the task of hunting undead.
>
> #### Reading Luna
>
> Luna is not especially difficult to read, as her zealous nature and drive is always on display. A successful **Deception (DC 12)** check leads you to believe she is not only being forthright and honest, but strongly believes everything she says.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Undead in the region?
>
> **A:**
>
> > You mark my words: there is something out there in the plateau that is causing this, and if it's not found and stopped immediately, this place could start looking like my homeland real fast, and I don't have much faith in the Ordinate's ability to defend itself.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Doubting the Ordinate?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The locals can't handle some bandits and monsters, what hope do they have against the undead?
> >
> > Risen dead are relentless, and their masters know that time is always on their side. Every fight is one of attrition, but only for you, they only grow stronger over time, each loss you suffer adding to their ranks. Worse, fighting the dead takes a toll. It breaks a person's spirit having to stare into the lifeless eyes of friends and family as you cut down their undead shells again and again.

> [!info] Social
> #### Luna's Pain
>
> A successful **`[[/skill diplomacy 12]]`**check sees a thread of deep, personal hurt in that statement. Luna's battle against the undead has left her with scars that reach into her very spirit, and a brief, haunted look in her eye confirms her mind went somewhere else briefly.

If they haven't already secured passage into the city, the party may be able to convince Luna to vouch for them if they have the right background. Any character with the **Path: Flameguard Militia** can easily convince Luna to vouch for the party, allowing them into the city:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Luna nods to you, then turns toward the blockade:
>
> > Hey! Here are the reinforcements we've been waiting for. More Flameguard for the hunt! We're going to send them into the city to get settled and resupply.
> >
> > Any objections?
>
> The Ordinate official and Burnished hand officer appear skeptical but seem unwilling to speak up.
>
> Luna smirks.
>
> > Didn't think so.

### Bypassing the Blockage

If the party is accompanied by Sin bearing an invitation to Cindarin temple they are permitted to pass without issue. If the party is journeying alone they must answer some questions before being allowed to proceed.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Sinvitation
>
> If the party is accompanied by Sin bearing the burnished hand documentation from Steros, or if the party obtained a copy for themselves, they are permitted to pass without issue. Otherwise, they will need to go through the Ordinate checkpoint, and answer some question.
>
> However, a party that wants to by pass the blockage entirely has some other ways to do about it, though none are guaranteed to work.
>
> #### Useful Connections
>
> - Any characters with the **Path: Cindaric Initiate** are able to speak with the Sages handling the refugees, and talk their way in. They will let the initiate's entire party though as well. (See "Cindaric Stoppage" above).
> - Any character with the **Path: Flameguard Militia** can speak with Luna, and get her to vouch for the party, taking them back into the city. (See "Heading Into Ordain" above).
>
> #### Sneaking Past
>
> It is possible to hide in a caravan about to move through the blockade, which requires the party to make a group **Deception (DC 14)** check. If at least half the party succeed, they are not discovered, and can ride the wagon into the city. Otherwise they are found, and kicked out.
>
> If the party wants to wait until the following day, they can hide among refugee groups being ushered across the bridge, which requires the party to make a group **Deception (DC 14)** or **Stealth (DC 14)**check. If at least half the party succeed, they are not discovered and get over the blockage safely. Otherwise they are spotted by Ordinate personnel and sent back the way they came.
>
> #### Alternate Routes
>
> The party may attempt to go around the bridge, either by land or sea, but it will present a sizable detour. The canyon itself is not easily gotten into except from specific locations along its edges, so the party will be looking at hours of travel before they are on the other side. However, no one will stop them from doing this, and it only costs them time.

#### Akon Attunement: Orderly Passage

If the party manages to traverse the blockade in an orderly fashion, advance each character's **Attunement: Akon (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Orbis Attunement: Deceptive Passage

If the party successfully deceives the the Burnished Hand to traverse the blockade, advance each character's **Attunement: Orbis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Primordis Attunement: Secret Passage

If the party successfully sneaks past or through the blockade, advance each character's **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### Concluding the Event

Once the party is across the blockade, they can carry on toward Ordain.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> After successfully traversing the bridge, whether by permission, force, or guile, the party may continue onwards towards [[Ordain Gazetteer]] and the [[A Sage Welcome]] Event which concludes this quest.
