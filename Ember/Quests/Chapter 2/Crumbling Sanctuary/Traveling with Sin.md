# Traveling with Sin

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event can occur multiple times while traveling with [[Sin Marmot]] and provides the party with an opportunity to learn about the Cindaric aspirant, her story, and her opinions on the current situation.
>
> This event can occur during a rest at any point during Chapter 2 if [[Sin Marmot]] is a member of the [[Party]]. In this event the party can:
>
> - Discuss the current situation with Sin and learn her thoughts on the matter.
> - Get to know Sin better by understanding her story.

### Thoughts on Events

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Farmstead[[Pillaged Farmstead]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > It's a shame about what happened to that little farmstead. It looked like it was such a nice and idyllic place before it was pillaged and razed to the ground. Those folks deserved better. Hell, the crops deserved better. And the fact that no survivors were found … it's an ominous occurrence, to be sure.
> >
> > We'd see our fair share of woodland raiding parties in the outskirts of the Corebright, but wanton destruction of nature like we found at the farmstead is something the Strider people would never suffer lightly. It's either aimless chaos, or someone was sending the farmers at the grange a very strong message.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Refugees[[Ambushed Refugees]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > It's unfortunate that Arcturian bandits feel emboldened to attack people so openly on the road like that. We're lucky that the refugees had someone as skilled in combat as Svala Bronwen in their group, otherwise it might have been an even grislier scene than the one we encountered. I hope we made the right choice in where to direct the survivors. Those people deserve some peace after what they've been through.
> >
> > As for the raiders themselves, we should keep our eyes peeled and ears open for any would-be assailants as we continue our journey to the east. They seem like an organized group, and we don't need any surprises of our own.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Undead[[Harrowed Crossing]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > Those bloated things we encountered at Keeper's Crossing were wholly unnatural, rotten in body and spirit alike. What a fright! I'd like to hope it was an isolated incident, but the phantom in Helkas suggests otherwise … and that minor apparition was nothing compared to this!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Corpin Sanctuary[[Corpin Investigation]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > The Sanctuary seems like such a quiet and peaceful place, it's hard to believe someone would want to upset that. But, I suppose it being built above a secret passage to the underworld kind of paints a different picture. The place is full of history; I just hope none of it bites us in the rear.
> >
> > That said, the Sages have all been rather nice, haven't they?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Pathways Ossuaries[[Emptied Tombs]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > That was a cold and dark place I don't think I'd ever care to visit again. I know my work with the Core of the World will eventually take me further into the lightless depths of Ember's Pathways, but those Ossuaries were stained with evil. And whoever is using that place for ritual magic can't be good news.
> >
> > It was rather convenient, though, for Avwynn to reveal herself as an ally. And I do mean that in a good way … I remain a tiny bit suspicious of her methods and motivations, but we can use all the help we can get get against the rising threat of necromancy.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Corpin Sanctuary Attack[[Corpin Condemned]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > That was a dark day for the Cindaric Sages. I suppose it was a historical moment to be a part of, but I can still hear the raspy voices of those skeletal creatures … and I shudder when I think of the look in Evesso's eyes when he commanded his minions to do his evil bidding. It was all so awful, like some kind of nightmare made real. I could go the rest of my life without ever seeing something to terrible again.
> >
> > To think, he was studying ancient necromancy in the ruins beneath the Sanctuary all along. My gut tells me this isn't the last of it. First Helkas, then Keeper's Crossing, now this. If anyone can offer proper guidance in times like these, it's the Speaker at Cindarin Temple.

### Thoughts on Characters

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Agraband

> **Q:** Lyla

I hate to put it like that, really I do, but if I had to worry about the concerns of House Cevher politics and society on top of my innate concerns for the safety of the natural world and its creatures, my little Keth head would pop.

> > Sorry, Lyla! I'm just being honest. Politics are simply too … political.

### About Yourself

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your story?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I'm from a treetop town in the Southern Corebright Forest known as Sirewood, which is situated among the verdant hinterlands of the Briar Peaks. A great place to grow up, if you ask me — surrounded by incredible living signs of the supernatural world, but also safe enough from the primeval leviathans that prowl the forest on all sides.
> >
> > My talents for druidic magic kicked in when I was pretty young, and I was inducted into the Primalists on my seventeenth birthday. I'm one of few Primalists that can actually harness the Growth, rather than just commune with it, so I started harnessing. After connecting to the Corebright itself, I knew there was something more; something greater than myself. Then, one day, a traveling Cindaric named Atticus came through Sirewood, and my world got a whole lot bigger.
> >
> > So here I am … in search of someone or something that can help me focus and refine that connection to the Core of the World. And if I'm lucky, maybe I'll take that deeper connection back home one day.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Primalists?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The Primalists are an ancient organization of druids that live among the Striders of the Corebright Forest. Thanks to our metaphysical connection with the natural world, we're able to become one with it by casting spells or transforming ourselves into various beasts of Ember. I've spent nearly a quarter of my life in service of the Primalists and their local efforts. And while the Primalists are a noble group — responsible for safeguarding the Corebright wilds, sometimes with their very lives — I feel a lingering responsibility to something greater.
> >
> > There is more to this life than nature magic. And please, don't mistake my words or intentions … the Corebright is, and always will be, sacred to me. But how can we protect the forest if we don't look out for the world around it?

> [!info] Social
> #### Cindarics and Primalists
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Souls** have a shared understanding of Sin's philosophies and can offer their own understanding of the subject to the conversation. The concept of [[Wildsong]] is nearly identical for the two faiths, they simply focus on different aspects of it.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Cindaric Order?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Growing up, I knew nothing of the Cindaric Sages or their benevolent order. Then, one day about two years into my training as a Primalist, I met Atticus and everything changed. This traveling Cindaric stopped in Sirewood on a westward pilgrimage to Akazea . While he was with us, he shared everything my young mind could absorb about the Cindaric Order, their benevolent philosophies, and their mission to safeguard the spiritual integrity of Ember and its people.
> >
> > From what I understand, the concept of Lifeforce as the Cindarics describe it is the very same as how Primalists view the Core of the World and its relationship to nature. I was eager to learn more. And when Atticus left Sirewood on his western passage, I found myself longing to find out precisely what the Sages have been up to all this time.
> >
> > I'd like to do my part … and I'm more than a little curious, to be honest. Souls are such wonderful, inscrutable things. Undoubtedly, the Sages of Corpin Sanctuary and Cindarin Temple have much to teach me about the Heart of Ember.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your journey?
>
> **A:**
>
> > After setting out from Sirewood, I made my lonely way east to Alavai, where I chartered passage on a ship to the Cascilian port of Hoist. From there, I traveled overland to Caxko and into the service of a frivolous group of adventurers who called themselves the Joyriders (with whom I spent a marvelous few weeks exploring the Dustlands). Fortune eventually led me to the company of Agraband Swift and the Strayhearth Caravan in Casla Brava, and the rest is history.
> >
> > My path takes me further eastward, to Corpin Sanctuary on Mial Mountain, and then on to the great city of Ordain — where Cindarin Temple and the tutelage of the Speaker awaits. The Arctus Plateau and all its wonders are still quite new to me. If didn't mention it, the value of your companionship on this long and winding road is immeasurable, my friends.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Hopes for Ordain?
>
> **A:**
>
> > All I know of Ordain is what I learned from Atticus and what I've picked up on the road. I understand it's a marvelous place made of majestic white stone, full of the splendors of the modern world. I'm eager to see Cindarin Temple for myself, if only to see how it's different from the Verdant Sanctuary in Sirewood. And I've heard tale of a remarkable neighborhood known as Orchard Lanes, where the fruit is apparently so delicious it drives the local way of life.
> >
> > More than anything, I actually hope the Cindarics and the other people of Ordain are accepting and have a place for a Primalist druid like me in their city. After all, I am an outsider, born of a place far removed from the metropolitan sensibilities of Ordain. Ultimately, I'd like to find out that this connection I feel isn't mine alone.
