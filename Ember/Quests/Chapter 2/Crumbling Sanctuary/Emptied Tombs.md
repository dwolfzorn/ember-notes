# Emptied Tombs

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> In this Exploration Event, the party discovers the [[Silent Ossuaries]] within the Pathways and learns a disturbing truth, that the ancient burial vaults have been emptied and there are signs of some profane necromantic ritual. In this event characters can:
>
> - Inspect the scene of an ancient burial site to discover evidence of active necromancy.
> - Parley with [[Avwynn Taol]], who approaches them from the shadows.
> - Learn details of an imminent attack on [[Corpin Sanctuary]] by the necromancer [[Evesso]] and his army of raised minions.

### Investigating the Area

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Grave Trouble
>
> A character with **Arcana (DC 14, Passive)** notes that a number of candles and offering bowls have been moved into a spot which has been prepared as a magic ritual site. It's very likely that magic was cast here recently, but how recently and of what nature precisely will require closer inspection.
>
> A successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check while examining the area notes that these tombs were disturbed over a long period of time. Whoever was removing the remains from their resting places likely did so across several visits. This plan has been in the works for a while, probably weeks, maybe months.
>
> Scanning the ground with a successful **Wilderness (DC 12)** check notes the presence of tracks leading out of the ossuaries. A large number of humanoids gathered in a central area and then began marching westward. The amount of trampling and damage done to the surroundings was the work of a large number of people.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The tracks are fresh, they likely left only a few hours ago. The footsteps look haphazard and clumsy, and don't seem to be moving fast. If you left right now, you might be able to catch up with them, but it was take a few hours at least.
>
> #### Left Behind
>
> Any character with a **Awareness (DC 14, Passive)** notices a writhing figure in the dark, gaunt and humanoid, and sprawled on the hard stone.
>
> If anyone in the party approaches the writhing figure, read aloud the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> An ancient skeleton of frail, shattered bones and paper-thin skin lays on the stone here, grasping fruitlessly toward you as pale light glimmers within the empty sockets of its skull. Its body is broken and incomplete, unable to rise or move itself no matter tireless its efforts are.
>
> The air around it tastes sour, not just from the ancient stink of rot, but from something worse, something which makes your skill crawl and triggers some small urge to flee in the back of your mind.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Old Bones
>
> There is no danger presented by this withered old skeleton, it cannot move or attack, but is a portent of danger to come. Characters wishing to destroy it have **+2 Boons** on their attack as it is considered **Prone**. It has effectively 1 health and will be destroyed if it receives any damage. Destroying it releases a burst of harmless but intensely unpleasant corruption energy.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Rising Evil
>
> A successful **Arcana (DC 12)** check reveals the obvious: magic was used to raise the dead, and this one was too weak to carry itself. However, the presence of this magic is deeply concerning.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The skill and power to raise a whole tomb's worth of dead would be considerable, especially if it was done all at once. It's possible there were multiple necromancers carrying this task out, or perhaps worse: one exceptionally gifted necromancer.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Undeath** recognize this as a skeletal minion, which are often employed in large numbers as fodder, distractions, and support for even greater adversaries. An entire tomb of skeletons would serve that purpose fantastically.
>
> Use of magic to attempt to communicate with the dead is utterly ineffective when used on this being, it is like who they once were has been erased, and they can only speak nonsense.

Characters may want to examine the ritual site nearby to understand what they are dealing with.

### Examining the Ritual

At the center of the tombs is the site of a profane ritual which still ripples with volatile abyssal energy and necrotic power.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### A Dark Lure
>
> Characters feel a powerful draw to approach the ritual site as something dark and alien stirs in their chest. A **Arcana (DC 14)** resists the lure, but failure results in the compulsion taking hold, causing them to approach the center of the ritual. Examining the ritual site results in the character experiencing both Up Close and Dangerous and Abyssal Murmurs as described below.
>
> #### Abyssal Murmurs
>
> It is necessary to step into the ritual space to examine its components and remnants. Entering the ritual space causes one to become afflicted with abyssal whispers for as long as they stand in the area. These whispers tease out the character's guilts, hatreds, and most destructive qualities, trying to draw them to the fore. Leaving the area causes the voices to fade immediately.
>
> - Use of **Talent: Rune: Soul** detects that this area has been magically desecrated and is permeated with evil.
> - The **Talent: Recognize Spellcraft** identifies that the magics used here are typically used in conjunction with necromancy.
>
> #### Up Close And Dangerous
>
> A successful **Arcana (DC 14)** check while looking over the ritual site notes the presence of several chalk symbols and discs of melted wax carved with glyphs on the ground. These symbols are from a school you're not especially familiar with, but can safely deduce are of the necromancy school.
>
> The ritual here was enacted recently, maybe a few hours ago at most. It was elaborate and powerful, and built unlike any ritual you've ever seen before. Unfortunately whoever created it was careful to destroy key aspects of its design, so you have no idea how you could replicate it.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The ritual appears to have channeled an immense amount of energy from somewhere. You're not sure if it was from Ember itself, or somewhere beyond, but there are evocation and conjuration elements that lead you to believe it was connecting to and pulling energy from something. That would account for the remarkable power the ritual would have been capable of.
>
> Inspecting the volatile ritual site requires a successful **`[[/skillCheck Arcana 15]]`** to resist the remnant abyssal energies in the area. Success subjects the user to **{Beckoning of the Void (Hazard 2, Willpower, Health, Void)**} from a growing pressure in the character's mind. This sharp headache may also cause nosebleeds or burst blood vessels in the eyes but is not fatal.
>
> Characters that remain in the aura for longer than the time spent examining the ritual may need to repeat this check at your discretion. This should not exceed more than once every 10 minutes, and the check DC increases by 1 for each consecutive check after the first.
>
> Characters that fail are afflicted with the **Abyssal Murmurs** described above for the next two days, even after leaving the site. Giving into their darker, more destructive urges proposed by these murmurs causes the whispers to persist for another day.
>
> At your discretion, if a character spends more than a week with the murmurs their Abyssal Attunement may increase (**Attunement: The Abyss (+1)**). After a while the murmurs will fade out, or perhaps worse, the character stops noticing them.
>
> #### Banishing Evil
>
> The Abyssal Whispers effect can be removed from an afflicted character using **Counterspell (Control, Aspect, Negate, DC 14)**, and a character with **Talent: Recognize Spellcraft** can take some time while others Recover to unravel the remnant magics here, weakening the abyssal presence to the point that it can't harm anyone inside its influence, though the whispers still remain for those within it.

#### Heart Attunement: Evil Banished

If the party manages to dispel the necromantic magic that lingers here, advance each character's **Attunement: Heart of Ember (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

#### Abyss Attunement: Undeathly Curiosity

If any character tries to learn the details of how the necromantic ritual was performed from the perspective of curiosity or interest in its power, advance that character's **Attunement: The Abyss (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Studying the Ossuaries

The event should begin with an exploration and investigation of the ancient tombs.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Historical Holdovers
>
> This location appears to match the hallmarks of ancient Arcturian burial styles. However, the location of it is strange, as the Arcturians weren't known to live underground, though they would have been in this region. These graves are somewhere around a thousand or two years old, and possibly even older.
>
> Tombs like this have been discovered all across the plateau, mostly in cave formations, and only above ground. This may be the first one ever discovered in the pathways.
>
> #### Arcane Oddities
>
> Examining the large lamps scattered around the ossuaries requires a successful **Arcana (DC 14)** check and reveals that they all still burn brightly with blue and purple light stemming from a flame contained in their glass bells. These flames are likely variants are eternal flame spells, but producing significantly larger flames than most. Creating flames that size likely took a considerable amount of gem dust for each.
>
> **Note:** Each of the lamps is nearly four feet tall and weigh upward of 20 pounds, while they can be moved and carried, they are much too heavy and unwieldy to ever serve as normal lanterns.
>
> #### Religious Remnants
>
> Characters can examine the burial holes and their stone covers, the hanging grave markers, and the carved shrine stones, assessing their religious significance.
>
> Studying the burial holes and their stone covers takes a bit of time and a successful **Society (DC 14)** check. On success, you note that the covers are all carved with various symbols that range from flowers to sewing needles. These hanging markers resemble traditional Arcturian death markers, albeit in a much more crude form.
>
> The hanging marks that remain each have wooden rings with stone medallions bound within. These in turn have braided necks that then turn into long strings of ribbon, paper, or cloth. Those that exist here have been stained by mold and rot and are largely impossible to derive anything useful from.
>
> There are signs the cave was once decorated with dozens of these hanging markers, but the natural materials making them up rotted away long ago.
>
> - **Critical Success**: You recognize the carved symbols on the covers look like old depictions of spirits. They also bear resemblance to traditional Arcturian grave markers, which might be where the modern symbols originally derived their design.
>
> Characters with **Talent: Fletching Novice** recognize that careful effort was made to carve the outer wooden rings, and each one is unique. Some contain symbols or sequences of images depicting people doing things, while others hold what might be writing.
>
> Characters with **Talent: Smithing Novice** recognize that both the large shrine stones and the stone medallions in the hanging markers were hand-made, likely with crude tools. While the large shrine stones are nothing more than stone loops with little ornamentation in most cases, the small marker medallions have all manner of varied and unique markings that likely spoke to qualities of whoever they were meant to commemorate.

### Hidden Watcher Revealed

While investigating the site of the ossuaries, Avwynn, who has been shadowing the party through the pathways steps out of the shadows and wishes to talk.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Good Intentions
>
> Avwynn has no interest in fighting the party and if they become hostile or attack her, she falls back to safety and tries to reason with them from the shadows, under the cover of a greater invisibility spell. If it's clear that the party has no interest in conversing with her, she'll fall silent and just observe the party from afar, trying to get a sense for what their intentions are.

> [!abstract] Avwynn Taol
> **[[Avwynn Taol]]**
>
> Level 12 (Boss) · Human Mystic
>
> ![Avwynn Taol](../../../assets/image113.webp)
>
> Tall, regal, and confident, this human woman carries herself with certainty and exudes a powerful confidence back with a steely, unwavering calm. Her icy blue eyes glimmer sharply against black sclera, betraying some hint of non-human ancestry, or perhaps magical twisting in her blood.

Avwynn will initially work to convince the party that she is not responsible and - in fact - was the one that passed the note under their dormitory door to enlist the party's aid:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > Before we dive into combat let me start off by saying I'm the one that slipped that note under your druid's door. I was trying to get you onto the necromancer's trail from the outset. I followed you down the elevator to lend a hand, but it looks like we were both too late.

> [!info] Social
> #### Reading Avwynn
>
> Avwynn is an inherently difficult character to read as she is well versed in deception and manipulation. Even in failure she appears to be entirely genuine and truthful.
>
> It takes a **Deception (DC 17)** to successfully read Avwynn, which leads a character to believe she is being truthful, but is naturally evasive. She has an agenda, and she is pursuing it, but it doesn't seem to align with the abyssal influence or necromancy witnessed here.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Avwynn's Intentions?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Simply put: I'm not really someone the Sages want to deal with. I'm from a splinter faction of the sages that broke with the doctrine pretty sharply. If they knew I was just walking through one of their sanctuaries I'd be in trouble, as would anyone that let it happen.

If the party already learned that Evesso is the necromancer, they may be more trusting of Avwynn initially. If they ask her to confirm that, or if she knew, she confirms it:

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Evesso's a Necromancer?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Yes, that's correct. I've been suspicious of him for a while, but failed to get anything concrete. I was hoping that nudging you would put enough pressure on him for a mistake to occur. Instead, here we are.
> >
> > You're not going to catch him, even if you leave right now, but you should still head back immediately, and try to save anyone you can. Plus, destroying the undead would be good for everybody, and Ember itself.

Avwynn is a skilled spellcaster, and can help confirm suspicions about the ritual if they ask.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the ritual?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The short answer: Necromancy ritual. The dead were raised, likely with the help of some Abyssal entity on Ember, and in so doing the world became a little more corrupted. While it's worse to raise the recently dead, animating the long-dead has equally detrimental impacts on Ember.

If the party asks her to elaborate on the detrimental effects:

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Effects of the ritual?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Short answer: think of it like walking rot. A little bit of mold on your food can be cut off and you're fine, but leave it sit long enough and it'll take over the whole fruit. These old undead are the spot of mold, and you need to go excise them.

If they mention or ask about the Abyssal energies around the ritual:

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Avwynn's a Sanguinary?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Right, that's to be expected. Raising the dead is the domain of the Abyss and empowers its presence on Ember. Using necromancy is incredibly dangerous for everyone and everything involved. It warps the mind and spirit of the caster, can harm anyone that comes to close to the left over abyssal energies, and directly harms Ember itself.

> [!info] Social
> #### Avwynn's Background
>
> Characters that success on a **Society (DC 16)** may suspect that Avwynn is part of the Sanguinaries, an religious order the broke from the Cindaric Sages long ago and were branded as heretical doomsayers and maniacal cultists.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Avwynn's a Sanguinary?
>
> **A:**
>
> > That's right, good catch. We and the sages share the same theological goals, at least publicly, but have taken entirely different roads in how we pursue them. Don't let them fool you into thinking we're a bunch of crazy doomsayers, the truth is pretty far from that.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** More about Sanguinaries?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I'd love to get into the cultural, theological, and ideological differences between my religion and the one it broke from, but we both have far better things to be doing. You need to get back to the Corpin Sanctuary, and I need to report back to my people and get some things in motion.

#### Primordis Attunement: Avwynn Revealed

If a character in the party identifies that Avwynn is a Sanguinary, advance that character's **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Concluding the Event

Once done speaking with Avwynn she departs, leaving them to carry on.
