# Lightless Halls

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration and Combat Event occurs in the northernmost of the three hexes which comprise the [[Primordial Bastion]], itself located at the southern end of the [[Fogbound Caverns]]. By exploring the sprawling dungeon of the [[Lightless Halls]] that dominate this section of the bastion, the characters can:
>
> - Locate a [[Sun Star]] capable of holding back Abyssal Darkness, allowing the party to illuminate the channels that run through the dungeon, and unlock the door to the enormous central chamber.
> - Survive the area's hazards, including various light-based and Abyssal energy traps.
> - Fight off Abyssal monsters that emerge from the darkness.
> - Unlock the final door to the next area of the Primordial Bastion.
>
> #### Area Walkthrough
>
> Exploration of this Event takes place on the [[Lightless Halls]] Area Map. A complete description of its contents can be found in the Area Walkthrough, [[Lightless Halls]].

### Ancient Beyond Measure

The structure of the Primordial Bastion is truly ancient, a towering stone edifice from the time when the [[World of Ember]] was being formed. Despite its age, the structure is in good condition — remarkably unblemished by the ravages of time.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Bastion At First Glance
>
> Before the party enters the Primordial Bastion, and at any time while within the structure, they can investigate the building around them.
>
> Any characters who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 16)** check observes:
>
> > The breathtaking scale of the stones and structures immediately catches your eye; they were clearly crafted by giants much larger than any you have ever encountered. A profound stillness surrounds you, a heavy silence that feels even more intense than the caverns behind you. However, it is not entirely gloomy. There is a sense of calm, resolute fortitude woven into the atmosphere. Furthermore, you observe that no flora grows on the stones of the Bastion; not even a thin layer of lichen, moss, or fungi. It is as if the stones themselves are utterly sterile.
>
> #### Taking a Closer Look
>
> Any character who investigates the stone blocks of the bastion and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check knows that there is something very different about the nature of the stonework here: a seamless style of interconnecting blocks suggests a mastery of construction techniques, with most of the stone blocks likely weighing one hundred tons, and some of the larger crossbeams twice or three times that weight. This was not created by the [[Shent]], nor the [[Aedir]], nor any known culture recorded by scholars.
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Crafts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character determines that the huge stone blocks that make up the structure are not really stone at all, and appear to be some kind of impenetrable metallic alloy. It seems resistant to any damage the character is able to inflict, even struck with full strength, or assaulted with magic. Yet, closer inspection reveals damage on the strange alloy — deep, thin, almost imperceptible slashes reminiscent of claw marks, though there is no way to tell exactly when or how this damage occurred.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Arcana (DC 14)** detects an extremely faint magical aura coming from the structure itself.
>
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - `[[/knowledge stars]]`: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character determines that whoever created this structure must have done so with magical aid; the stonework and materials exude a faint aura of enchantment. They can also sense that the structure is extremely old, and surprisingly appears to have withstood the passage of time without any degradation or fading of its magic. The structure feels warm to the touch; however, there is no aura of evil or malice. In fact, it feels natural, calming, and resolute.

### Entering The Bastion

Once the party reaches the Primordial Bastion, they must make their way inside and confront the horrors within. A full description of the initial Location that serves as the party's entrance to the Primordial Bastion can be found in the [[Lightless Halls]] Area Walkthrough.

### Solving the Runekey Puzzle

If the party successfully solves the puzzle in the [[Runekey Altar]] room, thus disabling the barriers of light and accessing the [[Sun Star]], they advance their Attunement.

#### Signara Attunement: Solve the Runekey Puzzle

If the party manages to successfully solve the puzzle of the runekey altar, each character advances their **Attunement: Signara (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### A Beast of Flesh and Darkness

After Gohema — the massive monster that waits in the [[Sealed Chamber]] — has been defeated, its remain leave a stain on the stone around it similar to those left by other Abyssal monsters that the party has faced. Attempting to cleanse, destroy, or purge the remains of this Abyssal monster is a noble pursuit, but in the end, only something extraordinarily powerful can remove such a potent and magical entity from the world. However, attempts by the party do cleanse or destroy the remains may reduce the degree of Abyssal taint that lingers in the area.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Cleansing the Beast's Remains
>
> The following are probable strategies to cleanse the remains, and the results thereof:
>
> - The [[Unknown]] and [[Unknown]] Spells have no effect on the remains.
> - A Cleric may reduce the degree of Abyssal taint by using the Divine Spark effect of [[Unknown]] within 30 feet of the remains. If they do, read the following:
>   > For a moment, nothing happens as the Abyssal stain in the room seems to resist the energy. Suddenly, with a terrible echoing snarl of anger that reverberates loudly around you, the darkness retreats and fades further into the cracks of the stone. As the flesh remains melt further into thinness, receding from the godly power of lifeforce, you now see a glinting armband protruding from the remaining scraps of otherwordly darkness.
> - The party may reduce the degree of Abyssal taint by pouring some of the essence of the [[Sun Star]] onto the remains. However, this partial cleansing also causes the remains to recoil for a moment, then burst apart, scattering stonework and flinging Abyssal matter and shards across the room. All creatures within 30 feet must make a successful `[[/save dex 14]]` or else take `[[/damage 2d8 necrotic]]` damage.

#### Heart Attunement: Cleanse the Abyssal Remains

If the party manages to reduce the level of Abyssal corruption left by Gohema's remains, each character advances their **Attunement: Heart of Ember (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Band of Primordial Life
>
> Any character with a `[[/check perception 16 passive format=long]]` or who makes a successful on a `[[/check perception 14]]` check spots the [[Band of Primordial Life]] among the dark remains on the floor of the chamber.
>
> - **The party reduced the level of Abyssal taint:** All characters automatically succeed on this check.
>
> Once spotted, any character who succeeds on a further **Arcana (DC 15)** check correctly determines the magical nature of the Band of Primordial Life without needing to cast [[Detect Magic]] or [[Identify]], is are aware that it is a wondrous item, but can determine little else.
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character determines that the band's style and runic lettering match the style of the Primordial Bastion, and while this item was evidently consumed by the strange beast, the band must have originally been forged by the First Giants. It is deeply connected to the life energies of the World of Ember and many would consider it a treasure beyond reckoning.
