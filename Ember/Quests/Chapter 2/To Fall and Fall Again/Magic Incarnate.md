# Magic Incarnate

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> In this **Social** event, the party can explore the Librarium and encounter the Elder Goddess Spectra herself, a surprising encounter with a being as old as the cosmos and with much to say regarding their encounters with the Abyss on their journey to Ordain.
>
> - Spectra will be interested in the Party's encounter with [[Writhing Whisperer]] from the [[Over The Moon]] quest.
> - Spectra will give the party more complete information about the Abyss if they completed the [[Bastion Ruins]] event from the [[To Fall and Fall Again]] quest.
> - Spectra will also speak briefly about the[[The Bleak Archive]] from the [[A Brush With Death]] quest.

#### Chapters 3 & 4

This Event is currently placed at the end of the "To Fall and Fall Again" Quest. It ties up a few loose ends and answers some lingering questions. However, it's likely this event will eventually be part of a breadcrumb for the next quest in the Abyssal storyline called Terror Below.

### Roaming the Librarium

The party can explore the Librarium of Spectra, perhaps intending to find an acolyte or follower, or they might rummage through the stacks of books in search of answers.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Researching Subjects
>
> The Librarium is filled with knowledge from across Ember, some of which is ancient, dating back to the founding of the city, while some of its books and scrolls are even older, having been brought here from distant lands. For any scholar or researcher in the party, it offers a treasure trove of information. However, it is also poorly organized, and spending any amount of time here can lead to hours slipping away quickly and without warning.
>
> Many scholars and researchers can be found crammed into corners, and some are happy to engage in conversation with the party, albeit briefly. They mostly specialize in arcane and obscure subjects, but the party may uncover one or two who share relevant and interesting information that may assist them. Within the Librarium the Players can search for specific knowledges. This section does not need to be a one time event, and the players may wish to return here another time to research again.
>
> #### Aedir Research
>
> Any character who succeeds on on a **Awareness (DC 16)** or a **Society (DC 16)** specifically looking for Aedir knowledge unearth the following, characters with **Knowledge: Aedir** have advantage on this check.
>
> - The Aedir were present on the Arctus Plateau beyond a few outposts. According to legends, they even built a strong vibrant city upon the headwaters of one of its major rivers.
> - **Result of 19+:** In the first section of a battered journal, a long-dead member of the Anachraenum Mirrium Jorcurlos, details their discovery and the exact location of an Aedir lost city said to be far to the south of the Forest of Stone. They followed the river that drains from the area toward the ocean. However, most of the journal is faded due to water damage.
> - **Result of 24+:** The latter section of the journal of Mirrium Jorculos is more accurate and while most of the pages are blank the location is said be:
>   > Carved into a great crescent cliff of stone, it is nestled atop the waterfalls of the river Herelion.
>
> #### Gods Research
>
> Any character who succeeds on on a **Awareness (DC 15)** or a **Arcana (DC 15)** specifically looking for God knowledge unearth the following, characters with **Knowledge: Gods** have advantage on this check.
>
> - Many gods of the Nineteen, whom the people of the Arctus Plateau worship, are said to visit the great city of Ordain in disguise. Some of them have favorite spots where they like to spend time among their followers or take in the city's metropolitan airs.
> - **Result of 18+:** The character encounters a priest of Katu, studying weather phenomena and charts. He informs the party that ancient Katu and his small family of Shard Gods reside upon a floating island, which for the last several hundred years has been discreetly anchored off the southeastern Arctus Plateau, far from any trade routes, roads, or well-traveled paths.
> - **Result of 20+:** The priest of Katu accidentally lets it slip that the Shard Goddess Raineka adores Ordain and spends most of her time in the city; her favorite spot is... Then the priest realizes that perhaps he should not share such information with strangers and hurries off.
>
> #### Magical Research
>
> Any character who succeeds on on a **Awareness (DC 13)** or a **Arcana (DC 13)** specifically looking for Magical knowledge unearth the following, characters with **Knowledge: Rituals** have advantage on this check.
>
> - Ordain has always been a city surrouned by magical forces, but the Ordani people have never been great wizards and there is no academy or place where wizards specifally gather together expcet in this very building. But they are few and far between.
> - **Result of 19+:** The character finds a battered and tired looking Wizard nestled deep into some books and while initially wary if the character is also a wizard and succeeds on a **Diplomacy (DC 15)** check the now friendly wizard teaches them the spell [[Arcane Eye]].
> - **Result of 24+:** The character assists the weary wizard for one hour. During this time, they develop an unusual rapport, and if the character also succeeds in **Deception (DC 16)**, they gain insight into the nature of magic and the intricate, interwoven fabric of reality. The wizard, who reveals themselves to be none other than the Star Mage Eveis Brightstone in disguise, teleports away, leaving behind a scroll of [[Scrying]].
>
> #### Shent Research
>
> Any character who succeeds on on a **Awareness (DC 14)** or a **Society (DC 14)** specifically looking for Shent knowledge unearth the following, characters with **Knowledge: Shent** have advantage on this check.
>
> - The Forest of Stone and the Wedgelands of the Arctus Plateau contain many ruins from the Shent culture. However, these ruins represent only a fragmented fraction of the culture as a whole and are the remnants of distant outposts on the periphery of a civilization that controlled nearly the entire remote Shana Mountains.
> - **Result of 18+:** The character meets a scholar who is convinced by a rumor that there is an ancient Shent ruin hidden somewhere in the mountains above the Bloodwoods, north of Ordain. Many seek its treasures, but it has yet to be found. The scholar is searching for more information and no additional clues.
> - **Result of 22+:** The character learns that a specific magical incantation is needed to reach the Shent mountaintop temple of lore. Though they don't quite discover the incantation, they discover among magical writings a page containing fragments related to the ruin, and a hidden message that reads:
>   > Under moon, under sun, the fire burns brightest, basking stone in radiant light.

Once the party has investigated the Librarium with some satisfaction, read the following.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Navigating through the overwhelming stacks of books and strange arcane devices, you come across a hidden alcove in the back of the building, where a large stained crystal window casts red, blue, and dazzling orange light across the chamber. In the centre of the room stands an awe-inspiring figure of myth.

### A Cosmic Being

The woman standing among stacks of books is the Elder Goddess Spectra. She is a friendly, approachable entity, and while she may not hold every answer, she is eager to help and answers as many questions as she can, albeit with some cryptic responses. If the party includes a Cleric of or followers Spectra, they recognize her immediately.

> [!abstract] Spectra
> **[[Spectra]]**
>
> Level 1 · Unknown Unknown
>
> ![Spectra](../../../assets/image177.webp)

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> While her dazzling appearance and aura clearly indicate that Spectra is a being of great power and influence, her presence in this dusty, tucked-away library is somewhat surprising. Nonetheless, she speaks to you all with warm and flowing tones, as if she has known you your entire lives.
>
> > Why, greetings! As canny and vibrant a group of eager young heroes as I ever saw! I see from your apparel that you are all adventurers of keen minds and some skill. What brings you to this little library today?

> [!info] Social
> #### Speaking with Spectra
>
> Spectra comes across as an excitable, earnest, and bright person who is genuinely happy to meet the party. She has vast knowledge of many different subjects, from magic to creatures, history, science, and people. However, she tends to babble a little bit, and it's somewhat difficult to get a straight answer from a direct question.
>
> If any character succeeds on **Arcana (DC 11)** check, they will likely experience something of an explosion in the person's mind as the raw power emanating from the entity in front of them rocks them backward with unbelievable force. Her passive aura is not damaging, nor blinding, and it feels warm without causing any issues with characters' overall temperature.
>
> Spectra appears earnest and upfront, but it's very hard to read her intentions. A successful **Deception (DC 20)** check notes that while she is honest and forthcoming with her answers, she will not reveal sensitive information to the party, either because she wants to protect them or because she doesn't quite understand their motivations just yet.
>
> The party can continue their conversation with Spectra for some time, during which time he provides additional information:
>
> - She often appears here in this library, as it is a lovely place to read and relax where no one bothers her without a desire to discuss interesting topics. Her presence here is kept somewhat secret; at least, no one tends to advertise it. But she often moves around the world and may not be here the next time the players require her aid.
> - Spectra adores those who can practice magic and has nothing but kind words to say to them.
> - She is even friendly with evil characters to some extent, and although it's clear she knows of their nature, she tends not to be too dogmatic. Even if a character is clearly a necromancer or a caster of dark magic, Spectra doesn't skip a beat.
>
> She greets any Cleric of Spectra with excited and warm regards, bearing on giddiness, and will jump up and give them a warm embrace, asking if they have been doing well.
>
> > "Oh my dear, it is lovely to see you again! I hope the world has been kind. How are you faring? Are you eating well? What is this you're wearing? Isn't it lovely!"

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> **Note:** While Spectra is an Elder Goddess and an extremely old and powerful being created during the creation of the Cosmos itself, she is not a particularly active participant in events and she is not omniscient. She is more of a passive observer that can be irritatingly cryptic at times. She is friendly and happy to chat with the party about the arcane nature of reality if they wish to get into specifics, and will be particularly fascinated with Wizards and Sorcerers, but she tends to push people towards seeking the answers themselves if asked direct questions.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Are you really the Elder Goddess Spectra?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Oh yes, in the flesh! Ordain is such a wonderful location for experimentation and analysis, with all these lovely records and extensive research into… hmm, well, it's mostly clothing, design aspirations, and different ways to write trade agreements while maintaining favorable and profitable positions within the political landscape of the city. But isn't that fascinating!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the troubles in Ordain?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Ahh I have not been paying attention really, has something been happening in the city recently then?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About magic?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Ah! Yes! I am the Goddess of Magic, and all facets of the arcane flow through me! Magic is like a hidden thread entwining the forces of our cosmos, woven into the fabric of reality, accessible only to those who dare to seek its elusive embrace! It is the dance of unseen forces, carefully coaxing motes of energy from spaces hidden, the whispers of ancient knowledge, and the art of shaping the very essence of existence. Magic is both a key and a lock, a bridge and a chasm, a riddle waiting to be unraveled! Even I will never truly master the arcane; it's simply unattainable, but that's part of the fun.
>
> Spectra winks at the party, and taps her fingers together playfully.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Abyss?
>
> **A:**
>
> Spectra closes the large book in front of her and sighs heavily.
>
> > Regarding the most pressing matter, yes, you have indeed encountered the influence of the Abyss. I can sense it on you, and it's not a good sign at all. I'm sure you have many questions, but please understand that while I will endeavor to provide you with the information you seek, I cannot offer you extensive knowledge of the Abyss.
>
> She pauses and taps her finger against the cover of her book slowly.
>
> > The nature of the Abyss itself is not only a source of unimaginable power and a rabid, uncontrollable ability to destroy everything it touches, but it is also an insidious force that seeks our utter annihilation. Giving it attention, attempting to understand it, and so on, merely feeds it and draws it closer to our world. While the Abyss may have touched you in various ways, the more you know, the greater the danger, and the more likely you will be destroyed or driven insane.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Writhing Whisperer from Over the Moon?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Ever since the Abyssal Shear, there have remained monsters of the foulest, more evil kind hidden in the darkest corners of our world. This thing you speak sounds to be like an entity from the Abyss itself and left alone; it would cause untold destruction in time.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Shattering?
>
> **A:**
>
> > It is not a subject I wish to discuss; it is not a fond memory, but suffice it to say the Cosmos suffered greatly until my sister stepped in.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About your sister Alar?
>
> **A:**
>
> Spectra tilts her head backward and smiles.
>
> > My sister is a treasure! I saw her only a century ago and she looked determined and as willful as ever! I believe she was investigating something about the Shattering, but we didn't talk about the matter for long.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Bleak Archive?
>
> **A:**
>
> > That place was older than you know; the Shent were wise to lock some of those things away. I always liked the Shent; they had a great handle on things.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Primordial Bastion?
>
> **A:**
>
> > There are ages of this world that extend far longer than the times before the Shattering. While the Shent, whom I'm sure you know, were an ancient culture that lived in the mountains to the north, the Giants themselves originally came from below. Although they are not quite as old as I am, they once shaped what you call the Pathways and encountered the Abyss, battling its influence to their ultimate downfall. The Bastion is one of their remaining legacies, still standing after all this time.
>
> If the party was able to work with the Luxaroth and save the Bastion, Spectra will say this:
>
> > Your actions within the Bastion are highly admirable, if not heroic, and I believe you may be exactly what this world needs to confront the horrors of the Abyss. If you wish, I will impart a blessing to you. It will remain active indefinitely and will provide you with a small protective ward against the Abyss in your future adventures.

> [!info] Social
> #### Spectra's Blessing
>
> Should the party accept Spectra's Blessing, each party member is given a permanent new feature called [[Spectra's Blessing]].

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Snarling Voice?
>
> **A:**
>
> Spectra peers carefully at the party.
>
> > That … is something you should ignore. I cannot stress enough that speaking more about this, or giving it any further attention, is damaging to reality itself.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Beast Mark?
>
> **A:**
>
> Spectra stares at it for a while, seeming to gather herself slightly, and for the first time in the conversation, she appears entirely at a loss for words. After a moment, she sighs and looks away.
>
> > As I said, giving Abyssal entities attention is extremely perilous, and no, I cannot remove that from you. Doing so would only fuel that thing's attention further and likely draw attention onto me, which... well, it would not be good. The only person who can remove that from your flesh is my sister, Alar. You'd best seek her out soon, and please don't talk to voices in the darkness, for Ember's sake …

The party can finish their conversation with Spectra at any time; it is ultimately up to the Gamemaster whether she appears here again outside of Quest Events, as she tends to teleport around the world a fair amount.
