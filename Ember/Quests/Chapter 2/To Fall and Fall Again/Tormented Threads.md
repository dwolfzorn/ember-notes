# Tormented Threads

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event occurs in a small ring surrounding Storsa's Strand on the Region Map, with a much higher chance of happening if the party previously found [[Aberin Lowd's Medical Records]] in [[Corpin Sanctuary]]. In this Event, the party encounters the fallen body of the adventurer Aberin Lowd. By investigating it, the characters can:
>
> - Discover a request from Aberin to tell his father about his death, and where his body lies.
> - Find evidence that Aberin spent time at Corpin Sanctuary, seeking to be healed from a growing madness caused by his adventures.
> - Locate a [[Defiled Journal]] that records Aberin's travels, but radiates Abyssal energy — suffering the consequences of that energy if they touch or interact with the book.

### Investigating the Overhang

If the party makes any movement towards the overhang, they immediately see the body of Aberin Lowd, slumped over against the rock face, and the message he scratched into the rock beside him.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you step closer, the source of the glint is clear — a body of a young adventurer slumped against the rock, the metal of its breastplate gleaming slightly as it catches the light. It is hard to tell much about the body from a distance besides that they were a Kivahr and look to have been dead for some time. A book bound in inky-black leather sits in front of the body in a patch of dark withered grass, and words have been cut into the rock above the body's head:
>
> *Tell Pa I’m Sorry. -AL*

A cursory examination of the body doesn’t reveal what happened to the adventurer, but with a closer look, the party can learn more.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Body
>
> Any character who makes a successful `[[/check investigation 15]]` check spots folded paper tucked into the adventurer's armor, along with a ring, the [[Inscribed Ring of Eternal Rest]].
>
> - One of the sheets is [[A Note For Pa]], a note from Aberin which asks whoever finds it to let his father [[Kendral Lowd]] in [[Storsa's Strand]] know that Aberin is dead, and pass on the location of Aberin's final resting place.
> - Another is a torn piece of paper from Corpin Sanctuary. If characters have seen [[Aberin Lowd's Medical Records]], they can identify it as a torn piece from that record. It lists dates from two seasons earlier and a series of treatments, all unsuccessful.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a `[[/check history 14]]` check recognizes the armor and clothing that the adventurer is wearing. It shows influences from both the [[Rustvar Valleys]] and the [[Golden Flats]], and likely comes from somewhere near the intersection of those two regions, close to where the party is now.
>
> - **Knowledge: Crafts**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful `[[/check perception 15]]` check notices ink-like blackness on the tips of the corpse's finger bones — the same color as the journal.
>
> #### Aberin's Final Words
>
> If the party uses [[Speak with Dead]], the corpse only mutters incoherently, with naught but a few words discernable amidst the muttering: *Daelin*, *Storsa's Strand*, and *Borehole*.
>
> Then, in a single moment of coherence, the corpse manages a final message:
>
> > I tried to get back to the light in the sky, to find the way, to crawl through. I'm sorry Pa, I tried to find her. I did, I did. Through the borehole. Find Daelin. Go to that place and the light in the sky but no light on the ground and the darkness inside and I'm sorry, I'm sorry Pa.

### The Mysterious Journal

If characters move closer to the journal, the decay on the withered grass beneath it seems to move.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The grass around the book begins to shift slightly as you approach, and a thin plume of smoke that smells of rot curls up from underneath its covers.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining From A Distance
>
> The party can use any of the following checks to inspect the journal from a distance:
>
> - `[[/check 15 arcana]]` to conclude that whatever is causing the book to smoke is not an effect of magic. It is something deeper.
> - `[[/check 15 nature]]` to determine that the grass immediately around the book is more than dead — it seems as if it has been permanently drained of all life.
> - `[[/check 15 perception]]` to make out the words on the front of the book, which say "An Account of Aberin Lowd" in **Language: Arcden** .
>
> When making any of the above checks:
>
> - **Knowledge: Abyssals**: The character has **+2 Boons** on the check, and knows automatically that the journal is an object of The Abyss.
> - **Attunement: The Abyss**: The character has **+2 Boons** on the check, and knows automatically that the journal is an object of The Abyss.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Direct Contact
>
> Interacting with the journal directly causes ill effects.
>
> - Any spell used to interact with the journal (such as [[Unknown]]) immediately ceases on contact with it, as if the journal forcibly rejected it, and deals `[[/damage 1d6 force]]` to the caster.
> - If a character touches the book with an object, they find themselves unable to hold onto that object, dropping it immediately and involuntarily onto the ground.
> - If a character touches the book with their hands, they feel a thick rot coat the back of their throat, as if they were choking, and they cannot breathe normally. If they put the book down, the effect stops — but if they pick it up again, they feel as if choking once more. Any character who touches the book also gains a Vulnerability to Necrotic damage until their next Long Rest.
>   > Your throat swells from the inside, as if some shadowy hand from within your own flesh squeezed tightly against your airway, slithering long fingers into your gut and clenching. Every effort you make to take in breath is a struggle, the air in your throat turning into a thick choking smog that grabs at your tongue and twists in your throat, as if it wants to make you speak its name.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Reading the Journal
>
> If characters hold and read the book despite the feeling of suffocation, they:
>
> - Gain 1 &Reference[exhaustion] level.
> - Learn the details below, which are also available as in-fiction journal entries on the description of the [[Defiled Journal]] Item.
>
> #### Within the Journal's Pages
>
> The journal provides the following account:
>
> - Aberin Lowd once traveled with his sister, Daelin, his wizard father Kendral, and his cleric mother Jai through the [[Pathways]], trying to bring the light of [[Lantyr]] and the wisdom of [[Spectra]] to the darkest corners of the [[Arctus Plateau]].
> - On one particular trip, Aberin was on watch, but became distracted by a flickering light in the distant sky. While watching it, he wandered further from their camp than he intended, drawn back only by his sister's screams. Terrified, he fled instead of returning to help. By the time he made it back to Storsa's Strand, his sister was missing, presumed dead in the Pathways.
> - Wracked with guilt for his distraction and cowardice, Aberin pledged to enter the Pathways and either rescue his sister or locate her final resting place — but proved unable to find a way back down.
> - Aberin repeatedly attempted to reach her via a place called the [[Shrouded Borehole]], but found himself eventually driven mad by his attempts. He lost his mind so thoroughly to The Abyss that even [[Corpin Sanctuary]] could not help him. At this point, his journal stops making sense, containing only overlapping sketches, symbols, and question marks.

#### Abyss Attunement: Defiled Journal Looted

The first character who attempts to take permanent possession of the Defiled Journal advances their **Attunement: The Abyss (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> Once the party has finished inspecting Aberin's journal, they can continue their investigation by traveling to the small hamlet of [[Storsa's Strand]], where they can inform Aberin's father Kendral about the adventurer's demise via [[Strand of Fate]].
