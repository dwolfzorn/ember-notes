# Twisting Shadows

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event occurs in the [[Pathways]], just southeast of the [[Shrouded Borehole]]. Upon accepting this Event, the area ahead of the party on the Region Map opens up to exploration. By investigating the nearby terrain in this Event, the party can:
>
> - Confront the Abyssal taint of various Pathways hazards.
> - Identify the corpse of Daelin Lowd, the deceased daughter of [[Kendral Lowd]] previously mentioned in [[Strand of Fate]].
> - Catch a glimpse of the [[Primordial Bastion]] in the distance, tempting the party to explore it.
>
> The Event — including the looming bastion — is depicted using the "Distant Bastion" Level of the [[Vista: Fogbound Caverns]] Vista.

### Hazards of the Shadows

Nearly any action the party takes explore this area has the potential to inflict mental anguish. Prolonged investigation occurs at the party's own detriment.

The fingers of rock that climb out of the ground are oily, dark, and horrifically twisted — blackened as if set alight by some ancient fire.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Fingers of Rock
>
> Any character who touches the strange black rock must make a successful `[[/save wis 14]]` saving throw, or else roll upon the Minor Abyssal Madness table below.

Meanwhile, the fog gathering between the party members' feet is not *immediately* dangerous, even to individuals who are not wearing shoes or footwear, but does present a danger to those who go out of their way to interact with it.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Encroaching Fog
>
> Any character who reaches down to touch the fog with their hand of fingers must make a successful `[[/save con 12]]` saving throw, or else roll upon the Minor Abyssal Madness table below.

On a failed saving throw, roll on the following table to determine which effect grips the character.

| Range | #### Minor Abyssal Madness |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | #### Disturbed Sleep  Whenever the afflicted character sleeps their dreams are consumed by visions of amorphous beings of shadow. People from their lives call to them for help as these abyssal entities absorb them. Eventually these entities become aware of the character, and begin to close in. The dreams always end with a sudden, all-consuming darkness and a sudden shock into consciousness. |
| 2 | #### Empty Skies  Once the sun sets the afflicted character will realize they cannot see the moons or stars in the sky. They are greeted by a vast, endless void of black which seems to radiate malice. The longer the character stares into this stellar abyss the more it feels like something is staring back at them, and the weight of this perception grows until they cannot dare to look up any longer, but looking away is no solace. They can still feel the Abyss watching them. |
| 3 | #### Black Tendrils  The afflicted character sees tendrils of blackness writing under their skin. No amount of effort can get them out, no magic or tool can reach them. Attempting to show other characters these tendrils is met with confusion, as they are not visible to anyone else... unless that character is also afflicted with this effect. |
| 4 | #### Shadowy Forms  The afflicted character begins to see things in darkness and shadow that nobody else can. While in dim light or darkness the character periodically has their perception checks spoiled by the appearance of forms in the darkness. These range from utterly inhuman to almost humanoid but too tall, or too long, or otherwise wrong in some way. Investigating these apparitions always turns up nothing. |
| 5 | #### Whispers from the Dark  The afflicted character hears voices calling to them from shadows. When the character is in or near dim light or darkness, and especially at night, they hear voices calling to them, beckoning them into the darkness. They can never see the source, and sometimes the voices sound familiar, coming from people they haven't seen in years, and shouldn't expect to see out here. |
| 6 | #### Phantom Malady  The afflicted character feels exhausted no matter how much sleep they get. They have all the physical indications of illness and fatigue, but cannot find any specific malady that is actually affecting them. They are always the last to wake after a Long Rest, and their whole body aches like they slept on bare floor. |
| 7 | #### Blank Eyes  The afflicted character cannot see the eyes of anyone they look at, instead every character they meet has hollow voids of blackness, and meeting their gaze causes a splitting headache that only subsides if they look away. While this imposes no specific mechanical penalties, the GM may apply disadvantage as they see fit. |
| 8 | #### Ichor Expulsion  The afflicted character develops an inescapable tickle in their throat that leads to a persistent cough. They periodically cough up a jet black phlegm, which looks like a mixture of tar and ink that is unpleasantly difficult to wash off their hands. Sometimes they swear it's even moving on its own. Worse, nobody else can see this expulsion when shown. |
| 9 | #### Rotting World  The afflicted character begins to perceive the world as filled with a growing amount of rot and death. Plants are dead, people look sickly and pale, animals look like they are on the verge of death. The stink of death and decays hangs in the air, and everything they eat tastes like it's started to go off. |
| 10 | #### Cadaverous Form  The afflicted character notes they are beginning to rot. At first it begins as a small ache in their toes or fingertip, a tingle in their lips. Eventually they begin to notice a blackness under their nails, patches of gray, withered skin, and worse.   No amount of magic, medicine, or healing seems to have any effect, and thoughts of amputation fill the character with a horrible dread that stays their hand. No matter what they say, nobody else can see this rot, to them the character looks fine.  ***Note:** The precise description of this decay and how it sets will vary by the character's ancestry, and is up to the Gamemaster and player to describe.* |

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Customizable Difficulty
>
> At the Gamemaster's discretion, the deleterious effects that the party is subjected to on this path to the Primordial Bastion can be made more extreme. The area is an anathema to living things of Ember; increasingly so the closer one gets to the bastion. You can, therefore, refer back to this section in order to ask players for further checks as they continue their journey.

### Investigating The object

If parties approach the large object they see in front of them, they immediately recognize it as the dead body of a young [[Kivahr]] woman wearing armor.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating the Area
>
> Any character who makes a successful `[[/check perception 14]]` or `[[/check investigation 14]]` check finds a small locked metal box.
>
> However, curiosity this close to the creeping fog has its dangers. For each character who fails a check to search for the box, roll a `[[/gmroll d4]]`. On a 1, that character becomes fascinated by the fog around them and has a hard time refocusing on the task at hand. They must succeed on a `[[/save wisdom 15]]` saving throw or else are compelled to reach out to touch the fog, and must roll on the Minor Abyssal Madness table above.
>
> If the party locates the box, any character with a `[[/check perception 16 passive format=long]]` or who makes a successful `[[/check perception 14]]` check notices that the metal is the same as that of the armor the body is wearing.
>
> The box can be opened with a key found on the body nearby or with a `[[/skill sleightofhand 14 tool=thief]]`. Inside the box is  **2** in small coins and a journal, [[The Final Days of Daelin Lowd]].

The body is positioned on one of the rocks as if it were sleeping, but is utterly unmoving — whoever this was is clearly dead.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Fallen Corpse
>
> The party can examine the body for more information via any of the following methods:
>
> - A `[[/check perception 15 passive format=long]]` or `[[/check perception 13]]` to note that the armor she is wearing is iron, but has been coated with an odd silver patina, as if the fog itself painted over her body.
> - `[[/check arcana 15]]` to determine that the body was magically preserved from decay for some time, though that magic has now worn off.
> - `[[/check medicine 15]]` check to notice that the body is positioned as if she was sleeping when she died. Her cause of death is not clear beneath the heavy armor she is wearing.
> - `[[/check investigation 15]]` check to locate a [[Inscribed Ring of Eternal Rest]] on a chain around the neck of the body, tucked into the armor. A small key also hangs on the chain.
> - `[[/check history 15]]` or **Knowledge: Warfare** to conclude that the armor is handcrafted and was likely made somewhere on the [[Arctus Plateau]] in and around the [[Rustvar Valleys]] area.
>
> When making any of the above checks to examine the body:
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on the check.
>
> #### Beneath the Armor
>
> The armor on the body is heavy and has almost fused to the body over time, but it can be taken off, gingerly, with a successful `[[/check sleightofhand 15]]` check. However, on touching the body, character's fingers become coated with fog. Any character succeeded on a check to remove the armor must also succeed on a `[[/save con 14]]` saving throw or else take `[[/damage 1d6 necrotic]]` damage.
>
> After removing the armor, characters can examine the body underneath. Signs of damage are apparent on the body, and characters can spot the [[Inscribed Ring of Eternal Rest]] and a small key both hanging on a chain around the body's neck.
>
> Any character who makes a successful `[[/check medicine 15]]` check concludes the Kivahr was felled by a crushing blow, though it likely took some time for the them to die.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> #### A Final Ring
>
> If characters have seen the [[Inscribed Ring of Eternal Rest]] on Aberin Lowd's body or in the possession of Kendral Lowd, they can immediately tell that it is the same ring.
>
> Any character who makes a successful `[[/check arcana 14]]` check or who uses an[[Unknown]] Spell determines that this ring has protected the body from decay for a time via the [[Unknown]] Spell.
>
> #### Final Words
>
> With a [[Speak with Dead]] Spell, the corpse can reveal the following information in response to questions:
>
> - Her name was Daelin Lowd.
> - She was with her family when they were attacked, but became separated from them.
> - She tried to find her way back to the surface but failed.
> - She tried to find her way into the Bastion, which she believed would offer her safety, but could not manage the journey.
> - She documented what she could of her last days in a journal stored in a metal box nearby.
> - She hopes her family made it back to [[Storsa's Strand]].
