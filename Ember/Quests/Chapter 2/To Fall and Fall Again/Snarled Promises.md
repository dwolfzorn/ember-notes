# Snarled Promises

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs at the southern end of the [[Fogbound Caverns]], just before the [[Primordial Bastion]]. By facing down the Snarling Voice — secretly belonging to the Outer God [[Vhismara]] — the characters can:
>
> - Choose to speak with the Snarling Voice, likely acquiring a Beast Mark [[Beast Mark]] in the process, and affecting how future characters regard the party.
> - Ignore the Snarling Voice, and reconnect briefly with [[Mioroth]] if they [[An Ancient Battle]] during the [[Over The Moon]] Quest.
>
> The Event is depicted using the "Lakeside Offshoot" Level of the [[Vista: Fogbound Caverns]] Vista.

### Howling in the Darkness

This Event directly introduces, for the first time, an Outer God — [[Vhismara]]. This particular entity is known by many different names but should be called the Snarling Voice in all interactions with the party.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Voice from Beyond
>
> While interacting with an Outer God would typically break a mortal's mind, the Snarling Voice is a faint manifestation of Vhismara's true power. While it might cause some immediate damage, the voice cannot do lasting harm to any of the party members at this time.

As the strange and terrible voice calls out to the party, read or paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Snarling Voice that rips into your mind seems to echo within and around you, and you hear a howling in the distance, as if a great pack of wolves hovers nearby. The voice snarls once more, dripping with menace.
>
> > Strange little creatures … hurrying this way and that, so curious, so … delicious …
>
> As the words linger in your mind, the voice falls silent and a profound sense of terror claws at your brain. Although the voice has finished speaking, it has not departed, and appears to be awaiting a response.

> [!info] Social
> #### Understanding the Snarling Voice
>
> The main choice that the party must make is whether to speak to the Snarling Voice or avoid the conversation. While the party does not have the ability to truly comprehend the voice, they can discover the following:
>
> - `[[/check arcana 16]]` to sense that there is something beyond the mundane about the Voice, but it is deeply corrupted, and not any form of magic or power that they are familiar with.
> - `[[/check insight 16]]` to determine that the Snarling Voice is interested in the party, and might be willing to offer power or knowledge, but at a great cost.
> - `[[/check history 16]]` to recall that others have had encounters with similar voices in the past and that they described being "marked forever" by the experience.

> [!info] Social
> #### Speaking with the Snarling Voice
>
> Characters pursuing an evil playthrough or attempting to amass Abyssal attunement may wish to converse with the voice longer, in an attempt to gain knowledge or power. This constitutes an acceptance of Abyssal evil, as a result, the party will be unable to engage with Mioroth during [[Snarled Promises]] later in this Event.
>
> Regardless, if characters choose to speak with the Snarling Voice, the consequences are likely to be severe. Vhismara is an Outer God, and their voice in any character's mind is bad … very bad. It causes a great deal of pain and mental anguish.
>
> If the party members choose to ignore the voice, instead proceed to [[Snarled Promises]].
>
> #### Questioning the Snarling Voice
>
> The Snarling Voice can and will answer any questions posed by the party, but:
>
> - Its responses make little sense — the only commonalities are its sense of hunger, and its deep fascination with the party members.
> - Any character who asks a direct question to the Snarling Voice receives a [[Beast Mark]] immediately, and must make a successful `[[/save con 18]]` or else take `[[/damage 2d8+5 psychic]]` damage. All other party members must also succeed on a `[[/save wis 16]]` or receive a Beast Mark as well. Repeated questions from the same character inflict additional Beast Marks, and require the same saving throws each time.
> - If the party asks directly what the Snarling Voice wants from them, dialogue for the voice's reply is provided immediately below.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What do you want from us?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Nothing … everything! Yes … everything you have … the veil is so very thin here … a window into the hidden ones little world … She thinks she is beyond me, safe … but she IS WRONG!
> >
> > I see it clearer now, I have her scent, I prowl through the darkness, ever searching … oh how I love to hunt … scents, smells … HUNGER … oh how I hunger … I smell such delights from … oh … from you.

If one or more party members receive a Beast Mark, mark the following Event Outcome. Having acquired a Beast Mark will impact the party's interaction with [[Luxaroth]] while within the Primordial Bastion, and with [[Spectra]] at the end of the [[Unknown]] Quest.

`[[/outcome marked]]`

### Ignoring the Voice

If the party members decide not to speak with the entity, try to run from it, dispel it, or otherwise attempt to ignore its voice, they will likely fair better. This is the only way to truly avoid meaningful consequences — if the party refuses to speak to the it, they do not receive any Beast Marks.

If they ignore the voice, read or paraphrase the following, then — if they also [[An Ancient Battle]] — proceed to [[Snarled Promises]] below.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A chilling snarl followed by a hoarse, insane howl of laughter can be heard as the voice drifts away from your mind. Finally, you hear:
>
> > Next time, perhaps …
>
> The presence disappears, leaving behind a furious headache and foreboding sense of dread that persists for hours.

### Mental Shielding

This section of the Event can only be completed if the party chose **not** to engage with the Snarling Voice, and they [[An Ancient Battle]] during [[Over The Moon]].

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Snarling Voice howls and rages; the force of its attention brings you to your knees, and the vast darkness seems to swallow you as great fangs close in around you. Just as all seems lost, a vast echoing chime breaks the hold over you and the darkness retreats in a rushing torrent. The Snarling Voice that had latched onto your mind howls with rage as it fades away, replaced by a great calm that settles upon you, and a different — and familiar — voice:
>
> > Ahh, my friends ... what a terrible place to be.

> [!abstract] Mioroth
> **[[Mioroth]]**
>
> Level 18 (Boss) · Memory Shent Seer
>
> ![Mioroth](../../../assets/image085.webp)
>
> Appearing as if from legends, a ghostly, semi-transparent, colossal figure looms over you even as he sits with his legs crossed. He is serene, calm, and intangible, as if made of pure energy and flickering strands of light. Shadows and motes of magic constantly evaporate from his body, and his only constant is his wise and gentle smiling expression. He looks faintly like a Kivahr, but as if carved from stone, with heavy brows, long limbs, and a muscular frame. The clothing he wears matches no recognizable style.

> [!info] Social
> #### Talking with Mioroth
>
> Mioroth's memory fades into view, though his image is flickering and his once calm demeanor is now harried and concerned. If the party asks what is wrong, dialogue for his reply is provided below. Afterward, Mioroth's image fades quickly, leaving the party members with no opportunity to ask additional questions.
>
> A successful **Deception (DC 15)** check suggests that Mioroth is not only greatly worried but seems to be in pain. It is unclear whether this is due to the effort to shield the party members' minds from the voice, or something else entirely.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What's wrong?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I cannot maintain myself here for long; this area of Ember is rife with corruption, and it takes all my efforts to shield you at this time. I compel you, dear heroes, to carry on towards the ancient seat; there, perhaps, the light may grant me some respite to talk with you again.
