# Bastion Wreckage

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration and Social Event takes place in the southeastern hex of the Primordial Bastion on the Region Map. In this Event, the party can take a moment to breathe and investigate the damaged chambers before them, now freed from Abyssal darkness. By investigating their surroundings, the characters can:
>
> - Study the bastion's inner chambers to learn more about the First Giants and their history.
> - Encounter the ancient Shent Giant [[Mioroth]], who both warns the party of dangers to come and pleads with them to help restore and safeguard the light above. Mioroth is deeply worried, and its clear the Abyssal influence here is a terrible strain on him.

The chambers here were once the main living section of the bastion, filled with a thriving civilization, before they were disrupted by war. If the party chooses to investigate the area around them, read or paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Between the twisted and broken walls and piles of rubble around you, you have the sense that a terrible battle once occurred here, many centuries ago. Whatever happened in this place, much of it has been lost to time — bodies, weaponry, and any other evidence of the combatants has long since been ground to dust and drifted away.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating the Chambers
>
> Further investigation can unearth additional details about the ruins. While making any of the following checks to investigate the chambers:
>
> - **Knowledge: Abyssals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on the check.
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on the check.
> - **Knowledge: Monsters**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on the check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 16)** check determines that while some of the damage here was caused by same the monsters of great power whose handiwork the character has witnessed elsewhere in the bastion, other damage was clearly inflicted by a different source.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character unearths the [[Skull of Merliota]] artifact from within the rubble.
>
> Meanwhile, any character who succeeds on a `[[/check history 15]]` check concludes that some of the damage marks on the stone were inflicted by a large mace or club.
>
> - **Knowledge: Warfare**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful `[[/check arcana 16]]` check senses the use of magic here in eons past. The lingering effects of this magic can still be felt, and while most of the energies here feel dark and twisted, that feeling is accompanied by a strong radiant energy, as if corrupt entities were confronted by a power infused with a radiant light.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character notes that flames melted some of the magical stone, suggesting the use of powerful fire magic.

### Flickering Memory

Once the party has finished investigating the ruins, a ghostly projection of the Shent Seer [[Mioroth]] appears. Read or paraphrase the following:

> [!abstract] Mioroth
> **[[Mioroth]]**
>
> Level 18 (Boss) · Memory Shent Seer
>
> ![Mioroth](../../../assets/image085.webp)
>
> Appearing as if from legends, a ghostly, semi-transparent, colossal figure looms over you even as he sits with his legs crossed. He is serene, calm, and intangible, as if made of pure energy and flickering strands of light. Shadows and motes of magic constantly evaporate from his body, and his only constant is his wise and gentle smiling expression. He looks faintly like a Kivahr, but as if carved from stone, with heavy brows, long limbs, and a muscular frame. The clothing he wears matches no recognizable style.

If the party meets all of the following criteria, Mioroth greets them as friends:

- The party previously interacted with Mioroth in [[Where Evil Lurks]] during [[A Brush With Death]], in [[Lunar Awakenings]] during [[Over The Moon]], or in [[Snarled Promises]] during [[To Fall and Fall Again]].
- The party [[An Ancient Battle]] if they completed [[An Ancient Battle]] during "Over the Moon."

In this case, read of paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Mioroth's image is clearly far removed from previous interactions, and he appears deeply concerned.
>
> > Dark times are upon us, my friends, and I have little time. I can stay here only as long as the light above endures, but we may be at a tipping point. You must hurry ahead and assist the one who guards the relic of Lantyr. Repairing it will once again safeguard this part of the world. I must ask for your help once more in combating the evils of this world and beyond.

If this is the party's first interaction with Mioroth, read or paraphrase the following instead:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Shent image begins to speak in a tongue that makes no sense to your ears. Stranger still, the sounds seem to come from all directions save the one toward the figure. Eventually the words transform into ones you recognize, the sensation akin to slowly remembering something that someone once told you:
>
> > This is a dark place you find yourselves in, heroes. My name is Mioroth and I must ask you for your aid in combating evil. Above you lies a relic of ancient days, a relic that holds back the powers from beyond. I must ask you to repair and aid the one who stands guard. Much of Ember's future safety hangs in the balance! Do so, and I will see you rewarded properly.

#### Working in Progress: [[An Ancient Battle]]

If the party [[An Ancient Battle]] in [[An Ancient Battle]] during "Over the Moon," they will receive a unique branch of conversation with Mioroth that will be implemented later in the development of *Ember*.

> [!info] Social
> #### The Light Above
>
> Mioroth's first priority is to discuss the issue at hand: the fading light of the Orb of Lantyr at the top of the Primordial Bastion, and the threat to the guardian who protects it. Mioroth will urgently convey the following information:
>
> - Light and the Lifeforce of Ember are all that hold back [[The Abyss]], but the ancient Orb of Lantyr that shines Light at the top the Primordial Bastion is failing and needs repair.
> - Even simply learning about The Abyss is hazardous to those who study it.
> - The attention of the Snarling Voice is dangerous, and it is best ignored.
> - The bastion was once a place of celebration, but has become a ward against evil.
> - The guardian above is a defender of all that is good, and tolerates no evil in his presence.
> - Mioroth is barely able to maintain his form here until the Orb of Lantyr is repaired.
>
> Once Mioroth has finished discussing more urgent matters, players may also entreat him to divulge more of his wealth of knowledge in the [[Bastion Wreckage]] section further below.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 12)** check determines that Mioroth is speaking truthfully about this urgent threat.
>
> Specific dialog options for Mioroth's discussion of the issue at hand are presented below:

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Mioroth's request?
>
> **A:**
>
> > As far as I know, there are two great forces in this world that resist the destructive powers of The Abyss. The first is the Lifeforce of our world, the power imbued within Shard Gods and, in small amounts, within every creature or person, including yourselves. The second is Light, a blinding radiance of energy that repels the darkness of Abyssal energy. The tower of this bastion holds aloft an ancient relic of Lantyr herself, yet it is fading. Without an external force to repair it, I fear it will break, and this part of the world will fall even further into the clutches of the great enemy.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About The Abyss?
>
> **A:**
>
> > That term is best left unsaid. My people knew the dangers of even simply *learning* about the space beyond our world. It also laid low the very first giants who dwelt within the Pathways here and built this very structure. Alas, what happened after the making of the world marked my kind, and though we thought we escaped to the surface and built our culture anew, it happened once again as the Shent fell to darkness during the events you know as the Shattering.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Beast Mark or the Snarling Voice?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I will not speak more of it, other than to say that it is a vast and terrible evil. You would do well to ignore it if you can and make every attempt to remove its attention from you if you have gained it. Only destruction and annihilation await those who dwell too deeply or try to uncover more about that … thing.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Primordial Bastion?
>
> **A:**
>
> > It was built long before my time, though I know it was once a place of joy and celebration. A settlement I believe to have been on the edge of a great path that led deep into the heart of the world. It became a battleground, however, where the First Giants fought one another in a great civil war triggered by the unleashing of dark powers and knowledge. It then became a bastion against the darkness and stood firm for uncounted years until very recently.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the guardian above?
>
> **A:**
>
> > He is a hero, a being not so different from myself, an echo of a once glorious people. Unlike me, however, he is not a memory or a ghost; while the light shines, he can act and defend it. Be wary of him, for he will tolerate no evil in his presence, but he is a resolute defender of all that is good in this world, and a steadfast ally.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Mioroth's flickering form?
>
> **A:**
>
> > My being and form are of no consequence at this moment. Though should you succeed here, I suspect you may once again be able to aid me, this time more directly. My existence is under threat from the rising darkness, just as this place is. While I still have some time, the darkness at my own door is growing beyond my ability to control.

### Learning From Mioroth

Mioroth has considerable knowledge about the past, especially the [[Shent]] and [[The Shattering]], but he is unwilling to go into great depth.

> [!info] Social
> #### Mioroth's Information
>
> During their conversation, Mioroth can reveal the following. Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 14)** check determines that while he is being honest, he is deliberately withholding information, and might reveal more if pressed.
>
> - He feels immense pressure to maintain his form in this space, as the Abyssal energies and darkness here significantly limit him and interfere with his ability to appear before you. The strain will become overwhelming soon, and the party has only a limited opportunity to ask him any questions.
> - He is a Shent giant, different from the First Giants who built this structure. His people arose later, after emerging from the Pathways and building great cities in the mountains. While he can manifest in this place now, the events that transpired here in ages past are things he has only heard about in myths and legends. He has little firsthand knowledge of what occurred.
> - Mioroth does not eat, sleep, or breathe. If asked, he will confirm that he's neither undead, or a ghost, though, but refuses to elaborate any further on how he came to be, his abilities, or his purpose.
>
> #### Learning About The Shent
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check can convince Mioroth to share more information about his people, the [[Shent]] — though he is still only willing to share information that he believes most would have learned by now:
>
> - The Shent were an ancient nation and culture that fell during the Shattering. The Shent built many wonders in these lands and great cities upon distant mountain peaks but they are all long gone now, he is merely a memory.
> - The [[Kivahr]] and [[Keth]] are direct descendants of the Shent, which is why the Kivahr slightly follow their physical form. The [[Hulg'run]] predate both, and were created by the Shent to be servants before gaining freedom and being treated as equals.
> - The Shent were wise, peaceful seekers of truth and worshipped the moons and [[The Cosmos]] of Ember. They also held great power of foresight and studied the [[Sea of Stars]] to such an extent that they foresaw the Shattering, many hundreds of years before it ultimately occurred.
>
> Mioroth cannot be persuaded to change his mind or give up more information than this.

### Concluding the Event

At the end of the conversation, Mioroth offers the party a safe and relatively intact place to rest before moving forward. He then fades away, wishing them good fortune. He believes that once the orb has been fixed, they will surely meet again.
