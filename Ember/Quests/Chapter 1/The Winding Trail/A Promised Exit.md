# A Promised Exit

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event occurs as the party approaches a locked and ancient door nestled deep within the [[Kaleidoscope Caverns]], south of the [[Shent Moon Temple]]. By investigating the ancient doors, the party can:
>
> - Parse out the secret passphrase needed to open them: "Hope in Destruction" (which can also be learned from the Shent Sage [[Mioroth]] after the [[An Ancient Battle]] Event).
> - Exit the Dripstone canyons and continue on their journey to reunite with Agraband Swift and the Strayhearth Caravan.

### The Sealed Doors

The ancient gate here is locked by powerful enchantments, and much like the other door can only be opened using the proper passphrase. Characters that try to use "Fate of the Moon" here will find that nothing happens, but they will be on the right track.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Gate
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 12)**check recognizes that the architectural style and weathered runes of this monument mark it as a relic of the [[Shent]], an ancient culture of giants which collapsed after [[The Shattering]]. The figures depicted on the crown are recognizable as Shent rebuking something in their midst while others flee, some carrying objects in their arms. The shape and darkness of it seem to represent the invading force of the Abyss during the time of the Shattering.
>
> - **Knowledge: Shent**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character recognizes the themes of sacrifice, survival, and perseverance in the face of adversity. The figures depicted across the top of the doorway are facing evil, possibly sacrificing themselves so that others can escape to safety. This would track given that the Shent were eradicated as a result of the Shattering.
>
> Any character who studies the imagery of the door and makes a successful **Science (DC 12)**check notices a strong theme of reverence toward the moons in the reliefs and inscriptions — the water moon Mayis is most prominent among them. The figures across the top of the door seem to have been added afterward. Given the prominence of Mayis on this door, much like the others, you suspect that this door was not originally meant to block entry, but to mark the presence of a moon temple or similar significant lunar element in the region.
>
> - **Knowledge: Shent**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character recognizes that this door was built with significant iconography of self-sacrifice, heroism, struggle, and protection. It seems that the figures on the door were giving all they could to rebuke some greater evil for the sake of others.
>
> Any character who examines the runes and makes a successful **Arcana (DC 12)**check notices several magical symbols carved into the stone, but they appear to be inert. At first they appear wholly decorative, but further investigation reveals interlocking runes of Transmutation, Divination, and Abjuration. Their purpose is unknown, however, aside from the Abjuration runes meant to protect the structure from outside influence. Examining the runes in even greater detail, the character understands that many of them were never connected to the others and only meant to be decorative, and are all variants of destructive Evocation runes. However, their presence masks rather intricate magical formulae that *does* serve a purpose. When activated by a command word which should be detected by the Divination runes, the door's Transmutation magic would manipulate a large area around the door, possibly freeing it to open.
>
> - **Knowledge: Shent**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: Strangely, like before, the active magic was embedded in a large amount of destructive runes. Yet the actual destructive runes are inert. More accurately, the active runes are deliberately threaded throughout and obfuscated by the inert destructive runes.
>
> A character with **Talent: Recognize Spellcraft** will note that the stone has active divination magic waiting for something, inert transmutation magics meant to do something when activated, and is heavily reinforced in the form of transmutation and abjuration wards.

Characters may also notice that there are runes of Pathward carved into the door, and as with the Shent moonstone they encountered several days ago, they may want to decipher the text.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Ancient Inscriptions
>
> Characters who can understand **Language: Pathward**, or those who make use of the **Talent: Versatile Translator**, are able to read some of the runes, deciphering the phrase below.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The translated inscription reads:
>
> > We fight for Ember until the last. We are not lost. We do not know despair. Purpose in oblivion, hope in destruction.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Opening the Door
>
> A character who speaks the inscription aloud triggers the door to open upon saying the words, "Hope in destruction." See [[A Promised Exit]].

### Failing Forward

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Just Like Last Time
>
> If the party is struggling, consider allowing **Science (DC 12)** checks to piece together the meaning. The above investigation should leave the party with two key aspects: Hope and Destruction. More specifically: *hope in destruction*. This is the door's passphrase.
>
> #### Outside Help
>
> - Contacting the caravan will result in Lyla and Clipper prompting the party to look for a phrase of passage like the previous door. Though, this does require them to understand **Language: Pathward** or use a talent to translate the text.
> - If the party helped [[Mioroth]] by defeating the [[Writhing Whisperer]] during the [[An Ancient Battle]] Event, the Shent sage has already provided the exit phrase, "Hope in destruction" (in which case you should remind the party that they already know the answer).
>
> #### Whispered Words
>
> If the party chose to side with and release the Writhing Whisperer, one or more of the characters receives a harrowing flash of memory left from the residual psychic energy of the Whisperer's telepathy:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Your vision briefly distorts; for a moment, your body as you know it is gone, replaced by a countless number of limbs reaching and grabbing and pulling your form through crystalline caves at breakneck speeds. You see the glare of daylight through a great stone doorway, but it is narrowing by the second.
>
> Shent cowards rush through the closing door, as others turn, preparing to face you. You are too slow — the fools have delayed you! — and as the great stone door collides, you hear one of the Shent bellow:
>
> > "Hope in destruction!"
>
> The stone shimmers as a pulse of magic washes over it, fusing it with the rest of the caves, locking you inside. You lash out in a rage, but are met with the sting of Shent spears and magic so ferocious that it makes you recoil in pain and fear.
>
> The memory unravels with a particularly strong hit, and your senses return with you on your knees, body shaking, and head ringing with pain.

### Concluding the Event

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> After you utter the passphrase, the entire door shutters and roars to life. The massive polished gems glow brightly, their surfaces reflecting the faces of the moons they represent. The stone slabs break apart and slide open, letting in air and light from the region beyond.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> After opening the sealed Shent door, the party can return to the Verdant Paths of the Forest of Stone. At this point, the party may wish to use their [[Messenger Coin]] to contact the caravan.
>
> From here, they need to backtrack west to help the Strayhearth Caravan clear the blockage in the [[Lending a Hand]] Event.
