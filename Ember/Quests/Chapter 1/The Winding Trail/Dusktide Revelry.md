# Dusktide Revelry

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social and Exploration Event occurs when the party arrives early or on time for the Dusktide Festival. By enjoying a few moments of communal merriment, the party can:
>
> - Learn various details about the mining town of [[Helkas]], including its local culture, persons of significance, and locations of interest.
> - Engage with the numerous attractions the festival has to offer.
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Dusktide Festival" Level of the [[Vista: Helkas]] Vista.
>
> #### Progression of Time
>
> Time advances by 30 minutes for each attraction that the party chooses to engage with. This represents both the amount of time spent on the attraction itself, as well as time spent socializing with nearby characters. If the party chooses to take a rest, partake in food and drink, or simply enjoy the spectacle of the festival itself, advance time by 1 or more hours as needed.
>
> This Event ends when the clock hits midnight (12:00am on Day 6). After this time, the various attractions of the Dusktide Festival are no longer available to the characters.

### Exploring the Festival

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Stepping away from the caravan, Agraband makes a grand show of announcing the festival to the disembarking travelers:
>
> > There is no fun to be had like the Dusktide festival in Helkas! Go and explore. Spread around your coin. Eat, drink, and be merry! The festival goes until midnight — after that we'll observe the moons under lantern light and shared drinks.
> >
> > Tomorrow's pursuits can wait. Tonight, we celebrate!

> [!info] Social
> #### Friendly Pursuits
>
> If the party checks in with the NPCs in the caravan, they will all give short updates on where they'll be and what they'll be doing. However, they decline any offers of company, preferring a chance to be apart from the caravan for a little bit after sharing a long road.
>
> **Agraband** will be at [[Folk Music]].
>
> > I'm going to go check in with my people here, sort out some caravan business first. Once I'm done, though, I'm going to throw myself into the festival. Follow the music — that's where I'll be!
>
> **Ankarist** will be at [[Esoteric Wares]].
>
> > Well, I've got an old friend who is running a stall this year. I'm going to see if I can find her once I'm settled in. I'm sure we'll run into each other out there.
>
> **Clipper** will be at [[High Striker]]
>
> > I have a long-standing grudge to settle with a floating stone. This year, victory will be mine!
>
> **Lyla** will be at [[Palestone Blades]]
>
> > First stop is an inn for a room. Once I get situated, I'll check out the festival attractions, maybe play a game or something. I'll keep an eye out for you.
>
> **Sin** will be at [[Dusktide Revelry]]
>
> > I'm with Lyla. I'm going to arrange lodging, then I'm going to see what sort of sweets and goodies the festival has!

As the group divides to pursue their various goals, the party's attention is immediately drawn to a nearby stage, where a performance has just begun.

### Lunar Legerdemain

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The applause and cheers are the first things to draw your attention, followed by flashes of light and plumes of colorful smoke. Upon a raised stage near the center of town, a pair of charismatic figures deftly direct the crowd's attention between their assistants' spectacles.
>
> These illusory tricks are an elaborate blend of combined practical and magical effect: creatures that materialize above the crowd and appear to pounce before disintegrating into a shower of sparks, dazzling rays of color rocketed into the sky beneath trails of smoke, arcs of illusory lightning that spiderweb over your head slow enough to touch — the audience's excitement reaches a crescendo as the opening act comes to a close and a tall, slender Fej man steps into view, dressed in fine clothing of gold and orange that matches his features.
>
> Suddenly, an elegant human woman draped in layers of flowing creme-colored silk calls out from the center of the crowd with a booming, magically-enhanced voice:
>
> > Azir, I think we need some volunteers for our next trick! I shall pick one, and you choose the rest!
>
> Upon the stage, Azir smiles and makes a show of looking out over the crowd with a hand held to his brow.
>
> > Very generous of you, Orrin. I do wonder who you've got your eye on. Surely you've already picked out your assistant!
>
> Orrin chuckles and says:
>
> > But of course! I claim Helkas' stalwart leader, Sadri Zhalimorne!
>
> With a flourish of her flowing silken sleeves the crowd parts around Orrin, revealing a gray-skinned Altyran woman who finds herself very much put on the spot. Undaunted, she steps up to the stage, waving to the crowd as it cheers for her.
>
> Azir offers a deep bow to Sadri, addressing her and then the crowd:
>
> > Welcome to the stage, Magistrate! Now, then, I need a few volunteers to step up, and I am so notoriously choosy! Let's see who catches my eye…
>
> A moment later, Azir's attention lands on you all, and the performers promptly offer you a spot on the stage. The surrounding crowd wastes no time, offering support for you through waves of cheers and claps.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Someone From The Audience
>
> You should select 1-2 characters from the party to be taken up onto the stage. If no characters volunteer, Orrin and Azir will select the characters with the **lowest** **Performance (DC 0, Passive)**.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Assessing the Performers
>
> **Orrin** (Neutral Good, Ordani Human, she/her) and **Azir** (Neutral Good, Ordani Fej, he/him) are exceptionally friendly and competent performers that are old hands at guiding audience members. Their natural routine is for one to speak while the other sets up the assistants, making sure the crowd is engaged and the performers are ready.
>
> Watching Sadri, you can tell that she is clearly out of her element on stage, but is a good sport and willing to do her best for the sake of the performance. If this is the first time the party has met her, she makes sure to introduce herself [[Dusktide Revelry]].
>
> #### Lunar Legerdemain
>
> Characters are prompted to help Orrin and Azir juggle a collection of three glowing magical moons that are identical in size but are of varying weight. Over the course of the performance, Orrin and Azir begin juggling the moons between the characters with increasing speed.
>
> Any character invited on stage can do their part by making a successful **Performance (DC 13)** check to not drop the moons. In the event of a failed check, however, Orrin and Azir utilize magic to keep the moons in alignment, joking about the "unpredictable nature of the cosmos."
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character's skillful display of juggling results in them being left with all three moons for a bit as Orrin and Azir explain the history of the Dusktide conjunction and how “Ember’s moons always move when and where they’re supposed to.”
>
> If Sadri succeeds at the task, the crowd cheers for her with enthusiasm and encouragement.
>
> If Sadri fails at the task, she does so quickly and to the great amusement of the crowd, but does so with grace.
>
> Characters that take part are thanked at the end of the display, and leave the stage to generous applause from the crowd.

After the performance, Sadri approaches the party to introduce herself, recognizing that they are newcomers to town.

> [!abstract] Sadri Zhalimorne
> **[[Sadri Zhalimorne]]**
>
> Level 1 · Altyra Trader
>
> ![Sadri Zhalimorne](../../../assets/image063.webp)
>
> You regard a steel-skinned woman with luminous golden eyes and side-parted bob of short-cropped silver hair. Adorned in a brown sleeveless tunic with leather shorts and boots, her countenance is equal parts purposeful and amused, and you can’t help but notice the lambent lines of bright copper that decorate her legs. A beaded Arcturian plastron necklace hangs around her neck, proudly signifying some kind of social station.

> [!info] Social
> #### Chatting With Sadri
>
> If the party took part in the onstage performance, Sadri says:
>
> > Well… that was something. Thank you for sharing the attention with me up there. I'm not bad with crowds, but that was certainly unexpected. You come in for the festival, or are you just passing through?
>
> Sadri is happy to chat with the party for a bit, and welcomes them to Helkas. If they share the news of the events in the Forest of Stone, she'll express interest and excitement about what she learns.
>
> If the party mentions the drakes:
>
> > I've been hearing reports of these creatures appearing in the area. The local hunters and trackers haven't had any direct run-ins yet, so we don't know anything about them really. You've single-handedly given us more information than we've collected in weeks.
>
> Afterward, she offers them  **1** for providing the town with useful information.
>
> > I make sure that Helkas rewards those who help the town, and sharing what you've discovered in the Forest of Stone absolutely counts as helping. Now, then, I do have to be on my way. Welcome again to Helkas. Please enjoy yourselves!

From here, the party is free to explore additional festival attractions or the town of [[Helkas]] itself.

### Festival Attractions

Each of the five Strayhearth Caravan characters are associated with one of the following attractions. Each NPC uses their respective activity as an opportunity to showcase a facet of their skills and personality.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Available Activities
>
> The festival takes place in the center of town, where an array of market stalls have been arranged for the duration of the festival.
>
> [[Folk Music]]
>
> > A space is being cleared for a music performance, with an ever-growing collection of musical instruments being assembled and tuned. A show is imminent there, and a small crowd is already gathering.
>
> [[Palestone Blades]]
>
> > A stall of targets and stacks of throwing knives sit alongside shelves of curious hand-carved trinkets. The attraction practically begs for a deft hand and keen eye to collect one if they can.
>
> [[Esoteric Wares]]
>
> > A large sign declares the presence of strange wares and magical oddities for those with enough coin. The stall owner seems to be spending more time chatting with potential customers than actually selling things.
>

### Folk Music

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A motley group of musicians — professional entertainers and folk minstrels alike — gather around with their various instruments to play Dusktide songs in the town center.
>
> With his trademark emerald lute at his fingertips, the renowned bard Raxwell Smalls conducts this charming ragtag band of locals and visitors. As the band prepares to play, he calls out:
>
> > Come, take up an instrument if you know how! All are welcome! As are tips, if you fine folks like what you hear from us!
>
> Taking up a weathered guitar from a rest nearby, Agraband Swift joins the band, chatting with them and preparing for a spirited performance. In anticipation of the impending entertainment, a lively audience forms, with all eyes on Raxwell and his motley crew.

> [!info] Social
> #### Jam Session
>
> **Raxwell Smalls** (Neutral Good, Ordani Human, he/him) is a handsome and powerfully charming bard with aster aspects, such as glowing emerald eyes and long-clawed fingertips, which he uses to strum his lute. He and the assorted performers are friendly, welcoming, and happy to discuss the finer points of music, life in Helkas and abroad, and more.
>
> Characters who perform with the motley band may be given a share of the tips earned from the performance; if they do well, they are invited by Raxwell to perform again. After the third successive performance, however, Raxwell kindly asks the character to make room for someone new.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Playing the Crowd
>
> If any of the PCs play instruments, they can join the performance and get a share of the tips that come in from the audience. Alternatively, less scrupulous characters might try to liberate unattended coin from onlookers.
>
> Any character can contribute to the performance with a successful **Performance (DC 12)** check, earning  **4d8** in tips after the show.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character's performance is so exemplary that the crowd's generosity is measured in silver, not copper; they instead earn  **4d8** in tips after the show.
>
> On a failed check, Raxwell gives the character a weak smile and clasp on the shoulder before quietly asking them to step off the stage.
>
> Any unscrupulous character wishing to lift some coin from the distracted crowd can do so with a successful **Stealth (DC 12)** check, lifting a few light purses and coming away with  **4d8**.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character instead manages to cut some weightier purses, coming away with  **4d8**.
>
> On a failed check, the character is caught by the locals and thrown out of the crowd. Watchful eyes make sure they don't get a second chance at causing trouble.

### Palestone Blades

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The stall before you is decked with little glowing carvings ranging from tiny Leviathan statues and local monsters to stylish jewelry and stone weapons. A ruddy brown Hulg'run stonecarver with a large pair of magnifying goggles strapped to their head sits hunched over a workbench. On the bench is a glimmering stone he is carefully whittling into some new objets d'art for the festival.
>
> A small target range occupies an adjacent stall, the finely painted sign nearby declaring one copper piece buys five attempts at a bullseye, and three hits wins a palestone carving.
>
> Lyla Cevher is already present, throwing knives with exceptional skill, winning a palestone bracelet just as you approach. Catching sight of you, she tilts her head toward the targets.
>
> > Fancy a little knife-throwing? Hit three bullseyes and you get one of Padragon's pretty little carvings. And if you can beat me… I'll give you a gold piece.

> [!info] Social
> #### D'arts and Crafts
>
> **Padragon Faeldspar** (Neutral Good, Arcturian Hulg'run, he/him) is incapable of speech, instead communicating with common sign language or a small slate board on which he can scribble replies. If asked about his background:
>
> > Been working with palestone almost my whole life. Used to be a miner, now a carver.
>
> If asked about his lack of a voice, he doesn't go into much detail:
>
> > Had a tough time in my early shaping years, decided silence was best for me.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Palestone Blades
>
> Any character wishing to win a game of Palestone Blades may do so by making three successful **Athletics (DC 12)** checks out of five attempts, winning a random palestone trinket (roll once on the [[Arcturian Trinkets]] table).
>
> Additionally, any character who takes Lyla up on her challenge will compete side-by-side with her. If they outscore her, they earn her hearty congratulations and  **1**, as promised.

### Esoteric Wares

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A middle-aged Signborn peddler, draped in blues and purples stitched with patterns of silvery stars, stands before a cart full of curiosities, calling out to passing locals. A brightly painted sign declares "Esoteric Wares!" with a sub-line promising each item guaranteed to be magically enchanted.
>
> The Ordani investigator Ankarist stands nearby, chatting with the peddler about her magical items and their origins between breaks to speak with customers. They chat like old friends. As you near the cart, her spiel is aimed at you directly:
>
> > Come, friends — you have the look of someone needing more magic in their lives! Peruse these strange and esoteric wares of mine! My prices are fair, and discounts await those with the moxie to claim them!

> [!info] Social
> #### First Impressions
>
> **Velaria Velevestrian** (Neutral Good, Arcturian Signborn, she/her) is a peddler of curios and magic items. She favors characters with confidence and presence.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 16)** check is able to convince Velaria to offer them a 10% discount on her wares.
>
> - **Knowledge: Trade**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> #### Familiar Faces
>
> Speaking with Ankarist, he can provide some background:
>
> > Oh, I've known Velaria for years. We first met in Ordain in the markets. I was a young investigator who was very curious about where she finds her items, and she was frustratingly vague with her answers. It took us a while to warm up to each other.
>
> Velaria backs up this story:
>
> > The stalwart investigator here was always so exceptionally curious about me! Always hounding me about the provenance of my items, and whether or not I had proper licenses — I tell you, I was convinced he had a crush on me by the way he was always hassling me. Alas, no, he just takes his job too seriously.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Esoteric Wares
>
> In addition to a few noteworthy pieces of standard equipment (such as finely-crafted gaming sets, musical instruments, and scented [[Candle]]), Velaria has a small assortment of Common magic items for sale:
>
> - [[Fisherman's Hook]]
> - [[Mirror of Masking]]
> - [[Sage's Pipe]]
> - [[Moon Ring]]
> - [[Spyglass]]
>
> If the party members lack adequate funds, Velaria could be enticed to accept a trade for any valuable items the characters might have found during their exploration of the Forest of Stone.

### Critters And Cakes

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The setup here is an odd one: a stall offering both animal feed and decadent-looking, moon-shaped cake pops sitting beside a collection of animal pens. A hand-painted sign declares the availability of "Sweet Treats and Sweeter Critters."
>
> Smoking a pipe from a perch on a well-worn stool, the stocky and wizened vrjnhar owner watches the animals with a small smile. Before them are a collection of expertly decorated treats each fashioned after the Dusktide moons: Mayis (the Water Moon), Ragen (the Fire Moon), and Orbis (the Dark Moon).
>
> Already in the pens, Sin Marmot can be seen gleefully speaking with the docile critters and doing her level best to endear herself to them.

> [!info] Social
> #### Sweet Keeper
>
> **Mishi Dolan** (Neutral Good, Arcturian Vrjnhar, she/they) operates the stall. This laid-back confectioner is a long-time animal lover who works as a baker in Helkas.
>
> > I mostly run the stall for the local kids, but I know everyone loves cute animals and cake… even if they pretend they don't.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Critters and Crumbs
>
> Admission to the Petting Zoo is  **-1**. Animal feed is available for an additional  **-1**; a single purchase provides a character with enough animal feed for the duration of their visit.
>
> Treats meant for humanoids can also be acquired for  **-2** each. The cake pops are sweet, moist, and delicious. The available options are the Water Moon Mayis, which is filled with a salted caramel, the Dark Moon Orbis, which is chocolatey and a little bitter, and the Fire Moon Ragen, a strong spiced cake.
>
> #### Petting Zoo
>
> Several furry beasts are contained in wooden pens here, including a Juvenile Carnelian Salamander, a trio of playful, friendly Oozes, a domesticated Beletor Cub, a double-headed Strainbird, and a fuzzy Mosshog.
>
> The first character to make a successful **Wilderness (DC 13)** check can befriend one of the animals and earn a free confection from Mishi.
>
> - **Knowledge: Beasts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

### High Striker

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Through the noise of the crowd, there's a bright chime, followed immediately by cheers and clapping. You quickly spot the source: a stall where contestants take turns swinging a wooden mallet against a calibrated lever to launch a magical lodestone into the air. A large sign with letters carved out of glowing palestone declares this game: High Striker.
>
> The previous winner walks off with an oversized leviathan doll, and taking their spot is none other than Clipper. With a look of determination, they strike the plate and send the stone shooting upward, though not far enough. Clipper watches the enchanted stone silently descend back to the striking plate with obvious disappointment. Their ethereal arms droop and begin digging through the pockets of their bag in search of another copper piece.
>
> Meanwhile, the burly fej barker with burning yellow eyes calls out over the din:
>
> > Come one, come all! Test your might and precision! Have you got what it takes to be a High Striker?

> [!info] Social
> #### Heavy Hitter
>
> **Hode Cinderhaft** (Neutral Good, Arcturian Fej, he/him) is a foreman of the Leviathan Mine. His High Striker stall has been a staple of the festival for as long as he's had the job; he pays for the whole setup out of pocket with no concern for making anything back. If asked, he's happy to demonstrate "how simple" the game is, conveniently ignoring the skill, practice, and physique he has as a veteran miner.
>
> #### Eye On The Prize
>
> Clipper is here attempting to win the prize with all their might, but can't quite manage it. A party member can help by winning the prize themselves. Doing so is worth nothing more than Clipper's endless gratitude, and the satisfaction of seeing them united with a giant stuffed leviathan that is equal parts adorable and monstrous.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Hammer Time
>
> For each  **-1** a character spends, they get a single chance to see how high they can launch a feather-falling lodestone using a heavy wooden mallet against a calibrated lever. The lightly-enchanted stone chimes and glows bright orange and emits a high-pitched tone when it reaches a height of 20 feet.
>
> Any character can drive the enchanted lodestone high enough to chime with a successful **Athletics (DC 16)** check. A character who successfully chimes the stone is awarded an oversized Stuffed Leviathan Doll as a prize. Losers are swiftly encouraged to play again.

### Concluding the Event

While most of the town is attending the festival, several businesses are also open to capitalize on the increased foot traffic and coin flow. If the party wants to make arrangements for food, drink, and lodging — or shop for equipment and the like — consult the [[Helkas]] settlement walkthrough.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> Once the party has finished exploring the festival, advance the clock to 12:00am (if midnight has not already arrived). Once the clock strikes midnight, this Event ends — mark it as as complete.
>
> Advancing time by 1 hour will cause the [[Dusktide Rising]] Event to begin.
