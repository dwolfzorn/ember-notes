# Clearing the Rubble

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration and Social Event occurs if the party decides to forego exploration and instead stay with the Strayhearth Caravan to help clear the rubble. By staying, the party can:
>
> - Labor to help clear the rubble from the Collapsed Cairn, so the caravan might continue on its journey to Helkas unimpeded.
>
> #### Discouraged Gameplay
>
> This Event involves the party spending hours working to clear rubble. This is not the intended gameplay, as it results in the party never experiencing any of the Side Quests in the area. However, the party is not committed to this, and can always leave to explore at any time before resolving this Event. Bear in mind that they won't get any of the time spent clearing rubble back, and may find themselves incapable of arriving in Helkas in a timely manner as a result.

> [!info] Social
> #### Agraband's Insistence
>
> Agraband attempts to not-so-subtly encourage the party to go and explore rather than sticking with the caravan to clear rubble. However, the party is free to do as they like, and Agraband won't stop them.
>
> > You know, I do appreciate the fact you're willing to help here… but I really do think your time and energy would be better spent looking for a secondary route, or getting around to the other side of this blockage. We've got plenty of people to work the mess on this side, and more hands won't necessarily help.

### Clearing the Rubble

Clearing the collapsed rubble requires a cumulative total of **48 hours of work**, with the participants — caravan members and characters alike — taking grueling shifts of **16 hours' work each day**, which incurs the **Exhausted** condition at the end of each workday.

The participants are allotted time to Rest, which clears their Exhaustion if taken. If unaided by the party, the caravan will fully clear the rubble in three days, allowing them to arrive in Helkas just barely on time for the festival.

### Working Faster

If the party remains behind at the site of the collapse, they can lend further aid to the process, enabling it to progress more rapidly.

Each work day, the party may undertake a **Group Challenge** to increase the effectiveness of that day's work.

#### Crucible WIP

This will eventually use the Crucible "Group Challenge" system which is not yet implemented. The following rules are temporary until the group challenge can be added.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Hard Day's Work
>
> Characters select a skill check from the list that they will roll for the group check. No more than two members of the party may attempt the same skill check. If at least half the party (rounding up) succeeds on their respective checks, they reduce the total time needed to clear the rubble by **8 hours.** Failure by the group provides no reduction in work time.
>
> - **Athletics (DC 14)** to climb, haul stone, allowing the work to proceed at pace and get some of the largest pieces of stone moved.
> - **Awareness (DC 14)** to work in the gloom of the tunnel without being too badly hindered by the inconsistent lighting, constantly moving shadows, and endlessly churning dust.
> - **Stealth (DC 14)** to navigate the most precarious parts of the collapse, surveying the rubble without causing unwanted collapses or getting hurt in the process. This helps plan out excavation for the coming days.
> - **Wilderness (DC 14)** to help identify and harvest lumber and vines that can be used to make the work easier.
> - **Medicine (DC 14)** to keep injuries to a minimum, manage the health of the workers, and patch up any minor wounds that do occur.
> - **Science (DC 14)** to identify load-bearing rubble and avoid dangerous hazards in the work which could cause undue injury and delays if missed.
> - **Performance (DC 14)** to bolster and inspire the workers, raising their spirits and hopes so that they can tackle the work ahead with gusto.

Unless the party finds an alternate route around the rubble, the Strayhearth Caravan will stay put here until the rubble has been cleared.

### Better Late Than Never

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Taking the Time
>
> If the party chooses to continue on this path and clear the rubble directly, then advance the clock as they work and rest.
>
> The expected rate of time passage should allow the party to break through the blockage in the afternoon of Day 5, allowing them to arrive in Helkas by evening. If the party succeeds on enough of its checks to cut down the work time, they might be able to clear the blockage fast enough to make an early arrival, but this isn't guaranteed.
>
> #### Region Map Transition
>
> At this moment, mark the **Rubble Cleared** outcome of this Event complete to trigger a transition on the Region Map and remove the rubble blocking the caravan's path.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Over the next several days, the grueling task of clearing the passage unfolds. The entire ordeal is marked with darkness, sweat, and dust, each breath tasting of stone and grit.
>
> Agraband works right alongside them, his smile never waning, his presence a comforting beacon in the chaos. The sounds of labor echo through the tunnel as the caravan members toil tirelessly, their voices occasionally breaking into song or laughter to keep spirits high.
>
> As the final chunks of rubble are hauled away and the wagons are able to clear the tunnel, a cheer rises up, reverberating through the newly opened passage. The exhausted faces of the caravan members glow with relief and triumph; their collective effort has paid off.
>
> Finally, with the path to Helkas open once more, the exhausted caravan begins to move. Wagons creak, wheels crunching over loose stones as the group slowly resumes its journey, albeit well behind schedule, and likely to miss the bulk of the festival.

#### Mayis Attunement: Clearing the Rubble

If the party chooses to stay with the Strayhearth Caravan to help clear the rubble, each character in the party advances their **Attunement: Mayis (+1)**.
