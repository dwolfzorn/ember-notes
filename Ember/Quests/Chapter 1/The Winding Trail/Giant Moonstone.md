# Giant Moonstone

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration and Social Event occurs as the Strayhearth Caravan approaches a mysterious obelisk: the [[Giant's Moonstone]]. By studying the ancient object alongside their NPC companions, the party can:
>
> - Learn more about the mysterious [[Shent]], an ancient civilization of giants.
> - Learn an ancient inscription which provides an important clue: "the fate of the moons." This inscription will prove useful later in the [[An Ancient Door]] Event, which occurs in the Side Quest [[Over The Moon]].
> - Receive a blessing of [[Mayis]].
> - Be introduced to Lyla and Clipper, companions in the [[Strayhearth Caravan]].
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Shent Moonstone" Level of the [[Vista: Verdant Paths]] Vista.
>
> #### Meeting Companions
>
> It is intended that the party spend some time during this Event meeting [[Lyla Cevher]] and [[Clipper]] if they have not already done so.

### Approaching The Obelisk

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Agraband addresses you, embracing the role of tour guide:
>
> > Behold, a moonstone! The Anachraenum historians believe these pillars were each attuned to a certain lunar cycle — and they still respond to the cycle of that moon to this day. What function they performed for their ancient builders, however, is knowledge lost to time.
>
> Lyla approaches the stone, walking around it. She is quickly joined by the Signborn who does the same, albeit walking the opposite direction. Narrowly avoiding collision, the pair stop and seem to notice each other for the first time. Lyla says:
>
> > Clipper, didn't take you for a fan of old rocks.
>
> The Signborn offers a cheeky grin of pointed teeth, and shoots back:
>
> > Same for you!
>
> Lyla immediately turns her attention back to the stone and says:
>
> > Actually, I studied ancient stonework as part of my training with House Cevher, but nothing we studied was ever in such good shape. It's amazing, really. The writing is in incredible condition considering the age and how exposed it is.
>
> Lyla gestures to the deeply cut symbols and runes in the stone. She and Clipper busy themselves poring over the moonstone, and lapse into contemplative chatter about the various aspects of it.

If the party would like, they can engage Clipper and Lyla in conversation, or examine the stones directly. Lyla and Clipper's topics of conversation are provided below, and they are more than happy to assist with the examination, especially if the party lacks the skills to glean anything useful from the ruin.

### Meeting Lyla

> [!abstract] Lyla Cevher
> **[[Lyla Cevher]]**
>
> Level 2 · Human Cevher Heiress
>
> ![Lyla Cevher](../../../assets/image097.webp)
>
> A Human who is sharply dressed in a beautiful and richly decorated coat that proudly displays her wealthy background. It's clear from an initial glance at her overall bearing and clothing style that she is from the city of Ordain itself and while she holds herself with a confident air, she is also friendly and welcoming with a slight smile and small laughter lines appearing around her eyes.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Music: Lyla's Theme
>
> When the party first interacts with Lyla, play her musical theme:  **Music: Lyla Theme**

> [!info] Social
> #### The Trading Heiress
>
> Lyla Cevher, heir to House Cevher, is clearly the wealthiest person in the caravan, judging by her clothing and mannerisms. Characters with the **Culture: Ordani** or **Knowledge: Trade** recognize Lyla as a member of [[House Cevher]] based on the colors and icons of her clothing. If they ask about her background, she'll confirm that she's not only a member of the trading house but also the daughter of Ralton Cevher, the head of the house.
>
> Lyla explains that she is returning to Ordain to attend to family matters. What she does not immediately reveal is that her father recently died, and she needs to be there to support her family and brother and to take over leadership of the Trading House.
>
> Any character that makes a successful **Awareness (DC 15)**check reveals that she's deeply preoccupied with a thinly veiled sadness, and that there is quite a bit more going on than she's revealed. It seems reasonable, however, that she would not be perfectly candid with new acquaintances.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your story?
>
> **A:**
>
> A small smile flickers across the young woman's face.
>
> > For once, my reputation fails to precede me. My name is Lyla, Lyla Cevher, and I'm a member of House Cevher. The name "Cevher" often carries with it a lot of preconceptions — key among them, people seem to think I'm either a pampered princess or a rugged miner with hands covered in rock dust, while the truth lies somewhere in between. And while I can certainly handle myself among the politicians of Ordain, I honestly prefer the feeling of a sword or a tool in my hands.
>
> She quickly taps her rapier guard at her hip, and her smile softens slightly.
>
> > In all likelihood, I'll be trying to outrun my reputation for the rest of my days. No matter what my skills or personal desires might be…

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** House Cevher?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Ordain has several major Trading Houses, each with its own area of expertise. House Cevher, which includes the majority of my family members as its leaders, and its many contracted workers, is focused on the extraction and distribution of rare earth materials — such as metals and stone. We're a mining house basically, and we have a long history tied not only to Ordain but also with the Arcturians of the Arctus Plateau.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your travel?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Like you, I'm headed eastward from Casla-Brava. I was staying with my great aunt Jeramia, helping expand Cevher's mining and trade operations and learning the ropes of administration and management. I had been living in the city for about four years, but … I have to return to Ordain. Strayhearth was the first caravan I found with an empty seat. And here we are.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Business in Ordain?
>
> **A:**
>
> Her softness disappears in an instant.
>
> > Important Cevher family business needs my attention back home.
>
> It's apparent that Lyla doesn't wish to discuss these matters any further.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Music: Shent Ruins
>
> Once the party's main interaction with Lyla is over, return to the default music for this scene:  **Music: Shent Ruins**

### Meeting Clipper

> [!abstract] Clipper
> **[[Clipper]]**
>
> Level 2 · Signborn Trader
>
> ![Clipper](../../../assets/image098.webp)
>
> You hear the sound of jingling bells as a purple-skinned Signaran with a slight build draws your attention. Crowned in a feathered mop of cerulean- and lavender-streaked hair, this young merchant's bright cobalt eyes and wide, sharp-toothed smile reveal an unmistakable affability.
>
> Their clothing suggests origins the world over; hints of Maziran style clash with various accents of places from Old Carinth to Ordain. A sash of hand-written parchment coupons hangs around their neck, and a string of curious, multi-colored stones adorns their waist. Tassels and coins also hang from their ostentatious garments, and two tiny bells chime and jingle upon each ankle.

> [!info] Social
> #### The Energetic Merchant
>
> Clipper is a goodhearted, curious, enterprising, and enthusiastic [[Maziran]] aspiring to mercantile greatness. If asked about why she's in the caravan and where she's headed, she shares a simple portion of her personal story, as presented below.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 13)**check understands that all the chaotic energy and endless inquisitiveness that Clipper exudes is absolutely genuine and not for show. Behind all this is a thread of earnest desire to succeed which she tries not to let show too much, possibly to avoid seeming too serious.
>
> #### Goods for Trade
>
> Clipper has a wide variety of wares available to trade. They can provide any common or uncommon items which are Mundane and no greater than Fine quality, subject to Gamemaster approval. Clipper will offer items at their standard list price. If the party has impressed Clipper by proving themselves heroes of Helkas during the [[Dusktide Rising]] Event, Clipper will provide wares at a modest discount.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your story?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Me? I'm just a not-so-simple Maziran merchant, quite a long way from home. Looking for a new place to **call** home, I suppose.
>
> The merchant's spectral Signborn arms begin juggling a small array of trinkets and baubles as they continue.
>
> > I like to say I'm a "purveyor of the things peculiar" and a "collector of odd curiosities." Sometimes, I even find it hard to part with my own inventory, as eclectic and interesting as it 'tis.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your travel?
>
> **A:**
>
> > As you can see, I'm currently on the road with Agraband. He's my mentor, you know! And my best friend. Friend-tor? Anyway, he's teaching me all sorts of things that are, and I quote: "vital for a traveling merchant to know."
>
> The Signborn merchant smiles, charmed by their own subtle impersonation of the old caravanner.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What are you learning?
>
> **A:**
>
> Clipper's eyes grow wide with excitement.
>
> > It might be easier to describe what I'm **not** learning, to be honest. It's so much. But I'm not complaining, of course! This has been the journey of a lifetime, so far, and I've already picked up more first-hand experience than I ever thought possible. Well, maybe not ever … magic, lore, economics — you name it, the captain is **more** than eager to talk about it.
> >
> > One of these days, I'll have my own enterprise like Strayhearth! And eventually, when I'm old and rickety like Aggie over there …
>
> They nod to the wizened bard nearby, who playfully nods back.
>
> > … I'll settle down and open up my own shop. A righteously awesome place, full of every curiosity under the sun. Until then, the road life is the only life for me. You gotta love all the stories we come across. And I've managed to sell my wares pretty successfully so far.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What do you sell?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I have quite a few interesting things in stock at the moment, now that you ask! On top of your standard tools and equipment, I have a small selection of peculiar trinkets and curios. Annnd, if you're thirsty …
>
> The merchant leans in for a whisper.
>
> > … I have a couple of potions and spirits that can heal your wounds or steel your resolve.

### Examining The Obelisk

The ancient menhir is a Shent moonstone, a pillar powered by a specific lunar cycle that was once used to monitor the area and communicate with other moonstones nearby. Curious party members may wish to discover who built the relic and learn of its purpose.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Inspecting the Area
>
> Any character who examines the menhir and makes a successful **Society (DC 13)** check recognize that the architectural style and weathered runes of this monument mark it as a relic of the Shent, an ancient culture of giants which collapsed after The Shattering. This moonstone must be at least 2,000 years old.
>
> - **Knowledge: Shent**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 12, Passive)** check notices that one face of the marker is sheltered by a nearby cliff and therefore markedly less weather-worn than others. Upon this face of the monolith are the trace remnants of a legible inscription which is described below.
>
> - **Knowledge: Weather**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> #### Deciphering Ancient Words
>
> The party may investigate or try to translate the remnant phrases engraved upon the menhir.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 12)** check recognizes the glyphs as an inscription in some ancient runic language.
>
> - **Language: Pathward**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Characters with adequate supplies like [[Scrivener's Toolkit]] may capture a rubbing of the inscription for later reference.
>
> #### Offered Assistance
>
> If the party fails to recognize the glyphs on the menhir, [[Clipper]] speaks up and cheerfully offers their assistance using their Glyphspeak inflection granted by their [[Signborn Lineage]].

When the characters decipher the ancient runes, read or paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The translated fragment from the ancient inscription reads:
>
> > Shadows end by spear restrained and sealed to never rise again. Let only those who understand the fate of the moons walk its path.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Phrase of Power
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 13)** finds it remarkable that the phrase "fate of the moons" is linguistically expressed in Pathward using a prominent runic glyph at the center of the inscription.
>
> - **Language: Pathward**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 15)** check recollects an ancient historical account following The Shattering that tells of the Shent doing battle against terrible Abyssal forces which destroyed much of their civilization. This inscription perhaps describes such a conflict.
>
> - **Knowledge: Legends**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.

Characters wanting to understand the magic in the moonstone can examine the runes and magical designs, learning more about its function. Unfortunately, this is one of the last surviving stones in the area, so not much can be done with it anymore.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Moonstone Magic
>
> Any character who examines the markings and magical runes and makes a successful **Arcana (DC 13)** check recognizes that this is a Shent moonstone: a pillar powered by a specific lunar cycle. The color of the crystal and symbols on the pillar make it clear that the moonstone was attuned to the water moon, Mayis. The knowledge of how to make these arcane lunar landmarks was lost with the Shent themselves ages ago.
>
> - **Knowledge: Shent**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The markings on the stone indicate it would have been able to communicate with other nearby moonstones. Additional designs in the runes indicate that the orbs also could have monitored the area around them, though what it could "see" is unclear.
>
> #### Out of Service
>
> Characters attempting to attune with, connect to, or utilize the moonstone to see how far the device reaches or if any other moonstones remain will be disappointed to find that it is the only one left in service in the area, and most of its functions have badly degraded due to its age. It is now little more than an unusual landmark upon the caravan route.

### Blessing of Mayis

Characters who understand that the Giant Moonstone is attuned to the water moon of Mayis may attempt to commune with the ancient Shent artifact. When a character touches the Moonstone, read or paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you lay your hand upon the ancient monument, the azure depths of the great crystalline orb begin to swirl within, reminding you of an aqueous, otherworldly maelstrom.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Moonstone's Blessing
>
> Any character who touches the Giant Moonstone can attempt to commune with the ancient artifact. Characters who commune this way while Mayis is present in the sky are empowered with additional temporary reserves of energy:
>
> You gain the benefits of an effect, **Patience of Mayis**, which grants the following benefits:
>
> - **If Mayis is Waxing or Waning**, you gain **+1 Maximum Focus**.
> - **If Mayis is Full**, you gain **+2 Maximum Focus**.
>
> *This effect will be provided in the Crucible system once Ember is upgraded to use Foundry Virtual Tabletop version 14.*

#### Mayis Attunement: Moonstone Communion

Any character who successfully communes with the Giant Moonstone advances their **Attunement: Mayis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### Concluding the Event

Once the party has finished examining the moonstone and socializing with the NPCs, they should get back on the caravan and get moving. Agraband is happy to stop, but doesn't want to lose too much time with delays.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> The Strayhearth Caravan will continue on its journey eastward through the Verdant Paths, winding its way northward until it reaches the [[Dry Outpost]]. Here, the caravan will camp for the night, triggering the [[Skies Above]] Event.
