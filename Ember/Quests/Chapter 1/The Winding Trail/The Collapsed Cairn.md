# The Collapsed Cairn

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event occurs as the Strayhearth Caravan approaches a ravine in the Forest of Stone — a battle between two large creatures causes the ravine to become blocked with rubble. By examining the battle's aftermath and consulting with their NPC companions, the party can:
>
> - Form a plan with [[Agraband Swift]] for how to proceed.
> - Venture forth independently of the [[Strayhearth Caravan]] to begin exploring the region.
> - Acquire their first [[Milestone Progression]] point.

### Battling Behemoths

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The caravan halts, entranced by the strange scene unfolding above.
>
> The two huge creatures continue their mortal struggle for a time, slamming each other against the rock and fighting for purchase against the slick stone. With a colossal heave, the bird-like creature slams down upon the draconic one and both crash into the foundation of the tower.
>
> In a bid to escape, the dragon weaves around the tower — but not fast enough. The great one-eyed bird fires a beam of golden-red light from its singular eye, directing it right into the dragon's face. Dazed, the dragon is helpless to the final attack of the bird, which slams it against the tower with such force that the whole thing breaks free of its base and begins to topple.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Region Map Transition
>
> At this moment, mark the **Spire Collapsed** outcome of this Event as complete to trigger a transition on the Region Map and a sound effect audible for all players.

`[[/outcome collapsed]]`

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The crashing of rock assaults your ears, and for a time you lose visibility of what is before you, but as the dust clears, the strange pale drake takes flight overhead and soars off into the eastern sky. Of the other winged creature, no sign remains.
>
> As the caravan takes stock of its surroundings, you are dismayed to realize that the natural tunnel beneath the rock archway before you is now entirely blocked by fallen rubble.
>
> It appears that the tower struck the archway during its collapse and careened over the far side of it. Great slabs of weathered stone and sections of the cliff face now fill the underpass, blocking all but the faintest glimmer of light from the far side and undeniably halting your progress.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Embattled Monstrosities
>
> The party may quickly consider what they know about the two creatures whose battle just caused this catastrophic landslide.
>
> A character who makes a successful **Medicine (DC 13)** check knows that the feathered, one-eyed creature was a fully-grown [[Suarrok]] — a deadly predator known for the potent alchemical properties of its singular monstrous eye.
>
> - **Knowledge: Dragons**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Monsters**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character deduces that the reptilian creature that survived the battle was likely a [[Pallid Drake]] — a vicious kind of lesser dragon known to stalk and kill any creature large enough to serve as a live meal.

### Surveying the Damage

The party can examine the blockage to get a sense of how bad it is and how much work it'll take to clear or bypass. At first glance, without any checks, the passage is completely blocked, and the approach into the natural tunnel — while wide enough for a caravan — is hemmed in by small rivers on either side, which narrow the approach into the mouth of the passage.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Rock and a Hard Place
>
> It is clear to everyone that while the collapse can be cleared by hand, it will be slow and difficult. All of the work will be done in a dark, narrow cave: the air will be choked with dust, the light will be scant, and the space insufficient to move debris.
>
> The caravan doesn't have any particularly specialized tools which would make clearing the debris easier. The caravan can utilize the yarnac to move some of the heaviest slabs, but most of the debris will have to be arduously cleared by hand. If mentioned to Agraband, he says:
>
> > Yes, I noted that as well. I feel foolish for not having more practical equipment that could deal with a situation like this. We'll just have to make do with what we have now.
>
> #### Getting Through
>
> A character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 14, Passive)** check recognizes that attempting to climb over the rubble to get to the other side is going to be exceptionally difficult. If the wrong footholds and handholds are used, causing the debris to unexpectedly shift, it could become fatal. The only way to mitigate the danger is to spend hours carefully clearing the rubble.
>
> - **Knowledge: Subterranea**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> If the party considers beginning that work, Lyla chimes in, saying:
>
> > I really don't recommend that. A fresh boulder fall like this is likely to shift on you suddenly. Clearing it will need careful planning and a lot of heavy lifting, especially since we don't have the proper tools. We do have the yarnac, but rigging them to clear this would take more time than we have.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Precarious Rubble
>
> The rubble is impassable as it stands. If the party attempts to circumvent the blockage by climbing, burrowing, or digging through, Ankarist quickly shouts a warning to deter them from this foolish course.
>
> > Don't be reckless. We are not in such a rush that it's worth being crushed to death.
>
> Characters who ignore this warning and attempt to traverse the rubble must succeed on three successive **Athletics (DC 25)** skill checks. Upon each failure, the character may suffer damage as **Shifting Rocks (Hazard 12, Fortitude, Health, Bludgeoning)** threaten to pin them in place.
>
> It is possible to turn back at any point and return to safety without any further checks. Any character who is killed or incapacitated by this damage can be extracted from the rubble at the expense of 1 hour of rescue time.
>
> #### A Tight Squeeze
>
> A Tiny or incorporeal creature may pass to the other side of the rubble unimpeded.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### **Digging Through**
>
> If the characters choose to stay and help clear the rubble, they can, though this will result in them missing the Side Quests in the region which are intended for Level 1 parties. If the players are on the fence, we suggest you encourage them to scout. If they speak to Agraband about it, there is 10gold pieces on offer for a scouting team willing to look for an alternate route.
>
> If the party is determined to clear the rubble rather than scouting, mark this Event as complete and advance time by 1 hour to trigger the [[Unknown]] Event.

### Finding Alternate Paths

Agraband curses his luck, but after assessing the situation, he is confident that the group can clear the rubble over the course of several days. However, that amount of time runs the risk of missing the Dusktide Festival at Helkas.

As the realization of what's happened makes its way through the caravan, more and more people begin to flock to Agraband to find out what's happening next — if he has a plan, if they'll be late, and so forth. Characters looking for updates or hoping to help will find their chance here by speaking with Agraband and the others in the caravan.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Frustrated Caravanner
>
> Agraband intends to keep the caravan parked where it is and put everyone to work clearing the rubble. Yes, he knows it may make them late, but it is the safer option. He laments that the collapse happened where it did, because this entrance is so narrow and difficult to work with.
>
> Agraband explains:
>
> > Honestly, I don't know much about the rest of the region. I've been down this route more than once and know that the canyon opens up substantially on the other side of the collapse. So if we can get people to the other side — you, namely — that would make clearing things easier.
> >
> > If you also clear a route there, I can send additional people to help, rather than cramming everyone into the collapse on this side.
> >
> > So, what do you say? I'll hire you on as scouts for Strayhearth. A nice 100 gold pieces for you to split if you agree to go look for alternate routes to the other side of the collapse!

If the party would like to get the opinions and guidance of other characters, both Lyla and Clipper may have useful leads that could help direct the party.

> [!info] Social
> #### Getting Some Help
>
> [[Lyla Cevher]] and [[Clipper]] have been listening to the conversation nearby. They each chime in with the following pieces of information:
>
> Lyla mentions an active mine in the area:
>
> > If I recall correctly, House Cevher has a small stake in a mine to the south, part of a village called "Yakoshta." I've never been there myself, but — according to the most recent shipping reports — it should be active. If you can track them down, the folks at Yakoshta Mine might have tools or blasting materials we could use. Worth a try.
>
> Clipper says they've heard about an expedition from the Anachraenum:
>
> > Last time I was in Ordain, I sold a few trinkets to a group of Anachraenum hopefuls. They were scheming up an expedition in the coming months to the northern reaches of the Forest of Stone, in search of ancient ruins. Maybe they're in the area and can lend us a hand! And maybe — just maybe — you can find some giant-sized treasure out there while you're looking!
>
> Ankarist counts himself out:
>
> > The caravan needs organization and strong hands. I can provide them with both. Besides, I'm not one for wilderness survival and bushcraft. I'd simply slow you down. There's plenty to do here.
>
> Sin chimes in about her immediate goals:
>
> > A few folks were injured by stray rocks and debris during the collapse. I'm going to make sure the people and animals here are tended to. We'll need both in good health to clear the rubble quickly and make it to Helkas on time.
>
> Agraband is heartened to hear this, and says:
>
> > This is a fine start! We'll get to work on clearing the rubble, while our scouts investigate these leads and perhaps locate some assistance. If we're lucky, we'll make it to Helkas just in time for Dusktide!

### Concluding the Event

If the party agrees to scout, Agraband and Clipper discuss and agree to provide the party with one half of a [[Messenger Coin]] which allows them to remain in occasional contact.

Agraband says that he will check in with the party at the end of each day, but encourages the party to check in whenever they have anything to report.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Matters of Punctuality
>
> This Event marks the point that the party becomes free to travel the surrounding area. While they can take however much time they want, their ultimate goal is to get to the other side of the blockage and get the caravan moving to Helkas. If the party takes too long, they will risk missing the Dusktide Festival.
>
> The party should be made aware of this timing, as Agraband was careful to plan his route to arrive early. This means there's a fair bit of time to find a route to the other side of the blockage and get the caravan moving.
>
> - The party will be **early** for the festival if they arrive before 4:00pm on Day 5.
> - The party will be **on time** for the festival if they arrive on or after 4:00pm on Day 5 but before 12:00am on Day 6.
> - The party will be **late** for the festival if they arrive after 12:00am on Day 6.
>
> #### Milestone
>
> Upon completion of this Event, the party gains their first Milestone Point. This is not sufficient for them to reach Level 2 yet, which requires them to earn 2 Milestone Points, but this is a good moment to communicate to the players what [[Milestone Progression]] is and to reinforce the mechanics for how leveling up works in *Ember*.
>
> #### Next Steps
>
> Upon completing this Event, the [[Party]] gains their own active group token on the Region Map, which allows the players to move independently of the Strayhearth Caravan.
>
> It is intended for the characters to accept Agraband's proposal to scout for an alternate route. If they do this, they will need to backtrack and explore the other routes in the Verdant Pathways. Each of the two alternate paths has a Side Quest associated with it:
>
> - The southern route will lead to the [[Ooze Control]] Side Quest, which will task the party with aiding the helpless people of Yakoshta Mine by fighting a cohort of frenzied, mutated oozes (and their misguided master).
> - The northern route will lead to the [[Over The Moon]] Side Quest, which will allow the party to investigate the ruins of the ancient [[Shent]] giants and fight a lingering Abyssal evil (among other local threats).
>
> As noted above in [[The Collapsed Cairn]], the party can also choose to stay and clear the rubble. If they choose to do this, mark this Event as complete and advance time by 1 hour to trigger the [[Clearing the Rubble]] Event.
