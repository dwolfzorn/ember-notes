# Leavetakings

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs on the road east of Helkas where the party has traveled alongside their Strayhearth companions of Ankarist, Lyla, and Sin. If following the road, this Event will occur specifically when reaching hex s.3208.2880. If departing from the trail, this Event will occur whenever the party next takes a **Rest**.
>
> The time has come for the three friends to now go their own ways and the party must make a pivotal choice about which companion, if any, to accompany further. In this Event, the party can:
>
> - Enjoy a final meal with the friends before parting ways.
> - Learn of Ankarist's plan to seek out the legendary Mythspire Observatory.
> - Learn of Lyla's plan to visit the Shard God Sigil in search of knowledge.
> - Learn of Sin's plan to journey to the Cindaric enclave of Corpin Sanctuary.
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Crossroads - Camp" Level of the [[Vista: Golden Flats]] Vista.
>
> #### Exclusive Choices
>
> It is intended that groups playing Ember do not experience *all of the content* the game has to offer. This is the first moment in the game where the players must choose one plotline to explore in detail at the expense of others. Ultimately, the party must make a final choice about which path to follow. This decision will dictate the majority of gameplay they experience in Chapter 2.

### Before Parting

The party stops to rest, taking a moment to share a brief meal and exchange farewells as their paths diverge. The party and their companions take some time to reminisce and solidify their newfound friendship before parting ways.

> [!info] Social
> #### Ankarist's Test of Wit
>
> Ankarist tests the party's wit with a series of riddles.
>
> Any character who listens to Ankarist's riddles and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14, Passive)** check can answer each of them correctly, impressing the drakon.
>
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Path: Academy Dropout**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who counter-challenges Ankarist with a riddle of their own and makes a successful **Deception (DC 18)** check stumps the drakon.
>
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Path: Academy Dropout**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> #### Lyla's Test of Taste
>
> Lyla asks everyone about their favorite foods. She talks about a few of the best dishes she’s had during her travels and how they differ from what’s served at her favorite restaurants in Ordain.
>
> Any character who speaks with Lyla and makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14, Passive)** check can tell that Lyla is subtly attempting to assess both how adventurous they are and how familiar they are with Ordain.
>
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Lyla loves the spicy fish stew found in Storsa’s Strand, which she thinks is more flavorful than the Ordain variety, and wonders what might be in their famous recipe. Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 15, Passive)** check can impress Lyla by guessing at the ingredients; later, they can work with her to try to recreate some of the taste by adding spice to the current night's meal.
>
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Lyla is looking forward to a dish of Fried Kintavi Leaves at the Ordani restaurant Shine, which she greatly missed while away. Characters with **Culture: Ordani** may know the savory appetizer or the restaurant; alternatively, they can share their own Ordani favorites, creating a stronger connection with her.
>
> #### Sin's Test of Nature
>
> Sin shows off her animal impersonation skills using,**Talent: Wildspeaker**, challenging the party to identify birds as she imitates their calls. She starts with easily recognizable calls but then tests the party with a range of obscure avian sounds from the depths of the Corebright Forest.
>
> Any character who listens to Sin and makes a successfula **Wilderness (DC 17)** check check are able to identify most of the birds that Sin imitates, impressing her greatly.
>
> - **Knowledge: Beasts**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Path: Primalist Disciple**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Characters who match Sin's own spellcraft with their own usage of **Talent: Wildspeaker** can carry on a full conversation with Sin, crafting a duet of birdsongs that resonate across the plains.

Resolved to their separate paths, each of the Strayhearth companions makes a final appeal to the party in the hopes that they will remain in their company.

### Ankarist's Investigation

> [!abstract] Ankarist
> **[[Ankarist]]**
>
> Level 2 · Drakon Veiled Chain Investigator
>
> ![Ankarist](../../../assets/image096.webp)
>
> You observe a stern Drakon warrior with a determined expression and piercing golden eyes. Clad in leather armor reinforced with steel, his martial prowess is immediately apparent in the way he handles the hefty greatsword at his side — a hulking blade with a wide, flared tip. This two-handed brand is obviously venerated by the Drakon, who regards the blade with marked discipline. A cloak pin on his breast bears the symbol of the Veiled Chain, the city of Ordain's noble protectorate.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Music: Ankarist's Theme
>
> Whilst conversing with Ankarist about his next steps, play  **Music: Ankarist Theme**.

Ankarist presents the group with a summary of the evidence he has collected about the origin of the drakes that attacked Helkas.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > The creatures are biologically draconic, without question, and their physiology is most similar to that of Vile Dragons, which are especially despicable creatures. Their blinded eyes and pale skin suggest that they grew up in lightless environments, such as caves or in the Pathways. However, it might also be an effect of whatever is making them ill.
> >
> > My superiors in the Veiled Chain are already tracking several incidents regarding sightings of similar creatures emerging from the cavernous Pathways in the Plateau, which reinforces the theory that they originated somewhere beneath the Arctus Plateau.
> >
> > The closest entrance to the Pathways that I am certain of is nowhere near where we are now — the creatures must have come from somewhere else. I have a contact nearby in the Rustvar Valleys hamlet of Skybrush, a Kivahr named Liestra Grann. She deals in antiquities and is knowledgeable about all manner of ruins, tombs, and caverns in the area.

The party can consider the evidence Ankarist has presented.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Considering the Evidence
>
> Any character who considers Ankarist's evidence and makes a successful **Medicine (DC 14)** or **Wilderness (DC 14)** check can reasonably conclude that Ankarist's hypothesis regarding the origin of the drakes seems reasonable.
>
> - **Knowledge: Dragons**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Subterranea**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Any character who recalls information about the Pathways and make a successful **Wilderness (DC 15)** check concludes that it's likely there are other places nearby that would allow the drakes to exit the Pathways, and would lead to their den or nest.
>
> - **Knowledge: Tracking**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Any character who considers the most likely place to find an entrance to the Pathways and makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 16)** check recalls that the Wedgelands to the north are a natural place to find a fissure or passage that could reach down into the Pathways.

Ankarist urges the party to accompany him on this quest:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Ankarist gazes at the horizon to the north. In the distance you see the dramatic peaks of the Wedgelands protruding out from the plains like spiked teeth against the sky.
>
> > No doubt it will be a long, tough trip, and I'd be glad to have some allies at my back. These creatures are dangerous and I'm not relishing the idea of going into the Pathways alone. There's plenty more than drakes to worry about there. I could use the backup.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your story?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I spent my youth in the Tayan Empire, getting mired in politics and scheming. Ultimately that life was not for me, and I left in search of something different. I found a different calling and a different purpose that led me to Ordain, where I have lived now for many years. It turned out that the Veiled Chain had need of my particular mixture of skills and I found myself well suited to the work.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Veiled Chain?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The Veiled Chain is one of the branches of government in Ordain. It's the investigative arm responsible for resolving some of the most severe issues that the city faces. We get involved in cases where community enforcement of laws fails or is incapable of handling the situation.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your interest in dragons?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Enough to know they are bad news. I saw the carnage they are capable of firsthand during my time in the Tayan Empire. I'm not sure which are more dangerous, wild ones or those bound in service to the Empress. In either case, wherever dragons appear, bloodshed swiftly follows.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About your magic?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I suppose you could say I have a knack for sorcery, and I've picked up a few tricks here and there. Mostly things to help with my work. Most non-magical crime can be solved by the Ordani community without getting the Veiled Chain involved, but when there's a dangerous spellcaster in play it's important to understand a bit of arcana. I've put one or two outlaw mages in Spellbreaker Tower myself.

> [!info] Social
> #### Ankarist's Power
>
> Any character with **Deception (DC 18, Passive)** senses that Ankarist is not being entirely forthcoming about the nature of his arcane capabilities.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The true source of your power?
>
> **A:**
>
> Ankarist scowls and his expression darkens slightly.
>
> > I suppose my explanation didn't fully satisfy you.
>
> He shakes his head wistfully before a vaguely approving smile softens his expression.
>
> > You show the instincts of an investigator!
> >
> > This is a private matter, and it's nothing that need concern you or that will affect our time together. I will share with you, however, that I have a patron who has been valuable in cultivating my capabilities. I'll speak no more of it now, but perhaps if we journey together I will have opportunity to confide in you further.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Main Quest Themes
>
> If the party joins Ankarist, they will embark upon the Main Quest [[Ancient Paths]], which will take them north to Skybrush, Mythspire Observatory, and the subterranean Pathways below. This Quest is a great fit for groups who enjoy dungeon exploration and combat against formidable adversaries.

`[[/outcome ankarist]]`

### Lyla's Mentor

> [!abstract] Lyla Cevher
> **[[Lyla Cevher]]**
>
> Level 2 · Human Cevher Heiress
>
> ![Lyla Cevher](../../../assets/image097.webp)
>
> A Human who is sharply dressed in a beautiful and richly decorated coat that proudly displays her wealthy background. It's clear from an initial glance at her overall bearing and clothing style that she is from the city of Ordain itself and while she holds herself with a confident air, she is also friendly and welcoming with a slight smile and small laughter lines appearing around her eyes.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Music: Lyla's Theme
>
> Whilst conversing with Lyla about her next steps, play  **Music: Lyla Theme**.

Lyla entreats the party to join her as she investigates the bandits. She's convinced that there's something bigger at work and would welcome the help to figure it out.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > I just don't understand. Why attack Helkas? Why the Dusktide Festival? Right now the only thing I know for certain is that this isn't some isolated group of brigands attacking whatever they see. Something more sinister is at play.
> >
> > I know we haven't been traveling together that long, but I've seen the way you handle yourself, and I'm about to put myself right in the middle of something. I'd love for you to come with me and help me figure things out. When we get to Ordain, I'll compensate you for your trouble.
> >
> > Luckily, we have a few leads to work with, and I know where to go to sort them all out. Oldcraft Lodge. Sigil — yes, that Sigil, the god of books — knows more about what's happening in the Arctus Plateau than anyone else I know. He's a dear friend and was always kind to me in the past, almost like a mentor, and we could all use some direction right now.

Lyla continues to share several clues that she gleaned while investigating the bodies at Helkas.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Coded Instructions
>
> Several of the bandits were carrying instructions written in code, but something about the ink they were written in makes it nearly impossible to break. If decoded, the notes could lead to the bandits' leader.
>
> > This was a coordinated attack. The bandits had to know what to do, where to go, and when to strike. These notes they were carrying probably have those instructions, but I can't break the code — probably something to do with this odd ink they're written in. Sigil may well know more about that — his whole expertise as a Shard God is things that are written down.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 14)** check knows that the ink used is probably shifting ink, a type of magical ink used by some thieves and spies to make codes harder to decipher. Codes written in shifting ink usually require something special to be done in order to decode them, which only those who carry the coded message know.
>
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Language: Thieves' Cant**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> #### The Leather Markings
>
> The bandits' leather gear had distinctive markings of a tower with a thick rope wrapped around it that likely point back to whoever crafted it. If Lyla can figure out whose mark it is, they could find the bandits' supplier.
>
> > I made a sketch of the markings on the bandits' gear. Most of them had the markings on them, and the leather was expensive. Someone is supplying it. I'm sure we can figure out who.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Trade** or **Knowledge: Crafts** know that the markings indicate that the person who crafted this leather is in a town on or near the Elenain River.
>
> #### The Strange Saying
>
> Several of the bandits had tattoos on them of a stylized gold coin with Arcden script that reads, "For Other Fortunes." It's possible that the saying has an origin or significance that could lead to their base of operations.
>
> > Then there are these tattoos that several of the bandits had. These strange gold coins. They used the same ink as what was in their notes, but this time the text was fully readable. "For Other Fortunes." I'm just not sure why it's significant. But don't worry — with Sigil's help, and yours, I know I can puzzle it out.
>
> The party may have already deciphered the origin of the phrase while examining the bandit corpse in the [[Dusktide Rising]] or [[Dusktide Destruction]] Events. However, its significance won't become clear until later.

> [!info] Social
> #### Lyla's Motives
>
> The party knows that Lyla is returning to Ordain because of her father's recent death, however they might suspect there is more to the story.
>
> 1. Rumors of attacks in the area have spread through the caravan, but this is more organized than any she has seen before.
> 2. The odd drakes are concerning, but she plans to focus on the bandits.
> 3. She's been away from the Arctus Plateau trying to learn new skills to modernize House Cevher's business practices and to boost her own confidence.
> 4. She has returned to the Arctus Plateau on her way to Ordain because of her father's recent death.
>
> Any character who considers Lyla's motives and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)**check knows that there is something else about her return to Ordain that is on Lyla's mind.
>
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Any character who presses Lyla for more information and makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check persuades her to share what is weighing on her.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why do you care?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I know I've been away for a few years in Casla-Brava, but an attack on a town so closely associated with my own house? It should be unthinkable — it's in everyone's best interests to keep trade flowing. If someone thinks they can target a place so closely tied to House Cevher, I need to know who they are and why.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What's on your mind?
>
> **A:**
>
> > To be honest… I'm heartbroken over my father's death. I should have been there with him these last few years. I suppose it doesn't matter now, though, and it's my responsibility to be the future of the House. But I can't shake the feeling that if I were home in Ordain, I could have stopped this from happening.
> >
> > The least I can do now is figure things out and show my father that my time away was worth it. All I can do now is honor what he cared about most — House Cevher's success.

> [!info] Social
> #### Lyla and Sigil
>
> If parties want to know more about Sigil, or Lyla's relationship with Sigil, she's willing to share.
>
> - Sigil is known as the "god of books," a Shard God dedicated to knowledge who maintains a large arcane library at Oldcraft Lodge.
> - Lyla spent many days at Oldcraft Lodge in her youth when her father was attending to House Cevher business in the Plateau. Sigil looked after her and served as a mentor and teacher, as a favor to her father Ralton, though Lyla doesn't know why the two were friends.
> - Sigil is very welcoming but also sometimes very confusing. If the party can't follow everything he says, they are in good company.
>
> Any character who considers what they know of [[Sigil]] and makes a successful **Society (DC 16)** check recalls the basic reputation of the Shard God as one of scholastic learning, research, and knowledge.
>
> - **Knowledge: Gods**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Path: Shard God Devotee**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Any character who reads into Lyla's words and makes a successful**Deception (DC 14)** check intuits that Lyla is being honest about her relationship with Sigil and thinks of him fondly.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who is Sigil?
>
> **A:**
>
> > When I first met Sigil, I thought he was a little… crazy? The amount of information and knowledge that he's able to collect and study all at once is incredible, and it lets him make connections between things that seem unrelated in a way that no one else can. He's just a little difficult sometimes, and has an odd way of conversing with others. With any luck, he'll at least have a clue as to why these bandits struck Helkas.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Relationship with Sigil?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I spent long days throughout my childhood at Oldcraft Lodge; he was my teacher and mentor. It took me some time to understand him, but once I was able to keep up, he gave me a place to rest and a book to read. Trade in the Early Days of Ordain & the Fall of House Komric. That book led to another, then another, and another. He gradually became a mentor and a dear friend. I'm glad I'll get to see him again.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Sigil's help?
>
> **A:**
>
> > With so much information rattling around in his head, Sigil sometimes loses track of what he's saying or goes off on tangents, and his mind seems to whirl around and around. The important thing is not to let it fool you. In the end, he usually has exactly what you need, even if it takes him a little while to find it.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Main Quest Themes
>
> If the party decides to journey with Lyla, they will embark upon the Main Quest [[An Old Friend]], which will take them south to Oldcraft Lodge, Arcturel, Nain, and Seawall. This Quest is a great fit for groups who enjoy political intrigue and subterfuge.

`[[/outcome lyla]]`

### Sin's Pilgrimage

> [!abstract] Sin Marmot
> **[[Sin Marmot]]**
>
> Level 2 · Keth Cindaric Aspirant
>
> ![Sin Marmot](../../../assets/image099.webp)
>
> A Keth with a friendly demeanor and wide blue eyes and a strange half-mask that covers her mouth. She seems to view everything around her with an air of wondrous innocence but her keen glances also suggest the ability to read any given situation quickly and she may be more capable than she appears at first glance.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Music: Sin's Theme
>
> Whilst conversing with Sin about her next steps, play  **Music: Sin Theme**.

Sin Marmot is disturbed by the attack on the Dusktide Festival and the unsettling evidence of necromancy that surfaced in the aftermath. She appeals to the party to accompany her eastward to Corpin Sanctuary and Ordain, where she can deliver news about the events in Helkas to the Speaker at Cindarin Temple.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Sin Marmot looks tired, as though sleep has been elusive. However, she appears relieved to see you, and very obviously has something on her mind. After taking a moment to collect her thoughts, she speaks quietly.
>
> > Normally, when a person dies on Ember, their spirit travels to a plane of death called the Spiritlands, where they awaken to experience an afterlife. Spirits shouldn't be appearing outside the Spiritlands after death, and they certainly shouldn't be transforming into specters. It was like something snatched the spirit out of a body before it could move on naturally.
> >
> > I've traveled a long way to get here, and there are plenty of miles left in these little feet of mine. I'll get to Corpin Sanctuary and Ordain just fine on my own. All the same, though, I think that you should come with me. Wild creatures and unsavory people are matters of concern, to be sure, but this … this is something bigger. Something unnatural, and something truly wrong.
>
> Sin looks eastward with a troubled stare, and then smiles as if she has momentarily set her concerns to rest.
>
> > And to be honest, I would very much like to have some companions for the road. It's a big world out there, and it's best to have friends with whom to share the trail. You are still planning to go to Ordain, aren't you?

> [!info] Social
> #### Matters of Life and Death
>
> Sin is willing to discuss matters of Ember's soul cycle with the party in more detail.
>
> Any character who speaks with Sin and makes a successful **Society (DC 15)** check can confirm this effectively summarizes the understood cycle of life and death. Something subverting that natural order is absolutely cause for concern.
>
> **Culture: Lumek** characters know that their homeland is under direct siege by legions of the undead commanded by powerful beings that are able to raise and control them. The Lumek dedicate many resources and lives to fighting this expansion. The incident in Helkas could be a sign that necromancy is spreading, or a new source of undeath has appeared.
>
> Any character who considers this unnatural phenomenon and makes a successful **Arcana (DC 16)** check recalls that the only school of magic capable of capturing spirits or animating the dead is Necromancy. Such spells are not taught by reputable sources; Necromancy is openly reviled in most places, and often outright illegal. Furthermore, the ability to wield such magic takes great arcane prowess — or an immense source of external power.
>
> - **Knowledge: Souls**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Undeath**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character recalls that necromancy is Abyssal in nature, horribly corruptive — not only for the user, but to Ember itself. The magic is as close to innately evil as anything can be.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Undeath?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Normally it just doesn't happen, it's not the natural order of things. However, I asked around a bit, and apparently that's not the first time people have seen something like this happen, and it's even more common in other places in the region… so there's clearly something going on, and it's not at all good.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What happened?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We don't know much. A spirit emerging from a corpse, thrashing, wailing, then dissipating. It almost looked like a spirit being ripped out, or destroyed. I don't know what caused it or what the source was. That's all beyond me.
> >
> > From what I understand, which is not a lot, normal magic can't really do that. It takes special knowledge and forces to control the dead and manipulate souls, which makes this whole situation that much more concerning!

> [!info] Social
> #### Sin's Intentions
>
> Any character who reads into Sin's words and makes a successful **Deception (DC 14)** check senses that Sin is deeply concerned with the safety of Ember and its people. She believes with ironclad conviction that the Cindaric Sages can help, and that they will have answers for what is happening.
>
> **Culture: Strider** characters or those with **Knowledge: Souls** or **Knowledge: Undeath** can likely empathize with Sin's concern. Any part of the natural cycle being subverted or broken is dangerous; it can lead to the collapse of other systems, changes in the natural order which might not be correctable, and could result in disaster.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your beliefs?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I grew up in the Corebright, was inducted into the Primalists at a young age, and was one of the few that went beyond just communing with nature and actually harnessing the Growth, which is what they called the power flowing from the Core of the world. I was able to connect with the Corebright itself, and that left me with the sensation that there was some deeper connection I could forge, but the Primalists don't know how to do that. They don't focus on the Core, just how nature is affected by it.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why the Cindaric Sages?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I met a traveling Cindaric Sage who was visiting the Corebright to learn about how the Primalists viewed and connected with the world there. Naturally, he and I worked and traveled together often. He had endless questions for me, and I picked his brain on a daily basis.
> >
> > He often spoke of connection to the Heart of Ember itself, and how the Lifeforce flowed from it. This is basically identical to what the Primalists believe, but the Sages engaged in a tireless pursuit of connecting to and understanding the Heart of Ember. They go beyond just the world that grows around us, and focus on the source of it all.
> >
> > That's what I was after. Once it became clear I'd learned all I could from my friend, I set out for Ordain to see if the Cindaric Sages can teach me more about Ember and its Heart.

> [!info] Social
> #### Cindarics and Primalists
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Souls** understand what Sin is talking about and can contribute their own understanding of the subject. The concept of Lifeforce is nearly identical for the two faiths, but they focus on different aspects of it.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Main Quest Themes
>
> If the party decides to escort Sin, they embark upon the Main Quest [[Crumbling Sanctuary]], which will take them east to Corpin Sanctuary and the metropolis of Ordain beyond. This Quest is a great fit for heroic groups who want to do good, confront evil, or reach Ordain as swiftly as possible.

### Concluding the Event

Before completing this Event, the party must make a decision about which path to follow, marking one of the Event outcomes at the expense of the others.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Forging your own Path
>
> If the party chooses to forge their own path, there is plenty in the world for them to find, although they will miss out on some of the game's iconic gameplay and milestone progression. Accompanying one of the Strayhearth companions is encouraged.

`[[/outcome none]]`

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> The next steps after this Event depend on the choice the party has made. It is recommended, especially for groups playing *Ember* for the first time, to travel with one of the companions, as the narrative and experience will be richer.
>
> - With Ankarist, the party will begin Chapter 2 with the [[Ancient Paths]] Main Quest.
> - With Lyla, the party will begin Chapter 2 with the [[An Old Friend]] Main Quest.
> - With Sin, the party will begin Chapter 2 with the [[Crumbling Sanctuary]] Main Quest.
>
> If the party chose to travel separately, they will not experience these Main Quests, but there are numerous Chapter 2 Side Quests to encounter as well as opportunities to reunite with each companion at a later point.
>
> #### Milestone
>
> Completing this Event awards a **Milestone Point,** likely advancing the party to Level 3.
