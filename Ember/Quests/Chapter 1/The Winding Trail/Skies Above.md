# Skies Above

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs as the Strayhearth Caravan makes camp in an old homestead. By investigating the homestead and interacting with their NPC companions, the party can:
>
> - Learn some of Ember's lore by gazing at the [[Cosmos]] on a clear night.
> - Meet Sin Marmot, a [[Strider]] journeying to Ordain to join the [[Cindaric Sages]].
> - Explore the old outpost, perhaps finding some forgotten items.
>
> This Event using the "Dry Outpost - Caravan Camp" Level of the [[Vista: Verdant Paths]] Vista.
>
> #### Meeting Companions
>
> It is intended that the party spend some time during this Event meeting [[Sin Marmot]] if they have not already done so.

### Speaking With Sin

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As the quiet evening draws on, you are joined by another member of the caravan, a Keth woman with bright blue eyes and hair like charcoal.
>
> > Hello! Having a nice evening so far?
>
> She gestures to the sky above.
>
> > I'd invite you to stargaze a bit with me, but the clouds would need to clear first. But that doesn't mean we can't talk while we wait!

> [!abstract] Sin Marmot
> **[[Sin Marmot]]**
>
> Level 2 · Keth Cindaric Aspirant
>
> ![Sin Marmot](../../../assets/image099.webp)
>
> A Keth with a friendly demeanor and wide blue eyes and a strange half-mask that covers her mouth. She seems to view everything around her with an air of wondrous innocence but her keen glances also suggest the ability to read any given situation quickly and she may be more capable than she appears at first glance.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Music: Sin's Theme
>
> Whilst the party chats with Sin, play her musical theme:  **Music: Sin Theme**

> [!info] Social
> #### The Aspiring Cindaric
>
> Speaking to Sin, one quickly learns a lot about her: she's a druid from the wild and dangerous reaches of the [[Corebright Forest]], was born and raised among the **Culture: Strider** of the region, and began learning druidic magic at an early age.
>
> A successful **Awareness (DC 12)**check reveals that Sin is being straightforward and earnest. She is both excited and intimidated about this epic journey she's recently embarked upon, but is doing a relatively good job of keeping her nerves in check.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your story?
>
> **A:**
>
> > It's a pleasure to meet you! My name is Sin Marmot. I'm a practitioner of primal magic — a druid, if you will — from the Corebright Forest, far to the west. Among my people, the Striders, I'm one of the youngest to pick up the staff, so to speak.
>
> The young Keth casually adjusts the position of her gnarled wooden staff, and you notice the amber glow of a pyrefly within a tiny basket slung from the staff's crook.
>
> > My connection to Ember started at a remarkably young age — according to the elders — and it's always been something that's called to me. Not all of my people have this connection, and those that do often take decades to hone their talents. For me, I suppose my connection to Ember is a little bit stronger than most.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Connection to Ember?
>
> **A:**
>
> > It's a bit hard to describe with words, but for as long as I can remember, I've always felt some kind of bond with nature and the elements, something I can feel in my bones. I can hear sounds in the wilderness that others often can't, and I can almost see a current of spiritual energy in the world around us. To be honest, sometimes it feels like it's out of my control, as if Ember is speaking through me. I know it sounds kinda funny, but magic is weird like that. Basically, what started off as natural talent bloomed into something greater — something I don't always understand, but it fills me with a real sense of purpose.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your travel?
>
> **A:**
>
> > About a year ago, a Cindaric Sage from Ordain came to my homeland, and my druidic circle in Sirewood was assigned the task of keeping him company. I learned quite a bit from that Sage, and it didn't take long to realize I wanted to know more about the Cindaric religion. So, after a few months of preparation and some hard goodbyes with friends and loved ones, I set off for the Arctus Plateau. And here I am!
>
> Sin gazes eastward at the gloomy horizon, looking wistfully at the overcast clouds which mask the presence of the waxing moons.
>
> > I'm on my way to Corpin Sanctuary on Mial Mountain. Hopefully, they've got room for a wayward Strider like myself. I can't wait to learn more about the Heart of Ember and how the Cindaric Sages commune with it.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Cindaric Sage?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The Cindaric Sages are the Arctus Plateau's most prominent and esteemed spiritual organization. The founder of Ordain herself started the religion, which is based on a holistic relationship with the Heart of Ember. In many ways, the Cindarics are like the druids of the Corebright Forest. I still have a lot to learn about the Heart of Ember and the cosmos as a whole, and the Cindarics seem like the right folks to help me get there.

### Stargazing With Sin

As the evening wears on, the storm desists and the sky mercifully clears, providing a brilliant view of the heavens overhead as the clouds dissipate.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Sin's glittering eyes turn upward again to check on the darkened sky, as they have several times over the last little while. This time, though, Sin gestures upward with a flash of excitement.
>
> > There we are, clear at last. Look, the night sky is incredible, right? I never tire of it. The skies out here don't look that different from the skies that I grew up with, which is a comfort while being so far from home.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Stargazing
>
> This is an ideal moment to introduce the party to the [[The Cosmos]] map and discuss its major elements. We recommend you switch to it for some of this scene, especially while it is being discussed with Sin.

At this point, the moon [[Orbis]] is waxing. It will eventually become full on the eve of the Dusktide festival in Helkas, where the party is bound.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Lunar Influence
>
> **Attunement: Mayis** As your eyes settle on Mayis, you can feel the faint push and pull of its endless waves. Within a few heartbeats, the tides of the great water moon have begun to connect with you, and an invasive cold washes over your body — as if being submerged into the depths of the very ocean. The moment is fleeting, gone in the blink of an eye, but it stands out as one of the strongest connections to the world or its lunar bodies you've ever experienced.
>
> **Attunement: Ragen** As you stare at the roiling form of Ragen in the sky, you can feel a stinging sensation in your chest. You've felt it before, a sharp pain like a red-hot spark searing your skin. But this time, you don't flinch. As you observe the crimson glow of Ragen overhead, you feel a fiery warmth spread throughout your limbs. This burning sensation is pleasant but brief, and although that initial spark has subsided, a warmth lingers in your mortal flesh for a short time to follow.
>
> **Attunement: Orbis** Standing beneath the chaotic glow of Orbis, you can feel the pull of the waxing spirit moon in your mind and heart. As your eyes affix themselves to the moon's pale glow, the connection is immediate — a tingling sensation spreads throughout your limbs, and every varied emotion you've felt today washes over you, like the disturbed silt of a placid lake. Your mind roils with joy, excitement, agitation, curiosity, and many other emotions you can barely control. Just as it feels like you might lose yourself to it, this cosmic link grows taut and snaps like a bare strand of thread. As your connection to Orbis subsides, you notice your ears ringing, and your chest is tight with the fading sensations of furtive emotions.
>
> #### Lunar Lore
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Science (DC 13, Passive)** check knows that all the moons have a similar influence on the world and its people — some feel the pull more keenly than others, but most everyone feels it at one point or another.
>
> - **Knowledge: Cosmology**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character recalls that each moon is home to elemental beings, or failing that, beings that are made of or heavily linked to the energies of that moon. In the case of Ragen, it's fire elementals; for Mayis, water elementals; and for Orbis, chaos spirits. These beings can be encountered all over Ember, drawn to places that resonate with their moons, and become especially common when those moons are at their apex.
>
> Any character who succeeded on the previous check, further considers the elemental influence of moons, and makes a successful **Arcana (DC 15)** check understands that while lunar elementals are generally dangerous, they have a long history of being neutral to or sometimes even helpful to the people of Ember. Water elementals of Mayis have been known to share knowledge with ancient civilizations, and the legends say that the first settlers to the Arctus Plateau were aided by ancient fire sages of Ragen. Orbis' beings are less helpful, due to their deeply chaotic nature.
>
> - **Knowledge: Elementals**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.

### Investigating the Homestead

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> After a bit of quiet between you and Sin, the druid's attention drifts to your surroundings.
>
> > Have you rooted around the old dry outpost yet? You never know what sorts of things are lurking under old stones and floorboards. Well, aside from the bugs. There are always bugs. But sometimes there are other things…
>
> Sin pops up and walks toward the old, dilapidated building, waving you along.

Characters who investigate the outpost more thoroughly can find a few clues:

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Dilapidated Outpost
>
> Any character who investigates the outpost and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 13, Passive)** check notices evidence that another group has camped here fairly recently: some burned logs near the fire pit are not fully turned to coal, and a refuse pit behind the outpost has discarded food scraps that are not yet rotten. You have not encountered other travelers along the road, but these signs make clear the region is not without inhabitants.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character notices a swollen floorboard that is slightly out of place. Investigating it, they find a small pouch of old and tarnished coins totaling  **2 50**.

Once the party has finished searching, Sin finds something for the party.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Lifting a slat of wood and peering into the pile of rotting paper, Sin calls out:
>
> > Hey, look at this!
>
> Fearlessly, she shoves her hand into the mess and pries out a worn, mildew-coated leather binder, along with a small collection of bugs which are quickly dispatched from her arm with a brisk shake.
>
> A moment later she's flipping through the pages, scrunching her nose at what she finds.
>
> > Looks like some diagrams and recipes from a hunter. Here, you should take this! I've never seen a hydroxol before, but if you run into one, the notes could be useful!
>
> She claps the moldy old folder closed and holds it out to claim.

Sin offers the [[Moldy Recipe Folder]] to the party. It contains some curious crafting recipes that the party could try making if they can find the creature noted within.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### What's a Hydroxol?
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 12)** check knows that hydroxols are gigantic, naturally territorial amphibious omnivores that nest in watery, secluded places.
>
> - **Knowledge: Beasts**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character recalls that these creatures could probably be found in this region, in the especially watery areas of the Dripstones.
