# Sickly Drakelings

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Combat Event occurs as the party ventures forth from the stalled Strayhearth Caravan and encounters several [[Afflicted Pallid Drakeling]] afflicted by a mysterious illness. By battling the creatures and examining their corpses, the party can:
>
> - Survive an ambush by dangerous and desperate drakelings.
> - Learn that these creatures are afflicted by a mysterious sickness.
>
> The party begins in the [[Verdant Paths]] Scene, an explorable [[Area Maps]] that's used to depict combat encounters.
>
> These drakelings are the first evidence the party will encounter of the malevolent influence of Zerranyss and his minions, foreshadowing the main conflict of the rest of the game. For more on the antagonists of this campaign, see [[The Villains]].

### Drakeling Ambush

As the party backtracks from the halted Strayhearth Caravan and the collapsed cairn, they are ambushed by a trio of Pallid Drakelings amidst the [[Verdant Paths]].

> [!abstract] Afflicted Pallid Drakeling
> **[[Afflicted Pallid Drakeling]]**
>
> Level 2 · Afflicted Pallid Drake Drakeling
>
> ![Afflicted Pallid Drakeling](../../../assets/image012.webp)
>
> This serpentine creature has a long thin body accentuated by thin, ragged spotted frills midway along its body. Its features are dragon-like, boasting a crown of wavy horns, a maw filled with small, razor-sharp teeth, and a constant drool of acidic saliva. Its cloudy eyes suggest blindness, while the presence of unsettling yellow pustules in unhealed wounds point to a strange affliction. Despite its sickly appearance, the creature appears agile and dangerous.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### How to Use: Combat Blocks
>
> The red box that is inset below is known as a Combat Block. These will appear whenever a Combat Event or combat gameplay occurs, and will provide all of the information you need to run the associated encounter.
>
> Each Combat Block will lead with an introductory paragraph that sets the stage, followed by a breakdown of the enemy tactics — the number of enemies; their opening, mid-combat, and closing tactics; how they make use of their weapons, abilities, and features; how they interact with the battlefield terrain; what happens when the enemy is defeated; and more.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Drake Divebomb
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 16, Passive)** is able to spot the southeast drakeling before it attacks. If no character spots the drakeling, the party is **Unaware** at the start of combat.
>
> #### Afflicted Pallid Drakeling Tactics
>
> A group of 3 [[Afflicted Pallid Drakeling]] are hiding here in an attempt to attack unsuspecting victims. Only one of these drakelings is initially visible, located southeast of the party atop the 20-foot-high ledge. The other two drakelings remain hidden at first, concealed in 20-foot-high clefts of stone to the west.
>
> At the start of combat, the drakelings will attempt to use advantageous terrain and the element of surprise to gain the upper hand.
>
> Over the course of combat, the drakelings will prioritize the following actions and abilities:
>
> - The drakelings utilize their Climb Speed to scale rocky surfaces that would otherwise be difficult terrain. From an advantageous perch, they will use either [[Pouncing Bite]] to engage vulnerable targets in melee, or use their [[Spit Bile]] to target enemies with ranged attacks.
> - More often than not, their animal intelligence compels the drakelings to attack the closest or most vulnerable enemy, rather than one of maximal potential.
> - Foul experimentation has left the drakelings **Blinded**. This condition is partially offset by the their [[Blood Sense]], but this limitation can nevertheless provide the party with an important tactical edge during the encounter.
>
> The affliction these pallid drakelings suffer from has left them feral and deranged, and they will furiously fight to their deaths.

Once the smoke clears, the characters can examine the bodies of these strange drakelings for evidence about their draconic nature and pustulant afflictions.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### After the Battle
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 13, Passive)** check notes that these creatures are not native to this region; they are an invasive species.
>
> - **Knowledge: Dragons**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Medicine (DC 13, Passive)** check recognizes that the pustules and lesions on the drakelings are the likely result of some kind of contagion, disease, or alchemical influence.
>
> - **Knowledge: Alchemy**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character can speculate that the affliction is probably the result of a chemical reaction.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Subterranea** recognize that the creatures' pale skin and clouded eyes are reminiscent of the characteristics found on some creatures who live deep underground.

The party may also wish to examine the bodies of the drakelings and what might be salvaged from their corpses:

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Belly of the Beast
>
> Characters with **Awareness (DC 15, Passive)** will notice that one of the drakelings has a humanoid finger wedged between its back teeth.
>
> Investigating the finger turns up a badly damaged severed humanoid finger still wearing a [[Simple Jeweled Ring]]. It has no maker's mark or inscriptions.
