# Sheltered Campsite

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This is the first Event of the Ember [[Main Quest Overview]] storyline. It is currently 10:00pm on Day 0, and the story begins with a campfire tale that introduces some of Ember's lore and history. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Learn about [[The Shattering]], a major event in Ember's past.
> - Learn about [[Akon]], one of Ember's moons.
> - Meet [[Agraband Swift]] and [[Ankarist]].
> - Understand the caravan's current objective: to reach the town of [[Helkas]].
>
> #### Setting the Scene
>
> Every gameplay Event in Ember begins with an opening passage called "Setting the Scene." Blocks of text displayed in this style are called "readalouds" and are intended to be narrated aloud by the Gamemaster. Begin every Event by narrating the "Setting the Scene" text before activating and continuing with the remaining details of the Event.
>
> #### Vistas
>
> As part of this Event, players are introduced to the first [[Vistas for Gamemasters]], a special type of environment used for storytelling and social interaction. This Vista depicts the caravan's campsite in the [[Verdant Paths]] Biome of the [[Forest of Stone]]. Let the players know that they can navigate the Vista by right clicking and dragging to pan the view of the scene.
>
> #### Time Progression
>
> Time progression is an important mechanic in Ember. At the top of the screen is a clock and calendar display that tells you the current time. Certain moments of gameplay will only occur at certain times, and actions you take throughout the world will cause time to advance with meaningful consequences. Encourage your players to take note of the current time (10:00pm on Day 0) and be aware that time progression will matter for the gameplay that they experience.
>
> #### Meeting Companions
>
> It is vital for the party to get introduced to the various traveling companions in the Strayhearth Caravan. These will be important recurring characters that appear throughout the game, so these initial introductions are essential for the story.
>
> In order to avoid overloading the players with new information all at once, we have designed the first Events of the game to provide an opportunity to meet and converse with these companions one at a time.
>
> - In this Event, the party has an opportunity to talk with [[Agraband Swift]] and [[Ankarist]].
> - In the [[Giant Moonstone]] Event, the party can chat with [[Clipper]] and [[Lyla Cevher]].
> - In the [[Skies Above]] Event, the party can spend time getting to know [[Sin Marmot]].
>
> We recommend you portray characters who aren't the subject of focus for a particular Event as being occupied with other duties at any given point in time. However, if the party is determined to meet everyone now, those character introductions can be pulled forward from later Events to occur here instead.
>
> #### Player Character Motivations
>
> If the players have yet to do so, they should determine precisely how or why their characters are traveling with the Strayhearth Caravan to Helkas, as described in the [[Creation Overview]] section of the [[Players' Guide]].

### The Caravanner's Tale

The Strayhearth Caravan consists of three massive six-wheeled wagons, a huge [[Yarnac]], and various [[Rask]] that serve the convoy as beasts of burden. The caravanner [[Agraband Swift]] owns and operates the enterprise with the assistance of the Signborn merchant [[Clipper]] and a small cohort of passengers — mostly merchants or travelers — who are along for the ride.

Some of these passengers are gathered around the fire, the most remarkable of whom are a trio named [[Ankarist]], [[Lyla Cevher]], and [[Sin Marmot]]. Each is traveling toward Ordain with the Strayhearth caravan for reasons of their own.

The game begins as the wizened caravan leader, Agraband Swift, entertains the company with the telling of a campfire tale that introduces an important moment in Ember's history.

> [!abstract] Agraband Swift
> **[[Agraband Swift]]**
>
> Level 3 · Human Caravaneer
>
> ![Agraband Swift](../../../assets/image095.webp)
>
> A lute is slung across the back of this aged human clad in an odd yet practical assortment of clothing and jewelry. The seasoned bard wears the salt-and-pepper locks of his long hair pulled back into a high half ponytail. His wide smile and calm demeanor lend him an air of experienced confidence, and a certain glint in his eye evokes the mirthful spirit of a beloved uncle. It's clear this man could spin a worthy tale at a moment’s notice.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### How to Use: Readaloud Blocks
>
> The parchment box that is inset below is known as a Readaloud Block. These will appear when text is meant to be read aloud to the players.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> With practiced bravado, Agraband gestures toward the strange slab of rock and says:
>
> > Ah yes, a fragment of Akon. Can you imagine such a place when it was whole? Think of where this came from, and the enormity of events that caused its plummet to this terrestrial soil. I'm sure you all know the tale, but please sit a moment and perhaps I might weave it upon your memories anew?
>
> Once everyone is settled in and paying attention, Agraband begins, his voice filled with dramatic gravitas befitting the start of a grand tale.
>
> > Long ago, the Heart of Ember, the source of creation and life, assembled the first and most-ancient pieces of our home: the world that formed around the Heart and the elemental moons which traverse the sky. Attracted by Ember's power like a spark in the darkness, the Abyss closed in swiftly to devour our fledgling realm.
> >
> > But the Heart fashioned the protective barrier of the Weave to shelter us, and no matter how desperately the Abyss clawed at it, the Weave kept us safe from the terrors beyond.
>
> With that, Agraband gestures up to the sky and the glittering stars.
>
> > Within our safe enclosure, Ember grew wild and ever-changing. While the Weave shielded us from threats beyond, it did not protect us from **within**.
> >
> > A misguided and arrogant people, at the apex of their empire's power, sought to prove themselves masters of Ember. They built a colossal tower grasping ever-higher to harness the light of Lantyr and the power of the Weave itself.
>
> Agraband's arm stretches up, fingers just falling beyond the light of the fire.
>
> > All of Ember shared the punishment their hubris wrought — and when the tower was broken, the moon of Akon shattered, and the Weave itself was rent asunder.
>
> With a sudden downward motion, Agraband snuffs out the fire before him, plunging the camp into a heart-stopping darkness.
>
> > The Abyss rushed into our world.
>
> In the gloom you swear you can see shapes moving around the camp. Some of the travelers nearby call out in alarm, while others assure them it's merely a trick from the bard, though even they don't sound fully convinced.
>
> Agraband lets this unease settle among the audience before continuing. With a flourish, the flames light anew, driving back the shadows and gloom of the night.
>
> > But all was not lost, for Ember had defenders, some of them quite unlikely. Even the mighty and terrible Alar stood alongside the other elder gods and rebuffed the Abyss. Alar repaired the Weave and prevented our annihilation.
> >
> > Not all could be saved, however, for Akon was broken beyond repair and from that shattered moon emerged dragons, clever and cruel. Banished from their home, they vanished into the mountains and pathways of Ember and hid until Alar's attention was needed elsewhere.
>
> Agraband offers a cheeky smile.
>
> > To this day, the dragons of old Akon prowl the lands of Ember, causing havoc as they please. But fear not, the Strayhearth Caravan is of no interest to such monsters, and we know well enough to steer clear of them!
>
> Agraband slowly lowers his arms, letting his final words linger in the quiet of the gently crackling campfire. He pauses and absorbs the rapt attention of the company, and with a smile says:
>
> > … and now, friends, to rest, for tomorrow will be a long day's passage. If all goes well, we should make Helkas several days ahead of schedule!

### Chatting With Agraband

After the conclusion of Agraband's tale, the caravan members begin preparing to bed down and rest, but there is still enough activity about. For characters looking to socialize more, they will spot both Agraband and Ankarist standing together talking.

>
> Each Social Block will lead with a description of the NPCs or other Actors that are being encountered, followed by breakdown of the encounter — NPC behavior and personality; the topics of conversation they're willing to discuss; checks associated with the encounter; and more.

> [!info] Social
> #### The Charming Caravanner
>
> Agraband is a friendly, chatty, and gregarious sort, always happy to talk about something, especially if it's his stories and adventures.
>
> Agraband was an adventurer in his youth, a bard once and still, and a caravanner now. If asked about his role in the caravan, he's all too happy to explain that he's the owner and operator of the Strayhearth Caravan and that most of the wagons and animals are his. He runs all sorts of different routes, usually based on what's selling and being sold.
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 13, Passive)** perceives that Agraband seems genuinely proud of his caravan and the entrepreneurial success he has created for himself and his companions. Characters with **Path: Caravanner** recognize that Agraband runs a tight operation and identify the telltale signs of a successful enterprise.

> [!warning] Gamemaster

> **A:**
>
> A look of bittersweet remembrance crosses the old raconteur's face for a brief moment as he continues.
>
> > The story of my youth is painted with tragedy. And those ill-fated circumstances carried me from Ordain, where I was born, to a small village outside of Toraga in my adolescence. From there, I stowed away on a caravan headed to Casla-Brava, and I never stopped traveling. For many years now, I'm pleased to say, "home is where the 'Hearth is!"

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your trade?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I've run this particular trade route for decades — with a few side treks, of course. The three cities of Ordain, Toraga, and Casla-Brava form a triangular trade network that encourages the steady exchange of goods throughout the Plateau. And thanks to our stellar track record, Strayhearth has managed to turn a tidy profit along the way!
>
> A glint in the bard's eye betrays his keen sense of pride.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The route?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The northern road we travel is a fortuitous one. We'll eventually make our way to Ordain on the eastern seaboard, but first, we'll visit Helkas and participate in the Dusktide celebrations there. You chose the right time to charter passage, my friends!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Helkas and Dusktide?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Helkas is a frontier mining town at the eastern edge of the Forest of Stone, where the Arctus Plateau opens up and stretches eastward as far as the eye can see. The people who live there are hearty folk, with good heads on their shoulders. And the mine itself is something rather special — formed from the inner reaches of an ossified leviathan.
> >
> > The Dusktide festival is actually a celebration of the mine's bounty of arcane resources — peculiar substances which only seem to appear or manifest during the lunar conjunction. The whole plateau benefits from these rare materials. And this legendary celebration is not to be missed!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Allies in Ordain?
>
> **A:**
>
> > As you might imagine, I spend most of my time on the road. But I do have a few good friends and family members in the city. Well, one family member to be precise — my nephew Jorey. Truth be told, after the death of my sister Selena (the boy's mother), I haven't kept in touch with him nearly as much as I should. He's a headstrong young man, full of the old Swift bravado.
> >
> > As far as friends go, my dear associate Fernis Ossa is one of the most intriguing people you could hope to meet. Her work for the Anachraenum is unrivaled. After all, you don't become the champion of the inaugural Expedition Challenge without knowing a thing or two about challenges and expeditions.

Characters in the party may have some prior knowledge or understanding about the Dusktide event.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Dusktide
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Science (DC 14)** check understands that Dusktide is an occasion when the spirit moon of [[Orbis]] and the water moon of [[Mayis]] reach their zenith together. During this time, strange phenomena emerge: water tends to become unpredictable, rivers may flow backwards, faster, or slower, and the seas themselves may become as smooth as glass or hungry and treacherous.
>
> - **Knowledge: Cosmology**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 14)** check knows that Orbis can be an unpredictable force, affecting both natural phenomena and people's emotions. Anyone attuned to Mayis or Orbis would be feeling especially sensitive to the upcoming cosmological zenith.
>
> Characters with **Attunement: Mayis** or **Attunement: Orbis** can feel the energy of the moons growing in them, creating a tumultuous ebb and flow of emotions in heart and mind. Though mild now, the sensation will grow stronger over time.

### History of The Shattering

Ankarist and Agraband are discussing the nature of [[The Shattering]] and the particulars of Agraband's tale. Ankarist seems to be especially interested in the topic of dragons — perhaps in part because he is a [[Drakon]]. Characters may join their conversation, or independently consider what they know about this legend.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### History and Legend
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Science (DC 12)** check knows that The Shattering marks the year-zero from which dates in Ember are now written; it also signaled both the end of the [[Age of the Tower]] and the demise of the ancient [[Aedir]], a hegemony which once spanned most of the known world.
>
> - **Knowledge: Aedir**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character recalls that historians believe the Aedir sought to build a tower so tall that they could ascend high enough to commune directly with [[Lantyr]] and harness her power. During The Shattering, both this "Sun Tower" and the moon [[Akon]] were destroyed. It's believed that this cataclysmic event gave way to the [[Abyssal Shear]], in which massive debris from the Sun Tower and Akon sundered [[The Weave]], allowing the unknowable energy of the Abyss to enter the world.

Agraband may provide the party with the above information himself if asked. The bard is appreciative of inquisitive listeners and is eager to share more context with anyone who volunteers an attentive ear. After the party has had its fill of socializing and talking history with Agraband, they will be encouraged to pack up for the night and get some rest. The caravan has a long day ahead of it, and Agraband needs his rest as well.

### The Next Day

> [!quote] Read Aloud

A team of wagoners readies the caravan's trio of six-wheeled wagons, attaching a hulking yarnac to each of them with tack and bridle. Travelers vie for who will get to ride on one of the mounted seats today and who will walk alongside the wagons on foot. You consider yourselves lucky for managing to secure seating thus far with regularity.
>
> As Agraband Swift passes your resting spot, he greets you with boisterous zest:
>
> > Be roused and ready, friends, for the winding trail beckons! We won't make it to Helkas tonight, but I know a place we can camp and stay dry. With any luck, we'll be in Helkas tomorrow — a full two days early!
>
> Another voice calls out to the caravanner:
>
> > Agraband, have you got a moment?
>
> Agraband is joined by the Drakon fellow you've come to know as Ankarist. He motions toward the massive shard of Akon you camped beneath last night.
>
> > I took your advice and looked at the shards of Akon once the sun rose. You weren't kidding about how different they look in the daylight.
>
> Agraband nods in agreement with Ankarist.
>
> > Quite! All the texture and smaller elements get lost in the shadow of the evening.

The party may also be curious to inspect the strange fragment of rock said to have come from the moon of Akon.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Fragment of Akon
>
> If a character lays their hand upon this rock, they experience the following:
>
> > You sense a distant and subtle rhythmic pulse, like the ticking of a distant clock. It is difficult to tell whether it is something real, imagined, or merely the beating of your own heart.
>
> This character gains **+2 Boons** to a check of their choosing made within the next 24 hours.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Science (DC 12)** check understands that Akon is one of Ember's primary cosmological forces which represents the fundamental concept of Order. Theologians believe that Akon's destruction has caused, or at least coincided with, significant change to Ember over the past two-thousand years.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 12)** check recalls that Akon was once the home of mythical and noble Wyrms before the Abyssal Shear forced a terrible change upon them. This transformation, according to legend, was the origin of Dragons, which are terrible and formidable creatures.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Science (DC 12)** check can tell that whatever mechanism once operated within this fragment of rock is broken and long-since defunct, its gears and machinery twisted and overrun by verdant growth.
>
> Use of the **Talent: Recognize Spellcraft** talent confirms that no trace of magical aura remains upon the fragment.

#### Akon Attunement: Shard of Akon

Each character who successfully investigates the Fragment of Akon by touching the ruin and succeeding on one or more of the checks listed above advances their **Attunement: Akon (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### Meeting Ankarist

Having grown curious about the party's mutual interest (or lack thereof), Ankarist engages them in what may be one of their most meaningful conversations with the Drakon to date. If one or more characters investigated the Shard of Akon, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The tall, two-horned shadow of Ankarist falls across your face, drawing your attention back to your Drakon traveling companion — who has approached your group with an unmistakable look of curiosity on his face. His smoky voice is tinged with an accent from the west, and he wastes no time with formalities.
>
> > I see you have a keen interest in the Shattered Moon as well. They say the wise and ancient Wyrms once tunneled beneath its surface, but now? It's rumored to be a broken, lifeless wasteland. Nevertheless, I feel a certain … harmony with this slab of meteoric rock.
>
> Ankarist runs his hand over the massive lunar shard once more.
>
> > This cosmic attunement of ours is a strange thing, is it not? Tell me: what "harmony" have you managed to find in the world?

Alternately, if none of the characters approached the Shard of Akon for a closer inspection, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The tall, two-horned shadow of Ankarist falls across your face, drawing your attention back to your Drakon traveling companion — who has approached your group with an unmistakable look of judgement on his face. His smoky voice is tinged with an accent from the west, and he wastes no time with formalities.
>
> > I see the Shattered Moon is of little interest to you. I myself am drawn to it. I feel a certain … harmony with this slab of meteoric rock.
>
> Ankarist runs his hand over the massive lunar Shard once more.
>
> > This cosmic attunement of ours is a strange thing, is it not? Tell me: what "harmony" have you managed to find in the world?

> [!info] Social
> #### The Stoic Investigator
>
> Ankarist initially appears serious, aloof, and reserved. He stands out as a Drakon, and keen-eyed characters can note an accent and mannerisms that betray his origins as a **Culture: Tayan**. If asked about this, he'll confirm the party's suspicions, noting he is a first-generation Drakon from the [[Tayan]] Empire — although he's lived in Ordain for the better part of a decade now. It should feel obvious to the party through these initial interactions that there is a lot more to Ankarist that isn't overtly apparent.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 16)** check gleans the following:
>
> - Despite his stoicism, Ankarist is being honest and doesn't harbor any ill intentions towards the caravan or the party. There are simply some (if not many) things he is not going to discuss with strangers.
> - Ankarist's line of questioning about attunement isn't merely an attempt to get to know them better as traveling companions: he's curious about what cosmological forces they're aligned with (and what that ultimately means for the safety of both Strayhearth and Ankarist's allies in Ordain).
>
> **Culture: Ordani** characters can guess that Ankarist is a member of the [[Veiled Chain]], an investigative arm of Ordain's government that provides protection, intelligence, and solutions to a wide range of legal or extralegal challenges.

Some of our cases await arbitration from the magistrates of the Hallows, while most are simply far too sensitive to discuss. I'm sure you understand.

>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 14)** check knows that the Drakon are an exceptionally young ancestry, only having appeared a generation or two ago, with the bulk of the population coming out of the [[Tayan]].
