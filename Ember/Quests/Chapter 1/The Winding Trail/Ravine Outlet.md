# Ravine Outlet

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Combat Event occurs as the party approaches the ravine separating the Dripstones and the Verdant Paths. They are attacked by an ill and disoriented predator. By battling the creature, the party can:
>
> - Survive the ambush by a sickened [[Suarrok Juvenile]], routing or killing it in the process.
> - Examine the monstrosity and determine precisely why it might be sick.
>
> This Event is depicted using the [[Dripstones]] Area Map.
>
> #### A Familiar Foe
>
> The party may have encountered this creature before:
>
> - This Suarrok Juvenile is the sickened creature in the [[Flying Predator]] Event.
> - If the party battled but did not kill the Suarrok Juvenile atop the [[Lookout Post]], then this is that specific creature. Otherwise, this is merely another Suarrok Juvenile that exists in the region.

### Suarrok Ambush

The party is attacked by a juvenile suarrok. This strange monstrosity is likely familiar to the party by now, having been encountered at least once in the area during the events of other quests. This one in particular is ill from having eaten a corrupted slime.

> [!abstract] Suarrok Juvenile
> **[[Suarrok Juvenile]]**
>
> Level 4 (Elite) · Winged Terror Suarrok
>
> ![Suarrok Juvenile](../../../assets/image061.webp)
>
> This creature is an unsettling thing, a strange mixture of bird and lizard, with massive, bat-like wings fringed with trailing feathers. A long, sinuous neck that coils and moves like a serpent holds a massive head with menacing, serrated beak that's perfectly designed for tearing through flesh. On this head rests a single, enormous eye that glows ominously, and as its gaze sweeps over you, a wash of stinging heat can be felt.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Strange Illness
>
> After each of its turns in combat, the Suarrok Juvenile hacks and coughs, spitting up a glob of glimmering goop. If it is flying when it does this, reduce its height by 10 feet; this may force it to land.
>
> #### Suarrok Juvenile Tactics
>
> The [[Suarrok Juvenile]] begins combat by landing near the party.
>
> At the start of combat, the Suarrok Juvenile will use its [[Searing Stare]] to inflict the most threatening enemy with the **Broken** condition.
>
> Over the course of combat, the Suarrok Juvenile will prioritize the following actions and abilities:
>
> - In melee, the Suarrok Juvenile will use its [[Swooping Strike]] and [[Raking Talons]] actions.
> - The Suarrok Juvenile uses its [[Elusive Flight]] talent to make hit-and-run attacks.
>
> The battle ends when the Suarrok Juvenile is **Weakened** or **Broken**, at which point it attempts to flee. Alternatively, if this is the same Suarrok Juvenile from the [[Aedir Signalpost]], it will instead fight to the death, maddened at its nest being defiled.

In the immediate aftermath of the battle, characters can take a closer look at the creature's spit, and if they defeated it, the carcass.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Familiar Foe
>
> Any character who experienced the [[Flying Predator]] Event can tell without a check that the creature is similar to the one they saw throwing up earlier.
>
> Otherwise, any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 12, Passive)** check recognizes that this creature is likely a [[Suarrok Juvenile]], a flying predator that has been spotted in this region in the past.'
>
> - **Knowledge: Monsters**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character recognizes that this creature was an adolescent and that suarrok can grow to be huge in size when fully grown. Battle against even a juvenile suarrok could be deadly, but facing a fully grown adult would likely be disastrous.
>
> #### Investigating the Goo
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 13, Passive)** can see that there is an odd glint to the bird's gooey spit.
>
> Characters who are familiar with the alchemical contaminant (whether from [[Yakoshta Mine]], the [[Aedir Signalpost]], or their encounter with a similar predator elsewhere in the Forest of Stone) do not need to do the below checks for information they are already aware of.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Medicine (DC 14, Passive)** check can understand that it is something that the creature ate and has reacted badly to, rather than evidence of some other sickness.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character identifies that the natural substance the creature ate looks like the partially digested remains of an ooze. It was recently eaten, suggesting its source must have been within a few miles of their current location.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 14, Passive)** check correctly deduces that the acrid nature of this substance is due to some toxic substance that was ingested alongside the organic material.
>
> - **Knowledge: Alchemy**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character concludes that the alchemical substance is the source of the acrid smell and any damage done by the puddles. It is dangerous to the touch and likely the reason that the creature threw up.
>
> The substance in the spit is too dissolved to feasibly collect.

If the party managed to defeat the suarrok without it escaping, they may directly examine the body.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Body
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Medicine (DC 14, Passive)** check reveals that the creature looks healthy, with no markers of disease, illness, or injury prior to those inflicted in the fight. The most likely answer is that the creature was poisoned by something it ate, as evidenced by the goo it was coughing up.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 14)** passive check can harvest some ingredients from the felled suarrok, including [[Suarrok Plumage]] and a [[Suarrok Eye]]. On failure, however, the damage to the suarrok from battle has rendered these components unrecoverable or worthless.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> Once the party has driven off or defeated the Suarrok Juvenile, they can continue on their journey. At this point, the party may wish to use their [[Messenger Coin]] to contact the caravan. Their next stops are either:
>
> - Returning to the Strayhearth Caravan in the [[Lending a Hand]] Event.
> - Leaving the caravan behind by proceeding directly to the [[Arrival at Helkas]] Event.
