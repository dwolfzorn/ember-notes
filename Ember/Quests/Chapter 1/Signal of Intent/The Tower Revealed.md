# The Tower Revealed

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> In this Exploration Event, the party confronts a challenging natural obstacle, discovers an ancient ruin, and is tantalized by the appearance of a mystical tower against the distant horizon. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Witness the brief appearance of the [[Aedir Signalpost]].
> - Examine the ancient shrine that sits beside the chasm edge and enact the means to make the tower visible for longer than a fleeting glimpse.
> - Decide how best to traverse a dangerous chasm between the party and the tower.
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Ancient Wayshrine" Level of the [[Vista: Verdant Paths]] Vista.

### The Flickering Tower

At some point during this Event, the party's inspection of the crevasse and the shrine should be interrupted suddenly by the unexpected appearance of the distant tower.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Reveal Timing
>
> As the Gamemaster, introduce this narrative moment whenever the party's engagement with prior clues seems to slow down or when they are at an impasse about what to do next.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Your inspection of the crevasse and the nearby shrine is suddenly interrupted as your gaze shifts reflexively upward in response to sudden movement. The light around you seems to shift, as if unseen clouds parted somewhere in the sky overhead. In the distance, where nothing stood a second before, a tower appears, jutting out of the ground like an enormous cylindrical tree of metal and stone. Its gray edifice is cracked and craggy, ravaged by nature and time, but it is still there, unmistakably looming over the ground around it. Then, as quickly as it appeared, it disappears, flickering briefly between seen and unseen until it vanishes completely.

This momentary glimpse of the tower affords each member of the party a split second to try to recognize what they saw.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Now You See It …
>
> The quick disappearance of the tower makes it very challenging to discern much information about it. Whenever the tower appears, each character is allowed to make one check from the following list, learning the corresponding piece of information upon a success.
>
> ****Society (DC 14)****
>
> The shape and profile of the tower match the signature architectural design of the ancient [[Aedir]] Empire.
>
> - **Knowledge: Aedir**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> ****Arcana (DC 14)****
>
> It is unlikely that the tower itself has moved, but rather that some magic or artifice has shrouded its appearance. If the tower is shrouded using magic, it must be an exceptionally powerful invisibility field to cover such a large area.
>
> ****Awareness (DC 14)****
>
> It seemed as if there was some extensive weathering or damage to the stonework on the facade of the tower, suggesting long-term abandonment or neglect.
>
> ****Wilderness (DC 14)****
>
> The general location of where the tower appeared is very close to — and far above — the location of [[Yakoshta]] in the valley far below.
>
> When making any of the above checks:
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character is quick enough to make a second check of their choice from the list above. This result only applies once per tower appearance.
>
> #### Now You Don't!
>
> If the characters try to wait for the tower to appear again, it will flicker once more in exactly `[[/gmroll 20d20 # Minutes]]` minutes. The unpredictable timing of such appearances means that unless the party is paying constant attention, the tower's momentary appearance is likely to be missed.

Any further information to be gleaned about the tower will need to be observed upon reaching the structure, but only if the party can safely cross the intervening chasm.

### The Crevasse

The party will likely wish to investigate the area and its shrine more closely before attempting to cross, however, they must eventually do so in order to continue on their journey. This is a perilous enterprise, but there are several methods by which the party might succeed.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Traversing the Crevasse
>
> The crevasse is 30 feet from side to side, and deep enough that descending is not a viable method of crossing the chasm.
>
> - If a character crosses while bearing one end of a rope, they could secure that end on the opposite side of the chasm.
> - A character who wishes to heroically jump the distance would require a horizontal jump distance of 30 feet in order to succeed.
> - A character might use magical aid to cross.
> - A character with rope and a [[Grapnel]] can successfully throw and affix the rope to a secure object on the far side of the crevasse by succeeding on a subsequent **Athletics (DC 14)** check.
>
> #### Bridging the Gap
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 12)** check notices that there is an aspen in the vicinity of adequate height and proximity to the ledge that, if it were toppled at the correct angle, might span the gap.
>
> When the party decides to fell the tree, they must designate one character to calculate the correct angle with a successful **Wilderness (DC 15)** check.
>
> - **Path: Academy Dropout**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> #### Falling into the Crevasse
>
> Should any character be ill-fated enough to fall into the crevasse, they and one nearby party member may both make a desperate **Athletics (DC 16)** check to attempt to arrest their fall. If neither character succeeds, the individual who fell into the crevasse disappears into the darkness below and perishes — their body is impossible to recover.

Regardless of the method used, once the party has safely navigated the crossing, mark the following outcome as completed.

`[[/outcome traversed]]`

#### Ragen Attunement: Crevasse Crossing

The first character who crosses the crevasse advances their **Attunement: Ragen (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Region Map Change
>
> Marking the crevasse as traversed removes an impassable boundary from the Region Map and allows the party to continue traveling toward the Aedir Signalpost.

### The Clarion Shrine

The party will likely wish to investigate the circular wayshrine constructed atop a low rise nearby, overlooking the chasm ahead.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The partially-ruined structure nearby is a circular colonnade, nearly twenty feet in diameter, built upon a rise of rock overlooking the surrounding area. In the center of its enclosure is a low, round pedestal with a slightly domed top covered in moss and dirt. The portions of this dome that are not covered by overgrowth have a smooth, almost glassy surface. A metal rod protrudes outward several inches from one side of the pedestal, like a singular spoke.

This small shrine served as a waypoint and control device for the invisibility field shrouding the [[Aedir Signalpost]]. Its magic could still function today, if the party invokes the correct command.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating the Clarion Shrine
>
> The characters can clear away moss and debris covering the shrine and pedestal over the course of 5 minutes.
>
> Any character who inspects the shrine and makes a successful **Society (DC 14)** check notes that the architecture of this small monument matches the design of the Aedir Empire.
>
> - **Knowledge: Aedir**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character also notes that this structure seems illogical to exist in isolation, and surmises that it must represent some sort of wayshrine or marker upon the path to a more significant destination.
>
> Any character who inspects the pedestal and makes a successful **Arcana (DC 13)** check notes that the pedestal's design suggests it is the focal point for some spellcraft or arcane ritual. **Illusory Sense** reveals latent enchantments of Illusion and Divination magic upon the glassy lens.
>
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success** or **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character recalls the Clarion Lens, an artifact generally associated with the Aedir that is designed specifically to control or penetrate certain illusions.
>
> If the party has learned of [[Guarded Neighbors]], then they are able to recognize that this is the "clarion shrine" mentioned in Golarne's message to Chiseqi.
>
> If the party has discovered the [[Multifaceted]], then they notice that the style of the metalwork of the [[Clarion Fork]] matches almost identically to the style of the metal spoke protruding from the pedestal here.
>
> #### Revealing the Unseen
>
> This Clarion Shrine is the intended mechanism for revealing the Aedir Signalpost tower. The tower can be revealed in any of the following ways.
>
> If a character strikes the [[Clarion Fork]] against the metal spoke:
>
> > A shrill and pure note rings out across the mountain highlands, cutting through the moist air. The sound is piercing — though not necessarily painful — to hear. In immediate response to this tone, the cloudy lens on the pedestal swirls and clarifies, revealing a distinctive shape suspended within the glassy dome; a spiral with three diagonal lines of differing lengths that cut across its curvature. This glyph shines with a pale blue light, and in the distance you behold the rippling motion of illusion as the horizon changes shape and reveals the striking silhouette of a tall tower isolated against the sky.
>
> If a character traces the geometry of Golarne's glyphsign upon the lens:
>
> > You trace a specific sign upon the lens: a spiral with three diagonal lines of differing lengths that cut across its curvature. The smudges left by your finger upon the lens suddenly flare to life with a pale blue light. The cloudy lens swirls and clarifies to reveal the geometry of that glyph repeated in vibrant color within its glassy dome. This glyph shines with a pale blue light, and in the distance you behold the rippling motion of illusion as the horizon changes shape and reveals the striking silhouette of a tall tower isolated against the sky.
>
> If the party has not discovered or gained access to either of these methods, the tower can be alternatively revealed through other means of magical detection, such as **Illusory Sense**:
>
> > In the distance you behold the rippling motion of illusion as the horizon changes shape and reveals the striking silhouette of a tall tower isolated against the sky.

Once the party has revealed the tower, mark the following Outcome as complete and award [[Attunement]] depending on the manner by which the challenge was surmounted (according to the guidelines below).

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Reducing the Challenge
>
> Revealing the Aedir Signalpost is intended to be a difficult challenge for a low-level party to overcome. They must either thoroughly explore the Forest of Stone to find one of the necessary methods or gain access to higher-level magic that allows them to penetrate the invisibility field.
>
> If you wish to offer an easier means of continuing this adventure, you can allow the tower's invisibility field to completely fail, either immediately or after some amount of time passes. This field is already weakened, and allowing it to fail completely will be compatible with the story that follows.
>
> #### Region Map Change
>
> Marking the below outcome causes the Aedir Signalpost to appear on the Region Map.

`[[/outcome revealed]]`

#### Primordis Attunement: Clarion Fork

Any character who helps dispel the tower's invisibility field using the [[Multifaceted]] advances their **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Aura Attunement: Golarne's Glyphsign

Any character who helps dispel the tower's invisibility field by tracing the sign of [[Guarded Neighbors]] advances their **Attunement: Aura (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Orbis Attunement: Alternative Solutions

Any character who helps dispel the tower's invisibility field using a different method advances their **Attunement: Orbis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### Concluding the Event

Having successfully crossed the crevasse and identified the location of the tower, the party may continue on in search of further answers regarding its nature.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> If the party successfully traversed the crevasse and revealed the tower, they can continue south along the ridgeline to reach the revealed [[Aedir Signalpost]] and begin the [[Traversing the Tower]] Event.
>
> #### Conditions of Progression
>
> If one or both of the [[The Tower Revealed]] or [[The Tower Revealed]] Outcomes in this Event are not completed, the party may need to fall back or repeat the attempt. These Outcomes can be retried later if the party is unable to accomplish them now.
