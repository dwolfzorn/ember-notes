# Multifaceted

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> In this Combat and Exploration Event, the party discovers unsettling remnants of a destroyed [[Shent]] machine within the depths of the [[Kaleidoscope Caverns]]. Clues found here foreshadow a terrible conflict against Abyssal forces, and investigation of the machine draws forth lingering malevolent echoes of Abyssal energy that assault the party! In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Discover and investigate the ancient remains of the dead Shent, and possibly learn the sudden and gruesome manner in which they died.
> - Investigate the carved crystals the Shent were making, and perhaps glean some insight into what they were doing with them.
> - Study and determine the purpose of the strange metal machine that sits in the middle of the caves — it appears to be Shent-made, but unlike anything seen before.
> - Survive an ambush by [[Abyssal Echo]] provoked by the party's investigation.
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Arcane Machine" Level of the [[Kaleidoscope Caverns]] Area Map.

### Examining the Shent Remains

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Remains of giantfolk are strewn about the cavern. Some are seemingly ripped apart. Others are broken where they smashed against crystals. Several were crushed by chunks of rock and crystal, which seem to have fallen from overhead. Aside from these grievous wounds, the bodies seem to be remarkably intact, as if undisturbed in the time since they were killed.
>
> Giant-sized tools and implements lie in their hands or near their bodies, suggesting they died suddenly — perhaps in the midst of work, or defending themselves. Numerous crystals line the walls and lie on the ground, many of them carved and adorned with runic engravings. There are countless additional crystals strewn about that appear to be half-carved, or shattered by some hard impact. All around the corpses you can see places where the crystals were being cut from the cavern walls and carved for some purpose.

The long-rotten and crumbling remains of dead Shent can be found all over, most lying next to their tools and among piles of shattered crystals and stone. The party may wish to investigate the giant remains.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Dead
>
> Any character with **Medicine (DC 14, Passive)** surmises that this destruction was very one-sided, and more of a massacre than a battle.
>
> - **Knowledge: Warfare**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check can deduce that the cavern has been undisturbed since the event; the bodies are well-preserved for their age, suggesting that the cave itself slowed their decay.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

The characters may wish to also investigate the ancient and broken tools strewn about the cavern.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Tools
>
> The huge tools lying here have become corroded beyond saving, let alone use. Among their rusted number are chisels, rasps, hammers, and picks all meant to be used in the carving of crystals. Strangely, many of these tools bear mystical symbols.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 13)** check detects the presence of crystals bearing Transmutation runes meant to make the tools sturdier, protect them from wear and corrosion, and even resize them dynamically to the user's hand. However, the crystals are cracked, cloudy, and burned out, surrendering the tools to the ravages of the elements and time.
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

In addition to investigating the Shent remains, the party may wish to inspect the crystal shards that were evidently being carved within this cavern.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Crystals
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 12)** check while examining the crystals recognizes that, in addition to the shards and chips that one would expect as a byproduct of crystal-carving, there are also crystals that have been fully shattered. Something or someone deliberately destroyed them.
>
> - **Knowledge: Ancients**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character also realizes that crystals in this cavern have all regrown since being harvested, a process which would have taken thousands of years. The carvers, their tools, and these crystals must all be equally ancient.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 12)** check while examining the crystals determines that they were all carved in unique styles and pattern groups. Each one bears various, interlocking runes with differing magical applications. The runes all fall under very archaic but easily identified archetypes: Evocation, Transmutation, and Abjuration, all meant to work in concert toward a single effect.
>
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character also realizes that the runes were actively shaping the magic in the crystals to draw out desired effects or impart specific functions. The crystals here are all either incomplete or entirely inert, the runes having burned themselves out over the years. When the crystals were newly made, they might have made strong power sources, or have been useful elements of more complex magical applications. Several of the crystals present had a notably different design from the others — focused on the Abjuration or Illusion and mind-affecting magics. Only a few of the crystals bear these markings, and none of them were ever completed. Without a finished one to study, the character cannot be entirely sure what their ultimate purpose was.

### Examining the Ancient Machine

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> An ominous dome of metal attracts your gaze in the dark, and as you draw closer the sight only grows stranger: a massive machine, tarnished and splayed open like a carcass. It seems that whatever damaged the object also killed whoever was nearby it. It is splattered with long dried and oxidized blood, and a broken, skeletal corpse of a giant lies scattered at the base of the structure.

The party may wish to examine the machinery to try to ascertain its purpose. If any character in the party touches the ancient machine, interrupt their investigation and proceed to the [[Multifaceted]] section below.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Machine
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Science (DC 14)** check while looking at the remnants can safely rule out mechanical failure. The damage the device sustained propagates inward, leaving one to surmise that the structure was attacked and the ensuing damage is what caused the subsequent failure. Notably, however, the metal is unlike simple bronze or iron; the force and power required to punch a hole through it would therefore have needed to be immense.
>
> - **Knowledge: Machines**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 15)** check recalls that the Shent are well-known for their prolific and intricate stonework. There are very few cases of Shent-built machines made of metal in recorded history, making this an exceptionally rare find, and one sure to cause an uproar with historians.
>
> - **Knowledge: Shent**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 13)** check while inspecting the machine notices a general absence of religious or lunar markings. This is strange, as most surviving Shent items and structures that utilize magic include some religious markings venerating or drawing from the moons — this machine has none.
>
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

Attempting to discern the purpose of the machine is likely to trigger the ambush in [[Multifaceted]] below, but such an investigation may be safely continued after that combat is resolved.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Discerning the Purpose of the Machine
>
> Any character who physically inspects the great machine and makes a successful **Arcana (DC 12)** check can surmise that it was intended to generate and project a field of magical energy. The presence of numerous shattered crystals inside the housing indicates that the cavern's crystals likely played a part in its function.
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Machines**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character gains a deeper understanding of the machine's purpose: it used archaic Abjuration, Conjuration, and Evocation runes to move power outward from some sort of core to the outer regions of the machine, where it resonated with the cavern crystals. The Evocation and Abjuration runes would have produced a massive, miles-wide field, mitigating the disorienting effects of the Kaleidoscope Caverns within that radius.
>
> It does not seem possible to repair the machine or restore its functionality, as it is too ancient and too devastatingly damaged.

#### Signara Attunement: Discerning the Machine's Purpose

The first character who determines the machine's purpose — to disrupt the disorienting effects caused by the Kaleidoscope Caverns environment — advances their **Attunement: Signara (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### Abyssal Echoes

Whenever a character physically touches the machine, interrupt their investigation with the following readaloud and subsequent combat encounter.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The ancient metal is cold to the touch and seemingly inert, and yet the grim diorama before you manifests a looming dread within the recesses of your mind. For a brief moment you can clearly imagine the moment in time when these giants witnessed the destruction of their work and the deep-voiced screams of horror as *something* came forth …
>
> Your imagination is interrupted by a subtle shimmer of motion at the periphery of your vision and the exact same giant screams that you imagined are now heard as an echo that replays and resounds throughout the cavern.

Rising from within the ribcages of the remains of several giants are horrible, smoke-writhed apparitions that immediately move to attack the party.

> [!abstract] Abyssal Echo
> **[[Abyssal Echo]]**
>
> Level 1 · Abyssal Harbinger Echo

Its softly glowing eyes radiate spiteful hatred and cunning as it glides through the air with measured movements. Grasping hands, as if yearning to escape its form, materialize and vanish instantly, while its two enormous clawed hands seem to appear and disappear at will.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Abyssal Echo Tactics
>
> The 3 [[Abyssal Echo]] begin combat distributed on different sides of the cavern.
>
> At the start of combat, at least 1 Abyssal Echo will target an enemy spellcaster with its [[Swallowed Screams]] action. The remaining Abyssal Echoes will engage a single target to take advantage of their [[Pack Hunter]] feature.
>
> Over the course of combat, the Abyssal Echoes will prioritize the following actions and abilities:
>
> - In melee, the Abyssal Echoes will use their [[Shadow Talons]].
> - Whenever able, the Abyssal Echoes will use their [[Swallowed Screams]] action to suppress enemy spellcasters.
> - When on the verge of death, the Abyssal Echoes will position themselves to maximize the effectiveness of their [[The Shent Carver's Remains]]
>   [[As the party prepares to leave the cavern, they have an opportunity to recover something useful from the remains of one last giant, which sit against the cavern wall to the southeast, drawing the eye.]]
>   > [!quote] Read Aloud
>   > [[You observe the broken remains of a giant hunched in a seated position against the cavern wall to the southeast of the device. The figure's skeletal fist appears to be clutching something close to their chest. Upon closer inspection, you see a curious trilateral crystal with a distinctive blue tint, different from the others in this cavern by virtue of its oblong dimensions and blue coloration. The crystal is fastened to a pendant chain, upon which dangles a distinctively shaped piece of metal, elegantly bifurcated into two twin prongs.]]
>   [[These are the remains of Chiseqi, the Shent carver whose memories the party may locate in the]][[Guarded Neighbors]] Event.
>   > [!tip] Exploration
>   > #### A Carver's Demise
>   >
>   > Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 14)** check can identify the crystal as a Recording Crystal, a magical gemstone that can be used to store spoken words and replay them in a language familiar to the listener. Only advanced mages are capable of the technique to create such crystals, but they can be activated by anyone regardless of magical talent.
>   >
>   > - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>   > - **Previously Identified Crystal:** The character automatically succeeds on this check if they identified a similar crystal in the [[Guarded Neighbors]] Event.
>   If characters touch the crystal while concentrating on the intention for the crystal to replay its recording, a voice emanates from it:

>   > The hesitant sound of a woman's voice emanates from the crystal — high-pitched and fluttering with anxiety, although carrying an undercurrent of sorrow.
>   >
>   > > My dear friend, I am sorry I was not here to meet you. Orders have come. We are leaving the Signalpost. We go to the Sunwell to make a stand until a portal can extract us. This land is lost to the encroaching darkness. Return north to your haven peaks and be safe, I implore you.
>   > >
>   > > Live, Chiseqi, and remember our friendship as I will cherish yours.
>   The message bears no indication of its author, however, the party may speculate as to whose voice is recorded here. The message was recorded by Golarne, a researcher stationed at the [[Aedir Signalpost]], who formed an unlikely companionship with Chiseqi in the months following [[The Shattering]].
>   Attached to the recording crystal is an unidentified [[Clarion Fork]], a device used in conjunction with certain Scrying Lenses to disrupt invisibility fields and reveal objects or locations concealed by ancient Aedir magi. If the party investigates Chiseqi's remains and obtains the Clarion Fork, mark the following outcome as completed.
>   `[[/outcome fork]]`
>   In the vicinity of Chiseqi's remains, the party can spot an additional valuable item that may be recovered.

>   > #### Something Useful Remains
>   >
>   > Any character with **Awareness (DC 14, Passive)** notices an intricately carved shard of prismatic crystal face-up on the floor of the cavern. It is engraved with runic markings that ripple with a faint glimmer if illuminated by a source of light.
>   >
>   > Any character who physically inspects the shard and makes a successful **Science (DC 14)** check can infer that it was likely expelled from the large machine during its destruction.
>   >
>   > - **Knowledge: Machines**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>   >

>   > If the party retrieves this item, they obtain an unidentified [[Shard of Mind Grounding]].
