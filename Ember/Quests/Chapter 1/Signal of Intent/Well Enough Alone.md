# Well Enough Alone

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> South of Helkas, a route ascends between looming spires of the Forest of Stone toward a highland ridge overlooking the rocky landscape. In a large clearing near the beginning of this trail, the party discovers the [[Overgrown Sunwell]]. This Exploration Event can be encountered any time the party travels to that Location. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Investigate the overgrown ruin and discern its original purpose.
> - Uncover clues of a terrible battle that happened here long ago when the [[Aedir]] made a stand against Abyssal invaders.
> - Undertake a substantial project to restore the Sunwell to a functioning state by clearing the overgrowth and restoring an [[Aedir Wellstone]].

### The Ruined Sunwell

The party may be enticed to investigate the ruins of the Sunwell and the gleaming metal they spied earlier.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Surveying the Ruin
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 14)** check confirms that the buried structure bears hallmark geometry associating it with Aedir design.
>
> - **Knowledge: Aedir**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character recognizes this specific ruin to be an [[Aedir]] Sunwell and knows the lore presented on the [[Overgrown Sunwell]] page.
>
> The party may survey the ruin for any clues. Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check confirms that the central monument is a singular structure with no evident entrances, and spots a section of the stonework on the rear side of the structure that seems to have been violently caved in. Something was slammed into the stone with extreme force, fracturing it in a radial pattern around the resulting impression. One of the large flora-covered mounds that sporadically dot the landscape can be seen at the base of the ruin, near the site of the impact.
>
> #### Climbing the Ruin
>
> Some characters may wish to climb the ruin to see what can be learned from its summit. No check is required as the ruin has adequate handholds, made easier by the heavy vines that cover its facade.

If the party climbs to the top of the Sunwell, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Atop the structure is a shallow basin, about twenty feet in diameter and nearly three feet deep. The stonework is covered in moss, but the absence of pooled water suggests either damage or an intentional drainage mechanism. In the center of the basin is a small aperture, about three inches across, that appears round but upon closer inspection is shaped from ten straight edges of equal length. Whatever the purpose of this socket, it is empty aside from an accumulation of dirt within.

The party may wish to clear one of the mounds of overgrowth to identify what is buried there.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Clearing the Overgrowth
>
> The work is hard labor as the vines are strong and the underbrush is dense. The party may spend 1 hour to attempt a group challenge to clear the debris, with each party member rolling one of the following checks or performing one of the alternative actions:
>
> - **Athletics (DC 16)** to clear the debris through brute force.
> - **Science (DC 14)** to perform a controlled burn or other similar technique.
> - **Wilderness (DC 13)** to use their knowledge of flora to identify specific vines to cut that weaken the fabric of the verdant covering to be more easily removed.
> - Use the Ennervate action from **Talent: Rune: Death** to cause plants to wither and recede.
>
> If at least half of the group members obtain a successful outcome, the task is successful. If the party fails the task, each member of the party becomes demoralized from the **Unrelenting Vines (Hazard 4, Bludgeoning, Fortitude, Morale)**.

### An Ancient Battleground

If the party clears enough of the overgrowth to identify what is buried underneath, read the following aloud.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You clear the clinging vines, leafy underbrush, and creeping moss away from one of the larger heaps, revealing a mound of dark earth from which protrudes the mangled and broken remnants of strange automatons. You notice heads plated with unsettling masks, powerful metal cores, and twisted limbs with finely articulated parts — all patinaed by oxidation and rust. The remains within these charnel mounds are coated in limestone deposits, grasped by taut vines, and covered in moss — like new muscle, sinew, and skin grown atop ancient metal bones.

This site was a small battleground where the remnant forces of the Aedir made a final stand against the Abyssal incursion in the aftermath of The Shattering. The party may wish to inspect the remains closely.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Battlefield Archaeology
>
> Any character who inspects the construct remains and makes a successful **Science (DC 16)** check identifies them as Aedir automatons, constructed by the ingenious artificers of the ancient empire.
>
> - **Knowledge: Aedir**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character identifies two specific types of constructs present here, models which historians believe were called "Skirmishers" and "Sentinels." Skirmishers were lithe but mostly humanoid in form, capable of extruding deadly blades of energy from their limbs. Sentinels were hulking four-armed bipeds with an array of mechanical weaponry. Both models of construct are undeniably categorized as machines of war.
>
> Any character who searches for clues and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 12)** check determines that the forces assembled here suffered terrible destruction at the hands of some unknown force. There are numerous construct remains, but no clue about the nature of the attackers can be found.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Warfare**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character carefully considers the weaponry of these constructs and the damage to their chassis. While there is no direct evidence to support the hypothesis, it seems as if these constructs could possibly have been destroyed by their own armaments, or ones similar to them.

### The Researcher's Remains

If the party investigated the construct remains, the party member with the highest **Awareness (DC passive)** notices an additional clue.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Hidden by the hulking form of one of the largest constructs, nestled within a gaping cleft in its ravaged chassis, you notice the telltale form of humanoid bones. The bones are badly deteriorated, but there is a recognizable shape of a body curled in a fetal position as if taking shelter or overcome with grief. Dangling from the bleached spine of the remains is a delicate chain pendant bearing the broken stump of a carved prismatic crystal. Upon the tightly coiled finger bone of the body's left hand remains a delicately coiled signet ring.

The body here is that of Golarne, one of the Aedir researchers who was stationed at the Signalpost. It is impossible for the party to identify the remains here, but some clues may be gained enabling the party to infer her identity elsewhere.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Golarne's Fate
>
> The crystal pendant is a broken and unidentified [[Shard of Mind Grounding]] which cannot be repaired by any known means. Inspection of the crystal pendant reveals no immediate clue as to its origin or purpose — however, characters with **Knowledge: Shent** who also succeed on a **Society (DC 15)** check can recognize the pendant as being crafted by Shent carvers.
>
> - If the party retrieved the [[Shard of Mind Grounding]] from [[Multifaceted]], they can immediately recognize this as a broken version of that item.
>
> The signet ring is [[Golarne's Signet Ring]] and is initially unidentified. Inspection of the ring offers only a single clue to its nature. Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 12)** check can identify an engraved glyph on the flat surface of one side of the ring.
>
> - Characters who understand **Language: Towyr** or have some other means of translating it can identify the glyph as the analogue of the modern letter "G," written in Towyr.
>
> The corpse is too ancient and disintegrated for any attempts at magical communication with it to succeed.

### Restoration of the Sunwell

The party may reason that the Aedir Sunwell can be restored.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Restoration Project
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 22)** check understands that restoration of the Sunwell would require fully clearing away overgrowth from the structure and inserting a functional [[Aedir Wellstone]] capable of powering the monument.
>
> - **Knowledge: Aedir**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Clearing the overgrowth from the Sunwell requires expending 2 days of hard physical labor for a group of 4 workers.
>
> - Laborers from Helkas or Yakoshta may be recruited for the project at an expense of  **-4** per day.
>
> There is no immediate clue about where to procure the necessary Wellstone.

If the party at any point performs the necessary restoration steps, mark the following outcome as completed (revisiting the Event if needed) and award [[Attunement]] as appropriate.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Region Map Change
>
> Marking the Outcome below changes the appearance of the Overgrown Sunwell on the Region Map to reflect its now-active state.

`[[/outcome restored]]`

#### Luxarum Attunement: Sunwell Restored

Any character who helps restore the Sunwell advances their **Attunement: Luxarum (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Abyss Attunement: Sunwell Defaced

Any character who intentionally destroys or defaces the Sunwell advances their **Attunement: The Abyss (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Sunwell Usage

If the Sunwell is restored, it can be used once per day to direct its solar energy as part of enchantment or spellcraft. The mechanics for this are not yet implemented and will be added as a separate Standalone Event in a future update.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> Having investigated the ruins of the Overgrown Sunwell — potentially discovering a number of clues in the process — the party may continue their journey. If the party intends to investigate the rumors of the hidden tower upon the bluffs, they will proceed south along the highland trail and encounter the [[The Tower Revealed]] Event.
>
> If the party did not activate the Sunwell during this visit, they may do so at any later point according to the same mechanics described earlier in this Event. Whenever the Sunwell is restored, whether now or at a later point, mark the [[Well Enough Alone]] Outcome as complete.
