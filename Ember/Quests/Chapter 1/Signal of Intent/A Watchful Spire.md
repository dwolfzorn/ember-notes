# A Watchful Spire

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs in [[Helkas]] once at least 2 days (48 hours) have passed since the completion of [[The Smoke Clears]]. The party meets **Lorettia Ben-Wren**, the eccentric proprietor of the occultist shop known as [[The Last Satchel]], who has a bold claim about a mysterious landmark in the region. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Speak with Lorettia to learn more about her warning and ask questions about what she knows (namely, the general location of the [[Aedir Signalpost]] southwest of Helkas).
> - Ask around town to learn if anyone else has additional information to offer.
> - Earn a modest reward from Lorettia for investigating the tower, along with the potential for greater rewards via magistrate [[Sadri Zhalimorne]] for putting the matter to rest.

### Lorettia's Concern

If the characters are willing to listen to her story, **Lorettia Ben-Wren** (Chaotic Neutral, Waerd Human, she/her) has a potentially troubling tale to tell — one that involves the location of a mysterious tower on the southeastern edge of the Forest of Stone, which Lorettia believes to be the base of operations for the recent attack on Helkas.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Conversation with Lorettia Ben-Wren
>
> Lorettia is typically a passionate student of Ember's supernatural wonders, and is often eager to trade anecdotal evidence with fellow enthusiasts. Today, however, her enthusiasm is replaced with equal measures of apprehension and concern. Following the apparent rejection from Sadri for aid, Lorettia sees the characters as kindred spirits in her pursuit for answers.
>
> During the course of the conversation, characters can attempt to discern the sincerity of Lorettia's words with a successful **Deception (DC 13)** check, which reveals her words and intentions to be true (to her, at least).

If the characters are interested in Lorettia's tale, the beleaguered occultist has the following things to say about the matter at hand:

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What are you talking about?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Last night, I went foraging for radiant bog caps — they're a kind of rare mushroom that only grows under the light of the moons — and my excursion took me on a path I hadn't really ever tread before, southwest of town, down near Yakoshta. As I was gathering my things to head back to town, I caught a glimpse of something standing above the southeastern edge of the forest. A tower of some kind, tall enough to see the entire valley. And the strangest part yet: the tower disappeared nearly as soon as I saw it. But it sort of "flickered" there a moment before it was gone again, like a candle flame getting snuffed out.
>
> Lorettia takes a quick look around to see if anyone's listening, raising her voice a bit in the process.
>
> > I know it sounds rather silly, but you must believe me when I say: I know I saw something. I'm just not entirely sure what that "something" was. I'd wager it has something to do with the recent attack. Those Otherhood raiders must be using the tower as some kind of remote headquarters. It makes perfect sense to me. I can't prove it, but something strange is afoot atop that overlook near Yakoshta.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Where is the tower?
>
> **A:**
>
> Lorettia levels a finger toward the southern horizon, at the edge of the Forest of Stone.
>
> > Nearly ten miles south of town, east of Yakoshta. I saw the flickering tower standing high on the butte — almost tall enough to see the entire Forest of Stone at a glance. Given the brambles and overgrowth, it's a miracle I saw anything at all. Magic is at play here. I'd bet my wand on it.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What makes you believe this?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Call it a gut feeling, but something tells me this isn't just another Shent ruin yet to be discovered — nor is it a figment of my imagination. I'm aware that around Helkas I'm known to be a bit … eccentric. But my occult practices have strengthened my intuitions, not dulled them.
> >
> > The attack on Helkas during Dusktide was rather convenient, wasn't it? Don't you think there's some validity to the notion that these rogues were stationed nearby, cloaked by magic and guile?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What should we do?
>
> **A:**
>
> > If I were as formidable as the "Heroes of Helkas," I'd go investigate the situation myself. But alas, I am but a simple apothecary.
>
> She looks over your group with a wry, encouraging grin.
>
> > After all, mysteries like these always have some kind of gold-plated reward at the end of their unraveling, don't they? All jokes aside, I'd hate to see our precious little town waylaid yet again as soon as you leave us.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Offering a reward?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I don't have much in the way of monetary reward for an endeavor like this. However, since this is a matter of public concern — whether anyone believes me or not — I'd love to make it worth your while, at least so we can get some answers and sleep well around here.
>
> Lorettia rubs her chin for a brief moment, weighing her options.
>
> > How does a ten-percent discount at The Last Satchel sound to you? I can also provide you with some provisions to help you on your way: a couple of potions, perhaps? If you wish, I can send you off with a bit of a care package to aid in the effort. What do you say?

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Quest Rewards
>
> If the party agrees to take up Lorettia's task and investigate the presence of the mysterious "flickering tower" situated on the southeastern edge of the Forest of Stone, she promptly provides the following items to aid them in their journey:
>
> - 2 [[Healing Elixir]]
> - 1 [[Rallying Tonic]]
> - 1 [[Antitoxin]]
>
> The 10% discount at [[The Last Satchel]] is only available upon successful completion of [[Traversing the Tower]]. Characters who wish to press Lorettia for a larger reward are met with promises of restitution if the investigation produces results substantial enough to be leveraged with Sadri and the other citizens of Helkas.

#### Aura Attunement: Encouragement

Any character who encourages Lorettia's conspiratorial outlook and agrees with her that the "flickering tower" is worth investigating advances their **Attunement: Aura (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Cora Attunement: Rationalism

Any character who overtly expresses skepticism to Lorettia or downplays the likelihood that the "flickering tower" is involved in the attack advances their **Attunement: Cora (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### Word About Town

After discussing the matter with Lorettia, the party can ask around town to seek further evidence from locals about whether or not there is any truth to the occultist's claims.

> [!info] Social
> #### Gathering Information
>
> If the party chooses to seek out details that may corroborate or refute Lorettia's claims, the following checks can help them gather the appropriate information. Various citizens of Helkas — including the assorted NPCs the party has met throughout their adventures — have their own opinions about Lorettia and her tale. Portions of these individual accounts are detailed in the Q&A questions below the following checks. We encourage you to lean on roleplay throughout the party's information gathering process, but please feel free to combine the checks listed here with the dialogue featured below them.
>
> #### General Knowledge
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 12)**check while asking around town is able to round up a few general details from locals. Lorettia is well-known for her absentmindedness and perpetually-distracted nature. Most folks are aware of her indulgence in mind-altering substances, and are quick to blame Lorettia's confusion about the so-called "flickering tower" on her lifestyle. Despite her flights of fancy, Lorettia is a skilled apothecary and learned occultist, whose knowledge and acumen have proven to be invaluable to the citizens of Helkas throughout the years.
>
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Trade**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character also finds one lone denizen of Helkas who supports Lorettia's claims with a half-forgotten anecdote about a traveling merchant who came through town several moons ago, who spoke of an "illusory monument" they spotted while traveling through the Forest of Stone. No one thought much of it at the time, and the traveler's story was never investigated or taken seriously.
>
> A successful **Deception (DC 13)** check confirms the relative veracity of the information that's been provided (based on how much truthful information a particular NPC actually knows).
>
> #### Specific Skepticism
>
> Meanwhile, a successful **Society (DC 18)** check introduces some skepticism from one or more folks about the [[Otherhood of Fortune]] operating out of the Forest of Stone via a permanent garrison — these people highlight the Otherhood's primary involvement in the coastal town of [[Seawall]].
>
> - **Knowledge: Crime**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** [[Sadri Zhalimorne]]
>
> **A:**
>
> > She means well, but Lorettia's attentions tend to drift, often leading her to places that indulge her unfettered curiosities. There is no tower on the hill overlooking Yakoshta. Our friends at Yakoshta would have seen it by now, let alone someone here in Helkas.
>
> Sadri pauses and takes a breath, as if reconsidering.
>
> > I'll tell you what: if you have the time, I'm willing to pay your group a commission of 10 gold pieces to scout the land east of Yakoshta. It's not a lot, but it's all we have for such a non-issue. And truth be told, it would be nice to soothe Lorettia's worrying. Consider it a favor for the whole town.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Reward from Sadri
>
> Sadri's offer of  **10** is available for the characters once they return from a thorough scout of the southeastern perimeter of the Forest of Stone, offered on good faith thanks to the party's heroic involvement in the Dusktide attack.
>
> The first character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 15)** or **Diplomacy (DC 17)** check is able to convince Sadri to sweeten the deal, resulting in an additional reward of 1 unit of [[Steelmarrow]] for a successful scouting mission to the so-called "flickering tower" (paid upon the party's return, following a full report to Sadri and her allies).

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Briannon Frost
>
> **A:**
>
> > There's no denying Lorettia is good at what she does. And while she's one of the town's more eccentric personalities, her head and heart are full of noble intentions, as far as I can tell. Any threat to Helkas is worth investigating, as far as I'm concerned.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Cordayne Kohle
>
> **A:**
>
> > The last thing this town needs is more trouble. It's hard to say whether Lorettia is the source of the trouble herself in this case. I'm afraid I just don't buy it. Helkas is the only settlement worth mentioning for miles. If there was a landmark out there near Yakoshta, we'd know. Somebody would have seen it. Besides, those ruffians came in on the heels of drakes — who knows what beasts of burden they have at their disposal to traverse the plateau?
> >
> > If you insist on investigating, I recommend you speak with Sadri before setting off. The town has its own interests, after all.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Eveline Doss
>
> **A:**
>
> > If you ask me, this whole area is full of weird shit and even weirder reactions to it. If I had the time, I'd climb up the hillside myself to settle the debate. Sounds like a mystery worth solving to me. But what do I know?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Hode Cinderhaft
>
> **A:**
>
> > I've heard a lot of fish stories while mining these hills, but this one takes the cake. Hell, if you make it out there and actually find an invisible tower standing over Yakoshta that we've somehow missed all this time, I've got a bottle of Arcturian brandy with your name on it.

### Concluding the Event

After hearing Lorettia's conspiratorial concerns and asking about town, the party can make ready to depart for whatever next step of their journey awaits.
