# Guarded Neighbors

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event occurs if the party investigates a specific section of the [[Kaleidoscope Caverns]] a few miles northeast of [[Yakoshta]]. By exploring this cavern, the characters can:
>
> - Discern fragmented clues about how this place was used by the ancient [[Shent]] culture.
> - Discover lost recordings that describe an unlikely relationship between a Shent carver and an Aedir researcher stationed nearby.
> - Translate a sequence of murals describing the calamitous events of [[The Shattering]].
> - Locate a hidden cache of supplies which contains some curious antiquities.

### Investigating the Pedestal

The party will likely begin exploration of this cavern by inspecting the crystal pedestal at the mouth of the cave mentioned at the conclusion of the introduction. Each of the pedestal's three sides has script in a different language.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Reading the Pedestal
>
> The three sides of the pedestal feature script written in **Language: Pathward**, **Language: Towyr**, and **Language: Common**.
>
> - Characters who know one of these languages can each recognize and read the language they know, without making a check.
> - Characters can also read each language by using the **Talent: Versatile Translator** talent.
> - For languages that are not familiar to the characters, the language can be identified with a successful **Society (DC 15)** check. Characters who have encountered the language before can also identify it with a successful **Science (DC 15)** check to notice the similarities.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 14)** check can confirm that one side uses an early version of the language that became the Common Coin (also known as Common). It is readable but distinct from the modern language.

Each set of symbols on the pedestal translates to the same phrase:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > Our journey ends here. We cannot foresee what will yet come, so we leave these words for whomever comes after.
> >
> > Illuminate the past by speaking our words and telling our tale. Begin with the beginning.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Activating the Pedestal
>
> Characters who succeed on a **Deception (DC 15)** check can guess that they should speak the words "the beginning."
>
> - Speaking the words in either **Language: Common** or **Language: Pathward** is straightforward.
> - Speaking the words in **Language: Towyr** is difficult, as scholars do not know how to pronounce the words — characters must succeed on a **Society (DC 18)** check to puzzle it out well enough to activate the pedestal.

When the pedestal is activated:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A glow emanates from within the purple depths of the carved crystal, bathing the area immediately around it with a soft lavender light. This light reflects ever so subtly from one crystal in the cave to the next, making the images carved into the walls stand out more clearly. This is not one large overlapping image but four separate murals, each on a different surface of the cavern walls.

### Seeing the Light

If the pedestal has been activated and is emitting light, characters with **Awareness (DC 16, Passive)** notice a crystal near the pedestal that appears to be somewhat out of place.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The sharp lines of the crystal don't connect the way they should, as if it was somehow removed from its resting place and then wedged back, and its glow is a slightly bluer tint than the other crystals nearby.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Recording Crystals
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 14)** check can identify the crystal as a Recording Crystal, a magical gemstone that can be used to store spoken words and replay them in a language familiar to the listener. Only advanced mages are capable of the technique to create such crystals, but they can be activated by anyone regardless of magical talent.
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Previously Identified Crystal:** The character automatically succeeds on this check if they identified a similar crystal in the [[Multifaceted]] Event.

If characters touch the crystal while concentrating on the intention for the crystal to replay its recording, a voice comes out of it:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A voice rings out from the crystal, young-sounding and bright, speaking in an exaggerated whisper.
>
> > I know. This is not a time to dawdle. This is a time to work. But my hands have made these same motions so many times and there's something to be learned from hiding a message in plain sight.
> >
> > If you discover this small rebellion, feel free to assign me an extra task. Write "Chiseqi" down on your list as many times as you wish. As I have been told more than once when struggling toward a new goal, the accomplishment is generally worth the consequence.
> >
> > I shall leave these notes as annotations to my carving, perhaps a small hope that our work will endure and someone may yet hear them.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Hidden Recordings
>
> Somewhere nearby to each of the four murals in the cavern is another Recording Crystal. These crystals are placed unobtrusively to evade notice except for the keen-eyed.
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 15)** while inspecting a mural discovers a Recording Crystal.

When the party investigates and discovers a Recording Crystal, the messages should be revealed in the following order, regardless of the order in which the murals are investigated.

#### Recorded Message 1

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > I am better at erasing my tracks than I thought — so far as I can tell, no one has read my last message. I quite like the idea of being able to speak into the void with no idea of who might listen — it's freeing, almost like speaking to the moons.
> >
> > I can tell **you** that I am growing weary of the chisel, or that these crystals sometimes make my head throb, or that I'm probably more curious than I should be about the tower the Aedir Empire is trying to hide up in the cliffs. I wouldn't admit it to anyone else, but the tower fascinates me. I wish there was some way to say hello.

#### Recorded Message 2

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > I have to tell someone, and you're all that I have, so here's my small confession: I went up to the cliffs today, to try to get a glimpse of what the Aedir do on that tower. I'm not sure why I'm so intrigued. Maybe it's that the Aedir are a bit of a mystery to me — they're arrogant and powerful and they appear to be everywhere, but we Shent don't interact much with the Empire. After all, we're fairly sure they're going to break the world one day. So why do I want to get closer?
> >
> > Perhaps it is curiosity, perhaps boredom, or perhaps it is an intuition that we must connect with those we distrust in order to survive this breaking world.

#### Recorded Message 3

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > I saw someone. Up on the cliffs. Orbis only knows why I've taken to going up there so frequently. Taking "walks," I say. In truth just staring at where I think the tower is, even though I lack the arcana to perceive it. There's probably a lesson in that, though I can't quite puzzle it out.
> >
> > All I know is that staring is why I saw her, running through the cliffs for just a moment, glancing back over her shoulder. As if she shouldn't be there. We have that, at least, in common.

#### Recorded Message 4

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > The woman I saw? A researcher in the invisible tower, which they call the "signalpost" — more likely a "spy post", but I digress. Her name is Golarne.
> >
> > We have talked together each evening beneath Orbis' shimmer. Perhaps we both know better, and yet the spirit moon has willed us forward. Golarne swears that she is only there for research, but the Empire doesn't waste an opportunity. She doesn't appear to enjoy alchemy more than I enjoy chiseling, but at least we can talk to each other and I can stop bothering you.
> >
> > She even gifted me a few trinkets that I've buried near the northern mural. The world is a shaky place these days. It never hurts to be ready.

### The Four Murals

There are four separate murals within the cavern, each of which is a piece of swirling line art that incorporates three-to-four runic symbols in a larger picture.

For each mural, its runes and picture can be used together to determine the meaning of the mural, and hint at a phrase that can be spoken aloud to activate the mural. Each mural embeds a number of key runes within its design. Decoding a mural requires identifying these runes, then uttering them aloud in the correct order.

Once a mural has been decoded and activated, it begins to glow with lambent illumination, casting bright light in the immediate vicinity for 1 hour. This light does not trigger any effects from Kaleidoscope Crystals in the cavern.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Interpreting the Murals
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 14)** or **Arcana (DC 14)** check recognizes these as runic symbols once used by the giants of the Shent.
>
> - **Language: Pathward**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> For each mural, characters may attempt the following checks to assist with solving that mural's puzzle:
>
> - **Awareness (DC 14)** to discern how many runes are incorporated into each individual picture by separating those runes from the broader picture.
> - **Wilderness (DC 14)** to clear away the natural decay that has affected the murals, including any heartmoss that has grown into the stone or mold that obscures the symbol.
> - **Arcana (DC 16)** to interpret what the individual runes on each stone mean. Once a rune is translated on a specific stone, it remains translated for any future stones without a check.
>
> When making any of the above checks:
>
> - **Language: Pathward**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on the check.

#### Signara Attunement: Decode a Mural

Any character who helps decode one or more of the murals advances their **Attunement: Signara (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### The Eastern Mural

The eastern mural depicts a scene of the ancient Shent traveling a great distance into a rocky landscape.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A swirling line drawing chiseled into the cavern wall depicts a group of humanoids standing around an empty vehicle, their backs to an unfamiliar line of rocky cliffs. A few of the cliffs appear to have shallow puddles on them. The cliffs are steep, but have lines that seem out of place, as if something has been traced onto them.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Decoding the Eastern Mural
>
> This mural contains three runes: **Shent**, **Stone**, and **Travel**.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 14)** check knows that the landscape reflects what the Forest of Stone would have looked like thousands of years ago, and that the puddles are likely natural ooze pools.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 14)** check recalls that the Shent are thought to have traveled through the Arctus Plateau 2000 years ago, in vehicles that look roughly like the travel rune.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 15)** check can interpret the runes to roughly mean:
>
> > “The Shent travel to the stone."
>
> - **Language: Pathward**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> If the word "travel" is spoken aloud within 20 feet of the mural:
>
> > The mural begins to emit a soft glow, and the runes that make up the humanoids appear to shift slightly on the wall, as if they were waving out at whoever watches them.

### The Southern Mural

The southern mural depicts a scene of the Shent gazing at the sky and venerating the moons.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The lines of this drawing depict a group of humanoids standing on a rocky plain, staring at an object in the sky above them. It appears as if rays or beams are coming down from the object, touching both the humanoids and a pool in the distance. Each ray looks like a slightly different symbol.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Decoding the Southern Mural
>
> This mural contains four runes: **Shent**, **Stone**, **Reverence**, and **Water**.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 14)** check recalls that the Shent held deep reverence for the moons.
>
> - **Critical Success** or **Attunement: Mayis**: The character also identifies the circular object as the water moon, Mayis.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 14)** check can place the location as somewhere in the vicinity of Yakoshta.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 15)** check can interpret the runes to roughly mean:
>
> > “The Shent of the stone honor and celebrate the moon and the creatures of the water.”
>
> - **Language: Pathward**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> If the words "honor" or "moon" are spoken aloud within 20 feet of the mural:
>
> > The rays on the mural appear to glisten and glow, and light shines from the depiction of the moon above, creating moonbeam-like light on the cavern around it.

### The Western Mural

The western mural depicts the arrival of the Aedir and the construction of their tower on the bluffs high above this area.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> In this mural, a large and imposing tower on a cliff blocks the orb in the sky. The tower is surrounded by a group of faceless humanoids made of sharp, angled strokes, different from those on the other stones. They stare down at the humanoids seen in the previous stone.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Decoding the Western Mural
>
> This mural contains three runes: **Shent**, **Tower**, **Others**.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check can identify the tower as having been in the direction they are traveling.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 14)** check recalls that the Aedir were active around this time period, and that the faceless humanoids depicted may be Aedir.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 14)** check can interpret the runes to roughly mean:
>
> > “The others watch the Shent."
>
> - **Language: Pathward**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> If the word "watch" is spoken aloud within 20 feet of the mural:
>
> > The glow in this mural seems to emanate from the tower depicted in it. The tower flickers almost like a fire, as do the faces of the humanoids surrounding it, almost as if there is something dangerous and out of control in their very presence. The crystals near the mural seem to flicker in response, casting a glowing light on the ground.

### The Northern Mural

The northern mural depicts the calamitous consequences of the Shattering and the destruction of the Sun Tower.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The land beneath the tower in this mural is splintered and shattered. Glyphs appear to have been created with the jagged lines in the land. In the midst of the splintered ground, the faceless humanoids flee the tower.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Decoding the Northern Mural
>
> This mural contains three runes: **Tower**, **Others**, **Break**.
>
> All characters know, without a check, that this stone references the Shattering, an event in which the Aedir broke the world.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 15)** check can interpret the runes to roughly mean:
>
> > "The world breaks. The others go."
>
> - **Language: Pathward**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> If the word "break" is spoken aloud within 20 feet of the mural:
>
> > Light appears to pour out from across the mural, lighting the nearby walls and floor.

### A Hidden Cache

The Shent carver Chiseqi stashed some supplies and trinkets near the northern mural in a crevice between large stalagmites.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Locating the Cache
>
> Any character who searches the cavern for items of interest and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 16)** check discovers the hidden cache.
>
> - **Listened to Recorded Message #4:** The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check if they learned of the cache's existence from listening to Recorded Message #4.
>
> Once the cache has been located, it can be easily removed from the ground. It contains:
>
> - Antique coins worth  **2**.
> - 1 [[Healing Elixir]] and 1 [[Rallying Elixir]] sealed within unbroken glass vials.
> - A charm bracelet, whose gems can be used as a Spell Component or sold for  **50**.
> - A carefully folded note tucked neatly at the bottom of the cache.

The note is written in **Language: Towyr**. If inspected by someone who knows the language or has the means to translate it, they learn the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The ancient note is written in a scribbled and uneven hand on ancient vellum.
>
> > Chiseqi,
> >
> > I wanted you to know how glad I am that we met. I remember your words and will use them to focus my task during the days ahead. It won't make the days go any faster, but I will see you beneath the stars once my work is done and I promise we will go exploring soon.
> >
> > As thanks for your friendship, I invite you to see our work as I do. Trace this glyphsign upon the lens of the clarion shrine I showed you. I will hear it when you do and meet you there.
> >
> > Golarne
>
> Beneath the signature is a sketched diagram of a spiral with three diagonal lines of differing lengths cutting across its curvature.

The depicted glyph is unique, and cannot be recognized as belonging to any particular language or known spellcraft. If the party discovers this note and the associated glyphsign depicted within, mark the following outcome as completed.

`[[/outcome glyph]]`
