# Good Ooze, Bad Ooze

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Combat Event occurs as the party travels through one of the [[Kaleidoscope Caverns]], wherein they are set upon by dangerous oozes. In the battle's aftermath, they meet [[Tauric]], a young [[Hulg'run]]. By battling the oozes and speaking with Tauric, the party can:
>
> - Learn more about the dangerous oozes in the area.
> - Consider Tauric's proposal to accompany him back to the settlement of [[Yakoshta]].
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Ooze Pools" Level of the [[Kaleidoscope Caverns]] Area Map.

### A Distant Glimmer

Characters can see a glint of metal in the distance, but only faintly. It stands out from the reflection of the kaleidoscope crystals — brighter and smaller, like a beam of light reflecting off of a metal object.

What the party does not know is that both the piles of iron and the heap of gold coins are oozes, using their [[False Appearance]] trait. It is impossible for the party to identify that the glimmering scraps are oozes due to this false appearance.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating the Glimmer
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14, Passive)** check identifies the following:
>
> - Two piles of iron scraps arranged in neat stacks that reflect light from the kaleidoscope crystals. Characters with **Knowledge: Crafts** discern that they are in good condition and could be used for crafting or repairs.
> - A small heap of copper coins. It looks as if someone was gathering copper coins and then either forgot about them or had to vacate the area in a hurry, leaving them behind. Any characters with **Knowledge: Trade** can estimate the pile as being worth approximately 1 gold in value.

### Ooze Ambush!

Once a character comes within 20 feet of any of the three oozes, all three reveal themselves and combat begins. Unless the party was particularly wary of the metal piles or had prepared themselves for an attack in some way, they begin combat **Unaware** .

> **[[Luminous Copper Ooze]]**
>
> Level 0.5 · Slime Metallic Ooze
>
> ![Luminous Copper Ooze](../../../assets/image069.webp)
>
> An amorphous slime of a creature slowly oozes forward, dragging bits of flaked copper and the occasional crystal within its gelatinous body. Though it seems formless as it moves, it reaches out in a long tentacle-like arm when confronted with a fight, readying itself to strike.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Luminous Copper Ooze Tactics
>
> The [[Luminous Copper Ooze]] begins combat as an indistinguishable pile of copper coins.
>
> At the start of combat, it will advance aggressively toward the party.
>
> Over the course of combat, the Luminous Copper Ooze will prioritize the following actions and abilities:
>
> - In melee, the Luminous Copper Ooze will strike out with its [[Pseudopod]] attack.
> - When struck in combat with a metal melee weapon, the Luminous Copper Ooze can use its [[Corrode Weapon]] ability to degrade the weapon's quality.

> [!abstract] Luminous Iron Ooze
> **[[Luminous Iron Ooze]]**
>
> Level 1 · Slime Metallic Ooze
>
> ![Luminous Iron Ooze](../../../assets/image066.webp)
>
> The viscous body of the luminous iron ooze is a silvery gray, filled with small fragments of metal and the occasional piece of glittering crystal. As it moves, its body shapes and reshapes itself, stretching and contracting in a viscous pool that extends a single gray-iron tentacle.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Luminous Iron Ooze Tactics
>
> The [[Luminous Iron Ooze]] begins combat an indistinguishable pile of iron scrap.
>
> At the start of combat, it will advance aggressively toward the party.
>
> Over the course of combat, the Luminous Iron Ooze will prioritize the following actions and abilities:
>
> - In melee, the Luminous Iron Ooze will use its [[Pseudopod]] attack and [[Magnetic Disarm]] reaction to damage and disarm enemies.

In addition to the oozes, the characters must navigate a dangerous hazard.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Contaminated Pool
>
> Prolonged exposure to the oozes and their internal contaminants has left the large pool in the center of the cavern dangerously electrified. Any character who steps into or starts their turn within the large pool nearest to where the oozes appeared risks harm from the **Electrified Pool (Hazard 4, Electricity, Fortitude)**. The pool, while dangerous, is too watered down for its contents to be useful if collected.

### After the Fight

After the party's encounter with the oozes, a small figure emerges from behind one of the kaleidoscope crystals.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A statue-like figure rises slowly from behind one of the large purple crystals. He is trembling as he steps out into the cavern's purple light, then rushes toward you, stumbling as he runs and waving his hands frantically back and forth.

> [!abstract] Tauric
> **[[Tauric]]**
>
> Level 1 · Hulg'run Scout
>
> ![Tauric](../../../assets/image102.webp)
>
> The young man's gray body appears to be carved from rock, with lines of blue agate running through the stone like veins. His color is matched by the gelatinous body of the small blueish-green ooze that sits on his shoulder, nestled into a hollow that seems to have been carved for the purpose.

If the party defeated the hostile oozes:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > Oh, thank you! Thank you so much! I thought Squish and I were absolutely done for! And I suppose without your help, we might still be.

If the party has avoided the ooze fight, Tauric instead attempts to usher the party out of the caves, saying:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > Did you see any oozes running about? We just saw some sliding around the cave — better get out of here before they come back!

The Hulg'run quickly introduces himself as [[Tauric]] and his [[Oozeling]] companion as **Squish**. He then asks the party for their help getting home.

> [!info] Social
> #### Tauric's Information
>
> Tauric is happy to talk about anything and everything he knows as long as the party is there to keep him and Squish safe. Key information includes:
>
> - He needs the party to take him to Yakoshta, the settlement outside of the Yakoshta mining operation.
> - His oozeling companion, Squish, is not dangerous. The miners had a friendly relationship with local oozelings like Squish, treating them like pets.
> - A new type of large glowing ooze is attacking everything in sight. These are the oozes that attacked the party.
> - There may be more information about what is happening with the oozes at nearby [[Ooze Island]], which the party may travel to during the [[Stone Life]] Event.
>
> For detailed responses, see below.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Where are you from?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Yakoshta Mine — maybe you've heard of it? It's not far. It's supposed to be one of the best up-and-coming mining operations in or around the Arctus Plateau. At least, that's what Sellen says. And since she's in charge, what she says goes.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why are you here?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I know everyone thinks of oozes as these killing machines, but in Yakoshta, oozes are our little buddies — they help us in the mines. When these larger glowing oozes started attacking, I just had to know why. I was going to check things out at the old ooze pool — I call it Ooze Island — when this group attacked and drove me in here. The pool's usually filled with friendly oozelings, like Squish. Want to swing by on the way back to Yakoshta? I can show you how oozes are supposed to work, and maybe something there will help explain what's making the oozes so angry.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About these hostile oozes?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I have no idea! They appeared a few weeks ago out of nowhere, driving the regular oozes away and attacking everything they run into. They're bigger than our normal oozes and glow all weirdly and there's no way to reason with them. I've tried talking to them, singing to them, everything, and it doesn't matter. They just want to fight.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About your ooze?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Squish is one of the only regular oozes left — these new glowing ones are different. They're mean and they're big and they ran off or killed most of the regular oozes. I do my best to protect Squish from harm, and in return, Squish watches over me like some sort of … like a watch ooze! With everything going on, you should consider getting a protective ooze pal of your own. You can't be too careful these days!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Yakoshta?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We've been out here for a couple of years. Sellen was the one who came up with the idea of mining in one of these old abandoned mines carved out by the giants who used to live in the area — the Shent. Sometimes I wonder if they had giant oozes hanging out with them, but I haven't been able to find any evidence of it in the ruins around here.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Recent changes?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Nothing specific that I could put my finger on, but Squish and I wander a lot, so I don't keep up with what goes on at the mine that much. Sellen would know more — she's back at the settlement and I'm sure she'd love to meet you, even if it's just to thank you for keeping me out of trouble.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As Tauric speaks, the ooze changes position, but it is always somewhere on his body, whether draped around the neck like a scarf, wrapped around the arm like a long bracelet, or shifting from shape to shape in its shoulder perch.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> Once the party has finished speaking with Tauric, they may move on; Tauric is happy to travel with the party to one of two places: Yakoshta (where Tauric lives), or Ooze Island.
>
> - If the party heads to Yakoshta directly, proceed to [[A Miner Matter]].
> - If the party heads to Ooze Island first, proceed to [[Stone Life]].
> - If the party chooses to go elsewhere to explore the Forest of Stone, Tauric decides to make his own way back — when the party reaches Yakoshta, he will be there.
