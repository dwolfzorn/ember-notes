# A Miner Update

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs just after the party has exited the mine, having successfully cleared it of oozes. By surviving their ordeal, the party can:
>
> - Report back to [[Sellen]] on what was found in the mine in [[Updating Sellen]].
> - Learn about [[Jasper]] ideas for ooze farming in [[Jasper's Plan]].
> - Hear [[Tauric]] disagreement with this approach in [[Tauric's Objections]].
>
> During these conversations, the party learns that the source of the contaminant must be in the hills of the Forest of Stone directly above the mine, but that the way up is blocked due to the recent earthquake.
>
> This Event is depicted using the [[Vista: Yakoshta]] Vista.

### Updating Sellen

> [!abstract] Sellen
> **[[Sellen]]**
>
> Level 1 · Hulg'run Trader
>
> ![Sellen](../../../assets/image103.webp)
>
> Between the light from the candles perched on her shoulders, the large pickaxe in one hand, and the clipboard in another, the woman before you seems nothing if not prepared for whatever faces her. The weathering on the carved stone of her body gives the sense that she's got more than a few stories to tell about what has gotten her this far along her journey, but whatever her past has brought her, she hasn't lost either the smile on her face or the determination in her eyes.

Sellen is thrilled that the party has saved the miners and defeated the oozes in the cavern, immediately offering them the promised reward of silver ingots (worth  **200** if the party came to Yakoshta alone, or  **225** if they accompanied Tauric), but she still fears that the mine and settlement may be in danger.

> [!info] Social
> #### Sellen's Worries
>
> Sellen is concerned that the party's actions helped in the short term, but the mine is still in danger as long as the contaminant is not stopped at its source. Given the information Jasper has shared with her, Sellen is sure that the contamination is coming from the hills above the mine, but she doesn't know how to get there.
>
> She can share any of the following:
>
> - Jasper told her about the substance coming into the mine (in the Glowing Ore Pit) that appears to be the reason that the oozes have turned aggressive. If the substance isn't contained, the ooze attacks are likely to start again and threaten the mine.
> - Jasper believes he has a way to contain the dangerous oozes within the mine, but Sellen doesn't trust it. Jasper has always had big plans, and they don't always pan out.
> - Sellen is a little worried about the morale of Yakoshta — between Jasper's ambitions and the attacks, she's worried that her miners may leave in search of work elsewhere.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 14)**check can confirm that Sellen is primarily concerned about the safety of Yakoshta and is giving them all the information she currently has. She trusts the party's expertise and opinion.
>
> #### Clearing the Caravan
>
> If the party asks Sellen for help unblocking the [[Strayhearth Caravan]], she is reluctantly willing to provide the heroes with a [[Barrel of Blast Powder]] in exchange for the party's pledge to seek out and resolve the true source of the contamination after the caravan makes it safely to Helkas. The party can assure Sellen of their commitment to that cause in one of the following ways:
>
> - The party may sincerely pledge to honor this agreement.
> - **Deception (DC 14)**The characters convincingly lie or equivocate about their intentions.
> - **Intimidation (DC 16)**The characters remind Sellen about the extent of disaster that would have occurred without their intervention, convincing her to provide the [[Barrel of Blast Powder]] without any firm commitment in return.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What's bothering Sellen?
>
> **A:**
>
> > From what Jasper's told me, there's something leaking into the mine that seems to be causing the oozes to attack. What's to stop it from turning more oozes into these dangerous things? We need to stop it at its source or Yakoshta is doomed.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The threat to Yakoshta?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We barely got out of the mine alive with your help — I don't want my miners dodging ooze attacks with no support. And even if we did find a way to contain them or defend ourselves better, the mine really needs a big win that lasts, or some of the miners will head somewhere else for work. Especially with everything Jasper's been saying.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Jasper's Plan?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Jasper's always been impatient, but giving him this supervisor position has made it worse. I thought that inner fire would help him oversee the mine, but he's always coming up with some big scheme to make us all rich, and it isn't helpful. If everyone's dreaming big, it's hard for them to be satisfied with the work in front of us.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Source of the Contaminant?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Jasper knows more about where exactly the contaminant is coming from, but I'm pretty sure it'll be in the hills above us in the Forest of Stone. I'm happy to offer another reward if you'll go up there and get rid of whatever is causing the leak.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Helping the Strayhearth Caravan?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Well … I suppose you stuck your neck out to help us in the moment of our need. You say the caravan is trapped behind collapsed rubble that blocks the road?
> >
> > I could be convinced to send you off with a barrel of blast powder if you promise me that, after you make it to Helkas, you'll try and get to the bottom of where this contamination is coming from. If you can solve this situation for us long term, it would mean a lot to Yakoshta and to me.
>
> Sellen rubs her temples and looks as if she's having second thoughts, but she meets your eyes and asks:
>
> > Do I have your word?

### Jasper's Plan

As the party speaks with Sellen, Jasper and Tauric approach, deep in an argument.

> [!abstract] Jasper
> **[[Jasper]]**
>
> Level 1 · Hulg'run Operator
>
> ![Jasper](../../../assets/image071.webp)
>
> The hulg'run man steps carefully, as if he is assessing everything around him with sharp eyes and careful determination. He wears a slight scowl on his face, as if he is above whatever is around him, but the severity of his expression is somewhat undercut by the brilliance of the gems embedded in his face, arms, and legs, which have been carefully polished to a sparkling shine.

> [!abstract] Tauric
> **[[Tauric]]**
>
> Level 1 · Hulg'run Scout
>
> ![Tauric](../../../assets/image102.webp)
>
> The young man's gray body appears to be carved from rock, with lines of blue agate running through the stone like veins. His color is matched by the gelatinous body of the small blueish-green ooze that sits on his shoulder, nestled into a hollow that seems to have been carved for the purpose.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Hushed but angry voices cut through the huzzahs of the crowd as Tauric and Jasper approach. Tauric holds Squish tightly in his hands and loudly whispers:
>
> > I can't believe you would even suggest this in front of Squish! Cover your ears, buddy.
>
> Jasper shakes his head and picks up his pace, gesturing in Sellen's general direction as he does.
>
> > It's not up to you or Squish to make policy. You have to let Sellen decide.

Jasper approaches the group to talk. He wants the party to find the source of the contaminant leaking into the mine and bring it to him so that he can stockpile and study it. This will allow him to start something he calls "ooze mining."

> [!info] Social
> #### Jasper's Ooze Mining Proposition
>
> Jasper explains the following:
>
> - He believes that he can attract oozes to the mine using the rune developed by the Shent.
> - If the miners give these oozes both metal **and** contaminant, they will turn into the dangerous luminous oozes, but this can be done in a pit designed to keep the oozes contained and the miners safe.
> - When the miners need metal, they can "process" one of the luminous oozes and collect what it leaves behind — pure metal.
> - Tauric may be friends with Squish, but the oozes have shown that they are willing and able to turn on the miners — this is the time for pragmatism, not sentiment.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 14)**check can tell that Jasper has a secondary, self-interested motivation for this idea.
>
> - [[Jasper's Diary]]: The character automatically succeeds on this check and knows that Jasper needs the mine to become more profitable so he can pay off urgent debts.
> - **Critical Success**: You sense that Jasper is intensely focused on the profitability of the mine, but for reasons that may go beyond simple greed. He seems to have an urgent need for the money, but is trying hard to hide that urgency behind talk about the viability of the town as a whole.
>
> Jasper is sure that the substance leaking into the mine will be found in the cliffs of the Forest of Stone. He believes there is a safe path up to them, but doesn't know where.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Ooze mining?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Did you see the metal left behind by that ooze creature? The leak that made it larger and more dangerous also seems to have multiplied the amount of metal found in its body. We've been using oozes for a while in the mine, feeding them small bits of metal dust to make them more useful to us. But the amount we can scavenge after they've been stopped from attacking? That's truly impressive.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What is your plan?
>
> **A:**
>
> > All we have to do is create metal oozes, use whatever is leaking down here to make them transform in a place we can control, and we have a metal-producing machine! Instead of mining the ore, we mine the oozes.If you can stop the leak but bring me more of whatever that substance is, I can get started on the safety precautions here — proper storage for the substance, a containment system for the transformed oozes, and whatever else would be necessary to keep the workers of this mine safe.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Effect on the oozes?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Tauric's ooze may look cute, but the transformed oozes were quick to attack us. Even if we only kill in self-defense while attempting to mine them, I'm sure we'll have plenty of metal at our fingertips. To be completely honest, I'm not sure if we should trust them for more than that, no matter what Tauric and Squish say about it. I'm sure you understand.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Getting oozes?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Were there oozes by that Shent symbol I put up in one of the empty caves? That confirms it! I've been fairly sure that the symbol attracts them, but I was attacked before I could be positive. If it does work, ooze mining will be even better. We take them from that area, give them the metal from oozes we've already processed, and the ooze mine becomes a self-sustaining system.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why do this?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We aren't supposed to be here at Yakoshta forever. From everything Sellen said when she brought us here, this mine is meant to be a way to build capital, create larger mines, and maybe even compete with Trading House Cevher in Ordain. But none of that happens if we aren't making enough profit to survive.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What's in it for you?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I won't lie — I've had my eye on another piece of adornment in Helkas. But I'm not the only one. Some of us would love a little more luxurious city living and a little less camping out in the middle of a forest. Even Hulg'run can get sick of being surrounded by stone.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Source of contaminant?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Given where the leak is coming from, the source must be up in the hills above us — I think the earthquake jarred something loose in all those piles of rock. Tauric might know more — he loves wandering around the Forest of Stone with that ooze of his.

### Tauric's Objections

The more that Jasper speaks, the more upset Tauric becomes, but he attempts to keep himself under control as he speaks, offering to help the party find the contaminant if they agree to clean it up and keep it away from Jasper.

> [!info] Social
> #### Tauric's Plea
>
> Tauric deeply opposes ooze mining, and will share the following:
>
> - Jasper's method of ooze mining is unethical — it tricks the oozes into coming closer to be friends, forces a transformation on them, and then harvests them for the ore they produce.
> - The right thing for the party to do is to find the leak, stop it from coming into the mine, and destroy any contaminant left.
> - He may know a way up to the section of the Forest of Stone that the contaminant is likely coming from, but he will only tell the party if they promise not to give it to Jasper, ending the ooze mining plan.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 12)**check can tell that Tauric feels as if he and Squish are personally under attack.
>
> - **Critical Success**: You sense some guilt. Tauric doesn't contribute much to the mine, as he prefers hanging out with Squish; he wonders if his lack of effort has brought the mine to a place where Sellen might entertain the idea of Jasper's plan.
>
> #### Convincing Tauric to Help
>
> Tauric agrees with Jasper and Sellen about the origin of the leak, and he's sure he knows where it is.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 13)**or **Intimidation (DC 15)**check is able to convince Tauric to help (by either convincing Tauric they won't hand the contaminant over to Jasper, or by threatening to let Yakoshta fall to the ooze invasion by moving on).
>
> Once adequately convinced, Tauric shares that the location can be found near a small lake, which he offers to mark for the party. He notes that there's a large pile of rocks blocking the path, which will have to be cleared if they want to move forward.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why not ooze mining?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Think of Squish here, minding his own business with the other oozes, when he's lured into a trap, force-fed some kind of poison, and then killed so his insides can be sold. I don't care how much more money the mine can make that way — it's just plain wrong.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What about the town?
>
> **A:**
>
> > It'd be wrong to do this to the oozes regardless of what that would mean for our town, but I really don't think our survival hinges on poisoning the creatures of Yakoshta. In fact, who knows what further havoc that could wreak on the local ecosystem, and what danger that might put us in?
> >
> > Anyway, if my time with Squish has taught me anything, it's that the oozes can be our friends and allies. We can still mine this area the same way people mine anywhere else on the Arctus Plateau — Jasper's so-called plan to "save the town" is really just greed.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Source of contaminant?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Squish and I walk around a lot while everyone else is deep in the mine, so we've seen a thing or two.
> >
> > There is a path up to the bluffs high above Yakoshta that you can find. The trailhead is a couple miles south of Helkas. It's a treacherous climb, but the views up there are spectacular. If the contaminant is leaking from something on the bluffs, you'll find it by following the trail.

### Concluding the Event

With the immediate threat to Yakoshta resolved — at least for now — the party can continue on their journey. They should keep in mind that the long-term fate of the settlement likely depends on finding a more permanent solution to the contamination that is pushing the local oozes into uncontrolled aggression.
