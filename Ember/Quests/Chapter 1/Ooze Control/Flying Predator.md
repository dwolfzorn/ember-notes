# Flying Predator

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event occurs as the party makes their way south through the [[Forest of Stone Gazetteer]]. By examining the flying predator and the substance that it regurgitated, the party can:
>
> - Attempt to identify the creature as a [[Suarrok Juvenile]].
> - Examine the gelatinous goo regurgitated by the predator.
> - Potentially discover that this goo contains bits of ooze and an unknown substance.

### A Distressed Predator

The creature has left behind two silvery-gray gelatinous puddles — each about 1 ft. in diameter — as well as a few scattered bits of goo. The puddles are the remains of a [[Luminous Silver Ooze]] and an [[Luminous Iron Ooze]], both made indigestible by a toxic contamination in the nearby Yakoshta mine. The remains have mixed with the creature's caustic digestive fluid, making them difficult to identify.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### About the Creature
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 12, Passive)** check while observing the flying beast recognizes it as a [[Suarrok Juvenile]], an aerial predator that has been spotted in this region before.
>
> - **Knowledge: Monsters**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character also recognizes that this creature is an adolescent, and recalls that the suarrok can grow to be huge in size when fully grown. Battle against even a juvenile suarrok could be deadly, but facing a fully grown adult would likely be disastrous.

### Sickening Goo

The slime puddles are caustic and hazardous, but they can be easily avoided if the characters so choose. If the characters wish to investigate the slime puddles, see below.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Caustic Slime
>
> The digestive fluid within the puddles is acidic to the touch; boils form on their surfaces and pop unexpectedly. Any character who closely examines the goo risk taking damage from **Caustic Slime (Hazard 4, Reflex, Acid, Health)**.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Regurgitated Substance
>
> Characters with **Awareness (DC 12, Passive)** notice that there are three different substances mixed together in the puddles.
>
> Any character with **Medicine (DC 12, Passive)** while examining the remains deduces that the retching was caused by something toxic the creature had eaten, rather than by some other illness.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character also identifies the puddles as the partially digested remains of oozes. They must have been recently eaten, suggesting the presence of oozes within a few miles of the party's current location.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Science (DC 14, Passive)** check correctly deduces that the caustic quality of this substance comes from a toxic agent ingested alongside the organic material — it wasn't the oozes themselves, but something that affected the oozes' biological composition.
>
> - **Knowledge: Alchemy**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> #### Collecting Samples
>
> If party members attempt to touch or collect the substance, they face the hazard described above. The substance can be carried in any standard vial, resulting in [[Caustic Phial]]. Only one such viable sample may be collected from the partially digested remains.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
