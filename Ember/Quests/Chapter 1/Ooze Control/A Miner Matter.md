# A Miner Matter

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs when the party reaches the settlement of [[Yakoshta]] for the first time. By interacting with the town's inhabitants, the party can:
>
> - Learn more about the recent ooze attacks on the settlement and the attached [[Yakoshta Mine]].
> - Agree to go into the mine and rescue miners trapped by the oozes.
>
> This Event is depicted using the [[Vista: Yakoshta]] Vista.

### Meeting Sellen

A middle-aged Hulg'run woman, [[Sellen]], strides toward the party from between the stone buildings of the Yakoshta encampment, quickly covering the ground between them. As she approaches, read or paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Hulg'run strides toward you with her hand extended, but her body is in a fighter’s stance, ready to either greet you or brawl with you, depending on what the situation calls for.
>
> > Welcome, friend — at least, I hope you're a friend! Yakoshta doesn't need any more enemies.

The party's introduction to Sellen changes depending on their encounter with Tauric.

**If the party has Tauric with them:**

Tauric and Sellen greet each other warmly. Tauric introduces her to the party as his sister, Sellen, the leader of this encampment, and asks her if she will give the party a silver ingot as a reward for getting him safely home. Sellen is not happy that he has been wandering off again, but is grateful to the party for returning him safely to Yakoshta. She gives the party a silver ingot worth  **5**.

**If Tauric made his way back to Yakoshta on his own:**

Tauric approaches them. The party receives no reward, but he is happy to see them, saying:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > You made it here! Squish and I barely got back to Yakoshta in one piece — I was worried you were done for!

He asks the party what they have been doing since they left him and introduces them to his sister, Sellen, as capable adventurers.

**If the party has not encountered Tauric:**

Sellen introduces herself directly and asks if the party has run into her brother Tauric, who has wandered off while she was dealing with her current crisis.

### Talking with Sellen

> [!abstract] Sellen
> **[[Sellen]]**
>
> Level 1 · Hulg'run Trader
>
> ![Sellen](../../../assets/image103.webp)
>
> Between the light from the candles perched on her shoulders, the large pickaxe in one hand, and the clipboard in another, the woman before you seems nothing if not prepared for whatever faces her. The weathering on the carved stone of her body gives the sense that she's got more than a few stories to tell about what has gotten her this far along her journey, but whatever her past has brought her, she hasn't lost either the smile on her face or the determination in her eyes.

Sellen is very willing to discuss her current situation. As she talks, other members of the Yakoshta encampment begin walking up, listening intently and occasionally chiming in with agreement. Culturally, the group is all Arcturian, and most are of Hulg'run ancestry, though there are a few Thornlings and Kivahr in the group as well.

> [!info] Social
> #### Sellen's Perspective
>
> Key information that Sellen may share during this conversation:
>
> - Until recently, the Yakoshta miners have been working steadily and productively, with the help of friendly oozes who seem to be native to the area. Recently, though, the oozes have changed and begun attacking.
> - Before these attacks, the miners had a friendly relationship with the oozes and used them in the mines as helpers, but these oozes are different. In fact, the oozes used to be the mine's secret weapon for efficient extraction of materials, but this new type of ooze they've been dealing with is big, angry, and impossible to work with.
> - Oozes began attacking the miners a few weeks ago, shortly after an earthquake. The ooze attacks have been increasing in intensity since. It hasn't been anything they couldn't handle to this point, and Sellen is confident that Yakoshta has things under control with a few new safety protocols in place. There's some rising concern, however, that the situation might not remain stable for much longer.
> - So far, nobody has died, or even gotten that badly hurt, but the risk is certainly there if things keep escalating. The mine foreman, Jasper, has been warning that something needs to be done.
>
> For detailed responses, see below.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the mine?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We've only been mining here for a few years, though I know it's hard to believe that with everything we've built here. Didn't hurt that we started with some good structure in the mine thanks to the Shent who used to live in this area, even if they were a few times our size. It's been pretty smooth sailing, too, at least until recently.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the miners?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We're a pretty close group. I know there are a few among us who think we could be moving faster and digging deeper, but we need to be careful. If we'd been digging willy-nilly when that earthquake hit a few weeks back, someone might have been hit by the rock collapses in the mine — as it is, we still need to build a few things back.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Recent issues?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We've always had oozes around this area. Friendly ones. But after that earthquake a few weeks ago, something changed. Like the ground moving shook the sense out of them. At first it was one or two oozes attacking, and we could mostly keep them in line in the mine or chase them out of the encampment, but it seems like their numbers are growing out of control. If this keeps up, we may have to shut the mine down. I've put new safety protocols in place, but I just can't put my people at risk, and Jasper has folks riled up about the danger.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Before the earthquake?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The oozes were different. Smaller. Friendly. And if we fed them the right bits of metal, useful. Some could have tools stuck to them to make things easier to carry. Some kept the rats out of the mine. But if this is what happens after, it really wasn't worth it.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who runs the mine?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Jasper is my right hand in the mines — keeps the operation running smoothly so I can focus on attracting traders, getting new workers out here, and all of that. We don't always agree on the right way forward — I'm a little more cautious, and he's a bit of a dreamer — but nobody works harder than him in those mines. I've been meaning to make him take some rest, but I'd have an easier time convincing the rocks to mine themselves. He's convinced he knows a way out of all this trouble.

### Trouble in the Mines

At some point, a crisis occurs in the mines, and people come running to Sellen for help. The party will inevitably get caught up in this, and Sellen will look to the party first for help, as they are far better equipped to deal with the dangerous oozes in the mines than her own workers.

If the party opts to Rest in Yakoshta, consider using this crisis to mark the end of their rest, the commotion causing them to be roused from bed to see what is going on.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> From a distance, you can hear loud overlapping voices ringing from the back of the encampment. You can't make out every word, but some are clear: rescue, danger, help.
>
> As you go to investigate the source of the rising commotion, you find a handful of miners gathering around Sellen, all yelling and arguing with each other and trying to get Sellen's attention while she struggles to get a clear answer out of anyone.
>
> As the voices begin to rise in volume, Sellen grabs a hammer from her belt and begins to strike the stone near her. The sharp sound of her iron hitting rocks silences the crowd. Her voice rings out loudly:
>
> > Calm down, calm down! Yelling isn't going to fix anything. We need to know what's going on, then we can figure out what to do.
>
> She points her hammer at one of the workers, who stands up a little straighter as he answers:
>
> > It's those slimes again, Sellen. This time they've got a couple of groups trapped. We need to get all the miners out of there!
>
> Sellen nods.
>
> > Don't worry, we will. And I think these folks here may be just the ones to help us.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Miner Task
>
> Sellen is willing to pay 40 silver ingots (worth  **200**) if the party clears the mine of oozes and rescues the trapped miners. If they helped Tauric, he vouches for them and she raises the amount to 45 silver ingots (worth  **225**).

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What can we do?
>
> **A:**
>
> > You look like you're packing a little more equipment and experience than most of my miners — do you think you could go in and help get them out? I'm happy to pay you for your trouble. I can offer you forty silver ingots.
>
> If the party helped Tauric, he immediately asks for them to receive a little more:
>
> > After everything they've done for me, Sellen, is that enough?
>
> Sellen shrugs, then nods.
>
> > Forty-five ingots then. Just help my miners!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Where are the miners?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I don't think they're that far in — according to Feldin here, the miners can hear shouting if they stand at the entrance, but they're yelling a lot about Jasper, so he may be hurt or further inside.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Any advice?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Hopefully you don't have to go too far in to rescue the miners trapped in there, but if you do, the safest way to avoid falling a few hundred feet is to stick to the carts. They'll always get you where you need to go … eventually.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What about Jasper?
>
> **A:**
>
> > He may be able to help. He's sure there's some way to fix or stop the oozes and has been working on it non-stop. If you can help him figure out what it is, maybe we could put this whole issue behind us and focus on the work that keeps Yakoshta going.

### Concluding the Event

If the party agrees to help, Sellen escorts them to the mouth of Yakoshta Mine and thanks them for their help. She offers them a miner's kit to help them on their way, which includes a [[Lantern]], [[Healing Elixir]], and a [[Pickaxe]].

Tauric also bids farewell to the party — he doesn't want to put Squish in any danger — but he will be waiting in Yakoshta to see what they've found.
