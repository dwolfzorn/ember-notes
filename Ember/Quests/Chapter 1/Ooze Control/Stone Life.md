# Stone Life

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event occurs if the party accompanies [[Tauric]] to a strange stone basin that he calls [[Ooze Island]], where tiny oozelings are known to gather. By exploring the basin and speaking with Tauric, the party can:
>
> - Learn how the Yakoshta miners domesticated oozes in the past in [[A Chat With Tauric]].
> - Locate ancient symbols that provide a clue as to why the oozes gathered here in [[Investigating the Island]].
> - Attempt to befriend an ooze of their own in [[Befriending an Ooze]]. If successful, they gain a single [[Oozeling]] as a companion.
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Ooze Pool" Level of the [[Vista: Dripstones]] Vista.

### A Chat With Tauric

> [!abstract] Tauric
> **[[Tauric]]**
>
> Level 1 · Hulg'run Scout
>
> ![Tauric](../../../assets/image102.webp)
>
> The young man's gray body appears to be carved from rock, with lines of blue agate running through the stone like veins. His color is matched by the gelatinous body of the small blueish-green ooze that sits on his shoulder, nestled into a hollow that seems to have been carved for the purpose.

Tauric can't contain his enthusiasm at seeing an oozeling, even briefly. As the party arrives at the island, read or paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > Squish, do you see that? It's an ooze like you! They're not all gone!
>
> Tauric gathers Squish in his hands. It swirls around his wrist in response like an elaborate bracelet. Tauric turns to you and explains.
>
> > Used to be, if you came to this island, it would be full of oozelings, and you could make friends with them if you wanted to. Now that little one we just saw seems to be all that's left of them, and these angry, glowing oozes are lurking around instead. I hate seeing this place so empty, but maybe there's a clue here about what happened.

Tauric is happy to discuss the island and oozes further. He is especially encouraging of party members who might want to befriend an ooze here, as he thinks everyone should have a friend like Squish.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What is this place?
>
> **A:**
>
> > It's where we make friends with the oozelings. Or did, before these new ones started attacking. I don't know why they come to this little island, but it's been happening since before our mining group arrived here. Don't know what I would have done without this place, honestly. I'm not too great at mining, but I am Yakoshta's chief ooze-ologist. Which is why I thought maybe I could figure out what's been going wrong.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What special things can oozes do?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We've been feeding the oozes metal dust for the last year or so — they change based on what you give them. Iron makes them magnetic, like moving toolboxes. Copper lets them heal injuries. Silver lets them zap pests with electricity, and gold gives them the ability to stretch out and do two things at once. Now that I think about it … maybe these new oozes ate something that didn't agree with them?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why would we want to make friends with an ooze?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Everyone should want to be friends with an ooze. Look at how cool they are! But if you're like Jasper and want to get practical about it … a little extra alertness from an oozeling companion goes a long way.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** How do we befriend an ooze?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Depends on what you want from it. If you just want a buddy, you can try feeding it some of the heartmoss growing everywhere around here, or entertaining it — Squish and I really bonded over battle poetry.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Anything else we can learn here?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Usually, if you're walking through here, you're surrounded by oozes, but with the basin empty, you might be able to see something that would normally be hidden away. I have always wondered why the oozes come here in the first place, but I haven't been able to figure it out.

### Investigating the Island

Tauric has been trying to figure out if there's anything to learn about the change in the oozes from the island, but has come up short so far. He encourages the party to check it out for themselves.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Abandoned Basin
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 14, Passive)**notices cuts in the stone at the bottom of the empty stone basin. Each party member can make one of the following checks:
>
> - **Wilderness (DC 12)**to deduce that the marks are not natural — they were intentionally carved into the rock.
> - **Society (DC 12)**to recall that the marks are typical of the carving techniques of the Shent culture that once lived in the Arctus Plateau. The Shent are believed to have used runes as part of their magic system.
> - **Awareness (DC 14)**to recognize that the marks form a pattern that appears to be symbols of some kind, though it is not fully clear what those symbols are or do. One faintly resembles an ooze, while another could be the shape of a large hand.
> - **Arcana (DC 12)**to confirm that the markings are definitely runic, and that they possess latent magic. With a critical success, or use of a spell to identify magic, the runes can be identified — they attract oozes to the area and provide a calming environment for them.
>
> When making any of the above checks:
>
> - **Knowledge: Shent**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

### Runic Symbols

If party members point the markings out to Tauric, he realizes that he has seen them before — the mine supervisor, Jasper, has been making a study of them for reasons that he has not yet revealed. Before leaving the area, Tauric copies the symbols down as part of his study of oozes.

If anyone touches one of the symbols, it emits a low hum and an ooze appears, making its way towards the center of the basin. If touched more than once, the symbol hums again, but no more oozes appear.

### Befriending an Ooze

Tauric suggests that the ooze that ran away when the party came to the island could be found and befriended — it's just scared and likely hiding somewhere nearby. He isn't used to having to track oozes down, but notes that they like being sung to and will sometimes come out in search of their favorite food, heartmoss.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Finding an Oozeling
>
> The party can attempt to track the ooze or lure it out of hiding.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 12)** or **Awareness (DC 12)** check can see the difference between the ooze's slime trail and the regular dirt and grass around it and determine which area of the island it has disappeared to.
>
> Once an oozeling has been located, the characters may attempt to befriend it using one of the following methods:
>
> #### Tame the Oozeling
>
> Physically grabbing the oozeling requires deft hands and special quickness. Any character who makes a successful **Stealth (DC 13)** check can grab the oozeling. Once it has been grabbed, it can be soothed with a successful **Diplomacy (DC 13)** check, otherwise it squirms away and flees to a different location on the island.
>
> #### Sing to the Oozeling
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Performance (DC 13)** check coaxes the oozeling out of hiding; it wriggles forward curiously, as if dancing to the song, right into the singing character's hands.
>
> #### Feed the Oozeling
>
> If the party sprinkles heartmoss on the path, the oozeling will crawl out from its hiding spot to investigate the delicious mossy treat. If the ooze's feast is interrupted, it will attempt to flee unless otherwise corralled. After eating, the ooze contentedly affixes itself to the nearest rock with a powerful natural adhesive. The ooze becomes complacent for a time, but prying it free from the rock can only be achieved with a successful **Athletics (DC 13)**check.
>
> #### Use the Ancient Runes
>
> If the party intuits that they should use the magic of the ancient Shent runes carved into the stone basin, as described in [[Investigating the Island]], the ooze voluntarily emerges from its hiding place and crawls into the hands of whoever activated the runes.

If the party successfully locates and befriends an ooze, they gain an [[Oozeling]] as a companion. Tauric warns that oozes like to be near familiar places, so it may not stick with the party if they get too far from the Forest of Stone, even after bonding.

> [!abstract] Oozeling
> **[[Oozeling]]**
>
> Level 0.5 (Minion) · Slime Metallic Ooze
>
> ![Oozeling](../../../assets/image066.webp)
>
> You spy a tiny translucent ooze that appears to regard you with a measure of curiosity, if its cautious and gregarious movements are any indication. This strange creature extends its viscous form into a hand-like tendril, which waves to you in friendly salutation.

The party may only befriend a single oozeling in this way. If their previously befriended oozeling perishes or disappears, they may return to this location after 24 hours have passed and repeat the process to acquire another.

The first time an oozeling is befriended, whichever character in the party played the most significant role in gaining its trust advances one of their attunements as described below:

#### Primordis Attunement: Tame the Oozeling

Any character who befriends an oozeling by tracking and taming it advances their **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Heart Attunement: Sing to the Oozeling

Any character who befriends an oozeling by singing to it advances their **Attunement: Heart of Ember (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Mayis Attunement: Feed the Oozeling

Any character who befriends an oozeling by luring it with heartmoss advances their **Attunement: Mayis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Signara Attunement: Use the Ancient Runes

Any character who befriends an oozeling by activating the ancient Shent runes advances their **Attunement: Signara (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.
