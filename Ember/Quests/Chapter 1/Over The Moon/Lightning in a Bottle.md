# Lightning in a Bottle

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs as the party enters the Fulgurite Blades camp. By speaking with the Fulgurite Blades, the characters can:
>
> - Meet the [[Fulgurite Blades]] and their leader, [[Sajor Velex]].
> - Learn what the Fulgurite Blades are trying to accomplish.
> - Learn about the [[Anachraenum]] organization, the [[Shent]] culture, and the [[Forest of Stone]] region in general.
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Fulgurite Blades Campsite" Level of the [[Vista: Verdant Paths]] Vista.

### Meeting the Blades

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you approach, several sets of eyes turn your way, and a massive Cor'ak, who had looked bored out of his mind until now, jumps up from his seat and begins walking toward you.
>
> > Hold there, this is a private camp, 'fore you go any farther, best introduce yourselves and what you want with us.
>
> A nearby kivahr woman looks up from a piece of ruin sticking out of the grass and shrubs, her eyes moving from you to the Cor'ak. A second later, her eyes fall back to her notebook. She scribbles something and calls out:
>
> > Sajor, we have visitors, and Rorhim's gotten to them first!
>
> The Cor'ak, Rorhim, offers a toothy grin in response.
>
> > Not gonna hurt them, just doing my job.
>
> At this point, you notice the arrival of a tall Nir'ae woman who stands at a distance and considers you for a moment. Nearby, a fidgety Thornling does the same, though he is muttering something to her which she seems to ignore.
>
> Eventually the woman who must be Sajor comes to stand with Rorhim, and says:
>
> > Was there something you wanted? As I'm sure Rorhim pointed out, this is an active Anachraenum field camp, so we're all rather busy.

The Fulgurite Blades are a party of four adventurers under the leadership of Sajor and her lieutenant, Rorhim.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Cleric of Spectra
>
> Any character with **Society (DC 14, Passive)** understands that Spectra is one of the few surviving and most significant Elder Gods of Ember. She is associated with the study of magic, and countless shrines to her stand around the world. Spectra has many followers, and she is known to empower some of them with a portion of her divine power, particularly favoring those followers who demonstrate a scholastic enthusiasm for the pursuit of knowledge. Kazra, the Kivahr, likely wields a measure of power from her goddess.
>
> - **Knowledge: Gods**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.

### Talking to the Blades

Sajor and the Fulgurite Blades are generally standoffish and a touch wary of strangers walking into their camp. If anyone points out that their banner had a note about looking for help, they will counter by pointing out that part of the letter was scratched out.

They have decided that they would like to investigate the Shent door by themselves and reserve for themselves the opportunity to scout the unbreached lands beyond.

> [!info] Social
> #### Reluctant Conversation
>
> This information can be learned from the Fulgurite Blades through speaking to them. They are generally standoffish; they don't want to deal with strangers nor are they looking for new friends. Any answers they give are terse and formulated to avoid follow-up questions.
>
> The Fulgurite Blades are an established team, and have worked together for about two years. The four of them are friends and loyal to each other.
>
> Sajor is their leader, and she has the near-unflinching loyalty of the other three. Any character who observes the team and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 13, Passive)** check understands that she is a very hands-off leader, and everyone has a clear picture of what their job is on the team.
>
> #### Trade Opportunities
>
> The Fulgurite Blades have a pretty well-stocked camp, and are willing to trade coins for their spare rations, rope, and tools. They might even have a couple of spare weapons on hand. They mostly trade fairly and are open to barter unless the party has gotten on their bad side.
>
> #### Getting to Helkas
>
> The Fulgurite Blades are only aware of the one main path through the Forest of Stone, but they suspect there might be other paths winding through the area. One such path could be on the other side of the huge door. Once the door is opened, the party is free to have a look and see for themselves.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who are you?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We are the Fulgurite Blades, adventurers and scholars working under the auspices of the Anachraenum.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** You are scholars?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Yes, and explorers. We are cataloging the ruins for Shent knowledge and artifacts.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** You left a sign?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We initially thought it would be useful to get help with uncovering and opening the doorway, but we've changed our minds. We have everything we need and we would prefer avoiding the complexity of coordinating with others. We apologize to have misled you, but your help is not required.

> [!info] Social
> #### Help Not Wanted
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 14, Passive)** check intuits that the Fulgurite Blades are being cagey about their reasons; the characters will need to press further to learn more:
>
> - **Diplomacy (DC 14)** or **Deception (DC 14)** to learn that they realized something about the door, and that perhaps the area inside has more value than at first glance.
> - **Intimidation (DC 15)** to force the Fulgurite Blades to acknowledge that they don't want help because they don't want to share the potential riches inside. This causes the Fulgurite Blades to become unfriendly (although not outright hostile) toward the characters and unwilling to help or work with them going forward.
>
> If the party offends or provokes the Fulgurite Blades, they are firmly asked to leave, and are warned not to return.

The party may ask about the Shent or the artifacts that the Fulgurite Blades are seeking. Kazra and Sajor are more interested than the other two members of the Fulgurite Blades to discuss this topic.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What are you looking for?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We are investigating Shent ruins in the area. There is a great doorway to the north and we're going to open it. The archaeological claims beyond that door are of interest to the Anachraenum and will further our reputation within the guild.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Shent?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The term "Shent" is used not only to describe the dominant ancestry, but also the culture. They lived over three millennia ago. They vanished almost entirely, and almost no record or history remains of them save for small references in old documents from other, equally ancient cultures.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About their artifacts?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The Shent were prolific stone carvers and built using a combination of traditional building techniques and some sort of magical transmutation to produce and shape stone without tools.
> >
> > They worshiped the moons of Ember, and also had means to harness their power for many purposes, possibly through rituals and magically attuned locations, though few working sites or examples exist in the world.

### Speaking with the Blades

The party may speak with each of the members of the Fulgurite Blades individually. Each has some response, although none are particularly interested in prolonged conversation.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Sajor?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The Anachraenum is an organization where the motivated, fearless, and dedicated can make a name for themselves while also discovering more of the world and its countless mysteries. Of course I want to make important discoveries and to be known far and wide for it!
> >
> > There is nothing wrong with being ambitious and collecting accolades. If Fernis Ossa can be a celebrity of Ordain then so too can I.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Rorhim?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Seek adventure, test your might, see the world! That's what you get with the Anachraenum, right?
> >
> > I don't really care much about the historical side of things, although I understand why it's important. I'd rather battle ancient Aedir constructs or cull tomb-robbing bandits than worry about the history of a clay pot.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Leeph?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Turns out it's far easier to lift things from ancient tombs and graves than the pockets of the living. Corpses don't get angry … except for when they do. That's never happened to me, but I've heard stories about it. If it does happen, well … that's what we keep Rorhim around for! I'd prefer not to deal with angry people, or corpses.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Kazra?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I am a priestess of Spectra, both trained and accredited. Some of my peers might say I'm one of the god's chosen — I seek knowledge on her behalf and wield a measure of her power. I am a scholar, but we aren't always historians first. Sometimes our purpose of exploration and discovery aligns with my goals to serve Spectra. I love being a member of the Anachraenum because I can honor Spectra while doing something that I love. I think, of the group here, I've been in the guild the longest — around eight years so far. Sajor joined shortly after me.

> [!info] Social
> #### Matters of Faith
>
> Characters that are followers of [[Spectra]] or fellow clerics will find that Kazra is more friendly with them, but still remains staunchly loyal to her team.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Are you hiring?
>
> **A:**
>
> Sajor shakes her head firmly:
>
> > No, we aren't taking new members into the Blades, sorry. And no, we're not in the business of escorting sightseers either. You can explore once the gate's open, but you're on your own if you do.
