# An Ancient Door

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event occurs as the party approaches the [[Ancient Door]]. By examining this monument, the characters can:
>
> - Learn more about the ancient [[Shent]].
> - Open a sealed gateway into a previously unexplored section of the [[Forest of Stone]].
> - Make use of an important clue learned during [[Giant Moonstone]].
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Ancient Shent Door" and "Ancient Shent Door (Open)" Levels of the [[Vista: Verdant Paths]] Vista.

### Examining the Area

Besides the door and some signs of recent activity, there isn’t much to find. However, the party should be able to piece together how to open the door with a bit of exploration.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Campsite
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 14, Passive)** notes that there is survey equipment and supplies gathered nearby, hidden beneath camouflaging nets. This equipment is in good order and looks relatively new. This, plus the banner, makes it clear that this location has seen recent activity and study.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 13, Passive)**check knows that the banner belongs to the [[Anachraenum]].
>
> - **Culture: Arcturian**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Culture: Ordani**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Saw banner in** [[Curious Party]]: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Met Fulgurite Blades in** [[Lightning in a Bottle]]: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> #### The Anachraenum in Brief
>
> The Anachraenum is an order of adventurer-historians that operates throughout the region and is based in [[Ordain]]. They are an association of adventurers, explorers, scouts, and scholars that travel throughout the region in search of treasures, secrets, and remnants of ancient civilizations. The guild is mostly made up of teams that take jobs together, as well as freelance agents who work alone.
>
> #### Tracks
>
> Any character who examines the area and makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 13)** check identifies the distinct footsteps of at least four different people who have passed through the area within the last few days. The trail heads off to the south. There is another group in the area, likely camped nearby (the Fulgurite Blades in [[Lightning in a Bottle]]).

Several old markers, remnants of decorative structures, and sections of collapsed wall provide context clues about the cycles of [[Mayis]], the flow of the seas, and the rise and fall of oceans — all dictated by the moon. These markers lie scattered around the edges of the camp.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Studying the Door
>
> Any character who examines the door's architecture and makes a successful **Society (DC 12, Passive)** check identifies the architectural style, weathered as it is, as Shent in origin. Though much of the structure's face has been worn away, there are aspects of the edifice around the edges that represent multiple moons and constellations in patterned arrays, as though the wall was tracking the passage of the moons.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character realizes that Mayis is the central aspect of this door: the supporting iconography is focused entirely on Mayis and how the other moons relate to its position.
>
> Any character who examines the imagery on the door and makes a successful **Science (DC 12, Passive)** check notices a strong theme of reverence toward the moons, with the water moon, Mayis, being most prominent among them.The specific focus on Mayis feels especially significant, with several barely decipherable symbols that indicate worship, reverence, or unity.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character recognizes several modifications made to the imagery that evoke themes of past, future, uncertainty, and hope for what is to come. These modifications are more recent than the base iconography, but are still quite old.
>
> Any character who studies the magical inscriptions on the gate and makes a successful **Arcana (DC 13)** check recognizes that Transmutation magic was used to fuse the stone to the canyon walls, Abdjuration magic was used to protect it, and Divination magic was used to interpret spoken speech. The Divination magic indicates there is a spoken command word or phrase required to open the gate.
>
> - **Talent: Recognize Spellcraft**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character also recognizes decorative Divination and Transmutation runes added alongside other modifications. Though they don't appear to serve an actionable function, the runes relate to themes of future and fate.
>
> #### Passphrase
>
> The correct passphrase to open the great door is **"fate of the moons,"** spoken aloud. The language does not matter, as the door itself has Divination magic meant to parse words and intent.

### Failing Forward

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### It's All There
>
> If the party is struggling, consider allowing **Society (DC 12)**checks to piece together the meaning. If the party decoded the passphrase from the ancient moonstone earlier, they already know what they need to say.
>
> #### Outside Help
>
> The party can also seek outside help to get past the door:
>
> - The [[Messenger Coin]] can be used to contact the caravan, allowing the party to speak to either Lyla or Clipper to get help, since both looked at the ancient moonstone and likely remember the inscription.
> - The Fulgurite Blades who camp nearby (see the [[Over The Moon]] quest) have a shared interest in opening the passage. Their leader, Sajor, has already figured out the passphrase, but she is waiting for her team to finish their studies around camp before moving forward.

> [!info] Social
> #### Caravan Assistance
>
> Speaking to the caravan allows Lyla and Clipper to quote the stone to the party, and the core passphrase is contained within it:
>
> > … shadows end by spear restrained and sealed to never rise again. Let only those who understand the fate of the moons walk its path.
>
> Further discussion of the door and what's been found may lead them to point out:
>
> > It seems like the passphrase is focused on the moon and its fate… and "fate of the moons" is in the old passage. Maybe try that?
>
> #### Fulgurite Blade Assistance
>
> Speaking with the Fulgurite Blades allows the party to get the passphrase through social means rather than exploration. This requires speaking with them at their camp, or at the door, if you prefer to have them actively exploring.
>
> Any character who speaks with [[Sajor Velex]] and makes a successful **Deception (DC 14, Passive)**check notices that Sajor knows more than she is letting on, as if she's curious if the characters will catch on. The party may attempt a group challenge, with each party member rolling one of the following checks:
>
> - **Deception (DC 15)**to trick Sajor into revealing the solution to the puzzle.
> - **Intimidation (DC 14)**to intimidate Sajor into revealing the solution. On a failure, Sajor is very unimpressed and will refuse to assist the party further.
> - **Diplomacy (DC 14)**to persuade Sajor to reveal the answer to the puzzle.

### Opening the Gate

If the party successfully opens the gate, read the following aloud, then mark the Outcome below it as complete:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A sudden, low hum vibrates through the ground as if something deep within the stone itself has awoken. You can see the intricate inscriptions on the gate begin to glow with a soft, silvery light, casting eerie patterns on the damp, moss-covered stones around you.
>
> Slowly, the gate starts to move. The massive stone doors, despite their age and size, part with surprising ease, revealing a verdant, wild, misty region beyond.

`[[/outcome opened]]`

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Vista Level
>
> Change the Vista Level to "Ancient Shent Door (Open)" at this time.

#### Signara Attunement: Uttering the Passphrase

The first character to successfully open the Ancient Door by speaking the passphrase advances their **Attunement: Signara (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> Once the party has opened the gate, they may travel through it, triggering the [[Untouched Wilds]] Event.
