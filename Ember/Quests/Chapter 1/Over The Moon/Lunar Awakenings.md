# Lunar Awakenings

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Combat Event occurs when the party reaches the [[Shent Moon Temple]]. By exploring the temple, the characters can:
>
> - Battle the [[Temple Invader]] and its water elemental allies: [[Water Mote]], [[Water Sprite]], and a [[Water Visitor]].
> - Reunite with the [[Fulgurite Blades]].
> - Meet the ghostly projection of the Shent sage [[Mioroth]], who will assign the party one final, dangerous task.
>
> #### Area Walkthrough
>
> The party begins in the [[Shent Moon Temple]] Scene, where the central gameplay of this Event transpires. A complete description of the temple and the gameplay that occurs there is detailed in the [[Shent Moon Temple]] Area Walkthrough.

### Opening the Temple

The party remains relatively safe until they open the doors into the temple. As soon as they do, they initiate a battle with the hostile elemental contained within. Proceed with the gameplay found in the [[Shent Moon Temple]] Area Walkthrough. Once the elementals have been defeated and the party is ready to leave the temple, return here to continue the Event.

### The Memory Remains

After the battle ends and the party is ready to depart the temple, a ghostly projection of a Shent seer appears to the party.

To avoid cluttering the Scene during combat, Mioroth's Actor is not present in the Moon Temple Scene initially. Once the party is about to see him, drag Mioroth's Actor from the Actor sidebar to a place in the Scene where the party can see him.

### Speaking to Mioroth

> [!info] Social
> #### What Mioroth Knows
>
> Mioroth has considerable knowledge about the Shent, the Shattering, and other topics of antiquity which could prove interesting or enticing to historians and scholars. However, Mioroth is unwilling to go into great detail about these topics because he knows how rare and potentially disruptive such knowledge could be. He has no interest in turning a handful of random strangers into the foremost experts on ancient history with no regard for how that might affect their lives.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 14)**check realizes that, while he is being honest and accurate with what he says, he is deliberately withholding information for some reason. If pressed, Mioroth might even be entirely forthcoming with his reasoning.

Mioroth is steadfast in his convictions, and cannot be persuaded to change his mind or give up more information. However, that is not to say he doesn't have a wealth of information he can share. He is more than happy to lend small bits of insight and knowledge to the party, especially if they help him with the Whisperer.

> [!info] Social
> #### About Mioroth
>
> Put simply, the projection is a memory and an echo of the ancient seer Mioroth.
>
> Mioroth does not eat, sleep, or breathe. If asked, he confirms that he's neither undead nor a ghost. However, he refuses to elaborate any further on how he came to be, his abilities, or his purpose.
>
> #### About the Shent
>
> This is where Mioroth will be most reserved. He avoids providing direct information beyond things he believes most would have learned by now:
>
> - The Shent were an ancient nation and culture that fell during the Shattering. The Shent built many wonders in these lands, and great cities upon distant mountain peaks, but they are all long gone now. He is merely a memory.
> - The Kivahr and Keth are direct descendants of the Shent, which is why the Kivahr somewhat resemble them physically. The Hulg'run predate both and were created by the Shent to be servants before gaining freedom and being treated as equals.
>
> #### About the Shattering
>
> Mioroth tells of a period when the skies were rent asunder, torn open by forces unfathomable. From those gaping wounds poured forth entities of bedlam, and malignant ruin. The realms of Ember buckled under the onslaught, with many meeting an early end in what was, at best, a narrow survival.
>
> The Whisperer was one of those deadly interlopers, and while Mioroth does not know what attracted it to the area, he is haunted by how it laid waste to the Shent here, doing so with terrible efficiency. The Shent in the area only barely managed to subdue and contain it, hoping that someone else would eventually be able to come and destroy it. That obviously never happened.
>
> #### About the Temple
>
> The temple was originally built as a grand planetarium. It was one of many built across Shent lands. This one in particular was meant to chart the celestial dance of Mayis around Ember, so that its cycles would be charted in the greatest detail. It was designed to draw power from that moon to drive it, so that the machine would never stop, which was perhaps not the best design choice.
>
> The item that was taken from its core is a planisphere, a deceptively simple piece of equipment that was one of the oldest inventions of his people and commonly used around the world by many other cultures. It could manually track the secret motions of the sky's errant march, and was the core of the planetarium, used to project accurate images of the heavens.
>
> #### About the Crystal Caves
>
> The crystal caves were sources of stone naturally infused with magic drawn from Ember. This was a distant mining community once, and they produced gems used all across the Shent realms. While they were deleterious in their natural form, if properly carved with runes they had myriad uses.
>
> The machine in the caverns was known as a "nullifier," a clever device built to suppress the maddening effects of the crystal caverns so that the Shent could work in them safely. It was a marvel of its time, unique, one of a kind, and destroyed by the Whisperer.

Once the party has finished speaking with Mioroth, they are free to leave the temple and carry on with their expedition.

### Concluding the Event

As part of the Event's conclusion, the entire party will also be affected by the following Mayis Attunement reinforcement:

#### Mayis Attunement: Meeting Mioroth

Any character who speaks with Mioroth advances their **Attunement: Mayis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

If one of the characters attempts to take the Planisphere for themselves, they will reinforce their Aura Attunement in the process.

#### Aura Attunement: Planisphere Claimed

Any character who attempts to take or withhold the Planisphere from Sajor advances their **Attunement: Aura (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

Alternatively, if the party convinces the Fulgurite Blades to help them clear the rubble blocking the Strayhearth Caravan, they advance their Ragen attunement:

#### Ragen Attunement: Fulgurite Assistance

Any character who convinces the Fulgurite Blades to help clear the rubble advances their **Attunement: Ragen (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.
