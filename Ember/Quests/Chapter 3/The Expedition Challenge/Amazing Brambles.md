# Amazing Brambles

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event in the [[Splinter Canyons]] challenges the party's capacity to traverse the dangerous environments of the [[Bramble Maze]], and tests their skills in navigation and wilderness survival. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Employ their skills and abilities to withstand the maze's array of difficult nature-based hazards.
> - Discover the lair of a deceased [[Juvenile Garganthus]].
> - Learn about a hidden route between the surface of [[World of Ember]] and the [[Pathways]] below.
> - Locate a totem constructed by the ancient [[Varún]] Giants, and retrieve the [[Maze Challenge Key]] that hangs from it.
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Bramble Maze" Level of the [[Vista: Splinter Canyons]] Vista.
>
> #### Group Skill Checks
>
> The [[Amazing Brambles]] section of this Event involves a sequence of [[Group Skill Checks]]. The party's progression through the maze will depend on the success or failure of each Group Skill Check.

### Penetrating the Maze

The first group challenge the party will face involves breaching the perimeter of the area.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The labyrinthine warren of brambles here is near impenetrable. You can only hope that if you get past the outer-most shell you will reach terrain that is not so actively suffocated by the massive vines.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Penetrating the Thorns
>
> The party must perform a group skill check using **Athletics (DC 16, Group)**to breach the outer maze of brambles.
>
> - **Ancestry: Thornling**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Culture: Oaken**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Path: Primalist Disciple**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - [[Blight]] or [[Tree Stride]] Spells: The entire party critically succeeds on this check.
>
> The outcome of this challenge is determined via the rules for group skill checks with the following results. Unless otherwise stated by the result, the party moves onwards to the next challenge.
>
> **Success**
>
> > You locate a section of thorns that is sufficiently pliant for you to squeeze through by force or by contorting your body through its aperture.
>
> The party is successful in bypassing the thorny barrier.
>
> **Critical Success**
>
> > You locate a section of thorns that is sufficiently pliant for you to squeeze through by force or by contorting your body through its aperture. As you proceed through the winding corridors beyond, you come upon a small clearing within which grows a large and unusual plant.
>
> The party benefits from a **Success** and locates the "Nimbus Pitcher" (see below).
>
> **Failure**
>
> > You force your way through the perimeter of thorns with clumsy effort, the countless briars snagging on your equipment and tearing at your skin. The passage, although short, is exhausting and leaves you bloodied.
>
> The party forces their way through the perimeter, but at some cost. Each character
>
> **Critical Failure**
>
> > You spend an hour trying to force or squeeze your way through the perimeter of thorns. It is a clumsy effort and the countless briars that snag on your equipment and tear at your skin are both damaging and demoralizing. The perimeter of thorns is impregnable and you are forced to retreat to the plateau upon which you started and reconsider your approach.
>
> The party suffers the consequences of **Failure** but is also unable to make progress and must repeat this challenge. The party may simply attempt the check again, or they can devise some alternative stratagem for bypassing the perimeter of thorns (at the Gamemaster's discretion).

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Nimbus Pitcher
>
> If the party achieved a Critical Success on this challenge, read the following:
>
> > Along the path you clear you discover a small clearing containing an unusual plant - a thriving tangle of purple flowers and deep pitchers hanging from sturdy vines. These pitchers appear to contain effervescent moisture and appear to be perpetually emitting steam, although they generate no perceptible heat.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Science (DC 14)**check recognize the flora as a Nimbus Pitcher, a rare plant with special properties that are valuable to alchemists.
>
> - **Knowledge: Plants**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Alchemy**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 14)**check can harvest `[[/roll 1d3+2]]` [[Nimbus Pitcher]] from the plant.
>
> - **Knowledge: Plants**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> - **Knowledge: Plants**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.

#### Ragen Attunement: Utilizing Strength

Any character who uses Strength to successfully penetrate the maze advances their **Attunement: Ragen (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Aura Attunement: Utilizing Dexterity

Any character who uses Dexterity to successfully penetrate the maze advances their **Attunement: Aura (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Akon Attunement: Utilizing Magic

Any character who uses magic to successfully penetrate the maze advances their **Attunement: Akon (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### Avoiding Gore Birds

The second group challenge tests the party's ability to avoid notice from a flock of gore birds which roost upon the canyon cliffs.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you continue navigating the treacherous environment, you see upon the canyon cliffs above you a huge flock of birds. Their excrement has bleached the rocks and their nests are wedged into any cranny that affords even an inch of space upon which to precariously balance. You recognize these birds as carrion eaters and fear they might be emboldened enough to attack living prey in numbers.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Carrion Flock
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Medicine (DC 15, Passive)** check can recognize this flock of avian creatures as a [[Gore Bird Ravage]].
>
> - **Knowledge: Beasts**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character also realizes that the swarm could be distracted via the careful placement of food scraps or shiny objects, which might capture their attention and aid in the party's passage.
>
> The characters will need to utilize stealth or guile to move through the area without being detected by the flock. The party must perform a group skill check using **Stealth (DC 18, Group)** to pass through the area unnoticed.
>
> **Success**
>
> > You narrowly escape notice by the birds, hearing a great cacophony from behind you as you leave their domain.
>
> The party is successful in eluding the birds.
>
> **Critical Success**
>
> > You effortlessly escape notice by the birds, sneaking past them silently and entirely undetected. As you weave your way through the brambles you stumble upon the ravaged body of a giant figure.
>
> The party benefits from a **Success** but also refer to the "Gored Giant" section below.
>
> **Failure**
>
> > Your attempts to elude notice by the birds are unsuccessful. No longer afforded the luxury of subtlety you have to flee the cacophony of hungry cries that swell through the canyon behind you. Several outlying wings of the massive swarm reach you and rip at your exposed skin.
>
> The party is unable to avoid detection. Each character suffers damage from the **Swarming Talons (Hazard 12, Reflex, Slashing)**.
>
> **Critical Failure**
>
> > The flock of birds easily detects you and soon you are swept up in a bewildering maelstrom of flapping wings, raking talons, and vicious bites. You are forced to flee as a cacophony of hungry cries that swell through the canyon behind you in pursuit.
>
> The party suffers the consequences of **Failure** and the characters become disoriented as they lose their sense of direction. Each character will gain **-2 Banes** on the fourth challenge,[[Amazing Brambles]].
>
> #### The Gored Giant
>
> If the party achieved a Critical Success on this challenge, read the following:
>
> > You stumble upon the body of a giant, its bones picked clean and bleached by the sunlight that passes daily overhead. The remnants of its equipment, broken armor, an enormous bone-spiked club, and tattered cloth are strewn about the clearing.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Giant's Remains
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 16)** check can recognize that the equipment and remains here are most likely [[Varún]] — a surviving culture of giants who are genealogical cousins to the ancient [[Shent]].
>
> - **Knowledge: Shent**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Ancients**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Medicine (DC 22)** check can identify that the giant likely died from terrible wounds sustained to its back, where the ribcage is broken and its bones are scored by deep gouges.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Crime**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Searching the area also reveals an aged leather pouch on the ground near the giant's remains, which contains a strange stone trinket from a bygone era.
>
> Any character who makes a successful an **Awareness (DC 15)**check can identify the bauble as a [[Varún Moonmedal]]. This particular medal is attuned to Cora.

### Withstanding Noxious Plants

The third group challenge tests the party's ability to withstand the delirious fumes of noxious plants.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The canyon before you narrows, and you notice that on both sides affixed to the rock are the rotund forums of globular plants that seem to almost breathe as they slowly increase and decrease in size. With every exhalation, a small puff of spores or pollen spout forth from a hole near the top of the plant. It seems there is no alternative way forward.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Noxious Plants
>
> Characters who make a successful **Wilderness (DC 15, Passive)** check can identify these plants as "Chokebellows," which are notorious for the temporary (or permanent) suffocation that can occur when creatures inhale their fumes.
>
> - **Knowledge: Plants**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character suspects that they could safely move and breathe in the area between exhalations of the Chokebellow fumes — if the approach is timed ever-so-carefully. The party also gains **+2 Boons** on all subsequent skill checks made during this challenge.
>
> The party must perform a group challenge using **Awareness (DC 15, Group)** to to carefully time their movements between the patterned "breaths" of the nearby Chokebellows.
>
> - **Ancestry: Altyra**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Ancestry: Hulg'run**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Immunity to the** ****Poisoned**** **condition**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> **Success**
>
> > You carefully time your movements through the growth of plants, taking brief moments to exert yourself and breathe between exhalations of the plants fumes. Your progress is slow and your lungs burn by the end, but you make it to the other side without incident.
>
> The party is successful in traversing the hazard.
>
> **Critical Success**
>
> > You expertly time your movements through the growth of plants, balancing your physical exertion with opportune windows to breathe. Your progress is slow, but you reach the far side without incident. From the safety of a plateau on the far side of this hazard, you appreciate a clear vantage point of the maze of brambles which helps reinforce your sense of direction amidst the confusing warren.
>
> The party benefits from a **Success,** but also gains **+2 Boons** on the Wisdom (Survival) check made during the final challenge, [[Amazing Brambles]].
>
> **Failure**
>
> > You attempt to time your movements between exhalations of the plants, but you quickly become overtaken by the burning of your lungs and the persistence of the miasma. You cannot avoid inhaling some of the fumes and the breath is sucked away from your lungs. With the help of your allies you press onwards to the far side but feel the burning effects of the choking vapors.
>
> Each character must **Chokebellow Fumes (Hazard 12, Fortitude, Poison)**.
>
> **Critical Failure**
>
> > Your attempt to time movements between exhalations of the plants are short-lived as you are rapidly overtaken by the burning of your lungs and the persistence of the miasma. You inhale the fumes and the breath is ripped from your lungs. Through some grace or fortune you crawl you way to the far side but your vision swims and you feel distinctly unwell.
>
> The party suffers the consequences of **Failure,** and every member gains the **Poisoned** condition until they complete a Long Rest.

### Rediscovering the Path

The final group challenge requires the party to maintain their overall sense of direction and avoid getting turned around or lost during their ordeal.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You approach the far end of the canyon where you think awaits your eventual destination … but wait … that spur of rock, that distinctive spiral of bramble, have you been here before? The overhanging rock occludes your view of the sky and your sense of direction falters.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Bewildering Maze
>
> The party must perform a group skill check using **Wilderness (DC 16, Group)** to ascertain their bearings.
>
> - **Knowledge: Tracking**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Path: Caravanner**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> **Success**
>
> > You reorient yourself and press onwards, passing through the tangled warren into a spacious enclave at the end of the narrow canyon.
>
> The party has completed the challenge and reaches [[Amazing Brambles]] at the end of the maze.
>
> **Critical Success**
>
> > You swiftly reorient yourself and press onwards, emerging from the tangled warren into a spacious enclave at the end of the narrow canyon. As you gaze upon the space before you, the light overhead highlights the rock face of the canyons with a spectacular bloom of radiant color, you feel invigorated knowing that you have weathered the maze and reached its terminus.
>
> The party benefits from a **Success**, and each character also may reduce their **Madness** by 10.
>
> **Failure**
>
> > The brambles surround you on all sides and you lose confidence in your progress. None of the multitude of routes before you look like clear passageways towards the clue you seek within this maze. Hours of frustrating trial and error pass until you stumble as if by chance into a spacious enclave at the end of the narrow canyon. Relief and exhaustion mingle together in your mind.
>
> Advance time by 4 hours. Each character gains the **Exhausted** condition .
>
> **Critical Failure**
>
> > The brambles surround you on all sides and you lose confidence in your progress. None of the multitude of routes before you look like clear passageways towards the clue you seek within this maze. Hours of frustrating trial and error pass until you stumble out onto a rocky shelf. It takes a moment for recognition to dawn on you that this is the exact point from which you began your exploration, now many hours ago.
>
> The party suffers the consequences of **Failure**, and must also return to the beginning of the Bramble Maze. They can either abandon the challenge or re-attempt it, starting once again from [[Amazing Brambles]].

### The Garganthus Den

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You gaze ahead into the terminus of the canyon that you spent strenuous hours traversing. Before you is a round depression, enclosed on all sides by sheer stone cliffs that reach towards the distant sky overhead. In the center of this depression you see a totemic pillar standing proudly, and slumped near its foot the large dark form of some creature. Behind the creature's body, you see an opening in the ground that descends into darkness.
>
> The totem itself is constructed from thick sections of thorny vine surrounded and reinforced by the cleaned ribcage bones of an enormous creature. The bones are lashed together in bunches which surround the central vine, creating a bone-encircled pillar that stands nearly five feet in diameter and twenty feet tall. Hanging from the totem are some scraps of pale cloth upon which have been painted runic markers in a variety of colorful paints.
>
> At the foot of the totem, a monstrous figure slumps motionless. It's hide glistens black, almost perfect onyx interspersed with bulging purple veins and vivid cyan spines. The creature's side has been torn open exposing the chest cavity of its carcass.
>
> A glint suddenly draws your eye — halfway up the bone totem dangles a bunch of metal keys fastened around the totem with a leather cord.

A Varún warrior created a totem here to commemorate their defeat of this garganthus as the conclusion of their ritualistic pilgrimage and hunt.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Totem of Conquest
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 16)**check can recognize the totem as being [[Varún]] in origin, a surviving culture of giants who are genealogical distant relatives of the ancient [[Shent]].
>
> - **Knowledge: Shent**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Ancients**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - Characters who previously discovered and identified the body of the Varún in [[Amazing Brambles]] have advantage on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character recalls a rite of passage for adolescent Varún warriors, which tasked them with lone travel to a distant land — where they would hunt a most formidable monster amidst the local wilderness.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Medicine (DC 16)** check can locate countless pock-marks in the bones that have been picked completely clean, and suspects that the nearby gore birds were drawn to this carcass (and made short work of the exposed remains).
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Beasts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> #### The Felled Garganthus
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 18, Passive)**check can recognize the felled creature as an [[Juvenile Garganthus]], a gargantuan creature whose formidable reptilian aspect evokes a sense of primordial savagery.
>
> - **Knowledge: Monsters**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Subterranea**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character has heard tales that Garganthus Hide is a precious material utilized by some of Ember's finest leatherworkers to create equipment that is prized for it's durability, flexibility, and light-neutralizing properties.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Athletics (DC 25)** check is able to harvest 2 [[Garganthus Hide]].
>
> - **Knowledge: Crafts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Alternalte, any character who possesses a [[Unknown]] can attempt a successful `[[/tool tool=leatherworker dc=25 ability=strength]]` check with **+2 Boons**.
>
> - **Knowledge: Crafts**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> #### The Challenge Key
>
> Hanging from the bone-covered totem is a bundle of [[Maze Challenge Key]], one of which is required by the party to progress in the Expedition Challenge. It does not require any noteworthy effort to retrieve, however as soon as the first character in the party touches one of the keys, read the following aloud:
>
> > As your fingers make contact with one of the gleaming keys, you feel a sudden flash of power emanating from the Frozen Tear, and a glimmer of magic leaps from the tear onto the bundle of keys — covering them in a momentary lattice of glowing permafrost.
>
> Any attempt to remove or destroy any of the other remaining keys is thwarted by a field of magical stasis that surrounds the bunch of keys.

Once the party has retrieved the key, they are interrupted as described below:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> No sooner have your fingers closed around the key and withdrawn it from the totem when you hear a cascade of rock pebbles and crunching footfalls descending the scree behind you, in the direction from which you entered the area. You turn instinctively to see a pair of figures making their way down towards you.
>
> One stands tall with the posture of a Wirrun, but with a flamboyant mane and tail of remarkable pink fur like that of a Kiska. The other is a stout Thornling, their head crowned by the magnificently broad cap of a blue mushroom.
>
> One of your rivals in the Expedition Challenge, the duo called "Mug & Cap" approach. Mug, limping slightly but seemingly undeterred, greets you with a jaunty wave.
>
> > Woulda beaten you here if it weren't for those stinking fume blasters … Oh well, you might have won this leg, but the race is still up for grabs.

> [!info] Social
> #### Rival Group
>
> The party's response to the arrival of Mug & Cap could range from amicable to antagonistic. If the characters try to be friendly, there is an opportunity to persuade Mug & Cap to share information about the challenge clues.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 22)**or **Intimidation (DC 20)** check is able to convince Mug & Cap to share the solution to the first Challenge Clue, revealing their knowledge of [[Jekeroka Villa]] and its exact location.
>
> - **Failure:** On the result of a failure, Mug & Cap will lie to the characters, and describe [[Unknown]] as the clue's location instead. Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 16, Passive)** check can tell they are lying.
>
> They party can also ask about the third Challenge Clue, to which Mug & Cap confess they have not yet figured it out. Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 16, Passive)** check can tell they are being forthright about it.

### To Paths Below

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Behind the deceased creature's body, a cavernous aperture drops into darkness below. There are precarious shelves and ledges of rock that ring the sinkhole offering the possibility of descent while simultaneously alluding to the danger inherent in such an attempt.

Once the party has completed this event, the Bramble Maze becomes a permanent connection point that the party can use to descent into the [[Pathways]], emerging within the great subterranean lake of the [[Mycelian Expanse]].

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Pathways Entrance
>
> To descend from here into the Pathways, return to the Arctus Plateau region map and change the party token elevation to -1 to enter the Pathways layer.
