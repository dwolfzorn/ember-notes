# Hidden Campsite

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event takes the party to [[Krator's Hall]] in the[[Wedgelands]] north of [[Ordain]]. Here, the party must investigate the mysterious [[Hidden Campsite]] and endure its uncanny challenges. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Explore the aged shelter of Krator's Hall, which serves as a private outpost for the Anachraenum.
> - Investigate the Well of Orbis, an inscrutable landmark that will not only test the party's knowledge of history and lore, it will challenge their skills of insight and deduction.
> - Earn the [[Well Challenge Key]] by enduring all 4 Well of Orbis Echoes and answering their questions about Ember's ancient histories — including those of the [[Aedir]] Empire, [[Orbis]], the [[Shent]], and the [[Sedyri]].
>
> This Event is depicted using the [[The Arctus Plateau]] Region Map.

### The Overgrown Hall

If the party decides to investigate the edifice of Krator's Hall, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Two features catch your eye as you draw closer to the large wooden hall, including the weathered Anachraenum symbol above the entrance, and a heavy lock set into the structure's large, iron-banded doors. Above the doorway, chaotic patterns of rust and corrosion creep across the iron face of the guild's logo, ravaged by time and the elements. And the heavy lock — which appears to be made of fine steel or some other alloy — is remarkably bereft of this decay. All about the entrance, you notice the fleeting tracks of local wildlife who must have come looking for shelter or a scrap to eat. Nevertheless, a peaceful stillness lurks here amidst the quiet eaves and shuttered windows of this somewhat neglected place.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Door
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 12)** check is able to readily notice that the massive doors to Krator's Hall are locked, and that their iron-banded oak has been warped and swollen from weather and age.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character also notices that the lock on the doors is equipped with a magical trap of some kind.
>
> Any character who also makes a successful `[[/check arcana 15]]` check can recognize the main property of the magical trap on the lock, and understands that it redirects any damage dealt to the doors or the lock back upon the person who inflicted it.
>
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Additionally, casting the [[Detect Magic]] Spell upon the doors will reveal traces of Evocation magic on the lock and the doors themselves.
>
> Picking the lock requires a successful `[[/check slt 16]]` check by a character who is proficient with [[Thieves' Tools]]. If the lock is successfully picked, the Damage Reflection Trap is disarmed (see below), and the characters can safely enter Krator's Hall.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Damage Reflection Trap
>
> The iron-banded doors to Krator's Hall are equipped with a special Damage Reflection Trap, which is centered on the lock and the doors themselves.
>
> The paired set of doors has 200 hit points and us made of iron-banded oak (AC 17). Meanwhile, the lock itself has 100 hit points and is made of steel (AC 19).
>
> Any character who deals damage to either the lock or the doors must succeed on a `[[/save dexterity 15]]` save to avoid taking the same amount of damage they dealt.
>
> Casting the [[Dispel Magic]] Spell upon either the door or lock will suppress the effects of the Damage Reflection Trap for a duration of `[[/roll 2d4]]` hours.

If the party manages to make their way into Krator's Hall, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Stepping into the chamber, you see that Krator's Hall is comprised of little more than a single enormous room, which is presently quite empty. A few empty cots line one side of the chamber, and a kitchen area with bare countertops decorates the other. You spy a note on a dusty countertop, which reads:
>
> > Don't make a mess, and please lock the door behind you.
>
> The solitude here feels almost oppressive, as if the sharp echo of your footsteps is the only visitor this place has welcomed in months.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring the Hall
>
> A brief examination of the hall reveals nothing of import, other than a few sundry items in the kitchen and resting areas. If desired, roll `[[/roll 1d2+1]]` times on the [[Corpse Loot]] Table and once on the [[Arcturian Trinkets]] Table to find out what forgotten Items or accoutrements the characters might discover.

### The Well of Orbis

As the party approaches the pool of shimmering water to the side of Krator's Hall, they realize that this is where the true challenge begins. Next to the pool is a sign written by someone from the Anachraenum, it reads:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > Well Challenge for the Expedition Challenge. Successful answers to the Well's questions will result in the acquisition of the Key.
>
> As you stand there pondering the meaning of these words, you notice that the waters of the pool begin to stir with soft movements that catch your attention.

The party must experience all four of the Well of Orbis Echoes in order to have a chance at winning the [[Well Challenge Key]] from this Keystone Challenge. Along the way, the characters will be able to score points from each Echo they experience, and must gain a total of 6 or more points for the most desirable outcome. However, a result of less than 6 points is not considered a failure — the party will claim the Well Challenge Key as long as they survive all four of the Well's abstruse and unpredictable Echoes.

Each time a character answers a question correctly, they'll hear a small magical "ping!" along with a quiet, almost imperceptible sigh; if a character answers incorrectly, they'll hear a subtle echo of their own voice instead.

Roll or select from the [[Well of Orbis Echoes]] table below to determine which Echo the party encounters when they approach the Well for the first time, or each time they finish an Echo's associated encounter.

|  | #### Well of Orbis Echoes |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | #### Legacy of Destruction  In an instant, a massive fireball appears over the water and explodes outward, but the force is merely an illusion, and you see the ghostly remnants of a tower flickering in the air. [[Hidden Campsite]] |
| 2 | #### Dragged Under  Massive pale hands, with skin stretched over bones, spring from the water and attempt to wrap around you. [[Hidden Campsite]] |
| 3 | #### Ending of the Stars  Flashes of light appear above the water, and a twinkling constellation forms in the air before, all at once, they are all swallowed by a deep, dark void. [[Hidden Campsite]] |
| 4 | #### Depths Revealed  Bubbles rise from the pool as a large fire seems to form under the water. Steam follows, and you catch a glimpse of a vast underwater city that crumbles into dust. [[Hidden Campsite]] |

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Well
>
> Any character with **Attunement: Orbis** feels comforted as they examine the Well.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 18)** check has heard of the Well of Orbis before, but knows nothing of its true history or esoteric properties (aside from those boasted by the tall tales of Arcturian legendry).
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Legends**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character also knows that the Well of Orbis is so inscrutable, even the Anachraenum doesn't know much about its history, origin, or true purpose on Ember.
>
> #### Secret Lore
>
> The Well of Orbis is a sentient object of an extremely strange and esoteric nature. While it possesses a degree of otherworldly intelligence all its own, the Well isn't "alive" in the true sense, and harbors no soul(s). Whatever powers the Well of Ornis is a mystery, and its purpose is as elusive as the passage of time.
>
> Members of the Anachraenum were the first [[Arcturian]] to chronicle the Well of Orbis nearly a century ago, leading many to suspect that the Well originated after the events of [[The Shattering]]. And despite the guild's countless years of study, the Well of Orbis remains an abstruse monument to this very day — and its link to the Spirit Moon is the only salient connection that has yet to be made.

### 1. Legacy of Destruction

This Echo will test the party's knowledge of the ancient [[Aedir]]. Be sure to allow characters enough time to answer each question before proceeding to the next Echo, and keep track of how many questions were answered correctly.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> In an instant, a massive fireball appears over the water and explodes outward, casting a wash of vibrant reds and oranges across the shimmering surface. The heat seems palpable, tingling against your skin, but as you brace for the impending impact, you realize that the force is merely an illusion. The force of the explosion fades into silence, revealing the ghostly remnants of a broken tower flickering in the air, ethereal and shimmering like a mirage.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Knowledge of the Ancient Aedir
>
> Any character that makes a successful **Society (DC 15)** check can identify the illusion before them as a simple depiction of [[The Shattering]], and can presume that this Echo pertains to the ancient Aedir of the Sunfire Empire.
>
> - **Knowledge: Aedir**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Legends**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character also gains **+2 Boons** on their choice of one of the Skill Checks associated with questions about the Aedir Empire below.
>
> Once the characters have had enough time to scrutinize the illusion, read the following:
>
> > A ghostly utterance fills your ears, as dispassionate and distant as the stars. No mere remark, this spectral voice asks you a rather pointed question …
>
> **When did the Aedir Empire fall?**
>
> The correct answer is "2523" or "twenty-five hundred years ago."
>
> Any character who makes either a successful `[[/check history 12]]` or `[[/check investigation 12]]` check is able to answer correctly.
>
> - **Knowledge: Aedir**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Legends**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> **How did the Aedir achieve military supremacy?**
>
> The correct answer is "Constructs" or "magical technology."
>
> Any character who makes either a successful `[[/check arcana 17]]` or `[[/check history 15]]` check is able to answer correctly.
>
> - **Knowledge: Aedir**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Machines**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> **With whom did the Aedir ally themselves during the war?**
>
> The correct answer is "Celestials" or "the forces/creatures of Luxarum."
>
> Any character who makes either a successful `[[/check arcana 17]]` or `[[/check history 15]]` check is able to answer correctly.
>
> - **Culture: Railen**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Aedir**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Celestials**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Warfare**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Score & Consequences
>
> Each time they correctly answer a question from the Well of Orbis, the party scores one point, which should be tallied in secrecy until[[Hidden Campsite]] occurs near the end of the event.
>
> If the party correctly answers every question, the Well speaks:
>
> > The hubris of the Aedir is an important lesson to learn, and one must always beware the dangers of excessive pride. Great power comes with great responsibility.
>
> If the party incorrectly answers one or more questions, each character must make a `[[/save 14 wisdom]]` save. On a failure, the character is pushed to the ground and knocked &Reference[prone], taking `[[/damage 1d6 psychic]]` damage in the process. The Well speaks:
>
> > You seem as foolish as the Aedir, and fail to understand the extremities of their arrogance. Those who do not learn from history are doomed to repeat it.

### 2. Dragged Under

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The surface of the water erupts as — with sudden and alarming speed — two massive spectral hands surge forth from the depths. These pale appendage loom above you, their woefully translucent skin stretched taught across the blackened bones beneath. And, before you know it, these grim hands attempt to grab you with chaotic ferocity.

This Echo serves as a hazard that can occur to anyone who interacts with the Well of Orbis. Anachraenum sages speculate that these pale, spectral hands are a manifestation of Orbis itself, and essentially work to guard the Well from interlopers (whatever their intentions might be).

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### The Hands of Orbis
>
> Each character located within 15 feet of the pool (which is likely to be each party member, unless implicitly stated) must immediately make a `[[/save strength 15]]` save. On a success, the character manages to break free from the initial hold; on a failure, the character becomes &Reference[grappled] and is dragged into the water. While Grappled this way, a character is also &Reference[restrained]. At the end of each of the Grappled character's turns, the character repeats the save, ending the condition on a success.
>
> - **Attunement: Mayis**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this save.
>
> Each character must roll &Reference[initiative] to determine the precise order they can act during this frantic moment. Any character who is restrained by the hands for three or more turns must roll on the [[Chaotic Visions]] Table below.
>
> Attempts to attack the hands are unsuccessful; no matter what the characters try, all attacks against the hands (whether magical or mundane) simply pass through their spectral form.
>
> #### Lending an Extra Hand
>
> Characters who aren't Grappled by the hands can attempt to assist allies who are. Any character who makes either a successful `[[/check arcana 16]]` or `[[/check athletics 16]]` check is able to grant an allied character **+2 Boons** on their next saving throw made to escape the Grapple.
>
> - **Expend 1 Spell Slot**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on the Arcana check.
> - **[[Rope]] or other tools**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on the Athletics check.
> - **Critical Success**: The Grappled character immediately escapes.

|  | #### Chaotic Visions |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | #### Shattered Reality  The character feels a brief connection to primordial ancient beings, and their minds are twisted by the sudden insight. They gain advantage on Intelligence checks or saving throws for the next hour but experience unsettling visions during that time, causing disadvantage on Wisdom saving throws. |
| 2 | #### Curse of the Forgotten  The character is temporarily cursed by the entities that dwell within the well. They suffer a -1 penalty to all ability checks and saving throws for the next 24 hours. |
| 3 | #### Glimpse of Chaos  The well's energy courses through the character, and they see a vast and untapped ocean of power, enhancing their abilities. For the next hour, they can temporarily upcast one spell to a higher level without expending a higher-level spell slot, but they suffer one level of exhaustion afterwards. |
| 4 | #### Spirit Manifestation  The well’s energies resonate with the character’s essence, and as they see a vision of a great battle between cosmic forces, but just as they are about to see the truth of things, something, or rather someone, pulls them away. When they emerge from the pool they are holding the [[Shifting Shard]]. |

### Claiming the Key

Once the party has experienced all four Echoes, the characters can claim the [[Well Challenge Key]], which is presented to them by the Well in dramatic fashion.

If the party answered six or more questions correctly, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The sound of some ethereal chime dances through the air as the Well’s arcane light begins to fade. Unsettling shadows replace the soft glow that once illuminated the area, and their twisted, tenebrous forms appear to writhe amidst the dusky gloom. A familiar yet unsettling voice slithers its way through your mind:
>
> > Knowledge can be a great gift … and in the proper hands, a weapon. Such armaments will serve you well in the trials and challenges to come. Congratulations are in order.
>
> As the ghostly voice recedes, a glittering key shimmers into existence before your very eyes, conjured out of thin air and hovering idly above the Well.

If the party answered fewer than six questions correctly, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The sound of some ethereal chime dances through the air as the Well’s arcane light begins to fade. Unsettling shadows replace the soft glow that once illuminated the area, and their twisted, tenebrous forms appear to writhe amidst the dusky gloom. A familiar yet unsettling voice slithers its way through your mind:
>
> > Despite your lack of understanding, bravery can help steer your fate. Tenacity deserves its own reward. Knowledge is key, but so is the will to carry on.
>
> As the ghostly voice recedes, a glittering key shimmers into existence before your very eyes, conjured out of thin air and hovering idly above the Well.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Completing the Challenge
>
> The [[Well Challenge Key]] magically appears in front of the party at this time, and the Keystone Challenge is concluded.

#### Orbis Attunement: Well Challenge Key

Any character who helped the party acquire the [[Well Challenge Key]] advances their **Attunement: Orbis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.
