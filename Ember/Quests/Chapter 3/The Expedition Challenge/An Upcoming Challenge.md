# An Upcoming Challenge

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs when the party visits [[All-Fable Keep]] in [[Ordain]], where they can meet with Loremistress [[Adelyne Goss]] and learn about the [[Anachraenum]] Expedition Challenge. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Meet with Loremistress Adelyne Goss to learn more about the storied history of the Anachraenum and their famed Expedition Challenge — an ultimate contest of grit and skill, open to adventurers the world over.
> - Meet a rival group of villainous adventurers known as the Salt Breakers, who wish to participate in the Expedition Challenge themselves.
> - Gather information to prepare for their own involvement in the upcoming Expedition Challenge.
>
> This Event is depicted using the [[The Arctus Plateau]] Region Map, and may occur either before or after the characters meet the adventurer [[Fernis Ossa]] during [[An Auspicious Acquaintance]].

### Meeting Adelyne Goss

As the party enters All-Fable Keep and walks through the entrance hall, they can eavesdrop on the exchange between Adelyne Goss (the prestigious Loremistress of the [[Anachraenum]]) and the Salt Breakers (an aggressive group of would-be guild members who hail from the [[Salt Flats]] town of [[Pryor]]).

If the party allows their conversation to finish, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > ... As I said earlier, you are welcome to participate in the Expedition Challenge. However, as we approach the start date, the guild is not accepting any new trainees at this time. We are simply too busy with other administrative tasks and ongoing efforts. Now, do you have any other pertinent questions, or can I help these folks behind you?
>
> As the woman finishes her scathing and officious retort, one of the young adventurers reaches idly for his dagger, as if his next thoughts were of violence. Instead, he merely grips the dagger's pommel with frustration.
>
> > No, madam. But thank you very much for your time. We'll return for the event. Good day.
>
> At that, the three travelers step aside, and the woman behind the counter beckons you forward with a few flicks of her wrist and a heavy sigh.
>
> > So … what can I do for you fine people?

> [!info] Social
> #### Speaking with Adelyne Goss
>
> Adelyne Goss is extremely busy in preparation for the upcoming Expedition Challenge. As the guild's Loremistress, she is usually occupied elsewhere, and is only temporarily handling the duties of the guild's receptionist here at the front desk. Her demeanor towards the party is initially curt and officious, but the characters can attempt to change this disposition to a more friendly one.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 18)** check can mollify Adelyne's tone, resulting in a more amiable exchange.
>
> - **Knowledge: Aedir**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Legends**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Society (DC 13)** check is able to recognize that Adelyne is [[Waerd]], based on the traditional Waerd armor she wears beneath her more Ordani-styled clothing. Paired with her visible injuries, it's reasonable to assume that she's an experienced monster hunter.
>
> - **Culture: Waerd**: The character automatically succeeds on this check. Recognizing their shared cultural history, Adelyne also recounts a brief tale for the character, detailing her death-defying battle with a [[Dragons]] in the Duskflare Jungles long ago.
> - **Knowledge: Politics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Path: Caravanner**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Regardless of Adelyne's disposition towards the party, conversation topics she is willing to discuss include (but are not limited to) the following:
>
> - Her duties as Loremistress of the [[Anachraenum]], and a brief account of her history as a monster-hunting adventurer.
> - General information about the Anachraenum and the Expedition Challenge.
> - Scant details about other contestants who plan on participating in the Challenge.
> - How to join the Expedition Challenge themselves.
> - General information about the ancient [[Aedir]].
> - General information about the ancient [[Sedyri]].
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 15)** check can tell that Adelyne is a very sincere and officious person, and that she takes her role in the Anachraenum with upmost seriousness. If she's hiding something, it has nothing to do with the guild or its Expedition Challenge (save for the innermost workings of its associated contests and trials).
>
> Specific dialogue options for Adelyne Goss are presented below.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Expedition Challenge?
>
> **A:**
>
> > This year's Expedition Challenge is expected to be a grand event. It will test the resolve of several adventuring parties new to Ordain, as well as a few trainees from within the guild's own ranks. I have no doubt this contest will drum up some eager and capable new members.
> >
> > The first half of the Challenge requires parties to indirectly compete with each other by locating and conquering three smaller trials, which can be done in any order. Each of these trials can be found at a mysterious location somewhere outside the city, and each of them will require contestants to obtain a special key.
> >
> > Once all three of these keys have been acquired, contestants can return to undertake the final challenge: a dangerous and enigmatic gauntlet known as the Chamber of Ageseros, located beneath our very feet, deep below All-Fable Keep.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What is the Chamber of Agaseros?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The Chamber of Agaseros is a proving ground for would-be Anachraenum members. It is a uniquely dangerous dungeon complex, created nearly a hundred years ago by a maniacal wizard named Agaseros. Despite his faults, Agaseros was a most powerful wizard, and a rather passionate member of the guild.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who is competing?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Multiple teams are competing in the Expedition Challenge this year, and many of them have already registered. Some of these groups are known to me — including a few headstrong Anachraenum trainees — while others are total newcomers to our organization. The group I was just speaking to, for example.
>
> She motions to the eager trio of adventurers, who are idling in an alcove nearby.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Can we register for the Expedition Challenge?
>
> **A:**
>
> > As luck would have it, I'm afraid I don't have any remaining registration sheets with me at this time. The pros and cons of putting the Loremistress to work at the front desk, I suppose …
> >
> > Now, if you happened to be sponsored by another member of the Anachraenum, that would certainly move this process along with substantial alacrity. Otherwise, I'm afraid you'll have to come back another day.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Aedir?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The most well-known ancient Empire, their legacy, and legendary collapse that occured during the Shattering are of course undeniable — but it has been over two-and-a-half-thousand years since the Empire fell, and much of their culture remains lost to us.
> >
> > Research and investigations into their daily lives, structures of government, belief systems … all of this and more is ongoing here at the Anachraenum. And we have a number of teams located around the world who have dedicated their lives to researching the Sunfire Empire. In fact, I believe one of our most esteemed members, Rai Jare, is currently putting together a commission to travel to the central continent of the Starshield itself.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Sedyri?
>
> **A:**
>
> > My personal area of expertise. The Sedyri are an ancient people that have predominantly been lost to history. Few commonfolk even recognize the name, but I have theories that suggest the Sedyri Empire once held sway over the Arcturian coastline, and further still.
> >
> > Recently, I learned of an island to the southeast called Kadisos, which is said to be connected to the Sedyri. It would be fascinating and fortuitous to be able to one day explore this island and discover its secrets for myself, but alas — that excursion is most unlikely, given my current responsibilities as Loremistress.

### The Salt Breakers

As the party speaks with Adelyne Goss, the trio of adventurers known as the Salt Breakers linger nearby in an entrance hall alcove, plotting their next moves.

If the party decides to engage the trio in conversation, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The trio of Arcturian adventurers grow silent as you approach, and their leader — a stern and expressionless fellow — crosses his arms and narrows his eyes. He addresses you, but his words seem to cut a bit more than they should for polite conversation.
>
> > First time at All-Fable Keep, friend?

> [!info] Social
> #### Meeting the Salt Breakers
>
> The Salt Breakers have already registered for the Challenge, but are frustrated with the ongoing wait for Anachraenum membership and general lack of support from the guild. They have traveled from the small salt-mining town of [[Pryor]], which is located just off the shore of the distant [[Salt Flats]] to the south of [[Ordain]]. Treat each of the Salt Breakers as [[Anachraenum Adventurer]].
>
> **Blister Brass (Neutral Evil, Arcturian Human, he/him)**
>
> This brawny human has burly, muscular arms and thick black hair. His short, messy beard has been shaven into a boxy shape, and he wears a combination of weathered leathers and mining tools. A large pickaxe hangs on his back.
>
> **White Bark (Chaotic Evil, Arcturian Thornling, they/them)**
>
> This Thornling is adorned with barbed skin as white as the salt from his hometown. White Bark is equipped with a variety of mining tools and leather harnesses, and a pyrefly lantern hangs from one of his thorny hips.
>
> **Maisie Vanrod (Neutral Evil, Arcturian Signborn-Wirrun, she/her)**
>
> Maisie has white skin and fur, coupled with the addition of two etherial Signborn arms, as red as the surface of Ragen itself. Her expression stoic. But the long, barbed spear in her hand suggests she's ready to spring into combat at a moment's notice.
>
> The Salt Breakers will answer questions from the party, but only briefly, and their responses are generally hostile or dismissive. This trio has little (if any) interest in revealing much about themselves to the party.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check can infer that the Salt Breakers see the party as rivals for the upcoming Expedition Challenge, and detect an subtle air of violent hostility.

### Concluding the Event

Since the party has no immediate business here at All-Fable Keep, they must go about their business. And while they can linger here in the entrance hall for an hour or two, guild members will eventually come along to respectfully usher them out.
