# Scholar in Need

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs while the party is traversing the [[Golden Flats]], and introduces them to the [[Ordani]] scholar **Losten Vax —** a hopeful participant in the [[Anachraenum]] upcoming Expedition Challenge who has stopped to investigate some ancient ruins. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Meet Losten Vax and gain insight into the upcoming Expedition Challenge, hosted by the Anachraenum of [[All-Fable Keep]] in [[Ordain]].
> - Explore the ruins and learn about the central monument — a Cinderspar pillar — and the ancient [[Sedyri]] culture that created it long ago.
>
> This Event is depicted using the [[The Arctus Plateau]] Region Map.

### Meeting Losten Vax

As the party passes by or approaches the stranger, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The figure on the hill appears to be Human, and they are holding a book in one hand as they peer intently downwards at the black rock with a frown upon their face. Their clothing is immaculate with long hemmed trousers, a thick waistband of shimmering red cloth, and a delicate floppy-looking hat to shade their eyes from the sun.
>
> He turns to you as you approach, his eyes springing fearfully to your appearance and weapons, and his hand reaches down to the small dagger he wears on his waistband.
>
> > Wait, stop right there … who are you!
>
> He does not back away and stands his ground, but it's clear that he is extremely wary of you and could bolt at any second.

> [!info] Social
> #### Talking with Losten Vax
>
> Before talking with Losten, the party must succeed in calming him down and earning some measure of trust.
>
> **Losten Vax (Chaotic Good, Ordani Human, he/him)**
>
> An intellectual and perpetually nervous scholar.
>
> Any character who makes either a successful **Diplomacy (DC 13)** or **Deception (DC 15)** check is able to convince Losten Vax that the party's intentions are friendly, and that his fears are unnecessary.
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check, thanks to a shared interest in relics of the past.
> - **Knowledge: Legends**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check, thanks to a shared interest in legendry.

If the party does not succeed in calming Losten, the Event concludes as the scholar disappears:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> With a small sound, like the chirping of a bird, this timid-looking scholar, now even more afraid, does not spare you a second glance as he slams his book shut and runs down the hill, disappearing into the grasses of the flats and quickly vanishing from sight.

If the party manages to befriend Losten, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The stranger seems eager for your company.
>
> > Oh, thank goodness! I thought you might have been bandits! My name is Losten Vax, and I’m a trainee with the Anachraenum. I found this strange ancient stonework on my way back to Ordain to participate in the upcoming Expedition Challenge, but I can’t for the life of me figure out which ancient culture it’s from!

> [!info] Social
> #### Conversation with Losten
>
> If they can convince Losten Vax that their intentions are good, the party can continue their conversation with the wayward scholar, during which time he can provide additional information about himself and the Expedition Challenge. Topics of conversation that Losten is willing discuss include (but are not limited to) the following:
>
> - A brief account of the Expedition Challenge in Ordain.
> - A brief account of the [[Anachraenum]], headquartered at [[All-Fable Keep]] in [[Ordain]].
> - The ruins here, which are marked by the presence of a central menhir made of black stone, a Cinderspar pillar of the ancient [[Sedyri]].
> - His personal travel plans, and whether or not he desires company for the road.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Deception (DC 13)** check perceives Losten as an honest, straightforward scholar, with noble intentions and a kind heart.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Expedition Challenge
>
> **A:**
>
> > The Expedition Challenge is the second event run by the Anachraenum; the first was held five years ago and was won by Fernis Ossa. Participating in the event grants full membership into the Anachraenum guild immediately, along with a pouch full of gold and the chance to borrow one of the guild's artifacts from the archives. You can compete alone or in teams, drawing in people from all over the region and even beyond! It's very exciting, and although I may not be much good at fighting or combat parts, I hope to find some like-minded souls to join up with in the city!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Anachraenum
>
> **A:**
>
> > The Anachraenum is also known as the Lore Hunters guild by many! It's a large organization of archaeologists, explorers, treasure seekers, and adventurers who travel around the world, exploring ruins and discovering treasure. Our main base of operations is in Ordain, known as the All-Fable Keep, where we store and catalog the artifacts and knowledge we find in the Amaranthine Archives.
> >
> > I'm what's called a "trainee," but I have mostly spent the last few years staying close to Ordain. I'm trying to make a breakthrough which will gain me more esteem in the eyes of my peers.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Black Stone
>
> **A:**
>
> > I'm not really sure what it is. It clearly seems infused with some kind of magic and appears very old, yet I can't find any references linking black stones like this to any culture I know in the region that would make sense. Unless it's Sedyri, but I only know that they were an ancient ocean-faring culture from long before the Shattering.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your travel?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I was in Helkas recently, but I left a few weeks ago to head towards Ordain. I had hoped to stay for the Dusktide Festival, a big celebration they have every year or so, but I was worried I'd be late and so I departed before the festival started. It was a shame to miss it.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Travel together?
>
> **A:**
>
> > No, I prefer to journey on my own — and I already have a few friends in the Anachraenum who I've promised to join with for the challenge ahead.

### The Sedyri Ruins

The party can try to study the black rock themselves, but they probably won't learn much. It is an extremely weathered and ancient stone, but they might learn a few details.

> [!tip] Exploration
> Any character who makes a successful **Science (DC 15)** check recognizes that this is indeed a black stone typically crafted by an ancient culture known as the [[Sedyri]]. Records, myths, and legends state that they were known for creating durable black stone structures using magic, fire, and water through a curious process similar to forging ores and metals.
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Legends**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: You recognize that this ruin is made of a material famously associated with the ancient Sedyri that they called Cinderspar. The material itself is extremely durable and can withstand weathering and the passage of time for many thousands of years. It is also very light and is constructed through the precise injection of near-boiling water via magical means into melted volcanic stone, creating bubbles of compressed water within the rock. The pillar is likely between 10,000 and 100,000 years old and predates even the [[Aedir]] Empire.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 15)** check understands that the stone is being held together with faint remnants of Abjuration magic, and would have crumbled to dust long ago without these enchantments.
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

#### Signara Attunement: Ruins Identified

Any character who successfully identifies the Sedyri ruins advances their **Attunement: Signara (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.
