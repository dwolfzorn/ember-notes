# Commissioned Work

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs when the party returns to [[Cherish Ellerie]] in her office in [[Gossamer]] to discuss the end results of their work investigating the artist slandering [[House Salva]]. By reporting to Cherish, the characters can:
>
> - Implicate Varinna, leaving her to take the blame for the murals and globlin attack if they choose.
> - Report on the outcome of their clash with [[Falar]] in Redwalk Ramble.
> - Get rewarded for their efforts based on what they accomplished during prior Events.
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Cherish's Office" Level of the [[Vista: Ordain Interiors]] Vista.

>

### Framing Varinna

The party can leave Varinna to take the blame for the murals and suffer the consequences, even though she is ultimately innocent. To do so, the following criteria must be met:

- The party never [[Mixed Media]] or [[Mixed Media]] in Flotsam during [[Mixed Media]].
- Either the party [[Matters of Perspective]] or [[Matters of Perspective]] at Redwalk Ramble during [[Matters of Perspective]].

`[[/outcome framed]]`

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A look of surprise crosses Cherish's face.
>
> > I have a hard time believing that Varinna would do anything like this. She seemed like a level-headed and reasonable person. Plus, she had an active and quite lucrative contract to produce a mural for House Salva… but, well, without any evidence exonerating her, it looks how it looks.
>
> With a disappointed sigh, Cherish grabs her notebook and scribbles down several new to-do tasks.
>
> > I'll go ahead and get her contract terminated, and start the paperwork on a court case against her.
>
> She gestures toward her window with her pencil, eyes rising to you for a moment.
>
> > It's a shame, you know? I quite liked her, but Salva can't take this kind of slander lying down. At any rate, thanks for the help. The door attendant has a purse of coin for you, payment for the work. He'll make sure you get it on your way out.
>
> You hear the door open behind you, and a quick glance back reveals a familiar attendant, waiting. He offers a smile and small nod as Cherish offers her farewell:
>
> > Stay safe out there, you never know who you can trust these days!

At this point, the party is free to depart and collect payment. See [[Commissioned Work]] for what the party is paid for their work.

#### Abyss Attunement: Varinna Framed

Any character who implicates Varinna with full knowledge that she is innocent advances their **Attunement: The Abyss (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

### Falar's Fate

As long as the party hasn't [[Commissioned Work]] for the murals, they can follow up with Cherish on the fate of Falar, getting slightly different results based on how things resolved:

If the party [[Matters of Perspective]] at Redwalk Ramble, Cherish responds this way:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > It's good you stopped Falar before he could unleash his illusions on the public. I can't imagine how bad it would have been if people had turned up to the Art Ramble set up as it was. Folks would have died, and that blood would have been on Salva's hands, at least partially.
>
> She pauses a moment as a thought occurs to her, which is followed by Cherish picking up her notebook to quickly jot down a note for herself. As she writes, she mumbles aloud:
>
> > I'm going to have to make sure future events have better security...
>
> Clapping the notebook shut, she looks at you.
>
> > Anyhow, thank you! The doorman will have your payment for services rendered when you head out. It was good working with you, but I hope the next time we cross paths it will involve a little less violence.
>
> You hear the door open behind you, and a quick glance that way reveals the attendant, waiting. He offers a smile and small nod. Cherish adds:
>
> > Take care out there, Ordain's a big, crazy place!

If the party [[Matters of Perspective]] at Redwalk Ramble, Cherish says:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Cherish seems relieved, as though a burden was lifted from her shoulders.
>
> > I'm glad that you managed to bring him to justice without killing him. He deserves to face the full fury of the Hallows for his actions. Not only was he slandering the good names and images of House Salva, he could have gotten people killed.
>
> She looks out the window at the Ordain skyline for a moment, and muses.
>
> > I wouldn't be surprised if they put him in Spellbreaker Tower and never let him out again. If he's lucky, maybe they'll let him have some painting supplies.
>
> She turns and looks back to you.
>
> > Anyhow, when you head out, the doorman will have your payment for services rendered. It was a pleasure working with you, and I hope we meet again under more pleasant circumstances!
>
> Behind you, the door opens, and a quick glance that way reveals the attendant waiting. He offers a silent nod and smile.

If [[Matters of Perspective]] from Redwalk Ramble, Cherish states:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Cherish lets out a faintly exasperated sigh, then sweeps her hair back over her shoulder.
>
> > I'm surprised he managed to elude you! Though if he was as potent an illusionist as Mistress Caberi indicated, perhaps giving you the slip and vanishing into the city would actually be pretty trivial for him.
> >
> > Still, it leaves me uneasy, with too many unanswered questions: Is he going to keep creating dangerous trap murals? Will he plan another sabotage at a House Salva event?
> >
> > Hopefully not, but I'm sure the Veiled Chain are looking for him, and nobody can hide from them for long.
>
> She waves her hands, banishing the thoughts and topic, moving on:
>
> > Anyhow, I'm sure you did your best. I'll make sure you get paid for your work up to this point. Thank you for trying at least. If Falar pops up again, I'll be sure to reach out. Maybe we'll have a second chance at getting him...
> >
> > Take care out there, and watch your backs!
>
> Behind you, the door opens, and the attendant steps into view, giving you a short nod and smile as he begins waiting for you to make your exit.

At this point, the party is free to depart and collect payment. See [[Commissioned Work]] for what the party is paid for their work.

### Receiving Payment

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you exit the building onto the street once more, the doorman offers you a small wooden box sealed with a ribbon. It bears the House Salva crest and is particularly heavy for its size. A small note attached to it says:
>
> > Payment for valuable services rendered to House Salva.

Inside the box is an amount of gold based on the party's performance in the quest:

- If [[Matters of Perspective]] or if the party [[Commissioned Work]] or [[Matters of Perspective]], they receive a  **250**.
- If the party [[Matters of Perspective]], they receive a  **500**.
