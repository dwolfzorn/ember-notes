# Color Commentary

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs as the party moves through [[Ordain]] and encounters one of several murals created to critique aspects of the city, its leadership, and recent happenings. By examining the mural, the characters can:
>
> - Discover a social issue affecting Ordain.
>
> The mural "Work in Progress" is unique and should only be discoverable after the party completes [[Outside the Lines]] and [[Mixed Media]]. All other murals can appear at any time.
>
> This Event becomes substantially more likely to trigger after the [[Outside the Lines]] Outcome in [[Outside the Lines]]. Once [[Matters of Perspective]] is completed, this Event becomes progressively less likely to occur with each passing day.

### The Work in Progress

This mural should only be discoverable once the party has completed [[Mixed Media]] and [[Matters of Perspective]]. It should take precedence over any other murals if the criteria for it appearing are met.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You round a corner and find a figure in the midst of painting a mural. The artist is startled, and in the natural moment of awkwardness, you realize the person before you is none other than Falar. It takes only a breath for him to recognize you in turn, and a look of concern flashes in his eyes as he does.
>
> He drops his paint and brush and begins running. You can hear him saying something as he breaks around a corner, but the words are unintelligible.

> [!abstract] Falar
> **[[Falar]]**
>
> Level 1 · Unknown Unknown
>
> ![Falar](../../../assets/image027.webp)

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Illusory Chase
>
> As Falar begins his flight, he casts [[Silent Image]] to create a false copy of himself in a nearby alley as a distraction, then takes a different route, leaving the decoy to draw attention.
>
> It is intended that the party won't be able to catch Falar in this Event.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Chasing Falar
>
> Any character with a `[[/skill perception 12 passive format=long]]` or who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 10)** check can pursue Falar to the end of an alley, trying to get a door open.

Characters that approach Falar in the alley should be given the following description:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you approach Falar, he begins pacing in a circle, panic setting into his posture before he glances your way and then turns to a closed door, which he tries to open.
>
> As you draw closer, you watch Falar fully panic, throwing himself against the door and trying to get it to budge. This is the moment you realize the truth: there's no sound or weight to his actions. He isn't looking at you but simply through you, and after a moment, his actions begin to repeat.
>
> This is an illusion, and Falar's gotten away.

`[[/outcome workProgress]]`

Searching the area reveals a few spots of glimmering paint leading off in the opposite direction of the illusion and the mural, but they quickly stop, making all attempts to track him in the city impossible.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Predicting Falar
>
> Any character with a `[[/skill insight 16 passive format=long]]` or who makes a successful **Deception (DC 14)** check deduces that Falar will head somewhere familiar and safe. While they don't know where he lives, they do know where his paint shop is. Searching his shop in [[Flotsam]] could yield insights or provide clues into what he's doing.
>
> - **Knowledge: Crime**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Any character who examines the glimmering paint specks and makes a successful **Arcana (DC 12)** check suspects that searching Falar's paint shop could lead to the discovery of components necessary to create paint globlins.

With Falar in the wind, the party can return to the mural to inspect it if they like. The mural consist of only vague shapes and has no clear image yet. However, the wet paint gleams brightly with latent magic.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Inspecting the Mural
>
> A character who inspects the jars and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check notices that the containers are identical to those Falar sells in his shop, but they are labeled differently, clearly not intended for sale.
>
> A character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 14)** check while examining the paint notices that the jars themselves are carved with various abjuration symbols meant to contain the magic in the paint.

### The Veiled Chain's Eyes

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The street here is painted with shadowy figures, with bright eyes arranged in such a way that they seem to follow you as you move past the mural. Upon closer inspection, each figure bears the symbol of the Veiled Chain.

`[[/outcome chainEye]]`

### The Burnished Left Hand

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This brutal mural depicts a Burnished Hand warrior with their armored foot on the chest of a prone monster, axe raised to behead it. The creature snarls and lashes out with wicked claws but can do nothing, pinned as it is.
>
> The art is done in mottled grays and whites, like an ancient statue, while in the foreground, in full color, a Burnished Hand warrior mimics the motion. Yet it is an Ordani citizen he has pinned underfoot, and their expression is one of panic as they hold their arms up to shield themselves from the coming blow.

`[[/outcome burnishedHand]]`

### The House of Criminals

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A series of wanted posters are painted on the wall here, each one depicting a figure wanted for theft, extortion, racketeering, or worse. Beside the posters, the wanted figures stand, each one clad in a Wandren uniform and amicably chatting with figures wearing badges of the Hallows and Veiled Chain.

`[[/outcome houseCriminals]]`

### The Temple Fortress

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This mural depicts the Cindarin Temple of Ordain as a fortress, with jagged barricades and spear walls of Cindaric Sages in crimson and gold armor, keeping hordes of Ordani from approaching. The crowds are not depicted as aggressive, but as destitute masses desperately trying to gain access to the grand temple.

`[[/outcome templeFortress]]`

### The Unwanted Stranger

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This mural depicts a group of Arcturian refugees, each one thin, haggard, and desperate in their look. They are huddled together and shielding themselves with bare arms as rocks rain down on them. The mural is situated so that the rocks have come from various windows on the building, as though the occupants themselves have cast the stones. Some of the refugees are already wounded, others lie on the ground, perhaps dead or nearly so.

`[[/outcome unwantedStranger]]`

### The Endless Mines

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This mural depicts the mouth of a mine surrounded by a crowd of mine workers, half of them injured in some way or another. A stream of bloody and battered miners are shown trudging out of the entrance, some carrying the limp bodies of other workers with them. For each miner leaving, a new figure is depicted marching inward to replace them.

`[[/outcome endlessMines]]`

### The Warmonger's Word

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The mural depicts a pair of faceless figures shaking hands and exchanging a bag overflowing with coin. One is dressed as an Ordani, in pale, flowing robes, and the other clad in the black and red uniform of the Tayan Empire. The Ordani's hands are stained by blood from the pouch of money, and a bloody dagger peeks out of the Tayan's sleeve.

`[[/outcome warmongerWord]]`

### The Hardened Heart

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This large mural depicts a crowd of Arcturian refugees huddled together around the feet of three Cindaric Sages. The crowd looks up to them, some raising their hands in begging gestures. The first sage is rotund in body, holding food and drink in either hand, partaking in both merrily. The second wraps a bandage around her hand as a single droplet of blood beads on a cut fingertip. Lastly, the third sage tacks a no vacancy sign up on the wall, his back turned on the crowd at his feet.

`[[/outcome hardenedHeart]]`
