# Overview

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Requirements
>
> This Side Quest occurs during Chapter 3, and begins while the party is traversing [[Ordain]]. There are no prerequisites for this Side Quest.
>
> #### Difficulty
>
> "Local Color" is designed for a party of Level 4 characters.
>
> #### Milestone Progression
>
> This is a Side Quest that awards 1 [[Milestone Progression]] upon successful completion of [[Commissioned Work]].

Long ago, an adventurer known as Falar struck out to find success in Ordain, and for a while he did well for himself. A man of charm and intelligence, he quickly found allies, made connections in high society, and built a small fortune for himself as a purveyor of magical knowledge and tricks for other ambitious adventurers. However, every luxury and laurel that Ordain can bestow, it can also take away, and through a series of poor decisions, chance encounters, and soured relationships, Falar watched everything he'd spent his life building vanish overnight.

Forced to sell off his extensive magical library to pay his creditors, Falar was left with nothing to his name but a few deeds to ramshackle buildings in Flotsam. Having lost all taste for adventuring, Ordain, and magic, he retired into obscurity and was never heard from again. For many years, he lived as a simple paint monger and even tried to rebuild himself as an artist, but Ordain has a long memory, and Falar still harbored grudges that made success difficult.

Now, years later, an anonymous artist has begun creating murals across Ordain, criticizing and drawing attention to aspects of the city, its culture, and its government that some might find distasteful or dubious. Their first target is the mighty House Salva and the iron hand it uses to control the vast textile and fashion industries that dominate the culture of Ordain. While this would be uncomfortable but tolerable in most situations, the artist has begun to escalate, and people may die as a result if someone doesn't intervene.

What begins with a chance encounter involving a mural, a House Salva member, and some chaotic elements becomes a series of events that will take the party across Ordain, from the working-class canals of Flotsam to the heart of House Salva's operations in Gossamer, all in pursuit of Falar as he creates a series of elementally charged murals. Will the party be able to uncover his identity and stop him? Or will they find merit in his complaints against Ordain?
