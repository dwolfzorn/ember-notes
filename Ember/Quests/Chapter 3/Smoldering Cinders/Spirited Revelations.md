# Spirited Revelations

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs in the wake of the party's fight in the Burial Grounds, and allows them to present their findings to [[Sionia]] in the [[Numinous Shrines]] district. In this event, the characters can:
>
> - Speak with Sionia about the encounter at the Burial Grounds during [[Grave Assignments]].
> - Help Sionia analyze the undead remains they collected from the Cindaric cremetorium.
> - Earn a potential reward for their clandestine placement of Sionia's five [[Ken Crystal]].

### Post Mortem

The party returns to [[Sionia]] at the Numinous Shrines district following their deadly encounter in [[Grave Assignments]]. At the Mall of The Nineteen, the characters will present undead remains to the shard goddess for scrutiny, along with their first-hand account of the harrowing battle and their unholy assailants.

After the characters have presented Sionia with the evidence, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Sionia helps you lay the undead remains down upon the hard flagstone floor of the mall, and kneels to situate herself slightly above them. As the shard goddess begins waving her hands in arcane gesticulation, an ectoplasmic mist of strange occult energy starts seeping out of the remains and into her fingertips. Her eyes glow bright with arcane power as she throws her head back with ecstatic dread.
>
> The Deathbinder speaks, but the ethereal voice you hear is one of multitudes.
>
> > The shade of the Everdark grows long. But our threat is strangely close at hand. These remains are riddled with a dark necromancy, more loathsome than any magic I've yet seen. Beyond Varun malediction … beyond the ghosts of Sunfire sorcery … our threat is furtive and unknown. A darkness beyond our sight.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Sionia's Examination
>
> Using esoteric magic, the shard goddess conducts a brief investigation of the undead remains.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Arcana (DC 15)** check is able to assist Sionia with her efforts during the investigation, and gains advantage on their next **Knowledge: Undeath** check.

> [!info] Social
> #### The Shard God's Analysis
>
> Sionia is able to share various pieces of information with the players during her examination of the undead remains. Such details include (but are not limited to):
>
> - What precisely happened during the party's encounter at the Burial Grounds (if they've yet to tell her).
> - The nature of the various undead remains that may have been retrieved:
>   - [[Vampyre Spawn]] remains.
>   - [[Harrower]] remains.
>   - [[Corpuleth]] remains.
>   - [[Skallith]] remains.
> - Sionia's emerging theory about what might be going on: some force, element, or entity is causing a surge of necromantic energy in the Arctus Plateau and beyond.
> - Sionia speaks reluctantly of the Lumek Flameguard, and their quest to rid the Barrows of any undead threats they might find, but recognizes the serendipity of this alliance.
>
> A successful **Arcana (DC 15)** check confirms the veracity of Sionia's arcane findings, as far as the character can tell.
>
> - **Knowledge: Undeath**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Deception (DC 13)** believes Sionia's account of her findings to be authentic (according to her knowledge, at least), and is able to sense an element of concern behind the shard goddess' all-knowing eyes.
>
> - **Critical Success**: Sionia is hiding something beyond simple concern, but the specific details of that "something" remain elusive. She is calculating something, perhaps her next move.
>
> Additionally, if the party learned the name of the necromancer [[Amelia Naxan]] and shares this information with Sionia, the shard goddess will relate some of her own personal history with the Blood Barons and her scorn for the Lumek.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Vampyre Spawn:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "This is unmistakably a creature of the Moiran wastelands. And based on your accounts of the various encounters between Westgate and the Burial Grounds, it seems the large hematophage you found feasting on the undead corpses was actually the cause of the poor fellow's original demise. This spawn was the offspring of the Corpuleth, as you call it."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Harrower:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "Whatever foul necromancy turned this Burnished Hand warrior into a withered revenant, it is a novel and curious magic — the likes of which we haven't seen. I regret that I know little else about the form and function of this … soot-handed harrower."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Corpuleth:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "Despite its overt similarities to the vampyres of the Moiran wastelands, this brutish hematophage is quite unlike any creature I've encountered or studied in my several centuries on Ember. It seems novel horrors such as this are part of whatever loathsome endgame is afoot."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Skallith:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "Skallith have been known to the Arctus Plateau for centuries, but their likes have not been seen since the time of The Shattering. Powerful necromancy is required to activate such a large cohort of undead soldiers in so quickly a time."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Mysterious Necromancer (Amelia Naxan):
>
> **A:**
>
> > "The Blood Barons are a scourge of the living planet, and their foul necromancy is characterized by the opposite of everything I represent — corruption, subjugation, and outright cosmic profanity. Amelia Naxan is among the worst of their ilk, and her acumen with the dark arts of necromancy is perhaps only rivaled by my own."

Once the party breeches the subject of what to do next, Sionia speaks.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A rustle of wind punctuates a brief moment of silence before the shard goddess speaks again.
>
> > Our tasks are clear. I must go abroad to learn more about the unseen powers at play. You must travel with the Flameguard into the shadow-haunted Arcturian Barrows, where their undying boogeymen await your scrutiny — and your blade. If this necromancer you speak of is nearby, those murky lowlands are as good a prospect as any …
> >
> > Fare thee well, adventurers. And keep your heads about you.
>
> With a smile and a wink, the shard goddess becomes one with the ghostly mists that perpetually surround her and coalesces into the form of a phantasmal raven that takes flight. Up and away she flies, into the starlit sky.

The Shard Goddess is fascinated, and muses what the implications of this finding might be for the long-term sustainability of the cycle of souls. Sionia tells the party they must travel to the Barrows, pursue this Necromancer, and learn more if they can about what is happening.

### Eyes for the Deathbinder

If the party managed to successfully complete their secondary objective by secretly placing Sionia's [[Ken Crystal]] throughout the Burial Grounds, the shard goddess rewards them at this time.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The shard goddess casts a watchful gaze about your group.
>
> > I can see you no longer carry the ken crystals. I presume your attempt to place them throughout the cemetery and the Cindaric crematorium were successful?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Ken Crystals:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "The ken crystals are simple but effective tools for divination. From their watchful vantage points, I'll be able to keep an eye out for errant necromancy in the cemetery. The city can't afford another incident like the one you described."

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Event Outcome: Sionia's Reward
>
> Characters who successfully install Sionia's 5 Ken Crystals in the Burial Grounds receive their own [[Ken Crystal]] as a reward, as well as the future favor of the Lonely Goddess. Mark the "Sionia's Reward" Event Outcome at the top of the page.
