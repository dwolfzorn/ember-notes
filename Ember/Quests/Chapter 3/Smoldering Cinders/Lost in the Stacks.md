# Lost in the Stacks

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This social and exploration event presents the party with a series of skill challenges in the Old City Library of [[Scholar's Nook]]. In this event, the characters can:
>
> - Make their way past the Old City Library front desk and into the lower stacks of the basement level.
> - Locate their ally [[Sin Marmot]], who has been trapped in a sticky cocoon of parchment moth silk, and free the young druid from her tenacious situation.
> - Receive a magical summons from the Second Sage [[Lilla Arien]], who has a new assignment for Sin (alongside an invitation for the party's help).

#### Early Access Work in Progress

This event is intended to use an Ordain Interior vista configured for Scholar's Nook, which is unavailable at this time. The interior scene must be imagined for now.

### The Front Desk

The front desk of Old City Library is supervised by a single [[Cindaric Adherent]] at all times. An obsequious adherent named **Magda Mosswell** (LN, Ordani Hulg'run, she/her) is the desk clerk on duty when the characters arrive for this encounter.

The party has two main methods of passage past the front desk and into the lower stacks, where Sin Marmot awaits their timely (and covert) assistance: socializing with Magda for permission, or sneaking by undetected. If the characters choose to approach the front desk directly, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you step towards the front desk, you find yourself meeting the judicious gaze of the Cindaric clerk on duty here. This stoney young woman casts a discerning glance at you above the lenses of her reading glasses, her head tilted down at her work.
>
> > Can I help you with something?

> [!info] Social
> #### Navigating the Front Desk
>
> Due to so-called "renovations" in the basement and sub-basement areas of the library, the lower stacks are currently off-limits to visitors. Magda will protest the party's ingress unless they have the means to convince her.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Deception (DC 15)** or **Diplomacy (DC 15)** check is able to gain permission from Magda to search the lower stacks despite of the renovations in progress.
>
> - **Distracting Literature**: Any character who offers the obsequious front desk clerk a tawdry romance [[Book]] to satisfy her appetite for salacious fiction will be granted entry. Such books can be found in shops all over Scholar's Nook.
> - **Path: Cindaric Initiate**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
> - **Path: emberObsidianResearcher**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
> - **Deceptively Dressed**: A character wearing the robes of a Cindaric Sage or Cindaric Adherent also gains **+2 Boons**. Use of a [[Disguise Kit]] can provide a convincing facsimile.
>
> Characters who fail this check must succeed on an additional **Deception (DC 13)** check to avoid triggering the suspicions of the desk clerk, which could raise unwanted awareness of Sin Marmot's predicament.
>
> - If the characters fail this Deception check more than 3 times, they may still proceed to the lower stacks to rescue Sin, but Lilla Arien and the other Cindaric Sages will be informed of the initiate's failure here at Old City Library. Check the "Library Demerit" Event Outcome above.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Sneaking By
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Stealth (DC 16)** check is able to sneak past the front desk clerk without being detected or arousing suspicion.
>
> - Criteria: Explanation of why the character automatically succeeds.
> - [[Invisible]]: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
> - [[Help]]: Characters that cause a distraction elsewhere in the library, or directly (such as talking with Magda) grant **+2 Boons** to another character.
> - @CriticalSuccess[dc]: Additional benefits that occur as a result of critical success. Set [dc] to the base DC of the check.
>
> If the characters fail this check or choose to simply storm their way past the front desk, they may still proceed to the lower stacks to rescue Sin, but Lilla Arien and the other Cindaric Sages will be informed of the initiate's failure here at Old City Library. Check the "Library Demerit" Event Outcome above.

#### Primordis Attunement: Sneaking Past Magda

Each character who manages to successfully sneak past Magda at the front desk advances their **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

#### Luxarum Attunement: Bribe Magda

Any character who manages to successfully bribe Magda with an appropriate Book advances their **Attunement: Luxarum (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Into the Lower Stacks

Once the party has successfully navigated the front desk, they can proceed into the lower stacks located in the library's basement level. Here, [[Sin Marmot]] has been trapped inside a cocoon by a colony of oversized parchment moths, who have invaded the stacks for their seasonal nesting.

The party must locate Sin among the various cocoons scattered throughout the lower stacks and release her from her silky imprisonment — hopefully without raising a fuss and alerting the other Cindarics upstairs.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You furtively make your way into the lower stacks of the Old City Library, descending quietly down a worn wooden stairway into the building's basement level. A musky, earthen scent hangs in the air, and you can't help but notice the familiar flittering of a tatterwing moth as it floats down past you from behind.
>
> The further you go, the more the origin of the musky odor becomes apparent … the nooks and crannies of the lower stacks are covered in a messy blanket of mulberry silk and keg-sized cocoons. You quickly spy a half-dozen giant moths crawling about this cluttered space and hanging from the ceiling, no doubt the furry engineers of this silk-spun calamity. Meanwhile, countless smaller moths flutter about the room without a care in the world.
>
> Sin Marmot is hidden somewhere in that tangled mass, and it's high time to find and free her — without causing a ruckus.

The medium-sized **Parchment Moths** are oversized cousins to the tatterwing moths the characters are accustomed to, and pose no physical threat to the party or their allies.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring the Lower Stacks
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Awareness (DC 15)** check or **Awareness (DC 15)** check while searching the room is able to locate one cocoon that stands out from the rest — a Sin Marmot-shaped husk that contains the party's missing ally.
>
> - **Knowledge: Beasts**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
>
> Alternately, magic can help the party locate Sin Marmot among the silky morass.
>
> - The [[Detect Magic]] spell can be used to locate [[Sin's Pyrefly Staff]], which radiates a mixed aura of illusion and abjuration magic.
> - The [[Detect Thoughts]] spell can help pinpoint Sin's location, but will not locate her outright, and provides advantage on Perception and Investigation checks used to find her.
> - The [[Locate Animals or Plants]] spell or the [[Locate Object]] spell can be used to find specific items such as Sin's equipment or plant-based spell components the character might be familiar with.
> - Additionally, the [[Speak with Animals]] spell will allow a character to communicate directly with the parchment moths themselves, who will reveal Sin's location following a successful **Diplomacy (DC 13)** check.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Wilderness (DC 14)** check is familiar with the ecology of parchment moths and their closest kin, and know the medium-sized beasts to be docile and friendly creatures — despite their taste for aged parchment and book bindings.
>
> - Characters with **Knowledge: Beasts** have advantage on this check.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Helping Sin Escape
>
> Once the party locates Sin among the crowded silken expanse, they must free the young initiate from the cocoon that envelopes her.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **`[[/skill strength 15]]`** check is able to pry open the cocoon wide enough for Sin to escape.
>
> - A character who fails this check must succeed on a **Stealth (DC 15)** check to avoid alerting the Cindarics upstairs with a loud commotion — such as a fallen bookshelf, a fumbled piece of equipment, or the sudden fluttering of parchment moths. They may still rescue Sin from the lower stacks, but Lilla Arien and the other Cindaric Sages will be informed of the initiate's failure here at Old City Library. Check the "Library Demerit" Event Outcome above.
> - If an allied character uses the [[Help]] action to assist with this skill check, both characters must succeed on the above Stealth check to avoid detection.

When Sin Marmot is finally free of restraint in the parchment moth cocoon, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Before you've even finished, your Keth companion is crawling her way out of the cocoon with a massive grin on her face.
>
> > You came! My sincerest thanks are yours forever. I started thinking I might never see the light of Lantyr again! I'm too young to die in a moth's cocoon. What would my gran say?
>
> Sin emerges from her confinement and surveys the silky, moth-covered mess that blankets the lower stacks with a sigh of resignation.
>
> > Guess I still have a bit of cleaning to do!

### A Message from Lilla Arien

Once Sin has been freed from her silken imprisonment in the moth cocoon, the characters can eventually make their way back upstairs and exit the Old City Library with little to no conflict or obstacle. However, there's one more encounter in store for them before the day is due: a message arrives from Lilla Arien on the wings of a lone **Pyrefly**, featuring a timely new assignment for Sin in another part of town.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The process of cleaning the silk from your garments is a sticky and cumbersome one, but — with a little due diligence — you and Sin eventually find yourselves entirely free of the viscid stuff.
>
> Just then, another winged creature floats in on the wind to join your group. As a lone pyrefly curiously makes its way into the fold, Sin instinctively holds out her palm, where the luminous insect lands with the softest touch. The voice of Lilla Arien emanates from the pyrefly, who is apparently serves as some kind of magical envoy for the Second Sage.
>
> > Initiate Marmot, I hate to pull you away from your duties at Old City Library, but a situation is developing that could use your timely attention. One of our fellow Cindarics has died under mysterious circumstances, and I'd like your help with an official autopsy of the poor fellow's cadaver. Your druidic magic and investigative acumen have been valuable assets for the order, and I think this is a great opportunity to prove yourself to the sages. But you must make haste and maintain secrecy. We don't need to spread panic among the other initiates, and this is a delicate matter, best solved by a small team of specialists like yourselves.
> >
> > Meet with Menedaeus at the rear door of Traveler's Rest in Westgate, tonight at the stroke of midnight. Menedaeus has been a trustworthy and capable aide to me for years, and will tell you everything you need to know. Good luck, Sin. And may the moons guide you.
>
> With that, the tiny pyrefly takes flight and drifts off and away on the breeze.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Timely Assignment
>
> Sin is eager to wrap up any loose ends here at Old City Library before heading out to prepare for the clandestine autopsy at Traveler's Rest later this evening. She'll say goodbye to the characters for now, while making plans to join them later. Other topics of conversation might include:
>
> - Sin's reaction to getting snared and restrained in the parchment moth cocoon.
> - Everyone's suspicions about the forthcoming midnight encounter in Westgate.
> - The best way to leave Old City Library without causing a fuss.
