# Helping Hands

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event invites the party visit Veneration Hall, the headquarters of the [[Burnished Hand]] in Ordain, and learn more about the militant organization (while being covertly interrogated by the Burnished Hand’s stoic leader Steros Kraver). In this event, the characters can:
>
> - Journey to Veneration Hall in [[Lower Ashvale]] (at least one day after [[Sagacious Initiation]]) and explore the garrison’s limited public areas, gathering whatever details and information they may.
> - Meet with the venerated warrior [[Steros Kraver]] to learn more about the Burnished Hand, including somewhat propagandized associations with the Cindaric Sages and potential recruitment options.
> - Choose to avoid suspicion by obscuring their motives from Steros and other Burnished Hand members, or otherwise reveal their true intentions.
> - Meet a low-ranking [[Burnished Hand Protector]] as they leave, who solicits the party to perform a wellness check on their behalf in the neighboring [[Fallbrook]] district.

#### Early Access Work in Progress

This event is intended to use an Ordain Interior vista configured for Veneration Hall, which is unavailable at this time. The interior scene must be imagined for now.

### Gaining Entry

The Burnished Hand Protector stationed outside the entrance of Veneration Hall is a brash young solider named **Tolus** (LN, Ordani Kivahr, he/him), who must be adequately convinced or persuaded if the characters wish to gain entry to the semi-private facility. However, the party can't actually fail this test of social skill, and the characters will ultimately gain entry one way or another.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you step towards the threshold, the young warrior looks down his chiseled nose at your group with no small measure of apprehension. It's clear this armored sentinel takes the duties of his station seriously. He addresses you before you have a chance to speak:
>
> > Welcome to Veneration Hall, home of the Burnished Hand and its Protectors. Please state your business. The barracks are closed to outsiders.

> [!abstract] Burnished Hand Protector
> **[[Burnished Hand Protector]]**
>
> Level 5 · Human Protector
>
> ![Burnished Hand Protector](../../../assets/image025.webp)
>
> You regard a heavily-armored Ordani warrior, whose bronze splint mail gleams with a gorgeous russet luster. A symmetrical crimson hand with the roots of an oak tree decorates this soldier's chest piece, and the well-oiled longsword at their side looks poised and ready for action.

> [!info] Social
> #### Providing Credentials
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Diplomacy (DC 13)**, **Deception (DC 15)**, or **Intimidation (DC 17)** check is able to convince Tolus that the party's invitation by Steros is authentic.
>
> - **Path: CindaricInitiate**: The character automatically succeeds.
> - **Knowledge: Crime**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
> - **Knowledge: Politics**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
>
> Alternately, any character who targets Tolus with the [[Charm Person]] spell automatically succeeds on these checks.
>
> Additionally, the party may use their growing infamy to gain entrance. Any character who relates a rousing story about a previous encounter with an undead creature automatically succeeds on these checks. Appropriate encounters include:
>
> - the Skallith horde from the [[Sanctuary of Death]]
> - the Vampyre Spawn encounter during [[The Secret Autopsy]]
> - the Sodden Corpses of the [[Harrowed Crossing]]
> - the encounter with Tethra Shùl and her Horrendors in [[Where Evil Lurks]]
> - any other appropriate encounters.
>
> Once Tolus the Protector has been suitably convinced, he opens the massive front gate and invites the characters to enter.

#### Primordis Attunement: Entry via Deception

Any character who uses their skills of Deception to gain entry to Veneration Hall advances their **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

#### Aura Attunement: Entry via Magic

Any character who uses magic to gain entry to Veneration Hall advances their **Attunement: Aura (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

#### Luxarum Attunement: Entry via Infamy

Any character who uses their infamy to gain entry to Veneration Hall advances their **Attunement: Luxarum (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### A Meeting with Steros

Following their successful entry, the characters are escorted through Veneration Hall to the office of [[Steros Kraver]], who they likely encountered at Corpin Sanctuary and Cindarin Temple. This meeting is both an opportunity for Steros to estimate the party's capabilities as potential recruits for the Burnished Hand, and a chance for him to gauge their overall intentions as persons of interest in the looming undead threat.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As another sentinel takes his place, Tolus ushers you on a brief tour into the depths of Veneration Hall, a spacious edifice marked by the smell of musk and metal alongside sounds of combat training and lively music.
>
> Moments later, you find yourselves entering the modest office of Steros Kraver, who sits behind a large wooden desk decorated with maps, scrolls, and folded parchment. His bronze helmet serves as an ersatz paperweight as a light breeze sweeps through the room, rustling the desk's accoutrements with regularity. The commander smiles when he sees you, with looks of recognition and satisfaction on his handsome face, like a man who just won a friendly bet.
>
> > I see you've accepted my offer for a visit to Veneration Hall. Welcome to our humble abode. Make yourselves comfortable. We have water for refreshment, and plenty of room for company. So tell me: how does a motley group of slayers like yourselves get involved in so many entanglements with undead creatures? Word of your accomplishments travels fast. And I'd like to know if you have what it takes to wear the bronze plate and take arms against enemies of the Cindarics.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Meeting with Steros
>
> Steros Kraver is interested in getting to know the characters, their capabilities, and their motivations. And while he presents an element of good faith, he's also quite curious about how and why the party has been involved in the series of events to date regarding the looming undead threat.
>
> After the characters are given an opportunity to regale the commander with stories of their previous exploits, additional topics of conversation include:
>
> - The [[Burnished Hand]] and their role as stewards of the [[Cindaric Sages]] and the great city of [[Ordain]].
> - A brief account of Steros Kraver's own history and his rise to the rank of commander of the Burnished Hand.
> - The importance of the Cindaric Sages and their faith.
> - The looming undead threat and what it means for Ordain and the Arctus Plateau at large, along with the need for new recruits to stem this unholy tide. Steros is curious about the potential roles the characters could play in his organization, whether at home or afield.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Deception (DC 13)** check is able to confirm the veracity of Steros' interest in the capabilities of each party member, and his earnest desire to see if they're a good fit for the Burnished Hand.
>
> - **Critical Success**: There is an element to the commander's inquiry that suggests an ulterior motive. Nothing nefarious, just something hidden beyond the charismatic keen of his steely eyes.
>
> Any character who targets Steros with the [[Detect Thoughts]] spell is able to sense feelings of ease and anticipation, and knows that the safety of Ordain looms large in the commander's mind.
>
> If the characters attempt to confront Steros for this light ruse, the commander will readily and happily admit his agenda. Indeed, who wouldn't want to know more about such famed and capable slayers of the undead — particularly when one is the commander of the Burnished Hand. Steros views it as his solemn duty to protect the Hand and the Cindarics they serve, and will to to any lengths necessary to uphold this righteous duty.
>
> Additional conversation points are detailed below, in the commander's own words.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Burnished Hand:
>
> **A:**
>
> The commander proudly knocks on the iconography of his breastplate with a soft fist.
>
> > "The Burnished Hand is everything to me. When I needed direction, I found it here. When I asked for a reason why, the order answered. We are the open hand in times of need, and the closed fist in times of trouble. Together with the Cindarics, we seek to keep the Heartblood of Ember pumping pure and strong, for an eternity to come.
> >
> > That's a long time, my friends. And we need good soldiers and honest protectors for the long road ahead."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Opinion of the Cindaric Sages:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "The Cindarics are Ember's chosen protectors. I truly believe that. Something inside each one of them stirs them to devote their very lives to the Heartblood and its ceaseless flow. We're lucky to have such custodians in our time. The Cindarics toil so that generations of Ordani and Arcturian people can thrive. It's a beautiful thing, and it deserves to be protected at all costs."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Opinion of Jon Vastil:
>
> **A:**
>
> With a deep breath, a quick sigh of pride escapes the commander's nostrils.
>
> > "Jon Vastil is a good man with a kind heart. He's also got a rather sharp mind for his age, and manages to keep my office fairly busy. As you're well aware, we've had to take on some fairly sizable operations lately, from the march on Corpin Sanctuary to an increased street presence throughout the city. These edicts come from the Holy Speaker himself, with my counsel of course; but his edicts are fair. Jon Vastil has guided the Cindarics for the better part of a century, and the old man deserves every ounce of respect he's afforded."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Opinion of Vinarith:
>
> **A:**
>
> The commander proudly knocks on the iconography of his breastplate with a soft fist.
>
> > "The Steward is a capable and learned Sage in his own right. In fact, Vinarith's divinations have helped us prognosticate more than one of these undead encounters, but not with the swiftness or precision needed to stop them. He takes after the Holy Speaker quite a bit, and has done much to refine the modern image of the Cindarics among younger initiates. When it comes to veteran sages, Vinarith knows his way around the temple, and is recognized by many of the city's residents as one of Ordain's brightest sons. I look forward to working with him more often."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Opinion of Lilla Arien:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "The Second Sage is a lovely woman, if a bit sheepish. Her matronly kindness precedes her, and she's a valuable asset to the order. I must admit, we don't often share much in terms of conversation, and I doubt we have too many things in common aside from Ordani pride and an adherence to the Cindaric faith. But she's a lovely woman all the same."

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Area
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Awareness (DC 15)** check while actively observing Steros and his office during the meeting is able to notice the following:
>
> - The maps on the desk detail various sections of the Arctus Plateau, including a swathe of the Wedgelands to the northwest and the Bloodwoods north of Ordain. Reveal the [[Skybrush]] and [[Talei]] points of interest to the players.
> - Many of the folded parchments on the desk consist of official communication to and from Cindarin Temple, marked with the seal of the Holy Speaker.
> - The chambers of Veneration Hall are well-kept, but the eaves are dusty and the corners are dingy, which suggests a cleaning routine that overlooks the smallest details. It seems the soldiers of the Burnished Hand prefer to spend their free time on practical matters like training and leisure.
>
> Additionally, the [[Detect Magic]] spell draws attention to the commander's [[Greataxe]] mantled on a weapon rack nearby, along with a [[Spell Scroll]] of [[Identify]] and two [[Spell Scrolls]] of [[Comprehend Languages]] on the desk.

Once the party has had ample time for conversation with Steros, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> With brusque alacrity, Steros stands and dons his helmet.
>
> > Well, friends, that concludes our time for now. I look forward to additional consultations in the days and weeks to come. But for now, I have business at the Rusted Fortress that requires some timely attention. Please, return at your leisure to discuss an alliance with our group once you've had ample time to give it some serious consideration.
>
> The brash young Tolus appears once more, ready to escort you back to the front gate of Veneration Hall. Your egress is swift and without incident.

### One Last Thing…

Following their meeting with Steros, the party is approached by a lower-ranking Burnished Hand Protector with a concerned request for them. This is **Vance** (CG, Arcturian Human, he/him), a typically lighthearted solider who is currently upset by circumstances beyond his control — a fellow protector hasn't reported in for duty in a few days, and Vance wants to perform a wellness check on his friend without alerting any official parties or his commanding officers.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you linger on the street outside Veneration Hall for a brief moment, you're approached by another Burnished Hand Protector with a look of concern in his eyes. The man beseeches you with a rural Arcturian accent, his voice measured and hushed.
>
> > I'm sorry to bother you, but I could really use your help. The stories of your exploits precede you. And I'm in dire need of some reliable assistance …
>
> The young solider leans in to whisper more softly.
>
> > … Assistance that is better left a secret. I can pay you in advance, for just a few hours of your time.

> [!info] Social
> #### The Protector’s Plea
>
> If the party is willing to hear his plea, Vance pulls them aside from the main thoroughfare to disclose the precise nature of the plight at hand:
>
> - Burnished Hand protectors are assigned daily-to-weekly shifts in various districts of the city as well as stations at the Rusted Fortress on Bura Island. Vance's best mate Ordan was scheduled to relieve Vance here at Veneration Hall three days ago, and Vance doesn't want to get his closest friend in trouble if he can help it.
> - Ordan keeps an apartment in the neighboring Fallbrook district. If the party is willing to perform a wellness check on Ordan, Vance will pay them  **50** pieces in advance for their effort.
> - Ordan was seen returning from his shift at the Rusted Fortress by a few other locals, but he wasn't seen leaving his apartment.
> - Vance and Ordan have been best friends since childhood, the kind of pure and honest camaraderie that is often hard to find.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Deception (DC 11)** check can determine that Vance's words are authentic, and that his concern for Ordan and his livelihood are sincere.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Diplomacy (DC 15)** check is able to convince Vance to offer the party a little more reward for their effort, which takes the form of a jewled heirloom [[Dagger]] worth 25 gp.
>
> Additional conversation points are detailed below, in Vance's own words.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Burnished Hand:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "I'm proud to serve with the Burnished Hand. The pay is good, and I sleep well at night, even if the hours are long. It feels good to help protect the city."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Steros Kraver:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "Steros is a bit of a hard-ass, but he always means well. One thing's for sure: he won't take kindly to finding out that Ordan's derelict of duty, even if he's only been shitting his brains out in Fallbrook for the past three days."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why are you so concerned about Ordan?
>
> **A:**
>
> > "Ordan likes to drink. And he loves to fight. This isn't the first time he's screwed up a shift; but if he keeps this up, it just might be his last. We go back pretty far, Ordan and me. Hell, I joined the Burnished Hand because of that brazen fool. And I'll be damned if he gets kicked out before I do. So it would mean a lot to me if you could just drag him back to work without a fuss."

Once the party accepts Vance's offer, he provides the characters with precise directions to Ordan's apartment in Fallbrook, which the party can find with total ease.

### Concluding the Event

After their encounters at Veneration Hall, the party can follow up on their investigation with a visit to the Fallbrook home of the missing Burnished Hand Protector, Ordan. There are no formal plans in place by Steros to recruit the characters at this time, although he's happy to keep the conversation going in the days and weeks to come.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> If the party wishes to follow up on Vance's task, the characters must journey to the nearby [[Fallbrook]] district for the events of [[The Derelict Protector]], wherein they'll encounter the errant Ordan — who has somehow become an undead creature himself.
