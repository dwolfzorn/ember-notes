# Speaker Silenced

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs in the wake of the party's fight in the Burial Grounds, and allows them to present their findings to [[Lilla Arien]] at [[Cindarin Temple]]. However, grave tidings of the utmost magnitude await the characters when they arrive, and the can:
>
> - Learn about the death of the Holy Speaker [[Jon Vastil]] alongside their ally [[Sin Marmot]].
> - Speak with Lilla Arien and other Cindaric Sages about everything that has transpired.
> - Help Lilla formulate an early hypothesis about why and how this undead uprising in the Arctus Plateau is happening, and what the Cindarics can do to stop it.

### Debriefing The Second Sage

[[Lilla Arien]], [[Sin Marmot]], and other Cindarics including [[Vinarith]] are gathered here in the Cindarin Temple sanctuary for an impromptu wake of Jon Vastil.

After Vinarith exchanges his pleasantries, the party can speak with [[Lilla Arien]] about everything that has transpired — from their encounter in the Burial Grounds (and Sionia's related thoughts), to the death of the Holy Speaker and the future of the Cindaric Sages. [[Sin Marmot]] is part of this exchange, although her participation is markedly reserved.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Lilla Arien takes a deep breath before turning her full attention to your group.
>
> > Initiate Marmot tells me you've had quite an excursion to the Burial Grounds, but she was reluctant to share all of the details until you arrived. Please, let's step away from the crowd to discuss things with a bit more discretion.
>
> The Second Sage motions you towards a quiet corner of Cindarin Temple, where your narrative might go unheard by the sorrowful throng gathered here for the Holy Speaker.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Conversation with Lilla
>
> Even though the day is painted with grief, Lilla Arien is eager to hear what became of the party's journey to the Burial Grounds north of Ordain. After the characters have had a chance to relate their story, the Second Sage informs them about the matters at hand facing the Cindarics:
>
> - Lilla expresses her own grief at losing Jon Vastil, who was like a father figure to her, despite how overlooked she's felt in recent months.
> - Lilla emphasizes how troublesome the timing is for such a tragedy. The Cindaric order has already under mounting pressure and scrutiny due to various conflicts in the region, including lingering earthquakes a surplus of refugees, errant monster attacks, civilian disappearances, and now — most notably — the looming undead threat.
> - The sages will soon call a council to order that will determine who shall assume the role of the new Holy Speaker. Lilla is confident that she's first in line for the position, given her history with the Temple and her expert tutelage at the foot of Jon Vastil.
>
> Sin Marmot is remorseful during the course of the exchange, and leaves most of the talking to the rest of the party members — chiming in only when necessary or appropriate.
>
> Additional conversation points are detailed below, in Lilla Arien's own words.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Regarding Jon Vastil's Death:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "The Speaker was a good man. My favorite teacher, and — despite our differences in age and station —one of my best friends. I haven't known many people of Ember to be as wise and sincere as he. I imagine I will think of him every day for the rest of my life. He leaves some large shoes to fill. I hope whoever is chosen to walk in those shoes is ready and willing and able."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Necromancer, Amelia Naxan:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "I'm unfamiliar with this Amelia Naxan, although tall tales of the Moiran wastelands are told far and wide throughout the Plateau. Based on your description of her, I'd say she's a formidable adversary, if she's actually responsible for your encounter in the Burial Grounds. It's certainly a lead worth following."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Corpuleth:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "This hematophage you describe sounds like a most loathsome creature. Truly an accursed being. You've done the city of Ordain and the Heart of Ember a great service by slaying the bloodthirsty brute before it could do more harm."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Skallith:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "These skeletal creatures sound like the same restless dead you encountered at Corpin Sanctuary. I'm no expert in the ways of arcane magic, but I'd wager the same necromancy that brought those decrepit bones to life on Mial Mountain is also responsible for the horde you fought in the cemetery. We simply need to confirm who summoned them, and how."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Regarding Sionia's Hunch:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "I'm no shard god, but I'm inclined to believe what Sionia has to say, particularly when it comes to addressing such accomplished fate-spinners as yourselves. Perhaps her pilgrimage will lead to some revelations. But in the meantime, we'll have to fend for ourselves. This developing situation grows more desperate each day. We can't afford to wait for the Deadbinder to save us."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Undead Threat:
>
> **A:**
>
> The Second Sage takes a moment to measure her thoughts as an unmistakable wave of concern washes over her.
>
> > "It seems your encounters in Westgate and Fallbrook weren't isolated incidents. These are dark times, my friends. The city is no stranger to calamity, but this escalation of undead activity is beyond the pale. First Corpin Sanctuary, now Ordain. What's next?"
>
> You all share the weight of the moment as Lilla considers what to say.
>
> > "The days to come will present us with a new Speaker, and with that new leadership, I pray … a sense of direction. We can't stand idly by as these soulless monsters unravel the very fabric of the Arctus Plateau."
>
> The Second Sage musters a tiny smile.
>
> > "Adonia wouldn't stand for it, now would she?"

Once the party (or Lilla) breeches the subject of what to do next, [[Vinarith]] joins the group once again with a few final words of his own (for now).

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A tall shadow cascades across the floor as the holy steward Vinarith joins your group once more. His countenance is earnest and staid, but full of assurance. He clears his throat and speaks with measured sincerity.
>
> > My friends, it pains me to say that the Second Sage is needed elsewhere. We have several matters throughout the temple that require her foremost attention.
>
> Vinarith looks to Lilla with a nod of recognition, as if subtly endorsing her capabilities. He continues.
>
> > As we prepare to convene our council to discuss the Holy Speaker's succession, I invite you to stay in close touch throughout the days to come. You are never strangers here. Please remember that, and consider us fast friends. Now, if you'll excuse us.
>
> The steward puts his hand on Lilla's shoulder and draws her close for a hushed consultation. The Second Sage waves you goodbye and offers you a smile of resignation. Sin Marmot gives her own farewell — a short salute, and a forced smile.
>
> Sadness lingers here in Cindarin Temple. And outside, the city of Ordain awaits with all its distractions, frivolous and fateful alike.
