# Numinous Rendezvous

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event allows the party to meet [[Sionia]], the Shard God of necromancy, and learn about her specific thoughts regarding the rising undead threat. In this event, the characters can:
>
> - Locate [[Sionia]] at her own shrine within the [[Numinous Shrines]] district.
> - Speak with the [[Shard Gods]] about her recent observations regarding necromancy and the undead, and how these phenomena relate to an undefinable change in Ember's [[Soul Cycle]].
> - Share information about their own experiences with the undead, leading to a clandestine mission from Sionia for which they are most qualified.
> - Receive [[Sionia's Blessing]] before they head into the potentially dangerous environs of the Burial Grounds.

### The Shrine's Riddle

In order to beckon Sionia from her idle activity, nearby and out of sight, the party must solve the shrine's relatively simple riddle: "The Deathbinder's Armaments" refer to the material spell components for Necromancy spells, which will metaphysically call the shard goddess' attention when left upon the stoney floor of her shrine.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Summoning Rite
>
> Characters with **`[[/skill passive arcana 15]]`** or who succeed on a **Arcana (DC 13)** check understand the shrine's riddle, and know that the correct offerings here are material spell components for Necromancy spells (including Spellcasting Foci featuring Sionia or Necromancy iconography, enchanted items with auras of necromancy, et al).
>
> - **Knowledge: Gods**: The character automatically succeeds.
> - **Knowledge: Undeath**: The character automatically succeeds.
> - **Class Insight**: Clerics, paladins, and warlocks whose spellcasting is powered by Sionia or other necromancy automatically succeed on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
> - @CriticalSuccess[dc]: Additional benefits that occur as a result of critical success. Set [dc] to the base DC of the check.
>
> Alternately, character who succeeds on a **Arcana (DC 15)** check is able to surmise what the inscription on the flagstone is referring to.
>
> - **Knowledge: Gods**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
> - **Knowledge: Undeath**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
>
> Characters who fail these skill checks can attempt them again once per hour by gathering information from locals, researching via books and scrolls, etc.

Once the party solves the shrine's riddle, Sionia appears from another undisclosed location nearby.

### Meeting Sionia

When one of the characters places a suitable offering on the inscribed flagstone, the shard goddess Sionia appears once more. Read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You place the offering on the floor of the unassuming alcove, and for a moment you consider the notion that you might be wasting your time. In a flash, the materials are enveloped in a ghostly flame, and the flagstone is barren once more.
>
> A somewhat familiar female voice greets you from behind.
>
> > That which is given is returned.
>
> You turn to see the alluring woman from the Temple of Sockets, her hand outstretched, palm open — containing the very item you left on the alcove floor. She hands the offering back to you with a look of studious curiosity.
>
> > You seek an audience with the "Deathbinder," do you? Well, here I am.
>
> She gestures at herself and her surroundings with sardonic humility.
>
> > Tell me, what business do we have?

> [!info] Social
> #### A Conversation with Sionia
>
> Although she recognizes the party from the brief encounter at the Temple of Sockets, Sionia is most likely unaware of their involvement with the incidents with the undead creatures in Fallbrook and Westgate. (News travels quickly enough in Ordain, but fame and notoriety take time). If the party wants to solicit Sionia for her advice about the rising undead threat, they must adequately convince her that their mission is a worthy one.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Diplomacy (DC 13)** or **Deception (DC 18)** check is able to convince Sionia that the party's goals are worthy of her time and attention.
>
> - **Class Insight**: Clerics, paladins, and warlocks whose spellcasting is powered by Sionia or other necromancy automatically succeed.
> - **Specific Items**: Characters who have any loose item, keepsake, or component from the [[Skallith]] the party encountered during [[Sanctuary of Death]] automatically succeed
> - **Knowledge: Souls**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
> - **Knowledge: Undeath**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
> - **Attunement: Orbis**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
>
> Characters can re-attempt this check each time they offer Sionia a new anecdote about one of their previous encounters with the undead (whether earlier in the campaign or an explicit part of the character's background and history).
>
> Once the characters have adequately convinced Sionia of the veracity of their cause, she's willing to discuss the matter beyond surface-level issues and simple pleasantries. Topics of discussion include (but are not limited to):
>
> - The events that led the party here to begin with, including the subject of their conversation with Conaris Haid. Knowledge is power, as far as Sionia is concerned.
> - A brief history of Sionia's advent to the Arctus Plateau, and why: to conduct her own investigation about the rising undead threat.
> - The [[Soul Cycle]]. Sionia explains in detail how this cosmic process ordinarily works, and provides evidence that it's currently malfunctioning. There are three major pieces of evidence in support of Umbra's theories:
>   - Sionia's reanimations have been stronger than usual of late, but there have also been elements of unpredictability, even for such a powerful necromancer as the Deathbinder herself.
>   - Sionia has encountered corrupted shards of [[Ascendancy]]. These hazy shards seem to have absorbed latent energies from beyond Ember, and react accordingly around undead creatures and places of necrotic power.
> - Details about various lesser undead that have been seen throughout the plateau. Sionia is particularly keen on categorizing any creatures the party has encountered thus far.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Deception (DC 13)** check can readily tell that Sionia's point of view about these matters is authentic, and feels as though she's been confident and trusting throughout her interaction with the party.
>
> Additional conversation points are detailed below, in Sionia's own words.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who are you?
>
> **A:**
>
> > "I am Sionia, a shard-bearer who some have come to know as the Deathbinder. Others know me as the Lonely Goddess, or even the God of Necromancy. But you may call me Sionia. My life as a so-called shard god has been a relatively short one by Ember's standards, and my concerns are largely the same as they were prior to my ascension. Ultimately, my past is unimportant. It is our actions that define us, don't you agree?
> >
> > I've come to the Arctus Plateau in search of answers about the mounting threat of suspicious necromancy in the region. Ordain is far from the Moiran wastelands to the west, and it troubles me to consider what kind of cosmic unrest might be at the heart of this matter."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Soul Cycle:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "All living creatures on Ember — from the lowest beast to the oldest shard god — have Souls. Souls are motes of Lifeforce from the Heart of Ember, joined with a physical body at birth. When a mortal creature dies naturally, both their Spirit (which is the accumulation of their life experiences) and their Soul (the spectral, metaphysical essence of the being) leave their body and drift toward the Spiritlands.
> >
> > Someone or something is interrupting the Soul Cycle. And I'm eager to find out who or what."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Regarding Necromancy:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "You're already quite aware of how welcome I was at the Temple of Sockets …
> >
> > Most of us are told since birth that necromancy is inherently evil — an instrument of chaos and malevolence and darkness. But this, like many notions of our time, is a half-truth. Like any tool, necromancy is as good or as evil as the one who handles it.
> >
> > My philosophy is simple: souls are the domain of Sockets, and are the immutable stuff of the Cosmos; but the physical remains we leave behind are nothing more than terrestrial matter. Just as a transmuter can shape flesh to stone with ethical consideration, necromancy can transform death into a semblance of life. Flesh and bones are as clay to the skilled necromancer. The clay itself is not evil, nor is the art of shaping it."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Looming Undead Threat:
>
> **A:**
>
> The shard goddess regards you with a measure of concern.
>
> > "Your party's encounters with the restless dead aren't the only evidence of this looming threat or its magnitude. My own reanimations have been suspiciously unpredictable of late, and the dead speak with troubled voices these days. This is surely a sign of things to come."

Once the party breeches the subject of what to do next, Sionia takes command of the conversation with a light display of magic.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> With a wave of her hand, Sionia conjures the apparition of a sprawling cemetery with Ordani architecture, full of overgrown grave markers and mausoleums. The image zooms in on a massive doorway in one corner of the cemetery, marked with a large Cindaric symbol on the front.
>
> > Our task is clear. You must journey to Ordain's Burial Grounds north of the city, where the Cindarics are known to deposit the mortal remains of the dead following certain embalming rites. Bring me the corpses of these undead creatures you encountered in the city before the Cindarics incinerate them according to their sacred ceremony. With the bones or flesh of these curious undead creatures in-hand, I can try to deduce their origin through magic and ritual of my own.
>
> The goddess looks to you with a measure of regret in her eyes.
>
> > I would go myself, if not for the solemn pact I've made with Conaris Haid and the Temple of Sockets. You see, I'm allowed to practice my magic in the city, but I've been forbidden to enter the Burial Grounds for any reason whatsoever. And considering how tenuous my alliance with the church is, I aim to maintain this accord."
>
> Sionia dismisses her illusion and presses her hands together in thought.
>
> > What say you? Is this macabre task within your capacities?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Burial Grounds:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "Most of the Burial Grounds are open to the public, with few exceptions: the Cindaric Crematorium where the undead corpses can be found is normally off-limits. However, your ally Sin Marmot can most likely acquire the credentials you need, along with precise directions to the Cindaric facility.
> >
> > The Burial Grounds are generally a welcoming place, where the mortal remains of adherents to all faiths of Ember can be found. The rise of the Cindaric religion in recent centuries has slowed the need for physical expansion of the cemetery, thanks to their funerary practices — which involve an elaborate cremation ceremony high above a pit of subterranean magma.
> >
> > The cemetery is attended by a cohort of priests and acolytes of various faiths, with a heavy Cindaric presence for obvious measure. With any luck, your clandestine journey to the crematorium facility will be an uneventful one."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Viable Undead Remains:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "The description of the creature you encountered in Westgate bears striking similarities to certain minions of the Moiran Blood Barons. But the only way to be sure is to get a closer look at the corpse, so that I might examine it with magic and artifice.
> >
> > As for the entity you fought in Fallbrook, it sounds somewhat novel and wholly new to me. The implications are murky and rather vast until I can investigate the remains with my own eyes.
> >
> > Bring back whatever remains you can from either corpse — something from both, if possible. Bones, flesh, organs … whatever you can retrieve and safely escort back here to me without arousing suspicion."

Sionia has a parting gift for the characters to make their potentially-dangerous journey to the Burial Grounds less deadly.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Lonely Goddess raises her hands, which dance on the air with inscrutable arcane gestures. Threads of magic flow out of her fingertips and individually towards your chests, and you feel an oncoming surge of divine inspiration.
>
> > One more thing before you go: a blessing to help bring you back safely, so that we might put this nightmare to rest.
>
> It seems the shard goddess has granted you some kind of divine and esoteric boon, one you perhaps hope you'll never have to use.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Sionia's Blessing
>
> Each character should add the [[Sionia's Blessing]] feature to their character sheet at this time, to be removed once the feature has been used.
>
> Additionally, each character gains one point of [[Heroic Inspiration]].

### A Secondary Objective

The party may assist Sionia with a secondary task if they choose, and will be justly rewarded if their efforts are successful. Before the characters depart, she turns to them with the additional offer.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Sionia begins gesticulating, and five scintillating crystal shards manifest before your very eyes, swirling before the necromancer goddess in a slow and hypnotic dance.
>
> > One more thing … Conaris said I couldn't step foot in the Burial Grounds, but he never said I couldn't scry upon them. If you wish to earn my favor, scatter these special crystal shards around the Burial Grounds — indoor and out — so that I might keep an eye on that measurably important corner of the world from afar.
> >
> > Simply hold each crystal aloft when you've found a suitable spot for it and speak the words:"Eyes for the Deathbringer." You will know the proper places for them when you're in their midsts. Do we have an accord?

Sionia asks the characters to install a series of scrying devices throughout the Burial Grounds so that she might monitor the area from afar. She has a measure of respect for Conaris Haid, despite his madcap ramblings and misguided dogma, and doesn't want to dishonor the pact she's made. However, current events are a sign that her watchful guidance can often be helpful (even if she can't physically be there herself).

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> After you accept the shard goddess' offer, the five crystals float gracefully through the air to hover in front of you, ready for the taking.
>
> > Then it's settled. You have everything you need. Now, make haste, and find us some lovely specimens, if you please.
>
> The wind stirs, sweeping dust from the street into your watering eyes. As your vision clears, it's plain to see that Sionia no longer stands before you, vanished like the breeze itself.

The characters are free to take up these 5 [[Ken Crystal]] and add them to their inventory.
