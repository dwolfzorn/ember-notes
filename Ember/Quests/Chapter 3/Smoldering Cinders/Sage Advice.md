# Sage Advice

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event allows the party to seek counsel with the Second Sage [[Lilla Arien]] several times throughout the course of the quest by reconnecting with her at [[Cindarin Temple]]. In this event, the characters can:
>
> - Speak with Lilla Arien about the various quest events leading up to [[A Conflagration of Lumé]] to learn her perspective on the matters at hand and the respective characters involved.
> - Take advantage of Cindarin Temple's other amenities during their visit.
>
> #### Event Repetition
>
> "Sage Advice" is a repeating event, and is configured to activate whenever the party returns to Cindarin Temple at this point. As a result, you may find yourself having to ignore repeat pop-up windows for this event when they occur from time to time.

### Character Advancement

The characters will have the opportunity to advance during the course of their visits with the Second Sage. This moment occurs the first time the party checks in with [[Lilla Arien]] after they've completed both [[The Secret Autopsy]] and [[The Derelict Protector]].

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Milestone Point
>
> Completion of [[The Secret Autopsy]] and [[The Derelict Protector]] events rewards a [[Milestone Progression]] point, potentially enabling the party to advance in level, but only after the characters have reported this progress to Lilla Arien.

During this same visit, Lilla will suggest two courses of action for the party: stop by the forthcoming Lumek bonfire ceremony in Performer's Plaza to meet with Luna Karrowrath and her Flameguard warriors, and visit the Temple of Sockets in Temple Ward to speak with the high priest Conaris Haid. These two leads will hopefully provide the characters with more answers about the mystery at hand.

During this particular encounter with Lilla Arien, be sure to read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Second Sage holds her chin in speculation for a brief moment before speaking.
>
> > These two distinct encounters with the undead start raising some serious concerns about the origins of these entities and its potential impact on the city. My knowledge of these things is limited … perhaps you should seek the counsel of a few people in the city more informed about the ways of the undying.
> >
> > Tomorrow night, Luna Karrowrath — the infamous champion of Lumé — will be hosting a ceremonial bonfire for her Flameguard warriors in Performer's Plaza. Seek her out, and ask her about the Blood Barons. Perhaps she can enlighten you regarding what became of our friend Ardus Tohl.
> >
> > As for the harrowing, withered soldier once known as Ordan … perhaps that old lunatic Conaris Haid can help you. Be sure to visit the Temple of Sockets in the Temple Ward to speak with the high priest. He can tell you more about the Soul Cycle, and the ways in which it has been broken. But be warned — the dodgy old fellow has a wandering mind and a rather short temperament.
> >
> > None in the city are more qualified than these two in matters of the soul. Or its enemies.

Lilla's pointed instructions here are distinct from her general opinions about the Lumek and the Temple of Sockets, which can be found below in their respective sections.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> Once Lilla has made the party aware of these two leads, the characters can investigate the events of [[A Conflagration of Lumé]] and [[Bickering Priests]].

### Visiting Lilla Arien

It's relatively easy for the party to track down [[Lilla Arien]] whenever they return to Cindarin Temple for her counsel (unless otherwise dictated for your narrative). The Second Sage can often be found in the sanctuary, the refectory, the library, or her private chambers.

> [!info] Social
> #### Learnings from the Second Sage
>
> Lilla Arien is a well-learned, well-liked individual in Ordain, and her reputation for kindness and empathy often precedes her. Throughout the party's many interactions with the Second Sage, they should find her to be an approachable and helpful ally — fully aligned with their quest to solve the mystery lurking at the heart of the rising undead threat. (Of course, any of the party's agendas outside of this are up to your discretionary judgement).
>
> General conversation points that Lilla is prepared to discuss include (but are not limited to) the following:
>
> - Sin's current progress during her training as an initiate.
> - If any of the player characters are a **Path: CindaricAdherent** themselves, Lilla takes an similar interest in their progress as well.
> - The party's overall wellness — physically, mentally, and spiritually.
> - Day-to-day affairs of the Cindaric Sages (omitting any potentially sensitive information, of which she is generally unaware).
> - The current task at-hand for the party's larger quest to investigate the rising undead threat.
> - Details about various aspects of the city of Ordain, including specific information on select districts, how and where to find standard amenities, persons of interest, and more.
>
> Unless otherwise indicated, any character who succeeds on a **Deception (DC 13)** check is able tell that Lilla Arien point of view is consistently authentic. More often than not, the Second Sage has nothing to hide.

### The Secret Autopsy

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Victim, Ardus Tohl:
>
> **A:**
>
> The Second Sage exhales with a sigh of regret.
>
> > "Ardus Tohl was a good man, and well on his way to becoming a good sage. It's hard enough to find adherents who embrace our path of servitude, let alone those who would eagerly perform their duties in the droll environs of a cemetery. This affliction of his, whatever it was, is certainly a shame.
> >
> > Ardus kept a dormitory here at the temple. Once his body was discovered, a thorough examination of his chambers was conducted, to no avail. It's difficult to say precisely when or where the mysterious affliction took hold. But his body was cold and as pale as a sheet when we found him."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Vampyre Spawn:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "It's as if Ardus Tohl had become infected with some terrible, monstrous curse or disease to become such a fiend before your very eyes. Far to the west, in the wastelands of the Moiran Blood Barons, there are creatures such as this … feral, blood-drinking cutthroats whose humanity has been corrupted its very core. Did this creature wound any of your party, by any chance? I'd hate to see you ravaged by some unseen poison or illness. Please, let me take a look at you …"

### The Derelict Protector

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Protector, Ordan:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "I don't know much about this young protector named Ordan, or the station he keeps within the Burnished Hand. Fallbrook is a lovely neighborhood, however, with ample housing for those of comfortable means. I believe the Hand pays their protectors well. And the soldiers certainly earn it. No doubt the young man earned himself some rest as well."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Thoughts on the Burnished Hand:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "The Burnished Hand is the sword arm of the Cindaric Sages. A group of tireless warriors who help protect the Cindarics and the entire city from a variety of would-be problems. Whether it's defending the city from outside attack, providing muscle for emergency relief, or the simple security details here at Cindarin Temple … the Burnished Hand have proven themselves to be invaluable allies in our ongoing struggle against the encroaching chaos of the Sheer."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Opinions of Steros Kraver:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "Steros is a proud man, but (I believe) he lacks the haughty pompousness of less-dignified fellows. He is a very capable leader of the Burnished Hand, and charismatic at that. Here at Cindarin Temple and during official ceremonies, he's all business. But he seems to be more grounded among his soldiers, and their world of bronze armor and endless combat training. I certainly hope to get to know him better in the years to come. He's a good friend to the Cindarics, and one of our best resources when the going gets tough."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Harrower:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "It seems as if Ordan was beset by some terrible evil to become a withered creature such as this. In all my years I've never heard of such a menace, even amidst the tall tales from out of the west of the Moiran wastelands and their inhuman armies. This was no vampyre you encountered, but something else altogether. Something insidious, to be sure. We should share news of this novel entity with the inner council of Sages, and Steros Kraver himself."

### A Conflagration of Lumé

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Regarding Luna Karrowrath:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "Songs are sung of Lumé's champion from here to the Cascilian Republic. She is known to be a beacon of light in the hopelessness of dark, and serves as a potent figurehead for her people and the Lumek Firebrands she leads into battle."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** On the Lumek Flameguard:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "The Lumek are known far and wide as formidable warriors, and one of the best hopes Ember has against the encroaching threat of the Moiran Blood Barons. Thanks to the Flameguard, the Barons are confined to their lightless wastelands, and the rest of Ember can sleep well at night knowing that sunlit lands like the Arctus Plateau are safe."

### Bickering Priests

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Regarding Sockets:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "The elder god of death is known to nearly all of us, whether we realize it or not. His domains are that of death, rebirth, and souls. I've yet to meet the Lord of the Bonelands, but from what I understand he's a rather cantankerous old fellow who loves a good laugh. No wonder his high priest here in Ordain is a stubborn madman as well. Souls are heavy things, I suppose, and a light heart must surely be needed to carry them through the ages of eternity.
> >
> > If you've yet to meet any priests of Sockets on your journeys so far, they're actually fairly common throughout the Arctus Plateau. We have a grand temple to Sockets here in Ordain, situated in the middle of Temple Ward."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Regarding Sionia:
>
> **A:**
>
> The Second Sage purses her lips with a measure of hesitation.
>
> > "Not much is known about the Lonely Goddess, other than her prodigious wizardry and her penchant for reanimation. But anyone who would tread on the sanctity of the Soul Cycle — shard god or otherwise — is someone to be carefully minded."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Opinions of Conaris Haid:
>
> **A:**
>
> The Second Sage cracks a sardonic smile.
>
> > "The High Priest of Sockets is a very unserious man. One who's devoted his life to a rather serious cause. The old codger acts like a fool, raving and drooling from time to time, but I imagine there's more to these bouts of madness than it seems. I'm not sure whether Sockets chose Conaris to serve him, or if Conaris was drawn to the Keeper of Souls by his own accord. But those two are a match made in Primordis, that's for sure."

### Numinous Rendezvous

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Sionia's Plan:
>
> **A:**
>
> The Second Sage regards you with a measure of concern.
>
> > "It may already be too late to retrieve those corpses from the Burial Grounds, but there's a chance they haven't been prepared or cremated yet. You'll need to make haste to the cemetery if this plan is to bear any fruit … rotten or otherwise."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Regarding Shard Gods and Other Deities:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "The shard gods are curious entities, each of them disparate, with their own interests and areas of concern. Various deities of The Nineteen are worshipped openly by the people of Ordain, although we Cindarics tend to hold a more secular point of view. Certainly, there are faithful among us here at Cindarin Temple; but dedicated service to the protection of Ember's Heartblood is paramount — and must supersede our individual faith in any god. All things serve the ever-beating Heart of Ember."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Burial Grounds:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "The Burial Grounds are built atop an overgrown hillside estate northeast of town, and play home to graves and monuments for people of every Arcturian and Ordani faith. Cindaric funeral practices differ from more archaic faiths, in that we prepare the dead in a special ceremony before their eventual cremation.
> >
> > Sionia's plan to retrieve the undead corpses seems like a reasonable one, and it's a shame we didn't think of it sooner. The Cindaric Crematorium where the bodies will be found is near the center of the graveyard's cluttered sprawl. Follow these simple directions to find your way, and always heed the groundskeepers."
>
> Lilla hands you a tiny scrap of paper with a map of the Burial Grounds sketched upon it, which presumably will lead you directly to the Cindaric facility you seek.

### Other Topics

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Soul Cycle:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "The Soul Cycle is a curious phenomenon, but it is a process that steers the fate of every person on Ember, whether they know it or not. When we're born, a tiny piece of the Heart of Ember coalesces into our soul before journeying to the surface, where it joins with our physical bodies; and when we die, our souls leave our bodies and journey to the Bonelands, where Sockets himself holds sway over the fate of each soul.
> >
> > But honestly, this is a matter best discussed with the priests of Sockets themselves, who know a great deal more about it than myself. You can always find the high priest and his disciples at the Temple of Sockets in Temple Ward, alongside sanctuaries to several other faiths."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Thoughts on the Cindaric Order:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "We are the custodians of Ember's light, and the healers of the living planet's Heartblood. For centuries, the Cindarics have kept a light burning in the advent of darkness — a darkness we've kept at bay for so very long, and one we will continue to fight. Alongside the goodly adherents of Sockets and other gods, we strive to protect the ever-changing world of ours from the chaos that might destroy it. The great city of Ordain is our most treasured protectorate, and each Cindaric feels the weight of their station when they walk through the grand halls of Cindarin Temple, where the great Adonia once prophesied the very age we live in. I've given the Cindarics my life, and thy've given me a grand and righteous purpose in return."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Opinions of Jon Vastil:
>
> **A:**
>
> > "The Holy Speaker has been like a father to me for many years. He's a good man. A wise leader. And a kind soul. He is the very heartbeat of the Cindaric faith. There is a calm in his spiritual guidance that feels … comfortable, and right. And pure. I trust him with my very being, and would lay down my life for him, should it ever come to that. That's how much he means to me."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Opinions of Vinarith:
>
> **A:**
>
> A slight wrinkle dances across Lilla's brow, which she fails to hide.
>
> > "The steward is a remarkably capable sage, and is very well-liked among the sages and adherents here. Jon Vastil himself is certainly fond of Vinarith, who has been like a personal attendant to the speaker of late. Indeed, it's become harder and harder to schedule a meeting with Jon without Vinarith's inclusion; but the Cindaric faith is one of holistic collaboration. Even if he can be a bit … erudite, Vinarith is undeniably successful with his recruitment efforts and is extraordinarily well-studied in the history of the Cindarics and the lore of the Soul Cycle. We're lucky to have him."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Opinions of Sin Marmot:
>
> **A:**
>
> The Second Sage smiles.
>
> > "Initiate Marmot is an extraordinary addition to the ranks of the Cindaric Sages. Her enthusiasm for life and her zeal for discovery are valuable assets for a Cindaric, who is expected to give back to Ember as much as they take from it — physically and metaphysically alike.
> >
> > Furthermore, Sin's spellcasting aptitude exceeds that of a standard adherent of her age, and I'm sure these talents will take her far in life. Her benevolent magic will no doubt be a boon to many people. We're grateful that you helped her reach Ordain safely, despite your trials and tribulations. I won't hesitate to say she belongs here at Cindarin Temple, among like-minded healers and dreamers. And so you, my friends."
