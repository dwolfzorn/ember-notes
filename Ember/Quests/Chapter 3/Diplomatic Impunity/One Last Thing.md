# One Last Thing

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This social event sees the party finally getting told what is going on, what all their jobs and actions have been for, and what their final task will be. During this event they'll meet with Ambassador Loris Tezran and Katerin Bastilla and do the following:
>
> - Learn they'll be infiltrating the Spellbreaker Prison, why, and what that will entail.
> - Have a brief aside with Katerin, who will make them a special offer they can think about.
> - If they have been working with the Veiled Chain, sow discord and distrust between the Ambassador and Bastilla.

### Katerin's commentary

If the party didn't complete either the [[Parcels & Pirates]] or [[Three Point Lift]] events Katerin says:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > Before we get started, I'd just like to point out that I don't appreciate being ignored. I didn't give you those assignments for my own amusement, but to make things easier down the line. Since you decided to ignore my tasks, I am looking forward to seeing how you manage what comes next.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Additional Commentary
>
> Rather than providing prewritten responses for all the possible event outcomes from Katerin's events, we encourage you to consider how your party handled her tasks and have her comment accordingly.
>
> She might be impressed if the party [[Parcels & Pirates]] and his Wind Raiders successfully, or if they somehow managed to [[Parcels & Pirates]] without violence. She might be disappointed or amused if they [[Parcels & Pirates]] of the wingsuits.
>
> She may also comment on if they [[Three Point Lift]] of the Cascillian device out of incompetence or disinterest. She might be impressed if they [[Three Point Lift]] the device, doubly so if they did it [[Three Point Lift]].

### Revealing the Plan

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Katerin speaks up:
>
> > All right, it's about time Loris let you in on the grand scheme, I think.
>
> Ambassador Tezran’s smile is functional and too polite to be real as he looks up from the documents.
>
> > Indeed. It’s time you knew the purpose of the errands you’ve been running.
>
> He gestures to the papers. You recognize sketches from the prison dossiers the guard at Spellbreaker Tower gave you, maps of the floors, notations on the guard rotations, and a circled annotation: Yellow Block, Cell 8.
>
> > All of your recent labors, from document forgery, to procurement of specialized equipment, was to supply us for what comes next: The Tower’s prison holds one of my countrymen, a mage by the name of Toron.
> >
> > He is in custody, and you’re going to correct that.
>
> Katerin lets out an amused snort.
>
> > From the inside.
>
> Tezran inclines his head, his annoyance masked expertly, but not completely.
>
> > Quite so. We're going to insert you into the Spellbreaker's prison levels as new inmates using the criminal personas you helped create. Once inside you're going to locate Toron, and then escape the prison, and bring him back here.

Given this revelation, the party will almost certainly have questions:

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** How are we getting in?
>
> **A:**
>
> Katerin lifts her glass.
>
> > I have a secured wagon and guards that will clap you in irons and drop you off at the tower. Combined with the forged criminal records, I've arranged for arrest and court orders for incarceration. We'll hand the whole mess over to the prison and they'll take you up the tower like any other criminal.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** How are we going to escape?
>
> **A:**
>
> Katerin waves her now empty glass toward you.
>
> > Those wingsuits you collected from Gastern? Use them from the top level of the tower and you should be able to ride wind currents to anywhere within a mile or two easily. There's a spare one for Toron in the set for a reason.
>
> She refills the glass as she continues:
>
> > You can, of course, get creative and find a different route, but that's for you do figure out. We already arranged one solution, so use it or don't.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What if Toron won't cooperate?
>
> **A:**
>
> The Ambassador's reply is cold:
>
> > Convince him. By word or by blade. Either way he is leaving that Tower.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why us?
>
> **A:**
>
> Katerin says:
>
> > It was going to be Gastern and his crew of maniacs. Luckily for us you turned up with Loris' bag of documents, and immediately became the better option. Which is good because there were concerns about Gastern's reliability… and stability. I'm sure you know what I mean.
>
> Ambassador Tezran adds:
>
> > You’re outsiders. Your names aren’t tangled in embassy reports or House ledgers. If this goes wrong, the incident ends with you, not us.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why Toron?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Toron failed one of his missions and was captured. Were he less important I would let him rot in the prison for his failure, but he knows too much, and his presence there serves as an ongoing security risk for the Empire which I cannot allow that to continue.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What does Toron know?
>
> **A:**
>
> > That is not your concern, his extraction and safe delivery to me, however, is.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Couldn't you get him out legally?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I've tried, but the Ordinate is dragging their feet, and wasting my time with manufactured delays and layers of bureaucracy. They are attempting to hold him for as long as possible, and I'm not interested in playing their game.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What about our criminal records?
>
> **A:**
>
> Katerin grins at that question, and speaks:
>
> > Well, there's a few tricks up my sleeves regarding that, you'll be fine. Short version is that I have enough pull within the Hallows that I can get those documents expunged for being fraudulent. The Hallows or Chain might come looking for you, they won't be able to really prove anything without legit records.

### No Backing Out

At this point, if the party becomes apprehensive, Katerin steps in to make sure the party sees "reason" and doesn't back out:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> When the conversation turns hesitant, Katerin rises from her seat, not threateningly, but with the ponderous certainty of a ship breaking anchor.
>
> > Now, let’s not do anything foolish. You’ve been busy for us, and in the process you’ve committed enough criminal acts to have the Hallows calling for your heads. Stolen Cascillian property, forged official records, stolen items from a foreign courier, attempted to smuggle materials into the Spellbreaker?
> >
> > That's pretty bad, I'd say.
>
> She gestures to the door.
>
> > If you want to walk away, by all means. But when the Hallows find out what you’ve done, and they will, you might not end up in the Spellbreaker, but they will put you in a cell.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Don't you need us?
>
> **A:**
>
> > You’re useful, but not irreplaceable. Besides, you've gotten everything in place already. All we'd have to do is find another team to go in and do the final part.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Aren't you implicated too?
>
> **A:**
>
> Katerin raises a hand, showing her palm, while offering a meanspirited smile.
>
> > Oh no, our hands are clean. Yours? Not so much.
>
> She motions to herself and the Ambassador, then past you toward the city beyond the embassy walls.
>
> > Besides, we've got enough clout and money to make whatever wild claims you spew just disappear. Ordain's a mostly upstanding city, sure, but the Ordinate don't seem to mind the dubious things all the Trade Houses get up to because it funds their little utopia.

### Katerin's Offer

After the party has spoken with the pair for a bit and has asked any questions they have, there is a moment where the Ambassador is pulled away from the meeting, giving Katerin a chance to make the party a counter-offer on the prison escape.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A soft knock at the garden doors breaks through the conversation. An attendant in Tayan livery steps into view, bowing low before speaking in measured tones.
>
> > Ambassador, forgive the intrusion. A representative from the Ordinate has arrived to discuss your most recent request. They are waiting in the parlor.
>
> Tezran’s eyes narrow, irritation barely masked. Rakavi’s tail gives a slow, sweeping flick behind him. After a heartbeat, he exhales and straightens to his full, commanding height.
>
> > Of course they are. Typical of the Ordinate to choose the worst possible moment.
>
> He glances toward Katerin, voice turning clipped, businesslike.
>
> > Continue the briefing. I’ll be back shortly.
>
> Without waiting for reply, the Tayan ambassador stalks off toward the embassy’s inner halls. Rakavi raises his head, the great predator giving brief consideration to following without being told, but thinks better of it and lays back down.
>
> As the door closes, the garden falls into a hush broken only by the rustle of Katerin's clothing as she leans forward. There's intent behind her eyes suddenly, sharp as a compass point. Her voice is low and conspiratorial, the words coming swiftly:
>
> > You’ve gathered by now that he wants Toron back, and ‘back’ doesn’t mean safe. The Redfiend doesn’t forgive failure, and Toron failed him badly enough that Loris wants him retrieved... for judgment. Which, in Tayan fashion, means death.
>
> Her voice softens, but her expression hardens.
>
> > That’s none of my business, officially. But unofficially, I don’t like playing errand-girl to executions and certainly hate to see a perfectly good mage go to waste. So here’s my side-proposal: when you escape with Toron, don’t hand him to Loris. Bring him to me instead.
>
> Katerin sits back again, gaze sweeping across you.
>
> > You don’t have to answer now. Just remember: when the dust settles, you’ll be the ones standing with Toron. You'll be the ones who get to decide his fate: life or death. Think carefully which will matter more.
>
> She swirls what’s left in her glass for a moment, watching the light catch it like a gold coin tumbling through water, then adds:
>
> > I'll also pay you a tidy sum for the trouble, enough to make up for burning your bridge with the Ambassador.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What will you do with him?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Make him disappear, firstly. But you mean after that, I know.
> >
> > Bastilla always needs more mages. Adding someone of Toron's caliber to my network would let me add Imperial magic tricks to the fleet, and that's more valuable than seeing the man killed and buried under flagstones in Highgate.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Won't that harm your alliance?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Not really, no. The Tayans need Bastilla and its fleet to move their goods and fund their wars. Loris knows that some personal vendetta against a failed spy or whatever Toron is won't register to the Empress. He'll swallow the loss and play nice… probably.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What if we tell him about this?
>
> **A:**
>
> > You could, and I'd deny it, and he'd become paranoid and it would strain the working relationship I have with him. However, he needs me, and my ships, and as long as my crews are reliable, which they are, he will work around not trusting me.
> >
> > Besides, I don't think that man has trusted many people in his life. I wasn't about to be an exception.

Once the party has finished this exchange and asked any questions they have check the Heard out Katerin outcome. After this point, the Ambassador returns.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The garden doors open once more, causing Rakavi's to head lift immediately and turn toward it. His eyes fix on Ambassador Tezran as he reappears and returns to the meeting.
>
> > Did I miss anything of significance?
>
> Katerin shakes her head in response.
>
> > Not at all, just fielding questions and making sure the crew is properly informed. I believe they have all they need. I am ready to call my men when they are ready to go.
>
> Loris nods.
>
> > Good, excellent. I am eager to get this operation underway.
>
> Katerin reaches into a coat pocket and takes out a golden coin, speaking to it:
>
> > Make ready the wagons, mister Forley.
>
> The coin barks back in a stranger's voice:
>
> > Aye, aye.
>
> Katerin pockets the coin and looks at you.
>
> > Transport will be here shortly, if you're ready to go.

### Betraying Katerin

If the party chooses to betray Katerin and tell the Ambassador about her side offer, toggle the Made the ambassador suspicious outcome, and read or paraphrase the following narrative:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The ambassador looks more annoyed than surprised by your revelation.
>
> > I shouldn't expect any different from the leader of a band of corsairs masquerading as a proper merchant navy. Let me be clear: Your mission is to bring Toron to me, and nobody else, and I do not suffer failure or betrayal gracefully. I trust you'll bear that in mind, and make the correct decision when the time comes.

#### Ragen Attunement: Sowing Discord

A party that chooses to reveal Katerin's offer to Loris advances their **Attunement: Ragen (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

#### Primordis Attunement: Schemes in Schemes

A party that chooses to keep Katerin's offer a secret from Loris advances their **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Delaying Departure

If the party would like some time to prepare or wrap up other pending tasks, they can do so. This gets the following response:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Katerin sighs and a look of annoyance plays across the Ambassador's face. Katerin speaks:
>
> > I had a feeling you might want to delay things once you knew the plan. I'll have my people waiting for you on the approach to the Spellbreaker district. Head that way once you're ready, and they'll intercept you, put you on their wagons, and take you in properly.
>
> Ambassador Tezran breaks in:
>
> > And don't tarry any longer than necessary. We have smuggled items and forged documents in play, wasting time risks them being discovered, which in turn risks this operation's chances of success.

From this point forward the party can depart, and undertake any other actions they want first. For example, they could report in with the Veiled Chain at the Hallows as part of [[Coming Clean]] which would allow them to begin a counter-operation against the Ambassador.

### Leaving for Spellbreaker

When the party is ready to leave for the prison, set the Party Ready to Leave outcome, and proceed with the following narrative section:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Collected out front of the Embassy you can hear the distant rattle of chain and hoofbeats growing louder, until two heavy wagons arrive, one bearing a squad of House Bastilla guards, and the other chains and manacles fit for securing prisoners. As the wagon creaks to a halt the armed contingent dismounts and prepares the second wagon to receive you.
>
> At your side, Katerin gestures to the wagon.
>
> > On you go, let's not drag this out. If the manacles are tight it's not personal, we need things to look right.
>
> As you board the wagon, the guards secure your ankles and wrists, the chains directly attaching to the wagon so you can't escape its confines.
>
> Once secured, the guards clamber back onto the first wagon, and the pair set off down the street, eventually leaving embassy rise, and angling toward the great tower that looms over the whole city.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> If the party chose to head to prison directly they should be moved directly to the Spellbreaker Tower where the next event, [[Doing Time]] will begin.
>
> However, if the party chose to delay departure they will need to move to the tower directly on their own when they are ready to carry out the next state of the plan.
