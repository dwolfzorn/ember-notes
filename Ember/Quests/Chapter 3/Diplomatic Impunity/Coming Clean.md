# Coming Clean

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This repeatable social event allows the party to report to the Veiled Chain, informing them about the events that occur in this quest. Reporting to the Veiled Chain allows the party to:
>
> - Learn about the Tayans and House Bastilla.
> - Get direction and guidance from the Veiled Chain.
> - Work toward a special ending in this quest.
>
> #### Manual Trigger
>
> Note that a party moving to the Hallows will not prompt you to activate this event as is typical for other events. This event needs to be manually triggered via the "Initiate Event" button found at the top and bottom of this page. This needs to be done each time the party chooses to visit the Hallows and report to the Veiled Chain, which they can do multiple times as the quest progresses.
>
> #### Repeatable Event
>
> This is a repeating event where the party can report to the VC about events going on in the quest. This event is available until the moment the party ends up in prison. The Outcomes provide the GM ways to track what pieces of information have been reported on, and can unlock a special ending encounter if the party commits to this path.

> [!abstract] Yeela Drevvin
> **[[Yeela Drevvin]]**
>
> Level 1 · Unknown Unknown
>
> ![Yeela Drevvin](../../../assets/image119.webp)

Yeela is a veteran agent of the Veiled Chain with decades of service to the city under her belt. She is keen-eyed, insightful, and dedicated to keeping the city safe. She specializes in profiling subjects with the aim of predicting their actions.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> In walks a human woman, Ordani by the style of her clothing, middle aged and fit. At first you mistake her for a stranger, but quickly notice the Veiled Chain crest pinned to her clothing, making her as an agent in its ranks.
>
> > Hello, I'm Yeela Drevvin, senior agent of the Veiled Chain.
>
> Yeela takes out a notebook and pencil, sitting down as she continues:
>
> > I understand you were witness to a crime and want to make a formal report. Go ahead and do so, and please be as detailed as you can manage. The more you recall, the more we'll have to work with here.

### Reporting the Courier

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > Well that's not good at all. I'll add this onto the pile of waterfront incidents lately. More concerning is the death of a foreign agent, which I'll need to get reported to the Ordinate so they aren't caught off guard when a diplomatic incident lands in their lap.
>
> She sets her pencil down and leans back in her seat.
>
> > So, I'm going to offer you a reward of twenty-five gold pieces for being good, law abiding citizens.
>
> Yeela reaches into a hip pouch and pulls out a smaller pouch that jingles audibly when it lands on the table.
>
> > And I also need to ask you a favor: Take the satchel of documents from the deceased courier to the Tayan Embassy located in Embassy Rise. I'd have one of our runners do it, but we're stretched thin at the moment.
> >
> > If you come back afterward, I'll give you another twenty-five gold for your trouble.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Can't you do it?
>
> **A:**
>
> Yeela shakes her head.
>
> > Not really, no. I've got to follow up on thus murder, get what information I can, and then report it up the chain to the Ordinate.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What if we get blamed?
>
> **A:**
>
> Yeela scoffs at the question.
>
> > Don't be silly, just explain what happened, and offer to return the reports. You might even get a reward for your trouble. Just be polite and mind your manners. You'll be fine.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What about the murder?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We will look into it, don't you worry. The Chain isn't going to view the death of a foreign agent as a minor incident, and the Ordinate will want answers.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Is violence on the rise in Ordain?
>
> **A:**
>
> Yeela takes a moment to consider that.
>
> > Yes and no. There are some districts where crime has increased, and some of those increases do include spikes in violence, but overall it's marginal, and not widespread.

#### Akon Attunement: The Rule of Law

Any character who goes along to report the crime to the Veiled Chain advances their **Attunement: Akon (+1)** at the conclusion of the event. Note: This attunement occurs only the first time the party reports to the Veiled Chain.

The next time the party can report in is after they complete [[Foreign Influence]] and met with Ambassador Loris Tezran.

### Reporting the Ambassador

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Yeela raises her eyebrow at the report as she takes notes.
>
> > Strong-arming you into service is unexpected. We know he's working angles and trying to make friends in the city, so Katerin Bastilla's inclusion isn't a surprise, especially given their ongoing trade deals.
> >
> > However, I am now curious what he's after. If you're up for it, I want you to keep cooperating, and see where this goes.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** You know Katerin Bastilla is involved?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We had suspicions. She's very ambitious and opportunistic, and the Tayans need money to fund their wars. Most of the major sea trade organizations in the area are wary of the Tayans, or their parent nations are under siege already, which makes creating trade deals… difficult.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why do you want us to cooperate?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Because there is a chance that the Ambassador's plan is a threat to the city, and having you on the inside means we can find out if it is or not. If you stop working with him he'll need to find someone new, and we can't guarantee they'll come to us like you did.

After this check-in the party's next report possibility is after [[Criminal Records]] where they met Katerin Bastilla and got assignments from her.

### Reporting Katerin Bastilla

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Yeela takes notes silently for a moment before speaking.
>
> > Very interesting, very interesting. The depth of Katerin's connections is always surprising. We figured she had friends in the Hallows given how some court cases have gone in the past, but being able to brazenly falsify criminal records in the Hallows is a bit more involved.
>
> She snaps her notebook shut.
>
> > You know the deal: Keep cooperating. See where this is headed. It seems like you're close to getting the full picture of the Ambassador's operation.
> >
> > If Katerin and the Ambassador bring you in on the full plan, come update us and we'll put a plan into motion to stop them.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Don't you have enough evidence already?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Not really, no. We have your testimony, and that's valuable since it's created avenues of investigation we can pursue, but these need time to bear fruit. The more you can tell us about what they are doing, the better.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Can you arrest Katerin?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Right now, no, but this evidence and the jobs she's got you doing make that into more of a soft no that we could turn into a yes in the near future.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Can you arrest the Ambassador?
>
> **A:**
>
> Yeela grimaces.
>
> > That's a much messier proposition. Foreign representatives have direct connection to the Ordinate, and while we can go after them if they break the law, getting charges to stick through all the political bureaucracy can be hard.
> >
> > In fact, even if it does, there's a good chance they get pulled and replaced before anything comes of it.
> >
> > Which is enough of a victory to some, I suppose.

The last check-in will be after the party completes [[One Last Thing]] and learns what the Ambassador's true intentions are. Note that they must report in before they go to the Spellbreaker Prison, since that will begin the next main event.

### Starting the Counter-Operation

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Yeela is silent for a long time after you give your report.
>
> > Well, that's bold. We figured the Tayans had some ulterior motives in the city, and that Toron was connected to Tayan espionage efforts, but this effectively confirms that.
>
> She thinks for a moment.
>
> > I think we can make this plan work for us. We know that Ambassador Tezran's got a paranoid streak, and doesn't trust Katerin any more than she trusts him. Obviously we can't have you breaking Toron out, but I think we do want to have you go along with the plan. You'll be inserted into the prison, and go along with the breakout.
> >
> > Knowing the Ambassador, he'll want to be nearby to ensure things go to plan, but won't risk his cover unless he has to. We need a way to force him to come to you on the tower, and if you do, we can have Veiled Chain agents waiting to capture him.

If the party [[One Last Thing]] then Yeela adds:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > The fact that you let him know about Katerin's offer is a huge help. He's definitely not going to trust you to come to him now, and we can take advantage of that.
> >
> > If you can get to the roof without raising the alarm, he'll likely come to pick up Toron directly. He'll probably want to leave you behind, but that's a sacrifice he'd be willing to make, I'm sure.

Otherwise she adds:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > So, it might be a long-shot, but I'll expect that if you got to the roof and didn't have your wingsuits to escape with, he'd come in directly to pick up Toron. He'll probably want to leave you behind, but that's a sacrifice he'd be willing to make, I'm sure.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why does it have to be without raising the alarm?
>
> **A:**
>
> > If you have to fight your way up to the roof of the tower and have guards and those Cascillian security machines chasing you he won't risk his neck. So you get to the roof and don't get swarmed, he'll take the chance.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Can't you just let us out?
>
> **A:**
>
> Yeela laughs at that.
>
> > I wish, that would make this a whole lot simpler, but no, there's two things that make that impossible.
> >
> > First one is that the Veiled Chain doesn't have direct influence or sway over the prison, and second is that knowing the warden, he won't agree to make it easier for you unless we arrive with Ordinate signed directions, which I doubt we could get.
> >
> > So no, you'll have to break out on your own.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why can't you get him in the Embassy?
>
> **A:**
>
> > It's technically foreign soil, it would take to get the Ordinate to sanction a raid on the location with the evidence we have right now. Plus, if the Veiled Chain launched a raid on the location it'd be exceptionally messy. Tayan infantry are no joke, and most of our agents aren't hardened combatants.

> [!tip] Exploration
> Characters with **Knowledge: Politics**, **Path: Imperial Conscript** or **Culture: Tayan** know that the Tayan Empire maintains a huge, professionally trained, and well equipped military that has seen decades of active combat, making it one of the most experienced fighting forces on Ember.
>
> Characters that make a successful **Society (DC 14)** check know that the Tayan Empire has been in near continuous war since it formed, either through aggressive expansion of its borders, or by lending military aid to allies on more distant fronts, and as such is incredibly well versed in the art of war.

#### Primordis Attunement: Double Agents

A party that reports to the Veiled Chain enough that it incites them to take action against the Ambassador advances their **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

Lastly, before the party leaves Yeela has a final few words for them:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > Well, I think that's it for my side. I'll have agents in place in case you pull off your side of things. Good luck in the Spellbreaker, Warden Holst runs a tight operation, getting out unseen won't be easy.
>
> After a moment's pause Yeela adds:
>
> > Oh! Also, because you've been a huge help in all this, I'll make sure your records get cleared. As a bonus, if you swing back here afterward and look me up, I'll try to have a reward waiting. I think you've earned something for the trouble.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> The party can return to the rest of this quest's events once they have reported in to the Veiled Chain. Since this quest is repeatable, they can return to this event later in the quest to update the Chain and get further guidance and insight if they want.
>

>
