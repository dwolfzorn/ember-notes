# Three Point Lift

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This combat event sees the party breaking into a warehouse controlled by the Cascillian Republic. Once inside, the party must steal a technological device for Katerin Bastilla. The party can:
>
> - Infiltrate the warehouse silently, avoiding guards and confrontation.
> - Assault the warehouse, fighting their way in and out of the building.
>
> #### Useful But Optional
>
> The Cascillian autotool device will be useful later in the quest, but is not vital, and a party that fails to or chooses not to acquire this item can still continue the story. However, the prison escape where it would be useful will undoubtedly be harder without it.
>
> #### Breaking the Law
>
> At present, there are no repercussions for being identified, or fighting the guards. This may change in the future with additional events or content related to this quest, but does not exist presently. If the party leaves behind bodies, witnesses, or evidence this can be used against them at gamemaster discretion.

#### Scene Under Development

The scene used for this event lacks walls, doors and lighting due to a technical limitation on how the scenes are configured. These limitations will be resolved in a future update.

The event begins the party a short distance away from the warehouse in a position where the guards are unlikely to notice or suspect them. This gives the party a chance to decide on their approach, watch the guards, and set up for success.

### Approaching the Warehouse

The Cascillian Marines patrolling the warehouse are members of a foreign nation, and trained soldiers. They are disciplined, watchful of outsiders, and know who should and should not be on the premises. This makes deceiving them or talking past them a fruitless endeavor.

However, if observed for a while it is possible to spot gaps in their patrols.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Military Presence
>
> There are 7 [[Cascillian Marine]]s patrolling the warehouse. They are commanded by 1 [[Cascillian Marine Officer]] located in the warehouse office. They also have 3 Trained [[Anydrath]]s in the water that know to alert the marines if they detect intruders.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Casing the Joint
>
> Characters can watch the guard patrols with a successful **Awareness (DC 18)** check. Characters with **Knowledge: Crime** or **Knowledge: Intrigue** have advantage on these checks.
>
> - **Result of 23+** notes that the waters around the dock are also patrolled by three large sea creatures.
>
> #### Entering the Warehouse
>
> Characters that successfully watched the patrols have advantage on this skill check and any stealth checks made to avoid unalerted marines and anydrath. However, this advantage is lost if the guards become suspicious or are alerted to intruder presence.

### Avoiding the Guards

At the start of this event the marines guarding this warehouse are calm, not on alert, and not suspicious of their surroundings. This gives the party the best chance to sneak in if they want to, but they need to take care, because any mistakes or signs of intrusion will put the marines on guard.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Sneaking Around
>
> The marines are alert and watchful on their patrols inside building. Though they are all armed, none of them expect trouble, and are not initially suspicious. As a result, the marines can be avoided with a successful **Stealth (DC 10)** skill check.
>
> If the marines notice any evidence of entry or disturbance they will be put on alert, informing their allies of the issue. At this point, the difficulty to evade them via stealth requires a **Stealth (DC 12)** and they will investigate any oddities in teams of two rather than going alone.
>
> #### Anydrath Patrols
>
> For parties that approach from the water, the Anydrath patrolling around the docks come to investigate anything they see in the water, coming within 15 feet to get a good look before rushing back to the docks to alert the marines. These Anydrath are trained by the Cascillian military, and can't be easily manipulated without using magic.

### Fighting the Guards

While it's not advised that a party fight the guards patrolling the warehouse, they can. Defeating the guards lets the party claim the item with impunity, but may leave witnesses, or dead bodies that the city authorities will have to follow up on.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Combat Tactics
>
> If combat breaks out in the or around the warehouse, and alarm is raised, the marines will quickly form up and work together to coordinate their efforts. They also call for their Anydrath companions to join the fight, likely outnumbering and overwhelming the party quickly.
>
> Collectively the marines fight ferociously but will attempt to retreat if more than half their number are defeated, and individually Marines will attempt to flee combat when reduced to half their maximum hit points. If forced to flee, they will favor diving into the water and swimming away over running.
>
> Marines that do flee via water can do so quickly with the help of their Anydraths if nearby.

### Finding the Device

Once the party has gotten inside the warehouse, they will need to find the item that Katerin sent them to find. The placement of the [[Cascillian Autotool]] in the warehouse isn't fixed, and should be handled in a way that befits the party's approach.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Finding the Desired Item
>
> To make sure that finding the object is not overly difficult or too abstract to accomplish, there are several ways to find the item in question. Any of these methods work, and once the device is found, the party can flee.
>
> - **Searching the Officer:** If the party has infiltrated the warehouse and managed to approach the Marine Officer that is patrolling the interior of the warehouse, it could be taken from them with a successful **`[[/skill sleightofhand 14]]`** skill check.
> - **Searching the Warehouse**: In the party has infiltrated the warehouse unseen and wants to search the warehouse while hidden, they need to make a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check which takes a few minutes. During this search the guards will continue patrolling, risking discovery.
> - **Tossing the Warehouse**: If the party managed to drive off the marines, forcing their retreat, they can search the warehouse which takes about ten minutes and will yield the item in question without needing a roll.
> - **Searching Bodies**: If the party decided to fight their way in and defeated the marines, they should be able to find the item on the body of the Marine Officer once they are defeated.
>
> #### Other Items of Interest
>
> Searching the warehouse, or downed Cascillian Marines yields a working [[Cascillian Rebreather]], and several [[Cascillian Tridents]]. The rest of the warehouse holds materials, goods, and items of use to the local Cascillians, but nothing particularly noteworthy to the party.

#### Ragen Attunement: To the Point

A party that foregoes stealth and takes the brazen (likely violent) route to acquiring the autotool advances their **Attunement: Ragen (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

#### Primordis Attunement: Nothing to Point At

A party that manages to acquire the Cascillian device without being noticed by the guards advances their **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

#### Aura Attunement: Avoiding the Point

A party that chooses not to engage with the warehouse and decides to ignore this event (thus failing to acquire the autotool) advances their **Attunement: Aura (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.
