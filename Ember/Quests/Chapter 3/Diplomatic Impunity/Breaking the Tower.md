# Breaking the Tower

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This combat event involves the party breaking out of the Spellbreaker Tower prison. How they choose to do this is up to them, and can be as stealthy or chaotic as they choose. Regardless of the methods they use, the party will:
>
> - Bypass the security that locks down the prison.
> - Defeat or evade the guards patrolling the various levels.
> - Escape the tower, and take Toron toward a chosen rendezvous.
>
> #### Manual Trigger
>
> This event needs to be manually triggered via the "Initiate Event" button found at the top and bottom of this page. Once the party has had sufficient time to plan, observe, and prepare their escape you want to begin the jailbreak events detailed below.

### The Veiled Chain Trap

For the Veiled Chain trap to be possible the following things need to be true as this event begins:

- The Outcome: [[One Last Thing]] needs to be completed, which was done by revealing that Katerin had made the party an offer to take Toron into custody, or by some other form of manipulation carried out by the party.
- The Outcome: [[Coming Clean]] needs to be complete, which indicates that the Veiled Chain have offered a counter-plan for the party to undertake.

If both states are completed, the party then has a more difficult task ahead of them: The need to escape to the roof of the tower with Toron without raising any alarms, or causing the tower to go on alert. If they can do this, then the Ambassador will be waiting and can be captured.

### Starting the Escape

The players may begin their jailbreak whenever they are ready. This can be at any time of day, wherever they are located in the prison, whether they are eating a meal, working out, or locked up in their cell. It's up to them to improvise their escape based on the resources and knowledge they've gathered.

We encourage you as the Gamemaster to simply have the guards follow their routine as normal and react to any oddities or disturbances they encounter while going about their patrols.

Since it is possible to fail an escape attempt, this event can be retried as many times as needed. See [[Breaking the Tower]] below.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Recovering the Items
>
> It should be assumed that the party knows where to find the items they delivered to the Spellbreaker Tower, and have been able to acquire those items before the jailbreak begins, as the escape itself is intended to be the main challenge.
>
> However, if you want to make retrieving the items part of the gameplay, you can have the guard from [[Visiting Hours]] quietly inform them where their items are stashed, and leave retrieval up to them. If you go this route, place the items in an empty cell nearby. To make retrieval harder, place the items in room they don't normally have access to, like a storage room, or a locked cell.

### Resolving the Escape

There are several ways the party can escape, but the intended method is to reach the top of the tower and then leap off with the wingsuits, gliding to safety. However, a party might find another place they can leap from the tower (Red Block and the Warden's Offices are potential alternatives) but they might also choose to exit the tower by heading down the stairwell.

While we don't have prepared gameplay for escaping down the tower stairs, since most of the guard forces are concentrated on the upper levels, a party that gets past the lowest security gates could escape fairly easily. Though, reaching the point is far easier said than done.

### Failing to Escape

It is possible that the party could fail to escape the tower. This results in them resetting their positions in the prison after being relieved of any items they have, including the wingsuits (if they had them on their person). These items can be found in a secured storeroom somewhere on the prison levels, allowing them a chance to reclaim them later.

Being captured and failing to escape doesn't prevent them from attempting another escape later, and is even expected. A party that fails to escape should have an easier time on future attempts because they'll know how fast guards can react, what doors they can bypass, and may even find new passages to improve their route.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Danger of Escalation
>
> We recommend cautioning your party against using lethal force unless absolutely necessary. The prison guards default to non-lethal attacks and don't kill prisoners if fights break out, but extreme force will prompt the guards to use more force in response.
>
> A party that kills may find themselves facing harsher treatment from guards if they fail to escape, or be locked down in a higher security (yellow block) or maximum security (red block) cell in response to their actions. Such an outcome could make future escape attempts harder.

### Springing the Trap

If the party has managed to meet the criteria to spring the [[Breaking the Tower]], and arrived at [[Spellbreaker Tower (Level 7)]], the top of the tower, Loris Tezran will arrive moments later.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Swooping in from on high, a great crimson Ancara lands on the roof of the tower, ridden by Ambassador Tezran. The pair strike the stone with a heavy thud, the great predator absorbing most of the impact with his powerful limbs.
>
> Loris dismounts even before Rakavi fully settles, and calls out to you:
>
> > I've decided to collect Toron myself. You've done well getting this far, but I don't think I can rely on you any further. I hope you don't hold it against me!
> >
> > Now. Hand over Torn, and we'll be going.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What about us?
>
> **A:**
>
> > You have the wingsuits don't you? Jump, as your planned! If you don't, well… you're on your own, I'm afraid.

If Toron is conscious, he is resistant:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Toron shakes his head.
>
> > I think I'll take my chances in the prison, given my options.
>
> Loris sneers.
>
> > I'm giving you an order!
>
> Toron barks out a bitter laugh.
>
> > Then you can add insubordination to my service log. I know what failure earns me under your leadership. I'd just as soon make my way back to the Empire on my own, Commander.
>
> Loris draws his sword.
>
> > Very well, we'll do this the hard way.

From this point forward, Toron and Loris become hostile to each other. The party may intervene, helping either side, or choose to stay out of it. However, the Veiled Chain quickly arrive:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Nearby cages pop open, a team of Veiled Chain agents posing as inmates coming view. They draw their weapons and call out:
>
> > Ambassador Tezran, Inmates of Spellbreaker Prison, stand down! You're all under arrest!
>
> Loris looks from the agents to you, and back, processing the betrayal.
>
> > Very cunning. I didn't think a double-cross was something you could manage.
>
> He points his blade at you.
>
> > I will show you what becomes of those that cross the Redfiend.

From this point Loris becomes fully hostile to the party, but they are joined by the Veiled Chain agents as allies.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Fighting the Redfiend
>
> Without access to magic, Loris Tezran relies on his pure martial abilities, using his [[Multiattack]] to make four attacks with [[Foolsbane]].
>
> As a masterful swordfighter, he uses his [[Reactive]] and [[Riposte]] abilities to defend himself, and take advantage of opportunities as they appear.
>
> His legendary actions include a [[Lunging Strike]] and two commands he can issue Rakavi: [[Command: Pounce]] and [[Command: Tail Swipe]] which he needs to be able to speak to use. If Rakavi is defeated, these actions become unusable.
>
> Loris is a ferocious fighter, but he does not have a deathwish, and will surrender if he falls below half his maximum hit points and is unlikely to win if he keeps fighting.
>
> Additionally, if Rakavi's life is in mortal peril (such as being below half its max HP), or if the beast has been killed, he can be convinced to give up with an action and a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)**, or **Intimidation (DC 14)** check.
>
> #### Fighting Rakavi
>
> Rakavi is an [[Ancara]] and uses its [[Sweeping Tail]] to knock over foes, followed by its [[Crushing Bite]] or [[Rending Claw]] after.
>
> It fights as long as Loris does, and surrenders only if they do.
>
> #### Veiled Chain Allies
>
> There are several Veiled Chain Agents hiding on the roof, posing as prisoners in cages when the trap is set. They fight to disable Loris and Rakavi, but refuse to kill either, and attempt to prevent anyone else from killing them. Their aim is to arrest Loris.

### Concluding the Event

#### Ragen Attunement: Breaking Out

A party that fights it way out of the tower without any regard for stealth or alarms advances their **Attunement: Ragen (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

#### Primordis Attunement: Sneaking Out

A party that successfully sneaks out of the prison or reaches the tower roof without raising alarm or entering combat advances their **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

#### Ragen Attunement: Redfiend Reversal

A party that successfully defeats Loris Tezran in battle on the top of the tower advances their **Attunement: Ragen (+2)** at the conclusion of the event.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Milestone Progression
>
> Completing this event awards a [[Milestone Progression]] point, potentially enabling the party to advance in level.
>
> #### Next Steps
>
> The resolution of this event depends on what the party has done in the prison.
>
> If the party was working with the Veiled Chain, and captured Ambassador Tezran, then they are released from the prison and the quest concludes with this event, though they will receive some unpleasant news later via the [[No Good Deed]] event.
>
> If the party escaped but is letting Toron go without turning him over, then the party can escape into the city, and end the quest chain here. However, they will remain wanted by the Hallows and Veiled Chain, and risk being apprehended if they draw too much attention to themselves going forward.
>
> #### Selecting a Destination
>
> If the party intends to turn Toron over to the Ambassador or Katerin, they will need to select a destination point for their escape, moving directly into the final event of the quest.
>
> If they take Toron to Ambassador Tezran they'll head back to Embassy Rise and get [[To the Redfiend]], if they take Toron to Katerin in Coinwealth Heights they'll get [[Personal Privateers]] instead.
