# Doing Time

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This exploration event sees the party delivered to the Spellbreaker Tower and submitted to the prison as new inmates. This is where the [[Criminal Records]] they helped created will come into play, which will determine what cell blocks they are placed into.
>
> During this event the party will:
>
> - Learn about the prison levels from the inside, how it works, and who is housed.
> - Meet with Toron and take steps to convince him to become part of the planned escape.
> - Prepare for the coming prison break.
>
> #### Area Walkthrough
>
> This event's gameplay transitions to the "Spellbreaker Tower" scene where the this event transpires. A complete room-by-room description of its environment occurs there is detailed in the [[Spellbreaker Tower]] area walkthrough. Text related to this event's resolution is retained in this event and should be referenced alongside the walkthrough.
>
> This area has several special rules and gameplay expectations, so it is advisable to review the [[Gameplay Details]] page either before or during the party's exploration of the location.

### Entry and Processing

The party begins at the [[Entry Checkpoint]] and will be moved through the prison by a group of three [[Spellbreaker Guard]], with two behind the group, and one ahead, leading the way.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A clerk sits behind a narrow window, his manner bored and brisk, leafing through pages of records you recognize your forged background.
>
> > Another fine batch of lowlifes. Excellent. Welcome to the tower. Now, let's see who we're working with today...
>
> As the clerk goes over your files and gets things in order the guards begin to confiscate your belongings, packing them away into chests.

At this point you should have the clerk read off the offenses of each character based on the persona they helped craft. Each one of these figures was apprehended, tried before the Hallows and found guilty.

In addition to these records they should be assigned placement in one of the prison levels: Blue, Yellow or Red. Their placement depends on the level of violence in their record, and how volatile they appear to be.

**Blue Block, Minimum security:**

Those who are unlikely to harm anyone are held here. They are allowed free movement in the block, open recreation, and communal meals. They generally receive the least scrutiny, and are rarely subject to searches or lockdowns unless there's an incident first.

**Yellow Block, Medium security:**

Those with capacity harm others and history of being dangerous are housed here. They are allowed out of their cells with supervision, and as long as they behave, may have communal meals, but are locked down for even minor offenses, and subject to frequent searches every day.

**Red Block, Maximum security:**

Those with incredible capacity for violence or those that cannot be trusted to control themselves are locked down here. They are not let out of their cells, are constantly watched, and receive meals delivered by armed guards.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Where to Place the Party
>
> It's recommended that you put the party in Blue Block if possible, but depending on the records they crafted, it might be more appropriate to put some of them in Yellow. We don't recommend you put any of the party in Red block to start, since that will severely limit character mobility in the prison.

### Into Holding

The party is walked through the prison level to [[Stairwell Checkpoint 1]] where they are alleviated of their items, armor, weapons, and anything else they brought with them.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The guard in the checkpoint points to the space in the center of the checkpoint.
>
> > Place your things in neat piles at your feet. You won't leave this level in anything but the clothes you're wearing. Once you've finished placing your items down, you'll be searched and moved aside.
> >
> > Your items will be stored in the tower, and returned to you upon your release, minus any items that are illegal to have in the city limits of Ordain. Those will be confiscated.

Once the party has finished being relieved of their items, they are taken up another level to [[Stairwell Checkpoint 2]]

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The march up the tower is not a difficult one, but the long, wide stairwells are without natural light, and the heavy stone walls make it clear nobody gets out of here easily. At each landing you spy locked gates manned by guards. Though none of them are heavily armed or armored, they look alert and focused.
>
> Each floor is the same: a series of gates opening and closing behind you as you move higher into the tower, tackling another flight of stairs, and passing through a level crawling with guards, until finally you arrive at a landing where a Cinadric Sage and a guard wait for you next to racks of clothing.

Here, the party is given a checkup by a waiting Cindaric Adherent, who will offer them mundane medical treatment for any injuries they may have. After that, they are instructed to put on uniforms here based on the cell block they are heading to. Privacy is a luxury not afforded here, sadly.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Once the party is changed, a senior guard appears to orient you:
>
> > Welcome to the tower, from here you're about to be taken to your cells. Before we do that, we're going to go over the rules in the Spellbreaker Tower. They are very simple: No fighting. No stealing. No hiding contraband. Follow the commands of the guards promptly, and we will have no issues.
>
> The guard paces slowly, the speech well practiced.
>
> > Meals are served the same time: morning, noon, and night, and we'll do our best to make sure everyone gets a chance to eat. If you can't make it to mess due to injury, illness or infraction, we'll make sure you're fed. Starving you is not one of the goals we aspire to here in the tower.
> >
> > We wish to treat you with dignity and respect, and believe such should be given back in kind. You will be afforded as much privacy and freedom as your condition or sentencing permits. But do not test us, we will do what is necessary to ensure safety and security are maintained.
>
> With that, the senior guard walks off, leaving you to change.

From here the party is moved up to [[Stairwell Checkpoint 3]]. Characters bound for Blue Block are sent through to an empty cell here, while Yellow block inmates are sent up another level.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Where to Place the Party
>
> It's recommended that you place the party in adjacent cells if possible, provided they are all on the same level.

### Establishing the Routine

Once the party has changed they are transferred to their cells and offered a meal if they are hungry. It's expected that you'll want to advance time at least eight hours after this point.

The prison serves food three times a day, breakfast at 9am, lunch at 2pm, and dinner at 7pm. Inmates are expected to be in their cells by 9pm. Otherwise, the characters are free to explore the prison and interact at will.

The party should be encouraged to interact with the prison, explore the area, and observe how the prison operates. Consult with the area walkthrough for the Spellbreaker Tower for how the tower operates while the party is present.

The walkthrough also includes the various inmates that can be encountered on each level, though you are encouraged to create your own colorful, strange, and dangerous characters to fill the cells as you see fit.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Time Served
>
> From that point onward the party should speak with Toron and begin preparing for their escape. This phase might take a few hours or could take days of preparation. Both are valid, but once the party is ready the next event should be triggered.

### Meeting Toron

Finding Toron is simple; the party know he is in Yellow Block, and is routinely allowed down into Blue Block during daytime hours to socialize and entertain himself due to generally good behavior. He often leaves his cell (when allowed) to eat, exercise, and otherwise spend his free time. Convincing him to join the escape however, is not. The task requires care as he expects spies, betrayal, and traps.

> Level 1 · Unknown Unknown

> ![Toron](../../../assets/image125.webp)

> [!info] Social
> #### Convincing Toron
>
> Convincing Toron to work with the party is not easy, and should not be attempted before the party speaks with him first. Saying the wrong thing could make these checks easier or harder.
>
> Failing to persuade Toron in any of the below methods does not prevent future attempts, and a party that spends more time with him may find other ways to make him agreeable.
>
> #### Persuading Toron
>
> A successful **Diplomacy (DC 18)** check can convince Toron to work with the party, though he will require the party to tell him the plan in detail so that he knows what to do.
>
> - **Result of 23+** Toron is convinced that the party and plan has merit, and will do his best to help prepare so that the escape is a success.
>
> #### Deceiving Toron
>
> A successful **Deception (DC 18)** check is necessary can convince Toron to work with the party without divulging all the details to him. He remains skeptical of their plan, and won't fully commit himself out of concern over it going badly.
>
> - **Result of 23+** Toron believes the party has a workable plan, and is willing to work with them, but dislikes being kept in the dark.
>
> #### Intimidating Toron
>
> A successful **Intimidation (DC 17)** check can force temporary compliance but he won't trust the party and views them as a threat, seeking to get away from them at first opportunity.
>
> - **Result of 22+** Toron is sufficiently cowed that he will remain compliant for the entirety of the escape, but his opinion doesn't change.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** How did you get captured?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I made a mistake, and my escape plan failed. Details don't matter beyond that.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** We can get you out. Are you willing to work with us?
>
> **A:**
>
> > You can try to get me out, but it's a tall order. Ordain runs a tight operation here, and the tower's magic suppression is unlike anything I've experienced. I don't think there's a way past it.
>
> If the fact the party is working for Redfiend is known, he also says:
>
> > Besides, with you being here on Loris' orders, I’d rather rot in this hole than let you turn me over to him.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What if we’re not working for Tezran?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Then you’ve got my attention. You pull this off, I’ll follow your lead... but gods help you if this is a trick.
>
> The party expressing disdain, hatred, or dislike for the Ambassador reduces the DC of any checks to convince Toron by 4. However, expressing the opposite will make the DC increase by the same amount.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Would you be willing to go into Katerin Bastilla's custody?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The head of the trade house? I suppose that could work out okay. I assume she wants my magical knowledge, and in return I'd be hidden. Might be trading one prison for another though...
>
> Extending Katerin's offer to Toron reduces the DC of any checks to convince Toron by 4. This can be combined with other DC reductions gained from social interactions with Toron.

If Ambassador Tezran is mentioned during any interactions, the following occurs:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The moment Ambassador Tezran’s name is mentioned, a muscle in Toron's jaw locks.
>
> > The Redfiend sent you, I assumed as much. If you expect me to just walk into his clutches, you're sorely mistaken. You'll have to drag me out of here, or kill me yourself.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why is he called The Redfiend?
>
> **A:**
>
> > It was the Empress’s gift after what he did to the Kelmezians. He burned a city to ash to prove his loyalty. Tens of thousands dead in a single night. It cemented his position in the royal court and put his name on the lips of every noble house in the Empire.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why would he want you back now?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Because I failed him. My failure’s a stain on his record. If he retrieves me himself, he gets to clean it publicly. Don’t think he’s doing it out of friendship or obligation as my commending officer.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** You expect the Ambassador to kill you?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Absolutely, without a single doubt. I'm dead if I let you take me to him, and I wouldn't expect him to treat you any better as a reward.
> >
> > His favorite place to keep his daggers is in the backs of his allies and underlings.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What's it like in the Empire?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Great, if you're on top. There's plenty of money, food, and luxury for the noble houses and their cronies. If you're born poor though you just have the factories and forges to look forward to. Or if you're unlucky, the mines and gas pits.

#### Ragen Attunement: Unwilling Participant

A party that coerces or forces Toron to cooperate with their plans advances their **Attunement: Ragen (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

#### Primordis Attunement: Willing Participant

A party that convinces or tricks Toron into cooperating with their plans advances their **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.
