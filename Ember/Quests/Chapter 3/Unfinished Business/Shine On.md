# Shine On

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs the first time the party takes a **Long Rest** following the [[Swift Healing]] Event, and introduces the characters to the effects of a Rank 1 Soulmark.
>
> In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Wake to discover an apparition of their friend [[Agraband Swift]] soul drifting away from his body, alongside an intensification of his supernatural wound: indicators of his [[Rank 1 Soulmark]].
> - Intervene in an attempt to prevent Agraband from suffering the worst possible outcome of this Rank 1 Soulmark.
> - Try to assess the likelihood of this happening again, learning more about the practical effects of life as a [[Death and Soulbound]] character in the process.
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Cozy Lounge" Level of the [[Vista: Ordain Interiors]] Vista. In the rare circumstance that the party is traveling outside of Ordain when this Event occurs, you may wish to change the Vista to something more geographically accurate.
>
> #### Prerequisites
>
> This Event may only occur during a Rest while [[Agraband Swift]] is a member of the [[Party]].

### Nightmares & Dreamscapes

In this moment, the characters are timely witnesses to the first manifestation of Agraband's Rank 1 Soulmark, and can interrupt the bard's supernatural nightmare to help him stave off its negative effects.

> [!abstract] Agraband Swift
> **[[Agraband Swift]]**
>
> Level 3 · Human Caravaneer
>
> ![Agraband Swift](../../../assets/image095.webp)
>
> A lute is slung across the back of this aged human clad in an odd yet practical assortment of clothing and jewelry. The seasoned bard wears the salt-and-pepper locks of his long hair pulled back into a high half ponytail. His wide smile and calm demeanor lend him an air of experienced confidence, and a certain glint in his eye evokes the mirthful spirit of a beloved uncle. It's clear this man could spin a worthy tale at a moment’s notice.

> [!info] Social
> #### Timely Intervention
>
> For the duration of this Quest, the mechanics of Agraband's [[Death and Soulbound]] nature are modeled after those of a Player Character rather than a traditional NPC. These rules are fully detailed in the Player Characters section of the Player's Guide.
>
> As allies who are present during Agraband's nightmarish episode, the characters can attempt to intervene, providing **+2 Boons** to Agraband's associated Wisdom save by immediately taking one of the following actions:
>
> - Casting the [[Calm Emotions]] spell with Agraband inside the area of effect.
> - Expending 1 use of [[Channel Divinity]] (Clerics) or [[Channel Divinity]] (Paladins) while laying hands on Agraband.
> - Issuing 1 dose of [[M’abb Salts]] to Agraband as an inhalant.
>
> Alternatively, if Agraband becomes the target of the [[Dream]] spell, he automatically succeeds on his associated Wisdom save.
>
> In all likelihood, this is the first time the characters have encountered a Soulbound character's Lesser Soulmark. Naturally, they may not instinctively know if they have useful skills at their disposal — or whether they can help at all. Thus, a few corresponding methods of discovery are detailed in "Bedside Manner" below.

If they act quickly, the characters can perform certain physical and metaphysical examinations to help steer them towards an action that could aid Agraband.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Bedside Manner
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 15)** check is confident in the ability of the [[Calm Emotions]] spell to help stem the tide of psychic episodes such as this.
>
> - **Knowledge: Souls**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character also trusts in the efficacy of the [[Dream]] spell to counteract the Lesser Soulmarks' adverse effects.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 16)** check is confident in the ability of a cleric or paladin's [[Channel Divinity]] class feature to help neutralize Agraband's current ailments.
>
> - **Knowledge: Undeath**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who possesses at least one dose of [[M’abb Salts]] and makes a successful **Medicine (DC 16)** or **Wilderness (DC 16)** check is aware of the rare mineral's ability to counteract a small array of psychic and spiritual maladies.
>
> - **Knowledge: Alchemy**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

### The Bard Awakens

If Agraband is left undisturbed, his nightmares continue for the remainder of their duration, and Agraband suffers all negative effects of the Lesser Soulmark as described in the rules for [[Death and Soulbound]] in the Player Characters section of the Player's Guide. If the characters attempt to wake Agraband without a successful intervention, the apparition persists until he wakes of his own accord. Read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Agraband Swift jumps awake with a shriek.
>
> > Wait! … Wait …
>
> Drenched in sweat and eyes wide with fear, he struggles to catch his breath.
>
> > I beheld a nightmarish vision, a harrowing reminder of our incident in the Smokerie. Were it not a dream, I'd tell you it was as real as the very air we breathe.

Alternately, if the characters manage to successfully intervene, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Your efforts to remedy Agraband's plight appear to be somewhat successful, as you can plainly see the apparition dissipate, retreating back into the bard's sleeping body. Just then, he startles from his slumber and regards you with a measure of bewildered anxiety.
>
> > What happened? Why are you all looking at me like that?

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Medical Examinations
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Medicine (DC 13)** check is able to confirm that Agraband's wound hasn't changed in size or appearance since the encounter at Traveler's Rest, despite the strange luminous pulsations that occurred during his nightmare.
>
> - **Critical Success**: By gauging the intensity of Agraband's psychic trauma (or lack thereof), the character can also successfully estimate the range of damage a Lesser Soulmark like this one could cause in the future.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Conversation with Agraband
>
> If and when Agraband is woken up from his troubled slumber, he's notably harrowed by the situation, but he's willing to talk about the incident as he slowly regains consciousness. Topics he's willing to discuss include (but are not limited to):
>
> - The imagery of the nightmare, which involved a horrific and phantasmagoric version of the incident at The Pit Trap.
> - The current state of discomfort associated with his mortal wound, which is notably minuscule and of no immediate concern.
> - Reasonable courses of action to help mitigate the damage another nightmare could cause.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 13)** check can validate the honesty of Agraband's accounts.
>
> Specific dialogue options for the aforementioned talking points are detailed below. While Agraband is willing to discuss the issue with some measure of fastidiousness, he's also eager to have a few moments to himself, where he can collect his thoughts in silence after such a harrowing incident.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your nightmare?
>
> **A:**
>
> Agraband's eyes grow heavy with remembrance as he recollects the images of his dream.
>
> > We were back at that infernal Pit Trap, you and I. But the dance floor — once an oasis of revelry — was a sea of monstrous faces and endless shadows. Jorey was there on the balcony, alongside his hero, and they were carving the limbs off a young dancer like … slices of cake. The room was so hot, but I felt so impossibly cold. And as I made my way across that bedeviled room, I felt a tugging sensation behind me.
> >
> > I turned to look back, and saw myself standing there, as if I hadn't moved an inch since the dream began. Just then, a horrible laceration streaked its way across my throat. And as I stared into my own eyes, horrified, I clutched at the wound in my chest. A moment later, I woke to see you standing over me. But I can still feel the chill of that shadow-haunted air …

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your wound?
>
> **A:**
>
> Agraband runs his fingers lightly across his lambent scar, reflecting on the odd nature of it.
>
> > It's strange … even though this wound persists and sheds its curious light, I feel no pain from it. I'll never quite forget the sting of Zira's dagger, though. And the pain of Jorey's betrayal.
> >
> > That's a wound I fear may never heal …

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What next?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Perhaps I should stay awake for a while and collect my thoughts. Thank you for your timely assistance, friends. It seems we may want to actually lean upon the madcap wisdom of Conaris Haid, as our friends at Traveler's Rest were so keen to suggest.
> >
> > I fear the High Priest of Sockets has a reputation that precedes him, however, and gaining his trust on these matters may be easier said than done.

### Formal Investigations

If the party is inspired to seek answers about Agraband's apparition (as it relates to his condition or as a novel supernatural phenomenon), the traditional methods of gathering information will only get them so far. Ultimately, they'll need to make their way to [[Conaris Haid]] at the Temple of Sockets for [[Answers From On High]] to find out what's really going on behind Agraband's undying nature.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Hitting the Books
>
> Attempts to study the nature of Agraband's condition in a formal capacity can lead the characters to interesting places like the Old City Library and the Librarium of Spectra in [[Scholar's Nook]], or the esoteric libraries of [[All-Fable Keep]].
>
> However, these efforts are ultimately unfruitful, as the emergence of Soulbound on Ember is a contemporary occurrence.

> [!info] Social
> #### Word on the Street
>
> Any character who spends at least one hour searching an appropriately busy section of town for rumors and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 12)** check is pointed toward the various temples in Ordain's [[Temple Ward]] or [[Numinous Shrines]] districts.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The recommendation is for the Temple of Sockets, with a personal referral for the High Priest Conaris Haid.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> No matter the result of this encounter, the party is free to continue their **Long Rest** (which should be a relatively uneventful one, per GM discretion). But the threat of another soul-sundering nightmare lingers, and will continue to do so until the party has helped Agraband settle his so-called unfinished business with Jorey.
>
> Due to Agraband's Soulbound condition and the nature of his eponymous unfinished business, this Event has a chance to repeat each time the party takes a **Long Rest** until they complete the [[Running the Gauntlet]] Event.
>
> As the party hunts for clues, they'll invariably be led toward the Temple of Sockets in [[Temple Ward]] for the [[Answers From On High]] Event, as previously recommended by Thessandra during [[Swift Healing]].
>
> If the party hasn't visited Traveler's Rest in [[Westgate]], a few reminders (including from Agraband himself) should point them in that direction so they can receive a professional assessment of Agraband's condition.
>
> Alternately, the party may choose to wait until the [[Game Day]] Event to continue their investigation, eventually leading them to the circumstances of [[Early Retirement]] and [[The Old Flame]].
