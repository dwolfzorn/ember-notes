# Game Day

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration and Social Event tasks the party with gathering some timely information at Grand Kalion Stadium in [[Arena Ridge]], where they'll attend a session of the local "Solar Games" featuring [[Zira Hestidero]] and The Undaunted. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Gain entry to Grand Kalion Stadium, where Zira and The Undaunted are scheduled to compete against another team in the gladiatorial Solar Games.
> - As attendees, gather information about Zira and The Undaunted, including the names and locations of two people formerly tied to Zira's inner circle.
> - Collect additional rumors about Zira and The Undaunted, which may or may not prove useful throughout the investigation to come.
>
> #### Prerequisites
>
> [[Agraband Swift]] must be a member of the [[Party]] for this Event to occur.

### For Ticket Holders Only

Although they can scrounge for a modicum of information outside the stadium walls, the characters will need to gain proper access to the Solar Games by procuring a ticket for all attendees. While purchasing tickets from the box office is the most conventional route, various methods of obtaining the tickets are available to the party, as described below.

> [!info] Social
> #### Rules & Regulations
>
> The following set of rules and regulations guide the day-to-day business at Grand Kalion Stadium, which holds upwards of 20,000 people in its tiered seating.
>
> 1. The cost of entry to the Solar Games is 2 gp per adult attendee. Tickets for attendees under the age of 14 are only 1 gp, and children must be supervised at all times.
> 2. Weapons and arcane accoutrements must be checked at the front gate with the Security Office. Attendees are given a ticket that corresponds to the locker containing their possessions.
> 3. Violence in the stands and adjoining facilities is not allowed. There is no second strike. Violators are promptly ejected by a contingency of Stadium Security staff. Treat Stadium Security as [[Ordani Bouncer]].
> 4. Gambling on events is not precisely forbidden, but there is no formal sportsbook maintained by Grand Kalion Stadium or its affiliates.
>
> The Solar Games are not the only event at Grand Kalion Stadium, but they are perhaps the most popular. The Solar Games take place twice a week, on Wednesdays and Fridays, with sets of matches typically starting at Noon and 3pm.
>
> #### Box Office Banter
>
> The Grand Kalion Stadium box office is currently supervised by the excitable **Holden Borios** (Chaotic Good, Ordani Signborn, he/him), a kid-at-heart cashier who has outstanding rapport with younger fans thanks to his obsession with tall tales.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 18)** or **Diplomacy (DC 18)** check is able to spin a tale convincing enough to earn them a 50% discount, requiring the paltry sum of 1 gp for entry. Intimidation tactics will result in the swift arrival of two or more Stadium Security guards.
>
> - **Knowledge: Legends**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> A fan of unheard stories, Holden Borios is a rather chatty clerk, and is happy to talk with the characters for a brief moment (as long as they don't hold up the line for very long). Topics of conversation include:
>
> - Some basic information about the Solar Games.
> - Zira Hestidero and The Undaunted, reigning champs of the Solar Games.
> - Their challengers, a team from Cutter's Lane known as "Cutter's Crushers."
> - Grand Kalion Stadium fun facts.
>
> A successful **Deception (DC 13)** check is all it takes to confirm the authenticity of Holden's conversation points, and suggests an overall demeanor of honesty and kindness.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Solar Games?
>
> **A:**
>
> A look of excitement crosses the Signborn clerk's youthful face, and his four arms begin punctuating the conversation with broadly-animated gestures.
>
> > Now, some folks prefer the simplicity of Fastball, or the majesty of the Crown's Cup. Personally, I'm a big fan of the Solar Games. The story goes, after Arcus Kalion retired, he sat down with a few of the Anachraenum's most esteemed strategists to come up with the Solar Games — which are as close as you can get to a dungeon crawl without the dungeon or its toothy denizens. No beasts, no bullshit. Just one neighborhood team versus another, running the modular gauntlet as the people's gladiators. What. A. Rush!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About The Undaunted?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Ah, yes. The champions of the realm. "The team you love to hate."
> >
> > They're good at what they do, there's no denying that. Zira's just a little … aggressive, for my tastes. I look at it this way: as long as the audience is happy and nobody really gets hurt, I don't care how unsportsmanlike a match gets. Unfortunately, the Undaunted have a habit of crossing that line every once in a while. Maybe that's why the roster changes so often …

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Cutter's Crushers?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The Crushers are probably in over their heads today. They're a proud bunch of athletes, raised by hard-working families in Cutter's Passage. So, just like The Undaunted, they've got something to prove out there. But the main difference between the two is that the Crushers, as brawny as they are, lack the speed — and outright brutality — that Zira's Undaunted have at their disposal. And Ned Graves, the Crushers captain, is a bit of a lout. Good guy, but dumb as a rock. Needless to say, all bets are on The Undaunted today.
>
> He casts a look past you at the line of would-be ticket buyers, which has slowly gathered length since your conversation started.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Grand Kalion Stadium?
>
> **A:**
>
> Using all four arms, the Signborn clerk gestures broadly at the sprawling facility surrounding him.
>
> > "Grand Kalion Stadium is an architectural marvel, designed to host a variety of events and entertainment spectacles. With a seating capacity that rivals the largest arenas in the known world, the stadium features state-of-the-art magic and technology, including a dynamic scoreboard and sound systems that enhance the spectating experience."
> >
> > At least, that's what the brochure says.
>
> He tosses you a wink and a smile, snorting beneath his breath at the pedantic humor.
>
> > Check it out for yourselves. Only two gold pieces a head!

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Scouting the Perimeter
>
> Characters who attempt to gain more insight about their surroundings here outside the stadium can do so by spending a few moments inspecting the facade and its patrons.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 12)** check while observing the stadium exterior is able to notice the following:
>
> - The burgundy and gray sign that reads "The Undaunted" appears slightly sun-bleached and weather-stained. A longer look suggests it's been intermittently defaced by spiteful missiles of eggs or rotten vegetables (however expertly cleaned and maintained).
> - The sound of some kind of pre-show can be heard echoing from within the stadium, and the crowd of thousands roars with equally raucous measures of cheers and jeers each time The Undaunted are mentioned.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character notices a group of three [[Ordani]] pre-teens helping another of their ilk sneak in through the front gate by providing a worthy (and absurd) distraction.
>
> #### Ticket Snatchers
>
> Alternately, one or more of the characters can attempt to gain illicit access to the Solar Games by purloining a ticket or sneaking in.
>
> A successful **Awareness (DC 15)** check is necessary to first identify an adequate mark (i.e. target) for ticket theft, followed by a successful **`[[/skill sleightofhand 15]]`** check to actually steal the ticket.
>
> - **Distraction:** The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check if one or more allies makes a successful **Performance (DC 13)** check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Stealth (DC 20)** check is able to sneak their way into the busy stadium without actually presenting a ticket.
>
> - **Size Small or Tiny:** The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Distraction:** The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check if one or more allies makes a successful **Performance (DC 13)** check.

> [!info] Social
> #### Gathering Information: Stadium Exterior
>
> The party can attempt to gather additional information about Zira Hestidero and The Undaunted outside the stadium (along with other information relevant to the tasks at hand). However, the details available here are rather limited, as most of the informants are already located inside.
>
> Any character who spends at least 15 minutes attempting to gather information outside the stadium and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check is able to round up one of the following rumors (roll `[[/roll 1d4]]` to determine which):
>
> 1. The Undaunted have a reputation for playing exceptionally rough, pushing their opponents (and the rules) to the limit.
> 2. Zira Hestidero is known for burning bridges, and has lost a few friends on her way to the top.
> 3. Cutter's Crushers are a decent team with several wins under their belts, but they play a slow and deliberate game. They're outclassed today when it comes to speed.
> 4. Ned Graves is the son of a nepotistic landlord named Carson Graves, who owns several buildings in The Smokerie where The Undaunted are from.
>
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character is also referred to a regular attendee of some renown inside, a local Solar Games historian of sorts named **Dani Brax** (Chaotic Neutral, Ordani Kivahr, she/they), who might be able to provide them with better information about the two teams.

### Game On!

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Stepping into the stadium proper, you're engulfed by a sea of auditory and olfactory sensations — from the undulating cacophony of the crowd, to the ozone smell of magitech on the field.
>
> At the northern end of the arena, you spy Zira Hestidero and a quartet of Undaunted adepts, who circle their positions on the starting platform like hyenas eyeballing a juicy carcass. To the south, five athletes clad in blue-and-white take their own places on a matching rostrum, shrouded in conspicuous apprehension.
>
> A sprawling obstacle course connects these two starting platforms — rife with hazards, traps, and pedestals bearing various polearms. At the center of the arena, a luminous orb fashioned into a likeness of the spirit moon hovers above a suspended dais.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Scouting the Interior
>
> Characters who attempt to gain more insight about their surroundings here inside the stadium can do so by spending a few moments inspecting the crowd.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Awareness (DC 12)** check while observing the stadium interior is able to notice the following:
>
> - With a great deal of certainty, [[Jorey Swift]] is nowhere to be seen.
> - **Roving Vendors** weave their way through the crowd at regular intervals, offering an array of food, drink, and souvenirs (for what are sure to be inflated prices).
> - In the arena, Zira's Undaunted have already established dominance over the Crushers, one of whom is bleeding from a light head wound.
> - **Result of 17+**: An exceptionally loud contingency of fans clad in Undaunted team colors occupies a section of the risers on the east side of the stadium. Towards the front, a burly Kivahr woman holds court for a throng of other attendees who follow her backseat coaching with enthusiasm.
> - A character using a [[Spyglass]] or similar item has **+2 Boons** on this check.

> [!info] Social
> #### Gathering Information: Stadium Interior
>
> If they're interested in doing so, the party can attempt to gather additional information about Zira Hestidero and The Undaunted inside the stadium (along with other information relevant to the tasks at hand).
>
> Any character who spends at least 15 minutes attempting to gather information inside the stadium and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check is able to round up one of the following rumors (roll `[[/roll 1d4]]` to determine which):
>
> 1. The Undaunted have a reputation for playing exceptionally rough, pushing their opponents (and the rules) to the limit.
> 2. Zira Hestidero is known for burning bridges, and has lost a few friends on her way to the top.
> 3. Cutter's Crushers are a decent team with several wins under their belts, but they play a slow and deliberate game. They're outclassed today when it comes to speed.
> 4. Ned Graves is the son of a nepotistic landlord named Carson Graves, who owns several buildings in The Smokerie where The Undaunted are from.
>
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**:The character is also referred to a regular attendee of some renown inside, a Solar Games historian named Dani Brax, who might be able to provide them with better information about the two teams. She always sits on the east side of the stadium on the front row.
> - **3 Total Successes:** The character receives the **Critical Success** result.
>
> The party can attempt to repeat this Intelligence (Investigation) check up to a total of 8 times (representing the duration of the match). When the match is over, the boisterous crowd pours out of the stadium like a flood, rife with the celebration and scorn of twenty-thousand fans.
>
> #### A Conversation with Dani Brax
>
> Once the party is successfully informed of the local Solar Games historian, they can track her down after a few moments of searching near the easternmost section of the arena's front row seats.
>
> **Dani Brax** (Chaotic Neutral, Ordani Kivahr, she/they) is a crude and jocular forty-something with nothing to lose other than her attention on the arena. Although she roots for several of the local teams that compete in the Games (and appreciates their disparate qualities), Dani has a perverse interest in violence, which puts The Undaunted in high (if fractious) esteem.
>
> The first character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 15)**, **Intimidation (DC 17)**, or **Diplomacy (DC 15)** check is able to convince Dani to share some of her insider knowledge about the Solar Games, including the following details:
>
> - The address of an upstart forge in the [[Smokerie]], operated by a former Undaunted athlete named [[Brackus von Tet]], who went into early retirement for reasons unknown.
> - The name of Zira's estranged lover, a mystic in [[Orchard Lanes]] known as [[Helice Korsos]], who provides arcane spellcasting services from her home office.
> - A few details about Zira and her teammates, including some of their favorite arena tactics and Ku'arta's influence on their style.
>
> - **Bribe With** [[Sash of the Undaunted]]: The character automatically succeeds on this check if they bribe Dani with this sash, found during the [[Status Effects]] Event.
> - **Knowledge: Warfare**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check by appealing to Dani's enthusiasm for tactics.
> - **Path: ShardGodDevotee**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check by appealing to Dani's fascination with eldritch lore surrounding The Undaunted.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What do you know about Zira?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I know she's about five-foot-eight of "grade-A ass-kicker." Quite a few of us think she's the best athlete to ever run the gauntlet. I might be inclined to agree. Of course, Arcus Kalion fans might disagree. I know pretty much what everyone knows about Zira Hestidero: she's an underdog from The Smokerie who turned out to be a savant when it comes to hazard sports.
> >
> > She's brutal, smart, and gorgeous. What more could you ask for in a Solar Games all-star?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The retired athlete?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The roster is a little light this season. One of my favorite players is missing, a bruiser named Brackus von Tet. I hear he set up shop as a smithy in the Smokerie on Chateau Lane, putting those big mitts of his to work forging arms and armor. A waste of talent if you ask me. No idea why he left the team, but it's a loss.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Zira's old flame?
>
> **A:**
>
> > To be clear, I think people's business is their own. But those two were a dynamite couple. Helice Korsos is a charming young woman. And Zira speaks for herself. But together? Man … Hotter than Ragen's insides. Zira loved parading her warlock girl around on game day. But that all changed a while back. Helice keeps to her shop in Orchard Lanes. Nobody's seen her around here in months, and the speculation is all reasonable, respectable stuff. Idle gossip, you know the kind. I wish them both the best.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Ku'arta's influence on the Solar Games?
>
> **A:**
>
> > What can I say? The God of Torment's patronage is a worthy addition to the lineup here. Let's face it: the Solar Games are called "The Solar Games" for a reason. Just look at how they're controlling the field out there … To the victor go the spoils, and victory is traditionally earned in this arena by wits, speed, and whatever it takes to win.
> >
> > For Zira, that means a few boons from a shard god from time to time. And The Undaunted are careful to avoid breaking the rules — well … maybe not careful, but they do tend to actually avoid breaking them somehow. There's a fine line between ripe and rotten, my old man used to say. Zira likes to ride that line here in the arena. And it's damn good spectacle, if you ask me.

### Concluding the Event
