# Family Business

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs when the party meets [[Lyla Cevher]] in the House Cevher Tradeway in [[Quarry Run]] after [[A Cevher Summons]]. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Learn about the House Cevher's current state of affairs from Lyla: her uncle was murdered, her brother is the prime suspect, and the other trading houses have seized the opportunity to remove House Cevher from the [[Ordinate]], leaving Lyla unsure of who to trust.
> - Agree to help Lyla by finding her brother's current location in Ordain and investigating her uncle Darius' death at his shop in [[Gossamer]].

> [!abstract] Lyla Cevher
> **[[Lyla Cevher]]**
>
> Level 2 · Human Cevher Heiress
>
> ![Lyla Cevher](../../../assets/image097.webp)
>
> A Human who is sharply dressed in a beautiful and richly decorated coat that proudly displays her wealthy background. It's clear from an initial glance at her overall bearing and clothing style that she is from the city of Ordain itself and while she holds herself with a confident air, she is also friendly and welcoming with a slight smile and small laughter lines appearing around her eyes.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you enter the room, Lyla looks up and smiles, though the expression quickly dissipates into one of steely determination.
>
> > Thank you for coming. Unless you've been absolutely ignoring the news, I assume you know the state of things? My poor uncle killed, my brother being blamed and nowhere around to defend himself, and House Cevher taken out of its seat on the Ordinate based on rumors and lies. To think that anyone would kill a man who only wanted to make beautiful things over what's probably some political dispute? I keep thinking — if only I'd gotten here sooner to stop it somehow. But I can't change the past.
> >
> > What to do going forward, on the other hand? That I have a pretty good handle on.

### The Current Situation

> [!info] Social
> #### Lyla's Losses
>
> Lyla is devastated at what she has lost, but that sadness is being covered with determination. A successful `[[/check insight 16]]` check reveals that she is taking action to keep herself from despair. Any talk of her emotions is quickly replaced with her thoughts on what she can do about the situation.
>
> Any character who makes a successful `[[/check history 16]]` check knows that the most likely culprit for this kind of action is one of the trading houses. Lyla doesn't want to believe they would be capable of it, but agrees.
>
> - **Knowledge: Politics**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Darius' Murder
>
> **A:**
>
> > I can't believe Uncle Darius is gone. He cared much more about making cloth than anything having to do with house politics, and yet I'm afraid that his murder is all about Cevher and not about him. How could it be? He was such a kind man. Nobody would want to harm him.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Funar's Disappearance
>
> **A:**
>
> > Everyone — and by everyone, I mean the other Trading Houses — are spreading lies about my brother, who wouldn't hurt a fly. I know he's still in the city … we each wear an earring that gives us a sense of how far apart we are … and the fact that he hasn't reached out must mean that someone's got him. I need to find him and save him from whatever's going on. That's why I need your help.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Removal of House Cevher from the Ordinate
>
> **A:**
>
> > I'm not sure who was behind House Cevher losing the Ordinate seat. House Bastilla holds the seat now, and they certainly aren't afraid to break the rules. But according to what I've heard, the deciding votes were from House Wandren and House Lillifeld. Ultimately, I'm not sure I can trust any of them. Whoever is behind it, we need to figure this out on our own. That is, if you'll help me.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Likely Culprits (requires **Knowledge: Politics** or `[[/check history 16]]`)
>
> **A:**
>
> > I grew up alongside the members of other trading houses — I would hate to think that one of them could be behind this. But you're right. Who else could it be? They seem to be the only ones who truly benefit.

### A Call to Action

> [!info] Social
> #### Lyla's Ask
>
> Lyla is determined to fix things for her family and asks the party for their help to find Funar and solve the murder.
>
> #### Darius' Murder
>
> Lyla gives the party an article containing the popular understanding of how Darius was murdered, which happened in his shop in the Gossamer district, Threadforge — [[Overheard In Ordain: A Stitch In Crime]]. According to her sources, the investigation has stalled and the shop is still closed up. She suggests that they investigate themselves and see if they can find any information that the investigators either missed or have yet to discover.
>
> #### Finding Funar
>
> Lyla wears an earring attuned to an identical one worn by Funar. While it doesn't give her his exact location, he must be within 20 miles of her current location, which includes most of Ordain. She has used her earring to create a [[Locator Rod]] that should be able to track him more closely, but it only has 10 charges — once all are gone, the party must return to Lyla to recharge it. Once it's within 200 feet of him, the jewel on the Locator Rod will switch colors.
>
> - Any character with **Knowledge: Politics**, or who previously identified the Trading Houses as the probable cause of House Cevher's troubles, have the sense that Funar Cevher is being kept alive near one of the Trading House centers. Lyla agrees and names a few of the major trading house districts:
>   - **House Lillifeld:**[[Lilifeld Stations]]
>   - **House Bastilla:** [[Coinwealth Heights]], [[Ember's Bounty]]
>   - **House Wandren:** [[Marlstone]]
>   - **House Salva:** [[Sunhaven]]
> - Any character with **Knowledge: Intrigue** can identify districts in Ordain that might be good for hiding people away. If asked, Lyla agrees with the list as possible starting places:
>   - **[[Fallbrook]]**: known for its underground tunnels
>   - **[[Lantern Roads]]:** has extensive back alleys
>   - **[[The Harborage]]:** used to move goods to and from the docks, including late at night

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Locating Funar
>
> **A:**
>
> > As I said, I know that Funar is in the city because of my earring. I've used that to create this Locator Rod, which you can activate to send you in the right general direction. See how the jewel in the center is gray? That means Funar is somewhere in the city, but nowhere nearby. The rod will pull you in toward Funar each time you use it, and when you're within 200 feet or so, the stone will turn to blue — Funar's favorite color.
> >
> > Would you mind moving around the city and seeing what you can find? If you run out of charges, just bring it to me and I can use my earring to recharge it. I'd join you, but I need to spend some time on Trading House affairs — right now, I seem to be the only one holding the Trading House together.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Solving Darius' Murder
>
> **A:**
>
> > Darius' shop was locked up tight by the Veiled Chain, who claim to be investigating it, but it's clear they've put it on the back burner. I'm convinced there's evidence they're overlooking about the case, if they've even looked at all. Could you go in and investigate on your own? If I do it, they'll assume I'm biased, but anything that you find, we can follow up with. Rumors are flying in the meantime — this ridiculous article gives a good rundown of the general "facts" that everyone is talking about.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Restoring House Cevher
>
> **A:**
>
> > Helping Funar and avenging Darius are important, but I'm responsible for more than just myself. I care about the house as a whole. And this isn't an isolated incident. There have been several strikes against House Cevher recently. We need to figure out who it is and stop their plans. But only after Funar is safe and we've struck back against whoever killed Darius.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Where to Start
>
> **A:**
>
> > If a trading house is behind this, Funar might be around one of their stronghold districts. House Lillifeld has Lillifeld Station, Wandren is in Marlstone, Bastilla has Coinwealth Heights and Ember's Bounty, and Salva dominates Gossamer, where Darius was murdered. I'd definitely start with one of those.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Where Else to Start
>
> **A:**
>
> > There are definitely some districts that would be easier for whisking someone away — or hiding them — Fallbrook has all those underground clubs, Lantern Roads is known for its alleys and tunnels, and there are always ships coming to and fro at the Harborage moving boxes.

### Concluding the Event

With her conversation with the group finished, Lyla turns back to her work, asking the party to fill her in when they know something new.
