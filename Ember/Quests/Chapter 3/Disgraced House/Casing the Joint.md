# Casing the Joint

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event occurs when the party travels to [[Marlstone]] following the [[Veiled Threats]] Event. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Disguise themselves as contracted laborers.
> - Scout the manor for important locations while avoiding detection.
> - Speak with other laborers about what they know.
> - Use the [[Locator Rod]] to discover where [[Funar Cevher]] is being held.
>
> #### Area Walkthrough
>
> The party begins in the [[Marlstone Manor - First Floor]] Scene, where the central gameplay of this Event transpires. A complete description of the manor and the gameplay that occurs there is detailed in the [[Marlstone Manor]] Area Walkthrough.

There are a few key pieces of information that the party can discover while scouting the manor, each of which will help them in the heist.

- **The Manor Layout:** The party immediately receives a rough sketch of the manor layout for their role as temporary staff, but can find more information about the manor, including the location of the hidden staircase in the [[Hephiss' Downstairs Closet]] that leads to the level below by checking out the books in the [[Library]], notes in [[Hephiss' Study]], or an old map found in the [[Servants' Office]].
- **Funar's Location:** By using the [[Locator Rod]], the party can discover that Funar is in a room below the [[Dining Room]]. They will receive a result that he is "nearby" in any adjoining rooms (the [[Breakfast Room]], [[Coatroom]], [[Sitting Room]], and [[Staff Bathroom]]).
- **Getting to Funar:** Information about how to open the hidden staircase in the [[Hephiss' Downstairs Closet]] can be found in a locked safe in Hephiss Wandren's [[Hephiss' Study]], upstairs in her [[Hephiss' Chambers]], or upstairs in her [[Art Studio]].
- **Making Preparations:** Throughout the manor, there are items that the party may find helpful during the heist, from extra sets of clothing to alchemy ingredients that can be used to create smoke to doors that can be propped open to enter from outside. These are noted in each room within the area walkthrough.

### Getting into Marlstone Manor

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you enter the manor, footsteps echoing on the stone floor and bouncing off of the finely carved ceiling above, you are greeted by a woman of middling years, who beckons you in and greets you in a voice that seems used to being heard and obeyed.
>
> > Greetings all! I'm Jalla Difae, the event planner for the upcoming Marlstone Gala, to be held in the manor behind me. Now I know that Hephiss has hired enough of you to fill an Arcturian caravan, but we're going to do things in as orderly a process as possible.
> >
> > Gardeners, you'll head through the Grand Hall behind me to the gardens out back. Decorators, you'll start your cleaning and decoration work in the Grand Hall. Everyone else, you'll be helping with the food in the kitchen — head through the gallery to your left and straight back. Make sure you get the equipment you need and list of tasks before you start, though — if you're not carrying the proper tools, you won't be able to work. No exceptions!

Each member of the party can decide whether they wish to fit in as a gardener, decorator, or cook — and whether they want to split up or scout together.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Choosing Your Role
>
> Different staff have access to different parts of the manor, and are easily identified by some aspect of their uniform. Characters can choose the gardener, decorator, or kitchen staff role by taking one of the following.
>
> - Gardener ([[Gardener's Toolbelt]])
> - Decorating ([[Satchel of Marlstone Decor]])
> - Kitchen Staff ([[Apron of Many Pockets]])
>
> Any character who makes a successful **`[[/skill sleightofhand 17]]`** check can take an additional uniform, but can only believably wear one at a time.
>
> A character without a uniform will be stopped by any NPC who sees them, and must make a successful **Deception (DC 15)** or be escorted out of the manor.

### Scouting the Manor

This Event takes place on the Area Map. As party members make their way through the Scene, they will encounter three types of rooms: **Open**, **Restricted**, or **Forbidden**, each of which is handled differently.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Tracking Suspicion
>
> As the party goes about their actions in this Event, they may raise the suspicion of the other members of the staff, gaining suspicion. As suspicion accumulates, it is more difficult for the party to accomplish their goals, as follows:
>
> - **No Suspicion:** The character can move freely.
> - **Low Suspicion:** The character is restricted in their movements. Open rooms are treated as restricted and restricted rooms are treated as forbidden.
> - **Moderate Suspicion:** As above, and all Charisma checks suffer **-2 Banes**.
> - **High Suspicion:** Guards are dispatched to escort the party out of the building, as they seem as if they are up to no good. See [[Casing the Joint]] below for details.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring Manor Rooms
>
> Access to manor rooms varies by the role the character has decided to take — rooms may be **Open**, **Restricted**, or **Forbidden.** Characters wearing the item associated with their role will not gain suspicion in a room that is open to their role unless they take a specific suspicious action that calls for a check (stealing an item, going through locked drawers, etc.).
>
> For more on accessing rooms, see [[Gameplay Details]].

### Questioning the Staff

If characters wish, they may attempt to talk to any of the other workers, though none of these NPCs are particularly eager to chat.

> [!info] Social
> #### Talking to a Fellow Gardener
>
> The Gardener — **Tavro** (Neutral Good, Ordani Human, he/him), an older gentleman with a somewhat bent back but a spry step, can be found trimming flowers in the [[Gardens]], near the foot of the eastern stairs.
>
> > One thing I'll say about the Wandrens — they know how to get plants that I've never seen anywhere else. And half of them don't even get shown out here to the normal folk.
>
> Tavro is busy, and isn't inclined to speak at length. However, any character who speaks to Tavro about the manor plants and makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 16)** or **Diplomacy (DC 16)** check can convince Tavro to share the following:
>
> - Hephiss Wandren grows exotic plants on the second story of the house, but won't let the regular gardeners up.
> - One of the vines that Hephiss grows extends down from the second story to outside of the kitchen. Someone recently tried to scale it to reach the second floor, but fell in the process. Tavro sometimes thinks about climbing it himself, but he's sure he wouldn't reach the top.
> - If asked about places to hide in the gardens, Tavro believes the best option would be the fountain away from the guardhouse, which is only lightly patrolled.
>
> - **Knowledge: Plants**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> #### Talking to a Fellow Decorator
>
> The Decorator — **Erisa Wandren** (Neutral Good, Ordani Human, she/her), a minor cousin of one of the Wandrens who keeps the name in the hopes that it will get her some social status, can be found in the [[Grand Hall]], adjusting a sconce on the mid-eastern pillar
>
> > My uncle was important in the Trading House years ago you know — almost became the head of the whole thing. Just think — I could have grown up here instead of worrying about the decor.
>
> Erisa is not particularly interested in talking to ordinary staff. However, any character who speaks to Erisa about the history of the manor and makes a successful **Society (DC 16)** or **Diplomacy (DC 16)** check can convince her to share the following:
>
> - Hephiss is insisting on showing her very mediocre art in the gallery, which is ruining the look. Hephiss apparently paints the art upstairs and works odd messages into the art — there are stairs by the staff room that the right person might be able to use to go up and see.
> - Erisa avoids the kitchen and dining room side of the manor, because she's convinced she's heard thumping sounds from beneath the floor.
> - Erisa tried to take a look at some of the books about the history of the manor in the library, but Hephiss ran her off and insisted she focus elsewhere.
>
> - **Knowledge: Politics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> #### Talking to a Fellow Kitchen Worker
>
> The Kitchen Worker — **Tizney** (Neutral Good, Ordani Fej, she/her), a would-be chef hoping to pick something up from a visiting chef, can be found in the [[Kitchen]] by the casks on the western wall.
>
> > I don't mind getting my hands dirty if it means working on one of **the** events of the season. There's always some new culinary wonder being put together, and I get to be a part of it — even if I'm spending most of my time chopping vegetables and straightening up rooms.
>
> Tizney is busy, and isn't inclined to speak at length. However, any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 16)** check can convince her to share the following:
>
> - Using the servants' corridor between the [[Kitchen]] and the [[Servants' Office]] is a good way to move through the house quickly without being bothered … if characters are interested in picking up anything unexpected along the way. This route also passes by stairs to the second level of the manor.
> - Tizney hoped to find a book in [[Library]] with more information about alchemy, but was called away before she could locate it.
>
> - **Culture: Ordani**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

### Locating Funar Cevher

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Using the Locator Rod in Marlstone Manor
>
> Within Marlstone Manor, the [[Locator Rod]] functions in Proximity Mode. If activated, the Locator Rod sends a sound to the creature that activated it, indicating one of the following states:
>
> - **Vicinity (steady tone):** Funar is in the general vicinity of this room or area. As Funar is located close to Marlstone Manor, any test of Locator Rod will at least indicate this level.
> - **Nearby (one note):** Funar is within a few rooms of this room or area.
> - **Adjacent (double note):** Funar is in a room either beside, above, or below this room or area.
> - **Here (triple note):** Funar is in this room.
>
> The description of each room in the Area Walkthrough notes what the [[Locator Rod]] indicates.

### Escorted Out

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Optional Event: Character Suspicion is Too High
>
> The following sequence of events occurs only if a member of the party with High Suspicion triggers suspicion again.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Avoiding the Guards
>
> Any character who reaches 5+ points of suspicion must immediately make a **Awareness (DC 18)** check. If the character succeeds, they hear all the signs that guards are en route. If it fails, they remain unaware.
>
> > Rhythmic footsteps echo down the hallway, and you can make out the faintest scraps of conversation: "went this way" and "pretending to be one of the staff."
>
> Any character who is aware that the guards are coming can make a successful **Stealth (DC 19)** check to evade them and sneak out of the manor without getting caught. However, if they remain in the manor, roll on the [[Guard Investigation]] table each time they enter a new room.
>
> Once a character is detained by the guards, they are removed from the manor. If they return and encounter a guard, they are immediately attacked.

#### Abyss Attunement: Escorted Out

Any character who is caught and escorted out while sneaking through Marlstone Manor advances their **Attunement: The Abyss (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.
