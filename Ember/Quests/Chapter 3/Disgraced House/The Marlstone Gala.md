# The Marlstone Gala

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Combat, Exploration, and Social Event occurs when the party travels to [[Marlstone]] following the [[Set in Motion]] Event. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Socialize with the upper crust of Ordain, including familiar faces from previous adventures and the gala's hostess, [[Hephiss Wandren]].
> - Consult [[Lyla Cevher]] and other allies throughout the evening for advice.
> - Steal Hephiss Wandren's key for [[Janix Mance]] so he can make a copy.
> - Find [[Juro Wandren]] ledger.
> - Find a way below the manor and locate [[Funar Cevher]].
>
> #### Area Walkthrough
>
> The party begins in the [[Vista: Marlstone Gala]] Vista, allowing the characters to speak with the gala attendants in the Grand Hall. Once the party decides to leave the Grand Hall, activate the [[Marlstone Manor - First Floor]] Scene, where the central gameplay of this Event transpires. A complete description of the manor and the gameplay that occurs there is detailed in the [[Marlstone Manor]] Area Walkthrough.
>
> #### New Mechanics
>
> There are two [[Set in Motion]] that you and the players should review before continuing with this Main Quest.
>
> #### Point of No Return (Chapter 3)
>
> This Event serves as a point of no return for certain Chapter 3 Main Quests. Once the characters complete this Event, they can no longer continue or complete the Events of [[Spreading Sickness]] and [[Smoldering Cinders]].

### Mechanic: Heist Points

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Heist Points
>
> If desired, you may give the party an allotment of 3 heist points, which they can use to counter any unexpected difficulties. When party members choose to use one of the heist points, you determine the skill check that is appropriate for what the party wants to do, based on how feasible it is that they could have done this planning. If the party could have conceivably done the set up during the scouting event [[Casing the Joint]], this check gains **+2 Boons**.
>
> Use the following DCs as a guideline:
>
> - DC 5: extremely plausible that the party planned ahead for this
> - DC 10: highly plausible
> - DC 15: somewhat plausible
> - DC 20: somewhat implausible
> - DC 25: highly implausible
> - DC 30: extremely implausible
>
> One member of the party is designated to roll the check. If they succeed, the situation is resolved thanks to the team's planning. If they fail, they are subject to the normal consequences of the experience.

### Access and Suspicion

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Moving Through the Manor
>
> Throughout their time in Marlstone Manor, the party may need to sneak off, whether it is to take the key to Janix Mance, return the key to Hephiss Wandren, locate the secret door to the lair beneath Marlstone Manor, find Juro Wandren's hidden ledger, or simply search for information and loot. Their difficulty in doing this depends on how much access they have been given to that section of the manor — **Open**, **Restricted**, or **Forbidden**. For more on access levels, see [[Gameplay Details]].

As the party moves through Marlstone Manor, they may trigger suspicion among the staff and guards, gaining suspicion with failed checks or bold actions as noted below.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Suspicion Levels
>
> Each suspicious act moves the party up a level of suspicion, from no suspicion at all to low to medium to high, with the following consequences:
>
> - **No Suspicion:** The party can move freely.
> - **Low Suspicion:** The party is being observed as they move around the manor. The DC of all Stealth checks raises by 1.
> - **Moderate Suspicion:** As above, and all Stealth checks suffer **-2 Banes**.
> - **High Suspicion:** As above, and all Charisma checks suffer **-2 Banes**. If suspicion is raised further from this state, characters are surrounded by guards and taken to [[Guest Bedroom 1]], which serves as an informal holding cell. They are then considered contained, and their suspicion level clears.
>
> Use the outcome boxes below to mark the party's current level of suspicion. They begin with no suspicion unless they had to sneaak into the party after Juro Wandren refused to put them on the list [[The Insider]], in which case they begin with low suspicion (mark the outcome below).

### Checking In with the Team

The party may wish to start by speaking with their allies at the party — Lyla Cevher and Juro Wandren. They can also check back in with the two over the course of the night.

> [!info] Social
> #### Lyla's Reminders
>
> Lyla is hesitant to speak with the party for too long, lest it draw too much attention to them, but is happy to check in with them briefly about the status of the plan. Her replies vary based on how far the party has gotten in the heist plan.
>
> - From the start of the evening until the party acts to advance the heist, Lyla's concern is about picking the right moment to strike.
>   > The timing of this is everything — too early or too late and you could call attention to yourselves. I trust you to find the right moment.
> - If the party has secured [[Hephiss' Keys]] but not copied them, she urges them to move quickly before Hephiss notices their absence.
>   > Quickly, get the keys to Janix Mance in the gardens. Hephiss will notice that her keys are missing sooner than later, and we need to have them copied by then.
> - If the party has the [[Copy of Hephiss' Keys]] and has not yet returned the originals to Hephiss, she offers to help return them to Hephiss if needed and warns that they must not get caught.
>   > You have a copy of the keys? Good. Let me know if you need help to get the originals back to Hephiss, though it seems like you have things well in hand.
> - If the party has not figured out where Funar is located, Lyla is convinced that he is being held underneath Marlstone Manor and suggests they find an entrance to a lower level.
>   > There have always been rumors about underground passages in Marlstone. My guess is that wherever Funar is being held, it's somewhere beneath us. Maybe there's a set of stairs somewhere that leads down there?
> - Once the party has located the door to the stairs below, Lyla promises to meet them there after excusing herself from the party.
>   > Perfect. We should be able to head down there soon. I need to make sure no one notices me sneaking away, but I will meet you there.

> [!info] Social
> #### Juro's Concerns
>
> Juro is focused on whether the party will help him get the ledger he needs from Hephiss' safe, but can share the following:
>
> - The ledger is likely in one of the restricted rooms on the floor. He's seen ropes blocking off nearby rooms in the [[Gallery]], [[Sitting Room]], and [[Grand Hall Lounge]].
> - He has been sending Hephiss artifacts from the Arctus Plateau at her requesst — often things that have been described as holy or touched by Lantyr — which he believes she may be experimenting on somewhere in the manor. A discussion of artifacts or ruins is also likely to distract her.
> - If the party is caught snooping, Hephiss won't want to make a scene — she'll probably have them stashed somewhere upstairs until the gala is over.
> - If the party doesn't bring Juro the ledger, he may sneak off and attempt to find it himself. Characters can dissuade him from this course of action with a successful **Diplomacy (DC 18)** or **Intimidation (DC 18)** check.
>   - If the check fails, Juro storms off to retrieve the ledger on his own. He is swiftly caught, and his prior conversation with the party is noted, raising the party's suspicion level by 1.

### Mixing & Mingling in Marlstone

The Gala is also attended by a distinguished list of notable guests, some of whom the party or Lyla may know and can chat with briefly.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Facilitating Conversations at the Marlstone Gala
>
> While the party is at the Marlstone Gala, they may wish to speak with some of the NPCs they have encountered in their adventures thus far. Those listed below are in attendance at the party — for each, there is a link to their actor for general information/a reminder of who they are, as well as a quote to start conversation and topics they might be willing to discuss.
>
> While all characters below are on the area map, this is a good opportunity to use the [[Vista: Marlstone Gala]], as most have portraits that can be placed in the scene and used during conversation.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Conversation with Hephiss Wandren
>
> While Hephiss is busy hosting, she projects the energy of calm and control. With a successful **Deception (DC 15)** check, the party knows that despite this, she is worried about what the guests might destroy in her house.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character notices Hephiss looking toward the front of the manor, specifically toward the [[Foyer]], [[Sitting Room]], or Gallery.
>
> If characters have visited the Gallery, read **Great Artists of the Arctus Plateau** in the Library, or have **Knowledge: Arts**, and speak to Hephiss about her artwork, they gain **+2 Boons** on all Persuasion checks with Hephiss during the course of this conversation.
>
> Hephiss doesn't have much time for the party, but she is willing to discuss the following:
>
> - **The layout and history of the house.** With a successful `[[/check history 16]]` check, the party can connect with her about architectural details and she will reveal that there's a hidden door somewhere on the first floor.
>   > I've always wanted one of those libraries where you pull a book out to reveal a secret door. Sadly, this place doesn't have that, but the secret door it does have is at least close enough that I can still smell the book binding.
> - **Her artwork.** Characters who have seen Hephiss' artwork in the [[Gallery]] and either have **Knowledge: Arts** or succeed on a `[[/check deception 16]]` check can flatter Hephiss about her artwork, getting her to confess that she has more artwork that she wasn't comfortable showing. She gives them directions and a temporary key to the [[Art Studio]], which becomes an Open room for the party for the next hour.
>   > I'm so glad you enjoyed my work. I've been working on some more complex pieces, but they didn't seem quite right for this environment. I'd be curious what you thought of them — I usually don't show my work in progress, but for a fellow artist? You can find it upstairs — it's locked up of course, but I always have a few extra keys around for those who appreciate the arts.
> - **Tinkering.** Characters who know of Hephiss' love of tinkering from visiting her Workshop can speak with her about tinkering with artifacts. Hephiss is willing to speak about this to anyone with **Knowledge: Machines** or who succeeds on a `[[/check persuasion 16]]` check, but they must first succeed on a `[[/save charisma 17]]` saving throw — on a failure, Hephiss knows the party was in her workshop and their suspicion level rises twice.
>   > Tinkering? What an interesting topic. But I must know — what made you think I was the one to talk to about it?

### Copying Hephiss' Keys

It is up to the party how they wish to take Hephiss' key, but creating a distraction will make the job easier by lowering the DC of checks and/or giving the opportunity to gain **+2 Boons**.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Creating a Distraction
>
> The party can pick whichever way they might think is best to create their distraction. The better they do at the distraction, the easier it is for the stealing the key challenge to proceed below.
>
> Distractions can be either **Minor** or **Major**. While there is no limit on how many the party can start at once, after two consecutive distractions they gain a level of suspicion with each additional distraction.
>
> #### Social Distraction
>
> Any character can create a **Minor Distraction** by making one of the following checks:
>
> - **Intimidation (DC 15)** to start a brawl.
> - **Diplomacy (DC 15)** to engage someone in a lengthy conversation.
> - **Performance (DC 15)** to stage a dramatic scene.
>
> When making any of the above checks:
>
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on the check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character creates a **Major Distraction**.
>
> #### Physical Distraction
>
> Any character can create a **Minor Distraction** by making a successful **`[[/skill sleightofhand 15]]`** or **Stealth (DC 15)** check to avoid detection. The nature of the physical distraction is up to the player and the Gamemaster's discretion.
>
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on the check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character creates a **Major Distraction**.
>
> #### Magical Distraction
>
> Any character can create a distraction by casting a spell, but will gain a level of suspicion if they are detected. Whether the spell creates a **Minor** **Distraction** or a **Major** **Distraction** is at the Gamemaster's discretion.

Once the characters feel as though the guests are properly distracted, they can attempt to steal Hephiss' key.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Stealing the Key
>
> Either Lyla or a member of the party can steal the key from Hephiss Wandren with a successful **`[[/skill sleightofhand 20]]`** check.
>
> - **Caused Major Distraction:** The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Caused Minor Distraction:** The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> If the party fails to steal the key, they may retry the check, but they gain a level of suspicion for each attempt.

Once the party has stolen Hephiss' key, they must reach Janix Mance.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Getting to Janix Mance (and Back)
>
> The party must make their way through the Area Map to Janix Mance, who is stationed in the [[Gardens]]. This is primarily an Area Map challenge, with a few wandering guards and rooms with a variety of access levels. The party can determine the best way to make it to Janix and back as suits them. Once they reach him, he will begin to copy Hephiss' Keys.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Talk with Janix
>
> Janix reveals that the key alone won't get them into the safe — they'll need to reveal the door. Hephiss isn't carrying anything else on her, so the revealing mechanism must be elsewhere in the house and can be searched for.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Returning the Key
>
> The characters can return the key to Hephiss with one of the following checks:
>
> - **Stealth (DC 15)** to place the key near Hephiss as if it had just dropped.
> - **`[[/skill sleightofhand 15]]`** to place the key in Hephiss' pocket.
> - **Deception (DC 15)** to return the key to Hephiss personally.
>
> When making any of the above checks:
>
> - **Knowledge: Crime**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Failing a check increases the character's suspicion level.

### Juro's Ledger

> [!info] Social
> #### Juro and the Ledger
>
> If the party wishes, they may attempt to retrieve Juro's ledger, which is located in Hephiss' Study on the first floor.
>
> - If the party retrieves the ledger, Juro is extremely grateful and promises to reward them at some future date. With his mind clear, he is also able to aid the party on any social checks that happen in his vicinity.
> - If the party does not retrieve the ledger before returning the key to Hephiss, Juro confronts them after this task is done, asking when they intend to retrieve it. With a **Diplomacy (DC 18)** or **Deception (DC 18)** check, characters can convince Juro that they are on their way to get it.
>   - If the check fails, Juro storms off to retrieve the ledger on his own. He is swiftly caught, and his prior conversation with the party is noted, raising the party's suspicion level by 1.

### Getting to Funar

The party likely knows where Funar is based on their recon, but there's room here to find him if they don't.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Confirming Funar's Location
>
> Funar is being held in a vault at the bottom level of the Manor, near the entrance to the Wandren hideout.
>
> The party can determine Funar's location by using the[[Locator Rod]]. The result of using the Locator Rod can be found at the top of each room in the Area Walkthrough.
>
> #### Getting into Wandren HQ
>
> To get into Wandren HQ, parties must do the following:
>
> - Discover that [[Hephiss' Downstairs Closet]] is hiding the entrance to the vault.
> - Push the button in [[Hephiss' Chambers]] to reveal the door in the closet.
> - Learn the passphrase to remove the keyhole cover. The passcode is "Ray Lanterelle," a combination of Hephiss Wandren's favorite artist's name and her favorite flower. The door itself gives hints as to the passcode, though other hints can be found around the manor.
> - Use the copy of Hephiss' key to open the door.
