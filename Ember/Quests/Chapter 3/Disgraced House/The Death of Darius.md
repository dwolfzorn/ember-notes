# The Death of Darius

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event occurs when the party chooses to investigate Threadforge, Darius Cevher's shop. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Find a way into the shop.
> - Investigate the clues left behind at the scene.
> - Free a mannequin construct pinned to the floor.

In [[Family Business]], Lyla provided the party with the most recent known information about Darius' murder, found in the form of an article in local gossip rag Overheard in Ordain — [[Overheard In Ordain: A Stitch In Crime]]. They can refer to this at any time as a reminder of the "official" investigation.

#### Work in Progress: Future Vista

In the future, this Event will take place in an Interior Vista representing Darius Cevher's destroyed shop. Currently, the Event will instead take place on the Region Map, using theater of the mind.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Exterior
>
> Threadforge has been locked since the murder of Darius Cevher, but signs of neglect are visible before the party enters the shop. Any character with a `[[/skill investigation 16 passive format=long]]` or who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check while examining the shop's exterior notices that a thick layer of fibrous dust has settled on the windows, that the posted notice is faded and brittle, and that no fresh footprints disturb the loose thread gathered on the ground outside the shop. The character can reasonably conclude that Threadforge has been undisturbed for at least two weeks — if the Veiled Chain investigated the scene, that investigation appears to have stalled.
>
> - **Knowledge: Crime**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on the check.
>
> #### Entering Threadforge
>
> The shop door is secured with a simple lock. Any character who makes a successful `[[/skill sleightofhand 16 tool=thief]]` can unlock the door. Alternatively, the door can be bashed open by any character who makes a successful **Athletics (DC 18)** check.
>
> Characters who test all of the shop windows find one partially unlatched, allowing access without a check.

Once the characters enter the shop, read or paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> It is clear that something violent happened here. Papers and spools of thread are scattered across a wooden worktable, some spattered with red droplets that are likely blood, the wooden fabric racks propped against the wall are half-askew, and in the center of the room, wearing a finely tailored jacket, a patchwork fabric mannequin is sprawled on the floor with a knife in the middle of its chest. Behind the worktable, a glowing outline on the floor of sprawled legs seems to indicate that the body was found on the far side of the shop.

### The Scene of the Crime

There are several places in the shop that the party can investigate to try to learn more about the murder.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring the Shop
>
> A simple search of the area reveals the following:
>
> 1. A crumpled piece of fabric in the center of the floor, stained with dried blood.
> 2. An outline indicating the position of Darius' body when it was found.
> 3. Bloody papers and spools of thread, strewn across Darius' worktable.
> 4. Broken wooden fabric racks leaning against the wall.
> 5. A cloth mannequin on the ground.
> 6. A knife embedded in the mannequin's chest.
>
> [[Detect Magic]] reveals an aura of Transmutation magic surrounding the mannequin.
>
> #### Fabric and Bloodstains
>
> A crumpled piece of fabric lies on the floor, soaked through with dried blood. The rug beneath it is stained the same dark red-brown color.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Medicine (DC 14)** or **Awareness (DC 14)** check while examining the stained rug determines that the blood soaked into the rug over several hours. The character can reasonably conclude that Darius' body lay here for some time before it was moved.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Crime**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> #### Body Outline
>
> The outline of Darius' body is marked behind the worktable, next to an overturned chair. There is no blood inside the outline or on the surrounding rug, though the area is dusted with a pale powder. This matches the public account of the murder, which claims that Darius was found behind his desk.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** or **Deception (DC 14)** check while comparing the outline to the bloodstained rug determines that Darius was not killed where his body was found. The character can reasonably conclude that his body was moved behind the worktable after the murder.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Crime**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 14)** check while examining the powder recognizes it as residue from [[Choking Fog Dust]], a substance sometimes used by criminals and thieves to disable their victims.
>
> - **Knowledge: Alchemy**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Crime**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> #### Papers and Thread
>
> Papers and spools of thread are scattered across the worktable. Dried blood is concentrated on the near side of the table, while the far side is dusted with the same pale powder found near the body outline. Coins are strewn among the paper and threads.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check while examining the table determines that the powder spilled from one of the papers or envelopes scattered there. If the characters have already identified the [[Choking Fog Dust]], they can reasonably conclude that Darius released the powder while handling his papers.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Crime**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character can also deduce that robbery was not the killer's motive, since the coins were left behind.
>
> The party can collect the coins from the desk, gaining  **4**.
>
> #### Fabric Racks
>
> Two wooden fabric racks lean against the wall. One holds a scrap of silver fabric stained with dried blood. The other is empty and visibly broken.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Medicine (DC 14)** or **Awareness (DC 14)** check while examining the stained silver fabric determines that the fabric lay in blood for several hours before it was moved onto the rack.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character can also deduce that the broken rack was lifted and placed against the wall after the struggle.
>
> #### The Stabbed Mannequin
>
> The patchwork mannequin lies sprawled on the floor like a body, pinned in place by a long metal knife driven through its chest and into the floor below. As the party approaches, the mannequin raises one silk-and-burlap hand and clutches at the knife handle, as if trying to pull itself free.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 14)** check while examining the mannequin recognizes it as a "fashion construct," a magical dressmaker's tool typically created from fabric scraps saved over the course of the designer's career.
>
> - [[Identify]]: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Crafts**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Culture: Ordani**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> A character who identifies the mannequin knows that fashion constructs can be set to record a subject's movements for up to 30 seconds, then repeat those movements on a loop. Designers usually use this feature to study how fabric moves across a customer's body and adjust garments accordingly.
>
> #### The Knife
>
> The knife has pierced through the mannequin and into the floor, pinning the construct in place. A small circular smudge marks the otherwise clean metal handle — a fingerprint, or what remains of one.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check while examining the knife determines that the fingerprint was smudged while the knife was being held. The character can reasonably conclude that whoever left the print used the knife to pin the mannequin down after the attack.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Crime**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

The characters can attempt to remove the knife.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Removing the Knife
>
> The knife can be pulled out of the floor with a successful `[[/check athletics 15]]` check. Once the knife is removed, a foul stench rises from the construct where it was pierced.
>
> > A foul scent rises from the hole in the center of the fabric where the knife pierced it, and the edges of the hole in the fabric are devoid of all color, as if it has been sucked out of the neighboring thread.
>
> Characters can try to identify the fumes. With a successful `[[/check nature 16]]` check, **Knowledge: Alchemy**, or **Knowledge: Crime**, they know that they are the end result of [[Withering Flesh Poison]], which is designed to rot the flesh but can also disintegrate non-living objects.
>
> If a character touches the knife, they are affected by the poison and must succeed on a `[[/save constitution 16]]` saving throw or take `[[/damage necrotic 1d6]]`.

Once the knife is removed, the fabric construct immediately gets up and begins moving:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The fabric construct rises and walks to a spot behind the central table in Threadforge, which is still filled with scraps of paper and fabric. It makes an action with its hands over the table as if grasping something, then begins violently shaking and stumbling into the center of the room. Before it can take more than a few steps, it jolts forward, spins around, and jolts backwards, falling to the ground in the position it was in when you arrived. Immediately, it rises again, walks to the spot behind the central table and repeats the entire sequence.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Deconstructed Crime
>
> Once freed, the fashion construct repeats the same sequence of movements: it stands behind the worktable, moves its hands as if opening or unfolding something, shakes and stumbles toward the center of the room, then jerks violently forward and backward before collapsing. After a moment, the construct rises and repeats the sequence.
>
> Any character who has identified the mannequin as a fashion construct knows that Darius likely set it to record his movements while he worked. The construct captured his final moments, then began repeating them after the murder. The knife pinning it to the floor prevented it from completing the sequence earlier.
>
> #### Opening the Paper
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** or **`[[/skill sleightofhand 14]]`** check while comparing the construct's hand movements to the position of the papers on the worktable determines that Darius was likely opening a piece of paper or an envelope.
>
> If the party has already examined the powder-dusted papers, the character can reasonably conclude that Darius opened the paper or envelope that released the [[Choking Fog Dust]].
>
> #### Shaking and Stumbling
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Medicine (DC 14)** or **Performance (DC 14)** check while watching the construct's shaking and stumbling determines that the movement resembles a coughing fit or sudden loss of breath.
>
> If the party has already identified the [[Choking Fog Dust]], the character can reasonably conclude that the powder caused these symptoms.
>
> #### The Stabbing
>
> The construct's violent forward-and-backward jolts are plainly caused by the stabbing itself. The motion indicates that Darius was stabbed twice: first in the back, then in the chest.

#### Mayis Attunement: Freeing the Mannequin

Any character who removes the knife from the mannequin advances their **Attunement: Mayis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Signara Attunement: Thorough Investigation

Any character who successfully identifies a clue advances their **Attunement: Signara (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.
