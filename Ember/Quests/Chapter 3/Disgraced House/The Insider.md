# The Insider

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration and Social Event occurs when the party travels to [[The Hallows]] following the [[Veiled Threats]] Event. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Win Juro's trust through a challenge of stealth, persuasion, or political skill.
> - Decide whether or not to recover an item from Marlstone Manor at Juro's request.
>
> This Event is depicted using the "Hallows" Level of [[Vista: The Hallows]] Vista.

### A Meeting With Juro

The party has seen Juro during his visit to Lyla's office, but may have also have met him in the past, depending on their choices in Chapter 1. If they completed [[A Smuggler Extraordinaire]] and met him during the event, he greets them warmly:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Juro smiles as you approach with a look of recognition.
>
> > Lyla's friends, of course. I'm glad you made it safely to Ordain. Something tells me you're looking for another favor, but Lyla's note wasn't fully clear. What's going on?

If he has only met them in this quest, he is kind, if more distant:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Juro looks at you appraisingly as you approach.
>
> > From the descriptions that she gave, you must be Lyla's companions. Her note wasn't fully clear about what you need from me. What's going on?

> [!info] Social
> #### Sharing the Plan
>
> The party's goal, as set by Lyla, should be to get Juro to add them to the guest list of the Marlstone Gala. Once they make that ask, he conditionally agrees to the plan if they meet his demands in [[The Insider]].
>
> With a successful `[[/check insight 16]]` check, characters sense that Juro is more amused by their questions than anything, but there is some worry below the surface. They also know that his affection for Lyla will keep him from betraying her.
>
> - Juro is visiting Ordain on the request of Hephiss Wandren, who has asked several questions about his meetings with Lyla before she arrived in the city.
>   - If the party did not complete [[A Smuggler Extraordinaire]]: He and Lyla briefly crossed paths in Nain.
> - The Marlstone Gala is a big deal — a place to see and be seen — but also a bit boring. Juro would love something exciting to happen there.
> - Juro and Lyla have a complicated relationship, but they go back for years and he'll always have her back. He doesn't elaborate on what happened in their past, focusing instead on the here and now.
> - Lyla's heist plan sounds like fun, and adding to the party to the guest list should be easy enough. Usually Hephiss approves of each guest to the gala, but she's been distracted recently.
> - If asked, Juro's worries have to do with recent changes in House Wandren. He doesn't want to speak much about it, but with a successful `[[/check persuasion 16]]` check, will reveal that a lot of Wandren seems on edge and most of the associates he would normally ask are out of town with no sign of returning soon.
>
> If the party does not ask Juro about being added to the guest list, or you want to cut the conversation short, he can mention it unprompted.
>
> > Is that everything? Lyla's note wasn't completely clear, but it seemed like it might have something to do with the Marlstone Gala.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What Brings Juro to Ordain — ([[A Smuggler Extraordinaire]]) Completed
>
> **A:**
>
> > Hephiss Wandren suddenly requested my presence not long after you all left me in Nain. She claims she's taking an interest in the next generation of House Wandren, I know a fishing expedition when I see one — something Lyla found during her travels has got Hephiss more than a little on edge.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What Brings Juro to Ordain — ([[A Smuggler Extraordinaire]]) Not Completed
>
> **A:**
>
> > Ah, Lyla told you I'm not a big fan of the big city? I don't mind it too much, but I prefer the Plateau, where no one's keeping much of an eye on what I'm doing. The only reason I'm even in Ordain is that Hephiss Wandren suddenly requested my presence after I crossed paths with Lyla — she was tracking down information on the Helkas bandits in Nain and I gave her a hand with some very official-looking papers. I'll say this, Hephiss asked a lot more questions about Lyla than my business dealings. I'm not sure why, but I think she's more than a little spooked.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Marlstone Gala
>
> **A:**
>
> > The Marlstone Gala is basically **the** event of the year in Ordain if you care about fashion and power. Or at least that's what people say. There are plenty of stories about deals beinng made and people making themselves known, but to be honest, nothing really big has happened there for years. The fashion is great, but every year I hope there's a screaming match or a construct gets loose or someone throws a drink.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Relationship with Lyla
>
> **A:**
>
> > Lyla and I go way back, but I'll let her tell those stories. Though she probably won't give me enough credit. Sure, I was a bit of a fool in my younger years, but honestly — break a few rules and some folks never look at you the same way again. I'll say this, though — then and now, I've always got her back. Always.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Lyla's Heist Plan
>
> **A:**
>
> > Lyla always was good at coming up with something. The plan seems like it'll work — as long as I don't tell Hephiss, that is. Don't worry, the secret's safe with me. I'd choose Lyla over most folks in the city, and with Hephiss as the other option … she's not exactly on my good side at the moment. As for adding your names, that's easy enough. We'll just call you the Heroes of Helkas. No one knows exactly what happened there, so no one will ask any questions, and Hephiss is too distracted to care.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Relationship with Hephiss Wandren
>
> **A:**
>
> > Hephiss and I have generally gotten along in the past, but all these questions she has about what I've done and what I know about Lyla? I don't like it. Not to mention —
>
> Juro appears to consider saying more, but stops himself.
>
> > Meet my challenge and I'll tell you all about it.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Juro's Worries
>
> **A:**
>
> > Folks in House Wandren have a tendency to come and go a bit, but right now more are gone than most. Something about it seems wrong. I wonder if it has anything to do with the way Hephiss has been acting … but we can talk more about that after I make sure you're up to snuff.

### Juro's Challenge

Juro is willing to work with the party, if they can prove that they are actually up to the task.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Juro smiles and gives you a single sharp nod.
>
> > Before I put you on the guest list, I want to make sure you'll be able to pull this off. After all, if things go wrong, my name's going to be on the line. I'll even give you two options — heist something very small or show me you can fit in among the high society types.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Smallest Heist
>
> Juro explains what he wants done.
>
> > A small heist. Of course, this should be easy for you. Much easier than what you have planned for the gala. All I want is for you to get me a free piece of fruit from that stand right there. Up to you how you do it, I only care that you do it.
>
> The characters can acquire the fruit in the following ways:
>
> - **Steal.** Any character who makes a successful **`[[/skill sleightofhand 17]]`** check can steal a piece of fruit.
>
> - **Knowledge: Crime**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> - **Persuade.** Any character who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 17)** or **Intimidation (DC 17)** check can convince the fruit dealer that a particular piece of fruit isn't fit for sale.
>
> - **Knowledge: Trade**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> - **Deceive.** Any character who buys a piece of fruit and makes a successful `[[/check deception 16]]` check can convince Juro that they stole it or otherwise swindled the dealer.
>
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Standing Out in the Crowd
>
> Juro explains the challenge, which uses three movable NPCs within the scene: Hallows Passersby A, B, and C.
>
> > Fitting in the crowd can be a hard thing, but it helps if you know who should be in the crowd to begin with. See that gentlemen in the green wrap eating a handheld bun, the spellcaster with the staff in the red robes, and the woman in gray looking at the vases? One of them will absolutely be at the gala, and other two would never make it past the door. Tell me who gets in and the challenge is won.
>
> The party must observe each passerby and deduce whether or not they would likely be invited to the gala. If the party is ever stuck, they can convince Juro to give them a hint with a successful `[[/check persuasion 15]]` check.
>
> #### The Gentleman in Green
>
> Juro's hint:
>
> > Remember, at the gala, it all starts with the outfit.
>
> Any character who examines the gentleman's cloak and makes a successful `[[/check investigation 15]]` check can identify its design as Arcturian, not Ordani, suggesting that the gentleman is a visitor from the Arctus Plateau. As such, it is unlikely that he will be invited to the gala.
>
> - **Path: Cosmopolitan Fashionista**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Crafts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Culture: Ordani**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character also spots small flecks of dirt and plant matter on the edges of the cloak, suggesting recent travel.
>
> #### The Gesturing Wizard
>
> Juro's hint:
>
> > Some outfits are less about how they look than what they represent.
>
> Any character who examines the spellcaster's staff and makes a successful **Arcana (DC 15)** or **Arcana (DC 15)** check recognize it as belonging to the [[Cindaric Sages]]. A member of this order would be unlikely to attend a high society gala.
>
> - **Path: Cindaric Initiate**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Culture: Ordani**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character can also deduce that this person is a lowly initiate of the order, based on the poor condition of the staff.
>
> #### The Woman by the Pottery Stand
>
> Juro's hint:
>
> > The clothing is important, but it's not just the look — it's also the materials. Any ideas what hers might be whispering?
>
> Any character who examines the woman's clothing and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 15)** check can tell that, though understated, she is wearing the latest in Ordani fashion, and that her accessories are made of high-end silk and leather. The character can reasonably conclude that, out of the three passersby, this woman would be the most likely to attend a gala.
>
> - **Path: Cosmopolitan Fashionista**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Crafts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Culture: Ordani**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character also spots an ornate embossment on the woman's leather belt: a symbol of House Bastilla.

The party only needs to complete one of Juro's challenges in order to move forward. Once they do, he agrees to participate in the heist if they promise to help him with one small favor ([[The Insider]]).

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Juro smiles and nods at you, looking genuinely pleased at your success
>
> > I had a feeling you could do it. Consider yourselves invited to the gala. That is, if you me a favor.

#### Primordis Attunement: Fruit Stolen

Any character who steals the fruit for Juro advances their **Attunement: Primordis (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Luxarum Attunement: Shopkeeper Convinced

Any character who convinces the fruit dealer to give them a piece of fruit advances their **Attunement: Luxarum (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

#### Aura Attunement: Juro Fooled

Any character who deceives Juro advances their **Attunement: Aura (+1)** at the conclusion of the Event.

`[[/outcome recruited]]`

If the party fails one challenge, they may attempt the other. If they fail both challenges, Juro won't add them to the list … unless they promise to do him a favor, without telling Lyla ([[The Insider]]).

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Juro shakes his head wryly.
>
> > This isn't the Arctus Plateau anymore — you have to be a lot more subtle here in Ordain. And if I vouch for you, it'll be me that Hephiss comes for if you get caught. I'm sure you can find your own way into the gala, and I promise if you do, I won't blow your cover. Unless, that is, you'd be up for doing me a favor?

### A Final Request

> [!info] Social
> #### Juro's Ledger
>
> Whether or not he trusts the party, Juro has one last ask — there is a ledger of his activities that Hephiss Wandren is "holding" for him and he wants it back, especially if Hephiss might end up being implicated in some crime.
>
> > If I'm going to put you on this guest list, I need something from you in return. Hephiss took my ledger from me when I got into town — it has all of my contacts and records in it, including some that the Hallows would probably frown at. She says it's for safekeeping, but I think it's to keep me in line. I need you to find where it is, take it, and bring it back to me. And don't tell Lyla, please. I don't want her worrying about it — or me.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 15)** check can confirm that he appears to be telling the truth, though he may be underplaying the value of what is in the ledger.
>
> Juro will not put the party on the guest list without this promise, but they can lie to him about their intentions with a successful **Deception (DC 16)** check.
>
> - **Passed Test:** The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check if they passed one of Juro's tests.

If the party refuses to help Juro, or is unsuccessful in lying to him, he refuses to help, though he promises not to expose them if they make their own way to the gala.

`[[/outcome refused]]`
