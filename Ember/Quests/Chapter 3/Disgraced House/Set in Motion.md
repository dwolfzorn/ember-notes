# Set in Motion

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social Event occurs when the party returns to [[Lyla Cevher]] following the preparations undergone in the [[To Copy a Key]], [[Revealed by Lantern]], [[The Insider]], and [[Casing the Joint]] Events. In this Event, the characters can:
>
> - Confer with their allies about the plan and make final preparations.
>
> This Event is depicted using the "House Cevher HQ" Level of the [[Vista: Ordain Interiors]] Vista.
>
> #### New Mechanics
>
> There are two [[Set in Motion]] that you and the players should review before continuing with this Main Quest.

### Final Preparations

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Lyla surveys the team, looking every bit the self-assured noble in her fancier attire.
>
> > As Janix mentioned, he's going in through the path you all found through Lantern Roads. But that's only the beginning of the plan. Does everyone know what they need to do? I'm happy to go over it one last time.

> [!info] Social
> #### Reviewing the Plan
>
> While Lyla is confident in the team (as confirmed by a successful **`[[/skill 16 insight]]`** check), she will review any of the following steps of the plan:
>
> - Janix Mance, using the path identified and tested by the party in [[Revealed by Lantern]], will make his way to the outskirts of Marlstone Manor and find a way to hide among the gardens. Parties may have found some suitable spots for him to hide while checking out the manor in [[Casing the Joint]]. If not, they must improvise.
> - Lyla and Juro will enter the gala on their own and mingle with the guests, looking for ways to potentially distract Hephiss Wandren.
> - The party will arrive to the gala, either through Juro adding them to the guest list ([[The Insider]]) or by using the same path as Janix Mance and sneaking into the party once on site ([[The Insider]]).
>   - If the party was not able to secure Juro's help in getting into the party, they may choose to wear disguises or simply hide among the crowd. If they choose to wear a disguise, they should put it on now.
> - Once the group is all in place, with Janix Mance outside and everyone else at the gala, the group must get the keys from Hephiss, bring them to Janix to be copied, return them to Hephiss, and find the exact location where Funar is being held so that he can be released.
>   - Depending on how extensively the party searched the manor during [[Casing the Joint]], they may or may not already have a route planned or know where Funar is being held.
> - If asked, Lyla is feeling confident. She's fairly sure that everything won't quite go to plan, but she trusts that this group will be able to handle whatever comes next.

In addition to the general planning, characters can briefly chat with the other members of the heist team about their overall feelings and impressions of the heist.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Chat with Juro
>
> Juro appears confident, but a successful `[[/check insight 16]]` check reveals some nerves. He desperately wants to retrieve the ledger that Hephiss Wandren has stored somewhere in the manor and is worried that if it isn't found tonight, it never will be. He'll also share the following:
>
> - He will go along with Lyla's plan as long as it is working, but if things go wrong, he has his own plan to get the ledger — he has brought some [[Choking Fog Dust]] with him, which he can release to try to clear the ballroom, creating enough confusion for the guests to scatter.
> - Juro doesn't want to be on Hephiss' bad side, or have the party shut down and guards remove everyone from the property, but if he has to, he'll prioritize what he needs. He suggests that the party get him the ledger to avoid him accidentally messing with their plans.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Chat with Janix
>
> Janix is excited to get going and get home safely, and prefers to focus on talking about things related to the mission.
>
> - Janix has the mold ready to go. While it may look like nothing more than a jar of clay to others, he thinks he can find other uses for it, to create copies of magical items that work almost as good as the original.
> - Janix is thrilled not to set foot in the gala, and does not intend to go inside no matter what happens.
> - If Janix is caught (which he doesn't think he will be), he promises not to rat out the group — he'll claim he wanted to crash the party to see how the other half lives.

### New Heist Mechanics

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Mechanic: We Already Thought of That
>
> At the beginning of the heist, the party receives 3 total heist points, which they can only spend during the `[[/eventstate otherhood2Gala]]` Event. If something during the Event doesn't go to plan, the party spends one of these points to explain how the obstacle has been bypassed by something that they planned for ahead of time.
>
> Let party members know that they will have three opportunities to call on their previous planning as the heist unfolds, with a single party member rolling a check against the feasibility that they could have actually made this plan ahead of time.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Mechanic: Suspicion Levels
>
> As the party moves through Marlstone Manor, they may trigger suspicion among the staff and guards, gaining suspicion with individual actions. As suspicion accumulates, guards may become harder to persuade, doors may be locked where they were previously open, and Stealth checks may need to be done under a watchful eye. During the Event, you will have outcome boxes that you can use as an internal reminder of the party's current level of suspicion and its consequences.
