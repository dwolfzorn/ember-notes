# Crowds in Palisade Cross

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This social event tasks the party with a trip to the [[Palisade Cross]] district, where they must confront the as-yet-unidentified agitators that have been sewing civil unrest among the locals. Once they meet the agitators in question, the party members must decide how they want to proceed. In this event, the characters can:
>
> - Eavesdrop on the agitators to glean more information about their motives.
> - Either help the agitators or attack them, earning their trust or forcing a confession in the process.

### Scanning the Crowd

The party will likely want to examine the crowd, and see if they can spot anything of note. The detained people will be the most immediate thing, but the agitators are also in plain view if they look more closely.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Refugees and Rebels
>
> Characters making a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check notice that several people in the crowds carrying fliers matching the style of those previously seen, but the source of them isn't immediately obvious.
>
> - **Result of 16+** Tracing the fliers as they move through the crowd, and seeing where they are missing in the hands of others, you're able to locate their source: A pair of unassuming citizens stopping people to talk to them briefly before handing out fliers taken from a satchel slung across their respective chests.

The agitators in questions are:

**Drayk (Chaotic Neutral, Ordani Human, he/him)**

Drayk's a short, stocky fellow missing an arm, with the empty sleeve pinned to his side. He has the look of a grizzled old sailor, permanently weathered by the sun and wind. His clothing is clean but plain.

**Jahash (Neutral Evil, Arcturian Kivahr, he/him)**

Jahash's tall, burly, and well past his prime. Long gray hair frames a weathered face and crooked nose that's been broken at least once. He moves with an obvious limp, and a rounded, puffy ear all but confirms this old fellow was a brawler of some sort once.

### Speaking With the Agitators

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Observing the Agitators
>
> To successfully spy on the agitators, a successful group **Stealth (DC 12)** check is necessary. The ample crowds and animals make this relatively simple, and as long as the party doesn't do anything to draw attention to themselves, they'll be able to trail the agitators.
>
> Watching them allows a few things to become clear:
>
> - Drake and Jahash never cross paths while handing out fliers, intentionally avoiding each other to cover more ground. Since they are actively talking to people in the crowd, approaching them directly would not be strange.
> - Jahash's bad leg means he doesn't move too much from his spot, and only wanders periodically.
> - Neither of them appear to be armed or armored in any obvious way, nor do they appear to be spellcasters. Given their age and injuries, it appears neither of them would be able to mount much of a defense if they were forced to fight.
> - While they are wary of their surroundings, and watching the crowds, they are watching for authorities, not random civilians in the crowd. It might be relatively simple to pickpocket them.
> - Every now and then Drayk wanders over to chat with Jahash. These meetings are always brief, lasting only a minute or two, and represent the best chance the party has to ambush the both them.

To get close to the agitators without drawing their attention, the party will need to exercise a bit of caution and deception.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Playing the Part
>
> A successful **Deception (DC 14)** or **Performance (DC 14)** allows a character to appear like any other member of the crowd, acting agitated and desperate. Doing this allows them to move toward the agitators without drawing attention.
>
> Alternatively, a successful **Diplomacy (DC 16)** check allows the party to directly approach the agitators and convince them that the party is curious about the movement and interested in knowing more.

Once the agitators have been contacted, they can be spoken with to gain insight into the movement, and what's going on in Palisade Cross.

> [!info] Social
> #### Talking Politics
>
> Interacting with either of the Agitators provides this information directly:
>
> - Drayk and Jahash are members of a growing movement of Arcturian and Ordani citizens that have grown tired with the elitism and detachment Ordain shows to its own people inside and outside its walls.
> - They are handing out a new flyer. This flyer proclaims:
>
> > THE CITY DOESN'T WANT YOU HERE
>
> It goes on to detail that the Ordinate, Hallows, and Trading Houses don't have an interest in helping the refugees. They won't ever dedicate enough resources to help everyone, and now that they aren't producing food and livestock in the flats, they are even less useful to the city.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Intrigue** or **Knowledge: Politics** recognize this is an inflammatory half-truth that can be easily defended with little effort. For a group of stressed, distracted refugees, any nuance is likely to be lost.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Isn't this misleading and inflammatory?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Hardly. This city's flooded with Arcturians that got nowhere to go, nothing to their name, and the city's got nothing to offer them. All they're gonna do it bring the banditry, disease and monsters to us.
> >
> > If the Trading Houses and Cindarics can't be bothered to look after us in the city, what makes you think they'll be bothered to help all these new outsiders?
> >
> > Even the Burnished Hand here are arresting locals and detaining refugees rather than dealing with the problems outside the city.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Is there some larger action planned?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Yes, actually! Right now we're just getting people informed and interested in the movement. A greater collective action is in the works, and when that time comes, we'll be able to get the people together and really do something.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Is there some way we can help the movement?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Yeah, you can distract the Burnished Hand nearby, and give the detained people a chance to escape.
> >
> > It's a load of crap they even got held, anyway. There was a disagreement between a couple refugees and some locals, and it blew up into an argument. Some of those bronze bullies were coming by, and decided to intervene.
> >
> > Problem is, they detained everybody, and are threatening to drag them off to the Hallows. There's no reason for any of that, but they aren't listening to reason.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why would they do that?
>
> **A:**
>
> > They are probably trying to make themselves into the city's guard force, if I had to bet. Rather than just being a band of monster hunters. Why else would they be getting into everyone's business and acting like they wield the law?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Have you tried talking to them?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Sure! They told me to take a hike unless I wanted to be lumped in with the rest of them.

### Helping the Agitators

Characters wanting to help the people being detained will need to speak with the Burnished Hand officer.

**Kabiro Klark (Neutral Good, Ordani Human, he/him)**

The burly, steely-eyed Burnished Hand officer is straight-backed and severe. The is a career monster hunter, practically having grown up in the Burnished Hand. He is loyal to them to a fault, and considers his duty to the Burnished Hand and the Arctus Plateau a calling.

> [!info] Social
> #### Reading Kabiro
>
> A successful **Deception (DC 14)** check allows a character to get a general read on Kabiro's personality. Generally speaking he's a career military man, or as close as a Burnished Hand warrior gets to being one. He takes himself, his position, and his duties incredibly seriously.
>
> - **Result of 16+** He comes off as humorless, and stubborn, but seems to mean well.
>
> #### Idle Hands
>
> A successful **Deception (DC 14)** while watching the rest of the Burnished Hand warriors allows a character to notice that with the chaos of the crowds going on around them, their attention is split and scattered. It would be relatively easy to distract them.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Crime** or **Knowledge: Intrigue** have advantage on the above check and also know that they could easily rile up the crowds to draw more attention, giving the detainees a chance to scatter.

#### Mayis Attunement: Helping the Agitators

If the party chooses to help the agitators, each character advances their **Attunement: Mayis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

> [!info] Social
> #### Riling the Crowd
>
> A successful **Diplomacy (DC 15)**, **Deception (DC 14)** or **Intimidation (DC 16)** check can be used to incite the crowd to action. Characters with **Knowledge: Crime** or **Knowledge: Intrigue** have advantage on this check.
>
> Depending on what the party chooses to do, and how they direct the crowd, this can have several different outcomes:
>
> - Directing the crowd's ire at the Burnished Hand for detaining people will result in violence. Stones and punches may be thrown, and the armored warriors will be forced to defend themselves. The ensuing skirmish with the angered crowd will result in them retreating from the area.
> - Directing the crowd's ire or attention toward the Hayren Arms (such as claiming they are offering first-come-first-served rooms) causes the crowd to rush the hotel, practically bowling over the Burnished Hand in the process, forcing them to redirect their attention, allowing the detainees to escape.
> - Inciting the refugees and locals to fight causes the Burnished Hand to try and break up the scuffles, creating a diversion necessary for the detainees to dissolve into the crowds, though some of them may decide to join the brawl.

#### Orbis Attunement: Riling the Crowd

If the party chooses to rile up the crowd, each character advances their **Attunement: Orbis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Trampling Hazard
>
> If the party incites the crowd to violence, they will need to make a successful **Athletics (DC 14)**, **Athletics (DC 14)**, or **Intimidation (DC 14)** check to avoid being shoved around, attacked, or trampled by the crowd. Failure results in `[[/damage 1d6 bludgeoning]]` damage, while failure by 10 or more results in `[[/damage 1d8 bludgeoning]]` damage.

### Reporting Success

Returning to either Drayk or Jahash the party can report on their success.

If the party previously completed `[[/eventState chain2Foxlairs]]` by assisting the agitators there, then they say this:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > I was watchin' from here, glad to see those bronze-headed thugs don't get to push locals around anymore. Anyway, thanks for the help.
> >
> > Now, I'm pretty sure that I've heard about you. You helped out some refugees in Foxlairs, and stuck it to some bastard nobles trying to throw their weight around. Or at least that's what I heard. I respect that!
> >
> > So here's the deal: We're gathering like-minded folk, potential allies, and supporters in the Verocorte estate in a few days. Come after midnight, we'll have three lanterns by the side gate located on Kelekin Alley if we're meeting. Use the passphrase "I won't be subordinate." to get in.
> >
> > Be there. You're gonna like what you hear.

Otherwise, they have this to say in response:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Looking genuinely surprised at your success they shoot you a smile.
>
> > I was watchin' from here, glad to see those bronze-headed thugs don't get to push locals around anymore. Anyway, thanks for the help.
> >
> > Now look, I don't meant to volunteer you or anything, but there are people like us all over the city that need help. Foxlairs for example? Whole lotta refugees needing somewhere to put their animals, and local merchants don't wanna let them. You might be able to unjam things there. Just saying.
> >
> > Anyhow, I'd love to stick around, but I need to be on my way. Thanks again for the help here.

### Attacking the Agitators

The party may choose the direct approach, here are the relevant skill checks and details for such an event:

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Easily Approached
>
> A party looking to ambush either Drayk or Jahash can do so easily. They do not put up much of a fight, and can be subdued without combat. They aren't looking to die for their cause (not yet at least), aren't armed or armored, and are not expecting a fight. They quickly surrender when faced with violence, especially where there's a risk they'll die.
>
> #### Deniable Assets
>
> Sura and Larz have been hired for this work, but aren't connected to the rest of the plot in any significant way.

Questioning Sura and Larz yields the following information:

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who are you working for?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Brynna Verocorrt, a rich noble who lives in Fallbrook. She wants agitate the locals against the Ordinate, and bring about social change.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What is the next step?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We're going to start handing out fliers for a rally in Fallbrook, gather up everybody, and get them to agree to join a new city-wide reform movement!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** When is this meeting?
>
> **A:**
>
> > In a few days, after sundown.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Where will the meeting be held?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The old Bronze Rask threater, in Fallbrook. They are hosting it there.
> >
> > The password to get in is: "I Won't Be Subordinate" given at the entrance.

#### Ragen Attunement: Attacking the Agitators

If the party chooses to attack the agitators, each character advances their **Attunement: Ragen (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Looting the Agitators

If the party kills Drayk and/or Jahash, picks their pockets, or robs them they can find the following items of interest:

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Robbing the Agitators
>
> A successful **`[[/skill sleight 16]]`** check is necessary to lift items from the bags of either Drayk or Jahash. Failure by 5 or more results in the targeted agitator becoming wary of the party, and going to meet with their counterpart to warn them.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Shared Inventory
>
> These items are carried by both Drayk and Jahash.
>
> #### Planning Letter
>
> This letter is about the upcoming meeting:
>
> > We're still putting together the details, but if anyone asks you: yes we're planning a proper rally. There's going to be a meeting at the Bronze Rask theater in Fallbrook, organized and paid for by Madame Verocorte. Expect that in the next week or so.
> >
> > We'll start gathering people up soon.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Intrigue** or **Knowledge: Politics** might suspect that a noble or merchant of means and influence is backing this movement. Why is a mystery, but political intrigue and ideological fights are not uncommon in the halls of power.
>
> #### Foxlair Note
>
> > I want you two to hand out fliers in Palisade Cross, around the inns and apartments. Start with Rylo's Rooms. From what we've heard, it's choked with refugees from out of town, and is a good place to hand out fliers and build interest in the movement. There's almost certainly going to be tensions there between locals, visiting merchants, and refugees, which will help our messaging.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Intrigue** or **Knowledge: Politics** recognize that this movement is very smartly tapping into displaced, desperate, and unhappy refugees that aren't receiving proper support from the city.
>
> #### Spare Fliers
>
> Their belongings include some spare fliers. All of them have the same leading line in bold font:
>

>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Intrigue** or **Knowledge: Politics** recognize this is an inflammatory half-truth that can be easily defended with little effort. For a group of stressed, distracted refugees, any nuance is likely to be lost.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> This event is completed when the party either collects evidence from the pair of agitators here, or earns their trust by helping them out with the Burnished Hand. Once they have done so, they can go to the other district to investigate that location.
>
> Parties that looted materials directly from the agitators can skip investigating both locations and move to the Midnight Meeting event, as they have enough material and information to find it.
