# Looking Into Lilifeld Station

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This exploration event sees the party visiting the Lilifeld Granary, a landmark and key location found in the Lilifeld Stations district. Here, the party can interact with the locals, meet some key figures, and examine the facilities. They can also:
>
> - Meet a visiting dignitary from Redrak Fields: Eamon Mariflor
> - Investigate the newly upgraded and warded grain silos meant to keep the city's food safe.
> - Discover any bouts of illness in the area, gaining additional insights and leads.
>
> #### Daytime Hours
>
> Note that this event is intended to take place between dawn and dusk, and will only appear during that period. This event will not occur at night.

### Meeting Serethus

If the party arrives the morning after [[Ill Tidings]] then Serethus is waiting. Use the following readaloud. If the party arrives midday or later, adjust his greeting for the time of day.

If the party arrives the same day as Ill Tidings, or investigates another location first, then Serethus is not present, and the event continues without the readaloud. In that case, his part in any of the events the follow should also be ignored.

> [!abstract] Serethus
> **[[Serethus]]**
>
> Level 12 (Boss) · Cor'ak Sorcerer
>
> ![Serethus](../../../assets/image127.webp)
>
> Tall and imposing, with a strong, bulky frame typical of the Cor'ak, this man's brown and tan are accented by splashes of vibrant purple. Long, purple horns that frame his head in a regal yet intimidating manner. His teal eyes are striking and seem to pierce through those he engages with. His robes are bright and vibrant, in Ordani fashion, and draped with glittering gems of all shapes and sizes.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you approach the center of the station's chaos, you spot Serethus coming your way.
>
> > Good morning!
> >
> > Pleasure to see you again. I see the foreman is already engaged, and the stations are as busy as ever. Let's not waste any time, and get our investigation started!
>
> He immediately walks off toward the group, clearly expecting you to follow.

### Inspecting the Silos

Tadrick invites the party to join the inspection tour of the granary. He’s certain that everything is safe and secure. Read the following aloud when the party moves on to inspect them:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The empty silo is cavernous and resonant, the great, rounded walls reflecting every noise made. Evenly spaced inside, and rising all the way to the darkened ceiling, is a series of carefully carved magical sigils, each one faintly glowing with magic.
>
> Nearby, Tadrick explains:
>
> > House Lilifeld just completed an extensive upgrade on all the silos here in the granary. This includes improved seals and transfer chutes, as well as specially designed wards that actively protect and preserve the stored food. It was extremely expensive, and we only just finished getting the last of the main silos upgraded.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why upgrade the silos?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Purely out of an abundance of caution. With Golden Flats’ supply dropping due to regional troubles there, Redrak Fields is taking on more of the production burden. This puts more pressure on a single source of our food.
>
> Todrick gestures toward the silo.
>
> > So, while the expense is great, keeping the stores safe means we have one less weak link in an already taxed logistics chain.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What do the wards do?
>
> **A:**
>
> Tadrick holds up two fingers.
>
> > Two things: they passively purify any grain flowing into them and, once sealed, they produce a preserving field that lets us keep the food longer, reducing spoilage. They were specially designed for House Lilifeld
>
> Eamon says:
>
> > You can bet I’ll try to convince your House to part with those designs; we could use the same wards on our silos.
>
> Tadrick offers a friendly chuckle and nod in response, saying:
>
> > I think you'll have an easy time of that; we need to make sure the supply lines are secure from origin to destination.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Anyone been sick recently?
>
> **A:**
>
> Todrick nods, crossing his arms.
>
> > Yes, actually. I had to send a few people to the healing house in Lower Ashvale. They were vomiting and had fevers.
>
> Del pipes up:
>
> > Were they anywhere near the food stores?
>
> Todrick shakes his head sharply.
>
> > Gods, no! I run a tight operation here. We don’t let anyone who is sick go anywhere near the food stores or handle any transfers. Not worth risking it. We’ve also been watching for anyone else not feeling well, but that initial wave of illness seemed to be it.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Silos
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Crafts** or **Knowledge: Machines** or making a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check knows that the silos have been expertly built, and are exceptionally sturdy. The locks on the seals are also precisely built and fitted so that nothing unwanted can get in.
>
> #### Examining the Wards
>
> Studying the wards requires a successful **Arcana (DC 16)** and reveals that the wards were expertly made, powerful, and narrowly focused on two tasks: purifying the contents of the silo, and halting decay of any organic matter inside.
>
> - **Result of 18+** The magic at play appears to be a clever mixing of magical elements typically found in spells like purify food and water and gentle repose. Together they would ensure that everything stored inside remains safe.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Investigation Takeaways
>
> The party should conclude that the grain is not contaminated, and not at any risk once stored. Furthermore, any tainted grains going into the silos would be purified and made safe.
>
> They should also walk away with the following info:
>
> - The party can follow up on investigations at Lower Ashvale to speak with the sick.

### Parting Ways

Once the tour is complete, the group begins to break up.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As the tour comes to an end Eamon turns to Tadrick:
>
> > This has been enlightening, Tadrick, thank you for your time! If you're available later, I'd very much like to talk to you in depth about the wards you're using.
>
> Tadrick smiles brightly, nodding back.
>
> > Of course, I would be happy to. Unfortunately it can't be today, I've got a fair bit to tend to before my day is over. Perhaps tomorrow?
>
> Eamon seems agreeable, and offers his hand, which Tadrick takes straight away.
>
> > Tomorrow, then. For now, good day, all!
>
> With that, Eamon and Del offer you farewells and take their leave. Tadrick looks to you and asks:
>
> > Was there anything else you needed for your investigation? If not, I will be on my way.

If the party has any questions they want to ask, they have a chance now. Refer to [[Looking Into Lilifeld Station]] for possible questions and answers.

### Serethus' Distraction

If Serethus is present, he will take this time to delay the party by trying to get them wrapped up in busy work.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Serethus speaks up:
>
> > Yes, actually, they want to look through all your intake and storage records for the last six seasons. The warding efforts are all well and good, but there are people sick and dying in Ordain, and if it's possible someone's tampered with your grain, they need to find it.
>
> Tadrick winces at the prospect.
>
> > That's going to be a lot of records, it'll take hours to pore through.
>
> Serethus gently clasps his hands together.
>
> > And the Chain is dedicated to doing whatever's necessary to keep the people of Ordain safe, and I'm sure house Lillifeld will do the same.
>
> Tadrick deflates slightly and nods.
>
> > Yes of course, I'll get you the necessary access…

The party will be taken to the granary offices and given access to hundreds of files, scrolls, folders, and log books that cover the steady inflow and outflow of food from the granary stations. This work will take hours to complete, and perhaps predictably, Serethus won't be helping.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Are you going to help us?
>
> **A:**
>
> Serethus offers an apologetic smile.
>
> > I am sorry, friends, I simply cannot. I have classes to teach, students to tutor, clients to consult for. If only I had the time, I would help. Fortunately, the city has you to rely on. I am sure you'll find the cause of this trouble!

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Why do this?
>
> Serethus wants to waste the party's time so that he has time to muddy the investigation further. Additionally, the longer they spend on this task, the more time the Mutagists have to carry out their experiments unhindered.
>
> The party doesn't have to do this task, and a wise party would recognize that it's a potential waste of time.

If the party agrees to do the work, they can, but they won't find anything useful.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Records
>
> It takes 72 hours or work to examine the records in full. Each character studying the records contributes to getting this work done. For example, a party of four that studies for an hour would reduce the work time by 4 hours to 68 hours remaining.
>
> Access to the records is provided by Tadrick, and the work can be done across multiple days if necessary. Since the records are all in order, and Redrak's agricultural production is not beset by issues, the records prove entirely uneventful.
>
> #### We're Wasting Our Time
>
> The party can stop doing this research at any time, there is no penalty for doing so, and no benefit to finishing it.

#### Akon Attunement: Met with Serethus

If the party completes the investigation at Lilifeld Station with Serethus present at the scene, each character advances their **Attunement: Akon (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

#### Aura Attunement: Missed Serethus

If the party completes the investigation at Lilifeld Station without Serethus, each character advances their **Attunement: Aura (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.
