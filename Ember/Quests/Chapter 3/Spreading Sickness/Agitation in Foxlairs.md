# Agitation in Foxlairs

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This social event tasks the party with a trip to the [[Foxlairs]] district, where they must confront the as-yet-unidentified agitators that have been sewing civil unrest among the locals. Once they meet the agitators in question, the party members must decide how they want to proceed. In this event, the characters can:
>
> - Eavesdrop on the agitators to glean more information about their motives.
> - Either help the agitators or attack them, earning their trust or forcing a confession in the process.

### Scanning the Crowd

The party will likely want to examine the crowd, and see if they can spot anything of note. The discarded flyer will be the most immediate thing, but the agitators are also in plain view if they look more closely.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Forgotten Fliers
>
> Characters with a **Awareness (DC 10, Passive)** notice a flyer resting on the ground, stained with mud (and worse). It has the same style as the other fliers previously seen. This one's headline boldly declares:
>
> > THE CITY DOESN'T WANT YOU HERE
>
> The rest of its text is too stained to make out.
>
> #### Wranglers and Refugees
>
> Characters making a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check notice that several people in the crowds carrying fliers matching the style of those previously seen, but the source of them isn't immediately obvious.
>
> - **Result of 16+** Tracing the fliers as they move through the crowd, and seeing where they are missing in the hands of others, you're able to locate their source: A pair of unassuming citizens stopping people to talk to them briefly before handing out fliers taken from a satchel slung across their respective chests.

The agitators in questions are:

**Sura (Chaotic Neutral, Ordani Human, she/her)**

Sura's a short, lean woman dressed to blend into the crowds of other Ordani. Her garb is colorful and well made, but not so eye-catching that she'd ever stand out in the crowds she's weaving through. She is wearing gloves and is effortlessly careful not to touch anyone or anything unless she has to.

**Larz (Lawful Evil, Arcturian Human, he/him)**

Larz is a tall, skinny fellow with a large nose and sunken eyes. He is deeply tanned from years spent outdoors, and has sunken eyes likely stemming from one too many sleepless nights. He is dressed similarly to the other refugees in the area, wearing rugged clothes meant for farming and travel, not leisurely urban life.

### Speaking With the Agitators

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Observing the Agitators
>
> To successfully spy on the agitators, a successful group **Stealth (DC 12)** check is necessary. The ample crowds and animals make this relatively simple, and as long as the party doesn't do anything to draw attention to themselves, they'll be able to trail the agitators.
>
> Watching them allows a few things to become clear:
>
> - Neither Sura or Larz cross paths while handing out fliers, intentionally avoiding each other to cover more ground. Since they are actively talking to people in the crowd, approaching them directly would not be strange.
> - Neither of them appear to be armed or armored in any obvious way, nor do they appear to be spellcasters.
> - While they are wary of their surroundings, and watching the crowds, they are watching for authorities, not random civilians in the crowd. It might be relatively simple to pickpocket them.
> - Every now and then they break away from the crowds to meet up and talk. These meetings are always brief, lasting only a minute or two, and represent the best chance the party has to ambush them.

To get close to the agitators without drawing their attention, the party will need to exercise a bit of caution and deception.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Playing the Part
>
> A successful **Deception (DC 14)** or **Performance (DC 14)** allows a character to appear like any other member of the crowd, acting agitated and desperate. Doing this allows them to move toward the agitators without drawing attention.
>
> Alternatively, a successful **Diplomacy (DC 16)** check allows the party to directly approach the agitators and convince them that the party is curious about the movement and interested in knowing more.

Once the agitators have been contacted, they can be spoken with to gain insight into the movement, and what's going on in Foxlairs.

> [!info] Social
> #### Talking Politics
>
> Interacting with either of the Agitators provides this information directly:
>
> - Larz and Sura are members of a growing movement of Arcturian and Ordani citizens that have grown tired with the elitism and detachment Ordain shows to its own people inside and outside its walls.
> - They are handing out a new flyer. This flyer proclaims:
>
> > THE CITY DOESN'T WANT YOU HERE
>
> It goes on to detail that the Ordinate, Hallows, and Trading Houses don't have an interest in helping the refugees. They won't ever dedicate enough resources to help everyone, and now that they aren't producing food and livestock in the flats, they are even less useful to the city.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Intrigue** or **Knowledge: Politics** recognize this is an inflammatory half-truth that can be easily defended with little effort. For a group of stressed, distracted refugees, any nuance is likely to be lost.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Isn't this misleading and inflammatory?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Not at all! What about it is either of those things? The people of the flats are here because situations outside the city got out of control: banditry, disease, monsters. But why?
> >
> > Ordain has the Trading Houses which all operate in the Golden Flats and have hundreds of thousands of gold tied up in those operations. They can't keep bandits at bay or hire mercenaries?
> >
> > Ordain has the Anachraenum and Burnished Hand they could pressure into helping protect the countryside too! Instead, one's out collecting knick-knacks for the rich, and the other's locked in a grudge match with the Cindarics.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Is there some larger action planned?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Yes, actually! Right now we're just getting people informed and interested in the movement. A greater collective action is in the works, and when that time comes, we'll be able to get the people together and really do something.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Is there some way we can help the movement?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Actually, now that you mention it, yes. There's a rich noble that bought up a bunch of the stable space to keep locals from being able to use it, and it's sitting empty! I want to do something about that. Or, more accurately, I want you to do something about it.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why would they do that?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I don't know, it seems needlessly cruel, doesn't it?
>
> They waggle the flyer in their hand.
>
> > Like the paper says: there are people in the city who don't want the refugees here. I don't think I really care why, the end effect is the same — making people suffer.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Have you tried talking to the stable boss?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I have.
> >
> > He's not going to budge on the space since it's all paid for. However, you might be more convincing than me! Go change their mind, and get those stable spots opened so that the refugees here have somewhere to put their animals.

### Helping the Agitators

> [!info] Social
> #### Talking Stable Space
>
> A successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** convinces one of the stablehands to talk about the situation here, and they point out that a wide swath of the stables has been rented out by a rich noble. They aren't at liberty to say who, but it has cut into available spaces, the remainder are going fast.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Trade** and **Knowledge: Intrigue** know that it would be hard argue with a standing reservation, especially since the stable boss could keep raising the prices with demand, knowing the reserving party would have to pay for it.
>
> - A further **Deception (DC 12)** check leads a character to suspect that whoever put the reservation for the spaces in has a lot of money to burn, and doesn't care about how much this stunt will end up costing them.

Characters may want to speak to the stable boss directly, they can request that chance via any of the stablehands.

**Nathira Jessos (Neutral Good, Ordani Human, she/her)**

The spry, gray-haired Nathira Jessos is the latest in the line of children running the Jessos Station here, and she is the one grappling with the influx of new people and animals to deal with. She might not want to admit it, but the rush is very good for business, and Nathira likes it when business is good.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Nathira turns to face you as stablehands direct animals to open spots in the stables behind you. She looks you up and down and immediately looks annoyed at having her work interrupted.
>
> > Yeah? What do you want? I'm a little busy today.

> [!info] Social
> #### Reading Nashira
>
> A successful **Deception (DC 14)** check allows a character to get a general read on Nashira's personality while talking to her.
>
> Generally speaking, she is honest but abrasive. Nashira doesn't seem to be a bad person, but is clearly motivated by profit, and probably has a hard time looking past that.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why aren't you helping the refugees?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I'd like to, but I'm not running a charity. Taking care of all these animals isn't cheap, nor is paying all my people. If the refugees can pay, and I have room, I'll make sure their animals get taken care of. It's not personal.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who reserved all the stable space?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Sorry, we don't divulge client information to strangers. You'll need orders of inspection from the Hallows, and I know we're not doing anything worth their attention right now.

If the party points out they are here working for the Veiled Chain, Nathira's response develops further:

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:**
>
> **A:**
>
> Nashira's scowl deepens.
>
> > All right, well, in that case you can go get the Hallows to send an inspector here with a signed order to inspect my work records, like I said. Then I'll divulge that information.
> >
> > I have a lot of respect for the work the Chain does in the city, but there's rules for a reason, and I'm going to have to ask that everyone follows them. You included.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Could we pay you to release the stables?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Potentially, yes. I'd accept fifty thousand gold to bump the current hold on the spots, then you can use them. Anything less than that, and I can't justify the loss of profit and reputation I'd suffer.

> [!info] Social
> #### Convincing Nathira
>
> Nathira can be convinced to open the stable slots with one of several successful checks including **Diplomacy (DC 16)** check to, **Deception (DC 16)**, or **Intimidation (DC 14)** from a character in the party.
>
> Generally speaking, appealing to her sense of goodness and the potential goodwill and fame helping the refugees would garner will be more effective than anything else. In that same vein, Nashira is worried about profit and reputation, and any direct threats made to them will work to coerce her into complying.

#### Mayis Attunement: Helping the Agitators

If the party chooses to help the agitators, each character advances their **Attunement: Mayis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Reporting Success

Returning to either Sura or Larz, the party can report on their success.

If the party previously completed [[Crowds in Palisade Cross]] by assisting the agitators there, then they say this:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Looking genuinely surprised at your success they shoot you a smile.
>
> > I'm glad you turned up to lend a hand. I honestly didn't think we'd see any success here.
> >
> > Say, I think I've heard about you. You helped out with some issues over in Palisade Cross, if I recall.
> >
> > Listen, we're gathering like-minded folk, potential allies, and supporters in the Verocorte estate in a few days. Come after midnight, we'll have three lanterns by the side gate located on Kelekin Alley if we're meeting. Use the passphrase "I won't be subordinate." to get in.
> >
> > Hope to see you there!

Otherwise, they have this to say in response:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Looking genuinely surprised at your success they shoot you a smile.
>
> > That's very impressive! I didn't think that the stable boss would open up the spots again. You're clearly an ally worth keeping around.
> >
> > You know… we've got people all over the city that need help. Palisade Cross, for example, has been a real mess lately with fighting and people getting detained. You might be able to do some good there, too.
> >
> > I'd love to stick around, but I need to be on my way. Thank you again for your help here.

> [!info] Social
> #### Unexpected Results
>
> A successful **Deception (DC 14)** check against Sura or Larz notes that they are actually somewhat disappointed that you were successful since the stables turning away the refugees was technically good for their cause and gave more fuel to their anti-Ordinate rhetoric.

### Attacking the Agitators

The party may choose the direct approach, here are the relevant skill checks and details for such an event:

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Easily Approached
>
> A party looking to ambush either Sura or Larz can do so easily. They do not put up much of a fight, and can be subdued without combat. They aren't looking to die for their cause (not yet at least), aren't armed or armored, and are not expecting a fight. They quickly surrender when faced with violence, especially where there's a risk they'll die.
>
> #### Deniable Assets
>
> Sura and Larz have been hired for this work, but aren't connected to the rest of the

Questioning Sura and Larz yields the following information:

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who are you working for?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Brynna Verocorrt, a rich noble who lives in Fallbrook. She wants agitate the locals against the Ordinate, and bring about social change.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What is the next step?
>
> **A:**
>
> > We're going to start handing out fliers for a rally in Fallbrook, gather up everybody, and get them to agree to join a new city-wide reform movement!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** When is this meeting?
>
> **A:**
>
> > In a few days, after sundown.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Where will the meeting be held?
>
> **A:**
>
> > There's an old theater in Fallbrook, the Bronze Rask, they are hosting it there.
> >
> > The password to get in is: "I Won't Be Subordinate" given at the door.

#### Ragen Attunement: Attacking the Agitators

If the party chooses to attack the agitators, each character advances their **Attunement: Ragen (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Looting the Agitators

If the party kills Sura and/or Larz, picks their pockets, or robs them they can find the following items of interest:

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Robbing the Agitators
>
> A successful **`[[/skill sleight 16]]`** check is necessary to lift items from the bags of either Larz or Sura. Failure by 5 or more results in the targeted agitator becoming wary of the party, and going to meet with their counterpart to warn them.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Shared Inventory
>
> These items are carried by both Larz and Sura.
>
> #### Planning Letter
>
> This letter is about the upcoming meeting:
>
> > We're still putting together the details, but if anyone asks you: yes we're planning a proper rally. There's going to be a meeting in Fallbrook at the old theater that Brynna's bought out. Timeframe is in the next week or so.
> >
> > Once this is set in stone we'll start gathering people up.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Intrigue** or **Knowledge: Politics** might suspect that a noble or merchant of means and influence is backing this movement. Why is a mystery, but political intrigue and ideological fights are not uncommon in the halls of power.
>
> #### Foxlair Note
>
> > The Jessos Station in Foxlairs is a good place to hand out fliers and build buzz for the movement. Someone with deep coffers paid to reserve a bunch of stable space to keep the refugees out, and the Jessos family is so profit oriented they will be hard pressed to do the right thing if it costs them coin.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Intrigue** or **Knowledge: Politics** recognize that someone is feeding the movement inside information, and is likely embedded in the city's merchant community.
>
> #### Spare Fliers
>
> > THE CITY DOESN'T WANT YOU HERE
>
> It goes on to detail that the Ordinate, Hallows, and Trading Houses don't have an interest in helping the refugees. They won't ever dedicate enough resources to help everyone, and now that they aren't producing food and livestock in the flats, they are even less useful to the city.

> Characters with **Knowledge: Intrigue** or **Knowledge: Politics** recognize this is an inflammatory half-truth that can be easily defended with little effort. For a group of stressed, distracted refugees, any nuance is likely to be lost.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> This event is completed when the party either collects evidence from the pair of agitators here, or earns their trust by helping them out with the stables lodging issue. Once they have done so, they can go to the other district to investigate that location.
>
> Parties that looted materials directly from the agitators can skip investigating both locations and move to the Midnight Meeting event, as they have enough material and information to find it.
