# Unhallowed

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This social event is a dramatic conclusion to the events of Spreading Sickness, and marks a dark turn for the party and their allies. The Veiled Chain will be badly set back by this betrayal, and its leadership rendered out of action for a while. The party's options are limited, but significant:
>
> - Save Shaar and Ankarist from imminent death.
> - Get the survivors medical assistance.
> - Put out fires and save vital documents.

#### Assets Under Development

A version of the Veiled Chain headquarters depicting the damaged state is planned but not yet complete. In the meantime, the event will show the undamaged version.

### Speaking to Serethus

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > I had hoped the ambushes and traps I set would have been more effective. You are all proving to be frustratingly resilient. I assume that Brynna is dead, of course.
> >
> > A pity. She had potential. Bright mind, keen intellect, drive and dedication to a cause greater than her. People who could have changed the world if they hadn't been murdered in cold blood… and you do so love your murder.
> >
> > But, it doesn't matter. You are always just a little to late, aren't you? Always just behind us. You ruined the quarry lab, but not before we finished the mutagenic compound. You turned the cistern lab upside down, but not before we sent off our first shipments of stable residuum.
> >
> > Now… well...
>
> Serethus gestures to the death and destruction around him.
>
> > And with the Chain broken, you'll never catch up.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who are you working for?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Giving you a name would only risk the security of our operations, and you've done enough damage as it is. I will say this though: their identity will be revealed when the time is right, and not a moment sooner.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why are you doing this?
>
> **A:**
>
> Serethus laughs.
>
> > All will be revealed in time, you just need to survive long enough to see it.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** How can you do this?
>
> **A:**
>
> > It's quite easy, if I'm to be honest. I serve something greater than a city, or its government. I have become part of something greater, something truly world changing. The pain, suffering, and death are regrettable, but necessary for progress. Eventually you will see, and you will understand.

### Ankarist Attacks

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Ankarist pushes himself to his feet, calling his sword to hand as he rises, but he is too slow, too weak to best Serethus, who catches his wrist in a clawed fist, and twists the investigator's arm until his hand opens, and the blade clatters to the floor.
>
> > I admire your tenacity, Ankarist. But you need to learn when you've been beaten. You and your friends had to survive the traps and turn up before I could finish my work here and gotten away cleanly!
>
> Serethus strikes Ankarist in the stomach, driving the man to one knee. Ankarist coughs a spit of blood then says:
>
> > I figured you out, so did Shaar. You aren't as clever as you think.
>
> Serethus sneers, the anger clear as day on his features, though they twist into a cruel smile as a thought occurs to him. Without flourish he produces a syringe filled with green fluid from his robes.
>
> > Then let me send a message to the survivors, Ankarist: Survival isn't always the triumph one might think.
>
> Serethus grabs Ankarist by the horn, and wrenches his head to the side, exposing his neck. With a swift stab, he drives a needle into the man's throat and presses the contents of the syringe into his body.
>
> Ankarist screams in pain and doubles over, hands grasping at the side of his neck, as though me might claw the poison from it if he tries hard enough. The investigator is quickly lost to convulsions, and collapses on the floor.

### Serethus Exits

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Serethus turns calls out:
>
> > Time's up, we need to go!
>
> In response, five figures appear from side rooms and passages, each of them carrying items, cramming books and papers into bags, or wiping blood off of weapons. By the look of their equipment, they are Mutagist and Wandren agents.
>
> One speaks up:
>
> > We've destroyed what we can't take, but lost two in the fighting.
>
> Serethus motions for his agents to gather around. As they do, his attention turns back your way, and he offers a smug, satisfied smile.
>
> > This is where we part ways. I am sure we will meet again. Until then, may cunning and wisdom guide you.
>
> With another gesture and invocation of magic he and the agents at his side vanish in a flash of magical light. A moment later the wall of magical force collapses, granting access to the rest of the chambers.

### Rescuing the Chain

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Trapped Chain Members
>
> Several rooms in the Veiled Chain have had fires set in them, and to make matters worse, these rooms have injured and dying members of the Veiled Chain trapped inside, incapable of extricating themselves without help.
>
> A character (or party as a whole) trying to save people needs to accrue three successful **Athletics (DC 14)** checks to get everyone out safely.
>
> Should a character (or party) accrue three failures before they get the necessary successes the fire and smoke grows too extreme to make any further attempts, dooming anyone left inside.
>
> Characters failing a check by 5 or more must also make a saving throw against the Fire Hazard (see below).
>
> #### Firefighting Assets
>
> The danger of these rescue efforts can be partially mitigated with proper resources or spells. All of the following things provide Advantage to rescue rolls made by characters or groups:
>
> - The water barrel (See [[Unhallowed]] above) can be used three times.
>
> - Casting [[Create or Destroy Water]] as part of a rescue attempt.
>
> - Casting [[Protection from Energy (Fire)]] on a character.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Fire Hazard
>
> Characters that fail a rescue attempt by 5 or more must make a successful `[[/save con 14]]` or `[[/save dex 14]]` saving throw (the player chooses which). Failure deals `[[/damage 1d6 fire]]` damage.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Searching the Halls
>
> Characters searching the halls for anything useful will need to make a successful **Awareness (DC 12)** check, and as a result finds a water barrel and buckets which provide advantage on attempts to fight fires and rescue people trapped in burning rooms. This barrel can be used three times in this way before it's expended.
>
> A higher roll will uncover additional items:
>
> - **Result of 14+** also finds a [[Healer's Kit]] in a cabinet in a ransacked side room. It has 5 uses in it, and is sufficient to stabilize the dying so that they'll survive long enough for help to arrive.
> - **Result of 16+** also finds a [[Potion of Healing]] which can be used to save a dying person.

### Assisting Ankarist

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### What's Afflicting Ankarist?
>
> Ankarist is &reference[poisoned], and &reference[incapacitated] due to a powerful mutagenic compound. Nothing short of [[Lesser Restoration]] will clear these effects and remove the poison effect.
>
> However, removing the poisoned state does not remove the alchemical and biomagical effects of the mutagenic compound. These have already begun to take root, and nothing the party has access to can stop its effects, not yet!

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining Ankarist
>
> A successful **Medicine (DC 13)** check reveals that Ankarist is badly poisoned and incapacitated by intense pain and muscle convulsions, but is otherwise stable.
>
> - **Result of 16+** You also note that there is already discoloration forming around the injection site. This discoloration can also be seen seeping into his veins, darkening them.
>
> #### Examining the Vial
>
> Characters studying the syringe of poison must succeed on a **Awareness (DC 15)** or **Medicine (DC 15)** check to discern the nature of the chemical. Characters with **Knowledge: Alchemy** have advantage on this check.
>
> - **Result of 15+** The syringe appears to be one taken from the cistern lab, and thus the dosage was meant for humanoids, not drakes. This is fortunate, since a larger dose would likely have been instantly fatal.
> - **Result of 18+** The lot number written on the label dates this mutagen from the later days of the quarry lab experiments. This means the compound was one of the more stable variants, so it shouldn't kill him in the long run, but he may suffer from physical mutations which might be worse than death.
>
> #### Worrying Implications
>
> The collected evidence and research about Mutagist efforts in the region should lead the party to expect something is happening to Ankarist internally. Since he's a Drakon, and has some distant connection to dragons and drakes, he is likely going to suffer some similar, horrible fate.
>
> - Research data about the mutagenic compounds created the mutated drake, which suffered horrible physical deformities as the mutagen changed it.
> - Most of the early tests on the drakes killed them after lengthy, painful periods of illness and suffering.
> - Injecting humanoids with the mutagen was almost always immediately fatal.

### Looking For Help

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Searching the Hollows
>
> A character wanting to return to the Hallows knows that it will take ten minutes to do so, effectively removing them from the scene, and making it impossible to assist in saving others in any way.
>
> However, in trade, they encounter Shaar and a team of Chain members already rushing back to the headquarters, and the group can move together.

### Shaar's Arrival

Once Serethus has departed, Shaar will arrive a short time later. This should be at least ten minutes, to give the party time to attempt rescuing people, and putting out fires, but can happen sooner at your discretion. When Shaar arrives, read the follow aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You hear movement nearby, and as your attention shifts that way you see a group of Veiled Chain agents rushing into the headquarters, with Shaar leading them. She issues orders rapidly, and the agents begin fanning out to grab the injured, rescue materials, and put out fires.
>
> Shaar quickly takes note of your presence, and moves toward you.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Where were you?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Chasing down leads on a conspiracy against the Hallows and Veiled Chain. Based on the state of things, we were all being led into traps meant to keep us busy while the conspirators dealt with the Chain H.Q… unfortunately, it seems they were successful.
>
> Ankarist speaks up:
>
> > My lead was also a trap, but it was easy to avoid. When I realized they were distracting us I rushed back, and ran into Serethus and and his people ransacking the place.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who is targeting the Chain and Hallows?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Serethus, The Mutagists, and House Wandren. What we don't know yet is how they are all connected, or what they are trying to accomplish.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What do we do now?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Save what and who we can, bury the dead, and move our operations to a more secure location, and take stock of the damage. We won't know what they stole and destroyed until we compare our files to case records.
> >
> > After the Chain is up and running again, we start tracking down Serethus and the Mutagists, and figure out what to do about House Wandren.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Can we afford to wait?
>
> **A:**
>
> > It's not entirely a choice, the Chain won't be able to work at full capacity until things are put back in order, and fielding agents without proper backup and resources is only going to cost us more people. We won't stop watching and investigating, but recovery is the priority.
