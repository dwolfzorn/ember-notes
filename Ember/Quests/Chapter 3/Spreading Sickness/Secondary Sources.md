# Secondary Sources

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This social event allows the party to bring all their evidence to the Veiled Chain and receive their next assignment from Ankarist. While discussing things with him, the party can learn:
>
> - What Ankarist has been up to, what he's learned, and what he's doing next.
> - What their investigation has contributed to, and what it all means.
> - Where the party needs to go next.

### Reporting on Lilifeld Station

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Agricultural Concerns
>
> The party can provide insight into operations at Lilifeld Stations, speaking to the level of preparation, safety and security on display with the grain stores, establishing at a basic level that the grain supply to the city is not tainted.
>
> A successfull **Arcana (DC 14)** check notes that the wards the party was shown were well designed and more than capable of keeping any stored grains or other food stocks safe.
>
> - Result of 19+ Notes that the designs were particularly well implemented, easy to check and maintain, and layered well so as to have long-term redundancy. House Lilifeld planned it out well.
> - A character with **Knowledge: Rituals** has advantage on this check.
>
> A successful **Deception (DC 13)** check notes that the visiting dignitary from Redrak was interested in getting the ward designs from House Lilifeld for use in their silohs as well.
>
> - A result of 18+ Notes that the dignitary expressed interest in making sure Redrak Fields was able to meet Ordain's needs and not lose as much stock to spoilage, and given how well the foreman and he got on, there's a good chance the deal will go through.
>
> #### Pointless Busywork
>
> If the party [[Looking Into Lilifeld Station]] as Serethus suggested, then they can also confirm that there are absolutely no issues with any shipments coming in from Redrak, nor any discrepancies in handling and storage.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Ankarist's Response
>
> **A:**
>
> > With the Golden Flats supply issues lately I'm glad we can still rely on Redrak Fields to feed the city. If both faltered things could become dire very rapidly. I understand that Del Kalais was there as well, he mentioned seeing you.
>
> If the party [[Looking Into Lilifeld Station]] he also adds:
>
> > I appreciate your thoroughness on looking through the records, but I don't know that was the best use of time. Be that as it may, it's good to have that insight. I'll make sure it gets entered into the report.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Serethus' Response
>
> **A:**
>
> If the party [[Looking Into Lilifeld Station]] at Lilifeld Stations he says:
>
> > Had you all actually agreed to meet me when I'd asked, perhaps I could lend some insight into what was seen! I'll endeavor not to take it personally, as we are all working toward the same end, and you still clearly did the work, but I do not appreciate being left in the lurch.
>
> Otherwise he says:
>
> > I would largely concur that the situation at Lilifeld Stations is stable… however I think it critical the Veiled Chain should consider dedicating a team or two to regular inspections to ensure that House Lilifeld is upholding the thoroughness displayed for the visiting diplomat and investigators.
> >
> > If the party did not [[Looking Into Lilifeld Station]] as Serethus suggested he will add:
> >
> > > Also, I suggested that your team should look into the last six season's worth of records for discrepancies. Since it seems they ignored that advice, perhaps you can spare another team to do so.
>
> Ankarist takes a moment to consider that and then says:
>
> > I will put the recommendation on the final report, but we're too thinly spread for that right now.

### Reporting on Lower Ashvale

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Medical Inquiries
>
> The party can provide a report on what they learned from the healing house in Lower Ashvale. They note that the sick are numerous but not overwhelming, none have died, and all should make a full recovery soon.
>
> A successful **Medicine (DC 13)** check notes that while the patients are not too numerous right now, and the illness is not inherently deadly, it's contagious enough that it could begin spreading. If necessary, restorative magic can be used.
>
> - **Result of 18+** If the disease is allowed to spread far and wide enough it could easily overwhelm the capacity of the Cindaric healing houses across the city. Long-term care of large volumes of patients is not what most of them are built for.
>
> If the party [[Ill in Lower Ashvale]] successfully they can report on the disease more directly.
>
> A successful **Medicine (DC 13)** check summarizes that the illness causes severe nausea and dizziness, sweating, vomiting, and weakness. However, treatment requires very little more than clean water and bed rest. However, it is spreadable through contact, especially if there is a lack of hygene.
>
> - **Result of 18+** Notes that there are two illnesses at play, one due to alchemical exposure, and one stemming from it as a secondary illness. They have very similar symptoms, with only slight variations between the two, which results in them being mistaken for a single malady. The alchemical malady is harder to cure because the alchemical exposure needs to be resolved first.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Ankarist's Response
>
> **A:**
>
> > Well, it's good that nobody has died yet, but I am increasingly concerned about the risk of this spreading. Wasting sickness can easily get out of hand. I'll make note of this, and hopefully the Hallows can help the Cindarics get out ahead of it.
>
> If the party [[Ill in Lower Ashvale]] successfully he adds:
>
> > Good investigative work, that distinction matters. We might have caught yourselves fighting two illnesses like they were one, never attacking the source.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Serethus' Response
>
> **A:**
>
> > So very good of the Cindarics to see to the ill. I am glad that they are keeping our city healthy. I think we should excise caution, perhaps caution the Hallows to quarantine the districts and search all the forges and water walls around the Burns. Time consuming, but the last thing we want is more outbreaks…
>
> If the party [[Ill in Lower Ashvale]] successfully he adds:
>
> > I really should have a look at those files myself, I am quite well read in matters of healing, and might be able to offer some additional insights. Not to say that our investigators missed anything, but in this case, thoroughness would be most useful.

### Reporting on The Burns

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Forging Connections
>
> The party can provide insight into the epicenter of illness, which is up north in the Burns, citing the Red Iron forge as the primary place of interest. The party can also report on the presence of looters, and how they handled them.
>
> A successful **Awareness (DC 12)** check recalls that the lab was abandoned mid-shift, and that nothing had been cleaned. Everything was coated with a white residue, and the air stunk of chemicals as well as the usual scents one finds in a forge.
>
> - **Result 17+** Further recalls that the structure was locked up, and didn't appear like it was disturbed at all afterward, which aligns with the statement that Janix Mance gave in regards to him shutting down and closing up the forge after the crew went home sick.
>
> #### Tainted Waters
>
> - If the party [[Sickness in The Burns]] they can report on the contaminated water there, and how the compound within coated every surface of the forge and was likely the source of sickness.
>
> - If the party [[Sickness in The Burns]] and found the contamination there, they can inform Ankarist of such, which helps narrow the search area going forward.
>
> If they took [[Tainted Water Sample]] they can hand those over, which he will have tested. However, if they tested the water samples themselves they can provide [[Water Sample Results]] which are more immediately definitive.
>
> #### Familiar Additives
>
> If the party previously reported on their findings during [[Grim Findings]] and also tested the water themselves, then a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check recognizes several of the chemicals matching those found in the Mutagist lab located in the underground quarry.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Alchemy** has advantage on this check.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Ankarist's Response
>
> **A:**
>
> > All right, so Mance is not at fault, and none of the forges are using suspect materials. That's good to rule out, but doesn't solve the problem.
>
> If the party [[Sickness in The Burns]] he adds:
>
> > This is good info though. Alchemically tainted water being splashed and vaporized in a forge would explain how multiple people ended up getting exposed. But, that doesn't give us a source.
>
> If the party [[Sickness in The Burns]] he adds:
>
> > That sounds like a source which dovetails nicely with my side of the investigation. Very good work, that'll help when we bring this all together.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Serethus' Response
>
> **A:**
>
> > I would like to caution that Janix Mance is an especially crafty, and guileful sort that should be watched. If he's not directly responsible for things here, he may yet be connected…
>
> If the party [[Sickness in The Burns]] he adds:
>
> > Yes, yes, that would be a possible vector. The heat of the forge would vaporize the compounds in the water, and so if that water was used for quenching, or dousing workers to keep them cool, well, there you are.
>
> If the party [[Sickness in The Burns]] he adds:
>
> > I thought the city had extensive water purification wards by now? Perhaps an in-depth investigation into that would be useful next?

### Putting It Together

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Ankarist gestures for you to come look at a map of the city. He takes a nearby grease pencil, and begins making marks.
>
> > While you were chasing down leads, I followed up on a report from the Hallows about the city water systems being worked on. The waterfront districts have extensive underground waterworks that channel spring and river water out to the rest of the city.
>
> He marks several areas around Koto Springs, Fallbrook, Quarry Run.
>
> > These recently got a series of upgrades — revised purification wards, new alchemical filters, and contamination alarms — so I decided to go have a look personally, and see if they were working properly.
> >
> > In short, I and several hallows inspectors ended up doing a full tour of the water systems, took samples, and double-checked everything. I can say with absolute certainty that the wards are working, and that the normal waste and toxins from the city are being properly filtered out.
> >
> > So, whatever the source is, it's coming in from above the city.
>
> He looks to you.
>
> > This leads us to your findings. You've ruled out grain contamination in the Stations.
>
> He crosses out Lilifeld Stations.
>
> > And as of today, I learned that the Dustforge and Smokerie have their own dedicated filtration systems, and there haven't been any issues from these higher districts.
>
> He marks the two districts above it with Xs.
>
> > So, with all that being the case, and the epicenter of the sickness being the Burns, and the fact that they would be pulling their water directly out of the Destine, that means…
>
> He circles the large body of water just beneath the waterfall in the canyon to the west of the city.
>
> > This is where the toxins are coming from.
>
> He looks directly at you, a fire in his eyes.
>
> > Gear up, and head out there tomorrow. You're going to see if you can find what poisoning all these people.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** You're not coming with?
>
> **A:**
>
> > No, I'm not. Shaar has reassigned me to the ongoing missing person's case. Things have gotten a bit more complicated there. For now, you'll be taking the lead, and reporting to me when you find something.
> >
> > Make sure to collect evidence as you go.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Is there anything we should bring?
>
> **A:**
>
> > There's a good chance you're probably going to need to go underwater and search the lake bed, so some goggles, and possible some potions to aid with swimming would be prudent.
> >
> > I would also buy some antitoxins just in case, don't want to get poisoned in the process of searching.
> >
> > Oh, and don't bring anything with you you don't want getting wet. Obviously.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why not go now?
>
> **A:**
>
> > You can if you want, but bear in mind that you're not machines. I used to work myself to the bone, until Shaar got after me about the fact I was wearing myself down and becoming less effective at my job. So, if I need to rest to stay sharp, so do you.

### Serethus Departs

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Serethus raps his heavy fingers on the wood twice and straightens up.
>
> > Well, it seems that you have all this in hand and under control. I'm not seeing any way I can offer more help, therefore I shall be on my way. I have plenty of other tasks that need my attention. Ankarist, fellow investigators, may wisdom and cunning guide you!
>
> With that he turns and leaves the space. Ankarist offers a farewell, but keeps his attention on you in case you have questions, or need anything.
