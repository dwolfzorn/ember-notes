# Sickness in The Burns

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This combat event covers the party's ongoing investigation into the source and cause of a growing sickness in the city. Since the city's ill end up here, there is a good chance the healers may have some insight into what the illness is. Here the party can:
>
> - Speak with Janix Mance and learn what he knows about the situation.
> - Potentially locate the source of tainted water that is afflicting the area.
> - Deal with some local looters trying to take advantage of the situation.

### Speaking with Janix

> [!abstract] Janix Mance
> **[[Janix Mance]]**
>
> Level 1 · Unknown Unknown
>
> ![Janix Mance](../../../assets/image130.webp)

If the party previously met Janix and completed [[To Copy a Key]], his greeting changes somewhat:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Janix lifts his machine-hand in greeting, and grins broadly upon seeing you.
>
> > Well, well, I was just thinking that it would be extremely fortunate if a band of adventurers turned up to start setting things right. Who knew I had oracular abilities!
>
> He gestures to the area around him.
>
> > I'd welcome to you to the hardest working district in the city, but as you can see, the Burns isn't doing so great right now. Bouts of illness have shut down several forges, shops, a restaurant or two. Lots of people are ill right now, and probably not the best place to be hanging around.
> >
> > So! Are you here of your own volition or did the city send you to look into things?

Otherwise:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Janix lifts his machine-hand in greeting.
>
> > Well met, I'm Janix Mance. I operate a forge here, the Burnside Arms. I'd like to say that I'm known far and wide for my work as a forge master, but if you've never heard of me, that says all it needs to.
>
> He gestures to the area around him.
>
> > As you can see, the Burns isn't doing so great right now. Bouts of illness have shut down several forges, shops, a restaurant or two. Lots of people are ill right now, and probably not the best place to be hanging around.
> >
> > Unless of course, you're here on behalf of the city, at which point you're just who I want to talk to.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What happened to your arm and leg?
>
> **A:**
>
> Janix lifts his hand and flexes it, looking over the metal digits.
>
> > Oh boy, that's a story for another day. Short version is this though: fire elementals are amazing allies to have in a forge, as long as you don't let them get too carried away.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** How many other people are sick?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Quite a few, actually. Every shop in the district has someone sick right now, and it's been continuing to spread despite our best efforts. I'd say, at this point, everybody knows someone that's caught some sort of affliction of the gut lately.
> >
> > The worst of them were taken to healing houses, obviously, the rest of the sick are staying home, drinking fluids and recuperating.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Is your forge affected?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Not directly, actually. I've got an apprentice out sick, and a few of them have sick family members, but we've so far managed to avoid the worst of it. I'm not entirely sure why, could be the wards I have in the place to manage air purity, or could be that I'm closer to the Smokerie. I couldn't tell you for sure.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** How long ago did the sickness set in?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Few weeks now, maybe months. It was scattered at first, a few people ill here and there, some workers out sick for a day or two. It started to pick up though, and is pretty widespread now.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Have the Hallows of Cindarics come to help?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Yeah, we've got a couple visiting Sages who are tending the ill, casting magic where they can. It's helping, but the number of sick is more than they can manage. Plus the sick aren't getting better, and even the ones that get healed end up sick a few days later.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Persistent Sickness
>
> Characters with a **Medicine (DC 14, Passive)** or making a successful **Medicine (DC 12)** check suspect that the source of the illness must be persistent in the Burns for people to catch the sickness again after recovering. This also means that the source is widespread, not localized.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Do you know the source?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I have suspicions, but no hard proof. I would look at the Red Iron forge up the way. One day the whole work crew there came down with the sickness, all at once, and so bad that we had to pile them into a boat and rush them to Lower Ashvale.
> >
> > I have the key to the forge if you want to look around.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Have you changed anything recently?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Nope, nothing so far. I checked through all my most recent fuel batches, oil stocks and forge additives too. Nothing out of the ordinary. So whatever the cause is, it's not coming from anything I'm supplying people.
> >
> > Besides, I work with all the materials myself, every day. If it was the cause, I'd be sick, too.

> [!info] Social
> #### Reading Janix
>
> Janix is being honest and forthright. A successful **Deception (DC 12)** confirms this, and yields further insight: the kivahr alchemist is more concerned about this illness than he lets on. His words are reassuring but there are threads of doubt beginning to fray along the edges of his social mask.

### Raising Serethus' Concerns

If the party wants to raise Serethus' concerns about Janix being the source, they can.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Have you introduced any new compounds in the area?
>
> **A:**
>
> > What? Wait, are you accusing me of this? No. I work with the Hallows to help make sure the forges in the city all follow a roughly equal set of safe working conditions so people **don't** get hurt. That includes anything new that enters widespread use in the city forges.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Serethus seems to think you might have been responsible.
>
> **A:**
>
> Janix scoffs loudly.
>
> > Serethus. Of course. I don't really care what some wannabe alchemist says about me, though I don't appreciate him slandering my name. Obviously I'm not the one responsible, if I was, why would I be willing to help you on your investigation?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Do you know Serethus?
>
> **A:**
>
> Janix scrunches his nose out of reflexive distaste.
>
> > Not well, I know he's from pretty far off and a very competent spellcaster, but I couldn't tell you anything concrete about his story, and I definitely don't care to. I do think he needs to stick with magic, and leave alchemy to the experts, though.

### Routing the Looters

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you approach the forge you see a small group of people trying to find a way into the building. Some are testing the windows, a few are trying and failing to pick the door. However, their sentry spots you and calls out:
>
> > We've got company!
>
> They all slide clubs and knives from their coats, and size you up. The number advantage they have is clear, and seems to have made them bold, but none of them look to be well armored or heavily armed.

> [!abstract] Ordani Ruffian
> **[[Ordani Ruffian]]**
>
> Level 1 · Unknown Unknown
>
> ![Ordani Ruffian](../../../assets/image131.webp)

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Ordani Ruffian Tactics
>
> A group of 10 [[Ordani Ruffian]] will attack the party. Their tactics are simple: they try to distribute themselves evenly across all combatants, and refuse to fight one-on-one if they can help it. In the off chance that they are victorious, they will take bags, valuables and weapons from the party, focusing on items they can just grab and run away with quickly.
>
> The Ruffians will flee if more than half their number are defeated. If the party kills a Ruffian, rather than knocking them out, they will begin to flee as soon as they've lost 1-2 allies. The Ruffians aren't looking to kill anyone, nor are they looking to get killed.
>
> #### Janix Mance's Tactics
>
> Janix Mance immediately falls back to a safe distance until the fighting is over, and refuses to engage in combat. He cannot be convinced or cajoled into fighting by any means.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Does the Burns usually have this sort of problem?
>
> **A:**
>
> Janix shakes his head.
>
> > Well, Ordain has its unseemly sorts, same as anywhere else, and they are always looking for easy paydays. However, the break-ins only really only started happening after people got sick and stopped coming to work around here.
> >
> > As soon as people are better these ruffians will move on.

### Searching the Forge

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Janix guides you to the shut down forge, the shutters and doors closed tightly. He takes a heavy key and unlocks one of the gates, lifting it up to grant access to the building. Inside, it appears that the forge was simply abandoned in the middle of a work day.
>
> > Couple people came through, doused all the fires, made sure nothing would burn down, but otherwise everything's been left as-is. Be warned: there's probably still puke and worse on the ground in places, so watch your step.
>
> Janix doesn't come in with you, but instead hovers at the entrance as he tucks the key into a belt pouch.
>
> > All right, well, I'll leave you to it, and just be waiting outside until you're done. I'm not going to risk my own health, but hopefully there's something to find in here. I'll lock it back up when you're done.
>
> Inside the dark forge space there's the stink of acids, oils, coal and burnt wood, alongside the stench of iron and human waste. Eyes cast down to the floor easily spot sources of the stink, spattered and dripped here and there.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Contaminated Containers
>
> A character who succeeds on a **Awareness (DC 14)** check notices a barrel and several buckets sitting against a wall next to a water pump fixed to the wall, the iron pipes descending into the ground. Looking at them, there an obvious sheen on the water, not unlike soap or oil collecting on the surface.
>
> - **Result of 16+**: The character also notices that there are several drinking cups sitting on a shelf nearby the barrel and buckets, they also are coated with the strange, oily sheen.
> - **Result of 18+:** The character also notices numerous troughs and barrels around the forge likely used for quenching metals, all of these have a mixture of oil and a green film collecting on their surface.
> - **Result of 20+:** The character also notices a powdery gray residue on most everything in the forge. At first it looks like ash, but it is too uniformly spread out to be, and looks as though it was applied while wet and dried to the surfaces.
>
> If the party completed [[Ill in Lower Ashvale]] then they should know they are looking for sources of alchemical hazards, and automatically succeed on the base roll above, but may roll with advantage to locate further sources and clues.
>
> A character with **Knowledge: Alchemy** or **Knowledge: Crafts** or who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check posits that the water is contaminated, and that the workers here were drinking said water, dousing themselves to keep cool, and vaporizing the chemical in the quenching troughs. It's no wonder they fell violently ill.
>
> #### Well: There's Your Problem
>
> Characters that test the wall by pumping water notice that it has a slightly sickly green tinge to it. Upon seeing this, it is safe to assume that the whole district's water supply is likely contaminated.
>
> - Characters with **Medicine (DC 12, Passive)** or making a successful **Medicine (DC 14)** check believe this is probably why the locals aren't getting better. If the water's tainted, they're only going to keep getting sick, or end up getting sicker over time.
> - Characters with **Awareness (DC 12, Passive)** or making a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check would suspect that the source of the contamination is likely from the Destine river, and as a result, the source of the contamination might be upriver somewhere.
>
> #### Signs of Sickness
>
> Examining the vomit in the forge doesn't yield any particular insight into the illness, beyond what the workers last ate before falling ill. However, a character with **Medicine (DC 12, Passive)** or making a successful **Medicine (DC 14)** check notices that the vomit appears to have been thin, possibly containing mostly water. It's likely that the forge workers had been drinking large amounts of water while on the job, probably to offset the volume of sweat caused by the heat in the forge.
>
> Casting [[Detect Poison and Disease]] immediately allows a character to notice the water as a primary source of toxicity. More than that, though, there is a mild haze of toxicity on every surface of the forge area.

After the party examines the forge, they may want to look for other wells or water sources in the area, and Janix, who is still nearby, will offer to show them. They can also ask Janix more questions.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Where does the district get its Water?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Well, most people pump the water from the river directly. Since the district is close to the headwaters, there's not much concern about city contaminants. A few pump water from the ground, though the aquifers are fed from the river, too.
> >
> > If the water's contaminated though, that'd explain why people are falling ill.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Testing the Waters
>
> Locating another well in the district is easy with the help of Janix Mance, who is more than happy to guide the party to it. If tested, the water is also contaminated, though the water is not as obviously green, meaning the contamination is weaker here.
>
> A character with **Awareness (DC 12, Passive)** or making a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** would note that this well is further from the waterfront, leading them to suspect that the source of the contamination is likely from the Destine river.
>
> #### Up A River
>
> Examining the Destine river is inconclusive, as the speed and volume of the water is too great to examine it up close. However, if the party draws water from the Destine, they'll note it does have the same strange sheen.
>
> #### Samples of Sickness
>
> The party may have chosen to take samples of the water from the forge barrels, either of the wells, or the Destine river itself, gaining a [[Tainted Water Sample]] in the process.
>
> This sample is contaminated with an unknown alchemical compound, and if examined while [[Detect Poison and Disease]] is active, is revealed to be a source of an unknown toxin.
>
> Testing the sample requires an hour and use of `[[/tool alchemist 14]]` and reveals a collection of dangerous chemicals that nobody should be consuming. The chemicals can be compiled into [[Water Sample Results]].

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### What's Going On?
>
> - Workers are drinking the alchemically tainted water frequently due to the heat of the forge area.
> - Workers are dousing themselves in water to stay cool during the work day, coating themselves in the toxin.
> - Workers are exposed to steam which coats them and which they inhale during quenching.
> - The tainted water is coming from local wells that draw from the destine directly.
>
> #### Why is there no tainted water further downriver?
>
> - The dilution in the Destine makes it a non-issue. The Burns is clearly close to the source!
> - The city's filtration systems are taking care of a good amount of the toxin in other places (Ankarist is looking into this, and it will come up later.)

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster

>
