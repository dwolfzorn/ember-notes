# Missing Persons

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This social event involves the party reporting back to the Veiled Chain about their findings during Hazardous Headwaters, only to be handed a new assignment to investigate a group actively agitating the people of Ordain and refugees coming in from the Golden Flats. The characters can:
>
> - Report on what they found in the hidden Mutagist lab and turn in any evidence they have.
> - Learn about the agitators attempting to turn the populace of Ordain against its leadership.
> - Get their next assignment in the city.

#### Vista Under Development

A scene representing the Veiled Chain headquarters is planned, in the meantime, the event will show the Hallows vista, which is where the entrance to their hidden operations can be found.

### Reporting the Lab

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Reportable Evidence
>
> If the party turned in any of the following items they can achieve the **Outcome: Recovered Mutagist Records & Materials**:
>
> - [[Mutagist Alchemical Data]]
> - [[Cistern Stockpile Logbook]]
> - [[Kaftor's Research Logs]]
> - [[Blaze Residuum]]
> - [[Blaze Residuum]]
>
> If the party found the [[Letter to Kaftor]] and turned it in, they can achieve the **Outcome: Reported on Mutagist Allies.**
>
> If the party recovered and turned in at least one of the bundles of [[Personal Effects]] that could be found in the forgotten cistern they can achieve the **Outcome: Turned in Victim Personal Effects**.
>
> For their efforts, the party is paid a  **75** for each bundle they turn in (a higher price than what the valuables would likely command if sold).

#### Signara Attunement: All Outcomes

If the party manages to complete all outcomes (recover the Mutagist records, report on the Mutagist allies, and turn-in the personal effects of the victims), each character advances their **Attunement: Signara (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

Once the party is done giving their report, read this aloud, or paraphrase it:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Shaar reaches out a delicate finger and taps their nails on the map near where you found the lab.
>
> > The fact this lab was run under our noses raises numerous very concerning questions. We'll send investigators to scour the location for any further evidence, and secure the location.
> >
> > However, that's going to be the end of your work there, we need to move you to a new task.
>
> Shaar addresses Ankarist:
>
> > Get them up to speed on the situation with the refugees and fliers, then get after the assignment I gave you.
>
> Ankarist nods in acceptance as Shaar steps away. The head of the Chain pauses, regarding you again for a moment.
>
> > Good work finding that lab. Shutting it down will save numerous lives, and hopefully set back these Mutagists and whatever they are trying to achieve. Perhaps you can replicate this success on your next assignment.
>
> With that said, Shaar steps away, leaving the hall directly.

### Update on Missing Persons

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Ankarist watches Shaar go then looks to you.
>
> > First of all, like Shaar said: good work. I don't like that the Mutagists have turned up again, and especially don't like that they are in Ordain now. However, Shaar and I will send a team to clean up the lab and gather whatever evidence you left behind.
> >
> > In the meantime, I'm putting you on another task…
>
> Ankarist shifts some papers about, setting a flier down on the table in front of you. In bold text it proclaims:
>
> > THE CINDARICS HAVE LET US DOWN
>
> The following text bemoans the state of the city, the sickness in the burns, refugees being turned away from temples, people being left to sleep on the streets with nowhere to go.
>
> Ankarist slides another flier to sit beside it, featuring the same style and same bold text, this time stating:
>
> > THE HALLOWS IS INEFFECTIVE
>
> The text below it points out that refugees coming into the city are choking the skywalk, slowing down commerce, that they are being preyed upon by criminals and unscrupulous merchants, that the Hallows and the courts are doing nothing to make the Trade Houses and Cindarics address the issues in Ordain, or beyond.
>
> Both fliers end the same way:
>
> > We will not suffer their indifference. We will not be subordinate. We will unite and be heard.
>
> Ankarist cross his arms over his chest and exhales out of clear frustration.
>
> > These have been circulating through the city, specifically through refugee-heavy areas. It's causing agitation in the city, especially among the refugees coming in from the golden flats. I don't need to tell you that we don't need more issues piled on top of the one's we've already got.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Do the fliers have a point?
>
> **A:**
>
> Ankarist grimaces as his eyes move to the fliers.
>
> > I hate to admit it, but, yes they do. The city is usually quiet, but with the influx of refugees, the rise of banditry in the flats, and now the illness? On top of all this, the Cindarics seem scattered. They aren't doing nothing, but they could be doing more.
> >
> > That's before we even get to the issues inside the city lately: Crime's spiked: burglaries, assaults, theft. This also means court volume's spiked. The Hallows has a lot to keep under control, and the city doesn't have a dedicated peacekeeping force.
> >
> > Using the Anachraenum and Burnished Hand is hardly ideal, either. A bunch of armed historians and monster hunters aren't the sort of people you send to keep peace in a city.
>
> Ankarist waves a hand at the fliers.
>
> > This isn't what we need right now, but it's not like there's no truth to it…

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Where are the fliers coming from?
>
> **A:**
>
> Ankarist lifts his shoulders in a small shrug.
>
> > We haven't tracked down the source yet. Any time we send investigators out to find the source they come back empty-handed. These fliers are being made somewhere and handed out by a group of people, that much we are sure about.
> >
> > They must know how to identify our agents, which raises some unpleasant questions about operation security, but that's a problem for the Chain's upper echelons.
>
> He taps the fliers.
>
> > This is the current concern.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What can the party do to help?
>
> **A:**
>
> > You're not officially part of the Chain, and we're banking on you not being a known quantity to whoever is agitating the populace. You're going to head out into the city and see if you can figure out who the source is and where they are operating out of.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Where should investigations begin?
>
> **A:**
>
> Ankarist indicates two locations on the city map:
>
> > We've identified two hotspots where the fliers are turning up a lot: Foxlairs or Palisade Cross. Hit them both, see what you can find.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why Foxlairs?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The refugees coming in from the flats are bringing their pack animals and livestock in droves, and they need somewhere to put them. Foxlairs has numerous stables, they are going to be here in large numbers. Expect the agitators to be handing out fliers there.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why Palisade Cross?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Palisade Cross has a lot of low-cost housing and several large inns, usuually it's where incoming sailors from the docks end up, but the refugees have flooded the area as of late.
> >
> > Secondarily, it's home to a sizeable population of lower income Ordani, and they are likely feeling the pinch with the city's resources being stretched thin. If I were looking to agitate the city populace, I'd also be targeting them.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Should we shut this operation down?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Short answer: yes.
> >
> > The longer answer is that the Chain's getting spread thin with things going on, so we don't have the assets to run strict oversight on this, plus, we're concerned out presence would compromise you. So… you'll be on your own for this investigation.
> >
> > You need to figure out where these fliers are coming from, and shut down the source. Confiscate the materials they are using, disable any machines or duplication magic they are using, and capture anyone involved.
> >
> > These people are actively turning the city populace against its own government. If left unchecked, they could destabilize the city, and the last thing we need are riots in the streets.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What new assignment are you on?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Sorry, that's been deemed need-to-know, and it's not something I can divulge. I can guarantee you that it's important, and related to everything else going on.
