# Ill Tidings

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This social event sees the party meeting with [[Ankarist]], [[Desham "Shaar" Phos]] and [[Serethus]] to go over what information they have about the current illness, and to hand out assignments to the party. During this event the party can:
>
> - Learn about the sickness that is spreading through the city.
> - Learn that there's also a missing person's case ongoing.
> - Get guidance and suggestions on where to go from Serethus.

### The Briefing

> [!abstract] Ankarist
> **[[Ankarist]]**
>
> Level 2 · Drakon Veiled Chain Investigator
>
> ![Ankarist](../../../assets/image096.webp)
>
> You observe a stern Drakon warrior with a determined expression and piercing golden eyes. Clad in leather armor reinforced with steel, his martial prowess is immediately apparent in the way he handles the hefty greatsword at his side — a hulking blade with a wide, flared tip. This two-handed brand is obviously venerated by the Drakon, who regards the blade with marked discipline. A cloak pin on his breast bears the symbol of the Veiled Chain, the city of Ordain's noble protectorate.

> [!abstract] Desham "Shaar" Phos
> **[[Desham "Shaar" Phos]]**
>
> Level 1 · Unknown Unknown
>
> ![Desham "Shaar" Phos](../../../assets/image126.webp)

> [!abstract] Serethus
> **[[Serethus]]**
>
> Level 12 (Boss) · Cor'ak Sorcerer
>
> ![Serethus](../../../assets/image127.webp)
>
> Tall and imposing, with a strong, bulky frame typical of the Cor'ak, this man's brown and tan are accented by splashes of vibrant purple. Long, purple horns that frame his head in a regal yet intimidating manner. His teal eyes are striking and seem to pierce through those he engages with. His robes are bright and vibrant, in Ordani fashion, and draped with glittering gems of all shapes and sizes.

If needed, Ankarist gives the party brief introductions to the people at the table:

> [!info] Social
> #### Shaar
>
> > Shaar is the head of the Veiled Chain, an expert in forensics, and likely has the best eye for detail in the city. They are overseeing this meeting and will stand by to lend further aid if needed, but they won’t be part of the main investigation.
>
> #### Serethus
>
> > Serethus is the resident expert on magical rituals, spellcraft, and alchemy. Like you, he is not a member of the Chain, and is attached to lend his expertise. Serethus has ideas on where to start, and I've got plans to assign those leads so we can divide efforts and cover ground rapidly.

Once the party is ready to get into the briefing, Ankarist begins:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Ankarist addresses you directly, as the others seem already up to speed:
>
> > In the past month or so a number of illnesses have stricken the city. While not itself an issue, and not even a Veiled Chain concern, the same illness keeps turning up, and the source is never determined. This is strange, and what drew our attention.
> >
> > It's always the same thing: presents as wasting sickness, with headaches, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, so forth. However, it never responds to typical medical treatment. While it does eventually run its course and vanish, it's remained a medical oddity.
> >
> > As of this month, it's back, and worse than before. Rather than appearing in a few isolated cases and then vanishing, it has struck a good number of people, which means we might have a chance to track it to the source.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### About the Illness
>
> A successful **Medicine (DC 14)** check confirms that the illness does indeed sound like a wasting sickness, which usually stems from tainted food and water. It should be relatively easy to treat with bed rest and fluids, but will keep spreading if the source isn't found.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Any leads on a source?
>
> **A:**
>
> Ankarist says:
>
> > We have only gotten reports on the spike in the illness, not its distribution. That is something that needs to be established though. Maybe that's something you can determine as part of your assignment. If we know where the sick are most concentrated, we can probably find a source.

### A foreboding Interruption

Once the first part of the above briefing is done, read aloud the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you you are discussing the situation, another agent from the Chain approaches, drawing Shaar's attention. They step aside to speak quietly for a moment, then Shaar returns to the group and interrupts:
>
> > Ankarist, something's come up with the missing person's case. I'm going to follow up on that information now. Report in when you have something Ankarist.
>
> Ankarist nods.
>
> > We can take it from here.
>
> Serethus nods as well, saying to Shaar:
>
> > Indeed we can! Wisdom and cunning guide you.
>
> Offering an appreciative nod in response, Shaar steps away, leaving the spreading sickness in the party's hands.

From this point, the briefing can continue.

### Serethus' suggestions

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Serethus looks to Ankarist.
>
> > May I?
>
> Ankarist nods.
>
> > Go right ahead.
>
> Serethus offers a broad smile, and addresses you directly:
>
> > I believe it prudent to cast a wide net, in the hopes of catching more fish, or in this case, information. I would advise you to begin in Lower Ashvale, Lilifeld Stations and the Burns. These locations give you the best chance of finding the source, as it targets the food, health and industry of the city.
> >
> > If there are no viable leads, then that at least helps rule out a few sections of the city!
>
> He falls silent, and waits, as if expecting questions.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why Lower Ashvale?
>
> **A:**
>
> > It has the largest healing house in the city.
>
> He says this as though it were answer enough, but a moment later continues:
>
> > Speak to the healers there and see what they know about the illness. They might have some insight into this disease and how to treat it. If we are lucky, they might even know where to begin looking for a source!
> >
> > I'll note: the symptoms do seem similar to wasting sickness, which is dangerous but treatable.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why Lilifeld Stations?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Well, violence and unrest in the flats has put more pressure on Redrak to deliver grain to feed the city. The increased need to produce food might have resulted in them being less stringent about their standards.
> >
> > That is not to say that Redrak is trying to poison us, just that it could be a cause!
> >
> > To that end, visit the stations during operating hours, speak to foreman Tadrick Soles, and ask to examine the grain silos. Also look over any produce coming in from Redrak.
> >
> > It might be poisoned, or worse, spoiling. If the city's food supply is tainted, the city could be in real trouble.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why The Burns?
>
> **A:**
>
> Serethus grimaces slighty.
>
> > This is a bit unpleasant to say, but, Janix Mance, a well known figure in the arts of Alchemy, is always trying to push the limits on process and method in the forges and workshops around the Burns. Such innovation is vital, but it can lead to recklessness and unforeseen consequences.
> >
> > It's… possible that he may have introduced something into the local smithy ecosystem which would account for illness coming out of the Burns. The smoke from the burns travels far with just a little breeze, and could be making people ill all over.

### Ankarist's Assignments

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Seemingly satisfied, Ankarist straightens up, grabbing a notebook off the table, and tucking it away.
>
> > All right, I have a couple contacts in the Ordinate I want to talk to about this, to see if I can turn up some new leads or get a better overview of things.
> >
> > While I'm doing that, I want you to follow up on Serethus' leads.
>
> Serethus speaks up:
>
> > While I'm no means a mighty adventurer, I nonetheless would like to attend the investigation with the team, and lend my expertise where possible! In fact, we should begin with Lilifeld Stations, I want to see the state of the food coming into the city, last thing we need is a sick populace!
>
> Ankarist nods to Serethus.
>
> > Fine with me.
>
> In response, Serethus beams a toothy grin and grabs his things.
>
> > Excellent, I have some things to tend to right now, but I'll meet you at Lilifeld Station tomorrow, bright and early! This shall be most exciting.
>
> As Serethus leaves Ankarist looks at you.
>
> > Any questions?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** To Ankarist: Do we have to start with the Lilifeld Granary
>
> **A:**
>
> > No, you don't, however Serethus seems to think that's a good place to start. Make sure you go during daytime hours so you can speak to the foreman, and get access to records if you need them.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** To Ankarist: When should we report in?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I'll be chasing down my own leads, and won't be back here for a bit, so there's no point having you report back in after each location is cleared. Instead, report back once you've checked out all three locations and can provide a broader overview of your findings.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> Once questions have duly been answered, the party is cut loose to go investigate, while Ankarist heads off to chase down his own leads. The party should proceed to one of the districts noted by Serethus. It's recommended they start with Lilifeld Station, but they can start with any location they like.
