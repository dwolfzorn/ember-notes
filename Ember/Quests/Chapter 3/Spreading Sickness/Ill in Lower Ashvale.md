# Ill in Lower Ashvale

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This exploration event covers the party’s ongoing investigation into the source and cause of a growing sickness in the city. Since the city’s sick often end up here, there is a good chance the healers have insight into the illness. Here, the characters can:
>
> - Study case files and glean additional information about the illness.
> - Speak with the healers at the house and get their accounts of events.
> - Learn that the situation may be more dire than initially reported.

### Discussing the Disease

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The receptionist places a hand on a stone affixed to her desk and says:
>
> > Malinda to the front desk, please. The Veiled Chain is here to ask about the spreading sickness.
>
> The gem glows and goes dark, flaring with light a few moments later. At the second flash, the receptionist smiles at you and says:
>
> > She'll be right down!
>
> After a few minutes of waiting you spot a dark-haired woman approaching, her uniform as pristine as that of every other staff member here, but adorned with more elaborate designs of gold and red, making her her seniority in the healing house clear.
>
> Stepping up to you, she wastes no time:
>
> > I'm Malinda Felgon, one of the chief healers of this house, and the one overseeing the sickness you're here to investigate.
> >
> > Please come with me.
>
> She begins walking out of the foyer, past rooms filled with the sick and healers attending them, and speaks to you the entire time:
>
> > I’m not surprised the Chain is already on this. I wonder who tipped them off... not that it matters. You've got eyes everywhere. Anyway, about the sickness:
> >
> > We've been doing our best to keep the afflicted comfortable and alive, and the overall outlook is positive for all of them. We're not expecting anyone to die, since all the patients were in generally good health to begin with. We believe they will recover with sufficient bed rest and fluids, but that's only the start of it.
> >
> > The veteran staff have been talking about the situation, and we're seeing a rise in Wasting Sickness cases coming in here and in other healing houses around the city.
> >
> > This could be the real problem: We believe there's a real risk of an epidemic if this isn't dealt with immediately.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What's the epidemic risk?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Since vomiting and diarrhea are common symptoms, there’s serious concern that these symptoms increase transmission, spreading it to others, who in turn spread it themselves.
> >
> > We have been seeing a steady rise in the number of cases here. I've also been in contact with other healing houses around the city and they've noted new cases as well. If this continues, the number of inbound patients would rapidly outstrip available potions and magical healing capabilities.
> >
> > It's not a winnable fight without finding the source.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Epidemic Insight
>
> Characters with **Medicine (DC 14, Passive)** or making a successful **Medicine (DC 16)** check know that these symptoms can contaminate food and water, can easily spread illness through contact, and persist on surfaces for quite some time.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What do you know about the illness?
>
> **A:**
>
> > It presents as an affliction of the bowels, or wasting sickness. Common symptoms are headaches, nausea, vomiting, hot flashes, diarrhea, and dizziness. It's not contagious, but contact with a patient's discharge can spread it.
> >
> > It response to typical wasting sickness treatments: Bed rest, fluids, and fever management at the most basic level, with restoration magic for more sensitive cases.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Where are the sick coming from?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The ill coming in are primarily from the north of the city, with most of the patients we have coming specifically from The Burns. If there's a source, it's probably around there. However, I'm not sure what you'd want to look for. It could be tainted food, tainted water, or something else.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Can we speak with or examine the patients?
>
> **A:**
>
> > No.
> >
> > Not unless you're an accredited healer recognized by the healing house network, or have a signed order from the Hallows. Access to the patients is limited to staff only.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why can't we speak to the patients?
>
> **A:**
>
> Malinda sighs and holds up two fingers.
>
> > Two reasons: Rules, and safety.
> >
> > First, rules states only healing house staff may directly interact with patients, and guests are limited to family and only during visiting hours.
> >
> > Second, these people are sick, some of them in delicate conditions. Letting a bunch of investigators traipse through the healing floors not only risks their safety, but your own.
> >
> > So, if the Veiled Chain wants direct access, they will need to request access through the Ordinate, by way of the Hallows.

### Studying the Disease

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Another healer arrives and approaches Malinda, waving her aside to speak privately. Judging by his uniform, he is a junior member of the house. After a brief exchange, Malinda nods, dismisses him, and turns back to you.
>
> > I have to assist with something on the floor, but before I go I want to give you access to all the medical reports we have on this affliction. Maybe you and the Chain will spot something we didn’t, or use what we’ve learned to find the source.
> >
> > Come with me.
>
> She leads you to an office, stopping briefly to collect several leather binders of reports and papers from a secure room before settling you in a quiet office.
>
> > These are diagnosis and treatment reports, test results, everything that I can legally let you see without a magistrate's orders or healer's accreditation.
> >
> > Leave the files and reports here when you're done, I'll put them back later.
> >
> > There's stationary in the cabinet on the far wall if you need to take notes.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Two Different Problems
>
> There are two elements at play: alchemical toxicity, which is the primary issue, and wasting sickness, which has arisen as a secondary symptom of the toxicity buildup in the city.
>
> The alchemical toxicity lies at the core of this medical mystery. It is an unsourced contamination building up in the afflicted and poisoning them. Its symptoms are deceptively similar to wasting sickness: stomach pain, nausea, vomiting, sometimes diarrhea, dizziness, and weakness. Treatment requires the afflicted to process the toxins out of their bodies naturally or by way of restoration spells.
>
> The wasting sickness afflicting the people of Ordain is mundane and is actually a secondary symptom of the alchemical toxicity. Its symptoms are well understood: diarrhea, abdominal pain, nausea and vomiting, fever, and dehydration (GM’s note: it’s dysentery). Transmission occurs when the sick contaminate their environment. Treatment is simple: clean water, simple foods, and keeping their strength up while they rest.

The first pile of records is a collection of diagnostic reports for all the incoming patients with the current malady. There are several dozen of them.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Diagnosis Records
>
> The first batch of reports are for groups of forge workers, smiths, and artisans arriving arriving from the Burns. They all exhibited the following symptoms:
>
> - Stomach pain, nausea, vomiting, sometimes diarrhea, dizziness, and weakness.
> - Use of [[Detect Poison and Disease]] confirms the afflicted as carrying disease contagions connected to their bodily excretions, and identifies them as a source of a toxin, as though they are venomous.
> - Bowel movements have a distinctly green tinge.
> - The sickness is observed to be infectious by way of exposure to the vomit and excrement of the patients.
>
> All diagnostic reports that follow are either for additional patients arriving from the rest of the city (including additional waves from the Burns), or patients falling ill while inside the healing house already. They all exhibited the following symptoms:
>
> - Diarrhea, abdominal pain, nausea and vomiting, fever, dehydration.
> - Use of [[Detect Poison and Disease]] confirms the afflicted as carrying disease contagions connected to their bodily excretions.
> - The sickness is observed to be infectious by way of exposure to the vomit and excrement of the patients.

Malinda also provided a stack of treatment records for patients which may lend some insight into how the healers have been responding to the illness, and how that illness has been responding to their efforts.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Treatment Records
>
> Treatment reports on the initial patients that arrived from the Burns:
>
> - Weakness, dizziness, and delirium does not respond to basic treatment, indicating that it may not be stemming from dehydration, but some other unknown issue.
> - Healers observed that patient's sweat and tears leave a powdery residue on their skin.
> - Recovery time is slow, fastest (non-magical) recovery time being 19 days with early signs of recovery only presenting after 10+ days in most cases.
> - Use of [[Protection from Poison]] or [[Antitoxin]] have proven effective at reducing symptoms and aiding in recovery, but do not cure the affliction.
> - Use of [[Lesser Restoration]] will fully remove the illness.
>
> Treatment reports on all other patients with this malady include:
>
> - Weakness and dizziness responds to treatment quickly, indicating it may stem from dehydration, which is to be expected.
> - Typical (non-magical) recovery time is 14 days with bed rest, light food and water. Immediate improvement shows as early as 4-7 days.
> - Use of [[Lesser Restoration]] has no effect on the illness.

Once party has finished looking over the medical reports, they can begin to piece together the nature of the illness, and get some indication of what is going on.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Analyzing The Data
>
> Poring over the files takes several hours of work, which can include a short rest. During this time, a character needs to succeed on a **Medicine (DC 16)** check to glean additional information. Characters with **Knowledge: Alchemy** have advantage on this check.
>
> - Characters with **Knowledge: Alchemy** know that improperly handled alchemical compounds can cause many symptoms similar to what's noted here, presenting as a mundane illness that refuses to respond to treatment.
> - **Result of 16+** The epicenter of this malady seems to be the Burns. However, the initial cases are clearly different from all following cases. It is clear that there's a primary malady, and a secondary illness stemming from it, likely a typical affliction of the bowels, or wasting sickness.
> - **Result of 18+** The illness sourced from the Burns seems to be stemming from some intrinsic source of infection or sickness in the patient. In many cases it almost seems like more of a poison or other implanted toxin. The patients don't seem to recover until their bodies have processed it out.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Investigation Takeaways
>
> The party should conclude the following:
>
> - The illness is sourced from the Burns, and whatever the initial patients from there had was somehow different from the wasting sickness that's spreading in its wake.

#### Mayis Attunement: Analyzing the Data

If the party manages to successfully analyze the data, each character who participates advances their **Attunement: Mayis (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Concluding the Event

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Next Steps
>
> After the characters finish asking their questions and reviewing medical data, they're free to continue their investigation elsewhere. They should proceed to the Burns or Lilifeld Stations next, though the investigation here should more strongly indicate the Burns as the next stopping point.
