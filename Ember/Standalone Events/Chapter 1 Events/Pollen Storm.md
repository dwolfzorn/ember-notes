# Pollen Storm

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This standalone event occurs in the [[Verdant Paths]] as a result of the region's dynamic weather, and happens no matter what pace the party has set for their travel. In this standalone event, the characters can:
>
> - React to the sudden arrival of a pollen storm, a manifestation of Ember's preternatural wonder and relative unpredictability.
> - Avoid the pollen storm's allergens, or else suffer the effects of them.
> - Experience one of a few brief encounters if they manage to avoid the storm.
>
> This event takes place on [[The Arctus Plateau]] Region Map. It is guaranteed to occur when the party first encounters a Pollen Storm and will not reoccur for at least 24 hours.

The pollen storm arrives thoroughly and with haste — whether the party is prepared or not. The characters must respond immediately to this environmental threat, which can have a negative impact on their short-term health (both mentally and physically). And if the characters act quickly enough, the untimely circumstances could prove to be nothing more than an inconvenience (if not the opportunity for a chance discovery as well).

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Environmental Mechanics
>
> Any area confronted with a pollen storm is subject to the following mechanical considerations:
>
> - The area is lightly obscured, impairing the vision of those within it.
> - Ranged attacks have **-2 Banes** when the target is 100 or more feet away.
> - Characters who don't succeed on the requisite checks are subject to the hazardous effects of the pollen itself (see "Pollen Contamination" below for details).

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Detecting & Avoiding the Storm
>
> If one or more characters has a successful **Awareness (DC 15, Passive)** check, the party is able to detect the storm early enough to make an active attempt to avoid it, granting them `[[/roll 2d5]]` turns to act before the cloudburst of pollen is upon them.
>
> - Characters with **Attunement: Mayis**, **Knowledge: Tracking**, or **Knowledge: Weather** have **+2 Boons**.
> - **Critical Success**: The party can also react to the imminent threat by participating in one of the Pollen Storm Encounters detailed below, which they discover while observing the storm.

Roll on (or choose from) the following table to determine what kind of encounter awaits the party when they react to the imminent threat of the pollen storm.

|  | #### Pollen Storm Encounters |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | #### Welcome Shelter  The characters discover a nearby cave or abandoned hovel, where they can take shelter from the storm as long as they need (the advent of additional encounters notwithstanding). |
| 2 | #### A Breath of Fresh Air  The party spots a jagged crevasse that splits the landscape, spewing warm subterranean air into the atmosphere above. This geothermal wind flow purifies the nearby air enough for the party to breathe here without hazard. |
| 3 | #### Shadowbark Oaks  The characters happen upon a copse of shadowbark oaks, whose arcane boughs shield the group from the pollen storm with traces of [[Primordis]] transmutation magic. |
| 4 | #### Ancient Purification  The party stumbles upon the sparse ruin of some ancient [[Shent]] structure or monolith, which vacuums the particle-laden air into its stony, abstruse form. The air surrounding this abandoned, rune-marked curiosity is remarkably clean. |
| 5 | #### Wandering Alchemist  A lone [[Arcturian Alchemist]] clad in protective gear wanders through the storm, harvesting the pollen for resale in a nearby settlement. Perhaps they have an extra [[Arcturian Respirator]] for sale, or a tent where the party can rest a while. |
| 6 | #### Abandoned Wagon  The party discovers the shelter of a hastily-abandoned wagon, which provides a small amount of solace from the storm. Characters who take a few moments to search the vehicle can roll `[[/roll 1d2]]` times on the [[Corpse Loot]] table. |

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Surveying the Pollen Storm
>
> The characters can spend one or more turns in an attempt to assess the storm's nature and the potential threat it poses to the party.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Wilderness (DC 13)** check is somewhat familiar with the Arctus Plateau's infamous pollen storms, and can provide some insight into their behavior and effects: the pollen can be damaging to one's intelligence, and is outright poisonous if inhaled or via orificial contacted in large quantities.
>
> - Characters with **Culture: Arcturian**, **Knowledge: Plants**, or **Knowledge: Weather** have **+2 Boons**.
> - **Critical Success**: The character is familiar with some of the natural lore pertaining to these storms, including the speculation of regional scholars — some of which posits that the pollen is inexplicably produced by Ember itself to pacify the creatures who roam its surface from time to time.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 15)** check knows the actual [[Storm Pollen]] itself to be a potent material component in certain illusion magic, as well as a common ingredient in a few Arcturian crafting recipes.
>
> - Characters with **Knowledge: Alchemy**, **Knowledge: Crafts**, or **Knowledge: Plants** have **+2 Boons**.
> - **Result of 21+**: The character is able to safely harvest `[[/roll 1d3]]` doses of [[Storm Pollen]] during the course of this encounter.
>
> #### Protective Measures
>
> Adequate methods of protection from the storm pollen include:
>
> - A suitable breathing mask or apparatus like an [[Arcturian Respirator]]. Alternately, an improvised mask provides **+2 Boons** on hazard checks made to withstand the pollen.
> - A timely dose of [[Dustweed]], which acts as an antidote to the pollen.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Pollen Contamination
>
> Characters who inhale or make orificial contact with storm pollen must face **Hazard 8 (Fortitude, Morale, Harmless)** to withstand its effect, which includes the following based on the result:
>
> - The character has **-2 Banes** on all Knowledge skill checks and is **Staggered**.
> - **Critical Failure**: The character also begins a coughing spurt and suffers **Hazard 4 (Fortitude, Severe)**.
>
> The negative effects of the pollen last for a duration of `[[/roll 2d4 hours]]` and can be cured by taking a **Rest**, by targeting the afflicted character with **Touch of Life**, or by consumption of an [[Antitoxin]].
>
> A character can only be affected by this pollen contamination once per day.

Read the following aloud the first time one or more characters become afflicted with the pollen's negative effects:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Particles of this curious pollen make their way into your system, and you find your senses warped by the strange allergen. As your eyes itch and your wits narrow, the world around you becomes ever-so-slightly more difficult to comprehend.

### Concluding the Event

The Pollen Storm event concludes once the characters have either successfully avoided the storm or endured its negative effects. Although the weather condition itself can endure (and potentially repeat), each character can only be affected by this pollen contamination once per day.
