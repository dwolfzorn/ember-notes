# Sanguinary Salutations

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Social and Exploration Event can occur during the day while traveling through the [[Golden Flats]]. In this Event, the party can:
>
> - Meet Eliren, a Sanguinary druid, and speak with her about the [[Sanguinaries]] history and philosophy.
> - Examine creatures recently transmuted by an arcane maelstrom, assist Eliren with her observations, and potentially contribute a useful insight to her work.

If the characters approach Eliren, read or paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The young woman startles at your approach, but quickly recovers with a warm smile.
>
> > Greetings!
>
> She withdraws her hands from the yarnac's chest and gives them a few brisk shakes. Then she extends one — still bloodied and coated in a strange, milky film — toward you in greeting.
>
> > My name is Eliren. On behalf of the Sanguinaries and myself, I offer you good tidings. With whom do I have the pleasure of speaking?

> [!info] Social
> #### Eliren, the Wandering Sanguinary
>
> **Eliren** (Neutral Good, Ordani Human, she/her) is a [[Sanguinary Druid]] tasked by [[Avwynn Taol]] with studying the effects of arcane maelstroms on the creatures of the Golden Flats. Kindly, soft-spoken, and patient, she is more than willing to engage the characters in light conversation about her current task (see "Eliren's Task" below) and the Sanguinaries' history and philosophy.
>
> A successful **Deception (DC 13)** check confirms that Eliren is speaking sincerely, and believes what she says to be true.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What are you doing out here?
>
> **A:**
>
> > "I'm studying the aftermath of a recent arcane maelstrom. These poor creatures were caught in its path, and I mean to learn what I can from what remains of them."
>
> Eliren's smile never dims, though her attention keeps drifting impatiently back toward the carcasses and specimen jars nearby.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who are the Sanguinaries?
>
> **A:**
>
> > "We seek to nurture life, wheresoever it may be found. There are many who mistrust us… and not always without reason. The Cindarics, for their part, have painted us as radicals since the schism. And, regrettably, there are elements within our organization who would prove them correct. We do not claim them."
>
> A trace of guardedness enters her expression; she is braced for suspicion. She wipes her still-bloodied hands reflexively on her robe.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** How does your work here relate?
>
> **A:**
>
> > "These creatures were transmuted into unnatural forms. If their bodies were so violently remade, what then became of their spirit? And what happens to that scarred energy after death? It is my task to determine whether these maelstroms pose a threat beyond what we can see."
>
> Her smile dips momentarily at the weight of her own words.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Eliren's Task
>
> Several creatures lie before Eliren, each mangled and grotesquely transmuted by the maelstrom's effects. When the characters arrive, Eliren has only just begun dissecting the yarnac, systematically removing its organs and preserving them in pickling jars for future examination. The creatures are:
>
> - The [[Yarnac]], its hide flayed and replaced by a translucent, sticky film
> - A [[Gore Bird]], its wings fused together by a brittle crystalline lattice
> - A Tiller Bird, its beak and talons overgrown and curled inward
>
> Eliren's primary concern — and one she will not voice out loud to the characters at this juncture — is whether these transmutations have altered the creatures' Lifeforce. If their Lifeforce was scarred, it may pose a threat to the [[Heart of Ember]]. Unfortunately, she has no way of proving this one way or another; for all her careful study, the question remains as philosophical as it is scientific.
>
> #### Helping Eliren
>
> Eliren gratefully accepts another pair of eyes (and hands). Any character who spends at least 1 hour examining the transmuted remains alongside Eliren can attempt one of the following checks:
>
> - **Arcana (DC 13)** The character finds that residual Transmutation magic still clings to the remains, but not evenly; some parts of the creature are more deeply altered than others.
> - **Medicine (DC 13)** The character discovers that the transmutations occurred suddenly, rather than over time; the uneven distribution of alteration suggests spontaneity.
> - **`[[/skill wilderness 13]]`** The character realizes that the transmutations appear to warp the creature's most vital adaptations: the yarnac its hide, the gore bird its wings, and the tiller bird its beak and talons.
>
> Regardless of whether the characters succeed on a check or not, Eliren is grateful for the assistance, and bids farewell — she stuffs the pickling jars into a large satchel and begins walking in the direction of Ordain. Once Eliren departs, the characters may scavenge the creatures' crafting components.

### Concluding the Event

Once the party has finished speaking with and assisting Eliren, they're free to continue on their journey.
