# Flash Flood

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Exploration Event can occur while traveling through the [[Splinter Canyons]] on any river-adjacent hex or hex containing a river. In this Event, the party can:
>
> - Demonstrate their wilderness survival skills by withstanding a deadly flash flood.
>
> The flash flood occurs over the course of 10 minutes, affecting all hexes inside the Splinter Canyons and escalating in intensity every 5 minutes. This event is more likely to occur if the party is traveling at a Fast or Reckless pace. Once the Event has occurred, it cannot occur again for at least 48 hours.
>
> #### Stages of Flooding
>
> The party must survive two stages of flooding:
>
> - [[Flash Flood]]
> - [[Flash Flood]]
>
> Each stage represents a Group Check — if at least half of the characters succeed, they proceed to the next stage of flooding without incident. If over half of the characters fail, however, roll on the [[Flash Flood]] table. After resolving the hazard, the party proceeds to the next stage of flooding.

At the beginning of each stage, the party may describe any tools they're utilizing to aid them.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Preparedness
>
> Appropriate tools, equipment, magic, or other precautions may grant **+2 Boons** on a relevant roll or an automatic success, per the Gamemaster's discretion.
>
> Such preparations may include: pitons, magical flight, floatation devices, spells that can be used to control water, or any other sensible method of withstanding rushing water.

### Stage 1: Rising Waters

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The river in the crevasse swells, drawing even with its banks. Though the waters look calm, they are rapidly intensifying by the minute. Even now, rivulets begin pooling beneath you and spilling through well-worn channels toward the canyon walls. It won't be long until the entire canyon is overcome by white-foamed rapids.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Stage 1: Rising Waters
>
> The characters must react to the rising floodwaters before they overtake the canyon floor with a group skill challenge. Every member of the party must make one of the following tests:
>
> - A successful **Athletics (DC 16)** check to pull themselves onto firmer footing, secure a handhold, or brace themselves against a perpendicular surface to withstand the oncoming surge.
> - A successful **`[[/skill wilderness 16 group]]`** check to stay ahead of the spillover, darting across slick ground before the flood closes around them.
>
> If the group check is failed, roll on the [[Flash Flood]] table.

### Stage 2: Canyon Rapids

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The river picks up speed, roaring through the canyons as a churn of white-foamed wrath; the water has swelled all the way to the canyon walls. It fills the canyon with a deafening rush. Branches, debris, and a few small, unlucky beasts caught in the flood's path are dashed against the cliff face and vanish beneath the white, borne swiftly to their deaths.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Stage 2: Canyon Rapids
>
> The characters must struggle to survive the canyon rapids without being swept away with a group skill challenge. Every member of the party must make one of the following tests:
>
> - A successful **Athletics (DC 18)** check to use their strength to cling to vines, roots, or stone outcroppings, withstanding the torrent by brute force.
> - A successful **Athletics (DC 18)** check to move with the rushing water rather than against it, keeping their footing or timing their movements to avoid debris, sudden drops, and the worst of the current.
>
> If the group check is failed, roll on the [[Flash Flood]] table.

### Rushing Water Perils

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Drowning
>
> A character that is forced beneath the water from the effects of a [[Flash Flood]] begins to &Reference[Suffocation]{Suffocate}. The number of minutes the creature Suffocates is determined by the table result.

### Concluding the Event

The flash flood rapidly subsides, allowing the characters to regroup, recover, and continue on their journey.
