# Rending Teeth

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This combat event takes place on the **Fogbound Caverns** area map using the **Lake of Whispers** composition. The party finds themselves surrounded by a large number of [[Abyssal Eel]]. They can either:
>
> - Recognize that the monsters have limited senses, and can be navigated around with the party being detected.
> - Survive combat with the ravenous swarm.

The event begins with the party in the center of the Area Map in a small depression, having been silently surrounded by Abyssal Eels as they moved through the [[Fogbound Caverns]]. Initiative is not rolled immediately as the eels have limited perceptive capabilities.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Assessing the Situation
>
> The party can reach the other side of the area and safely escape if they realize that the Abyssal Eels are not actively hunting them nor moving in their direction.
>
> With a successful **Awareness (DC 17, Passive)**, **Awareness (DC 15)**, or **Awareness (DC 15)** check, characters notice that the Abyssal Eels are ignoring the party and have not changed course or begun moving in any particular direction.
>
> 1. **Result of 17+** While the Eels are ignoring the party, characters can tell that they are clearly aggressive predators, with small scraps of living matter stuck in their jaws. They also react sharply to any movement from each other, seeming to have a hair trigger to attack.
> 2. **Result of 19+** By studying the Abyssal Eels closely, characters observe that they only react to movement within a very small area around them, roughly 10 feet, and can see for an additional 20 feet. It might be possible to zig-zag between them and navigate through the area without them sensing the party if they move with stealth.
>
> With a successful **Wilderness (DC 17)** check, characters are aware that Abyssal Eels, while dangerous, rely on nearby movement to find their prey. Characters with **Knowledge: Monsters** or **Knowledge: Abyssals** have advantage on this check.
>
> - **Result of 19+** Characters know that the perception limit for Abyssal Eels is 30 feet. If characters remain outside of that limit, or succeed in moving stealthily within that limit, they can get by the group without triggering combat.
>
> #### Evading the Eels
>
> The party can evade the eels by hiding from their rudimentary senses, but the eels' constant movement makes this difficult. To sneak by without getting the eels' attention, the party must succeed on a group stealth check of `[[/check stealth 15]]` (if half or more of the group succeeds, the group succeeds on the check).
>
> If the party has knowledge of the eels by succeeding on either of the more difficult checks above (those requiring an outcome of 19 or greater), they have advantage on the check as they stay ahead of the eels' predicted movements.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Abyssal Eel Tactics
>
> If the party attacks one of the Abyssal Eels or they move within the Abyssal Eel's blindsight (30 feet) without succeeding on a Stealth check, a combat encounter begins as the eels swarm into a feeding frenzy. When combat begins, the party should roll initiative. Abyssal Eels are added to initiative order as a single entity.
>
> The encounter with the Abyssal Eels may quickly become overwhelming and chaotic, as they will try to occupy the spaces of each other and of the player characters using [[Frenzied Swarm]]. The Abyssal Eels are voracious, swarming, predatory monsters with no regard for their own self-preservation; they fight to the death.
