# Voices in the Square

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This repeatable Social Event can occur while traveling through Speaker's Square in the Hallows. In this Event, the party can:
>
> - Witness an Ordani citizen publicly air a grievance that reflects class tensions within Ordain.
> - Ask them questions after their speech to gain additional insight.
>
> #### Randomization Steps
>
> To run this Standalone Event, follow these steps:
>
> 1. Roll on (or choose from) the [[Voices in the Square]] table.
> 2. Follow the entry's embedded link to the appropriate section.
> 3. Read or paraphrase that section's read-aloud text, then allow the party to ask the speaker questions.

### Grievance: Quarry Run Justice

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> An older woman stands before the crowd, flanked by a few grim-faced relatives and neighbors. As she begins to speak, her voice breaks; someone gives her arm a reassuring squeeze. When she begins again, her voice carries clean over the din, more powerful than her slight frame would suggest.
>
> > "My son died in the Stonehollow Quake, along with over a hundred others in Quarry Run. They died because House Cevher wanted more from us — they died so House Cevher could fill its coffers. Their precious Tradeway didn't so much as shake, but our bridges collapsed, our homes split open, and our people were buried under stone. For some, it was instantaneous. For an unlucky few, it took weeks to die. Our dead line the foundations of House Cevher's prosperity. More than a hundred gone, and still they admit to nothing. They speak around it as if we're fools, as if we don't know exactly what happened. I don't want their pity, and I don't want their gold. I want them to stand up and admit what they did!"

> [!info] Social
> #### Following Up
>
> **Bassita Noral** (Neutral Good, Ordani Human, she/her) is an [[Ordani]] woman who lost her son — Jonar Noral — during the Stonehollow Quake. She regularly comes to Speaker's Square to demand acknowledgement from House Cevher for their role in the disaster. Unbeknownst to her, the disaster had nothing to do with House Cevher's actions. She's willing to engage the characters in conversation about Quarry Run, the disaster, her mistrust of House Cevher, and her son's memory.
>
> **Culture: Ordani** characters and characters with **Knowledge: Politics** recall that House Cevher has invested significant resources in rebuilding Quarry Run following the Stonehollow Quake, though it's true that they've never publicly admitted any fault.
>
> A successful **Diplomacy (DC 12)** check confirms that Bassita is speaking sincerely, and believes what she says to be true.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What happened to your son?
>
> **A:**
>
> Bassita's jaw tightens.
>
> > "Jonar was helping reinforce one of the walkways when the quake hit. They pulled him out alive, or so I was told, but… they told me it was internal trauma. He lingered for days — my boy was a fighter — it took days for the pain to take him."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why do you blame House Cevher?
>
> **A:**
>
> > "Because everyone in Quarry Run knew they were digging too deep, too fast, and asking more than what we had. Then the quake happened, and — somehow — their Tradeway survived."
>
> Bassita gives a bitter laugh.
>
> > "You know it was the first thing I saw when the dust cleared? The Tradeway. Our whole world had slipped out from beneath our feet, and their silver tower kept standing. They profited, and we paid."

#### Akon Attunement: The Grieving Mother

If the party listens to Bassita and engages her in conversation, they advance their **Attunement: Akon (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Grievance: The Ailing Jon Vastil

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A well-dressed young man, his posture impossibly straight, his hands folded neatly at his waist, waits for quiet before speaking. When he does speak, his voice is gentle, his cadence careful and practiced, almost lulling.
>
> > "None can deny the years of service that Jon Vastil has given to Ordain. His wisdom has guided this city through hardship before, and I would not be so ungrateful as to speak lightly of his… condition. But illness changes the mind as readily as it does the body. It narrows a person's vision to the scope of their navel. And while the Holy Speaker withers within Cindarin Temple, the burdens of Ordain's commonfolk do not diminish with him. Ordain still suffers. Ordain still hungers for guidance. If Jon Vastil can no longer carry that burden as he once did, then perhaps it is time for others to step forward and serve the people's interests!"

> [!info] Social
> #### Following Up
>
> **Darin Voss** (Lawful Evil, Ordani Human, he/him) is a young, ambitious [[Ordani]] devotee of the Cindaric Sages who has recently begun speaking more boldly in public about Jon Vastil's decline. Outwardly, he presents himself as respectful, pious, and concerned for the city's future. In truth, he sees the Holy Speaker's illness as an opportunity to rise within Ordain's religious institution. He's willing to engage the characters in conversation about Jon Vastil, the [[Cindaric Sages]], the city's spiritual needs, and the failures of aging leadership.
>
> **Culture: Ordani** characters and characters with Proficiency in **`[[/skill Religion]]`** know that Jon Vastil has long been a revered spiritual figure in Ordain, though criticism of his detachment and declining health has grown in recent years.
>
> A successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check suggests that Darin's concern is not entirely selfless: he believes some of what he says, but is barely masking his own naked ambition.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why is Jon Vastil unfit to lead?
>
> **A:**
>
> > "I think illness can rob the senses of anyone, even the wisest amongst us. Ordain has needs that can't wait for a miracle. If the Holy Speaker can still rise to meet those challenges, then let him. If not… others must be ready to take up the charge."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What do you want to happen?
>
> **A:**
>
> Darin folds his hands.
>
> > "I want Ordain to be guided by someone who can see clearly, someone who hasn't grown myopic with sickness and age. If that means others must shoulder the Holy Speaker's burdens — if only for a time — then so be it. Ordain deserves someone to take action on its behalf."

#### Luxarum Attunement: The Ambitious Devotee

If the party listens to Darin and engages him in conversation, they advance their **Attunement: Luxarum (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Grievance: Disputes in Pine Harbor

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A cluster of longshoremen has gathered at the center of a crowd, broad-shouldered, hearty, and wearing the salt-stained clothes of their trade. One older gentleman stands half a head above the rest, speaking animatedly; the others nod along, arms folded, occasionally grunting their agreement.
>
> > "Pine Harbor runs because working folk haul. Because working folk lift. Because working folk lash. Because working folk break themselves in body and spirit to keep it moving. Let me ask you — have you ever seen one of those Trading House loafers lift a finger at the docks? And yet, every year, the Trading Houses reach for a little more of it. More control over the docks. More say over the hours. More fingers in our pockets! The Dockmasters talk about 'order' and 'efficiency.' But for who? They'd sell the whole harbor piecemeal if it meant a pat on the head from their masters in the counting houses! Enough is enough. Pine Harbor belongs to the union, and we will not capitulate!"

> [!info] Social
> #### Following Up
>
> **Jonel Regash** (Lawful Good, Ordani Human, he/him) is a veteran [[Ordani]] longshoreman and loud partisan of the Longshoreman's Union in Pine Harbor. He has spent over two decades loading ships, settling dockside disputes, and watching the Trading Houses accumulate more control over harbor life. He's willing to engage the characters in conversation about the union, the Dockmasters, the Trading Houses, and labor conditions in Pine Harbor.
>
> **Culture: Ordani** characters and characters with **Knowledge: Politics** know that Pine Harbor has long depended on a fragile and fluctuating parity between Dockmasters, the Longshoreman's Union, and the Trading Houses. Many locals have strong opinions about who truly keeps the district running.
>
> A successful **Diplomacy (DC 12)** check confirms that Jonel is genuinely angry on behalf of his fellow longshoremen, though he is also performing a little for the crowd to hold their attention.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What are the Trading Houses trying to do?
>
> **A:**
>
> Jonel snorts.
>
> > "Same thing as they always do — take. And they'll keep taking until there's nothing left, unless we stop them."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What does the Union want?
>
> **A:**
>
> > "Only a fair say in the work that keeps Ordain prosperous. Reasonable hours and fair wages. Dockmasters who answer to the people breaking their backs, not to the coin-counters who've never done a hard day's labor in their lives."

#### Ragen Attunement: The Fiery Longshoreman

If the party listens to Jonel and engages him in conversation, they advance their **Attunement: Ragen (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Grievance: Restaurant Unionization

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A young woman stands alone atop an overturned crate, shoulders set rigid as if she might need to run at any moment; her right wrist is marked by a fresh burn, and she reflexively rubs it with her thumb. When she speaks, her voice is deliberate and full of odd pauses, as though she has practiced and forgotten these words many times in private.
>
> > "The establishments of Restaurant Row are held together by people working too fast, too long, and too close to the knife's edge. Every week it feels like something changes — some shipment gone missing, a new demand, or some new ingredient we all have to build our menus around because House Lillifeld says it matters. And when things get hard, it's never the House that bears the burden, but us, the people cooking your meals, carrying your plates, feeding you, caring for you. I don't think it's wrong to want steadier hours, fairer treatment, and some say in the work we devote our lives to. I only think it's past time that we asked for it."

> [!info] Social
> #### Following Up
>
> **Sissana Alatrov** (Lawful Good, Ordani Human, she/her) is a young [[Ordani]] cook from Restaurant Row who has recently begun speaking in favor of labor organization among the district's workers. She is nervous in front of a crowd, but committed enough to keep showing up despite that fear. She's willing to engage the characters in conversation about House Lillifeld, working conditions, and her mission to unionize the district's cooks, servers, and runners.
>
> **Culture: Ordani** characters and characters with **Knowledge: Politics** or **Knowledge: Trade** know that House Lillifeld exerts significant influence over Restaurant Row's supply chains and culinary trends, shaping what its businesses offer on a daily basis.
>
> A successful **Diplomacy (DC 12)** check confirms that Sissana is frightened, but earnest, and fully believes in her cause.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why are workers talking about unionizing?
>
> **A:**
>
> Sissana rubs at the burn on her wrist before answering.
>
> > "Because we're all tired. We're tired of the shortages, of the constant changes, of not having control over our own menus, and most of all, we're tired of getting blamed when something goes wrong. We believe we have a better chance at getting fair treatment if we're together."

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What is House Lillifeld doing wrong?
>
> **A:**
>
> Sissana hesitates before answering.
>
> > "It's never only one thing. It's… a lot of things. Supply chains shifting, our menus getting voided, prices and expectations rising. And somehow the people making these decisions are never the ones getting burned, cut, shouted at, or sent home exhausted."

#### Cora Attunement: The Undaunted Cook

If the party listens to Sissana and engages her in conversation, they advance their **Attunement: Cora (+1)** at the conclusion of the event.

### Concluding the Event

Once the party has finished listening to the aggrieved speaker and asking them questions, they're free to continue on their journey.
