# Sunken Entrance

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Four ancient basalt pillars rise out of the shallow marsh, their surfaces weathered and pitted by countless seasons. Muck and clinging moss coat their bases, while faint carvings, almost erased by time, are still just visible from the right angle.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Ancient History
>
> Spending time studying the ruins as the party travels allows for a **Society (DC 18)** check. If successful, the character realizes the ruins belong to the ancient [[Sedyri]] culture, and vastly predate the city built over top them.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Legends** have **+2 Boons** on this roll.

### Setting the Pillars

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You see four ancient basalt pillars standing together, rising out of the marshy water. Their dark faces are pitted and softened by time, but still bear collections of intricate carvings that seemingly refuse to yield to the ravages of time and elements.
>
> The forms cut into the stone are easily identifiable as symbols for fire, ice, earth, wind, and many more. They are laid out in such a way that none have prominence over the others.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Symbols
>
> Each of the pillars have three symbols, and none of the symbols are shared between pillars. Counterclockwise from the northernmost pillar, the following symbols appear:
>
> - **Pillar one**: earth, wind, fire,
> - **Pillar two**: rain, earth, steam
> - **Pillar three**: water, ice, stone
> - **Pillar four**: magma, snow, air
>
> Examining the symbols with a successful **Society (DC 16)** considers that if the ruins are Sedyri in nature, which they are, then their settlements often had prevalent themes of fire and water. Characters with **Knowledge: Legends** have **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Result 20+ Another significant element of the Ashka culture were coastal settlements, and volcano iconography. Perhaps they might be represented in the symbols somehow.
>
> #### Activation Sequence
>
> These symbols need to be activated on their respective pillars to open the way forward: fire, water, magma, steam.
>
> Note that the order doesn't matter, but the combination represents the core water and fire elements, and what you get when they are combined. Magma is not only referential to the Ashka history, but represents a form of liquid fire.
>
> #### Scrutinizing the Pillars
>
> Examining the pillars with a successful **Arcana (DC 14)** check recognizes spell work carved into the stone around the symbols, with divination and evocation elements being prominent. It's possible these symbols react to being touched, and need to be activated in a specific way.
>
> - **Result of 16+** The character notes that there is a faint charm effect included in the stones, and is meant to affect the viewer, but what it's meant to do beyond that is unclear.
>
> #### Ancestral Magic
>
> A character with the **Ancestry: Ashka** will find themselves strangely drawn to these symbols, as though something about them resonates with their very blood.
>
> They can make a **Deception (DC 14)** check while examining the stones. On success they intuit one of the symbols they need to activate. They can do this multiple times, but the DC increases by +2 each time they successfully intuit a correct symbol, first time included.

### Opening the Passage

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The four symbols glow brightly and briefly then fade out. A moment later the water stirs, and bubbles roil up to the surface as stone slides away below you, revealing an passage of stairs that descend into darkness.
>
> The path almost certainly leads into some greater structure beyond and below, but accessing it will require some swimming.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Soaked Through
>
> Characters taking this passage will need to swim for about 25 feet, most should be able to do so safely, but they will be soaked through in the process, potentially ruining any items that shouldn't be gotten wet, like books, paper, food, and the like.
>
> This also renders things like torches unusable until dried.
>
> #### Contamination Hazard
>
> As the water is tainted by disease, rot and chemicals, eating any food exposed to the water requires a `[[/save con 14]]` or inflicts the &reference[poisoned] condition for an hour.

### Entering the Secret Lab

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Guided by the vile fluid underfoot, you follow the winding tunnels until the passage opens into an enormous underground cistern. The air here is warmer, clearer, and a haze of unnatural smoke lingers in the air overhead.
>
> Ahead, slow flowing water reflects the glow of shuttered lanterns which hang from walls of metal and wood that form makeshift structures. Aside a few of the structures are rusting iron cages, and large vats of bubbling emerald fluid.
>
> A secret lab, right below the very streets of Ordain!
