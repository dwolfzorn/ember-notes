# Gameplay Details

The following gameplay features apply to the Forgotten Cistern area unless specifically stated otherwise in a specific room description.

## Enemies

This area is the base of operations for a Mutagist field experiment and is full of their members. At most times, the following enemies are encountered here in the provided approximate number:

- 7 [[Mutagist Vivisector]]
- 2 [[Mutagist Bombardier]]
- 7 [[Mutagist Excisor]]
- [[Kaftor Brenk]]

There is also a nest of drakes in a collapsed portion of the map, among their number are:

- 5 [[Pallid Drakeling]]
- [[Pallid Drake]]
- [[Radiant Ultra Drake]]

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Significantly Outnumbered
>
> Because of the large number of adversaries in this area, an uncontrolled assault is likely to be deadly. The party would be well advised to use stealth or guile to avoid direct confrontation where possible.
>
> #### Resting
>
> It is not possible to take a Long Rest within this area without being detected. A Short Rest is only possible in a few specific rooms which mention this as part of their location details.
>
> #### Fleeing
>
> Should the party need to flee the area, they may do so by retreating to [[Sunken Entrance]] and swimming out, or by taking the stairs near the [[Cistern Stockpiles]] which lead to the sewers which the party can use to return to the surface.
>
> Truly desperate parties may attempt to take the flowing water tunnel located just off the [[Corpse Dump]] which will dump them out into the Destine river, though not after a harrowing, barely controlled swim through a lightness underwater passage.
>
> #### Captured
>
> If the party is subdued in combat, they may awaken from an unconscious state as captives held within the [[Rusting Cages]].
>
> The Mutagists will take any Human characters to be experimented on, strapping them to chairs in the [[Injection Room]], though they may be able to escape before anything heinous can be done to them.

## Sound

The cistern feature a constant low rumble of liquids coursing through pipes overhead, machines whirring, and flowing water, all of which resonate through the hard stone halls of the ruins. As a consequence, hearing in this environment is more challenging. Mutagists also wear masks, which further hinders their ability to speak loudly and clearly, forcing them to huddle together to speak effectively.

- Sounds of combat can only be heard within 40 feet of their origin, and they are muffled completely beyond the stone walls of a room if its doors are closed.
- The great stone doors scattered throughout the ruins create a substantial amount of noise and vibration when opening or closing, which will alert any creatures within 20 feet of them.
- Characters in this area have **+2 Boons** to any `[[/check stealth]]` skill check to avoid being heard but have **-2 Banes** to any `[[/check perception]]` skill check that relies on hearing.

### Illumination

While most lab areas are decently lit in a yellow-orange bright light, these lamps are sparse, and fade into dim light everywhere else. In some places, either torches or old magical light sources provide dim illumination — enough to navigate by, but obscure fine details.

### Terrain

Rubble and collapsed sections of the ruins, as well as the various bodies of water on this scene serve as &reference[difficult terrain] for characters moving through such spaces.

### Hidden Doorways

There are several ancient stone doors which begin open and hidden in the halls between the [[Extraction Lab]], [[Injection Room]], [[Living Area]], and [[Chemical Outlet]] rooms.

They can be operated by any **Ancestry: Ashka** character that uses an object interaction to trigger the magical mechanisms. These doors are made of heavy, magically reinforced stone with an AC of 18 and 60 hit points once closed.

There are also two gemmed doors on either side of the [[Glowing Cistern]] that require specific items to open, but can be operated by an creature. These doors are magically warded and sealed, and cannot be opened any other way.

#### Elements Under Development

The various hidden doorways will have interactive elements in future updates. For now the secret doorways can be manipulated manually.

### Elevation

Unless otherwise stated, most ceilings are 10ft high. Rooms that have overhead pipes have about 2ft of that space consumed by these fixtures, functionally reducing overhead ceiling height to about 8ft.

In the marsh area the water is 3-4 feet deep in most places, which would places most tokens in-scene at -3ft to -4ft elevation when in the water. This should put most medium creatures at waist deep, but may fully submerge smaller creatures.

Inside the ruins, most water is 5ft deep. While in the main cistern area, this increases to 15ft deep.

Bridges, platforms and walkways are at ground level (elevation 0) unless otherwise stated.

### Other Effects

The air is choked with the reek of rotting flesh, caustic chemicals, sewage, and stagnant air. The stench is not only unpleasant, but burns the airways of living creatures. Any perception checks that rely on the sense of smell are made with **-2 Banes** as a result.

Additionally, characters begin coughing audibly if they fail any such check by 5 or more unless they are wearing a protective mask or do not need to breathe. This can potentially be heard by other characters up to 30 feet away.
