# Crumbling Corner

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Once part of a larger space, only a small section of wall and toppled supporting stone remains unburied, with the rest crushed under untold thousands of pounds of debris.
>
> Against the wall a cracked relief of a mountain can be seen, most of its wall mounting destroyed, and scattered on the floor.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Nesting Drake
>
> A fully grown Drake can be found here. It is sleeping, and a stealthy party will not alert them immediately, but getting too close or making noise nearby will wakes them without fail.

### Ancient Relief

The party may want to examine the relief in greater detail.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Multifaceted Art
>
> A successful **Awareness (DC 12)** concludes that the relief can't be rotated as it currently is, the damage to the surrounding structure of it has jammed it, and the stone itself is immobile.
>
> A successful **Arcana (DC 14)** check while examining the stone notes that there are transmutation and evocation symbols carved into it. It seems likely that something can be done to magically release and turn the relief. However, there are no obvious controls present.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Rotating Relief
>
> If the party placed a flame emblem into the cross guard of the sword in the [[Secluded Grave]] room, the relief's description changes.
>
> Use the description appropriate to the status of the relief.

### Initial Position

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Against the wall is a stone relief of a great mountain, jagged and forbidding. Despite the age and damage, the detail work is astounding, and features numerous small details including mountainside towns, and a jungle city at the base of the mountain.

### Fire Emblem Rotation

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Against the wall is a stone relief of a shattered mountain, the peak of it a gaping vent. The detail work is incredible, with an ornately decorated eruption column coming from the mountain, streams of what must be lava flows, mountainside settlements in their paths, and a jungle city at the base.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Emblem Slots
>
> Easily mistaken for damage, a passive **Awareness (DC 12, Passive)**, successful **Awareness (DC 14)** or **Awareness (DC 12)** notes that there are two circular spaces where objects could be fitted into the relief.
>
> The first space is at the vent of the volcano, and the second is in the center of the jungle city.
>
> #### Rotating the Relief
>
> The relief is meant to accept the fire and water emblems. If the party slots those reliefs into the proper slots — fire in the vent, water in the city — the relief will rotate.
>
> Read or paraphrase the following when that happens:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> For a brief moment you watch as fire blooms in the volcano, lava flows down the mountain, and smoke roils out of the vent. Then the whole relief rumbles and shakes before rotating to reveal a new relief.

This reveals the third rotation as detailed below.

### Steam Emblem Rotation

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The stone relief reveals a harrowing sight: the mountain is destroyed, the settlements on its slopes are gone, and the city at its feet is now portrayed as intricately detailed ruins shot through with flows of lava that glows with a radiant light, and the stone itself seems to ripple and swirl with a haze like smoke or steam.
>
> Fixed to the relief are two emblems, one filled with swirling gray mist, the other glowing lava.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Extracting the Emblems
>
> The party can safely pull the emblems out of this relief. Doing so yields both the [[Sedyri Steam Emblem]] and [[Sedyri Lava Emblem]].
>
> A moment after they are claimed the relief will spin back around, revealing the previous relief, allowing the party to reclaim the other two emblems they had initially.

### Deciphering Sedyri Lore

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Ancient History
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Legends** knows that the reliefs depict events that supposedly occurred thousands of years ago and relate to the rise and fall of the Sedyri.
>
> A successful **Society (DC 22)** check (characters with **Ancestry: Ashka** have **+2 Boons**) know that the details of the Sedyiri culture and history are not well documented, mostly surviving on in Ashka communities around the world. One common element across them all are events and tragedies relating to a catastrophic volcanic eruption.
