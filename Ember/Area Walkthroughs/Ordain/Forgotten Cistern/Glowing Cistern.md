# Glowing Cistern

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The space opens into a vast, oval-shaped chamber, flanked by eight towering pillars on either side, each cut from a single block of black basalt. Down every pillar, curtains of glowing water stream continuously, their cold blue light bathing the entire cavern in a gentle, uneven luminescence.
>
> A broad stone bridge stretches out above the cistern itself, its flagstones slick with mist and lit from below.
>
> On both sides of this bridge, makeshift laboratories and chemical stockpiles crowd the arc of the chamber, their mismatched walls of timber and metal out of place amid the grandeur. Casks and crates spill over with volatile compounds, their sharp tang mixing with the cool damp of the glowing reservoir below.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Mutagist Occupiers
>
> This area is filled with a handful of Mutagists, and there is no way to gain entry to the area without alerting them, as both stone doors in the [[Corpse Dump]] and [[Sedyri Alcoves]] create loud rumbles and vibrations that alert the nearby enemies.
>
> Also, as soon as fighting breaks out on either side of the cistern, the other side's Mutagists with rush in to assist.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Water Pillars
>
> The massive pillars are the source of the magical water here, by the looks of things. Examining them up close with a successful **Arcana (DC 15)** confirms this through the presence of numerous transmutation and evocation symbols carved into the stone among the other decorative elements.
>
> It's unclear what the magic is doing beyond simply infusing the water with magical energy.
>
> Characters that have a proper vessel on-hand can take a sample of the [[Infused Water]] if they want to.

### Fighting Kaftor

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Arcane Adversaries
>
> The [[Mutagist Excisor]] here attempt to attack from range, avoiding close combat where possible. However, their [[Arcane Burst]] proves perfectly functional in close range as well.
>
> In terms of spellcasting, they all have mage armor cast (which is automatically applied to their AC) and use [[Ray of Enfeeblement]] to weaken the strongest enemies on the field.
>
> If injured they utilize their [[Vampiric Touch]] spells to recover some lost hit points while harming their foes.
>
> #### A Cut Above
>
> Kaftor Brenk also has access to a few unique abilities that make him especially dangerous. Firstly, if engaged in close combat he can use his [[Draining Swipe]] to not only recover hit points, but harm his enemies.
>
> He can also use his action to consume a powerful alchemical compound and [[Inject Blaze]], giving him his action back, and enhancing his performance.
>
> While he can only do this four times, he also has granted himself a unique [[False Ember Blaze]] which will trigger, saving him from death, which is not something his Ancestry can normally do.
>
> #### Fire Support
>
> The [[Mutagist Bombardier]] in this area attempt to hang back, choosing to [[Throw Alchemical Grenades]] at grouped targets. If engaged in melee combat, they are competent with their [[Axe]], and will defend themselves violently.
