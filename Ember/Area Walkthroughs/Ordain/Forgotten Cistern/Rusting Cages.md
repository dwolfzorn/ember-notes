# Rusting Cages

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You step into a half-collapsed chamber, three rusted iron cages stand in the dark here, each easily large enough to hold a person or two. The bars are mottled brown and red with age, streaked with trails of orange where water has run across the metal. A faint, sour smell lingers in the cool air, mingling dust, old iron, and something less pleasant.
>
> One of the cages is open, the heavy door hanging slightly ajar. The other two are locked shut with rusty padlocks. As you enter, you spot movement in one of the cages.

### Speaking to the Prisoners

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Prisoners
>
> Two of the cages contain haggard looking humans that are barely dressed, and look badly wounded, though one appears to be far worse off than the other.

The prisoners are two humans:

**Ror'ry (Ordani human, he/him)**

Dressed as Ordani native, this young man is disheveled, his fine robes stained and torn. He looks exhausted and frightened, his concerned gaze often flicking over toward the man in the cage opposite him. He is a merchant in the city, and related to the man in the other cage, who is his uncle.

**Micke (Arcturian human, he/him)**

An old Arcturian man, his simple garb is stained and torn, hinting at rough treatment and captivity. His body is riddled with swollen, red marks, hinting at numerous injects at the hands of the Mutagists. He leans on the bars of his cage, barely standing, a cold hatred in his eyes. He is an inventor from the plateau, and specializes in making constructs.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** How were you captured?
>
> **A:**
>
> Ror'ry explains:
>
> > My uncle Micke was coming to visit me in the city after some troubles out in the plateau damaged his farm. We were out shopping one evening, and got jumped by masked figures in black cloaks and armor. We were knocked out and woke up here.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What have they done to you?
>
> **A:**
>
> > They've been injecting us with substances and then draining our blood. I've only had it done to me once. It makes your blood burn, and fills your mind with nightmarish visions. It's like a bad dream, and it gets so bad you black out, then wake up back in the cage.
>
> Ror'ry gestures to the man opposite hime.
>
> > They've done it several times to my uncle, I'm not sure he'll survive another. You've got to get us out of here.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What do you know about the people here?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Nothing, really. I'm sorry, they are all masked, and they barely talk to us, don't feed us…. there used to be more people here. Some humans like us, a kivahr man, a nir'ae woman. They dragged them off into the ruins behind us.
>
> Ror'ry shudders.
>
> > There's something back there, in the darkness, I could hear it attacking them. Their screams were echoing off the stone, it was the worst thing I've ever heard… then just stopped all at once.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Can you get out of here yourself?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I probably could, yes, but my uncle looks like he can barely stand. I think you're going to have to help us, or clear the way first…

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Prisoners
>
> A successful **Medicine (DC 14)** check is necessary to examine the prisoners. They have both been subjected to multiple injections of unknown chemicals and given nothing resembling medical treatment.
>
> Ror'ry's wounds appear to be minor, and he is in relatively good health, likely thanks to his youth. Micke however, has undergone maybe half a dozen injection cycles, has multiple infected wounds on his arms, and is running a severe fever.
>
> #### Treating the Uncle
>
> He can be stabilized with a successful **Medicine (DC 18)** check and use of a [[Healer's Kit]]. However,
>
> While healing magic and potions will restore his hit points, he is badly poisoned, missing blood, and has endured several days of torture, he needs to be taken to a hospital if he is going to have any hope of survival and recovery.
>
> Additionally, due to his injuries, he can only move at half his speed, and cannot defend himself. Luckily, his nephew is more than able and willing to help move him, but that leaves the both of them vulnerable if attacked.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### No Easy Way Out
>
> Parties that want to get the prisoners out of the ruins safely will need to first clear a safe path to the Glowing Cistern, which will allow them to get to the stairs located on the eastern side of the bridge.
>
> Ror'ry and Micke are too weak to utilize the sunken passage by themselves, but a dedicated party with the right spells and items could extract them from the dungeon via that route if they are willing to expend the resources to do so.
>
> #### Their Only Hope
>
> It should also be noted that leaving them for the Chain to rescue will result in their deaths due to other events that are forthcoming in this quest. While the party has no way of knowing this, they should be reminded that these men are witnesses and can provide testimony if saved, and leaving them behind is a risk.

### Examining the Cages

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Corroded Confinement
>
> A quick inspection reveals the locks are flimsy and worn. A character can pick any of the cage locks with only a `[[/tool thief 8]]` check. With a little strength, even forcing the cages open is possible with a successful `[[/check str 14]]` check, though the noise would likely draw attention.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Nesting Drakes
>
> Nesting Drakes can be found in the nearby [[Drake Den]]. Forcing open the cages, or making too much noise will wake them, and put them on alert. However, they are used to Mutagists being in the area, and don't come to investigate initially.
