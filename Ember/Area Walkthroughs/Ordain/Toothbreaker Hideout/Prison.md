# Prison

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Four cruel gibbet cages are arranged around the room, their rusted bars reflect a dull gleam in the flickering torchlight. A befouled washtub sits in the center of the room and the scene is presided over by a solitary Toothbreaker guard who sits upon a bench against the western wall. An alarm bell is mounted to the wall just overhead and leather whip is coiled at the guard's feet.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Imprisoned
>
> It is possible the party arrives in this area as a result of being imprisoned. If so, take a moment to position each character inside one of the vacant cages.
>
> Each cage is locked by a secure metal padlock. The [[Toothbreaker Prison Key]] opens these locks, which is in possession of Kierelin the Interrogator in the [[Unknown]], Ukkfal the Ringmaster in the [[Cistern Bloodring]], or on the master keyring of [[Raster Thorn]] himself. The guard in this room does not have the keys.
>
> #### Forced to Fight
>
> Each day, the characters are withdrawn from the cages and pitted in a sequence of fights in the [[Cistern Bloodring]]. See that location page for details on those staged fights. As long as the prisoners are compliant, they are provided with rations and allowed to rest in between these battles.
>
> #### Escaping
>
> Characters held in this prison are detained indefinitely until they escape or perish. Specific rules are not provided for how the party might escape, it is up to the characters and the Gamemaster to improvise a suitable gambit by which to obtain their freedom.
>
> Without adequate tools, it is not possible to pick the lock of the cage, however a character could attempt to force the cage open with a successful **Athletics (DC 25)** check.
>
> #### Non-Compliance
>
> If the party attempts escape, is particularly provocative towards the guard, or does not comply with the guard's instructions they will be denied rest and rations, leading to escalating @Condition[exhaustion the longer they remain imprisoned.

If the party enters this area not as prisoners, they immediately provoke the Toothbreaker guard to sound the alarm.

> [!info] Social
> #### Prisoners
>
> If the player characters are not prisoners here, the cages are instead occupied by other captives that the Toothbreakers have abducted. The three prisoners in clockwise order from the north-west are:
>
> 1. Sleger Endrik, an Arcturian human woodworker (he/him).
> 2. Vytho Whitecrest, a Cascilian human sailor (he/him).
> 3. Muse Kindry, an Ordani human ceramicist (she/her).
>
> The prisoners are in varying states of malnutrition and sleep deprivation. They are each terrified of the Toothbreakers and their Scalemaws and will not attempt escape unless the party can assure them that the Toothbreakers between the Prison and the exit have been eliminated.
>
> - Characters with **Awareness (DC 15, Passive)** notice that all four prisoners are human which is statistically unusual given the cosmopolitan makeup of Ordain.
>
> - If asked, the prisoners speak of another captive who was brought through recently, an Ordani named Eltien Fen who was taken away hours ago and has not returned.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Prisoner Knowledge
>
> The prisoners — as written — know little else of value to the party, but they can be a useful mechanism for handing out hints to the players if you want to direct their attention towards any element of gameplay in the hideout.
>
> Feel free for the prisoners to have witnessed or overheard something that could be useful to the players.
