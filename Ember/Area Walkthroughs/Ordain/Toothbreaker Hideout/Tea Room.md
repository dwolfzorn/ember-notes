# Tea Room

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The hideout's dangerous atmosphere fades away as you step into this large octagonal chamber, delicately illuminated by the green glow of four glass lamps and striations of soft natural light that beam down from the city overhead. Reflecting off the cerulean waters that surround the room's central platform, the light dances on the chamber walls and ceiling with a dreamlike calm, as tranquil and soothing.
>
> The circular stone platform that dominates the chamber is connected to the rest of the room via a set of metal walkways. An ornamented table made of wrought iron sits in the middle of this platform, decorated with the remains of a tea set and a glass hookah. A smaller table occupies the southern edge of the platform, covered in the scattered pieces and checkered board of a half-forgotten game set.
>
> You notice something else by the foot of the central table: a discarded sheet of paper containing a neatly-scrawled list of dates and names.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Searching the Tea Room
>
> Characters who succeed on a **Society (DC 15)** check or who have completed the `[[/eventState rubyGroveTales]]` event recognize the crimson fluid in the tea set as [[Ruby]], a rare concoction brewed in [[Talei]] to the north of Ordain. However, the tea has gone cold and has lost it's potency; drinking it has no lingering effects.
>
> Characters with **Culture: Waerd** or who succeed on a **Performance (DC 13)** check recognize the game as "Bastion", a contest of strategy popular among Waerds.
>
> - **Critical Success** - You recognize the game state of the board is such that the team controlling black pieces is positioned to inevitably win the game should play have continued.
>
> #### A Rat in the Hallows
>
> Raster's most recent tea party guest was Eltien Fen, a Hallows agent-turned-informant who met a gruesome end in the [[Interrogation Room]].
>
> If the characters examine the paper note at the foot of the wrought iron table, they'll notice:
>
> > The note contains a table of dates, times, and names — strange looking names, at that. A curious symbol is scrawled next to many of the entries: a horizontal ellipse with a diagonal slash through its center.
>
> Characters who succeed on an **Awareness (DC 16)** check ascertain that the names are codenames used to anonymize an individual and the records seem to be a history of observation, marking the dates and times where an event occurred.
>
> - Characters with **Knowledge: Intrigue** or **Path: Nightwatch** have **+2 Boons** on this check.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### What Happened With Eltien?
>
> **Eltien Fen** (LE, Ordani Human, he/him), a corrupt bureaucrat who served in the [[Hallows]] as an informant for Raster Thorn, recently delivered the news that Toothbreaker operations have been under active surveillance by the [[Veiled Chain]] for several weeks. Raster did not take this revelation well, and viewed Eltien's failure to provide adequate counter-intelligence as a fatally unforgivable one.

> [!info] Social
> #### Meeting with Raster
>
> If the party persuaded Taamsin to arrange a meeting with [[Raster Thorn]], that meeting occurs here. Raster is accompanied by Taamsin, three Toothbreaker Thugs, and one Scalemaw — which swims in a slow, constant circle around the platform.
>
> It's presumed that the party has arrived to this point under false pretenses, and that they have no actual business with Raster. However, it's also possible that certain groups may wish to continue their ruse. Doing so requires success on a **Deception (DC 25, Group)** check.
>
> - Characters with **Culture: Waerd** have **+2 Boons**on this check.
> - Characters with **Culture: Tayan** suffer **-2 Banes** on this check.
>
> Otherwise, it is assumed that the party has used their ruse with Taamsin to separate Raster to a more vulnerable position in order to attack, which occurs at this time.
