# Flow Control

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This circular room initially seems to surve little purpose aside from facilitating access between areas, but upon entering you notice water running through a chute in the middle of the floor and two large metal wheels connected to pipes on the southern wall.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Sluice Controls
>
> The two wheels to the south of the room control the sluice gates north of this room and underneath [[Scalemaw Training]]. The valves are kept in the open position as the Toothbreakers have no reason to inhibit the flow of water through the hideout.
>
> If the party wishes to close these valves, either purposefully or simply out of curiosity regarding their function they may do so. They are both rusted and partially stuck from years of disuse. Turning each valve requires a **Athletics (DC 18)** check.
>
> - Attempting this check regardless of the outcome emits piercing screech that alerts enemies in adjoining areas unless both doors to this room are closed.
>
> - Application of oil or other lubricant can be used to quieten the sound and avoid alerting nearby guards.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Interactive Element
>
> The flow control valves are interactive objects which are only usable by the Gamemaster since a check is required to turn them. When both wheels are turned, the water from the western canal will drain!

If both sluice controls are activated, then the water flow from the north and northeast is stopped and the [[Western Canals]] will empty. Read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> After a tremendous screech of protest, the sluice gates shut with a heavy thunk and the sound of turbulent water subsides. The inflow of water from the north slows to a crawl, and then a trickle, and then solitary drips as the chute beneath your feet becomes drained of water.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Draining the Western Canals
>
> Emptying the canals is not especially useful to the party, but it does have two potential benefits:
>
> - It exposes a hidden [[Scalemaw]] who is swimming within the canal water there.
> - It provides an opportunity to collect [[Mudflisk]] from the silt in the canal bed, if the party is aware to look for it.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Investigating the Drainage
>
> Toothbreakers who notice that the canal has been drained will recognize that it is unusual and make their way towards this area to investigate the cause. If they encounter the party they will be immediately hostile, otherwise they will restore water flow after approximately 10 minutes of diagnosing the issue.
