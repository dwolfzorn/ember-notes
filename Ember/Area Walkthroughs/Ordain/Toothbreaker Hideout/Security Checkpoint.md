# Security Checkpoint

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This dingy passage turns left and terminates abruptly at a metal door, eight feet high and completely solid aside from a narrow metal eyeslit that is currently closed from the other side.
>
> Discarded bottles and rags litter the stone floor in front of this rather unwelcoming and nondescript threshold. Taking a moment to listen, you hear no audible sound from within — save for the ambient thrum of flowing water that seems to reverberate through the walls.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Security Door
>
> The security door here is equipped with a heavy iron crossbar, which makes it nigh impossible to open from the outside while secured in this way. The door also features a traditional lock that uses a key, but while the Toothbreaker Hideout is occupied, the guards within keep this crossbar in place.
>
> - The locked door is not possible to pick open or otherwise finesse.
> - The locked door can be forced open with a successful a **Athletics (DC 30)** check, which loudly bashes it inwards, tearing the door free of its hinges and crashing into the room beyond. Any attempt to force open the door, whether successful or not, immediately causes the [[Toothbreaker Thug]] in the nearby [[Guardroom]] to raise the alarm.

Most groups that seek entrance into the Toothbreaker Hideout via this route will have to socially negotiate a reason for entrance with the guards inside the adjoining [[Guardoom]].

> [!info] Social
> #### Talking Your Way In
>
> A devious or persuasive party may be able to talk their way into the complex. A knock on the door is greeted with a gruff response:
>
> > Leave now if you don't want to get hurt. Badly.
>
> The party has only a brief moment to either leave or provide a compelling reason for entry, otherwise the Toothbreakers begin firing crossbow bolts through the open eyeslit of the door. Some topics that could be of interest to the Toothbreaker guards include the following (if not similar topics that the Gamemaster is willing to entertain):
>
> #### Bloodring Betting
>
> If the party has knowledge of the [[Cistern Bloodring]] and establishes that they wish to bet on fights, one or more characters must succeed on a **Diplomacy (DC 15)** check to convince the guards that they are enthusiastic gamblers who are unbothered by the depravity of the underground arena.
>
> - Characters with **Knowledge: Crime** or **Path: Underworld Mechanic** have **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> If the check is successful, the guards will demand an up-front entrance fee of  **-50** as a tithe to the gang. If the check is failed or the entrance fee unpaid, the guards will insist the party leave or respond with violence.
>
> If the party enters in this way, they are escorted by the guards to the [[Cistern Bloodring]] to meet Ukkfal the Ringmaster.
>
> #### Scalemaw Fascination
>
> The party can claim to be zoological enthusiasts who are fascinated by Scalemaws. The Toothbreakers are susceptible to this ploy because of the pride they have in their companion creatures, and they are willing to indulge the party's curiosity if one or more of the characters succeed on a **Wilderness (DC 16)** check.
>
> - Characters with **Knowledge: Beasts** or **Path: Primalist Disciple** have **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> If the party enters in this way, they are escorted by the guards to the [[Scalemaw Training]] area to meet Vaafo the Scaletamer.
>
> #### Business Proposition
>
> The party can pretend to have a business proposition for the gang that would result in a substantial payday or other advancement of the Toothbreaker's agenda. Such a proposition could also include compromising information about adversarial organizations like the [[Veiled Chain]] or the [[Burnished Hand]].
>
> One or more characters must succeed on a **Deception (DC 15)** check to seem convincing enough to deserve the attention of their supervisor.
>
> - Characters with **Knowledge: Intrigue**, **Knowledge: Trade**, or **Path: Nightwatch** have **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> If the party enters in this way, they are escorted by the guards to the [[Planning Room]] to meet with Taamsin the Mastermind.

If negotiations go poorly or the party chooses to provoke the guards, combat will erupt between the two chambers.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Fighting Your Way In
>
> As long as the security door remains closed, the [[Toothbreaker Thug]] in the [[Guardroom]] will remain there and fire crossbow bolts through the eyeslit of the door. If the party does not retreat or demonstrates any capability to be a threat, these guards will immediately sound the alarm, summoning more Toothbreakers Thugs to reinforce the chokepoint.
>
> See [[Gameplay Details]] for a description of their tactics.
