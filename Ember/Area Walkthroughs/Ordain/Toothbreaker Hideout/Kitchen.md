# Kitchen

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This trapezoidal room is illuminated by the firelight of a large brick oven on the slanted southwestern wall. An array of savory scents fills the chamber, including the delicious fragrance of seasoned meat and roasted potatoes, some of which decorate a small table at the room's center.
>
> A lone Toothbreaker thug stands before the oven. Immersed in their culinary duties, this cook fastidiously spices a rack of meat as they rotate it above the oven's embers, seemingly unaware of your presence.

A single [[Toothbreaker Thug]] works as a cook in this kitchen chamber.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Focused Barbecue
>
> The cook is focused on tending their barbecue. Characters who move through the room can avoid detection with a successful **Stealth (DC 13)** check.
>
> #### Raster's Meal
>
> The food on the central table is being prepared for [[Raster Thorn]].
>
> - Characters with **Awareness (DC 14, Passive)** notice a scrap of paper on the table which lists a scribbled food order annotated "for Raster".
>
> #### Poisoning Raster
>
> If the party has learned of Raster's deadly [[Mudflisk]] allergy from the healing house notes in [[Unknown]] they can attempt to poison this meal by adding Mudflisk into the food. If the party has other forms of poison, they could add this to the food to produce an effect, however, adding Mudflisk is a guaranteed method of killing the Toothbreaker leader.
>
> The cook is cautious to not keep any Mudflisk anywhere near the kitchen, so the party would need to procure a source elsewhere. Mudflisk can be easily obtained elsewhere in Ordain or by scouring the basin of the [[Unknown]] after it has been drained.
>
> - Adding Mudflisk to the food requires a successful **Stealth (DC 15)** check to do so without alerting the cook.
> - If Mudflisk is added to the food without the cook becoming aware and if the party is able to retreat from the room without raising suspicion, the cook will proceed to deliver the meal to Raster when it is finished (within the next hour).
> - If Raster has no reason to suspect the food is poisoned, he eats it and shortly thereafter is affected by &Reference[suffocation]. Each time that Raster gains a level of &Reference[exhaustion] from this suffocation, he may make a `[[/save con 19]]` saving throw to end the effects and avoid further suffocation. If Raster does not succeed on this sequence of saves he will die once his Exhaustion level reaches 6.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Raising the Alarm
>
> There is no alarm bell in this room, but there is one up the stairs to the north in the [[Sparring Alcove]]. If the Toothbreaker Thug here becomes aware of enemies, they will run towards the north stairs calling out for help in an attempt to ring this bell.
