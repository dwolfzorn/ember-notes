# Coatroom

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Access to the Coatroom
>
> Access to the Coatroom is **open to all** at all times. See [[Gameplay Details]] for more on access levels.
>
> #### Locating Funar Cevher
>
> See [[Casing the Joint]] for details.
>
> - Funar Cevher is **nearby**.

### Pre-Gala

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Coats and jackets fill the hangers of the room. Most are everyday fare, likely belonging to the staff who are working to prepare the house for the gala.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Quick Search
>
> Characters who search through the coat room and succeed on a `[[/check perception 15]]` check find a discarded [[Apron of Many Pockets]].

### At the Gala

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> An array of coats, jackets, and scarves have filled the room, taking up every hanger spot. Between the vibrant colors, soft fabrics, and occasional stuffed pockets, the room looks like a display case of cutting-edge fashion and casual luxury.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### In their Pockets
>
> This is a high-traffic staff room, but pickpockets may find something of value.
>
> - With a successful `[[/check perception 16]]` check, characters can identify a pocket with something in it that's worth taking. If they fail the check, any pocket they attempt to take something from has only worthless items.
>   > The coat pockets are filled with items that take up a lot of space but have no real value - old marbles, a crumpled up handkerchief, the pit of a fruit still damp with saliva.
> - If they succeed on the check, the character can attempt to take from one of the following, receiving the item in parentheses if they succeed in taking the item:
>   - A pocket that sags down as if weighted with something heavy (a coin pouch with  **6d10** )
>   - A pocket with the top of a closed bag peeking out of the top (a pouch of [[Dust of Disappearance]])
>   - Something round that sloshes when you move by it (a bottle of TBD)
>   - Something that glints just a little in the light (a[[Petalzon]])
> - To take an item, characters must succeed on a `[[/check sleightofhand 17]]` check, or they are spotted by the staff. Once spotted, characters must succeed on a `[[/check deception 16]]` check to explain themselves or they gain a level of suspicion.
