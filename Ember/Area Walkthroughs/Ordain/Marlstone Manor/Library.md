# Library

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Access to the Library
>
> Access to the library is **restricted** at all times, but the doors to it are closed, not locked. They can be easily opened without a check.See [[Gameplay Details]] for more on access levels.
>
> #### Locating Funar Cevher
>
> See [[Casing the Joint]] for details.
>
> - Funar Cevher is **in the vicinity.** Note: this is the baseline result when using the Locator Rod in and around Marlstone Manor.

There is no significant difference between the library before the gala and during the gala.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Books line the shelves that wrap around the room. Each section is orderly, with everything neatly labeled and organized — making it easy to notice if anything is out of place. The only exception is the large desk at the back of the room, whose surface holds a stack of paper and books left open, as if the person reading them might wander back in at any moment.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Library Desk
>
> Characters can make a cursory search of the desk in the library without a check, finding the following:
>
> - A note that reads: *Hephiss - the new system is ready, but it only works if the books remain in the right place. Use the phrase from the catalogues for the book that you want and it will be retrieved for you. Please do not retrieve yourself - trust me, you won't enjoy it.*
> - A book titled "**Unexpected Alchemy: New Uses for Familiar Extract**," which is opened to a page titled "Beyond the Bath (C)," which details the following items:
>   - Cloud Oil, which is known for the way it makes the user feel as if they are floating while bathing, contains Nimbus Water, collected from the Nimbus Pitcher Plant, and can be used to make airborne steam and scents spread across a room.
> - A blank piece of notebook paper with indentations on it where someone has pressed onto the page.
>   - With a successful `[[/check investigation 16]]` check, `[[/check history 16]]` check, or **Knowledge: Intrigue**, characters know that the indentations could be read by rubbing it with charcoal (which can be found in the Lounge fireplace, on the desk in Hephiss' study, and in several artisan's tools (Calligrapher's Supplies, Cartographer's Tools, Painter's Tools).
>   - If revealed, the charcoal imprint has a library passphrase on it, which retrieves a book when spoken out loud — **Radiance Rewarded**, which retrieves the book **"Luxarum: Realm of Holy Beauty**."
>   - **"Luxarum: Realm of Holy Beauty"** is an old text that has been partially faded. Characters who read through it learn that the beings from the inner realm Luxarum are often known as Celestials because of their use of radiant fire as a source of magic and life.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Using the Library Catalogue
>
> A note by the hand-bound catalog books reads:
>
> > Hephiss, I have been adding to the collection as you requested. You are nothing if not thirsty for knowledge. In addition to the book on alchemy, you may enjoy the artifacts guide and the history of the manor - it's had several colorful inhabitants!
> >
> > Use the passphrase and the book will be retrieved for you. A system you may wish to use yourself for anything you may want to hide away - I've added a guide to the shelf.
>
> Characters can flip through the library catalogue at their leisure, but must succeed on a check to find a worthwhile book and learn its passphrase. Available books are:
>
> - **A History of Marlstone Manor** (requires a successful `[[/check history 17]]` check or **Knowledge: Intrigue** to learn the passphrase "**The Walls Have Memories").**
>   - Reading the book reveals that there were once tunnels beneath the manor that connected to a room in the first floor's East Wing.
> - **Great Artists of the Arctus Plateau** (requires a successful `[[/check history 17]]` check or **Knowledge: Arts** to learn the passphrase "**A Painters Pride")**
>   - The book is very well worn, revealing that Hephiss Wandren is a fan of the arts. One page, in particular, featuring the artist Indigo Ray, who is known for his self-portraits, is particularly dog-eared.
> - **Passphrases and You** (requires a successful `[[/check investigation 17]]` check or **Knowledge: Intrigue** to learn the passphrase "**This Is Not A Passphrase")**
>   - Reading the book reveals that passphrases can be used to hide and reveal doors that might not otherwise be found, and should relate to a person's hobbies and interests.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Defensive Shelving
>
> With a successful `[[/check perception 16]]` check while looking at the library shelves, or with `[[/check perception 18 passive]]`, characters can see a slight shimmer in the air around the outside of the library shelves, caused by a defensive field meant to protect the books from unnecessary disturbances.
>
> - Characters who succeed on an `[[/check arcana 17]]` check, or who use Identify, know that the bookshelves are warded from being touched directly and will emit force energy towards anyone who attempts to remove a book.
> - If characters attempt to physically remove a book from the library shelf, they must immediately make a `[[/save dexterity 17]]` saving throw. On a failure, they take `[[/damage 1d6 force]]` damage and are forcibly pushed backwards 20 feet. On a success, they are unaffected, but are unable to remove the book.
>   - If a character is pushed backwards into a wall or solid object, the sound alerts a Guard, who enters the library immediately - as it is a Restricted area, characters must succeed on a `[[/check persuasion 17]]` check or `[[/check history 17]]` check to convince the guard they are in the library in good faith. On a failure, they gain a level of suspicion. On a failure of 3 or more, they gain 2 levels of suspicion.
