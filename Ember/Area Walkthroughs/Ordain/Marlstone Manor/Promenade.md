# Promenade

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Access to Promenade
>
> Access to the Promenade varies. (See [[Gameplay Details]] for more on access levels.)
>
> - **Pre-Gala: Open** to Decorators, **Restricted** to Gardeners and Kitchen Staff.
> - **Gala: Open** to Guests.
>
> #### Locating Funar Cevher
>
> See [[Casing the Joint]] for details. If the party uses the[[Locator Rod]] in this area, they find that Funar Cevher is **in the vicinity.** This is the baseline result when using the Locator Rod in and around Marlstone Manor.

### Pre-Gala

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Though many of the rooms of this floor are off-limits, the promenade seems to be considered an extension of the main gala floor, with the same decorative touches, placed for those who prefer watching the action at the gathering to being front and center.

### At The Gala

If the party is in full swing with no major incident, guests gather here in groups for quieter conversation.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Though the music from the band below can still be heard here, it is slightly hushed, and snatches of conversation can be easily picked out from the various small groups of Ordani dignitaries sitting around the small tables, mostly about the general quality of other people's attire, whether the Hallows is up to the new challenges in the city, and whether anyone will dare to tell Hephiss Wandren that she's really not that great an artist.

If the party has caused a major physical disruption (fire, smoke, stampede, etc.), the crowd reacts in panic:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You can hear gasps and small yelps from the crowd on the veranda as they back away from the main floor, voices overlapping and growing in volume. Though it is hard to pick out individual words in the chaos, the sentiment is clear - worry, confusion, and concern about what might happen next.

If the party has caused a major social disruption (loud argument or dispute), they react with interest:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The guests on the veranda have grown quiet, their conversations dropped to little more than a whisper, as everyone leans towards the nearby voice as if it will help them hear what exactly is happening more clearly.
