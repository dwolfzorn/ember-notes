# Hephiss' Downstairs Closet

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Access to Hephiss' Downstairs Closet
>
> Access to Hephiss' Downstairs Closet is **forbidden** at all times. See [[Gameplay Details]] for more on access levels.
>
> #### Locating Funar Cevher
>
> See [[Casing the Joint]] for details.
>
> - Funar Cevher is **in the vicinity.** This is the baseline result within Marlstone Manor.

This room appears to contain a closet, but in reality hides the door to the staircase that leads down into Wandren HQ. It remains the same pre-gala and during the gala. Instead, the room changes based on character actions as noted below.

### If Door Is Hidden

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Though this appears to be a closet like any other, the objects all appear slightly out of place in a way difficult to put your finger on, and the smoky scent of Hephiss Wandren's perfume is thick in the air despite its relative emptiness.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Discovering the Door
>
> Characters may suspect that there is a doorway in the closet, but it will not appear without a trigger being pressed in Hephiss' Bedroom. Before the doorway appears, party's can confirm its position with the following:
>
> - With a successful `[[/check 18 investigation]]` check, characters notice that there is a significant amount of space unaccounted for behind the closet wall.
> - If characters capture or question the guard stationed in Hephiss' study, they can reveal that there is an entrance in the closet.
> - With an Identify or Detect Magic spell, characters can determine that the wall leading down to the vault is behind an illusion spell triggered from elsewhere.
> - If characters strike the walls of the closet with an axe or similar object and succeed on a `[[/check strength 18]]` check, the tool embeds itself as normal in all walls except for the wall that is also a door, which is made of metal. On hitting the metal wall, it clangs and bounces off, though the wall appears the same.

### If Door Is Revealed

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Where a wood closet wall once stood, an intricate metal door is in its place, covered with symbols and images that stand out not just in this manor, but in this city, and maybe Ember as a whole. Towards the bottom of the door, in the middle of etchings, where a key might go, is a thick metal cover that appears bolted into place.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating the Locked Door
>
> Characters who succeed on a `[[/check history 17]]` check, have **Knowledge: Celestials**, or who know **Language: Harmos** know that the door is covered with writing in Harmos. Those who know the language can read that it is a blessing that is meant to give the door strength and allow through only those who share the right words.
>
> Characters who succeed on a `[[/check perception 17]]` check can see a slight sheen on the door, as if there is a substance painted on it, and smell a slightly acrid smell. Those with **Knowledge: Alchemy** or **Knowledge: Crime** can identify the scent and sheen as belonging to [[Thieves' Lament]].

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Removing the Keyhole Cover
>
> The entire door has been painted with [[Thieves' Lament]], and will burn and mark anyone who touches it physically. It is also trapped - anyone who touches it must make a `[[/save dexterity 16]]` saving throw. On a failed save, they take `[[/damage radiant 1d4]]` damage. On a successful save, they take half-damage and hear a whisper.
>
> > As you pull your hand back from the jolt, a voice whispers, as if from within your ears - "use your words, Hephiss"
>
> The damage of the door trap increases by one size of die each time the door is touched within the same hour, using the pattern noted below. The voice can give additional hints as noted.
>
> - First touch: `[[/damage 1d4 radiant]]`
> - Second touch: `[[/damage 1d6 radiant]]`
>   > Use the passphrase ,Hephiss. Here's a hint - two words long.
> - Third touch: `[[/damage 1d8 radiant]]`
>   > Really, Hephiss? Begin with the end of your favorite artist's name, as if Lantyr beats down on you.
> - Fourth touch: `[[/damage 1d10 radiant]]`
>   > That's going to leave a mark. Just like your favorite flowers, and the second half of your phrase.
>
> The trap can be disarmed with a spell that negates its magic (Disintegrate, Dispel Magic, etc), but the keyhole cover remains in place.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Revealing the Keyhole
>
> The keyhole can only be revealed by using the correct passphrase, "Ray Lanterelle." Once spoken, the keyhole cover disappears, and the door can be opened using [[Hephiss' Keys]] or the [[Copy of Hephiss' Keys]].
