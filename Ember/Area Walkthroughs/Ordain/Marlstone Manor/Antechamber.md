# Antechamber

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Access to Antechamber
>
> Access to the Antechamber varies. (See [[Gameplay Details]] for more on access levels.)
>
> - **Pre-Gala: Open** to Decorators, **Restricted** to Gardeners and Kitchen Staff.
> - **Gala: Open** to Guests.
>
> #### Locating Funar Cevher
>
> See [[Casing the Joint]] for details. If the party uses the[[Locator Rod]] in this area, they find that Funar Cevher is **in the vicinity.** This is the baseline result when using the Locator Rod in and around Marlstone Manor.

### Pre-Gala

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Though it is open to the floor below, there are spots in this hidden nook where i almost seems as if you are in your own small corner of the manor, hidden away from prying eyes. Nearby, you can hear run a faint murmur from the room to the west, though the words are indistinct.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Listening In
>
> If there are staff in the room, characters can move close enough to the door to Hephiss' Bedroom to eavesdrop, or distract them as follows:
>
> - With a successful `[[/check deception 17]]` check, the party can tell the staff they are needed elsewhere or there is an emergency.
> - Any character carrying an item that might cause a stain can spill it on a member of the staff with a successful `[[/check sleightofhand 17]]` check.
>
> With a successful `[[/check perception 17]]` check, characters who are standing against the wall can identify the voice as being Hephiss Wandren and hear a few words - tonight, experiment, and Funar.
>
> - With a successful `[[/check insight 17]]` check, the party has the sense that the words are being said in a self-congratulatory manner.
> - With a success of 3 or more, characters can hear all of what Hephiss is saying.
>   > You're going to wow them all tonight, Hephiss. And tomorrow, back to the experiments. You will figure out what he knows and use it to your advantage. And maybe something you create could be the way to get rid of that pesky Funar.

### At The Gala

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Two pairs of strangers whisper in a corners of the room, as if on a date in the middle of a party, but while the couple at the back of the room lean towards each other with affection, the one standing by the door gesture angrily in each other's direction, locked in some kind of private war.

> [!info] Social
> #### The Bickering Couple
>
> The couple standing by the door to Hephiss' bedroom are deep in an argument about whether to go down to the lower level and dance. With a successful `[[/check persuasion 17]]`, the party can join the conversation, steering them towards either a calm resolution to go dance (clearing them from the room) or a fierce argument (using them as a distraction).

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Opening the Door
>
> The door to Hephiss' chambers can be opened with a `[[/check dexterity 17]]` check with Thieves' Tools, or by using either [[Hephiss' Keys]] or [[Copy of Hephiss' Keys]].
