# Servants' Office

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Access to the Servants' Office
>
> Access to the Servants' Office varies. (See [[Gameplay Details]] for more on access levels.)
>
> - **Pre-Gala: Open** to All
> - **During Gala:** **Restricted** to Guests, **Open** to Staff
>
> #### Locating Funar Cevher
>
> See [[Casing the Joint]] for details. If the party uses the[[Locator Rod]] in this area, they find that Funar Cevher is **in the vicinity.** This is the baseline result when using the Locator Rod in and around Marlstone Manor.

### Pre-Gala

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Staff come and go through the office, usually stopping only briefly to pick up a paper or drop off a note for another member of the staff. With as many exits and entrances as the room has, the door may shut occasionally, but it rarely stays that way for long.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Notes on the Table
>
> Characters who succeed on a `[[/check perception 17]]` check or who search through the papers and notes on the table find a Map of Marlstone Manor that indicates that the following rooms are forbidden to both staff and guests - Hephiss' Library (West Wing), Hephiss' Study (West Wing), Hephiss' Workshop (West Wing),
>
> - If characters succeed on the perception check by 3 or more, they notice a small note stuck to the back of the paper, which reads as follows:
>   > Not surprised the Workshop is on the forbidden list. I snuck in there to check it out and it's full of weird trinkets and diagrams I can't figure out. I don't think anyone saw me, but now there's a guard posted up in the study next door, so who knows?

### At The Gala

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Two of the Manor's guards sit in quiet conversation. As you enter, they barely look up, barking out a few words before going back to their discussion.
>
> > You're in the wrong place - bathroom is across the way.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Dealing with the Guards
>
> The reaction of the guards to the party depends on if and how they exit the room:
>
> - If the party leaves by one of the doors to the lounge, they pay the party no mind.
> - If the party leaves by the servants' entrance and do not appear to be servants or guards, they stop the party and redirect them to the lounge exit doors. The party can convince the guards to let them through their chosen exit with a successful `[[/check 17 persuasion]]` check, a `[[/check 17 history]]` check to feign interest in the house's architecture, or a similar check at the GM's discretion.
>
> If the party does not exit the room, the guards confront them, demanding that they leave.
>
> - With a successful `[[/check persuasion 18]]` or `[[/check intimidation 18]]` check, the party can convince the guards either that they are needed elsewhere (causing the guards to leave the room), or that the party should be allowed to stay.
> - With a successful `[[/check sleightofhand 18]]`, characters can steal a [[Marlstone Staff Key]] from either of the guards.
