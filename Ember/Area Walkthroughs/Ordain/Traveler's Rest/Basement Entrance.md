# Basement Entrance

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A short corridor is cut into the tall stone sidewalk here, with stairs leading down a few steps to a heavy wooden door — notably featureless, save for the sturdy steel hardware that comprises the entry's locking mechanisms.

This basement entrance is primarily used as an inconspicuous access point for the delivery of standard goods and services related to the ongoing operation of the Traveler's Rest downstairs locations, including the morgue and medical archives.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Locked Basement Door
>
> The street-facing basement entrance to Traveler's Rest is a fortified doorway, equipped with two heavy-duty locks and a sturdy wooden crossbar on its interior side. Characters can readily see that the two outward-facing locks are made of high-quality steel.
>
> To open the basement door, it must be unlocked with both a [[Traveler's Rest Key]] and a [[Traveler's Rest Basement Key]], and the wooden crossbar must also be removed (which can theoretically only be done from the inside).
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check while searching the door is able to confirm that although the door is locked quite securely, there are no associated traps.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character is able to confirm the presence of the sturdy wooden crossbar on the opposite side of the door, and can safely assume that this extra security measure makes lockpicking here a somewhat futile effort.
>
> In order to open the door from the outside, both locks must be picked and the wooden crossbar must somehow be removed.
>
> - Any character who makes a successful `[[/skill sleightofhand 15 tool=thief]]` is able to pick the bottom lock, which corresponds to the [[Traveler's Rest Key]].
> - Any character who makes a successful `[[/skill sleightofhand 15 tool=thief]]` is able to pick the top lock, which corresponds to the [[Traveler's Rest Basement Key]].
> - The wooden crossbar can only be removed from the interior side of the door. If a character can somehow manage to gain visibility of the interior side of the door (an unlikely feat), the [[Mage Hand]] spell is strong enough to remove the crossbar.
>
> Alternatively, the party can bash the door in with a successful **`[[/skill strength 30]]`** check. One failure is ignored. A second failure results in the prompt arrival of one or more [[Cindaric Adherent]] and a [[Burnished Hand Protector]], who are prepared to call forth additional help if necessary.
>
> - Removal of the wooden crossbar lowers the difficulty of this task, requiring a successful **`[[/skill strength 20]]`** check instead.

> [!info] Social
> #### A Cindaric Welcome
>
> The Cindarics of Traveler's Rest use this basement entrance for the day-to-day needs of the morgue, including various ingoing and outgoing goods and services. The door stays closed and locked at all other times.
>
> If the party attempts to knock on this door to gain entry to the basement area of Traveler's Rest, different times of day will result in the following responses:
>
> - During daylight hours, one of the [[Cindaric Adherent]] who work the downstairs locations will answer the door within 5-10 minutes of the knocking.
> - During evening hours, the door will go unanswered for an indeterminate amount of time. Characters may have to wait up to an hour for someone to arrive, and the dispositions of the Cindarics who work these locations after normal hours are comparatively inhospitable. The party is likely to draw unwanted attention to themselves if they persist in this approach.
>
> Unless the party is here to investigate the circumstances of [[The Secret Autopsy]], their social encounter here is less than fruitful. The Cindaric who opens the door has the following limited conversation points for the party:
>
> - This basement entrance is generally reserved for deliveries and outgoing processions (when a cadaver is transferred away from Traveler's Rest to its final resting place, which may or may not be the [[Burial Grounds]] northeast of Ordain).
> - Visitors and patrons of Traveler's Rest are encouraged to enter through one of the main entryways, including the [[Main Office]], the [[Library]], or the various [[Courtyard]] doors.
> - Traveler's Rest is one of Ordain's famed healing houses, where the injured and the infirm can benefit from the mundane and magical healing methods of the Cindaric Sages who work here.
> - These downstairs facilities serve various mortuary needs, and are off-limits to the public. Additional questions can be answered by one of the friendly Cindarics upstairs.
>
> At your discretion, one or more characters may attempt to get additional information out of the Cindaric with a successful **Deception (DC 18)** or **Diplomacy (DC 15)** check.
>
> - **Path: Cindaric Initiate**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Intimidation is outright unsuccessful, and results in the Cindaric immediately closing and locking the door before the characters can react.

If a Cindaric Adherent answers the door, you may read the following aloud or substitute your own social encounter:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The sound of the city is the only thing that keeps you company for a moment, a thrum of lively activity, indifferent to your interlude.
>
> A few minutes later, you can hear the telltale sounds of the locks on the basement door unlatching, along with the sound of a heavy wooden crossbar being removed from its cradle on the other side.
>
> The door swings outward only a few short inches, and an officious voice issues from within:
>
> > Salutations, friends. What business do you have with Traveler's Rest that isn't suited for the front desks?

### [[The Secret Autopsy]]

The basement entrance serves as the covert meeting place for the party and their ally [[Sin Marmot]] for the [[The Secret Autopsy]] Event of the [[Smoldering Cinders]] Side Quest. Refer to the Event text for more details.
