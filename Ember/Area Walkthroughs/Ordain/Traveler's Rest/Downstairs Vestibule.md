# Downstairs Vestibule

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This vestibule appears to serve as a storage area as well as a point of access from the upstairs locations here at Traveler's Rest. Crates full of miscellaneous equipment and barrels full of water, wine, or some other liquid are stacked here in ample supply. The wooden floors here creak beneath your feet as you take note of the low din of the healing house above.

This antechamber connects the downstairs locations to the upstairs areas via a stairway that leads to [[Jessa's Private Quarters]]. Due to this proximity to the head sage's personal chamber (despite Jessa's friendly open-door policies with her staff), the downstairs vestibule is the least-used of the healing house's two interior stairways.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Creaky Floors
>
> The floors of the downstairs vestibule are old and rickety, which normally adds an element of charm to the place, but is otherwise problematic for characters who wish to sneak through the area undetected.
>
> Any character who walks on the rickety wooden planks as they move through the downstairs vestibule must make a successful **Athletics (DC 15)** check to avoid the floor's loud creaking noises.
>
> - Failure compromises the character's ability to Hide here in the downstairs vestibule for a short time. All Dexterity (Stealth) rolls made by the character to Hide here for the next 5 minutes are made with **-2 Banes**.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring the Vestibule
>
> This intermediary chamber is stuffed full of miscellaneous wares. Characters who take a moment to search the downstairs vestibule are able to spot the following:
>
> - Many of the [[Barrels]] here do indeed contain various liquids. At least 4 of them are labeled "*WATER*," another 3 are labeled "*WINE*," and 2 are labeled "*HARD* *CIDER*" and stamped with a logo of a tree that reads "*SWEET RELIEFS, EST. 2483. ORCHARD LANES - ORDAIN*."
> - Other sundry items stored down here include crates full of medical-grade textiles, stacks of loose tools, and other equipment.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check while searching the room is also able to notice the following:
>
> - The wooden chest near the northeast corner of the chamber is locked with a heavy padlock. See "The Locked Chest" below for additional details.
> - One of the barrels contains the residue of a heavily used supply of [[Duskflame Powder]].
> - The character can assemble enough tools from the equipment here to comprise a set of [[Carpenter's Tools]] and a [[Dungeoneer's Pack]] without the Rations.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character is also able to find a scattered set of [[Thieves' Tools]] here, culled from the various lost & found components of other tool kits. Additionally, the character can smell the Ordani Purfume located in the Locked Chest (see below).
>
> #### The Locked Chest
>
> The wooden chest here serves as the healing house's little-used lost & found, and contains various items that have gone unclaimed over the last few months. The chest itself is made of sturdy Wedgelands oak and is locked with heavy padlock. It can be unlocked with a [[Traveler's Rest Key]].
>
> Any character who makes a successful `[[/skill sleightofhand 15 tool=thief]]` is able to pick the lock. Alternately, a successful **Athletics (DC 20)** check is able to damage the chest enough to open it. The contents of the chest include:
>
> - A bottle of Ordani [[Perfume]] in a velvet [[Pouch]], which can be smelled from outside the chest by any character with a `[[/skill perception passive 15 format=long]]`.
> - A silver [[Mace]] worth 105 gp, engraved with the holy symbol of the Hearthken, the shard goddess [[Steelsong]].
> - A dog-eared [[Book]] of Waerd Nursery Rhymes.
> - 2 sets of [[Fine Clothes]] and 3 sets of [[Traveler's Clothes]].
> - 2 random [[Arcturian Trinkets]].
