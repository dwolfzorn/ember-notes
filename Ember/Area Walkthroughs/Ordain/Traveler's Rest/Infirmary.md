# Infirmary

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Many of the beds of this large, open-air infirmary are occupied by ill and ailing patrons of the healing house. But despite their unfortunate reason for being here, there is an overt air of calm throughout this place. A chart of dates, times, and procedures sits on a small table at the foot of each bed, and the curtains here allow a modicum of natural light to reach the room from outside.

This room serves as the main infirmary for patients of Traveler's Rest. The beds here are mostly used for short-term occupancy, but provisions for extended stays are certainly made from time to time.

> [!abstract] Cindaric Adherent
> **[[Cindaric Adherent]]**
>
> Level 3 · Human Cindaric Initiate
>
> ![Cindaric Adherent](../../../assets/image024.webp)
>
> This scholarly healer is clad in garments of bright gold and lush crimson, the colors of the famed Cindaric Sages of the Arctus Plateau. Their shoulders and tunic are marked with the familiar four-leafed diamond that signifies the Cindaric order, and their hands are marked and calloused by the wholesome abrasions of honest labor. The sage regards you with a benevolent smile.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring the Infirmary
>
> A simple search reveals the following: each cot is accompanied by a small side table bearing a clay basin. Several of the clay basins contain traces of a herbal tincture that smells faintly of mint; one of them contains a full, untouched dose of the tincture. A character can carefully decant it into a suitable container.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Medicine (DC 14)** check while examining the tincture recognizes it as [[Cindaric Mint Tea]], a palliative draught meant to induce sleep.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Plants**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> If stolen, the [[Cindaric Mint Tea]] can be given to the Burnished Hand Protector in the [[Recreation]] room for a small reward.

> [!info] Social
> #### Cindaric Conversation
>
> The Cindarics on duty here are busy, and are generally unwilling to engage in long conversation. However, any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 12)** or **Diplomacy (DC 12)** check is able to solicit some additional information, including:
>
> - The mint-scented tincture kept beside certain beds is a mild palliative draught, used to help patients sleep through pain or fever. It is not considered dangerous, though the Cindarics avoid giving it to anyone who needs to remain lucid for treatment or questioning.
> - The healers here see all manner of common illnesses and lingering complaints, from fevers and fatigue to infected wounds and chronic pain. Cases that prove unusually stubborn are typically referred to one of the more experienced Cindaric Sages in the [[Lower Ashvale]].
>
> - **Path: Cindaric Initiate**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> A successful **Deception (DC 13)** check confirms the relative veracity of the Cindaric healer's various conversation points, along with an honest desire to be equally helpful to all patrons (no matter their personal disposition).
>
> #### Local Small Talk
>
> The Ordani and Arcturian patients here are willing to pass the time with ordinary conversation. Common topics include the following:
>
> - How long each patient has been staying at Traveler's Rest, and whether their condition seems to be improving.
> - Opinions on the Cindarics' bedside manner, remedies, and food from the [[Refectory]].
> - Local gossip based on what the party is currently experiencing; for example, if the party has discovered a mural from [[Local Color]], they might hear gossip about graffiti.

> [!info] Social
> #### Bedside Manner
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Medicine (DC 12)** check can demonstrate their basic aptitude in conversation with the Cindaric attendant, who then allows them to treat patients in the infirmary.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Helping Out in the Infirmary
>
> Once their assistance is accepted by the attending Cindaric, a character can approach a patient and attempt to diagnose their condition: roll on or choose from the [[Aterican Diseases]] table to determine the patient's condition (do not share this information with the character).
>
> #### Physical Examination
>
> The character gets an overview of any obvious exterior symptoms (rashes, bruises, etc.). A character with a `[[/skill perception 16 passive format=long]]` or who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check reveals any exterior symptoms that are easy to miss (if present at all).
>
> #### Patient History
>
> The character interviews the patient and gets their interpretation of their symptoms, which may or may not be entirely accurate. A character with a `[[/skill persuasion 16 passive format=long]]` or who makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check gets them to open up about symptoms they might find embarrassing or sensitive (if present at all).
>
> #### Diagnosis
>
> Once the character believes they've gathered enough information, they can attempt to diagnose the illness by consulting the [[Aterican Diseases]] table and cross-referencing the patient's symptoms. In the event that their information matches more than one illness, they must choose one.
>
> #### Outcome
>
> After diagnosing the patient, the character can present their thoughts to the attending Cindaric, who will lend them their [[Traveler's Rest Key]] in order to enter the [[Alchemy]] closet and concoct a cure.
>
> Upon returning to the infirmary and administering the cure, the patient may — or may not, if the diagnosis was incorrect — recover. If a character ever gives an incorrect diagnosis, their privileges in the infirmary are revoked.

### [[Spreading Sickness]]

If the characters return during the [[Spreading Sickness]] Side Quest, the infirmary is overfilled with people suffering from the mysterious wasting illness. They are tended to by one or two exhausted Cindaric Adherents, who aren't in the mood to answer questions.
