# Study

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This narrow chamber serves as modest living quarters. A bed sits beneath a small window overlooking a quiet cobblestone street. Beside it stands a writing desk, its surface a messy sprawl of loose pages and open books.

This bedroom chamber acts as a private study area for the residents and visitors of Traveler's Rest, and often serves as guest quarters for important guests and dignitaries.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring the Chamber
>
> A simple search reveals the following: in addition to the usual comforts provided by Traveler's Rest, the room contains a wash basin, a folded spare blanket, a water pitcher and clay cup, and an unlocked footlocker for the current guest's belongings.
>
> Any character with a `[[/skill perception 13 passive format=long]]` or who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 11)** check notices a few personal effects left in plain view, their exact nature depending on whoever is currently staying here (see "Who Goes There?" below); describe one or two visible items that suit that guest.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character also notices an additional personal belonging, keepsake, or trinket on the current guest's person. This item should reinforce the guest's possible profession, recent travels, present hardship, or reason for staying at Traveler's Rest.

> [!info] Social
> #### Who Goes There?
>
> Roll on (or choose from) the [[Traveler's Rest Itinerant]] table to determine who is currently staying in the Guest Quarters. They readily engage in conversation, revealing the following:
>
> - Where they most recently came from, where they expect to go next, or what business brought them to Ordain in the first place.
> - One additional personal detail appropriate to the current guest, such as their profession, recent hardship, notable possession, or local connection.
>
> A successful **Deception (DC 13)** check confirms that the guest is mostly forthright (for a complete stranger), and that their stated reason for staying here is genuine.
