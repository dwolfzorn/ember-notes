# Gameplay Details

## Antimagic Field

The entirety of the [[Spellbreaker Tower]] district in Ordain is shrouded in a powerful [[Antimagic Field]] that emanates from the tower itself. As a result, all levels of this tower are also under this effect.

## Objectives

Parties arriving here as part of [[Diplomatic Impunity]] (specifically the [[Doing Time]] event) will be in the prison with the express purpose of breaking out, and taking another inmate ([[Toron]]) with them.

To do this they should each have a [[Wingsuit]] (plus one spare) hidden in the tower for them. The intent is that they will circumvent security, find a secure jump point, and leap from the tower, sailing to safety.

However, this is not the only way to escape. Alternate routes are available and explained below.

### Other Escape Routes

**Walking Out**

Whether they fight or sneak their way out, a party that manages to get down to the first floor of the tower and take the stairwell found at the [[Entry Checkpoint]] will have a clear shot to safety. Narratively they'll have to travel several flights of stairs down to do so, but resistance should be minimal at that point.

A clever party might utilize stolen guard uniforms to make this easier, and could theoretically walk right out of the prison with one of the numerous shift changes that take place every six hours.

**Climbing Down**

All told, the tower is about 300 feet tall. With at least 6 spools of rope (50 feet each) a party could climb down to the ground level. Since the tower has no windows and minimal guard presence outside, there is almost no risk of them being seen in the act.

However, acquiring enough rope (taken from the store rooms) is easier said than done. A resourceful party could employ bed sheets to create enough makeshift rope though, however this would take considerable time and effort to do, and the sheets would need to be sourced without drawing attention.

The other difficulty is finding a secure tie-off point for the rope. The best locations for this would be the [[Sky Cells]] on level 5, or the top of the tower on Level 7.

## Illumination

Interior areas of the Spellbreaker Tower are lit by mundane lanterns and braziers which are situated throughout the tower. Any light sources located in areas where inmates can access them are locked shut and require a guard key to open.

Unless otherwise noted, these light sources shed bright light in a 10 foot radius and dim light for another 10 feet.

The prison is lit at all hours, though the guards may extinguish lights in parts of the prison that aren't being used.

Exterior sections of the tower (including the exposed areas of the fifth level featured on this map) are lit by moonlight, which counts as dim light (unless otherwise dictated by weather conditions).

## Terrain

The entirety of the Spellbreaker is made of extremely dense and sturdy black basalt. There are no signs of traditional masonry, and any decorative elements in the walls and floors appear to have been carved into the stone. The ground here should be treated as normal terrain unless otherwise noted.

In some places there are sections of hardwood flooring, these are built and laid over the stone flooring for purely aesthetic value. Pulling up the slats of wood only reveals more stone below.

To prevent suffocation from the extensive use of oil lanterns and braziers in the tower, all levels feature feature obscured ventilation chutes that run through the wall out to the face of the tower, allowing for easy air exchange.

The walls are made of the same material and require siege weapons to break, unless someone finds existing weaknesses to exploit. Cells feature iron bars at the entrance which are equally sturdy. Some rooms may have wooden partition walls which are not as sturdy.

### Prison Doors

There are several types of doors in this prison:

**Wood Doors**

Found on all levels of the tower but become rarer on higher security levels where controlling movement is more important. These doors are braced with iron and use heavy, sturdy wood. They have AC 17, 25 HP, and are DC 15 to open.

**Light Gates**

Appearing on most levels, but commonly used on Blue Block, they are used on lower priority areas. These doors are made of iron, are sturdy, and either fixed on heavy hinges or tracks. They have AC 18, 50 HP, and are DC 16 to open.

**Medium Gates**

Found most checkpoints between levels and cell blocks, these are the most common gate in the tower. These doors are fixed to heavy hinges or rails and are exceptionally sturdy. They have AC 19, 60 HP, and are DC 20 to open.

**Heavy Gates**

Found on the central security room and the highest security cell block, these gates are made of heavy, reinforced steel bar and plating, and fixed to shielded rails. Nearly unbreakable, they have AC 20, 75 HP, and are DC 25 to open.

## Elevation

The ceilings of all levels are 20 feet unless otherwise stated.

### Guard Presence

There are two kinds of guards in the Spellbreaker Tower, the standard [[Spellbreaker Guard]] and their heavier counterpart, the [[Spellbreaker Riot Guard]].

Standard guards can be found on all levels, and are generally alert while on shift. They tend to be more relaxed the lower the level is, with Levels 1 and 2 being the most relaxed, and least alert.

Generally speaking neither standard nor riot guards are interested in violence, and attempt to deescalate altercations before they come to blows. If combat does break out, guards won't kill.

Riot guards appear in only one place, Level 5, Red Block, and they are more heavily armed, with crossbows and shields. These guards are willing to use lethal force if the security of the tower and the lives of everyone inside it are at risk, such as if Red Block was to suffer a breakout. However, they don't kill unless given no other choice.

### Inmate Presence

The prison is home to numerous inmates that fall into three types: The [[Blue Block Inmate]], [[Yellow Block Inmate]] and [[Red Block Inmate]]

**Blue Block Inmate**

The least dangerous denizen of the cell blocks, these inmates are typically a citizen with a magical curse or malady that makes them dangerous to others but otherwise not likely to cause trouble while magically suppressed. Most are allowed to roam the Blue Block level with only light supervision, being locked up in the evening or if they cause trouble.

**Yellow Block Inmate**

Typically someone with sufficient mundane or magical skill to be very dangerous and some history of violence or danger associated with them. They might be cursed or possessed, or they might be a dangerous killer unfit for the Hallows jail. These inmates tend to be locked up around the clock for everyone's safety, but a rare few may be trusted to mingle with the Blue Block.

**Red Block Inmate**

Easily the most dangerous entity in the prison are these inmates. Even without magic they are resourceful, ruthless, and likely to be dangerous to others. These are serial killers, magical assassins, or otherworldly monsters that pursue their whims unbidden by the law.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Magically Suppressed
>
> It should be noted that all of the actors provided for the prison are configured without any magical abilities since they reside in a tower that utterly suppresses them. This means it is likely that these inmates would be substantially more dangerous than their listed CRs should they ever managed to escape the prison.
>
> Keep this in mind should any inmates escape and appear later.

### Prison Hours

The prison is patrolled 24/7 with guards rotating out every 6 hours. Supplementary personnel, such as cooks and healers (all of them [[Cindaric Adherent]]) are escorted inside the prison during the morning, and are escorted out before sundown.

**Blue Block**

Inmates on Blue Block are afforded ample time to wander, socialize and relax outside their cells, only being forced inside if they cause problems, or after sundown.

**Yellow Block**

Inmates on Blue Block are afforded ample time to wander, socialize and relax outside their cells, only being forced inside if they cause problems, or after sundown.

Yellow Block are occasionally let out and allowed to visit Blue Block's amenities and eat with them if they have not cause any problems in recent memory.

**Red Block**

Red Block inmates are never let out under any circumstances.

### Intercom Panel

In each central security room there is a mechanical intercom system which allows the security teams to communicate with each other, broadcast to the floor, or trigger an alarm. Guards use this to make announcements, and call for help without raising alarms.

### Alarm States

The controls for the alarms are clearly marked, and has two settings: "Local Alarm" or "Full Alert."

The local setting causes an alarm to go off across the level it's activated on, alerting the guards to a problem, and prompting them to secure checkpoints and stairwells. Guards activate a local alert if inmates become unruly, if a fight breaks out, or a similar issue arises.

The full alert setting causes an alert to go out across all levels, inciting the guards to lock down the prison, and for new guards to begin reinforcing the blue, yellow and red blocks with riot guards. Guards activate a full alert only if there is a breach in the red block, or if a full level is in riot and a risk of breakout is present.

### Gate Controls

On levels 3 ,4 and 5, there are a series of colored buttons. These buttons connect to various checkpoints around the prison, and allow the guards to open the doors.

When a guard wants through a door that they can't open themselves, they enter sequence of long and short button presses. The guards in the control room respond with a flash of the button to indicate they are waiting for the second pattern, which the guard the enters.

If correct, the security operator triggers the door unlock mechanism by holding the button down. The doors lock themselves once closed, requiring the process to be repeated.

In some cases the guards in the central room can see the checkpoints directly, and make a judgement call based on that. As long as the guard is not acting strangely or in distress, they will open the door promptly.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Incorrect Gate Codes
>
> If an incorrect first or second pattern is issued then the door won't open and one or more guards will go to verify who is at the gate.
>
> The patterns changes every day for every level, and levels don't use the same patterns.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Handling Gate Codes
>
> The codes are not a physical game item or piece of info for the characters to collect, and there are no rules for determining what the codes are for a day, instead it should be handled in abstract: either the party has a code for a door, or a full level, or they don't.
>
> #### Finding the Codes
>
> It is possible for characters to determine the challenge and response patterns a number of ways:
>
> - Observing guards, perhaps with a successful Perception check would let a character figure out a code for a single gate.
> - The central security rooms should have notebooks with the day's current challenge and response patterns.
> - Some guards may (foolishly) carry a note with the patterns for their gate or for the level, which a character might notice and be able to steal.

### Central Elevator

A central elevator runs through the tower, granting access to multiple levels. This elevator can be called and controlled from any level it services, which include Level 1, Level 2, Level 4, Level 5 and Level 6.

This elevator takes a minute to travel one level, and cannot be stopped once in motion. The shaft that contains the elevator does not descend past the lowest level of this area map, and the machinery is hidden somewhere in a lower level.

The shaft is lined with stone bricks that can be climbed, however each level is automatically sealed with a medium iron gate when the elevator platform isn't present to prevent falling. Opening this gate from the inside while climbing should be very difficult and done with **-2 Banes** under most circumstances.
