# Treasure Room

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The narrow stone passage gives way to a small room laden with golden coins, gilded vessels, and fat ingots of pure gold worth a small fortune. Even without light the gold seems to glow faintly on its own.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Curious Coinage
>
> Examining the coins, they don't appear to be ones minted in Ordain and each feature a shrouded female figure on both faces.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Trade** know that these coins were not minted by any nearby city, guild, or government that they know of.
>
> Characters with **Culture: Ordani** or making a successful **Society (DC 16)** check recognize that the figure on the coins is Avilla, the Spellbreaker, the very shard goddess that made this tower centuries ago.
>
> #### Glittering in the Gold
>
> Eight large [[Lamberite]] can be found in this collection. A successful **Wilderness (DC 16)** check notes that these are a well known semi-precious stone found all over Ember and the source of the glow is not well understood except for the fact that it's not inherently magical, but a natural quality of the gemstone itself.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Trade** or **Knowledge: Crafts** know that these gems are commonly used in decorative light fixtures, jewelry and art pieces.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### A Wealth of Weight
>
> The total value of the hoard here is close to ten thousand gold, however the weight of the ingots and coins are considerable, making transport of the coin hard, especially for a party wearing prison uniforms and lacking proper packs and gear.
>
> A party hoping to extract this wealth should have a hard time doing so, with the collective weight of the coins slowing them down, even if they manage to find bags and packs in the prison to carry them.
>
> Parties hoping to glide or climb their way to safety will quickly realize the extra weight of the gold makes both routes impossible.
