# Notable Inmates

This list represents a roster of potential inmates the party can encounter throughout the prison blocks of Spellbreaker Tower. These inmates are not present in the preconfigured scenes, but can be represented by the provided inmate stat blocks. Feel free to create your own inmates, perhaps ones connected to characters in the party, or related to their backgrounds.

### Blue Block Inmates

**Serce Darran**

A painter of some renown who specialized in realistic depictions of Ragen and its elementals. Out of the blue she went mad and began setting buildings on fire. Turns out a quirk of her high Ragen attunement is being easily possessed and influenced by corrupted elementals. The tower suppresses her elemental powers, but not the lingering pyromaniacal tendencies. Resides in Blue block when the urges get out of control, and lives on a lower level normally.

**Norban Grozburn**

A spellcaster convicted on several charges of destruction of property, intent to cause mayhem, assault with dangerous magic, and arson. He was arrested after flying over the city throwing fireballs. Utterly de-fanged in the tower, he is eccentric and antisocial, but harmless.

**Nemi Korill**

Claims to hear the Abyss commanding her to harm people. Has assaulted half a dozen people with weapons, and critically injured several. The tower seems to suppress the voices, and she is presently serving out her sentence, but is expected to be moved into a tower community afterward.

**Marrit Marrison**

Cursed with poison skin so that they could never get close to anyone, they attempted to live a normal life but eventually became bitter and disillusioned and began using their poison to make people ill, nearly killing several. Presently undergoing rehabilitation in Blue Block, and is expected to be moved into a lower level community in the future.

**Vrover Bindman**

A former Agrimage who was convicted of mass poisoning and destruction of property after he began tainting land and the crops growing on them. He is supposedly an agent of an "earth lord" who demands all the earth be turned rancid. Though magically disarmed, he refuses to recant his cause or be rehabilitated.

### Yellow Block Inmates

**Taskel the Tinker**

A clockmaker and artificer of seemingly limited skill, Taskel killed twelve people and mangled twice as many in the last decade with "faulty" devices that would catastrophically fail in dangeorus ways. Chain investigation into his creations determined he was sabotaging the devices intentionally to harm people. His cell is searched weekly.

**Flint Karsha**

A famed musician, renowned for being able to play any instrument you give him. Beloved in every city he visited, it wasn't uncommon for fans to leave town with him. Trouble was, they'd never be seen again. His killing spree was ended when he was caught trying to lead a group of fans out of Ordain. Is not allowed access to music instruments, even while magically suppressed.

**Corlin Juddit**

A former member of the Cindaric faith, and a healer, they turned from performing life-saving surgery to collecting organs and selling them to an unknown buyer. They refused to give up their contact, and to this day show no remorse.

**Mira Silvane**

An herbalist and alchemist renowned for her medicines, she was jailed after she decided to make several batches of healing potions into lethal toxins, killing half a dozen people including two prominent Anachraenum adventurers.

### Red Block Inmates

**Skwang the Magnificent**

Former stage magician; cheerful, his state routine involved making members of the audience disappear. Sometimes they never reappeared. Arrested in Ordain for attempted murder after a Veiled Chain investigation revealed that he would kill and hide the audience members if it was his final act in a city. He has been linked to over eighteen unsolved disappearances following his shows.

**The Bleak Weaver**

A strange, intelligent [[Young Cheliceraeth]] spider found hunting citizens in [[Quarry Run]]. Seemingly invincible, it was fought to a standstill at which point it became dormant. Without any way to dispatch it permanently, the Hallows ordered it stored in Red Block. The tower has not suppressed whatever force makes it immortal.

**Blood Drinker**

A hulking vrjnhar that refuses to speak. They seem to serve as a chosen warrior for an unknown deity or force that demands they drink the blood of the slain. Their raw strength alone makes them dangerous, let alone the potent magical abilities the tower is actively suppressing.

**The Cube**

This hovering, humming, carved stone cube originated from the Cascillian Republic, though its origins have never been clearly established. It radiates a purely malevolent energy and when not suppressed is capable of drawing psychic energy from nearby beings, causing them to become erratic and violent before dying.

**“Mercy”**

Occupying the most locked-down cell in the tower's Red Block, Mercy appears to be nothing more than a middle-aged human woman. However, their rumored abilities are numerous, and change with each source; they might be are either a supernatural assassin, a rogue archmage, a Shard god, a being from beyond Ember, or something else entirely.
