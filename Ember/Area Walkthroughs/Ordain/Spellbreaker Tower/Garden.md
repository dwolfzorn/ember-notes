# Garden

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Long planters line the perimeter of this platform, and crimson broadleaf ferns grow up alongside decorative shrubs with golden foliage. The warm colors are contrasted with points of stark white and blue flowers.
>
> At the center of this garden a large circular fountain bubbles away loudly, as a stone-faced status of Avila the Spellbreaker looks on to the horizon.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Spellbreaker
>
> This statue is a small-scale recreation of the greater visage of Avilla that appears on the tower itself. Though some artistic liberties have been taken with the rest of the spellbreaker's form which the tower's depiction doesn't provide, it effectively captures the essence of the goddess.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Crafts** or making a successful **Wilderness (DC 13)** check note that the stone is the same basalt as the tower is made of, but has distinctly different patterns, indicating it was likely quarried from the region, but not from the same body of stone as the tower.
>
> A placard at the base notes the statue was donated to the spellbreaker prison sixty years ago, and that the sculptor wished to remain anonymous.
