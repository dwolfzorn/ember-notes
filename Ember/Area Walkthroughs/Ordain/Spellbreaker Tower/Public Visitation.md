# Public Visitation

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Six stone tables stand in two neat rows with a seventh in the middle of them. The entire space is hemmed in by light iron bars that rise all the way to the ceiling.
>
> Each table has space enough for four to sit together for public group visitation, and are fitted with iron shackles and chain in the event an inmate needs to be restrained here.
>
> A security checkpoint controls passage in and out of the area, while granting the attending guards clear view of everyone inside.

Visitors heading into these rooms and inmates coming out of these rooms are searched by an attending guard before they are allowed to move on.

Inmates and groups may visit here. Inmates are only shackled if they are troublemakers, or from Yellow or Red Block, though it is rare for Red Block inmates to ever be let out of their cells.
