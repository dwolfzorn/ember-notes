# Medical Cells

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The cells here are ten by fifteen foot rooms cut into the black basalt, each opening framed by light iron bars and jail door set into the dark stone. Lanterns light the rooms and halls between them.
>
> Inside each cell is a low stone bed topped with a thin mattress and light bedding folded tight. Alongside them a small stone table and stool provide a place for occupants to sit.
>
> The air carries a faint, sharp trace of cleaning spirits and boiled herbs, as if the medical ward sits just out of sight.

Inmates are kept here to await or recover from medical treatment.
