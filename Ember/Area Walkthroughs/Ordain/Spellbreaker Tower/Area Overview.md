# Area Overview

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Area Map Context
>
> The area map for the Spellbreaker Tower depicts the top seven levels of the tower including the tower's roof. This area contains a prison with escalating levels of security, and is meant to contain troubled figures who are a danger to themselves and society for various reasons including criminal ambitions.
>
> This multi-level location serves as the key scenery for the [[Diplomatic Impunity]] quest events [[Doing Time]] and [[Breaking the Tower]].
>
> - This map represents only a small portion of the tower, there are potentially another eight levels beneath this area map which house citizens of ordain who are cursed or otherwise afflicted with magical maladies that require them to be quarantined.
> - Entry to this tower is chiefly achieved through the ground floor of the tower, and leads the party to the [[Entry Checkpoint]]. However, admittance to the upper levels is limited to guards, support staff, incoming inmates, and visitors.
> - Additional info and mechanics regarding the various chambers of this section of the tower are located in the [[Gameplay Details]] section of this walkthrough.

## Gazetteer Entry

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Spellbreaker Tower rises stark and angular, in stark contrast to Ordain’s loftiest buildings, its sheer faces and sharp corners softened by the intricate sculpture of a hooded woman cloaked in a flowing robe that becomes part of vast, curling banners that wrap the exterior. Unlike the gleaming white of the rest of Ordain's skyline, the spellbreaker is a dark spire of stone pulled from the earth below the city, and shaped into a pillar stark reminder of a god's power.

The Spellbreaker Tower is Ordain’s most infamous edifice, known for being both a place of healing for those afflicted by magical maladies, and the last line of confinement for individuals too dangerous to be allowed in the world. A powerful aura of divine power here nullifies magic in the tower and surrounding areas.

- **District Type**: Institutional
- **Population**: 300 (Low. Patients reside here temporarily, while prisoners are permanent residents. Overall population of both is presently low.)
- **Lifestyle**: Controlled. (Veiled Chain officers, prison guards, Cindaric Healers and inmates occupy this location. Most activity is closely monitored.)
- **Services**: Counseling, curse-breaking, magical suppression, high-security containment.

The massive tower serves a dual purpose as Ordain’s maximum-security prison, and as a place where people far and wide may come to shed magical maladies and curses. The structure’s inherent aura of anti-magic ensures that no spell may be cast, nor magical item work, nor curse hold sway for long as the Spellbreaker's aura holds to its name.

While the lower levels of the tower, known as "The Foundation" tends to the cursed and enchanted, the upper levels of the tower are reserved for containing dangerous beings and items that could harm the city.

The entirety of this district is affected by a powerful [[Antimagic Field]] that emanates from the central Spellbreaker Tower.

### Culture

Life in the district is regimented, and methodical, whether that is working or being treated in the foundation levels of the tower, or as an inmate or guard in the higher prison levels. Visitors to the Spellbreaker must check in with the Sentinel Tower in the main courtyard before proceeding, and their every movement is watched.

The district is monitored and staffed by a small army of Cindarics, and members of the Veiled Chain, both supplemented by a small army of attendants and guards selected from the city.

### Landmarks

**Sentinel Tower**

The broad, lowest levels of Spellbreaker Tower are devoted to patients seeking refuge from curses, magical traumas, or syndromes beyond the reach of city magics. Here, consultations and mundane treatments are dispensed by the Cindarics, with simple beds offered to any in need. While the Foundation is open to most everyone, the doorways to the upper levels are carefully guarded.

**The Foundation**

Forming the broad base of Spellbreaker Tower, the Foundation is the vast ground-level of the prison, itself incorporating several floors of cell blocks and security areas. Most non-critical prisoners are housed and processed here, with only special prisoners moving up the tower. The Foundation features its own medical wing, a utilitarian cafeteria, visitation galleries, and an intake ward that handles visitors and prisoners coming and going from the tower.

**Overwatch Garrison**

Affixed to one side of the tower’s core, the Overwatch Garrison is a secure, multi-story facility built to house the Spellbreaker’s guard force, attending sages, clerks, and support staff. It features an armory and barracks, a galley and small infirmary ensure staff remain rested and ready. The garrison also features a large, covered, rooftop recreation yard with sweeping views of the Arctus Plateau, giving the occupants a rare chance to breathe fresh air while on rotation.

**The Gallery**

Built high into the tower, the gallery is a heavily secured vault where magical items are stored for safekeeping. All manner of dangerous items are retained here, their effects suppressed by the divine force the permeates the tower. The Gallery is guarded round the clock, and only the head of the guards and the the tower's warden may enter it or take others inside.

### Locals

**Warden Cyran Holst (Lawful Neutral, Ordani Human, he/him)**

A gaunt, quiet leader, Holst has spent nearly three decades balancing the expectations of the Ordinate, the Cascillian Republics' demands, and the needs of the Tower's inmates. His commitment to routine and fairness underlies every decision, rendering him respected by Ordinate, Cindaric, Cascillians and Veiled Chain alike. There is no aspect of operation within the tower prison levels that he doesn't know about.

**Marle Sinterra (Lawful Good, Ordani Drakon, she/her)**

A Tayan ex-patriate, she is the leader in charge of training and overseeing the regular guards, and making sure that the items in the Gallery are safe. Marle’s seriousness is balanced by a genuine concern for her team’s wellbeing. She keeps a low profile outside the tower, rarely socializing with locals.

**Security Specialist Barik Vell (Lawful Neutral, Cascilian Human, they/them)**

A practical, unsentimental technician who is the most recent in a long line of academic specialists arriving from the Cascillian Republic to study the tower and maintain the security systems within. He is expertly versed in every lock, gate, machine, and mechanism in the prison, even those not of Cascillian origin.

**“Mercy” (Chaotic Evil, Arcturian Human, they/them)**

Occupying the most locked-down cell in the tower's Red Block, this inmate is legendary within the prison itself. Every other inmate and guard has heard of them and has a theory about their background and is happy to discuss it. Appearing to be nothing more than a middle-aged human woman, their rumored abilities change with each source; they might be are either a supernatural assassin, a rogue archmage, or something else entirely. Only Warden Holst and the Hallows know the truth, and neither of them are willing to release any details, not even their true name.

### Lore

Rumors about the tower's nature and origin are are numerous, ranging from plausible to far fetched. Some swear that the Spellbreaker lives (or slumbers) under the tower, and that the goddess is consuming the magic in the area. Others say that the tower was built by the Cascillian Republic, who pays the Ordinate large sums to house its most foul spellcasters off-site.

In truth, the Spellbreaker Tower's history is not especially well known since most of the inciting events around its creation occurred nearly six centuries ago, and has largely passed out of living memory. However, a few pieces of knowledge endure, committed to history texts, and described by witness accounts:

The tower was raised almost six hundred years ago by the goddess known as the Spellbreaker, lifted from the very stone of the spire that it stands on. The divine power within the tower that unravels even the most powerful magic was placed there by the goddess for reasons only she knew, and the Cindaric order had some connection to the goddess and events related to the tower's creation and her vanishing.

Two centuries ago, a generation of master stonemasons clad the Tower with Avilla’s image, reshaping its silhouette into a grand tribute to the goddess that built the tower. The lack of mag made this a long, laborious process that required massive support cranes to be built to move the stone sections into place. About a decade ago the Stonehollow Quake left cracks in the tower’s decorative edifice, and required several years of dedicated work to repair.
