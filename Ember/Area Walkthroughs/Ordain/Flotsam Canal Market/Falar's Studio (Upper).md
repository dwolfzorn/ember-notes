# Falar's Studio (Upper)

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This zigzag chamber is unmistakably the studio of some prolific visual artist, and — judging from the abundance of paints and other materials here — an artist that specializes in the creation (or use) of novel pigments, mediums, and techniques. Over a half-dozen tables placed about the room are littered with countless jars, urns, bottles, and cans containing various oil paints and chemical solvents. Odors of alcohol and turpentine linger here, along with the earthy smells of natural pigments.

Along with [[Falar's Studio (Lower)]], this Stoneside-level dwelling is owned by the retired adventurer [[Falar]], a wizard-turned-shopkeep who plays a central role in the [[Mixed Media]] and [[A Sketchy Situation]] Events of the [[Local Color]] Side Quest.

The spacious studio here serves as Falar's private workroom, and bears the trademark signs of an experienced artist. This upstairs chamber spans the length two downstairs areas (and the 10-foot canal between): [[Falar's Studio (Lower)]] to the west, and [[The Bread Barge]] to the east.

The easternmost door here leads to the Stoneside platform via a wooden gangway that can be raised and lowered using a lever downstairs. Additionally, the illusory painting that obscures the stairway downstairs is a one-way image, and can't be seen from this side of the permanent illusion.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Interactable: Destructible Shelves
>
> The nine shelving units in the chamber here are destructible, and can be destroyed by any character who targets them with an &Reference[attack] action (AC 15, 8 hp).
>
> Each of the shelving units are [[Area Maps]], and can be toggled between their undamaged or destroyed states via the interaction tool in the Scene Controls interface on the left side of the screen.
>
> #### Upper Gangway Controls
>
> A lever next to the door on the eastern wall controls the wooden gangway that spans the gap between Falar's Studio (Upper) and the Stoneside area of the map. This timber gangway is normally raised, but can be lowered by the characters during [[A Sketchy Situation]].

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring the Studio
>
> A brief examination of the room reveals the following:
>
> - The studio appears to also serve as an ersatz living area, with signs of impromptu bedding and other accoutrements.
> - The studio contains nine shelving units that cater to the storage of paint and other art materials, including at least one set of [[Painter's Supplies]].
> - Several pieces of curious art here match the style and mediums of the House Salva mural from the [[Drawing Attention]] event, and also appear similar to [[Varinna's]] style.
>
> Any character who has previously visited Varinna's Studio and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check while examining the curious art is able to determine that the art pieces were painted to resemble Varinna's style, but that they are clearly from the hand of another artist. Some of Varinna's original art can be found nearby, no doubt used for reference during the creation of these forgeries.
>
> - **Knowledge: Arts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who spends at least 10 minutes while searching the room is also able to locate the following:
>
> - An inert [[Anachraenum Medallion]] that seems to have lost its arcane potential.
> - Several invitations to wealthy galas, dated many moons ago.
> - Falar's [[Notes on Elemental Binding]].
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check while searching the room also discovers:
>
> - [[Falar's Curious Note]].
> - A [[Spell Scroll]] of [[Arcanist's Magic Aura]].
>
> A character who casts the [[Detect Magic]] spell while searching the studio is able to locate the presence of the 5 [[Paint Globlin]] using their [[Paint Form]] to hide here, but the precise nature of the creatures isn't readily apparent.
>
> - Characters with **Knowledge: Elementals** can identify the hidden Paint Globlins as elemental creatures.
> - Characters with **Attunement: Cora** or **Knowledge: Cosmology** can identify the latent presence of lunar magic from the earth moon [[Cora]].

The moment one of the characters disturbs any of the paints or paintings here (including the Varinna forgeries), 5 [[Paint Globlin]] leap out of the forged paintings to attack the characters. Read the following aloud:

> [!abstract] Paint Globlin
> **[[Paint Globlin]]**
>
> Level 1 · Unknown Unknown
>
> ![Paint Globlin](../../../assets/image028.webp)

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Challenging Art
>
> These strange elemental creatures are intensely chaotic, and revel in creating mess and disorder.
>
> #### Paint Globlin Tactics
>
> The 5 [[Paint Globlin]] emerge from the forged paintings.
>
> At the start of combat, at least 2 of the Paint Globlins will use their [[Glob Lob]] attacks against potential ranged threats, while the rest of the Globlins will use their [[Paint Roller]] bonus action to close ranks with enemy characters.
>
> Over the course of combat, the Paint Globlins will prioritize the following actions and abilities:
>
> - They will happily plow through shelves and furniture when using their [[Paint Roller]] ability, showing no regard for the damage they will do.
> - Paint Globlins may use their action to topple the shelves of paint onto players, dealing `[[/damage 1d6 bludgeoning]]` damage if they are adjacent to the shelf when it falls over.
> - In melee, the Paint Globlins will use their [[Clobber]] attack.
> - From range, the Paint Globlins will use their [[Glob Lob]] attack.
> - When a Paint Globlin dies, their [[Death Burst]] feature triggers, potentially inflicting the &Reference[blinded] condition on nearby enemies. Blinded enemies become preferred targets for other Paint Globlins.
> - The Paint Globlins will fight to the death, and will either pursue enemies outside of the immediate area or target them with ranged attacks.
>
> The battle concludes when the Paint Globlins are defeated.
