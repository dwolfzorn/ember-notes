# Gazetteer Reference

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you make your way through the narrow aisles of impromptu merchant stalls and past the open doors of lenders offering fair trades for foreign coin, the voices of the shopkeeps and their customers blend into a steady hum. In and around them, the buildings are a mix of old wood, new stone, and generic metal; much like the goods on the carts surrounding you, no two pieces are ever quite the same.

This bustling riverside market decorated with reclaimed ship timber is known for the sheer number and variety of goods being sold in its common shops, rotating markets, and seasonal bazaars. Always open for business and usually jam-packed with merchants, customers, and more than a little clutter, Flotsam can be overwhelming for first-time visitors, though locals swear it has the best prices in all of Ordain.

- **District Type:** Commercial (trade, finance) with some Residential
- **Population:** 2,215 (Dense with shoppers and tourists during the day, with only a moderate population of merchants and artisans as full-time residents)
- **Lifestyle:** Scrappy (Getting less for more and making something out of nothing are considered art forms)
- **Services:** Financial Services (Banks, lenders, currency exchange) and Goods (Odds and ends, usually at a bargain)

### Culture

While there are plenty of places in Ordain to buy goods, there's nothing quite like the clutter and buzz of Flotsam, which has a unique mix of rotating stalls for local Trading House merchants, rented stands for traders visiting Ordain with goods from foreign lands, and bankers happy to store and trade coin to keep the commerce flowing. Even Flotsam's residential housing is a bit of a mix, with both long-term residential housing for local merchants and artisans who want to be close to their merchandise and short-term roots for rent for visiting traders. Often, those who live here bring some flavor of what they do or sell with them, giving the residential streets behind the shops the feel of a mosaic of cultures and ways of life.

Despite the seeming chaos, with merchant after merchant offering goods and sales, Flotsam does have some method to its madness. The Trading House market is the most permanent fixture, with rows of fixed wooden stalls that are distributed evenly between the five Houses and generally sell the same goods every day. In the rotating market outside of the Trading House market, on the other hand, stalls and carts are generally rented, changing hands frequently. Flotsam's regular bazaars fall somewhere in the middle - during these high-energy all-day events, carts are given out for free by lottery, giving merchants who are just getting started or unsure about the value of Flotsam to show off their wares to Ordani buyers.

### Landmarks

**Levee**

The facade of this riverside tavern is crafted entirely from driftwood culled from the bay south of Ordain, collected by its proprietors for generations. The Levee’s 5-star kitchen serves up Ordani versions of international cuisine; the flavorful menu is kept relatively short, but rotates each season. The popular tavern’s witty head chef Bodi Frumme is a well-traveled romantic who has a fondness for foreign chefs and employs at least 3 of his exes.

**Slipknot**

One of the largest cart rental shops in Flotsam, Slipknot is notable because it does a live auction for carts each morning instead of doing long-term reservations. Often, merchants can get the carts cheaper than they would elsewhere, but only if they don't bid too high. Owner Verix Andail (CG Ordani signborn, they/them) also uses the crowds that the auction generates to bring in merchants who might benefit from their other services, like coin changing and short-term storage, but has been dogged by recent rumors that they are fixing their auctions.

**Trading House Market**

This large stone building has rows of permanent stalls for each of Ordain's Trading Houses, where they showcase smaller goods related to their main area of interest, ranging from wearable accessories (House Salva) to unique spice blends (House Lillifeld) to ocean maps (House Bastilla). For Houses with fewer physical goods, stalls are often used for demonstrations of artisanship, with House Cevher putting on showcases of stoneworking and metalsmithing alongside the small trinkets they might put out for sale.

**Waterfront Bazaar**

The theme for the Waterfront Bazaar is always a little different, but its general appearance remains the same - a twisting network of stalls and tents that makes its way from Flotsam's main plaza to the floating docks on the river at the edge of the district and is one of the best places in the city to find goods from abroad. The market’s tents and awnings are haphazardly (if lovingly) fashioned from the sails of decommissioned ships, giving the entire area the appearance of a checkered blanket of recycled textile. It is said that there is some way to tell what is being sold by staring at the pattern but if that is true, the secret is not wildly known.

**### Locals**

**Chef Fancie Frumme (CN Lumek Fej, she/her)**

The most recent of Bodi Frumme's exes to join the cooking staff of Levee, she has taken his last name in what she claims is a Fej custom, despite the short length of their courtship, which, like many of Bodi's romances, only lasted about 4 months. While some of the other staff at Levee claim that she is simply trying to elevate herself using Bodi Frumme's name, no one will deny that she can cook, and her Fej desserts — including her famous Sugared Smoke — are the talk of the town.

**Mauny Setsun (N Arcturian human, he/him)**

Retired from sailing after being cursed by a figurine that gave him crippling fear of the water, Mauny now sells maps in the Trading House market on behalf of House Bastilla. He hopes to one day break the curse and be able to sail again, working toward his goal both by working at a market not too far from the Destine River that flows into Ordain and by keeping an eye out for any items in Flotsam that might shake the curse and get him back on the water.

**Hypatia Sissos (LN Ordani human, she/her)**

Hypatia is in charge of administering the Trading House markets, which often consists mostly of convincing the merchants of one house that they are not being given a disadvantage compared to the merchants of another. As a result she knows much more about trading house relations than most, though she sincerely wishes she didn't. Hypatia routinely develops new rules and regulations for the markets, mostly to keep the merchants busy with something other than questioning her, and does not care in the least that most simply ignore them.

**Tillamer Wins (CG Ordani human, she/her)**

Sick of getting lost in the Flotsam markets, Tilamer has been working on a system of message stones attached to Flotsam carts that will allow her to know what is being sold each day and create fast-drawn maps for visiting tourists. The initiative is controversial, with some merchants believing that it will attract new customers to stalls that might not otherwise get attention and others convinced it will lessen overall foot traffic as buyers go to exactly the stall they need.

**### Lore

Legend has it that Flotsam was created after a merchant's boat capsized in the river, and rather than let their goods float away, the crew decided to sell the floating crates at a discount. While this is likely not true, nearly every shop in Flotsam claims to have a plank from that boat somewhere on their property that visitors can touch or take a shaving from, for a nominal price.**
