# The Sortilege

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The crystal doorbell above the threshold to this arcane emporium resounds with an otherworldly cascade of chimes as you step into the shop's wondrous expanse. All manner of reagents and spell components line the shelves here, and countless magical implements lay locked away in glass display cases — beckoning you to inquire about their abstruse enchantments.
>
> A rune-scarred Wirrun mage operates the store from behind these counters, clad in a shimmering robe of dark gossamer. She addresses you with a welcoming gesture, her deep voice thrumming with sultry resonance like the purr of some great cat:
>
> > We've been expecting you.

One of Flotsam Canal Market's more esoteric destinations, this Promenade-level magic shop caters to spellcasters of all stripes — with a respectable inventory of wands, staffs, wondrous items, implements, components, and more.

> [!info] Social
> #### Conversation with the Shopkeep
>
> **Ro Chastain (Chaotic Neutral, Ordani Wirrun, she/her)**
>
> This enigmatic wizard is a purveyor of spell components, alchemical reagents, and arcane implements. Known for her wry sense of humor and her proclivity for riddles, Ro Chastain is one of Flotsam's most peculiar denizens, and — as the owner of the mysterious Sortilege — is the source (if not subject) of more than one local legend.
>
> Ro Chastain sells a broad selection of reagents and spell components for all disciplines and traditions of magic, as well as a handful of interesting magic items (see "Exploring the Shop" below).
>
> Ro also provides [[Spellcasting Services]] as a 6th level Wizard.
>
> Conversation topics that Ro is willing to discuss include (but are not limited to):
>
> - Her history as a purveyor of the arcane, and her knowledge of local arcane experts and phenomena.
> - Observations on [[Falar]], who she considers to have been a capable mage during his prime.
> - Her lack of information for (or relative interest in) [[Varinna]].
>
> A few specific dialogue options for Ro are provided below.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Falar?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Falar, you say? Well, there was a time when the paintmonger was a capable mage in his own right. But those days are behind us. He came in to the shop one afternoon a few years back, and traded nearly all of his reagents and implements for gold. The deal was too good for me to pass up. And it seems Falar has traded in his wand for a paintbrush.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Varinna?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I don't know the artist myself, but her reputation among the locals is one of appreciation (when they aren't unsettled by her style or choice of subject matter, that is).

In addition to speaking with Ro, the characters may explore the shop.
