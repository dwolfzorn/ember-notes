# The Bread Barge

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The welcoming smells of fresh-baked bread and savory meats waft through the air of this permanently-moored canal boat, which plays host to a snug little bakery known as "The Bread Barge," as indicated by a loaf-shaped sign affixed above the front door. Inside, Ordani bakers come and go between satisfied patrons who feed their faces with overstuffed sandwiches and piping-hot soups. It's hard to imagine a cozier place to grab a bite to eat here on the canal.

This aged river barge has been permanently affixed to the canal docks and converted into a restaurant/bakery. The northern end of the barge, where the kitchen can be found, is located offscreen. The Dockside eatery caters to all sorts of clientele, with a particular focus on quick and affordable meals for people on-the-go — sandwiches, soups, wraps, and massive Arcturian sweet potatoes (roasted and buttered to perfection).

If the party visits The Bread Barge during [[Mixed Media]], they will likely have a series of questions to ask the staff to help aid their investigation. The following social and exploration blocks presume the characters are in search of information relevant to the [[Local Color]] Side Quest.

> [!info] Social
> #### Conversation with the Staff
>
> The restaurant and bakery are staffed by a variety of part-time folks with a wide range of experience, but the two head servers are usually on-hand during their respective working hours — and are always eager to make a good impression on new patrons. &Reference[food]{Meals} range in cost from Poor to Wealthy, but all are high quality.
>
> **Xavi Orfeld (Lawful Neutral, Ordani Kivahr, he/him)**
>
> This perpetually-groggy stargazer is the Barge's head server during daytime shifts. Although he enjoys his role here in the restaurant with a fair deal of enthusiasm, Xavi's head is always in the cosmos, thinking of far-off places and stranger realms than this.
>
> Conversation topics with Xavi include (but are not limited to) the following:
>
> - His favorite facts and legends about Ember's moons and Inner Realms.
> - A list of daily specials, which always include the oversized Arcturian sweet potatoes, prepared in an interesting style du jour.
> - Details about the Bread Barge owners, who are members of [[House Lilifeld]]. Rumor has it the owners (like many locals) have become increasingly dissatisfied with Lilifeld's excessive overreach in recent months.
> - If the party asks him about [[Falar]], Xavi will reveal that the paintmonger breezes through the barge on occasional mornings, but rarely talks and never stays long.
>
> **Tula Jondak (Chaotic Good, Ordani Human, she/her)**
>
> This wry twenty-something scholar is the Barge's head server during evening shifts. By day, Tula studies Ordani law in the hopes that one day she'll save enough coin to move closer to [[Assembly Hills]] — where she diligently aims to serve as a magistrate of Precept Hall.
>
> Conversation topics with Tula include (but are not limited to) the following:
>
> - Her goal to become a magistrate of Precept Hall in Assembly Hills.
> - Her study routine, and her duties as head server here at The Bread Barge.
> - A list of daily specials, which always include the oversized Arcturian sweet potatoes, prepared in an interesting style du jour.
> - Details about the Bread Barge owners, who are members of [[House Lilifeld]]. Their opinions of the Trading Houses are somewhat mixed. On one hand, Lilifeld keeps the city fed; on the other, they've allowed the wealthiest of Ordain's citizens to determine the rules of modern commerce.
> - If the party asks her about Falar, Tula will reveal that the paint monger sometimes comes here for evening meals before heading to his studio next door.

The food is ample here, and the atmosphere is supremely relaxing — so much so, in fact, that purloining some grub is a relatively easy task:

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring the Barge
>
> Any character who makes a successful **`[[/skill sleightofhand 13]]`** check is able to steal `[[/roll 1d2]]` [[Rations]] worth of food. Everyone is allowed to get caught once, with merely a word of warning as the initial punishment. Repeat offenders, however, will be eighty-sixed with haste (and potentially get thrown overboard by multiple staff members and patrons).
