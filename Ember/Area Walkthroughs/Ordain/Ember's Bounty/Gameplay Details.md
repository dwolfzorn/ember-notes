# Gameplay Details

A naval ship so large it serves as a city district, the Ember's Bounty is known far and wide as the mobile domain of [[House Bastilla]], and plays host to a wide variety of obtainable goods and services for the enterprising people of Ordain. The Ember's Bounty is a boisterous place, where action and opportunity lurks around every corner — and this modest swathe of the upper deck is no exception.

## Objectives

The characters will journey to this section of the Ember's Bounty in search of the nefarious pirate lord [[Gastern Faviyos]], who has already been paid to hand over some equipment to the party for delivery to [[Katerin Bastilla]]. While they're aboard the famed House Bastilla vessel, the characters can:

- Take advantage of the location's various amenities, including leisure and entertainment as well as trade.
- Meet some of the locals and gain insight into their assorted lives, including their relative affiliations with House Bastilla and Ordain at large.
- Meet with the chaotic Gastern and his [[Wind Raider Boarder]] allies in the [[The Game Room]] to secure the equipment for Katerin, which will require a measure of negotiation (if not outright combat).

Most of the characters that populate this area map exist here with or without the presence of Gastern and his cronies. If the party wishes to revisit the area, locations and encounters remain generally the same as described within the body of this walkthrough, with the only notable exception being the [[The Game Room]] — which is similar in most aspects, but no longer serves as the temporary headquarters of Gastern and his allies.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Local Rules & Regulations
>
> By edict of Katerin Bastilla herself, the denizens of the Ember's Bounty must abide by three simple rules: no stealing, no killing, and no damaging of the ship.
>
> Any character who breaks one or more of the three rules will be thrown overboard by 6-12 [[Arcturian Sailor]] who arrive to the scene within `[[/roll 1d2+1]]` rounds. Acts of murder are likely subject to greater penalties, with individual punishments based on the specific circumstances at hand.
>
> Characters thrown overboard are placed on a watchlist and denied subsequent entry if they attempt to board the ship in the future. The full rules for falling overboard are featured below in the Elevation section of this page.

## Illumination

Interior areas of the Ember's Bounty are lit by mundane lanterns, which are situated throughout the ship at various intervals. Most of these lanterns are fueled by lantern oil, although a scattered few are known to be lit by inkaro pearls. Unless otherwise stated, these lanterns shed bright light in a 10 foot radius and dim light for another 10 feet. Meanwhile, table lamps like those in the [[The Game Room]] shed their own bright light in a 5 foot radius, casting dim light for another 5 feet.

Exterior sections of the Ember's Bounty (including the exposed areas of the main deck featured on this map) are lit by moonlight, which counts as dim light (unless otherwise dictated by weather conditions).

## Terrain

The hardwood floors of the Ember's Bounty are crafted from planks of Arcturian oak, cedar, and pine. The ground here should be treated as normal terrain unless otherwise noted. The interior walls of the ship are crafted from similar hardwoods.

The ship's doors are made of reinforced oak (AC 17, 25 HP, DC 16 to open), and are unlocked unless otherwise stated. When ship doors do happen to be locked throughout the Ember's Bounty — whether this deck or otherwise —the associated lock complexity most often ranges from Good (DC 15 to unlock) to Superior (DC 20), with a few higher-difficulty exceptions.

## Elevation

The ceilings of the various cabins and chambers located throughout the Ember's Bounty are 15 feet high, unless otherwise stated. The northernmost flooring of the exterior deck is situated 5 feet higher than the central section of the deck, which is readily accessible via the short flights of steps that connect them.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Cargo Hold Pits
>
> The two pits on the map depict open-air access to a large cargo deck located 40 feet below. Any character who happens to fall into one of these pits must succeed on either a **Athletics (DC 16)** or **Athletics (DC 16)** check to catch the railing as they tumble over it, taking `[[/damage 4d6 bludgeoning]]` damage on a failure.
>
> Any characters who fall into the cargo hold this way can return to the main deck via the [[Mechanical Lift]], which takes the character a total of `[[/roll 1d2 + 1]]` turns. Alternately, characters with a fly speed can navigate the distance between the cargo deck and the main deck more directly.

The wooden railing that lines the edges of the ship's deck is 4 feet high. Meanwhile, the network of ropes that hang above the exposed areas of the main deck are suspended roughly 10-15 feet high above the wooden planks of the floor.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Falling Overboard
>
> Any character who is thrown overboard immediately falls at least 60 feet into the roiling waters of the ocean below, and must succeed on a **Athletics (DC 15)** or **Athletics (DC 15)** check to avoid taking `[[/damage 3d6 bludgeoning]]` damage. On a successful check, any damage resulting from the fall is halved.
