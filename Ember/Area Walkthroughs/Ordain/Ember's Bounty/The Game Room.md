# The Game Room

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Stepping into this large, smoke-filled cabin invites an assault on the senses. The dim glow of lantern light is painted with the hazy tint of cigar fumes and close-quarters humidity, and you find yourself drowning in a cacophony of noise — including everything from the clinking of glass and shuffling of cards, to the squeaking of furniture and the din of idle chatter. Above it all, the melodious sound of a piano dances towards you from the northern end of the room, where a moody bard plays some half-forgotten tune.
>
> Several world-weary faces turn to examine your group as you make your way into this crowded chamber, which is packed with all manner of patrons, crew and visitors like. Sailors sit shoulder-to-shoulder with landlubbers in search of good times or easy money; and (if the far-flung accents and curious atmosphere are any indication) everyone here has a story to tell.

The Game Room is the veritable centerpiece of this section of the main deck, and provides a lush environment for patrons to while away their extra hours — with activities like eating, drinking, gaming, gambling, and the occasional musical performance taking the spotlight.

The crowd here is comprised of assorted [[Arcturian Sailor]], [[Ordani]], and [[Ordani Ruffian]], who are engaged in the Game Room's activities to varying degrees. Meanwhile, the cabin is attended at all times by a work staff that includes: a trio of **Bartenders**, two **Cooks**, and a rotating cadre of **Card Dealers** (all of which are **Arcturian Sailors** employed by [[House Bastilla]]).

> [!info] Social
> #### Carousing
>
> The Game Room is open for business 24 hours a day, and has a modest assortment of drinks and food available for purchase. The drinks are watered down, and the meals are dubious, but the prices are reasonable.
>
> Although a variety of minstrels and performers are known to play here, the musician most commonly associated with the room's piano is a playfully sardonic bard named **Robbi Mars** (Chaotic Good, Ordani Human, he/him).
>
> If the party wishes to gather information, any character who succeeds on a **Awareness (DC 15)** check is able to round up some details relatively pertinent to the task at hand in [[Parcels & Pirates]], including:
>
> - The precise location of [[Gastern Faviyos]] and his [[Wind Raider Boarder]] crewmen, seated at a square card table in the darkened northwest corner of the room.
> - Information about the various vendors and crewmen stationed throughout this particular section of the main deck (the cabins featured on the area map and beyond), along with details about their associated shops and services.
> - Unsubstantiated rumors pertaining to House Bastilla and various districts of Ordain (per Gamemaster discretion, based on whatever quests or events are active for the PCs at the time).
> - Characters with **Knowledge: Crime** or **Knowledge: Intrigue** have **+2 Boons** on this check.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Games of Chance
>
> Several games of skill and chance are available to play here in the Game Room, including backgammon, poker, and dominos (among other, more regional games).
>
> Characters who wish to engage in these games of chance can do so at their leisure. Finding an opponent is easy, as someone is always willing to play, and gambling is often encouraged (even, if not especially, when it's a match between close friends or associates).
>
> You can allow the characters to play these games in real time if the activity suits your table. However, you should also feel free to utilize the following Gambling Skill Challenge as an approximation to expedite these supplementary moments.
>
> - Any character who attempts to win a game of chance must succeed on 3 out of 5 skill checks, chosen from the following list of potential checks: **Deception (DC 15)**, **Deception (DC 15)**, **Awareness (DC 15)**, **Awareness (DC 15)**, **`[[/skill sleightofhand 15]]`**.
> - Each skill check can only be attempted once. The player character chooses the skill for the first check, followed by the opponent's choice of the second skill check; participants continue alternating (with either the PC or the NPC choosing the skill at hand) until win or lose.
> - Betting is handled between the participants, with no oversight from the House Bastilla crew stationed nearby. Altercations, however, are dealt with per the "Local Rules & Regulations" described in the [[Gameplay Details]] section of this area walkthrough.
>
> #### Kitchen Supplies
>
> Any character who takes the time to search the Game Room's scullery to the southeast (without getting caught) and succeeds on a **Awareness (DC 14)** check is able to find a [[Barrel Room Key]], which unlocks the [[Barrel Room]] located to the west nearby.
