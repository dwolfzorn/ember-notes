# Gazetteer Reference

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Nestled beneath fuming smoke stacks and ashen clouds of perpetual exhaust, this grimy district is marked by soot-stained windows and the ambient staccato clanging of countless steel anvils. Here and there, you notice local denizens going about their business amidst the area's incessant haze, which cloaks the cobbled streets and alleys in a sweaty aura of curious intent. It's easy to understand how this place came to be known as The Smokerie, a neighborhood where the forges and the people alike smolder with anticipation.

The Smokerie is a grimy, ash-stained neighborhood defined by its assortment of smithies, its lower-income housing, and its proximity to the slag and cinders of the nearby Dustforge district. Yet, in spite of these relatively harsh conditions, The Smokerie is a place where opportunity abounds and friends can be made from the least likely of strangers. The rhythmic sound of countless hammers striking their anvils echoes through each crooked street and twisted alley of this dingy sector of Ordain, where people of modest means settle in to either start chasing their dreams or forget about them altogether.

- **District Type:** Commercial and Residential
- **Population:** 2,524 (Medium. Smithies, forges, and homes with moderate foot traffic).
- **Lifestyle:** Poor-to-Comfortable (Smiths, metalworkers, apprentices, laborers).
- **Services:** Smithing, manufacturing, material processing, skill training.

When it comes to laying low in Ordain, the Smokerie is a great place to begin (that is, if you can handle the incessant cacophony and the smell of scorched air). Despite the neighborhood’s less-savory qualities, it nevertheless plays home to several upstanding citizens and artisans of influence; many of Ordain’s best blacksmiths happen to be some of the city’s finest people, and here in The Smokerie they're known to take on apprentices in larger numbers. Locals colloquially refer to these mentorships as "Academies" (but this stewardship is rather informal compared to the pomp and circumstance of other Ordani institutions).

They say the Smokerie has a way of keeping you warm when the rest of the city grows cold. But, then again, they say a lot of things in Ordain.

### Landmarks

**Burnside Arms**

The celebrated workshop, forge, and storehouse of the famed Kivahr artificer [[Janix Mance]]. Known far and wide for the skillful work of its apprentices as well as its forgemaster, the Burnside Arms is also manned by a cadre of construct helpers — who are ready to assist Janix (or the entire neighborhood) at a moment's notice.

**The Edge of the World**

This 24-hour forge and armory operated by the blacksmith **Eilo Vark** (Chaotic Good, Hulg'run Arcturian, he/him) is known for the keen quality of its assorted blades and its skilled proprietor's appetite for rare foreign tales. If there is an exotic weapon to be found anywhere in Ordain's urban confines, certainly it will be found here.

**The Parched Lark**

This beer-soaked tavern of ill-repute is managed by a retired privateer named **Tomnas Grey** (Chaotic Neutral, Human Arcturian, she/her), and serves up some of the cheapest rooms and trashiest talk in town.

**Home Away From Home Hostel**

This resilient and ramshackle youth hostel is run by the irascible caretaker **Celesta Po**e (Chaotic Neutral, Human Ordani, she/her), whose enigmatic past is a subject of constant and entertaining debate. The Home Away From Home keeps its doors perpetually open for the wayward and the aimless, even if the soot-stained hostel isn't always the safest place in the city for them to foster good habits or healthy relationships.

**The Pit Trap**

Inconspicuously situated in some unremarkable alley on the Smokerie's west side, The Pit Trap is a seedy warehouse nightclub of questionable repute. The Pit Trap is known for its sultry environment, and caters to a wide range of clientele — from casual locals in search of an unexpected good time, to big spenders who enjoy the location's impeccable sense of discretion. Fortunately for the regulars, the location of this sordid establishment remains a well-kept secret throughout the city (as does the weekly password for entry).

### Locals

****[[Janix Mance]]** (Neutral Good, Human Kivahr, he/they)**

This artful blacksmith operates a forge in the heart of the Smokerie called The Burnside Arms. Not only is Janix a proud and competent artificer and blacksmith, the Skybrush native is also a collector of antique arms and armor from all four corners of Ember (a passion he cultivated as a young Kivahr among the giant hinterlands back home in the Rustvar Valleys).

****Eilo Vark** (Lawful Good, Hulg'run Ordani, he/him)**

This friendly fifty-something blacksmith operates the Edge of the World forge and armory, and possesses an extensive knowledge of arms and armor the world over. If there's a niche interest or curious detail about modern and historical weaponry to be known, Eilo will most certainly familiar with it.

****Tomnas Grey** (Chaotic Good, Human Ordani, she/her)**

Known for her provocative worldly tattoos and her revolving door of would-be suitors, this retired privateer is the proprietor of the Parched Lark tavern. Although she exudes an air of strictness with new acquaintances, Tomnas Grey is a bawdy, adventurous woman at heart who never turns down a good time (unless it inconveniences her peace of mind).

### Lore

The Smokerie is aptly named after the embers of its countless foundries and the slag and cinders of the nearby Dustforge. Some denizens of other, more affluent districts of Ordain are known to deride The Smokerie for its begrimed industrialism, and false rumors about regarding fiery accidents and citizens scarred by molten metal are not uncommon — but these tall and troublesome tales hold very little credence. Not all is fair in The Smokerie, however, as illicit deeds are fostered behind the closed doors of the Home Away From Home by its Kuarta-worshipping owners (who tend to train the unsuspecting youths in their eldritch ways).
