# Private Mezzanine

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The iron stairs leading upward are barred by velvet ropes and watched by hawk-eyed bouncers. Only the worthy are allowed to the upper level, and they alone are arbiters of that worthiness.
>
> Beyond them is the private mezzanine, and like the entry mezzanine opposite it, this space is comprised of metal grating on iron girders overlooking the Pit Trap's lowest point. Here, ornate wooden benches with plush cushions and decorated tables laden with food and drink rest on rich crimson carpets, each one separated from the next by standing partition walls made of paper and wood.

> [!info] Social
> #### Important People Only
>
> Access to this private mezzanine is at the discretion of the club staff alone, and a pair of [[Ordani Bouncer]] make accessing this area difficult unless you're on their lists.
>
> A successful **Diplomacy (DC 18)** or **Deception (DC 18)** check can allow a character to talk their way in. Alternatively, a bribe of 50 gp will also get a character and their companions access to the upper level.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Forced Exit
>
> Characters who are caught on the private mezzanine causing trouble, disturbing other patrons, or being there without permission from the bouncers will be physically removed from the club and not let back in.
>
> #### Bottle Service
>
> In the event that a fight breaks out, all of the tables in the private mezzanine feature `[[/roll 1d4+1]]` bottles of spirits and wine that can be utilized as improvised weapons.
>
> Characters can make use of these improvised bludgeons with an action, and the intended target must make a successful `[[/save dex 14]]` saving throw. On failure, the target is struck by the bottle, dealing `[[/damage 1d10 bludgeoning]]` damage and shattering the bottle.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Private Booth Service
>
> There is one private booth available in the private mezzanine that characters can occupy once they are past the bouncers. From this booth they can order drinks from the [[Bar]].
>
> Additionally, they can order a bottle of spirits for the table for 100 gp, or a bottle of wine for 200 gp.
