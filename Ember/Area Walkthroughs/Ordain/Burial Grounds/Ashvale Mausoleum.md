# Ashvale Mausoleum

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The exterior of this squat mausoleum is somewhat austere compared to other structures of the cemetery. The tomb's solitary door appears made of some kind of bronze alloy, and a placard beside it labels this edifice as the "Ashvale Mausoleum."

This mausoleum serves as the final resting place for a handful of celebrated [[Burnished Hand]] soldiers who dutifully served the Cindarics in recent centuries.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Locked Door
>
> The heavy bronze alloy door to the mausoleum is locked, and can be opened with a [[Burial Grounds Skeleton Key]] or a successful `[[/skill tool=thief 18]]` check.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Inside the mausoleum's dank interior, you spy six ledger stones overlooked by four stone statues of heavily-armored warriors. At the rear end of the crypt, a large sarcophagus dominates the chamber, silent and stoic in the shadows.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring the Mausoleum
>
> Six ledger stones are set into the marble floor of the mausoleum, and a large sarcophagus occupies the building's rear. The four stone statues of armored warriors are crafted to resemble Burnished Hand soldiers from its earliest days.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Society (DC 13)** check is able to identify this mausoleum for the monument it is, and is also aware of the following historical context surrounding the edifice.
>
> Signifiers of [[Oaken]] culture are present here among the architectural embellishments — particularly upon the blades and armor of the four statues, and the mausoleum's stained glass windows.
>
> The mausoleum is named after the historical "Old Ashvale" district of Ordain, where Lower Ashvale stands today, a neighborhood where the interests of the Cindaric Sages and their Burnished Hand allies are paramount.
>
> - **Ancestry: Oaken**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Path: Cindaric Initiate**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The Burnished Hand soldiers interred here were known as the Ashvale Six, and each was a formidable legend in their own right. Before their time as Burnished Hand protectors, the Ashvale Six were famed monster hunters, and fragments of their tales are told from here to the Tayan Empire.
>
> #### The Cursed Sarcophagus
>
> The sarcophagus at the eastern end of the chamber is locked and contains a solitary item, a rare and antique book of ancient [[Oaken]] myths known as [[Predators of the Prowling Death]].
>
> Any character who takes a moment to Search the mausoleum must succeed on a **Awareness (DC 11)** check to notice a sentence etched into the stone lid of the sarcophagus, written in **Language: Hardac**:
>
> > What is Ember's greatest legacy?
>
> This is a riddle to uncovering the pass phrase capable of disarming the magic trap that's been placed on the sarcophagus lock: "Resolution" (or, alternately "Resolve"), referring to the [[Resolute Legacy]] cultural feature.
>
> Characters who succeed on a **Awareness (DC 13)** check are able to discern the pass phrase after cross-referencing their collective knowledge.
>
> - **Ancestry: Oaken**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Path: Monster Hunter**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> A successful `[[/skill tool=thief 18]]` check is required to pick the lock. Alternately, a character can force the heavy sarcophagus lid open with a successful **`[[/skill strength 25]]`** check.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Cursed Sarcophagus Trap
>
> Any character who succeeds on either a **Awareness (DC 16)** or **Arcana (DC 14)** check while searching the sarcophagus is able to notice the telltale signs of an arcane trap on the locking mechanism.
>
> - The first character who opens this sarcophagus without first speaking the pass phrase "Resolution" (alternately "Resolve") or dispelling the magic trap becomes the target of a special [[Bestow Curse]] spell's **Curse Ability** activity. All characters within 20 feet of the sarcophagus must succeed on a `[[/save wisdom 20]]` saving throw or else have **-2 Banes** on Wisdom ability checks and saving throws for a duration of 10 days.
> - The spell also magically stains the characters with a metallic bronze dye, intended to identify them as grave-robbers to the Cindarics and Burnished Hand in Ordain. The dye has a duration of 10 days, and it can only be removed using a [[Dispel Magic]] spell.
