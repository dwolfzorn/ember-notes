# Funeral Hall

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This massive chamber is lit by the soft, orange glow of the colossal magma pit that lies far, far below the scorched stone platform raised above its center. Lined with a dozen marble benches and a guard rail for viewers, this grand hall plays host to the final stage of Cindaric cremation ceremonies — when a fully-prepared body is lowered into the molten liquid that seethes with fiery rage deep beneath the Burial Grounds.
>
> At the southern end of the room, two shallow alcoves are crammed full of half-burnt candles as petty, final offerings for the deceased. And a collection of six robed statues lines the walls to the east and west — effigies of notable Cindarics who look upon the fire-lit scene with eternal reverence.

The key feature of this enormous Funeral Hall is the 120-foot-deep magma pit that it's built around — known to some as the Heartblood Maw — which facilitates the final step of the Cindaric funerary rites: the cremation itself.

Congregants are invited to gather here after passing through the Chambers of Remembrance, Communion, and Reflection accordingly; and funeral-goers are known to both sit on the marble benches and stand near the edge of the pit itself during the ceremony. "To each their own," as the sages are fond of saying.

The door to the south usually remains closed and locked until the closing of the cremation ceremony, when attendees exit the facility arm-in-arm.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring the Funeral Hall
>
> Any character who searches the Funeral Hall is able to spot the following details:
>
> - While a few of the candles in the alcoves to the south are lit, most of them have either burned down to nubs or have grown cold with disregard.
> - A couple of loose [[Prayer Books]] of various faiths are scattered about the hall, forgotten beneath a stone bench here or there.
> - A lever is situated on the northern edge of the central, circular platform above pit. (See "Operating the Platform Elevator" below).
>
> #### The Candlelit Alcove
>
> Any character that succeeds on a **Arcana (DC 12)** understands that people of various faiths are known to light candles here and leave a small offerings according to their respective god’s traditions. This small ceremony is often accompanied by a temporary divine boon.
>
> - Any character who takes 1 turn to light a candle and leave their own offering instantly becomes the target of the [[Bless]] spell.
>
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Gods**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> #### The Six Sages
>
> Any character that succeeds on a **Society (DC 14)** or **Arcana (DC 14)** check recognizes the statues on the curving eastern and western walls as effigies of famous Cindaric Sages throughout history.
>
> - **Path: Cindaric Initiate**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: Cindaric effigies like these are sometimes known to be enchanted with beneficial magic to ease the troubled in times of distress.
>
> Once per day, each of these 6 statues can be activated to radiate the effects of a [[Calm Emotions]] spell in a 15-foot radius for a duration of 1 minute.
>
> - This effect is activated when a character touches the statue's Cindaric holy symbol and says the command phrase "By Adonia's Grace."
> - These words are etched in **Language: Arcden** across the statue, cleverly hidden in decorative patterns (successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check to spot while searching the statues).
>
> A successful **Arcana (DC 15)** check confirms the following:
>
> - The statues are enchanted with some sort of spell-like effect.
>
> - **Path: Cindaric Initiate**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Additionally, the [[Detect Magic]] spell reveals an aura of Enchantment magic on the statues.

### The Heartblood Maw

If and when one of the characters attempts to survey the edge of the fiery magma pit that dominates this chamber, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You can feel the soft, steady blast of heat before you even reach the edge of the massive pit at the rear of this enormous chamber, which appears to stretch down into the earth for more than a hundred feet. Down below, magma roils with constant fiery chaos. The Heartblood of Ember itself … radiant, magnificent, and pure. A headlong tumble into this gaping maw would mean certain death.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Operating the Platform Elevator
>
> A lever at the northern end of the platform controls the central elevator, which can be lowered into the magma 120 feet below. The mechanical elevator is quite slow, and travels 10 feet up or down per turn.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Arcana (DC 15)** check is aware of the platform elevator's purpose:
>
> - During a Cindaric funeral's final moments, a fully-prepared corpse is traditionally lowered into the magma by a senior sage after being placed on the stone slab at its center. Occasionally, another person of interest or a close family member will perform this solemn task.
>
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Machines**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Falling into the Maw
>
> Any character who falls into the Heartblood Maw (without managing to somehow escape) dies a sudden, fiery death as they're submerged in the pit's molten magma — a demise more painful than they ever imagined.
