# Embalming Room

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This wide chamber seems to have two vaguely distinct sections, each defined by its own precise stage of the embalming process. Mortuary slabs at the south end of the room appear tarnished with the crimson stains of bodily fluid, while a single stone slab to the north contains a full-prepared corpse wrapped in a traditional Cindaric funeral sheet. A clean water basin adorns the western wall; and shelves positioned around the room are decorated with all manner of tools, balms, components, and reagents.

This chamber serves as the facility's Embalming Room, where individual corpses are prepared for cremation according to sacred Cindaric rites. Younger sages work at the south end to prepare bodies with ritualistic ointments, salves, and wrappings, while veteran sages complete the final touches at the station to the north.

A large steel door is located on the eastern wall at the northern end of the chamber. This threshold is unlocked, and can be controlled with a lever on each side.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring the Embalming Room
>
> If the characters decide to search the room, they'll find the following:
>
> - The corpses of 2 ordinary Ordani citizens, who appear to be unconnected to the party's investigation. The stone slabs they're laid upon are cluttered with incense burners and half-burned sticks of incense.
> - The shelves on the walls contain an array of incenses, herbs, spell components, salves, unguents, perfumes, and alchemical reagents. At your discretion, spellcasters in the party can loot material components they might need for various spells.
> - A clean water basin on the western wall, accompanied by a solitary jug.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Awareness (DC 13)** check is able to notice or locate the following:
>
> - The incense here hasn't been lit for a few hours, but ashes remain settled on the mortuary slabs from an earlier session.
> - The water basin doesn't appear to be attached to a water source of any kind, and the water is remarkably clean.
> - The first character to succeed on this check finds 2 vials of [[Holy Water]] and enough components to assemble a [[Healer's Kit]].
>
> Any character who examines the corpses for details must succeed on a **Medicine (DC 13)** or **Awareness (DC 13)** check to determine the following:
>
> - The corpse at the south end of the room appears to have been the result of a drowning. Meanwhile, the corpse at the north end of the room is too obscured by its funeral wrappings to be fully examined.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> A successful **Arcana (DC 13)** check confirms a few details:
>
> - The corpses are currently benefitting from the effects of a [[Gentle Repose]] spell, one of Ember's "sacred" Necromancy spells.
> - The water in the basin is sourced from permanent versions of spells like [[Create or Destroy Water]] and [[Purify Food and Drink]].
>
> Additionally, the [[Detect Magic]] spell reveals an aura of Transmutation magic on the water basin and an aura of sacred Necromancy on the corpses.
