# House Cevher Mausoleum

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Carved from an array of semi-precious stones, the various grave markers and statues surrounding this pear-shaped mausoleum bear the familiar names and faces of deceased members of House Cevher, Ordain's famed Trading House. A placard above the door labeled "HOUSE CEVHER" confirms the stately crypt's ownership, and the seams around its heavy iron doors provide the barest glimpse of the half-dozen graves that lurk inside.

This mausoleum is one of many such tombs owned by [[House Cevher]], and contains the remains of various Cevher personalities who predate or abstain from the Cindaric faith.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Locked Doors
>
> The two iron doors to the mausoleum are locked, and can be opened with a [[Burial Grounds Skeleton Key]] or a successful `[[/skill tool=thief 18]]` check.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The ledger stones in this pear-shaped mausoleum live up to the name posted on the placard outside. Rather than the traditional white rock you see throughout the cemetery, these grave markers appear to be made of various semi-precious stones — agate, rose quartz, and moonstone among them. This crypt seems well-attended, as evident by the somewhat recent footprints that track through here, and the lambent inkaro pearls of the braziers.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring the Mausoleum
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Society (DC 13)** check is able to identify this mausoleum for the monument it is, and is also aware of the following historical context surrounding the edifice.
>
> - The corpses interred here are the remains of eight family members of [[House Cevher]], Ordain's dynastic Trading House. The ledger stones mark the graves of family members lost during a plague of Crimson Cough several generations ago, including the geologist Druna Cevher and her twin children, the famed warrior Lelynd Cevher, and others.
>
> - **Knowledge: Trade**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Culture: Ordani**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The easternmost stones here comprise the graves of a two House Cevher cousins, Erna and Ora, who were known for their innate divination abilities.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Arcana (DC 16)** check is able to identify the ledger stones of Erna and Ora Cevher as unique substitutes for arcane foci with a special function.
>
> The two sconces on the southern wall each contain one [[Inkaro Pearl, White]]. These have been replaced recently by the Lamplighters who work the Burial Grounds to facilitate forthcoming visitations from House Cevher descendants.
>
> - [[Identify]]: The caster automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Once per week, an arcane spellcaster who uses these ledger stones as an arcane spellcasting focus can cast the [[Divination]] spell, whether they know the spell or not.
>
> #### Sarcophagus Contents
>
> Both of the sarcophagi here are locked. The sarcophagus on the western wall contains the mortal remains of Druna Cevher and the following:
>
> - A tarnished [[House Cevher Signet Ring]].
> - A dusty [[Magnifying Glass]] with the initials "D.C." etched into the wooden handle.
>
> The sarcophagus on the southern wall contains the remains of Lelynd Cevher and the following:
>
> - A suit of bronze [[Chain Mail]] coated in a patina of verdigris.
> - A [[Longsword]] with a faceted garnet set into the pommel, worth a total of 50 gp.
>
> A successful `[[/skill tool=thief 20]]` check is required to pick the locks on the sarcophagi. Alternately, a character can force the heavy sarcophagi lids open with a successful **`[[/skill strength 25]]`** check.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Trapped Sarcophagi
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Awareness (DC 13)** check while searching the mausoleum is able to notice the telltale signs of matching mechanical traps on the two locked sarcophagi here.
>
> A subsequent **Awareness (DC 15)** or **Arcana (DC 17)** check confirms the nature of the trap: tiny holes in the stone floor surrounding each sarcophagus seem to be connected to tiny conduits, which likely emit or expel some kind of substance from an unseen reservoir or extra-dimensional space beneath the mausoleum.
>
> - **Knowledge: Machines**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - @CriticalSuccess[dc]: Additional benefits that occur as a result of critical success. Set [dc] to the base DC of the check.
>
> Each trap can be disarmed with a successful **`[[/skill sleightofhand 16]]`** check. Failure results in triggering the trap, and the trap can be triggered indefinitely.
>
> If character attempts to open one of the two sarcophagi without first disarming the trap, all characters inside the mausoleum must succeed on a `[[/save constitution 18]]` to avoid taking `[[/roll 2d6]]` poison damage and 1 level of Exhaustion from a strange poison mist that seeps into the chamber via the tiny holes in the floor.
