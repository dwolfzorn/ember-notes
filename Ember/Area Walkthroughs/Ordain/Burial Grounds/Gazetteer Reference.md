# Gazetteer Reference

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This mile-wide cemetery is crowded with countless mausoleums and crypts, where the worldly remains of Ordain’s deceased linger for time immemorial. Near the center of the grounds, a tower crowned in a silver-laced statue of Thayloc attends this disordered but majestic sprawl. The air here is thick with reverence and gloom, and the dark hollows and low stairways of this awe-inspiring necropolis suggest the presence of a shadow-haunted catacomb beneath.

The colloquially-named Burial Grounds serve as Ordain’s largest cemetery, spanning nearly one mile from edge to edge. Located northeast of the city limits, the Burial Grounds endure as one of the most hallowed examples of Ordain’s intrinsic sense of community. Shrines to the shard gods Thoma, Theyloc, and Janar dot the landscape, and seem to keep watch over the cemetery’s aging masonry.

People of all faiths are laid to rest here in the Burial Grounds northeast of the city. However, most of Ordain’s citizens are ceremonially cremated in the cemetery’s subterranean magma pit (according to sacred Cindaric rites). If fate doesn’t choose for them, other citizens elect to leave their remains among the graveyard’s select repositories, from modest urns to elaborate mausoleums. Furthermore, a special cohort of priests from many different denominations maintains a sullen vigil here. These clerics also serve as lamplighters for the Burial Grounds — utilizing the rarest of inkaro pearls to stave off errant eldritch influences.

A sprawling subterranean complex beneath the crypt-strewn cemetery grounds houses an array of catacombs and vaults, the central magma pit, Cindaric ritual chambers, and a hallowed shrine to the Elder god Sockets.

### Landmarks

**Swiftbolt Spire**

Near the center of the cemetery's haphazard sprawl, this venerated sentry tower houses a shrine to [[Thayloc]], topped with a bow-wielding, silver-laced statue of the eponymous shard god. The entirety of the Burial Grounds can be seen from the Spire's lofty balcony, which also overlooks the whole city of Ordain to the southwest.

**Foremother Fountain**

This inviting shrine to the shard god [[Thoma]] is marked by the purifying waters of a grand and holy fountain. Citizens who visit the Burial Grounds to honor the dead are known to stop by the fountain to make an offering, whether they worship "Grandmother Comfort" or not.

**Eventide Hall**

Marked by a shrine to the shard god [[Janar]], the edifice known as Eventide Hall is a collection of grave-strewn courtyards and connected mausoleums that house the remains of celebrated warriors and hunters of Ordain. Several exquisite statues decorate the area, depicting scenes of legendary monster-slayers in mortal combat with terrible beasts and hulking leviathans.

**The Cindaric Crematorium**

This fortified subterranean facility houses various funerary chambers used for the ceremonial observance of deceased Ordani citizens, as well as the ritual preparation of their mortal remains for cremation. At the core of this facility, a deep magma pit known as the Heartblood Maw serves as the site for the final step of the Cindaric ceremony — when a fully-prepared cadaver is surrendered to Ember's fiery womb, so that their soul might travel freely to far-off Mort'oliss. Outside the Crematorium, various mausoleums and graves dot the uneven landscape, overgrown with the telltale crimson grass of the Redrak Fields.

### Locals

****Ruxandra Haeld** (Lawful Neutral, Kiska Ordani, she/her)**

This wily thirty-something cleric of Thayloc who maintains a majority of the evening vigils at Swiftbolt Spire. Like many adherents of the God of Ashes, Ruxandra can be a rather sullen and stoic individual when the situation calls for it; but a humorous spirit lurks at the core of her personality, which she tends to subvert in the name of duty and piety. Treat Ruxandra as a [[Priest]] with an expanded spell list.

****Elder Jessos** (Lawful Good, Human Ordani, he/him)**

This stoic sage supervises the Cindaric acolytes who maintain the subterranean Cindaric Crematorium. A headstrong sellsword in his youth, the venerable Jessos has traded in his warring ways for the simple life of a Cindaric ritualist. Treat Elder Jessos as a [[Cindaric Sage]].

****Lamplighter Rabette** (Chaotic Good, Kivahr + Aster Ordani, she/her)**

The hairless Aster known as Rabette is a humorously droll devotee of Sockets who works as the cemetery’s senior lamplighter. Her uncommonly pale Kivahr skin shines with a dull glow like moonlight, which serves as a supernatural light source as she makes her way through the shadow-haunted corners of the Burial Grounds.

### Lore

Ordain's Burial Grounds are perhaps steeped in more lore than the rest of the city, and rightfully so — considering the countless souls that are interred within, or who were surrendered to Ember's core via the Heartblood Maw. Some of the Arctus Plateau's most harrowing legends and ghost stories have roots or branches that stem from the Burial Grounds, where the histories of some of Ordain's most celebrated heroes and villains have been laid to rest along with their bones. Such tales include the legend of Qorahad the Cruel, an indomitable champion of the shard god Kuarta, whose remains were buried here in a fortified mausoleum long ago.
