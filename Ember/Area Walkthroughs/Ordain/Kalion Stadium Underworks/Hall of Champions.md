# Hall of Champions

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A lone Undaunted trainee appears to be polishing the pedestal of a large stone statue in this massive chamber, where a half-dozen monuments have been erected in honor of ancient Ordani gladiators — legendary personalities, by the look of their collective bearing and ornamentation. The imposing statue of a particularly formidable champion dominates the central area of the room, a lightly armored, muscular warrior whose clenched fists are shrouded in hand-sculpted arcane spectacle.

This area is currently occupied by a single [[Undaunted Trainee]], along with the lingering phantom of [[Regus Halamattrix]], the original champion of the Solar Games. The ceiling of this massive chamber is taller than most of the Underworks at 35 feet high.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring the Hall of Champions
>
> The statues located here are monuments to various champions and all-stars who have gained legendary status throughout the history of Grand Kalion Stadium and the Solar Games. Chief among them is the statue at the center of the chamber, which depicts the late Regus Halamattrix, who was the very first champion of the Solar Games.
>
> Any character who performs the Search action while exploring the Hall of Champions is able to locate a placard beneath each statue with a small descriptor of the person being honored. Clockwise from the northeast, they read:
>
> - "Clotho Drax."
> - "Hannigan Stethlas."
> - "Bronn Swifthammer."
> - "Demetra the Dauntless."
> - "Zoriax of Kalvard."
> - "Ora Feyd."
>
> Meanwhile, the statue at the center of the chamber stands larger and prouder than the rest, depicting a brawny Ordani gladiator covered in an impossible array of scars. A shiny golden plaque on the floor before the statue reads:
>
> > Regus Halamattrix. Inaugural Champion of the Solar Games.
> > "Death is no excuse for defeat."
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 15)** check is familiar with the tale of Regus Halamattrix, and how he rose to glory in the arena by winning amidst a succession of near-death experiences.
>
> - **Knowledge: Legends**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Warfare**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Raise the Alarm
>
> Upon detecting an intruder, the [[Undaunted Trainee]] will attempt to alert The Undaunted in other areas, specifically:
>
> - The 2 Undaunted Trainees in the [[Hall of Records]].
> - The Undaunted Adept in the [[Hall of Recovery]].
> - The Adept and Trainee sorting equipment in the [[Hall of Preparation]].
>
> #### Undaunted Trainee Tactics
>
> At the start of combat, the Undaunted Trainee will use their [[Reckless Movement]] feature and attempt to flee to raise the alarm, as described above.
>
> Over the course of combat, the Undaunted Trainee will prioritize the following actions and abilities:
>
> - In melee, the Undaunted Trainee will take advantage of their [[Pack Tactics]] feature to maximize damage dealt by their [[Dagger]].
>
> The battle ends when the Undaunted Trainee is reduced to under half their maximum Hit Points, at which point they surrender (see below).

> [!info] Social
> #### Coercing the Trainee
>
> Once the Undaunted Trainee surrenders, any character may convince him to share some information about the facility by making a successful **Deception (DC 15)**, **Intimidation (DC 15)**, or **Diplomacy (DC 17)** check. This information includes:
>
> - Spirits roam the halls here and spell doom for any would-be enemies of The Undaunted.
> - Most of the Undaunted team members are gathered for a ceremony further inside the temple, past the Hall of Challenges.
> - Zira can read minds. She probably already knows the interloping party is here.
>
> A successful **Deception (DC 13)** check reveals an element of truth behind the first two rumors, and a falsehood about the third.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The Undaunted Trainee can be seen looking with anxious regularity at the statue in the center of the room. But he'd sooner risk death during an attempt to escape rather than explain himself any further.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Phantom in the Statue
>
> The statue here is haunted by the undying spirit of Regus Halamattrix, the first champion of the Havoc Games. He watches over this place in grim perpetuity.

If the party chooses to physically interact with the statue of Regus Halamattrix, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The statue feels strangely cold to the touch, even for the subterranean depths of the Underworks. For a brief moment, you feel as though your hand is affixed to the monument against your will. Looking up, you notice the statue's countenance moves — as if it's come to life. With a voice like a low, raspy wind, the statue speaks:
>
> > Only fools would approach this unholy memorial without fear. Tell me: who dares entreat with the observer of the Underworks?

> [!info] Social
> #### A Conversation with Regus
>
> If the characters summon the phantom of Regus Halamattrix by interacting with his monument, the undead champion will appear the following round. Because of his hubris and enthusiasm for tribulation, Regus is willing to have a conversation with the party, albeit laden with insults and provocation. Topics include (but are not limited to):
>
> - His history as the first champion of Ordain's Solar Games, and his legacy as an unkillable gladiator.
> - [[Ku'arta]]'s clandestine influence on the people of Ordain via the violence of the arena and its contestants.
> - The sanctity of this Underworks facility as a temple to the shard god of torment, however forgotten it may have become.
> - The party's accomplishments as warriors or athletes in their own right, including their associated victories in battle.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 18)** or **Diplomacy (DC 18)** check is able to convince Regus that the party's motives are aligned with his own — resulting in a friendly disposition from the phantom (however temporary it may be). If the characters manage to turn Regus into an ally, he'll also provide them with the following information:
>
> - The general layout of the Kalion Stadium Underworks, including the presence of the hidden temple at the western end of the complex.
> - Details about the [[Chamber of Bargains]], including how to entreat with Ku'arta and what the bargain entails.
> - Helpful tips for navigating the hazardous Hall of Challenges to the west.
>
> - **Recognized Regus Halamattrix:** The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check if they succeeded on the History check to identify Regus Halamattrix.
> - **Wrong Holy Symbol:** The character gains **-2 Banes** on this check if they openly wear any holy symbol of a god other than Ku'arta.
> - **Knowledge: Intrigue**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: Regus may be convinced to intervene during battle on the party's behalf, but won't turn against another adherent of Ku'arta without adequate reason (such as defilement of the temple or betrayal of the Shard God's edicts).
>
> Any character who fails by 10 or more instantly angers the phantom, initiating combat.
>
> If the party manages to befriend Regus, he'll recognize Agraband as a fellow Soulbound, and reveal that aspect of his history to the characters.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Your story?
>
> **A:**
>
> > You are humbled by the presence of greatness, for I am Regus Halamattrix, chosen of Ku'arta and supreme contestant of the Solar Games. Many have suffered the humiliation of my eminence. I suspect you shall be no different, you pitiable worm.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** This place?
>
> **A:**
>
> > As the city endures its ceaseless changes, the Underworks persist, forgotten but powerful in their grand design. For decades, I trained here with the likes of Ordain's finest warriors and athletes — losers and wretches all. None could thwart the might and mercilessness of Regus Halamattrix!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Zira Hestidero and The Undaunted?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The young champion may yet prove to be a powerful implement of Ku'arta, but I presume her ambitions exceed her grasp. These acolytes of hers are not spineless dredges, however, and their humble beginnings have made them strong. Many of them have braved the Hall of Challenges and lived to tell the tale.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Can you help us?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Mind the gauntlet to the west; the Hall of Challenges is no trifling matter. And pay heed to the Reliquary, where a weapon of mine is kept hidden: the Pellucid Glaive, a trophy from my time in the arena. Zira shields the relic with an arcane glyph. Speak the words "Tormenter, guide my hands" to avoid her chicanery.

If the party befriends Regus, take a moment whenever most appropriate to interject with the following:

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Regarding Agraband?
>
> **A:**
>
> The phantom directs his gaze at Agraband as a snide grin creeps across his spectral face.
>
> > Your friend has the mark. I, too, was graced with undying purpose. My Soulbound nature was the secret of my legacy. And I dare say this damnation was worth the price I had to pay. Regus Halamattrix bows down to no lord, even the God of Torment himself.
>
> The apparition's fists crackle with translucent arcane energy as the spectral remnants of his scars seethe with lambent intensity.

If the party ends up angering Regus Halamattrix, they'll have to fight the phantom, potentially alongside the Undaunted Trainee.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Regus Halamattrix Tactics
>
> The phantom champion is a cruel adversary who enjoys tormenting his enemies with a steady flow of taunts and denigration.
>
> At the start of combat, Regus will use his [[Horrific Visage]] feature against as many enemies as possible.
>
> Over the course of combat, Regus will prioritize the following actions and abilities:
>
> - In melee, Regus will use his [[Multiattack]] action, replacing one of his strikes with [[Life Drain]] if he is under half his maximum Hit Points.
> - Whenever able, Regus will use his Bonus Action to [[Vanish]] in between attacks.
> - Whenever able, Regus will use his [[Ghostly Telekinesis]] Legendary Action to disarm characters wielding powerful weapons or arcane implements.
> - Whenever an enemy is reduced to 0 Hit Points, Regus will use his Bonus Action to [[Consume Life]].
>
> The battle ends when Regus is slain.
