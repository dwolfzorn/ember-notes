# Steam Room

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you step inside, the sudden rise in temperature hits you like a wall. The air is thick with sweltering steam that instantly causes you to overheat. The walls and floors seem to be glinting with a thin layer of moisture, and the stone is darkened and discolored by the heat. At the center of the room is a large bronze grate covering an open pit of softly bubbling molten rock that glows with an intense red glow. The lava churns and bubbles gently, sending up bursts of steam and the occasional hiss as it interacts with the surrounding air.
>
> Surrounding the pit, each of the seven walls features a large basin filled with glowing coals, and pipes along the walls appear to be filled with water that occasionally drips on the coals or expels steam of its own to keep the room pressurized.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Test of Endurance
>
> Once all the party members are within the room, the door behind them closes and then seals shut. The challenge within is fairly straightforward, the party must survive 1 minute in the Steam Room.
>
> Once the door is sealed, begin a combat encounter and have the party roll initiative. Over the next ten rounds, they must make a series of increasingly complex checks and attempt to counter the rising heat in the room as best as they are able. While sealed within the room, the party cannot leave through any means, abilities, or spells, including teleportation magic.
>
> The spell [[Elementalism]] is helpful in a very minor way and allows a character to direct the flow of the steam slightly. Unfortunately, this only gives a single character in the party mild relief and +1 to their constitution checks while in the room.
>
> The spells [[Sleet Storm]] or [[Ice Storm]] can be used, but this spell has no effect on the lava basin in the middle of the room, merely creating more steam as the sleet evaporates. The spell can douse one of the basins fully if it is centered upon it, which grants the party a slight reprieve and a +1 bonus to any Constitution checks for the entire party.
>
> The spell [[Control Water]] can be used to try to redirect the flow of water from the steam pipes. This has a tangible effect on the amount of steam buildup in the room and the rising levels of heat, and the party gains a +2 bonus to any constitution checks they need to make while in the room.
>
> Every round, each member of the party must attempt the following checks and saves in order.
>
> | 1st Round | The heat is oppressive and begins to affect a character's ability to think clearly. They must roll a `[[/save con 11]]` and on a failure, the character suffers **-2 Banes** on all ability checks for the next round due to fatigue. |
> | --- | --- |
> | 2nd Round | As the steam vents erupt unpredictably, the characters must avoid the steam escaping from the nearby walls with a `[[/save dex 17]]` save. A failure results in `[[/damage 1d6 fire]]` and **-2 Banes** on the next environmental hazard in the room. |
> | 3rd Round | Characters can attempt to stand firm against the steam and resist the heat with a `[[/check ath 13]]` check. On a failure, they inadvertently take too many deep breaths of steam and begin choking, giving them a level of exhaustion. |
> | 4th Round | Everyone in the party has a brief moment to regain their breath as the steam seems to die down for a few seconds. The party can all roll a `[[/check med 14]]` and is successful, regaining `[[/heal 1d4]]` hit points. |
> | 5th Round | As the steam in the room thickens and the weight of the heat becomes nearly overwhelming, the party must make a `[[/save con 16]]` save, if they fail, each character receives one level of Exhaustion. |
> | 6th Round | Characters can try to identify a particular spot around the room that might help in avoiding damage. Success with a `[[/check inv 15]]` allows them to gain **+2 Boons** on a single following check related to environmental hazards in the room. |
> | 7th Round | Steam erupts violently from the lava basin in an explosion that catches the party off guard. Everyone must make a `[[/check dex 18]]` or become Blinded for 1 minute. |
> | 8th Round | Players can attempt to find another safe place between the steam pipes by identifying hot spots and the patterns of the eruptions with `[[/check sur 20]]`. On a failure, they accidentally stumble into a highly saturated area, suffering `[[/damage 2d10 fire]]` and `[[/damage 2d10 force]]` as they are engulfed in boiling steam and knocked prone for the next round. |
> | 9th Round | Characters who are proficient in arcane knowledge can attempt to manipulate the magical energies of the chamber to create a temporary shielding barrier against the heat. Success with a `[[/check arc 18]]` check from any character provides the entire party with fire resistance for the next round, as their understanding of the magic in the room helps to create a protective aura. |
> | 10th Round | As the heat reaches its peak, pressure builds in the room, and random steam jets erupt violently. The party must make one final `[[/save con 16]]` save, and if they fail, each character takes `[[/damage 3d10 fire]]` as the steam in the entire room begins to scald the party. |
>
> Once the rounds are over, the steam and heat in the room suddenly disappear, and the temperature returns to normal. As the thick steam clears, the party finds an ornate-looking pedestal in the center of the room.

## Ornate Pedestal

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Interactivity
>
> A player character can interact with the orante pedestal without investigating it. Doing so releases the Hydral in the [[Main Reservoir]] with a resounding roar sound that echoes around the chamber.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Orante Runic Pedestal
>
> The pedestal in this room is not trapped, nor is it a complicated device. It merely unlocks the final boss, and interacting with the gems on its surface is enough. It's likely the party may find this suspicious, however, so there are few clues on the pedestal to hint at its benign nature.
>
> - If a character casts [[Detect Magic]] and or rolls a `[[/check arcana 13]]`, they discover that the pedestal is connected by a thin magical thread to somewhere else in the chamber behind them.
> - Anyone attempting to investigate the pedestals cannot find any traps or anything suspicious, but if someone rolls a successful `[[/check inv 18]]`, they discover that the pedestal is not dangerous and placing your hand on the gem is safe.
> - Anyone with **Knowledge: Rituals** can somewhat read the runes, and they are runes of unlocking rather than anything dangerous.
