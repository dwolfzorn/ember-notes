# Rare Book Collection

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you enter the small room, you see that the walls are lined with dark oak shelves, sagging slightly under the weight of countless books and scrolls. In the center of the room, five rare books rest on ornate stands. Each book appears to be a masterpiece in its own right, bound in luxurious or rare materials.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Dangerous Collection
>
> Within the room lies a number of rare books, the challenge within is to refrain from touching any of them, and instead the party needs to identify where all of the books came from in Ember. Speaking the answer out loud is enough to solve the puzzle.
>
> #### Bestiary of Jurin
>
> This book sits upon the stand to the north-west.
>
> > The cover is a deep jungle green, crafted from smooth leather that feels supple to the touch. Embossed across the front is an intricate design of intertwining vines and leaves. Large deep claw marks run across the book's length and have cut deep enough to rip out some of the paper inside.
>
> The answer is Leviathans, or Jurin. Anyone with **Knowledge: Leviathans** recognizes that book immediately, otherwise, a successful `[[/check nature 14]]` check reveals the answer.
>
> #### The Chained Sorrows
>
> This book sits upon the stand to the west.
>
> > The cover of this book is dark, weathered, and worn. It is covered with locked chain links that prevent it from being opened. Under the chains, in the center of the cover, a striking image of a large, singular, glowing red eye.
>
> The Abyss, or the Red Eye. Anyone with **Knowledge: Abyssals** recognizes that book immediately, otherwise, a successful `[[/check arcana 14]]` check reveals the answer.
>
> #### Chronicles of the Lost
>
> This book sits upon the stand to the south-west.
>
> > The cover of this book is midnight blue, resembling a star-filled night sky. The edges are painted with ancient runic symbols that seem to flash and sparkle like stars.
>
> The answer is Shent, or Giants. Anyone with **Knowledge: Shent** recognizes that book immediately, otherwise, a successful `[[/check history 14]]` check reveals the answer.
>
> #### Codex of Luxarum Myths
>
> This book sits upon the stand to the south-east.
>
> > This large tome appears to be bound in luminous, shimmering gold that attracts the eye from any angle. The front cover is decorated with celestial motifs, and a large spear is easily visible on the front, shining like the sun itself.
>
> The Answer is Luxarum, or Celestials. Anyone with **Knowledge: Celestials** recognizes that book immediately, otherwise, a successful `[[/check religion 14]]` check reveals the answer.
>
> #### Compendium of the Watcher
>
> This book sits upon the stand to the north-east.
>
> > This book seems to be strangely alive, featuring a stark black and red cover, textured to resemble worn, ancient stone. Across its cover are several small green eyes that seem to watch anyone as they approach.
>
> The answer is the Watcher, or Spiders. Anyone with **Knowledge: Subterranea** recognizes that book immediately, otherwise, a successful `[[/check nature 14]]` check reveals the answer.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Sealed Collection
>
> The rare books within the room cannot be opened, nor can they be removed from their stands. This is because a very powerful ward lies upon them. If anyone attempts to touch, remove or open one of the books, read the following:
>
> > As you try to touch the book, a faint sigh, like wind, can be heard for the briefest moment before a loud crack explodes around your hand, and red flashing lines appear over the cover in a shield.
>
> The character suffers `[[/damage 2d6 lightning]]` damage from the magical shield, akin to a magical burn, and cannot avoid the damage in any way.
>
> Anyone who wishes to investigate the books can learn the following:
>
> - If a character casts [[Detect Magic]] or rolls a `[[/check arcana 18]]`, or if a character has **Knowledge: Rituals**, they recognize the wards cast over the books is perhaps some of the more intense and powerful you have come across in the chamber so far and it would be nearly impossible to remove it without high level dispelling magic.

## Blue Pedestal

Completing the challenge here reveals a pedestal, which appears from nothing in the center of the room.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Interactivity
>
> A player character can interact with the blue pedestal without investigating it. Doing so unlocks the two Blue Runic Doors on the 1st floor.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Blue Runic Pedestal
>
> The pedestal in this room is not trapped, nor is it a complicated device. It merely unlocks any blue door within the Chamber, and interacting with the gem on its surface is enough to unlock the door. It's likely the party may find this suspicious, however, so there are a few clues on the pedestal to hint at its benign nature.
>
> - If a character casts [[Detect Magic]] and or rolls a `[[/check arcana 13]]`, they discover that the pedestal is connected by a thin magical thread to somewhere else in the chamber.
> - Anyone attempting to investigate the pedestal cannot find any traps or anything suspicious, but if someone rolls a successful `[[/check inv 18]]`, they discover that the pedestal is not dangerous, and placing their hand on the gem is safe.
> - Anyone with **Knowledge: Rituals** can somewhat read the runes, and they are runes of unlocking rather than anything dangerous.
