# Shrine to Spectra

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Dominating the room on the northern side is a colossal statue of Spectra, the elder Goddess of magic. Carved from the same stone as the rest of the Chamber, the statue stands majestically against one wall, towering over the room at least twice the height of a person. Her form is gracefully poised, draped in ethereal robes with mirrored hands, outstretched as if in the moment before casting a spell. The floor of the shrine is scattered with hundreds of unlit candles, and nothing moves within the room, which is eerily silent.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Magical Devotion
>
> The room presents a challenge based on the parties' deviation, or at least their ability to uncover what devotion might be in the context of Specta, the elder Goddess of Magic. They must interpret the teachings of Spectra through both arcane knowledge and divine understanding.
>
> - If a character rolls a `[[/check insight 12]]`, they might believe that the challenge involves lighting the candles in the room. This can be done, but it does not lead anywhere.
> - Anyone attempting to investigate the statue can roll a `[[/check religion 15]]`. On a success, they are suddenly overcome with insight, allowing them to tap into a divine aspect of magic. This gives them the idea to kneel before the statue and draw an arcane symbol into the air. A further `[[/check arc 12]]` or anyone with **Knowledge: Rituals** determines that the statue needs to have a spell cast directly at its outstretched hands.
> - If there is a follower of Spectra in the room, these checks are made easier by adding +3 to every roll.
>
> Once a party member casts a spell at the hands, it doesn't matter which spell as long as it creates a tangible effect that can appear within the room. A magical sound is heard, and the challenge is complete.

## Cyan Pedestal

Completing the challenge here reveals a pedestal, which appears from nothing in the room.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Interactivity
>
> A player character can interact with the cyan pedestal without investigating it. Doing so unlocks the Cyan Runic Door on the 1st floor.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Cyan Runic Pedestal
>
> The pedestal in this room is not trapped, nor is it a complicated device. It merely unlocks any cyan door within the Chamber, and interacting with the gem on its surface is enough to unlock the door. It's likely the party may find this suspicious, however, so there are a few clues on the pedestal to hint at its benign nature.
>
> - If a character casts [[Detect Magic]] and or rolls a `[[/check arcana 13]]`, they discover that the pedestal is connected by a thin magical thread to somewhere else in the chamber.
> - Anyone attempting to investigate the pedestal cannot find any traps or anything suspicious, but if someone rolls a successful `[[/check inv 18]]`, they discover that the pedestal is not dangerous and placing your hand on the gem is safe.
> - Anyone with **Knowledge: Rituals** can somewhat read the runes, and they are runes of unlocking rather than anything dangerous.
