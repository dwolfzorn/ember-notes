# Central Column Chamber

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A massive chamber lies before you, with a massive central column reaching up towards a vaulted ceiling high above. Looking down, however, you can also see that the column descends into the depths below, where you can see the faint glimmer of water. The surrounding walls, etched with arcane runes, are pulsating slowly and faintly with energy, and the hum of infusing magic is even stronger here than in the entrance foyer.
>
> The column in front of you has two bronze platforms sticking out from the center and is currently allowing you passage toward the middle. Three doors lie across from you on the other walls, all of which seem to be sealed shut.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Interactivity
>
> The party must rotate the central column, ascend and descend, and attempt to access the different rooms of the Chamber, following the path laid out and completing every room and its challenges in order. However, it is possible to interrupt this careful order, depending on whether the party is able to gain access to the [[Barrier Control Room]].
>
> In the middle of the Central Column structure, there is a small bronze lever with an arcane rune. Players can interact with the lever here to rotate the entire central platform, including the two protruding bronze elements, 90 degrees clockwise.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Rotating Platforms
>
> Within the Central Column Chamber is a large vertical pillar stretching all the way upward nearly 100 feet from the bottom of the [[Main Reservoir]] through the first floor and ending on the second floor of the chamber.
>
> On both the second and first floors, the central column has independent sections that can rotate 90 degrees clockwise once the lever is pulled on the central column itself. On these floors, platforms extend from the central column sections and connect to several doorways and entrances.
>
> Any character that walks over to the central column notices the following immediately:
>
> > The stonework of the column is smooth and unmarked, and seems to have been recently cleaned. There appear to be stairs here in the middle, and on the column itself, there is a small bronze lever with a shimmering arcane rune above it.
>
> If a character attempts to investigate the lever, a successful **Awareness (DC 15)** check or anyone with **Knowledge: Machines** or **Knowledge: Rituals** discovers that the entire column is likely capable of rotating once the lever is pulled.

## Jumping or Falling

The massive central chamber has lots of vertical space, and the party might think it's possible to skip the stairs on the central column and try to jump down to the first level or the ground floor of the chamber. However, this is prevented by Arcane Barriers.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Arcane Barriers
>
> Within the Central Column Room are two Arcane Barriers, which are 5 feet below and underneath the rotating platforms on the second and first floors. These barriers prevent the party from descending or ascending without turning the Main Column and using the stairs to traverse the chamber. They prevent anything from passing through them, including any attempts to teleport between the barriers, as well as using any class features or spells.
>
> Any character with a `[[/skill prc 14 passive format=long]]` or who succeeds on a **Awareness (DC 12)** check notices the following:
>
> > While the darkness of the chamber makes it difficult to see, a lattice of arcane energy spans the entire room here — a nearly-invisible barrier that seems to prevent access between the different floors of the complex.
>
> In the event that a character falls onto or chooses to jump onto these barriers, they suffer `[[/damage 4d6 lightning]]` damage and are magically thrown back to the previous position. They are also &Reference[Paralyzed] for `[[/roll 1d4]]` minutes
