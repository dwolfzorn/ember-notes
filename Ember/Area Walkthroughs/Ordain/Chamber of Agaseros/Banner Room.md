# Banner Room

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Stepping into the room, you see four thick pillars, each intricately carved with runes and symbols. Hanging around them are several Anachraenum banners featuring the bladed compass symbol, shining with their own inner light. Against one wall, a large stone pedestal commands your attention. Its surface is engraved with more arcane symbols, suggesting it was once a focal point for a display.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Interactivity
>
> A player character can interact with the yellow pedestal without investigating it. Doing so unlocks the Yellow Runic Door behind them.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Yellow Runic Pedestal
>
> The pedestal in this room is not trapped, nor is it a complicated device. It merely unlocks any yellow door within the Chamber, and interacting with the gem on its surface is enough to unlock the door behind the party. It's likely the party may find this suspicious, however, so there are few clues on the pedestal to hint at its benign nature.
>
> - If a character casts [[Detect Magic]] and or rolls a `[[/check arcana 13]]`, they discover that the pedestal is connected by a thin magical thread to the door behind them.
> - Anyone attempting to investigate the pedestals cannot find any traps or anything suspicious, but if someone rolls a successful `[[/check inv 18]]`, they discover that the pedestal is not dangerous and placing your hand on the gem is safe.
> - Anyone with **Knowledge: Rituals** can somewhat read the runes, and they are runes of unlocking rather than anything dangerous.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Yellow Runic Door
>
> Anyone who walks up to the Bronze door can attempt to investigate it with a variety of different checks.
>
> - With a **Awareness (DC 12, Passive)** or successful **Awareness (DC 12)** check, it's clear that the surface of the door is smooth, except for banding lines of reinforcing bronze. Similar to some of the other doors, but this one has a number of yellow gems glinting softly upon its surface, and there is no slot to place your hand.
> - With a successful `[[/check inv 14]]` check, or anyone with **Knowledge: Machines** realizes that the large double door is made of solid bronze, likely weighing several tons. The mechanism is likely complex, and you can see some of the rotating gears that will allow the door to slide into the wall.
