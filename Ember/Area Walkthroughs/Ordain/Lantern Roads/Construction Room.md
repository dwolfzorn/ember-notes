# Construction Room

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Metal tables covered with wires and cords and tools line the wall of the room. It has seemingly been abandoned, though there is a wheel protruding from a back wall, and as you draw closer you see a hint of movement out of the corner of your eye.

With a successful `[[/check perception 16]]` check or `[[/check perception 18 passive]]`, characters see the movement of one of the three creatures hidden among the objects on the table. These are[[Skither]], who immediately attack.

> [!abstract] Skither
> **[[Skither]]**
>
> Level 2 · Skither Servitor
>
> ![Skither](../../../assets/image051.webp)
>
> Small and fast-moving, this creature seems at first like something to ignore, little more than a piece of trash with jagged wings crudely shaped to look like a insect. The creature's solitary eye and claw-like appendages seem to surge with fiery radiance as it strikes, leaving streaks of amber light in its wake.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Skither Tactics
>
> If the party detected the Skithers before the attack, the Skithers are surprised. If they did not, the party is surprised.
>
> Once battle begins, the Skithers attempt to stay close together to take **+2 Boons** of their [[Pack Tactics]] ability, but will block the exits to the room if they can. Skithers primarily attack directly using [[Talons]], but if the party is confined in a tight space, will use [[Self Sacrifice]] to damage the party with a dying action.
>
> Each Skither may use [[Tyraphic Sending]] to send a message to the Aburyx located in the [[Training Room]], but will avoid this action until they are at half-health or lower.

Characters who have previously encountered the Skither, or have **Knowledge: Celestials** know that Skithers are often used to assemble and care for machines on Luxarum, the inner realm inhabited by the celestial Tyraphem.

Characters who search through the pieces on the table for 10 minutes, or who succeed on a `[[/check perception 16]]` check can find two metal sheets. They each hold a diagram and a word engraved in a language other than Common.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Metal Sheets
>
> Characters who examine the sheets of engraved metal may discover the following:
>
> - The language on the sheets is **Language: Harmos**. Characters who have seen other writing in this language in the facility, have knowledge of the language, have **Knowledge: Celestials**, or succeed on a `[[/check 16 history]]` check know that the sheets have Harmos writing on them, used by those from the inner realm Luxarum.
>   - Anyone who can read **Language: Harmos** knows that the labels for the three sheets are Assembly, Blessing, and Control.
> - The first sheet (Assembly) has pictures of many small pieces and gears on the edges of the sheet and a large metallic creature with tentacles in the center.
> - A diagram on the second sheet (Blessing) shows two of the metallic creatures - one on the top of the page and one on the bottom. In the center is a large flame. An arrow points between the top creature and the flame and then between the flame and the bottom creature.
> - The third sheet (Control) shows a flame above a wheel, and an arrow that indicates that the wheel should be turned.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Wheel on the Wall
>
> This wheel is identical to those found in the [[Blessing Chamber]] and [[Construction Room]] and operates in the same fashion. If characters have already operated one of the other wheels, they recognize it immediately.
>
> #### Identifying the Torch Holder
>
> Characters can clearly see the wheel on the wall, and the torch holder above it, without a check.
>
> > The metal wheel juts out of the wall as if molded from the same piece of metal. Directly above it sits a small hoop, jutting out from the same wall.
>
> Characters who examine the hoop and succeed on a `[[/check 15 perception]]` check note the smooth edges, as if something has been routinely used in that slot, wearing down the edges over time.
>
> - With a success of 3 or more, characters also note a small amount of damage around the torch holder slot from whatever was placed in it. It is similar to the damage that would be caused by a large amount of heat.
>
> Characters who succeed on a `[[/check history 17]]` check or have **Knowledge: Celestials** recognize the hoop as a torch holder that is used to hold a source of radiant fire.
>
> #### Using the Torch Holder
>
> Any torches that the characters have on them will fit into the torch holder slot, but nothing happens when they are placed.
>
> > You can hear a slight click as the torch is placed in the torch holder, and it shifts downward slightly, but there is no other effect.
>
> The wheel does not and will not move until characters put a [[Lit Kynryth Torch]] in the torch holder.If characters light their own torches (or any stick of a similar size) in the radiant fire in the bowl, it becomes a [[Lit Kynryth Torch]]. They may also acquire one from a defeated Kynryth in the immediate aftermath of its death.
>
> Once a proper torch has been put in the torch holder, the wheel extends from the wall.
>
> > With an audible echoing click, the wheel in the wall begins to glow, ever-so-slightly, and it extends further from the wall.
>
> #### Turning the Wheel
>
> If the torch holder has not been used, the wheel cannot be turned, but once it has extended from the wall, it is easily turned without a check. Each time the wheel is turned a quarter turn, the Middle Dome in the [[Central Chamber]] rotates, as noted in [[Not All Who Wandren]], making a deep grinding noise that can be heard throughout the facility without a check.
