# Central Chamber

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Locating Funar Cevher
>
> If the party uses the[[Locator Rod]] in this area, they find that Funar Cevher is **adjacent to this room** (in a room directly above, below, or next to it)**.**
>
> - With an additional charge of the rod, the party can determine that Funar is in a room **next to this room.**

### When Entering The Chamber

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Beneath the glassy floor at the edge of the chamber, metal gears move with a relentless rhythm that is interrupted only by a faint and consistent thumping sound. The curve of the large metal dome in front of you, by contrast, sits absolutely still, shielding nearly everything in the chamber ahead from your view.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating the Chamber
>
> Characters who inspect the chamber learn the following:
>
> - **The Metal Gears in the Floor:** With a successful `[[/check perception 17]]` check, characters notice that only some of the gears around the edge of the room are turning.
>   - With a successful `[[/check investigation 17]]` check or **Knowledge: Machines**, they can tell that the turning gears are attached to something outside of the central chamber.
> - **The Sound of Thumping:**
>   - Characters who succeed on a `[[/check perception 15]]` check, or who have `[[/check perception 17 passive]]` can clearly hear a thumping sound, like someone banging on a wall, coming from outside of the chamber.
>     - If they succeed on the check by 3 or more, or has `[[/check perception 20 passive]]`, they can pinpoint the origin of the sound as coming from near the Western Entrance of the Central Chamber and hear muffled shouting beneath the thumping.

### The Metal Domes

The primary object within this chamber is the set of nested metal domes in the center. Characters can only see the outermost of these when they enter.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The walls of the dome seem to glow from within, producing a faint golden light that you can feel as much as see, as if some slight vibration has settled on your skin and begun worming its way in.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating The Outer Dome Wall
>
> The Outer Dome wall has been coated in [[Thieves' Lament]], an alchemical substance that burns those who touch it and scars the hands.
>
> - Characters who use the Identify or Detect Magic spell find no evidence of magical properties in or around the wall. Spells cannot travel through the dome to the interior walls.
> - Characters who succeed on a `[[/check perception 15]]` check or have `[[/check perception 17 passive]]` can see a slight sheen on the metal where the substance was painted on.
>   - Characters who succeed on a `[[/check nature 18]]` check, or who have **Knowledge: Alchemy** know that it is an alchemical substance of some sort. It has soaked into the wall and cannot be removed for further study.
>
> If characters strike the wall, they can hear a faint echo without a check:
>
> > The sound of your strike echoes from the other side of the wall, as if there is some emptiness on the other side of it, but it soon fades into the background hum.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Touching the Outer Dome Wall
>
> If characters put their hands against the wall, they are subjected to the effects of [[Thieves' Lament]].

If characters walk around the dome, they find the opening in the Outer Wall without a check.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The wall ends in a small opening marked by a deep groove in the floor. On the other side, only a few feet away, is another metal wall. It looks similar to the first, but there is a faint sizzling in the air around it, as it if is charged with some kind of energy.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating The Middle Dome Wall
>
> Characters who succeed on a `[[/check arcana 17]]` check, or who use the spell [[Identify]] (or a similar spell), know that the wall has been magically altered to give an electric shock to anyone who touches it and that it is being fed by magical energy from a source nearby.
>
> If [[Dispel Magic]]or a similar spell is used on the wall, it loses its hum and has no negative effects when touched, but this only lasts for 10 minutes. After that time the wall is armed again.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Touching the Middle Dome Wall
>
> If the middle wall of the dome is touched while it is active, it immediately crackles with energy and discharges a bolt of electric energy. Whoever is touching the wall must succeed on a `[[/save dexterity 16]]` saving throw or take `[[/damage 1d6 lightning]]` damage.
>
> > As the energy courses through you, sending a jolt of pain through your body, an image comes to your mind - fire, as glorious as the sun, being held in the hands of a powerful creature.

If characters align the first two rings, the inner ring is exposed. If characters approach it while it is still closed, it hums with power.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The hum in the room intensifies as you draw closer to the inner ring of the metal — unlike the others, there is no glow, but you feel a heat beneath your own skin, as if the vibration wants nothing more than to melt you down and remake you.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating the Inner Dome Wall
>
> **Dangers of the Inner Dome Wall:** Characters who succeed on a `[[/check arcana 18]]` check, or who use the spell [[Identify]] (or a similar spell), know that the wall has been magically altered to send radiant energy at anyone who passes through its opening into the center of the chamber.
>
> **The Vibration in the Air:** The feeling of a vibration in the air is a combination of the machinery and the use of celestial energy.
>
> - With a successful `[[/check perception 15]]` check or **Knowledge: Machines** , characters know that some of the vibration in the air is from the metal gears that power the facility, but not all of it.
> - With a successful `[[/check arcana 16]]` check or [[/knowledge cosmos], characters know that there is a magical energy in the air that contributes to the vibration and that the source of the magic is different than that typically found and used on Ember.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Touching The Inner Dome Wall
>
> When characters pass through the opening of the inner dome, they must make a `[[/save constitution 16]]` saving throw - on a failure, they take `[[/damage 1d6 radiant]]` damage from the dome wall.

### Aligning The Chamber Rings

The positioning of the domes within the Central Chamber is controlled from switches in the Wandren HQ Training Room, Construction Room, and Blessing Room, as noted in [[Not All Who Wandren]] .

### The Inner Chamber Revealed

Once all three rings are aligned, characters can enter the inner chamber:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Inside of the interlocking metal shells is a tangle of machinery that looks as if it has come to life within its metallic cocoon, with thick golden vine-like cords all grasping in different directions, extending like arms from a curved metal control station along the inner chamber wall. Between them, the table is covered with levers and etched glowing tiles, which emit plumes of light in a rhythmic pattern that seems as if it must have some meaning.
>
> But all of this is the backdrop. In the center of the metal circle, within a silver metal band on the floor that even the intertwined cords won't touch, a creature is suspended in a beam of silver light.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating the Inner Chamber
>
> The console in the center of the inner chamber is divided into four sections, each with a glowing panel in its center that is etched with a single word in a language other than Common or Arcden and surrounded by various buttons and levers.
>
> Characters who take a closer look can determine the following:
>
> - With a successful `[[/check history 16]]` check, **Language: Harmos**, or **Knowledge: Celestials**, they know that the writing on the console panels is in Harmos, the language used by the Tyraphem who dwell in the inner plane of Luxarum.
>   - Any characters who know **Language: Harmos** can read the writing - the panels read "Hold."
>
> **The Beam of Light:** Characters who succeed on a `[[/check arcana 16]]` check, or who use the spell [[Identify]] (or a similar spell), can identify the beam of light as a field of imprisoning magic generated from a Holding Ring built into the center of the floor.
>
> - If characters succeed on their arcana check by 3 or more, or if they have **Knowledge: Celestials**, they can tell that this magic has its source in the inner realm of Luxarum, where the Tyraphem dwell.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### A Prismatic Beam
>
> The edge of the beam of light operates similarly to a Prismatic Wall - any character who touches it must make a `[[/save constitution 16]]` saving throw.
>
> - Regardless of the result of the check, they are pushed back 10 feet.
> - If the saving throw fails, they also take `[[/damage 1d6 bludgeoning]]` damage from the concussive force.

If characters attempt to go inside of the beam of light or speak to the creature within it, it immediately speaks to them. (See [[Not All Who Wandren]]).

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating the Console
>
> Characters who look closely at the Console can discover the following:
>
> - Light flashes in the panels in each section of the Console in a different pattern. With a successful `[[/check investigation 16]]` check or **Knowledge: Machines**, characters see that the lights all pause for a moment at the same time in each cycle.
> - If characters touch a single panel, it holds its glow for a few seconds, and then begins repeating its cycle.
>   > The panel glows a little more brightly for a moment, then begins to fade.
> - If characters touch two or three panels at the same time, they glow more strongly.
>   > The panels all glow for the same amount of time, becoming brighter still, and they feel almost warm beneath your touch.
> - Characters who succeed on a `[[/check investigation 17]]` check can determine that pressing all four console lights at the same time will deactivate the central cage.
