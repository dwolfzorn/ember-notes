# Terracini's Restaurant

### Outside of the RestauranT

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Climbing to the Roof
>
> Characters can reach the rooftop via the ladder that sits against the side of Terracini's. With a successful `[[/check perception 15]]` check in the vicinity of the restaurant or `[[/check perception passive 17]]`, they spot the ladder at the back of the shop. It can be climbed without a check.

### Inside the Restaurant

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Between the warmth of the cookfire, the rich smells of food in the midst of being prepared and enjoyed, and the conversation between the guests at the small intimate tables around the corners of the room, Terracini's Restaurant exudes a sense of comfort from the moment you enter.
>
> A man, wearing shadowy clothes that are a more gray and muted version of what you've seen elsewhere in Ordain seems to slightly obscure the light as he walks by.
>
> > Welcome to Terracini's, where I am Terracini, and you are already family. Please, feel free to take a look over our menu.
>
> As he waves you over, he draws slightly closer and whispers, too softly for anyone else to hear:
>
> > And if you really are family, you may find what you need in the terrace upstairs. If not, please stay down here.

> [!info] Social
> #### Talking To Terracini
>
> Terracini will not readily elaborate on what he means by being family, answering all questions with generic responses. With a successful `[[/check insight 16]]` check, characters know that he is trying to do his best not to get in trouble with the wrong people by saying the wrong thing. He will share any of the following:
>
> - He is related to the famous thief Terracini, but much prefers cooking to crime. He only uses the name to drum up business.
> - The cloak he wears is modeled after Terracini's, allowing the wearer to better sneak into and out of the shadows. It is one of his most prized possessions.
> - Characters should only go to the Terrace section of the restaurant if they have business with the Beacon Brigade, though he won't stop them from visiting. If someone in the party has the Brigade Key or Courier Bag, he is impressed and they have **+2 Boons** on the checks below.
>
> With a successful `[[/check intimidation 17]]` or `[[/check persuasion 17]]` check, characters can convince Terracini to tell them the following:
>
> - The Terrace section of his restaurant is often used by associates of the Beacon Brigade. He much prefers when they are jumping over rooftops or sneaking through the Shadowbox side of town, but he feels he can't refuse them.
> - If they want to get to the other side of the neighborhood, the best way through is the rooftops, though he's heard their might be a path in the Shadowbox section of the neighborhood.
>
> If characters succeed on the intimidation check by 3 or more, they can talk Terracini into selling them his cloak - it costs **`[[/award 200 gp]]`**, which will help offset the money he's losing to the gang. The cloak is an [[Ashen Cloak]].

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Introductions
>
> **A:**
>
> > Penni Terracini, head chef. Serving up food just as good as what they make over in Restaurant Row. And before you ask, or demand money, I'm not really related to **the** Terracini, though letting people think you're the cousin of a famous thief does help drum up business. It's the reason I got this thieves' cloak.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who can go upstairs
>
> **A:**
>
> > You know that the Beacon Brigade has a base on the other side of the neighborhood? Only those who are approved by them are supposed to head upstairs. I don't want to get on their bad side - who knows what Vitt and the rest of them might do. His house is right across the way from this place - I need to stay on his good side.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** His cloak
>
> **A:**
>
> > It's a cloak for sneaking around - cost me an arm and a leg and dropping Terracini's name to get it. I hope I don't have to sell it, but giving away free meals isn't great for my business.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who uses the Terrace/Beacon Brigade info
>
> **A:**
>
> > Even the Beacon Brigade likes good food, so some of their members, and associates, use that terrace. It's pretty close to their territory, so I've promised to keep regular customers away. They aren't all bad - I think the couple up there right now are some shopkeepers they're trying to wine and dine.
> >
> > Still , I wish the brigade would stick to other rooftops or skulking around Shadowbox Alley.
>
> Requires a successful `[[/check 17 persuasion]]` or `[[/check 17 intimidation]]` check.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Getting to Beacon Brigade territory
>
> **A:**
>
> > The rooftops are the only real way that I know to get over to where Vitt and his crew hang out, though I've heard there's a path through the Shadowboxes - on the other side of the abandoned theater.
>
> Requires a successful `[[/check 17 persuasion]]` or `[[/check 17 intimidation]]` check.
