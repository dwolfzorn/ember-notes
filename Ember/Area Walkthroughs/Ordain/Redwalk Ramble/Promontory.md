# Promontory

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A great mass of lava has engulfed the promontory ahead. Thick, sulfurous smoke roils above it in a choking cloud. The molten floe churns in slow eddies and whorls at the promontory's edge, threatening to pour into the lower plaza before vanishing suddenly in midair.

This area has been transformed by Falar's illusion magic into a circulating mass of lava. A foul-smelling cloud of smoke conceals the whole area.

#### Effect Under Development

This hallucinatory terrain feature may lack a combination of walls, lighting, sounds, and similar effects. These enhancements will be added in a future update, and tied to the interactable object functionality.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Hallucinatory Terrain: Lava Floe
>
> In addition to the general rules described on the [[Area Overview]] page, this hazard has the following characteristics:
>
> - Treat the area as [[Difficult Terrain]].
> - Treat the area as [[Heavily Obscured]] due to sulfuric smoke.
> - A character who enters the hazard or who begins their turn in the hazard must make a successful **Athletics (DC 12)** check to keep their footing amidst the shifting floes or suffer `[[/damage 2d6 fire]]` damage.
>
> Once the area's [[Chiaroscuran Beast]] is slain, the hazard vanishes.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Magical Anchor
>
> These elemental predators are each anchored to a specific piece of illusory terrain and prefer to remain inside that area, though they can leave it if necessary.
>
> #### Chiaroscuran Beast Tactics
>
> At the start of combat, the Chiaroscuran Beast will enter a flat surface using [[Merge Surface]] and quickly engage a character with their [[Quick Sketch]] ability.
>
> Over the course of combat, the Chiaroscuran Beast will prioritize the following actions and abilities:
>
> - In melee combat, they utilize their [[Scratch]] attack to harm enemies, and prefer to attack isolated, unaware, and vulnerable targets inside the illusory terrain they are tied to.
> - When hurt, they can enter a flat surface using [[Merge Surface]] and recover lost Hit Points with their [[Redraw Form]] ability.
> - When a Chiaroscuran Beast is defeated, the terrain it is linked to dissipates, returning that section of the battlefield to normal.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Veteran Mage
>
> This old, veteran mage and adventurer has been busy weaving magic into the Redwalk Ramble, and now waits atop the Promontory to goad the party while peppering them with magic from on high.
>
> #### Falar Tactics
>
> Before combat begins, Falar has already cast [[Mage Armor]] on himself. Additionally, Falar is completely immune to his own illusory terrain, and can walk through it unharmed and unhindered. The creatures on this map will obey his commands when given.
>
> At the start of combat, Falar will utilize his [[Illusory Arsenal]] to create a weapon based on the situation, then cast [[Mirror Image]] on himself to further enhance his defense.
>
> Over the course of combat, Falar will prioritize the following actions and abilities:
>
> - He will cast [[Shield]] or [[Counterspell]] as a reaction to block incoming attacks and spells.
> - He will cast either [[Mind Spike]] or [[Phantasmal Killer]] on targets that are especially dangerous or persistent, favoring other spellcasters who might be able to disrupt his own spellcasting.
> - He will cast [[Hypnotic Pattern]] to disrupt groups of enemies, but is not averse to using the spell on single targets that are especially persistent or dangerous to him.
>
> Falar fights with bravado and cunning, but will begin to falter when he falls below half his Hit Point maximum. At that point he will consider retreat, using [[Invisibility]] (if available) to escape from the area unseen. If cornered and presented with surrender or death, Falar will give up.

### Reasoning with Falar

> [!info] Social
> #### Parley With the Old Illusionist
>
> At any point during the encounter, the characters may attempt to convince Falar to abandon his crusade against Ordain, and surrender. If they wish to do so, the party must complete a skill challenge by succeeding on a total of 3 **Diplomacy (DC 16)** skill checks.
>
> Any of the following lines of reasoning are likely to work:
>
> - Ruining Varinna will cause House Salva to take away her contract, ruining her future. Falar is making her a victim of circumstances she had no part in.
> - Harming the innocent people of Ordain will only make him into a villain, and if people die by his hand the Veiled Chain will make sure you never leave the Spellbreaker Tower.
> - He clearly hasn't run out of ideas or ingenuity, there's no reason he couldn't pick himself up and start rebuilding his life properly.
> - Owning the creation of his Murals, and standing behind the message might make him a target of the Trading Houses, but he's not alone in his sentiments and distaste for them.
> - Things are difficult in Ordain, and what the city needs is someone who will fight for its people, not another problem to keep them awake at night, and these elementals are just that.
> - Mistress Caberi still remembers you, the old you, charming, gregarious, creative. You clearly left a mark on people in the city, it's not too late to recapture that.
>
> #### The Flexible Approach
>
> Players should be encouraged to use what they have learned about House Salva, Falar, Varinna, and the people of Flotsam to influence the old mage. Certain knowledges, paths, or cultures may provide advantage, and alternate skill checks to Persuasion could be viable, with a DC at your discretion.
>
> Falar is not so far gone that he can't be reasoned with, but convincing him to stand down should not be impossible.

### On a Normal Day

If the party visits this area outside the [[Matters of Perspective]] Event, read or paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The plaza narrows into a broad stone promontory overlooking the lower grounds. Its embossed masonry sits atop the natural rock like a pale crown; the water below laps audibly against the stone. From here, Ordain's crimson architecture appears to cascade toward the harbor as a russet fall.

The promontory offers Ordani citizens a clear view of both the performances taking place in the [[Lower Plaza]] below and the city beyond. It is a favorite spot for landscape artists working "en plein air."
