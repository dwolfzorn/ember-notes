# Makeup

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A pair of vanity tables sit ready for cast members to dress themselves up, each one covered in collections of brushes and bundles of makeup, and featuring a large mirror.
>
> A writing desk is scattered with papers sits nearby, covered in papers, pens, and ink bottles. A small pile of letters and a scattering of written pages sit untouched on the desk as well.

The party may want to search the tables. The vanities hold nothing of note, but the writing desk may lend insight, and hold damning evidence depending on how events played out elsewhere in the theater.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Vanities
>
> The vanity tables feature large mirrors and assorted collections of brushes and makeups. Most of them are on the older side, and probably in need of replacing.
>
> #### Writ Large
>
> The desk features various pens, quills, ink bottles and several pages of half-finished scenes for a play. A bundle of letters sits on the top of the desk next to a tray of letters. All of them appear to be addressed to the theater or their local troupe.

Characters interested in looking through the letters may find something interesting.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Fan Mail
>
> If a character opens any of the letters, they find various letters addressed to the playwright, actors, or the theater at large. Each letter thanks them for their performances, or critiques some aspect of a play or skit. While interesting to read, there is nothing of importance to find in the letters.
>
> #### Letters of Complaint
>
> One bundle of letters are comprised entirely of missives complaining about the theater shutting down, shows being cancelled, and disappointment over the Bronze Rask being closed to the public for "private use."
>
> Some of the letters are especially mean spirited, and all of them are addressed to the owner, one Dory Doss.
>
> #### Agreement with Brynna
>
> A character that searches the drawers of the desk will find a series of letters on House Wandren stationery. They are all addressed to Dory Doss, and goes over a long-term contract with her to purchase exclusive use of the theater.
>
> The letters are only one side of the conversation but a successful **Deception (DC 14)** check gleans that Dory Doss was especially dogged in their negotiation with Brynna, working the price up as high as possible to ensure the theater and its displaced troupe would be taken care of during the hiatus.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Arts** or **Knowledge: Trade** or making a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check recognize that the amount being paid by Brynna is likely far in excess of what this theater would normally make, and would ensure its operation long-term.
