# Area Overview

## Area Map Context

The area map for the [[Bronze Rask Theater]] depicts the historic theater and the excavation behind it, as well as the events that transpire in the area as part of the [[Spreading Sickness]] quest events.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Normal Theater Use
>
> If you want to use this theater as a place to visit for shows and portray it as an operational venue you can do so easily, but will need to ignore certain parts of the walkthrough text.
>
> Specifically, ignore any text that relates to the Wandren and Mutagist plot as it relates to the [[Spreading Sickness]] quest event [[Midnight Meeting]].
>
> This includes the combat encounter, and placed enemies, and probably any opportunity to explore the backstage and tunnel areas of the map.

## Environment

Situated in the city of Ordain, in the Fallbrook district, this multistory theater is old and regal, standing for several centuries, and has served as a center for the arts for generations of Ordani locals. The structure is made of white stone like the rest of Ordain, but features extensive wood construction inside, all of which was been relatively well maintained.

## Ecology

The structure has issues with critters and insects living in the small places where one expects, but no plants or animals of note grow in this building.

## Lore

Built several centuries ago, this is one of the older locations in Fallbrook. The theater has been known by several names, and has been home to hundreds of performers attempting to make a name for themselves or simply trying to entertain their fellow Ordani.

The stage has seen countless plays, concerts, and performances, and the theater's current name even stems from one such performance. The highly detailed bronze rask statue located in the lobby came to rest there as a result of a particularly impressive performance one night. A foreign noble in attendance was so struck, so delighted by the performers and their show that they gifted the troupe the immensely heavy statue as a show of appreciation.

Having nowhere else practical for the statue to go, it ended up in the lobby, and before long the theater's old name was forgotten as it was given a new moniker: The Bronze Rask.

In recent months the theater has been closed and disused, having been hired out by Brynna Vercocorte for "private use" which has displaced the local performers. The intent is to reopen the theater once Brynna's contract runs out on it, though won't be for some time.
