# Lobby

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you enter the lobby you are met with a large bronze statue of a Rask standing upon a broad bronze base. Light catches on the polished ridges and intricate detail work on its muzzle and hammer-patterned fur. The metal bears no mark of abuse or age, clearly well cared for.
>
> On either side of the lobby, ticket booths sit empty, behind the openings, shelves and cubbies wait in neat rows, now still and unused. To the left and right, stairways climb upward, and signs fixed to the newel posts point upward, plainly marking the way to the balcony.
>
> Ahead, a pair of heavy double doors of dark wood marks the entrance to the theater proper. Brass push-plates gleam where hands have polished them smooth.
>
> A pair of men stand in the lobby, watching you as you enter. Once the doors swing shut behind you one asks:
>
> > Will you submit like the rest?
>
> You offer back the passphrase:
>
> > I will not be subordinate.
>
> With that, the man gestures to the double doors, offering directions:
>
> > Take the doors into the theater, and get comfortable. There's food and drink, feel free to help yourself.

The lobby is guarded by a small team of Wandren guards.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Lobby Guards
>
> Four [[Wandren Patroller]] guard the lobby. Two guard the main doors, and two block the stairs up to the lobby.
>
> They are alert but not hostile unless they are provoked. When fighting begins in the theater they enter through the double doors.

There is little to see in the lobby, though the party can examine the bronze rask statue which the theater derives its name from.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Bronze Rask
>
> Characters with **Culture: Ordani** and **Knowledge: Arts** know that the rask statue in the lobby was gifted to the theater several decades ago by a visiting noble or dignitary. Story goes that they were so impressed with the play put on here that they were compelled to give a gift, and so they commissioned a statue of their favorite animal. With nowhere else for it to go, the statue ended up in the lobby, and eventually the theater came to be known for it, which is how it ended up with its current name.
>
> Characters without the above culture and knowledge combo must succeed on a **Society (DC 16)** skill check to know this information.

#### No Balcony Access

The balcony area of the theater will not be available until the release of Foundry VTT V14 and the inclusion of scene level technology. Until this time, consider the balcony closed for renovation and physically inaccessible.

A later encounter states that there are enemies on the balcony, they are instead found on the main stage for the time being.
