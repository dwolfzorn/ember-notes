# Main Floor

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The theater hall descends in three sweeping terraces toward a central floor crowded with neatly ranked chairs. The hall holds a hush, broken only by the faint settling of old wood and creaking ropes in the rafters. The air carries the mellow scent of beeswax and polished wood.
>
> Far above, the ceiling vaults upward, beyond the balcony overhead, past the hanging lamps that light the central space, and into the large, windowed cupola. Toward the stage, a network of ropes, beams and narrow walkways crisscross the open space.
>
> Ahead, the stage waits, lit by gleaming gold sconces.

## Encounter Details

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Malevolent Main Course
>
> Characters that ate or drank any of the food must instead make a `[[/save con 16]]` saving throw against a potent poison. Success leaves the character &reference[poisoned] for 10 minutes, while failure renders the character &reference[unconscious] for 1d10 hours.
>
> Characters rendered unconscious by this poison immediately wake up if they suffer at least 1 hit point of damage from any source.
>
> #### Choking Gas Bombs
>
> The Ordani locals and Arcturian refugees in the basement will be unable to escape due to the immobilizing poison in the food and drink. Characters on the stage, like Brynna, are out of reach and remain safe as long as they stay up there.
>
> Characters caught in the smoke must make a `[[/save con 14]]` to resist being &reference[blinded] and &reference[incapacitated] for 1 minute, but can repeat their save at the end of their turns. Successfully saving ends the effect.

This encounter also has a special macro to help with setup.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Group Hostility Macro
>
> The [[Toggle Subordinate Disposition]] turns the Wandren actors and Ordani Ruffians hostile. Use this when the encounter begins and the enemies are revealed to the party.

### Bad Eats

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Plans and Poisons
>
> The Mutagists are utilizing a two-part poison to subdue the party and civilians here for easy capture. The first part of the poison is in the food and drink, and has no immediate effect, but becomes activated when the Mutagists throw in gas bombs later.
>
> The gas bombs themselves simply cause choking and partial blindness, but combined with the poison in the food, they create a powerful sleeping poison that renders most people unconscious.
>
> For the commonfolk in the scene, this is immediately effective without saves, but the party is sturdier and will be able to make saves to resist the poison.

## Encounter if Cover Intact

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Crowd Control
>
> The two [[Mutagist Grenadier]] on loan to Brynna can wade into the toxic clouds without issue, and focus on placing [[Manacles]] on any creatures that are defeated or disabled by the poison. If engaged in combat they utilize their [[Axe]] to defend themselves, and avoid killing commonfolk or cityfolk. This courtesy is not extended to the party once they reveal themselves.
>
> #### Riot Patrol
>
> The largest fighting force consists of 4 x [[Wandren Patroller]] that work in teams to capitalize on their [[Pack Tactics]] ability.
>
> Their goal is to keep the crowd in the smoke so that it can do its work. The patrollers pull their attacks so as not to kill anyone, but are otherwise very rough with anyone trying to flee.
>
> #### Upstage
>
> [[Brynna Verocorrt]] will hold per position on the stage and direct efforts from relative safety. However, if she's engaged in combat, she is a highly competent Wandren Tracer. Refer to the Hidden Blades section below for her combat tactics.
>
> If half her forces are defeated by the party she will attempt to flee.
>
> #### Overwatch
>
> There are 2 x [[Wandren Tracer]] on the [[Balcony]] that begin selecting targets to fire at from above. They shoot to disable if possible.
>
> #### Turncoats
>
> The people that join Brynna and move near the stage avoid combat, but if forced to fight should be treated as [[Ordani Ruffian]]. They fight using the simple weapons they carry, and attempt to surrender or flee if they are reduced to half their maximum hit points or less.

## Encounter if Cover Blown

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Hidden Blades
>
> Use the above encounter, but add 3 x [[Wandren Tracer]] to the battle near the party. These nimble fighters use hit-and-run tactics via their [[Cunning Action]], [[Nightstep]], and avoid harm using their [[Evasion]] abilities.
>
> They excel in close combat with [[Swift Stab]] from their short swords and [[Throw Knife]], both of which utilize their [[Sneak Attack]] for increased damage and [[Limblock Toxin]] to disable enemies.
>
> They also favor dark environments, utilizing their [[Nightstep]] ability to quickly teleport between shadows.
>
> While these enemies are dedicated to the cause, they aren't willing to die for it, and use their speed and agility to flee the fight if either Brynna is defeated, or more than half their allies have fallen in battle.

### Risk of Defeat

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### If The Party Fails
>
> If the party entirely fails their saves and is immobilized, they will still have a chance to escape and turn things around. Have the party wake up in the cage found in the [[Backstage]] area. They will be there along with some other commonfolk, and more are being brought in periodically.
>
> The party is without their weapons and spell books (the items are nearby) but they have everything else the entered the theater with.
>
> The heavy lock on the cage can be picked from either side of the cage, but is easier if done from the outside. A single successful **`[[/skill thief 16]]`** check is necessary to pick the lock. Attempting this from inside the cage is done with **-2 Banes**.
>
> Alternatively, the party can wait for more prisoners to be brought in and attack when the cage is opened.

## Searching the Aftermath

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Worrying Missive
>
> A pair of letters addressed to Brynna can be found on her person once defeated. If she managed to escape they can instead be found on the writing desk found in the [[Makeup]] area.
>
> A successful **Awareness (DC 16)** determines that the paper, handwriting and ink are identical to each other. Characters with **Knowledge: Forensics** have **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> If the characters still have the [[Letter to Kaftor]], they will note it's also a match for the other two.
>
> #### The First Letter
>
> > As promised, I've conferred with leadership and forwarded your proposal.
> >
> > It was met with general approval, as expected. Therefore, you have clearance to send the majority of your new flock underground, after you supply the lab with sufficient human test subjects. They should be healthy and hearty, ideally.
> >
> > I know you will want the most strong-backed of them, and that is fine. The lab needs survivors, not people who can fight back or rip themselves free of restraints. Sort your bodies accordingly!
> >
> > Be aware that the Veiled Chain is attempting to uncover and disrupt both our operations, and are being especially tenacious in their efforts. I'm doing my best to throw them off, but expect undercover Veiled Chain operatives and hired adventurers.
> >
> > I've forwarded descriptions and information on the party they have been relying on recently. Be aware of them, and make necessary preparations for their arrival.
> >
> > Wisdom and cunning guide you.
>
> #### The Second Letter
>
> This letter lacks any signature or identifying marks, but is dated shortly after you raided the lab. The hand writing is identical to the first letter.
>
> > You have every reason to be concerned. The loss of the lab at the hands of the Veiled Chain has set us back substantially. However, I am personally taking steps to ensure that the Veiled Chain will no longer be a problem.
> >
> > You simply need to deal with the undercover operatives the Chain has in play. Once you have done this, and the Chain is broken, we will be able to reopen the lab and continue our work with impunity.
> >
> > As always,
> >
> > Wisdom and cunning guide you.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Connection to Serethus
>
> Serethus used the phrase "Wisdom and cunning guide you." when the party was breaking up after **Ill Tidings**. It's a minor connection that keen characters (and their players) might notice here.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Magic Armor
>
> If Brynna was defeated in combat, a suit of magical [[Studded Leather Armor +1]] can be looted from her body. The leather is expertly made and died dark, and the metal studs are all a polished bronze, fitting the typical House Wandren style.
>
> If Brynna escaped but you still want to award the party with this armor, you can place this armor on one of the other fallen Wandren fighters, or found in the [[Wardrobe]] area racks with a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check.
