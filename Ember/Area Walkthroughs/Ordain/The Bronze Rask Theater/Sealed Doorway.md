# Sealed Doorway

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This passage, half collapsed on one side, expands out into a large chamber of white stone that reaches from floor to ceiling. Each block is perfectly cut and set then polished smooth.
>
> Time has not been kind to this space, as cracks have formed throughout the walls and ceilings, and fixtures have suffered damage from falling stone slabs. Curiously, much of the rubble has been moved aside and collected into piles.
>
> At the end of the space a massive white stone door spans one side of this area, its face gilded with old bronze or gold metal.
>
> Two figures in mining gear and wearing illuminated helmets stand before the massive door, idly chatting with each other. Their voices echo off the stone, words becoming indistinct in the process.

## Examining the Chamber

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Ancient Chamber
>
> Characters that examine the chamber recognize that it might be the oldest stone encountered so far. A successful **Society (DC 16)** theorizes that this chamber might have been part of some of the earliest of Ordain's construction, and is several centuries old. The area has likely been long forgotten, built over and lost due to collapse.
>
> Characters with the **Culture: Ordani** have **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> #### Gilded Barrier
>
> Characters attempting to open the door note that it is sealed and will not budge no matter how hard one might try. Attempting to cast portal opening spells such as [[Knock]] are also entirely ineffective.
>
> A successful **Awareness (DC 15)** check notes that there are no obvious or hidden mechanisms by which to open the door. It is possible the door only opens from the other side.
>
> A successful **Arcana (DC 16)** check on the gilded door recognizes an exceptionally old symbol once used by the first Cindaric Sages, meaning this door is at least as old as the organization is.
>
> Characters with **Path: Cindaric Initiate** have **+2 Boons** on this check.

## speaking to the Miners

The miners are **Donak** and **Karnt**, they are Arcturians both, and former residents of [[Arcturel]]. They were put out of work due the actions of the [[Silver Beam Consortium]] and the events that eventually lead up to the [[Glitter in the Dark]] quest.

The miners have no interest in fighting and if threatened will surrender immediately, preferring to be taken to the Hallows rather than risk being killed by a group of armed assailants.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Unlucky Landmovers
>
> These miners are unwitting accomplices of of Brynna Vercocorte, house Wandren and the Mutagists. They were paid to do a dubious job, and know nothing about the events going on.
>
> They are innocent bystanders caught in this mess, and had Brynna's plans gone as expected, they would have been scooped up and captured like all the other commonfolk in the theater.
>
> #### Temporary Presence
>
> Note that if the party leaves the theater after the Midnight Meeting event without speaking to the miners first, they will be gone on any return visits.

The party may want to speak with or question the miners, the following topics are relevant to the area map.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who hired you?
>
> **A:**
>
> > > Brynna Vercocorte. She wanted us to dig through to a chamber, this one, I guess! Been working on it for weeks now, most of the time was spent trying to get through the basalt. Only just broke through a little while ago.
> > >
> > > We've got legit work order papers and everything, not on us, but we insisted on them.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why are you hanging around?
>
> **A:**
>
> > > Brynna told us to hang tight down and that she'd be along with our payment. It's been a bit though, we were discussing if we should just go and hit her up for payment later, or something.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Are you with House Wandren?
>
> **A:**
>
> > > No way! We used to work out in Arcturel, but lost our jobs after the Silver Beam consortium moved in and started buying up the mines and bringing in their own crew. We've been taking whatever rock and stone jobs come up since.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Do you know anything about the Mutagists?
>
> **A:**
>
> > > Who? You mean the creeps in the masks? Nah, they've been lurking around for a bit now, we keep well clear of them. The way they look at you, it's like you're meat to be carved up, not a person.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What do you know about the chamber and door?
>
> **A:**
>
> > > Nothin'. It's the same white stone as the rest of the ruins, and most of Ordain, at that. It's not local stone, so it was quarried elsewhere. Again, like the rest of Ordain.
> > >
> > > The symbol on the door is weird, and there's no way to open it that we're aware of, not that we were asked to. But we did get curious and see, all the same.
