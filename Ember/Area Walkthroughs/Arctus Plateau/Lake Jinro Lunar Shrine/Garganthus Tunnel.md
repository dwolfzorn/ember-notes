# Garganthus Tunnel

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A huge cave opening has been violently carved out of the earth here. Claw marks adorn the edges of the cave's dark and rugged mouth, which constantly exhales a hollow baritone wind.

### The Garganthus Lair

This small cave is where the [[Juvenile Garganthus]] here first emerged upon the surface of Ember. If the characters search the cave interior, they'll discover a few details about how and why it came to be — including hints of the inaccessible [[Pathways]] caverns that lurk beneath. Little else of value can be found here.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The cave interior, lined with the skeletal remains of various creatures, tells a story of a hungry abomination that made its way to the surface world from somewhere dark and terrible far below. Your stomach tightens and your eyes sting as they confront a fetid, ammonia-laced odor left behind by the subterranean monstrosity.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Cave
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 11, Passive)** while searching the cave can recognize the persistence of huge claw marks throughout most of the cave's interior, as if the Juvenile Garganthus clawed its way out of the earth.
>
> - **Knowledge: Subterranea**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character can also detect the presence of collapsed earth at the rear end of the cave, with little chance of detecting what lies far beyond.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Wilderness (DC 12)** check is able to identify the skeletal remains of the various lake-dwelling creatures the Juvenile Garganthus has consumed here, which include the skeletons of beavers, grassland herons, and giant catfish (among others).
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Subterranea**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character can provide a detailed survey of the area, noting that an impassible cave-in presides over the rear section of the cave. While it once led somewhere below the island, the collapsed earth currently obstructs this bygone passage for at least 50 or more feet. Any attempt to traverse the wall-to-wall rubble would prove overwhelmingly dangerous or futile. Most likely, the Juvenile Garganthus attempted to traverse a narrow fissure here smaller than itself, and destroyed the earthen tunnel in the process.
