This lonely lakeside island near the western shores of Lake Jinro is named after the archaic lunar shrine that sits upon its grassy hillside precipice. Dedicated to Ember's moons, the shrine was created by Arcturians who dwelled here many centuries ago, and its esoteric secrets have been lost to time.

### Environment

This layered hill of sandstone and granite features a switchback path that winds its way towards the mound's grassy precipice, where the eponymous Lunar Shrine stands against time immemorial. Surrounded by the tranquil waters of Lake Jinro, the Lunar Shrine's island is a remote and solitary place, where remnants of civilization — like the shrine itself and a solemn beach strewn with rusty blades — are left to be forgotten.

### Ecology

The remote nature of the Lake Jinro Lunar Shrine has resulted in a rather simple ecology, marked by sparse wildlife and natural overgrowth. A scattering of evergreen and deciduous trees dot the island's shaggy, shrub-dotted landscape, along with a covering of golden grass that grows bare near the water's edge. Wildlife here includes the varied fish and shellfish of Lake Jinro alongside avian species like kestrels and gulls, who visit the island to feed on the area's assorted fish and insects. Lately, the island has also become home to a Juvenile Garganthus, a brutal pathways predator that is most certainly out of place upon this otherwise tranquil isle.

### Lore

The Lunar Shrine is is generally regarded as one of the Age of Discovery’s earliest innovations, and is said to have been created by an alliance of Arcturian druids and geomancers who sought to craft a powerful scrying tool for their cosmological rituals. Led by a soothsayer named Menodora Vhael, this alliance came to be known as the Selenic Order, and was regarded for nearly a century as one of the region’s most enigmatic if favorable organizations. Yet, whatever events caused the Shrine to be abandoned long ago are as mysterious as the inner workings of the landmark itself.

Beyond its historical context (or perhaps because of it), the Lake Jinro Lunar Shrine is purported to be a powerful spot for divinations and other rituals — particularly for those capable of weaving magic and transcending the mundane. A half dozen smaller altars line the path that winds its way upward to the island’s ascent, each of them keyed to one of Ember’s specific lunar bodies. Characters who offer the appropriate ritual elements or components at each shrine are granted a temporary cosmic boon, thanks to the potent Arcturian magic that lingers here.
