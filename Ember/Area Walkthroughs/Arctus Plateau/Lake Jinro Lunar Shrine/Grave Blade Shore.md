# Grave Blade Shore

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This rocky beach is decorated with an array of rusty swords, which jut from the water-lapped ground in solemn reverence to the unnamed warriors who once wielded them. Many of these ancient blades hang lurched in the sodden earth with aged resignation, while others stand resolute and unbothered by the relentless assault of time.

### The Armory Beach

This nameless graveyard of rusted blades is a rather enigmatic feature of the island, and is the source of various rumors among people who speak of this storied place. The characters may examine this unnamed monument to fallen warriors to discover various clues about the island's ancient history, including the origin of the blades.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Forgotten Armory
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 12)** check has heard rumors of this place while traveling the [[Golden Flats]]. Some say it's an essential part of one of the Shrine’s lunar rituals, while others maintain that the presence of this forgotten armory suggests the spellcasters of the Selenic Order who built this place were known for their martial prowess as much as their magical gifts.
>
> - **Knowledge: Legends**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: Legends and research suggest the true origin of this beachside monument. The first blade left here was driven into the earth by the Selenic Order's leader, a soothsayer named Menodora Vhael, as a vow to her deceased partner — the brilliant [[Maziran]] swordmaster Eriss Khan. In the years that followed, each time the Selenic Order came to this place, an acolyte would leave a blade of their own here upon the beach. And on and on it went, until today.
>
> #### Examining the Blades
>
> A few of the blades adorning the beach here are considerably more interesting than their rusted mundane counterparts.
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 13, Passive)** check while searching the armory beach is able to locate the following:
>
> - An assortment of 15 rusty [[Longsword]], 6 rusty [[Rapier]], and 5 rusty [[Shortsword]]. These corroded, rust-covered blades are in extremely poor condition and are Shoddy quality.
> - A relatively untarnished iron [[Shortsword]].
> - An untarnished [[Silvered Longsword]].
> - A curious blade at the center of the beach made of some strange alloy. This is in fact the [[Blade of Eriss Khan]]. If any character attempts to remove the Blade of Eriss Khan from its resting place, they are subject to the hazardous effects of the magical blade's violent curse. See "Removing the Blade of Eriss Khan" below.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 12)** check while examining the swords can detect the telltale signs of Maziran smithing in the qualities of the Blade of Eriss Khan.
>
> - **Culture: Maziran**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Crafts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Arcana (DC 15)** check is able to notice a significant arcane component to the nature of the Blade of Eriss Khan and its creation.
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Society (DC 12)** check has heard dozens of tall tales about what happens when the sanctity of shrines and monuments are disturbed, and extends the same consideration to this place.
>
> Furthermore, if [[Amalthea Stonecraft]] is with the party, she strongly advises them against disturbing the blades strewn here upon the shore, but has little to offer in the way of explanation and is focused on the task at hand.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Removing a Blade from the Beach
>
> Any character who removes any of the blades here (silvered, magical, or not) cannot benefit from the blessings of the Altar Stones along the [[Switchback Path]] or from the boons associated with Leaving an Offering (see below).
>
> #### Removing the Blade of Eriss Khan
>
> If the [[Blade of Eriss Khan]] is removed from its resting place, its curse becomes active and afflicts whichever character takes it from the beach. The curse is exceptionally powerful, and a character does not have to Invest the Blade of Eriss Khan to become cursed by it.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Leaving an Offering
>
> If the party members decide to leave offerings of their own here, they can each receive a temporary magical boon.
>
> Any character who drives a *blade of personal significance** into the ground here, to leave in perpetuity, gains **+2 Boons** on their next check and any hazard that would affect them does so with **-2 Banes**. These effects last for 24 hours.
>
> * - A blade of personal significance could be an heirloom, a sword the character has used for more than one level, or simply a blade the character covets.
