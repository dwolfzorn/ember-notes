# Lower Bar

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you descend into the lower bar you are met with air that is rich with the scent of malt, sharp citrus, and the heavier sweetness of fruit-laden liquors mixed with the faint sting of smoke.
>
> The centerpiece of the tavern is a truly massive circular table were patrons gather to share a drink. It dominates the common floor — its broad surface crafted from rings of contrasting stone, each band a different color. At its center is a brick-lined opening not unlike a well.
>
> In the corner is a raised stage of worn planks, a modest raised stage sits ready for music or tales. The day's performers do their best to compete with the din of Rallyhome's occupants, but only those closest to the stage truly get to enjoy whatever show is being put on.
>
> Against one wall stands a lengthy bar topped with golden stone, behind it, shelves spill over with bottles and crafted decanters filled with spirits in every hue. Both ends of the bar feature brass-bound taps ready to dispense liquid merriment.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Rallyhome Well
>
> At first glance this hole looks like the mouth of any other well, but its placement is curious and it features a funnel-like mouth meant to direct items into it.
>
> A successful **Awareness (DC 12, Passive)**notices that now and then patrons throw coins into the well, often before leaving the tavern, or just as they are sitting down to drink.
>
> - A further **Society (DC 12, Passive)** expects there is some local ritual or custom at play here that the locals might know more about.

> [!info] Social
> #### Local History
>
> Asking the locals, or any of the bar staff about the Rallyhome Well yields some insight into its history and purpose:
>
> > During the construction of Rallyhome this well was placed to let patrons throw coins or other tokens down into the sinkhole way down below.
> >
> > As the city grew, and new levels were added, the town agreed to respect the Rallyhome well, and continued to extend the well downward through each level, ensuring that it always allowed for the full descent.
> >
> > It's considered good luck to toss a coin into the well before beginning or ending any outing into the Sinkhole Depths below. Failing to do so, or worse, refusing, not only earns you a miserly reputation in the bar, but will curse you to suffer misfortune and bad luck belowground.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Politics** or **Knowledge: Crafts** suspect that this had to have caused some considerable headache to sort out, since building a well between suspended levels is no simple feat.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Subterranea** or **Knowledge: Crafts** know that the well would descend for hundreds of feet then open up into a freefall of several hundreds more at least. Such a fall would be utterly fatal.
>
> #### Lost Treasure
>
> Characters that ask about the fate of things thrown down the well receive a further bit of legend and color:
>
> > Just where all the coins, gems, and tokens thrown down the well end up is an enduring mystery. Many adventurers have gone into the depths looking, working out maps and search patterns with the hope of finding some pile of riches, but nobody ever has.
> >
> > Rumor has it that unscrupulous individuals have even tried to intercept items thrown down by setting nets or catches, but they never find any success, as though the well bypasses their traps entirely out of spite!
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Forensics** or **Science (DC 16, Passive)** suspect that the scatter range of items would create a very large search area, and would make finding more than the odd coin or gem largely impossible.
>
> #### Matters of Safety
>
> Characters asking about the danger of falling down the well receive assurances they aren't the first to wonder about that:
>
> > The well has feather fall protections places in it that keeps living folk from falling to their deaths. In fact, each level has an outlet where people can get free of the well.
> >
> > Every now and then some fool jumps in as a dare after one too many Open Veins, and their friends have to go fish them out of an access gate. They never get hurt, though.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Rituals** or **Arcana (DC 16, Passive)** know that weaving such magic into the stone and making sure it lasts without needing constant upkeep or replacement would have been quite an undertaking. The effect itself might not be terrible complex, but making the magic robust enough to be repeatedly triggered takes a skilled mage.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Mixed to Order
>
> Rallyhome offers a wide variety of wines and spirits, and most prices are modest, somewhere between a few copper and a couple silver for a single serving. They also have a few specialty drinks:
>
> - **Fool's Gold** — Served in a crystal goblet, this pale gold liqueur swirls with glimmering gold flecks. The drink is syrupy and sweet, with a metallic aftertaste. It's 1gp and twice the price it should be on purpose.
> - **The Open Vein** —Served in a tall glass that's cold as ice, the drink features layers of gold, red, brown, and amber liquors. It's sweet up front, becoming bitter, then has a smoky finish. It's 5sp a glass, and hits harder than a miner's pick. Drinking one requires a **Wilderness (DC 15)** or **Science (DC 15)** to space the consumption out sufficiently for safe consumption, failure leaves a character **Poisoned** .
> - **Sinkhole Draught** — Served in a squat, round cup, the liquid within is blue and slightly glittering. It smells of the earth, and when stirred, glows faintly. It's bright and floral at first, then develops a salty, earthy flavor that numbs the tongue slightly. It's made with a bioluminescent mushroom liquor made in Arcturel. Costs 6sp per cup.
> - **Druid's Draught** — Served in an earthenware mug, this murky green-brown mixture of beer, spirits and muddled herbs has a deeply herbal flavor that lingers long after the mug is empty. Locals swear that it'll cure whatever ails you, costs 1sp per mug.
