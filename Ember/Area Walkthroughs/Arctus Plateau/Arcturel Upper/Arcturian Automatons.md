# Arcturian Automatons

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Arcturian Automatons presents itself as a boxy, striking building made entirely of weathered bronze. Its surface is burnished to a muted sheen, panels joined with visible rivets and seams. A weathered sign beside the door bears the painting of a humanoid construct and the words:
>
> > The Chessman: Tackles any task. Works any post.
> >
> > Each one made to order.
> >
> > Inquire within!

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Operating Hours
>
> Arcturian Automatons is open until sundown, afterward the doors are locked and the Chessmen inside will act as security.
>
> It is possible the the proprieter, Vortholomew Chess is present after work hours, tinkering with designs or working to fulfill an order, but he doesn't let anyone in without a prior appointment.

Inside the building, one finds a collection of constructs for sale.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Set throughout the shop floor, tall and slender automatons, each a wonder of precise craftsmanship, their limbs and torsos sheathed in overlapping metallic shells. Some of them are wrapped in warm, rosy copper, others the rich gleam brass, while others bear the gray shadow of hammered iron.
>
> A long wooden table and shelves of scrolls sit toward the back of this shop where the shop's owner conducts business.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Acquiring Automatons
>
> Each Chessman automaton costs 1500 gold pieces and takes 30 days to be built to the buyer's specifications, and all new orders are put on a waitlist. The current waiting time for a new construct is half a year.
>
> There is no means to expedite this. The floor models are for display and demonstration only.
>
> #### Examining Automatons
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Machines** or **Knowledge: Crafts** recognize that these constructs are incredibly well made, with precise construction tolerances and a keen eye for detail. They are masterwork automatons.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Aedir** recognize the style of the machines is more akin to that of Aedir constructs and automatons than classical Arcturian designs which tend to feature more natural materials like wood and cloth.

If the party enters the back area, which is generally off-limits to customers, they will find Chess' workspace.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A long wooden bench stretches along one wall, its scarred surface crowded with gears, rods, and curious lengths of interlocking metal. Partially assembled components, fine tools, and discarded sketches are scattered across the workspace here.
> At the back of this room is a door leading to crowded storage area where every available space is filled with something: boxes of bolts and springs, trays of washers and pins, battered tins of grease and oilcloth, spare tools slipped into a rack wedged against a wall and forgotten behind unmarked crated.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Lots to See, Nothing to Find
>
> These back room areas are packed with items and pieces of machinery to examine, but there is nothing of particular note in this space. These parts and materials are all meant to create or maintain constructs, and are utterly mundane, even if the machines they go into are anything but.
