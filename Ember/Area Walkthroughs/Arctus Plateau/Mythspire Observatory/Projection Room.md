# Projection Room

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You enter a broad chamber where the floor is dominated by a pair of long metal rails, fixed firmly into the ancient stone and stretching in parallel from the room’s center to a nearby balcony. At either end of the rails is a circular recess etched with runes.
>
> Set into the walls are two crystalline fixtures with large chest-high surfaces: one holds an array of blue and red gems set into the walls. Both clusters are fixed over extensively rune-covered structures of stone. One of them appears to have been badly burned.
>
> Opposite these fixtures, the room curves into a long, smooth wall, utterly unbroken save for a border of runes at its outermost edges.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Ancient Projectors
>
> A successful **Arcana (DC 15)**check while examining the runes around the crystal machines, the recess at the end of the rails, and the curved wall determines that they all worked in concert.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Machines** suspect that this may have been used for a variety of tasks — testing orbs, charging them, or extracting precise information from them.
>
> The gems would project magical light, which was meant to hit something sitting in the cradle, and project light onto the wall beyond.
>
> The presence of illusion and divination runes present on the crystal likely provided some predictive and visual elements to the projection.
>
> - Characters with **Language: Pathward** can read the controls and determine that this was once used to project light through crystal orbs onto the large, curved wall and had several modes including knowledge retrieval, surface inspection, and attunement. The instructions do not elaborate on how these states worked, or what they did.
>
> #### Powerless Ruins
>
> A successful **Arcana (DC 14)**check while examining the rune-covered machines here determines information based on the result.
>
> - **Success** Though you're not sure what caused it, at some point a portion of the machinery on this wall suffered a catastrophic failure that left the stone badly burned, destroying several of the runes carved into it. The gems as well have suffered cracks, rendering them inert.
> - **Critical Success** Attempting to fill in the gaps left by the destruction, you believe this machine was meant to control the flow of magical energy through the room. Extensive evocation and abjuration runes were used to dampen and control the energy; if they failed, it could have resulted in the damage you see here.
> - Characters with **Language: Pathward** can read around the scarring and damage and surmise that this controlled all of the power for the central dome's projection system.
