# Tower Descent

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you descend into the darkness you can see a vast chamber awaiting you, large glimmering rune-stones affixed to the wall serving as the only source of light. The space is square, and adorned with ancient reliefs of towering figures twice your height at least.
>
> The stone lift thuds abruptly at the end of its descent, the impact resounding through the dim chamber beyond. Your arrival disturbs the cool, dry air here, causing the scent of rot to drift in to greet you from somewhere nearby.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Familiar Wall Crystals
>
> Glimmering, rune-carved crystals embedded in the wall faintly glow, their fading magic still powering the lift and providing contrast for the carvings in the stone.
>
> Characters that completed the Over the Moon quest recognize these crystals as the runed crystals being made in the forest of stone ruins. They were likely being used to power the lift here.
>
> A successful **Arcana (DC 14)**check here notes that these crystals are all nearly depleted, but haven't failed yet. While they could be removed, they would make returning to the upper chambers impossible if taken out.
>
> #### Ancient Carvings
>
> A successful **Science (DC 13)**while looking over the carvings notes that there are seven figures, and each bear a large gem around their neck. They all appear to be directing their attention skyward, though damage to the upper sections of this chamber and the inherent darkness make it impossible to tell what they are looking at.
>
> Examining the carvings with a successful**Society (DC 14)**check leads you to believe these are priests of these ancient people, whom you suspect were Shent. Given what you know of Shent religion, this is probably depicting seven priests of the sky venerating or communing with the seven major celestial bodies. Why is a mystery, as there's not enough context in the carving.
>
> Looking over the carvings while making a successful **Society (DC 16)**check leads you to believe that these are not actually the Shent. The depictions of them are wrong in small ways, from poses, to garb, to the general style of the carving. You suspect these may actually be ancestors of the Shent.
>
> Characters proficient with **Language: Pathward** recognize an engraving at the base of the reliefs stating:
>
> > The seven sky watchers observe the motions of the cosmos as honored Vanexis stand guard. Blessed are those born beneath the skies of Ember, may they never walk in starless voids again.
>
> #### Unfamliar Creatures
>
> Depicted alongside the giant figures in the stone are numerous graceful yet lanky cat-like beings with long feathered tails and similarly feathered wings. They do not look up as the humanoid figures do, but instead keep their eyes level, as though watching over the figures.
>
> These creatures are unfamiliar, and don't appear in any lore, history, or religion of the Shent people.
>
> - Characters proficient with **Language: Pathward** that read the above inscription can infer that the creatures depicted are "Vanexis."
