# Sanctorum

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The domed ceiling of this large circular chamber is crowned with a roof made of glass, which allows sun- and moonlight to shine down into the sanctorum with a perpetual and natural elegance. The chamber itself is a remarkable monument to the natural world: four tiers of moss-covered earthen steps are stacked in ascending concentric circles around a shallow pool in a basin at the room's center. A loose arrangement of benches and pillows on these steps confirms the purpose of this place as a gathering and meditation chamber. Meanwhile, a wooden platform overlooks the southwestern edge of the basin like a tiny pier, adorned with a wide altar that appears to be decorated with druidic accoutrements and half-burned candles.

This large circular chamber serves as Corpin Sanctuary's main place of worship, and is generally reserved for Cindaric communion, sacred rites, and other special occasions.

> [!info] Social
> #### Sanctuary Conversation
>
> At any given moment, someone is present in the Sanctorum, including at least one [[Cindaric Adherent]] who serves as a steward of the area. These stewards maintain a watchful eye over the Chamber, and will readily approach any visitors to the area with warm greetings The stewards work in 4 hour shifts, and each of the Corpin Sanctuary Sages who lives here (including major and minor NPCs) is committed to at least one shift a week.
>
> If the characters attempt to start a conversation with one of the Cindaric Sages here, topics of discussion include the following:
>
> - The history of Corpin Sanctuary and the Cindaric Sages who dwell here.
> - Current rumors of the Golden Flats and the Arctus Plateau at large.
> - A genuine interest in the party's journey to the Sanctuary, and what they plan to do next.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Searching the Sanctorum
>
> Any character who makes a successful `[[/skillCheck awareness 13 check while searching the Sanctorum is able to locate 6 Cindaric prayer books strewn about the seats here.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character is also able to spot a curious circular seam at the bottom of the pool at the center of the room, and may suspect it conceals a door or passage, but a proper understanding of what lurks beneath eludes them.
>
> A successful **Society (DC 13)** check while searching the area confirms that this chamber is where the Cindaric Sages usually conduct their tradition's ceremonies and rituals. The chosen leader of any given ceremony would take their place on the wooden platform in front of the altar, where they would conduct the sacred rites.
>
> - **Knowledge: Souls**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
>
> A successful **Wilderness (DC 13)** check allows a character to recognize some of the species of flora in the chamber to be of significant cultural or historical import to the Arctus Plateau.
>
> - **Culture: Arcturian**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
> - **Knowledge: Plants**: The character gains **+2 Boons**.
> - **Critical Success**: The character recognizes some of these plants as beneficial and ethically sustainable. They can take 15 minutes to harvest enough flora to replenish [[/roll 1d4+2]]` uses of a Healer's Kit.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Secret Elevator
>
> A secret stone trapdoor is expertly concealed beneath the pool at the center of the chamber, which hides a clandestine elevator that connects the Sanctorum with the Pathways far below.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Security Measures
>
> In times of trouble, the Cindaric Sage that happens to be on duty as steward here will barricade the Sanctorum Entrance with a crossbeam of sturdy oak reinforced with steel and alert the other denizens of the sanctuary. See [[Sanctorum Entrance]] for further details.
