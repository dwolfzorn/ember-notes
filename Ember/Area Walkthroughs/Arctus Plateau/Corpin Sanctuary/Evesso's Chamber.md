# Evesso's Chamber

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This small square chamber is sparsely decorated with the simple accoutrements of a private bedroom, including a rustic wooden wardrobe on the eastern wall and shelves lined with tools of the Cindaric trade to the west.

This room serves as the private chamber of [[Evesso]], the necromancer masquerading as a Cindaric Sage. Like the dormitory's other chambers, this room is equipped with a comfortable bed stuffed with down feathers, a footlocker full of personal effects, a rustic wooden wardrobe, and shelves stocked with druidic miscellany.

Evesso's Chamber is rather stark and draconian compared to the other rooms here, and hides a few details that hint at the necromancer's secret identity.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring Evesso's Chamber
>
> Any character who searches the room finds [[Evesso's Note]] pinned beneath a stone bowl on the bedside shelf.
>
> A successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check uncovers the presence of some other interesting items and features of the chamber, including:
>
> - The seams of a secret door on the western wall, which leads to the [[Profane Altar]]. Further inspection reveals the residual presence of an arcane glyph on the secret door that resembles a fractured circle of six interlocked rings, which is also found inscribed on Evesso's Note.
> - A pouch containing various ritual magic components.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 18)**check is able to associate some of the material components in the Component Pouch with various magical rites and spells that would (curiously or coincidentally) be associated with necromancy or divination.
>
> #### Secret Door
>
> Characters with a **Awareness (DC 15, Passive)** are able to locate the seams of the secret door if they didn't actively search the room. There is no apparent keyhole or button on or near the door.
>
> The secret door can only be opened by reciting the passphrase "malediction" in Common (the 5th word on the 16th line of the 53rd page).
>
> - This phrase can be found on the Page 53 in [[Nineteen Nights in Haxim]], the page that corresponds to the 2nd number written on Evesso's Note.
> - "Nineteen Nights in Haxim" is currently located in the [[Library]].
>
> Any character that uses the [[Recognize Spellcraft]] talent is able to discern traces of Abjuration magic surrounding the secret door, and identify the glyphs inscribed upon it as an arcane lock designed to await a spoken passphrase.
