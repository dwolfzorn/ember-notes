# Tower Antechamber

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The southeastern gate of Vortest Tower welcomes you into a single circular chamber whose tall ceiling stretches into abject darkness. A round table made of some curious stone sits at the center of the room, surrounded by eight chairs padded in strange blue leather; in the middle of this table, a small round fire pit crackles with softly-glowing azure embers.
>
> An assortment of other furniture decorates the tower chamber, all of it somewhat esoteric to your eyes and made from an peculiar ash-colored wood — as if the style and structures were inspired by a foreign culture you can't readily place.

The Tower Antechamber has been decorated to look like the sparse and esoteric dwelling of a hermetic spellcaster. In fact, it serves as an ersatz waiting room for the would-be guests of [[Corvana Vortest]], and is actually an arcane method of otherworldly transit that can transport characters to a remotely-contained pocket of the Inner Realm of [[Signara]].

The method of using this arcane transportation is hidden among the chamber's book-laden shelves to the northeast, where one peculiar tome conceals a lever used to activate the otherworldly transportation device.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Searching the Antechamber
>
> Characters who take a few minutes to explore the room and its contents will notice the following:
>
> - The table at the center of the room is made of a petrified version of the same ash-colored wood the rest of the furniture is crafted from. The enchanted azure embers that glow in the fire pit burn with no heat, and radiate dim light in a 10 foot radius.
> - The potted plants that decorate the western wall are especially curious, and don't resemble any flora the characters have seen on the surface of Ember.
> - The eastern bookshelf is stocked with a collection of tomes related to the academic study of various esoteric subjects, including: mythical creatures of Ember, anecdotes and essays on the [[Pathways]], and voluminous encyclopedias regarding the world's flora and fauna. Whenever a character attempts to remove any of these tomes from the Tower Antechamber, the books magically disappear from the character's possession and reappear on the shelves in their original location.
> - The northeastern bookshelf is lined with a collection of tomes — written in a variety of languages — that pertain to [[The Cosmos]], [[Signara]] history, and varied theories on the [[Soul Cycle]]. As with the other bookshelf, whenever a character attempts to remove any of these tomes from the Tower Antechamber, the books magically disappear from the character's possession and reappear on the shelves in their original location.
> - The dark gray stone that comprises the walls and tiled floors of the antechamber is fascinating in its own right, and shines with a luster that seems almost enchanted; it's as if the veins of mineral ore in the stone are ever-so-subtly and perpetually moving.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 15)**] check is able to identify the origin of the furniture as Signaran in both material and design, and notes the particular presence of aged and rarified tomes on the northeastern bookshelf — recognized by the archaic modes of language used in their text.
>
> - **Path: Scion of Darkness**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Crafts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Fiends**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 15)** check can identify the plants as Signaran in origin.
>
> - **Path: Scion of Darkness**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Fiends**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Plants**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 13)** check is able to detect the faint footprints of one or more medium-sized creatures with taloned, three-toed feet. These tracks can be found throughout the room, with a distinct terminus at the northeastern bookshelf along with comings and goings from the [[Front Gate]].
>
> - **Knowledge: Fiends**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check, and can identify the tracks as fiendish in nature.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Awareness (DC 13)** check while searching the bookshelves is able to locate the presence of a curious tome on the northeastern shelf, labeled "House Vortest" in **Language: Eonic** like the one in the relief on the front gate. This check can be repeated as often as necessary.
>
> #### The Curious Tome
>
> The curious tome labeled "House Vortest" is actually not a tome at all, but rather a lever used to activate the transdimensional magic ascribed to the Tower Antechamber. Characters who take a closer look at the curious tome note the following text in **Language: Common** upon its spine:
>
> > A House's Creed is its Compass
>
> Any character who pulls upon the book while speaking the House Vortest creed — "Enlightenment And Beyond" — activates the curious tome and results in immediate [[Vortest Tower]] (detailed below). Pulling on the book without speaking the House Vortest creed has no result, and the book is magically immovable.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Awareness (DC 13)** check while searching the bookshelves is able to locate an aged tome labeled "Heraldry of Signara," which details (among other information) the House Vortest creed: "Enlightenment And Beyond."
>
> - **Path: Academy Dropout**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Fiends**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Alternately, any character who succeeds on a **Society (DC 17)** check is able to recall the House Vortest creed from previous study of famous mages of the [[Arctus Plateau]].
>
> - **Path: Scion of Darkness**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Additionally, any character who consults the [[Stonecraft Manuscript]] is able to find a curious new passage in the otherwise blank section labeled "The Signborn's Secret," which specifically mentions the creed.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Scene Level Transition
>
> When the curious tome is activated, the Scene Level transitions from Ember to Signara. The characters themselves are transported, but do not physically move.
>
> #### Returning to Ember
>
> After the Curious Tome has been activated and the Tower Antechamber has transported the party to Signara, they cannot return to Ember without first resetting the transdimensional mechanism via the [[Jaunt Manipulator]], which is currently held by [[Corvana Vortest]] herself in the [[Primary Bedroom]].

### Transport to Signara

Once the characters have activated the curious tome, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The entire tower begins to shudder and shake as the room grows dark. Arcane runes start to glow beneath your feet, casting narrow beams of a bright blue light up towards the umbral ceiling. The entire front gate begins to glow with a similar azure brilliance, and — to your amazement — two matching doors of soft blue light appear at opposite intervals around the room.
>
> Your head swells with nauseating discombobulation, and a moment later you come to your senses … Where the front gate once stood, an open portal to what seems like another world awaits; beyond, a bridge made of pure blue light stretches out to another platformed chamber.
>
> Meanwhile, the two new doors of blue light remain, revealing more of this remarkable otherworldly terrain on the other sides of their translucent arcane thresholds; a bewildering landscape marked by luminous azure enchantment and shimmering darkness.

All characters located inside the Tower Antechamber when the curious tome is activated are transported to a remote pocket of Signara, where the eight-chamber sprawl of the Vortest Tower complex is connected by a series of arcane bridges made of solid, indestructible light.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Opening the Energy Doors
>
> The two new doors located at even intervals around the antechamber can only be opened from other chambers or devices located throughout the transdimensional complex:
>
> - The door to the west can be opened by a panel located on a wall in the [[Western Sitting Room]].
> - The door to the north can be opened by a panel located on a wall in the [[Northern Sitting Room]].
> - Alternately, the [[Jaunt Manipulator]] can open or close all three doors.
>
> The southeastern doorway that has replaced the [[Front Gate]] now stands open, wreathed in a halo of blue light that matches the luminance of the other doors.
