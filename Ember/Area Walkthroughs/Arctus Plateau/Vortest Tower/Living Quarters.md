# Living Quarters

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This oblong chamber appears to be some kind of den or living quarters, as suggested by the sprawl of comfortable furniture, a pair of lovingly cluttered bookshelves, and an adjoining room. An air of leisure pervades this chamber, which is sealed off by a door made of solid, lapis-colored energy.

The living quarters here consist of a collection of three smaller chambers, including a den, a guest bedroom, and a guest bathroom.

Once the characters open the doors to this area, they'll be attacked by two [[Jahud Assassin]] from the realm of [[Signara]], who have been sent here to slay [[Corvana Vortest]] and extract the item known as the [[Orphic Reliquary]] for their [[Daemos]] master [[Kerastes]]. Unfortunately for the jahud, several chambers of Vortest Tower (including this one) were outfitted with powerful temporal traps, and the fiends are currently incapacitated in a state of suspended animation.

The party's timely arrival to the chamber inadvertently deactivates the temporal trap, and releases the Jahud — who leap into action and attack.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Opening the Energy Doors
>
> The two energy doors that lead to the living quarters den are closed and locked, and can be opened via the following methods in addition to those described in [[Vortest Tower]].
>
> An exterior control panel is located on the rightmost column next to these doors, which features a luminous touch pad in the shape of a five-fingered humanoid hand. Any character who is an ally to Corvana Vortest, including the party members, can gain entry by placing their hand upon the touch pad and speaking the House Vortest creed as featured in the [[Tower Antechamber]]: "Enlightenment and Beyond."
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)**check is able to locate a crack in the stonework left of the eastern door large enough to allow a Tiny or smaller-sized creature to enter the bedroom section of the living quarters. Larger creatures cannot squeeze through this crack, but alternative methods of travel — such as the [[Misty Step]] Spell — can be applied.
>
> #### Spotting the Assassins
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 17)**check while peering through the lapis translucence of the closed energy door is able to notice the 2 Jahud Assassins lurking inside, who were attempting to furtively sneak through the den when the temporal trap captured them.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Suspended Animation
>
> 2 [[Jahud Assassin]] lurk here, caught in a state of suspended animation caused by the magic of a temporal trap located in the living quarters. The trap has already been triggered and cannot be reset by the characters. 1 jahud is located in the northernmost part of the den, while the other lurks in the guest bedroom to the south.
>
> This state of suspended animation ends as soon as the doors to the chamber are opened, releasing the jahud — who emerge from the temporal suspension relatively ignorant of the reason for party's arrival, but generally aware of the threat they pose to the mission for Kerastes.
>
> #### Jahud Tactics
>
> At the start of combat, both jahud will begin by targeting characters with their [[Plasma Ray]] attacks before closing in to engage the party in melee.
>
> Over the course of combat, the jahud will prioritize the following actions and abilities:
>
> - In melee, each Jahud makes use of its [[Signaran Dagger]] and [[Bladed Bracer]] attacks against an individual target until that target is rendered unconscious.
> - If one or more characters target the jahud from afar with ranged attacks, the jahud will attack the offending enemy characters with [[Plasma Ray]] before repositioning themselves to gain cover.
> - If a Jahud manages to break line of sight, it will try to activate its [[Natural Invisibility]] and position for a [[Backstab]]
>
> The jahud assassins will fight to the death in service of their interplanar mission for Kerastes, biologically programmed to continue their relentless pursuit of Corvana Vortest and the Orphic Reliquary.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Living Quarters
>
> Characters who take a moment to search the living quarters will be able to discover the following:
>
> - The furniture is mostly crafted of petrified ashwood and appears posh and well-kept, if somewhat dusty and ignored.
> - The bookshelves are lined with a variety of mythological tomes and keepsakes. Any character who spends at least 5 minutes to search the shelves more extensively will find `[[/roll 2d2]]` books with eccentric titles, and can roll twice on the [[Arcturian Trinkets]] table.
> - The tome on the lectern to the north is an aged book of otherworldly fairy tales written in Eonic labeled "Veerad's Fables." It sits open to two dusty pages featuring a story called "The Fletcher and the Fiend," which has no apparent connection to the events at hand. Any character who takes the 15 minutes necessary to read this story gains **+2 Boons** on skill checks made to identify the [[Jahud Assassin]] or their fiendish motives.
> - The potted plants here, while native to [[Signara]], are relatively mundane.
> - The door to the bedroom section of the Living Quarters is unlocked. The bedroom is dusty and neglected, and the dresser cabinet on the southern wall is empty, save for a half-dozen coat hangers and one set of [[Dress Clothing]]
> - The door to the bathroom section of the Living Quarters is unlocked. The bathroom is clean and well-kept. The facilities are made with semi-enchanted plumbing technology that conjures potable water and allows waste to be expelled to an unknown location.
