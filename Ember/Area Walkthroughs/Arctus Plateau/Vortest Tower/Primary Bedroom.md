# Primary Bedroom

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This edifice appears to host a primary bedroom adjoined by a glass-floored foyer. Therein, behind the lapis glow and electrostatic thrum of an energy door, a four-armed figure appears frozen in suspended animation, locked in timeless combat with a trio of fiendish assailants who approach her with a fixed and petrified ferocity — their weapons raised in motionless aggression.

This area serves as the main bedroom of Vortest Tower, and contains the bedroom proper, an adjoining bathroom, and a foyer — where [[Corvana Vortest]] is currently trapped in a state of suspended animation along with two [[Jahud Assassin]], thanks to the effects of a temporal trap.

Once the characters open the door to this area, combat between Corvana and the jahud will immediately begin, with the party caught up in the mix. The characters must help the [[Signborn]] mage survive the attack and explain their reason for being here if they wish to gain her allegiance.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Opening the Energy Doors
>
> The energy door that leads to the primary bedroom's foyer is closed and locked, and can be opened via the following methods in addition to those described in [[Vortest Tower]]:
>
> - An exterior control panel is located on the rightmost column next to the door, which features a luminous touch pad in the shape of a five-fingered humanoid hand. Any character who is an ally to Corvana Vortest, including the party members, can gain entry by placing their hand upon the touch pad and speaking the House Vortest creed as featured in the [[Tower Antechamber]]: "Enlightenment and Beyond."

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Suspended Animation
>
> The 3 [[Jahud Assassin]] here and [[Corvana Vortest]] are all caught in a state of suspended animation caused by the magic of a temporal trap. The trap has already been triggered and cannot be reset by the characters. All 3 jahud are positioned around Corvana in the bedroom proper.
>
> This state of suspended animation ends as soon as the doors to the chamber are opened, releasing Corvana and the jahud — who all emerge from the temporal suspension relatively ignorant of the reason for party's arrival. The characters will need to act fast if they wish to convince Corvana of the veracity of their cause.
>
> #### Jahud Tactics
>
> At the start of combat, the temporal suspension is released, and 2 of the jahud here will focus their attacks on Corvana Vortest in an attempt to kill her and take the [[Orphic Reliquary]]. 1 of these jahud will attempt to grapple Corvana and utilize its [[Penumbral Hide]], while the other makes use of its [[Signaran Dagger]] and [[Bladed Bracer]] to carve away at her Health. Meanwhile, the 3rd jahud located here will fall back in an attempt to break line of sight and make use of its [[Natural Invisibility]] so that it can backstab the first character that tries to intervene.
>
> Over the course of combat, the jahud will prioritize the following actions and abilities:
>
> - When the party intervenes, one of the jahud targeting Corvana will break away to assist the invisible one in dealing with the intervening characters.
> - If things start going poorly, the one grappling Corvana will target one or more of the party members with its [[Plasma Ray]], focusing efforts on spellcasters or the first characters to join the others in melee.
> - If one or more characters target the jahud from afar with ranged attacks, the jahud might attack the offending enemy characters with [[Plasma Ray]] before repositioning themselves to gain cover.
>
> The jahud assassins will fight to the death in service of their interplanar mission for Kerastes, biologically programmed to continue their relentless pursuit of Corvana Vortest and the [[Orphic Reliquary]].
>
> #### Corvana's Tactics
>
> Corvana is a powerful spellcaster and does not hesitate to leverage a battery of composed spells from the Lightning and Fire runes in order to keep the assassins at bay.
>
> Over the course of combat, Corvana will prioritize the following actions and abilities:
>
> - If a Jahud manages to become invisible and she isn't **Restrained**, she will try to reveal them by leveraging **Aspect of Illumination** to cause them to glow.
> - If Corvana manages to maintain control of the Reliquary, she'll make haste to move further away from the jahud, using the party as a shield if she has to.
> - Once she gains some distance, Corvana will cast **Repellent Aspect of Control** on the nearest jahud, suggesting that it "retreat as quickly as possible."
>
> Corvana will protect the Orphic Reliquary at all costs, including her own life. Upon defeat, she can be revived by the party as if she were a Player Character.

### Meeting Corvana Vortest

After combat, the party must adequately explain the reason for their visit if they desire the Signborn mage's hard-earned trust.

> [!info] Social
> #### Conversation with Corvana
>
> Once the smoke of battle has cleared, Corvana will demand that the characters explain who they are and precisely why they are here.
>
> If the characters are forthcoming and honest with their answers (and prove to her that they don't pose any threats), Corvana will most likely oblige them with answers to any questions they might have.
>
> Topics of conversation that Corvana is gratefully willing to discuss include (but are not limited to) the following:
>
> - Who the characters are and the reason for their visit.
> - Details about the jahud assassins and their nefarious mission.
> - Information about Vortest Tower and its connection to [[Signara]].
> - How to return to terrestrial [[World of Ember]].
> - Details about her career as a mage and her mission for [[Nalgomith]].
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Deception (DC 13)** check is able to confirm the veracity of Corvana's claims, but suspects she is hiding something from them.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character is assured that Corvana's hidden agendas are more benevolent than nefarious in nature, and likely have to do with her responsibilities here at the tower or specifics of her past.
>
> Specific dialogue options for Corvana are detailed below.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who are you?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Well met, visitors. I am Corvana Vortest, and this is my home — a place usually reserved for intimates and my thoughts alone. I truly appreciate your intervention with these assassins, but I hope your visit is one with purpose.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the assassins?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Those malevolent fiends are assassins from the Inner Realm of Signara — creatures knonw as jahud — sent to slay me and plunder the tower. Fortunately, they were unsuccessful in these endeavors.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Vortest Tower & Signara?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I see you managed to make your way into the heart of the facility … Welcome to my own little pocket of Signara, friend. Vortest Tower has served as a conduit to the inner realm for generations, and I am merely the latest to steward this place and its responsibilities. Tell me: have you come in search of spells? Or portents, perhaps? What, precisely, do you desire?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Returning to Ember?
>
> **A:**
>
> > The journey back to the Arctus Plateau is a mere trifle.
>
> Corvana produces a small metallic cylinder from her pocket, adorned with crystalline buttons full of scintillating light.
>
> > The Jaunt Manipulator allows me to control the facility's accoutrements. Once you're ready to leave, I'll help you see your way back to the antechamber, where we'll all make the trip back to Ember.

If the party has come here in search of Corvana Vortest's story for [[The Book Of Tales]], additional details about the mage's tale can be found in [[The Signborn's Secret]] event.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Bedroom
>
> Characters who take a moment to search the primary bedroom will be able to discover the following:
>
> - The furniture throughout is crafted of petrified ashwood and appears posh, well-kept, and well-used.
> - The foyer area contains a small wardrobe cabinet, a modest bookshelf, and a potted Signaran plant. An [[Arcturian]] wicker basket sits near the front door, used for the impromptu storage of gloves and boots.
> - The bedroom area is lavishly comfortable and well-decorated, adorned with a variety of ashwood furniture. It's easy to tell that the room feels only half lived-in, as if Corvana spends much of her time elsewhere (such as the confines of the workshop).
> - The door to the bathroom section of the primary bedroom is unlocked. The bathroom is clean and well-kept. The facilities are made with semi-enchanted plumbing technology that conjures potable water and allows waste to be expelled to an unknown location.
>
> Any character who succeeds on an **Awareness (DC 18)** check while searching the bedroom is able to find a jewelry box hidden in the vanity that contains Corvana's most valuable personal effects and heirlooms, which include:
>
> - 3 [[Opalix]].
> - A [[House Vortest Signet Ring]].
> - A hollow [[Obsidian Pendant]] containing the ashes of a dead companion.
>
> Corvana Vortest does not take kindly to anyone who would defile the sanctity of her private chambers, and will react accordingly to sneak-thieves and cutpurses.
