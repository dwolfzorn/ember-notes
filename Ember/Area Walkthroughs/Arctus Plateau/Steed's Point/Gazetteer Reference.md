# Gazetteer Reference

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The collection of buildings left in this overgrown field are all in varying stages of destruction and decay - here, a door has been ripped off of its hinges; there, a roof has caved in. Between the half-broken ruins and skeletal frames of what appear to have once been houses and barns, hedges grow everywhere. These massive brambles weave in and around each of the buildings, sprouting vines with thick spiky thorns like teeth, as if every available piece of unrestrained wildlife was trying to swallow what once lived here whole.

While this abandoned village is currently infested by the gore birds, jurtak, and other passing predators, this desolate and dangerous place could one day prove to be a welcome area of respite, learning, and trade once again, if only the creatures that prowl its ruins could be driven off and the dwellings that form the settlement salvaged and repaired.

Steed's Point is named for its founder, Bertron Steed, an agrimage who moved from Nain to build a small village that would give him more opportunity to farm and experiment with his craft. Once a popular place for local trade and the swapping of agrimagical knowledge, the village was lost to a series of jurtak attacks. Today, the jurtak continue to fight over the ruins of Steed's Point with the seemingly endless flocks of gore birds hat famed agrimage Kali Andrella is rumored to have called to the village in a failed attempt at a final defense.

### Environment

In its heyday, Steed's Point was a valuable stop for those crossing the Arctus Plateau due to its fertile fields, productive mill, and collection of oddities made by tinkerer and village founder Bertron Steed. Now, it lies in ruins. Most of its structures have been ruined either by Jurtak attacks or the ongoing battles between the Jurtak and Gore Birds in the area, and overgrown by hedges, vines, and thorns that are believed to be a reflection of the strong agrimagical resonance in the area.

### Ecology

Steed's Point is dominated by three things: brambled hedges and vines that grow in, around, and over its central buildings; the jurtak who have set their sights on turning this into an aboveground shelter, and the gore birds that perch on the tops of those buildings that are not yet covered in thorns and vines, attacking the jurtak in large flocks when possible. Nothing else has been able to thrive or grow here since Steed's Point's fall.

### Lore

After its founding by Bertron Steed, Steed's Point quickly became a small trading hub for other small settlements in the area looking for innovative items ranging from charmed seeds to enchanted equipment, drawn in by Bertron's piece de resistance, an impressive construct made of woven straw accented with metal parts, known as Rattletrap.

As knowledge of Steed's Point spread, it also became a place for agrimages outside of Redrak Fields' more formal Agrimage Consortium to learn and trade info, attracting those who liked to try new techniques and meld disciplines. It is rumored that Moss Ritherin got the idea for seed settlements after spending time in Steed's Point, and famed traveling agrimage Kali Andrella chose it as her place to settle down, becoming an adopted daughter of sorts to the aging Bertron.

This all changed after a series of earthquakes stranded a pack of jurtak in the area. Unable to return to their normal underground habitat after a tunnel collapse, the creatures targeted Steed's Point as a potential new shelter and attacked the village relentlessly. Kali Andrella rallied the town to fight back and await help, but the reinforcements she hoped for never came. In the final battle, Kali attempted to fend off the jurtak by calling down a wave of Gore Birds to attack on Steed's Point's behalf, but it was to no avail - Bertron died in the siege and the remaining villagers fled, leaving the gore birds and jurtak in a war to claim the lost village for their own that has been going on for at least the past decade.
