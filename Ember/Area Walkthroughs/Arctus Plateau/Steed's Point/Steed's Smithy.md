# Steed's Smithy

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The crumbling stonework of this squat, square smithy seems to have suffered the most brutal tests of time. Light streams through its rickety, perforated roof from above, cascading across a scattered array of weather-worn blacksmith's tools and other forgotten supplies. The forge at the center of the dilapidated chamber has likely been cold since the fall of Steed's Point so long ago.

This ruined edifice is what remains of Bertron Steed's once-proud forge, where he crafted a countless array of ingenious creations — including the sentient construct known as [[Rattletrap, the Rickety Man]], the Rickety Man.

Here, the characters can discover vague details about Bertron Steed himself from the various tools and accoutrements he left behind during the village's untimely demise.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Exploring the Smithy
>
> If the characters take the time to explore the interior of the ruined smithy, they may find a few items of note:
>
> - An array of tools and trinkets scattered about the area, including a rusty [[Smith's Toolkit]] and a disorganized set of tools for woodcarving.
> - A hammer stand located in the northeast corner of the smithy. 12 ordinary hammers of various shapes and sizes are still stored here, moss-covered and forgotten. These hammers are all marked with the initials "B.S.", which are etched into the bottom of the handle on each.
> - Alongside these mundane tools, an item of particular interest rests on the hammer stand: the enchanted [[Shardsteel Hammer]], which is integral to the events of [[The Book Of Tales]] and is also marked with Bertron Steed's initials.
>
> The forge itself is perhaps the most striking feature here, despite its aging dormancy. A large, hammer-worn anvil nearby shows aged signs of ample use. And the meager coals within the forge have weathered away to little more than blackened soot.
>
> #### Analyzing the Scene
>
> Any character who succeeds on an **Awareness (DC 13)** check can accurately deduce that this place was hastily abandoned long ago (as evident by the weathering on the edifice and tools within, as much as from the lingering signs of damage from attacking Gore Birds).
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Arcana (DC 13)** check is able to recognize several esoteric accoutrements of an agrimage spellcaster among the otherwise standard array of tools, including the vaguest traces of spell components and the like.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The Shardsteel Hammer is more than meets the eye, and bears obscure runic markings that indicate a connection to agrimagic, suggesting that it can be used as a Talisman for agrimage casters.
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Crafts** or **Knowledge: Machines** can tell that the blacksmith that once worked here practiced classic blacksmithing and artisanal woodcarving in tandem, based on the way the tools are so completely intermingled and organized (among those that are organized, that is).
>
> Characters with **Knowledge: Artifacts** or **Knowledge: Rituals** can recognize the Shardsteel Hammer as a potential spellcasting focus without identifying any of its other properties.
>
> Any character who succeeds on an **Wilderness (DC 15)** while pondering the location is inspired to wonder what it was like for the excessive amount of local flora when Steed's Point was besieged.
>
> - **Knowledge**: A character with **Knowledge: Plants** gains **+2 Boons**.
> - **Earth Magic**: A character with **Talent: Rune: Earth** gains **+2 Boons**.

> [!info] Social
> #### Speak with Plants
>
> Some of the brambles and shrubs that grow nearby are old enough to have been here when Steed's Point met its untimely fate.
>
> Any character who uses Earth or Control rune magic to communicate with this elder vegetation is able to learn a few firsthand details about what transpired here long ago.
>
> From the plant's perspective, the rise of Steed's Point is likely more of a mystery than its demise, which the plant will be able to recall with precise detail (from its limited point of view).

>

> **Q:** About the Blacksmith?
>
> **A:** You sense heat, taste the strong minerals in the water that spills over your earth and filters to your roots. You feel the vibrations of footsteps as beasts move around you. You feel the cycles pass where the waters and footsteps are missing.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Other things here?
>
> **A:**
>
> You sense the withering of strange energies that was over your branches as something dark passes by in the dark hours. You feel your branches bend and shift as little things climb within, leaving rich droppings on your soil.
>
> You feel the ebb and flow of magic in the land, an old and familiar magic, a magic that was common many cycles ago, and was missing, but came back.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About Rattletrap?
>
> **A:** You feel strange kinship, but also revulsion at what was once like you, living plant, not dead, and wrought into something else. The construct uses the dead limbs of your fellows, but is something else!
