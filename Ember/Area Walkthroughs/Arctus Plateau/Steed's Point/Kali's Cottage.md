# Kali's Cottage

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Guide
>
> The encounter in the cottage depends on how the players reach it:
>
> - If players are on their first visit and come through the hedge maze, see [[Hedge Maze Entrance]] below
> - If players come via the Secret Passage in the Northeast corner of Steed's Point, see [[Secret Passage Entrance]] below.
> - If players are returning to the cottage after having previously spoken to Kali, see [[Return Visit]] below.

> [!abstract] Kali Andrella
> **[[Kali Andrella]]**
>
> Level 1 · Unknown Unknown
>
> ![Kali Andrella](../../../assets/image084.webp)

If characters came to Kali's cottage via the hedge maze, she lets them in via the side door from the maze:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Unlike the rest of Steed's Point, Kali's cottage is in perfect condition, almost as if it isn't in the center of a town under attack. The agrimage stands in the center of the room next to a full containers of blooming flowers that vaguely resemble the blooms on Steed's Point's many brambled hedges. A light rain is falling on the flowers as if out of nowhere, and as you enter, she makes a gesture, dismissing them.
>
> "Welcome to Steed's Point," she says, with a bitter laugh and a shrug of her shoulders. "I suppose I should be glad to have visitors after all this time, especially such determined ones. I only wish you could've seen Steed's Point the way it used to be."

Proceed to [[A Conversation with Kali]].

## The Secret Passage

If characters discover the [[Overgrown Trapdoor]] in the Northeast corner of Steed's Point, they may use the Secret Passage beneath to go directly to the back door of Kali's Cottage:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The door above your head feels less wooden than thatched, almost grassy, as if you are lifting a section of overgrown moss with your hands. As you push up, the darkness of the tunnel that you have been walking through is replaced by a faint light. There is no breeze here, and also no destruction.
>
> Wherever this is, it is inside and somehow protected from the violence and ruin around it. Before you can fully take in the scenery, the moss of the trap door begins to grow and reach out for you, grabbing hands and legs as it attempts to prevent you from going any further. A door to a small home swings open and a woman stands in the doorway, staring you down.
>
> > You would come ready to sneak into my house with no warning? Do not let the half-ruined buildings around here fool you. I know how to defend myself. But first, what gives you the audacity to try to sneak up to and into my home?

The party can calm Kali down with a successful `[[/check 17 persuasion]]` check. If the check fails, they have **-2 Banes** on further charisma checks with Kali. Proceed to [[A Conversation with Kali]].

## Conversation with Kali

Kali is willing, if not eager, to speak with the party - if for no other reason than she has not had visitors in some time. While she is often cagey with information, the items that characters have collected while in Steed's Point can help convince her to talk more openly.

> [!info] Social
> #### Getting Kali to Open Up
>
> As noted in [[Hedge Maze]] in the Hedge Maze, Kali's attitude toward characters who entered via the Hedge Maze varies based on their response to her question.
>
> - Fame-seekers have **-2 Banes** on charisma checks
> - Money seekers have **-2 Banes** on insight checks
> - Those who seek to protect and preserve have **+2 Boons** on charisma checks
> - Knowledge seekers have **+2 Boons** on insight checks
>
> Characters who share an item of Steed's Point with her - the [[Woven Map, Steed's Point]] or [[The Clever Fox (Engraved Sign)]] - can retry a single failed persuasion check, as she is appreciative and willing to hear them out.
>
> #### What Kali Knows
>
> Kali has been living in the cottage since the fall of Steed's Point, protecting it against the jurtak. She believes that she is the only one who has stood by Steed's Point, except maybe for Bertron Steed's construct Rattletrap. With a successful `[[/check insight 17]]` check, characters can sense some guilt around the fall of Steed's Point.
>
> With a successful `[[/check persuasion 16]]` check, characters can get Kali to share what happened when Steed's Point fell - an earthquake brought the jurtak to the village and it was overrun. All efforts to fight back failed.
>
> - If they succeed by 2 or more on the check, Kali adds that she is the one who led the charge to defend Steed's Point, even after others believed it should be left behind.
>
> Kali is attached to Steed's Point because she thought of its founder, Bertron Steed, like a father and raised her daughter Moriah there - Moriah then left to become a famed traveling hunter. With a successful `[[/check insight 17]]` check, characters can tell that there is some hurt or bitterness regarding Kali's relationship with Moriah.
>
> - With a successful `[[/check persuasion 16]]` check, characters can get Kali to admit that she and Moriah parted on bad terms.
> - If they succeed by 2 or more on the check, Kali shares that she regrets the bad parting.
> - If they succeed by 4 or more on the check, she adds that she also feels guilty, as she had no way to ask Moriah for help when Steed's Point was attacked.
>
> Kali is the reason for the infestation of gore birds - a last gasp attempt to save Steed's Point that has continued keeping the Jurtak at bay. With a successful `[[/check insight 17]]` check, characters are aware that Kali is still somewhat afraid of the jurtak because of how viciously they overran Steed's Point when it fell.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** A question?
>
> **A:**
>
> Who Kali Is:
>
> The great Kali Andrella, or at least that's what they once called me. Traveling agrimage, helping towns and cities across the Arctus Plateau thrive. Bit of a knack for getting into trouble - that's why there were so many stories about me. But I always found a way out…until the jurtak came.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why are you here?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I ask myself that now and then. My being here is what keeps at least this little corner of it safe, and I owe the place that much.
> >
> > Who else is going to do it? Rattletrap? Maybe, but he doesn't have my skills.
> >
> > There might be some amount of self-inflicted suffering in the act, too, being alone in the rotting corpse of a town you used to love, and all.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Fall of Steed's Point?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Not sure if you're from around here, but if you are, you know the earth shakes every so often.
> >
> > I was even trying to figure out why, just as a side project. Maybe If I'd focused on that, I would have known that we were all just one big shake away from a hole to the pathways opening up, and Jurtak decided to pay us a visit.
>
> With additional persuasion success:
>
> > People begged me to run, but I wanted to stay and fight. They believed me when I said we had a shot at driving them back.
> >
> > We ended up running anyway, well, the survivors did.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Kali's Relationship with Moriah?
>
> **A:**
>
> > My daughter is almost as stubborn as her mother. And not here, so not much more to tell you.
>
> With additional persuasion success:
>
> > We were never gonna see eye to eye, not with her wanting to run hither and yon and me wanting her to stay put and learn from my tales of when I was the one out wandering.
> >
> > We're a little alike in that way - wish I'd had the chance to tell her that, before she left. I don't think she's ever coming back.
>
> With full persuasion success:
>
> > I could use her help though… we could save this place.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What do you know about the Gore Birds?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I brought 'em here, at the end, when Bertron was gone and… and I didn't want to lose everything else. I don't like the things particularly, but they work in a pinch.
> >
> > Been fighting the jurtak ever since.
> >
> > Hope they didn't give you too much trouble.

Kali is willing to keep talking to the group, but doesn't know much of what is going on outside of her cottage. She does offer a better way out of the cottage than the way they came in, lowering the previously hedges that lead back toward [[Steed's Point Fields]]. Going forward, the party can use this entrance as a way to reach Kali's Cottage.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> **Note:** If the party contains Edivel, the young agrimage is eager to learn from Kali, and follows up with additional questions in [[A Dying Art]]. Any benefits that the characters carry from the conversation with Kali above (**+2 Boons**/**-2 Banes** on a roll, for example) apply to this additional conversation as well.

## On Return Visits

If characters have previously visited Kali, they may enter through the front door, which was previously covered in brambles. She also asks them how things have been going and is willing to prepare an [[Herbalism Kit]] for the group, but has no other real information.
