# Hedge Maze

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The hedges in this section of Steed's Point narrow into wall-like structures, on each side of a winding path ahead of you. As you step between them, they seem to somehow get taller, looming on each side of the slippery grass of the overgrown path beneath your feet. It does not seem as if anyone has stepped foot here in some time.

There are three chokepoints within the hedge maze, each with its own dangers:

- The first bend ([[The Dreaded Corner]])creates a sense of dread meant to prevent anyone in the maze form coming any closer.
- The second bend ([[The Flower Trap]]) restricts movement with large thorns and contains flowers that emit a poison gas.
- The final section leading up to the door ([[The Final Questioning]]) is an impassable trap that allows Kali to question anyone who has made it that far.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Slippery Grass
>
> The slippery grass within the hedge maze is difficult terrain. In addition, characters have a chance to slip each time they move forward. Whenever characters take a movement action, roll a d4 `[[/roll d4]]`. On a 1, the character slips on the grass and falls into the hedges on each side. They must succeed on a `[[/save dexterity 18]]` saving throw or they are scratched by the hedges, taking `[[/damage 1d6 piercing]]` and `[[/damage 1d6 slashing]]` damage.

## The Dreaded Corner

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Somehow the hedges seem to get even taller as you round the corner. They move as if they have a mind of their own, sprouting thorns and reaching out to do something cruel and unspeakable.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Fearful Growth
>
> The hedges at this corner are designed to create fear. Each creature who passes the corne**r** must succeed on a `[[/save wisdom 17]]` saving throw or drop whatever they are holding, become frightened, and immediately begin walking back the way they came. They can be convinced to move past the corner despite being afraid with a successful `[[/check persuasion 16]]` or `[[/check intimidation 16]]` check, or with a spell like [[Calm Emotions]].
>
> Repeated exposure to the hedges lessens their effect; each time the same character passes the corner, the DC of the wisdom save lessens by 1.

## The Flower Trap

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Thorned vines extend in front of you, creating a makeshift wall - the gaps are large enough that smaller creatures might be able to get through, but not without scraping against the thorns. As you begin looking for a way forward, flowers along the hedges bloom and begin spraying a pungent pollen directly on you.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Impenetrable Foliage
>
> While the flower wall may appear to be impenetrable, characters can figure out a way through with a successful `[[/check survival 17]]` or `[[/check perception 17]]` check. If those checks are failed or they do not attempt them, they are subject to the pollen from the flowers nearby. They must succeed on a `[[/save constitution 17]]` saving throw or take `[[/damage 1d8 poison]]` damage.

## The Final Questioning

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you step into a small clearing, vines come down around you, as if you have been imprisoned in a cage of leaf and thorn. As you survey the flowers and grass of your prison, a whispered voice comes to your ears as if from on the breeze.
>
> > Well I will give you this, you are persistent. What I don't understand is why? What has brought you to the remains of what was once Steed's Point? Why do you visit the once great agrimage Kali Andrella?

> [!info] Social
> #### A Simple Question
>
> Kali will give the characters a single opportunity to share what brought them to Steed's Point. If they try to ask her any questions, she refuses to answer until they do. With a successful `[[/check insight 17]]` check, characters know that Kali is most interested in protecting Steed's Point and will respond well to arguments about saving or preserving something important to the world.
>
> Kali responds as follows to the following broad reasons for the party's arrival:
>
> - **Fame & Glory:** Kali is not impressed by the group’s desire for fame as an end unto itself. They have **-2 Banes** **on all charisma checks** for the entirety of their next conversation with Kali.
> - **Money:** Kali is dismissive of the group, believing that they do not know what is truly important, and becomes closed off. Characters have **-2 Banes** **on all insight checks** for the entirety of their conversation with her.
> - **Knowledge/Curiosity:** Kali is bitter, believing that all her knowledge was ultimately not good enough to permanently save Steed's Point. With this revelation, characters understand Kali better and have **+2 Boons** **on all insight checks** for the entirety of their next conversation with her.
> - **Protection/Preservation:** Kali is inclined towards the party's point of view. They have **+2 Boons** **on all charisma checks with Kali** for the entirety of their next conversation with her.
>
> Regardless of how the party answers, Kali lets the party into her cottage after responding back.
