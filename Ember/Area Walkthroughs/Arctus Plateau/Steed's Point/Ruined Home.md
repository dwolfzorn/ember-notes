# Ruined Home

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Like many of the houses of Steed's Point, the roof of this home has been ripped away, leaving destroyed beams of wood on the floor, but many of the open gaps in the roof have been covered by tendrils of vine, as if the winding vines and their green and purple leaves were trying either to protect or consume what remains inside.
>
> As you enter, the vines shift, but do not react, as if waiting to see what you will do in the ruins.

The wall immediately facing the characters when they enter is covered in vines - if cut or burned, they immediately grow back even thicker - there is no way past it to the other side

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Placard
>
> With a successful **Awareness (DC 17)** check, characters spot an [[The Clever Fox (Engraved Sign)]] nestled in a pile of vines.
>
> > Among the ripped and destroyed bits of wood, a metal placard sits in a nest of vines, seemingly untouched by any of the destruction around it.
>
> With a successful **Arcana (DC 15)** check, characters know that this placard was created using a form of metallurgical enchantment that might be practiced by an Arcturian agrimage or artificer, and has been enchanted to remain unaffected by physical damage. The sign has no other magical qualities or abilities.
>
> Characters who possess the [[Woven Map, Steed's Point]] see that the fox symbol on the map matches the symbol on the placard. If characters have met Moriah Foxhaven, they know that this placard was hers and the letters are her initials. If they have not, they can determine that the letters are initials with a successful **Awareness (DC 16)** check - if the party is travelling with Edivel (as part of [[Thorny Predicaments]]), the young agrimage will remember that Kali Andrella's daughter had the last name "Foxsomething."

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Memento Box
>
> With a successful **Awareness (DC 17)** check to search the house, characters notice a floorboard the Southwest corner of the room is oddly ajar. The floorboard can be lifted using a tool like a crowbar with no check required - if pried up by hand, the character must succeed on a **Athletics (DC 18)** check to raise it.
>
> Beneath the floorboard is a small wooden box. It has a carved symbol of a fox and the initials MF on it, which matches both the metal placard found in the room and the symbol on the [[Woven Map, Steed's Point]].
>
> > The wooden box looks as if it has been carved by many different hands using different tools - the styles are similar, but there are subtle differences in the depth of a cut or the turn of a line that give the sense that this has been passed down and added to over time.
>
> The wooden box is locked shut, but can be opened with a successful **Stealth (DC 17)** check using a [[Lockpicks]]. Inside is a jeweled necklace containing a [[Messenger Coin]] and a note, written in **Language: Common** titled [[A Farewell Note]].
