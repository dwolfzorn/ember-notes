# Vine Path

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> While the path here is overgrown with vines and brambles, it nevertheless seems a reasonable way to traverse the southern expanse of the village, as a clearing awaits on the far end.

The overgrown alleyway here is fitted with a magical Entangling Vine Trap that can ensnare characters and creatures who traverse it without care. The area here is Difficult Terrain.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Path
>
> A character with **Awareness (DC 14, Passive)** notices the long-dead skeletal remains of a bipedal saurian creature, half-buried deep within the bramble. They appear to have been caught in the brambles and unable to escape, which lead to their demise.
>
> Any character who succeeds on a **Wilderness (DC 16)** check is able to identify the skeletal remains as those of an adult [[Jurtak]] — a species of predatory aberrations that usually inhabit subterranean biomes, which puts these remains somewhat out of place.
>
> - **Past Experience**: If the party have previously encountered Jurtak while exploring the [[Pathways]] or while completing [[Rocky Rescue]], they automatically succeed on this check.
> - **Knowledge**: A character with **Knowledge: Monsters** or **Knowledge: Subterranea** has **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> The corpse is clutching a [[Javelin]] made of bone tipped with a sharpened stone, and is clad in [[Heavy Hides]].
>
> Retrieving these items or getting close enough to the skeletal remains to inspect them risks triggering the Entangling Vine Trap (see below).

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Entangling Vine Trap
>
> The alley south of the Dilapidated Shed is dominated by a magical Entangling Vine Trap that was planted here by the agrimage Kali Andrella.
>
> Any character who moves through the area must make a successful **Athletics (DC 16)** check or trigger the trap. A creature doesn't need to step on the ground to trigger the trap; the brambles and vines can detect intruders up to a height of 5 feet.
>
> Any creature in the area when the trap is triggered suffers **Barbed Thorns (Hazard 3, Reflex, Health, Piercing)** and are **Restrained** until the trap resets. A Restrained creature can take an action to make a **Athletics (DC 15)** check to free frees itself from the entangling vines and is no longer Restrained by them
>
> The trap resets each morning at dawn. The [[Gore Bird Ravage]] infesting neighboring areas make short work of any living creature who gets caught here for very long.

Once one or more characters are caught in the vine trap the nearby Jurtak will take this opportunity to launch an attack.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Jurtak Attack!
>
> After the trap is triggered, the [[Jurtak Warrior]] and [[Jurtak Hunter]] in the [[Southern Clearing]] will sneak up to attack the party.
>
> Additionally, if the 2 Jurtak Warriors in the [[Dilapidated Shed]] have avoided detection, they will attempt to sneak in behind the characters to flank them.
>
> #### Jurtak Tactics
>
> The Jurtak Warriors utilize their [[Acid Spit]] ability before closing in on the party to fight in melee with their [[Glaive]]. If for some reason they cannot reach a target, they will engage from a safe distance by hurling their [[Javelin]].
>
> Meanwhile, the Jurtak Hunter will take aim with its [[Longbow]], targeting prioritizing targets that are caught in the Vine Path trap or who are already engaged in melee combat with one or more Jurtak Warriors.
>
> All Jurtak start combat with their first weapon attack coated in [[Jurtak Poison]]. They reapply it before attacking if they can spare the action points to do so, and aren't being engaged by combatants in close range.
>
> If **Broken** or **Weakened** the Jurtak will break and flee.
