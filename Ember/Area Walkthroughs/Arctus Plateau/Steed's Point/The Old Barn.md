# The Old Barn

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Past a trampled set of hedges sits the remains of an old barn. A large portion of its roof is gone, as if the tiles and wood had been relentlessly torn off of it piece by piece, leaving sections of splintered wood that are scattered on one side of the ground.

The barn is rigged with a Briar Hedge Trap detailed below, and though it can be detected, it is likely the party will haplessly trigger it as they enter the barn unless they are, for some reason, moving cautiously. If characters trigger the trap, read or paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you step inside, the flattened vines in the entrance behind you begin to rustle, lifting from the ground in a tangle of leaf and vine, becoming thick and impassable. Ahead, what was once an exit is similarly blocked by a hedge that has grown as if from nowhere, thick and thorny and as high as your head.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster Note
>
> The trampled vines can be triggered by interacting (using the ​ **Interaction Tool**) with the ​ control icon. Note that the icon is not initially visible, but by pressing the ALT key you can display it. Consult [[Area Maps]] section of the Player's Guide for further guidance.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Detecting the Trap
>
> Characters can detect the hedge trap with a successful **Awareness (DC 18, Passive)**, **Wilderness (DC 16, Passive)** check, or if they have the foresight to use **Earthen Sense** before crossing the vines, to reveal that these vines are definitely not native and seem out of place compared to the surrounding plantlife, those with Earthen Sense can identify that the vines resonate with signs of magical manipulation associated with the Earth Rune. Otherwise, these trampled vines are indistinguishable from any other vines and roots that might be present.
>
> #### Trap Effects
>
> The trap activates at both exits from the barn as soon as characters cross into the barn from either direction. An impassible hedge of briars covers all movable space in its path. The hedge prevents forward movement, but does no damage unless it is touched or attacked.
>
> If touched, its thorns rapidly extend. Any character within 5 feet of the hedge faces **Piercing Thorns (Hazard 6, Reflex, Health, Piercing)**.
>
> #### Examining the Trap
>
> Once the trap is activated, it can be easily identified as a Briar Hedge Trap by any character that makes a successful **Arcana (DC 17)** check. This trap can be deactivated by locating and pushing on a branch within the hedge that appears out of place.
>
> - **Relevant Knowledge**: A character with **Knowledge: Plants** or **Talent: Rune: Earth** automatically succeeds.
>
> Any character that succeeds on an **Wilderness (DC 17)** check while examining the thorny barrier notices that it has a strange internal structure with several branches that seem different from those around them.
>
> - **Prior Success**: A character that successfully identifies this trap (as above) automatically succeeds.
> - **Relevant Knowledge**: A character with **Knowledge: Plants** gains **+2 Boons**.
>
> #### Disabling the Trap
>
> The briar hedge trap can be deactivated by carefully pushing against the branch that deactivates the trap with a successful **Athletics (DC 18)** check. On a failure, the character triggers the hedge's physical attack (see Trap Effects above).
>
> While the hedge repels physical attacks, it can be destroyed with fire. Any use of **Talent: Rune: Flame**, such as **Arrow of Flame** or **Touch of Flame** will destroy a single hedge. The magical hedge can also be counterspelled with **Counterspell (Earth, Influence, Negate, DC 15)**.
>
> #### Self-Setting Traps
>
> Disarming the hedge trap is only temporary, and after 10 minutes the hedge reactivates (if the party is still within the barn) or resets (ready to retrigger if crossed over again). If a hedge is magically destroyed, it slowly grows back over the course of 24 hours.

As soon as the party is trapped inside by the hedges, a pair of opportunistic gore birds attack.

> [!abstract] Gore Bird Ravage
> **[[Gore Bird Ravage]]**
>
> Level 4 · Gore Bird Swarm
>
> ![Gore Bird Ravage](../../../assets/image080.webp)
>
> A large swarm of tiny, keen-eyed birds circles above, their charcoal-colored feathers a stark contrast to their sharp red beaks. It is nearly impossible to see how many of them there are, but as they grow closer, making an unsettling cawing noise and rustling their crimson tailfeathers, you can smell the rot of the putrid flesh they so often feast on.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gore Birds
>
> Gore Bird often attack in waves, using their [[Dying Call]] to alert other gore birds nearby to join the fight if they are close to death.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Searching the Barn
>
> With a successful **Awareness (DC 16)** to search the barn after the Gore Birds are dispatched, parties find a bag of copper and silver coins  **2 4** buried beneath a haystack.
