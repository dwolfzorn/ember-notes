# The Group of Wagons

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> In the center of a group of wagons, two women are working to bring packages down — one is slender, short, and tan with close-cropped hair and singe marks on her Arcturian clothing, while the other is broad-shouldered and pale, with shoulder-length hair that has been awkwardly cut and a bandage around her waist. A young Hulg'run boy sits nearby, tossing a stone from one gray, chiseled hand to the other at a rapid, almost frantic pace.

The two women are **Tay** (Neutral Good, Arcturian Human, she/her) — the woman in bandages — and **Laria** (Neutral Good, Arcturian Human, she/her), who is **Exhausted**. Tay does all the talking while her companion breathes heavily and intermittently lapses into a coughing fit. The young Hulg'run boy is **Vilby** (Lawful Good, Arcturian Hulg'run, he/him).

When the characters approach the group, read or paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The woman in bandages looks you up and down as you approach.
>
> > You look like the capable kind — feel like giving us a hand over here? I'm Tay, and that one's Laria. You could help us move these boxes, or try and get Vilby, the boy, to talk. And if you can do something for Laria's cough, that would be wonderful.

The two women will allow the characters to stay and engage in conversation if they agree to help.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Unloading the Boxes
>
> The characters can unload the boxes over the course of 30 minutes.
>
> #### Laria's Cough
>
> Any character who studies Laria's symptoms and makes a successful **Medicine (DC 14)** check can accurately diagnose her . It can be cured by targeting her with an appropriate spell. So long as Laria remains ill, she poses a risk to the other refugees at Oldcraft Lodge.
>
> - **Knowledge: Forensics**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> If the characters cure Laria, she thanks them profusely and rewards them  **1**.

If the characters try to talk to Vilby, they find him unresponsive, focused on tossing the stone back and forth between his hands.

> [!info] Social
> #### The Despondent Boy
>
> Any character who observes Vilby and makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 12)** check can tell that the boy is deeply worried about something.
>
> If pressed further, Vilby doesn't respond; he stops tossing his stone and fixes a hard glare on the characters.
>
> Laria chimes in:
>
> > He hasn't said a word since our village burned down. One of Vilby's best friends went in a separate direction with their family, toward Corpin Sanctuary. I hear they're turning folks away there, so we're hoping the family will head this way next. Vilby's worried about who they'll meet on the road — isn't that right, kid? We figure that's why he's got that stone, to protect us if he needs to.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Performance (DC 14)** or **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check can cheer up Vilby with a rousing story of heroics or a simple reassurance that he'll see his friend again. The boy smiles faintly, then hands the characters his stone.
>
> #### The Group's Journey
>
> Once the boxes are unloaded, Tay thanks the characters for their help and rewards them  **1**. She then opens up about their group's journey, which took them to Arcturel not too long ago:
>
> > After our village burned at the hands of those horrible bandits, we thought to settle in Arcturel. Mining work is steady, and we don't mind the labor. Plus, a town built in a hole in the ground seems safer in comparison to life aboveground these days.
>
> She trails off momentarily, as if remembering something unpleasant.
>
> > But even Arcturel isn't safe these days. The elevators were breaking down, people were getting sick — from what we heard, it was House Cevher's fault. Anyway, we needed to keep Vilby safe, so we headed this way.

Tay doesn't have any more details about Arcturel's troubles, but will be interested to learn of any developments from the [[Unwelcome Diversions]] Event.
