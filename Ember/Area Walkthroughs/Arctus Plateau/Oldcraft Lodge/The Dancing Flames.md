# The Dancing Flames

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> From a distance, the campfire twists and turns as if a large group of people were leaping and twirling together. Two travelers are warming themselves by the glow. One is dressed in a bright multicolored patchwork coat, while the other wears plain gray leather that highlights the fiery streaks beneath her skin.

The two travelers are **Gilara** (Neutral Good, Arcturian Fej, she/her) — dressed in gray — and **Kilson** (Neutral Good, Arcturian Human, he/him), dressed in the patchwork coat.

When the character approach the group, read or paraphrase the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you approach the pair of travelers, the woman in gray nods at you, then mutters under her breath. Her companion sighs.
>
> > Don't mind Gilara. She's just convinced that our troupe of performers being set upon by bandits is somehow all my fault. My name is Kilson.
>
> He gives a deep bow.
>
> > How may we be of assistance?
>
> Gilara gives a derisive snort and mutters something else beneath her breath.

The characters can speak with Kilson and Gilara about what happened.

> [!info] Social
> #### Gilara's Mutterings
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 16, Passive)** can make out two words that Gilara keeps muttering: "for" and "fortunes."
>
> #### Kilson's Story
>
> Kilson will speak candidly about the following:
>
> - The pair were part of a traveling group of performers attacked by bandits while traveling to Helkas from the south.
> - The bandits seemed especially interested in an alchemical flame repellent Gilara uses in her “walk through fire” act. If the characters took a sample of the alchemical substance used by the bandits, they can confirm that it is the same substance.
> - Kilson bragged about the substance a few days before the attack to a merchant in a long cloak. Gilara did not trust the merchant, who claimed to be working for someone called "the Captain" and was heading toward Arcturel.
>
> > Gilara and I are all that is left of Mischief and Mirth. I was Mischief, obviously, and Gilara was Mirth, though you'd never know it to look at her. Comedy, color, a few cantrips — things were going great until the bandits attacked us. Which wasn't my fault!
>
> Any character who listens to Kilson's story and makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check can tell that he does, in fact, feel responsible.
>
> #### Gilara's Story
>
> Gilara is unwilling to engage in lengthy conversation, but can be convinced to share her side of the story with a successful **Intimidation (DC 15)** or **Diplomacy (DC 15)** check.
>
> - She remembers that the phrase "For Other Fortunes" was used by both the suspicious merchant who inquired about the flame repellent gel and the bandits who stole it from them a few days later.
> - Gilara knew that the merchant was not to be trusted and wishes Kilson had listened to her. He gave her a bad feeling and she was glad to hear that he was headed in a different direction, toward Arcturel.
>
> Any character who listens to Gilaras story and makes a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check can tell that her anger toward Kilson is masking her own guilt.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Kilson's Guilt/Kilson's Mistake
>
> **A:** I'm all about making people happy, so when the merchant showed up, I wanted him to know what we had to offer. I may have bragged a little bit about the flame-repelling gel that Gilara developed to help with her "walk through fire" act, but I thought the man was trustworthy. I should have listened to Gilara and kept my words about her supplies to myself.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** The Strange Merchant
>
> **A:** Gilara never liked him, and he did ask a lot of questions. All I really remember is that he wore a long, black cloak and said he was working for someone called the "Bonny Captain." I wouldn't have thought of it again except for two things: first, for someone claiming to be a merchant, he didn't buy anything, and second, everything he asked about was taken by the bandits a few days later. By that time, he was probably halfway to Arcturel!
