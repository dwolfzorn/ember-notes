# Sigil's Living Room

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Although the exterior of Oldcraft Lodge is relatively unassuming, as soon as you step inside the main room, you are immediately dazzled by a chaotic cacophony of sights and sounds. Bright yellow and pink motes of magic dance in the air, casting an enchanting glow around the space. Mystical runes are casually scattered in piles across tables and shelves. The room is filled with books of all sizes, colors, and ages; some sit half-open, while others are hardly more than loosely bound piles. Tall bookshelves line the walls, packed with more books and scrolls, and colorful rugs adorn the polished hardwood floor.
>
> Standing near a large, comfortable-looking chair is a figure who appears to radiate magic in gentle waves. After only a few moments, the effects of his magical aura subside, and he turns toward you with a puzzled expression on his face.

### A God of Books

The man standing in the room is the Shard God Sigil. He is a friendly yet confusing being, and conversations can be difficult to understand as he attempts to follow random trains of thought. If the party includes a Cleric of Sigil or another character who follows Sigil in some way, they recognize him immediately.

> [!abstract] Sigil
> **[[Sigil]]**
>
> Level 18 (Boss) · Human Shard God
>
> ![Sigil](../../../assets/image092.webp)
>
> The old man is small, dwarfed by his large cloak, a long winding beard that gains a deep gleam of magic where it curls around his waist, and the sheer number of papers around him. And yet he is focused, moving his eyes and hands seamlessly from one to the next, as if each open scroll and loose sheet of vellum is in precisely the place he expected and needed it to be. He looks at you much the same way, glancing up as he flips through a book in his hands and makes a precise note in the margins, as if assessing you to determine where you belong.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The figure tilts his head slightly, looking at you for a brief moment before his eyes dart down toward your equipment, and he offers a warm smile before instantly changing to a frown. He begins speaking in a warm, melodic voice that reminds you of a wizened grandfather.
>
> > Stop, please. You are new.
> >
> > Don't step on the yellow runes. The resonance is ... brittle today, and you are heavy.
> >
> > No, no, three and a half — the displacement is negligible, but the boots are muddy.
> >
> > Why are you vibrating? Oh, it’s your heartbeat. Far too fast. Slow it down, please.
> >
> > There is food in the kitchen to your left.
>
> Without any other explanation, he returns to the tome he was reading on the stand next to him, and small golden runes begin to move around it in odd, mesmerizing patterns.

> [!info] Social
> #### Speaking with Sigil
>
> Sigil views the world very differently from others, seeing it as a grand machine in which each detail is observable, capable of being recorded, understood, and analyzed. He is often hyper-focused on a topic, at least until he gets distracted, which happens several times within a minute. He is also highly sensitive to sensory inputs, and slight changes in his surroundings are distressing and unwelcome. He doesn't look at faces; he examines belts, boots, or the magical aura around a character's head. When stressed, he speaks in highly informative yet wildly confusing bursts or in incredibly short, clipped sentences.
>
> If any character makes a successful **Arcana (DC 13)** check, they immediately see that Sigil is tightly controlling his magical aura, but it is immense. The character sees a glowing aura surround him for a few moments as it flickers and waves around him like a storm. His aura is neither damaging nor blinding, and it feels warm without affecting the overall temperature.
>
> The party can ask Sigil any number of questions; some of the most likely are listed below in the Q&A sections. They can continue this conversation for as long as they wish, during which time he provides some additional information:
>
> - He is not all that fond of leaving the safety of his lodge, where he has many powerful wards and spells laid out meticulously to protect him from any dangers, although he does sometimes visit his good friend [[Steelsong]].
> - He loves books and research above all other topics, and he can absorb boundless amounts of information. The issue is making sense of the smallest details, and he will candidly admit that he loses track of all kinds of things quite often.
> - He doesn't like to be touched, and if anyone gets too close to him, he might physically flinch or create a small magical hand to gently push them back to a "correct" standing distance.
>
> #### Clerics or Followers of Sigil
>
> If anyone in the party is a follower or even a cleric of Sigil, he stops and blinks rapidly after the initial greeting. His tone becomes even more relaxed, and he smiles broadly.
>
> > Oh! Oh, it's you! I recognize those arms! Your aura is ... noisier than I remember. More "adventurous." It’s messy. But your presence is nonetheless … calming. I hope you are doing … well? Yes, yes.
>
> Sigil will then attempt to ask his follower about their scholarly studies and what new information they have to share. If a party member launches into detail, Sigil will become almost giddy, and a fresh book will appear out of the air, and he begins scribbling constantly. He interrupts often, asking for more details about small, insignificant elements such as what color the sky was during the battle, or how the weapon smelled.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> **Note:** While Sigil is a Shard God and wields a considerable amount of magical power, he is not someone who can solve the party's problems with a wave of his hand. He avoids direct intervention both because he doesn't want to get involved and because he is actually quite cowardly. He is happy to answer questions, but his answers often need some deciphering.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Are you a god?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Yes, yes, a Shard God to be precise, or at least that's what you all call us. I prefer Ascendant God, as it's more accurate. Oh, and godhood is a misleading metric of power, before you ask. A storm is powerful, but it cannot thread a needle. Incidentally, you will likely be pleased to know that you are asking an interesting question.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Don't you have a magical library?
>
> **A:**
>
> > No.
>
> There is a long pause as he continues reading his book before twitching slightly, as if realizing he was being too blunt.
>
> > Ah! I mean, yes, but it is currently closed because I have some delicate instruments inside that I don't want disturbed. The gate there would lead you in. Please come back again later; the door will be open, I'm sure.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why are you helping these refugees?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Because the alternative is distressing … I may not be able to help with battles, fighting, and the like. I find that kind of thing quite frightening, but allowing anyone to be extinguished by bandits or hunger is ... wrong. If I have the capacity to help the people of the Plateau during this crisis, I will do so. Besides, the children brought me a flower yesterday. It had exactly five petals. Perfect symmetry.
