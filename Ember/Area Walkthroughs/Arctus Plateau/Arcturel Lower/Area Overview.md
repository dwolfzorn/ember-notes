# Area Overview

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Area Map Context
>
> The [[Arcturel Lower]] Area Map depicts a small section of [[Arcturel]]. It is featured in the [[Unwelcome Diversions]] Event of the [[An Old Friend]] Main Quest, the [[The Crystal Quarryman]] Event of [[The Book Of Tales]] Side Quest, and the [[Scene of the Crime]] Event of the [[Glitter in the Dark]] Side Quest.
>
> - The map depicts a single Level, and its areas are organized into two sections: The Dives and the Level 3 Mine.
> - For the [[Unwelcome Diversions]] and [[The Crystal Quarryman]] Events, the party begins inside the elevator, across from the [[Waterborne Distillery]].
> - For the [[Scene of the Crime]] Event, the party begins inside the lift, across from [[Wrestful Repairs]].

## Gameplay Details

The areas of Arcturel Lower have the following features unless specified otherwise in the text.

### Levels & Elevation

The Arcturel Lower Scene features a single Level, and its areas are organized into two sections: The Dives and the Level 3 Mine.

- **The Dives:** The Dives is set to an elevation of 0 and has 10-foot-high ceilings.
- **Level 3 Mine:** The Level 3 Mine is set to an elevation of 0 and has 20-foot-high ceilings.

### Illumination

At this level of the sinkhole, traditional fires do not stay lit without help — all unprotected candles are quickly extinguished. Instead, locals use the glowing mushrooms on the sinkhole walls, [[Chasm Candle]], and the occasional Inkaro pearl for lighting.

- **The Dives:** Lighting is dim but steady.
- **Level 3 Mine:** Lighting is bright, coming primarily from Inkaro pearls, which are hung like lanterns throughout the mine. The Inkaro pearls can be temporarily affected by the actions of the Device in the center of the mine (see [[Area Overview]] below).

### The Device

The Device is a large metallic machine located in [[The Hub]] at the center of the mine. Built by the [[Aburyx]] Kilner, the Tyraphem who now inhabits the mine, it is being actively tended to and defended by a pack of [[Skither]]. If fully activated, it would eventually collapse part of the tunnel system in Lower Arcturel, creating a large disaster that could be blamed on House Cevher.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Mine Effects
>
> When the characters enter certain areas of the mine while the Device is **Powered**, it creates a [[Lower Arcturel Mine Effects]]. Once all results of this table have been drawn, reset the table.
>
> #### Depowering the Device
>
> The Device can be **Depowered** using the hidden switch in [[Secondary Storage]]. To see this **Interactable Object**, you may hold down the Left Alt Key.
>
> #### Destroying the Device
>
> The Device has AC 20, 30 Hit Points, immunity to Poison, Psychic, and Radiant damage, and a &reference[damage threshold] of 10.

### Vorg Infestation

The abandoned mine tunnels have been overrun by [[Vorg]], who lurk beneath the earth to ambush creatures walking overhead.

> [!abstract] Vorg
> **[[Vorg]]**
>
> Level 4 · Vorg Burrower
>
> ![Vorg](../../../assets/image075.webp)
>
> Seemingly endless segments of metal speed across the ground, dragged by the clicking and clacking of countless legs on each side and multiple pairs of surprisingly dexterous pincers. Within this metallic body, a orange-yellow glow is visible from the creature's pointed spine and through what passes for eyes, burning with a heat that emanates from its body into the nearby air.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Vorg Ambush
>
> Whenever a character passes within 10 feet of a Vorg, it emerges to attack.
>
> #### Vorg Nests
>
> Certain areas of the mine are marked as a "Vorg Nest." Whenever the characters enter or make a lot of noise in an area marked as a "Vorg Nest," roll `[[/gmroll 1d20]]`. On a result of 10 or higher, all Vorg on the Area Map move as far as possible toward the characters. Then, if one or more Vorg are within 10 feet of a character, they emerge from the earth and attack.
>
> #### Vorg Tactics
>
> At the start of combat, the Vorg will burrow into a position that maximizes their [[Burrower]] action.
>
> Over the course of combat, the Vorg will prioritize the following actions and abilities:
>
> - In melee, the Vorg will use their [[Raking Talons]] action.
> - If surrounded, a Vorg will attempt to climb to safety, then use its [[Clinging Grip]] on the following turn.
>
> The battle ends when the Vorg are slain or are **Weakened** or **Broken**, at which point they burrow into the earth and flee. If a Vorg cannot escape, it positions itself to affect as many enemies as possible with its [[Radiant Death Burst]] feature.
