# Area Overview

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Area Map Context
>
> The [[The Bleak Archive]] Area Map for depicts the entirety of the Location as discovered in [[Where Evil Lurks]] during [[A Brush With Death]].
>
> - The Bleak Archive provides the party an opportunity to free [[Skybrush]] from the eldritch affliction of [[The Bewilderment]] by putting [[Tethra Shùl]] to rest.
> - To put Tethra Shùl to rest, the party must open the [[Unknown]] by solving the puzzle of facility's revolving mirrors, then destroy or consecrate Tethra Shùl's remains within — the process for which is described in [[Where Evil Lurks]].
> - The party arrives near the [[Unknown]], which encompasses the main entry to the Bleak Archive via its large stone doors leading to the [[Unknown]]. Thorough investigation by the party may also reveal the nearby [[Unknown]], an alternate entrance which leads to the [[Unknown]].

## Gameplay Details

The Bleak Archive not only serves as a containment facility for ancient Abyssal relics, it acts as the ersatz tomb of the Varùn wraith known as Tethra Shùl.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Repopulating Minions
>
> [[Tethra Shùl]] can summon a [[Corrupted Cadrithor]] once per day at midnight to replace any that may have been slain, up to a maximum of 6 Corrupted Cadrithors.

### Levels & Elevation

The Bleak Archive features a single Level, whose rooms organized into four sections: Entry, East Wing, West Wing, and Vault.

The ceilings within the Bleak Archive are 30 feet high, unless otherwise stated.

In some cases, a pit descends into darkness. These depths are 40 feet deep. Any creature that falls from the main floor or a metallic platform to the pit floor suffers a **Serious Fall (Hazard 12, Fortitude, Health, Bludgeoning)**. Climbing the pit walls requires a successful **Athletics (DC 16)** check.

### Illumination

Light and dark play important roles in the Bleak Archive. Beams of arcane light are responsible for triggering the various arcane doors via the facility's revolving mirrors, and the Corrupted Cadrithor minions of Tethra Shùl navigate the vault's mundane and magical shadows with supernatural ease.

Keep in mind the following important light sources:

- The primary sources of illumination in the Bleak Archive are the crystal dodecahedrons set within wall-mounted stone sconces throughout the vault. These pale blue crystals shed bright light in a 15 foot radius and dim light in a 30 foot radius.
- The beams of arcane light shed dim light in a 5 foot radius.

### Terrain

**Flagstone Floors**

The flagstone floors of the Bleak Archive are crafted from magically-treated slate, and are resistant to all damage types other than the most powerful of magic. The ground here should be treated as normal terrain unless otherwise noted.

**Interior Walls**

The interior walls of the vault are crafted from similar stone as the flagstone floors.

**Grated Metallic Platform Floors**

The grated metallic platform floors located within the [[Unknown]] and the [[Unknown]] are crafted from an unknown adamantine alloy.

### Mirrors & Walls

A mirror array puzzle located throughout the Bleak Archive is responsible for reflecting two narrow beams of arcane light from the [[Unknown]] towards the [[Unknown]]. Keep in mind:

- The beams of light originate from the two crystals mounted on the statue in the Main Gallery.
- These beams can be redirected through a series of various passages to eventually reach the [[Unknown]], where the two beams in tandem serve as a magical key to the True Vault.
- Each mirror stands 8 feet tall, and can be rotated 360 degrees on a y-axis.
- The beams of arcane light originate from powerful [[Shent]] lunar magic and create dim light in a 5 foot line.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Action Economy
>
> Characters may rotate a mirror once (one single 60-degree turn) as a costless Action on their turn. Otherwise they may rotate a single mirror an unlimited number of times by performing an Action as if using an item.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Light Banishes Evil
>
> Whenever a [[Corrupted Cadrithor]] is struck by one of the beams, it is instantly destroyed — leaving naught but a wisp of tenebrous smoke in its wake.
>
> Any creatures with a sensitivity to sunlight or with a weakness to Radiant damage that are struck by the beam suffer **Searing Light (Hazard 11, Fortitude, Health, Radiant)**.
