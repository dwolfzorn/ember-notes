# Antechamber

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This hexagonal antechamber is illuminated by the pale, blue glow of three crystal lanterns that seem to cast a perpetual arcane light. The skeletal remains of five large humanoids lie upon the floor here, covered in countless layers of dust. Clad in tarnished armor and rotted vestments, these ancient warriors seem to have suffered an untimely and mysterious fate. The room is otherwise decorated with a scattered array of urns and canopic jars that line the cobwebbed walls.
>
> A set of double doors to the north is etched with curious imagery: a geometric palimpsest of runic markings and archaic text surround the figure of some wizened sage, who holds aloft a staff crowned in six crystals that plainly resemble Ember's moons.

In this area, the characters may locate clues about a previous group of visitors that came to the Bleak Archive long ago — a raiding party led by [[Tethra Shùl]] herself. These raiders were ultimately betrayed and killed by the [[Varún]] warlock.

## The Doomed Giants

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You perceive five distinct skeletons of large sized humanoids scattered about the Antechamber. Each of the skeletal corpses is adorned with tattered tunics, and they seem to possess the telltale equipment of warriors — now moldering with verdigris and covered in countless layers of dust.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Remains
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 10, Passive)** notices that the skeletons look out of place here, as if they were visitors to the Bleak Archive themselves. The style of their rotten garments and the make of their archaic weaponry is unlike the esoteric architecture of the vault's Antechamber.
>
> A character who succeeds on a further **Society (DC 13)** check identifies the tattered rags adorning the skeletal corpses as ancient Varùn in origin.
>
> Meanwhile, any character who makes a successful **Medicine (DC 13)** check determines the various causes of death for the Varùn warriors:
>
> - Two of the Varùn warriors seem to have simultaneously stabbed each other with their swords, which still protrude from their respective ribcages. These swords resemble larger versions of the [[Unknown]] wielded by [[Dereth Erekos]] in [[The Abandoned Lute]].
> - Two of the Varùn warriors appear to have simultaneously shot each other with their shortbows. A half-dozen oversized arrows are scattered about each of these skeletons, with one or two protruding from skull and bone. One of the arrowheads is missing: the cursed [[Rune-Marked Arrowhead]].
> - One of the Varùn warriors, located near the northern doors, appears to have been burned to death by some powerful incineration. The bones still bear signs of ancient scorching. Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 15)** check is able to identify the incinerated skeleton as the likely target of a potent Flame rune Spell.

> [!info] Social
> #### Speaking with the Dead
>
> If a character uses [[Unknown]] to communicate with the Varùn skeletons, they can learn the following information:
>
> - They came to this place as aides to the warlock Tethra Shùl.
> - Tethra Shùl betrayed them using strange eldritch magic that turned them against each other.
> - Tethra Shùl was granted new forms of eldritch power by some unknown or otherworldly being.
> - There is a dangerous magical trap on [[The Bleak Archive]], and it incinerated one of the Varùn warriors in a horrible death.
> - Various recollections about the pre-Shattering era of Ember during the [[Age of the Tower]], as well as about [[The Shattering]] itself.
>
> Additional details about the Tethra Shùl's Varùn raiding party can be found in the "Lore" section of the [[Unknown]].

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Ashes of the Ancient Shent
>
> The urns and vases in this room contain the ashes of Shent heroes from long ago, including the dusty remains of sages and spellswords who battled [[The Abyss]] in the days before the Shattering.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 14)** check while examining the urns recognizes the inscriptions and etchings on the vessels to be honorific in nature.

## The Palimpsest Doors

The two stone doors to the north are covered in layers upon layers of runic etchings. This palimpsest of carvings displays the image of a wizened Shent sage bearing an arcane staff in one hand and a blank tome in the other. The staff, held aloft at the seam between the monolithic doors, is crowned with an inverted Holy Symbol of [[Sockets]].

The doors are locked and trapped by powerful magic. When Tethra Shùl came to this place long ago, she sacrificed the five members of her raiding party to gain entrance unscathed. The party must solve the door's riddle and contend with its enchantments if they wish to do the same.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Deciphering the Doors
>
> Characters who can understand **Language: Pathward**, **Language: Eonic**, **Language: Harmos**, or **Language: Caligon** are able to discern the following phrases within the overlapping etchings of the palimpsest in the characters of their respective languages:
>
> > THE WAY IS BARRED FOR EVIL HEARTS
>
> - A hint regarding the arcane trap affixed to the doors. See [[The Bleak Archive]] below.
>
> > SCRIBE YOUR NAME WITH THE DUST OF DEVOTION
>
> - This clue points to the Shent ashes contained in the urns scattered about the room — the method for disarming the Fiery Doorway Trap.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 13)** check notices the enchanted nature of the trapped door. It's also clear that the various phrases etched upon the door (whether they understand them or not) are related to a command or key.
>
> Additionally, any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 15)** check recognizes the sage's pre-Shattering garb and iconography as Shent in origin.
>
> Finally, a successful further **Society (DC 16)** check identifies the holy symbol of the Elder God [[Sockets]] at the head of the Sage's staff, as well as the dangerous connotation of its inversion — an omen to heretics who might challenge the so-called Keeper of Souls.
>
> Any character who spends at least 10 minutes studying [[Dereth's Journal]] is able to find handwritten notes that feverishly describe the method of opening the door, a vision that came to the bard in a dream.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Fiery Doorway Trap
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 15, Passive)** notices that the doors here are magically trapped, detecting the faint glimmer of dangerous arcane luminance deep within the beveled grooves of the etchings.
>
> The trap can be disabled for 24 hours if someone conducts a successful **Counterspell (Flame, Cone, React, DC 18)**. A character who uses [[Recognize Spellcraft]] receives +2 Boons to attempt this.
>
> Any character who attempts to open the doors without disabling the trap activates a @Spell[[flame.cone] Spell which emanates in a 15-foot Cone from the etching of the sage's staff located near the center of the two doors.
