# Mist-Veiled Approach

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> After navigating the steep hillside precipice, you come to a rocky promontory that overlooks a craggy, mist-veiled crevasse below. Two large doors — decorated with runic markings and crafted from a curious flaxen alloy — are cut into the hillside here, flanked by two statues of canine creatures bearing a regal, almost celestial presence. Above each statue, a blue crystal lantern shaped like a dodecahedron shines a pale light. And all around you, lambent cerulean Moon Blossoms decorate the ground, despite the presence of fertile soil. There is an ominous air to this bleak and lonely place.

## Archive Exterior

In the side of this mountain stand the ominous gates of Ebbok Zhùr, the Bleak Archive.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The stony ground here is swathed in a low-hanging blanket of mist, along with a scattered array of lambent cerulean Moon Blossoms — which seem to grow brighter the closer they get to the entrance.

Characters who look more closely at the doors leading to the Archive are able to notice a few details: runic etchings traced all along the door's surface, and a bit of esoteric graffiti carved in a wicked-looking language. The doors have no handles, and — other than the esoteric graffiti — are totally unmarred, as if the ravages time and weather have passed them by.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Surveying the Vault Exterior
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 13)** check recognizes the esoteric holy symbol of the Elder God [[Sockets]] hidden within the etchings on the doors.
>
> Meanwhile, a character who succeeds on a separate **Society (DC 16)**] check recognizes the architecture here as the expert work of the bygone [[Shent]]. They also recognize iconography in the door's etchings as depicting the legendary Shent sage Heroth, the Harrow-Hearted.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character remembers that in addition to his arcane prowess, Heroth was known to be a war priest of Sockets, an Elder God that stood proudly and successfully against many Abyssal incursions of the ancient past. This proximity to [[The Abyss]] came with rumors about Heroth's corruption — rumors staunchly denied by his disciples and allies.
>
> Characters who understand **Language: Pathward** can decipher various runes within the door's etchings, revealing the phrase:
>
> > THE DEVOUT TRACE THE DEATHLESS PATH
>
> Meanwhile, characters who understand **Language: Abyssal** are able to translate a phrase that has been graffitied here:
>
> > SPEAK THE WORDS TO SUMMON THE MASTER
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 18)** check identifies the statues here as diminutive sculptures of celestial hounds known as Cadrithors, which were known to be loyal companions to the Shent and their allies, often serving as guardians and protectors.

The entrance to this place is secured using an arcane trap.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Baneful Doorway Trap
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 13, Passive)** notices that the doors here are magically trapped, detecting the faint glimmer of dangerous arcane luminance deep within the beveled grooves of the etchings.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character identifies that the shape of the Holy Symbol of Sockets is a centerpiece to the etchings and of some importance to the door's mechanism — regardless of whether they are aware that the symbol itself relates to Sockets.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 13)** check notices the enchanted nature of the trapped door. It's also clear that the Pathward phrase etched upon the door (whether they understand the phrase or not) must be related to a command or key.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character recognizes that the Abyssal graffiti is *also* command-related clue, despite the Abyssal text's alien nature.
>
> Any creature who attempts to open the door without performing a gesture of the holy symbol of Sockets must face **Judgment of Sockets (Hazard 4, Willpower, Morale, Corruption)** and are **Weakened** until they finish a Rest.

The front doorway to the Bleak Archive is locked by powerful magic, and can be opened by two primary methods: recognition of archaic Shent rites or Abyssal assistance from [[Nethehepticas]].

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Opening the Gates
>
> Any character who traces a gesture of the **Holy Symbol of Sockets** into the air or upon the surface of the door is able to open the door via the proper method. Once the gesture is traced, the **Baneful Doorway Trap** is disarmed, and the runic etchings on the doors swell with arcane brilliance as the portal slowly opens.
>
> Alternately, any character who speaks one of the Abyssal phrases from the [[Night Terrors]] of Nethehepticas is granted entry via the Abyssal Horror's distant otherworldly influence:
>
> - "Away to Ebbok Zhùr."
> - "The Night Terror beckons the brave."
> - "The Sleeper walks in Skybrush."
> - "The Bleak Seed has been sewn."
>
> As soon as a character speaks one of these phrases aloud in Abyssal, four spectral claws of amethyst shadow coalesce and pry open the arcane doors — overriding the aged Shent magic that has kept them shut.
>
> Any character who spends at least 10 minutes studying [[Dereth's Journal]] is able to find handwritten notes that feverishly describe the method of opening the door, a vision that came to the bard in a dream.

## Alternate Entrance

Navigating the ravine to the east of the front gate allows the party to reach a covert secondary entrance to the Bleak Archives via the [[Unknown]].

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Spotting the Hidden Breach
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 14, Passive)** notices an inconspicuous hole in the cliff face, obscured by shadow and a bramble of ash-colored vine.
