# Gazetteer Reference

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A curious landmark is carefully tucked away in the cliffside here, facing towards the southeast. This stony facade appears to be the structured entrance to a some cave or subterranean facility, and is marked by the presence of a wide sandstone perron beneath a rocky overhang. At the top of this sheltered stoop — which is littered with detritus both natural and manmade — a large set of reinforced doors have stood for time immemorial, and lead to some unseen area beyond.

Jekeroka Villa is the ancient name of a modern Anachraenum dig site located southwest of Ordain, where the forgotten workshop of an Aedir Maejis has lurked for centuries untold. The guild managed to unseal this wayward Aedir ruin nearly a decade ago, and in the years since it has served the Anachraenum as a suitably challenging training facility for potential recruits. Furthermore, Jekeroka himself was a spellcaster who trafficked in secrets, and the lingering mysteries of his workshop await further scrutiny from Anachraenum scholars and their allies.

The site itself is relatively small, with only a few chambers adorned with remnants of ancient Aedir furniture slabs, mechanical apparatuses, and scrolls that have long since disintegrated into shells, crumbling at the slightest touch. Dust motes dance in the shafts of light that enter when the large entrance door is open, creating an ethereal, silent atmosphere. At the heart of the dig site lies a much larger central chamber, dominated by several large pools of elemental energy that bubble and boil quietly. Its properties have fascinated the Anachraenum for years, and no one is sure what exactly keeps the strange liquid active.

While the dig site is a common place for researchers, it has been designated for another purpose as the Anacrahem prepares to host the second Expedition Challenge. The dig site is also one of the locations where Anacharanum Trainees will face carefully constructed challenges and trials.

### Environment

The Aedir Digsite is a lovingly preserved site by the Anachraenum. Most Aedir structures have been weathered and worn over the millennia, and many details have been lost, but as explorers enter the site, they are greeted by the sight of intricate patterns upon the walls and floors, and glyphs etched into the chambers’ geometric pillars, depicting scenes of arcane rituals and celestial alignments. The pale stone itself shimmers with a faint luminescence, hinting at the potent magic that once thrived here.

### Ecology

The digsite is maintained by the Anacharenum as a place of study and research into the daily life and history of the Aedir Empire, but nothing lives within the chambers. The guild ensures the door is sealed with their own magic when no one is present, preserving everything within. Only the strong roots of trees snaking down from above have penetrated the structures and pressed through the ceiling and walls in some places.

### Lore

The Anachraenum has determined that Jekeroka Villa was once the ancient home of the Aedir, one of the most powerful Magi of the Aedir Empire. He likely lived on the Arctus Plateau before the events of the Shattering. While this is true, the villa and Jekeroka lived roughly a thousand years before the Shattering itself. He sealed this location about three and a half thousand years ago before disappearing. Strangely, Jekeroka was somewhat of a scholar, historian, and explorer, which echoes the Anachraenum's goals. He collected and researched artifacts and cultures from history himself, such as the ancient Sedyri and Vaznor. He also pursued magical experimentation, and the elemental energy he produced and created is a strange mix of several cosmological concepts, still powered by a connection to distant and mysterious magical forces.
