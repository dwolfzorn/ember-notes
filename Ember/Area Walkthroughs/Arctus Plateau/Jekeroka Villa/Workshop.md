# Workshop

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This small chamber is relatively unremarkable, save for a stone table at the southern end of the room and the trio of arcane lanterns that hang from the ancient walls. A thick layer of dust covers the entire space, and small particles of this soot float on the breeze beneath the pale illumination of the three lanterns, cloudy crystal orbs lit by some perpetual magic. If anything of value was once stored here, the guild has likely hauled it away for closer inspection some time ago.

This dusty chamber appears to be a sparsely-decorated workshop of some kind, its original contents all but removed and forgotten. The floor here is covered in a thick layer of sabulous grime, which helps cleverly conceal the pressure plates of a set of four hidden dart traps.

If the party can successfully navigate the trapped floor, they can activate the alloy toggle switch that adorns a stone table at the southern end of the room.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Hidden Dart Traps
>
> **Trigger**: A creature moves onto a pressure plate
>
> **Duration**: Instantaneous
>
> When a creature moves onto one of this trap's pressure plates for the first time on a turn or starts its turn there, a 5-foot-wide volley of darts shoots out of countless imperceptible holes perforating the eastern wall, up to a range of 20 feet.
>
> Each creature in the volley's path must succeed on a `[[/save dex 15]]` save, taking `[[/damage 1d6 piercing]]` damage on a failed save or half as much damage on a successful one.
>
> #### Detect and Disarm
>
> While searching the room, a creature can examine the trapped areas and make a **Awareness (DC 15)** check, detecting the dart holes on a successful check. Plugging all of the holes with wax, cloth, or detritus prevents the darts from firing.
>
> While searching the room, a creature can examine the sections of floor that form the pressure plates and make a **Awareness (DC 15)** check, detecting the pressure plates on a successful check. Wedging an [[Iron Spike]] or similar object under each pressure plate prevents the corresponding trap from triggering.

If the characters decide to take a closer look at the switch, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The stone table is adorned with little more than a few layers of dust and a large metallic switch. Even from here, you can tell that old Jekeroka crafted the switch from some strange alloy, and it gleans beneath the lantern light with overt significance.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Activating the Workshop Switch
>
> This room contains one of three switches necessary to summon the guardians of the [[Trial Challenge Key]] in the [[Main Chamber]].
>
> Made of some curious unbreakable alloy, the toggle switch in this chamber is located in plain sight on the stone table in the alcove at the southern end of the room, and can be activated as an Action.
>
> Unless the [[Librarium]] and [[Meditation Room]] switches have both been activated, there is no immediate perceptible effect when activating the Workshop switch. All three of these switches must be activated in order to summon the [[Anachraenum Aetherial]] that guard the Trial Challenge Key in the villa's Main Chamber.
