# Librarium

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Inside the small room, stone shelves line every wall, holding hundreds of crumbling scrolls, many in various stages of decay. The ceiling like the other rooms curves inwards and comes together to a central apex above. The air is thick with dust, and the strong smell of old parchment fills the space. Magical lights flicker in alcoves, casting an uneven glow over a central table, which is covered with cracked charts and what appear to be heavily aged maps.

The Librarium contains five collections of ancient scrolls, all of which have been equipped with hidden Blinding Needle Traps to thwart would-be plunderers. This forbidden knowledge is altogether a distraction from the room's actual purpose…

If the party can assemble the clues gathered here, they can activate the alloy toggle switch that lurks concealed at the apex of the Librarium ceiling.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Activating the Librarium Switch
>
> This room contains one of three switches necessary to summon the guardians of the [[Trial Challenge Key]] in the [[Main Chamber]].
>
> Made of some curious unbreakable alloy, the toggle switch in this chamber is concealed in shadows at the apex in the ceiling, and once located can be activated as an Action.
>
> Unless the [[Meditation Room]] and [[Workshop]] switches have both been activated, there is no immediate perceptible effect when activating the Librarium switch. All three of these switches must be activated in order to summon the [[Anachraenum Aetherial]] that guard the Trial Challenge Key in the villa's Main Chamber.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Locating the Switch
>
> Any character who takes a moment to examine the table in the center of the room will notice an assortment of crumbling navigational charts, maps, and other documents. A freshly-written note concealed among the stack reads:
>
> > A hidden switch lurks somewhere in this room. Follow our clues to find it.
>
> Any character who makes a successful `[[/check perception 12]]` check while studying the contents of the table can locate a detailed map of the Librarium, with certain highlighted areas that correlate to specific wall markings here in the room.
>
> - **Critical Success**: Scrawled annotations on the map mention the phrase "Towards the watchful eyes."
>
> Any character who succeeds on either a `[[/check inv 14]]` or `[[/check perception 14]]` check while examining the walls of the chamber can locate several glyphs of eyes (drawn by the Anachraenum), which all appear to be looking upwards towards the ceiling.
>
> Any character who succeeds on either a `[[/check inv 15]]` or `[[/check perception 15]]` check while examining the ceiling of the chamber can locate the concealed switch, which is subtly "hidden" in plain view among the preternaturally dark recesses of the room's canopy.
>
> - **Attunement: Signara**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Darkvision**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Attunement: Cora**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Aedir**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Subterranea**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Alternately, any character who manages to move within 5 feet of the switch will automatically discover it.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Blinding Needle Traps
>
> **Trigger**: A character interacts with the scrolls or fails to disarm the trap
>
> **Duration**: Instantaneous
>
> When a character disturbs one of the five scroll collections here, a long poisoned needle shoots out of a tube concealed among the scrolls and stabs the character.
>
> The character must succeed on a `[[/save dex 15]]` save, taking `[[/damage 1d6 piercing]]` damage on a failed save. A character who takes damage this way must succeed on a `[[/save con 16]]` save to avoid gaining the &Reference[blinded] condition for a duration of 1 minute.
>
> #### Detect and Disarm
>
> While searching the room, a character can examine each of the five scroll collections and make a **Awareness (DC 15)** check, detecting the corresponding collection's needle tubes on a successful check. Once the trap is detected, a character can take an Action to try to disarm the trap, doing so with a successful **`[[/skill sleightofhand 15]]`** check. On a failed check, the character triggers the trap.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Forbidden Knowledge
>
> Characters who manage to disarm the Blinding Needle Traps can safely search the scroll collections, but only once per scroll collection (up to a total of five), and only after that particular collection's traps have been disarmed.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check while searching the a scroll collection is able to locate the equivalent of one [[Book]] of ancient [[Aedir]] history or lore.
>
> - **Path: Academy Dropout**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character also finds a Damaged Map among the scrolls, which depicts Jekeroka Villa during the [[Age of the Tower]]. The section of the map that details the Librarium is notably missing.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 15)** check while examining the map can safely presume that casting the [[Mending]] Spell on the map will repair the text. Otherwise, the party may stumble onto this revelation on their own (or via the help of a friendly ally).
>
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Crafts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> If the Damaged Map is magically repaired, it reveals the location of a hidden compartment on the eastern wall here. Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 12)** or **Awareness (DC 12)** check while using the map is able to open the compartment, where the illuminating [[Jekeroka's Ledger]] awaits, undisturbed for nearly two and a half millennia.
