# Terraced Pools

Characters with **Attunement: The Abyss** feel a heightened connection to this place, experiencing the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you near the nexus of the waterfalls, you can feel a roiling, twisting sensation in your chest. It extends out of your body, crawling down your limbs like a thousand invisible insects. After a moment, the feeling subsides… but in its place is the lingering sensation that you are being perceived. Something knows you are here.

Then proceed with the normal area description.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you take in your surroundings, you realize that you are surrounded by death in many forms, from the stark line where living plants turn rotten in tainted earth, to the numerous corpses of large humanoids that must be remnants of the Shent.
>
> All around you the corroded remains of armor and weapons poke out from centuries of dead growth, making it evident that you are standing on the site of a one-sided massacre that claimed the lives of numerous warriors.
>
> At the middle of the crater-like depression is a huge, tarnished, rusting Shent sword. Much too large for any mortal of Ember to use, half its length is driven into the earth. Pinned through by the ancient steel is an unsettling, almost amorphous creature that writhes around the corroded blade, grasping at it like any wounded thing might.

## Considering the Creature

The creature pinned beneath the ancient blade is a Writhing Whisperer, a terrible Abyssal monster that first appeared during the [[Abyssal Shear]].

> [!abstract] Writhing Whisperer
> **[[Writhing Whisperer]]**
>
> Level 6 (Elite) · Abyssal Harbinger Whisperer
>
> ![Writhing Whisperer](../../../assets/image065.webp)
>
> Ever shifting and reshaping itself, producing new limbs as quickly as it retracts existing ones, occasionally you glimpse flickers of stars and lights swirling around under the surface, but they are always consumed by blackness a moment later.
>
> It has no eyes, no mouth, no perceptible form that lets you ascribe humanity or awareness to it, and yet you can feel its alien intelligence, feel it probing your mind, trying to find ways in. You can sense it trying to understand what you are in much the same way you are trying to parse is inscrutable form in return.

The party may attempt to identify what kind of creature this is.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Abyssal Interloper
>
> The Writhing Whisperer is beyond the knowledge of most mortals of Ember, as its kind has not been witnessed for centuries, if not millennia.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 30)**check identifies the creature.
>
> - **Knowledge: Abyssals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Upon an unlikely success, the character recognizes the attributes of a "Whisperer," a terrible monster mentioned in accounts of great horror during the aftermath of the Shattering. They recall that such creatures are likely to manipulate and twist the minds of others.

As soon as any member of the party is within 120 feet of the Whisperer, it becomes aware of them due to its ability to sense thoughts and communicate telepathically. Once perceived by the Writhing Whisperer, the party is immediately assaulted by intrusive telepathic contact.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Looking upon the being in its totality causes your mind to recoil. It is not of this world … and after a moment spent looking too long, the mass becomes aware of you in return.
>
> Its voice reaches out to your mind like a long, bony finger pressing into your forehead. It is like oil and sand in your mind, sliding over and grinding against your thoughts.
>
> > Mortals … sharp minds … gifts … we offer.
>
> The words, if they can be called such, are awkward, as if lacking total familiarity with their meaning.
>
> > A gift … we provide … for mutual gain.
>
> The voice continues sliding its way through the recesses of your mind.
>
> > Approach … release … reward … receive.
>
> The intrusion stills as if awaiting some response.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Abyssal Intrusion
>
> First contact with the Writhing Whisperer has an immediately deleterious effect on the minds of the party. Any character who is exposed to the Writhing Whisperer's telepathy must withstand the **Abyssal Intrusion (Hazard 4, Willpower, Psychic, Harmless)** as it begins to weave itself into their minds for easy communication. Characters who fail immediately incur a single effect from the [[Minor Abyssal Madness]] table.

The Whisperer is a being of absolute evil, but it will honor a pact made in exchange for its release. It is impossible for the party to discern this, however, due to the [[Inscrutable]] nature of the creature.

## Assessing the Environment

The party may also want to examine — from a distance — the massive blade driven through the Whisperer, which they can do without provoking the creature.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Pinning Steel
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 14, Passive)** notes that the blade appears to have been hastily carved with numerous magical symbols and runes, many of which appear to be blackened and burnt. In addition, there are two very large red gems in the crossguard of the sword. These could be valuable if removed, and appear decorative rather than tied to the sword's magic.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 14)** check while examining the sword and its runes surmises that the markings draw on numerous magical fields. They were built around preserving the steel and holding the monster in place. However, many of the runes are burned out, and it likely won't hold forever.

As soon as the party approaches the creature or otherwise acts against it, proceed to the encounter described on the [[Waterfall Nexus]] page.
