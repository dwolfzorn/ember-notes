# Waterfall Nexus

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The knee-deep water surrounding the mound in the center of this space is brackish, and an acrid smell overcomes your senses. You feel a tingle of anticipation as you inch closer — unbeckoned excitement that seems to pull you forward.

Moving through this area can be hazardous.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Treacherous Terrain
>
> The water in this area is knee-deep and constitutes &reference[difficult terrain]. Creatures that become **Prone** within the water are submerged and cannot speak or breathe.
>
> This area is [[Area Overview]], and unsafe for the party to remain in for a lengthy period.

The party must decide whether to do battle with this foe, negotiate with it, or flee.

## Speaking to the Abyssal

Conversing telepathically with the Abyssal is deeply unpleasant and inescapable, as the being can insert its words unwanted into the minds of any creatures it senses nearby. It can transmit words, distant emotions, faint images, and even imagined sensations.

> [!info] Social
> #### An Abyssal Offer
>
> Parties willing to unleash this Abyssal entity into the world will be handsomely rewarded, with each character gaining a unique talent. If the party accepts this offer, see [[Writhing Grave]].
>
> The [[Writhing Whisperer]] can naturally sense the thoughts and emotions of creatures around it. While it finds mortal minds simplistic and noisy, it can quite easily sift through them to find things to use. Consider having the Whisperer appeal to the interests, fears, wants, and goals of the party to help its bid for release. It does not change the nature of the offer, but it can make it more tempting.
>
> #### Peering Into the Abyss
>
> The Writhing Whisperer is [[Inscrutable]], and immune to any attempt or effect that would sense its emotions or read its thoughts.
>
> Manipulation is quintessential to the nature of this creature. However, the Whisperer does tend to be truthful. It only selectively omits details or twists the truth to avoid harming its chances of becoming free.
>
> Any character may attempt a **Deception (DC 30)** check to ascertain the Writhing Whisperer's intentions.
>
> A character that fails this check senses the creature wants to end its confinement — to be free, to explore, to wander, and not be trapped in one place, wallowing in ancient rot.
>
> A character that succeeds on this check senses that the Whisperer wants to be free of its binding — to roam, to explore, and be free. It has been trapped for thousands of years, and yearns to break loose.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The Whisperer not only wishes freedom, but to seek out other mortal beings and pluck at their thoughts like so many strings on a lute.

The Whisperer opens any discussion by reiterating its offer of power. It wants nothing but to be free, and is prepared to grant a portion of its own power to make this happen. If the party asks what exactly they are being offered in exchange for setting it free, the Whisperer struggles to conceptualize it, communicating the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> An unsettling feeling of amusement tries to take root in your mind. You realize that the sensation is the creature's giddy laughter, mixed with deep contempt.
>
> > If you agree. If you help. I will give what all want, crave, and need — strength.
>
> It seems to ponder the word, and alternatives.
>
> > Strength ... knowledge … growth … expansion … improvement …
> >
> > "You are lesser … release me, and I will make you … more."

The party can speak with the Whisperer to learn more about it, though such knowledge may not be especially comforting, as it is a truly alien creature.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What are you?
>
> **A:**
>
> You feel a flurry of thoughts blooming in your mind, concepts forming and being discarded as a frustration not your own grows in your head.
>
> > I am what the void beyond is made of. Mortal words cannot describe the totality of nothingness. I am the non-light beyond the Weave.
>
> The trickle of thoughts and feelings stops and crystallizes.
>
> > Names — there are none for us. Your primitive minds would know us by emotion alone.
> >
> > Fear.
> >
> > Sorrow.
> >
> > Despair.
> >
> > Death.
>
> Each word is accompanied by a sudden stab of its associated emotion.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What do you want?
>
> **A:**
>
> The concepts that return to you are simplistic and primitive:
>
> > Hunger … recovery … regrowth …
>
> You sense that the creature is weakened, perhaps significantly, and that it desires time or opportunity to heal.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Shattering?
>
> **A:**
>
> The creature remains silent for a time. A sensation like dismissal weaves through your minds. The Whisperer's voice forms in your head:
>
> > Ember’s destiny ... threads you cannot weave together. Minds too small ... grasping at shadows … you cannot even see the light that casts them.
> >
> > No. Not your concern.
> >
> > Do not ponder ... lest you unravel.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What happened here?
>
> **A:**
>
> The thoughts that return are straightforward:
>
> > Hunting … the prey … it resisted … animal … animal! Much death.
>
> You feel unbidden excitement swell within you:
>
> > Crushing … breaking … grasping and tearing!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** About the Shent? About Mioroth?
>
> **A:**
>
> You feel a sensation of ennui, as if the question failed to take hold.
>
> > Is it dead? Or will it yet be?

## Fighting the Whisperer

Should the party confront the Whisperer in combat, it will no longer negotiate and will not hesitate in its attempts to destroy the party.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A feeling of desperate rage invades your minds, inciting a fear that makes your blood run cold. The Whisperer seizes the sword with its many tentacles and begins to strain against it, shimmers of magic rippling through the old, tarnished steel. You hear its pained voice in your head:
>
> > Oblivion … always they seek oblivion!

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Impaled Foe
>
> The [[Writhing Whisperer]] is affected by the [[Impaled]] condition, which renders it immobile and diminishes its ability to Dodge. However, the Writhing Whisperer remains dangerous, and is capable of fighting at full capacity despite being pinned in place.
>
> #### Writhing Whisperer Tactics
>
> At the beginning of combat, the Writhing Whisperer uses its [[Grasping Form]] Action to inflict the **Restrained** condition.
>
> Over the course of combat, the Writhing Whisperer will prioritize the following actions and abilities:
>
> - In melee, the Writhing Whisperer will use its [[Abyssal Whip]] Action.
> - At range, the Writhing Whisperer will use its [[Abyssal Whispers]] Action to inflict the **Disoriented** condition and force affected enemies to move toward it.
> - Whenever able, the Writhing Whisperer will use its [[Extrude Tendril]] to summon a [[Writhing Whisperer Tendril]].
>
> The battle ends when the Writhing Whisperer is destroyed, triggering its [[Abyssal Remains]] feature.
>
> #### Breaking Free
>
> Once the Whisperer is reduced to under half its total Health it will successfully rip itself free of the Shent blade that impales it. When this happens, disable the [[Impaled]] active effect and read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A soundless psychic scream rocks the battlefield as the Whisperer rips itself free of the blade, its amorphous form tearing open as a result. The Abyssal creature slumps briefly to the ground, and the air is filled with psychic static from the being's suffering as an ancient wound is ripped open anew.
>
> > If I am to die … let us share the experience.
>
> You can feel its attention fixate upon you once again.

Once the Whisperer is finally slain, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> With the final blow against the Whisperer landed, its form shudders and buckles into itself as though being pressed upon by an immense weight. Ripples of psychic screams emanate from it, sensations of fear, hatred, and pain following immediately after.
>
> Then the creature quickly unravels, turning into a viscous, oil-like substance that is all but impossible to collect or get hold of. In its wake is nothing but silence.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Over the Moon Quest
>
> If exploring this area as part of the [[An Ancient Battle]] Event in the [[Over The Moon]] Quest, return to the Event page and proceed from the [[An Ancient Battle]] section of gameplay.

## Aiding the Whisperer

If the party wants to entertain the idea of helping the Whisperer, the solution to its predicament should be fairly obvious: a massive sword is driven through its body and needs to be pulled out. However, if the party needs guidance, the Whisperer can offer it, albeit cryptically.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A mixture of frustration and anticipation ripples through your mind.
>
> > Draw the sliver of corpse-made steel from this eternal vessel. Let this form return to that of un-binding. The sword … the faceted shard-shackle … bound with cosmic energies that are anathema to this vessel.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Removing the Sword
>
> The sword is huge, and deeply sunken into the earth. It can be extracted with twenty minutes of physical effort, made shorter with appropriate tools, such as a [[Shovel]]. The Abyssal will not attack or harm anyone attempting to remove the blade, but it is too weakened to meaningfully assist.
>
> Destroying the sword may also be an option if it is subjected to concentrated attacks. Care should be taken when targeting the blade. If the Whisperer is struck — even unintentionally — it will immediately attack the party, tolerating no carelessness from what it considers to be lesser life forms.

If the party frees the Whisperer from beneath the ancient blade, read the following aloud.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As the sword loosens from the earth, the Whisperer slips free. A ripple of relief and unbridled glee crawls into your mind, followed by a sensation of gratitude.
>
> > As promised. Power. Strength. Strength to endure what petty and irrelevant trials await your short, meaningless lives.
>
> Tendrils of blackness snap out, piercing your forehead as the Whisperer's mind joins with yours. Power surges through your body. You feel your mind being broken apart, spread too thin, and folded over like parchment as this being begins to work parts of you into new shapes. Your muscles ache, your heart races, your lungs refuse air and you feel for a moment like death would be a relief. Then all goes black.

While the party is momentarily unconscious, they receive an infusion of Abyssal potency.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Psychic Backlash
>
> Assisting the Writhing Whisperer causes a backlash of psychic energy to affect the entire party. Each character must face **Abyssal Madness (Hazard 6, Psychic, Willpower, Morale, Harmless)**. If affected, Abyssal energies suffuse the character's body and they become permanently afflicted by one random effect from the [[Minor Abyssal Madness]] table.
>
> #### Abyssal Infusion
>
> A party that chooses to release the Writhing Whisperer gains the [[Abyssal Insights]] feature. They can use this feature once per day, and as their attunement with the Abyss grows, so does the feature. Characters who successfully saved against the psychic backlash above may elect to refuse the feature if they so choose.

After receiving or rejecting this granted boon, the characters regain their senses.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You awaken on your back, soaked through with the brackish water of this grave. There is no sign of the Whisperer, only the vacant remains of the giant blade that pinned it to the earth.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Over the Moon Side Quest
>
> If exploring this area as part of the [[An Ancient Battle]] Event in the [[Over The Moon]] Side Quest, return to the Event page and proceed with the [[An Ancient Battle]] section of gameplay.

## Aftermath

The party may want to look over the corpses in the nexus before or after dealing with the Whisperer. There is not much to be found, but examining the remains can confirm suspicions or provided simple insights into the events the unfolded here.

- If the party released the Whisperer, proceed to [[An Ancient Battle]].
- If the party destroyed the Whisperer, proceed to [[An Ancient Battle]].

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Flora
>
> Any character who examines the ground and makes a successful **Wilderness (DC 14)** check sees signs of the local plants growing along but not past the line of corrupted earth; the old, dead growth piles up, indicating that the taint spread over time rather than appearing all at once.
>
> #### Old Bones
>
> Any character who examines the dead giants and makes a successful **Medicine (DC 12)** check confirms that they have been dead for thousands of years. There's hardly anything to glean from them, though it is strange how the corruption or rot in this space has not seeped into them like everything else.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 12)** check notes numerous rusted, broken, discarded Shent weapons and artifacts in the area around the central mound. They are all corroded and weather-beaten beyond use, and many are likely to crumble if disturbed, but they could prove valuable to historians.
>
> Spending some time gathering these items yields [[Shent Artifacts]] which are worthless pieced out, but valuable as a collection to an antiquarian.
>
> #### Giant Gems
>
> One [[Spinel]] and one [[Chipped Spinel]] can be pried from the crossguard of the great sword by any character who makes a successful **Athletics (DC 14)** check. Doing so takes about 20 minutes of work.
