# Rotating Elevator

If closed, the elevator can be opened from the outside by pulling a lever located next to the lever that disables the magical darkness, on the freestanding wall in the center of the entryway.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The metal walls of the inside of the elevator are copper-colored, with a hand-shaped indentation in the wall by the door, as if someone had forced their palm into the metal. Below the indentation is a panel with four circular niches, arranged as if they were the top, bottom, left, and right points of a diamond. The top, left, and right niches all hold metal discs inscribed with different glyphs. The bottom niche is empty.

Any character who takes a look at the glyphs can tell that the left and right discs are mirror images of each other.

| Position | Basic Meaning | Full Translation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Left | Left Turn | Rotate Clockwise |
| Right | Right Turn | Rotate Counter-Clockwise |
| Top | Sky | Ascend |

## Operating the Elevator

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Interactive Object
>
> The elevator is an ​​ **Interactable Object** which can be rotated, raised, or lowered by the Gamemaster only.
>
> #### Disabled Functions
>
> Before finding the [[Elevator Glyphstone]] and restoring it to the elevator control panel, the elevator can only be rotated to visit other areas of the Entry level or ascend to the Top level.
>
> As the Gamemaster, you will be prompted with an option to descend, but this should not be used until the party has repaired the elevator's functionality.

### Entering and Exiting

Characters open and close the elevator doors by placing their hands against the hand-shaped indentation in the elevator wall.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As you place your hand against the indentation in the wall, the metal shifts, feeling suddenly soft and pliable under your touch, and molds itself around you as the doors move. When you take your hand off of the metal, the indentation remains, fitted to the shape of your hand.

### Rotating

Touching the inscribed circular discs on the left or right side of the diamond of niches rotates the elevator by a quarter turn. The elevator will only rotate properly when it is closed.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The glyph in the center of the metal disc you press glows and the disc warms slightly to the touch. With a soft hum, the elevator slowly begins to rotate in a gentle but constant motion, stopping when it has moved a quarter of the way around the circle. As it ceases moving, the glyph ends its glow and returns to normal.

### Changing Floors

Touching the disc at the top of the diamond raises the elevator one floor. This has no effect if the elevator door is open.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The glyph in the metal disc glows beneath your fingertips, and the elevator creaks as it begins to ascend, slowly rising inch by inch until it comes to a stop.

Characters can only make the elevator descend if they find the elevator Descent Disc and put it into the empty slot in the elevator panel as noted below.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The glyph in the metal disc is slow to warm this time, and the elevator jerks as it descends, shaking you as it lowers to the floor below.

## Repairing the Elevator

To fully operate the elevator, the party must find and replace the [[Elevator Glyphstone]] that has been removed from the elevator's control panel.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Finding the Missing Disc
>
> The missing glyphstone that controls the elevator's descent can be found in the nest of a Suarrok Juvenile that has been collecting jewels, coins, and other objects of value and storing them in its nest in the [[Lookout Post]]. It can be easily identified by its size and shape, as well as the glyph written on it, which is a mirror image of the glyph that raises the elevator.
>
> Anyone who knows Common can identify the word "Below," while those able to translate basic Towyr identify its meaning as "Descend".
>
> #### No Substitutions
>
> The function of the disc is tied to the glyph written on it, not its positioning in the panel. Characters can pry one of the existing discs out of its niche and slide it into the bottom niche, but it will perform the same function that it did previously (if the left disc is moved to the bottom, for example, it still turns the elevator left).

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The glyph eagerly gravitates into position in the empty socket. A subtle humming sound and gentle light emit from it; you get the sense that it is once again functional.
