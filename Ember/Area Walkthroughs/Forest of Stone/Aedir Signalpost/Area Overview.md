# Area Overview

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Area Map Context
>
> The [[Aedir Signalpost]] Area Map depicts the entirety of the ancient fortification. It is featured in the [[Traversing the Tower]] Event of the [[Signal of Intent]] Side Quest.
>
> - This map depicts four Scene Levels, each representing a different layer of the tower.
> - The party arrives in the [[Exterior]].

## Gameplay Details

The areas of the Aedir Signalpost have the following features unless specified otherwise in the text.

### Levels & Elevation

The Aedir Signalpost features a total of four vertical Levels of elevation, including:

- **Entry:** The ground Level is set to an elevation of 0 and has 20-foot-high ceilings.
- **Lookout:** The topmost Level is set to an elevation of 200 feet.
- **Basement:** The basement Level is set to an elevation of -20 and has 20-foot-high ceilings. It is initially inaccessible via the elevator until the control disc is recovered from the [[Lookout Post]] atop the tower. Alternatively, this Level can be reached via [[The Cliff Face]], which can be accessed through a crack in the wall found in the [[Western Storage Alcove]].
- **Underforge:** The bottommost Level is set to an elevation of -60 and has 20-foot-high ceilings.

### Illumination

The Entry, Basement, and Underforge Levels of the Signalpost are entirely dark except for the presence of a few remaining flickering lights that produce Dim Light.

The Lookout Level is outdoors and subject to exterior conditions.

The interior lighting of the Signalpost will be repaired if the generator in the [[Secret Basement Room]] is recharged and reactivated. In this case, the area is entirely illuminated by Bright Light.

### Terrain

While the ground of the Signalpost is in disrepair, it should be treated as normal terrain unless otherwise noted.

### Enemies

The following enemies are encountered here:

- 6 [[Crevvet]]
- 1 [[Suarrok Juvenile]]
- 1 [[Broken Aedir Sentinel]]

### Translating Towyr

Throughout the tower, the party will encounter writings in Towyr, the language used by the Aedir. Due to the incorporation of some of these words into modern Common, it may be possible for characters to understand their basic meaning: throughout the tower, texts that can be translated are presented in a table, with the words that can be identified in the text on the left and the fully translated meaning on the right, as seen below:

| **Basic Meaning** | **Full Translation** |
| --- | --- |
| Simplified words or concepts that can be extracted from the text. | Full translated text known to anyone who speaks or properly translates from ancient Towyr. |

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Understanding Towyr
>
> Characters who speak **Language: Towyr** or use the **Talent: Versatile Translator** talent achieve a **full translation**.
>
> Characters who can understand **Language: Common** can interpret the **basic meaning** of a passage of text by making a successful **Society (DC 15)** check.
>
> - **Failure:** Translation of a passage may not be re-attempted until the party successfully translates some other passage of text that shares certain key words in common.

If the party fails on a translation attempt, read the following to help explain how they might improve their chances:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Try as you might, you cannot quite make sense of the words in front of you. The roots of Common in ancient Towyr are too distant and tenuous for you to make sense of, and there is not enough to work with. If you were able to successfully translate some other passages, you might be able to identify certain words in common.

## The Secret Room

The secret [[Generator Room]] contains the power source for the tower and can be opened only by activating 4 levers hidden throughout the tower:

1. Embedded in the chess board in the [[Unknown]].
2. Attached to the bed within the empty [[Unknown]].
3. In the [[Unknown]] workshop.
4. Hidden within a bookshelf in [[Unknown]].

The following clues to the locations of these levers can be discovered:

1. A sketch of the basement that includes the Generator Room in [[Unknown]].
2. Notes from Golarne that emphasize the word power, found in each room where a lever is located.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Activating the Levers
>
> If characters activate a single lever, but have not activated all 4 of them, they hear a click, but nothing else.
>
> > A quiet click echoes from nearby. In the distance, somewhere in the tower, a gear creakily turns for a moment and then goes silent.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check knows the general direction of the grinding stone (if not on the basement level, they know if it is above or below them, and if on the basement level, they know which direction it is from them).
>
> The door to this chamber now stands halfway open. Tiny creatures can enter the room while it is in this state. Any character who makes a successful **Athletics (DC 16)** check forces the door the rest of the way open. Alternatively, the characters can use a [[Blast Flask]] to blow the door open.
>
> If characters have activated all four levers, the sound of dragging is heard again, and this secret chamber is now fully opened.
>
> > The gear clicks, and not only does a distant gear turn, but you hear the groaning, scraping sound of marble against rock followed by a louder final fastening of some ancient mechanism. You may have found what you are looking for.
