# Entrance Alcove

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Two large metal doors stand recessed within a pair of entrance alcoves. If they were locked at some point, time has worn away whatever kept them closed — you can see spiderwebs and pieces of greenery growing through the cracks between them.

## A Dark Entryway

The doors from the front swing inward and open easily, without lock or bar.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The heavy stone doors of the tower swing inward with surprisingly little effort. You peer within the gloom of the tower's interior. It is dark and empty, exuding a sense of long abandonment.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Regarding Traps
>
> This area introduces one of the first trapsin Ember's explorable Area Maps. As a Gamemaster, you will see a red rectangle on your screen just inside the tower doors. This indicates the area of the trap.
>
> Each trap will have a corresponding Hazard block in the gameplay text (see the "Darkness Trap!" block below) which identifies the triggering mechanism and consequences of a trap.
>
> Whether the trap is armed (or *disarmed*) is controlled via the **Trap Trigger** behavior of the relevant **Scene Region**. Most traps like this one are "pressure plates" which are triggered automatically when a Token moves over it.

A pressure plate inside the tower entryway is connected to an orb of magical [[Engulfing Darkness]] which suffuses the area in magical gloom. If a player steps on the triggering plate:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A tile clicks beneath your feet, and it instantly grows dark inside of the tower. Utterly and completely dark, as if all light around you has been swallowed by a deep and eternal shadow.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Darkness Trap!
>
> The Entrance Alcove is trapped by a magical darkness trap, which is triggered when a character steps on the 60-foot-wide, 10-foot-long pressure plate at the eastern end of the chamber. This pressure plate cannot be disabled.
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 14, Passive)** is able to spot the pressure plate.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 16)** check knows that magical darkness spells were used as a defensive measure by the Aedir and that there would typically be a physical disarming mechanism somewhere nearby.
>
> - **Knowledge: Aedir**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> #### Tripping Hazard
>
> As characters make their way through the dark, they may trip over the ground, which is rough from years of disuse. While in darkness, the entryway area is considered Difficult Terrain.
>
> Each time a character moves through the room, roll a `[[/gmroll 1d4]]`. On a result of **1**, they stumble over something on the floor and suffer damage from the **Tripping Hazard (Hazard 4, Fortitude, Health, Bludgeoning)**.
>
> #### Disabling the Darkness
>
> The Darkness field is disabled by the control lever behind the fortified barricade on the west side of the room.
>
> Alternatively, the Darkness may be dispelled using the [[Dawn Beacon]] Iconic spell.

The magical darkness emanates from a circular orb mounted atop the east wall of the room and fills the space completely. The orb itself is totally obscured while the Darkness effect is active.

## Departure Notes

Once there is light in the room, the wall in its center can be clearly seen:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A gray metal wall stands in the center of the room. Pieces of paper, largely faded, have been taped to it — one has bold red writing, one in a thin and neat blocky blue, and one in green that varies in thickness and color, as if written by someone whose hands were shaking.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Inspecting the Notes
>
> Of the pieces of paper on the wall, only the notes in red, blue, and green can be clearly made out. Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 14)** check knows that the writing is from the Aedir Empire, and is also aware that the language is no longer used, but that there are several words in Common that come from the language because of how widespread it was when the Aedir Empire fell in the wake of the Shattering.
>
> - **Language: Towyr**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 14)** check knows that these pieces of writing were either written with [[Spellwrought Pen]], or on paper that has been magically treated to resist fading and disintegration. As a result, determining exactly when they were written is not possible.
>
> #### Deciphering the Text
>
> Rules for translating these notes are provided here: [[Area Overview]].

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Three Perspectives
>
> The written perspectives of the three Aedir researchers can be found both in the entryway and throughout the tower. Any character can tell that the three sets of handwriting are different without a check.
>
> - **Red Ink**. Text written in red ink with bold and blocky text is by **Remel**. Remel's writing is also usually accompanied by sketches.
> - **Blue Ink**. Text written in blue ink with precise thin lines is by **Bejai**. Bejai has clear-cut to-do lists.
> - **Green Ink**. Textwritten in green ink with chaotic and sloppy handwriting is by **Golarne**. Golarne's memory aids point to the location of the levers to the Secret Basement Room that can be found throughout the tower.

### The Red Note

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Each scarlet red glyph that fills the page is drawn with paint instead of ink, in bold artistic strokes that give the line and curve of words a sense of depth and motion. In the corner of the page, still bold but drawn at a smaller scale, is a small model of the tower, much as it looks today but without depicted damage or deterioration.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Deciphering the Red Note
>
> The red note was drawn by an artist, which can be determined by any character without a check.
>
> Any character who examines the model of the tower drawn on the page and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** check lines up the image with what they see around them. They learn that the tower has four total levels, and that the entry level that the party now occupies is the second highest of the group.

| **Basic Meaning** | **Full Translation** |
| --- | --- |
| Protection and preservation | Protect the Tower. Activate concealment. Preserve the Aedir Empire. |

### The Blue Note

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The sets of glyphs on this page are written in a thin blue ink, one below the other. Several are crossed out or faded and can no longer be read, but four remain legible and untouched.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Deciphering the Blue Note
>
> The blue note looks like a to-do list, which can be determined by any character without a check.
>
> Any character who reads the blue note and makes a successful **Science (DC 14)** check can also identify the author as someone who is precise and organized.

| **Basic Meaning** | **Full Translation** |
| --- | --- |
| Science and sight | Leave the laboratory. Take the elevator up to the top. Keep watching. Record audio for any intruders. |

### The Green Note

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The note is written in a forest green and has several false starts — glyphs that have been written down and scribbled out and replaced with similar ones, then underlined. The ink is also quite uneven: thicker lines can be seen in some places and thinner ones in others, though they all appear to be by the same hand.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Deciphering the Green Note
>
> Any character who examines the green note and makes a successful **Science (DC 14)** check can determine that one word in particular in the note is the one that has been emphasized most strongly; it is similar to the Common word for **power**.

| **Basic Meaning** | **Full Translation** |
| --- | --- |
| Power and memory | Note the recipe for power. Remember and stay safe. |
