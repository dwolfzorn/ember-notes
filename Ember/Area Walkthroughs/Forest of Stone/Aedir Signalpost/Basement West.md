# Basement West

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> An assortment of tools and supplies are strewn across nearly every elevated surface of the room, from the circular worktable that lines the outside wall to the bronze tables in the center. The supplies are a mix of familiar and strange, common and wondrous, but none are rusted — unlike most other things in the tower, they appear to have been recently cleaned.

The party may wish to peruse through the tools on these workbenches, although tampering with them initiates the hazard of the Steam Trap described below.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Signs of Danger
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 15)** check can confirm that the tools on the tables were recently cleaned by the application of a substance that likely covered the entire room, as the worktables and barrels in the room show the same level of cleaning.
>
> - The trap can be discovered before it is triggered with a successful **Awareness (DC 15)** check to note the drops of condensation on the long table of tools beneath each of the bronze spouts, or by using the spell [[Find Traps]] or similar, or through the use of the [[Recognize Spellcraft]] talent, which reveals an aura of magic around both the worktables and the bronze spouts.
>
> #### Sorting through the Tools
>
> Most of the tools found in the Basement are of no real use to the party, but there are some standout items. The characters can search through the tools in the following ways:
>
> - **Society (DC 15)** to identify the bulk of the tools as metalworking tools that would have been used by the Aediri.
> - **Arcana (DC 15)** to identify a [[Spellwrought Pen]] among the items on the table.
> - **Awareness (DC 15)** to spot a few scattered gems on the table, likely used as component pieces in magical works. In total, the gems are worth 50 gp. They also notice that all of the papers and books on the table have the same symbol either on or near them.
>
> When making any of the above checks:
>
> - **Knowledge: Aedir**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on the check.
>
> Any character who examines the symbol and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14)** or **Society (DC 14)** check identifies it as one that shields the item it is on from environmental damage.
>
> - **Knowledge: Aedir**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on the check.

## Steam Cleansing Trap

This trap uses the natural steam cleaning functions of the basement (meant to keep tools sterile) and uses them against intruders by flooding the area with super-heated caustic steam.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Steam Cleansing Trap
>
> This room contains a complex Steam Cleansing Trap which, once activated, becomes worse as more steam and caustic chemicals are released into the air.
>
> Once triggered, place all characters who are inside the area of the trap into a Combat encounter and have them act in initiative order. The trap acts as described below on both initiative count 20 and 10.
>
> #### Trap Trigger
>
> This trap is triggered when the weight on any of the three worktables in this section changes (by objects being removed or added).
>
> #### Trap Actions
>
> The trap acts on initiative count 20, when it releases more steam into the room and on initiative count 10, when it adds cleaning agents to the steam that are harmful to the party.
>
> **Steam Release (Initiative 20).** Hot steam is released from the spouts in the wall and begins filling the room. Characters within the room must make a series of checks to avoid the injurious release of steam. The difficulty and danger of this trap escalates the longer it is active:
>
> - **Round 1**:**Mild Steam Burn (Hazard 2, Fortitude, Health)**
> - **Round 2:** **Minor Steam Burn (Hazard 3, Fortitude, Health)**
> - **Round 3:** **Steam Burn (Hazard 5, Fortitude, Health)**
> - **Round 4+:** **Major Steam Burn (Hazard 6, Fortitude, Health)**
>
> **Alchemical Cleaning Agents (Initiative 10).** Alchemical cleaning agents are released into the steam. Any creature that ends their turn within range of the steam must make a successful **Medicine (DC 15)** to take precautionary measures against breathing in the toxic air. Failing this check results in the character being **Poisoned**. If the character is already poisoned when making this check, they suffer a coughing fit and are knocked **Prone**.The cleaning agents disperse into the steam and become harmless after 1 round.
>
> #### Deactivating the Trap
>
> The trap can be deactivated by activating two cutoff valves located against the wall on either side of the elevator. Characters can spot the valves if they are within 10 feet, have clear line of sight, and have **Awareness (DC 16, Passive)**.
>
> Activating the valve requires a character to perform the **Use an Object** action. The trap is disabled once both cutoff valves have been turned.
>
> The trap can also be neutralized by disabling the heating elements that produce the steam. Those heating elements are magical, but can be disabled by freezing it using the Condense action from **Talent: Rune: Frost**.
>
> #### Trapped Inside
>
> When the trap begins, the doors to the other half of the basement and to the elevator close and lock automatically. The basement doors can only be opened in one of the following ways:
>
> - Unlocked with a successful **Stealth (DC 15)** check using lockpicks
> - Forced open using a prybar or similar tool (one of which can be found among the tools on the table) and a successful **Athletics (DC 16)** check.
>
> Once a door is opened, the steam dissipates over the larger space and becomes harmless beyond its initial 5-foot radius.

## Secret Room Lever

One of the four levers required to open the [[Generator Room]] is found in this area.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A Powerful Tool
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 16)** check while looking around the room notices one note in particular, which has been wedged between tools so that it sits up straight on the table. It reads:
>
> > "Use a POWERful Tool." It also has a sketch of a rectangular table on it, along with several items: a spring, a length of pipe, and a circular gear with a hole in its center.
>
> Through roleplay, or after a successful **Science (DC 15)** check, characters can identify each of these items as belonging to the table to the left-hand side of the elevator when facing the elevator. The only item not accounted for in the drawing is a lever that is attached to the outer edge of that table.
>
> If a character pulls the correct lever, refer to [[Area Overview]] for details. Interacting with this lever does not trigger the Steam Cleansing Trap.
