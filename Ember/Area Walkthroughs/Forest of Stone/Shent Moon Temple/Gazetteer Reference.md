# Gazetteer Reference

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Your eyes are immediately drawn to the grand structure ahead, an ancient Shent temple rising majestically from a broad, glistening lake like a monolith of timeless power. Its two-tiered stonework foundation supports towering stone walls, each crafted from massive stone slabs. Slanted channels on each side channel water down into the surrounding area, filling the place with a constant roar of flowing water. The whole edifice is timeworn and weathered, with its foundations seemingly made of even older materials, but it remains a remarkably impressive sight.

This spectacular temple of ancient Shent design sits upon a small island at the heart of the Dripstones. Its most distinguishing feature is a great lunarium suspended within the temple interior, its various orbs attuned to Ember's moons. The original purpose of the temple is lost to time, but it retains its powerful connection to the elemental energy of the moons — particularly the aqueous energy of Mayis.

### Environment

The lake measures approximately two miles in both length and width, forming a somewhat round and circular shape. Numerous small streams and rivers flow into and out of it, and a small island sits at its center. On the island stands an ancient temple, magically built from large stone blocks and bronze-like metal bands. The architecture was designed to fit Giants who are 15 feet tall, giving its doors, halls, and atrium an outsized, grandiose appearance suitable for larger beings.

### Ecology

Especially verdant and lush, this area of the Dripstones is home to tiny creatures almost everywhere, including insects, fish, and small bats. However, the dominant creature type near the temple is elementals, beginning with countless small water motes that remain in the area, creating currents so thick in the air that they obscure the surroundings. The area is also rife with larger, more invasive water elementals. Although many of these creatures are harmless and non-hostile, the Temple Invader and its minions are highly protective of the font of magical energy they've claimed for themselves.

### Lore

While most of the ruined structures in the Forest of Stone were built by the Shent over three thousand years ago, they were constructed near the end of the Shent era and are relatively new from a certain perspective. The Water Temple, however, is much older and was built close to eight thousand years ago. The Shent of that time worshipped the moons as deities in their own right and built many temples across the surface world. When they encountered the Forest of Stone, they correctly determined that the lush, clear, flowing waters were somehow connected to Mayis. Over time, Shent practices evolved, and the temple underwent several phases of abandonment, reconstruction, and repair; by the time of the Shattering, it had become a holy site of some renown. Within the temple rests an ancient machine that the Shent used to communicate with the moons and worship Mayis though the presice manner of its mechnisima is now lost. The central artifact, the Planisphere, is an especially complex and prized object, one that the Shent valued highly.

Due to thousands of years of worship and magical accumulation, the now long-abandoned temple still retains a deep connection to Mayis, and in recent years, an active connection between the temple and Mayis has allowed water elementals to take control of the site.
