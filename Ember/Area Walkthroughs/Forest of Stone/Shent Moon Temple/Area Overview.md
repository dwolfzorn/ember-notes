# Area Overview

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Area Map Context
>
> The [[Shent Moon Temple]] Area Map depicts the entirety of the Shent Moon Temple. It is featured in the [[Lunar Awakenings]] Event of the [[Over The Moon]] Side Quest.
>
> - The map depicts a single Scene Level: Temple Grounds.
> - The temple has been taken over by hostile elemental creatures.
> - The party arrives outside the [[Temple Exterior]].

## Gameplay Details

The areas of the Shent Moon Temple have the following features unless specified otherwise in the text.

### Levels & Elevation

The Shent Moon Temple Scene features a single Level, and its areas are organized into a single section: Temple Grounds.

- **Temple Grounds:** The Temple Grounds is set to an elevation of 0, and its interior has a 70-foot-high ceiling.

Just below the ceiling, a ring with rotating machinery is situated at 60 feet. At ground level is a large central platform. In the corners, stairs rise up to 10 feet.

The surface of the water around the central platform is set to height 0. Applying any negative height to a token will place it underwater. The water itself reaches down as far as -30 feet. Anything lower than that places tokens under the ground level of the map entirely.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Submerged Machinery
>
> The spinning machinery presents a danger to any characters between a depth of -15 to -30 feet.
>
> Characters that begin their turn in the water surrounding the central platform must make a successful **Athletics (DC 13)** check or be pulled 10 feet downward and moved 10 feet clockwise by the machinery churning the water.
>
> Between -15 and -30 feet below the surface of the water the machines sweep past rapidly, and risk catching creatures in them. Any character who ends their turn at or between these depths must succeed on a **Athletics (DC 13)** or take damage from a **Machine Impact (Hazard 4, Fortitude, Health, Bludgeoning)**.
>
> Characters with a Swim Speed have **+2 Boons** for this hazard.

### Sound

The temple features a constant roar of flowing water and cascading waterfalls inside and outside its walls. As a consequence, hearing in this environment is more challenging. Characters need to raise their voices to speak over the din of flowing water.

### Illumination

The [[Temple Exterior]] is subject to exterior lighting and weather conditions. The exterior of the temple also features several ancient Shent water lights, which cast 5 feet of bright blue light and 10 feet of dim blue light. They are obviously magic.

Inside the temple, the central platform is brightly lit thanks to a large glowing bubble shield. The rest of the temple is dimly lit with swirling blue light and energy being slowly directed into the center of the temple.

### Terrain

The area is broken up into the [[Unknown]], which covers all spaces outside the central structure, and the [[Unknown]], which covers everything within the doors of the central structure.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Wading In
>
> The area around the temple is surrounded by knee-deep water (for Medium creatures) and limits movement due to [[Combat]] for characters wading through it.
>
> Creatures that end up **Prone** in this water are submerged, suffering the @Condition[drowning] condition until they stand up again. Characters that can breathe water will not have this problem.
>
> #### Slippery Slopes
>
> The four aqueduct channels flowing out of the temple can be freely climbed and traversed without issue, though crossing the rushing water is Difficult Terrain.
>
> Attempting to climb, fight, or rush across the water may cause creatures to lose their footing, resulting in falls and sliding down toward the water, but this is left to the GM's discretion.

### Inhabitants

The party will encounter the [[Fulgurite Blades]] as impromptu allies, but their cooperation may be short-lived. See [[Temple Interior]] for more details.

### Enemies

This temple is rich with elemental energy that has attracted the attention of water elementals in the region. As a consequence, the temple has become a hotbed of water elemental activity, and the amorphous beings now infest the place.

- [[Temple Invader]]
- At least 3 [[Water Sprite]]
- Possibly 1 or more [[Water Visitor]]

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Resting
>
> It is possible to rest outside the temple as long as the temple doors have not been opened. Once the temple is opened, however, the elementals within will become hostile and attempt to drive the party off if they refuse to leave.
>
> #### Fleeing
>
> Should the party need to flee the area, they may do so by retreating from the temple interior toward the edges of the map. The elementals will not pursue them beyond the immediate area around the temple that the map covers.
