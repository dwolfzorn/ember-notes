# Central Lunarium

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Unavailable During Combat
>
> Investigation of the environment is unavailable until the combat encounter described in the [[Temple Interior]] is resolved.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Rising out of the center of the platform is a large stone pillar with a dozen unlabeled stone dials around a large central basin, now filled with water. With the destruction of the greater machinery it is connected to, the pillar and its controls are rendered inert.
>
> It seems the central column had several spaces for pieces of stone, crystal, and metal to be slotted into it, possibly manipulating how the machine functions. Many of these slots are empty now, and likely have been for centuries.

## Exploring the Temple

Once the elementals are defeated, the party can explore the temple in relative peace. Examination of the grand machinery that spans throughout the temple can reveal some clues toward its history or purpose.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Interior
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 13)** check reveals the presence of subtle inclusions of Abjuration, Transmutation, and Divination symbols throughout the structure, albeit very old, archaic ones. Perhaps these were meant to shape and channel energy through the structure, and to reinforce weak points in its construction, keeping the sunken machine from shaking the whole building apart over time.
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Shent**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 13)** check recognizes the typical, deep reverence for the cosmos that permeates all [[Shent]] construction that has been found so far. Symbols, images, reliefs, and similar features depict the Shent people using this structure to chart the path of [[Mayis]] and predict tides and rains for the nourishment of crops. This location is part-temple, part-scientific outpost.
>
> - **Knowledge: Cosmology**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Shent**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> **Ancestry: Hulg'run** characters feel a special connection to this place:
>
> > The energy that flows through the ancient stonework of this temple resonates with the very core of your being. Although you don't have a physical heart, the thrumming you currently feel in your chest is perhaps as close to a heartbeat as you'll ever know. There's no mistaking that this place was made with the same ancient magic used to create the Hulg'run people.
>
> #### Giant Cogs
>
> Any character who spends time surveying the remains of the ancient machinery and makes a successful **Arcana (DC 13)** check can determine that this machine was a massive planetarium dedicated to a single celestial body: Mayis. It drew power in from an external source, converted it, and used it to power the extensive magical markings. This machine used a mixture of Illusion, Conjuration, Divination, and Evocation magic to draw energy from Mayis and turn it into an illusory projection of the cosmos with a focus on the cycle of Mayis.
>
> - **Knowledge: Cosmology**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Machines**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character realizes that the damage to the machine is so extensive that repairing it would be a considerable effort. Even with the effort and resources necessary, it's possible there's vital Shent magic, materials, or components that would be impossible to recreate. It seems that the extensive Abjuration warding on the machine failed long ago, leaving the magical energy unregulated. This failure, coupled with something going awry in the temple's connection to Mayis, effectively turned the machine into a massive beacon for elementals to gather around.

## Fulgurites in the Lunarium

While the party is examining the temple, Sajor and the Fulgurite Blades begin actively investigating the most prominent artifact within the temple — the pillar that sits upon the central pedestal of the temple lunarium and the [[Shent Planisphere]] it contains. If the party is attentive, they may notice Sajor and the Fulgurite Blades rooting around in the ancient machinery.

> [!info] Social
> #### Eyes on the Prize
>
> Any character with **Awareness (DC 16, Passive)** notices the Fulgurites converging on this artifact before they get a chance to pry it free from its housing.
>
> Sajor has recognized its value and intends to claim the object before the party has a chance to interfere. If asked, Sajor is open about her intentions. She states plainly that their group is here specifically to recover artifacts of archaeological significance, and she is not open to dividing or sharing such a find.
>
> If confronted, any character who makes a successful **Deception (DC 15)**check confirms that Sajor's motives are genuine, though somewhat self-interested. She seeks renown and prestige among the Anachraenum and views this find as one that will elevate her reputation within that guild.

If the party notices the Fulgurites paying special attention to this pillar, they may wish to investigate it themselves.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Lunarium Pillar
>
> Any character who examines the pillar and makes a successful **Awareness (DC 12)** check discovers an assortment of [[Shent Machine Shards]] and a socket that houses the [[Shent Planisphere]] (if it has not already been taken).
>
> - **Knowledge: Machines**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character deduces that the removed disc was likely responsible for controlling the timing of the machine. Without significant repair and this unique artifact, the temple will never function again.

If the party does not notice the Fulgurites' examination of the central pillar, or if they do not stop them from removing its contents, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The grunt of exertion and the scrape of stone draw your attention. Rorhim is bent over the central pillar of the machine, assisted by Sajor. The pair are muscling a cover off of the central column of the machine.
>
> Rorhim grunts out a confirmation while straining:
>
> > Definitely got deformed when this thing broke down.
>
> With a final, heavy pull, the cover snaps off, nearly sending Rorhim to the ground. As he tosses the cover into the water, he asks:
>
> > That what you expected, Sajor?
>
> Sajor's smile is broad and pleased.
>
> > Paydirt.
>
> She kneels down, pulling out some fractured cogs and discs, eventually followed by a series of interlocking discs. Rorhim peers over her shoulder easily, actually curious for once.
>
> > What is it?
>
> Sajor turns the thing over in her hands, sliding the discs around to see how their notches and holes and carvings line up. She makes a surprised sound under her breath.
>
> > It's a planisphere, a pretty archaic device meant for tracking stars. But this one is special. Obviously because it's Shent, but also because it looks like they accounted for Mayis specifically. Either way, it'll do the trick.
>
> She tucks it away into her pack, looking well pleased with herself.

> [!info] Social
> #### Looting Dissuaded
>
> If the party notices the Fulgurites, they can attempt to stop them from taking further actions with one of the following methods:
>
> - **Diplomacy (DC 16)** to convince them to slow down and talk first.
> - **Deception (DC 18)** to distract them by diverting their attention to some other focus.
> - **Intimidation (DC 20)** to urge them via guilt, obligation, or threat to stop.
>
> If the Fulgurites are successfully deterred, the party can claim the [[Shent Planisphere]] themselves, although Sajor will confront them and ask for it moments later. She intends to keep it in her possession because it is potentially a very valuable archaeological find, and she does not want to lose sight of it. This item will allow her to pay her team, keep the group afloat, and gain needed stature within the Anachraenum.
>
> #### Conversing with the Fulgurites
>
> There are a number of topics that the party can discuss with the Fulgurite Blades, including any topics presented in [[Lightning in a Bottle]] that were not already covered.

If the party made a favorable impression on Sajor and the Fulgurite Blades, read the following aloud:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Sajor considers you for a moment and says:
>
> > You know, it was interesting working with you. You'd make a decent adventuring team.
> >
> > You should drop by the All-Fable Keep if you ever make it to Ordain and see about joining the Anachraenum. I'll put in a word for you when we get there.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What is the Planisphere?
>
> **A:**
>
> > It's a planisphere, which itself isn't new. They are used to track time and the movement of celestial bodies in that time. They are actually pretty common, and not very special.
> >
> > This one, however, is very special. It came out of a Shent machine that's thousands of years old. Did they make them the same? Did they track the same bodies in the sky? I don't know, but this thing can tell us!

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Why do you want it?
>
> **A:**
>
> > I will return it to Ordain, to All-Fable Keep, where the Anachraenum will pay for its return. These funds will provide supplies, materials, spellcraft components, and more that our group needs.
> >
> > Exploration and academic discovery don't pay the wages by themselves. Coin must come from somewhere, and today that somewhere is an ancient Shent ruin.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Is selling it right?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Well, look, I'm not just selling it on the open market. It will go through the curators in the Anachraenum so it can be studied. It will either end up in our long-term vaults or sold to an interested scholar or collector who specializes in Shent artifacts.
> >
> > It will be studied, and any knowledge we learn will add to the body of knowledge we have about the Shent.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What can we have?
>
> **A:**
>
> Sajor shrugs.
>
> > Have a look around, see what you can scavenge. Maybe scoop up some of the ice left behind by the elementals. I believe that has some alchemical properties, and might be worth something.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What are you going to do?
>
> **A:**
>
> Sajor looks at her team, then back to you.
>
> > Oh, that's easy — patch up wounds, survey the location, and take notes until Kazra's satisfied. We'll also scoop up anything else of interest, then head back to camp.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What should we do?
>
> **A:**
>
> Sajor shrugs at you.
>
> > I don't know. I'm not your boss. Aren't you out here for a reason?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Help for the Strayhearth Caravan? If on Good Terms
>
> **A:**
>
> Sajor contemplates the request and exchanges glances with Rorhim.
>
> > Well … all right, you've been fair to us and we need that road cleared anyway so we can get back to Helkas once we're done here. We can double back the way we came and find your caravan on the road. Between my spells and Rorhim's muscles, I suspect we can have that rubble cleared in no time.
>
> Sajor coughs skeptically, saying:
>
> > Do you want to come with us? Or are you intent on throwing your lives away tracking down some ancient abyssal?

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Help us fight the abyssal?
>
> **A:**
>
> Sajor scoffs loudly.
>
> > Not a chance. No matter how curious I or my team might be, we're not gonna follow some specter of an ancient race to the site of an ancient evil to do whatever it is you said he wants.
> >
> > That sounds like a terrible idea, especially since I've got my team's payday in hand. I'm not gonna trade it for an early grave.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What do you know about the Shattering and Abyssals?
>
> **A:**
>
> Sajor shrugs.
>
> > Not much. The Shattering happened, Abyssal entities attacked, and most people in the world died.
>
> Kazra specifies:
>
> > In truth, historians do not know much about the events of the Shattering, and not much survived. However, that doesn't mean it didn't happen. The signs of the destruction can be found everywhere, but few records survived.
>
> Sajor nods.
>
> > Yep, and what records did survive read like horror stories, not historical accounts. Honestly, if even a quarter of that information about Abyssals is true, then only certain death awaits for anyone who decides to mess with them.
