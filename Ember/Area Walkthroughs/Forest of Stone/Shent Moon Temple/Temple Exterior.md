# Temple Exterior

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This massive Shent temple bears two long walls of stone per door, each one bearing two short columns of chipped, weathered stonework. Each of these columns holds floating orbs of water held aloft by an unseen force. The orbs themselves glow brilliantly, casting rays of blue light in all directions, illuminating the grounds.
>
> Four massive artificial waterfalls sit at the corners of the structure, pouring large amounts of water down onto long stone channels that guide it toward the greater body of water around the structure. The noise is considerable, and drowns out the world beyond the temple.
>
> Massive stone doors sit on each wall of the temple, each covered in the symbols of Mayis, the water moon in grand detail, and the rest of the cosmos of Ember is secondary form.

The party may inspect the architecture or exterior details of the structure before entering.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### History in Stone
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 14, Passive)** check notices that this structure was built for people that were substantially larger than average-sized humanoids of Ember. Judging by the size of the stairs alone, one might guess that the ancient Shent were at least fifteen feet tall.
>
> - **Knowledge: Crafts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Society (DC 13)** check while examining the architecture surmises that the main structure was constructed by the Shent at least 2,500 years ago. It could very well be older, since the Shent are one of the oldest cultures to exist on Ember.
>
> - **Knowledge: Shent**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character can also identify some of the patterning in the stonework here as that of the final era of Shent culture — likely built a short time before the entire culture collapsed and mysteriously vanished from the world.

Once the party has concluded an investigation of the exterior, they must proceed into the temple by opening one of the four sets of double doors which frame each side of the temple. All doors are unlocked and can be opened through the combined effort of two characters pulling in opposite directions from their central seam.

The doors open into the spacious [[Temple Interior]], where the party is immediately greeted by the peril within.
