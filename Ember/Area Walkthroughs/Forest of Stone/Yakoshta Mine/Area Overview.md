# Area Overview

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Area Map Context
>
> The [[Yakoshta Mine]] Area Map depicts a small portion of Yakoshta Mine's deepest and most active workings. It is featured in the [[Saving Jasper]] Event of the [[Ooze Control]] Side Quest.
>
> - This map depicts two Scene Levels, each representing a different layer of the mine.
> - The area is under attack by rampaging oozes.
> - The party arrives in the [[Unknown]].

## Gameplay Details

The areas of Yakoshta Mine have the following features unless specified otherwise in the text.

### Levels & Elevation

The Yakoshta Mine features a total of two vertical Levels of elevation, including:

- **Upper Mine:** The upper mine is set to an elevation of 0 and has 20-foot-high ceilings.
- **Lower Tracks:** The lower tracks are set to an elevation of -10.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Deadly Falls
>
> The bottom of the chasm is 300 feet below the tracks that run above it. A fall from this height is almost certainly fatal. A character that falls from a platform into the chasm has a final opportunity to save themselves from falling by succeeding on a **Athletics (DC 13)**. If successful, the character grasps a nearby handhold and hauls themselves back to safety, becoming **Prone** instead. On a failure, the character falls to their death and their body is unrecoverable except by magical means.
>
> #### Less-Deadly Falls
>
> At your discretion, you may allow for a character that falls to instead tumbling below and breaking their fall with a **Scaffolding Impact (Hazard 5, Fortitude, Health, Bludgeoning)**.

### Illumination

The kaleidoscope crystals throughout the mine provide dim illumination. These crystals have been cut back, which counteracts the immediate effects of disorientation normally found when exposed to [[Kaleidoscope Crystal]] — the party is not affected unless they Rest within the mine or they break the crystals apart.

Within each cavern, unless indicated, the light of the kaleidoscope crystals is supplemented by magically lit lanterns that hang from hooks on the walls. These lanterns provide 15 feet of bright light and an additional 15 feet of dim light. A lantern is affixed to both mine carts, which produce 15 feet of Bright Light and an additional 15 feet of Dim Light.

### Terrain

In general, the mine has normal terrain. Climbing over barricades or climbing up or down to a different Scene Level counts as &reference[difficult terrain].

### Inhabitants

During the [[Saving Jasper]] Event, the following friendly inhabitants are encountered here:

- 5 [[Arcturian Miner]]: Shel, Trianda, Gravin, Valka, and Danith.
- [[Jasper]]
- A group of [[Oozeling]]

### Enemies

During the [[Saving Jasper]] Event, the following enemies are encountered here:

- 2 [[Luminous Silver Ooze]]
- 2 [[Luminous Gold Ooze]]
- 1 [[Luminous Copper Ooze]]
- 1 [[Giant Luminous Ooze]]

### Mine Cart Operation

Each cart occupies a single 5-foot square and can hold one creature of Medium size or smaller. Alternatively, it can be filled with items equivalent to a 5-foot cube in size and weighing less than 1000 lbs.

The front of the cart holds a stone panel and an activation lever marked with three symbols:

- A wheel with an arrow through it: when activated, the cart begins moving forward.
- An X: when activated, the cart is brought to a stop.
- A wheel with an arrow opposite to the first: when activated, the cart begins to move in reverse.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Operating the Cart
>
> Each mine cart is an ​ **Interactable Object**. To see all Interactable Objects on a Scene Level, you may hold down the Left Alt Key.
>
> As the Gamemaster, you can Left Click on the Interactable Object to bring up a dialogue window. In this window, you'll see up to three fields:
>
> - Backwards
> - Forwards
> - Unreachable
>
> Left Clicking on a Backwards or Forwards location will cause the cart to move toward that destination. Whenever the cart moves, it also moves whatever character is occupying it; this character will travel with the cart until stopped.
>
> To stop the cart, the Gamemaster must pause the game by pressing the Spacebar key. When the game is un-paused, the cart will not resume moving until activated once again.

### Track Switch Operation

Each track is controlled by a series of switches, which determines what locations are reachable via mine cart.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Visualizing the Tracks
>
> As the Gamemaster, you may visualize the current track layout by Left Clicking on Token Controls and then Left Clicking on the​ **Show Tracks** button.
>
> #### Operating the Tracks
>
> Each track switch is an ​ **Interactable Object**. To see all Interactable Objects on a Scene Level, you may hold down the Left Alt Key.
>
> As the Gamemaster, you can click on a track switch to toggle it to its opposite position; this will update the track visualization as described above.
>
> #### Repair Dialogues
>
> There are two ​ **Interactable Objects** that must be repaired by the players before they can be used:
>
> - The [[Makeshift Barricade]] switch.
> - The [[Junction]] switch.
>
> When the Gamemaster Left Clicks one of these Interactable Objects, a dialogue window appears, asking if the required component should be installed. Once the component is installed, the switch is repaired, and it can be activated like any other switch.

As a result of the ooze attacks, many of the track switches have been damaged or made somewhat dangerous.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Disrupted Switches
>
> Each time the party attempts to redirect a switch, roll on or select a result from the table below to determine if the process is disrupted.

| Range | #### Yakoshta Mine Track Switches |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | #### Electrified Switch  The switch has become electrified by a silver ooze. Any party member that touches it directly receives an [[/hazard 4 fortitude morale electricity]{Electrical Discharge}. |
| 2 | #### Contaminated Switch  The switch has contaminated ooze goo on it. If it is not removed, any party member that touches it risks a **Mild Acid Burn (Hazard 4, Fortitude, Health, Acid)**. |
| 3 | #### Stuck Switch  The switch was damaged by the recent earthquakes and gets stuck halfway. Any character who makes a successful **Athletics (DC 14)** check can move it the rest of the way. If oil or another lubricant is applied, this check is made with **+2 Boons**. |
| 4—6 | #### Functioning Switch  The switch works as planned. |

### Kaleidoscope Crystals

If characters wish to extract a [[Kaleidoscope Crystal]] from one of the caverns, either to use as a light source or as a method of distraction for the luminous oozes, they must break it off of the cavern wall. Breaking a crystal requires either a successful **Athletics (DC 14)** check while using a suitable tool (pickaxe, hammer, etc.) to rip it from the cave wall.

In either case, failure results in some portion of the crystal shattering, causing the release of magical energy that immediately inflicts a [[Kaleidoscope Crystal Effects]].

### Alchemical Contaminant

The contaminant is leaking down into the [[Glowing Ore Pit]] from above, as noted in the readaloud for that section of the mine. A pure sample of [[Aedir Construct Fluid]] can be taken by using a vial to collect it from the overlook above the ore pit. Elsewhere, the contaminant has been partially digested by the oozes and instead yields [[Caustic Phial]].

### The Giant Ooze

The [[Giant Luminous Ooze]] in [[Excavation Pit]] can be defeated in two ways:

- Heroes may face this adversary directly in battle, potentially with the aid of [[Oozeling]], [[Kaleidoscope Crystal]], or [[Blast Flask]].
- Alternatively, a clever party may trigger a deadly explosion using the [[Barrel of Blast Powder]] found in the [[Supply Cache]]. The steps necessary to accomplish this plan are detailed in [[Ooze Go Boom!]].
