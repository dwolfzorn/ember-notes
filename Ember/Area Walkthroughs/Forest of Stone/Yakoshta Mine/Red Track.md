# Red Track

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Suspended wooden railways weave and cross throughout the gleaming cavern ahead. Several tracks are suspended from the cave ceiling far above by rope or cables, while others are affixed precariously to the rock walls that frame a seemingly bottomless chasm.

## Red Mining Tracks

The red mining tracks are the lower set of tracks. They have been largely untouched by the damage to the mine and are mostly viable to either walk on or to travel by mine cart, though there are a few sections that are inoperable.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Red Mine Cart Location
>
> When characters enter the mine, the red mining cart is at the bottom of the ladder down from the [[Loading Zone]].

## Mine Cart Operation

Each cart occupies a single 5-foot square and can hold one creature of Medium size or smaller. Alternatively, it can be filled with items equivalent to a 5-foot cube in size and weighing less than 1000 lbs.

The front of the cart holds a stone panel and an activation lever marked with three symbols:

- A wheel with an arrow through it: when activated, the cart begins moving forward.
- An X: when activated, the cart is brought to a stop.
- A wheel with an arrow opposite to the first: when activated, the cart begins to move in reverse.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Operating the Cart
>
> Each mine cart is an ​ **Interactable Object**. To see all Interactable Objects on a Scene Level, you may hold down the Left Alt Key.
>
> As the Gamemaster, you can Left Click on the Interactable Object to bring up a dialogue window. In this window, you'll see up to three fields:
>
> - Backwards
> - Forwards
> - Unreachable
>
> Left Clicking on a Backwards or Forwards location will cause the cart to move toward that destination. Whenever the cart moves, it also moves whatever character is occupying it; this character will travel with the cart until stopped.
>
> To stop the cart, the Gamemaster must pause the game by pressing theSpacebar key. When the game is un-paused, the cart will not resume moving until activated once again.

## Track Switch Operation

Each track is controlled by a series of switches, which determines what locations are reachable via mine cart.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Visualizing the Tracks
>
> As the Gamemaster, you may visualize the current track layout by Left Clicking on Token Controls and then Left Clicking on the​ **Show Tracks** button.
>
> #### Operating the Tracks
>
> Each track switch is an ​ **Interactable Object**. To see all Interactable Objects on a Scene Level, you may hold down the Left Alt Key.
>
> As the Gamemaster, you can click on a track switch to toggle it to its opposite position; this will update the track visualization as described above.
>
> #### Repair Dialogues
>
> There are two ​ **Interactable Objects** that must be repaired by the players before they can be used:
>
> - The [[Makeshift Barricade]] switch.
> - The [[Junction]] switch.
>
> When the Gamemaster Left Clicks one of these Interactable Objects, a dialogue window appears, asking if the required component should be installed. Once the component is installed, the switch is repaired, and it can be activated like any other switch.

As a result of the ooze attacks, many of the track switches have been damaged or made somewhat dangerous.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Disrupted Switches
>
> Each time the party attempts to redirect a switch, roll on or select a result from the table below to determine if the process is disrupted.

| Range | #### Yakoshta Mine Track Switches |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | #### Electrified Switch  The switch has become electrified by a silver ooze. Any party member that touches it directly receives an [[/hazard 4 fortitude morale electricity]{Electrical Discharge}. |
| 2 | #### Contaminated Switch  The switch has contaminated ooze goo on it. If it is not removed, any party member that touches it risks a **Mild Acid Burn (Hazard 4, Fortitude, Health, Acid)**. |
| 3 | #### Stuck Switch  The switch was damaged by the recent earthquakes and gets stuck halfway. Any character who makes a successful **Athletics (DC 14)** check can move it the rest of the way. If oil or another lubricant is applied, this check is made with **+2 Boons**. |
| 4—6 | #### Functioning Switch  The switch works as planned. |
