# Old Ore Pit

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> This largely empty space has been fully mined out, but there are a few piles of ore that appear ready for transport. Silver paint on the side of a crate of pickaxes reads "In Emergency, Break Crystals!"
>
> Two miners are wielding pickaxes against a copper-colored ooze with the same glow and aggressive behavior as the oozes you encountered at the Loading Dock. One of them uses the pickaxe to break open a Kaleidoscope Crystal. As soon as the crystal shatters, the copper ooze stops in its tracks and moves its pseudopod toward the object, giving the second miner the opportunity to land a killing blow.
>
> With the ooze dead, the first miner glances inside a bag they are carrying, as if reassuring themselves that whatever was inside the bag is still there.

As party members enter the room, they encounter a pair of miners who have together held off a single [[Luminous Copper Ooze]]. The ooze has now dissipated into [[Luminous Copper Flakes]].

**Valka (Chaotic Good, Arcturian Hulg'run, she/her)**

A young Hulg'run who is shorter than most of the others and has tufts of moss growing out of splits in the rock of her body, perhaps suggesting some Thornling heritage, is carrying the large bag. It contains the wheel from the junction. She wanted to protect it from any ooze damage, but will give it to the party with a successful **Diplomacy (DC 14)** check.

- If the party mentions being sent by Sellen, or if they make it clear that they are trying to save Jasper, they automatically succeed on this check.

**Danith (Lawful Neutral, Arcturian Hulg'run, he/him)**

A Hulg'run, whose face includes geometric spikes that might resemble piercings on another humanoid, is catching their breath.

> [!info] Social
> #### Exhausted Miners
>
> Valka and Danith are eager to leave the mine at once, but will answer a few brief questions before leaving. If asked how to reach a certain part of the mine, they will gladly share directions with the party.
>
> If pressed for information about the operations of the mine or Jasper's activities, they try to leave, but will cooperate with a successful **Diplomacy (DC 12)** or**Intimidation (DC 13)** check.
>
> If cooperative, they will share the following information:
>
> - Breaking a [[Kaleidoscope Crystal]] will temporarily mesmerize nearby oozes, but can be risky for whomever breaks them open.
> - They removed the crystals from this room, but more can be found around the mine — the closest location for them is in the [[Mine Cart Loading Station]].
> - It's unclear who wrote the sign about using the crystal, but they surmise it was probably Jasper.
> - Valka took the [[Yakoshta Junction Wheel]] to protect it for Jasper, who seemed to think the elevator mining cart could help against the oozes.
>
> #### The Junction Wheel
>
> The miners can be persuaded to hand over the [[Yakoshta Junction Wheel]] as long as the party is honest in their intentions to use it to help rescue Jasper and other miners.
>
> Otherwise, they will only hand over the wheel with a successful **Diplomacy (DC 15)** or **Intimidation (DC 13)** check. If this check is failed, they refuse to hand over the wheel and run for the exit.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** How did you hold off the ooze?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Just followed the sign on the floor and broke some of the Kaleidoscope Crystal with our pickaxes — got them to stop what they were doing and stare long enough for us to get our hits in.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Will any crystal work?
>
> **A:**
>
> > No idea, but you can try — luckily they're all over the place here. If you're looking for a bunch, check the room right to the south of us, where we usually load up the ore carts. But don't forget, messing with them can mess you up a bit, so maybe only use them when you really need to.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** Who painted the sign?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Couldn't say for certain, but it does look a bit like Jasper's handwriting. No time to check though — even with the crystals we barely stayed alive. Time to get out of here.

> [!question] Q&A
> **Q:** What is in the package you're carrying?
>
> **A:**
>
> > Wheel for the junction down the road. Didn't want the oozes messing it up. Before Jasper ran off, he was muttering something about the mining cart and the elevator, so I'm keeping it safe for him. Or I was, anyway. Now I've gotta save my own hide.

If party members investigate or are curious about the painted sign, they can determine the following information.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Anonymous Painter
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 15)** check while examining the sign notices that the paint came from a can of silver paint siting nearby. A brush lies next to the paint can. The handle on the brush has a J on it (for Jasper).
>
> If the party has been to [[Jasper's Office]], they can easily tell that the handwriting on the floor matches his without a check.
>
> If the party has been to the [[Ooze Pool]], they can match the paint used on the floor with the paint on the wall in the Ooze Pool with a successful **Awareness (DC 12)** check.
