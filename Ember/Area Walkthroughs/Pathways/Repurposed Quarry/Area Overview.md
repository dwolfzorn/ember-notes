# Area Overview

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Area Map Context
>
> The area map for the [[Repurposed Quarry]] depicts the entirety of the quarry lab location when it is discovered during the [[Ancient Paths]] quest event.

## Gazetteer Entry

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A gaping maw cut into the pathways bustles with activity in the darkness. Shadows flit back and forth against the rock face, and figures move across the metal catwalks between structures. Pillars of smoke rise in plumes, dissolving into the gloom overhead. A narrow tunnel cut into the stone appears to be the only way to access it.

Once a simple source of stone, this subterranean site has been transformed into a sprawling laboratory. Hidden deep in the Pathways, dubious experiments and alchemical ventures abound here, shrouded in secrecy and shadows.

Nestled in the pale stone of the north eastern corner of the mycelian expanse, this old quarry has become something more than a place to acquire slabs of rock.

### Environment

The quarry's terrain is primarily composed of pale white stone cut deep into the subterranean expanse. The walls feature a fast-moving waterfall flowing into the center, flooding the lowest depths. Structures of decaying metal and weathered wood traverse the pit, connected with a series of moving platforms suspended from rails. The 40-foot drops and sheer cliffs demand caution from those navigating its confines.

### Ecology

Void of fauna, the quarry is colonized by typical Pathways flora. Slime and moss cling to walls, making surfaces slick and treacherous as low ground cover, flowers, and strange grasses find purchase anywhere they can.

### Lore

Adopted by the Mutagists, the quarry serves as a clandestine lab where their experimental work to develop some sort of mutagenic compound takes place. With animal pens, labs, mixing stations and copious chemicals, the lab space is as fully featured a work space as any specialist could want.

Little is known of their intentions or the nature of their research, and when the location is first discovered it will be in the throes of destruction. Whatever work was being carried out here has been completed, or terminated, and is slated to be moved elsewhere.
