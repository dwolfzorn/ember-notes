# Waterfall Crossing

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A cascade of water sweeps thought a channel in the stone, filling the air with mist and leaves the rocks underfoot are slick with a sheen of moss and moisture, presenting a treacherously slippery crossing. The unending rush of water floods the lower level, and creates an overwhelming roar that reverberates off the stone around you.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Slippery When Wet
>
> The waterfall is moving too quickly to easily walk through. Characters attempting to wade through the waterfall must make a successful**Athletics (DC 14)** check or be swept off their feet and thrown into the water of the lower level.
>
> Characters can attempt to make a running jump across the waterfall, but will need to cross a distance of 20 feet in order to land safely, as 5 feet of stone on either side of the water are very slippery and loose. To successfully clear the distance requires a **Athletics (DC 16)**, but anyone who does not critically succeed gracelessly slips on the other side, suffering an embarassing but otherwise harmless **Slip and Fall (Hazard 1, Fortitude, Health, Bludgeoning)**.
>
> Critical failure results in the jumper either misjudging their landing or falling significantly short of their target, the result is the same: they are swept away by the water and thrown into the water below.
