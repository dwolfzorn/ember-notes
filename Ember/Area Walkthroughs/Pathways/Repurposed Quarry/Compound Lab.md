# Compound Lab

**Work in Progress!** While this combat should come pre-configured in the scene for the **Repurposed Quarry - Middle** area, there is a chance that the actors have been cleared. For Early Access you may need to add the actors to the compound lab for this encounter.

The required actors are listed in the encounter below.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Wooden posts rise to meet the ceiling, remnants of the walls between them still visible in places. This hastily expanded workspace is filled with complexly arranged glassware brimming with strange alchemical mixtures. Four separate arrangements methodically distill, filter, stir, and transforms compounds for unknown reasons.
>
> Behind them all, a desk, strewn with an assortment of books and papers that hold the promise of more information. To its immediate right a window looks out upon a churning waterwheel, its motion driving something unseen elsewhere.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Head of Studies
>
> Kalasak is the overseer and director of this lab, and a dangerous individual in his own right. Upon first encounter he attempts to reason with interlopers, drawing them into a sense of safety, or at least reducing their vigilance. He is accompanied by three (3) [[Mutagist Vivisector]] that follow their overseer's lead.
>
> Before combat begins, Gamemasters should use the "Apply Toxin" or "Apply Poison" action as Kalasak secretly prepares to poison the unaware party.
>
> Once he sees an opening, Kalasak attempts to use his already poisoned [[Dagger]] on an unwitting foe, before turning the blade on any other close proximity enemies.
>
> Throughout this combat, Kalasak makes liberal use of the alchemical workbenches in the small room to hurl available alchemical concoctions at the party. He knows that failure to defend this lab and subdue the invaders will mean certain death for him, and as a result he has nothing to lose if defeat is looking likely. He fights ferociously, and without mercy.
>
> If Kalasak becomes Dead his [[Fatal Contingency]] immediately activates, preventing him from being stabilized, and removing any chance of interrogating his corpse via magic.
>
> #### Dangerous Suspensions
>
> There are four tables with different alchemical compounds loaded into them. Kalasak can use these to grab and throw vials of dangerous chemicals. These tables can also be smashed, causing them to spray dangerous compounds and shards of glass in all directions.
>
> GMs can use the following explosives from Kalasak's Backpack, but these are designed to reflect him throwing them from the proximity of each specific work bench.
>
> - [[Alchemist's Fire]]
> - [[Caustic Phial]]
> - [[Electrocharge Ampoule]]
> - [[Frostdrop Phial]]
>
> Both Kalasak and the Party can choose to destroy the tables with a successful **Athletics (DC 12)** by flipping the table or hurling an object into it to knock it over. Doing so causes the table to explode in a shattering of glass spraying compounds in a 5 foot blast, those affected suffer **Hazard 2 (Fortitude, Health, Piercing)** from glass shards and **Hazard 2 (Fortitude, Morale, Poison)** damage Once a setup has been destroyed, Kalasak can't benefit from it to concoct an alchemical attack.
>
> If any of the workbenches survive the combat, the party can loot up to 2 of the bombs the bench provided, any others aren't in usable condition.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Alchemical Accounting
>
> As long as the tables aren't destroyed, ransacking their collections of vials and bottles yields a collection of [[Rare Chemicals]] per table.
>
> Kalasak's desk holds various items:
>
> - A personal journal containing [[Kalasak's Observations]].
> - A stack of [[Experiment Reports]].
