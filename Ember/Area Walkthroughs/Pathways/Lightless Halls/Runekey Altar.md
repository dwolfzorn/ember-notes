# Runekey Altar

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The large chamber is illuminated by intensely glowing barriers that stretch across the room and reach all the way to the ceiling above, blocking access any further into the room. Below your feet lie large primordial runes meticulously inscribed onto stone panels. Along the walls, a series of stone stelae adorned with smaller runic symbols seem to have been placed deliberately to match the panels on the wall.

## The Light Barrier

The light barrier feels solid to anyone who approaches it, as if it is an impenetrable wall.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating the Light Barrier
>
> Any character who makes successful `[[/check arcana 16]]` check determines that the barrier of light is of an ancient primordial magic of almost unimaginable power.
>
> - **Critical Success**: The character recognizes this to be a permanent [[Unknown]] which cannot be countered except through use of powerful high-level magic.
>
> If characters touch the Light Barrier while it is active, they hear a sizzling sound in their ears and feel a compulsion to back away.
>
> > Though nothing appears to happen, the sizzling sound of burning feels like it drills into your ears, and you smell the faintest whiff of smoke. You feel an overwhelming urge to stop — at first whisper-quiet against your eardrums, and then louder and louder and louder.
>
> If characters stop what they are doing upon feeling this compulsion, there is no lasting effect. If they continue in their course of action, they must make a successful `[[/save wisdom 16]]` saving throw or else feel a deep desire to leave the wall along and get away from it, as if they gained the &Reference[Frightened] condition.

## The Runekey Puzzle

The stone floor panels in front of the party appear different than any of the stone around them. Each panel is etched with its own symbol.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Examining the Stone Panels
>
> Characters who inspect the panels more closely can use the following strategies to gain more information about them:
>
> - `[[/check nature 14]]` to determine that that the stone of the panels is of higher quality than the stone that surrounds it, as if designed to withstand the test of time. It is the same type of stone as the slabs on the wall, which are each carved with a single symbol.
> - `[[/check history 14]]` or an understanding of **Language: Pathward** to recognize the runes as a language similar to Pathward. Given the age of the Primordial Bastion, it is likely that Pathward evolved from this language.
> - `[[/check arcana 14]]` to sense intense magical energy coming from the panels on the floor, and reason that they are used to disable the light sensor.
>
> While making any of the above checks:
>
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on the check.
>
> Any stone panel touched by a character immediately glows. If touched again, the glow dims.
>
> If all seven panels are touched, but were not touched in the correct order, the panels all blink, then go dark. In that case, read or paraphrase following:
>
> > The light from the panels flashes and then fades. It seems as if something has not yet been activated correctly.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Interactive Rune Puzzle
>
> To solve each of the two rune puzzles in this chamber, players (or the Gamemaster) must activate the runes in their correct logical sequence to complete the phrases described below.
>
> Each phrase is a sequence of seven runes, and if completed in the incorrect order, the puzzle will automatically reset. If completed in the correct order, the runes will remain illuminated and the magical Light Barrier will disappear.
>
> #### Using Blind Rolls
>
> We recommend using blind rolls for skill checks made during this puzzle so that players do not know whether they have successfully or unsuccessfully interpreted each glyph. This lack of meta-information will improve the roleplaying experience of solving the puzzle.

### The First Puzzle

The only clue as to the meaning of the runes in the panels are engraved stone stelae on the side of the room:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The stone stelae are engraved with symbols and drawings that hint at their meaning. You see several of the runes from the floor of the chamber replicated on the images, as if waiting to be interpreted and understood.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Investigating the Stone Stelae
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Arcana (DC 18)** understands that the runes on the floor must likely be invoked in a specific order which serves as a type of passphrase.
>
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character automatically succeeds on this check.
>
> #### Using the Stelae
>
> Players may reference the stelae on either side of the chamber to translate each of the runes which appear upon the floor. The table below provides the definitions of each glyph in left-to-right order (for example, the first glyph on the left translates to "Eternal").
>
> #### Deciphering a Glyph
>
> Deciphering a glyph requires a **DC 12 ability check** using the ability and skill associated with that glyph in the table below. Each glyph also includes an associated Knowledge Area which allows for an automatic success in place of the roll.
>
> On a success, the character discerns the **Approximate** meaning of the glyph, while on a failure, they are unable to decipher the glyph. When making any of these checks:
>
> - **Language: Pathward**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on the check.
> - **Knowledge: Shent**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on the check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character discerns the **Exact** meaning of the glyph.
> - **Critical Failure**: The character mistakenly concludes the **Incorrect** meaning of the glyph.
>
> Glyphs must be deciphered one at a time.

| Pos. | Checks | Exact | Approximate | Incorrect |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Society (DC undefined)****Knowledge: Rituals** | "Eternal" | "Time" | "Lost" |
| 2 | **Awareness (DC undefined)****Knowledge: Intrigue** | "Unknown" | "Mystery" | "Surprise" |
| 3 | **Society (DC undefined)****Knowledge: Trade** | "World" | "Home" | "Mountain" |
| 4 | **Deception (DC undefined)****Knowledge: Warfare** | "Destruction" | "Harm" | "Nothing" |
| 5 | **Arcana (DC undefined)****Knowledge: Abyssals** | "Beyond" | "Behind" | "Underneath" |
| 6 | **Arcana (DC undefined)****Knowledge: Gods** | "Causes" | "Creates" | "Building" |
| 7 | **Wilderness (DC undefined)****Knowledge: Cosmology** | "The Weave" | "Barrier" | "Egg" |

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The First Keyphrase
>
> The puzzle is correctly solved by activating the following runes in the order **2-5-7-6-1-3-4** producing the following phrase in ancient Pathward.
>
> > Unknown beyond the Weave causes eternal world destruction.
>
> Any character who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 25)** check may deduce the correct sequence using subtle clues in the ligatures of each glyph which imply how they are intended to connect.
>
> Once all runes have been activated in the correct order, the runes begin to glow more brightly and the Light Barrier in front of the party dissipates.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> For a moment, the light of the barrier in front of you glows more strongly, matching the rapidly brightening glow of the etched panels at your feet. As the panels continue to shine light, ever more brightly, the barrier before you dims and fades to nothing, with a faint gentle whisper which you instinctually understand to mean:
>
> > The abyss beyond the Weave will destroy everything that Ember is.

### The Second Puzzle

Beyond the first Light Barrier, the party encounters a second set of runes on the floor and second Light Barrier in front of them.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Ahead of you, on the floor, sits a raised set of engraved stone platforms, nearly identical to the ones that glow behind you. In front of them, another barrier of light stretches across the chamber, but beyond it you can see a dais that holds something on it that glitters and sparkles in the light.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Deciphering the Glyphs
>
> Without a check, characters can see that the stelae in this section of the room appear to be nearly identical, though age has worn away symbols in the markings on each.
>
> This puzzle is solved using the same mechanics and rules laid out in the [[Lightless Halls]] section above, requiring characters to discern the meanings of the following glyphs in left-to-right order.

| Pos. | Checks | Exact | Approximate | Incorrect |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Wilderness (DC undefined)****Knowledge: Machines** | "Protect" | "Shield" | "Imprison" |
| 2 | **Awareness (DC undefined)****Knowledge: Celestials** | "Light" | "Warmth" | "Eye" |
| 3 | **Arcana (DC undefined)****Knowledge: Fiends** | "Future" | "Next" | "Forward" |
| 4 | **Deception (DC undefined)****Knowledge: Shent** | "Dreams" | "Goal" | "Sleep" |
| 5 | **Awareness (DC undefined)****Knowledge: Forensics** | "Search" | "Quest" | "Hide" |
| 6 | **Arcana (DC undefined)****Knowledge: Gods** | "Lantyr" | "Mother" | "Torch" |
| 7 | **Wilderness (DC undefined)****Knowledge: Fey** | "Life" | "Grow" | "Shrub" |

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Second Keyphrase
>
> The puzzle is correctly solved by activating the following runes in the order **5-6-2-1-3-7-4** producing the following phrase in ancient Pathward.
>
> > Search Lantyr's light, protect future life dreams.
>
> A character who succeeds on an **Awareness (DC 25)** check may deduce the correct sequence using subtle clues in the ligatures of each glyph which imply how they are intended to connect.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As before, the light barrier pulses powerfully, harmonizing with the rhythm of the ancient glyphs. The barrier fades away, leaving behind a gentle whisper which you understand to mean:
>
> > The guiding the light of Lantyr will shelter our bodies and shield our dreams.

## The Sun Star

The artifact protected behind the puzzles and Light Barriers in this room is one of the First Giant's last great creations, the [[Sun Star]]. This item is a portable device that contains immense radiant energy capable of holding back Abyssal energy and [[Abyssal Darkness]]. It is a key item that is required for the remainder of the [[Lightless Halls]] and also the [[Bastion Apex]] ahead.

When the party reaches the ornate stone altar in his room, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Before you stands a massive stone altar adorned with exquisite carvings and patterns. Now that the barriers of light have been deactivated, you can clearly see a glittering object radiating intense energy sitting atop the altar.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Sun Star
>
> Any character who makes a successful `[[/check arcana 15]]` check or or otherwise successfully identifies the item determines that the glittering [[Sun Star]] is an artifact of great magic, which brings light to Abyssal darkness.
>
> The Sun Star can be carefully picked up from the altar at any time. Only one person can hold the Sun Star at a time.
>
> #### Examining the Altar
>
> Any character who takes a closer look at the altar and makes a successful `[[/check history 15]]` check finds a pattern in the inscriptions that they recognize from ancient texts, translating to "light of sun."
>
> If characters have identified a similar inscription on the Stone Bowl at the end of the [[Void Bridge]] or elsewhere in the area, they know it is the same inscription without a check.

#### Work In Progress

Application of the light generated by the [[Sun Star]] is not yet automated for the character which acquires it. As the Gamemaster you must configure that character's token to emit light with the following configuration:

- Dim = 30 feet
- Bright = 15 feet
- Attenuation = 1.0
- Luminosity = 1.0
- Color = #fedb90
- Alpha = 0.5
- Priority = 1 (this is important!)

>
