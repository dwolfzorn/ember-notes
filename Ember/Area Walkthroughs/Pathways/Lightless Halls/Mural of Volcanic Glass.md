# Mural of Volcanic Glass

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A glittering bridge of light stretches across the room to a plinth on the other side, where a beautiful mural made of volcanic glass awaits. Flanking the bridge are a number of massive statues of giants, standing as if on guard against intruders. Their features are set in grim and unyielding expressions with downturned mouths, while their eyes are fixed upon the bridge itself.

The statues in this room are more than decorative; they guard the mural and require anyone crossing the Light Bridge to the mural to show proper deference. If characters do not figure out how to proceed correctly, they will trigger a violent response from the guarding statuary.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### A Hum of Warning
>
> As the characters approach the bridge, any character with a `[[/skill perception 15 passive format=long]]` or who makes a successful **Awareness (DC 13)** check notices a faint humming sound, and senses a prickle of energy against their skin.
>
> A further success on a **Arcana (DC 16)** check reveals that the hum is a form of arcane energy that resonates from the statues which line the edges of the bridge.
>
> - **Knowledge: Rituals**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
>
> As characters move further toward or onto the bridge of light, the sound grows in volume, becoming louder and louder. It only subsides if they back away from the bridge or proceed with their hands clasped and heads bowed in deference.

The statues here are eternal guards who watch over the room and the path towards the mural ahead.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Observing the Statues
>
> Any character who makes a successful `[[/check investigation 15]]` check notices that while the statues each have different expressions, all have two notable aspects in common: their hands clasped and their heads bowed.
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Critical Success**: The character reasons from the statues' positioning that anyone crossing the bridge must clasp their hands and bow their heads in order to cross safely.
>
> Meanwhile, any character who makes a successful `[[/check insight 15]]` check gets the sense that the statues are watching and judging to see what anyone approaching the bridge will do.
>
> - **Knowledge: Artifacts**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Light Blast
>
> If any character attempts to walk across the Light Bridge without bowing their heads and clasping their hands together, read the following:
>
> > The eyes of every statue spring to life, blazing with an intense light. Powerful light beams streak out and blast the bridge in front of you.
>
> Any characters on the light bridge takes `[[/damage 1d4 radiant]]` and must also make a `[[/save dex 16]]` saving throw, taking `[[/damage 2d8 radiant]]` on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one. All characters on the light bridge are also pushed 10 feet toward the edge of the Light Bridge that is closest to them. This may send them over the edge.
>
> #### Void Space
>
> If any party member falls off the sides of the platforms or is pushed during a combat encounter, they fall down and into the glittering void of lights at the bottom of the chamber. They end up in the [[Containment Chamber]], and upon arrival, suffer the ill effects specified on that page.

## The Volcanic Mural

The panel at the end of this room is a massive mural of interlocking panels of primordial volcanic glass depicting renditions of giants, the [[Pathways]], and a towering figure even greater than the figures around them. If anyone approaches and begins studying the mural in front of them, read the following:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> A huge mural of incredibly detailed figures and pictograms stands before you. The stones of the mural appear to be made of a glittering black volcanic glass that holds no runes or markings, as if it was formed separately from the stonework around you. The mural holds a mix of images — figures performing rituals with their faces illuminated by ceremonial fire, a central towering figure that makes its way through an ethereal landscape, smaller figures being guided through twisting passages of water, fire, and air.

The puzzle in front of the party is a complex mechanical lock that seals the [[Treasure Room]] door in the [[Void Bridge]] chamber. The party must piece together several aspects of ancient First Giant lore in order to solve the puzzle.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Studying the Volcanic Mural
>
> Any character with a `[[/check perception passive 17 format=long]]` or who makes a successful `[[/check perception 15]]` check notices faint lines in the mural, as if it is arranged from separate pieces, and a keyhole in the center of the mural, below the feet of the center figure.
>
> Meanwhile, any character who studies the mural in detail and makes a successful `[[/check investigation 15]]` check can see that the mural is mounted on a circular rotating mechanism that consists of interlocking panels that can be rearranged in any desired sequence. The central figure appears set in place, but the other five images appear to have gaps between them that would allow them to be pulled out of their current position and placed in a new order. There are also five large crystals embedded in the mural in a circular pattern around the central panel — one for each of the movable pieces. The crystal in the panel at the bottom right corner of the mural glows ever-so-slightly.
>
> - **Knowledge: Machines**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.
> - **Knowledge: Subterranea**: The character gains **+2 Boons** on this check.

### The Mural Panels

To solve the mural puzzle, characters must place the story told by the mural in the correct order. Each piece of the mural can be investigated independently.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### The Five Panels
>
> When characters enter the room, the panels are arranged in the order 2-3-4-1-5 and the crystal in the fifth panel is glowing at half brightness. Panels may be moved by switching them with each other. Once all five panels are set:
>
> - When a crystal glows at half-brightness, it is in the right order, but one or both of the panels next to it are in the incorrect order.
> - When a crystal glows at full brightness, it is in the right order and both panels next to it are also in the correct order.
>
> The notes for each panel below indicate the position that the panel begins in, as well as where the panel appears in the correct final order.

#### Interactable Panels

Later in Ember development, these panels will be Interactable Objects. For now, the movement of panels and glow of the crystals should be handled by the Gamemaster via narration and theater of the mind.

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### The Glowing Orb
>
> > This panel depicts swirling energies and elements, a chaotic blend of fire, water, earth, and air combined in a cosmic dance. In the center, a glowing orb is surrounded by powerful figures that appear to represent the forces of life and the elements.
>
> This panel represents The Creation of the [[World of Ember]]. It begins in **fourth position**. Its correct placement is in **first position.**

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Robed Figure In The Mist
>
> > Crafted with intricate detail, this panel portrays a solitary cowled figure cloaked in a deep half robe, with massive, elongated arms emerging from swirling mist. Energy radiates from beneath the hood, and they stand with hands open in supplication.
>
> This panel represents the Birth of the Cowled Figure. It begins in **first position**. Its correct placement is in **second position.**

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Figures of Stone and Earth
>
> > This panel features the central cowled figure in the process of creating smaller humanoid figures from the stone and earth around them. Although much smaller, these figures are still colossal in their own right.
>
> This panel represents the Making of the Giants. It begins in **second position.** Its correct placement is in **third position.**

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### Many Passageways
>
> > In this panel, the twisting shapes of the Pathways are clearly visible as the cowled figure and its creations craft passageways and tunnels beneath the earth. These Pathways have faint energy lines that emanate from them like a lifeforce. Around the edges of the panel are scenes of trade and communication, of cities growing and civilization spreading.
>
> This panel represents the Shaping of the Pathways. It begins in **third position**. Its correct placement is in **fourth position.**

> [!tip] Exploration
> #### A City Grows
>
> > This panel portrays a magnificent city in its prime, with grand structures crafted from blocky shapes reminiscent of the structure you are standing in, but its heights defy the laws of physics. Sections of the buildings float in the air, and towers twist and spiral into a sky, all within a massive domed chamber next to a radiating orb of power and energy.
>
> This panel represents the Crafting of the Forgotten City. It begins in the **fifth position**, which is its **correct placement.**

### A Secret Compartment

With the panels properly aligned and the crystals activated, a secret compartment opens, revealing a key shaped like a winding path. Players must insert this key into the central keyhole in the mural.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> As the key is inserted, the chamber begins to rumble, and a deep grinding sound starts to build. The grinding sound intensifies and resonates throughout the halls, drowning out everything else, its sound like the relentless scraping of stone against stone. Small stones and debris fall from above as stone slabs shift, and finally, a deep booming sound rings out from somewhere else within the bastion. When the final echo of the boom ends, the halls are silent once again.
