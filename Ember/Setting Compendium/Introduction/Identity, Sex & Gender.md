# Identity, Sex & Gender

The Ember Setting is fantasy. Imaginative, wondrous fantasy, and it is doing its best to create something new while paying homage to older ideas and concepts. The world of Ember is massive, and features a variety of cultures with differing beliefs that may be controversial, some of the ideas and beliefs held by certain cultures are not universal. This game may, at times, present themes of hatred and intolerance that may affect certain characters based on their sex, gender, or other aspects of their identity. When dealing with topics of this nature it is important to be clear that the ember team does not condone nor accept such intolerant viewpoints; rather, we want to confront these topics head-on.

The Ember Setting as a whole is not built upon modern oppositional ideas surrounding identity, sex, or gender. Racism, homophobia, and transphobia do not exist in the same way in Ember as they do in our world. In fact, across almost every culture in the setting, there is no societal hatred nor persecution of anyone perceived as "different". The setting has been carefully constructed from the ground up with these things in mind.

The massive variety of Ancestries means that any differences in physical appearance simply aren't seen the same way as it is on Earth. Racism does not exist in Ember. People see one another as people or mortals rather than as their specific ancestry, and there is a shared understanding of what it means to struggle in a world filled with monsters and chaos. This goes a step further with the Aster, who, while rarer among the world's cultures and peoples, are never, in any case, considered oddities or different in any negative way but are seen as blessed by the Heart of Ember. Tensions related to Ancestry that may appear have far more to do with cultural conflict than mere appearance.

Homophobia does not exist in Ember; lacking earth's post-puritanical concepts of sexuality, love between consenting partners is viewed as part of the societal norm, regardless of gender. This draws inspiration from many ancient earth cultures. Some cultures of Ember have patriarchal societies but also matriarchal ones as well with most cultures having no particular bias or inequality between genders. Transphobia does not exist in Ember; many cultures have widely-celebrated heroes or deities have that have either transitioned or present genderless identities. Most notable among these is [[Alar]], who is principally the Goddess of Creation and the most powerful being in the Cosmos.

With this in mind, stories in Ember that present queer issues do so not as struggles against systemic oppression and prejudice, as this is something that doesn't exist except in extremely specific, more character-driven examples, instead and unfortunately for them, there are simply many other struggles in Ember that people need to overcome.

There are areas of Ember where hatred and intolerance ***do exist*** among people who have deeply rooted historical biases for specific groups. One example of such is the prejudice among many cultures toward the [[Aedir]] who caused the calamity known as the Shattering. There are other more modern examples as well; for instance, in the present, as the Tayan Empire is attempting to conquer the Kalmezian City States there is dislike and even hatred held between the peoples of both cultures. Overall, however, discrimination stems from the conflict between peoples, cultures, and individuals whose actions are their own and specific to Ember.

For those Gamemasters who wish to engage with topics of hatred and prejudice in Ember, it is important to remember that people do not form hatred in a vacuum. Hatred and prejudice are learned behaviors, and they do not start on a global or societal level. The average person in a world like Ember does not care about wider global sociopolitical issues as they are not directly affected by them, they care more about issues that have a direct impact on their own lives.
