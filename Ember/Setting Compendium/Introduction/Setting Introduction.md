# Setting Introduction

## Manifest Deities

The Ember Setting is filled with hundreds of Ascendant Deities called Shard Gods, who walk upon the surface world and live among the mortal peoples. They are very real, tangible beings with huge impacts as they express their ideas, fight among themselves, and change the outcomes of events around them. Some Shard Gods are truly ancient, having existed since the first peoples arose on Ember. Others are incredibly young and have ascended much more recently. Every deity is a complex character with their own motivations and goals, and they are just as fallible and susceptible to mistakes and failures as the mortals around them are.

Even the Elder Gods of the Cosmos, of which only five exist, reside within the cosmos itself and walk the surface talking to mortals, maintaining their ideas of Cosmic Balance and safeguarding the world.

## Singular Cosmos

The Ember Cosmos is exactly as described: a singular Cosmos where everything exists in Physical Space. There are no other planes of existence, parallel universes, or dimensions. This means that everything can be reached through conventional travel or teleportation magic, even the most esoteric and mysterious realms of Ember such as the Spiritlands, where a mortal Soul travels to when they die, or the wonderous and deadly Inner Realms that rest upon the inner surface of the Weave.

## The World Within

The Ember Cosmos is like a multifaceted gem. It has a protective bubble called the Weave that shields everything within from the horrors and madness of the Abyss. At the center of the Cosmos is the World of Ember, where numerous cultures, mortal peoples, and deities live and survive. Then, deep within the world itself resides the Heart of Ember, some say the entity or object that created the cosmos long ago.

## Portentous History

Many cultures on Ember today have histories dating back two-and-a-half thousand years, either before or after the Shattering — when the colossal Aedir Empire ruled over vast territories and built the astounding structure of the Sun Tower before its cataclysmic destruction. However, the history of Ember stretches back long before the Aedir. Indeed, Ember's oldest cultures have histories that reach back into a past so deep and unknowable, all that remains are the myths and legends of the forgotten people who founded them.

## Breathtaking Scale

Ember is a very different kind of setting that offers characters new definitions for discovery and scale. As the world's six moons drift across the sky, the colossal Broken Tower looms above everything — visible no matter where one might be on the surface of the living planet. Even Ember's ancestries and cultures are all unique to the setting, and are deeply tied to its vast history, innovative worldbuilding, and imaginative storytelling. Everywhere you look, the world of Ember serves as a potent reminder about precisely how fantastical and wondrous a setting can be.

## New Horrors

During the Abyssal Shear that occurred not long after the Shattering, the protective barrier of the Weave was breached, allowing the maddening and terrifying influence of the Abyss to flow into the Cosmos directly. This event was arguably even worse for the Cosmos than the preceding Shattering, and Abyssal Monsters were able to spread across the cosmos physically for the first time. The Abyssal Shear also introduced entirely new concepts into the Cosmos, such as Undeath and Oblivion. After the Shear, Dragons, terrifying scaled monsters linked intrinsically to abyssal energy, were created and have ravaged the cosmos ever since.

## Magical Omnipresence

Magic is everywhere in the Ember Setting; it is so common, depending on certain circumstances, that it can be seen crystallized into visible motes that drift through the air. There are many types of Magic, from the Lifeforce of the Heart of Ember, the mystical magic of Light, Darkness, and Shadow of the three Inner Realms, the elemental magic directed from the Elemental Moons, and even the combinations of these forces known as Arcane magic.
