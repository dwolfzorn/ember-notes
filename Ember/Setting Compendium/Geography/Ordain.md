# Ordain

- Keywords: Grand, Beautiful, Peaceful
- Dominant Ancestry: [[Human]]
- Secondary Ancestries: [[Kivahr]], [[Vrjnhar]], [[Keth]], [[Hulg'run]], [[Ashka]]
- Technology: Late Bronze Age, early iron/steel production, enhanced with magic
- Religion: [[Cindaric Sages]], [[The Nineteen]].
- Politics: [[Ordinate]], Shared Power between the [[Cindaric Sages]], Trading Houses, and [[Hallows]].
- Architecture: Rounded red rooftops, glittering white stone with faint rain staining and red foliage, black volcanic rock foundations.
- Settlement Type: Metropolis
- Population: 575,000

Ordain is a vast and diverse city that has grown quickly into a powerhouse of influence in the eastern known world. Its various political frameworks are intentionally designed to ensure stability both in the short and long term, supported by a responsible and proactive population committed to maintaining order. As a result, the city is safe, peaceful, and often focused more on local matters such as trade disputes, taxes, construction, and the social well-being of its citizens rather than any expansionist or militaristic matters.. There is minimal interest in allocating the city's resources or focus to external issues, and the city government almost never interferes with other nations’ problems or issues, except in specific compassionate circumstances, such as providing assistance after monster attacks or natural disasters.

## Governance & Laws

Ordain has three major political groups: the [[Cindaric Sages]], the Trading Houses, ([[House Bastilla]], [[House Cevher]], [[House Lilifeld]], [[House Salva]] & [[House Wandren]]) and the [[Hallows]], with elected representatives sitting on the governing body called the [[Ordinate]] or the Ordinate Council. There are also many other organizations that make up the city, such as the [[Anachraenum]], the [[Veiled Chain]], and the [[Burnished Hand]], and these groups also follow the city's overarching rule of law. Fundamentally, the city operates under a strange system of representative democracy, where the three major factions elect three representatives to the Ordinate, resulting in nine members who legislate, adjudicate legal conflicts, and manage the city’s overall budget, which includes managing taxes across the city.

Traditionally, this sharing of power across different groups has led to a highly stable government, as no one group or individual is ever in a position to enforce unjust or disruptive laws alone. This, however, does not mean that conflict between the groups and their representatives does not exist, as discussions often flare into long, heated arguments and political maneuvering to sway others to certain causes. As the Trading House representatives are often solely concerned with the city's economy and their own houses' wealth, they are frequently at the forefront of increasing tax discussions, while the Hallows, which represents the ordinary people of the city, are vehemently opposed. The Cindaric Sages representatives are often neutral in these matters and are swayed or convinced through various means.

The process of electing representatives differs for each of the major groups. For the Trading Houses, each greatly desires a representative to be elected from their own house, and every five years, intense campaigns erupt behind closed doors among the Trading Houses' members in the Assembly on [[Assembly Hills]]. Representatives are chosen through a series of votes, with the top three candidates receiving the most votes being elected. In contrast, the Hallows also hold elections for three members every five years, but the general populace of the city votes for candidates in very public elections where political campaigning is heavily regulated so that it is as fair and open as possible. It is deemed highly inappropriate for Hallows representatives to highlight their individual character traits, and instead, they tend to focus on appearing connected and compassionate with people's current concerns as possbile in order to garner the most votes. Finally, Cindaric Sage representatives are chosen from among their own members in secret, and in most cases, unless it would otherwise cause dissent, the Holy Speaker appoints three of the most competent individuals, who sit on the Ordinate for a decade.

Once the ruling council of the Ordinate has eventually agreed upon a new law, it's the responsibility of the various other groups in the city to enforce it. This is also aided by the city’s overall high degree of societal well-being and community involvement, which means ordinary citizens often take the law seriously and help with enforcement on an individual level. Several of the city’s organizations are also key parts of the process, such as the [[Veiled Chain]], which ensures threats like violent crime or organized crime are investigated, and the Hallows itself, where people are taken to be judged and sentenced for any crimes they commit. Other organizations, such as the [[Anachraenum]], the [[Burnished Hand]], and the guards of the various Trading Houses, act in official capacities when required to arrest and maintain order directly on the streets of the city, although this is rare.

Ordain enforces a structured yet measured system of justice, guided by the Hallows and overseen by the Ordinate. Punishments emphasize restitution and civic responsibility over cruelty, though grave offenses are met with severity.

- **Theft and Fraud:** Offenders are required to return stolen property or its full value and serve a period of compulsory community labor.
- **Assault:** Penalties range from home or Hallows detention to formal trial and restitution, depending on the severity of the act.
- **Misuse of Magic:** Charges are determined by the nature of the offense. Offenders may be temporarily restrained under abjuration wards and held in Spellbreaker Tower or face standard criminal proceedings.
- **Necromancy:** Outright forbidden within the city. Practitioners face imprisonment within the Spellbreaker Tower or exile pending trial.
- **Disturbing the Peace:** Typically punished with fines and, in repeated or severe cases, a short-term peace-binding enchantment such as *geas* or *command.*
- **Smuggling:** Confiscation of goods and fines equivalent to their market value are standard. Repeat offenders risk exile following trial.
- **Murder or Attempted Murder:** Tried before the highest Hallows courts. Conviction carries long-term Spellbreaker imprisonment or, in exceptional cases, capital punishment.
- **Violation of City Ordinance:** First offenses receive formal warnings; subsequent violations result in fines or enforced community labor. Severe or repeated breaches may lead to trial and exile.
- **Vandalism:** Offenders must repair or pay for damages and serve community labor. Repeat or malicious cases may result in enchantment bindings or exile.

## Magic & Technology

In Ordain, magic is an intrinsic part of life, embraced by the people in everyday life without any formal structures such as schools or strict academic institutions. Instead, the practice and understanding of magic is shared among individuals. Magical items and objects are widespread across the city as Ordain imports large quantities of magical tools or objects from cultures such as the [[Lumek]], the [[Rhivan]], or the[[Kithil]]. Markets are filled with merchants showcasing magically enhanced goods, tools, and even weaponry. Finding someone within the city who can help with a magical problem is not difficult, as several shops and stores run by wizards or mages exist throughout the various districts, trading in services and goods to help with everyday issues.

Finding more advanced magical aid is slightly more challenging but follows the same pattern. Throughout the city are various powerful entities that may be able to assist with a dire situation or problem. The resident Custodian Star Mage of Ordain, [[Eveis Brightstone]], is one such entity. She is responsible for the APN connection and resides in Lumiere Wharf. Eveis is primarily concerned with the maintenance, protection, and use of the teleportation gate, but they are known to help those in great need occasionally. Other powerful magic users include several of the high priests of the larger temples in the [[Temple Ward]] district, and the Curator of the Anachraenum[[Adelyne Goss]].

## Culture & Society

Culture in Ordain is overwhelmingly [[Ordani]], with communities of other cultures scattered throughout the various districts. While Ordain as a city and the Ordani are extremely supportive of other cultures, and the city is a diverse multicultural hub to some degree, other cultures tend to be largely self-contained, and the dominance of the Ordani way of life is evident across the city as a whole. This means that Ordani festivals or holidays, such as the Bloodruby Burning, Seasons End, Worker's Day, and Hearts Feast, are celebrated throughout the city regardless of cultural background. Additionally, other cultures adopt Ordani cultural practices, including strong social interactions and interest in various clothing and shopping trends.

Within the city, there is a strong emphasis on community involvement, with festivals, markets, and a vibrant nightlife being crucial social events where citizens connect, share ideas, and celebrate. Culinary arts and diverse dishes are an integral part of Ordani culture, influenced by traders and travelers. Local cuisine prioritizes fresh ingredients and exhibits bold spices or sweetness, with creative and interesting new concepts emerging constantly. Communal meals are common, where families and friends gather in restaurants or outside brasseries to share dishes and drinks that highlight flavors, colors, and the occasional touch of magic, such as foods that may glitter under moonlight or change flavor.

Perhaps the most important cultural touchstone of Ordain is the almost omnipresent fascination among the Ordani with clothing and fashion. To the Ordani, attire is not merely a matter of practicality or aesthetics—it is the purest expression of identity, artistry, and social philosophy. Every fold, color, and fabric carries meaning, communicating profession, faith, or mood with subtle precision. Styles shift with the seasons and with political or spiritual trends, and the skilled artisans who craft garments are held in the highest regard, their creations treated as both personal statements and living art.

Religion in Ordain is centered around the teachings of the [[Cindaric Sages]], a predominantly passive belief system that traditionally works seamlessly with the Ordani lifestyle. The Cindaric Sages do not attempt to convert or convince others; instead, helping and supporting the people of Ordain is seen as their primary duty. In addition, many residents worship various individual deities from [[The Nineteen]], a pantheon of deities representing various aspects of life, nature, and magic that are all accepted and followed by people in the eastern regions of the known world.

## Landmarks & Districts

Ordain is a vast city spread over multiple miles of the Arctus Plateau and encompassing over fifty distinct districts. These districts vary significantly, with many serving specific functions, such as being the home territory of one of the Trading Houses or the base of operations for an organization like the [[All-Fable Keep]] for the Anchraenum. Some districts are dedicated to trade, featuring warehouses, storerooms, granaries, and transport offices, while others cater to residential life or reflect Ordain's passion for clothing, offering countless shops, eateries, and venues for socializing over fine wines in the evenings. The river Destine runs through the middle of the city and flows into the Bay of Ordain, and many of the districts next to the river feature large, expansive canal systems to move people, goods, and services quickly and efficently.

Beneath the city, Ordain has miles of sprawling sewers, drainage channels, and waterways carved deep into the black basalt rock foundations. These are far removed from the traditional, dirty, and foul-smelling sewers found elsewhere in the world; they are largely clean, massive in size and depth, and are maintained by a combination of manual labor and magic by the city's inspectors, who are employed by the Hallows.

Ordain is not only home to thousands of houses and everyday buildings but also to a number of colossal mega structures that took years or even centuries to complete. These include the two largest structures in the city, the [[The Ordinate]], which stands atop the city's central plateau, and the [[Cindarin Temple]], the home of the Cindaric Sages and the center for the religion. Other large and impressive structures include the vast All-Fable Keep at the southern end of the city, home to the Anachraenum, the [[The Hallows]] complex in the north of the city, and [[Spellbreaker Tower]], the city's prison facility in the west.

## Currency & Economy

The majority of Ordain's economy relies on the Trading Houses and their impressive holdings across the [[Arctus Plateau]] and beyond. Within Ordain itself, a vast array of valuable goods is produced through local manufacturing in workshops and specific districts, which transform raw materials into luxury items. Key exports and items include exquisite textiles and clothing, high-quality ceramics, and finely crafted jewelry, which are highly sought after in distant markets and within the city itself. Additionally, the city is famous for its spices and herbs, known for their unique flavors and medicinal properties, gathered from across the world and refined to create a rich culinary landscape. Ordain is also home to many forges and smithies in the northern part of the city, which gather and refine precious metals and gemstones mined from nearby and distant regions.

Ordain is a massive importer of raw foodstuffs to feed its large population and has a strong trading relationship with nearby [[Arcturian]] settlements such as Toraga, which exports significant quantities of wheat and barley, as well as the [[Kithil]] city of Luen, which exports large quantities of fruits and vegetables to Ordain. The city also imports goods such as fine wines from Kortos Isle vineyards, luxury crafted wooden and stone items from various Arcturian settlements, and technological innovations from other cultures, such as the [[Tayan]] Empire.

Ordain's five coins are considered the standardized currency not only in Ordain or the Arctus Plateau but also in the Lowlands, amongst the Kithil, and traded in large quantities in many other nations, kingdoms, and cultures in far-off places.
