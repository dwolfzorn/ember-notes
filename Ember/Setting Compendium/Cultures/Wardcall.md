## The Frozen Oath

The Wardcall is the name given to a loose alliance of three kingdoms, Wintec, Katic, and Pertta, bound under the rule of High King Rathern Orian. Though outsiders often speak of the Wardcall as a single realm, its people rarely do. Wintarins, Katins, and Perttins are distinctly different in various subtle ways that outsiders often overlook, such as their regional accents and armor styles. It is considered deeply offensive to confuse someone with a member of another kingdom.

The people of the Wardcall are known for their restraint and ceremony, their strict sense of order, and their unshakable belief that endurance is a virtue above all others. To them, honor is not glory; it is survival. Much of their strength lies in the Ironfrost Templars, who hold the northern border keeps against the Vartic Clans, Skinchangers, and horrific monsters that roam beyond the Frostline. To many outside the Wardcall, they are the steadfast guardians of the north: disciplined, solemn, incorruptible. Yet those who have traveled their border towns and seen their forges at night speak differently.

The three kingdoms and the Ironfrost Templars have access to strange armor technology called Wardskin. This skin-tight armor covers the wearer completely up to the neck and hisses steam in the cold air. How it is made is not openly spoken of, though the process is said to take place in the Machinarims, temples of fire and steam that outsiders are forbidden to enter. There are stories, half-remembered and quickly dismissed, that the forges of the Wardcall burn hotter than metal alone can sustain.

Forty-three years have passed since Blood Binding, which saw the Wardcall region ripped apart in a bloody civil war and raised Rathern Orian to his throne. His victory ended the feuds of the old kings and border barons, and his peace has held since, but only barely. As the High King ages, questions of succession draw old tensions to the surface once more. The border keeps are restless. The temples to the Trine of Wards whisper, and the sharp blades so often turned north towards the monsters and Skinchangers may soon turn inward again.

The Wardcall’s official faith, the Trine of Wards, claims absolute religious authority across the three kingdoms. Its hidden temples, the Machinarims, dominate both the spiritual and industrial life of the north, where the sacred and the mechanical are bound together. Ward Clerics oversee every ritual and sermon, patrolling towns, keeps, and cities to root out doubt and deviation. Their presence is constant, watchful eyes beneath iron masks, ensuring obedience through faith and fear. Yet the Trine’s dominion is far from complete. Across the frozen lands, many still whisper prayers to the Gods of the [[Auris Bor]], the ancient pantheon of the north, and their forebears, the Bejak. Among these, [[Themis]] is one of the few [[Shard Gods]] still spoken of, though never publicly worshipped. To the Trine, Themis is a dangerous influence, inspiring vigilante acts and personal vendettas under the guise of divine judgment.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Related Cultures
>
> Historically, the Wardcall kingdoms were founded by a series of [[Bejak]] Kivahr families who traveled east into the snowy mountains. Despite the historical link, the harsh winters and fear of monsters have molded the Wardcall into a very different culture from the Bejak, and they maintain a distance that is only bridged by shared trade. In all, the Wardcall have few friends that they can rely upon nearby which forces a degree of isolation.
