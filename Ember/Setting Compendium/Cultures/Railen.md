## Zealous Conquerors

Martial power, strength, and displays of fighting prowess are highly respected traits among the Railen, who are a highly homogeneous society; they are taught from birth to revere cultural isolation. The Railen express a deep sense of superiority over other cultures and a strong belief that their ideas and way of life are the greatest in the entire world. However, there is some small amount of ancestral diversity within the culture, with humans and other ancestors of various cultural backgrounds present in Ancalen. And despite these other ancestries being treated as second-class citizens in many cases, the Railen people are not outwardly or obviously bigoted. Instead, Altyran Railen are provided more respect and positions of authority in governance, military, and commerce. Many Railen owe this privilege to a deep, nearly sub-conscious belief that their connection to Luxarum — and the Light of the Tyraphem has given the Altyran people greater abilities along with the very responsibility to achieve greatness.

The Ekat is a group of one hundred of the most powerful members of Ancalen society, and are easily recognized by the enchanted armor they wear, known as Lightplates. These Lightplates are a singular relic split among the one hundred Ekat users. In preparation for their inevitable death, a Lightplate wearer will do everything possible to "retire" their armor and pass it to the next worthy warrior. Lightplates are smooth, clean, and seamless in appearance, which gives the Ekat members an almost otherworldly quality while wearing them. Lightplates are also marked with designs that clearly state the wearer's current rank among the Ekat. It's understood that the Lightplates were a gift from the Tyraphem of Luxaraum at some point (at least, the Railen themselves suspeect this is the case). The only way to advance among the Ekat ranks is to defeat a member at a higher rank; and while such clashes are rarely deadly among lower tiers of Ekat, higher-rank conflicts are intense, brutal, and occasionally deadly.

The most powerful members of Railen culture are the Triarcs — Szure, Tazarl, and Lucon — the three highest-ranking members of the Ekat and the principal rulers of the Luminous City. In reality, the Triarcs are all Shard Gods, siblings that ascended together somewhat recently (around six hundred years ago) and have been solely responsible for guiding the Ancalen culture ever since. In addition to their martial strength, each member of the Triarchy is also worshiped by Railen society, as they represent the pinnacle of their militaristic traditions and cultural beliefs.
