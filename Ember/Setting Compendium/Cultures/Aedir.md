## Vestiges of the Aedir

The Aedir, also called the Aedir Empire or the Sunfire Empire, was an ancient civilization that collapsed two and a half thousand years ago at the end of the Age of the Tower. Despite the passage of time, the name Aedir remains widely recognized across the peoples of the Aterican continent. This is due to the lasting impact that the Aedir had on many foundational cultures, but also because their once crowning achievement, the colossal Sun Tower—now known as the Broken Tower—which still dominates the horizon from nearly every point on Ember's surface.

> Common Cascilian phrase, while sailing in the direction of the Broken Tower, "**Lightbreakers**." followed by spitting to one side.

Some cultures view the Aedir as conquerors, others as sages who guided their people in their early days, but, generally, the events of The Shattering and the breaking of the world in its aftermath have led many to view the Aedir in a negative light, even after thousands of years. According to myth, the Aedir tried to build the Sun Tower to ascend into the heavens, commune directly with Lantyr, and harness her power. However, their pride and arrogance blinded them, and the tower became unstable, ultimately destroying itself. This event is called *The Shattering* because of the cataclysmic destruction of the Aedir capital of Towir and the moon Akon, which was destroyed by a piece of the tower in the following days. Many scholars and historians claim that this is not the full story, and it's almost impossible to determine what really happened; nevertheless, the people of the world believe that the Aedir deserved their terrible fate.

The term 'Aedir, was used for a sinulgur continuous culture spanning nearly ten thousand years, despite the significant changes within that culture. During the early years of the Age of the Tower, the Aedir were remarkably peaceful, gradually expanding their civilization across the Starshield. They only left their shores after several thousand years. However, several events began to alter their society, most notably the mythical [[Forsaken War]], which propelled them toward a more militant and aggressive empire. As they began construction of the Sun Tower, their culture was considered powerful but highly fractured by factional noble houses and interests—some advocating wisdom and peace, others pushing for war and conquest. In reality, the Aedir lacked a true centralized ruler, despite having a powerful imperial family residing on the Starshield. Despite this, by the end of the Age of the Tower and as the Sun Tower neared completion, the Aedir had already earned a negative reputation as conquerors, which was often true, as some of its factions openly dominated nations across the world and were attacking the Oaken in a large-scale campaign at the time of the Shattering.

Today, ruins and relics of the Aedir remain scattered across the shores and lands of the Aterican supercontinent and in many other distant places, such as Kessia and the shores of Jurin. Many Aedir structures on Aterica were left abandoned by their peoples, who fled the horrors of the Abyssal Shear, while others are fallen chunks of the Sun Tower itself that fell back to the surface world after the Shattering.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Related Cultures
>
> The Aedir encountered many other cultures and peoples as they settled and explored the world. Some earned their deep respect, while others, like the young Kelmezians, were seen as immature and needing guidance. The Oaken culture was already ancient and well-established from the Aedir's perspective. Initially, the two cultures were quite friendly upon meeting, with details of their long relationship reserved for another time. However, at some point, relations deteriorated, leading to a brutal war that lasted several centuries. This conflict only ended after the Aedir were wiped out during the Shattering. Other ancient cultures, such as the Shent, also held little love for the Aedir, maintaining distance despite a tentative peace between them. It's also thought that the Aedir subjugated the last remaining holdout of the Sedyri, but very little information has survived on the subject.

## Identity

Aedir were said to be extremely positive, wildly extroverted, and capable of pushing themselves and others to new heights. This often meant others saw them as arrogant and untroubled by consequences, and there are numerous stories of Aedir who got themselves into dangerous situations. This viewpoint is often reinforced by decisions the Aedir made that affected cultures and peoples without seemingly considering the consequences. There are even myths and legends about the Aedir that have been passed down as fables that teach moral lessons, and it's common for the Aedir to appear foolish or unthinking and would often meet justified ends. This, however, is not supported by the few historical records that have survived, and the Aedir were rarely caught off guard; in fact, they are recorded as unusually capable, powerful, and able to handle almost any situation. This is perhaps why their eventual, cataclysmic fall was so shocking to the world.

## Daily Life

Legends state that the Starshield before the Shattering was a place of complete paradise, where every Aedir lived in titanic mansions, with machine servants of silver and gold that completed every menial task, leaving the people to endlessly practice art, music, and dance. These stories have perhaps a grain of truth, but as an outwardly looking and driven people, tales of endless parties and limitless excess seem more akin to gross exaggeration than truth. Very little accurate information remains about the daily life of the Aedir; however, they were clearly keen traders, builders, and explorers, as evidenced by their massive spread across the world.

## Language

The language of the Aedir was called Towyr, and it was said to be beautiful, extremely fast-paced, and difficult to learn. Speaking Old Towyr is a lost art that has disappeared almost completely from the world. Even historians cannot speak the ancient forms of the language entirely, and only a few words and phrases that are directly translatable have survived.

The “modern” form of Towyr was Imperial Towyr, a later amalgamation created to simplify the language's complexity. It was in use for several thousand years before the Shattering, and many of its words and base letters were adopted by other cultures.
