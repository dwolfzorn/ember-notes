## Vibrant Academics

The Rhivan are a society of scholars, academics, historians, and teachers living in one of the strangest places on the surface of Ember. Most people confuse the Rhivan people with Star Mages, though the latter are a specific organization, and the Rhivan society includes many ordinary individuals. Their population consists of those without magical abilities, as well as those with minor talents or abilities, or those who attended the renowned Star Academy for a few years before leaving for various reasons. Dropping out of the Academy is regarded as a significant achievement, as it recognizes someone’s talent, skill, or drive to reach that stage and be accepted. The Rhivan are a highly expressive and scholarly society that values knowledge and deep thinking across numerous subjects. Besides the magical elements of the Star Academy, libraries, research centers, and knowledge repositories are widespread in Rhivan settlements and cities. Wandering Rhivan scholars are as common as the Star Mages who run the Portal Network.

Despite the focus on learned and academic pursuits, Rhivans are also deeply religious and particularly venerate [[Ven'avé]]& [[Spectra]] with a number of magical ceremonies and customs. The Weaver herself, who is typically never seen anywhere else on Ember, regularly visits and spends time in the Star Academy, and she is even rumored to play a crucial role in Star Mages's graduation. Spectra is another deity that often visits the Star Mages in a far more casual fashion, seen eating, drinking, and arguing about various theories.

One of the most unique challenges the Rhivan must face is their attachment to the ground itself. Living on one of Embers' spines means that the Gravitational line is incredibly weak; even the simple tasks like walking are difficult. The Rhivan have adapted to this problem through various methods, such as wearing specialized metal-weighted boots and enchanted anchors to stay grounded. Large gravitational zones are maintained by Star Mages and arcane machines, allowing the Rhivan people to cultivate food.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Related Cultures
>
> Due to their strong magic, especially the ability to create and maintain a portal network that crisscrosses the world, the Rhivan are connected to almost every culture and have good relations with almost all of them. Most cultures and nations believe the Star Mages of Rhiva positively impact their lives by helping to negotiate trade and impolitic relations, calming tensions or simply sharing news and information. The only culture that truly dislikes the Rhivans is the Cascillians.
