## Velvet Icons

Ordain is one of Ember's great cities, known for its embrace of trade and acceptance of cultures from beyond its borders. The Ordani take pride in their ability and willingness to get along with others, and are always willing to give those they deal with the benefit of the doubt, but they are as shrewd as they are friendly, ensuring that they're getting the best deal they can for themselves and avoiding being taken advantage of in any way.

Bolstered by years of peace, most Ordani believe that they can settle anything, even major conflicts, all on their own. Within Ordain, most districts have groups that are ready to jump into action in the rare times of trouble that disturb the overall peace of the city, handling issues on their own before reaching out to higher authorities. Even outside of Ordain, the cultural value of getting involved and getting things done remains strong.

Ordani pride in their city, while sometimes grating on those from other places, has stopped Ordain from trying to expand its reach beyond its current location — most Ordani are interested more in making Ordain culturally (and financially) richer through deals and trade than in trying to extend their physical borders. That richness, and Ordani pride in the city, are both expressed most clearly through Ordani fashion, which tends to be bold and colorful.

## Customs & Tradition

Shaped by life in one of Ember's safest and most cosmopolitan cities, Ordani pride themselves on being welcoming, warm, and cultured, and are eager to show it. Many know a smattering of words in many different languages and a range of greeting customs, so they can greet strangers in the manner they are accustomed. Similarly, when traveling, Ordani favor cultural exchange when possible, sharing stories of Ordain and asking those whose lands they are visiting about what makes their part of Ember special. Though Ordani are known for talking about their city more than most, even if they haven't lived there in some time, they value treating others with admiration, appreciation, and deep respect, and are generally seen as well-meaning and friendly.

While the Ordani will rush in to help in times of trouble, they prefer to take everything else at a leisurely pace, especially when it comes to shopping, trading, and buying goods. They often ask question after question while shopping, inquiring about the materials made to make an object or its method of construction, and use that information to make the best trade or purchase they can. Somewhat consumerist in nature, Ordani are often keen to trade, viewing the objects they acquire and sell as markers of their cultural exchange with others as well as a way to stay ahead of changing trends and fashions. While this can make them seem acquisitive and shallow to critics, residents of Ordain are used to having many of their basic needs met and being able to express themselves through what they own, wear, and display, and they often see their very visible use of goods as a way to enhance the city's trade position and showcase their relative comfort and peace.

## Religion & Beliefs

The [[Cindaric Sages]] are a major force in the governance of Ordain, providing social services across the city that range from medical care to basic food provisions — all part of their devotion to Ember as a whole. Despite this large presence, following the Cindarics is often thought of as a calling or role as much as it is a religious faith, and is seen as separate and more formal than the devotion or worship any individual might offer to a god of their choosing. It tends to be less popular among Ordani communities outside of the city than it is within Ordain.

With the Cindaric Sages focused on honoring and communing with Ember as a whole, those who follow individual deities generally choose from among [[The Nineteen]], who are well known in the area thanks to the influence of the Arcturians. Religious tolerance is high in Ordain, and worship of nearly and/or all deities is seen as a personal matter, rather than something that outsiders should have an opinion on. Those who do worship within the city are often seen in the [[Numinous Shrines]] district, which has a variety of temples, altars, and shrines. Popular Ordani include [[Sockets]], who has a large shrine dedicated to him in the Temple Ward; [[Lantyr]], who is broadly popular across Ember; and[[Vekalla]], whose blessings are sought by the many sailors and traders that come to and from the port city, as well as those who bring Ordani culture to other cities.

Some might say that the worship of fashion in Ordain almost rises to a religious level among some, and people in the city often refer to the [[Soul of Fashion]] as a concept that falls somewhere between general cultural belief and an actual religious faith. A few take the concept more seriously, seeing the act of wearing fine clothes as a type of religious devotion, but they are relatively rare.

## Clothing & Adornments

Ordani clothes are a vibrant rainbow of color in the midst of the dazzling white of their city's architecture — wearing sashes, robes, and even armor in a variety of bright and bold colors. While the moderate heat of the city often means loose and light garments, Ordani also try to use a variety of fabrics, incorporating materials and designs that come to the city through its trade with other groups and nations. Keeping up with fashion is often seen as a citywide hobby, with local gatherings and assemblies providing as much opportunity to investigate and analyze the fashion choices of their peers as to meet.

When traveling, Ordani are often much more practical, wearing splashes of color in otherwise utilitarian outfits that are focused on the task at hand — and can often be spotted outside of Ordain by the addition of a particularly colorful scarf, hat, or handkerchief in what is an otherwise business-first get-up.

## Cuisine & Diet

There is no lack of intriguing cuisine within Ordain, and its status as a great trading city has allowed those within the city access to a wide variety of foods and ingredients. House Lilifeld, the Ordani trading house responsible for much of the trade of food items in Ordain, is renowned for its chefs, partnering with the seafaring House Bastilla to procure spices and ingredients from across Ember. As a result, Ordani culture tends to prize variety in food, as well as unexpected combinations of cuisines that originate elsewhere and are only made possible because of Ordain's role as a hub city.

As Ordain's Cindaric Sages also provide basic sustenance in the city to those who need it, eating out is fairly common, and those with talent in the kitchen often end up taking their talents to one of the city's many restaurants. Outside of the city, Ordani travelers are known for seeking the most interesting cuisine in the places they visit, and though they sometimes annoy those they encounter with their talk of the food in Ordain, they often single-handedly keep the restaurant industry alive in the places they call home.

## Technology & Magic

As Ordain has continued to grow as a city, it's embraced the use of technology and magic, including those from afar, and is a place where dazzling feats of both artifice and magic can be found. The Ordani are open-minded about it all, willing to use magic or technology across disciplines with no real concerns. The general belief is that if a magical practice or new technology is dangerous in any way, it will be found and handled by either the individual who brings it into the city, their local community, or, if needed, the Hallows, who run the city's inspection and safety departments. This attitude remains even for Ordani outside of the city, who tend to look at technology and magic as opportunities for discovery and trade, even if they are of no direct or immediate use.

## Social Structure & Government

Life in Ordain is overseen by three groups - the Cindaric Sages, the Hallows, and the Trading Houses. The three share power and responsibility for daily life within Ordain, and while they have their power struggles, they generally are able to maintain an even balance of power between them. As a result, the Ordani have fairly high trust in the government to take care of any issues to large to be handled by individuals. Despite the general openness and cultural curiosity of the Ordani, it can be hard for them to relate to the idea of a government that is in opposition to or oppressive of its people, and when found in situations where those in charge oppose their actions or goals, they tend to be more trustful of authority than ready to revolt against it.

## Language & Communication

The traditional greeting of Ordani merchants across the world is:

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > Do you speak the Common Coin my Friend?

This is both a reference to their language but has a double meaning as they are also asking whether someone is happy to trade. Thus, most refer to the Ordani language as Coins but is also known as Common.

The language is derived from the original language of the ancient Kelmezian City States and so at its root, it shares many similarities with most other human-dominated cultures. Unlike other cultures however common is considered a beautiful language with its descriptive and emotional words flowing with singing-like quality whilst more common terms for things have sharper, shorter, and faster notes for easy-to-understand interactions.
