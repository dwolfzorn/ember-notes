## Twisted Heirs

The Nalvani are a people forged in the shadow of ruin. They are among the last heirs of the [[Aedir]] Empire, clinging to an ancient world that seeks only to forget them. They are secretive, unyielding, and obsessed with the majesty and power of their once-vast civilization. They venerate the dead Gods of the Solura, debasing themselves and outsiders in blood-fueled rituals. They are a terrible, cruel, and relentless culture, only prevented from expanding to encompass others by their furious and deadly infighting among their numerous noble houses. To cross a Nalvani is to risk death, betrayal, or worse, as their patience is endless, and their vengeance is cruel.

Unfortunately, the Nalvani practice a horrific form of slavery, which they call Reclaiming, which is seen by other cultures and kingdoms as disgusting and dehumanizing. Slavery is not economic; it’s political and ideological. It is used as a punishment for failure, dissent, or defiance. A Nalvani or outsider who disobeys or underperforms may be “reassigned” to servitude, emphasizing obedience and fear of the system in which the Nalvani live. The Nalvani noble houses regard their slaves as instruments of their "empire." In the hidden halls of their enclaves, entire wings are dedicated to those "reclaimed" and then trained, conditioned, and exploited to rebuild the Aedir legacy. Names are stripped, and magical bonds are carved into flesh, while the fear of reprisal or rebellion is seared from the Reclaimed minds.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Related Cultures
>
> The Nalvani are known to other cultures at least at a surface level, but with their reputation as slavers and worshippers of blood they are whispered about as if they are monsters of myth rather than a living people. The [[Kessian]], however, are all too aware of the Nalvani’s presence and harbor an unrelenting hatred. Any Nalvani who attempts to infiltrate Kessian lands is captured without hesitation, and few ever survive their imprisonment.
