## Nocturnal Worshippers

The Kithil are a reculsive culture of moon worshippers and farmers who reject the radiant light of the sun. While most cultures glory in the light of Lantyr, the Kithil see her presence in the sky as unwelcome, intrusive, and dangerous. To Kithil, Lantyr is the blinding light that drowns out the power of Ember's moons and makes it easier for fabled creatures known as the Voracta to hunt them. Their aversion to the sun and reverence towards Ember's moons has made them an almost wholly nocturnal culture, sleeping during the day and working at night, with evenings that begin with a shared meal around a communal flame.

Kithil live in a region where the landscape constantly shifts due to frequent eruptions of geologically active vents, geysers, and earthquakes. They perceive the world beneath them as chaotic but seek peace by looking up to the sky, which deeply influences their culture and construction methods. They usually build their settlements and other structures on long stilts made from a material called Nupti, which bends and sways with movement, bringing them closer to the sky. This also applies to their farms, which are large structures built above the ground instead of being cemented into it, with plants extending their roots into volcanically enriched water.

As a people, the Kithil care deeply about knowing, learning from, and sharing with those around them, and despite thier strange habits, they are deeply committed to the ideal of family and community. To that extent, they reject the cosmopolitan life, which they see as isolating, and instead favor connected villages, each a large unit that works together to farm, solve problems, and enjoy life as best they can. Kithil also strongly believes in enjoying the moment, and their adoration of the night sky leads to large celebrations and frequent festivals, with dances and songs that correspond to whichever moon is currently highest in the Ember sky.

### Customs & Tradition

The deep bond between Kithil and the moons is worn on their bodies through intricate tattoos that trace their life's journey. A Kithil's first tattoo is given by those who raise them, but they choose the rest themselves, including their Marking—the first tattoo they select for themselves, signifying their status as an adult in the community. Other tattoos may reflect their connection to a specific moon of Ember or commemorate significant events, experiences, and relationships. Usually, the true meaning of each tattoo is only known to the Kithil who wears it, and sharing that meaning happens only with those they trust most.

Kithil's communal spirit influences their views on sex and love — while many Kithil are romantically monogamous, deep bonds between friends often have a level of intimacy that other cultures might consider romantic, and sharing sexual intimacy is often seen as no more special — or taboo — than sharing a secret or a good meal. This is especially true during the new year, when Kithil traditionally celebrate having survived another year by "sharing life" with each other, which can range from giving someone a plant to participating in a communal sexual experience around a campfire. Other cultures sometimes judge the Kithil culture as debaucherous, which Kithil find more amusing than concerning, though they usually tone down the new year celebrations when away from the villages to avoid causing issues with the locals.

Kithil are fascinated by the cultural norms of those who live under Lantyr's gaze, and many take part in the Kithil practice known as Sunning in their late teens — going out to explore the world during the daytime and learn about other cultures and places before returning home. While sunning, Kithil tend to be deeply curious about even the most mundane tasks performed by other cultures, eager to experience a new way of life and absorb all they can of it before returning to their village to share what they have learned with those around them.

### Religion & BelieFS

Most Kithil worship [[Thoma]], who became a shard god while leading a group of Keth away from danger during the Shattering, as the group that she led to safety eventually became one of the founders of Kithil culture. Like the Kithil, Thoma is strongly associated with the night, and many Kithil believe that she can provide guidance in the dark, whether it is about the right physical path to take or the correct way to move forward in life. In addition to Thoma, popular deities within Kithil communities are often those who are either associated with night or with revelry, including [[Remira]], who seeks both pleasure and consent, and [[Volta]], who is seen by some as Thoma's counterpart — the dawn to her dusk.

### Clothing & Adornments

Kithil care much more about their tattoos than their fashions, and as a result their clothing tends to be simple and straightforward, though some Kithil will echo some of the markings on their body as accents on their clothes, especially if they can have those details sewn in by nearby Arcturian or Ordani artisans. As most Kithil are Keth, it is common for them to wear transluscent veils over the face to hide some of their teeth, and the same fabric is sometimes used on Kithil clothing, providing a clearer view of that tattoos beneath the fabric.

### Cuisine & Diet

The shared meal around the cookfire is a fundamental part of Kithil culture, but the emphasis in their cooking is on shared ingredients and the celebration of the act of cooking, not any particular ingredient or spice. Generally, Kithil favor simple base recipes that work well even as individual ingredients change, and that can be easily shared out of a single pot, like soups and stews. The Kithil expression "no two soups the same," which refers to the way that seemingly similar situations can unfold very differently, is taken from their belief that each individual meal is shaped not just by the food that is served but by who has gathered to share it.

### Technology & Magic

Traditionally, Kithil generally practice lunar magic, shifting both the spells they use and the way that they approach magic as the moons change, using spells that draw on fire when [[Ragen]] is ascendant, for example, or spells that improve speed when [[Aura]] is on the rise. Similarly, it is seen as lucky to begin projects that seem more resonant with a particular moon when that moon is dominant, sowing seeds during [[Mayis]] in the hopes of rain and building new structures under [[Cora]] to give them strength. The association between activities and the moons can be different from village to village, but they reflect a general belief that being flexible and changing activities is wise, with many Kithil learning a little bit about everything from smithing to repairing clothing to herbalism so that nothing is lost if disaster strikes unexpectedly.

### Social Structure & Government

No matter how large they become, Kithil settlements are always known as villages. These settlements generally spread outwards across the land as opposed to building up as a city might, with individual Kithil and small groups living in stilted buildings around a central cookfire, protected from wild animals or natural dangers. Often, these buildings have elaborate window slats and shades that shut out Lantyr's light during the day, but allow the moonlight to come into each home at night. Kithil prefer using moonlight to candlelight or magical light where possible, and their windows are designed to allow enough light in to see and work by even on more overcast nights, though they will use other sources of illumination during times of truly inclement weather.

Within a village, groups of homes center around a cookfire, shared by a group that works together on tending crops and hunting. Cookfires generally use consensus to make major decisions, but cookfires are fluid, with individuals moving from one to another as fits their needs. Whoever comes to a cookfire is generally welcomed as long as they have no harmful intent towards those they are sharing food and time with, and any decisions that need to be made about what to do next are made by whoever is at the cookfire when a topic is raised, no matter how new they might be to the group.

### Language & Communication

Kithil speak the language Mithia, as well as the Common Coin, and usually know a smattering of Arcden due to trade with Arcturians in the Plateau. Whichever language they are speaking, the Kithil communication style is consensus oriented, and Kithil often check in verbally to make sure they are understood while speaking, especially when speaking to those from other cultures.
