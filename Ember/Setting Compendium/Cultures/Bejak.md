## Beast Tamers

The Bejak are primarily a nomadic people, traveling great distances across the northern plains of Aterica. They hunt massive beasts and creatures in a seasonal dance that has been ongoing since the Shattering. While they maintain a capital in the northernmost part of their lands, called Vellatagé, this ancient city is a place dedicated to their gods and serves as a spiritual connection. However, the heart of their culture remains mobile. They are also a deeply pacifist society on the surface, with a strong cultural aversion to direct physical violence among each other. This doesn't mean they are not violent or capable of defending themselves against monsters or outsiders, but the idea of attacking or killing each other is inconceivable. Because of this, the Bejak have developed a workaround: the Beast Clash. Nearly every Bejak is bonded to a tame creature of some kind, ranging from small to large, and these beasts act in their stead whenever a conflict between two Bejak arises. These clashes between tamed beasts are rarely bloody; instead, they are displays of tactics and control, used to settle everything from arguments to violations of Bejak law.

To many, the idea of a society without violence in this way verges on societal bliss; however, this is unfortunately untrue. Social life for the Bejak is a constant tug-of-war between the direct family members who travel together and the Greater Family of the nation. While family bonds are ironclad and exert immense pressure to prioritize kin, the Bejak recognize that isolation is a death sentence. They celebrate the departure of youth to join new families with joyous ceremonies, yet this outward harmony masks a bitter competitive streak. Families frequently find themselves at odds over prime foraging grounds or ancestral migration routes. These resource wars are the primary catalyst for Beast Clashes, where the pressure to provide for one's family creates deep friction that bubbles away under the surface.

Taming a creature for the Bejak is not a simple process and goes far beyond merely feeding it to make it a pet. Instead, it is a sacred, transformative rite. Through a complex ritual of mutual agreement, a Bejak forms a magical bond with a creature, granting the beast a spark of true sentience and magical power. This partnership is a two-way street, as it also enhances the physical or magical abilities of the Bejak.

> #### Related Cultures
>
> The Bejak are seasonal migratory traders and herds that travel across the vast expanses of the Hente Plains by nature, they are not opposed to traveling even further afield, though they do not have a fondness for ocean travel which restricts their range. They have a strong cultural connection to the Wardcall due to their shared history, a love for the Oaken who aid them in thier struggles with Trolls each year, and a great relationship with the Fikost to their north as well. Perhaps the only culture the Bejak tend to ignore is the Kithil, as they have opposing beliefs that make it hard to relate to one another, but they are still perfectly cordial. Finally, the Bejak culture surrounds the Great City of the Sphere and many Bejak has slowly been incorporated into more urban life. Most Bejak view the Sphere as a valid alternative despite its less-than-stellar reputation on the world stage.
