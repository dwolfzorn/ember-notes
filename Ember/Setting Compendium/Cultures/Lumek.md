## Wardens of Flame

The Lumek live under the oppressive cloud of constant survival, with unimaginable pressure to contain one of the world's greatest evils. They have been locked in a desperate fight against a remorseless and cruel enemy for centuries, and it has shaped their way of life and thinking to a degree that many other peoples of the world find hard to understand. When the Moiran people to their north began to fall, and the [[Ossarchate]] emerged, the Lumek were stirred into action and resistance by [[Lumé]], the daughter of Naor, and since then, they have fought in the cycles of Fire & Death that many accept will one day overwhelm them.

The Lumek are constantly on guard against the undead to their north, but every decade or so, typically eleven years, the [[Ossarchate]] launch an all-out attack southwards that can last for months. These seemingly never-ending attacks are called the cycles of Fire & Death. The fighting is brutal; thousands die, outposts and fortifications are overrun, second, third, and fourth lines of defense are penetrated, and the resolve and morale of the defenders falter as the Lumek are forced back again and again. The only shining light amidst the chaos and death is Lumé herself. Charging between the lines, annihilating lesser undead, and clashing furiously with greater undead monsters without respite. Faith and religious fanaticism in Lumé, and in her ability to defend and keep their people safe, is therefore absolutely justified. Most of the Lumek see Lumé as a savior and a figure of total adoration that is only marred by ancient rumors of her mother's murder.

Daily life is often dominated by hard training, drills, and the ever-present air of coming doom. Despite the hardships, however, daily life for the Lumek is vibrant and ever-moving; their cities and settlements are always considered bustling, where the people go about their day with grace, speed, and an undercurrent of artistry.

## Customs and Traditions

The Lumek are a dualistic culture in many ways; outsiders often gain very different insights into how the Lumek truly feel, depending on whether they are currently at war, approaching a conflict, or recovering from a recent struggle against the undead. That said, the Lumek are happy to share their cultural practices and traditions with outsiders, but they tend to be less interested in incorporating outside ideas. They efficiently manage their duties and are always aware of how long certain tasks take, knowing that war and conflict may loom just over the horizon. Gathering around a fire is a central aspect of Lumek daily life, serving both as a means to cook meals and as a space for relaxation and community bonding. It is traditional for Lumek households to be centered around a Firekep, a large, beautifully constructed fireplace made of metal and finely carved stones, often adorned with intricate designs that tell the family’s history. Celebrations for births are initiated quickly, as newborns are considered pivotal to the survival of their culture and the Lumek value large families, seeing each new life as a vital contribution to their collective strength and ability to endure the cycles of Fire & Death. The Lumek always burn their dead shortly after passing, with swift blessings and ceremonies. They do not maintain graveyards of any kind, as they believe that the spirits of the deceased are best honored through fire and are deathly afraid of the undead arising within their communities.

Every time the Blood Barons relent and the undead either retreat or are vanquished, the Lumek embrace a time of jubilant celebration that stretches over several weeks. For them, fire symbolizes not just a weapon but an ode to life itself. Their grand festivals become a vibrant display of this belief, showcasing fire in countless forms. Enormous bonfires leap towards the sky, handheld sparks flicker and dance in the air, while skilled performers breathe flames and twirl fiery instruments with graceful abandon. Accompanied by the resounding beat of great drums, songs of triumph and joy fill the air, creating an atmosphere of unrivaled merrymaking. This unique period allows the Lumek to mend their wounded spirits, embracing the warmth and energy of the flames as they revel in renewal and hope.

## Religion & Beliefs

The Lumek view themselves as fighters and warriors by necessity. When it comes to their personal hopes and dreams, the vast majority would like nothing more than to live simple lives and practice art, music, and dance. This view makes them extremely dutiful, and they recognize that their sacrifice keeps the world safe, even if they suffer. This sometimes presents itself as dry, fatalistic humor, among others, and deeply held cynicism regarding the fate of the world and the purpose of all things. Still, Lumek can also be beacons of positivity as they express themselves fully with the time they have.

In times of conflict, the clerics, direct followers and champions of [[Lumé]] channel their divine and elemental powers to protect their people and repel invaders, often invoking the blessings of Lumé to enhance their abilities. In daily life, they use their powers to heal, bless crops, and maintain harmony within the community. The rituals performed by these clerics often involve offerings to Lumé and elaborate ceremonies that celebrate her protective nature and the elemental force of fire.

## Clothing & Adornments

Lumek clothing is a blend of practicality and artistry, featuring hardy, resilient forms alongside refined and beautifully crafted pieces with flowing shapes. The color palette frequently includes striking reds and blacks, with many Lumek embellishing their attire with [[Amberal Bronze]] charms believed to protect against undeath, disease, and ill health.

While most inhabitants of the Firelands do not wear armor on a daily basis, they all possess defensive clothing and weaponry, often keeping these items close at hand in case fighting breaks out. During their festivals at the beginning of a new cycle after the defeat of a Blood Baron attack, Lumek wear special celebration attire. These festival garments are much lighter and less practical, allowing for greater freedom of movement when dancing. They often feature an array of decorative elements, including gemstones, precious metal adornments, and beautiful charms.

## Cuisine & Diet

Lumek cuisine revolves around a common dish called Luksa, a broad term for a mixture of dense grains, meats, and a specific pungent spice blend. Luksa is easy to prepare, and different Lumek communities have their own versions of the dish, utilizing locally available ingredients. All the spices in Luksa are sourced from plantations and farms across the Lumek Homelands, known for their aromatic and extremely spicy qualities; most other ancestries cannot eat Luksa unless it has been toned down significantly for them. Given that the Lumek culture primarily consists of the [[Fej]]Ancestry, their food and cuisine are notably nutrient-dense and high in calories. They also tend to serve large portions, and when not engaged in defending the Firelands, they heartily indulge in lavish feasts.

## Technology & Magic

In addition to their fighting prowess taught to them through necessity and hard training, the Lumek are master blacksmiths. Most of their technology revolves around a unique process where the ashes of their dead are used to create weapons and armour, known as [[Amberal Bronze]]. A rare metal that is considered holy and typically not allowed to leave the Firelands' shores unless permitted by Lumé herself. Lumarin Steel comes in several different grades, and it is rumored that the ashes of the more faithful defenders and champions imbue the metal with power, making them stronger and giving them both the ability to cause more damage to undead but also enhance the fighting abilities of its wielder. It should come as no surprise then that the Lumek burn their dead in holy ceremonies to collect thier ashes and use them in the fighting to come.

There are three major blacksmithing institutions across the Firelands, each with a specific focus. These schools teach incredibly ancient and traditional crafting processes. The three schools are the Vanbrel, which teaches weapon crafting and aggressive weapons of war; the Julmea, which teaches armorsmithing and defense construction techniques; and finally the Farimer, which teaches practical technologies for crafting everyday items used in farming or industry.

As for magic, the Lumek do not have any formal schools or academies for teaching or studying the arcane. Their understanding of magic is deeply rooted in their cultural practices and traditions rather than structured education. They are generally open to various forms of magical practices introduced by visitors. However, the Lumek place a high value on magic users who possess innate or ancestral connections to elemental forces, particularly those that resonate with fire or the elemental moon of [[Ragen]] itself. Any magical connection to fire is seen as a mark of honor and respect, and those who can harness these gifts are often revered within their communities. Many Lumek who wield magic are also priests and clerics of Lumé. Her followers play a crucial role in both warfare and everyday life.

## Social Structure & Government

The Lumek are governed by their queen and ultimate ruler, the Shard Goddess [[Lumé]]. There is a profound level of devotion, fanaticism, and faith in her ability to keep them safe, with everyone doing everything possible to contribute to the war against the undead. In turn, Lumé rules with absolute power across the entire realm but delegates much of the day-to-day governance to city leaders who are elected after every Cycle of Fire & Death, or to local leaders who tend to retain their position based on ability, age, or a combination of both. They oversee daily operations and administration, ensuring that resources are allocated effectively for essential activities such as farming and defense. This involves organizing and training individuals to be sent to the front lines or forming volunteer groups to prepare citizens for their roles in the ongoing conflict against the undead. To outsiders, it may seem strange at first, but there is very little variance in social classes, and groups are not treated differently. For example, there is no special respect or acknowledgment for warriors or those who have fought on the front lines. This is because everyone believes that all members of Lumek culture have a duty to fight or support their efforts, and whether you are fighting or farming, you should still be honored for your sacrifice.

## language & Communication

The Lumek language is called Luma and is a very fast, easy-to-learn spoken tongue with a level of depth and complexity that can take a lifetime to master. There are very few long words but many short expressive words and phrases; for example, the Lumek has nearly 80 different words to describe fire.
