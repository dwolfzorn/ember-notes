## Sun Worshippers

The Kessian realm is a land of sun-drenched temples and ancient secrets. Long before The Shattering Humanity emerged upon the Aran Peninsula and began spreading across the world, in small bands and families, seeking new opportunities known as the Human Migrations. Before the Shattering, the Aedir fully supported the Humans' drive to expand, at least at first, and even helped them in various ways. One such way was settling the northern coast of the continent of Kasi (the ancient Aedir name for Kessia). This aid meant that for a long time, the Aedir played a crucial role in the development of the early Kessian settlements, and many Aedir lived and worked alongside Humans. After the Shattering, many of the Aedir who survived integrated into the flourishing Kessian culture.

In the aftermath of the Shattering, the Kessians began to worship Lantyr above all others, which evolved into daily rituals and ceremonies dedicated to honoring the sun and seeking her blessings. Kessians have also mastered harnessing solar energy to power their technology and sustain their way of life in the harsh desert environment. In Kessia, society follows a distinctly matriarchal structure; women are naturally regarded as leaders and governors. While Kessians are very proud of this social tendency, they continue to face ongoing issues with equality and have implemented various reforms and laws aimed at addressing ingrained biases.

Kessians reside in a region that was deeply affected by the Shattering; the once-glowing jungle colonies of the Aedir were wiped out and completely destroyed across what is now known as the Obsidian Desert. To this day, the Kessians live with the aftermath of that destruction, and as abyssal energy swirls and monsters emerge from the red sands and obsidian monoliths at the continent's center, they have little choice but to oppose them. For thousands of years, Kessians have stood resolutely against the Abyss, actively containing the spread of corruption, and they have become highly skilled at detecting and fighting it. While Kessians directly combat the Abyssal influence from the Obsidian Desert, they also fight its invasive and harmful effects on their people, often taking extreme measures. Some see their inquisitorial methods as excessive, but they are necessary for society's survival, and their relentless pursuit of corruption has continued as long as the Obsidian Desert has existed.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Related Cultures
>
> The Kessians live upon a harsh and uncompromising continent, with a deep ocean separating them from most of the known world to the north. Despite this, the Kessians are excellent sailors and navigators and have strong ties to many cultures, including the Kelmezians and the Lumek, both of which are similar in many aspects culturally. The Lumek especially view Kessians as distant family, and the two peoples often help one another in times of crisis, and the Kessians have fielded an expeditionary force to help the Lumek against the undead Blood Barons for nearly 800 years.
