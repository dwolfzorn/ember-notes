## Sky Devourers

Far beyond the distant Tayan Empire, in the furthest west of the known world, lie the Maziran Kingdoms, a fractured realm of mountain peaks, ancient fortresses, and unyielding pride. To most of the world, Mazira is little more than a myth, spoken of in tales of Skyriders, cities carved into the mountains, and endless wars. Even more feared are the seasonal Skyfalls, when the heavens are said to rain black water and swarms of terrifying monsters called Jagkat tear apart those foolish enough to step beyond the protection of fortress walls. These swarms descend in madness, ravaging villages, towns, and cities, with relentless hunger, and every Maziran grows up beneath their ever-present shadow.

Though the Mazirans of today are largely Human, their culture descends from a far older people, the Cor’ak, whose civilization carved its mark deep into the bones of [[Akazea]] mountains. Mazirans define themselves through tradition, ancestry, and loyalty to their homeland. Ever'sy stone temple, stepped valley farm, and battle standard bears echoes of the Cor’ak legacy.

Yet for all their pride, the Mazirans are deeply divided. Their eight known kingdoms, with a whispered ninth said to lie beyond the western wastes, are locked in perpetual rivalry: warring, trading, and scheming in cycles as old as their history. Kingdoms rise and fall; alliances shift with the seasons. Only [[Norzi Ashigurin]], the Shenin of Lunari and Lord of Storms, dares claim dominion over all Akazea, his banners reaching farther than any ruler for hundreds of years.

Maziran cities are dense and vertical, linked by tramways that climb cliffsides or hang suspended over vast chasms. While many industries have embraced full industrialization, traditional methods, slower, meticulous, and deeply valued, still hold an important place in Maziran society. Their craftsmanship is unmatched: metalwork, stone architecture, and magically-assisted mining flourish beneath the mountain peaks. Many Mazirans wield earth-attuned magic, shaping stone and ore through sheer will, carving their cities directly from the mountains themselves.

Among the proudest callings in Mazira is service in the Azeken, an ancient order of Skyriders who swear sacred oaths to the Shenins and the only ones who dare travel openly during a Skyfall. Each Azeken bonds with a winged mount called a Tekrel, a terrifying creature of Akazea’s high ranges. They serve out their contracts for a single season before returning home, free to offer their services to the highest bidder.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Related Cultures
>
> The Maziran Kingdoms are united under a shared cultural identity, yet remain divided into multiple rival kingdoms, often engaged in open warfare with one another. In addition, the Mazirans are facing an external threat: the [[Tayan]] Empire, which landed on their eastern shores nearly fifteen years ago. Since then, the Kingdom of Kanre has been subjugated. Surprisingly, the Mazirans appear relatively unfazed by the Tayan conquest and hold no particular animosity toward the Tayans. They maintain generally good relations with many neighboring cultures, including the [[Kelmezian]], who are currently attempting to rally the Mazirans to set aside their internal conflicts and unite against the Tayan threat.
