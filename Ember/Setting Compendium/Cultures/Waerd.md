## Monster Slayers

The Waerds of the Lowlands are the world's foremost monster hunters, known for their relentless battles against creatures of the Duskflare Jungles and each other. They define themselves by their ability to defeat monsters and rivals. Six massive city-castles called Bastions dominate the Lowlands, where life centers on shifting alliances and skirmishes; long-term peace is almost unthinkable. Despite ongoing political conflicts, Waerds respect each other pragmatically; they follow strange codes of honor and rebuilding periods to prevent their wars from driving them to extinction. In these battles, death is rare, and victory is measured by individual courage and endurance rather than slaughter. For Waerds, fighting one another is simply a test or practice for the real challenge, heading into the jungles to slay monsters.

At the core of this philosophy of hunting and fighting is a deep reverence for [[Lanespear]] Her influence can be felt throughout the Lowlands, from fortified shrines overflowing with monster trophies to the elite companies of tattooed warrior-priests who bear spears in her honor. While Lanespear herself roams the Duskflare Jungles in search of colossal prey, she indirectly guides the Bastions by training members of all Waerds, no matter the political situation.

While the Waerd culture includes many humans, the true history of the subcontinent remains likely far more mysterious and strange. It is believed that a large, hidden population of Signborn inhabited the area for unknowable thousands of years, even during the long years of the Aedir Empire. After the Shattering, humans discovered the Lowlands, and eventually, the Signborn, and both Ancestries merged into a single, unified culture that exists today. This explains why Waerds have unique customs and traditions, such as viewing themselves as living chronicles rather than recording history on paper. They ritualistically tattoo their bodies throughout their lives to mark every successful hunt and significant battle. These intricate patterns serve as a visual history, with colors and styles varying by their Bastion of origin.

Their obsession with hunting monsters extends to armor and weaponry itself, as the Waerds specialize in biomimetic craftsmanship. They scavenge hide, bone, and essence from the creatures they slay, incorporating these materials into their armor and weapons to turn the monsters' strength against them. For a Waerd, a warrior’s worth is written in the ink on their skin and the jagged, runic trophies they wear into battle.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Related Cultures
>
> The Waerds have strange relationships with surrounding nations and cultures, as the Waerds focus heavily on interior conflicts between themselves and fighting the monsters of their homeland. Most other cultures are simply disinterested in dealing with the Waerds and vice versa, so the majority of contact and connections are simply cordial. For example, there are no official embassies from any of the Waerd Bastions in Ordain, one of the largest trading cities nearby, though Ordani Trading Houses do have offices in several of the Waerd Bastions.

## Customs & Tradition

Waerds live by a series of complex codes, often simply called "The Codes," as if everyone knows what they are. Ironically, to many Waerds, the codes they follow can vary widely. To outsiders, this seems almost mad, and trying to understand the rules that Waerds actually follow is often pointless. Despite this, the Wareds are oddly honorable among themselves and pragmatic about the lack of clear definitions. Each year, Wareds typically set aside specific times called the "Hunting" or the "Scouring." During the "Hunting", entire communities shift focus, dedicating themselves to long-season hunting trips. Families or communities venture into jungles to hunt monsters and creatures. During the "Scouring", the best warriors and hunters of the separate Bastions form semi-autonomous military units and engage in fierce skirmishes and battles between each other. While nearly all Wareds participate in these two annual periods, those living and working in bastions—engaged in trades, food production, or other professions—only undertake brief hunts or skirmishes, spending a few days a year in the jungles, wilderness, or battlefields instead of entire seasons.

## Religion & Beliefs

While individuals Waerds recongise the Nineteen as a distant, all-encompassing Pantheron of deities with varying roles in society, they are almost fanatically devoted to the Shard God Lanespear. She is not a distant deity but a primal teacher of the spear and hunt who embodies the perfect hunter and slayer. Shrines to Lanespear are common and often referred to as Trophy Halls filled with preserved heads, colossal bones, thick scales, and calcified hearts of legendary beasts. For Waerds, a life without challenge lacks meaning, and avoiding or fearing to confront danger in any form is seen as the ultimate sin. They are highly pragmatic about death, and their culture holds little fear of dying, provided an individual accomplishes a great feat at least once in their life to prove themselves.

## Clothing & Adornments

To outsiders, Waerds often appear as the very creatures they hunt. Their look is a patchwork of fur, scales, bones, hardened leathers, and delicately shaped metals that resemble claws or horns. Young Waerds usually wear little or no clothing until they start hunting independently, even as children, by returning with smaller creatures and learning to craft their own clothing. As they mature and defeat stronger enemies, they create armor and weapons from cured monster hides, chitinous plates, and sharp teeth. Beneath these extremly customized garments, Waerds also cover their bodies with ritualistic tattoos, viewing their skin as a record of their hunting and martial achievements. For many Waerds, protecting themselves with monster parts is just as important as leaving certain parts of their skin exposed to display their Ancestral Ink.

## Cuisine & Diet

Among the Bastions, it is said that nothing is ever wasted. No kill is left to rot; every part has a purpose. This applies not only to weapons and armor but also to their industries and especially their food. Their diet is rich in meat from monsters and creatures they kill, prepared in a variety of complex dishes. Meats are heavily spiced, marinated, and then sautéed, grilled, deep-fried, boiled, or steamed. A popular delicacy is Marrow-Mash, a high-protein paste that is slowly cured, spiced with intense flavors, and served in hollowed-out monster horns.

## Technology & Magic

Waerds generally dismiss most forms of magic. While they understand and respect the potency and power of wizards casting fireballs, they believe that such magic isn't very effective in many situations. Most mages are considered weak and unable to defend themselves in close combat. As a result, most Waerds view magic more as a supplement than a path to true power. Instead, they depend on the formidable strength of the numerous monsters and creatures in the Duskflare Jungles. If these creatures' scales, teeth, or bones hold magical properties, Waerds will gladly use them for their own use. The older a Waerd is, the more likely they are to have defeated powerful monsters and possess equipment, charms, or objects imbued with innate magic. Waerd technology appears quite basic at first glance—major Bastions are built from stone, but most villages, stones, or buildings are made from wood or large monster parts, and their technology advancement is glacial.

## Social Structure & Government

The Waedrs are united by their beliefs and customs, rather than a single government or nationhood. In the Lowads, there are six major Bastions, with power shifting between these massive city-fortresses depending on which Bastion can claim more territory each season. Leadership is held by the six Euntwé, experienced veterans and elders, who are partly elected and have gained power through physical dominance. Their skin is almost entirely black with ink. Age is only one aspect of a Waerd's social standing, as it usually signifies experience and respect. However, an individual's skill and ability to defeat monsters are even more important. In rare cases, Waerds who have slain legendary monsters, such as dragons or Leviathans, are instantly promoted to leadership within their communities.

Those Waerds who have personally met Lanespear and earned her favor are called the Kinthyr. The Kinthyr stand apart from other Waerds in many ways. They are bestowed with wings and wear oddly ancient-looking armor adorned with glowing symbols that suggest a connection between the Waerds and Signara. Like their Mistress, they mediate the conflicts of the Lowlands, fighting for both sides. They also heal and restore fallen warriors and, in the event of a great warrior falling to monstrous incursions, carry their bodies back for veneration in fiery ceremonies.

## Language & Communication

One of the most surprising aspects of Waerd culture is their language. Many assume it would be simple and limited, but in reality, it is incredibly complex, with more descriptive words than most other languages on Ember's surface. Words are spoken with refined, flowing elegance, making it considered a beautifully hypnotic tongue. They lack a standardized written system and instead use a combination of ancient glyphs passed down through their Ancestral Ink and pictograms carved into bark or trees, which guide other hunters in the wild.
