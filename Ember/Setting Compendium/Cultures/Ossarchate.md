## Dominion of Death

Shrouded beneath the eternal gloom of [[The Evernight]], the Ossarchate is a realm where death has triumphed utterly over life. The skies above are sealed by a permanent veil of black, magical clouds, casting the land below into a grey half-light where neither sun nor moon is ever seen. It is a place of silence and decay, a necrotic dominion where the dead rule over vast kingdoms without end.

The Ossarchate sprawls across a blasted wasteland known as the [[Moiran Bonelands]], eternally scarred by the cataclysm called Moira’s Lament. Across these grey wastes, immense limbs, most often hands, seem to burst from the ground, thrusting upward as if fleeing from the depths below. Each of these titanic limbs appears to be made of a white, stone-like material, yet is utterly impervious to harm or mark. These pale monoliths are worshipped by the undead and feared by any living soul foolish enough to venture into the Bonelands, for they exude a terrible aura that erodes the mind, draining hope, purpose, and memory, until nothing remains but madness and despair.

The realm is divided among a host of independent rulers known as the Blood Barons, lords of undeath who carved their territories from the living and from each other as the Evernight descended. Each Baron rules with absolute power, their castles rising from the mist like jagged tombs, glowing with ethereal lights and the sound of crackling machinery. Within these fortress-laboratories, the living are bred, harvested, and transformed; necromantic fields keep the captive populations docile while their flesh and souls are shaped into new soldiers for the Ossarchate’s ever-growing armies.

Traditionally, the Blood Barons are in a constant state of war with every neighboring culture, nation, and kingdom. However, they are equally eager to absorb the territories, cities, and resources of their rivals, often turning their foes against one another through cunning or corruption. Yet despite their endless rivalries, all of the Blood Barons remain bound by a shared devotion to the Ossarchate’s grim ideal: the perfection of existence through the denial of mortality. To them, life is weakness, an error in the greater cosmos that can only be corrected through undeath and the destruction of all life.

The Ossarchate is regarded by nearly all as a stain upon the cosmos, a blight so abhorrent that every living being instinctively recoils from it. Even [[Elder Gods]] and [[Shard Gods]] oppose its existence, viewing it as a violation of the natural order and an affront to the [[Cosmos]]and the [[Heart of Ember]]. Yet the Ossarchate endures, and the true terror lies in the scale of its power. Among the Blood Barons, some are merely formidable commanders, but others are unfathomably powerful beings whose mastery of necromantic energy rivals or even surpasses that of Shard Gods. Four of these Barons are said to possess strength so potent that their wills bend reality itself to their command.

The sheer necromantic power of these individuals flows outward, empowering their generals, lieutenants, and the countless armies under their control. It is whispered that their combined might is so immense that the Ossarchate could stand against entire pantheons, and even many Shard Gods regard it with fear or even terror.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Related Cultures
>
> The Ossarchate stands in direct opposition to nearly every living power in the world. Its borders are shunned by the [[Kelmezian]], Striders, and even the [[Tayan]], all of whom view it as a festering blight. The [[Lumek]] wage a constant and unrelenting war against the Ossarchate, fought in brutal cycles of Fire and Death. To the Lumek, the Ossarchate is not merely an enemy, but a stain upon creation itself. The Ossarchate is also openly at war with the [[Cascilian]] Republic, though this conflict typically erupts only when the Blood Barons’ armies attempt to cross the Golden Bay by water. Despite this, the Ossarchate influence leaks beyond its shadow-shrouded lands, through necromantic relics, cults, and whispers of eternal life sold to desperate mortals.
