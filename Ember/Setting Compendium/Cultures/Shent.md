## Echoes of Majesty

The Shent (or Tunori, in Pathward) were an enlightened and wise society that met its end during the events of [[The Shattering]] two and a half thousand years ago. Despite the passage of time and the catastrophic events of The Shattering, many of their ruined and towering cities still stand defiantly among the biting cold and shimmering mountains, forever immortalized with the name the Giants Mountains.

The modern cultures of Ember and its people are far less aware of the Shent than their peers of the period, the Aedir. However, many cultures surrounding the Giants Mountains have deep ancestral and formative connections to the Shent. In addition to deeply held respect and appreciation for their actions during The Shattering itself, many of these people believe the Shent sacrificed themselves to protect the world of Ember. It is believed that the Shent held powers of foresight that allowed them to see the events of The Shattering coming, but they decided they would stand against the evils seeking to destroy the world even if it meant their end. They created the Keth and Kivahr ancestries to carry forward different aspects of their beliefs and ideals; this, in turn, created the Bejak, Kithil, and Strider cultures directly, in addition to the Wardcall, which arose much later.

While the Shent culture no longer exists, there are ancient members of the society that have wondrously survived. Long before the Shattering, perhaps before the powers of foresight manifested, the Shent built small stone servants to help them maintain their settlements upon, between, and below the mountains. Eventually, these servants, perhaps due to the Shent's benevolent miscalculation, gained both sentience and sapience, becoming the Hulg'run. A great many Hulg'run remained with the Shent as they fell, but some fled and joined the fledging cultures and peoples to the east and west. In rare cases, Hulg'run can live extremely long lives, and some still remember the Shent as they were, though they seem to hoard their secrets closely and speak little of what the Shent were truly like.

Today, the Shent are gone, with only memories, tales, and legends remaining of their once beautiful and ancient culture. Their ruins bear terrible scars, displaying acts of unimaginable power, where lingering Abyssal magic and the horrors of The Shattering still echo across time.

## Identity

The Shent were said to be extremely peaceful and wise, focused on gaining a measure of the world around them by deeply reflecting one's self and purpose as part of the greater whole. They held a deep fascination with the cosmos, specifically the lunar cycle of the Elemental Moons and the Stars. This is evidenced by many preserved ruins that display intricate and detailed carvings of the moon cycles in various ways, with giant figures studying and worshipping.

## Daily Life

Very little accurate information is known about the daily life of the Shent; some stories speak of their reference for the world around them and how they would not eat the flesh of animals, while others speak of great feasts, with roaring fires and food of every kind imaginable. Part of the problem seems to stem from the differing beliefs and stories from separate cultures derived from the Shent and a lack of any clear written history (likely all destroyed during the Shear). What is known is that they were a people who constructed some of the largest structures in the world aside from the Aedir, and their great halls and spires would have meant trade, social gatherings, and ceremonies would have been extremely grand affairs.

## Language

Pathward is no longer a spoken language; at least, it is no longer shared or taught by its remaining speakers. There are extremely old Hug'run who know how to speak Pathward, but they don't tend to have any reason to share its secrets. Pathward is said to be a very slow, measured, and thoughtful language with a strange emphasis on rhetorical questions.
