# The Cultures

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Lore Summary
>
> 1. Cultures in Ember include colossal city-states, sprawling kingdoms, merchant theocracies, or wandering nomadic clans. This is because in the Ember setting, culture focuses more on people, food, identity, etc, rather than lines on a map.
> 2. Cultures are further split into four sub-categories: Prominent, Regional, Distant, or Ancient.
>   - Prominent Cultures are those next to Ordain.
>   - Regional Cultures are those closely related through trade.
>   - Distant Cultures are barely recognized or unknown in Ordain
>   - Ancient Cultures have fallen and are no longer active in Ember.
> 3. The two most important cultures for the Shard of Fear game, are [[Arcturian]] and [[Ordani]]. Both of which are present on the Region Map itself.

Cultures on Ember form an incredibly diverse tapestry, from vast, multi-ancestry empires and kingdoms where numerous languages flourish in vast markets, to isolated, xenophobic communities that have protected their secrets for thousands of years. Some cultures resemble traditional kingdoms led by monarchs or regents, while others are nomadic clans, meritocratic magocracies, or loose alliances of tribal elders, and some are fully developed democracies.

In many cultures in present-day Ember, you will find metropolitan hubs where ancestries coexist in a range of fragile or comfortable multicultural unity. This is historical, as the ancient Aedir empire, which ruled for most of the Age of the Tower, encouraged multicultural cultural harmony, a philosophy that seems deeply ingrained within many societies today.

Some cultures are characterized by their impressive stone architecture and written laws, while others survive solely through oral traditions of traveling caravans or the rhythmic chants of forest dwellers. However, every culture is profoundly influenced by the history of the Shattering, their neighbors, and the existence of gods, dragons, and powerful entities that strive to impose their own agendas.
