## Innovative Crafters

After a long journey across Ember fleeing the undead, the early Arcturians decided that if they were to put down roots in the Arctus Plateau, those roots would be deep ones. Taking inspiration from the enduring strength of nature, they built their community for the long-term, crafting lasting homes and tools made in harmony with the land around them.

To this day, Arcturians believe strongly in craftsmanship, passing well-crafted items and the knowledge of how to create them from one generation to the next. Though they respect tradition, they think of themselves as flexible innovators, celebrating the progress of each new generation both for its connection to the past and its ability to build for the future. The practice of agrimagic, which Arcturians created as a melding between spellcasting and their work with the land, comes from this type of creative innovation, as does the Arcturian practice of creating small constructs out of natural materials to help protect or support their communities by doing routine tasks.

Though they are sometimes seen by other cultures as naive or untouched by hardship, Arcturians believe that their years of peaceful coexistence with the cultures around them have given them a deep strength, centered in tight-knit communities that make use of the fertile land of the Arctus Plateau. They have little patience for unnecessary conflict, or for anything they see as frivolous, preferring to focus on what is needed to get things done and nothing more. For the parts of life that are thought of as practical, though, Arcturians love to add embellishments that show the care they've put into their actions, and take great pride in expressing that care through the items they make or share.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Related Cultures
>
> Arcturians are a peaceful and non-confrontational society that has existed in peace for many generations. They have good relations with everyone around them, especially the Ordani, who live nearby in the great city of Ordain. Some cultures view Arcturians as complacent because of their long history of peace, with more conflict-driven cultures like the Waerds, Lumek, and Oaken believing that the Arcturians are naive when it comes to world events and will require help in defending themselves if they ever face real struggles. Aside from this dismissiveness, Cascilians are the only nearby culture with a less than positive view of the Arcturians, as they do not approve of the practice of Agrimagic.

## Customs & Tradition

The home is one of the centers of Arcturian life, with most homes holding multiple generations within their walls. Each new generation changes and adds to the home they live in, which can make the interior of an Arcturian home feel like a hodgpodge to outsiders, but makes Arcturians feel physically grounded in their history and relationships. Many Arcturian customs feature the making, giving, and exchanging of handcrafted items, from family members providing hand-stitched clothes for each year of a newborn's future life to close friends or lovers trading everyday items as a way to show intimacy and affection. Returning an item that was given in good faith is considered extremely rude, which some traders use to their advantage by giving an item to a customer and then demanding payment for the kindness.

For those who are new to a community, or times when an appropriate existing item isn't available, buying or trading for items is considered equal to inheriting, receiving, or creating them — Arcturians work hard to make sure that those who pass through their lands generally feel welcome and know that not everyone shares their love of craft. That said, it is considered polite for someone who brings or gifts a purchased item to either know who the original crafter of the item was or put in some effort to haggle for it - something considered an art in and of itself.

## Religion & Beliefs

The grouping of gods known as [[The Nineteen]] gained their name as a result of the help they provided the early Arcturians when they faced off against undead Varun giants during the Mourning War. By the end of the conflict, nineteen deities had stepped in to help the newcomers to the Arctus Plateau, from those who fought alongside the Arcturians to those who helped them rebuild after the conflict. The grateful Arcturians deemed them The Nineteen and have had some reverence for the group ever since.

Among the Nineteen, the most popular on the Arctus Plateau are [[Lantyr]], who is popular in many communities on Ember, but especially loved in the Arctus Plateau for the sun's role in agriculture; [[Sigil]], who makes his home in the middle of the Plateau at the Oldcraft Lodge; and [[Volta]], who often visits the Plateau during his travels across Ember. Though not one of the Nineteen, [[Aythorn]] is also gaining some popularity with Arcturians, as they often accompany Volta on his journeys through the region.

Beyond the Nineteen, the [[Cindaric Sages]] are fairly popular on the Arctus Plateau, especially in the region immediately surrounding [[Corpin Sanctuary]], which has long been home to a group of Cindaric Sages who combine their worship with acts of kindness in the community.

## Clothing & Adornments

The Arcturian love for craft extends to their clothing, which often seems straightforward or plain, but contain intricate craftsmanship that only fellow Arcturians or artisans might notice. Even the color of Arcturian garments, which often feature a shade of green, is a nod to craftsmanship — creating the color from the naturally orange and yellow materials of the Arctus Plateau takes the work of skilled fabric makers. Arcturians also favor detailed stitching and ornamental elements made from natural ingredients, like grass or tree sap, but tend towards simple silhouettes that are easy to move in and fabrics that stand the test of time.

Among Arcturians, it is considered a mark of sophistication to be able to identify the region or specific artisan associated with the clothing of anyone you meet. Shoddily-crafted clothing is generally considered to have been a gift and isn’t looked down upon, but any form of mass-crafted clothing, or clothing that is impractical for movement (including many of the styles worn by their neighbors in Ordain) is seen as a mark of either arrogance or carelessness on the part of both the maker and the wearer.

## Cuisine & Diet

Most Arcturians have a garden of some kind near or incorporated into their home (often built around over time so that it is difficult to see or access by anyone who does not live there) and tend to favor grain and vegetable agriculture over raising animals for meat. Meat is seen more often in larger settlements, especially those that are visited regularly by caravans and traders, and is often associated more strongly with festivals and large gatherings than daily cuisine.

Ture, a yellow-kerneled grain that grows in pods in most Arcturian gardens, is a staple of traditional Arcturian cuisine. It is combined with liquids and spices to make a stew, turel, that is filling and nourishing, but very bland while on its own, typically becoming a showcase for a dominant ingredient or flavor. While Arcturians can speak eloquently about the way a specific herb or vegetable changes the flavor of turel, they tend towards subtle flavorings, and their cuisine has been dubbed by an outsider or two as "plain grains." In settlements on the trade routes, this is less the case, as Arcturians are quick to embrace the influence of other culture's cuisines and add new herbs and spices to their gardens where they can.

## Technology & Magic

Arcturians are known for their practice of agrimagic, a form of spellcasting that supports local agriculture by imbuing items with magic for the growth and protection of the community. This practice has driven a good deal of their technological development, as it allows them to remain connected to the land while still pushing for innovations that help crops grow more freely, prevent infestations by nearby creatures, purify water sources, and perform other acts that, while magical, are focused on making the land and community as strong and fruitful as possible.

The other notable technology developed by Arcturians is in itself a type of agrimagic - many Arcturian settlements use small constructed companions made from natural materials. While these figures are not fully sentient and are not designed for combat, they are able to follow smaller commands and can help smaller settlements continue to thrive, even if their population is limited.

## Social Structure & Government

Most Arcturian communities grew out of settlements formed during one of three major events in the history of the Arcturian experience — the initial settlement of the Arctus Plateau by travelers seeking a new home, the resettlement of the Arctus Plateau in the aftermath of the Mourning War between the Arcturians and the undead Varun giants, and the shift to take advantage of the caravans that make their way to the city of Ordain from beyond the Plateau. In each case, communities developed around a group of Arcturians who decided to make a specific place their home, building on that home until it became large enough to split off into an an entire community, often centered around a shared farm or garden.

Today, homes are still the centers of most Arcturian settlements, with individual homes acting and voting as a unit when it comes to making decisions about major town matters. During these decisions, deference is often given to the homes that have been around the longest, and those that have the most generations under their roof, as they are thought to have the greatest stake in what the community will become. In addition to the typical Arcturian settlement, artisan settlements often spring up around a particularly talented crafter, who will build homes for the apprentices and colleagues who become their chosen family. Tradeways tend to be a bit larger, as stopping points for the trading caravans that travel through the plateau.

Though they operate independently, individual settlements know and communicate with each other, avoiding conflict — if any disagreement threatens, the oldest home in both settlements sends a representative to a neutral location, where a compromise is usually (eventually) worked out.

## Language & Communication

The Arcturian language is known as Arcden, or Old Speak. Known for its variety of descriptive words, it is used by Arcturians to craft intricate sentences that have their own element of craft, especially in their poetry and song. Most speak the Common Coin in daily life, as it is the language of trade and travel, but Arcturian poetry and folk songs, which are often shared during celebrations, use Arcden to celebrate the natural world. Some believe that their lyrics hide information about the location of old relics and hidden sites, but this has never been proven.

Some scholars have noted the use of words in Arcden that appear to come from the time of the Shent, and may have been learned by the Arcturians from some of the last surviving Shent, or during their travels across Ember. Though the exact origin of these loanwords is still unknown, their existence has occasionally helped those who study Shent ruins make some sense of artifacts whose purpose has long since been forgotten.
