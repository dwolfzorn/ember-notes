## Ancient Urbanites

The Kelmezian City States are ancient, towering cities that spread across the badlands and deserts of south-west Aterica. It is believed that many of these cities were once the original settlements of Humanity, and before that, the ancestral homes of the Jarn clans. Today, these cities are old, chaotic urban environments where technology from hundreds or even thousands of years ago remains in active use. Jurimax Engineers tirelessly work to keep the cities functioning, though often in a futile, never-ending effort. The term Kelmezian has become more common in recent years, but residents strongly identify with their city of birth, from the crumbling factories of Old Carinth to the water-covered canals of Varena. To support their incredibly dense populations, the cities have perfected vertical agriculture through massive artificial farms, silos, and estates that pulse with life deep within the urban landscape. This dense urban density is managed by a complex network of noble factions, senates, and councils, all of whom must balance the grandeur of their towers against the crumbling, often abandoned lower levels.

The potential for overwhelming chaos and crime in such packed environments is held in check by the Nightwatch, an organization that operates with a fervor that borders on the fanatical. The Nightwatch are the true masters of the Kelmezian cities, with a unified command structure across every city in the region, and as their agents free-run across rooftops and through the shadowed underlayers, they hunt their criminal targets with relentless precision. In most Kelmezian cities, the Nightwatch is authorized to use deadly force, making them the singular, terrifying pillar of order in an otherwise lawless society.

The Kelmezian culture has become more unified recently due to the looming war with the Tayan Empire. The city of Yarinu has already fallen, lying in ruins that the Tayans use as a supply depot, while the people of Kalaxa are forced to abandon their homes as the front lines draw closer. This existential threat has forced a grim transformation as the ancient cities have slowly pivoted their industries towards war production. In addition, it's claimed that the Kelmezian 43rd Corp, holding the Tayan 2nd army at bay in the east, is actually winning and pushing back the invaders, which has sparked a massive upsurge in cultural identity and pride.

> #### Related Cultures
>
> The Kelmezian City States currently border two openly hostile cultures, the Railen in the south, where tensions are set to explode at any moment and the active war with the Tayan Empire to the north. This has caused a seismic shift in its relations with other cultures around the world as the Kelmezians desperately search for allies and support. The Mazirans across the Crossing Sea would make a natural alliance, as they are also under attack by the Tayan Empire, but unfortunately, they are divided amongst themselves. The Lumek believe the Tayan Empire is expanding only to secure a better future for people generally and they have no desire to enrage a nation that may one day be in a strong position to help them combat the Blood Barons. Thus, the Kelmezians find themselves isolated and solely responsible, at least for now, for halting the expansion of the Tayan Empire.
