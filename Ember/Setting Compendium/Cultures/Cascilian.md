## Free Mariners

The Cascilian Republic is a young seafaring nation that broke free from tyranny three hundred years ago. Historically, the Cascaal region was governed by powerful Mage Kings who ruled over city-states with despotic glee. After a brutal and bloody uprising, the survivors of the Cascilian people established a nation led by themselves, who attempt to govern fairly and uphold just laws. This history and its aftermath led to a deep and enduring hatred of mages of any kind. This animosity drives them to seek out and punish powerful mages, leading to their current fierce conflict with the Star Mages of Rhiva, whom they see as lying manipulators. Within the republic's borders, all magic is banned, and while executions of magic users are rare, imprisonment is common, and the republic maintains a large prison on the Isle of Coral.

Just as the nation is known for its hatred of mages, it is perhaps more famous for its mastery of the ocean. The Cascilian Navy and its Marine branch are among the most formidable forces on Ember. When the Mage Kings ruled the area, they enchanted their ships with ocean magic to prevent the undead from Ossarchate from landing on their shores. However, since the republic's founding, the Navy and Marines have evolved into something new. They still actively repel the undead, but now employ a technology called Ochanic, inspired by biomimicry, with ship designs modeled after the overlapping scales of deep-sea fish, sail designs inspired by the shapes of fins, and sleek hulls based on shark bodies. They also use strange "engines" that move with undulating pulses like those of a squid. While the navy continues to maintain surface fleets, much of its recent strength lies beneath the waves, with many powerful ships capable of striking from the depths.

In addition to their formidable Navy and ships, the Cascilians utilize a magic-absorbing mineral called Ortarec, which can be infused into wood, stone, and metal. Ortarec is mined from the Burial Plains, Dustlands, and the edges of the Giants Mountains. Ortarec is carefully controlled. An open secret kept by the Republic is that Ortarec's absorption only works up to a certain point; once that threshold is surpassed, Ortarec explodes in a colossal blast.

>
> The Cascilian Republic is closely aligned with the Lumek to the south across the Yawning Sea; both cultures and nations stand firm against the creeping expansion of the Blood Barons and their undead armies. The Cascilians are distant and even standoffish with Arcturians to their west, who they view as careless and unmindful, but they have a positive attitude toward the Ordani, who they believe are much more capable of recognizing the dangers of magic; in fact, the Cascilian Senate is very much in favor of forging closer ties with Ordain. However, they are fiercely, even fanatically, opposed to the Rhiven culture, which hosts the Star Mages of the Starsworn Academy. This is due to historical and political reasons but anti-magic sentiments and distrust are deeply ingrained within the Cascilian society.

## Customs & Tradition

Life in the republic is characterized by community unity, a history of oppression, and a strong desire to stay free in a world dominated by powerful forces they desperately try to hold at bay. While the republic appears strong, both militarily and culturally, controlling its borders and keeping its citizens safe from within and without, it is actually encircled by forces that constantly challenge its authority. This instills in Cascilians a deep-seated sense of vigilance, always preparing for any eventuality. This attitude extends beyond simple gut feelings, influencing all of Cascilian society, including their coming-of-age trials at sixteen, when every Cascilian learns to navigate a small ship alone through treacherous waters, known as a Salt-Trial. There is a strong belief in self-reliance, and seeking divine help is seen as a sign of mental weakness.

## Religion & Beliefs

Cascilians hold no faith in gods, even the Shard Gods who occasionally visit to offer help or guidance. Instead, they follow a spiritual philosophy called Deptism, which emphasizes strength, honesty, and resolve. This belief is widespread, and although small groups of worshippers for gods or powerful beings exist secretly, most Cascilians consider reliance on divine intervention a sign of weakness. They acknowledge these powerful entities exist, but that a mortal should not worship or venerate them. As a democracy, Cascilians strongly believe their families, communities, and nation are entirely capable of making important decisions, and that the collective strength of their republic can guide them through any challenge or crisis.

## Clothing & Adornments

Throughout the republic, the ocean is regarded not only as a vital provider of safety and material goods but also as a symbol of power and strength. This view influences Cascilian fashion, which emphasizes materials that shed water, resist rust, and visually connect wearers to the sea. As a result, Cascilians wear clothing and armor inspired by fish and marine life, crafted from rust- and rot-resistant metal alloys or cloth and featuring sleek designs. These clothes are often incredibly long-lasting, and handed-down elements are passed between families, upgraded, and adapted to individual preferences. Most citizens also wear a small Ortarec shard on chains, serving as a talisman that hums or warms in the presence of active magic, acting as a personal alarm.

## Cuisine & Diet

Almost their entire diet is dictated by the waters of Golden Bay and the Yawning Sea to the south. While they do grow vast fields of golden corn on land to feed their population, Cascilians consider any foods gathered from the ocean as far more important. Fish is considered one of the primary ingredients, and Cascilian fishing ships and fleets traverse the waters and oceans around the republic, with some even crossing the Yawning Sea in defiance of Godkiller Orinth. How they manage to appease his wrath or remain hidden from his gaze is unknown. In the capital of Hoist, one of the most famous recipes is Bisam, a hearty, salty broth made from rendered fatty fish bones, char-grilled vegetables as a garnish, and a large serving of slow-cooked Jurk Fish on top, the skin blistered and made crisp by direct flame.

## Technology & Magic

The republic strongly despises any form of magic, resisting anything that cannot be fully explained by logic, mathematics, or material understanding. Among Ember's cultures, the Cascilians are unique in their pursuit of science without any magical aid whatsoever. They instead observe the world around them, particularly the oceans and seas, and see the strength and power of its creatures and plants as something they can emulate and refine to meet their needs. Consequently, their ships, buildings, clothing, and various objects reflect this approach, leading them down a path of biomimicry. Cascilian technology mimics the forms and functions of natural creatures and elements. Instead of magic, they depend on the wind, waves, and, in rare modern cases, mechanical pulses and hydrostatic pressure.

## Social Structure & Government

Life in the republic revolves around community service, often requiring several years in the Navy or Marines. This creates a Stratocratic Democracy where only those currently enlisted or retired veterans can vote or run for office. Although this might seem restrictive, nearly the entire population serves in either organization for at least a short period, making participation in elections almost universal. The head of the republic is called the Grand Admiral, a position held by the commander of both the Marines and the Navy. They act as the head of state and are advised by a council of elected representatives from major port cities. While the Grand Admiral handles most daily decisions and military matters, the Cascilian Senate is responsible for passing laws and debating reforms that affect daily life. Legislation tends to be strict, emphasizing the "Common Good" of all Cascilians over individual freedoms.

## Language & Communication

The Cascal language is called Cascal and is often seen as terse and guttural by outsiders. Words are often sharp and percussive, and its citizens converse in short sentences filled with nautical jargon and slang derived from naval operations.
