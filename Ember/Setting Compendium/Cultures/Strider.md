## Growth and Decay

The Striders of the Corebright Forest are a hardy people deeply connected to nature, living in harmony with the natural world. They dwell among the treetops of colossal Elderbark trees, residing high in the canopy or within massive trunk villages, isolated from the outside world. They have little concept of progress, choosing instead to live exsist in a state of symbiosis with the trees, plants, animals, and monsters that surround them. Since the Corebright is a wild and untamed region, it is no surprise that many Wild Gods and Leviathans reside there, and the Striders not only worship these beings but have also become increasingly intertwined with the ecosystems they oversee. These local entities embody specific domains, such as the "Lord of the Sun-Dappled Ferns" or the "Matriarch of the Deep Roots and are treated as active, present neighbors whose favor is courted through the maintenance of their specific territories.

Despite this outward harmony, the Striders are often embroiled in internal schisms and conflicts between sects, factions, and groups, each holding different ideas about how nature should be interpreted. One faction might worship a Wild God that exemplifies the strength and beauty of growth, while another may revere a Leviathan dwelling in the tangled root systems beneath the forest floor, following its example of embracing decay and rebirth. These disagreements often boil over into violence, and much like the natural world itself, striders are not above killing and attempting to supplant another group.

To the rest of the world, the Striders are barely noticed on the global stage. They stay hidden in the forest unless they feel threatened. Some neighboring nations, like the Tayan Empire, do not recognize strict borders around the Striders—instead viewing the Corebright Forest as both a threat to control, via the Kirargy Knights, and a resource to exploit. Recently, Osscarhate has made several attempts to attack and clear the forest edges near their territory, even sending armies into the trees to wipe out the Striders and kill as many Wild Gods and levthians as possible. However, these actions did not end well for the Blood Barons and demonstrated to the world that the Striders can unite against a common threat, as seen when they annihilated the undead host that entered the Corebright.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Related Cultures
>
> The Corebright Forest directly touches upon three other cultures, the Tayan Empire in the west, the Blood Barons in the south, and the Cascilian Republic in the southeast. The Tayan Empire doesn't consider the Strider culture to be a threat in any way and treats the Corebright Forest as something they will eventually control through slow expansion and clearing of its trees. The Striders are somewhat fearful of the Tayan Empire, however, and make few efforts to oppose them. The Blood Barons cannot expand into the Corebright Forest effectively due to its overwhelming scale, the ferocity of its creatures, and the lack of denser populations to replenish its numbers; thus, the Blood Barons and the Striders ignore one another. The Cascilians and the Stiders have very good relationships, and the Casilians turn a blind eye to the Druidic magic practiced by the Striders in return for lucrative trade deals.
