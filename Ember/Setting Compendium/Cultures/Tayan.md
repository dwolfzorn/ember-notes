## Manifest Imperialism

The Tayan Empire is a vast and fiercely expansionist state that dominates the western known [[World of Ember]] surface. Through a combination of military might, political cunning, and industrial innovation, the Tayan armies have spread to distant corners of the continent of [[Aterica]]. Born from the fractured Northern Carawn Kingdoms seventy years ago during an event known as the [[Night of Swords]] in the great city of [[Caren'ac]], the city has become the heart of centralized authority. Caren’ac is a testament to the empire’s power, a gleaming metropolis built around a series of concentric lakes. Its towering spires are now crisscrossed with Aeth-Pipes, where red-vapor lamps glow in the dusk, and the constant hum of mechanized industry fills the air.

The rise of the Tayan Empire across the region was swift and often brutal, driven by a doctrine of Manifest Destiny that claimed the Tayan Empire as the preeminent vanguard against the world’s greatest evils, including undeath and monsters. Its armies are spread across multiple fronts: the 2nd Army battles the [[Kelmezian]] City State Alliance, the 3rd campaigns against the [[Maziran]] Kingdoms across the [[Crossing Sea]], the 4th presses north against the vicious Skitari, and the 6th, newly formed, trains in secrecy, its first deployment a closely guarded mystery.

Society within the empire is tightly controlled, with the Empress slowly curbing the power of certain great noble houses that once dominated the empire's cities. Through the erosion of Founded Rights, arranged marriages, engineered debts, and public trials often executed by the fearsome Vyrn, formally known as the Directorate, these families are culled or absorbed, their influence reduced as the empire consolidates control over Aethyra production and key industrial resources. The [[Kirargy Knights]], once the pinnacle of honor and defenders of law, now find themselves increasingly sidelined; some serve the empire directly, commanding armies or guarding cities, while others struggle to maintain relevance in a world reshaped by Aethyric machines.

Central to the empire’s transformation is Aethyra, a volatile alchemical liquid distilled from sulfur, Metizol, and Lithite. Stable until activated with small rods of Lithite, it produces either a red-orange steam or a devastating explosive force, depending on the purity of Lithite, powering everything from urban infrastructure and machinery to experimental rifles, Munitions, armored warframes, and massive mobile siege engines. The cities themselves pulse with emerging uses of Aethyra, steam and vapor rising from the more populous cities with industries now humming day and night, creating a rhythmic industrial heartbeat.

>
> The Tayan Empire is at open war with two neighboring cultures: the Kelmezians to the south and the Maziran Kingdoms across the sea to the west. Their expansionist ambitions have given them a terrible reputation among most of the more distant cultures; however, the Lumek are impressed with their technological progress and have been happy in recent years to host a Tayan-funded military enclave within their borders. The Striders to their east are mostly ignored, although the slow destruction and clearing of the massive Corebright Forest by the Tayan Empire is taking its toll on relations.

Life in the Tayan Empire is both vibrant and perilous: citizens enjoy unprecedented convenience in illuminated streets, mechanized workshops, and public bathhouses, but loyalty is demanded, and the shadow of the Vyrn ensures that freedom carries a constant risk. The air smells faintly of sulfur and metal, the streets hiss with pressurized vapor, and the red-orange glow of Aethyric lamps bathes every corner, serving as a reminder of the empire’s power, its progress, and the ever-present eye of the Empress.

## Customs and Traditions

The Tayan Empire is still a relatively young nation, and as such lacks a unified cultural identity. The Empire is a patchwork of traditions inherited from the many city-states, clans, and territories of the Northern Carawn Kingdoms that were unified under the Imperial banner. Each major city and its surrounding regions retain their own distinct customs, festivals, and social expectations, creating a cultural landscape that is as varied as it is vibrant. Over time, the Tayans have become known for a general reverence for order, personal honour, and civic participation, values championed by the Empress and woven into public life. Whether through local festivals, commemorations of unification, or the everyday rituals of hospitality and respect.

Some cities honor ancient seasonal rites, such as lighting sky lanterns over the Restless Bay or creating small markers as offerings to nature spirits, among the northern cities surrounded by the Vanthar Forest. As time has passed, many of the larger and more developed cities, especially those closer to the heartland, have begun developing new, distinctly Tayan traditions, from the Swords Day Festival to the formalized religious ceremonies under the Three Temples that have elevated within the Empire and the pilgrimages to the Temple complexes themselves where the three main [[Shard Gods]] of the Empire reside personally. While the Empire also publicly encourages local practices, some are seen as problematic and have been gradually eliminated over the years; however, overall, the Empire views its vast cultural differences as a strength.

## Religion & Beliefs

Culturally, the citizens of the Tyana empire follow the pantheon of the Tanir, which includes deities more closely associated with their political enemies, such as Malae or Fenra Irdragon. Officially, the Empire's view on religion is to turn a blind eye to these older beliefs, but unofficially, the Empress enacted a policy of Systematic Dissolution many years ago, a process that slowly encourages her citizens to worship only deities approved by the Empire. To that end, the Empire has three major temples, entire organizations dedicated to the worship of a single god. In the capital of Caren'ac, the Temple of Selai and her Court of Silver is the largest and most influential. Followed by Tirass in the city of Kawn and Zhaen in the city of Kayhurn. To Tayan citizens, their gods are not distant beings but are spiritual leaders and guides that anchor their daily lives and obedience to the laws of the Empire. Pilgrimages to these temples are common, and great processions are sometimes organized for the devout to travel safely from one city to another.

## Clothing & Adornments

Tayan fashion prioritizes a cultural desire for "Refined Elegance," favoring structured silhouettes, high collars, and heavy fabrics like wool and treated leather that resist the oily residue of Aethyra vapor. This also gives the military an iconic mix of ancient knightly flair and modern practicality, with most of the empire's foot soldiers adorned in sharp, distinctive garments. The color palette is dominated by Imperial Crimson, Iron Grey, and deep black. Ordinary citizens wear simple clothing that mirrors this, reduced to basic shapes and ideas, while higher-ranking merchants and members of the bureaucracy wear clothing akin to uniforms, with Gorget-Pins or metallic badges that denote their civic rank. Members of the nobility who belong to the great houses try not to display their wealth and status, inlaying their coats and clothing with gold embroidery on the layers hidden from casual view. While the Tayan nobility try not to directly display their status, they wear decorative chains or embroidered ropes, subtly referencing their financial and political ties to the Empress.

## Cuisine & Diet

Tayan food was once as varied as its people, but with the rise of the Empire and the resulting increased regional safety and security, and unrestricted trade, the population has experienced an unprecedented boom. This surge has created new challenges in food production and supply, especially in supplying its many vast armies. To address this, the empire cultivates large quantities of Iteper, a rapidly growing root crop that thrives almost everywhere, alongside extensive fields of wheat and fruit. As its industry developed, the empire also began producing large amounts of salt from huge mines and vinegar in large-scale factories, which helped preserve food and addressed the issue of widespread food procurement and delivery. A popular staple for workers is the "Caken Pasty," a dense, salt-cured meat-and-root-vegetable pie that can last several weeks on a shelf or in the back of a cart without losing flavor.

## Technology & Magic

As the empire gradually formed, one of its most remarkable achievements was the discovery and production of Aethyra, an alchemical liquid made from sulfur, Metizol, and its catalyst component, Lithite. Nearly all of its technological advancements can be linked to Aethyra in some way. The Tayan Empire has had access to Aethyra for the last thirty years or so and has slowly begun to fully incorporate it into its military, through the use of powerful handheld explosives known as Aethyra Munitions. The Tayan military has also developed ranged weapons that fire bullets at high speed using the energy from these explosions, powered by Aethyra, but most are still experimental and reserved for elite troops. Aethyra has also become integrated into civilian life in major cities, sparking an industrial boom as the high-pressure steam generated by Aethyra reactions powers increasingly complex machinery. Aeth-pipes run beneath main streets, carrying pressurized vapor from central Crucible Houses to public baths, workshops, and lighting pylons. Workshops now operate continuous shifts thanks to reliable power from small Aeth-cells. Simple mechanization—lathes, looms, presses, and drills driven by pressure shafts—has become common. Noise and vibration now dominate the industrial districts, creating a rhythmic pulse that marks the workday.

Alongside this emerging technology, the Tayan Empire also maintains a strong tradition and a desire to continue using magic, which still plays a prominent role in its military. In fact, magic use remains commonplace within the empire, but it is gradually becoming a luxury reserved for nobility or the wealthy.

## Social Structure & Government

To most, the Tayan Empire functions as a Centralized Autocracy, ruled officially by the Empress's will and desires, whose word is law. However, beneath the surface, it is a battleground for rival factions kept in check by her iron will and attention. When she is distracted or unable to monitor everything, issues can slip through the cracks. But the Empress is believed to have eyes everywhere, and she is almost unimaginably well-informed about events happening within the Empire and beyond its borders.

- The Empress & The Vryn: At the heart of the Empire sits the Empress and her family, who rule not through consensus but through absolute power. While she allows governors to handle local affairs in her name, she maintains her authority through her personal enforcers known as the Vryn. This shadow organization acts as the Empire's iron fist of control, using engineered debts, public trials, and accidents to bankrupt and eliminate any noble Holds or political figures that oppose the Empire's goals.
- The Mechanica: This newly formed organization is the true backbone of the Empire’s power and is led by none other than Lorgi himself. The Mechanica controls the distillation, transportation, and regulation of Aethyra. Since every factory, streetlamp, and munition requires Aethyra to function, the Mechanica holds a significant amount of influence over every other faction. They are quickly becoming the new age elite, often clashing with the old nobility, who see these engineers and alchemists as upstarts with greasy hands.
- The Fading Noble Holds: Once sovereign rulers over cities and the sole noble class of the Northern Carawn Kingdoms, the Holds are now in a state of managed decay. Those who survived the Night of Swords and have fallen in line are allowed to keep their titles and estates only on the condition of complete loyalty. Their rights and privileges are being replaced by a slow and effective process of turning ancient lords into middle managers or displacing them entirely.
- The Imperial Army: The largest and most favored branch, the Army is a juggernaut of military power, with massive armies comprising well-trained and efficient soldiers led by brilliant commanders hand-picked by the Empress.
- The Imperial Navy: While the Army conquers the land, the Navy secures the Seas. They are often at odds with the Army for resources as they protect the vital trade routes that keep Caren’ac wealthy.
- The Kirargy Knights: The Old Guard of the Empire, once the proud elite of Tayan society, remains highly influential. Many have joined the military as elite commanders, yet numerous traditionalists continue to strive to protect the people, battling monsters, even as they watch honor give way to expansion, brutality, and efficiency.
- The Temples of the Shard Gods: The three main Temples serve as the moral and spiritual anchor that prevents the Empire from collapsing into civil war. Many Shard Gods publicly support the Empire, and their priesthoods have immense political leverage. The Empress must maintain a delicate balance with the Temples: appease their whims to a degree while curbing their influence.

## Language & Communication

The name of the Empires offical language is Imperial. A precise, standardized language designed for efficiency and command based upon the older Arcden language and newer stranger words. It has replaced many local dialects to ensure the vast bureaucracy and military can function without friction.
