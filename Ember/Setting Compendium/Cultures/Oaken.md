## Sacred Elders

The Oaken are among the oldest cultures on the surface of Ember, maintaining an unbroken lineage since the early days of the Age of Beasts. This longevity is remarkable for several reasons: it spans not just hundreds or thousands of years, but an estimated several hundred thousand years of continuous existence. Their longevity as a culture is impressive, but the fact that the Oaken also live on the small continent of Oakengarde, considered a "Death Realm" due to its extreme hostility, makes this feat almost unimaginable. The environment, flora, fauna, and weather on Oakengarde are incredibly lethal, where merely stepping onto the forested floor is considered a lethal hazard. Consequently, the Oaken cannot reside permanently on the mainland of Oakengarde. Instead, they establish seasonal or temporary settlements that they abandon and rebuild every few years or decades.

Oakengarde is also one of the few places in the Ember Cosmos that did not become infested with monsters after the Shattering. The native creatures were so hostile that even horrific entities from beyond could not slide their way into the ecosystems or threaten the dominance of the apex predators living there. When an Oaken tracks an Elder Behemoth or Jeterio through the jagged forests, their goal is not to tame the land around them or protect themselves. Instead, they hunt to facilitate a violent adaptation. By slaying a dominant predator, they create a power vacuum that forces the surrounding ecosystem to adapt, change, and produce something even more terrifying to fill the void. To the Oaken, this cycle is a religious duty; they believe that by forcing the environment to adapt, they are forging themselves into the ultimate lifeforms.

The Oaken are primarily composed of the Vrjnhar, who are the longest-living ancestors on Ember. As a result, their warriors and hardy survivors live long lives, engage in epic battles, and become incredibly powerful. They rarely stay confined to their homeland either, often venturing into the world to hunt monsters and massive creatures. When the Oaken traverse the seas, they do so in sleek hulls of harvested fossilized wood, reinforced with iron-hard timbers infused with [[Amberal Bronze]]—a magical mineral mined from the forests, fossil-veins, and mountains of Oakengarde. Amberal is also incorporated into their clothing and armor, forging heavy plates that blend the prehistoric spirit of their land with their bodies, enhancing their durability.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Related Cultures
>
> The Oaken have a positive relationship with the Ordani, the Arcturians, and the Kithil; all these cultures have historically enjoyed the help and aid of small groups of Oaken warriors over the centuries. However, the Wardcall have a far less enthusiastic response to the Oaken, they believe the Oaken are responsible for creating their greatest enemies, the Vartic. The Oaken are firm friends with the Bejak, who regularly help with Troll packs and invasions.
>
> Both the Waerds and the Oaken have an intense curiosity about each other, believing that their respective beliefs and the practice of slaying monsters are remarkably similar yet different in several ways.
