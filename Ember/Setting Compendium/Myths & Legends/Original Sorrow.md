# Original Sorrow

- Origin: Early Human
- Knowledge: Well known by [[Kelmezian]][[Kessian]], [[Rhivan]], [[Tayan]], [[Cascilian]], [[Waerd]], [[Lumek]], [[Arcturian]] & [[Ordani]].

The young [[Ancalen]] King Mathwe sails to Venbrec to speak with its ruler, the ancient king Harloc, and asks for his daughter Sorrow’s hand in marriage, thus forging an alliance between the two human Kingdoms. Harloc agrees to the match and alliance and arranges a great feast, with the celebrations lasting for two days, until tragedy strikes and Mathwe’s half-brother Morol, kills the [[Aedir]] ambassador to King Harloc while drunk. Harloc is outraged and demands the head of Morol until Mathwe offers to bring the old king a magic bracelet that will allow its wielder to turn anything they wish into silver.

Mathwe sails east and sets ashore on a small island to resupply but the island proves to be a sleeping Leviathan and it dives into the ocean, nearly drawing Mathwe. He is only saved by the grace of the passing Goddess [[Vekalla]]. Who warns Mathwe of the dangers of traveling east and that he must seek the Clouded Mountain upon which birds nest in great numbers, there he would find the bracelet he seeks.

Alas, while Mathwe is gone, his half-brother Morol, who was tasked with the protection of Prnicess Sorrow grows restless and jealous of his brother, he attempts to seize the throne with his group of followers and takes over the city of Ancalen. He arrives outside the doors of Princess Sorrow’s apartment, but she refuses to let him enter, he tries for three days and nights, using threats, promises and carefully placed words but the princess refuses all his advances. Eventually, Morol grows tired with her refusals and breaks down the door only to find Sorrow standing upon the window sill. Morol rushes forward, but with a cry, she throws herself into the air, wishing to be with Mathwe in death. Her pleas are heard by the God Taleisan, who turns her into an eagle as she falls.

Mathwe arrives at the great Clouded Mountains and travels upwards, braving the harsh winds and monsters on the path. He reaches the top of the mountains and a great nest filled with birds of many different types, all with smaller nests scattered within and filled with eggs of every size and color. Mathwe reaches the center of the nest and finds a mound of treasures and the bracelet he seeks glittering in the sunlight. As he reaches out, however, the countless birds around him screech, cry angrily and attack Mathwe and his men. At this moment, Princess Sorrow finds Mathwe upon the mountains and attempts to embrace him but Mathwe only seeing a great eagle, strikes out with his sword and cuts the bird flying at him in two. As he does so, the birds retreat and fly away into the sky. Mathwe returns to his boat and sails back to Venbrac with the bracelet he promised King Harloc.

With King Harlock satisfied, Mathwe returns to Ancalen to find the city in chaos. He learns of his half-brother's betrayal and storms the palace. He finds his half-brother weeping, and with a cry, he tells Mathwe that the princess was turned into a great eagle because of his jealousy, ambition, and anger. Mathwe realizes with horror that the bird he killed on the Clouded Mountain was his bride and falls to the floor broken. Mathwe then sets sail once again, leaving his regretful half-brother alone upon the throne of Ancalen, and is never seen again. King Morol is later murdered by [[Aedir]] assassins in his sleep.
