# The Spellbreaker

- Origin: Ordani
- Knowledge: Well known by [[Arcturian]] & [[Ordani]].

*The founding of Ordain is well recorded, with many detailed firsthand accounts that have survived throughout the centuries. However, a less well-documented event and tale from around 600 years ago tells of Avilla, the First Shard God of Ordain. Her story has become akin to a folktale and legend for the people of Ordain, but many of the details and true facts remain unknown.*

*Historiain Drevin Markus* -  AS2403

## Avilla of Ordain

During the construction of the grand Cindarin Temple in [[Ordain]], the [[Cindaric Sages]] allegedly* discovered an earth-shattering secret. The tall plateau upon which the temple was being built concealed a mythical [[Ascendancy]], lodged in the black basalt foundations. The Sages kept the discovery secret for many years and treated the Heart Crystal as a sacred relic, hiding it away in a deep chamber. Over time, many attempted to attune to its radiant Lifeforce, but all who tried were consumed in the process.

One day, a child initiate sought comfort and quietude at the foot of the Heart Crystal. The young girl was named [[Avilla]], and she was raised almost entirely by the Sages themselves after a cruel warlock had killed her family. As an orphan, she became an initiate of the Sages out of necessity, and she was said to have been a gloomy and withdrawn figure. No one outside of the Cindaric Sages knew of the Heart Crystal's existence, but she often sat within the chamber to be alone with her thoughts, as did many other Sages at the time. Over the course of several years, an inexplicable understanding seems to have formed between Avilla and the Heart Crystal, without anyone within the Sages noticing. As the sun rose one day over the city, the Sages were shocked to discover Avilla calmly sitting within the chamber, with a now inert and lifeless Heart Crystal in front of her. She had become the first [[Shard Gods]] of their order and of Ordain.

Avilla had little interest in using her divinity at first and had not communed with the Heart Crystal to gain power, merely engaging in meditation and attempting to find peace. While the Sages rejoiced and proclaimed her ascension to the people, asking her to help them, she struggled with her newfound gifts. Over time, the divine power that she now controlled became an uncomfortable weight, and she began to withdraw even further from those around her. She sought anonymity where she could and refused any attempts by the people of Ordain to venerate her.

## The Spellbreaker

As Ordain grew, it drew the attention of distant hostile forces, and dark storm clouds gathered on the horizon. Strange ships of unknown design** and make sailed into the bay of Ordain, driven forth by winged creatures of the sea. In an instant, the city erupted in chaos as spellcraft and magery sprang forth, smashing into the buildings and ships across the bay. The fledgling [[Burnished Hand]] rose to combat the threat but had little answer to the magical forces arrayed against them. The city itself seemed lost until Avilla walked out upon the water into the middle of the battle and drew upon her divine power. For miles around, magic was silenced; no spellcraft nor artifact had any effect, and mages of all kinds clutched their heads in pain. Protected from the mages' evocations, the Burnished Hand turned the battle by firing fire arrows into the helpless foreign ships, and the entire fleet sank into the dark waters***.

In playing this crucial role in defense of the city, Avilla displayed a shocking amount of power that caused the city's population to recoil in fear. Whispers began during the battle's aftermath that she was internally fighting her own gifts and that she was losing control. The whispers and rumors spread rapidly, and Avilla made the decision to leave the city. Before she left, she met with the Holy Speaker of the Cindaric Sages at the time and made a secret pact. She raised a tower on a plateau on the western end of the city and infused this tower with the essence of her power. Throughout its halls, the wild currents of magic stilled, spells having no effect. This tower became a place of solitude and silence, where those haunted by magical afflictions could find respite and where dangerous magics could be contained.

Avilla then disappeared without comment or ceremony. She had no desire to remain in a city that saw her as a hero, a deity, and a menace all at once. However, the tower she built remains a lasting tribute to her legacy as the first Shard God of Ordain. Her story serves as a cautionary tale about the unchecked threats posed by both mages and Shard Gods in general, leading many in the city to perceive her as a tragic individual who sought solitude and had untameable power thrust upon her.

* *My own research into the tale suggests that the Sages found the Heart Crystal somewhere else in the world, and there are hints that they had to bring it up to the plateau where the temple was being built.*
** *No one is entirely sure who was in control of the strange ships that attacked Ordain. My own theory is that they came from the extremely distant continent of Atou, based on a few scattered artifacts dredged from the Bay of Ordain over the years.
*** No records exist of any survivors, and the tales and stories do not mention anyone being held captive after the battle.*
