# Ordinate

The Ordinate is the ruling council of Ordain, a nine-member body responsible for shaping the city’s laws, policies, and long-term vision. It stands as the final arbiter in disputes too large or complex for any single organization to resolve. While many of the city’s major powers, such as the Trading Houses, the Cindaric Sages, or the Anachraenum, manage their internal affairs autonomously, matters that cross boundaries or affect the city as a whole ultimately fall under the Ordinate’s jurisdiction. These include trade tariffs, infrastructure, and city-wide budget allocations, public health initiatives, and the maintenance of civic harmony.

- **Balance of Power**: The Ordinate is divided evenly among three factions: the **Cindaric Sages**, the **Trading Houses**, and the **Hallows**, each holding three seats on the council. This balance prevents any one interest from overwhelming the others. The Trading Houses push for economic freedom and prosperity; the Hallows advocate for fairness, labor rights, and the will of the people; and the Cindaric Sages act as a moral and intellectual counterweight, emphasizing the greater good and long-term stability. Though this structure promotes debate and compromise, it also fosters rivalry and maneuvering behind the scenes as each faction seeks to guide policy to its advantage.
- **Advisory Groups**: While only the nine seated members possess voting rights, several **advisory groups** exert significant influence over the Ordinate’s decisions. Representatives from the **Anachraenum**, the **Burnished Hand**, and the **Veiled Chain** are often invited to consult on specialized matters—ranging from arcane discovery to defense, intelligence, and security. As these groups have influence over their members and those around them, they are also asked to help enforce whatever new rules have been established within the areas where they hold power or sway.
- **The Will of the People:** Ordain’s citizens place deep trust in the Ordinate, viewing it as the living embodiment of their collective will. Once a ruling is made, compliance is swift and rarely contested; communities often take it upon themselves to ensure adherence without the need for force. For those who knowingly defy an edict, however, the Hallows serve as both judge and executor of the city’s justice. For those who are unaware of a change, or unwilling to abide by it, they may be taken in hand by the organization they belong to, or judged by the Hallows if they knowingly break a rule, though violation for violation's sake is rare.

## Operations

The Ordinate functions through a sprawling network of clerks, recordkeepers, and stewards who manage the daily flow of petitions, reports, and proposals from across the city. Every week, the Ordinate council convenes within the Ordinate, a vast marble chamber at the heart of Ordain, to debate, draft, and vote upon laws. These meetings are public by tradition, with citizens permitted to observe from the gallery—though the most sensitive discussions occur in private sessions known as Closed Deliberations.

When a proposal reaches the Ordinate floor, it passes through a three-stage process: Presentation, where the case is argued; Review, during which evidence and testimony are heard; and Judgment, where the nine members cast their votes. In the event of a stalemate, the Speaker of the Ordinate, chosen by unanimous agreement at the start of each term, is granted a deciding vote.

Once a ruling is ratified, it is disseminated throughout the city via the Hallows’ network of Noticeries, as well as through public orators and scribed bulletins. Enforcement is coordinated jointly between the Hallows and the organization most affected by the ruling—be it the Trading Houses, the Cindaric Sages, or another civic body.

## Key Members

The Ordinate’s nine seats are currently held by a blend of visionaries, pragmatists, and schemers whose influence shapes the fate of Ordain. While each represents their own faction, alliances within the chamber shift as often as the city’s tides. Recently, the newest members include [[Katerin Bastilla]], who took over from Ralton Cevher, and [[Okia Tyrwar]], who won an election against [[Conaris Haid]] and [[Steros Kraver]]who replaced [[Jonico Daridane]].
