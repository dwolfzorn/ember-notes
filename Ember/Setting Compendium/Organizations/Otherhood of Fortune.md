# Otherhood of Fortune

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Spoilers
>
> The Otherhood of Fortune is a villainous organization, and the content within this page contains spoilers for the story of Shard of Fear.

The Otherhood of Fortune is a loose organization of bandits, outlaws, and ruffians, typically acting outside of the city of Ordain and led by various independent leaders of extremely questionable morality. The actual name Otherhood is rarely used as connections between these smaller groups is a secret within a mystery and the name is used by its principal leaders and high-ranking members with more understanding of the "Grand Plan" rather than its more rank-and-file grunts. Spread out across the Arctus Plateau the Otherhood is perfectly situated to take full advantage of the chaos, disruption, and unrest that has emerged over recent years. The various smaller groups have each taken on specific areas of opportunity. Banditry, theft, robberies, and even full-on occupation of abandoned villages and farms and resource-rich locations.

- **Rich Opportunities**: The Otherhood thrives on chaos, drawing recruits from desperate refugees, displaced farmers, and idle workers, then seizing abandoned or vulnerable assets such as mills, mines, and supply routes. Their reach is so broad that the Trading Houses, the Veiled Chain, and other organizations cannot stamp them out; every victory against one group is undercut by the rise of another elsewhere.
- **Seawall Stands**: A powerful faction of the Otherhood has coalesced around the seaside town of Seawall, where the charismatic outlaw “Sticks” has convinced the populace that the Trading Houses are the root of the Plateau’s suffering. His propaganda is laced with lies, yet his message carries enough truth to resonate with those desperate for stability, giving the Otherhood a rare stronghold within a settled town.
- **Unseen Strings**: Though fragmented, the Otherhood’s scattered bands show uncanny coordination, often striking in ways that weaken supply chains, destabilize markets, or exploit unrest at opportune moments. This suggests that someone, or something, is quietly guiding their actions toward a larger, more dangerous goal.
