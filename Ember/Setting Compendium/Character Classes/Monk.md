# Monk

Whether they understand the specific wellspring of their abilities or not, Monks of Ember are powered by the cosmic energy of [[Lifeforce]], which manifests itself in many ways to these elite warriors of mind and body. And while they may or may not venerate the [[Heart of Ember]] that provides this metaphyiscal power, each Monk on the living planet benefits from its inscrutable glory. Monk subclasses of Ember represent various cultural and historical traditions that have shaped the way their associated warriors interact with the world, and affect how a Monk's spiritual Focus is able to help them reshape reality itself.

## Warrior of the Astral Self

Warriors of the Astral Self on Ember believe that their body is an apparition, a spiritual tether to the Heart of Ember that persists as an illusory manifestation of their soul's ideal physical form. Most of the Astral Self Monks that walk the earth of Ember are ascetics who consider the pollution of their terrestrial apparition to be a threat to their entire sense of self; and many of the warriors who study this tradition's teachings end up living solitary lifestyles marked by reclusiveness or disarrangement. One of Ember's most prominent monastic schools for Astral Self Monks is the Arcageris — a [[Waerd]] temple in the [[Lowlands]], whose "arcing" Monks adventure to places of power throughout the world in search of metaphysical wellsprings that will help them Focus their concept of the great "Cosmic Force."

## Warrior of the Elements

Warrior of the Elements Monks channel their Focus via the metaphysical wellsprings of Ember's six moons, and are capable of tapping into the magic of each of these lunar bodies to enhance their fighting. Some warriors who study this monastic tradition have a patron moon they tend to favor in their techniques, while others embrace a holistic view of all six moons and their relationship to the cosmos. The earliest monasteries dedicated to the Elements can be traced back to the [[Oaken]] of [[Oakengarde]], who have subsequently established temples and schools throughout Aterica that cater to would-be adherents of their time-honored ways.

## Warrior of the Open Hand

There are no finer masters of unarmed fighting than Ember's Warriors of the Open Hand, whose nimble melee combat techniques can be as graceful as they are deadly. Open Hand Monks are known throughout the world, but their monasteries are exceptionally few. The most venerated institution of this monastic tradition is Swiftwind Temple, located in the foothills of the Runa Highlands, where an ancient [[Hulg'run]] master named Kyan the Swift-fist still teaches the Open Hand techniques he began developing during the time of the [[Abyssal Shear]]. Furthermore, a handful of master Kyan's students have set off into the world and formed monasteries of their own, where new hopefuls can learn the ways of the ancient unarmed experts.

## Warrior of Mercy

Warriors of Mercy are capable of using their spiritual Focus to manipulate the ebb and flow of Ember's metaphysical Lifeforce, enabling them to both heal and harm others. It comes as little surprise to learn that this monastic tradition therefore originates from one of the lunar bodies that orbit Ember rather than from its terrestrial earth. Indeed, the first Warrior of Mercy monks were a group of [[Zeph]] siblings named the Sisu Sisters, who founded a legendary martial arts school on the hollow surface of [[Aura]], the Air Moon. In the centuries since the Sisu School's inception, the teachings of this monastic tradition have made their way to various temples and schools on the surface of Ember, where they persist to this day. And even when they aren't of Zeph ancestry, modern Mercy Monks wear ceremonial masks to honor their lunar forerunners.

## Warrior of Shadow

Warriors of Shadow are Ember's most disparate group of Monks, capable of channeling the tenebrous essence of [[Primordis]] through their spiritual Focus. These clandestine warriors don't often belong to a public-facing tradition or organization, preferring to navigate the world via their own subtle, unseen methods — engaging in activities reserved for the furtive and the obscure. Shadow Monks often study under the secret tutelage of a powerful master, who learned the ways of Shadow from another master before them. This cycle continues in modern day Ember, with networks of Shadow Monks scattered across the face of Aterica and beyond.
