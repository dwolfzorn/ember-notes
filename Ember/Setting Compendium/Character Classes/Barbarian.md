# Barbarian

A Barbarian's Rage can originate from a variety of sources — whether it stems from the blood of the Barbarian's ancestors, the cosmic influence of a shard god, or the primeval Heart of Ember itself. The Paths a Barbarian can walk are numerous, and often carry these indomitable warriors to places unseen by their kith and kin. Barbarians of Ember need not be uneducated brutes with provincial attitudes, but they do tend to shirk many elements of local propriety wherever they may roam.

## Path of the Beast

Barbarians who follow the Path of the Beast are bestial warriors whose souls are infused with primal, animalistic traits. These feral traits manifest during the Barbarian's Rage, physically transforming the warrior and enhancing their destructive potential. Many of those who walk the Path of the Beast on Ember hail from some of the world's most distant and ancient cultures, whose histories are deeply connected to the wild, untamed legacies of the [[Age of Beasts]]. ## Path of the Berserker

Barbarians who walk the Path of the Berserker are violent warriors defined by their natural born fury. Their Rage manifests via an unquenchable thirst for battle — a frenzy that drives them to deal greater damage, which can eventually reach a level of mindlessness that effectively steels the Barbarian against sources of charm or fear. Berserkers are known to hail from nearly every culture on the face of Ember, which leads some scholars to believe that the primal Rage that fuels Barbarians is innate to many (if not all) creatures of the cosmos — no matter how minuscule, whether they harness it or not.

## Path of the Wild Heart

Barbarians who tread the Path of the Wild Heart possess a metaphysical kinship to the various animals and beasts of Ember, and their Rage is a supernatural manifestation of this primal connection. Path of the Wild Heart Barbarians can not only communicate with beasts, they can eventually take on their physical aspects — harnessing the power of wild animals via magical means. What most people don't fully comprehend is that the Path of the Wild Heart is guided by the very Heart of Ember itself, which is tethered to the soul of every living creature on the planet, beasts and Barbarians alike.

## Path of Wild Magic

Barbarians who walk the Path of Wild Magic possess souls shaped by the raw, untamed forces of Ember's Cosmos. Their Rage is harnessed from the chaotic and uncontrollable supernatural essence of the universe, and manifests itself via a random array of wild and uncanny magic effects. This connection also grants Path of Wild Magic Barbarians the innate ability to sense magic around them, and eventually allows them to bolster themselves and their allies in kind.

## Path of the World Tree

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> The term "Yggdrasil" is not used in Ember, but this doesn't mean that Barbarians who walk the Path of the World Tree are not present in the setting. Rather than a connection to Norse mythology, Path of the World Tree Barbarians in Ember are metaphysically linked to the ancient forests of [[Jurin]] — a distant continent located to the southeast of Aterica, far across the Carrow Ocean, known to many as "The Leviathan Preserve."

Barbarians who tread the Path of the World Tree are extremely rare individuals who possess a unique ancestral connection to a far-distant land known as Jurin, the Leviathan Preserve. Path of the World Tree Barbarians tend to be more frequent among [[Cor'ak]] and [[Thornling]] peoples, whose origins in Jurin are noteworthy. The so-called Leviathan Preserve is a place of ancient primordial forests and towering trees of unimaginable scale; a tree known as the Root-father stands at the heart of this distant realm, a singular tree of such power and scale that it rivals even the height of the Broken Tower itself. Barbarians who walk the Path of the World Tree are able to draw upon the primordial power of this tree to fuel their primal Rage, which manifests via the spectral branches of the Root-father itself.

## Path of the Zealot

Barbarians who walk the Path of the Zealot gain their powers from one of Ember's deities, particularly those who prize qualities of passion and avidity among their adherents. For a Path of the Zealot Barbarian, their fanatical Rage is a moment of holy (or unholy) communion, which manifests itself via a powerful transformation of divine transcendence. Numerous shard gods and pantheons hold sway throughout the world of Ember, but some cultures tend to foster more fanatical dispositions than others — such as the [[Lumek]], the [[Kessian]], or the [[Waerd]]. Within these cultures, Path of the Zealot Barbarians tend to fight alongside clerics and priests of their respective faiths.
