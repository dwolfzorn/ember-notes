# Fighter

Warriors from nearly every walk of life on Ember have the potential to become a Fighter, which is arguably the world's most ubiquitous character class. Throughout the eons of the living planet's evolution, the various peoples of Ember have taken arms against some would-be foe at one time or another. And whether a Fighter is part of a traditional faction's teachings — like those of the undead slayers of the Lumarin Homelands or the salaried soldiers of the Tayan Empire — or merely a warrior of their own device, these paragons of martial combat are capable of wielding almost any weapon known to humanoid kind.

## Battle Master

Ember's Battle Masters are called such with good reason. Through rigorous study and application of Ember's numerous combat traditions — often using techniques that stretch back to the ancient [[Oaken]] masters of eons past — Battle Masters have become supremely proficient at the art of war. This warfare acumen allows these martial strategists to control the field of battle with a deftness and precision unheard of, enabling them to outmaneuver foes with powerful demonstrations of their vast combat knowledge. Battle Masters are known to take leadership positions in all manner of armies and war parties throughout Ember — from the [[Waerd]] tacticians of the [[Lowlands]] Bastions, to captains of armed [[Cascilian]] vessels. And, in addition to their inherent value to military groups the world over, Battle Masters are celebrated throughout the Arctus Plateau for their ability to keep organized groups of ruffians and monsters at bay where it matters most.

## Champion

Champions in Ember constitute a broad category of individuals that can be found in every culture throughout the cosmos. These deadly warriors seek victory above all else, and this pursuit of triumph is what drives their potent combat mastery. Champions typically possess a single-minded focus for martial perfection, which supersedes any other concerns. Naturally, this means they tend to act individually rather than working in teams, and are celebrated for their lethal effectiveness in duels and other one-on-one encounters. Thanks to their daring deeds, many Champions of Ember eventually rise to prominent positions of power, fame, and influence — and their accomplishments become the stuff of legend, writ in stories and song to inspire the people of Ember for generations to come.

## Eldritch Knight

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> The term "Eldritch Knight" does not exist in Ember. However, the concept of traditional Eldritch Knight Fighters persists via the alternatives listed below (among others).

Arcane Knights of Ember balance their combat prowess with the traditional study of arcane magic, resulting in a powerful combination of martial skill and spellcasting abilities. Arcane Knights learn from the same repository of magical knowledge as Wizards, and devise their spell lists accordingly — often with an emphasis on spells that will complement their combat expertise. Arcane Knights are not a specific order, nor do they belong to a specific organization. Instead, these magic-wielding Fighters tend to appear among many cultures throughout the world (even ever-so-rarely among those places where magic itself might be shunned).

Chosen of Spectra are a variant of Arcane Knight, similar in almost every respect. More specifically, these magical Fighters worship and revere the Elder Goddess Spectra, and gain their magical insight from her divine mysteries. Chosen of Spectra are often confused with clerics or priests, and this error is an understandable one, since the Chosen of Spectra are known to proselytize the word of the Elder Goddess far and wide. These specialists are far less common than standard Arcane Knights, and tend to only arise after a chance meeting with the goddess herself.

## Purple Dragon Knight

> The term "Purple Dragon Knight" does not exist in Ember. However, the concept of traditional Purple Dragon Knight Fighters persists via the alternative listed below (among others).
>
> #### Dragon Companion
>
> A Purple Dragon Knight's Purple Dragon Companion is referred to as a "Tayan Wyvern" for the purposes of a Kirargy Knight character.

The Kirargy Knights of the [[Tayan]] Empire is a stalwart force of formidable warriors known for their unrivaled devotion to the Dragon Empress herself, who bestows a select few with impressive Wyvern mounts, which they raise from hatchlings to "adulthood" — becoming large enough for the Kirargy Knights to ride them into battle. In exchange for this gift and other magical boons, most Knights enforce the Empire's strict justice via service in its vast and powerful military. Occasionally, errant Kirargy Knights are sent on missions far afield from the Tayan Empire, with orders that service the intricate gamut of the Dragon Empress' machinations; often, this means protection for the Empire's allies — or envoys to its enemies.

## Psi Warrior

Psi Warriors of Ember harness the metaphysical power of their minds to enhance their fighting prowess and push their physical might to its very limits. And while the modern practice of this sophisticated combat tradition can be found throughout Ember, the first Psi Warriors are said to have originated within the ranks of the ancient [[Aedir]] Empire — where a cunning Maejis set out to "improve" the Empire's warriors by gifting them with an enhanced mental aptitude and the ability to change reality itself with their very minds. This cruel experimentation resulted in an entire generation of Psi Warriors, trained from birth and conscripted into the Empire's Hosts once they reached adolescence. Many modern Psi Warriors are the descendants of these ancient Fighters. However, the defunct Aedir Empire is not the only source of Psi Warriors on Ember; thanks to the living planet's inscrutable magic, Psi Warriors (as rare as they may be) come from all walks of life, and often hail from Signaran or Aster lineages.

## Rune Knight

Rune Knights of Ember have incorporated the runes of the ancient [[Shent]] Giants into their fighting styles. Because the genesis of these magic-laden symbols originates in a time long gone, the source of a Rune Knight's runic mastery is a source of particular interest; perhaps the Fighter found an ancient weapon or piece of armor inscribed with the runes, or maybe they stumbled onto a stone tablet or menhir etched with ancient Shent text. Whatever the source of this knowledge, Rune Knights have learned to carve magic runes to enhance their own gear and abilities — a practice they're keen to use as often as their experience allows.

### Bonus Proficiencies

3rd-level Rune Knights of Ember gain proficiency with smith’s tools, and learn to speak, read, and write Pathward (rather than Giant).
