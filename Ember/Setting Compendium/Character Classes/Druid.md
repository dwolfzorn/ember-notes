# Druid

The primal magic of Ember's Druids stems directly from the [[Heart of Ember]] itself, and is a manifestation of the living planet's Lifeforce. Each Druid's connection to nature is an extension of this phenomenon, and the various druidic circles of Ember champion their own methods regarding how best to serve the Heart and its cosmic needs. Anyone on Ember can become a Druid if their connection to the world's deific core is strong enough, but some of the most powerful and prolific Druids throughout time have been [[Aster]] by birth — a notable coincidence for circles who seek to cultivate a prominent (and powerful) membership.

## Circle of the Land

Circle of the Land Druids throughout Ember are known to hail from a wide variety of peoples and places, but the groups most readily associated with the traditions of this order are perhaps the [[Cindaric Sages]] of the [[Arctus Plateau]] and the [[Primalists]] of the [[Corebright Forest]]. While the Cindaric Sages are not generally considered by to be Druids in the traditional sense, their teachings and tenets run unmistakably parallel to the primal spellcasting paradigms of other Circle of the Land Druids throughout Ember. Furthermore, the [[Strider]] of the Corebright Forest are known for the Primalists who hold important stations within their woodland society; and these life-affirming druids are acknowledged far and wide for their innate spellcasting talents.

## Circle of the Moon

Circle of the Moon Druids have learned to harness the power of Ember's six moons to fuel their magical transformations and spells. This relationship with the lunar bodies of the cosmos enhances a Moon Druid's shapechanging abilities beyond their normal ken, and contributes to their comparatively untamed dispositions. The Circle of the Moon has countless orders throughout Ember of various shapes and sizes, which are prone to as much change and migration as the lunar bodies they revere. One of the more prominent associations of Circle of the Moon Druids stems from the shapechanging [[Primalists]] traditions of the [[Strider]]. Indeed, a new sect has arisen among the Druids of the Corebright Forest; referred to as the Moonstruck, these particular Circle of the Moon adherents are known for their reclusive lifestyles and their proclivity for spending a great deal of time in their wild shape forms.

## Circle of the Sea

Druids who join the Circle of the Sea draw their power from the ceaseless ebb and flow of Ember's tides — from the calm of the gentle lakes and streams, to the ferocious energy of the deepest ocean currents. These rare, but often powerful, individuals are known to tap into the primal power of Ember's storms. Some become particularly adept with seafaring and maritime activities, like guiding ships through dangerous seas or serving as stalwart protectors of Ember's aquatic wildlife. One notable sect of Circle of the Sea Druids is the infamous "Special Forces" of the [[Cascilian]] Republic, who are widely recognized as masters of the oceans (despite their relative secrecy).

## Circle of Spores

Druids who become one with the Circle of Spores venerate rot and decay, and their primal magic is powered by the cosmic energies associated with entropy, change, and decomposition. Since death plays such a large part of the natural order of the cosmos (including Ember's mysterious Soul Cycle), Circle of Spores Druids are intimately familiar with the metaphysical potential that lingers in mortal forms, and the capacity for putrefaction to create new life and new forms of energy. Various orders dedicated to the Circle of Spores can be found throughout Ember — including the reclusive Grimbriar sect of the treebound [[Strider]], the intrepid Rotfinders of the undead [[Moiran]] legions, and the resourceful Mycons of the subterranean [[Pathways]].

## Circle of the Stars

Ember's modern Circle of the Stars originates within the foothills and peaks of the Kaustic Hinterlands, where the ancestors of the world's oldest cultures of star-worshippers still endure today. While some of the traditions associated with Circle of the Stars Druids were founded by the ancient [[Shent]] Giants, most latter-day folks associate this sect of seers and dreamers with the [[Kithil]], who often pair their devotion to the night and stars with heady worship of the Elder Goddess [[Ven'avé]]. Beyond the reclusive Druids that inhabit this area east of the Shana Mountains, Ember's Circle of the Stars has also found purchase in lands far afield of the order's origins — with sects operating out of places like the lofty canopies of the [[Duskflare Jungles]] and the wide-open spaces of the Awakened Savanna.

## Circle of Wildfire

Circle of Wildfire Druids are somewhat rare throughout Ember, and are most commonly found among a fervent sect moon-worshipping [[Kithil]] known as the Ustrinas. These Druids ultimately seek to restore life to the ravaged fire moon of [[Ragen]], and their assorted rituals and prophecies revolve around the advent of this most cosmic of conflagrations. Other Circle of Wildfire sects can be found scattered across the world in likely (and unlikely) places, including the Firewardens of the [[Arctus Plateau]], the [[Lumek]] Hearthguard of the Lumarin Homelands, and the Sun-seers of [[Kessia]].
