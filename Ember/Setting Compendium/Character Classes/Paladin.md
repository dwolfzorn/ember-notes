# Paladin

Ember's Paladins receive their divine power from one or more of the world's Elder Gods or Shard Gods, who grant the holy warriors various deific boons based on their respective domains of influence. Anyone on Ember can become a Paladin, if their faith and desire are strong enough. In fact, some Paladins take up oaths that defy categorization or fealty to a god, and are instead driven by some inner conviction that perhaps only the Paladin themself might understand.

## Oath of the Ancients

Paladins who take the Oath of the Ancients are rare individuals of Ember who have have a holy connection to one of the ageless [[Casia]] of [[Primordis]]. Among the oldest entities in creation, the enigmatic Casia bestow their mortal champions with the primordial magic of their native Inner Realm; and these boons allow Oath of the Ancients Paladins to channel the arcane essence of Primordis itself, to protect both themselves and those around them. Precisely how this bond between Paladin and Casia is formed is often a source of mystery itself, although various sects of Oath of the Ancients Paladins are known to reside within the [[Lowlands]] of the Waerd — where the holy warriors of Primordis can subtly influence terrestrial events relatively unnoticed.

**Associated Deities**: [[Nimaelle]], [[The Silent Pack]], [[Vesper]]

## Oath of Devotion

As stewards of justice and order, Oath of Devotion Paladins tend to serve gods and organizations of Ember who embody the same heroic ideals. More often than not, these virtuous holy warriors serve at the behest of one of the world's more noble and benevolent [[Deities]], such as [[Raineka]], [[Lumé]], or the elder god [[Lantyr]] (among others). Oath of Devotion Paladins also tend to find themselves allied with organizations whose tenets and goals are aligned with Ember's nobler pursuits — like the heroic protectors of the [[Burnished Hand]] who serve the [[Cindaric Sages]], or the holy [[Lumek]] warriors of the Lumarin Flameguard.

**Associated Deities**: [[Alar]], [[Lantyr]], [[Lumé]], [[Raineka]], [[Sockets]], [[Thoma]]

## Oath of Glory

The call of destiny is incessantly strong for Paladins who take the Oath of Glory, and this type of headstrong, star-crossed warrior has existed on the face of Ember since time immemorial. Nearly anyone who can wield a sword has the potential to take up an Oath of Glory and become one of these stalwart holy warriors, but the most storied among them are blessed by the gods or Ember itself with qualities that serve their cause — like preternatural strength, raw charisma, and unerring courage. In one way or another, most (if not all) of Ember's shard gods, elder gods, and god-like beings seek out champions to serve as conduits for their own deific prestige; Oath of Glory Paladins are the heroes and antiheroes that answer this call, and can hail from any walk of life. Paladins such as these can also often be found among the [[Waerd]], who prize the undertaking of glorious deeds, to be celebrated in legends for ages untold.

**Associated Deities**: [[Janar]], [[Ku'arta]], [[Lanespear]], [[Lorex]], [[Obrisire]]

## Oath of the Noble Genies

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> The term "Genie" does not exist in Ember. However, the concept of traditional Oath of the Noble Genies Paladins persists via Ember's "Oath of the Elemental Moons."

Paladins who take up the Oath of the Elemental Moons in Ember have sworn allegiance to the cosmos itself, and place their fealty in the metaphysical arms of the planet's quartet of elemental moons: [[Cora]], [[Aura]], [[Ragen]], and [[Mayis]]. The four abilities of the Elemental Smite trait learned by these Paladins at Level 3 have been renamed as following: Crush of Cora, Escape of Aura, Fury of Ragen, and Surge of Mayis. Meanwhile, the "Lunar Spells" and "Lunar Splendor" also granted at Level 3 behave as originally intended, with no changes to the spell list, etc. Elemental Moon paladins hail from all corners of Ember, but are most commonly encountered throughout the lands of the [[Kithil]] and the [[Varún]].

## Oath of Vengeance

Righteous punishers driven by the desire for retribution, Paladins who take the Oath of Vengeance embody their sworn vendetta through their everyday actions and the grander paths they walk in life. A Paladin's vengeance can take a wide variety of forms throughout Ember — from a sworn dedication to right some cosmic wrong, to something as relatively simple as an innate need for personal retribution. As a result, Oath of Vengeance Paladins can be as loyal to a metaphysical cause or concept as they are to a specific deity and their tenets. The temples of [[Thayloc]], however, are noteworthy for their high concentration of Paladins who swear by this Oath, and bastions dedicated to this loose affiliation of Vengeance warriors can be found throughout Aterica.

**Associated Deities**: [[Nite]], [[Thayloc]]

## Oath of the Watchers

Paladins who swear by the Oath of the Watchers dedicate their lives to protecting Ember from the persistent and malevolent encroachment of otherworldly influences — whether that extraterrestrial power stems from the [[Abyssal Shear]], or the curious depths of the Inner Realms ([[Primordis]], [[Signara]], and [[Luxarum]]). Most of the Paladins who swear by this oath protect Ember's mortal realms from the loathsome, unknowable chaos of the Abyss, and the horrors that lurk beyond the Weave (like the Paladins of the [[Lumek]] Flameguard. Some, however, oppose the influence of the Inner Realms, and stand proud against the celestials of Luxarum, the fiends of Signara, or the fey of Primordis. Whatever motivates these hostilities, Watcher Paladins are sure to extend their divine aid to others in times of need.
