# Bard

The Bard Colleges of Ember — whether organized or scattered to the winds — help define the various ways a Bard's artistry is able to come to life via their magic. Bards are ultimately drawn to their respective Colleges by any number of reasons, but this call to adventure is often rooted in the glamorous and awe-inspiring magic of the cosmos. Many of Ember's Bard Colleges began as terrestrial schools of artistic study, where hopefuls would learn beneath the tutelage of talented experts; however, as the years have passed, so too have the traditions of these private institutions — into the hands of errant bards the world over.

## College of Creation

Ember's College of Creation originates with the fabled [[Cindaric Sages]], who believe that the [[Heart of Ember]] is the prime mover and central force of the cosmos. Those who follow Cindaric teachings learn songs, harmonies, and meditative chants that connect them back to the first days of creation itself. Some Creation Bards have studied under this storied tradition with the Cindaric Sages (also known as Heart Singers), while others have managed to find their way to the Heart of Ember's primeval song without the Sages — via folk music or ancient words and melodies handed down through centuries untold.

## College of Eloquence

The first College of Eloquence on Ember was established by Grand Retorician Molnar, a powerful Bard of the [[Cascilian]] Republic who founded the oratory College within the bayside city of Hoist. For generations, the Eloquence Bards here have been taught to hone their craft within the Halls of Virtue — where the art of persuasion is considered a fundamental and powerful skill. Nevertheless, these teachings have recently spread beyond the borders of the republic. Thanks to the intrepid seafaring that defines Cascilian society, teachings from the College of Eloquence have made their way to Bards from Akazea to Oakengarde (and everywhere between).

## College of Dance

Inspired by the constant shifting of Ember's cosmos, Bards who study from the College of Dance manifest their arcane magic via the preternatural power of physical movement. Their artistic expression through motion is a terrestrial reflection of the cosmic harmony that binds and reinforces the universe and all things in it. Naturally, Dance Bards are dexterous performers, whose speed and agility are keenly augmented by the metaphysical power of their craft. The College of Dance finds its roots in several cultures throughout Ember, including the [[Lumek]] Fire Dancers of the Lumarin Homelands and the [[Maziran]] Zarsa Weavers of World's End. More than any other Bard College, these disparate traditions have many elements in common - stemming from an unprecedented amount of shared respect for each other's style, values, and heritage.

## College of Glamour

Ember's College of Glamour originates from the Fey-fraught Inner Realm of [[Primordis]], where the metaphysical concepts of shadow and illusion reign supreme. Founded by powerful [[Casia]] and their Feyfolk allies, the College of Glamour is steeped in magic both inspirational and beguiling. Bards of this College — also known as Shadow Bards — are capable of weaving powerful spells infused with the chaos and whimsy of "The First Forest," and are known to frequent all corners of Ember's expansive cosmos. And while this College's fundamental origins can be traced back to Primordis, nearly every culture of Ember plays host to their own College of Glamour, with regional traditions and artistic mainstays that reflect their particular roles in society.

## College of Lore

Bards associated with the College of Lore are Ember's great collectors and purveyors of stories, whose magic is manifested via the recitation of myths, legends, folk tales, and such that reach back time immemorial. A scholarly approach to their craft allows Lore Bards to study magic that would otherwise be inaccessible to them, and they weave these esoteric spells with the same natural ease as their songs. Bards of the College of Lore tend to revere the mysterious entity known as [[Laeora]], and the most dedicated of her adherents refer to themselves as "Truth Speakers." The College of Lore has a foothold in several of Ember's great cities, often aligned with [[Anachraenum]] strongholds and the like. The devout academics who comprise the ranks of the College of Lore throughout Ember are known as the world's most accomplished storytellers, skilled raconteurs who spin yarns like a sorcerer shapes reality.

## College of the Moon

Ember's College of the Moon is steeped in the legends and lore of the [[Duskflare Jungles]], the homeland of the [[Waerd]], where the influence of ancient and mysterious magic runs deep. Bards who study the teachings of the College of the Moon are capable of channeling the primal power of the Duskflare wilderness through their art, which is also subtly and curiously tinged by the Fey magic of [[Primordis]]. Duskflare Folktales and Blessings of the Waerdwells are common manifestations of this College's teachings, which have begun to spread beyond the borders of the [[Lowlands]] Bastions in recent centuries.

## College of Valor

Bards who learn their craft from the College of Valor study the heroic deeds of Ember's ancient champions, and carry their bardic talents into battle. Some of the most celebrated Valor Bards hail from the [[Oaken]], a culture of proud monster-hunters and dauntless survivors, whose storied myths and legends date back to the mystery-laden [[Age of Beasts]]. The Oaken's Skalds of Valor are known the world over as keepers of legend whose songs can stir their allies to perform incredible deeds. But the College of Valor is not restricted to the traditions of [[Oakengarde]]; Valor Bards are frequent among many of Ember's cultures, from the folk raconteurs of the [[Arctus Plateau]] to the learned remainders of the ancient [[Aedir]] Empire.
