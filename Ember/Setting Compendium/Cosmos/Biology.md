# Biology

While the shared Lifeforce from the [[Heart of Ember]] ensures that most species are magically compatible, often due to common origins, this rule is not absolute, as compatibility breaks down in certain extreme situations.

## Fundamental Incompatibility

The primary limit to compatibility is the vast difference in physiology. If two creatures have fundamentally different reproductive methods or exhibit a significant difference in their physical nature, the process cannot succeed. For example, a creature that lays eggs cannot reproduce with one that utilizes a womb. Just as a small insect cannot reproduce with a large warm-blooded creature.

Another limit to compatibility stems from the influence and introduction of monsters during [[The Shattering]]. The alien nature of monsters seems to prevent reproduction in all but the rarest cases, and typically, the resulting offspring from such a merging is considered a monster itself.

A less permanent but still profound incompatibility arises when a creature or its ancestry undergoes extreme, targeted changes driven by powerful entities such is the case with [[Hulg'run]]. If a creature or ancestry is changed to the point that they can no longer reproduce with any other creature, then they become isolated to their own type. This specialized incompatibility can sometimes be bypassed through magical intervention, but is often insurmountable.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### The Adaptation of Life
>
> The evolution of creatures within the Ember Setting occurs through radical diversification of the life-giving power that the Heart of Ember imbues into every new soul through a process called Divergent Alteration. The Heart of Ember unpredictably bestows new traits upon the offspring of an existing species or manifests entirely new life forms. Scholars speculate that these alterations are not random mutations, but rather that the Heart of Ember is intentionally experimenting with new forms of life in order to ensure the continuation of existence. Regardless of the truth of this process, all cultures understand that it is not uncommon for children to be wildly different from their parents. Offspring bearing these changes are often called [[Aster]], however sometimes such changes simultaneously affect a wide group or are reinforced over a prolonged period of time, resulting in new species with a large enough population to sustain their unique traits.
>
> In addition to the unique traits imprinted upon new life by the Heart of Ember, creatures on Ember are also shaped by the exerted will of powerful entities. [[Elder Gods]] have been known to also introduce fundamental or sweeping changes to groups of creatures. The most prominent recorded example of this involves [[Alar]] and the creation of [[Dragons]]. More frequently, the [[Wild Gods]] direct the [[Wildsong]] to nurture flora and fauna of Ember towards more resilient or adaptive traits. Most of the current mortal ancestries of Ember were in some way shaped or directly created by powerful gods, for example the [[Ashka]] and the [[Nir'ae]].
