# Itakurius

- Occupation: Philosopher, Historian
- Period: Unknown (Possibly around 2,000-3,000 years before the Shattering)
- Affiliation: Unknown
- Ancestry: [[Ashka]]
- Famous Works: Itakurius's Theory of Universals, Embrace of Tyrants, The Lost Empire

Itakurius was an ancient Ashka philosopher and historian. Her writings cover various subjects, including the natural sciences, philosophy, magical experimentation, history, and the arts. Very little is known about the life of Itakurius except that she was an extremely prolific writer who seemed to have also been an accomplished Wizard. Her writings have survived partly because many of her enchanted and protected tomes resist decay. Even so, every record so far recovered is a priceless tome, and only eight complete works exist; the rest are scattered pages and incomplete scrolls discovered in remote ruins.

### Personality

Very little is known about her personality other than what can be conveyed through their writings. By her own accounts, they were meticulous and extremely thorough in their analysis of the things around them and extremely technically minded. Many of her recovered works are filled with as many diagrams, mathematical computations, and magical formula as they are words. She did not seem to show personal opinion on matters except in rare cases where they reference the Sunfire Empire of the [[Aedir]]. Here, a slight edge of contempt and dislike creeps into some of her phrases.

### Connections

There are a few references to Itakurius living within a great city on a jungle island, but these are often brief. In these references, she sometimes refers to herself as a magical adept of some fame. Given the context, lack of hubris, and humble nature of the rest of her writing, it could be inferred that she was likely an exceptional, even legendary figure in her time.
