# Drevin Markus

- Occupation: Historian
- Period: (2310- 2402 AS.)
- Affiliation: [[Anachraenum]]
- Ancestry: [[Wirrun]]
- Famous Works: The Complete Works of History, Post Shattering by Drevin Markus

Professor Drevin Markus was a prominent and influential member of the Anachraenum that died a hundred years ago. He authored several works that are still used as the basis of knowledge for events after the[[The Shattering]] around the Arctus Plateau and beyond. He was famous for being bookish, cowardly, and difficult to work with. This meant he rarely traveled outside of Ordain and instead sent members of the Anachranum all across the known world in search of seemingly unimportant pieces of information to collaborate on his own theories and opinions. He lived his entire life in [[Ordain]] and only left All-Fable Keep to give talks and public speeches on history.

### Personality

Professor Markus was said to be a singular intellect with a vast knowledge that seemed to connect seemingly unrelated snippets of information into wild ideas and stunning leaps of the imagination. Despite his brilliance, he was also said to be a cantankerous, lecherous, and thoroughly dislikable character who often clashed with other members of the Anachraenum. He was famously short of temper and spent the last few years buried so deeply into his books it was little surprise to anyone when he was found quite dead amidst a mountain of books, but none of his students had wanted to interact with his slumped form.

### Connections

During his time, Drevin Markus was considered a towering figure of historical study; he spoke often and loudly about his own works and was well-regarded publicly. His lectures and public talks were extremely captivating, and he was responsible for spreading knowledge of history throughout Ordain. He had many intellectual admirers who supported his work from across the known world, and he was able to amass a sizeable fortune that he passed onto the Anachraenum upon his death, smoothing over some of his rougher edges in the minds of his fellows.
