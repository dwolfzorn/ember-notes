# Shard Gods

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Lore Summary
>
> - Origin: Created by communing with a Heart Crystal.
> - Mortal Aspects: They are ascended mortals that retain their physical bodies.
> - Worship: They can hear and grant distant prayers, intervening from anywhere in the cosmos, but are severely limited in power based on distance.
> - Soul Anchors: They have no impact on the Soul Cycle.
> - Destruction: They can be killed.

The Shard Gods of Ember are exceptionally powerful immortal individuals who gained their power through ascension. They are often part of sprawling webs of ancient ties, pantheons, and shared interests across thousands if not hundreds of thousands of years. Each Shard God has its own motivations surrounding the governance of their chosen aspects of power and domains and the people they hold dear. They can be fickle, resolute, charming, manipulative, or dangerously direct, and there is as much variation between Shard Gods as there is between mortals.

In the present age, hundreds of Shard Gods live upon the World of Ember, less now than before the events of [[The Shattering]], but there are still six major pantheons containing multiple Shard Gods upon [[Aterica]] alone. Shard Gods can only be found upon the [[World of Ember]], and while some may travel to places like the Elemental Moons or the Inner Realms, this is rare. The vast majority of Shard Gods live among the cultures and peoples that worship them, sometimes hidden and guiding events from behind the scenes or openly displaying their power. Often, a Shard God will remain to guide their people or descendants, staying and living in the same area for millennia.

Shards Gods are not created as such; they are ascended mortals (or, in extremely rare cases, ascended creatures) that achieve godhood by touching and communing with a [[Ascendancy]]. This process is not well understood, and the vast majority of mortals believe that to become a Shard God, all that is required is to discover a Heart Crystal and touch it. This is not accurate, however, and there are mysterious and obscure steps upon the path to ascension. Purity and goodness are seemingly not required, as there are many Shard Gods who are greedy, malicious, and foul. In fact, the criteria for Ascension is something that many spend their entire lives studying. Talking to a Shard God about what they experienced might be one of the first steps of that process, but unfortunately, few, if any, are able to describe the process in any detail, and it varies wildly enough that a single point of view cannot be trusted.

Mortals on the surface of Ember are well aware that while Shard Gods are extremely powerful beings, they can be killed or wounded, and there are many tales and stories of Shard Gods dying in conflict with cosmological forces, against one another, or even powerful mortals in a few rare situations. Despite this, many mortals dream of becoming a Shard God, which fuels their exploration and adventuring spirit to new heights as they search for Heart Crystals, some spending their entire lives pursuing power. Some of the Shards Gods active in the world today are very young, younger than some living mortals. [[Aythorn]], a member of the [[Nineteen]], is roughly three hundred years old and ascended so recently that they barely have any followers other than the people they have directly interacted with. There is no strict overarching hierarchy within the ranks of Shard Gods, but some Pantheons have more strict and ordered roles for their members. The ancient pantheon of the [[Aedir]] was partially ordered among family lineage and age.

## Aspected Power

The chosen power of a Shard God over a particular element, force, or essence is often their most defining trait. But this isn't something that the Shard God actually has any special power or control over. Shard Gods are not granted any specific aspected power or domain upon ascension; instead, they tend to gravitate towards concepts that resonate with their own personalities. This means that Shard Gods can have competing titles and areas of concern. When describing a Shard God, it's more accurate to call them "a God of" rather than "the God of" in this respect, i.e., in the present, there are currently three Shard Gods of Fire and four of Battle in various places of the world. Some believe that Shard Gods are not Gods at all, but this opinion is considered heretical and blasphemous; after all, Shard Gods are common enough that almost all mortals worship them in some form or another.

However, this thought exercise may have some basis in truth; the worship of followers and belief does not create nor ascend a Shard God, and many do not require temples or priests. Though some Shard Gods insist worship is necessary for their survival, they are merely vain and capricious beings who enjoy devotion. Praying and worshiping a deity is simply a convenient way for Ember's mortals to connect with the gods who are aligned with an aspect they devote themselves to. Some Shard Gods reject this premise entirely and do not even play the game, instead insisting that they are just powerful beings and no gods at all, but this a mysterious theological argument among scholars as Shard Gods can answer prayers and grant power to their followers, indirectly in small amount or in large amount to mortals they treasure. In addition, they possess several strange abilities and powers that suggest they are not merely powerful immortals.

A Shard God's power is typically a set amount dictated by the age of the Heart Crystal they ascended with, and they are imbued with vast amounts of what many refer to as Lifeforce that originates from the [[Heart of Ember]]. It does not increase with age after ascension nor from the belief of their followers. However, Shard Gods can grant this power and blessings to their followers. While this has an extremely minor effect on their total power, and it only diminishes them by a small amount, with enough followers, a Shard God's power can noticeably reduce. However, this is just borrowed power, and if the Shard God chooses or upon their follower's death the Shard God receives this power in return.

Every Shard God can imbue followers with power; the most common are priests, clerics, paladins, and ordinary mortals who pray to them. But a Shard God is not merely a distant figure to be worshipped, and making personal connections is fully possible. Meeting and serving a Shard God directly has several benefits, and in rarer cases, it's likely that a mortal will be imbued with greater power and will make a pact with their chosen deity.

## Mortality & Destruction

Shard Gods are immortal and do not age but they are not invulnerable and can be killed. This is extremely difficult not only because of the pure power that would be required but Shard Gods are imbued with Lifeforce and this gives them remarkable abilities to resist damage and regenerate. If a Shard God is killed, their Lifeforce will be reabsorbed back into the Heart of Ember.

Shards Gods are imbued with Lifeforce and fundamentally oppose Abyssal Energy. They cannot be corrupted in the same way that mortals can, though in rare cases, they can be "twisted." This is still so rare that only one case is known, and the information should not be shared.
