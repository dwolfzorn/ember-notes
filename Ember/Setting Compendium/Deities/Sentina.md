# Sentina

The Sentina slowly emerged as the [[Shent]] culture formed following the events of Unveiling, when the [[Giant]] left [[Pathways]] and began to settle the jagged [[Shana Mountains]]. At the staggering height of Mount K’hente, they founded the colossal city of She’lu, a city of silver, stone, and celestial geometry. For thousands of years, the Sentina oversaw the Shent culture, with many of its members living among the people in the city of She'lu itself. I was an age of quiet contemplation, with countless star-gazers, seers, and scholars living among the freezing peaks at the heart of the [[Aterica]] continent. At the head of the Sentina was [[Ven'avé]], whom the Shent considered the most important deity in existence despite her often infuriating silence, followed swiftly by Orizon, who lived in the Grand Temple of the Stars within She'lu and helped guide the Shent on a more daily basis.

This age of peace and reflection ended violently during [[The Shattering]], as the Shent came under direct attack by forces of the Shroud. As horrific monsters tore through the city of She’lu, many of its [[Shard Gods]] perished alongside their people. However, whispers remain that the Sentina did not vanish entirely. Vesta'ec had already parted from his fellow Sentina gods before the events of the Shattering, and his followers, known as the [[Varún]], managed to survive the conflict. It is not known exactly what happened to Vesta'ec, however.
