# Laeora

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Author of the Setting Compendium
>
> Laeora is responsible for the majority of the Setting Compendium writing, drawing on her own perspective, gathered notes, and experiences across the ages. While not everything is written by her, the Setting Compendium of the Ember setting could be considered her Amber Historie, said to contain a largely accurate account of the world's forces, creatures, monsters, and beings, even those found on the Elemental Moons and the distant Inner Realms.

Laeora is an extremely well-known figure within the Ember [[Cosmos]]. She has been a celebrated hero of renown since long before the [[Aedir]] Empire, with records of her appearance going back to the [[Age of Beasts]]. Her identity is something of a mystery as she doesnt seem to be a deity, nor does she seem to be mortal. She is focused on history, recording events, and learning about the world. However, the way people view her has begun to shift recently, as she seems to be taking more opinionated stances on political and social issues. Many scrolls, accounts, and hidden and secret texts are sometimes recovered from ancient days, with her name appearing as the author. Even today, Laeora is credited with numerous historical records. She appears in the most unlikely of places, and while some consider her to be a strange tale or myth, most scholars agree that she is as long-lived and as knowledgeable as she claims.

Many believe Laeora to be a [[Shard Gods]], something she corrects whenever the opportunity arises. Despite her denials, her appearances are rare enough that debates regarding her true nature and her striking similarity to known deities can persist for decades. Even other Shard Gods are often perplexed by her true longevity and source of power, as she offers few explanations for either when she surfaces in populated areas.

Despite her immense age and wisdom, Laeora's memory is not a perfect historical record. Paradoxically, while she can recall the broad origins of something like the Aedir Empire, she struggles with the details of those distant eras. She tends to forget highly specific, localized events that occurred in ages past, particularly the mundane ones, as well as emotional contexts she never properly processed. This vulnerability is the singular reason she keeps the Amber Historie, a small, plain leather tome that serves as her personal, external memory. This tome is meticulously filled with her own observations, theories, and accounts from across millennia. It is her most treasured possession; without the detailed, physical record provided by the Amber Historie, the truth of many foundational events in Ember's history would be irrevocably lost.

Laeora is vehemently opposed to the rise of the [[Tayan]] Empire and views [[Calyn Kariset]] as a tyrant. This has put her in a very unique situation, and she doesn't seem to hold back from helping repel the Tayan armies' advances, either by attempting to interfere with their many conquests or by exposing the Tayan imperial ambitions and evils through her writing, and is a key member of the organization known as the [[Vanguard]]. Curiously, her opposition seems somewhat personal, but Laeora has never elaborated on her motivations.

> [!quote] Read Aloud

I have met vain, power-hungry gods, resisted their tirades, and withstood their searing attempts to destroy my mind. I have witnessed the greatest magics and untamed sorceries lay waste to cities, armies, and entire nations, enough devastation to break the earth and tear the very sky asunder.*

## Appearance

Laeora appears as a [[Human]] woman of medium height, with an athletic build. Her most distinctive features are her soft, mousy-brown hair, which mysteriously shines like gold at the ends, and her glittering, bright-blue eyes with dark black shadows framing them. She is otherwise perfectly ordinary in all other physical aspects. Her attire is practical and understated; she favors a plain brown coat with traveler boots of exceptional quality. Across her chest, she wears crisscrossing leather bands over a dark shirt, which often serve as a makeshift harness for her pouches, small scrolls, and notes bound tightly to these straps for security. Strapped to her side is a seemingly simple, single-bladed sword of plain steel. On her other side is the Amber Historie, a small, plain leather tome with a small black pen attached by a blue ribbon. This tome is said to be an ancient artifact of legendary power, as it contains the written words of Laeora's entire life and her experiences of the world.

Laeora does not tend to wear armor, but she compensates for her lack of defense with incredible speed and agility. Accounts of her prowess with her blade are common in both ancient legends and recent reports from her battles against the Tayan Empire, where her sword skills are legendary. In combat, a soft glow can be seen surrounding her body and her sword. This distinct white and blue expression of her power grows stronger the longer she fights.

## Influence and Followers

Her appearances over the last several decades fighting against the Tayan Empire have led many [[Kelmezian]] and [[Maziran]] to adopt her symbol, and she has gathered a small, dedicated following. While her militant followers focus on her excellent tactical knowledge and martial power, she is also considered an extremely compassionate leader who genuinely cares about those under her command; thus, her followers adore her and fight furiously in her name. However, she is not always present at the front lines and still seems to wander a great deal, often giving warnings to other nations and peoples regarding the threat posed by the Tayan Empire's expansion.

It's also said that during the Sunfire Aedir Empire, Laeora had a large cult-like priesthood that worshipped her aspect of wisdom, but very little is known about this group. The few scraps of information do speak of how this group was also capable warriors, and it seems Laeora is no stranger to conflict.
