# Spectra

**Areas of Concern:** Magic, Knowledge, Mystery

**Typical Followers:** Scholars**,** Spellcasters

**Domains:** Knowledge, Other

**Pantheons:**[[The Nineteen]], [[The Tanir]], [[Sunalin]], [[Solaru]], [[Sentina]]

**Symbol:** Swirling motes of magic, which never quite look the same twice.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Spectra**
>
> > Embrace the knowledge of magic and the magic of knowledge.

While other entities might govern specific elements, Spectra is the Elder Goddess of Magic, who represents the delicate, high-level synthesis of the arcane and the craft of magic alongside the combination of the fundamental forces. Spectra is traditionally regarded as the patron of passionate scholars, exceptionally gifted mages, and those who have dedicated centuries to unraveling the nature of the cosmos. To the average mortal, she often appears aloof or utterly uninterested in the world’s affairs, as her edicts and ideas are notoriously dense, requiring obscure knowledge and a level of theoretical understanding that many cannot grasp even after a lifetime of study. However, this perceived coldness is merely a side effect of her intense focus. In truth, Spectra is a remarkably warm and excitable figure. When she finds a mind capable of following her logic, she becomes a tireless conversationalist, often found in the great libraries of the world, losing herself in ancient texts and the exchange of radical new ideas.

Spectra sees herself as a researcher, and her journey as a tireless pursuit of understanding. She is content to dedicate an unbelievable amount of resources, attention, and time to a new project, but is often undone by her lack of concentration and her inability to see a project through to the end. To many, this makes her appear wildly unfocused, hyper-fixating on one problem only to pivot and dive into another without a moment's notice.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > Spectra makes the air taste like ozone, old parchment, and an unidentified flower in full bloom. The magic gets so thick you can practically stir it with a spoon. It’s wonderful for your spells, but terrible for your head. Everything around her just... vibrates.
>
> ~Laeora

Among the other [[Elder Gods]] and even among many [[Shard Gods]], Spectra is very active and approachable, frequently walking the surface world in various guises to satisfy her endless curiosity. Despite this, she remains largely detached from the ordinary problems of mortals. Wars, successions, and petty politics rarely catch her eye, and her attention is drawn only to magical phenomena. On the rare occasions she offers direct assistance or power, it is almost never to resolve a local conflict, but rather to solve a magical mystery that has piqued her hyper-fixated interest. For Spectra, the preservation of mystical knowledge and the solving of a good puzzle will always take precedence over the rise and fall of empires.

## Appearance

Spectra appears as a breathtaking convergence of arcane colors and symbols. As an Elder Goddess, she can take any form she wishes, but consistently prefers a feminine humanoid form that towers over mortal ancestors. She appears wrapped in a dress of the deepest midnight black, a darkness so profound it seems more like a light-absorbing void than woven fabric. At the nape and hem, constellations and swirling magical colors pulse and flash, twisting in complex and mesmerizing patterns of raw magical arcana. Fragments of uncast spells swirl in a perpetual, humming dance around her, as she moves, flickering into existence and then disappearing just as quickly. Her skin is as dark as polished obsidian with wide, glowing eyes of golden, glittering amber. Crowning this somber visage is her hair, a brilliant corona of pure arcane energy that matches the shifting colors of the magic on her dress. It is not styled but rather frames her head like the flares given off by a raging fire, shedding bright sparks of magic when she turns her head in excitement.

## Influence and Followers

Spectra has a large following among scholars, librarians, and researchers, but mages and wizards are often her primary followers throughout Ember and the Cosmos. In some respects, she is a more "present" Elder God than her peers, and she can often be found in libraries, academies, and magical schools in equal measure. Her visits to these places are rare in the grand scheme of things, with many years or even centuries between them, but they are often documented with precision. Perhaps her most visited library is the [[Star Academy]] of the [[Star Mages]] in [[Rhivan]], where she has her own vast complex chambers, where she likes to surround herself with adoring followers, each attempting to gain her attention with a new discovery or idea. She is likely partially responsible for many leaps forward in magical understanding and the creation of new spells, and she forms close bonds with many bright Star Mages dedicated to knowledge and magic.

Her status as an Elder Goddess is rarely questioned among mortals due to both her long history and detailed accounts of her visits, as well as to her often widely disproportionate display of power. This was the case when she "removed" an exceptionally deadly monster of the Shroud from the Kaustic Hinterlands in  AS1107, alongside three mountains. Unlike many other deities, Spectra has a well-established personality, and mortals know she doesn't get involved in everyday affairs. This means that even if people are aware she is in the area, very few will seek her out. Instead, her arrival carries a dual reputation: she is a source of wonder, but also a harbinger of disaster. To see Spectra in the streets, or to hear that she is in the area, is a sign that a profound magical phenomenon or a catastrophic magical event is imminent. She doesn't cause the disaster; she is there to research it or see how things play out.

Spectra is very close to the [[Casia]] and Fey of [[Primordis]], whose magical nature and mystery have always been deeply fascinating. Equally strong is her bond with the [[Daemos]] and Fiends of [[Signara]]. While she remains indifferent to the bloody conflicts or the political maneuvering of the various Fiendish factions, she finds their fanatical, almost obsessive approach to research deeply relatable. She spends a great deal of her time on Signara itself, openly supporting several Fiends in their private laboratories. To Spectra, a Fiend’s willingness to spend a thousand years perfecting a single, obscure Sign is not a sign of madness, but a trait of a kindred spirit.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Spectra as a Deity
>
> Using Spectra as a deity for a Cleric or Paladin, or as a Warlock Patron, is to enter into an intense, high-level apprenticeship with a mentor who is as brilliant as she is distracted. Following her is less about rituals and worship and more about engaging in a continuous, frantic dialogue regarding the nature of the cosmos. She grants power to those who can provide her with fresh insights on magic, solve complex magical puzzles, or act as field testers for her latest theorems. Interacting with her is warm but chaotic. For a follower, this relationship requires a high degree of self-sufficiency and a thick skin, as Spectra’s hyper-fixated attention can drift as she engages with something else. She is the ultimate peer patron, supporting growth with genuine excitement, provided you can keep her interested.
