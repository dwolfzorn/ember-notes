# The Deities

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Lore Summary
>
> 1. Four Types of Deity: Gods in Ember fall under four types; some are somewhere in between.
> 2. Alien Entities: Outer Gods are real and powerful, but rarely, if ever, worshipped directly.
> 3. Static Power: Gods have their own power; they are not empowered via worship or prayers.
> 4. Political Worship: Worship is a social and political tool used to gain favor, not a necessity for the divine.
> 5. Independence: Forgotten or independent deities are still incredibly dangerous, even without followers.

The deities of Ember do not rely on mortal faith or worship to sustain themselves or fuel their abilities. Their power is not a resource tied to prayers; instead, the amount of power they hold is fixed. For a Shard God, their power is determined at the moment of ascension by the purity and size of the Heart Crystal they touched. For an Elder God, their power is an immutable consequence of their role in the creation of the Cosmos.

The presence of hundreds of Shard Gods walking the surface world means that cultures venerate and constantly worship varied pantheons composed of many deity types, from Elder Gods to Wild Gods and Shard Gods, but few make any distinctions between them, and for most mortals, gods are gods. Lastly, many mortals build grand temples and offer sacrifices to appease a deity's temper or seek their favor, but no amount of collective belief can increase a god’s power.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Quick Reference: Four Deity Types
>
> Within the Ember setting itself, there are four types of deities, all of which are subtly different from one another in various ways. Often, a deity's true categorization may not be clear or immediately apparent, even when placed in categories within the Setting Compendium itself, as the Setting Compendium is not an entirely true and accurate source of knowledge.
>
> #### Outer Gods
>
> These entities originate beyond the cosmos itself. Their powers are utterly alien and incomprehensible, often manifesting as paradoxes, impossible geometries, the unraveling of minds and the creators of monsters. They are the ultimate existential threat, and their followers often seek power through forbidden knowledge that drives them mad. Outer Gods embody destructive concepts (Oblivion, Annihilation, Terror)
>
> #### Elder Gods
>
> The Elder Gods are the primordial architects of the Cosmos, born during Creation and inextricably linked to the Heart of Ember. They maintain the fundamental reality of the Cosmos, and while their power is vast, they are often distracted by cosmological issues rather than daily mortal concerns. Elder Gods embody fundamental concepts (Space, Souls, Time)
>
> #### Wild Gods
>
> The Wild Gods are ancient shapers of Lifeforce, creating the natural world and maintaining the vast, complex ecosystems through their combined force, known as Wildsong. They are the keepers of the natural world and help keep the cosmos in balance, often coming into conflict with the ambitions of the Shard Gods. Wild Gods embody natural concepts (Growth, Seasons, Weather)
>
> #### Shard Gods
>
> Shard Gods are mortal ascendants, having communed with a Heart Crystal to ascend. Some Shard Gods are ancient, while others are far younger and have arisen recently. All are distinct personalities with complex goals, motivations, and flaws. Shard Gods embody mortal concepts (War, Love, Trade)
