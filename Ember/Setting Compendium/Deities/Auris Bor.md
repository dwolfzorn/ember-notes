# Auris Bor

At the heart of the Auris Bor is Vólun, the Father Bear. A truly ancient [[Shard Gods]], Vólun is a primordial being whose origins predate [[The Shattering]], stretching back to the era of the [[Vaznor]] during the [[Age of Beasts]]. It's said that even when the Vaznor encountered Vólun, he was already an ancient being, but most consider such stories myths. His transition from a Vaznor deity to the Father of the Auris Bor meant that he eventually became a symbol for strength and endurance in the face of great trials, and ever since, he has been a bedrock of the north.

The relationship between the thirteen gods of the Auris Bor is intensely personal. They are siblings, children, and rivals, and while they may bicker or hide secrets, they are deeply protective of one another and their people. Most of the members reside in a single city at the northern end of the [[Bejak]] triblelands, called Vellatagé. This is home to the Kistrind, the Kindred Hall, a vast structure made of stone and ancient wood, constructed by Hrymer. Both Vólun and his wife, [[Sokali]], reside in the hall alongside their two sons, Hrymer and Astric, and their daughter, Eirun. [[Themis]] is wed to Eirun, and they have two children, Varros and Gildaré, though Gildaré is lost. Nerrissae was once Sokali's adopted daughter and lived in Kistrind until her father drove her from the hall. The other deities of the Northern Host either travel to Vellatagé seasonally or at random, or are closely associated with the pantheon due to intense rivalry, as is the case with [[Erisagosa]].

While the Bejak culture is most closely associated with the Auris Bor pantheon, both the [[Fikost]] and Vartic clans honor the Northern Host. The nearby [[Wardcall]] has shifted from the Auris Bor to the Trine of Wards, although many still build small shrines dedicated to the ancient deities of their ancestors in secret. In ancient times, many of the oldest Auris Bor gods were worshipped by the Vaznor, but this changed after the [[Troll Invasions]].
