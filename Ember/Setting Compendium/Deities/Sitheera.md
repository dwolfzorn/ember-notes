# Sitheera

Moniker: The Watcher, The Spider Goddess

Typical Followers: Scholars, Historians, Academics

Domains: Unknown

Pantheons: None

Symbol: Six large green eyes within a black void.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Sitheera**
>
> > This world has such fascinating little creatures.

Sitheera is one of the most subtly unsettling and potentially dangerous entities within the Ember [[Cosmos]], yet thankfully, most mortals remain blissfully unaware of her existence. The name Sitheera is seldom used or known by mortals save for a chosen few; instead, the name The Watcher is used instead. While her true nature is not known with any certainty, Sitheera seems to meticulously record events rather than attempt to interfere with or disrupt the natural order. Her attention is consuming and focused on all aspects of the world and of mortal affairs, and her deep fascination can sometimes manifest as an obsession with specific individuals. Those few researchers foolish enough to delve deeply into occult and esoteric lore sometimes stumble into her waiting embrace.

Sitheera is also strongly associated with the multitude of [[Spiders]] found across Ember, with some scholars postulating that she is the Mother of all Spiders, the source of any widespread knowledge of her existence. If this is true, it suggests that Sitheera is an ancient entity of terrifying power, likely introduced during the tumultuous era of [[The Shattering]], when Spiders first began to appear on the [[World of Ember]].

Historians and scholars are often naturally drawn to her embrace and to her followers, Sitheera is surprisingly patient and kind. She rarely communicates with words, instead guiding her servants through a potent, innate subconscious desire. This "pull" compels her followers to seek out and record greater, more unusual, and more meaningful events, often leading them to stride into increasingly complex and dangerous encounters. To serve Sitheera is to commit oneself to a lifetime of witnessing the world's strangest and most catastrophic moments, always from the best, or worst, possible vantage point. Sitheera demands absolute, unyielding loyalty from her patrons, where disobedience is unacceptable. Attempting to resist her directions is met with the swift appearance of her children, as she begins sending spiders to enforce compliance. If compliance still fails, her patience snaps, and she becomes direct: dragging the Warlock into dark, inescapable pits within their own dreams, and holding them there until they can no longer resist her relentless, green-glowing gaze. A follower of Sitheera always serves, and always watches.

## Appearance

Not much is known about Sitheera's true appearance; she is often depicted by her Spider children as an amorphous mass with countless green, shining eyes and a multitude of long, spider-like limbs. But her mortal followers tend to also depict her as a more humanoid being, albeit in a monstrous feminine form with a mass of insect or spider-like limbs as hair and six glittering green eyes.

## Influence and Followers

Sitheera attracts certain scholars, historians, and diehard explorers obsessed with the world's greatest mysteries, and these are also the kinds of individuals she actively seeks out, valuing their ability to perceive, categorize, and archive the unusual events she demands to witness. These chosen followers are driven less by a desire for power and more by an insatiable need to know and record the hidden truths of the Ember Cosmos.

Outside of her select scholars, Sitheera's reputation as the Mother of Spiders and her potential origins during the Shattering make her widely regarded as a primordial monster and an evil being. Due to this association, she is also worshipped by various cults, malignant covens, and secretive groups who seek her power to unleash dark, uncontrolled forces upon the world. These cults venerate the spider as a symbol of patience, deadly silence, and the inescapable snare of fate, seeing Sitheera as the ultimate embodiment of cosmic malice.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Sitheera as a Deity
>
> Using Sitheera as a deity for a Cleric, a Paladin, or as a Warlock Patron is extremely rare across the surface world. This is primarily because her connection to spiders and her assumed role as a progenitor of such monsters introduced during the Shattering leads the vast majority of societies and established religions to view her as a monster herself. Any open devotion to Sitheera is met with immediate suspicion, persecution, and the swift attention of local authorities or religious groups seeking to purge her influence.
