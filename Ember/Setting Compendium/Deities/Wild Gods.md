# Wild Gods

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Lore Summary
>
> - Origin: Slowly created through centuries of soaking in Lifeforce from the Heart of Ember.
> - Natural Aspects: They are geographically anchored in their own domains. They rarely, if ever, leave their place of power.
> - Worship: They do not hear distant prayers. To gain their power, you must stand in their presence and receive a literal fragment of their essence.
> - Soul Anchors: They *can* safeguard their followers' souls, weaving them into the land as Nature Spirits.
> - Destruction: They can be killed, but if their domain is destroyed, they don't necessarily die; they are severed from the Wildsong, which results in violent, unpredictable insanity.

The Wild Gods are the ancient, mysterious stewards of the natural order in Ember, born from the world itself rather than through ascension or the birth of [[The Cosmos]]. They are powered by the pure Lifeforce that flows from the [[Heart of Ember]] into the soil, water, and air. Over centuries, or even millennia, a creature that remains in a place where Lifeforce flows particularly strongly will slowly absorb this creational energy. Typically, mortal ancestries are not long-lived enough to complete this process; therefore, [[Elementals]], [[Leviathans]], and other exceptionally long-lived creatures are the most common beings to reach this state.

Once this infusion reaches a tipping point, the being connects with and also shapes the [[Wildsong]], a broad, cosmos-spanning field of energy that forms the natural world and binds all living things within the Ember [[Cosmos]] together. Through the Wildsong, they oversee the turning of the seasons, the adaptation of wildlife, and the protection of their domains from destruction. Because their power is drawn directly from the natural flow of Lifeforce within a given region, they are inextricably linked to the areas they protect and rarely leave their specific places of power.

## Divine Power

The Wild Gods do not grant power through prayers or remote divine guidance. Unlike the [[Elder Gods]] or [[Shard Gods]], who may hear a distant plea, the Wild Gods are so deeply woven into the Wildsong that the individual voices of mortals are often lost. Instead, their patronage is an intimate, physical act. To empower a follower, a Wild God must ritually bestow a fragment of their own Lifeforce in person. This sacrifice of essence creates a direct, tangible link between the deity and the mortal, a spark of the Wild God and the Wildsong that the follower carries within them.

As the living embodiments of nature, Wild Gods are both the creators and the subjects of the wild spaces they inhabit. They do not merely rule the forests, jungles, swamps, and rivers; they are the spiritual manifestation of those ecosystems. While most represent the vibrancy of growth and the turning of seasons, their influence can occasionally manifest through the darker, harsher aspects of the natural order, such as decay, the necessity of hunger, or the brutality of survival. Interestingly, Wild Gods can manifest in the same areas, overlapping and co-existing, but each typically represents a different aspect of nature.

## Soul Cycle Connection

Wild Gods interact with the [[Soul Cycle]] in much the same way that an Elder God can. They can anchor mortal souls within their own wild domains, preventing their followers from being drawn to the Spiritlands. When a mortal dies as a servant of a Wild God, the divine essence and the imbued Lifeforce they carried return directly to the Wild God. The Wild God then anchors their followers’ souls to the local ecosystem, weaving the spirit into the very fabric of the land.

These anchored souls do not become ghosts; instead, they become Nature Spirits. In this state, manifesting as sentient flora, animals with ancient wisdom, or protective whispers in the wind that guide travelers through the wilds. While all souls eventually return to the Heart of Ember, for a Nature Spirit, this process is slowed to a primordial crawl, often taking hundreds, if not thousands, of years.

## Mortality & Destruction

Wild Gods possess physical forms, ancient bodies of flesh, stone, or elemental energy, and can be slain. While a Wild God does not instantly perish if their domain is destroyed, the loss of their land is a fate often considered worse than death. A Wild God whose domain has been razed or corrupted remains physically as it was, but it loses the ability to draw new Lifeforce from around it. This can be catastrophic, and the Wild God almost inevitably falls into a state of violent insanity.
