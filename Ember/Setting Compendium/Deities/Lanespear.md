# Lanespear

**Areas of Concern:** War, Martial Strength, Monster Fighting

**Typical Followers:** Soldiers, Fighters, Waerds

**Domains:** War, Order

**Pantheons:** [[The Nineteen]]

**Symbol:** A war spear with a long banner wrapped around it.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Lanespear**
>
> > Strength through battle.

Lanespear is a deity associated with relentless war, survival, and strength. Her demeanor is harsh and unforgiving, disapproving of failure and laziness, encouraging her followers to train their martial skills while urging them to remain vigilant against the constant monster attacks from the Duskflare Jungles. She is considered the leader of the diverse Lowland Bastions, but she does not rule them as a queen or some kind of ultimate leader. Instead, she offers guidance, teachings, and the occasional interference in their various conflicts. Although very little is known about her past or how she became a Shard God, she is rumored to have been a follower of the hunter god Janar, but is now seen as a more warlike deity. She encourages the Bastions to resolve conflicts through honorable combat, in order to steady their resolve, train thier bodies and minds, and allow them to defend against monsters from the Duskflare Jungles.

She seldom travels beyond the Lowland shores, as she views the peaceful Arctus Plateau, Ordani, Arcturains, and even Kithil as weak and cowardly. More frequently, she is seen battling monsters and traveling between the Bastions. Since Lanepsear does not govern the Bastions directly, she rarely interacts with ordinary people, often appearing aloof or distant. However, she is approachable to renowned warriors, monster hunters, or adventurers, with whom she enjoys discussing martial techniques, monster behaviors, and combat strategies. For those who truly excel, she will personally bless and guide them, many of whom become her followers. These followers, known as the Kinthyr, are often praised as some of the most honorable and skilled warriors within the Lowlands.

## Appearance

Lanespear is a whirlwind deity of un-flinching morals, willpower, and mental fortitude. Combined with a blazing strength of arms with her long-bladed runic spear, she is often seen by many to be unstoppable. She appears as a very tall Signborn with dark brown skin, four arms, and long black hair. Two slender, glowing horns sit atop her head, their tips emitting a red and purple hue that matches her intense eyes. Her body is adorned with intricate purple and red Waerd Tattoos, complemented by her elaborate runic scale mail.

## Influence and Followers

Lanespear has a fervent following in the Lowlands, and most of the Waerd Culture worships her in various ways. Some follow her teachings, others adhere to the Codes of War she established, or dedicate trophies of monster kills at various fortified shrines and temples within and around the Bastions. She is also revered by many warrior-priests who form Companies, wielding spears and tattooed like their goddess, fighting for the side in Bastion conflicts that promises the greatest glory.

Those who have personally met Lanespear and earned her favor through great deeds are called the Kinthyr. They are bestowed with wings and wear runic armor crafted from powerful creatures and monsters. Like their Mistress, they mediate in the conflicts of the Lowlands, fighting for both sides. They also heal and restore fallen warriors and, in the event of a great warrior falling to monstrous incursions, carry their bodies back for veneration in fiery ceremonies.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Lanespear as a Deity
>
> Using Lanespear as a deity for a Cleric or Paladin, or as a Warlock Patron, is not for the faint of heart or those lacking willpower. She can be extremely demanding of her followers and expects them to endure life's harsh realities alone. She may occasionally intervene when her followers fight a powerful monster, but otherwise, she expects them to struggle, strive, learn, and grow stronger by themselves. She is famously known to reject her followers' calls for assistance, even in desperate situations. When she does appear, she speaks in a commanding, authoritative voice.

> [!info] Social
> #### Relationships
>
> Lanespear remains distinctly apart from many of the other deities of the Nineteen. She actively dislikes and is greatly dismissive of the more passive and peaceful gods, like Sigil, Thoma, and Volta. She sees them as weak and believes they could be doing much more with their powers to safeguard mortal lives.
>
> She has a close bond with Malae, and the two of them are good friends. While they do not see each other often, they both have a deep respect for one another.
>
> Lanespear is also allied with the 'Family of Storms'. Although Raineka is known as a goddess of healing and peace, she is frequently seen in the Lowlands, assisting people in surviving monster attacks and healing the sick. Because of this, Lanespear tends to become much more gentle around Raineka and often takes on a nearly motherly demeanor.
>
> For some reason, she hates Thayloc with a passion that is frightening to behold, although the reason for this rivalry is not understood.
