# The Tanir

The Tanir pantheon is not a unified family; rather, it consists of a system of Temple Houses, with power residing in the capitals of three distinct cultures: the [[Tayan]], [[Maziran]], and [[Kelmezian]]. In Tanir, divinity is intertwined with politics; weaker or newer [[Shard Gods]] often pledge loyalty to [[Selai]], Noduro, or [[Tirass]], serving as their lieutenants or becoming their children to ensure their worship. Each House has its own goals and motives. In the Tayan Empire, the faith is refined and regal, centered around [[Caren'ac]], where Selai and her Court of Silver mostly align with the Empire’s interests. In the Kelmezian City-States to the south, the attitude becomes melancholic and pragmatic; Tirass is seen as a stern ruler of ancient seas, and his associated gods oppose the expansion of the Tayan Empire. Meanwhile, in Lunarai, the Mazirans view Tanir through the lens of Noduro’s iron-clad logistics, treating worship as a formal petition to a ruler.

Despite this, the gods are deeply interconnected: a Maziran merchant might offer a tithe to Noduro for his caravan’s success, but he will also whisper a prayer to [[Verador]] for winds, or silently plead with [[Orru]] to safeguard the night-roads. This interconnectedness means all gods are worshipped in some form or for various reasons.
