# Ku'arta

Areas of Concern: Bargains, Suffering, Power

Typical Followers: Monks, Criminals, The Desperate

Domains: Other

Pantheons: None

Symbol: A chain made of glowing symbols

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Ku'arta**
>
> > If you can do it, prove it

Ku'arta is an ancient deity from a time long before the [[Age of Rediscovery]]. Some believe he was once [[Jarn]], while others suggest he predates even that and is connected to the mythical and infamous [[Theroch]]. He has a long history of conflicts, exploits, and malevolent acts. Though not actively worshipped today, he is feared and well known throughout the world in dark underworlds and seedy taverns. One famous story recounts how Ku'arta personally murdered the high priestess of [[Lantyr]] in [[Old Carinth]] thousands of years ago, simply because she was widely beloved and beautiful, igniting a permanent feud with Lantyr and her followers. Many attempts have been made by other gods to kill Ku'arta, but he has survived them all.

Ku'arta is indifferent to concepts of good and evil, caring only about direct actions that achieve interesting, outlandish results through increasingly difficult challenges. This attracts a diverse group of followers, outcasts, fugitives, criminals, and some just seeking power. To many, he is a stereotypical villain and someone who promotes evil acts, but despite this vilification, Ku'arta has some redeeming qualities. He seems to genuinely care for his followers and has a nearly parental fondness for those who complete his tasks and grow stronger. He is seldom angry or vindictive; if a follower fails a task, he remains understanding and calm. If someone can no longer worship him or fulfill their duties, he strips them of the power he granted and moves on with a strange sense of regret. Unfortunately, he does not ignore them entirely and may send another follower to hunt down and eliminate a failure due to a sense of `putting them out of their misery`.

Following the rise of the [[Ossarchate]] and the undead in the [[Moiran Bonelands]], Ku'arta became an unusual protector against the undead invading the eastern lands of the [[Kelmezian]]. Initially, the undead crossing the mountains disappeared, and the Blood Barons lost contact with entire armies. It was later discovered that Ku'arta possessed a secret method of destroying undead in a terrifying manner. The exact nature of his power is unknown, but the Blood Barons fear him and his followers. Ku'arta often sends his followers to kill large groups of undead in the Ossarchate as part of a challenge, which is oddly a beneficial act given his reputation.

## Appearance

Ku'arta appears as a tall, masculine human with a slender build and an inviting, wide smile. His hair is described as nearly fluorescent white, complemented by pale skin and striking purple eyes. He often wears a grin and has a mischievous, playful nature. However, his mood can quickly shift to twisted and darker tones the more time you spend with him. His most noticeable feature is a prosthetic metal jaw with sharp fanged lower teeth. His followers are often encouraged to mutilate themselves similarly by replacing their jaws in the same manner.

## Influence and Followers

Following Ku'arta is said to be a double-edged sword. He is a god who delights in worship, perhaps more than any other, and rewards his followers with great power, but only if they can accomplish increasingly difficult and progressively more sadistic challenges. These challenges may start relatively mundane, such as searching out a hidden place or killing a certain monster, but gradually become harder to accomplish and increasingly evil until Ku'arta begins demanding extreme acts of cruelty, such as competing against other followers to see how many people they can kill before being caught. Most mortals who start worshipping Ku'arta eventually end up killing themselves while trying to carry out an impossible task, and Ku'arta finds this incredibly satisfying in a way that is hard to describe. Followers of Ku'arta are rare because of this and are hunted, outlawed, and even executed when discovered, depending on the culture.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Ku'arta as a Deity
>
> Using Ku'arta as a deity for a Cleric, Paladin, or as a Warlock Patron is a path of pain and suffering. Ku'arta is inherently manipulative and abusive, and his followers are at his mercy, dancing to his whims. He regards his followers as treasured playthings, appearing to care for them on one hand, yet pushing them toward actions that may ultimately lead to their death. He speaks to his followers with a kind, warm voice, and he is a very present and active deity, one of the few that will intervene if necessary directly, but doing so always comes with a price. Any public display of devotion to Ku'arta is swiftly met with suspicion, persecution, and immediate intervention from local authorities or religious groups aiming to eliminate his influence, and most of his followers tend together in secret cults.
