# Vesper

**Moniker:** Mother Mask

 **Areas of Concern:** Illusions, Obsession, Longing

  **Typical Followers:** Fey

 **Domains:** Unknown

 **Pantheons:** None.

 **Symbol:** A cracked feline mask, surrounded by swirling mists and shadows.

 > [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Vesper**
>
>  > Reality is a burden my dears; let my veil be your comfort.

  Vesper is one of the [[Casia]] and the ruler of Naloria Vale, a region of [[Primordis]] where shadows have weight and both light and darkness are mere afterthoughts. She is a true mistress of illusions, to such an extent that she can make things real by simply believing them so. Her magic is so ancient that other Casia consider it thick, dense, and even unsettling, seemingly woven from the raw essence of the Shadow Realm itself.

 She presents herself as a kind being, but only at a surface level. While her open kindness and empathy are not a mask, they are only the first layer of her personality. She speaks to those who need comfort and protection from the harsh reality of the world, providing exactly what you want to hear, crafting magical illusions that comfort the soul. But her gifts always come with a price, as mortals' desires will keep them locked in lies until they lose the ability to discern illusion from reality. She finds mortals eternally fascinating, in part because she wants to help them, but more so because she is obsessed with their imaginations and fantasies. She will pluck a secret desire from a mortal's mind and manifest it, a dead lover, a lost parent, or a pile of unimaginable riches, just so she can weave a new illusion into her complex web.

 Vesper’s most enduring legacy is the creation of the [[Zeph]] during the [[Age of Beasts]]. Her heart was once ensnared by [[Nymbohr]], the [[Elementals]]. In a desperate bid to enthrall him, Vesper traveled to the Wind Moon, [[Aura]], where she studied the primal nature of the winds and began weaving increasingly dense illusions to enhance the moon's elemental beauty.

 Almost by happenstance, she manifested the first Zeph, beings born from the marriage of shadow-magic and the untamed winds of Aura. For a time, Vesper and Nymbohr doted on their children, but the Zeph were never meant to be playthings. Their inherent need for freedom clashed with Vesper’s desire to control every thread of their existence. When Nymbohr and the Zeph ultimately spurned her love to claim their own independence, Vesper retreated to Primordis, broken and enveloped in sadness, leaving her children to build a society on Aura, a civilization born of her magic, yet forever wary of her reach.

  ## Appearance

  As a Casia, Vesper moves with a feline, predatory grace, her form draped in liquid shadows and translucent silks that shift and whirl in unending patterns that reflect the dreams of her followers. Her fur is completely black, but when she exerts her power, it shimmers with the violet-grey hue of a rainstorm, battered by raging winds. Her eyes are unblinking sapphire stars that seem to shimmer with an impossible number of reflected pinpricks of light. When she moves, a faint ghostly resonance, like the sound of a hundred soft flutes, can be heard playing from the echoes just behind the listener's line of sight.

   ## Influence and Followers

 Within the Shadow Realm of Primordis, Vesper rules the shifting landscapes of Naloria Vale from a throne of shadows, and she has a number of dedicated fey followers. While she is outnumbered by the more aggressive Casia of Primordis, her absolute mastery over shadow-magic and illusions ensures that few dare to encroach upon her borders. On the surface world, Vesper’s influence manifests as a haunting call to the grieving, the broken, and those who find the "real" world unbearable. Many of these followers are unintentional as they stumble into her influence through dreams or where portals to the Shadow Realm bleed into Ember. Mortals tend to see her as a maternal, empathetic savior who offers a sanctuary from pain, unaware that her empathy is a slow path towards losing themselves in illusions.

 > [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Vesper as Deity
>
>  Using Vesper as a deity for a Cleric or Paladin, or as a Warlock Patron, is a rare and dangerous path. First, it requires a mortal to be aware of her, sufficiently creative yet broken, to attract her attention, as Vesper grants power only to those who connect with her deep need to aid those who are desperate. Mortals gradually give more and more of themselves to Vesper, slowly losing memories they might find dear to them, even if they are painful. Her demands are often simple, but she cannot help but push her mortal followers toward interesting, often dangerous paths in order to later reap the seeds she has sown and claim the dark, painful memories that doing so might bring.
