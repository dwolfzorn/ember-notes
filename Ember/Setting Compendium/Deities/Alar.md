# Alar

**Areas of Concern:** Creation, Mortality, Protection

**Typical Followers:** Dragons, Beings of the Inner Realms

**Domains:** Life, Other

**Pantheons:** [[The Nineteen]], [[The Tanir]], [[Sunalin]], [[Solaru]], [[Sentina]]

**Symbol:** An ascending comet

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Alar**
>
> > Protect those in need.

Alar is a supremely powerful being, the first [[Elder Gods]]. Unlike the Other Elders, Alar does not have a singular cosmic aspect that she seems to be associated with. While some call her the Goddess of Life and Creation, she is also associated with various other ideas, such as Tyranny, Deception, Ambition, and Protection. However, she often rejects such rigid labels and categories, finding them too restrictive for a cosmos that is ever-changing. She uses this ambiguity to her advantage, wielding her powers with masterful subtlety and nuance, preferring to mask herself behind a myriad of mortal guises and shifting personas to better navigate the complexities of existence.

Alar sees herself as a guardian of life and a protector of mortals and ancestries across the cosmos. She is a figure of immense resilience and deep empathy, becoming emotionally attached to mortals and seeking to understand their complex lives. However, she is often her own harshest critic, often replaying the choices and mistakes she made eons ago in her mind, wondering if a kinder word or a swifter action would have resulted in a different outcome. When she fails to protect a mortal she cares about or a people she adores, she doesn't just feel regret, she feels a profound, personal failure that can trigger her volatile temper or send her into periods of mournful isolation and withdrawal. She is a guardian who desperately wants to save everyone, struggling with the reality that even a goddess cannot fix every broken thing.

Outwardly, Alar masks this internal turmoil with a distinctly confident and expressive personality. Her identity is a complex tapestry that has evolved across the ages, allowing her to be both chaotic and lawful, compassionate and capricious. She often indulges in the games and stories of mortal kingdoms and empires, limiting her vast power to engage in petty conflicts or interfering with the plans of other deities, sometimes for no reason other than to distract herself from her own heavy thoughts. While she is more than capable of using her knowledge and physical form to manipulate or appeal to mortals' desires, this calculated exterior is governed by a strict personal code. She abides by clear rules and draws firm lines regarding how she engages with others, ensuring that even her most capricious whims never violate her fundamental duty as a protector of life.

### Ancient Form

Alar was once a very different deity. In the years after she arose, extending throughout creation and the entire [[Age of Beasts]], she used her power more openly to stoke conflicts, manipulating events in her favor as she saw fit and at a whim. During this time, she appeared in a masculine form and was worshiped by few upon the surface, sometimes attacking or confronting both other deities and mortals in the pursuit of her goals. Her only true followers during this time were the beings of the Inner Realms, especially the Fiends, and her influence shaped mortals' perceptions so much that even today, many distrust and fear her. She slowly changed throughout the [[Age of the Tower]], becoming far less antagonistic. It was after [[The Shattering]] that her feminine form appeared in full, and references to her masculine aspects are extremely rare in the present.

She has changed in so many ways, and every time she cares and attempts to understand, she’s simply trying to make up for ancient mistakes. ## Appearance

Alar is quite comfortable changing her form, often appearing as a member of any mortal ancestry upon Ember to wander the world unnoticed. When she chooses to reveal her power more openly, she appears as a towering figure, sometimes reaching twelve feet in height, resembling a [[Human]]-[[Nir'ae]] mixed ancestry with an incredibly extensive crown of horns sprouting from her head. Her elegant arms and legs possess an unnatural flexibility, flowing with a strange, otherworldly grace as she moves. Her eyes are said to glow intensely with the raw power of creation, shining with the brilliance of gold and deep red fire. Across her pale skin and hair, twisting patterns of gold and silver constantly shift and flow, tracing the path of her movements.

In the most ancient stories, it is said that when Alar truly wishes to unleash her full power, she assumes a colossal, draconic form known as the Wyrm of Creation. In this aspect, she is described as possessing six wings, three tails, and a fanged maw of razor-sharp teeth, spanning many miles in size. However, it is worth noting that tales of this form are largely based on surviving accounts from the chaotic aftermath of the Shattering, and scholars debate whether these descriptions are literal truths, over exaggerations, or entirely fictitious myths.

## Influence and Followers

Alar is loved, worshipped, hated, and cursed by all kinds of beings across the Ember [[Cosmos]]. She is a divisive figure who can often split opinions within societies, cultures, and even individual families. As such, Alar is rarely worshipped openly, with only a few shrines and temples dotted about the world. Most of her followers are clerics and priests who preach her teaching under the guise of working with other gods. In some cultures, Alar is culturally disliked, and her worship can lead to persecution as they view her as a breaker of the natural cycle and name her Soul Stealer. Alar spends most of her time among mortals and, in rare situations, reveals her divinity as an [[Elder Gods]]. Still, she prefers working with individuals rather than organizations or nations. Several first-generation [[Drakon]] have worked with her since their creation, understanding and revering her in ways few other mortals have been privy to.

She is also worshipped with a strange fanaticism by the beings from the Inner Realms, the [[Casia]], [[Tyraphem]], and [[Daemos]], as many believe she is their creator. While they would do practically anything Alar commands, she tends to stay out of their circles of concern and doesn't align with their goals, ambitions, or politics. This control and devotion has its uses, and techinically, Alar could control the most powerful combined force in the cosmos if she so desired. However, she has little interest and has largely stayed away from the Inner Realms other than bringing souls to them as part of her ideas around the [[Soul Cycle]].

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### **Using Alar as a Deity.**
>
> Using Alar as a deity for a Cleric or Paladin, or as a Warlock Patron, is a path of empathy and redemption. Alar is an intensely personal and expressive presence, and she expects her followers to act as her eyes and hands in the world, seeking out conflict to resolve it, protecting the sanctity of Ember, and defending life wherever it is threatened by true evil. Alar is a deeply supportive patron who genuinely cares for her followers, but she can also be demanding; she is her own harshest critic, and she often pushes her champions to be equally self-reflective and resilient.
>
> Her magic is said to be vibrant and fluid, often carrying a warmth that feels like a physical embrace. However, this connection is not without its weight. Because she carries the remorse of her ancient, aggressive past, her power can sometimes flare with a volatile temper if her followers witness cruelty or injustice. Most importantly, Alar is the only Elder God who will directly intervene on behalf of her followers in times of great need.
