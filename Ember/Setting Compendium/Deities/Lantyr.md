# Lantyr

**Areas of Concern:** Energy, Light, Life

**Typical Followers:** All

**Domains:** Light, Other

**Pantheons:** [[The Nineteen]], [[Sentina]], [[Solaru]], [[Sunalin]], [[The Tanir]], [[Auris Bor]]

**Symbol:** A roaring ball of yellow cosmic fire.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Lantyr**
>
> > Life thrives in the light.

Lantyr is one of the strangest entities in the Ember [[Cosmos]]; she is both an [[Elder Gods]] and has a physical presence that serves as the literal sun for the world. While mortals look up and see a comforting orb of yellow fire, this is merely a refractive illusion caused by the atmosphere and magic. Her true form is a colossal, roiling sphere of intense light-blue plasma, which only becomes clear once you get uncomfortably close to her in the [[Sea of Stars]]. The surface of Lantyr is an intensely dangerous landscape of pure energy. While it primarily exists as a churning sea of fire, Lantyr can solidify her essence, creating translucent, glowing platforms. These solid islands allow a rare few to walk on their surface and even support strange creatures and life forms. Within these zones, the temperature is remarkably mild, and the air is breathable, as Lantyr intentionally creates safe spaces to host her strange creations. However, should a traveler step off the edge of these sanctioned paths, they would instantly plummet into the burning, blue sea of pure energy below.

Lantyr's exact nature is paradoxical to many, as she appears to be the origin of light and life but is, in fact, the singular source of all energy within the Cosmos. Lantyr is a fierce nexus of power that keeps the Cosmos balanced and moving behind the scenes, and the light and heat she generates are merely the waste products of her existence. The fact that mortals grow and stay warm by her presence is a secondary consequence of her being. This actually defines her completely, as to many, she has a distant, calm, and seemingly uncaring personality. She doesn't see herself as a motherly figure or a caretaker, but as a perpetual cosmic regulator, spending every moment focused inward, keeping her energy in check so that the rest of the Cosmos can thrive in safety. Lantyr’s withdrawn nature also comes from her awareness of her own volatility, as she understands that being close to anything or anyone would consume them. Her deep kindness is an act of perpetual restraint.

To that end, Lantyr is perhaps one of the most well-known Elder Deities, the one worshipped and adored by every culture and people of Ember since the dawn of sentient thought. Looking up and seeing her glide gracefully across the sky, bringing warmth from the dark, is hard to miss, and mortals, as well as those upon the Moons and in the Inner Realms, hold Lantyr in the highest regard. She grants her powers freely to those who follow her but never interacts with them, meaning countless temples, priesthoods, cults, and groups have formed to worship her divine light and life-giving nature throughout history, despite receiving total silence in return.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > I've always personally found Lantyr's light to be a comfort, as if she's shone upon me with a measure of kindness and care. Throughout my travels, I've certainly met people and groups with different interpretations. I’ve seen fanatics who want to burn the world just to purify it, doomsayers waiting for the day she finally goes cold, and oppressive tyrants who think her light is nothing but a weapon to cancel out the dark.
>
> ~Laeora

## Appearance

Lantyr is an entity of staggering proportions, a roiling, spherical mass of energy that rivals the size of the Elemental Moons. To look upon her from the Sea of Stars is to see a churning ocean of cerulean fire, where tectonic-sized plates of solidified energy drift across seas of screaming plasma. While she appears as a comforting golden orb from the surface of Ember, this is merely an illusion cast by the atmosphere and a certain degree of magic of unknown nature that seems to shield the world from her true color and nature.

## Influence and Followers

Sun worship is one of the most historically common ways of communing with a deity on Ember, across numerous cultures and ancestries, and it is an easy path to divine power, as Lantyr does not discriminate among her followers. From the most ancient days to the present, Lantyr has enjoyed large numbers of worshippers, with practices that take many forms. Beyond the grand temples, Lantyr’s silence has given rise to a vast spectrum of isolated faiths and local traditions. These range from entire city-states like Anacalen, which worship her divine power and strength, to the Heralds of the Dawn, an ancient sect of mountain-dwellers who view the rare glimpse of her unrefracted blue light as a moment of ultimate mental clarity. Elsewhere, solitary Sun-Watchers watch over ancient [[Aedir]] Sunwells and protect them from the ravages of time. There are also many nomadic tribes, travelers, or sailors, who treat her as a beacon and constant marker in the sky.

Lantyr's largest current priesthood is the Clerics of the Radiant Eye, who form the cornerstone of the [[Sunalin]], where she is revered as the Sun Mother. For the [[Kessian]] people, her worship is less about philosophical meditation and more about societal structure and enforcing a strict, near-fanatical opposition to forces of evil and monsters.

During the [[Age of the Tower]], the Aedir began worshipping Lantyr as a primary deity during their initial rise as a civilization and culture. They saw her warmth and light as a great affirmation of life itself and regarded her almost as much as their creator, Keia. Over time, their interpretation and knowledge of Lantyr increased, and they began to regard her as a glorious partner in their advancement. They did not just worship her but built their entire society and technology around her power, creating Sunwells that tapped directly into her divine energy. By the time the Aedir Empire reached its height, their faith had transformed into a doctrine. They no longer saw Lantyr's light simply as a blessing or a divine patron to worship, but as a tool to be mastered. The Aedir became convinced that they were destined to use her boundless energy to propel the Sunfire Empire forward, viewing the Sun not as a remote mother but as the engine of their own transcendence.

The most terrifying manifestation of her faith is found in the Inner realm of [[Luxarum]]. The [[Tyraphem]] worship her as the creator of Light, despite this being a grand lie. Most of their worship is funneled through a religion known as the Blinding Truth, a philosophy that holds only a Tyraphem Lord called [[Taryakel]] can interpret Lantyr, and that all other beings are merely shadows waiting to be consumed or cast aside. Lantyr is generally considered by the Tyraphem to be a furious, angry, and burning deity, and anything that is not "of the Light" is to be purged.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Lantyr as a Deity
>
> Using Lantyr as a deity for a Cleric or Paladin, or as a Warlock Patron, is for the disciplined and the fanatical, or for those who seek to tap into raw energy without constraint. While Lantyr does grant her power to anyone under the right circumstances, she does so without guidance or personality, simply because it is in her nature to do so. Lantyr never speaks to her followers, which makes her unique among the deities on Ember, and her followers often become disillusioned by her lack of true warmth or attention. Worshipping Lantyr is also a dangerous path for a would-be follower, as there is no safety valve and no divine intervention if the power she grants begins to consume the user.
