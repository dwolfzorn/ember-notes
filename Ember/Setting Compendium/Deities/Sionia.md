# Sionia

**Areas of Concern:** Death and the Undead, Solitude, Scientific Research

**Typical Followers:** Necromancers, The Undead

**Domains:** Twilight, Knowledge

**Pantheons:** [[The Nineteen]]

**Symbol:** A face made of swirling dust

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> #### Edict of Sionia
>
> > Seek to understand, not to be understood.

Though Sionia is considered to be evil by most mortals and almost all cultures repudiate her and what they consider to be her practice of necromancy, she is firmly aligned against the corruption and evil of true undeath. Sionia uses her powers with care, only animating the long dead and avoiding any interference with the Soul Cycle of Ember. Her continued work with the dead is born from a desire for understanding — she wants to know how the soul cycle works and is willing to sacrifice personally for the answers she seeks. This sacrifice has left her estranged, lonely, and deeply concerned with self-protection, but she still feels that it is worth the cost, especially as it leaves her able to help those who few others will — the Soulbound.

Sionia attempts to help the souls of those who remain bound to their physical form and free them if she can. Many Soulbound worship her, as do necromancers and the undead, but she avoids their love as much as she avoids others' hate, instead withdrawing from the world to remote places and spending centuries in isolation and meditation. She is most often found in forgotten ruins and hidden places, where she feels at peace and is safe from being hunted by those who revile her, including the priests and followers of Sockets, Elder God of Souls.

## Appearance

Sionia appears as a beautiful human woman, whose long white hair seems to dissipate into ghostly mist. She generally dresses in white and golden silks and is sometimes mistaken for a ghost at a distance, seeming to float ethereally on the horizon. She generally has a kind, if deeply sorrowful, expression on her face. Staring too long into her eyes is known to bring most mortals to tears.

## Influence and Followers

Sionia has very few true followers who actively reach out to commune with her, in part because she ignores those who do and in part because the worship of her is so thoroughly reviled. She does have a few chosen followers who she will answer, but they are generally those who have met and interacted with her personally, and understand her true nature as someone who wants to understand the cosmos, including the process of life and death.

Despite her apparent disinterest and general lack of response, there are many who worship Sionia and do deeds in her name, including individual necromancers, cults that worship undeath, and the[[Ossarchate]], who are undead themselves. Sionia finds this worship offensive and has been known to hunt down and kill these followers if words of their deeds reaches her — a fact that has yet to dissuade others from following in their stead. A few have even attempted to catch her attention with vile acts that will lead her to kill them as an act of twisted worship.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Sionia as a Deity
>
> Using Sionia as a deity for a Cleric, Paladin, or general devotion is very rare and usually hidden — a follower of Sionia will generally be reviled by all but a few necromancers and the undead, and is in danger of being hunted down by Sionia if their acts of necromancy go against her personal code. The one exception to this rule is those who have interacted with Sionia directly, often by coming across her while she communes in some hidden corner of Ember. Those who treat her with respect find her to be kind, despite her sometimes acerbic tone, and may have their prayers answered in future.

Despite his followers hunting her relentlessly, the only shard god who generally has any contact with Sionia is Sockets, Keeper of Souls. He periodically attempts to persuade her to help him watch over the Spiritlands, where the souls of those on Ember migrate when they die, but she has always refused. Beyond Sockets, shard gods generally either avoid Sionia or actively denounce her.
