# Sokali

**Areas of Concern:** Winter, Snow, Voyages

**Typical Followers:** Scouts, Mystics

**Domains: Other**

**Pantheons:** [[Auris Bor]]

**Symbol:** A silver feline eye peering through a frost-cloud

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Sokali**
>
> > Step softly, leave no imprint upon the snow.

Sokali is an ancient Shard God of snow, winter, and predation, who ascended long ago during the Age of Beasts. She is the creator of the Wirrun ancestry and the wife of the deity Vólun, and is considered one of the most important deities in the Auris Bor pantheon. She is seen as a wild force that rarely interacts with people, including her own followers. Instead, she roams the snowy tundra, observing the migratory herds and sometimes preying upon them herself. Despite her wild nature, she is quite good-natured when encountered unexpectedly or when she chooses to interact with others. She simply enjoys spending long periods alone and is very at peace with her solitude. Sokali isn't worshipped by followers of the Nineteen or the Tanir, despite some mortals in those regions knowing of her, but she is sometimes vaguely referenced or prayed to by travelers or those who traverse the colder areas of the Wedgelands in the Arctus Plateau. She is a much more prominent deity among the Bejak and the Wardcall, and the principal deity among the Fikost.

She is the daughter of Katu and Vekalla and is well known among mortals who worship the northern pantheon of Auris Bor as a goddess of winter, snow, and hunting. However, this worship often takes on a more animalistic, predatory slant. Many worship her as a natural deity who brings or follows the winter months, while others are suspicious of her existence and see blizzards as her domain, a domain they could well do without. Sokali gets along well with her family, but since her birth, she has always been distant and has sought out remote places. After her ascension, she does not see her mother and father very often, but she makes a point of traveling south every winter to see her sister Raineka.

## Appearance

Most of Ember's Shard Gods resemble mortals, often walking among them as influential leaders or guiding figures. However, Sokali is an exception; she prefers to appear as a massive snow leopard, with an icy mane and sharp fangs made of a strange, solid white metal. When she chooses to present herself in a more approachable form to her followers, she appears as an elemental-like figure, a Wirrun woman composed of snow, with long, flowing white hair and clothing made of ice.

## Influence and Followers

Sokali is revered by almost all of the Wirrun, who view her as their creator. They offer her meat from their kills and build enchanted markers and monuments of ice in the most desolate regions of northern Aterica to venerate her wild nature. The Wirrun do not have a priesthood as such, but they do have a group that calls itself the Wildclaws. These half-priests and half-druids transform primarily into snow leopards, and they teach other Wirrun about the natural world and survival skills. They also participate in the yearly Spirit Hunt, a ritual said to cross the boundary between life and death, where the Wildclaws somehow travel to the Spiritlands and stalk the ancient spirits of the dead as a test of skill.

Elsewhere in the world, she has a more traditional following among the Bejak and Wardcall cultures. Her followers in those places venerate her for her supposed ability to control the winters and to bless people's voyages and travel across vast distances.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Sokali as a Deity
>
> Using Vekalla as a deity for a Cleric or Paladin, or as a Warlock Patron, is for anyone who rejects traditional society and seeks a closer bond with nature. Sokali is a primal deity who values those who explore the natural world and develop deep relationships with nature itself. While she is not a Wild God, she is closely associated with their principles, attracting many druids and hermits who adopt animal and beast forms. Sokali regards her followers with a distant affection, never quite forming genuine bonds but still guiding and supporting them. She gently tries to teach them about the world's harsh realities. Her communication often uses visual scenes of hunts, nature, or life to convey meaning, leaving followers to interpret her messages.

> [!info] Social
> #### Relationships
>
> Sokali is the daughter of the Shard Gods, Katu and Vekalla. She was always a distant child who preferred the outdoors and nature. For a time, she lived with her parents on the Isle of Storms after her ascension, but she later moved on to explore the colder regions of the world, where she felt more comfortable in the harsh wilds. She often visits her family, where she met and formed a close bond with her sister, Raineka, and the two make sure to see each other at least once a year.
>
> While in the north, after creating the Wirrun, she met the Shard God Vólun, and the two were initially fierce rivals. A long saga is sung of their relationship, but suffice it to say, eventually the two fell in love and had many children, including their two sons, Hrymer and Astric, and their daughter, Eirun. While she is often traveling, she can sometimes be found in the Kindred Hall in the Bejak city of Vellatagé, the official home of the Auris Bor itself.
>
> Sokali has a strained relationship with her adopted daughter, Nerrissae. The two are often at each other's throats, literally, but they sometimes reconcile enough to be cordial.
>
> It's said that Sokali has been attempting to hunt and kill Erisagosa for thousands of years, but the ancient Ice Wyrm has always escaped or fought her off. The true nature of their relationship, however, may be more complex.
