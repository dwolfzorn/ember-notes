# Malae

**Areas of Concern:** Curses, Herbalism, Vengeance

**Typical Followers:** Gardeners, Witches, the Wronged

**Domains:** Twilight, Other

**Pantheons:** [[The Nineteen]], [[The Tanir]]

**Symbol:** A bundle of herbs bound with thorn

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Malae**
>
> > Punish those who wrong you.

Malae is seen by many as a merciless and uncompromising deity. While she is worshipped as one of the first truly ancient and great powers in the world of Ember, she is also greatly feared, and her very name is often spoken in hushed tones. While Malae is not evil, far from it, she has garnered an extremely negative, some would say villainous, reputation over many thousands of years of myths and stories that highlight her wrathful and terrible nature. Few speak of her warmer, more benevolent side or of her opposition to those who are oppressive or unjust. It is, however, true that she is a deity with an extremely waspish and wrathful attitude, and when angered she is a very alarming and even terrifying being.

Some believe that Malae is the first Shard God to walk the surface of Ember. This is not entirely true, but she is likely the first Jarn Shard God, and thus she is possibly hundreds of thousands of years old. By all accounts, she was a very normal woman with a loving family. There are many variations of the story of how Malae became the being she is today, but most are terrible tales of death, betrayal, and loss, often with Malae temporarily helpless to assist those she loved. At some point, according to the legends, she became a Shard God by communing with a Heart Crystal that was exposed to the sky, jutting out of the ground in those elder days. She has always been followed by tragedy, violence, and anger, but the events of those days are so long ago that only Malae herself likely knows the truth.

Malae believes strongly in being ready to take action when the moment comes. She spends most of her time wandering the world, studying and collecting plants, and has vast knowledge of herbalism and potions, part of her preparedness to strike when needed. Some of her devotees believe that she was once unable to act at a critical moment, now lost to time, and that both her wrath and her devotion to preparation stem from this incident.

One myth about her wrathful nature is that she cursed an entire ancient Kelmezian city after they tried to kill one of her devoted followers. She then went to great lengths to transform the entire city into Harpies, and such creatures have been called "Children" of Malae ever since.

## Appearance

Malae appears as a short, beautiful human woman with a presence some call chilling. She has pure black hair that flows smoothly around her shoulders, framing a delicate face with two yellow piercing eyes. Her slender frame moves gracefully, and she wears flowing black clothing that seems to ripple gently with her movements, hugging her form. These garments are often adorned with intricate, ghost-like flowers and plants that seem to shimmer and fade in and out of existence.

## Influence and Followers

Malae has few priesthoods or large temples dedicated to her worship, but most human-dominated cultures are aware of her and the old stories and legends. If they give her any thought, most ward against drawing her attention and whisper her name to prevent her from cursing them. A few notable followers venerate her as the goddess of herbs and plants for the deep knowledge she has cultivated over the years, and they understand that her more wrathful side is a consequence of the harsh world of Ember. These followers are often referred to as Witches by others and given a wide berth, though they are shunned. Very few people in the world will interfere or trifle with a follower of Malae.

Some monsters, notably Hassratch, will sometimes worship Malae as well, idolizing her wrathful and cruel side, but Male herself wants nothing to do with such beings and finds their existence to be an affront to nature.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Malae as a Deity
>
> Using Malae as a deity for a Cleric, Paladin, or Warlock Patron is an extremely rare pursuit among the peoples of Ember. While she is not the evil deity many believe her to be, Malae remains a prickly, difficult goddess with few followers who truly understand her nature. Nearly all her followers are considered Witches. While Witches are neither banned nor hunted across the surface world, they are often shunned—meaning followers of Malae tend to have thick skin, are deeply committed to justice, and seek vengeance for the wronged at all costs. Malae herself treats her Witches with care and love, granting them often greater power than other gods do. When she speaks to her followers, Malae is known for her kindness, speaking softly and soothingly.

> [!info] Social
> #### Relationships
>
> Malae's closest relationship was with the ancient Shard God Janar. They were said to be extremely close in ancient times, but after Janar was apparently killed, Malae slipped into a furious, cold rage that lasted for weeks. She is rumored to have recovered his body from the jaws of Godkiller Orinoth personally, although how this is possible is unknown. Other rumors persist that Malae was far more than simple friends with Janar and that they were lovers, but few would risk the Queen of Curses' wrath to ask her directly about the matter.
>
> She is friendly with the Shard God Lanespear, and Malae is most often seen in the Lowlands, traversing the Duskflare Jungles and studying the ever-changing plants there.
>
> Malae has a long, and some say ancient, relationship with the strange entity known as Laeora. The exact nature of their relationship is unknown, but the two are very close, and it's not uncommon for them to meet and exchange knowledge about the world.
>
> It's said that Malae is searching for a way to permanently kill the sapient Leviathan Godkiller Orinoth, but so far her efforts have not been successful.
