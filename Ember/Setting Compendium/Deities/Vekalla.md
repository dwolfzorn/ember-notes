# Vekalla

**Areas of Concern:** Trade, Ships, The Wind

**Typical Followers:** Traders, Sailors, Pirates

**Domains:** Peace, Other

**Pantheons:** [[The Nineteen]]

**Symbol:** A full sail, with wind lines blowing behind it.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Vekalla**
>
> > Ever onwards, like the wind.

Vekalla is a luminous deity of bargains and barter, often called The Mediator. She has a rich history of calming conflicts and fostering peace. One of her most renowned stories involves her approaching the two warring Kelmezian Kingdoms of Korta and Ancalen in the days before the Shattering, ending a three-hundred-year rivalry. Many associate her with trading and ask for her blessing and guidance in commerce. She is equally revered by sailors and traders. Nearly all sailors and ship captains of the Ordani, Arcturians, and Waerds carry charms or taliams with her symbol. This is partly because Vekalla is a highly active and approachable Shard Goddess who enjoys conversations and problem-solving with her followers. It's common to find her floating beside ships along coasts or open waters, chatting and gossiping with crews. Though she is never portrayed as old, she is older than most realize, having once been a Jarn and witnessing many pivotal events in Ember's history.

Vekalla is the wife of Katu, with a long history together spanning hundreds of thousands of years, and is considered a member of the 'Family of Storms' and lives on the elusive, wandering Isle of Storms itself. Despite occasional arguments that can cause localized typhoons, they remain deeply in love with each other and dote on one another. She is the mother of Sokali and her adopted daughter Raineka, and she often travels great distances to visit and check on them. Both daughters find this somewhat embarrassing if they are among mortals at the time. Vekalla sometimes quarrels with Katu because they often have conflicting views on the natural world. She tends to be much more compassionate toward mortal concerns than her husband, and when Katu causes storms or threatens sailors, Vekalla can become especially wrathful toward her husband.

## Appearance

Vekalla is a deity with numerous shifting forms, much like the winds and storms themselves. Sometimes she appears as a Human dressed in white with dark grey skin and long, flowing white hair, often when relaxing on the Isle of Storms or when she wishes to blend in unnoticed among mortals. More frequently, she manifests in her impressive Windform to bless ships. In this form, Vekalla stands over a hundred feet tall, resembling a vast cloud of white, blue, and pink in a vaguely humanoid shape.

## Influence and Followers

Vekalla's followers mainly include sailors, caravaneers, traders, travelers, and merchants. Sailors, especially those crossing the Yawning Sea, regularly pray to her and offer her rare charms or objects. They believe this devotion is worth the cost, as they think her attention can ward off the Leviathan Godkiller Orinoth, who roams the region, or simply keep them safe from storms. She frequently visits her temples along the southeastern edge of the Aterican continent, often bringing gifts and rare goods. She also spends days engaging in conversations and chats with mortals.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Vekalla as a Deity
>
> Using Vekalla as a deity for a Cleric or Paladin, or as a Warlock Patron, is for those longing for new experiences and a strong desire for personal freedom. Vekalla appreciates anyone willing to travel in search of meaning, believing that exploring new lands, peoples, and ideas fosters growth and wisdom. She values understanding and those eager to learn how to apply themselves. When communicating with Vekalla, she can be very expressive, often chatting about random topics for hours, but she also enjoys listening to others. Her voice is clear and steady, often tinged with excitement or a reflective depth.

> [!info] Social
> #### Relationships
>
> Vekalla is the wife of the Shard God Katu and one of Ember's oldest married couples, having met hundreds of thousands of years ago. They share a very enduring and loving relationship that, while sometimes tumultuous, is rooted in deep mutual affection. Together, while still mortal, they had a child called Sokali, and the three of them discovered the legendary Isle of Storms, a floating, wandering island reputed to contain many radiant Heart Crystals embedded in its stone. The entire family then ascended and has remained close ever since.
>
> Along with Katu, Vekalla adopted Raineka, who became their adopted daughter and shares many personality traits. They both love each other deeply and spend long days together on the Isle of Storms.
>
> Vekalla is close friends with the Wild God Nite, and the two are said to value each other's wisdom. It's said that Vekalla once saved Nite's life thousands of years ago during the Shattering, but no one knows the full extent of the story.
>
> Vekalla has a strong dislike for the Shard Goddess Malae. It's thought that the two of them don't see eye to eye on several issues, namely being kind toward mortals in general, but it may be more personal.
>
> Several old sailor stories claim that Vekalla sometimes rides upon the back of Obrisire, but most consider these tales to be fiction.
