# Thayloc

**Areas of Concern:** Loss, Retribution, Death of the Undead

**Typical Followers:** Warriors, Refugees

**Domains:** Twilight, War

**Pantheons:** [[The Nineteen]]

**Symbol:** A broken silver arrow, still in flight

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Thayloc**
>
> > Swift is my arrow for rotting hearts.

Thayloc is a deity consumed by anger, regret, and loss. Once, he was a god of merriment, archery, and festivals, mainly concerned with organizing feasts and celebrations for the following day. His exceptional skill with the bow was well-known, and as a former adventurer with a storied history of fighting monsters, he was often lavished with praise by the people of Moria. However, because he was somewhat vain and irreverent, he was not taken particularly seriously. He often claimed to have "retired" from danger and peril, and only those seeking a skillful teacher or those who enjoyed his renowned feasts worshipped and loved him in those days.

While Thayloc enjoyed many hundreds of years of peace after his ascension, the events of the Evernight changed him almost completely. He somehow survived the cataclysmic destruction of the city of Miris and fled the Morian kingdoms, attempting to protect a host of refugees, but ultimately, he and many of the Morian people were cornered by Elwin Davos. In that encounter, Thayloc was almost killed, and nearly everyone he was protecting was massacred. In the aftermath, Thayloc recovered among the Lumek, bitterly lamenting his failure to do more and to see the events unfolding in his own city. He began helping the Lumek and the Goddess Lumé defend the firelands, but his anger and hatred for the undead were all-consuming, and he soon left to hunt them down on his own, spending long months within the now Morian Bonelands, preying on and destroying countless undead.

Thayloc became a beacon for those affected by the Evernight and for many surviving Morians who fled to other kingdoms and nations. Soon, his we joined his endless battles, small groups of loyal, fanatical, and displaced hunters, all of whom shared his hatred for the corrupted monsters of undeath and the Ossarchate in particular. Across the southern parts of Aterica, Thayloc is now revered by many, though most consider him an extreme and reclusive god. His uncompromising focus and otherworldly ability with his mythical Heartbow are the stuff of legends.

## Appearance

Thayloc is a very tall Human, approximately seven feet in height, with soft brown hair that has silver roots. His skin is extremely pale and muted, nearing a light grey. His eyes are said to emit a silver glow in darkness but usually look dark grey. He wears durable black leather armor, partially covered by a shining silver mail. On his back is his great Heartbow, a legendary weapon believed to be older than the world. It doesn't need arrows and fires bolts of silver energy and force. When normal undead are struck, they often burst into flames of silver fire.

## Influence and Followers

Thayloc has many followers across southeastern Aterica, especially among the Lumek, but also among the Acturians, Ordani, Cascilians, and even the Kithil. After the Evernight and the rise of the Ossarchate, many feared that a tide of undeath was about to sweep over the world, but Thayloc, alongside Lumé, helped to calm such fears. As such, Thayloc is worshipped as a god of resolve, retribution, and revenge. He is often invoked after terrible events or tragedies and receives a constant stream of prayers asking for blessings in the face of great trials. His priests are often at the forefront of the world's effort to destroy the undead. They arm themselves with bows made of silver and are always the first to charge into darkened tombs and cursed ruins.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Thaylock as a Deity
>
> Choosing Thayloc as a deity for a Cleric, Paladin, or Warlock Patron appeals to those who have endured profound losses, seek justice and revenge, and are overwhelmed by anger and hatred. Making a pact with Thayloc is less about true justice and more about being driven by such intense fury that other concerns fade away. Many of his most loyal followers find this path ultimately leads to their doom as they push themselves into increasingly dangerous situations. Interacting with Thayloc himself is often difficult, as he is brooding and despondent, yet strangely brilliant, which fosters fanaticism and deep reverence among his followers.

> [!info] Social
> #### Relationships
>
> A resolute friend and ally of Lumé, Thayloc often fights side by side with Lumé, and the Lumek consider him one of the greatest destroyers of undead in the world, next to Lumé herself. Thayloc spends most of his time fighting the undead in hit-and-run attacks along the eastern coast of the Moiran Bonelands with a small band of his followers, but when the Undead attack the Lumek Firelands as part of the cycles of Fire & Death, he is often on the front lines.
>
> Thayloc despises Sionia. The two have come to blows in the past in massive conflagrations of power. It's said that although Sionia is the stronger, she refuses to harm Thayloc, something he finds incredibly frustrating, and he bitterly resents her "mercy".
>
> Thayloc is firmly aligned with Sockets and believes that all beings must follow the natural Soul Cycle. He believes this perhaps more strongly than Sockets himself and is dangerously fanatical. Soulbound are no exception, and Thayloc often demands that they release their souls to the afterlife if they meet in person.
