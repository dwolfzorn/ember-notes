# Thoma

**Areas of Concern:** Night, Divination, Resilience

**Typical Followers:** Kithil, Night Shift Workers, Diviners

**Domains:** Twilight, Other

**Pantheons:** [[The Nineteen]]

**Symbol:** A full moon shadow

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Thoma**
>
> > Find your way in the dark.

When the Keth fled from the Spine of Ember during the Shattering, Thoma emerged as a leader, helping to guide a group towards the area that the Kithil now inhabit. Unlike others, Thoma's group traveled almost exclusively at night, staying safe from predators and using her uncanny sense to find a safe passage forward in the dark. As she scouted one night, Thoma found a [[Ascendancy]] and became a shard god, using her newfound power to find those paths more clearly and look into the future just enough to find a safe way forward for her charges.

Thoma's immediate thought after ascending was to ensure the safety of those traveling with her, and she channeled her power into finding somewhere that they could be safe from the dangers of the Shattering, enhancing her ability to find a safe path forward into a form of divination that allowed her to know which way would lead towards safety. This ability only worked in the darkness, perhaps as a result of her resonance with the moons from her Keth heritage, and Thoma dedicated herself to the idea of moving forward through the darkness.

Once the Keth reached the lands that would become the Kithil villages, Thoma withdrew somewhat, going on journeys through the world in the night to try to help those who felt lost in the dark to find comfort and determination instead. But no matter how far she travels, she always returns to the place that she guided her people to, and continues to try to provide them with guidance, making her one of the deities most directly involved in the mortal world.

## Appearance

Thoma appears as an older Keth woman whose skin is covered in glowing tattoo-like markings that are constantly shifting, and thought to provide insight into possible futures. There is a moonlit glow to her teeth, and swirls of motes that look almost like stars briefly appear around her footsteps. Among her followers, finding and following these motes is often called "following Thoma's path," and the dappled light of the moon in the woods is sometimes referred to as Thoma's smile.

## Influence and Followers

While Thoma is not worshiped much outside of the Kithil villages, her influence with the Kithil culture is significant. She has feared in the past that they might rely too much on her guidance, and is known to withdraw from time to time to avoid the Kithil using her too heavily to determine their future; nevertheless, she still visits frequently to check in, and an appearance from the Seer of the Stars is considered to be a sign of good luck.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Thoma as a Deity
>
> Using Thoma as a deity for a Cleric, Paladin, or general devotion is common among Kithil, but can be found in others who have a particular resonance with night or the moons. Thoma is known for being more responsive to her followers than most, perhaps due to her relative youth as a shard god or her personal stake in the survival of the Kithil as a culture. While she does not like to interfere with the paths that her followers are taking, she often uses some of her power to look into the future and send obscure signs about which might be the best path to follow.

Between Thoma's nocturnal nature and the time she spends looking after the well-being of her followers, she hasn't been able to cultivate as many friendships and accords with other shard gods as she'd like. She does have a friendly relationship with [[Sokali]] (who has a similar connection to the [[Wirrun]] people as Thoma does with the Kithil), and she enjoys spending time with some of the other solitary shard gods to offer them comfort and community — periodically checking up on [[Sionia]] and [[Malae]], in particular. Her most significant relationship is with [[Volta]], a shard god she has never met. Even though the two prefer very different times of day, they've established a unique correspondence: leaving each other messages in hidden corners of the world, featuring their observations about life and various issues of the day.
