# Nite

**Moniker:** Nature's Wrath

**Areas of Concern:** Natural Disasters, Preparedness, Recovery

**Typical Followers:** Diviners, Builders, Aid Workers

**Domains:** Life, Other

**Pantheons:** [[The Nineteen]]

**Symbol:** A footprint made of lava

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Nite**
>
> > Prepare for the worst.

Though Nite is largely regarded as a bringer of calamity, her true focus is on teaching the people of Ember that in every disaster there is an opportunity for preparation, survival, and recovery. She monitors Ember closely and is often the first to know of an impending natural disaster, whether it is a random occurrence or one caused by the actions of a fellow god, alerting her followers through visions and dreams. Though this has only added to the perception of her as a bringer of calamity, she uses the fear that she instills in some mortals to ensure that they take her warnings seriously and are better prepared for disasters that she has been unable to prevent.

During disasters, Nite attempts to save those who she can, and bears witness to the effects of the destruction, doing her best to note where help is most needed and where greater injury or loss of life could have been prevented with better preparation. This has only added to the perception of her as the cause of calamity, but she cares little for her reputation, focusing instead on the work at hand.

In the aftermath of disaster, Nite and her followers promote community and healing, with many members of her faith helping to provide relief and coordinate recovery efforts. These actions are often seen publicly as evidence of Nite's guilt about her destructive nature, and while she remains feared for her power, many have grudging respect for practitioners of her faith and pray to her in the hopes that she will take mercy on them and show them compassion, sparing them from the worst of a disaster.

## Appearance

Nite appears as if she is made from volcanic basalt, streaked with threads of white and gray, with eyes and hair that appear to be the color of molten lava. She exudes a physical warmth that increases as she becomes excited or agitated, but is never hot enough to burn or injure any mortal nearby. Nite dresses simply and unpretentiously, in unadorned grey robes, and generally walks barefoot, with her footfalls leaving fleeting prints of burning lava that vanish after she passes.

## Influence and Followers

Nite is a well-known shard god, with many praying to her as a way of preventing impending doom. While many offer her veneration simply so she will spare them from what they believe to be her destructive nature, builders and architects follow her in the hopes that their work will withstand any disasters that befall the buildings they create, and often leave the imprint of a single footprint hidden in the mortar or metalwork of a building as a way of attracting her good favor.

Nite's most ardent and organized followers are the Seers of Calamity — diviners who receive her visions about upcoming disasters and attempt to warn those who may be at risk. The Seers are dedicated both to divination and communication, with networks that run across Ember, spreading Nite's messages and directing aid to those who need it. Though Nite often communicates with the Seers indirectly in her own form, when she walks among mortals she is generally in disguise, crafting a persona to interact with her faithful who is unknown to everyone except her most senior priestesses and diviners.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Nite as a Deity
>
> Using Nite as a deity for a Cleric, Paladin, or general devotion is fairly common, though it is more common to pray to her idly to avoid destruction than to be one of her permanent followers. For diviners, or those who aspire to be, Nite will send visions and warnings if a disaster is imminent. Her other followers, including builders, healers, and aid workers, generally follow her principles of preparation, restoration, and recovery without guidance, though it is said that Nite will sometimes leave footprints in the ground leading one of her followers to what they need.

Nite watches the world, and the other deities, for potential disaster, but does not interact with many other shard gods unless she is asking them for help with a current or potential crisis. As a result, the shard god she sees most often is Katu, who has a tendency to visit unnatural disasters on those who earn his ire. She is occasionally successful in dissuading him from destroying parts of Ember, but not nearly as often as she would like.
