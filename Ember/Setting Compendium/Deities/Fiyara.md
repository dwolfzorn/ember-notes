# Fiyara

**Areas of Concern:** Hatred, Spite, Destruction.

**Typical Followers:** Cultists

**Domains:** Fire

**Pantheons:** None

**Symbol:** A charred burning claw, surrounded by orange and purple smoke.

Fiyara was once the radiant protector of the moon [[Ragen]], a goddess of warmth who nurtured the Pyrrhen and the moon's many creatures with maternal ferocity. However, the catastrophic events of [[The Shattering]] changed her beyond recognition, devastating her realm and fracturing her mind. Driven to insanity by the loss of her [[Wild Gods]] domain, Fiyara turned upon her own siblings, [[Lorgi]] and [[Ravela]], in a civil war that physically tore Ragen apart. Now banished from the moon and imprisoned within the Volcano of Hekkiro on the surface of Ember by her brother, she has transformed into a deity of pure destruction. She now seeks to consume life in a conflagration of Voidfire, a dark, ethereal flame capable of burning through a mortal’s soul and awakening their deepest impulses of jealousy, wrath, and spite.

Since her imprisonment within the Volcano of Hekkiro, Fiyara has not been idle, constructing a sprawling fortress of black metal and churning lava. Ever since the Shattering, her corrupting influence has twisted countless creatures and peoples nearby, creating an army of hate-filled elementals. She is a desperate, caged animal with the power of a star, slowly leaking her influence into the surrounding lands.

A mysterious and ancient connection exists between Fiyara and the [[Dragons]] of Ember. The breath of [[Cruel Dragons]] bears a striking resemblance to her Voidfire, leading scholars to believe she may have played a role in their creation. Dragons have been known to venerate Fiyara with a fanatical reverence, viewing her as the source of their destructive fire and power. There may also be a connection between [[Drakon]] and Fiyara, but this is even less clearly understood.

>

## Appearance

Fiyara is a titanic entity of shifting, dark-violet flame. She appears as a massive humanoid torso of fire, emerging from the jagged, glowing rift at her waist, which keeps her trapped beneath the Volcano of Hekkiro. Upon her head rests a blackened, twisted metal crown, and her two arms are similarly charred, ending in massive claws.

## Influence and Followers

Across the Cosmos, Fiyara is seen as a loathsome figure, a betrayer who attacked and attempted to kill her own family. Most mortals are unaware of her, but those who uncover her history are universally horrified by her existence and the unnatural properties of her Voidfire. This soul-burning flame does not ignite on its own; instead, it tends to manifest in blighted regions already weakened by decay or the lingering corruption of other monsters. In these dark pockets of the world, mortals already consumed by their own grievances find themselves susceptible to her influence.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Fiyara as a Deity.
>
> Using Fiyara as a deity for a Cleric, Paladin, or Warlock Patron is an especially dangerous path of slow inenitable corruption, where a follower will slowly slip into uncontrollable hatred and rage as time passes. To receive her power, a mortal would typically make a pilgrimage to the outskirts of the Hekkiro Volcano and be close enough to her influence to become enthralled. Or if a mortal is unlucky enough to find a place where Voidfire already burns, Fiyara finds the cracks in the mortal’s mind, such as unresolved grudges, jealousy, or a desire for vengeance. Once they devote themselves to Fiyara, the mortal becomes a conduit for Voidfire, becoming more consumed by hate and rage. Over time, her followers begin to resemble their goddess; their skin becomes ashen and cracked, their eyes glow with a dim light, and they find it increasingly difficult to feel any emotion other than a cold, simmering rage.
