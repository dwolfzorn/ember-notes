# Lorgi

**Areas of Concern:** Directness, Technology

**Typical Followers:** Alchemists, Scientists

**Domains:** Fire

**Pantheons:** [[The Tanir]]

**Symbol:** A fiery steam furnace overflowing with flames, surrounded by gears and pipes.

Lorgi is one of the [[Elementals]], an extremely ancient, some say primordial, being that arose during creation. He is known as either the Fire Shaper or the Blast Lord, depending on who you ask, and he has an intense fascination with technology and progress. Unlike other [[Wild Gods]], who remain aloof, Lorgi is a remarkably approachable entity with a long-storied connection to the mortals of Ember. He is frequently credited with guiding fledgling cultures toward technological enlightenment, serving as a charismatic mentor who delights in conversation and genuinely cares about the daily lives of mortals. Despite his vast power, he remains well-informed about current events, often proving more knowledgeable about local politics than the people he is talking with.

Curiously, although he seems to be a Wild God bound to the moon of [[Ragen]], he is not physically confined to it. Instead, he wanders the [[Cosmos]] and has spent countless millennia walking the surface of Ember. Exactly how he is able to do this is unclear. Scholars and researchers suggest that he has discovered a way to maintain a connection to his domain through some invention or advanced magical knowledge. In recent years, Lorgi has settled within the Tayan Empire. Establishing a monumental workshop in the capital city of [[Caren'ac]] and assuming leadership of the Mechanica, an organization dedicated to the creation of Aethyra.

## Appearance

Lorgi appears Human, albeit with a strangely animalistic face and two horns resembling those of a beast. His skin is a deep, earthy red, patterned with rippling dark brown markings that shift across his form like cooling magma. In certain areas, his skin appears cracked, revealing an intense, pressurized fire that sputters and hisses beneath the surface, as if he is a living engine perpetually on the verge of an eruption.

His eyes are typically a calm, dark brown, but they are said to ignite with a fierce, predatory red whenever he hunts or is pushed to battle. He dresses in a sophisticated blend of intricate, deep-black clothing and heavy armor, accented with rich crimson. Upon his hips rest his twin Fire Lancers. These two curious magical weapons are masterpieces of both engineering and elemental power, acting as direct conduits that channel the raw power of the Force of Fire.

## Influence and Followers

Lorgi maintains a diverse and widespread following, ranging from the Fire Elementals and Pyrrhen upon the Charred Moon of Ragen to the many mortals across the surface of Ember. To the Pyrrhen, Lorgi is a figure of profound awe and strength; he is seen as a savior who held the world together following the devastation of the [[Abyssal Shear]] and the tragic corruption of his sibling, [[Fiyara]]. However, his reputation varies widely by geography. In regions outside the Tayan Empire, Lorgi is often viewed through a more fearful lens as a god of war or a warmonger, fueled by his close association with the militaristic Tayans and his own personal destructive exploits.

Despite his terrifying power, Lorgi remains remarkably approachable and conversational, a trait rarely seen in beings of his experience. He deliberately keeps his circle of direct followers small, as he is an exacting and grueling mentor. He expects those he grants power to be as strong-willed and ruthless in their defense of others as he is himself. His influence is most visible within the Tayan Empire, where he has found a society whose principles and goals mirror his own fascination with progress and the defense of the Cosmos. By leading the Mechanica, Lorgi has personally overseen the development of the Tayan Empire’s most significant technological marvels. Most notable among these is the infamous Aethyra.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Lorgi as a Deity.
>
> Using Lorgi as a deity for a Cleric, Paladin, or Warlock Patron is to enter a demanding partnership with a master engineer who favors industrial progress in the pursuit of defending the Cosmos. Unlike other deities who communicate through visions or distant voices, Lorgi connects with his followers through a singular, magically infused object known as The Firex. This intricate piece of machinery, which looks like a brass-cased pocket watch, serves as a direct two-way communication device, allowing his followers to contact him. Lorgi deliberately keeps his circle of direct followers small, selecting only those with the ruthless willpower to act and to pursue technology and science with a clear mind and steady pair of hands.
