# Sockets

**Areas of Concern:** The Soul, Death, Rebirth

**Typical Followers:** Scholars, Gravekeepers, the Mourning

**Domains:** Peace, Order

**Pantheons:** [[The Nineteen]], [[Sentina]], [[Solaru]], [[Sunalin]], [[The Tanir]], [[Auris Bor]]

**Symbol:** An abstract symbol representing the soul

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Sockets**
>
> > Safeguard the passage of souls.

Sockets is, to put it mildly, a stubborn, cantankerous, and sarcastic personality who delights in sharp wit and playful banter. On the surface, he frequently makes light of his station, cracking jokes about death and the afterlife, yet he remains meticulously careful with his wording and actions. Beneath this openly quarrelsome persona lies a deity deeply committed to his role as the Keeper of Souls. While other gods might demand worship, build sprawling empires, or maintain a cold distance from the living, Sockets is a professional who carries out his duties with tireless dedication. He is an [[Elder Gods]] who has seen every story, every tragedy, and every joke the [[Cosmos]] has to offer, yet even after eons, he still offers a smile and a wink to those who need a small measure of comfort in the face of the end.

This fierce protectiveness defines his existence. Sockets views every soul as a precious fragment of the cosmos and sees himself more as a humble shepherd than the shadowy, frightening figure mortals often imagine. This approachability is reflected not only in his mannerisms but also in his appearance; he frequently shifts his form to blend into various cultures. Though he is the master of Mort’olis (The Spiritlands), he spends as much time walking the surface of Ember as he does his own realm, observing the living with the keen, patient interest of a gardener watching over a particularly rowdy patch of weeds.

Sockets does not hunt wayward spirits or drag them to the afterlife by force; instead, he possesses a seemingly limitless cosmic gravity. This spiritual pull serves as the natural destination for every soul that leaves its body, creating the path that leads inevitably to the city of [[Eternas]] on the shores of the Spiritlands. Through his constant presence, the [[Soul Cycle]] itself functions, though it remains a mystery whether Sockets is the true architect of this cycle or if he is simply the hands-on steward for the [[Heart of Ember]]. He also serves as the living battery for the Soul Barrier that keeps Mort'olis contained. He ensures the dead remain in their realm, not out of cruelty, but out of the firm belief that a spirit lingering too long in the living world would merely exist in a rotting limbo. While his principal servants, the Servants of the Deathless, handle the bulk of the Rite of Passing, Sockets is known to personally appear for souls of particular note. If a mortal has done something truly extraordinary, funny, or brave, they might find a man in a dusty coat waiting for them at the threshold, offering a joke instead of judgment as he walks them toward the gates of Eternas.

Sockets is the only one who feels like a friend. I love the crusty old fart, truly. He’s the kind of person who understands you at a glance, shows real empathy, and places a gentle hand on your shoulder.

## Appearance

Sockets can take many shapes and forms, often appearing as various different ancestries, but most commonly he appears as a sharply dressed older Human with a short, neatly trimmed white beard and short-cropped hair. His skin is aged, leathery, and worn, with laugh lines and wrinkles in the corners of his eyes. Wearing a worn short coat, long trousers, and a high-neck shirt in an unknown ancient style. His most striking feature, however, is his eyes, which appear as inky, bottomless black voids with the spinning light of an orange star as a pupil.

## Influence and Followers

Sockets enjoys a sprawling following across the surface of Ember. His followers often form local communities or monastic orders dedicated to assisting their neighbors with the transition from life to death. These priests and caretakers teach that the end of life is not a tragedy to be feared, but a natural passage to the next life, a place of reflection where the soul can gain new experiences and process their old life. Usually, Sockets priests and clerics are also focused on guiding and helping people through trials, teaching that new experiences, both good and bad, and the challenges of a long life are something to be celebrated.

Sockets himself is a rare visitor to these temples. His visits are often separated by centuries or even millennia, leading to a cycle in which his grandest cathedrals fall into ruin and are rebuilt by new generations, without him ever noticing the gap. He is famously hands-off with his organized religious order, preferring to communicate with individual followers and priests. He is well-known for his fondness for certain individuals and on very rare occasions, Sockets finds a particular mortal’s wit, perspective, or spirit so worthy that he grants them a reprieve from the Soul Cycle. These individuals continue to experience the world for hundreds of years, bypassing death until they are finally ready to move on.

While Sockets wanders the surface, the daily administration of the afterlife falls to his principal followers, called the Servants of the Deathless. Residing within the towering, pearlescent spires of Eternas, these entities are no longer mortal, but their exact nature remains mysterious. Many are the extraordinary souls who, upon reaching the Spiritlands, chose to stay and serve rather than move toward oblivion.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Sockets as a Deity
>
> Using Sockets as a deity for a Cleric, Paladin, or as a Warlock Patron is a path for the pragmatic and the empathetic. Unlike the distant silence of other Elder Gods, Sockets is a conversationalist who offers his followers small snippets of wisdom, witty barbs, and sharp-tongued observations. He rarely provides direct intervention, preferring to act as a reliable guide. While he may mock your mistakes or groan about the extra work you’re causing him, there is a profound, hidden warmth behind his words. To follow him is to have a partner in the tireless labor of life. A patron who treats you like a friend and ensures that even in the darkest hour, you’ll at least have a good joke to lean on.
