# Steelsong

**Areas of Concern:** Craftsmanship, Invention, Fire

**Typical Followers:** Artificers, Smiths, Inventors

**Domains:** Light, Other

**Pantheons:** [[The Nineteen]]

**Symbol:** A forge embedded with a hammer

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Steelsong**
>
> > The hammer strikes hardest when hot.

Steelsong is a diligent and warmhearted deity who delights in the company of mortals, often spending her days working, laughing, and drinking with them. She is considered a young Shard God, having risen among the Arcturian people roughly a thousand years ago. Her legend is celebrated as a grand epic by bards and storytellers, depicting her as a determined woman who was driven forward by destiny and fate, who braved the ancient underground ruins beneath the Shana Mountains and communed with a Heart Crystal, which was entrusted to her by the last surviving Shent Seer. Whether such tales are entirely accurate is up for debate, though Steelsong herself often sings variations of them while drinking among her followers. Since her ascension, she has been captivated by ancient Shent teachings and has encouraged the recovery of lost Shent knowledge. Even a few giant families descended from the Shent that had hidden themselves away in the Shana Mountains since the Shattering were drawn to her forge, where she used their great strength to build even greater and more powerful machines and structures.

Steelsong lives in the Krackos Enclave deep within the Ruinous Crags. Her massive forge is a wondrous, awe-inspiring sight, a shining jewel in an otherwise hellish landscape. Fed by lava connected to the Pathways, the forge is a blend of fortress, city, and smithy dedicated to craftsmanship and invention. Steelsong herself is a towering, giant-like figure, which puts her on par with some of her Giant followers. The Krackos Enclave is a bulwark against the Varún, who view the Goddess as an upstart. It is often attacked by various tribes over the centuries in great, bloody conflicts, but the city is defended by dedicated, well-trained followers who are equally devoted to protecting it at all costs.

## Appearance

Steelsong appears as a young, muscular woman towering over most mortals at nearly twelve feet tall. Her dark, steel-like skin gleams under the light, and her glowing blue eyes give her a piercing, almost otherworldly appearance. Elegant bands of golden and bronze metal twist and curl around her entire form, emphasizing her impressive physique. She wields a massive great hammer named Ruenfoe, forged from ancient Shent metal and crackling with energy. She often uses this formidable weapon in battle against Varún Giants, with a roaring fury that few can match.

## Influence and Followers

Steelsong has many followers in the Arcturian, Ordani, Kithil, and Waerd cultures. Among the Nineteen, she is considered the deity of progress and invention, which garners her a great deal of support among those willing to push society and culture forward. Her temples and places of worship are often forges, workshops, or foundries, where rituals and worship are conducted alongside the crafting of goods. Her followers outside the Krackos Enclave tend to be less militant and more focused on crafting and building. Within the enclave, however, her direct followers are much more focused on every aspect of warfare, including weapons and armor, as well as inventions that will help against the Varún Giant threat.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Steelsong as a Deity
>
> Using Steelsong as a deity for a Cleric, Paladin, or Warlock Patron reflects her friendly, easygoing spirit. She doesn't demand much from her followers and is pleased to support those passionate about invention, crafting, and a relaxed attitude. However, she also greatly respects warriors who confront evil, and she has been known to manifest formidable weapons for followers who choose a more combative path. It is said that becoming a follower of Steelsong is simple: just spend a night drinking with her. Her jokes, charm, and warm nature easily attract many to her side.

> [!info] Social
> #### Relationships
>
> Steelsong is good friends with the bookish Shard God Sigil, and Sigil himself has a magical way to transport himself to the Krackos Enclave, where he imparts knowledge and information to Steelsong and her followers. It's said that Sigil helped during a Varún attack on the forge, which would be a remarkable effort by the typically cowardly deity.
>
> Steelsong dislikes the 'Family of Storms,' which includes Katu, Vekalla, Raineka, and Sokali. The Varún often use natural elements like storms, blizzards, and other phenomena to their benefit, especially during winter, to attack Krackos Enclave. This dislike, therefore, might not be directly related to the gods themselves but rather to their claimed aspects and areas of concern being linked to her enemies.
