# Themis

**Areas of Concern:** Justice, Vengeance, Battle

**Typical Followers:** Champions, Warriors, Scouts

**Domains:** Twilight, Light

**Pantheons:** [[Auris Bor]]

**Symbol:** Radiant wings encompassed by swirling darkness.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Themis**
>
> > Mercy without judgment is weakness; judgment without mercy is corruption.

Themis is an ancient deity, a close friend of the Shard God Volún and the husband of the goddess Eirun, and has two children, Varros and Gildaré, both of whom are also Shard Gods. He is thought to be older than the [[Auris Bor]] pantheon, and many believe he was once a Varak. He embodies the concept of impartial fairness and necessary retribution. Unlike deities who watch from afar, Themis operates on a deterministic philosophy: once he perceives an imbalance or an injustice against the vulnerable, his intervention is swift and absolute. Despite his ancient origins, Themis was also said to have a close bond with the deity [[Finor]], becoming enraged by his death.

During the Forsaken War, following the death of Finor, Themis served as a commander of the forces of the Aedir and the Luxarum, often leading his troops on the front lines. It was during the war that he was grievously wounded, and many believe this was when the darker side of his personality began to show as he became overwhelmed by grief and rage, unable to fight, protect, and save the mortals and other deities around him. This trauma shifted his divinity toward a self-sacrificial obsession with mortal preservation.

In the wake of the Shattering, Themis became a shield for isolated pockets of survivors, especially the young Kivahr ancestry who eventually founded the cultures of the Bejak and the Wardcall. To both, Themis is a very active part of their cultures, a being that shows little interest in slow ceremonies, adoration, or in watching things unfold; he often clashes with other members of the Auris Bor, as he views their non-interventionalist stance as a betrayal of their duty. He was blind sided by the formation of the Trine of Wards among the Wardcall, and despite many still worshipping him in secret, Themis does not openly travel in his deific form. It's not entirely clear why he would do this either. It's thought that every member of the Ironfrost Templars still carries his symbol, and he aids them on many occasions against the Vartic. He is also responsible for bringing Humans across the Shana Mountains and integrating them among the Bejak and the Wardcall.

## Appearance

Themis manifests as a towering, winged human figure clad in slate-gray plate armor, with a tall helm and a full-face guard that seems to absorb the light around it. His six radiant, feathered wings flicker in the light and shadows that bleed from his hands and fingertips. He is rarely seen without his twin relics: The Blade Inumbrate, a greatsword that hums with a cold light, and The Mourning Star, a spear that leaves trails of starlight in its wake. His voice is low and controlled, speaking with the weary, grounded cadence of a veteran commander

## Influence and Followers

Themis has many followers in the northern parts of Atercia, especially among the Bejak and the Wardcall. He is the patron of the Ironfrost Templars, an order of warriors who see themselves not just as his servants but as his children, even though officially Themis has no place among the Wardcall because of the influence of the Trine of Wards. Themis has few priests or clerics, as he prefers soldiers and warriors who take direct action. Those who do follow him are often fanatical in their belief in his divine will. Worship of Themis involves the Ritual of the Names, where warriors recite the names of their fallen comrades to him, believing that he carries their memories within his own heavy heart.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Themis as a Deity
>
> Choosing Themis as a deity for a Cleric, Paladin, or Warlock Patron appeals to those who feel a profound, heavy sense of duty toward the defenseless. A pact or oath with Themis is not a pursuit of glory, but an acceptance of a burden; his followers are expected to be an impenetrable shield, absorbing the world’s darkness so that others may live in the light. This path often leads to a life of extreme self-sacrifice, as Themis drives his followers to stand their ground in even the most hopeless sieges. Interacting with Themis is humbling, as he speaks as a weary commander who knows the name of every soldier under his care. This fosters an intense, familial loyalty among his followers, who view themselves as his sons and daughters, willing to die alongside him.

> [!info] Social
> #### Relationships
>
> His closest connection is with his wife Eirun, as she remains his closest confidant, though his obsessive duty to the mortals often keeps him estranged from her side. She, on the other hand, adores and understands his nature and supports him as much as she can.
