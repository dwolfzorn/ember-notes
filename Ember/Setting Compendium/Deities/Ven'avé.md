# Ven'avé

**Areas of Concern:** Fate, Stars, The Weave

**Typical Followers:** Seers, Oracles

**Domains:** Other

**Pantheons:** [[Sentina]], [[Auris Bor]]

**Symbol:** A star surround by glittering threads

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Ven'avé**
>
> > Playing with the intricate and complex paths of fate.

Ven'avé, the Weaver of the Sea of Stars, is the [[Elder Gods]] of fate, prophecy, and the intricate patterns of luck. Among her fellow Elder Gods, she is perhaps the most elusive, characterized by a shy, whimsical nature that keeps her far removed from mortals on the [[World of Ember]]. She is said to see [[The Cosmos]] differently than any other being, viewing the world around her as a woven mass of threads that twist, turn, and disappear and reappear in tiny flashes of light. Through this rich tapestry, she can glimpse possible future events, which makes her appear distant and out of touch with reality to those who encounter her. Despite her reclusive nature, she shares a profound and ancient bond with all the other Elder Gods, all of whom view Ven'avé as the little sister. She is especially close to both [[Spectra]] and [[Alar]], and when she is not playing with the paths of fate, she can sometimes be found pestering [[Alar]] with cryptic foretellings or trying to "help" [[Spectra]] with her new magical obsession.

Ven'avé sees herself as a curator, or a creator, and, while others like to watch the cosmos, she prefers to play with fate itself, focusing on a specific past event and exploring could-have-beens and what-ifs. She has a notably relaxed, almost sedentary personality. She appears to others as lazy and unconcerned, but only because she is deeply occupied with her own very specific form of entertainment.

Ven'avé spends the vast majority of her existence within her hidden sanctum, the Vesvstar Loom, a massive, ancient asteroid that drifts through the [[Sea of Stars]]. This workshop is sometimes anchored directly onto [[The Weave]] itself, much like the Inner Realms. Inside, the asteroid is rumored to house the Tapestry of Fate: a cosmic wonder created by Ven'avé, which serves as both a record of the past and a constantly shifting reflection of what Ven'avé herself creates by predicting the future and creating alternate versions of events long past. The Vesvstar Loom is a place of profound secrets, and few have ever set foot on its sacred floors, guarded by numerous safeguards, wards, and defenses created and maintained by every other Elder God. It is considered one of the safest places in the cosmos.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> > You’d think a Goddess of Fate and an Elder Goddess, someone with a direct line to every past event and the future, would be a historian’s dream, a walking library of perfect clarity. That is, until you meet her.. and you realize she’s less of a grand oracle and more of a jumbled mess who looks like she’s just rolled out of bed on the wrong side of the cosmos. She isn't interested in recording history; she’s too busy trying to remember what she did yesterday and whether her current game is shaping up to be a "good" one.
>
> ~Laeora

## Appearance

Ven'avé appears as a wisp-like figure of gentle, glowing starlight. Her small stature often leads people to mistake her for a teenager rather than an Elder Goddess. Her long white hair gradually dissolves into shimmering threads of starlight, which can be cut to reveal glimpses of fate. She typically speaks in soft, sleepy tones, but prefers to communicate through subtle shifts in her motions and lights, understood only by other Elder Gods. She glides through the air with a weightless, curious grace, and in her presence, mortals often feel both instantly calm and strangely intimidated, as if she can see through them and glimpse their future choices.

## Influence and Followers

Ven'avé is not a principal deity per se, with no large temples or large groups of followers anywhere in Ember or even the cosmos. (Except by the [[Star Mages]] and the [[Rhivan]] people, who regard her as a somewhat distant patron, overall, though, they are more closely linked to Spectra) This is not because she is an unknown force but rather because she is so well known that people generally understand her complex nature. Luck and fate are not forces that can be relied upon, and although mortals may hold the odd charm of luck or fate and may ask the Weaver for her blessing in desperate situations, such prayers are rarely, if ever, answered.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Ven'avé as a Deity
>
> Using Ven'avé as a deity for a Cleric or Paladin, or as a Warlock Patron, is an exercise in immense patience. Unlike more active gods, Ven'avé is almost unusable for the average devotee. She does not seek people out, ignores the calls of even the most desperate, and doesn't see things like mortals do. Following her is a path usually reserved for the Star Mages or obsessive seers, and oracles who are comfortable with a deity who feels more like a distant memory than an active patron. In extremely rare occurrences, she might offer a cryptic, half-muttered observation about a thread of fate that hasn't even happened yet, which can make following her extremely frustrating.
