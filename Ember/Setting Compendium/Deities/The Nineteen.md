# The Nineteen

Elder Gods Alar and Sockets have disagreed for eons about the nature of mortal souls on Ember, but when undead Varun began spreading across the Arctus Plateau, both recognized the fundamental danger the undead posed to the mortals fighting against them. Together, the two gods called for aid from their fellow deities — a call heeded by gods across Ember, each with their own reason for jumping into the fray.

Ultimately, nineteen deities helped to end the Arcturian-undead conflict known as the Mourning War, each assisting in their own way. Some gathered information and helped to strategize. Others jumped into battle, fighting side by side with the Arcturians. Still others helped to heal the wounded and rebuild the places destroyed by the conflict. While all took different roles and joined the battle for their own reasons, they ultimately worked together to eliminate the undead threat in the area— a powerful symbol of cooperation that became an inspiration for the early Arcturians.

Though thousands of years have passed since the Mourning War, the Arcturians have continued to uplift and venerate the Nineteen, putting aside any harsh feelings they might have towards individual members of the pantheon to celebrate what they were able to do together. Though it is not used as frequently in modern times, the phrase "Honor the Nineteen" was common in earlier eras to quell disputes and remind Arcturians what can be accomplished when disparate interests pull together as one.
