# Taryakel

**Moniker:** The Solar Tyrant

**Areas of Concern:** War, Deception, Vanity

**Typical Followers:** Tyraphem

**Domains:** Unknown

**Pantheons:** The Blinding Truth

**Symbol:** Radiant wings surrounding a golden mirror.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> **Edict of Taryakel**
>
> > Perfection is a light that leaves no shadows, nor darkness untouched.

Taryakel is perhaps the most dangerous being on the Inner Realm of [[Luxarum]], and an entity as old as [[The First Dawn]]. He views the millions of [[Tyraphem]] as his own reflected light and has a vanity that seems to surpass all others. To Taryakel, the faith of his kind is a resource to be harvested; he has spent eons subtly altering the rites and prayers of the Tyraphem so that every ounce of devotion intended for [[Lantyr]] is instead directed towards himself and his own ego.

Taryakel does not possess the frantic ambition of the younger Tyraphem Lords; he possesses the absolute certainty of a true narcissist. He is soft-spoken, radiating a calm, terrifying, and warm persona that belies his inner cruelty, his self-importance, and deep need for attention and admiration. He treats the eternal war of Luxarum as a holy performance where every death is a sacrifice to his own glory and the glory of light. He is the master and leader of the main religion of Luxarum, known as the Blinding Truth, a philosophy that suggests only he is able to interpret the light from Lantyr, and all other beings are merely shadows waiting to be consumed or cast aside.

When it comes to his personal relationships, Taryakel is a being of cold, calculated interest. He recognizes the [[Elder Gods]], but views each and every one as lesser than himself. Even Lantyr, who he publicly venerates, but in truth he believes her to also be a lesser being. He holds a deep, visceral loathing for entities from the other inner realms, such as the [[Daemos]] and the [[Casia]], viewing them as creatures directly opposed to his shining light. However, his most active hatred is reserved for[[The Abyss]]. While he is a narcissist, he is no coward, and he views the Abyss as the ultimate enemy, a glitch in the fabric of existence that threatens to dim his radiance. During the [[Abyssal Shear]], he personally led his legions to the borders of Luxarum and beyond into the [[Cosmos]], not out of a desire to save the lives of others, but to ensure the filth of the Abyss did not smudge the pristine Light of his domain.

## Appearance

Taryakel is a blindingly radiant entity whose physical form is often obscured by the sheer intensity of his wings, which are not wings at all but more like roaring solar flares that lash out and incinerate anything that enters his personal space. His face is a featureless, polished Golden Plane so that when other Tyraphem kneel before him, they see only their own desperate, flawed reflections. His golden armor is flawless, ornate, overdesigned, and breathtaking to behold.

## Influence and Followers

On Luxarum, Taryakel rules legions of other Tyraphem and celestials from his high seat within the Palace of Eternity, and his rule is questioned only by other Tyraphem Lords. On the surface world, Taryakel’s influence manifests as a form of righteousness that attracts misguided zealots, paladins, and those who oppose the great evils of the world, such as the undead. Many of these followers carry out his demands, assuming they are serving a righteous, warm entity, but ultimately, he possesses zero empathy or understanding of them. He operates from a state of absolute narcissism, viewing himself as a divine entity of light in a cosmos of distorted echoes and lesser lights. To Taryakel, his Tyraphem and the mortal followers of the surface are not individuals but instruments to further his own ends. One of his fervent beliefs is that history is irrelevant; only the present and the future matter, and he compels his followers to erase historical records or artifacts that do not highlight his own radiance.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Using Taryakel as a Deity
>
> Using Taryakel as a deity for a Cleric, a Paladin, or as a Warlock Patron is rare across the surface world for two reasons. First, such devotion requires knowledge of Luxarum and a specific understanding of Taryakel, even if that knowledge is based on half-truths or outright lies about his benevolence. Second, Taryakel is only interested in communing with mortals who spark his curiosity or reflect his own vanity. To earn his support, a mortal must be fanatical enough to accept his absolute demands without hesitation, even when those commands are of questionable morality or require acts of profound cruelty.
