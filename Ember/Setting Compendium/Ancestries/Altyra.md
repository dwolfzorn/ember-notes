# Altyra

Altyra were created from [[Human]] and follow the basic humanoid shape and form closely. From a distance, this ancestry is often confused with Humans, and it's only when examining them closely that the somewhat alarming differences begin to appear. Their skin is often unmarred, with few blemishes or defects, it's also strangely textureless, and it has a slight metallic sheen that seems to shimmer under direct light and glows slightly at night. Their skin colors range from silvers, golds, whites, creams, dark iron greys, and, in rare individuals, more intense colors such as vibrant copper. Faint lines, swirling patterns, or seams intersect and crisscross an Altyra's body, following the contours of the muscles in some cases giving them an almost constructed appearance as if the limbs have been stitched together.

The Altyra ancestry has the remarkable ability to transform their bodies into metallic constructs, if only for a short time; this transformation is stunning, unnerving, and fascinating in equal measure. Altyra bodies store large amounts of liquid metal content in the form of their "blood," and they can temporarily draw on that metal to fuel their transformations. Their alternative form varies greatly between Altyra, such as a protective metal casing surrounding the body, increasing size and strength, manifesting weapons, or even sprouting wings. In some rare cases, the limbs can disconnect and float next to the main body but cannot travel far without causing great pain. Finally, their faces are often covered by strange light-emitting symbols. These symbols are often [[Luxarum]]-based words, but the meaning is extremely difficult to decipher, even for the owner. Individual Altyra typically have a set form they can generate, which manifests for the first time during late puberty.

An Altyra's eyes are the most prominent physical feature when not transformed. They glow with an intense golden, orange, or white light, and with no iris or pupil, they can often appear intensely piercing or even frightening when immersed in darkness. As the Altyrans grow and manifest their transformative powers during puberty, their previously smooth and hairless heads are often adorned with new hairstyles matching local trends. The fine metallic hair itself is an adaptable aspect of their transformation and remains purely cosmetic.

### Biology

An Altyra's "blood" is often described as liquid metal and is the same color as their skin and hair, though much more vibrant. When transforming, the liquid metal blood is manipulated to enhance and create new forms. Still, the process is draining for most, so it can only be done periodically and briefly. Even when not being manipulated, their metallic blood is greatly responsible for various traits, making Altyra hardier and tougher to kill, and they can regenerate lost limbs over long periods of time. Unfortunately, their metallic nature comes with a cost; when subjected to freezing temperatures, Altyra slow considerably, and their flexible forms become almost impossible to express. In addition, their form becomes brittle, and they are rumored to shatter and crack apart, with pieces of their body falling away entirely.

The metal content in their skin does gives them a slight shimmer under daylight and moonlight and a low level of bioluminescence at night. Their blood is responsible for regeneration over long periods, and an Altyra can even regrow lost limbs, but this process is slow and takes many years.

Altyra have only vestigial bones in their arms and legs, and their muscles do most of the work, both in keeping the body structure together and standing upright. Their muscles are metallic and far stronger than normal flesh, which is part of how an Altyra can transform. Because of the lack of purely organic tissue and flesh, Altyra have a strong poison and disease resistance. This was both a critical factor during their creation and early years and has also served them well in the centuries since.

### Cultural Connections

The majority of Altyra upon the surface of [[World of Ember]] live in the city of [[Ancalen]], whose [[Railen]] culture is militaristic and isolationist and whose wealth was partly built on their ability to repurpose the treasures and resources of surrounding cities that fell during the time of the [[Virulent Plague]]. Ancalen is one of the Great Cities of the world, but it is a city that has been closed to outsiders until relatively recently, and even today, sections of the city are restricted.

As time has passed, Altyra have spread elsewhere on Ember in small numbers. Many who leave the confines of Ancalen do so to escape the dogmatic and restrictive ideals of the Ancalen council of three. They try to reject such teachings and endeavor to interact with, learn from, and assimilate into other cultures. Most other cultures in Ember view Altyra in a positive light, with slight trepidation only if they learn that the Altyra in question is from the city of Ancalen.
