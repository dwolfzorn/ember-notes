# Drakon

Drakon are the only ancestry on Ember to derive directly from [[Human]]. They share many physical characteristics, both in shape and form, but most importantly, a Drakon's face is remarkably human. However, their most prominent features are entirely unsettling and can only be described as [[Dragons]], with long sweeping horns, scaled skin, and long-scaled tails. Thankfully, their Human traits are often more dominant and, combined with the inherent innocence of the very young; this has enabled them to be accepted and welcomed by the majority of cultures despite some of their other more monstrous physical features.

Drakon grow thick, protruding scales that cover almost their entire bodies. On the lower arms, legs, and tails, these scales do not simply follow the body's contours but are large, angular, and dangerous. On the face, the front of the neck, and the chest, the scales change dramatically, and although they are still present, they become almost invisible and look like Human skin. When touched, these facial and chest scales feel soft and leathery and are much less capable of deflecting blows than elsewhere, making Drakon particularly vulnerable to attacks in these areas. This weakness is often compensated for with armor that only covers the chest, neck, and face, usually leaving the arms and legs bare, as the incredible natural strength and thickness of the scales make those areas almost impossible to harm. Drakon have heavily scaled tails, with varying lengths and thickness, but are often heavy and thick at the base. Their arms, hands, feet, and legs are usually longer than Humans, appearing slender and elegant with claws upon their fingers and toes. A Drakon's scales are surprisingly kaleidoscopic; muted reds, oranges, blues, and purples are the most common, but other rarer colors also appear. The more pronounced scales of the body are almost always darker towards the base and lighter towards the tip and can also range in tone and hue. Facial and chest scales are often starkly different from the rest of the body, taking on traditional human skin pigments.

While the scales are a significant aspect of a Drakons appearance, the most striking physical feature are the large sweeping and elaborate horns that grow upon the top of their heads. Multiple sets of larger and smaller symmetrical horns are common, covering almost the entire head. These horns are not present at birth and may only appear as small nubs protruding from the skull, but as Drakon age, the horns continue to grow. This is often a clear indicator of how old an individual Drakon is by the size and amount of horns. Among the horns, hair is also common but is drastically different in texture from human hair as it is tough, metallic, and heavy.

### Biology

Drakon’s inner biology and organ structure are almost identical to that of humans. However, they have much richer and more oxygenated high-temperature blood and cores that can metabolize and enrich even tiny amounts of air, keeping their bodies running under extreme conditions. This means a Drakon is very resistant to intense temperatures and comfortable in freezing and searing environments. They can also breathe normally in thick smoke, choking gases, and at high altitudes where the air is thinner, such as the tallest mountains and even the [[Sea of Stars]] without any additional aid. Unfortunately, this significantly impacts Drakon's stamina, requiring breaks and moments to catch their breath during intense bouts of activity. If a Drakon pushes themselves too hard, they can even cause self-inflicted blackouts and fall unconscious for several hours.

It's unknown if there is some kind of biological trait at work here but all Drakon have an inherent ability to manipulate a particular form of magic called Drakonfire. Some believe this red and purple flame causes no harm to a Drakon, and they can also choose whether the fire is harmful to others. Still, generally, Drakonfire is extraordinarily dangerous and burns unnaturally even when entirely immersed in water. Drakonfire can manifest in various ways depending on the Drakon; some can blast hot plumes or beams toward their enemies, while others can surround their bodies with a fiery shield that protects them from harm.

### Cultural Connections

Drakon arose very recently in the [[Tayan]] during the [[Night of Swords]] from a very small number of Humans. They have a total of three generations: the first, their children, and their grandchildren, with the youngest generation's oldest members ranging from newborns to late teenagers. Most Drakons, therefore, are concentrated in the Tayan empire as this is where they were created. Still, Drakons are extremely curious, capable, and adventurous and over the last seventy-two years, they have begun to explore and discover many distant lands. They can now be found almost everywhere on both Atercia and Kessia.

Drakon arose without any memories of their previous lives and seemed unconcerned about their mysterious origins. The fact that they are connected in some way to [[Dragons]] is of little relevance to most, but some are fascinated by their origins and seek any and all information regarding their creation and Dragons themselves. Most people who encounter Drakon for the first time are surprised by their appearance but are not particularly concerned or view them in any negative light, as many believe they are Aster.
