# Fej

Fej are Humanoid in appearance but have a strong resemblance to [[Nir'ae]], with some striking differences. Their rough-looking skin is finely cracked and banded in places with alternating bands of dull and bright material, and it often appears glassy yet dull at the same time. This skin is marred, pitted, and even rougher around the joints and the face, with this effect becoming much more pronounced on older Fej. The skin is almost always dark gray or black, with some rare individuals exhibiting lighter skin tones, such as light gray or white. Still, there is no indication of color except for a slight shine, which may sometimes suggest a slight blue or purple coloration.

While their skin is colorless, traces of glowing fire are unmistakable and can be seen glowing beneath the skin's surface as if the Fej are powered by an intense internal furnace. This fiery glow that can be seen flickering beneath the skin is a trait that all Fej universally share. In almost all cases, this fire is red or orange, sometimes making Fej appear monstrous and malicious. However, different fiery colors are recorded and considered exceptionally fortuitous, with those individuals often seen as blessed and pushed into leadership roles. The fire commonly brightens and is more visible in places where their skin is thinner, such as around the eyes, under the nail beds, or within the mouth. Fej hair is almost always black and greyscale in tone, like their skin; however, some individuals find that their hair has a mysterious smoke-like property that causes it to fade away into nothing towards the tips of the hair. This isn't something a Fej is born with but happens to Fej as a consequence of acute emotional distress or anger and can last for many years.

The eyes of a Fej glow with a burning intensity and are often the first feature that others are drawn to. Their eyes are always the same color as their internal glow, with a burning pupil, slightly less intense iris, and finally, a dimmer yet still gleaming sclera. It's rumored that Fej can see through fire itself and innately recognize the temperature of materials and objects around them, helping them construct weapons, armor, and other metallic objects.

### Biology

Fej are, in many ways, a fire made manifest in a Humanoid body, closely tied to the fire moon [[Ragen]]. They are primal and strangely elemental, with their internal biology almost entirely replaced by magical energies instead of organic tissue. Their blood is a rich, thin, molten fire and flows within their bodies at high pressure from their core, a rhythmic ball of fiery energy that acts as the Fej heart. This means that even minor wounds cause a spray of fiery blood to erupt from the Fej before their fast metabolism and magical energy closes the wound. When wounds are severe enough or savage in nature, Fej have been known to explode into a fiery ball of destruction, casting fire across the area. Fej do have skeletons to hold their forms upright, but it's thought they are akin to obsidian glass. Unfortunately, this cannot be confirmed, as when a Fej dies, its form bursts into flame and turns to ash over only several minutes.

Fej metabolisms are much higher than other ancestries, making them strong, agile, and capable of great bursts of elegant, almost otherworldly speed powered by their fiery core. This makes them exceptional warriors and fighters but comes with a cost, as they require their own special food types that are either calorie-dense or infused with magical energy. They can eat and drink regular food, such as plain bread or rice, but this often doesnt fuel them enough to maintain their peak efficiency.

Famously, Fej are almost entirely immune to the typical dangerous effects of fire; they are fire resistant to such a degree that walking into a raging bonfire only tickles the skin and makes a Fej slightly uncomfortable. Only extremely high temperatures, such as molten lava or [[Dragons]] flame, is capable of bypassing this resistance. As a consequence of their fiery bodies and biology, Fej are particularly weak to water. They do not drink liquids in any significant quantities, prefer solid foods, and become burnt out if immersed in water, quickly becoming exhausted, which makes them slow swimmers.

### Cultural Connections

Fej rarely leave their homelands, and the Fej ancestry is almost indistinguishable from the[[Lumek]] culture in many ways. They are the dominant ancestry of the Lumek and have lived in the Lumarin Forelands since ancient days. They only barely managed to survive the [[The Shattering]] but clung on and rebuilt their culture. Since the events of the [[The Evernight]], they have been remade into a fighting force of unparalleled efficacy and determination under the direct leadership of the Shard Goddess [[Lumé]]. The Lumek have now been battling the undead [[Moiran]] to their north in the Cycles of Fire & Death for generations, and their is no sign of the stalemate ending soon.

Some Fej do travel and explore, more often to recruit allies and to confront undeath wherever it may be found. Fej are a somewhat common sight in the city of [[Ordain]] as the city has a close trading connection to the Lumek, supporting them in their struggle. Some Fej spend time in the city to learn about the [[Cindaric Sages]] and their teaching, which they find fascinating. Most Fej found in places around the world would likely have a close connection to their homelands, but it's possible to find small family groups incorporated and integrated into many of the cultures of Aterica. The largest population of Fej outside of the Lumek is found across the oceans in Kessia, and they work closely with [[Kessian]] in their struggle against the horrors of the Obsidian Desert.
