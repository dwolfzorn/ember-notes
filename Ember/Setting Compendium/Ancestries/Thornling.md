# Thornling

Thornlings are a very small plant-like ancestry with, large round heads and equally large eyes. Their facial features, combined with their small stature, can make them appear "cute" and delicate to others, but Thornlings are natural survivors — tough, adaptable, and hard to kill. Their skin is made of a resilient bark and the thorns that sprout from their body when they age are a strong defense mechanism against predators. They are also able to gradually adapt and transform their outward appearance to blend into their surroundings, though their bodies and heads almost always feature leaves and other plant-like shapes. The healthier these appear, the better the emotional state of the Thornling.

Though some Thornlings have been known to live incredibly long lives, on the whole Thornling lives are shorter than average. This, along with their ability to survive encounters and experiences that might do serious damage to other ancestries, has given them a reputation of being free-wheeling and risk-taking, whether they are on their own in the wilderness, often in communities centered around the large bodies of water they use for sustenance, or living among other ancestries across Ember.

### Biology

Thornlings have no organs or bones — their internal structure is akin to tree or plant with tough fibrous internal structures. This makes them extremely hardy — a Thornling's skin-bark is nearly impenetrable, and their limbs have tree-like strength regardless of the thickness. They also take little damage even in situations that might harm others, recovering instantly after falls from large heights, for example, and can regrow lost limbs. Thornlings have no blood, but do contain a yellowish viscous liquid. This functions more like sap than blood, and leaking the substance is not a sign of danger or a grievous wound. Despite this, Thornlings always appear wide-eyed and youthful — the coloration of Thornling skin rarely changes from birth unless it has adapted to its surroundings in some way, and a Thornling's age is reflected solely in the number of thorns on their head.

Thornlings do not have the capacity to consume solid food, instead getting all of their nutrients from liquids. They have a great need for water, drinking more of it than any other humanoid, and it can have a somewhat intoxicating effect on them, making them feel more optimistic, lively, and outgoing. This can lead to celebrations and light-hearted pranks after heavy rains, or when at the edges of large bodies of water. Baths are a particularly enjoyable luxury for most Thornlings, and traditional baths known as Taro generally involve cooling water followed by warm water soaks infused with minerals or flowery scents. Thornlings also greatly enjoy flavorful drinks with a high water content, including alcoholic spirits and teas. Sharing a drink with a companion that hasn't had it before is considered a great sign of trust and friendship.

Because of their need for water, Thornlings are vulnerable to dry conditions and generally avoid dry environments like deserts or tundra unless there are scattered water sources. If kept away from water for too long, their leaves and bark will slowly shrivel, causing exhaustion and pain in the short term and dessication and collapse in the long term, though this takes many years and can be reversed if enough water is poured over them.

>
> Sexual reproduction between different ancestries is very possible and can result in children with mixed ancestry. Players can play as characters with mixed ancestry, but this requires consultation with the GM and may not be ideal for first-time players or GMs. See [[Biology]] for more information.
>
> Thornling are not humanoid, and thus not able to reproduce with any other ancestries. Some believe that the blessing of life that makes Thornlings possible can incorporate physical aspects of any ancestry who involved in their creation, but not much is known of the process outside of Thornling circles and such changes would be best expressed via an [[Aster]] ancestry.

### Cultural Connections

Thornlings generally live in small family groups, centering their lives around a fresh body of water. Able to successfully thrive in places that others can't, some Thornling settlements live in relative isolation. They generally ignore those who pass them in the wilderness unless the travelers interfere with their lives or limit their access to water in some way, but travelers may still be wary of them, as it can be hard to know what action might trigger a response from the Thornlings, who care about the natural world around them and are fearsome in their near indestructibility.

The ability of Thornlings to survive on their own is countered by a deep personal wanderlust that may have been instilled by some design of their creator. The spread of Thornlings to places across Ember is due in part to this love of exploration and adventure which often centers around the discovery of some new source of water. Once a Thornling settles down in a new place, a small community often grows around them and the source of water they have found, with the new Thornling community taking on the culture of those around them.
