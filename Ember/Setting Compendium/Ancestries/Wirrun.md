# Wirrun

Though they are tall and slender, with long limbs and somewhat delicate appendages, Wirrun are well adapted to life in the coldest and uninhabitable lands of Ember. Their light bones and ability to walk effectively either on the balls or soles of their feet make it easy for them to traverse both slippery ice and thick snowbanks, and their innate elemental magic helps them retain their footing even when conditions are at their iciest. Their eyes, which range in color but have no visible pupils or irises, can see clearly even during extreme weather, working alongside their flexible and sensitive ears to navigate blizzards and heavy fog with ease. In warmer climates, Wirrun often feel vaguely uncomfortable, and though they can be found throughout Ember, often create cold environments where they can feel more at ease.

Wirrun retain some physical traits from their original role as servants of the fiendish Daemos, including their generally lanky frames, claws, and sharp teeth, but most of their most dominant traits come from their creation by Sokali, including their thin but unfreezable hair. Though their skin ranges from pale white to purple to charcoal grey, Wirrun all share the same hair color — a stark white that echoes Sokali's long mane— and sometimes call themselves the Wintermaned in her honor.

### Biology

Wirrun are not only cold-blooded, but also supernaturally resistant to cold temperatures, perhaps as a result of the motes of silver found in their lavender colored blood. They can reportedly walk through raging blizzards with no ill effects. In warmer climates, on the other hand, they often feel some discomfort, with the severity ranging from one individual to the next. Especially high temperatures can make some nauseous and weakened. Wirrun who settle in warmer areas are said to acclimate to the temperature eventually — some more successfully than others.

Wirrun are omnivores with a strong preference for meat, which is generally their most reliable food source in far-off northern Aterica. Those who travel south and spend time in warmer climates vary their diets, but it is rare to find a Wirrun who does not eat meat as some major part of their diet. Though their creator, Sokali, helped to adapt their digestion to be able to thrive on this diet, their sharp teeth come from their original role as servants of the fiendish Daemos of Signara. This, among their adaptations for hunting — sharp claws, keen eyesight, an ability to pursue their prey across snowy terrain — can make them appear frightening to others, even when they do not intend any harm.

The Wirrun adaptation to cold is well known, but their origin with the Daemos of Signara is an affinity for symbol magic and words of power. In particular, Wirrun voices are strong and have a slight magic to them, which allows them to be heard through fierce blizzards, in the midst of raging storms, and across vast distances.

Individuals with Wirrun heritage have been known to prefer cooler temperatures, even if they do not inherit their parent's cold adaptation.

### Cultural Connections

The Fikost culture is made up primarily of Wirrun in and around the northern Hoarfrost Sea, with its center in the great floating city of Rimefrost. The presence of trolls and other creatures in the area, which the Wirrun hunt but also are wary of attacks from, tend to keep most Wirrun in and around this northern area. Some Wirrun, though, do travel further south and into warmer climates, either becoming accustomed to the warmer temperatures or using magic to keep themselves cool despite the weather around them, shielding their bodies from some of the heat of these regions.

Among Wirrun that travel further south, they are most likely to be found among the Bejak and Wardcall culture, as their environments are colder than those frequented by other cultures and they share the Wirrun and Fikost dislike of trolls. Elements of Fikost culture can sometimes be found in Bejack and Wardcall cities and settlements with large enough Wirrun populations. Some Wirrun will also venture further south to trade, often ending up in Ordain, where there is a large enough population that one of its districts has an ice spa designed for Wirrun merchants.
