# Ashka

Ashka are a slender snake-like ancestry with several aquatic traits that make them ideally suited for semi-aquatic environments such as swamps, coastlines, and humid jungles. They can also breathe underwater for extended periods, far more than most other ancestries. Their most prominent physical trait is their hooded and flared head shape that falls across their shoulders and often connects to their back muscles. These hoods are not always present but the majority of Ashka exhibit them in some form. Their bodies are covered in delicate scales and softer facial skin with intricate and colorful patterns intertwining their entire bodies and heads. Some Ashka break these conventions, and their appearance is less smooth and rougher overall with pronounced scales, ridged brows, and rougher, completely scaled bodies even on the face. Their necks are slightly longer than those of many other ancestors, and coupled with their flexibility, they have the ability to quickly twist and turn, observing the world around them in all directions. Ashka also possess long thick tails that are partially prehensile and sometimes capable of impressive feats of dexterity and propel them through the water.

Ashka possess several less obvious but key traits, such as webbed hands and feet that allow them to swim more efficently and nostril slits with heat-sensing pits that allow them to track and sense warm-blooded creatures. It is thought that Ashka may have been created as proficient and adaptable hunters for semi-forested mangroves and jungles as they are exceptionally well suited to those wetter environments. Unfortunately, this makes them sensitive in other areas, and they are far less comfortable in dry climates and biomes, experiencing dry skin, scale conditions and lung diseases.

An Ashka eye deviates from most serpentine creatures and are not commonly slit like a snake, instead possessing a round pupil and coming in a wide range of colors. Their sclera is rarely white and instead a rich yellow or orange, giving Ashka a slightly predatory appearance. Ashka are known to be able to sense creatures with their thermal imaging pits on their face. Still, it's not known if this is somehow connected to their eyes, as they have exceptional vision overall. The combination may be responsible for their remarkable ability to perceive nearby and distant objects under extremely difficult conditions.

### Biology

Ashka biology uniquely differs from other ancestries and seems carefully and tightly controlled or crafted in several aspects. Their skeletons have many hundreds of thin bones, which gives them incredible flexibility, and the position of the key organs is neatly organized with their hearts resting in the center of their chests between large lungs. Ashka are also mesotherms, a strange cross between cold-blooded and warm-blooded creatures. They can raise their body temperatures consciously but typically run their bodies cold-blooded, making them fond of warmer temperatures and fire. However, this comes at some cost, and an Ashka’s body requires a certain amount of humidity to remain healthy as their bodies use and require more water than other ancestries.

Ashka are particularly adept at underwater movement with many physical traits such as webbed hands and feet and thick muscular tails, which aid them in underwater movement. Their blood is also able to hold onto more oxygen than other ancestries, giving them the ability to hold their breaths for long periods, but this comes at a cost, and Ashka are vulnerable to air pollutants that can affect the lungs and prone to lasting lung damage that affects them permanently.

### Cultural Connections

The Ashka do not currently have any significant cultural dominance they can call their own on the surface of Ember. However, it is claimed that long ago, during the [[Age of Beasts]] and the [[Age of the Tower]], they had their own rich and storied culture called the [[Sedyri]]. This is something that most Ashka are taught by their parents, which may seem ancestry-based from the outside but can sometimes include others and may indeed originate from this incredibly ancient culture. Most Ashka live in small family groups, and as part of larger cultures, they often form small enclaves and try to maintain some semblance of their own cultural identity. These enclaves try to keep the history and traditions alive as best they can, but each successive generation becomes increasingly integrated into the cultures around them. Ashka are rare among most cultures but not unheard of and can be found in many far-off lands, more often as sailors, traders, or adventurers.
