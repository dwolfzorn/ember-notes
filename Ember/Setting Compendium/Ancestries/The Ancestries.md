# The Ancestries

## Diverse Possibilities

Every Ancestry in Ember is deeply tied to the world's cosmology, history, and geography, with boundless opportunities for customization and expression. Making a choice of which Ancestry to play is a critical choice for a player and we wanted that choice to have both meaning and a way to immerse someone new into the world. The sixteen playable Ancestries all have different ways to experience the story, with custom abilities and features that continue our central theme, an imaginative setting filled with wonder.

## Aster - Creative Expression

Due to the nature of the Ancestries and their connection to the creative Lifeforce of the [[Heart of Ember]], there is a seventeenth option for anyone that would like to play a completely original character, an [[Aster]]. Aster are individuals who are changed by the Heart of Ember at birth, making them wholly unique. This change is sometimes simply cosmetic, with spines, feathers, or extra limbs or appendages, but it can also change the individual’s internal biology. Aster can be found across ancestries, creatures, and within any culture; it always occurs at birth and is a permanent choice that allows players to decide for themselves how they would like to present themselves. With this in mind, it's perfectly acceptable for someone to grab a character from another setting and explain their appearance as an Aster!

## Mixed Ancestries

Among mortal ancestries, the subtle and complex weaving of ancestries often results in children of mixed ancestry who may display a wide range of physical characteristics reflecting their parents' appearances and traits. Mixed-ancestry individuals are relatively common, if not widespread, across Ember, and as cultures and ancestries have become more intertwined in the current age, they are becoming increasingly common still.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Mixed Ancestries In Game
>
> For practical purposes, players may choose to create characters of mixed ancestry; however, it is recommended to consult with the GM beforehand, as such characters may introduce unique roleplaying and mechanical considerations. Mechanically, you must still select one Ancestry which defines the mechanical benefits and features that apply to your character, as noted in[[Ancestry]].
