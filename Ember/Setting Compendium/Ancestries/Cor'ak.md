# Cor'ak

The Cor’ak are unique among the ancestries of Ember, descending from primordial [[Leviathans]] and inheriting their considerable strength and power. Their flesh is tough, leathery, and infused with elemental earthly energies and primal might. This is not just limited to their outer skin, which is hard and tough, but also the softer skin on their inner arms, hands, and chest. Their entire bodies have a remarkable resistance to physical attacks, and this resistance is so strong in certain individuals that they often feel little need for armor. A Cor’aks skin is also commonly marked by subtle swirling patterns of various colors corresponding to volcanic rocky crystals. These markings are not always obvious from a distance, but small crisscrossing lines encompass their chest, midriff, and core, appearing like fine fissures in rock. Overall, Cor'ak are large, bulky, and stocky, often able to retain impressive muscles with little effort, thanks to their unique metabolisms. In addition, they only exhibit three thick fingers and toes on their hands and feet.

A Cor'ak's most distinguishing feature is their strange facial structures and large, often circular crowns of rocky material on top of their heads. This crown or crest frequently appears bone-like or horn-like in color and tone, but its vastly more durable and small crystalline rock formations glitter across the surface. Usually, smaller rocky protrusions or vestigial spikes of the same almost unbreakable material can emerge alongside the main crowns across the cheek, neck, shoulders, and forearms as a Cor'ak ages.

A Cor'ak eye is a glittering crystalline orb, often a bright singular color that matches the tiny crystalline materials embedded in their head crowns. Cor'ak have poor vision overall, finding it challenging to track quick movements and faster attacks. This often worsens as they age, and they become slow and methodical in their movement overall and prefer to take their time performing all manner of daily tasks.

### Biology

Cor’ak possess a dense structure of internal organs that more closely resemble their Leviathan progenitors, this helps them withstand extreme physical forces and strong gravitational effects, shrugging off overwhelming blows with ease. Even if a Cor'aks tough hide is pierced, they are not particularly vulnerable and feel only minor amounts of pain. When significantly injured, their rich crystalline-infused blood can expand around the wound, creating a rocky, crystalline shell. This shell helps seal the wound quickly and protects the Cor'ak from any further damage. It's also said that when Cor'ak are killed, this shell can sometimes cover their entire bodies and the Cor'ak can choose to remain within their bodies as slumbering spirits instead of moving on to the [[Mort'oliss]]. It's rumored that after thousands of years, they can be reborn and walk the surface of Ember once more.

Cor'ak are one of the largest and bulkiest ancestries on Ember, and among the cities and cultures of [[Aterica]], they often feel out of place and uncomfortable. This is elevated by their primordial origin and instinctual dislike of urban environments, leading them to generally avoid such places. Although rarely studied in detail, Cor'ak also seem to possess a rare ability to cause fear in other ancestries, especially when appearing suddenly from the dark. Though this fear often quickly passes, it is thought that a Cor'ak's appearance is akin to viewing an ancient predator made flesh, which is somewhat true in many ways.

### Cultural Connections

The Cor’ak ancestry is responsible for establishing one of the oldest cultures Ember has ever known: the [[Maziran]]. The Mazira persisted untold thousands of years on the distant lands of Akazea. They lived in relative peace alongside Leviathans as sages and custodians of the earth. Eventually, at some points during [[Human]] early expansion out of the Aran Peninsula, they sailed across the Crossing Sea and encountered the Mazira for the first time. Over centuries, humans have become the dominant ancestry, growing in population and constructing cities and entire nations, but they did so alongside the native Cor'ak. This meant the Mazira culture remained culturally prevalent. While there are differences from more ancient beliefs, many Cor'ak believe the Mazira culture today continues their original traditions. Today, Mazira culture is represented by a relatively disparate array of ancestries with a large population of Humans, but the Cor’ak are still greatly revered and respected.

Outside of Akazea, Cor’aks are exceedingly rare. They seldom travel outside their homeland, but recently, small numbers of curious explorers and more adventurous Cor'ak have been seen in distant realms such as the [[Lumek]] Homelands, the [[Cascilian]], or glittering [[Ordain]]. However, most Cor'ak found in such places can usually trace their family history back to their moon-struck continent.
