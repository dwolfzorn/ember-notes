# Nir'ae

Though the Aedir Empire stretched across Ember at its height, encompassing many different ancestries, it was most closely tied to the Nir'ae, an ancestry whose magical energy could be clearly seen in their iridescent birthmarks and skin patterns. These patterns, caused by exposure to the arcane artifacts under the Empire's control, were often seen as a mark of someone favored by the Empire. That changed when the Empire fell, bringing about the cataclysmic Shattering. Many Nir'ae were killed, the world plunged into chaos, and the markings that were once a point of pride began to be viewed with mistrust.

In the present day, Nir'ae are very rare but can be found throughout Ember. They remain tied to the forces of the cosmos and have retained their connection to magic despite the many generations that have passed since the fall of the Aedir Empire. Nir'ae may draw power from great arcane artifacts and, though it has been generations since the Aedir Empire was lost, they can still commune with Ember, gaining some measure of power during the moons' zeniths.

### Biology

Nir'ae are tall, lithe humanoids whose long, tapered ears frame their wide-eyed countenance. These ears can be moved independently from the rest of the Nir'ae face and from each other, and can be used to express emotion or add extra meaning. Similarly, the color of the irises in a Nir'ae’s large eyes are known to change several times throughout the seasons of their lifespan, often tied to major moments of magical expression or personal change. Their skin tones and hair colors, which remain the same throughout their lifetimes, come from a wide palette of shades, with hues that reflect their proximity to Lantyr at the time of their birth.

The most striking physical feature of the Nir'ae is their iridescent birthmarks, which generally run across the sharp angles of their face and torso. The patterns appear on a Nir'ae at birth and can grow and shift with age. They're also more than cosmetic, generating a magical effect that provide the Nir'ae some protection from spells and harmful magic, and allowing them to draw upon the magical forces of Ember at times and places where they are especially potent, such as during a moon zenith.

These markings, along with the ancestry's inherent connection to magic, comes from the Nir'ae's long exposure to the arcane influence of the Eye of Aedon and Heart of Ember during the many thousands of years that the Aedir Empire made their home in the Starshield.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Nir'ae Heritage
>
> Sexual reproduction between different ancestries is very possible and can result in children with mixed ancestry. Players can play as characters with mixed ancestry, but this requires consultation with the GM and may not be ideal for first-time players or GMs. See [[Biology]] for more information.
>
> Nir'ae are Humanoid and thus able to reproduce with other Humanoid ancestries. Due to the relative rarity of the ancestry and the fact that some Nir'ae hide their origins when within the broader world, little is known about their descendants.

### Cultural Connections

For most on Ember, Nir'ae are an ancient memory. Most of the Nir'ae were killed when the Sun Tower built by the Aedir Empire exploded, killing everyone on the Starshield and beginning the era known as the Shattering. Though the Aedir Empire contained members of a variety of ancestries, many of their more visible and well-known leaders were Nir'ae, leading some to tie the ancestry to the Empire's hubris and view them with suspicion or distrust.

Several cultural expressions popular during the Aedir Empire's heyday, including the use of cosmetics to emphasize or substitute for Nir'ae skin markings, became deeply unpopular overnight, and many Nir'ae joined new cultural communities and distanced themselves from anything having to do with Aediri culture. The largest exception is the hidden people of the Lorai, who left the Starshield long before the Shattering for a life underground, deep beneath the mountains of northern Oakengard. Most Nir'ae who can be found in the present day come from that area, though there are small isolated groups elsewhere in Ember.

When living outside of the mountains, some Nir'ae still hide their true ancestry and lineage, even though the present day reaction to them tends more towards fascination and surprise than prejudice, especially on the Arctus Plateau and in Ordain. Others have become interested in the past of the Aedir Empire, traveling Ember in search of the Empire's lost history, and others have no real tie to what they view as ancient history and have fully integrated themselves into another modern culture.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Playing as a Nir'ae Character
>
> Nir'ae are the rarest of the playable ancestries in Ember, and their tendency to isolate themselves could make them more challenging to play. We suggest that the GM and player discuss the decision to ensure that both are comfortable before choosing this route. With that mind, it can be a rewarding choice, as there is a great deal of lost lore about both the Nir'ae, and the Aedir Empire that they were once the dominant ancestry of, to be found within Ember as written.
