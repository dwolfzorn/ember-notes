# Kivahr

Created by the Giants of the [[Shent]], the Kivahr are powerfully built and extremely strong, with gray skin, dense bones, thick muscles, and taller than average stature. Though their dense musculature makes their physical power apparent, they are also durable, capable of withstanding large amounts of physical pain and duress, and of retaining focus even in deeply trying situations. This may have been the result of an intentional design decision by the Shent to help the Kivahr survive the dangers of the Shattering, but the details of their creation have long been lost to time.

In addition to their physical endurance and strength, Kivahr have a connection to the Shattered Moon of Akon that provides them mental focus — something which should be impossible. Akon appears to give Kivahr an ability to concentrate when at its zenith, which many Kivahr have learned to replicate to some extent in their daily life. For Kivahr who are able to tap into it, this focus can provide added concentration, the ability to make logical deductions more rapidly, and, in rare cases, even the ability to rapidly heal their own bodies.

### Biology

Kivahr are a large, muscular ancestry with exceptionally dense bones, thick and wiry muscles, and tough skin that heals quickly after injury and is prone to developing scars in the process. Kivahr biology also makes it nearly impossible for them to retain fat and gives them a greater ability to build muscle than most other ancestries, regardless of their body type. As a result, Kivahr often carry muscle in places that others do not, including as a layer around their ribcages and in their wrists, giving them the appearance of having longer than average arms and a slightly hunched posture.

Kivahr eat and consume the same food as most other humanoid ancestries, but tend to eat in greater quantities and do not process or store fat in a typical manner, instead storing energy in their muscles that can be drawn on to sustain them during prolonged periods of caloric deficit. This allows Kivahr to survive for several months without food if needed and gives them great endurance.

In addition to their musculature, the most distinctive biological features of Kivahr are their gray skin, which often gives them a chiseled, stone-like appearance. Kivahr blood is similar in consistency to that of other ancestries, but it resembles liquid copper, with a bright orange tinge and slightly metallic appearance. When dry, their blood is a stark green/cyan color, resembling oxidized copper. While the glittering eyes of Kivahr appear on the surface to be different than other ancestries only in the way they sparkle, a closer look at their pupils reveals small components that move together in a steady rhythm — a relic, perhaps, of the way they were constructed.

Players can play as characters with mixed ancestry, but this requires consultation with the GM and may not be ideal for first-time players or GMs. See [[Biology]] for more information.

> Though Kivahr are a created ancestry, and do not descend directly from the ancient Jarn, they are compatible with other Humanoids on Ember. Individuals with Kivahr heritage, while not as muscled as their Kivahr ancestors, often retain some of the ancestry's dense musculature and above-average height.

### Cultural Connections

Kivahr are the dominant ancestry in two cultures that live adjacent to the Giants Mountains that the Shent once called home — the knowledge-seeking [[Bejak]] to the North and the more warlike [[Wardcall]] to the East. Both cultures draw from elements of Shent culture, including a belief in thinking before acting. These beliefs have been reinforced by several ancient Hul'grun who live in cities with large Kivahr populations, where their knowledge of Shent culture is prized. In addition to their connections with some Hul'grun, the Kivahr have also retained some ties to the [[Keth]], another ancestry created by the Shent, and both Bejak and Wardcall cultures trade readily with cities dominated by the[[Kithil]]culture, which was founded by the Keth.

Beyond the Bejak and Wardcall communities on the outskirts of the Giants Mountains, the confines of the Great City of [[The Sphere]], and, in recent years, in the[[Fallbrook]] district of Ordain and the Arctus Plateau in general.
