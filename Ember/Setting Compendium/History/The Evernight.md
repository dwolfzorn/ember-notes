# The Evernight

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Lore Summary
>
> The city of Miris is suddenly annihilated in a titanic explosion caused by an abhorrent magical ritual. In the aftermath, a vast cloud of impenetrable darkness — the Evernight — settles in the sky, covering the lands that would become known as the Moiran Bonelands. The region is then overrun by powerful undead hosts, crushing nearby cities and settlements.
>
> - **The Unholy Kingdom:** Emergence of the Ossarchate and the Blood Barons.

In the weeks leading up to the event that would become known as the Evernight, nothing amiss was reported in the city of Miris. Life continued in one of the largest human kingdoms, [[Moiran]], in the world at the time. The people of Moiran were a strong, dignified people who had allied themselves with the [[Lumek]] to the east and had spread across the region. Alas, in the waning of  AS869, without any warning, Miris and the entire royal bloodline were annihilated, leaving behind a scarred, magical wasteland now known as the Desolation.

Some survivors contend that the explosion was the result of a high-level magical ritual gone catastrophically awry, while others believe the Moiran inadvertently unsealed an ancient, primordial evil buried beneath the city. Regardless of the cause, the consequences were immediate. A thick, impenetrable shroud of unnatural black clouds settled over the region, blotting out the sun and plunging the land into a permanent, freezing twilight. This "Evernight" acted as a conductor for necromantic energies, allowing undeath to run rampant, turning thousands into monsters. From this sea of carnage arose the Blood Barons, undead lords of immense magical power. The Blood Barons seized control of the vacuum left by the fallen Moiran Kingdom, forging the region into the [[Ossarchate]], an empire of the dead.

### Valiant Defenders

To the east, the Lumek watched the collapse of their allies with horror, quickly realizing that the Evernight was a monumental threat to be contained. The initial waves of mindless undead, throwing themselves at hastily organized Lumek defenders, were soon replaced by legions led by the Blood Barons themselves. What followed was a series of grueling battles. Though the Lumek eventually emerged victorious, the cost was staggering; their strength was so thoroughly depleted that any hope of a counter-offensive into the heart of the Ossarchate was abandoned.

Retreating to a strategic bottleneck, the Lumek focused their remaining resources on fortifying the City of Ash. This fortress-city became the final bulkhead against the encroaching tide of the dead. For centuries, the two powers have remained locked in a stalemate of attrition known as the Cycles of Fire and Death. These are not mere skirmishes, but periodic, high-intensity wars where the Lumek’s mastery of flame and steel is pitted against the inexhaustible might of the Ossarchate.
