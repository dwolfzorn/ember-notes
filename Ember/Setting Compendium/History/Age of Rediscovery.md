# Age of Rediscovery

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Lore Summary
>
> The Age of Rediscovery is the era following the Shattering and the Abyssal Shear, marked by the slow rebuilding of many surviving cultures and peoples in a changed, far more dangerous world. Much of the history immediately after the Abyssal Shear is shrouded in lost records, chaos, and desperation, but over time, nations and kingdoms begin to rebuild and flourish, rediscovering knowledge of the Aedir and other ancient cultures.
>
> - **Mortal Ancestries:** The creation of the Kiska, Keth*, Kivahr* & Drakon.
> - **Human Expansion:** Much like the ancient Aedir, Human ancestry has spread across much of the known surface world on the continent of Aterica, founding many diverse kingdoms and nations. These include cultures of the Tayans, Moirans, Cascilians, Arcturians, Ordani, and Kessians.
>
> * The creation of both the Keth and Kivahr may have occurred just before the Shattering.

Roughly two and half thousand years have passed since the [[The Shattering]], and in the years following, destruction, chaos, and death on a large scale affected nearly every existing culture and ancestry. Every single [[Aedir]] colony overseas from the Starshield fell. Its peoples either fled or were destroyed, and only a few cultures remained intact and rode out the storm of darkness and the emergence of monsters. The early [[Kelmezian]] peoples, made up primarily of Humans, resisted the onslaught, as did the [[Lumek]] and the [[Oaken]], who were spared much of the destruction due to their already tenacious nature. The young ancestors of the Keth and Kivahr, who had only recently founded cultures such as the [[Strider]], [[Kithil]], and [[Bejak]], miraculously escaped unscathed. The [[Shent]] who had existed in the center of Aterica, living across the spine of Ember, were also utterly destroyed. Only a few of its people escaped the cataclysm and now either live in isolation in hidden remote settlements or joined the defiant Varún culture.

Many[[Leviathans]], who had claimed much of the world since the [[Age of Beasts]], were also affected and killed by the emerging monsters and [[Dragons]] or forced from their ancient territories. Some remained, but these beings were perhaps the strongest, largest, and most dangerous of their kind, and most Leviathan survivors remain unchallenged by mortals even today.

The Shattering saw the death of countless [[Shard Gods]], including the entire pantheon of the Aedir. Many Shard Gods were confronted by monsters and dragons after the events of the Shattering itself and were hard-pressed to resist. Those caught alone without aid often fell to the darkness and chaos that swallowed the world during that time. Shard God families or pantheons managed to survive more easily, such as the family of [[Katu]] and [[Vekalla]].

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> Most cultures and nations agree that the time period after the shattering is the beginning of a new age. Many proposed names are suggested, such as the Age of Rediscovery or the Age of Humanity, but some scholars insist that the world now resides in the shadow of the Broken Tower and thus should be named the Age of Shadow.

One of the most devastating early consequences of the Shattering was the emergence of [[Bestiary Overview]]. Monsters is a broad term that encapsulates a wide variety of horrific and terrifying foes that eagerly dug their way into the fabric of the world. So omnipresent have monsters become that cultures in the present are always on guard against overwhelming forces that seek to destroy them. In some cases, this has destroyed entire realms and peoples throughout the current age, including perhaps most famously, the [[Moiran]] nation, which became consumed by undeath and created the Blood Barons. The emergence of Dragons is included within the monster category in many ways. Beings of such overwhelming strength and fury that they caused the death of Shard Gods and smashed apart nations, such was the case with the Oderita Kingdom, which fell to [[Karaxsus]] the Flaming Dread.

Today, most of the younger kingdoms, nations, and cultures of [[Aterica]] and [[Kessia]] are still expanding, growing, and spreading throughout the world. [[Human]] are often the driving force of these realms, but they are not alone in their ability to adapt and overcome the ever-present dangers and challenges of the world. The most impressive example is the rapidly advancing [[Tayan]], which in the last seventy-two years has conquered and seized lands from the Vathar Forests across the Crossing Sea in [[Maziran]] and began its conquest of the [[Kelmezian]] city-states in the south. While Humans are often seen as responsible for expansion, this is a narrow viewpoint, and many other ancestries are both welcomed and critical to the success of human-dominated cultures. In some cultures and nations, humans may be the largest single group, but other ancestries combined make up a far greater percentage of the population. They are often instrumental in presenting diverse viewpoints that fuel further expansion, and the idea that Humans alone are responsible for growth is laughable. There are many other dynamic and expanding cultures that are not human-dominated, such as the [[Bejak]] and the [[Wardcall]] Kingdoms of Kivahr, The Jaedith Dominion of Kiska, The [[Railen]] of Altyra, and [[Waerd]] of Signborn. Overall, the world is still recovering from the Shattering in many different ways, and there remains countless opportunities for nations, organizations, and even individuals to strike out into wilder and untamed or lost regions to found new kingdoms and recover lost knowledge.
