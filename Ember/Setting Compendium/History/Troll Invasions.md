# Troll Invasions

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Lore Summary
>
> After the Unveiling, the Giants slowly push the small packs of Trolls out of the Shana Mountains in the center of the Aterican continent. Unfortunately, this causes the Trolls to swarm in massive numbers and attack the ancient cultures of the Vaznor and the Windbare, utterly annihilating them over the course of five hundred years.

In the aftermath of [[The Unveiling]], which saw the [[Giant]] begin to settle in the [[Shana Mountains]], they soon found that the cold, perilous peaks were already inhabited by small packs of creatures called [[Trolls]]. Possessing both ancient magical abilities and vast physical strength, along with a considerable height difference, the Giants were nearly invulnerable to the Trolls' attempts to resist. Over the course of a few decades, the Trolls were pushed out of their ancestral peaks and forced into the northern lowlands, and with unimaginable fury, they surged like a tide into the territories of the ancient [[Vaznor]] and Windbare. It is thought that before the displacement, the Trolls were vicious but never truly united in any great numbers. Unfortunately, the northern lowlands proved to be an abundant breeding ground, and their population exploded.

What followed was a brutal, one-sided conflict that spanned half a millennium. The Trolls were relentless, driven by a primal need for new territory. As the invasions unfolded, it became clear that whenever the Troll packs found or took over an area or settlement with abundant food resources their population would quickly multiply in a matter of months. While the Varak (the people of the Vaznor cities) held significant advantages, most notably their advanced technology and their ability to fly, they lacked the raw physical strength to hold ground against the sheer mass and power of a Troll horde. The Varak could harass from the skies, but they could not stop the trolls from overrunning their streets and tearing down their flying settlements whenever they attempted to land.

One by one, the shining jewels of the north were extinguished, and the vast Vaznor kingdoms fell into ruins. The last great Vaznor bastion, the city of Kettera, stood as a defiant island in a sea of Trolls for centuries, but it eventually succumbed, marking the functional end of the Vaznor. After the fall of the last Vaznor city, the northern reaches became the territory of the Trolls, and they have claimed the vast region ever since. Over time, Trolls have pushed further south, often clashing with emerging cultures and nations, and while the Trolls are often a terrible blight, they have yet to boil over into an unending swarm, at least so far.
