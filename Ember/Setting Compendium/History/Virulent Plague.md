# Virulent Plague

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Lore Summary
>
> As the Age of Rediscovery unfolds, the Aran sub-continent is hit by a devastating contagion known as the Virulent Plague. It sweeps through the bustling Human settlements of the Kelmezian Kingdoms, and a mass exodus occurs. Many of the affected cities are entirely abandoned in the aftermath, and Humans begin spreading across the Aterican continent.

According to accounts, the Virulent Plague began in the Fortress city of the First Throne in  AS272. Theories regarding its origin remain a subject of intense debate: some scholars suggest a malevolent entity or force, others believe it was a targeted curse, while many maintain it was simply a natural (though uniquely vicious) pathogen that proved resistant to the healing magics of the era. Regardless of its source, the plague’s lethality was absolute. Transmission occurred through both physical contact and air exposure, but its most devastating trait was a fluctuating dormant period. Lasting several days to several weeks, this window of asymptomatic infection allowed carriers to travel far and wide before realizing they were ill, resulting in rapid, uncontrollable regional transmission before the full extent of the plague was recognized.

The progression of the disease was as agonizing as it was swift. Once the symptoms manifested, individuals suffered from sudden fevers, chills, nausea, and debilitating pain in the throat and chest. As the infection reached its terminal phase, the extremities, the fingers, toes, and nose, would turn a bruised, dark blue. The most horrific symptom, however, was the expulsion of a fine, dark blue and green mist known as the Virulence. This mist was caustic, rapidly burning organic matter, including the victim’s own skin, nearby foliage, and animals. This Virulence would accumulate on surfaces, sprouting small, pulsating bulbs and growths that were extremely caustic to the touch. The substance proved nearly impossible to eradicate; it was highly resistant to magic and could be removed only by extremely high temperatures and sustained flame.

### Destruction of the Kingdoms

Prior to the outbreak, the region's [[Human]] settlements were divided into a network of small, independent kingdoms. While they ostensibly recognized the authority of the First Throne and High King Kostros Irdragon, a descendant of the legendary [[Fenra Irdragon]], this fealty was symbolic at best. In truth, the kingdoms were fully independent, and when the plague emerged, this disunity proved fatal. The initial refusal to coordinate or share resources led to a total breakdown of order. As a result, the [[Kelmezian]] Kingdoms fractured, and within several months, once-vibrant cities were silenced and filled with the dead, their streets choked by the blue-green mist of the Virulence.

To the north, a few city-states on the periphery managed to survive by enacting ruthless measures. They shuttered their borders and enforced absolute quarantines on all arriving refugees, aided by powerful entities like the [[Shard Gods]] [[Malae]], capable of sensing the dormant infection. However, even this assistance offered little comfort; the power of many other Shard Gods proved insufficient to cure the disease, and reports suggest that at least three such deities were themselves consumed and killed by the plague's effects. Ultimately, the Kelmezian kingdoms were shattered forever within the [[Aran]] sub-continent. Of the great city-states of that era, only Nasjar, Carinth, and Verena survived the devastation, taking on the name of the Kelmezian City-States instead.

### Origin of the Altyra

Amid the height of the outbreak, as the population of the Aran Peninsula was decimated, several [[Tyraphem]] outcasts saw a unique opportunity to study the newly created humanity. They had observed the creation and spread of Humans with both curiosity and unease, as Humans were different from other ancestries on the surface in ways they could not fully explain or understand. Thus, the Tyraphem began a series of clandestine experiments within the city of [[Ancalen]] during the height of the plague, capturing infected and non-infected humans alike. These experiments, which the Tyraphem viewed as both an act of mercy to save Human lives and an expression of their own curiosity, resulted in the creation of the [[Altyra]] ancestry.

The newly formed Altyra were immune to the effects of the Virulent Plague and able to thrive as most of the places around them crumbled. They quickly came to view the Tyraphem’s experiments as a blessing from Ember that elevated them above the other “weaker” ancestries. They sent parties to acquire goods and technology from neighboring cities abandoned due to the outbreak, and over many years the [[Railen]] culture formed, centered in Ancalen.
