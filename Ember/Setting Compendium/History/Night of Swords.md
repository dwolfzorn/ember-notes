# Night of Swords

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Lore Summary
>
> The Night of Swords was a coup enacted by an ambitious Kirargy Knight named Calyn Kariset seventy two years ago in the city of Caren'ac. The insurrection was seen by many as justified, and Calyn used the initial support to found the Tayan Empire. One unintended consequence of the Night of Swords was the origin of Ember's youngest ancestry, the Drakon.

The Night of Swords is an infamous coup that occurred only seventy-two years ago in the city of [[Caren'ac]]. The event was spearheaded by [[Calyn Kariset]], a renowned and formidable [[Kirargy Knights]], who, backed by her loyal Order of the Dragon, orchestrated a swift execution of the Caren'ac Kingdom's nobility. Calyn had been the only Kirargy Knight to ever bond with a [[Cruel Dragons]] in the history of the [[Tayan]], and she was famous as an honorable and fierce warrior. At some point, she must have become disillusioned with the political landscape of the Tayan Kingdoms, especially within Caren'ac, which arguably had become a cesspool of decadence, excess, and corruption. In a single, blood-drenched night, the despotic King Vardegan, his entire family, and the majority of the ruling caste of nobles, including their families, were systematically dragged from their estates and murdered.

The fallout of the slaughter sent shockwaves through the region, including among the other Kirargy Orders and Noble Holds in nearby cities. Half of the Knightly orders viewed Calyn’s actions as an act of madness, moving immediately to disavow her. However, Calyn was more than prepared, and as the dust settled that morning within the city of Caren'ac and with the pre-arranged support of the remaining Kirargy orders across the region, she seized the vacant throne and declared herself Empress, transforming the remnants of the Caren'ac Kingdom into the Tayan Empire.

### The First Days

In the aftermath, Calyn became an extremely divisive figure. To the common people, she was a transformative hero; she immediately enacted sweeping reforms, granted new freedoms, and established a series of surprisingly just laws. On the other hand, she was brutal in her methods to maintain order and had little patience for dissent. The Temple of [[Selai]] within Caren'ac, and the [[Shard Gods]] Selai herself, viewed the formation of the Tayan Empire with cautious acceptance. Seeing the previous King Vardegan as a corrupt despot, Selai felt that the coup was a path toward stability and renewed purpose for the people, and her passive endorsement provided just enough legitimacy for Calyn to cement her rule.

This religious backing was mirrored by several of the Great Noble Holds, which chose to align rather than risk future destruction. Calix Hold, Tyren Hold, and Vyssar Hold, active in the cities of Braken, Vence, and Corla, were among the first to pivot, accepting the formation of the Tayan Empire without opposition. These nobles traded their independence for high-ranking positions and safety within the new Imperial hierarchy, becoming the mercantile backbone of Calyn’s reign. However, Oryn Hold, a fortress-estate centered in the city of Point, refused to work with the emerging Empire. Although too far removed for Calyn to crush immediately, the Oryn family began setting the stage for more organized resistance and the eventual formation of the organization known as the [[Vanguard]].

### Calyn's Folly & The Origin of Drakons

Another event, kept secret by the Empress (she became aware of it much later), is known as Calyn's Folly, which took place alongside the Night of Swords. Calyn did not discriminate in the destruction of the noble families. One family, the Talmonts of Talmont Hold, was killed as well, despite remaining largely above the corruption of the city and the Caren'ac Kingdom at large. Unknown to Calyn, the Talmonts were responsible for containing an ancient seal deep beneath their estate, just outside the city of Caren'ac, from the time of [[The Shattering]] through their bloodline. When the last Talmont was killed, it released a long-dormant monster of incredible power. Before long, the Kirargy Knights assigned to kill the Talmonts had been killed and enslaved, and the dead Talmonts had been raised to assault the city. It was at this point that several powerful entities stepped in, including, eventually, the [[Elder Gods]] [[Alar]] and the Shard God Hestol.

The alien monster was killed in a massive magical conflagration, something most of the city saw and heard but never fully understood. Hestol was slain in the conflict, but in a final, sympathetic act of preservation, Alar gathered the dying Shard God's fading energy and used it to catch the souls of the Talmonts, Kirargy Knights of the Dragon, and innocent people caught in the crossfire. She forged them into entirely new forms, imbued with draconic resilience and the fading light of Hestol, and created the [[Drakon]]ancestry.
