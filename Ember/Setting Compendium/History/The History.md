# The History

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Lore Summary
>
> The history of Ember is divided into four distinct ages, with each era lasting less time than the one before it. This accelerating timeline seems to be increasing volatility and chaos as the World moves toward the present day.
>
> Due to the sheer scale of world history and the cataclysms that have reset civilizations, much of the past remains shrouded in mystery. The further back one looks, the more lost history there is to uncover, with ancient records eventually dissolving into myth and legends.
>
> - **The First Dawn:** The creation of the Ember Cosmos, the Inner Realms, the Elemental Moons, and the Elder Gods. In addition, the first sapient ancestries arise, the Theroch and the Xaxin.
> - **Age of Beasts:** The age of the Leviathans and the creation of many mortal ancestries, such as the Co'ark, Jarn, Giants, Varak, Vrjnhar, Saurvek, Ashka, Carrow, Leafia, Zeph, and Wirrun. In addition, the First Shard Gods appear. This age ends with the Unveiling.
> - **Age of the Tower:** The Nir'ae appear and eventually create the Aedir Empire, which spans the world. This age also sees the creation of Fej, Thornlings, Hulg'run, and Humans. This age ends with the cataclysm known as the Shattering.
> - **Age of Rediscovery:** The present age and the creation of the Kiska, Keth, Kivahr & Drakon ancestries.

The [[World of Ember]] in the present age is a land of staggering contrasts, defined by the weight of ancient secrets. It is a place where epic myths are not merely stories, where vast, sunless ruins and silent monuments whisper of a long-lost majesty of kingdoms and empires long dead. Yet atop these remnants stand vibrant, dynamic kingdoms and young nations, fiercely determined to carve out a future amid ever-present dangers. In some cities, it's easy to forget that dangers grow perceptibly darker just beyond thier walls. Beyond the flickering torchlight of civilization, the wilds are reclaimed by monsters that roam the dark, while ancient evils hide deep within the Pathways below.
