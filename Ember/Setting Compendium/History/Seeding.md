# Seeding

The Seeding is known throughout Ember as the Season of Restlessness, or the first season of spring, a sixty-day period during which the world of Ember awakens from the [[Stilling]]. As [[Signara]] begins to wane in the sky and [[Primordis]] becomes more prominent, mortals find themselves grappling with a frantic internal "itch" that defies easy explanation. Sleep becomes restless, and settlements hum with nervous energy as people spring-clean their homes or make plans for the year ahead.

Across the surface world, the transition from the Stilling is rarely gentle; the Stilling's snow and ice does not merely melt but often shatters under sudden pressure. Travel becomes harder due to sudden meltwater, flash floods, and roads turning into mud. Yet the restlessness benefits traders and merchants, who often use the feeling of unrest to set out on new journeys. Life also begins to awaken from dormancy over the winter as plants sprout once more. Predators, starved by the long winter and emboldened by the seasonal unrest, can be at their most dangerous, hunting with a desperate intensity, making the Seeding a time of both vibrant renewal and profound, erratic peril.
