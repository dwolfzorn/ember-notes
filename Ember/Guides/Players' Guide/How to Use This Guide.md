# How to Use This Guide

Welcome to the Ember Players' Guide! This document is your main companion while playing the Ember game. Whether you’re setting up your first character or looking up a specific rule during a session, the information in this guide will be very helpful — but is not mandatory to read.

There are many ways to play roleplaying games, and instead of reading everything first, you can jump straight into Character Creation and learn about Ember as you play. The Ember game includes a guided Character Creation interface (which you can access via instructions on the [[Creation Overview]]page).

That interface will show you all available character options as you step through the process. Reading this guide in addition to using that interface will provide helpful additional context about Ember's world, and the unique changes that Ember makes to the rules of the game system you are using, but you can also simply work through the Character Creation interface and learn the rest as you play!

## Guide Structure

The Players' Guide has three main categories, each explaining a fundamental part of the Ember game:

- **Character Creation:** What you should know when creating a character
- **Playing the Game:** What you should know before starting play or during play
- **Exploring the Settin**g**:** Story and reference material that will come up again and again as you explore.
  ## Navigation & Formatting

To help you find what you need quickly, we use specific formatting styles throughout this guide. You will also find this formatting used in the same way throughout the Ember game.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> These yellow-bordered blocks are called **Gamemaster Blocks**, and typically contain important information or notes that directly address the Gamemaster — though sometimes also the player! They can contain mechanics, rules, and important summaries. These blocks convey meta information that a player or Gamemaster should know, but that a character within the game world wouldn't.

> [!danger] Hazard
> These red-bordered blocks are called **Hazards**. They are typically used to highlight extremely important information, or information about dangerous obstacles, such combat mechanics, combat tactics, and traps.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> These beige-background blocks are called **readalouds**, and are typically used to indicate that the information comes directly from a character's voice within the world. Readalouds are also extensively used by the Gamemaster to read Quest and Event text during the game.

Throughout this guide, you will see many links formatted like [[Players' Guide]]. They can be clicked on, and will take you to another page, or a different section within the current page. Some of the links in the Players' Guide will direct you to other pages within this guide, while others will open game content such as the Setting Compendium.
