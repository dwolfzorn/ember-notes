# Magic and Spellcraft

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Contextual Information
>
> The material presented on this page differs depending on whether you are playing using the *Dungeons & Dragons* or *Crucible* game system.

Because of the unique nature of the Ember setting, there are certain limitations or features of the various [[Magical Forces]] which govern the cosmos that differ from other popular fantasy settings. Some of these important nuances are described below.

## Magical Runes

Each of the twelve [[Runes]] of [[Spellcraft]] draw upon certain fundamental forces of the cosmos to empower magic shaped using that rune.

**[[Rune: Control]]**

Spells crafted using the Rune of Control invoke specific glyphs intrinsically used by the fiends of [[Signara]]. Only [[Daemos]] are able to manipulate the full power of such glyphs, but other spellcasters have learned to harness a measure of their power.

**[[Rune: Death]]**

Spells crafted using the Rune of Death are crafted by tapping into dangerous [[The Abyss]] energy. This magic flows directly from [[Kinalathi]], although the provenance of undeath is unknown to all except a handful of Ember's most enlightened minds.

**[[Rune: Earth]]**

Spells crafted using the Rune of Earth draw upon fundamental elemental energy of [[Cora]], allowing powerful spellcasters to control gravitational, tectonic, and corrosive forces. Some of the most dangerous and volatile practitioners of caustic magic are devotees of [[Slinerak]].

**[[Rune: Flame]]**

Spells crafted using the Rune of Flame draw upon fundamental elemental energy of [[Ragen]]. Some of the most fervent pyromancers are devotees of [[Ravela]], [[Lorgi]], and in the most extreme cases [[Fiyara]].[[Dragons]], [[Drakon]], and other types of dragonkind also utilize the Rune of Flame, although their connection to it is shrouded in mystery

**[[Rune: Frost]]**

Spells crafted using the Rune of Frost draw upon fundamental elemental energy of [[Mayis]]. The Lady [[Areyter]] is said to be the most powerful user, as are the ferocious Behemoths that live upon Mayis. During the Age of Beasts, the [[Giant]] of the Shent harnessed and mastered the Rune of Frost to construct their towering mountain cities, and even today, many Giants maintain a close affinity to this Rune.

**[[Rune: Illumination]]**

Spells crafted using the Rune of Illumination draw upon the light of [[Lantyr]] itself. The most fervent practitioners of radiant magic are the countless [[Tyraphem]] that dwell in [[Luxarum]]. Before the Shattering, the world-spanning empire of the [[Aedir]] were also said to master and harness the Rune of Illumination.

**[[Rune: Illusion]]**

Spells crafted using the Rune of Illusion draw upon the elusive shadow magic of [[Primordis]] and the [[Casia]] who are masters of its arts. A dangerous perversion of this rune has been observed among servants or followers of [[Kyasifer]] whose illusions twist and torment the mind.

**[[Rune: Kinesis]]**

Spells crafted using the Rune of Kinesis use gravitational manipulation to infuse objects with kinetic energy, one of the most fundamental forces in the cosmos. Kinesis powers [[The Weave]] and manifests in myriad ways. The Elder Goddess [[Alar]] is believed to be its most powerful wielder, although some speculate that her strength may be rivaled or even surpassed by the Outer God [[Vhismara]].

**[[Rune: Life]]**

Spells crafted using the Rune of Life draw upon the ever-present resonance of the [[Wildsong]] to manipulate primal natural energy. Channelers of this rune are most notably found among the [[Strider]] culture or as devotees to the [[Wild Gods]] who act as custodians of the natural order.

**[[Rune: Lightning]]**

Spells crafted using the Rune of Lightning conduct the voltaic energy of storms. These currents flow from the fundamental elemental energy of [[Aura]] and the Elemental Sovereign [[Nymbohr]], who is known as the Lord of Lightning. Many believe that the Rune of Lightning is a critical component in the construction of constructs and the [[Wyrms]] of [[Akon]] once mastered its mysteries.

**[[Rune: Oblivion]]**

Spells crafted using the Rune of Oblivion dare to touch the entropic energy of [[The Abyss]] and its pure forces of destruction. Recognized usage of this rune is universally reviled by all cultures of Ember, who forbid such spellcraft. Occultists who dare to channel such power are often — either directly or unknowingly — servants of [[Sha-Xotha]].

**[[Rune: Soul]]**

Spells crafted using the Rune of Soul can subtly manipulate the ever-flowing current of the [[Soul Cycle]] originating from the [[Heart of Ember]]. Most usage of this rune is culturally accepted throughout Ember, but major works of spellcraft that interfere with the current of souls draw the attention and ire of [[Sockets]] who presides over its continual flow.

## Teleportation

Teleportation magic is tightly restricted and controlled by the Rhivan [[Star Mages]], who limit its use. Long-distance teleportation magic is likely to be unavailable to you as players, though some shorter distance spells are commonly used.

## Reviled Magic

Necromancy is nearly universally reviled on the [[World of Ember]]. Undeath is a fundamentally corrupting force of the [[Cosmos]], only introduced to Ember through the events of the Shattering, and has horrified every known Ember culture. No peoples, cultures, or nations tolerate the existence of necromancers, or of anyone who seeks to defile or corrupt the cycle of life.

Not all spells that belong to the Necromancy spell schools are reviled, nor are all spells that deal Necrotic damage. Only spells that draw upon the power of [[The Abyss]] or the corruption of undeath are believed to be abhorrent to the natural order. Player characters who are observed wielding such magics on the [[Arctus Plateau]], and in most other places on Ember, are likely to be treated as a malevolent threat. Other spells invite debate between scholars regarding the morality of their use and, as with any spell, can be used for good or evil according to the actions of their caster.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Reviled Spells
>
> The following spells specifically are unanimously reviled and will not be viewed as acceptable to anyone who witnesses their casting and understands what they observed. Using them will likely lead either to immediate conflict or immediate reports to local authority figures. It could also cause pivotal characters in the game to loathe or become hostile towards the party.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Abyssal Corruption & Necromancy
>
> While the peoples and cultures of Ember strictly forbid the practice of Necromancy and Abyssal Magic, we believe the best stories come from difficult choices. If your actions lead to evil outcomes or the use of reviled magic, you will accumulate corruption both narratively and through the [[Attunement]] system. We encourage you to embrace this if it suits your character! Just keep in mind that the full narrative weight and mechanical consequences of this corruption are far reaching and their full weight needs to be managed by your Gamemaster.

## Resurrection

The [[Soul Cycle]] in Ember is one of the fundamental laws of the Cosmos. Resurrection magic is possible, but only if it does not directly oppose the natural order of the Soul Cycle. It is considered to be an acceptable form of intervention, rather than subversion, as long as:

1. The person has died very recently.
2. Their Soul has not yet made the conscious choice to move on to the Spiritlands.

> [!info] Social
> #### Communication with the Departed
>
> Use of the ability to speak with the dead may or may not be deemed acceptable depending on the context. It is not reviled for priests or mages to use Speak with Dead to communicate with the departed, provided it is done respectfully.
>
> In Ember, magically communing with the dead works by pulling a small fragment of the soul back into its former body. This shade of the creature's former consciousness can impart portions of its past knowledge.
>
> Speaking with the dead imposes an undeathly facsimile of life upon the target which might be easily misconstrued as a reviled spell if its usage is misunderstood by the observer.
