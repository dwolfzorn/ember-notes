# Ember Background

Rather than choosing from a set of pre-defined backgrounds, you create a Background during character creation in Ember by combining your choice of [[Cultures]] and [[Players' Guide]]. Selecting these features during character creation automatically populates the Background section of your character sheet.

## Culture

Your Culture describes the region and peoples of Ember whom you most closely identify with as "home". This does not necessarily describe where you were born, although it can. Your chosen Culture will grant you a traditional Skill Proficiency, an Ember [[Knowledge Area]], and a signature feature unique to that culture.

Certain Cultures may have predominant Ancestries which compose them, but every Ancestry is compatible with every Culture. There are eight cultures available to choose from:

- [[Arcturian]]
- [[Cascilian]]
- [[Kithil]]
- [[Lumek]]
- [[Oaken]]
- [[Ordani]]
- [[Tayan]]
- [[Waerd]]

These cultures are those which are geographically closest to and most prominent in the [[Arctus Plateau]] where the Ember game takes place.

## Path

Your Path describes a facet of your character's history: what deeds you have accomplished, what training you have received, or what organizations you are affiliated with. Your chosen Path will grant you a choice of Skill Proficiencies and Tool Proficiencies based on the core competencies of that path.

There are currently 22 Path options available. Each Path option provides a backstory seed, two Skill Proficiencies, and one Ember Knowledge Area, all hallmarks of that Path. Additionally each Path provides a Tool Proficiency or an additional known language, and starting equipment (on top of to what was granted by your chosen Class).

- [[Academy Dropout]]
- [[Anchorite Marine]]
- [[Caravanner]]
- [[Cindaric Initiate]]
- [[Cosmopolitan Fashionista]]
- [[Crown Apothecary]]
- [[Flameguard Militia]]
- [[Imperial Conscript]]
- [[Lightsworn]]

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Telling Your Own Story
>
> The provided Path options in Ember suggest some backstory seeds which will be compatible with Ember's narrative and provide frequent opportunities to engage with it. We recommend using one if it is your first time playing Ember. However, if you want to change or entirely replace some suggested details of your Path, you should feel empowered to do so.
>
> In this case, you should choose whichever Path provides a set of proficiencies and knowledges that you find appealing, but change the backstory details or even name of that Path to represent the story you want to tell for your own character.

### Soulbound

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Soulbound are mortals whose souls cannot or will not move on after death. Soulbound characters can hail from any ancestry, culture, or walk of life, but they are all united by a common element: every Soulbound has some Unfinished Business that tethers the living essence of their soul to their unliving mortal form. Unlike the undead, who are corrupted or twisted by evil forces, these souls simply defy the constant pull towards the city Eternas and its Rite of Passage until they have completed their final tasks.

During the Path step of character creation, you may choose to undertake the exceptional challenge of beginning your character's journey as [[Soulbound]]. Being Soulbound will make your character's journey more difficult, as there are meaningful mechanical penalties which this feature imposes upon your character.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Hard Mode
>
> Undertaking this challenge is equivalent to playing Ember in "hard mode" and is not recommended for first-time players.
