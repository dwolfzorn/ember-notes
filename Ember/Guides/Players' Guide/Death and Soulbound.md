# Death and Soulbound

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Soulbound are mortals whose souls cannot or will not move on after death. Soulbound characters can hail from any ancestry, culture, or walk of life, but they are all united by a common element: every Soulbound has some Unfinished Business that tethers the living essence of their soul to their unliving mortal form. Unlike the undead, who are corrupted or twisted by evil forces, these souls simply defy the constant pull towards the city Eternas and its Rite of Passage until they have completed their final tasks.

When a player character dies in Ember, they're presented with an existential choice: leave the mortal world behind (resulting in the creation of a new character), or remain tethered to their body by becoming [[Soulbound]]. Becoming Soulbound grants them a Soulmark, binding their souls to the mortal plane until their unfinished business is resolved.

## Soulmark Progression

The are three categorical severities of the [[Soulbound]] condition that have been observed. Mechanically, these are expressed as Soulbound Ranks. When a Soulbound character dies and subsequently clings to life, the rank of their Soulbound Rank increases and the effects of their Soulmark intensify.

As a Soulmark progresses, the negative mechanical properties of the feature continue to escalate — taking a dangerous and volatile toll on the character's body and mind as they struggle to resist the cosmic pull of Eternas.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Creating a Soulbound Character
>
> Players can optionally elect to be Soulbound immediately during character creation, in which case they begin their story under the effects of the[[Soulbound]].
>
> Assuming this burden will add difficulty and hardship for the character and for the party as a whole, so it should not be undertaken lightly. The only benefits from doing so are for narrative reasons in seeking a compelling or potentially tragic tale for your hero.

## Becoming Soulbound

If a player character dies during the course of gameplay, a conversation with the GM can illuminate their next steps, including whether or not the deceased character is destined to become Soulbound. Should that character decide to persist until their unfinished business is complete, they'll gain a [[Soulbound]] instead of remaining **Dead**.

Once Soulbound, such a character can die a total of two more times before their tether to the mortal world grows too insubstantial to sustain. With each death, the intensity of their Soulmark deepens.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Extreme Situations
>
> If the manner of a character's death is extreme to the degree that the character's body is no longer available (for instance, via disintegration or by falling into magma) they can't become Soulbound — their soul lacks a sufficient corporeal vessel to occupy. Otherwise, the Soulbound affliction can overcome even the most grievous of wounds (with extreme visual markers of their Soulmark to match).

## Final Outcomes

When a Soulbound character with a [[Deathly Soulmark]] experiences their final death, that character's soul is corrupted by loathsome forces beyond the fabric of reality — resulting in their grim and unholy transformation into an undead creature.

If a Soulbound character can find contentment by completing their unfinished business, they may relinquish their tenuous hold on mortality before meeting this terrible fate. At any time outside of an active combat encounter, the player of a Soulbound character may choose to end their journey, accepting their end and allowing their soul to rejoin the [[Soul Cycle]] and pass on.
