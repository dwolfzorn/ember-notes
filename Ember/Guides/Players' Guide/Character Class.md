# Character Class

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> All of the character classes supported by the Fifth Edition game system are compatible with and represented in Ember. For most of these classes and their subclasses, specific narrative considerations have been indicated to provide players and gamemasters alike with recommendations on how the creation of these characters can intersect with the unique lore and worldbuilding of the Ember setting.

Ember uses the standard classes from *Dungeons & Dragons Fifth Edition* for player character creation*.* The twelve core classes featured in the 2024 *Player's Handbook* are available to all players. Additionally, the Artificer class is only available if you have *Tasha's Cauldron of Everything* by Wizards of the Coast installed.

Each of these character classes has been carefully incorporated into the lore and setting of Ember, with various levels of adjustment for narrative and gameplay considerations. These changes are recommended for anyone who would like to immerse themselves more fully in the game world, but they are not required — the Ember experience should emphasize freedom of expression and personal choice among its ideals, beginning here during character creation.

Character progression for each of these classes in Ember follows the same rules as it does in any other *Dungeons & Dragons Fifth Edition (2024)* game.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Reviled Magic
>
> Some Necromancy spells within Ember are fueled by the eldritch magic of [[The Abyss]]. The accumulation and use of certain spells can have dramatic effects on gameplay, which should be considered before creating a spellcasting character. During character creation, we recommend you familiarize yourself with the concept of [[Magic and Spellcraft]] in Ember, and discuss your character concept and background with the Gamemaster.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Regarding Subclasses
>
> Because of Ember's unique setting and lore — including its novel ancestries, cultures, and history — a substantial amount of the text regarding the game's various subclasses has been reworked and rewritten to accommodate this brand new world. The character class pages throughout this journal feature a new description for each subclass that has been affected by Ember's setting and narrative, while a few subclasses remain relatively untouched. We encourage players and gamemasters alike to embrace the narrative opportunities these modified subclasses have to offer.
>
> These advised changes should be presented to players during character creation as a starting point to enhance their immersion within Ember's distinct narrative, but they aren't the only paths PCs can take during advancement. Ultimately, these tweaks to the original subclasses are optional, and players should be allowed to create the characters they want to portray the most.
