# Vistas

Throughout gameplay, you will be pulled into scenes called Vistas. Vistas serve as visual representations of a place on the [[Region Map]], usually focusing on a very particular part of that place, such as a small campsite, a building, or a street within a city. For characters present in the scene, Vistas include character portraits — art that depicts the characters from a first-person perspective, as if your player character is viewing those characters.

Every Vista in Ember is made using a complex set of Assets. These Assets are layered so that when you ***right-click and hold***, you can move the viewpoints around. Additionally, the layering of Assets and perspective-shifting in the Vista creates a parallax effect that gives an illusion of depth.

## First-Person Perspective

Vistas are designed to be first person, in which you are looking at the scene through your own eyes, with the viewpoint directly in front of your face. Every design — from how characters are portrayed to the Assets and objects — takes this into account, including the overall angular perspective.

## Character Portraits

Character portraits depict many of the wonderful and diverse **Non-Playable Characters** that are part of [[Quests]] and storylines in the Ember game. These character portraits are loaded automatically when an [[Event]] is started and a Vista scene is loaded.

Character portraits within a Vista can be moved around the scene by dragging them with a mouse click and hold, or by clicking on the character portrait to select it, then using the WASD keys to move it left, right, up, or down — up and down move the character portrait forward and backward in the Vista's layers, potentially shifting them in front of or behind preconfigured assets.

If you wish to add a character to the Vista, simply drag them from the Actors sidebar on the right. If a character has a portrait assigned, they will automatically appear in the Vista. By default, only a Gamemaster can add an Actor to a Vista, but the Gamemaster can allow players to do so by granting them the "Create Token" permission.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Adding Your Own Portraits.
>
> You may also insert your own custom character portraits into the game world. We do not make this process immediately obvious, as we believe that the barrier to creating artwork for custom Ember characters is high, and not every player will have access to such artwork. If you wish to use a piece of custom artwork and place it in a view, you need to ask your GM to add the artwork to their Assets folder, then assign the artwork to your character by editing your character sheet and clicking on the portrait. This will bring up a list of Assets you can select.
>
> #### Asset Overwrites Following Ember Updates
>
> Be careful when adding Assets through a folder, as — depending on the placement of the Assets in the folder structure — these Assets may be overwritten by future Ember updates.
