# Creation Overview

Character creation is one of the most important and enjoyable parts of any tabletop roleplaying experience. Choosing your starting attributes, [[Ancestry]], [[Ember Background]], and appearance are essential choices made at the beginning of the game that influence many aspects of gameplay as well as your character's persona.

Character creation in Ember is designed to be seamless, allowing you to select options from a one list after another, but with a few important alterations from the default process for the game system you are playing.

## Character Creation Process

To create a character in Ember, you will select:

| [[Ancestry]] which defines your character's biological heritage. |
| --- |
| [[Culture]] which identifies the culture of the world that your character came from. |
| [[Path]] which contributes to your backstory prior to the game's beginning. |
| [[Attunement]] which selects the cosmological entity your character has an affinity towards. |
| Talents that select your character's initial capabilities. |

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Character Appearance
>
> Finally, you will customize the appearance of your [[Dynamic Token]]which visually represents your character within the world.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Creating a Character
>
> You can spend time reading about all the available character options, but our recommendation is to simply enter the character creation interface and explore as you go.
>
> To create a new player character, click the **Create Actor** button at the top of the **Actors** sidebar tab. Created characters of the "Player Character" type will automatically enter the Character Creation workflow.
>
> #### Permissions Required
>
> If you do not have permission to perform this action, instead ask your **Gamemaster** to create an **Actor** on your behalf and assign it to you as an **Owner**.

## Character Connections

During or after the Character Creation process, each player should decide upon their character's reason for traveling with the Strayhearth Caravan and whether or not they have any pre-existing relationship with the other players. It's not required for the player characters to know each other already — in fact, our writing assumes that they are strangers who have met upon the road.

The Strayhearth Caravan consists of three massive six-wheeled wagons, the huge [[Yarnac]] that serve as beasts of burden, the caravaner [[Agraband Swift]] who owns and operates the enterprise, the Signborn merchant [[Clipper]], and a number of [[Arcturian]] along for the ride (including merchants, passengers, and the like).

Members of the Strayhearth Caravan have already been traveling together for several days, so it can be assumed that each of the player characters have already been introduced and know each other's names and likenesses. You may find it creates less pressure for the group by encouraging them to share character names and appearances out-of-character before the beginning of play; this way, everyone is already acquainted when roleplaying begins in earnest.

Choices of [[Cultures]], [[Path]], [[Ancestries]], and [[Attunement]] can inform these decisions, but you should feel free to craft a unique backstory with connections that speak directly to the character you want to portray. For instance, your character could be an [[Arcturian]] [[Caravanner]] on a journey back to [[Ordain]], or even a [[Tayan]] [[Star Dreamer]] from far-off [[Caren'ac]] — the sky's the limit.

> [!danger] Hazard
> Once you have a rough idea for a backstory, your Character Sheet includes a Biography tab where you can write down notes or entire paragraphs of text.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### It's Your Story
>
> Please don't feel forced into making immediate backstory decisions! If you want to spend a little more time in-game trying to figure out the specifics of these inter-character connections, by all means do so. There will be plenty of time to add elements to your character's backstory while you explore the Arctus Plateau and get to know the setting more.
