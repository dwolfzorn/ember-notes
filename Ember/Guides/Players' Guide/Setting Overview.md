# Setting Overview

Ember is an original imaginative fantasy setting for online roleplaying using Foundry Virtual Tabletop. Ember is vast, diverse, and self-contained. There are no alternative planes or parallel dimensions — the Cosmos of Ember and its World are the entirety of the game's universe. Choices here matter and can have major consequences that may not be recognizable at first.

## The Ember Setting

The Ember Cosmos is a singular bubble of reality, meaning every location is accessible by travel or teleportation, from the moons in the sky, to the Spiritlands, to the Inner Realms on the surface of the Weave — which protects everything within it from the alien evils from without. This means Ember is not part of some vast multiverse; it is the only plane or dimension in existence. In the center of this bubble is Eiru, known as the World of Ember, home to a host of diverse cultures and distinct ancestries living upon its surface. Deep inside lies the Heart of Ember, a mysterious entity shaping the Cosmos.

Across Eiru, hundreds of powerful ascendant deities, called Shard Gods, influence events — some ancient, others new, but each is a complex, fallible character with their own goals and ambitions. In addition, five Elder Gods maintain Cosmic Balance and often intervene in mortal affairs. History spans beyond memory, with civilizations dating back hundreds of thousands of years, blending myth with truth, and the world suffers from ancient mistakes and cataclysms made by cultures that came before. The world features colossal structures of crumbling empires, vast wilds of untamed nature, and cities of towering stone. Above, glittering moons drift across the sky. Yet beauty exists alongside horror. A cataclysmic event known as the Shattering allowed the maddening influence from beyond the Weave to flow freely into the world. New terrors emerged, such as Undeath, Oblivion, and Dragons, ravaging the cosmos and reshaping mortal and divine pantheons alike. With all of these cosmic influences, magic permeates every corner of the world. It can be as subtle as drifting motes in the air or as forceful as the elemental energies channeled from the moons.

Ember is a setting of immense scale and wonder, a place where gods walk, magic breathes, and history stretches beyond comprehension, a setting designed to reward exploration, imagination, and storytelling on a truly epic scale.

## Setting Compendium

The Ember Setting Compendium is extensive and offers a wealth of information for those who wish to explore the world in greater depth, whether for character creation and developing a backstory, or for Gamemasters who want to understand every detail and nuance of the lore. That said, the content in the Setting Compendium is entirely optional and not required to play Ember. It provides a rich and detailed foundation for the *Ember: A Shard of Fear* storyline and potential future content, but players are welcome to enjoy the game without reading a single page. The choice to delve deeper is yours, and the world is designed to be fully playable regardless.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Spoilers & General Information
>
> The Setting Compendium is organized to provide different levels of information depending on the reader’s role and purpose. Content is categorized into two tiers of visibility: Player-Facing, and Gamemaster-Facing.
>
> **Player** sections are always visible and contain information that is generally known by most, if not all, inhabitants of Ember. This includes cultural norms, geography, major historical events, and widely recognized lore. Players are encouraged to explore these sections to understand the world their characters inhabit, without encountering spoilers for hidden plotlines or advanced mysteries.
>
> **Gamemaster** sections are expanded to contain more specialized information that is only known to select individuals (such as scholars, experts, or key figures within the setting). These sections provide deeper context, hidden histories, and the inner workings of various factions, organizations, and deities. Access to this material allows Gamemasters to craft richer narratives, foreshadow events, and present secrets to players in organic ways.
