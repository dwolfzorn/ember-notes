# Welcome to Early Access

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Hello, and welcome everyone to Early Access! This is an incredibly exciting milestone for the Ember team as we open the doors to a wider audience, and we're thrilled that more of our wonderful backers and supporters can now experience our game. Since launching Ember with Alpha and Beta testers, we've made significant progress—adding entire systems, questlines, hundreds of actors and items, maps, and artwork. Despite this progress, Ember still has a long journey ahead and is far from finished, but if you're looking for a truly unique and one-of-a-kind roleplay experience with your group, welcome!

## About Early Access

The Ember team is creating the game through an open development process, which allows you, our supporters and backers, to see and experience an early version of the game while it is still under construction. Traditionally, Early Access denotes a project that is playable but still far from finished, which is exactly where we are with Ember. Almost every cornerstone feature of Ember is implemented in some form, but some features are still partially underdeveloped. Furthermore, significant portions of the game content are not yet designed or rigorously play-tested.

#### Under Construction

Please remember that the overall Ember Early Access experience is a work in progress. You will almost certainly encounter bugs, missing features, or content, and while we hope these deficiencies will not ruin your experience, it is important to expect a certain level of "Jankiness" compared to the final product.

## Early Access Rules

When you play Ember, you'll likely have many comments and thoughts about the storylines, mechanics, balancing, or even your pure enjoyment of the experience. We would love to hear your impressions. Here are a few guidelines we ask you to follow:

- You are free to invite players who don't have Ember Early Access to join your game.
- You are free to talk about Ember Early Access; there is no NDA.
- You are free to post screenshots, make videos, or livestream your experience.
- If sharing content about Ember Early Access, please remind your audience that what they are seeing is still an early stage of development! If your players discover bugs or issues, please encourage them to report or share their feedback with the team!

## Early Access Goals

As we move from Beta to Early Access, our goals remain the same with a slight shift in focus. We are very close to implementing all of our major gameplay and software systems. As of the start of Early Access, most of these systems are close to complete, while others are still in their infancy. We are deeply committed to finishing these systems as quickly as possible over the next few patches.

Furthermore, over the course of Early Access, we will be adding new game content, refining existing content with balance adjustments or clarifications, and expanding support for the Crucible system by converting more of our gameplay to be Crucible-enabled.

Our goal is to complete our remaining systems first, then shift focus to content production for the latter half of the game. We plan to launch Ember once this content is finished, although we anticipate ongoing creation and refinement even after the launch.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Should I Play Ember Now?
>
> If you are debating whether to begin an Ember campaign now or to wait for the game to be more complete, you should consider the following guidance:
>
> Early Access is playable and fun! But the game is not yet done, and the game experience that you will have playing Ember at this stage will be inferior to the quality and scope of what the final game will deliver. If you would rather play a fully polished and playtested experience, then you should wait.
>
> However, if you or your group want to experience the game now and are interested and excited to help with the playtesting process — assisting us to make the game better for future groups, then jump in, familiarize yourself with the content, and remember to take notes for feedback!
