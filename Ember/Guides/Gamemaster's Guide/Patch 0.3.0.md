# Patch 0.3.0

Welcome to an Ember content release as part of our ongoing **Alpha Two** test process. This update focuses on the city of Ordain and support for the Crucible game system.

#### Compatibility

Ember patch `0.3.0` requires core Foundry VTT version `13.347` or higher and either:

- D&D5E system version `5.1.8` or higher.
- Crucible system version `0.7.8` or higher.

## Welcome to Alpha Two!

With this patch, we are now in the "Alpha Two" phase of Ember's development. In practice, this isn't that different from Alpha One, it just means we are marginally closer to where we need to be in order to welcome our Kickstarter beta backers.

### Updated Timelines

**Beta One** will begin once we have completed the entirety of Chapter 3 content and added some of the other foundational game systems that are necessary for Ember gameplay. We are anticipating that we can begin Beta One sometime in **October** (initially stated as September).

After our Kickstarter backers have had a period of exclusive Beta access, we will begin selling optional paid beta test access upgrades for Kickstarter backers or other pre-purchasers who do not have Beta access. This period of buy-in Beta access will likely begin in late **November** around which time our team will be at **PAX Unplugged** in Philadelphia showing off Ember to the masses!

## Chapter 3

The pinnacle feature of this big update is the addition of four main quests which form the heart of Chapter 3 gameplay. These four quests are summarized below. These quests are implemented (for now) with support for D&D5E play, with Crucible support for them coming later in our development process. Our team is incredibly proud of what we've put together with this section of gameplay and we hope you love playing these incredible quests as much as our team loved creating them.

### The Expedition Challenge

Shortly after the party reaches the glittering city of Ordain and begins exploring the metropolis, they are approached by the famous Anachraenum adventurer Fernis Ossa, who has been keeping track of their exploits in the Arctus Plateau. Fernis is impressed by the party's skills and convictions, but more importantly, she has a task and request for the party that no one else in the city can complete.

Fernis reveals that two years ago, the Guildmaster of the Anachraenum died under mysterious circumstances. Since that time, changes among the Anachraenum organization have left Fernis feeling troubled. To discover what happened, she needs outsiders to join the Anachraenum and help her. A timely opportunity to do this presents itself in the form of the upcoming "Expedition Challenge", a spectacular celebration and test for aspiring Anachraenum members to prove their adventuring mettle. In exchange for their assistance, Fernis offers the party access to her former stronghold called the Concourse Hideout. This reward does not come freely, however. The Concourse has recently become overrun by the "Toothbreakers", a violent criminal organization that now controls that waterfront district.

In "The Expedition Challenge", the party learns of Fernis' suspicions, aids the famous adventurer by reclaiming the Concourse Hideout, and enters the Expedition Challenge to compete alongside rival teams of Anachraenum aspirants. Should the party prevail and prove victorious in that challenge they will forge their names in history alongside the Anachraenum's most famous members.

### Disgraced House

In the wake of her uncle Darius' murder, the disappearance of her brother Funar, and the expulsion of Trading House Cevher from the Ordinate, Lyla Cevher is determined to right the wrongs done to her family and repair the damage to House Cevher's good name. But she can't do it alone. Lyla reaches out to your party for help - to investigate the murder, track down her brother's whereabouts, and find who or what is behind the assault on all things Cevher.

After investigating the scene of the crime, tracking Funar Cevher's current whereabouts, and learning more about the head of a rival Trading House, the place to help Lyla accomplish all her goals is clear- the annual Marlstone Gala, an event where the who's who of Ordain comes out to see and be seen. Together with Lyla and any allies they can recruit, the party will have to pull off a heist, right underneath their enemy's nose.

No heist is easy, especially not under the bright lights of Ordain - the party must scout the location, find a way into the party, use the glitz and glamour as a cover to move throughout the massive Marlstone Manor, and make their way into its hidden depths and their incredible dangers. Along the way, they can uncover not only what is truly happening to House Cevher, but also what lies beneath the glittering surface of one of Ordain's most exclusive nights.

### Spreading Sickness

During their time in Ordain, the party is duly summoned to the Hallows to once again join forces with Ankarist of the Veiled Chain. Their mission: to investigate a new and dangerous sickness that has appeared in the city. This situation is of critical importance, and the characters must learn of a new, dangerous sickness that has appeared in Ordain. Already spread thin due to other issues in the city, the Veiled Chain now relies on the help of the party to make the difference where they can't. However, this problem is not as simple as it seems, and old enemies lurk in dark, forgotten places within Ordain.

Vetted by Ankarist and proven by their previous actions, the party is given the autonomy to conduct their investigation as they see fit. The Chain is stretched thin, and Ankarist will conduct his own investigation while the party chases down their leads. Will the party make the right choices? Can they find every clue needed to stop the plots of foes hidden in Ordain’s alleys and parlors, or will they find themselves in over their heads?

In “Spreading Sickness,” the party is drawn into a troubling web of plots meant to destabilize and destroy Ordain from within. As their investigation unfolds, they begin to uncover a deeper conspiracy that seeks to manipulate, kidnap, and turn the people of Ordain against each other. Allies are few, support is thin, and danger grows in every shadow and alley as the party draws closer to the source the spreading sickness.

### Smoldering Cinders

Strange things are afoot in the great city of Ordain, and it's up to the party to find out precisely why … Shortly after their arrival to the Arctus Plateau's storied metropolis, the characters join their ally Sin Marmot at Cindarin Temple to witness the eager druid's timely initiation into the order of Cindaric Sages. It is a triumphant day for the adventurers — full of pomp, celebration, and warm welcomes. And while Sin is ushered into her first official assignment at the Old City Library, the characters are invited to explore Ordain on their own, where new opportunities await discovery within the city's magnificent urban sprawl.

A few days later, however, the characters will receive a distressed message from one of the Cindarics that sets them on a collision course with one of the most devious plots the Plateau has ever reckoned with. Two separate encounters with the living dead in two disparate city districts will propel Sin Marmot and the party on a mission to unravel a mystery that lurks at the heart of her beloved order of Cindaric Sages. Along the way, they'll meet priests, gods, and monsters the likes of which they've never seen.

What begins with the druid's initiation into the Cindaric order ends in a perilous confrontation with a surreptitious enemy from a foreign land. During the course of this adventure, the party will learn about the Cindaric Sages and their tenets, and will quickly find themselves at the forefront of Sin's struggles as a novice Cindaric. Do the characters have what it takes to aid the young druid in her most desperate time of need and halt the rise of this furtive undead menace? The party's timely intervention will be crucial to stem the tide of unforeseen change within the ranks of Ordain's most trusted sages.

## New Features

In addition to these massive content additions, the 0.3.0 update has a bunch of new features to note:

### Dynamic Tokens

- When placing a new Token which has a random dynamic token configuration into a Scene, pre-randomize the appearance of that Token so that all players experience the same appearance for each Token in the Scene. This includes the tokens placed when a gameplay event is spawned, so now you and all your players will see the same looking characters rather than seeing aesthetically different randomizations.
- Human-type hairstlyes (used by Human, Drakon, Altyra, Fej, Kivahr, Nir'ae, and Signborn) now have two-tone color combinations allowing for a highlight color in addition to the hair's base color.
- The animation framework for the dynamic token has been optimized for better performance across a variety of performance modes. This makes including a large number of dynamic tokens in your scenes more realistic from a performance perspective.
- Add a framework for the Dynamic Token Maker to migrate preexisting dynamic token data for part name changes or other alterations which require data compatibility.

### Event System

- Configured encounter spawn groups can now condition on specific event outcomes for more fine-grained control over how an event spawns.

### D&D Integration

- Improved resting integration with the new D&D party sheet added in system version 5.2.0.
- Automatically configure some D&D system settings on first Ember import, like using milestone progression or requiring party rest rather than individual character rest.
- Integrate Ember's travel paces into the D&D party sheet, now allowing each caravan to choose between Hidden, Slow, Normal, Fast, and Reckless paces - which have impacts on your group's movement speed but also the probability of certain events happening!

### Crucible Integration

- Dynamic tokens for small ancestries like Thornling and Keth are now upscaled in Crucible to compensate for the size of their mechanical hitbox (3x3) being smaller than a medium sized creature.

### Codex

- Complete creature and character biographies are now visible to gamemasters in the Codex instead of only the public player-facing summaries. This allows GMs to use the Codex as a reading and review tool to learn about all the creatures and characters in Ember.
- Improve the Codex to present Gamemasters with full text details for Biomes and Locations in the Discoveries tab.
- Improve the layout of the Codex to ensure that the central panel is scrollable to support long-form text.

### Content Creation

- Manually placed AmbientLight documents now automatically link to the closest parallax layer based on their configured elevation!
- Improve the workflow for replacing static Actor portrait and prototype token texture files when an Ember Dynamic Token is updated.

### UI Changes

- Eliminate the EmberEventsControl application (the event prompt window at the bottom of the screen) in favor of folding discovery and event controls into the EmberCalendarNavigation application (the Ember-specific calendar and timekeeping UI element at the top of the screen).

## Bug Fixes

This update also fixes a number of bugs:

- Blood Sense (Crucible) now correctly removes the banes that were otherwise accrued from the blinded condition.
- When spawning an event that automatically creates a combat encounter, ensure that the actorId of each Combatant is populated so that players have permission to roll their own initiative.
- The Fulgurite Blades were not automatically spawned in the Lunar Awakenings scene (now they are).
- Fix an audio bug with compositions configured to possibly use zero layers which prevented zero from ever being chosen.
- Fix bug related to parallax transformation of a light source that had no background mesh.
- When initializing the Ember game make sure that all players have Ownership of the Party actor.
- Fixed an issue which occured when closing and then re-opening character creation on the token configuration tab.
- Fix bug which prevented the Codex from rendering properly in Crucible due to differences in its Actor data model.
