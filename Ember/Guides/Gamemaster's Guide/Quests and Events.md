# Quests and Events

Gameplay in Ember happens through **Events** — prewritten plot moments that involve exploration, social, or combat challenges. Events come in two types:

**Quest Events**

Events that take place as part of a **Quest**. These Events are individual moments in an overarching storyline.

**Standalone Events**

Events that provide one-off moments of gameplay that may reference Quests, but are not specifically part of their narrative.

Events are the primary way you engage with the world of Ember and its adventures. They provide challenges and potential hardship, but also opportunities for advancement and rewards in the form of [[Milestone Progression]], [[Attunement]], items, equipment, and more.

## The Event Engine

Events occur in response to a sophisticated probabilistic model which determines the probability of any given Event occuring at a given time and in a given [[Region Exploration]]— and, in the case of Quest Events, any necessary prerequisites.

Events can be triggered one of three ways:

1. When the party remains in its current location for one hour or longer, based on the relative probabilities of possible Events in the currently occupied hex.
2. When the party completes a move into a new hex, based on the relative probabilities of possible Events in the destination hex.
3. A Gamemaster may choose to manually initiate an Event regardless of its probability or prerequisites.

When an Event is selected to occur, that selection takes the form of a *recommendation* to the Gamemaster. The Gamemaster has the ability to accept that recommendation and begin the Event, to decline it in which case no Event occurs, or to request an alternative suggestion which proposes a different Event from the set of possibilities. While you won't have to worry about any of this as a player, it can be helpful to understand what's happening behind the scenes — discovering and initiating Events is key to progressing in Ember.

In particular, Quest Events may award [[Milestone Progression]] which are necessary for player characters in Ember to increase their Level, while any Event may grant [[Attunement]].

## Quests and Milestones

Quests in Ember are overarching narratives that connect multiple Events to tell a complex story. Quests are long sections of content — each Quest is likely to require between four to eight game sessions to complete. Completing Quests earns [[Milestone Progression]] to advance the Level of player characters, so it is advisable for you to actively seek out quests and endeavor to complete them.

There are two categories of quests in Ember:

1. **Main Quests**, which tell the central story of our campaign, *A* *Shard of Fear*. Main Quests typically award 2 Milestone Points each — 1 Milestone Point near the middle of the quest and the other near its conclusion.
2. **Side Quests**, which provide supporting narrative to enrich exploration of the world. Side Quests typically award 1 Milestone Point near the conclusion of the Quest.

## Discovering Quests

The first Main Quest of the game is called "The Winding Trail." It commences automatically at the beginning of the game. Other Quests have to be sought out by exploring the world and visiting new Locations. You may also encounter rumors that point the way to places where Quests can be encountered.

Quests are intentionally designed to be easy to find, so don't fret that you are missing them by failing to investigate subtle clues. Every Quest has multiple on-ramps that lead to the beginning of its narrative.

## Progressing Quests

As you experience Quests, a record of the Quest's Events is recorded in the [[The Codex]] in two ways — chronologically on the **Journal** tab and organized by Quest in the **Quest Log** tab.

It's important to note that quests in Ember are intentionally never marked as "complete." There are often downstream consequences of the decisions you make in a Quest, and we purposefully do not reveal whether a particular storyline is ever truly over.
