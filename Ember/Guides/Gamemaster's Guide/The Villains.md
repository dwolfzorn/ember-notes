# The Villains

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Significant Spoilers
>
> This page contains **significant spoilers** for the plot of Ember, *A Shard of Fear*. We *recommend* for a Gamemaster to read it so that you have an understanding of the primary villains of the campaign and their motivations.
>
> Some Gamemasters, however, may prefer not to have this full knowledge — so proceed **only if you wish to have all the available information** up-front instead of learning things as you play.

## Zerranyss

The [[Vile Dragons]] known as [[Zerranyss]] is the principal antagonist and central villain of our story. Zerranyss is motivated above all else by a cruel and selfish desire to accumulate vast power, which he would use to establish a malicious dominion over Ember and its denizens.

Zerranyss also seeks revenge against the [[Shard Gods]] [[Lumé]], who once nearly killed the Vile Dragon in a battle at the frontier of the [[Lumek]] Homelands. After being defeated and disgraced in this way, Zerranyss has grown more disdainful and cruel, using bitter resentment against Lumé and others to drive them down the path of conquest. Zerranyss is a cunning and conniving villain, forming and orchestrating a complex strategy to undermine and subvert power in [[Ordain]] — one of the great cities of the world and most importantly an access point to the underground Shard Caverns wherein Zerranyss believes a [[Ascendancy]] of Ember may be found. Such a crystal would allow Zerranyss to perform an ascension ritual which — if successful — would imbue the formidable dragon with the untold power of a Shard God.

Zerranyss is first encountered in his human guise as [[Vinarith]] during Chapter 3. Vinarith does not come across as a villain; in fact, he could be roleplayed as an earnest and hardworking man. While it's important that the characters don't immediately suspect Vinarith as a villain, it's nearly impossible for the party to understand just how critical this character is in the grand scheme of the overarching story. So some suspicion of his actions is perfectly acceptable if they question some of his actions.

## A Complex Strategy

The player characters enter the story at a point where [[Zerranyss]] has already been hard at work, carefully constructing a strategy that would see him triumph over his enemies. Each of the schemes in this elaborate plot plays some particular role in the plan to usurp Ordain and the surrounding region. Zerranyss has several primary goals:

1. Seize control over the Great City of [[Ordain]], its resources, power, and influence, and gain unrestricted access to the Shard Caverns below.
2. Locate an elusive [[Ascendancy]] within the vast Shard Caverns by capturing and enslaving mortals and forcing them to mine the Pathways beneath the city.
3. Obtain secret knowledge on how to undertake what Zerranyss calls "the Ritual of Ascension," by procuring knowledge from other beings who possess an extensive understanding of the process.
4. Grow in strength to withstand the traumatic and deadly Ritual using experimentation and the formulation of the Mutagen by his [[Mutagists]] followers.

## The Inner Circle

These nefarious machinations are overseen by a cadre of loyal lieutenants pledged to serve Zerranyss. The most impactful of which are listed below, but others willingly serve the dragon or are keen to exploit the chaos his plans will bring about.

- [[Serethus]], an ingenious alchemist and leader of the nefarious [[Mutagists]].
- [[Amelia Naxan]], a Blood Baron necromancer and fellow enemy of [[Lumé]].
- [[Pyix]], an inscrutable Tyraphem mage from Luxarum.
- [[Sticks]], the roguish figurehead of the mysterious Otherhood of Fortune.
- [[Steros Kraver]], the stoic commander of the Burnished Hand, who Zerranyss will attempt to corrupt throughout the campaign's events.

## Controlling the Ordinate

Gaining control of Ordain is a complex challenge, because the city is governed by a triarchy of organizations called the [[Ordinate]], which is comprised of representatives from the [[Cindaric Sages]], the [[Hallows]], and the Combined Trading Houses comprised of [[House Bastilla]], [[House Cevher]], [[House Lilifeld]], [[House Salva]] and [[House Wandren]]. Controlling Ordain requires controlling the Ordinate — or a sufficient majority of it to wrest control from any remaining opposition. Zerranyss' strategy to control the Ordinate involves the following crucial steps:

1. Gain a seat on the [[Ordinate]] personally under the guise of [[Vinarith]], a leading member of the Cindaric Sages. At the start of the story, under his disguise as Vinarith, Zerranyss has already taken control over this seat, taking the place of the elderly current Holy Speaker of the Sages who is too ill to leave the Cindaric Temple.
2. Secure another of the [[Cindaric Sages]] seats through the corruption of [[Steros Kraver]], commander of the [[Burnished Hand]]. At the beginning of the story, this has not yet been accomplished, but as events unfold, it is virtually inevitable, as Steros is, unfortunately, highly susceptible to manipulation.
3. Destabilize the Trading Houses by provoking infighting among the houses through lies, manipulation, and outright violence. Zerranyss uses the Tyraphem [[Pyix]] to gain control over [[House Wandren]], while using [[Sticks]] and the [[Otherhood of Fortune]] to gain leverage over [[House Bastilla]]. As the story unfolds, Pyix manages to implicate [[House Cevher]] in a grand conspiracy that causes chaos among the trading houses.
4. Use [[Serethus]] to misinform and misdirect among the [[Veiled Chain]] to prevent law enforcement from interrupting or thwarting the scheme and keep the [[Hallows]] distracted.
