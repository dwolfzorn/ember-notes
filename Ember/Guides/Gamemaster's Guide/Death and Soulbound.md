# Death and Soulbound

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Soulbound are mortals whose souls cannot or will not move on after death. Soulbound characters can hail from any ancestry, culture, or walk of life, but they are all united by a common element: every Soulbound has some Unfinished Business that tethers the living essence of their soul to their unliving mortal form. Unlike the undead, who are corrupted or twisted by evil forces, these souls simply defy the constant pull towards the city Eternas and its Rite of Passage until they have completed their final tasks.

When a player character dies in Ember, they're presented with an existential choice: leave the mortal world behind (resulting in the creation of a new character), or remain tethered to their body by becoming [[Soulbound]]. Becoming Soulbound grants them a Soulmark, binding their souls to the mortal plane until their unfinished business is resolved.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Limited Player Knowledge
>
> Players are presented with information about this mechanic on the [[Death and Soulbound]] page of the Players' Guide. You should review that page before reading this one, which presents additional information about Soulbound that are intended only for the Gamemaster.
>
> #### Soulbound Progression Macro
>
> The [[Soulbound Progression]] macro is provided to conveniently automate the initial Soulbound application and rank advancement.

## Becoming Soulbound

Whenever a player character dies, treat them as **Dead** for the remaining duration of the encounter that resulted in their death. Upon conclusion of the encounter, we recommend you privately confer with the player whose character has died. Remind them of the [[Soulbound]] feature and ask them to make a choice about whether they want their character's journey to continue as Soulbound or whether they'd like this death to be final. Players should clearly understand that choosing to become Soulbound introduces a suite of mechanical disadvantages or penalties that will challenge them as an individual (and ultimately the party as a whole).

- If the player chooses to let their character continue as Soulbound, wait until at least one hour of in-world time has passed, then add the [[Soulbound]] talent to their character sheet and revive the character with half their maximum [[Character Mechanics]] restored.
- If the player chooses to let their character die, the character's soul departs — beckoned by the preternatural call of the [[Soul Cycle]] — and they can only be restored to life by [[Magic and Spellcraft]] magic that subverts this natural cycle.

Repeat this process if and/or when a Soulbound character dies again, replacing their Soulmark with the next appropriate rank of the talent from the Ember Character Options compendium pack. However, please note: characters who possesses a **Greater Soulmark** have reached the end of their adventure, and become an undead creature instead of returning to life (see Hostile Soulbound Characters below).

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Extreme Situations
>
> If the manner of a character's death is extreme to the degree that the character's body is no longer available (for instance, via disintegration or by falling into magma) they can't become Soulbound — their soul lacks a sufficient corporeal vessel to occupy. Otherwise, the Soulbound affliction can overcome even the most grievous of wounds (with extreme visual markers of their Soulmark to match).
>
> If the entire party is killed and some players choose to become Soulbound, we recommend devising some way of reviving them outside of the immediate danger that resulted in their deaths.

## Non-Player Characters

The [[Unfinished Business]] Chapter 3 side quest, which features the return of [[Agraband Swift]] of the [[Strayhearth Caravan]], is intended to introduce player characters to the concept and mechanics of the [[Soulbound]] affliction. And while Agraband's stat block and gameplay during the course of the quest are modeled after those of a player character, it is entirely possible for the party to encounter other Soulbound characters throughout the course of the campaign — whether these NPCs possess a Soulmark before the party ever meets them, or if they happen to somehow become Soulbound because of the party's actions.

How might a NPC become Soulbound in your campaign, and when? These are questions you'll ultimately need to answer for yourself, but a few situations where nonplayer characters might receive a Soulmark include (but are not limited to):

- An ally of the party has some unfinished cosmic business they must settle, which may or may not directly involve the PCs themselves.
- A denizen of Ember is marked by destiny for some greater purpose, which impacts the lives of the party or the people around them.
- A powerful or noteworthy enemy identifies one or more of the party members as a nemesis, with a desire for cosmic vengeance.
- Countless other methods, limited only by your discretion and imagination.

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Letting the Dice Tell the Story
>
> If you'd prefer to let the dice determine whether or not a non-player character becomes Soulbound when they die, you can do so randomly using the following table. Soulbound are always rare, but you can calibrate the extent of this rarity to suit your narrative goals.

| Soulbound Probability | Roll | Required Result | Probability |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Low | `[[/gmroll 3d20]]` | 3 | 0.0125% |
| Medium | `[[/gmroll 2d20]]` | 2 | 0.25% |
| High | `[[/gmroll 1d20]]` | 1 | 5% |

## Hostile Soulbound

Soulbound characters aren't guaranteed to be allies of the PCs. Perhaps you want to extend the life of a villain the players have taken a particular liking to; maybe it's just a matter of encounter balance, or narrative convenience, or the unpredictable thrill of the dice.

There are essentially two categories of hostile Soulbound NPCs: enemies who already exist in the world as Soulbound characters (with one of the three ranked Soulmarks), or enemies who obtain a Lesser Soulmark during the course of gameplay (via combat or otherwise). It's important to keep in mind, however, that Soulbound characters are exceedingly rare on Ember — if desired, be sure to introduce them to your campaign with a measure of caution and care.

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### The Final Death
>
> Some encounters with Soulbound NPCs will almost always result in hostility: when a nonplayer Soulbound character with a [[Deathly Soulmark]] dies, that character becomes a malicious undead creature, and will attack any living creatures in the vicinity while preserving the health of its new loathsome form.
>
> The specific type of undead creature the Soulbound character becomes depends on the character's total character level at the time of their death (or Challenge Rating, in the case of a NPC). Consult the table below for a list of appropriate undead creatures, or choose one that is appropriate for your encounter.

| Character Level or Challenge Rating | Undead Creature |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | [[Skallith]] |
| 2 | [[Skallith Warrior]] |
| 3-4 | [[Corrupted Cadrithor]] |
| 5+ | [[Vampyre Spawn]] |
