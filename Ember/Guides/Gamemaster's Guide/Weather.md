# Weather

While exploring the Region Map, you will encounter random Weather Formations affecting a group of hexes. Weather Formations modify the gameplay experience, and change their shape and position over time. Examples of common Weather Formations are listed below, although there are other more rare Weather Formations. Each Weather Formation has the following attributes:

**Type**

The type of event, such as Rain, Fog, Dust Storm, Wildfire, or others. Different types of Weather Formations may have different mechanical effects or impact on Event probabilities. On the Weather Overlay and its corresponding legend, different types of Weather Formation are presented using different colors.

**Strength**

Weather Formations have multiple strength tiers and generally are the strongest at their center with gradually tapering strength as you move towards the outer fringes of the event area. On the Weather Overlay, the size of round dots which visualize the Weather Formation represent its strength. For example, for the Rain Weather Formation, the weakest strength is a Drizzle while the greatest strength is a Tempest

**Size**

Weather Formations have a certain size specified in miles as a radius from their origin. The size of a Weather Formation varies over its lifetime. Some Weather Formations grow in size while others shrink.

**Speed**

Most Weather Formations move across the region according to the global Wind speed, though Weather Formations generally move somewhat slower than this base wind speed. The speed of a Weather Formation is a factor which adjusts how much slower it moves relative to the prevailing Wind speed.

The Weather Overlay provides a visualization tool that can allow you to understand the current weather mechanics that you are experiencing. The Weather Overlay can be toggled on or off by clicking the ​​ icon in the Scene Controls interface element found on the left side of the screen. While this overlay is active, it displays a legend of Weather Formations which are currently active within the Region Map. Each Weather Formation is color-coded on the Region Map with dark lines that separate attenuated strength levels within that Weather Formation area.

## Gamemaster Information

As the Gamemaster, you have access to controls in the Weather Overlay ​​:

**Clear**

Clear all current Weather Formations from the region.

**Create**

Create a new Weather Formation of a specified type, strength, and size in a desired location.

**## Weather Hazards**

**Pollen Storm**

Any area confronted with a pollen storm is subject to the following mechanical considerations:

- The area is &Reference[Lightly Obscured].
- Ranged attacks have **-2 Banes** when the target is 100 or more feet away.
- Characters who don't succeed on the requisite Saving Throws are subject to the hazardous effects of the pollen itself (see "Pollen Contamination" below for details).

> [!danger] Hazard
> #### Pollen Contamination
>
> Characters who inhale or make orificial contact with storm pollen must succeed on a **`[[/skill constitution 16]]`** check to withstand its effect, which includes the following based on the result:
>
> - The character has **-2 Banes** on Intelligence Skill Checks and Saving Throws.
> - **Result of 6-**: The character is &Reference[Poisoned].
>
> The negative effects of the pollen last for a duration of `[[/roll 2d4 hours]]` and can be cured by taking a &Reference[Long Rest] or by targeting the afflicted character with either the [[Lesser Restoration]] or [[Protection from Poison]] spells.
>
> A character can only be affected by this pollen contamination once per day.
