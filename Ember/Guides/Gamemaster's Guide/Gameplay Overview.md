# Gameplay Overview

Ember contains a number of unique mechanics that are added on top of the game system used to play it. The most important of those mechanics are summarized below, then expanded upon in the following pages.

## Gameplay Pillars

Like most tabletop roleplaying games, the fundamental experience of Ember is one in which you are presented with a situation, and asked, as your character: *what do you do?*

Ember's gameplay emphasizes three pillars: exploration, social interaction, and combat. The exploration pillar is the gateway by which the rest of Ember's gameplay is experienced. We have designed the World of Ember to be immersive and inviting, and your exploration of it will uniquely determine what story you experience.

Ember's core gameplay loop is:

1. In order to discover Ember's story, accomplish your party's goals, and advance in power, you must explore the world by moving your party around Ember's massive hex-based **Region Map**.
2. Whenever you move your party or otherwise pass time in the game world, there is a chance that this will trigger an **Event** — a specific challenge or narrative moment in Ember’s story, typically focused on one of the three pillars of exploration, social interaction, or combat.
3. As you complete Events, you will discover important people and places, and uncover long-term mysteries.
4. Completing Events (and the **Quests** that many Events belong to) will cause the story and world to evolve and your characters to increase in Level, opening doors to new gameplay.
5. Events and their outcomes will be recorded in your Codex, leaving you with clues to pursue to continue your journey.

This loop repeats over and over, with hundreds of hours of prewritten encounters, narrative moments, and lore to discover.

The Events themselves are not just the written story moments you might expect from a prewritten adventure, but also illustrated and interactive pieces of on-screen gameplay, unique to Ember's deep integration with Foundry. Events might include, among other features:

- [[Area Maps]] which are top-down maps that you can explore and interact with as your individual character.
- [[Vistas]] which are front-facing backdrops upon which characters can move about and talk to other characters.
- Other illustrated (and even sometimes animated) creatures, characters, and environments.

Below, we'll learn a bit more about how Events are encountered through the process of exploration, and how they impact both your character and the World of Ember at large.

## Region Exploration

One of the most important decisions your party will regularly make is where to travel on the **Region Map,** which can be viewed by clicking the ​​ icon at the top of the screen.

Your position on this map — made up of over ten thousand hexes across two vertical slices — impacts who you can talk to, where you can go next, and most importantly, what Events you might encounter.

**Hexes and Fog of War**

The position of your party on this map is depicted by a moveable Token with a customizable banner. While you can always see the hex that your party is in, many other hexes begin the campaign shrouded by **Fog of War**, which obscures the contents of that hex until your party moves close enough to see it. The **terrain** and **elevation** of a hex can affect both how far you can see, and the speed and danger of movement through that hex.

Many hexes have an associated **Location** to explore, or a recommended Area Map or Vista to use for scenes that take place in that hex.

The [[Region Exploration]] page of this guide has further details on the information above. While your Gamemaster and the Ember game itself will handle most of the details, when making decisions about movement on the Region Map, it can be useful for to fully understand what's on the map, how to interact with it, and other mechanical implications of your movement through it.

## Quests and Events

Gameplay in Ember happens through **Events** — prewritten plot moments that involve exploration, social, or combat challenges. Events are broadly categorized as one of two types: Quest Events and Standalone Events.

**Quest Events**

These Events are part of a larger **Quest** — a series of linked Events with meaningful choices through which you forge your own unique path through the Quest. The primary story of Ember, *A* *Shard of Fear*, is told through a series of **Main Quests** (though your party will also have the option to engage in **Side Quests** along the way). Quest Events are the only source of [[Milestone Points]] by which you can increase your character's Level — though not all Quest Events award a Milestone Point.

**Standalone Events**

Standalone Events are individual encounters not directly linked to a larger thread. They do not award Milestone Points, but may benefit you in other ways.

Additionally, Events might bring you to particular illustrated scenes: Area Maps and Vistas.

**Area Maps**

Events that take place in an Area Map allow you move your individual Character Token around in their immediate environment, and interact directly with elements you see on screen, like puzzles and traps.

**Vistas**

Events that take place in a Vista show you a portrait of your character and any others nearby — all moving around on an illustrated backdrop of the local environment. Within a Vista, dialogue from an important character can even appear on screen alongside their portrait.

The[[Quests and Events]] page of this guide has further details on the information above, but as a player, you don’t need to worry about whether you’re in a Quest Event, Standalone Event, Main Quest, or Side Quest. In fact, Ember never directly tells you whether you’ve finished every Event for a particular Quest — the choices you make can have far-reaching consequences, and whether you’ve ever *truly* finished addressing a particular story is up to you to figure out.

## The Codex

The Events you've encountered and the choices you made within them will be recorded in the Codex, alongside other important discoveries from your journey. You can view the Codex by clicking the ​​ icon at the top of the screen. The Codex has five important tabs:

**Journal**

The Journal tab in the Codex — separate from the Journal entries in the Foundry sidebar — tracks completed Events, including the time the party completed them, and a short summary of what happened, including important decisions made by the party.

**Quest Log**

The Quest Log tab displays summaries of completed Quest Events specifically, grouped by Quest, rather than a chronological list of all completed Events.

**Discoveries**

The Discoveries tab lists Biomes and Locations that you have encountered while exploring the Region Map, including when you first encountered them, and some detailed information on the Biome or Location.

**Bestiary**

The Bestiary tab lists creatures and monsters you've encountered during your game, including when you first encountered them, and some detailed information on the Biome or Location.

**Characters**

The Characters tab lists important characters you've met during your game, including when you first encountered them, and some biographical information, such as name, pronouns, Ancestry, Culture, their character portrait, and more.
