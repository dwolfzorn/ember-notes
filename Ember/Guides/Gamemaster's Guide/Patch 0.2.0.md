# Patch 0.2.0

Welcome to a major Ember content release as part of our ongoing **Alpha One** test process. This major update includes new quests, new areas to explore, new characters to encounter, and way more!

#### Compatibility

Ember patch `0.2.0` requires core Foundry VTT version `13.341` or higher and D&D5E system version `5.0.0` or higher.

#### Important Playtest Instructions!

If you are actively playtesting Ember it will be important for you to manually trigger the newly added [[Leavetakings]] event and select the correct event outcome in this event. If you do not do this, certain Chapter 2 content will not function correctly!

## New Quests

This update adds the final two **Chapter 2** side-quests to the game.

- [[Glitter in the Dark]]
- [[To Fall and Fall Again]]

### Glitter in the Dark

Rumors are sweeping through the Golden Flats about an unsolved crime in the sinkhole city of Arcturel, a place where lawlessness has a loose foothold and lost things are seldom found. It seems one of the ubiquitous constructs used for manual labor in Lower Arcturel's industrial sector went haywire and killed an innocent bystander, and now the murderous automaton is missing — having fled the scene of the crime to places unknown in or around the Sinkhole City itself.

Naturally, a pall of paranoia and civil unrest has settled on the various districts of Arcturel, whose citizens live in mounting fear of the errant "Renegade Construct" and its homicidal tendencies. In an effort to stem this tide of turmoil, House Cevher and other businesses of Arcturel have teamed up to hire some outside investigators to track down the missing construct and mete out some proper recompense. Enter the party, who may very well discover a new definition of justice before Arcturel's score with the Renegade Construct is settled.

"Glitter in the Dark" will task the party with tracking down the so-called Renegade Construct and examining the mysterious events that surround the incident with the deceased citizen, a miner named Kellen Lorde. This investigation will lead the characters from the upper reaches of Arcturel to the darkest subterranean corners of the Sinkhole Depths — where the party will come face-to-face not only with their quarry, but with their very sense of right and wrong.

### To Fall and Fall Again

An ancient attack. A mysterious body. A whisper in the night. Something is changing deep in the Pathways beneath Ember that has begun to affect the lives of those on the surface — and attracted the attention of powerful forces locked in an age-old struggle.

In the midst of these larger than life events, a family has suffered an unthinkable loss. One child has fallen in the darkness of the Pathways, another to the anguish of trying to find her. Their father now lives a simple life, but even his daily existence is tainted by the effects of the corruption that has begun to make its way up from the deep.

In “To Fall and Fall Again,” your path will head into the Pathways below Ember toward a light in the darkness, facing monsters, temptations, and ancient powers. As you make your way to millennia-old ruins, you can learn some of the secrets of those who built them, uncover the power that still remains hidden deep within, and confront a corruption as old as time.

## Quest Changes

In addition to these new quests, there are some significant quest changes for the main quests in Chapter 1 and the beginning of Chapter 2:

### The Winding Trail

The end of [[The Winding Trail]] has been reworked.

- Parties which arrive late to Helkas experience [[Dusktide Destruction]] before also experiencing [[The Smoke Clears]] as all other parties do.
- [[The Smoke Clears]] has been rewritten to be appropriate for all groups. During this event, Sadri Zhalimorne pushes the party more emphatically to return to the Forest of Stone and clear up the Chapter 1 side quest they did not experience on the way to Helkas before journeying onwards into Chapter 2.
- A new event, [[Leavetakings]], now concludes Chapter 1 wherein the party will gain their final milestone point and make a decision about which main quest companion to journey onwards with into Chapter 2.

If you are actively playtesting Ember it will be important for you to manually trigger [[Leavetakings]] and select the correct event outcome in this event. If you do not do this, certain Chapter 2 content will not function correctly!

### Chapter 2 Main Quests

The chapter 2 main quests with Ankarist, Sin, and Lyla have also been reworked:

- All 3 quests now depend on the outcome selected in [[Leavetakings]]
- The "Departing With …" events have been removed from these quests, as that content is now covered by the Leavetakings event.
- Each of these 3 quests has a new "Traveling With …" event: [[Traveling with Ankarist]], [[Traveling with Lyla]], and [[Traveling with Sin]] which are repeating events that can happen throughout Chapter 2 to strengthen your relationship with your companion by discussing ongoing events with them and learning their perspectives on the evolving world and the characters you meet.
- Each of these 3 quests has a new "Reuniting With …" event which standardizes the mechanism by which a party can reconnect with that companion if they did not travel with them initially in Leavetakings.
  - [[Reuniting with Ankarist]] occurs in Skybrush
  - [[Reuniting with Lyla]] occurs in Nain
  - [[Reuniting with Sin]] occurs at Corpin Sanctuary

## Other Content

In addition to these major pieces of content, there are numerous other content improvements featured in patch 0.2.0.

- Added a suite of audio for the metropolis of Ordain including both music and environment sound for Ordain's three major biomes: Ordain Flats, Ordain Spires, and Ordain Docks!
- Arcturian Constructs have been added as a non-playable template to the Ember Dynamic Token maker with support for woven, chessian, and silver beam variations.
- The [[Well Enough Alone]] event in "Signal of Intent" now has a tracked outcome which governs the party's success (or not) in ascending the bluffs. A hex-to-hex boundary now prevents movement unless the event is successful.
- The [[Bridging the Gap]] event in "Thorny Predicaments" now visually toggles the appearance of Brevin and enables or disables an impassible hex-to-hex barrier depending on whether the Brevin Bridge is active or not.
- Added a number of creature portraits for Yarnac, Rask, Tricket, and Ooze which allow these commonly domesticated creatures to be used in Vistas as non-humanoid tokens. These portraits are found under `modules/ember/assets/portrait/creatures`.
- Added numerous "background characters" which can be used as miscellaneous crowd or background actors in vistas. This artwork is found under `modules/ember/assets/portrait/background`.
- Add particle effects to the Splinter Canyons vista with the Water Surge composition.
- Improve the rendering of the Sea of Stars in the Cosmos Map to appear more infinite and animated using a custom shader.
- Improve the rendered quality of a large number of vista assets and backgrounds.
- Improve the Corpin Sanctuary area map to feature the Pathways elevator and Evesso's profane altar.
- Balance changes for Svala and Gafto the Howler in the "Ambushed Refugees" event in "Crumbling Sanctuary".
- Added a Profane Altar active effect to Evesso which applies during "Sanctuary of Death" in "Crumbling Sanctuary".
- Added standalone events which can occur in the Fogbound Caverns or Sinkhole Depths biomes.

## Software Features

- Differentiate the control icon or highlight color for GM-only interactables vs. interactables which can be controlled by players.
- Restore D&D system specific short and long rest event logic so that Ember gameplay events can properly interrupt a Short Rest or Long Rest. Remove the restriction that prevented events of the social or exploration type for triggering during this context.
- Capture explicit Dynamic Token configuration for unlinked tokens as part of Event encounter setup.
- Update EmberRegionTokenRuler for new v13 waypoint label changes.
- Add a custom journal enricher that can reference a specific event or event outcome and render it as a link with a checkbox for whether it has been completed or not.
- Allow deleting a Caravan token as long as it is not the primary `ember.partyToken`
- Better align ProseMirror WYSIWIG styles with Ember journals.
- Implement new `EmberNarrativeEvent#spawnEncounter` method which can spawn the configured encounter for an event in the background, independent of the active Scene on the canvas.
- Support custom token texture src and scale in `EmberAreaMap#getActorSpawns`
- Automate adding the chosen main quest companion to the Party actor as an event hook in the Leavetakings event.
- Allow `EmberBeamLightSource` to be occludable.
- Add support for new event coefficients for chapter completion progress and main quest companion party membership.
- Added a reusable`MagicalPlatformShader`, to create magical platforms or bridges with a lot of options (glow, patterns, resolution, colors, etc.) for visual effects.
- Optimized the light sources in the Ember Cosmos Map to be more performant since they are not reliant on walls or collision.
- Adapted the rendering complexity of the Ember Cosmos Map depending on the client's configured Performance Mode.

## Bug Fixes

- Improve rules around the visibility of gazetteer pages and their Note objects on the region map canvas. Players should be able to see the pinned locations of any gazetteer location that they have discovered, but not other undiscovered locations.
- Fix a bug which caused completed Standalone Events to break the Codex display because they have no root quest node.
- Adjust for `CONFIG.Canvas.fogManager` for V13 stable changes and fix restoration of CONFIG variables in Ember managed scenes.
- Improve game state flags for chapter completion progress so that it automatically recomputes upon event state changes.
- Fixed a bug in the `WaterSamplerShader` which caused the processed texture to be offset in the incorrect direction.
