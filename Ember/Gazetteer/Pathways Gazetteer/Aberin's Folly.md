# Aberin's Folly

Located several miles east of Rock Bottom, this ramshackle campground endures as a rundown relic of Arcturel's past, choked with abundant mushroom growth and the ink-black darkness of the Depths. Once known as Aberin's Hold, Aberin's Folly is what remains of an ill-fated mining camp that unfortunately fell prey to the area's natural (and supernatural) hazards. And thanks to the subterranean biome's lack of a meteorological weather system, the essence of the camp's "temporary" structures and abandoned inventory have remarkably stood the test of time.

Aberin's Folly has fallen into total disregard by the people of Arcturel and will only be found on the oldest of local maps. Nevertheless, the camp's accoutrements and geographical vantage make it a suitable enough place for rest or surreptitiousness. Made of various treated leathers, the tents are still quite functional and duly represent the pride inherent in classic Arcturian craftsmanship. A splintered array of crates, barrels, and bins decorate the tent-side areas, alongside a handful of tool-littered tables and rickety wooden chairs. A bygone scattering of grime-covered mining equipment has also been left here, but a small fire pit smolders at the camp's center — suggesting recent occupancy and a sign of life.

## Environment

The campsite itself sits upon a low rocky butte above an overgrown gully, marked by uneven tiers of craggy brown stone. The soil here is hard and compact, which makes matters of sure-footing and solid construction relatively safe. And while some of the area's bioluminescent vegetation sheds a welcoming dim light, the local environment mirrors that of the Sinkhole Depths at large — where timeless, foreboding shadows paint the outskirts and areas between.

## Ecology

The flora and fauna at Aberin's Folly aren't altogether unique from other areas of the Sinkhole Depths. The large awnings of countless rust-cap mushrooms dominate the area, alongside a few modest specimens of lambent broadleaf trees and smaller species of fungi. Meanwhile, ample patches of Bristlevine weave their way through the area's valley — posing a somewhat hazardous traversal if one isn't careful while crossing them.

Wildlife here includes the ubiquitous [[Crevvet]], which lair in small dens among the camp's refuse, and various insects, arachnids, and reptiles native to the biome's furthest reaches. Visitations from larger, more dangerous creatures of the Sinkhole Depths are infrequent, but do happen (indeed, one such incursion is responsible for the location's woeful renaming). Predatory creatures like [[Jurtak]] and [[Juvenile Garganthus]] have occasionally been known to prowl these grounds and pose an imminent threat whenever they do.

## Lore

Despite its meager and slipshod appearance, Aberin's Folly has a storied history tethered to the earliest days of Arcturel, when a prospector named Aberin Hyle established a mining post here known as Aberin's Hold. In partnership with House Cevher and various stakeholders of Arcturel, this enterprise flourished for two seasons before calamity beset the modest camp.

As the story goes, a large six-limbed aberration crawled from somewhere out of the darkness one unassuming night to wreak havoc on the tiny settlement. Before the denizens of Aberin's Hold even knew what hit them, this terrible and malefic creature took several lives, slaughtering livestock and humanoids alike. The survivors quickly fled, abandoning the remote encampment for the years to come. In the wake of its demise, the ill-fated camp has come to be remembered as Aberin's Folly, if indeed it is remembered at all. What the settlers of Aberin's Hold didn't know is that the malefic creature in question was a Garganthus, which prowls the Sinkhole Depths to this very day — along with other unsavory denizens of the Pathways.

Lately, the camp serves as a makeshift hideaway for a group of awakened Chessmen known as the "Downsiders", who conceal themselves here far away from the prying eyes of their humanoid masters in Arcturel. And the brambles of the gulley hide their own secret: the forgotten remains of a few slain settlers and the rusted chassis of several nameless automatons.
