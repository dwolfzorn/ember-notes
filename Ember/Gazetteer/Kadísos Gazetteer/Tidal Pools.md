# Tidal Pools

**Work In Progress!** This biome and its accompanying entry are not in the scope of Alpha One Gameplay. All text provided here is currently **in development** and **incomplete**. This entry will be revised and expanded on in future updates.

The tidal pools on the southern side of Kadisos offer a unique and treacherous landscape. As the tides roll in and out, these rocky beaches are constantly replenished with water, creating a dynamic ecosystem filled with life and perils.

### Environment

The terrain of the tidal pools is characterized by large rocky outcrops scattered across the beaches the flood pools, creating natural barriers and hiding spots for both predators and prey. The rocks are often covered in seaweed, making them slippery and hazardous to traverse.

As the tide rolls in, hot sea water from [[The Cauldron]] meets cooler water from the [[Reaver Ocean]], creating huge plumes of steam and mist which shroud the island daily.

### Ecology

Along the sandy areas of the beach, hardy plants such as beach grasses and dune shrubs can be found dug in and holding fast against the constant flow of sea water. These resilient plants have long roots that anchor them firmly in place.

In the tide pools themselves there are huge kelp beds, slime slicked rocks, bundles of collected debris and fishing nets, and all manner of sea life growing through them.

This biome teems with life both great and small, from starfish and crustaceans huddled between rocks to huge, invasive tidal snails, massive scavenging crustaceans, and lurking kadhana lizards looking for an easy meal.

### Lore

The true terror of the tidal pools lies in the presence of Krasarioc, a lumbering behemoth that roams the area in search of food. Less an apex predator, and more a force of nature, few upon Ember's surface can confront it without meeting a grim fate. The remains of Krasarioc's kills, including the crushed shells from its prey, attract other opportunistic hunters and scavengers, while sea life teems in the deep depressions of its footsteps.
