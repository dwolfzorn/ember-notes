# The Cauldron

**Work In Progress!** This biome and its accompanying entry are not in the scope of Alpha One Gameplay. All text provided here is currently **in development** and **incomplete**. This entry will be revised and expanded on in future updates.

Nestled in the heart of Kadisos, the Cauldron Lake is a unique and treacherous biome that captivates and challenges adventurers with its ever-changing landscape.

### Environment

The terrain of the Cauldron Lake is characterized by its volatile nature. The shores of the lake are composed of fine volcanic sand beaches that stretch out towards the churning waters. These beaches are hot to the touch day and night. The heat of the lake and surrounding shores mixed with the offshore sea air creates a near constant haze that hangs over the area which often turns into heavy gray fog that stinks of the sea.

Traversing through Cauldron Lake requires caution and adaptability due to its ever-changing conditions and extreme temperatures. Only those brave or foolish enough venture here in search of its rumored hidden city beneath its surface.

### Ecology

Along the coast of the lake the flora is limited due to the intense heat and high salt content present in the volcanic sand. Only a few hardy grass species have managed to adapt and survive in this extreme environment, with the vast majority of plants stopping at the tree line.

The fauna of the Cauldron Lake is an enigmatic and bizarre assortment of creatures that have adapted to survive in the extreme conditions of the superheated water. The unique environment has given rise to species that are unlike anything found in other biomes on Kadisos.

Beneath the surface of the lake are strange variations of common sea plants, specially adapted to survive in the extreme heat. These strange plants are coiled around the shattered remains of the ancient people that once called this island home.

It is worth noting that more typical jungle life tends to avoid the beaches due to the intense heat and shifting terrain caused by geothermal activity. The strange and resilient fauna of the Cauldron Lake serves as a testament to the adaptability of life in even the most hostile environments.

### Lore

Though one might mistake the natural pattern of the cauldron for the breathing of Ember itself, the cycle of superheating and cooling it sees is actually cause by an ancient structure deep within the lake. An out of control magical reactor leaks energy into the water, causing it to heat up in predictable cycles.

Legend holds that the great city of Rejarh can be found down there, the last great settlement of an ancient people whom the Ashka are descended from.
