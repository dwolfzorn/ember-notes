# Jade Mountains

**Work In Progress!** This biome and its accompanying entry are not in the scope of Alpha One Gameplay. All text provided here is currently **in development** and **incomplete**. This entry will be revised and expanded on in future updates.

Rough mountains rise out of the land of Kadisos like a crown, casting long shadows over the lower regions, and capturing clouds so that their ran can pour down into the central bay of the island.

### Environment

The sharp, rugged features make the mountains extremely challenging to navigate, and even traversing the rocky foothills is a daunting task for seasoned travelers. With no established paths or trails to guide the way, most opt to follow natural pathways created by the elements over time. These pathways offer some semblance of safety in an otherwise hazardous environment, so long as the weather doesn't turn them against you.

### Ecology

Vegetation struggles to take root among the unforgiving slopes and jagged cliffs. Occasionally, small patches of stunted shrubs and hardy alpine flowers can be found in sheltered areas or where moisture collects. In the few areas where soil can accumulate, some resilient plant species manage to survive. Low-lying mosses and lichens cling to the crevices in the rocks, adding a touch of green amidst the barrenness.

The mountains appear barren at first glance, nothing but rocks and a few sparse plants but no shortage of small critters can also be found in this biome. The only truly large and notable creature to call this biome home is the Gravestone Goat, a large, mountain goat capable of navigating the difficulty terrain with ease, and also known for its ability to blend into its surroundings, appearing identical to stone. It is notorious for ambushing unwary travelers.

### Lore

Driven up from the waters ages ago, the jade mountains were once a very different sight, but the destruction of the ancient Seydiri people by way of a massive magical explosion left the landscape of Kadisos changed forever. No living mortal remembers the old, softer, rolling mountains and hills of Kadisos, only the jagged, shattered stone that sit in their place now.
