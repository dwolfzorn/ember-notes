# Yakoshta Mine

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This location exists within the [[Kaleidoscope Caverns]] in ⬢ s.3218.2868, and is a mine used by the nearby workers of Yakoshta to gather crystals and ore.
>
> - This location is depicted by the area map [[Yakoshta Mine]].
> - This location is featured in the [[Saving Jasper]] event.

### Environment

The area where Yakoshta mine has been built reflects its initial creation by a culture with a large population of giants: high ceilings, deep chasms, and a large entrance. The active section of the mine, where the current mining operations are focused, is filled with tools and ore, and now includes a set of mining tracks that help to move ore throughout the mine.

### Ecology

Kaleidoscope crystals can be clearly seen throughout the mine, but they lack the power of crystals found in other location, likely due to some method of the clearing of the area done by the Shent. The upper parts of the mine (above the level where the new miners could reach) and chasms below the ground generally look more rundown than the middle section where Sellen and her miners have been doing their work.

### Lore

Not much, if anything, is known about the Shent era of the mine — Yakoshta's leader, Sellen, became aware of its presence from a conversation with a trader who passed by the caves holding the mine when looking for a shortcut through the area and sheltered in them during a storm. Eager to find a mine that didn't need to be created from scratch, Sellen and the miners that she leads moved within the year, constructing a mining rail system that allows them to pass safely over the mine's large chasms.

As they have settled into life here, the miners have begun slowly gathering ore from places that the Shent either ignored or never reached, and have been helped by their relationship with docile oozes who exist in this area and can be fed metal to give them new abilities. Unfortunately, something has recently changed - shifting the oozes and making them larger, luminescent, and aggressive.
