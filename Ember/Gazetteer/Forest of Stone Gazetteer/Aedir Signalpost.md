# Aedir Signalpost

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This location exists in the [[Verdant Paths]] in hex ⬢ s.3218.2870 . It was once a hidden signal tower built by the Aedir in the latter years of the [[Age of the Tower]].
>
> - This location is depicted by the [[Aedir Signalpost]] Area Map.
> - This location is featured in the [[Traversing the Tower]] Event.

### Environment

The terrain surrounding the Aedir Signalpost is rugged and steep, showing signs of having been leveled during early construction phases. Though the remnants of a path can still be utilized to reach the tower's base, thousands of years of exposure to the elements has eroded much of the terrain. The tower itself, abandoned and crumbling, holds unknown hazards for would-be explorers.

### Ecology

The Signalpost area has become a nesting site for [[Suarrok]] — large bird-like creatures that have claimed the tower pinnacle as their own. The florae in the immediate vicinity consist of hardy, wind-resistant shrubs and low plants adapted to the rocky soil. Small groups of [[Crevvet]] can also be found burrowed in compact dens around the outside of the tower.

### Lore

Known in imperial records as Aegis-Height 32, this watchtower was a vital border outpost during the Age of the Tower. It was designed to monitor the [[Shent]], whose culture and cities lay further to the west and north. Originally, the [[Aedir]] had no ill intent toward the Shent and merely wanted a strategic platform to observe them, but over time, the watchtower became increasingly militarized, and several sentinels and constructs were transported from elsewhere to protect and defend against theoretical Shent aggression. The watchtower was also equipped with sophisticated lenses and signal equipment that enabled the Aedir to observe the Shent from great distances.

When the Sun Tower shattered and the world was broken, Aegis-Height 32 was quickly abandoned. The Aedir fled or were killed by the approaching horrors of [[The Abyss]], leaving behind the volatile substances that powered their machines and constructs. The resulting leak is a glowing, amber-colored contaminant that has begun to seep through the porous rock of the spire. Following natural fault lines in the rock, this toxic ooze has dripped directly into the [[Yakoshta Mine]] located in the caverns below.
