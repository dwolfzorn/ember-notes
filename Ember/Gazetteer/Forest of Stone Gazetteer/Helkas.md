## Locations of Interest

Undoubtedly, the most remarkable landmark in Helkas is the hulking corpse of the Leviathan known as Q’ordreth, which casts a shadow across Helkas from the northern end of town. Throughout the years, an impressive scaffolding system has been erected within and around the so-called Duskmaw Leviathan’s stony remains (which is a strange sight to behold no matter how long you look at it).

**[[The Cragside Tavern]] Tavern**

The aptly-named Cragside Tavern is a charming watering hole that caters first and foremost to travelers and the local working-class. Maintained by the lively barkeep **Eveline Doss**, the Cragside is a place where people from every corner of Aterica can rub elbows with the storied laborers of Leviathan Mine.

**[[Leviathan Mine]]Mine**

Leviathan Mine is the source of several rare arcane components — one of which is unique to Helkas, while another can only be harvested during the lunar conjunction known as Dusktide. This strange enchanted mine is sometimes referred to as "The Jewel of Helkas," and is responsible for the frontier settlement's enduring prosperity.

**[[The Armarium]]General Store**

This graciously-outfitted general store is owned by the shrewd entrepreneur **Cordayne Kohle**, one of Leviathan Mine’s key stakeholders. The Armarium has nearly anything a traveler of the Forest of Stone could need for a safe and successful expedition. Cordayne minds the store most of the day, along with his assistant shopkeep **Thaddeus** (Neutral, Human Arcturian, he/him).

**[[Manfryd's Anvil and Stone]] Blacksmith**

The forges at Manfryd's Anvil and Stone are owned and operated by the prodigious blacksmith/stoneworker **Manfryd Grimm**. In tandem with a traditional coal-heated forge, Manfryd utilizes a special Gloamstone forge powered by arcane Duskflame, which serves as a valuable asset during the precarious crafting of Steelmarrow armaments.

**[[The Last Satchel]] Arcanist Shop**

The shelves of The Last Satchel are lined with an array of occult wares and arcane materials, including novel spell components developed by proprietor **Lorettia Ben-Wren** from the extracts of Leviathan Mine. Lorettia is a patron of her own alchemical arts, and is known for keeping late hours at the shop while studying her reagents with unfettered focus.

**[[Glinthome]] Temple**

This modest temple is dedicated to [[Spectra]], the Elder Goddess of Magic. Lit by arcane braziers of cold Duskflame, this humble place of worship is decorated with an array of delicate Steelmarrow embellishments, and serves as an inviting sanctuary for the people of Helkas. It also provides its caretaker, the tender-hearted cleric **Briannon Frost**, with a sense of individual purpose.

## Characters of Interest

**Briannon Frost Lawful Good, Arcturian Wirrun, she/her**

This teenage cleric of [[Spectra]] operates [[Glinthome]], a local temple dedicated to the Elder Goddess of Magic. A fledgling diviner and a promising cleric, Briannon Frost is a tenderhearted young empath who appreciates acts of service and who is never discouraged from professing her profound enthusiasm for Ember’s marvelous cosmology.

****Cordayne Kohle** Chaotic Good, Ordani Human, he/him**

One of [[Leviathan Mine]]’s key stakeholders, this shrewd 44-year-old entrepreneur owns and operates [[The Armarium]] when he isn't serving as [[House Cevher]]'s local liaison. Cordayne is regarded as one of the Ordani Trading House's most skilled brokers, and is known for his insatiable curiosity.

****Eveline Doss** Neutral Good, Arcturian Human, they/them**

This lively twenty-something barkeep manages the [[The Cragside Tavern]]. Despite their relatively young age, Eveline has a real soft spot for nostalgia and loves a good story about a time long-gone from someone who cherishes the past.

****Hode Cinderhaft** Lawful Neutral, Arcturian Fej, he/him**

This forty-something Fej mineworker is a gregarious storyteller who oversees operation of the lifts and hoists of Leviathan Mine in Helkas. If a rumor has passed through Helkas, it’s danced into the longing ears of Hode Cinderhaft, working class hero and collector of myths.

****Lorettia Ben-Wren** Chaotic Neutral, Waerd Human, she/her**

This terminally-preoccupied spellcaster owns the occultist's shop known as The Last Satchel. Despite her distracted demeanor, Lorettia is a passionate student of the supernatural wonders that constitute Ember and is often eager to trade anecdotal evidence with fellow enthusiasts.

****Manfryd Grimm** Chaotic Good, Arcturian Kivahr, he/him**

This handsome and soft-spoken blacksmith is the eponymous proprietor of Manfryd’s Anvil & Stone, the only major smithy in Helkas. One of Manfryd’s most notable physical features is his prosthetic right forearm, a simple tungsten-alloy innovation created by his brother [[Janix Mance]] that enables the talented blacksmith to overcome a tragedy of his not-too-distant past.

**[[Sadri Zhalimorne]] Neutral Good, Arcturian Altyra, she/her**

Sadri Zhalimorne is the ambitious Magistrate of Helkas. A well-traveled lawmaker, she often wears a wry grin that reveals her gregarious nature with playfully sardonic flare. Sadri owes much of her success to prominent allies in [[House Bastilla]], and has several debts to pay via her newfound station.

## Life In Helkas

Living in Helkas can be as hard or as easy as one ultimately desires, depending almost entirely on what they seek out of life here at the edge of the Forest of Stone. Sure, there is opportunity aplenty — but favorable circumstances are hard-won, and the toil required to achieve is often mighty. This scrappy philosophy of rural industry means the people of Helkas work hard and play hard. Recreation is celebrated most thoroughly when it's earned, and one's social station often depends on how helpful they are to the community and/or its mining and trade endeavors. This is not as draconian as it seems, however, for the people of the Arctus Plateau consider "help" to be a rather wide array of goods and services — this includes everything from material resources, tools, and equipment; to general courtesy, warm company, and timely inspiration.

### Culture

Its status as a frontier town of lucrative repute has turned Helkas into a veritable melting pot over time as more and more of Ember’s disparate ancestries and cultures began settling among its rocky environs. As one might expect, many of the local traditions pay heed to this prosperity, the most notable of which is the spectacular Dusktide Festival that only takes place when Ember’s Spirit Moon and Water Moon are in celestial alignment.

The most prominent holiday associated with Helkas is the annual Dusktide Festival, which celebrates the special alignment of Ember's lunar bodies needed to activate the arcane potential of Leviathan Mine's esoteric resources. Other traditions include an anniversary celebration of the town’s founding known as Hewer’s Eve.

Religion has a relatively minor presence in Helkas; a lone shrine to the Elder Goddess [[Spectra]] (known as [[Glinthome]]) marks the only formal place of worship. Many citizens of Helkas celebrate their faith in their own ways, with private meetings of prayer and reverence taking place in homes or public areas like [[Helkas Green]].

### Politics

Helkas is governed by a Town Council of eight volunteer representatives who are elected annually to their station. A loose set of laws and ordinances are recorded in the town’s founding manifesto: a hand-written tome known as the Helkas Missives (kept under glass at the Cragside Tavern, which serves as the Council’s periodical meeting place).

Helkas maintains close ties with [[House Cevher]] of [[Ordain]]. Serviced by continental logistics companies like [[Agraband Swift]]’s Strayhearth Caravan, this alliance has proven valuable for both settlements, who benefit from steady access to each other’s bespoke goods and services.

Additionally, the [[Anachraenum]] cultivates an ongoing relationship with Helkas that affords the treasure hunter’s guild a “first look” policy when it comes to exceptional artifacts discovered within and around the local mines. These two covenants don’t often find themselves in conflict with each other, but disagreements between the Trading Houses and the Anachraenum over precious resources from Helkas are not entirely unknown.

### Defense

While some Helkas locals call for the implementation of an official constabulary, a volunteer militia of lightly-trained citizens forms the town’s primary defense force. These efforts are generally supplemented by the intervention of the countless adventurers, explorers, and armed traders who make their way through town.

### Lore

It is speculated that the Leviathan of Helkas — referred to as Q’ordreth in ancient texts — emerged from the subterranean bowels of Ember during the Spirit Moon's Zenith; though its cause of death is unknown, archaeological examinations of its bones have revealed deep gouges suggestive of battle wounds. When Q’ordreth came to rest, it rapidly ossified, forming the valuable mineral clusters that are mined today. These arcane minerals leeched into the surrounding soil, and a copse of magenta aithus trees sprang up around the half-buried leviathan. When the Trading Houses of Ordain eventually caught wind of the settlement's unique landmark (roughly 300 years ago), settlers established a provisional camp to begin mining the strange materials found within. That humble camp eventually became the village of Helkas, a town whose reputation hinges on its valuable exports and an ongoing coincidental reputation for bad luck.
