### Environment

The Moonstone is surrounded by dense, overgrown plants, trees, and vines, some of which have intertwined with the stone pillar and curled around it. Nearby, large reddish-pink bulbs can be seen, and most of the plants here appear exceptionally lush, nourished by water motes that constantly emerge and vanish around the blue crystal. Towards the base of the nearby cliffs are a series of small, shallow pools of incredibly clear water.

### Ecology

The surrounding area seems to be filled with smaller creatures, beetles, flies, and various insects that scuttle and flit around the Moonstone itself. Several fish can be seen floating lazily in the nearby waters. When the elemental moon of Mayis is dominant, the Moonstone begins to radiate an increased amount of magical energy and is overrun with tiny, harmless swarms of water motes that dance and swirl around it like enchanted drops of rain.

### Lore

This ancient monument is a moonstone, a marvel of Shent toechology and magic, constructed over three thousand years ago, long before the events of the Shattering. It comprises a thick, reinforced stone pillar with a huge crystal orb inset into the top. All moonstones are crafted to channel a specific moon's energy, and this one is built for the elemental moon of Mayis. The purpose of the moonstone was to monitor a certain area and relay information about local environmental conditions, such as humidity and temperature, but it also functioned as a communication relay. This moonstone is one of the few that remains active in the region, although its power has significantly faded; nevertheless, it still draws energy from the water moon. [[Mayis]] and is especially potent when the moon is dominant in the sky, and can provide a unique boon for characters who are duly attuned.
