### Environment

The Bloodwoods possesses a faint, otherworldly energy that causes the forest to move physically in chaotic patterns; it is an ever-changing landscape that demands vigilance and adaptability from its inhabitants. The Black Aspens, all part of one living organism, rule the biome, as towering Marble Oaks, resembling stone statues, break up the dense aspen growth and serve as landmarks. The forest floor is a patchwork of crimson grass and russet colored variants of common plants, thriving in the dark earth enriched by magical residue. Only one settlement, [[Talei]], clings to its southern edge, while failed expeditions, lost caravans, and overgrown camps serve as a grim testament to the land's hostility toward intruders.

### Ecology

Within the Bloodwoods, common creatures are transformed by primordial magic into aggressive, formidable beasts. [[Gore Bird]], Grass Runners, and more call this place home, and all are warped by it. Its most prominent native species, [[Amerasps]] — black-and-gold, wasp-like creatures — burrow into Black Aspens and construct nests around amber deposits. In time, these colonies harden into [[Leshy]] cocoons, from which humanoid sap creatures emerge. The Leshy, bound to one another in shared consciousness, embody the forest’s will and are met with caution, even when they do not show open hostility.

### Lore

During [[The Shattering]]and the subsequent [[Abyssal Shear]], this area was the scene of a final confrontation between the Shent Shard God Kyra and an Abyssal Horror of Sha-Xotha. Their fierce battle decimated the region, rending a deep fissure into the [[Pathways]] below. For many years, Abyssal energy fluctuated across the region until the Casia Nimaella attempted to mend the damage by planting a seed from [[Primordis]] in the wound. Over time, the Bloodwoods grew and covered the scar. Yet Abyssal wounds rarely heal properly, and the forest now festers with chaos, destruction, and ancient magic left untamed.
