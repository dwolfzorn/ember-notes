# Lake Argath

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This location exists at ⬢ s.3199.2876 in the [[Rustvar Valleys]], north of [[Lake Jinro]].

The tranquil waters of Lake Argath are fed by a small tributary that flows from the Wedgelands to the northeast, and act as the source for the River Vestus to the south, beyond the steep drop of an unnamed waterfall. Argath is a place known for its quietude as much as its vicinity to untamed wilderness, and — despite it lacking most hallmarks of civilization — is steeped in the legends and lore of yesteryear.

### Environment

The environs of Lake Argath are remarkably clean, stirred by the constant flow of fresh mountain water that springs forth from the northeastern tributary, and siphoned off via the southern waterfall. The Wedgelands mountains that surround the valley where Lake Argath is situated invariably shield it from winds that would otherwise disturb the remote locale's cool and serene waters.

### Ecology

Lake Argath's proximity to the mountainous hinterlands of the Wedgelands has resulted in a somewhat mixed ecology, marked by abundant wildlife and natural overgrowth. A scattering of evergreen and deciduous trees dot the lake's somewhat desolate perimeter, where the golden grass of the plains has given way to dustier footing, marked by gravel and brown rock. Wildlife here includes the varied fish and shellfish of Lake Argath alongside avian species like kestrels, gulls, hawks, and vultures. Indeed, thanks to the area's unbridled wilderness, the relationship between Argath's birds of prey and scavengers is quite a cooperative one. Meanwhile, mammalian creatures like wild rask and feral nothrin feed on the area's flora, while preyed upon by hinterland predators including crag lizards and abyss-warped beletors.

### Lore

Adventurers and survivalists (along with those who can afford their professional employ) are known to journey to Lake Argath during their more whimsical and aimless excursions. However, the legends of the giants loom large over this area, where tall tales of Varùn folklore tend to become a memorable part of the visit. Such fireside narratives include the spooky ghost story known as "Old Tall Bones" and the whimsical legend known as "The Giantess and the Drake".
