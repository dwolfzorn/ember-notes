Seawall, notably built on top of a previous Aedir colony known as Avadaol, is a formidable port city carved from the cliffs by the sea. Originally built as a safe haven against the unchecked and dangerous wilderness of the plateau, the city's ancient fortifications make it an almost impregnable stronghold.

## Landmarks - Tower Crags

The vast majority of the city exists in this upper district called the Tower Crags, and a mixture of residences, businesses, and the like.

**The Ancient Tower Government Center**

An ancient Aedir tower that serves as home of the city's watch, as well as its leadership and government offices.

**The Cliffs Residential Area**

A wealthy residential area built into the cliffs, overlooking Lower Seawall and Gray Harbor. Access is limited to a single bridge and a few lifts; its residents are the rich, connected, and influential.

**The Rising Tide Inn Inn**

Known for its comfortable rooms, fine food, and stunning views of the sea below.

## Landmarks - Stone Haven

Directly below upper seawall, Stone Haven is partially built into the cliffs of the city, partially built on the water, and is the city's roughest neighborhood. In this part of the city the alleys are dark, hiding the worst elements of the city from the light of day.

**The Old Door Inn Inn**

A ratty building that offers cheap rooms and no questions asked.

**The HolesInn**

A series of artificial coves dug out of the cliff walls by the Aedir a millennia ago. From here, barges move cargo and people between Gray Harbor and Lower Seawall. The area also functions as a drydock for damaged ships in need of repair, and as a quiet place for more clandestine activities.

## Landmarks - Gray Harbor

A sprawling dock caked with layers of long-dried sea salt, giving the district its name. This space is lined with warehouses and stores, providing a legitimate market and front for more illicit deals waiting to be made.

**The Salted Anchor TavernTavern**

A place for watered down drinks and shady business deals.

**Merak's Trading OutpostTrading Hub**

The central hub for all inbound and outbound oversea trade.

## Life In Seawall

### Culture

Seawall's people are a mix of smugglers, bandits, fences, and honest merchants united by a historic animosity toward the Trade Houses and the great city of Ordain. This shared distaste allows the masters of Seawall to keep attention focused outward, giving them cover for their own illegal and disruptive actions. This outward aggression also helps to mask the flow of bandits and raiders disguised as traders and merchants, as everyone knows that Seawall has no love for Ordain.

### Economy

Since the rugged terrain surrounding the city forbids any possibility of agriculture, the city's survival relies on constant overland trade from nearby Nain and Molte, with more surreptitious dealings facilitated by its extensive, shadowy dock areas masked by a vigorous fishing industry.

### Politics

Historically the city was overseen by a council of leaders that did their best to represent the needs of the various districts of the cities and their people. However this council was recently dissolved behind closed doors and replaced by a single charismatic leader and their inner circle of lackeys, sycophants, and lieutenants.

This change has rubbed many the wrong way, but little can be done about it without severe risk to life and limb, as the new leader is not as even-handed as the old council was. As a result, many have chosen the route of least resistance, keeping their head down and doing their work, though a few still fight back in their own ways.
