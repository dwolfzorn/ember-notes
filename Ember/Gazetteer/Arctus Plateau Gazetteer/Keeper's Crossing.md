# Keeper's Crossing

The ruined bridge known as Keeper’s Crossing was once a proud stone-and-timber viaduct that previously connected each bank of the River Elenain along the Golden Way, the region’s most frequently traveled mercantile road. However, naught but moss-covered rubble and river detritus can be found here after the great earthquakes that rocked the Golden Flats two decades ago; and the arcane waters of the notorious River Elenain remain unforgiving. Nevertheless, the moss-covered remains of the eastern and western abutments stand defiant and resolute.

### Environment

Because of its ruinous condition and proximity to the River Elenain's turbulent and enchanted waters, Keeper's Crossing is a remarkably damp and sodden location, marked by muddy and overgrown earth that resulted from the bridge's destruction years ago. Footing here is unsure and slippery, even during the most benevolent of weather — conditions dictated by the arcane river's somewhat unpredictable currents.

The bridge itself once sat 40 feet above the water's surface level, where a ramshackle array of planks and ropes are now stretched across the swift runnel. Those who wish to cross the river without being washed downstream by the torrent must safely ford the river using the various rocks and makeshift bridges that span the watercourse.

### Ecology

The ecology of Keeper's Crossing is similar to that of most other locations along the River Elenain in the Golden Flats, with an array of Arcturian flora like sapgrass and waterwells in abundance, alongside fauna including gore birds, tillers, and the iridescent trout that swim up- and downstream along the 'Nain.

### Lore

Originally named for the Ordani shopkeep who commissioned its construction, Keeper’s Crossing became a major landmark along the region’s most traveled trade way, and was fundamental in Ordain’s early growth. A small array of docks beside the bridge — all but annihilated by the quakes — once catered to ferry captains and riverboats from Nain, and brought a long-forgotten vibrancy to this place that has been utterly lost to time.

Like much of the plateau, Keeper’s Crossing is steeped in local legend. Rumors abound of a still-hidden cache from the merchant caravan that was caught up in the earthquake’s tumult, lost somewhere in the rubble below the current. Whatever the accuracy of the landmark’s tall tales, the ruins of Keeper’s Crossing stand as a testament to Ember’s effortless ability to reclaim the land and sea for itself.
