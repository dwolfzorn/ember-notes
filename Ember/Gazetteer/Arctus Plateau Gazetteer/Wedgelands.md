# Wedgelands

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Biome is located within the northwest region of the [[Arctus Plateau]]
>
> - Travel through this Biome is generally [[Region Exploration]].
> - This Biome is visually depicted by the [[Vista: Wedgelands]] Vista.

The towering peaks referred to as the Wedgelands are known for their treacherous landslides, perilous alpine hazards, and brutally unpredictable wildlife. This biome defines the majority of the Arctus Plateau's mountainous terrain, which often plays host to preternatural creatures, awe-inspiring vistas, and timeless myths of the ancient Giants and their kin.

Few settlements manage to endure within the harsh mountain environs of the Wedgelands, but the presence of outlying camps is not altogether unheard of, along with a few well-known key points of interest — like Cevher Estate (owned by the eponymous Trading House) and [[Corpin Sanctuary]] (famed citadel of the Cindaric Sages). Outside of such remote sanctuaries and precipitous estates, the scattered remains of ancient cities and outposts of the [[Shent]] Giants dominate these desolate lands.

### Environment

The Wedgelands are known for a transitional mountain climate, and most of the biome can be identified by its steep hills of brown rhyolite and gray slate covered in amber moss and sporadic waterfalls. The exceedingly rare forests of the Wedgelands are marked by the russet hues of their rugged trees and shrubbery. The environment here is equal measures exalted and severe, and the unforgiving nature of Ember's primordial majesty is constantly on full display.

### Ecology

The biome's steep rocky slopes offer less-than-favorable conditions for wildlife habitation aside from an infrequent grove or pasture near a potent water source. An array of wild beasts populate the Wedgelands (like bears, wild cats, goats, and more), along with a scattering of rare and uncommon monstrosities. Hardened creatures like the massively-winged Huntsman Vulture and the six-legged lupine Gurrath are known to prowl the mountainous biome's uncaring wilderness.

Although excessively rare, flora of the Wedgelands is suited to hot summers and mild winters alike; and few varieties of vegetation can survive here. Noteworthy specimens include the restorative Goldroot, the pernicious Gravelthorn, and the resilient Scragwood tree.

In those areas where the Wedgelands border upon fog-shrouded Barrows, undead creatures like those found in the legendary Varùn burial lands can also be encountered.

### Lore

Despite the harsh landscape and unforgiving climes of the Wedgelands, these mountainous realms are laden with archaic legends and tales of historical significance — most notably, the myth-laden stories of the ancient Shent and their descendants, which linger among the ruins of their civilizations. Arcturian storytellers are known to spin epic yarns about the mythologies of these time-forgotten mountain people and their role in [[The Cosmos]], and adventurers from every corner of Ember seek the fortune and glory these regional tales promise.

In addition to chronicles of the ancient [[Giant]] and their kin, Arcturian folk tales are rich with stories about strange elemental spirits of earth and air that originate from the lowest of Wedgelands valleys and steepest of Wedgelands peaks. These narratives are often the stuff of fantasy, and commonly used by rural Arcturians to explain strange occurrences and keep ornery children in check; but they do occasionally possess some wondrous credence, as the cosmic influences of [[Aura]] and [[Cora]] are never far from hand.

The western Wedgelands are also steeped in the necromantic energy of an immortal [[Abyssal Horrors]] known as [[Nethehepticas]], who slumbers within a dark sanctum beneath the neighboring [[Barrows]]. The influence of this ancient evil has permeated adjacent biomes like the Wedgelands and [[Rustvar Valleys]] to such an extent that the rising threat of undead malevolence draws nearer every day.
