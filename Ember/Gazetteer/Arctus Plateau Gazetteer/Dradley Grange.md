The current proprietor — a convivial homesteader named Egon Dradley — is known by equal measure for both his hospitality and his prowess with a flute; indeed, when the work day is done, the whimsical melody of Dradley's woodwind can oft be heard ambling throughout the tranquil holding.

### Environment

Facilities of Dradley Grange include: the sprawling two-story farmhouse, a guest house with a half-dozen rooms for rent, four barns for locals and visitors alike, two stables, three fields, and a pear orchard that's stood here since the grange itself was founded.

### Ecology

The crops and livestock cultivated at Dradley Grange are known to be as wholesome as the people who propagate them. Indeed, the residents of Dradley Grange are known for their exemplary hospitality and their respect for outsiders of all stripes.

Crops grown at the grange include tomatoes, lentils, and sugar cane; while livestock cultivated here primarily consists of cows, chickens, the goat-like nothrin, and domesticated rasks — who help to both tend the farm and curtail the chicanery of outsiders.

### Lore

This rural farmstead was founded nearly a hundred years ago by Keleste and Bron Dradley, two young lovers from Ordain who sought a simpler, more holistic life than the one afforded them in the city. In the generations that have followed, their children and their children's children have kept the property and its sterling reputation in check.
