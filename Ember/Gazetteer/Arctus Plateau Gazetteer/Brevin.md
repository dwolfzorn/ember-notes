# Brevin

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
>  This settlement exists at ⬢ s.3201.2898 in the [[Golden Flats]], north of [[Bramble Gully]].
>
>  - This location is visually depicted by the [[Vista: Brevin]] Vista.
> - This location is featured in the [[Thorny Predicaments]] Side Quest.

 Brevin is one of the first of a new type of settlement known as “seed settlements." While the Arcturian druid Moss Ritherin, who pioneered this movement, often talks of having an entire city built from a single seed, Brevin is a smaller town, created by planting hundreds of them, each with its own part to play in the literal growth of the area. While Brevin sometimes appears to have been created somewhat haphazardly due to the overlapping natural materials, a closer look will find a delicate balance that allows for many different types of plant life to thrive and grow together.

  ## Landmarks - Moss

 Brevin is centered on its Four Fountains, which sustain the local Thornlings, who draw most of their nourishment from drink. Though each fountain was shaped through the labor of Stonewalker Beetles and the planting of Hollow Reed piping, each of the Brevin fountains bears a unique aesthetic that shapes the quarter grown around it. Over time, each district adopted the name of its fountain as a colloquialism. One fountain stands at the very heart of Brevin, while the other three radiate outward, supporting clusters of homes and shops. Lighthearted debates about which of the fountains has the best water are common.

  **Moss FountainSustenance Fountain**

This fountain stands at the very heart of Brevin, near the gathering area known as The Root. It appears simple, but has the largest variety of plants in its district — it is customary for visitors to scatter a few seeds around its base as a token of good luck.

**The RootVillage Center**

This large, trunk-like platform at Brevin's center serves as a communal gathering space. It is flanked by Moss Fountain, The Roost Inn, and a variety of merchant shops. To attract outside visitors, the buildings here have been enchanted to grow faster and larger than the average Brevin residence… and, as a consequence, more haphazardly.

**Temi's TonicsPotion Shop**

A pop-up shop that sells spices and spiced beverages. Owned and operated by the eponymous Temi.

**The Seeds of FashionClothing & Armor Shop**

The Seeds of Fashion is a clothing and armor shop that is pioneering "hand-grown clothing" that molds itself to the wearer. Its proprietors are Perri and Valein Anther.

**The RoostInn**

The Roost resembles a hollow tree with many windows, and is one of the few places that can easily accommodate travelers of all sizes due to its verticality. Triss Carpel, the Steward of Brevin, is often on hand to greet visitors here.

**## Landmarks - Bloomspray**

**Bloomspray FountainSustenance Fountain**

This fountain is awash with colorful perennials. The district is home to decorative artisans and artists, drawn toward its bright foliage.

**NectarTavern**

A flower-themed bar in Bloomspray where all drinks are sipped from inside living flower glasses. Discounts are available for those who promise to plant the remnants of their cup in a location at least 10 miles from Brevin to help spread these floral varieties, and to those who bring a bloom from a Nectar Glass. The bar keeps a detailed map of where Nectar Glass flowers have been found in the Arctus Plateau, and can be a good place to learn local gossip.

**## Landmarks - Crossways**

**Crossways FountainSustenance Fountain**

This fountain lies nearest to the Brevin Bridge and features complex lattice work at its base. Druidic architects tend this quarter, guiding and reinforcing Brevin's growth.

**The Brevin Bridge(Eventual) Grass Bridge**

A crowning feature of Brevin, this interwoven grass bridge has been trained to grow down into the Splinter Canyons and up to the other side. Once it reaches its destination, it will tighten into a permanent crossing, the first of its kind.

**## Landmarks - Riveril**

**Riveril FountainSustenance Fountain**

This fountain is ringed by smaller cascading fountains, and is home to many of Brevin's original seeders. Some are anxious to found something larger and more ambitious, even if it means leaving Brevin unfinished.

  **Heart’s ClimbAythorn Shrine**

A site of worship for Aythorn, consisting of an obelisk that has been fully consumed by vines and Heartmoss, making it climbable. A small hollow at the top can be used for rest and contemplation and is often thought to be a gift from the popular trickster god. While some climb to this perch, most worshippers spend their time at the sculpture's base, enjoying the way it casts shadows on the ground around it.

## Life in Brevin

Most of Brevin's residents are a group of Thornlings who left their previous home of Bramble Gully to build something new, and they share in a common ideal of communal effort. As they prepare for the unveiling of an ambitious hand-grown bridge stretching across the nearby Splinter Canyons, the community is now starting to grow beyond its original size and purpose — there are rumblings of disagreement between those who favor starting similar seed settlements elsewhere and those who want Brevin to grow into a major influence on the Arctus Plateau.

**### Culture**

 Like most Arcturians, Brevinites value hard work for mutual gain, but more often value effort over outcome. Many of the projects they work on will eventually die off when their season has ended, so they place less value on individual enduring objects than the average Acturian, focusing more on the cycle of life as a whole. Even the best tender of plants will have some that die due to weather or disease, and those living in Brevin believe that any difficulties in life are matters of circumstance that can be overcome with the support of those nearby, just as a sick plant might be staked to a healthier one as its roots regrow. Without the large multigenerational houses common elsewhere in the Arctus Plateau, Brevinites have created their own substitute — branches. These groupings of those with the same situation or outlook on life range in focus from the physical areas surrounding each of Brevin's fountains to groups that have aligned due to role or place in life, including parents, merchants, druids, the seeders who laid the groundwork for the town, and travelers who call Brevin home, but often spend long periods of time in Ordain or on the Arctus Plateau.

 ### Defense

 The seeds used to create Brevin were developed to be naturally repellant to dangerous local wildlife and many of its plants have natural defenses that can be triggered if the right substance is added into any of the local fountains, ranging from the ability of the grass to grab onto the feet of intruders to the large thorns hidden within many of Brevin’s vines. Despite creating these precautions, most in Brevin don’t think about them and generally feel they have nothing to fear. With their proximity to Ordain, even with its position on the other side of the Canyons, they feel generally protected, and occasionally reach out to the Veiled Chain if they stumble into anything that they aren't able to deal with. Even with the rising influence of the Otherhood of Fortune putting many on edge, there remains no organized militia or law enforcement group within the town — if worse comes to worst and something damages the place, Brevinites are sure they will be able to grow it again.

 ### Politics

 The politics of Brevin often resembles the intertwining vines found throughout the town — a central council known as the Central Branch consists of representatives of each of Brevin’s many branches. Though this can seem complex, Brevinites easily keep track of which branches they belong to and each resident is attached to at least 3 or 4 members of the council, ensuring that their voices are heard on local issues. Even amid growing philosophical conflict between those who want to create new versions of Brevin elsewhere and those who want to pour resources into making Brevin from a town into a thriving city, most residents belong to enough branches that they still remain connected to those they vehemently disagree with and can live harmoniously despite the conflict.
