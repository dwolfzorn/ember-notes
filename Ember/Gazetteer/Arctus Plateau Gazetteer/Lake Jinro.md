# Lake Jinro

> - This location is featured in [[The Manuscript Awakens]].

Formed in a basin from the River Vestus, Lake Jinro sits on the northern edge of the Arctus Canyon's westernmost reaches. The majestic tranquility of this place played no small role in the construction of [[Oldcraft Lodge]] on its eastern shores, and a scattering of farmsteads and rural holdings can be found by those exploring the rest of the lake's perimeter.

### Environment

Lake Jinro is connected to the River Vestus to the north and south, and a small tributary runs from the westernmost edge of the lake into the hinterlands beyond. The environs of this massive lake reflect the majesty of the Golden Flats proper, from the tall grasses that surround Jinro's sandy perimeter, to the area's meandering weather patterns marked by windy sunshine and scattered precipitation. The surface of Lake Jinro is remarkably serene, and the locale is consequently a rather inviting one. Even during the hottest summers, the waters of Jinro remain cooler than air temperature — fed by a subterranean flow from one or more chasms that lurk unseen at the bottom of the lake.

### Ecology

Lake Jinro's size and canyon-side placement provides for a marginally diverse array of flora and fauna. In addition to the rampant amber-hued grasses and shrubs of the Golden Flats that permeate the area, the lake's rural perimeter is decorated with a scattering of evergreen and deciduous trees made lush by Jinro's halcyon waters.

As one might imagine, Lake Jinro's placidity is a main attraction for regional wildlife, who can participate in the local ecosystem with aplomb. Fauna here includes varied fish and shellfish alongside avian species like kestrels and gulls, plus a variety of mammal wildlife like wild rask and feral nothrin. Deeper in the lake's depths, an impressive array of aquatic life can be found — with qualities curiously similar to those of the squamous creatures of the lightless Pathways.

### Lore

Lake Jinro has a storied history for residents of the Golden Flats, who regard its tranquil waters and grassy shores with a healthy amount of reverence (as far as natural landmarks go). Much of this can simply be attributed to Jinro's natural majesty, while some of the esteem is due to the lake's prominence in legends and lore surrounding Oldcraft Lodge and its fabled assortment of occupants. Indeed, Lake Jinro has been the location of interest detailed in several Arcturian folk tales and creation myths, including the tall tale of "Nabb the Crab" and a sorcery myth from the Age of Sunlight known as "The Watery Graves of Phin Din'ae."

Furthermore, several narratives (both true and false) surround the source and function of the [[Lake Jinro Lunar Shrine]], which occupies a grass-strewn island near the center of the lake. Pilgrims and vacationers come from miles around to visit the island shrine, if only to gawk at it from the banal safety of the lake's far shores. And it goes without saying: stories about the lake's peculiar relationship to Oldcraft Lodge are never far from the tongue of any self-respecting Arcturian bard.
