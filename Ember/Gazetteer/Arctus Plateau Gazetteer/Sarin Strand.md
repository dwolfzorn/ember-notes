# Sarin Strand

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This Biome is located along the eastern coast of the [[Arctus Plateau]] and on Bura Island.
>
> - Travel through this Biome is generally [[Region Exploration]].

The Sarin Strand is a rough and unpleasant biome, with almost nothing to forage, hard-to-navigate terrain, and ample danger.

### Environment

The Strand is broken into two distinct areas: the coastal bluffs, and the inland crags. In both cases, the terrain makes traveling in a straight line difficult, as the rocky land often bars passage, requiring travelers to either go around or undertake a risky climb to go over. Along the coast, these bluffs grow even more dangerous, as the uneven rock masks many sheer drops to the unforgiving seas below.

While there are ample outcrops and caves to camp under or in, these locations are also common gathering places for the more dangerous creatures and residents of the region, and can easily turn a quiet night in camp into a sudden midnight melee, with treacherous rock underfoot working against defenders at every step.

### Ecology

The region has little in the way of plant life. There are no trees to speak of, only hardy grasses, shrubs, the odd flower, and knobby roots. The region is so rocky that there is little soil to grow in, leaving it quite desolate most of the time. Only the hardy Widow Thorn grows here naturally, with bright colorful blooms that beg to be picked — but hide needle-like thorns with exceptionally deadly poison, hence the flower's name.

While the crags and cliffs teem with hardy creatures, only one is synonymous with the Strand: the [[Orvidrath]]. Once mistaken for dragons, these long-bodied, multi-legged creatures navigate the Sarin Strand with little difficulty, resting comfortably at the top of the food chain. Its mottling of dark reds, grays, and blacks allows it to easily blend with the stone; it prefers to nest in coastal caves, to which it drags prey.

### Lore

There is only one notable settlement in this region, [[Seawall]], a fortified position that once served as a mining and supply outpost for the [[Aedir]] in older ages, and now is a bustling haven for travelers, pirates, smugglers, traders, and fishers. It is isolated and unwelcoming to strangers who do not have their wits about them.

While there is a highway that runs from Seawall up the coast toward [[Ordain]], it is rarely used, and in a terrible state of disrepair. Worse, it is often watched by bandits, and many sections run close to Orvidrath lairs. Both prey readily on travelers foolish enough to take the road without proper protection.
