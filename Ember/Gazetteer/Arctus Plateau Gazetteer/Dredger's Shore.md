# Dredger's Shore

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This location exists at ⬢ s.3189.2917 in [[Redrak Fields]], on the eastern shore of [[Lake Orial]].
>
> - This location can be explored within the [[Ushna Dredging Docks]] Area Map.
> - This location is heavy featured in `[[/eventState disturbedEarthDredging]]` and subsequent events.

The Dredger's Shore is not a single dense town so much as a chain of small lakefront communities gathered around six major docks, each associated with one of the founding families that arrived from the Bejak homeland some four centuries ago.

> [!info] Social
> #### At a Glance
>
> - **Settlement Type**: Lakeside Dock Community
> - **Size**: Modest, Sprawling (200–400)
> - **Primary Cultures**: Bejaki, Arcturian
> - **Predominant Ancestries**: Kivahr, Keth, Human
> - **Governance**: A loose council of the six founding families oversees the settlement, with each family representing its own dock, pasture, and lakeside community.
> - **Architecture**: A mixture of Arcturian artistry and rugged Bejaki practicality.
> - **Economy**: The primary export is magically enriched mud harvested from Lake Orial; secondary trade includes yarnac wool, woolen garments, and carefully prepared yarnac meat.
> - **Religion**: Some private shrines to various gods can be found, but the prevailing central religious practice is Bejaki seasonal offerings, and rites of gratitude, which are widely observed.
> - **Notable Livestock**: Yarnac are central to the settlement’s labor, culture, and identity. All herds have direct lineage to the first animals brought to the plateau by Bejak settlers.

## Landmarks

The most notable locations are its half dozen family docks, each with its own cranes, mud-troughs, drying platforms, and packing warehouses, as well as the Six-Family Hall. The surrounding communities are quieter and more domestic, filled with family homes, wool sheds, cookfires, and fenced fields where yarnac graze between shifts.

**The Ushna Siltworks Processing Yard**

This wide, muddy work yard contains the settlement’s largest filtering tanks, drying racks, barrel stations, and pulley-cranes. Here, raw lakebed sludge is strained, sorted, packed, and sealed for transport to Redrak proper, filling the air with the smells of wet earth, lake water, oiled rope, and fresh-cut barrel wood.

While the Ushna Siltworks is not unique, and the five other docks in the area share many of the same features, often built by the same hands, it is the oldest of the docks by far. It is overseen by the Ushna family, most prestigious of the six families in the community.

**The Yarnac Commons Pasture and Gathering Area**

A broad stretch of fenced land where yarnac rest, graze, and receive care from handlers and family attendants. To preserve the integrity of the land, the commons are broken up into six distinct areas and the herds are rotated through them regularly.

The six areas that make up the commons see rotation in their use, sometimes being turned into community gardens, or serving as a festival ground, while other sections host the yarnac herds. It is also the place where the community holds rites honoring aging or fallen yarnac whose strength sustained the community.

**Six-Family Hall Council Hall**

This long, low building blends Bejaki styling with the practical artistry of Arcturian construction, its central hearth surrounded by six carved columns representing each founding family. The names of every member of the family is carved here, along with the date of their birth and death. Any whose name is entered on the columns has a voice here, or may lend theirs to another.

The council of six family heads meets here often to settle disputes, discuss community events, coordinate work, and debate questions affecting the whole lakeshore. Though each of the docks and families have freedom to conduct business as they see fit, matters of trade with Redrak must be brought to the hall before any ink can be put to parchment.

**Barrel Road Trade Route**

This packed-earth road leads away from the community toward Redrak proper, marked by carefully manicured shoulders, and ample signage. The road is one of the finest in the region, being carefully maintained by a dedicated work crew comprised of locals. This team works tirelessly, packing and leveling it several times a year to make sure that trade is never delayed, and product is never lost.

## Characters of Interest

The most influential people in the community are the six family heads, but they are swiftly followed by the dockmasters, yarnac handlers, coopers, and elders who help preserve Bejaki customs. Authority is personal here, built on the skill one has cultivated to benefit the community, and how readily one gives their time to it.

**Yaphta Ushna Lawful Neutral, Bejaki Kivahr, he/him**

Yaphta is the great-grandson of Ushna Dock’s founder and the current head of its operations. Stern, practical, and deeply proud of his family’s legacy, he treats dockwork as both a livelihood and a sacred inheritance. He is next in line to become the head of his family when his mother decides to retire her postion in the coming years.

****Mavri Karlan Neutral Good, Bejaki Kivahr, she/her****

Mavri is one of the six family heads, and the most experienced yarnac handler in the community. Despite a complete lack of magical ability, she is borderline revered for her ability to connect with and coax the most stubborn of yarnac. She spends equal time training young yarnac and dockworkers to get along.

****Tovan Redsai Chaotic Good, Arcturian Human, they/them****

Tovan oversees Redsai's Roadmen, a band of militia engineers who patrol the Barrel Road looking trouble, whether that takes the form of a dangerous, axle breaking rut or a wandering predator looking for an easy meal. Tovan is a cheerful, loud, tower of muscle that is often mistaken for a Kivahr when seen from afar.

****Eshka Vurr Lawful Good, Ordani Keth, she/her****

Eshka is a skilled woolworker who used to be a fairly renowned weaver in Ordain but retired to the county to work in wools directly at the source. She makes garments, and blankets for the community and is a respected member of the community with several apprentices that help run her workshop.

****Dorun Hallask Neutral, Bejaki Kivahr, he/him****

Dorun is one of the figures of the six families, and his crew of carpenters and engineers are responsible for maintaining the cranes and pulley systems of the Siltworks. He has little patience for carelessness, but he is generous with apprentices and crewmen who show discipline and competence.

**## Life In Dreger's Short

Daily life on the shore of Lake Orial follows a steady cycle of work and rest. Mornings are slow, punctuated by low calls of yarnac being fed and harnessed for the day's work, followed by breakfast and tea as the community comes alive. Work begins midday, where cranes groan overhead and yarnac drag scoops of mud ashore below. Come afternoon, all is still save for the yarnac released to graze the commons. Workers bathe and eat, and by sundown families are spending time together tending gardens, playing music, and telling stories.

### Culture**

A bastion of Bejaki customs and traditions can be found here, especially among the Kivahr families that trace their lineage back to the home country. Here, yarnac are honored as companions in labor rather than simple beasts of burden, and their care is treated as a communal responsibility. When a yarnac grows old or dies, its body is used with solemn gratitude: wool is saved, meat is prepared carefully, and stories of its strength are often told before the meal is shared.

### Defense

The closest the community has to a militia is Redsai's Roadmen, and they primarily concern themselves with the main thoroughfare out of the area, but the people of this community are strong and brave, and fiercely protective of their home. Only fools pick trouble here, because no one stands alone on the Dredger's Shore.

### Economy

The lifeblood of this settlement is the magically enriched mud of Lake Orial. It is Packed into barrels, and sent on to Redrak proper to be turned into soil for the rest of the Redrak Fields region. The settlement also profits from yarnac wool and woolen garments, while meat from old or fallen yarnac is sold or shared, albeit somewhat rarely. Fishing and agriculture exist, but most of that food stays within the lakeshore communities.

### Politics

The six founding families govern through an informal but respected council. Each family speaks for its own dock and surrounding households, and generally focuses on the collective good of the community. While no single family holds ultimate power or authority over the area, the Ushna family carries considerable prestige and influence due to the age of its dock, and the fact it was one of the first families to arrive from Bejak.

### Lore

The Ushna family established the first permanent dock here almost four centuries ago after arriving in Redrak Fields from the Bejak homelands. What began as a single family operation slowly grew into a chain of docks as more families arrived and put down stakes. Over generations, the settlement became a place where Bejaki culture and Arcturian craftsman ship have blended into a powerful, and vital part of the regional agricultural practice.
