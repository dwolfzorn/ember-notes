# Salt Flats

The only permanent residents of the Salt Flats reside on its offshore island, a few seasoned explorers who make their living preparing would-be adventurers for expeditions into the Flats. As such, most encounters in the region are with solo or paired travelers searching for Signaran crystals or unique ecological finds — more often, however, one comes across their bodies, mummified by the salt air and stripped of possessions.

### Environment

Only the bravest (or most foolhardy) venture into the Salt Flats, usually for one of two purposes: to prove their mettle against the harsh environment, or to search for crystals with Signaran resonance found amid the stark expanse of white crystalline hills and acidic blue waters.

### Ecology

Only a handful of tough, flowering plants have managed to survive the Salt Flats' unforgiving conditions, pushing roots past the mineral crust and deep into the earth. Most have white or silver flowers that make them hard to spot from a distance, their petals etched with crystalline, geometric patterns.

In addition to its stationary plants, the Salt Flats are also home to the Walking Grasses: a remnant of early explorers' attempts at settling the area by planting grass designed to thrive in the Flats. While the grass grew wild and plentiful for a short time, it eventually became dry and brittle; once dead, it became uprooted by the wind and blown into large, tumbleweed-like masses. Driven across the Flats at speed, these rolling thickets can knock out anyone in their path.

Animal life on the Flats has adapted to withstand the abrasive, particulate wind. The Shardmole — a small, armored mammal — survives by burrowing beneath the crust, its plated back shielding it from grit and debris. [[Salt Spiders]] dwell in the deep tunnels, relocating frequently enough to avoid eradication.

Frog-like aquatic creatures called Swelltoads inflate a primary air sac and cling to the bottom of pools to wait out windstorms. Above, Scourwings ride the violent thermals that rise from the Flats, their narrow, scythe-like wings slicing through the particulate air; their scaled hides resist abrasion, and a clear inner membrane shields their eyes, allowing them to hunt even during storms. They descend only when prey is exposed — Swelltoads forced to surface, or Shardmoles too old or sick to burrow in time.

### Lore

This desolate expanse of salt-covered earth, raised from the Reaver Ocean by forces unknown thousands of years ago, is a treacherous region of natural and unnatural dangers. It is home to all manner of hostile life, and is one of the most unwelcoming places on the face of Ember.

Little is known about the place, but the cavernous spaces beneath the surface may hold valuable crystals, and countless lives have been lost venturing into those caverns in search of them.
