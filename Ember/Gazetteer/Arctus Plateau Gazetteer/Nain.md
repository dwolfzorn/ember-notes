# Nain

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This settlement exists at ⬢ s.3216.2893 in the [[Golden Flats]], northwest of [[Seawall]].
>
> - This settlement is featured in [[A Smuggler Extraordinaire]]. [[The Book Of Tales]], and [[Next Year In Nain]].

This weather-swept village is occupied by two main social groups of Arcturians — the Stone-hearted and the River Walkers — and serves as a small trade hub for the surrounding farmsteads.

## Landmarks - The Thruways

The Thruways serve as the settlement's central district, marked by a network of river channels and sturdy stone structures.

**The Barrel BottomTavern**

This seedy, ale-soaked tavern is frequented by adventurers, fisherman, and locals of all stripes, and plays host to noteworthy scoundrels like Juro Wandren and Helton Stath.

**The EastwharfTavern**

This market and docking bay on the eastern edge of town caters to local waterway commerce and hosts a variety of merchants peddling various Arcturian wares.

**Zenodora's General GoodsGeneral Goods Store**

When tools and provisions are what you need, Zenodora's General Goods has you covered. This moss-covered equipment store owned by Zenodora Vann is a favorite stop for adventurers and traveling merchants who make their way through Nain.

## Landmarks - The Riverwinds

The surrounding Riverwinds district is distinguished by its floating wooden structures and weather-resistant agrarian marvels.

**Angler's WayFishing Bridge**

Angler's Way is a narrow stone bridge that spans several of Nain's channels and serves as a popular fishing area for urban piscators.

**Old Northside MillSmithy**

This waterwheel-powered smithy and granary of exceptional Arcturian design features a variety of semi-automated mechanisms that manage to increase its productive capabilities twofold. Elefield Beck is the head smithy.

**The Stonecraft EstatePrivate Residence**

This aged two-story townhouse is the residence of Amalthea Stonecraft, the Arctus Plateau's most accomplished storyteller. The estate is cluttered with art and literature of the Arctus Plateau and beyond, along with an accumulation of curiosities collected throughout several lifetimes. A detailed history of Ember can be found among the shelves if one knows precisely where to look.

## Life In Nain

The Arcturians of Nain are split between two main groups, separated by their survival-based ideologies: while the Stone-hearted believe in lasting structures that can withstand the test of disaster and time, the River Walkers have embraced an ideology of coexisting with nature's whims, living within wooden structures that ebb and flow with the River Elenain. Members of both social groups provide ferry services on the river, but the competition between them seems to customarily drive prices up rather than down (leaving some outsiders to believe that the two groups actually collude to benefit from the trade to Ordain).

Despite the dangers inherent in its waterways (or perhaps because of them), many people of Nain make use of a complex system of water wheels and aqueducts to help power their simple agrarian machinery. The constantly shifting arcane properties of the river's water make the maintenance of these systems routinely difficult. As a result, Stone-hearted locals are always searching for better resources and raw building materials.

### Culture

The children of Nain are known to send parchment sailboats up and down the river. Hand-written stories and songs adorn these papercraft vessels, which have a bizarre way of washing ashore in remote places all throughout Ember. Some adults in Nain continue this tradition by placing messages in bottles or hollowed gourds rather than parchment boats.

Religion in Nain is of low-to-moderate importance to its people. While many of Nain's residents maintain lives of secular pragmatism, other citizens look to the gods to help shape the fortune of their relatively tumultuous days. Various shrines throughout town honor gods like Lantyr, Janar, and Vekalla.

The people of Nain observe several holidays, such as Trawler's Haul, an annual contest where ramshackle boats and homemade vessels are sent over the waterfall in a death-defying plunge. And Docking Day, when the Stone-hearted and the River Walkers put aside their disagreements to celebrate the founding of Nain.

### Politics

Trade in Nain is the primary concern of the town's commercially-driven Town Council, which is comprised of several members from each group and a few neutral parties (along with a few covert operatives of the [[Otherhood of Fortune]].

### Defense

Like many Arcturian settlements, Nain relies on a voluntary militia to assist the town in times of trouble. Fortunately, the riverfront hamlet's stout bulwarks afford extra security against the region's aggressive fauna just as much as it protects Nain from the ceaseless assault of supernatural storms.

### Lore

Arcane maelstroms regularly manifest along the banks of the River Elenain here, causing calamity both up- and downstream in their wake. Similarly, the water of the River Elenain is known to sometimes spontaneously transmute; this alchemical havoc can prove difficult for the people of Nain, who sometimes go to great lengths to protect their tools, structures, and vessels from preternatural wear.

Nain is routinely threatened by freak tornadoes and the unexplained transmutation of its local waters. Throughout the years, several smaller villages built along the banks of the River Elenain were lost to natural disaster, leaving little more than ruins behind. This unpredictability led to the development of Nain's social stratification.
