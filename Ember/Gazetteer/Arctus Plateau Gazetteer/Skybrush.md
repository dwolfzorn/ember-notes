# Skybrush

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
>  This settlement exists at ⬢ s.3198.2885 in the [[Rustvar Valleys]], northwest of [[Storsa's Strand]].
>
>  - This settlement is visually depicted by the [[Vista: Skybrush]].
> - This settlement is featured in [[A Brush With Death]], [[Reuniting with Ankarist]], and [[Mapping the Myth]].

 Said to “brush the sky,” this colloquially-named Rustvar settlement is known for being the Arcturian community at the highest elevation outside of the Hersa Mountains — a subject of pride for its citizens, who have acclimated to their high-altitude lifestyle with zeal. The people of Skybrush are predominantly an adventurous and self-made folk, with roots that spread as far as the roads of the Arctus Plateau can carry them.

 Skybrush may be somewhat remote, but it stands protected and ready to accommodate. Both the vantage of the Canopy district's arboreal architecture and the ramparts of the ancient giantkin ruins have proven time and time again to thwart any would-be interlopers and errant wildlife alike. Meanwhile, outside the town limits, smaller homesteads dot a forested, suburban sprawl.

 ## Landmarks - The Canopy

 **The RoostTavern**

An ale-soaked watering hole in the Canopy where spirits can be lifted during times of trouble, The Roost is one of the most reliable sources of information and rumors in Skybrush. Amenities are available here to those in need, including an all-hours kitchen and a half-dozen rooms for rent.

**Invocation RowShrines**

This Canopy-based cul-de-sac houses an array of five shrines to [[Alar]], [[Spectra]], [[Thoma]],[[Janar]], and [[Malae]].

**The Wystful ApothecaryApothecary**

This frontier pharmacy owned and operated by Hescott Wyst is a reliable source of affordable spell components, natural herbal remedies, and common alchemical reagents.

**Cradley's Uncanny BeastsMount Stables**

Situated in a precipitous cavern on the northern edge of Skybrush, this eponymous collection of stables owned by Goose Cradley hosts an uncommon variety of domesticated beasts, including [[Giant Bats]] and [[Dire Wolves]]. These well-trained steeds command a rather hefty price of 500 gp each — unless the buyer is willing to take up a peculiar errand from the eponymous owner for a relative discount.

**The Long HaulConsignment Shop**

A consignment shop owned and operated by the pawn broker Liestra Grann, The Long Haul carries a wide variety of eccentric wares uncommon to more traditional retail outlets. Situated near the southeastern edge of town, The Long Haul's cylindrical two-story edifice is often a welcome sight for travelers in search of retail therapy and creature comforts.

 ## Landmarks - The Understory

 **The BlockhousePrison & Constable's Office**

This two-story edifice in the Understory district houses the Skybrush gaol and a guardhouse office for the town Constable, along with an apartment on the second floor. The gaol itself houses four iron-barred cells secured with heavy locks; characters without the [[Skybrush Constable's Key]] must succeed on a `[[/tool thief dex 18]]` check or a `[[/check str 20]]` check to open.

 **HollowstoneNeighborhood**

This Understory neighborhood of squat Arcturian houses is built in a small gulley on the southeastern edge of town. The homes of Qory Hult and Mrs. Lillium Estweather preside among the dwellings here, alongside several others.

**The Rustic ForkRestaurant**

Many residents of Skybrush consider The Rustic Fork to be a shining example of precisely how good a simple menu of rural Arcturian cuisine can be. The daily specials may change, but the charming, timeworn folk tales of its proprietor stay the same.

**Skybrush General GoodsGeneral Goods Store**

This aptly-named emporium of adventuring gear, tools, and trade goods also carries a modest variety of weapons and armor. An assortment of in-house smithies, led by an Arcturian craftsman named X, are available for simple repairs and commissions.

**Tavoc's DeepQuarry**

This subterranean quarry of ore and stone is the modern incarnation of an ancient Shent mine at the edge of town, and provides a few dozen Skybrush citizens with their hard-earned livelihoods.

## Characters of Interest - The Canopy

**[[Liestra Grann]]Chaotic Neutral, Arcturian Kivahr, she/her**

Liestra Grann is a headstrong Arcturian pawn broker known for her witty banter and ability to suss out a killer deal. She spends her days making a good living by orchestrating the trade of Shent and Varùn artifacts from her Understory storefront, the Long Haul (alongside an array of illicit goods and services, which she only offers to vetted clientele).

**Kel KornanLawful Neutral, Ordani Kiska, she/her**

This accomplished stone and timber broker maintains an apartment in Skybrush, from which she facilitates business matters for [[House Cevher]]. She purchased a cursed [[Rune-Marked Sash]] from Liestra Grann, which has brought her under the damnable sway of [[The Bewilderment]].

**Revelry WystChaotic Good, Arcturian Aster/Human, she/her**

This talented young jeweler was once the muse and "true love" of Dereth Erekos, but recent events have propelled the glamorous ingenue to a grim spotlight. Her days are now marked with sorrow and fear of the lingering evil that threatens Skybrush.

**Hescott WystLawful Neutral, Arcturian Human, he/him**

This shrewd Arcturian apothecary is Revelry Wyst's overprotective father. Hescott operates The Wystful Apothecary from the boughs of Skybrush's Canopy district, and is exceptionally wary of outsiders in light of recent circumstances.

**Gedron TathChaotic Good, Arcturian Kivahr, he/him**

Gedron Tath is an eccentric Arcturian artist whose macabre creations have earned him a somewhat dubious reputation among the citizens of Skybrush. He purchased a cursed [[Rune-Marked Band]] from Liestra Grann, which he wears like a bracelet (and which has become responsible for his own affliction with [[The Bewilderment]]).

**HadrasLawful Good, Arcturian Kivahr, he/him**

Hadras is a tenacious priest of [[Thoma]], learned in the ways of many faiths and generally welcoming of their practitioners. A true servant of the people, Hadras is eager to reflect the rites and rituals of those who come to him seeking divine succor.

**Danton ShainChaotic Good, Ordani Human, they/them**

[[House Bastilla]]'s most qualified expert on Shent artifacts, Danton Shain is a studious Ordani socialite who paradoxically likes to keep a low profile, preferring the privacy of their home parlour to the bustle of local gatherings.

**Shax the SeeressChaotic Neutral, Arcturian Kiska, she/they**

This sly witch of [[Malae]] harbors a curious appreciation for entropy and the arcane. Despite her relatively macabre interests, Shax is eager to help those who come to her with interesting need (and appropriate coin) in-hand.

**Goose CradleyChaotic Neutral, Arcturian Human/Kivahr, he/him**

This madcap Kithil animal handler is the eponymous proprietor of Cradley's Uncanny Beasts. Unconventional to the core, Cradley is known for his tendency to strike up fair trades in exchange for rather peculiar errands.

## Characters of Interest - The Understory

**[[Mica Valaston]]Lawful Good, Ordani Hul'grun, they/them**

Mica Valaston is Skybrush's obdurate and cynical Constable, and stands ready to exercise the duties of their station at a moment's notice. Despite their inherent skepticism, Mica is eager for the party's capable help, and is notably equipped with the [[Skybrush Constable's Key]].

**Qory HultNeutral Good, Arcturian Human, he/him**

This twenty-something Arcturian is a once-proud mineworker who purchased the cursed [[Rune-Marked Arrowhead]] from Liestra Grann. Now, Qory anxiously awaits an untimely death in the Blockhouse gaol after murdering his wife (a result of the Confusion effect caused by the curse of [[The Bewilderment]]).

**Mrs. Lillium EstweatherNeutral Good, Kithil Keth, she/her**

This elderly Skybrush local is Qory & Veera Hult's busybody neighbor. She wears her Kithil roots proudly and enjoys a life of retirement (which allows her to relentlessly exercise her rather nosy dispositions).

**Gurty HoldstoneLawful Good, Arcturian Human, she/her**

Many locals consider this spirited thirty-something washerwoman to be the heart of Skybrush. Gurty chastises any visitors for the ill-fated timing of their arrival to the accursed treetop hamlet, but provides helpful guidance nonetheless.

**Mr. Elyas ErekosNeutral Good, Arcturian Human, he/him**

The despondent father of Dereth and Branos Erekos, this retired mine worker's gregarious temperament has given way to grief. His attempts to remain stoic after the death of one son at the hands of another are rather tenuous.

**Mrs. Chrysta ErekosNeutral Good, Arcturian Human, she/her**

The grief-stricken mother of Dereth and Branos Erekos, inconsolable in the wake of the violence that surrounds her family. Her selfless duties as an experienced governess have fallen to the wayside.

**MattockNeutral Good, Lumek Fej, he/him**

This grizzled, scar-covered mine worker has seen more action than most of Skybrush. A retired warrior who decided to settle down in a foreign land, the stout-hearted Mattock is rather protective of this treetop hamlet he calls home.

## Life in Skybrush

The ardency of the town's settlers is evident in Skybrush via the resourceful artistry and enterprising craftsmanship of its people. Many Skybrush residents generate folk art and music as inspired as the work of any Ordani professional, and the treetop hamlet's homes and businesses are bedecked with various signs of this personal ingenuity and design. The people of Skybrush are proud of their connection to the ancient Shent and Varùn ways, and view their habitation of the ruin-marked landscape as a reverent one (despite the relative exploitation of the area's historical gravesites and monuments).

The mountain settlement is defined by 2 distinct districts: the Canopy and the Understory. There isn’t a great deal of societal stratification between these two districts; homes and places of commerce are generally located where they happen to be based on practical requirements (but there are certainly exceptions). These districts are built in and around the towering boughs of a grove of gravelbark pine trees as well as the cavernous ruins of decrepit Shent and Varùn monuments.

### Culture

In addition to several holidays that honor the regular phases of Ember's moons, the people of Skybrush (both faithful and secular alike) observe an annual Hunter's Eve that marks a celebration of the Shard God Janar's many conquests and the various Leviathans he managed to slay.

Religion is of relatively low importance in Skybrush compared to a more secular reverence for the Cosmos, but a humble scattering of five shrines to various gods serve local citizens of faith. These shrines honor Alar, Spectra, Thoma, Janar, and Malae.

### Defense

As remote as it is, Skybrush persists under recurring threat of encroachment by local wildlife both monstrous and mundane. Access points between the Canopy and Understory districts have been constructed with these assaults in mind, and offer a variety of quick escape or assault options for citizens if and when an attack occurs. Meanwhile, a cadre of bat-riding cavaliers and a volunteer ground force serve as Skybrush’s most capable defenders.

### Politics

Skybrush is a relatively lawless settlement, with community decisions guided by silver and gold (with a small measure of the common good thrown in for safe measure). A town council of eight is elected annually, but is unofficially comprised of members from each of the eight top business enterprises in town rather than the voices of average citizens. Arguably, there are few “average” citizens in Skybrush — for it takes a certain kind of stoicism, inquisitiveness, or wanderlust to call this dangerous mountainside community home.

### Lore

 Skybrush began as a scrappy camp of Kivahr archaeologists and adventurers who established a base of operations in the foothills of the Varùn Lowlands, where ruins and vaults of the ancient Shent and other giantkin promised financial opportunity and cosmic enlightenment. Ingenious to the last, these settlers built some of their homes among the treetops — to both honor the ancient Shent accords with the moon, and be spared from the biome's most menacing ground predators. These treetop dwellings became the camp's trademark, and the name Skybrush was formally adopted by its flourishing people.

 The earliest days of Skybrush actually precede the founding of Ordain by roughly 100 years, but the advent of the great Arcturian metropolis encouraged a rapid expansion of the frontier hamlet's modest sprawl. As Ordain's need for gravelbark pine and mineral ore grew (along with the private interests of groups like the Anachraenum and Ordain's Trading Houses), so did funding for Skybrush development, alongside a swift rise in the Human population.

 Today, Skybrush not only serves as a profound historical waypoint for the ancient traditions of the Shent and Varùn giants, but as an important trading post for travelers and explorers of the western plateau.
