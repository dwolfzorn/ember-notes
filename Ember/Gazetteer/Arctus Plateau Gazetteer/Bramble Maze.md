# Bramble Maze

> [!warning] Gamemaster
> #### Gamemaster's Summary
>
> This location exists at ⬢ s.3202.2900 within the [[Splinter Canyons]], southeast of [[Brevin]].
>
> - This location is featured in [[Amazing Brambles]].

The site itself is relatively small, and the maze grows within a small ravine that is mostly blocked at the surface, leaving only distant filtered light streaming down onto the brambles, which further block out the light even more. Navigation through the maze is possible, and a single route can be followed by using the natural markers in the brambles and flylight insects that call the maze home.

### Environment

While the location is often visited by the Anachraenum, it is still an natural location and is not maintained permanently by the guild. Its path can change or adjust after one of the many flooding events that the Splinter Canyons experience, and even veteran Anachraenum members come to the maze to challenge their skills every few years.

### Ecology

The predominant plant species in the maze are the extremely thick, often between two and five feet thick, tree-like barbed plants, which have evolved to thrive in the low-light conditions. The brambles are interspersed with vibrant glowing insects called Flylights, which have adapted to the dark by developing bioluminescence, creating a mesmerizing yet eerie atmosphere. Other plant life includes hardy mosses and fungi that cling to the damp surfaces. A few small, nocturnal creatures have adapted to the maze’s environment, using the dense thickets for camouflage and protection against predators. There may be occasional sightings of larger creatures that venture into the maze, but they are rare, as the thorns and brambles serve as a natural deterrent, limiting their movement.

### Lore

Long before the Anachraenum discovered the maze, local Arcturian folklore spoke of it as a sacred site guarded by nature spirits known as the Bramble Wraiths. According to legend, these ethereal beings were tasked with protecting the delicate balance of the ecosystem. It is said that the Wraiths could manipulate the growth patterns of the brambles and thorns, reshaping the maze at will to trap those who would harm the land, while guiding those with pure intentions. Over the years, the Anachraenum have determined that this was likely an ancient tribe of Thornlings, possibly dating back nearly a thousand years. Nothing of their presence survives, as they likely built structures using purely natural materials, which explains the curious, maze-like structure that was probably grown to protect a hidden village at some point.
