# The Trawl

- **District Type:** Residential and Industry (shipping, cargo freight, warehouses)
- **Population:** 17,639 (Fairly dense central location with mixed-use dwellings)
- **Lifestyle:** Flashy (residents revere the pirate's life and earning reputation through bold action)
- **Services:** Cargo ships (leasing, repairs, cargo storage)
- **Influence:** House Bastilla's main base of operations for active members

### Culture

Though much of the work in the Trawl involves the not-so-glamorous tasks of moving cargo onto and off of cargo ships, the influence of House Bastilla gives everything a bit of flair, as locals attempt to impress both the Trading House and the captains that dock at the Trawl with public expressions of their competence and bravery. House Bastilla encourages this action, as it keeps coin moving through the district and into the Trading House’s pockets, and uses its Tradeway not just as a place to store and sell goods, but a “see and be seen” area that gives anyone looking to make a name for themselves a literal platform on which to do it.

One side benefit of the flair and flash that the Trawl is known for is the distraction it provides from some of House Bastilla’s more illicit activities. The Trading House’s official stance is that there is nothing beneath the docks except for storage, but it is well known that the Underdocks are used to bring goods that might otherwise be examined by the Hallows for their potentially dangerous magic into the city. For a price, many workers at the Trawl will store these arcane goods in the Underdocks while unloading ships, coming back at night to move them further into the city. Unfortunately, with the Underdocks prone to occasional flooding, arcane items are sometimes lost to sea in the area, and some believe that the waters in this area have strange and arcane powers as a result.

In one of the city’s most exciting annual events, the Platform Plunge, a few brave souls jump from the platform outside of the House Bastilla Tradeway into these waters to prove their bravery and show off for ships looking for new crew. It is rare to take part in the plunge twice, and some claim to have come back changed by whatever arcane forces might be lurking in the water, though this has never been proven.

### Landmarks

**House Bastilla Tradeway**

Located in a repurposed fish market that they’ve had on loan from Lillifeld for at least 100 years (an endless source of mild bickering between the two). The back half of the tradeway is reserved for goods going to the trading houses, other shops, and other major players in Ordain, but the front half offers a number of goods for sale directly to the public, and can be a great source for those looking for obscure arcane items or items from foreign ports.

**Platform**

This rowdy boardwalk and tavern, located outside of the House Bastilla Tradeway, is a popular place for merchants to gather, trade information, and look for new recruits. As a result, hopeful sailors often show off in the area, spending coin ostentatiously or showing off their knot tying skills in between drinks. The Platform Plunge takes place from this area each year, and is hyped up by the Tradway-owned bar, which rakes in a decent amount of coin selling seats to the spectacle each year.

**Surrelli's Ships**

The place to go if you want to either buy/lease a merchant or try to procure passage on one. The owners, Survan and Elli (each of whom took those names when they joined the shop - a requirement of the original owners), often try to suss out who can be taken advantage of by challenging those who want something from House Bastilla to "earn their way on board" with obscure items or challenges of strength and skill while they endlessly delay helping, but a simple bribe can smooth the way forward every time.

### Locals

**Telaviel “Three Hands” Ren (Neutral Evil, Ordani Human, he/him)**

One of the main shopkeeps in the Tradeway, he retired from a career as a sailor after losing his taste for ship life during a particularly rough storm 20 years ago. He often refers to his exploits while on The Waterchaser, the ship he called home, which sank with all hands about 3 months after he left the sailing life behind. There are occasional rumors that he was thrown off of the ship for stealing and sank it himself out of vengeance, but nothing has or ever will be proven.

**Irivani Andiva (Chaotic Neutral, Kithil Human, she/her)**

Irivani Andiva was raised in Ordain, but always preferred life in the dark, becoming a devotee of Thoma and moving to the Kithil villages in her early teen years. Since her return to the city as an adult, she's worked her way up in House Bastilla, and claims to be a small-time merchant giving tours of the River Destine to new visitors. In truth, Irivani runs the traffic of arcane goods that the Hallows might frown on through the Underdocks, often sneaking goods under and past the watchful eye of Vala Surrel, her longtime counterpart in the trade of legitimate items. Irivani is very charming, using her personality to convince captains to work with her to get their goods into Ordain, and she has never lost a full shipment where the captain followed her exact orders, which often involve the use of magical decoys placed to lead Vala astray.

**Vala Surrel (Lawful Neutral, Ordani Kiska, she/her)**

The current Harbormaster of the Trawl, Vala is a fairly influential member of House Bastilla. She made her way to Ordain as a stowaway on the ship of the infamous Darion the Soulthief, and immediately began using her eye for arcane items to price items out and gain the coin needed to move up in the Trading House. Vala oversees all traffic and trade of legitimate goods through the docks, and is in a stalemate with her "counterpart," Irivani Andiva, who manages much of the traffic of less legitimate merchandise through the Underdocks. Though Vala generally works days and Irivani operates at night, the obvious chemistry between the two has led to a betting pool at local tavern Platform about if (and when) the two might become an item. While Vala ignores this, she has kept every magical decoy that Irivani has ever made to deceive her.

### Lore

While the many local beliefs about the arcane-touched water in and around the Trawl are overblown by a large magnitude, in part to steer smugglers away from any thought of stealing from the ships under House Bastilla protection, those who have the ability to detect magic or have an ancestral resonance with it have been known to see a shimmer on the water in and around the Trawl. There are also persistent stories about those who have been somehow changed by the waters, though they will never quite say how, and it is unclear whether or not they are true.
