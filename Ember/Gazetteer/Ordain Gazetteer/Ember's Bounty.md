# Ember's Bounty

- **District Type**: Commercial
- **Density**: Unknown (Crowded. Decks churn with pirates, merchants, and thrill-seekers day and night. The crew, hangers-on, and cutthroats number in the low hundreds.)
- **Lifestyle**: Maritime (Sailors, merchants, pirates and the like are found here.)
- **Services**: Sale and barter of stolen, exotic, or forbidden goods; rough food and drink for the daring. Provisions and drink are cheap, but rare or magical wares can be expensive.

Bastilla’s flagship, Ember’s Bounty dominates any harbor lucky (or unlucky) enough to host her. The Bounty rarely strays far from its fleet, and when it makes berth by a city it becomes the epicenter of vice and trade. While docked it offloads a small army of pirates looking for distraction on leave, and welcomes merchants and criminals of all stripes to come make deals and pick through the loot on offer.

### Culture

Life on the Ember’s Bounty is chaos at the best of times, with hundreds of sailors and officers working together to keep the massive vessel afloat. While the Bounty’s leadership maintains some order while at sea, all bests are off when the ship docks. Drunken brawls, gambling, and wild revels are commonplace when the ship has nowhere to be, with consequence decided on the spot by whatever officers happens to be nearby.

The ship is crewed by a large number of sailors hailing from Ordain, all of them loyal to House Bastilla, or serving under mercenary contracts with their loyalty given to coin and treasure alone. Visiting crews of Bastilla ships always receive priority in all dealings, from accommodations and food to best deals on offered goods.

The ship is so large and the holds so crowded that the vessel runs with several Quartermasters, each in charge of their own sections of the hold. They alone handle negotiation and sale of the goods, and in cases where coin is not sufficient, can be bartered with directly.

Though the ship has a Captain, it at times feels less like any one person is in command, and more as though the ship itself is a living organism with many pieces working in concert.

### Landmarks

**The Heaving Stalls**

When anchored, the sprawl of the ship's deck becomes covered in stalls and displays of goods, with pirate-merchants standing by to take coin or discuss barter. Many stalls also list items which are held below deck, which one of the ship Quartermasters are needed to view and purchase.

**The Grand Galley**

Amidships, this sheltered hall runs lengthwise and is stacked with plank-benches and tables that make up the ship's communal mess. Guests are welcome to come sit and eat, drink, and watch the chaos unfold. Prepared food and drink can be had here for a modest sum, and all manner of foodstuffs, from rare ingredients to uncommon vintages of foreign booze can be bought from the quartermaster here.

**The Batteries**

A deck unto itself, the Batteries is a gun-deck lined with converted ballistae meant to defend the ship. Each one is etched with magical runes and mystic symbols that allow them to fire rapidly without needing ammunition. These mystic ballistae can unleash bolts of magical fire, acid, lightning to keep enemies at bay. Its existence is kept a secret from the outside world, and the crew can count on one hand the number if times the gun ports have had to be opened.

**The Endless Holds**

This massive, compartmentalized cargo hold is like a maze, and many a soul have ended up lost amongst the countless stacks of crates, barrels, and chests. Nothing is labeled, and only the quartermasters aboard the ship truly know what each container holds. It is said that the masters of the hold have magical devices that allow them to know the contents of their sections at all times.

### Locals

**Captain Stonne Torish (Neutral, Cascillian Human, he/him)**

Captain Torish carries himself with a reserved but ironclad dignity. Weather-burnished and silver-haired, he often oversees the Bounty from the immense quarterdeck, surrounded by his officers and most trusted crew. As most captains, Stonne leaves the day-to-day operation of the ship to his quartermasters and only truly takes commend when the vessel is sailing to a new destination.

**First Quartermaster Lynet Sarrow (Lawful Neutral, Kelmezian Human, she/her)**

Lynet is the ship’s logistics brain, strict and unflappable, overseeing all intake and movement of cargo. She primarily deals with the second quartermasters under her supervision, and otherwise leaves them to mind their goods. However, in the event that there is a dispute, she is regarded as the final judge of barter-worthiness of all items.

**Quartermaster Tion Marvek (Lawful Neutral, Bejak Kivahr, he/him)**

Tion is lean, sharp-featured, and famously unflappable. One rarely sees him without his arcane ledger — a thick, rune-bound tome said to magically update itself as his cargo holds change their contents. Tion deals primarily in magical items, magical reagents, spell components, scrolls, spell books, potions, and similar items of mystical power.

**Maasathtar “Moss” Enki (Chaotic Good, Lumek Fej, he/him)**

A broad-shouldered, tattooed man with an ear for gossip and a love of strange spice combinations, “Moss,” Maasathtar reigns supreme in the Grand Galley. He delights in sourcing the rarest ingredients the Bounty’s nomadic market can offer and inflicts the culinary experiments on any foolish enough to agree to try it. His kitchen is always open, brimming with experimental dishes and hearty stews that keep spirits buoyed in any weather. He oversees all trades in the Galley, not unlike a Quartermaster.

### Lore

Most accounts state that the ship appeared roaming the seas about a century ago, but its actual origins remain a great mystery. Its size along makes it too large to have been built in any but the largest shipyards, and many believe that it is Cascillian in origin, but no record of its construction exists in any known shipyard.

The make and design of the great vessel are unique, lending further mystery to its history and creation. The ship also features extensive mystical reinforcement that would have taken great skill and resource to create, including alchemically treated iron that never rusts, transmuted wood that never leaks, and magically threaded sails that catch the wind like no other.

As a result, rumors about the ship are numerous, and range from plausible to insane. Some say that the ship was made by the Cascillian Republic in secret and that the ship was stolen by House Bastilla. Others claim that it was build by the Trading house directly, again, in secret. Some believe it was created by one or more gods, and given to a captain for some unknown purpose. However, the real origin of the ship remains unknown, and the crew are happy to feed the rumor mills and never tell the truth, likely because not even they know.

Given the piracy, crime, and debauchery it brings with it, elements in the Ordinate actively attempt to make Ordain itself hostile to the vessel, but House Bastilla always manages to bribe and intimidate the right people to keep the docks open to their flagship.
