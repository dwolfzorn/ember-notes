# Quarry Run

- **District Type:** Residential and Commercial
- **Population:** 7,422 (Medium. Town homes, apartments, and shops with moderate foot traffic).
- **Lifestyle:** Tight-knit (A community of "live together work together" quarry workers, haulers, and stone cutters).
- **Services:** Stonemasonry (including education), Sentimental Trinkets, Hauling, Storage.
- **Influences:** Closely tied to House Cevher, which has its headquarters in the area.

### Culture

Quarry Run is still very much a place in recovery, after losing over 100 members of its tight-knit community of haulers, quarry workers, stone masons, and House Cevher-affiliated traders in the Stonehollow Quake of 2309, which brought down several of the district’s famous stone arches and decorative towers. In the afermath, locals came together to rebuild, creating memorial benches, new expressions of stoneworking prowess that emphasize safety, and the occasional dig at House Cevher, which was widely blamed for the quake.

Families that once guarded their trade and craft secrets from their neighbors started openly sharing as the community came together to clear the rubble, take down any structures that could possible collapse in the future, and create memorials to those who were lost. In the decades since the event, this spirit of communal sharing has remained part of Quarry Run.

Today, those who grow up in Quarry Run, or are simply interested in stonemasonry, metalworking, or mining, are brought through a series of apprenticeships with local experts that are intended to spread knowledge. The niches in the stone pillars at the community's center, while not created for this purpose, have been a great way to attract those who might be interested in trying their hand at some small work of craftsmanship. But not every rift has been healed by time. With House Cevher largely blamed for the Stonehollow Quake, relatives of those lost in the tragedy have demanded a series of reforms and payments that they believe will make House Cevher safer, but that the House believes is starting to feel like retribution for a tragic accident that they were not actually to blame for.

### Landmarks

**The Cevher Tradeway**

A large stone edifice that holds the business offices of House Cevher. While it is carved from stone on the outside, to match the facades of the other buildings nearby, the inside is divided into rooms and areas that feature the different materials that House Cevher works with, from the grand Silver Staircase to a long hallway known as Iron Alley. This metal reinforcement protected the Tradeway from any damage during the Stonehollow Quake, leading some to believe that Cevher protected its members better than the public around it.

**Folly Plaza**

The gates and archways on the outskirts of Quarry Run end in this large plaza, which features a number of stone columns in varying styles, many of which include niches that can hold small items, good wishes, fortunes, and even coins. The Plaza also holds Quarry Run’s famous Whispering Columns, which take short messages whispered in one place and broadcast them, at random times and with distorted voices, at another. Though these were once used primarily for people to make frivolous confessions and a source of tips for the Veiled Chain, they now occasionally include accusations of House Cevher's negligence, even though it has been 2 decades since the Stonehollow disaster.

**The Quarry Arches**

Though the gate between Stonehollow Quarry and Ordain was once used to regulate traffic in and out of the city, the need for haulers to move large quantities of stone into Ordain on a regular basis led to it becoming permanently open and renamed locally to the Quarry Arch. Other arches soon followed, used primarily to direct quarry haulers to the proper warehouse and provide walking bridges for foot traffic when larger goods were moving through the streets. After several of the walking bridges collapsed during Stonehollow Quake, doing the majority of the damage, they were all taken down and reshaped into a number of benches throughout the district dedicated to the victims of the disaster. Their pillars still stand, and some argue that new magically-reinforced bridges should be created between them, but these have yet to be attempted.

**Wrought Works**

This metalworks shop focuses on the ways in which stone and metal can work together, and was created in part by a group of stone and metal workers who helped to build the Cevher Tradeway building. Wrought Works is known for its part in the apprenticeship program, which lures in new potential apprentices by working with passersby to weld small metal figurines that can be put into the niches in the Folly Plaza columns, representing recent accomplishments, odes to loved ones, and small works of art. It also offers work spaces to craft people who need specialized equipment, including the enterprising **Darzeni Volle**.

### Locals

**Kallie Cevher (Chaotic Good, Ordani Aster, she/her)**

One of the younger members of House Cevher, Kallie has made it her mission to repair some of the rifts caused by the Stonehollow quake within the district by writing the “true story” of the incident for its 20-year anniversary. Though her more brash style is often at odds with older members of the house, and there are many who wish she would leave the subject of the disaster alone, she is convinced that she’s in the right and will one day mend enough fences to be considered for House Steward.

**Elwin Lathel (Neutral Evil, Ordani Human, he/him)**

Elwin worked for years as a hauler, moving goods to and from the quarry, and complained about it the entire time. Eventually, he managed to take over the Whitestone Storehouse after its previous manager was killed on the day of the Stonehollow Quake, though unbeknownst to most, Elwin simply killed the man during the chaos. Despite this, Elwin has become one of the loudest critics of House Cevher’s role in the quake, demanding payouts from House Cevher for victims that he distributes while skimming some of the money off the top.

**Darzeni Volle (Neutral Good, Ordani Human, she/her)**

Darzeni, who trained as an ironshaper, lost the use of her legs to a rock crush during the Stonehollow Quake and dedicated herself to creating assistance devices that combine metals with magic to give mobility to those who suffer injuries that the Healing Houses are unable to heal. While she is most proficient with iron, she is always looking to expand her offerings, and has been seen following Silverbeam constructs through the city or quizzing Arcturian construct makers on their techniques in an attempt to broaden her skills.

**Everly Wainsight (Lawful Neutral, Ordani Human, they/them)**

Everly is technically employed by House Cevher to keep an eye on things in the district and prevent vandalism of the follies pillars, but they also report any major activities to the Veiled Chain and look for any structural issues in the stonework in the area on behalf of the Hallows. While getting paid by all three has brought them a decent amount of wealth, they give a good deal of it away, hiding coins in the Stone Folly niches for passersby to find.

### Lore

While House Cevher was not, in fact, to blame for the earthquake known locally as the Stonehollow Quake, they have been unable to prove it, as the timing of the quake aligned with a push by the House to dig more deeply into the Quarry. The House briefly investigated internally, but eventually decided not to try to prove its innocence, instead focused on putting money towards repairs and working on the development of wards to prevent deaths if there are any future quakes.
