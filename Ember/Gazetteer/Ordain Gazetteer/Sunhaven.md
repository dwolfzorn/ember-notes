# Sunhaven

- **District Type**: Residential and Institutional
- **Population**: 6,523 (Moderately dense population of residents and clerics of the Temple of Lantyr)
- **Lifestyle**: Righoutsness (Clerics, officials, Kessian traders and settlers)
- **Services**: Spiritual guidance, exaltation, religious education, and practice.

Sunhaven was once a sparsely populated part of the city, with a few scattered Ordani buildings on its edge, until a large group of Kessians arrived, traveling halfway across the world from the Kessian continent and settling in the area. Today, the district is home to a few scattered Kessian natives or travelers who have settled or arrived more recently, however, most inhabitants are descendants of Kessain families who now consider themselves more culturally Ordani or a mix of both cultures.

Alongside the cultural elements, the Kessian settlers brought with them their devout worship of the Sun Goddess Lantyr. The district not only maintains this tradition but has aggressively built upon it. Today, this worship falls under what is locally known as the "Radiant Eye." This group is considered fairly extreme, and some of the recently arriving Kessians believe the worshippers belong to a sect rather than the primary religion in Kessia, which is why many call it the Sect of the Radiant Eye. These worshippers are deeply fanatical and have connections with many powerful supporters from across the world, including groups from the Kessian homeland.

### Culture

The district's population is a mix of Ordani and Kessian descendants, which means that a proud Kessian cultural element is visible throughout the district. The people are profoundly influenced daily by the worship of Lantyr, revered as the bringer of light, warmth, and life. Families often begin their day with morning rituals, lighting candles or lanterns to invoke her blessings or spending time in the sun among the community, seated or kneeling in the streets, or attending daily services in the Temple of Lantyr. Songs, exaltations, and the loud calls of those expressing their devotion are common.

Much like the Kessian culture itself, women hold important roles in both family and community leadership, making decisions and guiding those around them. Although the strict matrical leadership system is not enforced in the city, unlike distant Kessia, many of the district residents abide by these ideas at a family and community level, and the High Priestess of the Temple of Lantyr is one in a long line of feminine leaders.

### Landmarks

**The Grand Temple of Lantyr**

A stunning architectural marvel made of whitestone and golden sheathed metal tiles. This massive temple is adorned with intricate carvings that tell the stories of the Kessian people, their mythology, and belief system. Inside, soft golden light filters through mesh-covered windows, creating thousands of small beams that reflect off the polished golden stone floors. The temple hosts daily worship services, one at dawn and another at midday, along with special ceremonies and festivals that draw people from all over the district to honor Lantyr at certain times of the year. The current High Priestess of the Radiant Eye is the venerated Okia Tyrwar, who leads both the clerics overseeing the temple and the majority of the district's population.

**Kessian Embassy**

A small, oddly quiet building hidden away in the depths of the district that is currently boarded up. Kessia itself does not maintain regular contact with Ordain, and its trading ships are few and far between. The previous ambassador passed away several years ago, and the only remaining official then decided to retire and open a café.

**Golden Path**

A serene walkway along the district's main road leading to the Temple of Lantyr, illuminated by warm orange lanterns that cast enough light in the evenings for the street to be considered as bright as daytime. This path is popular for evening strolls and is lined with benches and carpeted areas for kneeling in quiet reflection. Along the way, there are small altars dedicated to Lantyr where people can pay their respects or offer thanks for blessings received.

**The Fabled Rift**

This large tavern is a popular spot for adventurers, wanderers, and seekers of wisdom, as it features a deep rift in the earth that descends into a spiraling dark maze. Many believe that the rift is a gateway to the Pathways, and locals have many strange stories about the canyon, which is said to be a remnant of a battle between light and darkness, where one can feel the tangible tension of opposing forces. In truth, it is relatively shallow, and its passages have been mapped. At least, that's what most people say who have returned from exploring its depths, but every now and then, people or groups go missing and are never heard from again.

### Locals

**High Priestess Okia Tyrwar (Lawful Neutral, Ordani Human, she/her)**

Okia comes from a long line of clerics of the Radiant Eye, and her grandmother was also the high priestess during her time. She was raised in Sunhaven and has dedicated her life to worshipping Lantyr, becoming one of the temple's most outspoken figures in recent memory. Her leadership is marked by deep ties to the traditions of both Kessian and Ordani cultures, but she believes that the vast majority of the population is far too lax in their veneration of the gods and of Lantyr herself, seeing the recent troubles and increasingly turbulent times as a consequence of their inaction. She is ardently passionate about her followers taking measures to enact change and to help others, and her rousing speeches have reached beyond Sunhaven and into the city population as a whole, which recently led to her being elected by the Hallows to a seat on the Ordinate itself.

**Kerit Malroth (Chaotic Neutral, Kessian Kiska, he/him)**

Malroth comes from a strict Kessian family in the city of Red Rhuin, where they place great importance on devoted worship of the Sun Goddess, Lantyr. Unlike many in Ordain, he is originally from Kessia and traveled across the seas to join the clerics of the Radiant Eye when he arrived in the city a decade ago. However, unknown to many, he is actually a member of the Kessian Iquistorias and was assigned by the organization to report on both Ordain and the Sect of the Radiant Eye itself. Not only is he fanatical, but Malroth is also deeply mistrustful of others, seeing corruption by the outer darkness everywhere.

**Lira Natus (Chaotic Good, Ordani Human/Nir'ae, she/her)**

Mira is a passionate resident of Sunhaven who travels across the city attempting to expose the calmer and more measured teachings of Lantyr as she sees them. As a descendant of both Ordani and Kessian heritage, she deeply appreciates her eclectic background and seeks to promote coexistence and cultural celebration. Energetic and determined, Mira often rallies her friends and neighbors for sun parties, helps those in need, and maintains the city as a whole. Unfortunately, most within Sunhaven, including her own family, consider her to be a borderline heretic, and only the laws of Ordain prevent her from being targeted by violence or reprisals.

### Lore

The district has gone through many changes over the years and started as a sparse area where a few Ordani families began to build on the edges of an expanding city. In  AS2397, a large group of Kessains arrived and settled in the area, increasing its population. They quickly set about constructing buildings in their own cultural design and erected a central temple for worship, which quickly became a place where Ordani residents themselves came to worship. This attracted the attention of several trading houses and the Hallows, who, realizing the potential this held for attracting support to their various causes through public opinion, began financially supporting the growing priesthood of Lantyr.

Nearly twenty years later, the first stones were laid for a vast grand temple to the Sun Goddess. That temple then took a further fifty-nine years to complete, during which time its scale and scope were expanded, leading to the huge edifice overlooking the city today.
