# Grand Meadows

- **District Type:** Recreational with Commercial and Institutional
- **Population:** 37 (Very Low. Groundskeepers, with moderate-to-heavy foot traffic).
- **Lifestyle:** Modest-to-Comfortable (Paid groundskeepers and career Cindarics).
- **Services:** Leisure, food & drink, souvenirs.

The mile-wide park complex known as Grand Meadows is defined by an assortment of exterior landmarks, including the expansive Nestor Field, the verdant Springside Arbor, and the tranquil Abbot's Pond, alongside beloved structures like Adonia's Gazebo, the Wayroot Greenhouse, and the Vonwyn Playscape. The stone paths that weave through and around these locations of interest are often lined with street vendors and ambling citizens, which instantly betrays the Grand Meadows as an inviting place of organic harmony.

Historically, the swath of land that comprises Grand Meadows was identified by Adonia herself as an area worthy of conservation, and one of her seminal edicts as the first Holy Speaker of the Cindaric Sages catered precisely to this effort. Ever since, the terrain northwest of Cindarin Temple has been protected and tended to by the sages themselves alongside a guild comprised of select residents from surrounding districts (both paid and volunteer alike); and a small collection of dormitories and apartments near the district's perimeter cater to these munificent folks.

### Culture

The culture of Grand Meadows tends to be defined by its visitors rather than its local inhabitants, which are few and far between. As a shared common space, the Meadows represent all the diversity Ordain has to offer. And thanks to the district's intrinsic sense of harmony and natural balance, the people who populate and visit this historic neighborhood tend to be warm in spirit and welcoming in disposition.

### Landmarks

**Nestor Field**

"Slow your wheels in Nestor Field," they say. This urban stretch of grassland represents the largest of the eponymous Grand Meadows, and is marked by its wide, sweeping hills and the presence of a trio of sacred trees — known collectively as the Sagetrees — located in equidistant isolation throughout the meadow. Nestor Field is large enough to accommodate gatherings like weddings and organized sporting events alongside the scores of Ordani locals who simply enjoy lounging and relaxing among the meadow's grassy expanse.

**Adonia's Gazebo**

This beautiful stone and glass gazebo sits near the shores of Abbot's Pond at the end of a short pier, and was built to honor the city's founder Adonia, the first Holy Speaker of the Cindaric Sages who also established the Grand Meadows and its conservancy. Adonia's Gazebo is used for various purposes, including religious and cultural ceremonies, local gatherings, and civic meetings; when it's not in use for some other official purpose, the Gazebo is often home to relaxed readers who appreciate the historical edifice for its tranquil shade.

**The Vonwyn Playscape**

Designed by the famed sorcerer-architect Adlas Vonwyn, this modern playground was created as a recreational space for the children and adolescents of Ordain, who proudly enjoy the Playscape's subtle implementation of low-tier enchantments — recognizable in such features as the location's faerie fountain, living topiary, and musical trampolines.

### Locals

****Karsen Stole** (LG, Ordani Human, he/him)**

This aged Cindaric Sage serves as the dutiful Senior Groundskeeper of Grand Meadows, and is responsible for the administration of local staffing and the conservancy of the Meadows themselves. Although some regard the even-tempered Sage Stole to be a rather prickly fellow, those who know him well understand this tough exterior to be a ruse.

****Neverly Phandren** (LN, Ordani Human, she/her)**

Neverly is a boisterous Events Planner who works under the attentive guidance of Ordain's Greensmans Guild. When she isn't wandering the Meadows, she can often be found stationed near Adonia's Gazebo and the surrounding waterways, which she takes great inspiration from.

****Brina Thoulios** (NG, Arcturian Human, she/her)**

This kind-hearted street vendor makes a good living in Grand Meadows selling fresh fruit and Arcturian flowercraft dolls as keepsakes and souvenirs. A native of Helkas, Brina is well-learned in nearly every plant species that can be found in the gardens and conservatories of the Meadows.

### Lore

A variety of tall tales and rumors surround the three Sagetrees that grow within the confines of Nestor Field, ranging from harmless urban legends to barmy conspiracy theories. One of the more popular tales is the legend of The Sage's Suitor, the story of a Waerd druid of the Duskflare Jungles who fell in unrequited love with Adonia; as the story goes, the Suitor had nothing to offer Adonia for her hand in marriage, so he planted three seeds in her beloved Grand Meadows to represent the potential for love between them. Another well-known tale is the story of the Oaken Aster named Kassus Brak; they say when Kassus passed away, all that remained of him were three enchanted seeds — which eventually became the regal Sagetrees recognized today.
