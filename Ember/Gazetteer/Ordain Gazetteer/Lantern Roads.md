# Lantern Roads

- **District Type:** Residential with some commercial (niche shops, mail services, illicit goods)
- **Population:** 8,512 (Moderately dense population of residents who appreciate privacy and thieves who use that privacy for contraband)
- **Lifestyle:** Secretive (Both regular residents and thieves take advantage of the shadows and alleys in the district and don't ask too many questions)
- **Services:** Lanterns, courier services, movement of illicit goods
- **Influences:** Unofficially affiliated with Trading House Wandren

### Culture

Lantern Roads is easy to get lost in, mostly by design. It was once a storage area that began working to deter thieves and bandits by keeping goods moving from place to place, and its residents usually own more than one space in the area, moving from one to the next over the course of the year and using the shadowboxes on their windows to provide clues about their current location. This has made the district an ideal place for those who move illicit goods through Ordain, though the area also attracts those who wish to keep a low profile or hide out from those who may wish them harm. Many of the otherwise small homes and shops have secret access to tunnels, false walls, and other ways to move people and goods from one place to the next without moving out in the open, which is complemented by the work of spellcasters who use magical light and darkness within the neighborhood’s winding streets to draw the eye of observers away from where important deliveries and pick-ups are happening.

In addition to their practical use in indicating what can be found inside of a particular home or establishment, or in sending messages to hidden contacts, the tableau windows of Lantern Roads have become a distinctive decorative element of the area, often shown off during local festivals, when they are used to tell stories of Ordain's history and share current gossip. Different groups within Lantern Roads use different strategies to create these shadowbox windows, with some preferring small mechanical devices called shadowcasters that create specific and elaborate scenes when backlit, some using ribbons and hand-made puppets, and others relying on illusion magic. This has also made the district somewhat popular with avant-garde theater houses, whose odd light shows and limited audiences fit in beside the other specialty shops in the region.

### Landmarks

**The Lighthouse**

A safehouse operated by House Wandren that connects the alleys of Lantern Roads with the lofty heights of Marlstone via a network of back alleys. Named in jest because its ground floor entrance is found in a completely shadowed doorway. Currently occupied by Vitt Wandren.

**The Prancing Goat**

A tavern and waystation for gossip that moves throughout Lantern Road, hosted by whoever manages the property that it currently sits within. It can always be found by looking for a goat hidden within a tableau in the area - whichever shadowbox currently has it in the background holds the bar's current location.

**Shadowbos Souvenirs**

Believed to be one of the oldest buildings in Lantern Roads, it is one of the few that does not use light in its picture window, opting instead for a display of swirling shadows. It also does not move, making it a strong reference point for anyone visiting the area. Despite its somewhat foreboding facade, it holds only a small souvenir shop, which sells small shadowcasters to those who wish to make their own shadow displays at home and is a staging ground for locals who make their money on tours.

**Terracini's Tavern**

Taking its name from the legendary thief Terracini, and run by **Penni Terracini**, who is no relation and was born Penne Talarand before taking over an empty storefront in the area, this Tavern always looks completely empty, other than when it hosts a show by one of the avant-garde illusionist groups that use it as a theater space. That said, there are a number of deals that go down in the Tavern, which uses some of the same magic found throughout the Lantern Roads to hide a few corners and shadowy back rooms where the real action happens.

### Locals

**Cavilla Rossin (Neutral, Arcturian Human, she/her)**

Cavilla relocated to Lantern Roads to hide out after inheriting a bunch of goods from a group of bandits that attacked her and died in the process, leaving her with all of their goods. Unsure what to do with the somewhat ill-gotten gains, she spends her time in Lantern Roads trying to move some of the goods and do "good works" that will assuage a bit of her guilt about some of the items, which include some sentimental treasures that the bandits once stole.

**Fozil Dalm (Lawful Good, Ordani Signborn, he/him)**

The owner of a barely successful watchmaking enterprise, **Just in Time**, which sells watches that are notoriously bad at consistently telling time. He has been trying to use his lack of business sense to become one of House Wandren's contraband runners for years, but it is well-known in the district that he is an informant for the Veiled Chain, and locals wishing to keep their enemies close periodically buy watches from him to keep his business afloat.

**Penni Terracini (Chaotic Neutral, Ordani Human,she/her)**

A failed thief who has rebranded themselves, taking on the name of the famed Lantern Roads scoundrel Terracini and running Terracini's Tavern, which serves drink but also hosts a group of illusionists who hold impromptu theater shows that showcase the latest in light and illusion magic.

**Vitt Wandren (Chaotic Neutral, Ordani Human, they/them)**

Vitt keeps a general eye on how goods are moving through Lantern Roads as part of their role as a member of House Wandren. They take liberal bribes from those in the area in a protection racket that threatens to disassociate local residents and stores with House Wandren if they don't pay up. Vitt has noticed some of the recent changes within the House and is resistant to them, not because of any loyalty to Ordain or the House, but because they believe it will cut into their profits.

### Lore

Lantern Roads was originally home only to storage areas for goods that came into Ordain by sea, but after a series of high-profile thefts during Ordain’s early days, many members of House Wandren began living in their storehouses and storing goods in their homes. To confuse thieves, groups of Wandren associates would work together to purchase properties and use them interchangeably for living, storage, and sales, making it unclear where valuable goods would be stored and could be intercepted.

The origin of the use of lightboxes is less clear, though some believe it was a ploy by the great thief Terracini, who agreed to turn himself into authorities by asking them to meet him at the "House of Shadows" on a specific day and then paid his neighbors to create shadowboxes so that they could not determine which house was the correct one.
