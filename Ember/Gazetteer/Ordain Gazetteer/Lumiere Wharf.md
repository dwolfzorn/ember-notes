# Lumiere Wharf

- **District Type**: Commercial with some Residential
- **Population**: 1,000 (Low. Crowds gather for trade, spectacle, and business at all hours but few live here.)
- **Lifestyle**: Eclectic (Rhivan residents, wandering visitors, and cityfolk flood the district daily)
- **Service**: Lodging, Retail shops, trinkets and souvenirs, restaurants, teleportation services.
- **Influences**: The presence of numerous Rhivan visitors, merchants, and transplanted citizens has created a small enclave of Rhivan culture not found anywhere else in the city.

Defined by the interplay of Ordani and Rhivan culture, the constant flow of traffic from within and city and beyond, and ever present glow of magic, Lumiere Wharf is Ordain’s symbolic and literal portal to the Rhivan realms.

Across Lumiere Wharf, Ordani stone construction is layered with elements of the Rhivans: numerous windows, crystalline lamps, and flourishes of blue and gold that contrast the white and red of Ordain. Most striking are the levitating sapphire crystals: some small as a lantern, others the size of a wagon, with the largest standing two stories high. Each crystal casts azure light across the avenues at all hours, lending the wharf the sensation of perpetual twilight in the darker hours of the day.

### Culture

Locals in the district are a mixture of Ordani and Rhivan transplants, which has lead to the district becoming a self-contained pocket of Rhivan culture. These assorted locals and foreign visitors mingle in the gem-lit streets of the district every day, creating a lively and eclectic atmosphere unlike anywhere else in the city.

It is not uncommon to encounter impromptu musical performances, magical demonstrations, market stalls offering goods both mundane and arcane, and the exchange of half a dozen languages in any crowd.

The Rhivan staff of the Destine Sanctum work closely with the city’s Trading Houses and bureaucrats to smooth potential conflicts and keep incidents to a minimum. Permission for portal travel is strictly regulated, and even the boldest smugglers think twice about crossing the Sanctum’s ever-present watchers.

### Landmarks

**The Rhivan Gateway**

An immense teleportation circle inlaid into the wharf’s central plaza, this fixture connects Ordain to the Arcanic Portal Network run by the Rhivan Star Mages. It was built and is maintained by a local contingent from the Starsworn Academy and is large enough to accommodate entire caravans of goods. When the gateway activates, the nearby crystals flare bright blue, pulsing in tune and time with the gateway's energy.

**The Destine Sanctum**

A multi-tiered structure blending pale Ordani stone with Rhivan love of high-peaked rooves and numerous windows, it is home to a multitude of offices as well as private residences. Serving as embassy, trade consulate, and home for the Rhivan Star Mage that oversees the portal, this structure is busy at almost all hours. Visitors are constant, with petitioners, bureaucrats, tourists and traders flowing through it like the tides. Its upper levels are closed via magical wards and serve as residence to the Rhivans that operate the location.

**The Roundshore Market**

A bustling row of market pavilions laid out along the river wall and overlooking the Destine. The Roundshore Market is home to merchants, performers, cooks and more gathered to peddle a mixture of local good and Rhivan imports. All manner of things can be found here, and its proximity to a bridge and local ferry docks means it sees heavy traffic at all hours.

**Celestinian Hall**

Once a historic Ordani estate belonging to a now forgotten great house, it was renovated extensively to become a mixture of tavern, inn, and temple. This mulit-story building serves as a popular stopping point for newly arrived and local Rhivans alike. The shrines to Spectra and The Weaver are well cared for by its owners, and the building is never empty of patrons.

### Locals

**[[Eveis Brightstone]] (Lawful Neutral, Rhivan Carrow, she/they)**

Assigned to Ordain some time ago by the Starsworn Academy, the aquatic Carrow oversees the day to day operations of the Gate and Sanctum in the district. She is both a magical consultant for the Ordinate and local Rhivan mages, and the leader of the team responsible for keeping the gate calibrated and stable.

**Tillan Harke (Neutral Good, Ordani Human, he/him)**

A clever interpreter and guide, Tillan offers his services to new arrivals, providing translation, cultural advice, guidance and arrangements for touring Ordain. He is always ready with a recommendation for his cousin’s restaurant across the river for those that want a bite.

**Cassan Vey (Lawful Good, Rhivan Human, he/him)**

Head of the Destine Sanctum's trade offices, Cassan oversees operations with a cool and detached demeanor. He spends his day mediating disputes, certifying foreign wares, and ensuring taxes are paid on time and in full. He is known and regarded well for his fairness and rigid adherence to trade law.

**Nestos Narvo (Lawful Good, Rhivan Human, he/him)**

Owner and operator of the Celestinian Hall, Nestos is both a highly dedicated and religious Rhivan, and a fastidiously dedicated businessman. He bought and founded the hall decades ago with his two sisters, and they still operate it to this day.

**Vella Seriane (Chaotic Neutral, Rhivan Signborn, she/her)**

A flamboyant Rhivan entertainer and illusionist, Vella retired to Ordain in her old age and bought a stake in the Celestinian Hall where she is a familiar sight. She spends her evenings dazzling crowds with stories, illusions, and elaborate light displays.

### Lore

Long ago, this district was the site of a faltering riverside market and dockland. Once part of a coastal spur leading from Exile's landing meant to facilitate movement of goods from the eastern shore of the river, it quickly was bypassed by ongoing development.

Larger, newer market spaces closer to the residents drew away merchants, while modern wharfs and docks on nearby shores diverted ferries and barges with their superior capacity. While attempts were made on more than one occasion to revitalize the district, nothing ever stuck, and it was left as a mish-mash of houses, businesses, old waterlogged docks, and empty lots.

The district limped along for decades until a treaty granting Rhivan access to the land for development was signed. All at once the district became a flurry of activity, buildings were leveled, locals were bought out and displaced and the massive Arcanic Portal Network gateway was constructed. Before long, trade and new residents flowed into the city, followed by coin and resources. The district swiftly transformed in the following decades, becoming the beacon of trade and culture that it is now.

A little known fact about the district is that the numerous floating blue crystals were engineered as stabilizers for the Gateway’s surges, blending function with beauty in classic Rhivan fashion. Each one is tuned to reflect magical energy back toward the gateway, both protecting the city from potentially disruptive energy, and feeding the portal so that its function remains constant and predictable.
