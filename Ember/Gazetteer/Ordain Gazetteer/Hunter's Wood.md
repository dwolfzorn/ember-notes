# Hunter's Wood

- **District Type:** Residential (villas, lodging houses) with some commercial (tours, creature parts)
- **Population:** 7,979 (Moderately dense mix of transient hunters and trackers, alongside wealthier locals who enjoy living close to nature)
- **Lifestyle:** Outdoorsy (Whether out in the nearby Arctus Plateau or in their manicured gsrdens, locals appreciate and value the natural world)
- **Services:** Tourism (monster hunting tours) and materials (creature parts)

### Culture

While the dirt streets of Hunter's Wood don't appeal to everyone, the district remains popular among locals who want the feeling of being away from it all while still remaining in close proximity to the Hallows, Ordinate, and other places of influence in Ordain.

Those who prefer the aesthetic of the wilds to the reality live in majestic second-story villas that use illusion magic and clever construction to appear as if they are sunlit glades or moon-touched grottos, while those who hunt, track, or explore in the Plateau outside of the city live in the basic but serviceable lodgings camouflaged within the trees in the district's forested ground level. Many of these local hunters and druids are quite talented, and Hunter's Wood has become known in Ordain as a place to find rare creature parts, take tours into the Plateau, or train in the use of nature magic with the help of the Anachraenum.

Despite the residents' very different approaches to natural living, they generally get along well, in part because keys to the Hunter's Gate are limited in number. While many are passed down through families, especially among the villa residents, a few change hands each year, given to those who have made significant contributions to the district at an annual gathering called the **Call of the Wild** that is well-known in neighboring districts for its liberal use of birdcalls as an expression of celebration.

### Landmarks

**The Bottomless Grotto**

A local tavern and impromptu trading post hidden behind an illusion of a waterfall. Decorated with furniture shaped to look like tree roots and wild vines, it features a wall carved with a map of the nearby section of the Arctus Plateau. Dangers and treasures have been marked on the map by trackers, using a shorthand not well understood outside of the district.

**The Hunter's Gate**

A small gate that connects this section of Ordain to a "wild" section of the Arctus Plateau frequented by Ordani hunters, trappers, and trackers. A protective enchantment designed by the Anachraenum prevents its location from being found by anyone without a key, even if they have previously traveled through it, and is believed to attract enough wild creatures to the area that it is impossible to overhunt.

**Logroll**

This low-cost lodging area in the center of Hunter's Wood offers places to rest for those who don't have much coin or need much comfort. Its offerings range from bedrolls in weatherproofed tubes decorated to look like fallen logs to tree-slung hammocks for those who prefer sleeping in the open air. Though all of its beds are known for producing less than restful nights, proprietor **Pavel Lenai** (Ordani human, he/him)claims this is a purposeful feature that prevents hunters from accidentally sleeping too deeply while out in the wild.

**Villa Renari**

A lavish villa that uses a combination of elaborate illusions and water spells to create a babbling brook running through the center of a sunny glade. The brook distorts conversations, making eavesdropping difficult. As a result, the villa a popular place to talk about confidential cases or share secrets among Hallows officials in the area.

### Locals

**Dariana (true neutral, Ordani keth, she/her)**

Dariana runs the Bottomless Grotto and is determined to make it one of the top taverns in Ordain. Recently introduced season-long scorecards for hunters to use to prove their prowess by capturing particularly dangerous creatures of the Plateau. Though this is leading some groups to take excessive risks, it is also driving business to the Grotto, especially since she started offering side bets to patrons on which hunter will take the prize.

**Jalik Blaines (chaotic neutral, Waerd human, she/her)**

Jalik has tried for multiple years to make her walking tour business, "Plants: A Waerd's eye view," into a success, but customers find her more surly than personable. This, along with a few non-fatal poisonings caused by her policy of letting her customers taste whatever they want as long as it won't kill them, has caused her to lose her Hunter's Gate key to more tourist-friendly businesses. Bitter over the loss, Jalik has been introducing dangerous plant life into hidden corners of the district, hoping that she will be able to run to the rescue of anyone who stumbles upon them and be given a new key as a hero of the district.

**Filp Kindering (lawful neutral, Ordani human, he/him)**

A prominent judge within the Hallows who inherited Villa Renari despite not particularly liking nature. Has been trying to get in the spirit by frequenting the Bottomless Grotto, to the dismay of those who like to get raucous in the tavern and worry that he is judging them.

**Desty Lam (chaotic good, Ordani human, they/them)**

A merchant who regularly ambles through Hunter's Wood looking for those going through the gate to offer potions and other supplies. Desty fancies themselves a brilliant hunter in the making, though their "tips for the wilderness" are always wrong. Will give a deep discount to anyone who can get them a key to Hunter's Gate.

### Lore

Hunters' Gate was used in the early days of Ordain by those who provided food and natural materials to the growing city, including some of the traders who would go on to found the agriculturally-minded House Lillifeld. While Ordani policy might have normally walled it off to prevent against creature attacks, it is home to several prominent members of the Hallows who like the idea that they could quickly escape the city if they ever needed to. The current key system, which limits who has access to the gate, was developed as a compromise.

The existence of Hunter's Wood is also seen as a defense against any escapees from Spellbreaker Tower, who could theoretically be tracked and hunted down by any locals in the area in an environment they might not be otherwise expecting.
