# Lourd's Depot

- **District Type**: Commercial and Transport
- **Population**: 2,219 (Low. The depot is busy at all hours as ferries travel across the river.)
- **Lifestyle**: Maritime. (Dock workers’ dormitories and sailors-only inns are common.)
- **Services**: Cargo handling, storage, passenger ferries, dockside markets, maritime lodging.

Straddling the bustling docks of Lourd's Depot is the Destine river, the eastern branch heading off toward the bay of Ordain, while the eastern branch flows into what used to be a lake but has since become a meticulously maintained waterfront for the depot itself, Restaurant Row, and Pine Harbor to the west.

The district handles much of the northbound goods and is famed as the point where heavy cargo is sorted and disbursed to smaller wagons or ferries. Stacks of crates, merchant banners, and busy cranes define the skyline, while dockside markets overflow with provisions for sailors, travelers, and the city’s working class.

### Culture

Life in Lourd’s Depot revolves around steady work and swift turnarounds. Crews of laborers come and go with the tides, unloading goods day and night, while small-time merchants, and porters stocking up along the piers.

The locals here maintain a rigid sense of duty and honor where a handshake is as good as a written contract, so long as you don’t break your word. Welshers quickly find that their word is worth nothing, and every deal must be in writing, even if they later make amends.

### Landmarks

**Walking Tours**

A small kiosk run by members of the Anachraenum offers local history tours of the district. Guides gather in the central plaza, collecting groups and recounting tales of Lourd’s landing, the vanished lake, and the city’s early days.

**Fork's Ferry**

The oldest ferry operation on the plateau, Fork’s Ferry is famed for its reliability and ability to transport you to any of the adjacent waterfront location for only a silver. Veteran crews work in rotating shifts to ensure that passengers are getting moved across the city’s waterways in regular intervals. The old wooden hulls the crews run aren't fast, or particularly agile, but they are never late.

**The Southern Hold**

A large, secure warehouse that rents out storage by the month for a modest fee. Items stored here are kept secure and can only be accessed by the client and the hold's staff. The Hold is known for its efficiency and discretion.

**Old Crane Tavern**

Located in a a narrow avenue just off the main plaza, the Old Crane Tavern rings with the sound of music and revelry from midday to the evening hours as dock workers and ferrymen pack into its hall. The tavern takes its name from the district’s oldest hand-cranked crane which holds up the tavern's sign.

### Locals

**Vestra Lumar (Lawful Neutral, Ordani Fej, she/her)**

Dockmaster of Lourd’s Depot, Vestre is a brisk, fire-eyed woman who manages scheduling and disputes with a firm, fair hand. She walks the piers at dawn and dusk, keeping careful track of incoming and outgoing cargo. She is known and somewhat infamous for her excellent memory and refusal to cut corners on paperwork.

**Hario Harrick (Chaotic Good, Ordani Kivahr, he/him)**

The most seasoned ferryman on the Fork’s Ferry line, Harrick is an affable, bearded veteran who leads his crew with skill and confidence. A former merchant captain, he retired to a quieter life as a ferryman, though one would argue his life is just as hectic now. He takes pride in the fact he can greet all his regular passengers by name.

**Aldin Farrow (Neutral, Ordani Human, they/them)**

Head clerk of the Southern Hold, Aldin is mild-mannered and precise, easily overlooked among the bustling dockworkers. They are known and somewhat infamous for their numerous relationships with House Bastilla captains, and rumors abound they keep track of numerous pirate treasures.

**Udurin Peck (Lawful Good, Ordani Human, he/him)**

A chatty, gray-haired tour guide for the Anachraenum kiosk, he's been with the guild for decades, and is a dedicated scholar that knows a little bit about almost everything in the city. Peck is a font of obscure city lore and always ready to greet a visitor and show them around.

### Lore

Besides its primary function as a commercial and ferry dock, Lourd's Depot also serves as a historical landmark. Named for being the landing site of Zuryx Lourd, an ambitious merchant looking to establish a foothold in the region and capitalize on the young Ordain's growth.

They arrived with several ships, built a series of warehouses in what was once a natural lake, and began to operate or fund ventures on the eastern banks of the city.

Though Lourd and their original depots are long gone and most have forgotten the origin of the name, the district still serves the same purpose: collecting and moving vital supplies to feed the various ventures and operations in the area.
