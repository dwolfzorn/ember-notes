# Performer's Plaza

- **District Type:** Recreational and Institutional with Residential and Commercial
- **Population:** 4,681 (Medium. Civic centers and apartments with heavy foot traffic).
- **Lifestyle:** Modest-to-Comfortable (Artists, performers, civil servants, laborers, culinary pros).
- **Services:** Public gathering facilities, information services, housing, food & drink.

Performer's Plaza is named after the large public commons at the district's center, which is capable of amplifying the voices of those who perform within the confines of its sweeping, convex stonework. The plaza lives up to its name daily, as countless musicians, artists, and street performers shuffle among its ample stone stages and airy palisades. Of course, only one artist may occupy the stage of honor at any given point in time; known as Exhibition Court, this small amphitheater makes the most use of the plaza's marvelous architecture, and is reserved for larger shows and whatever performances the local Performer's Guild deems the most worthy or adequate.

Most of the time, the central plaza is used for recreation and public gatherings, and the enclosing neighborhood caters to these gatherings with a variety of eateries, taverns, and market stalls. Noises emitted here travel farther than anywhere else in town; and while that means practically little in modern Ordain — where sounds are quickly swallowed by the din of the city — a certain belief that the music and voices of the people can inspire the whole of Ordain from one central location endures.

### Culture

The people of Performer's Plaza are a proud lot, and while there is a fundamental contingent of Ordani locals, the lively neighborhood also attracts a fair amount of transplants to its progressive and welcoming streets. As one might imagine, several Arcturians populate the area, along with Imperial, Cascillian, and Luma expatriates. If Ordain has a "melting pot" outside of its most commercial districts, Performer's Plaza is most likely that place.

### Landmarks

**Exhibition Court**

Exhibition Court is the name given to the central-most amphitheater of Performer's Plaza, where a convex stone commons is surrounded by dozens of rows of tiered stone benches. Outside of the public concerts and plays for which it is famous, Exhibition Court also serves as a public gathering area — a vivacious park made of elegant mortar and stone. Whether or not a show is happening, people can be found gathered around Exhibition Court at nearly all times of the day.

**The People's Voice**

This large edifice in Petitioner's Plaza is one of Ordain's famed Noticeries — public buildings where information is freely traded, including everything from job postings and solicitations for adventuring services to public compliments and complaints (anonymous and otherwise). Due to its location in Performer's Plaza, The People's Voice also provides information regarding citywide initiatives related to the arts, like details about concerts and live shows in other districts.

**East Street Market**

This sprawling indoor/outdoor complex hosts a score of different merchants and vendors, whose respective stalls and alcoves can be as diverse as the people of Ordain themselves. Nearly half of the circuitous place resembles a farmer's market, playing host to a variety of fresh fruit and meat vendors alongside a wide array of international eateries. Other merchants provide equipment and trade goods, often shifting their inventories and prices to take advantage of whatever notices might be posted this week at The People's Voice. If you can't find what you're looking for at the East Street Market, someone here can guide you in the right direction.

### Locals

****Ash Danwren** (CG, Ordani Human, he/him)**

This vociferous clerk for The People's Voice is known for his intense passion for Ordani gossip, which many locals find more humorous than engaging (often to his amusing chagrin). Nevertheless, Ash dutifully mans his post with a stalwart eye for news that might have an effect on local citizens and the people of the surrounding plateau. Ash believes music can be a powerful tool for change, and encourages most of the minstrels and bard he encounters to heed that call for progressivism.

****Merida Hardbark** (NG, Cascilian Thornling, she/her)**

A retired smuggler who settled in Ordain nearly a decade ago, this wily old merchant runs a popular stall in the East Street Market that specializes in curious beneficial saps, alchemical herbs, and botanical spell components, alongside a small array of plant-based clothing and equipment. Merida is an experienced information broker, and always has an opinion about the jobs and notices posted at The People's Voice, as well as general Ordani and Arcturian gossip.

****Eli Eld** (CG, Ordani Human, he/they)**

This moody young street musician is a kithara player known for his sonorous tenor voice, a soft and haunting sound that's perfectly at home within the amplified confines of Performer's Plaza. A relatively new talent, Eli's so incredibly charismatic and skilled that other local musicians and street performers tend to stand back and listen rather than compete for space. Many consider Eli to be the voice of a whole generation of young Ordani, and his compelling songwriting and relatable lyrics are beloved by all.

### Lore

A community watch group known as the Street Sweepers is established in Performer's Plaza, made up of a cadre of concerned citizens of all stripes who strive to ensure the people and their interests stay protected. Merida Hardback of the East Street Market is one high profile member of this clandestine group, which is all but a mystery to most everyone outside of the district.
