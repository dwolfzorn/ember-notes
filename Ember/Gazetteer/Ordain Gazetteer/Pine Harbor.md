# Pine Harbor

- **District Type**: Commercial (Docks, Marine Shipping and Warehousing)
- **Population**: 2,288. (Light. Docks work all day, but house is limited to quarters for sailors and dockworkers.)
- **Lifestyle**: Maritime. (Dockworkers, sailors, foremen, private guards and merchants are common.)
- **Services**: Cargo handling, shipping administration, warehousing, haulers, weather reports.

Pine Harbor is the city’s busiest eastern-bank harbor and is often the first taste of Ordain for foreign traders and travelers. Its docks connect the bustling Lourd’s Depot to the north and the tiered sprawl of Highcrane to the south, serving as the vital anchor for Ordain’s daily trade.

### Culture

The docklands of Pine Harbor is a place of relentless labor, strict schedules, and a tightly knit community. Here, the Trading Houses grind against independent foremen, unyielding Dockmasters, and the inescapable cost of commerce.

The entire district runs to an order dictated by the Pine Harbor Dock Office and the Dockmasters there. Though aligned with the Trading Houses in purpose, they are beholden to no master. Through them schedules are decided, and Trading House agents negotiate dock assignments, work fees and tariffs.

On the docks though, they must yield their authority to the army of independent longshoreman without whom nothing would move. Operating from cranes to wagons, these workers organize into unions lead by trusted foremen who in turn negotiate pay and shifts with Dockmasters. Enterprising captains may strike deals with foremen for preferred rates, and the Trading Houses retain the services of private stevedores to ensure their ships are never left waiting.

### Landmarks

**Pine Harbor Dock Offices**

An imposing structure overlooking the harbor’s docks, this multi-storied building houses the schedules, shipping manifests, and team of dockmasters who orchestrate every arrival and departure in Pine Harbor.

**Pine Crescent Warehouses**

A stretching semicircle of stout, stone-and-timber warehouses equipped with iron-barred windows and watchful lanterns, storing everything from rare spices and silks to mundane grain and lumber. The warehouse section is named for and built over the land where pine timber used to accumulate from the eastern mill runs, forming a haphazard crescent of logs.

**The Shellback Station**

A rowdy dockside tavern and boarding house perched on the edge of the main quay. Each evening it fills with sailors and dock workers, laborers looking for a hot meal and a strong drink. with laughing sailors, grumbling laborers, and the warm glow of torches.

**Harborwatch Spire**

A tall, weather-stained watchtower that looms over the docks and allows the harbormasters to keep watch over the coming and going of ships. It also serves as a weather station where meteorological diviners prepare reports for captains looking to take specific trade routes.

### Locals

**Dockmaster Yolen Brack (Lawful Neutral, Ordani Human, she/her)**

Sharp and analytical, Yolen oversees docking schedules and takes pride in running a tight, incident-free harbor. She knows every incoming ship for the day and what dock team will be slated to handle it. She has plans for any delays that might threaten the day’s order, and believes being prepared is the key to success.

**Haxton Narm (Lawful Good, Ordani Human, they/them)**

A meticulous manifest clerk in the Dock Office, Haxton is the person to see for lost cargo or obscure paperwork. They keep a careful ledger of everything that passes through Pine Harbor and go out of their way to help honest traders navigate the bureaucracy.

**Berrin Lathe (Neutral, Ordani Vrjnhar, he/him)**

Owner of The River’s Rest, Berrin is burly, welcoming, and fiercely protective of both patrons and staff. Rumor holds he used to be a pirate but developed a conscience and prefers pouring ale and sharing news now.

**Abel Howls (Neutral, Arcturian Zeph, he/him)**

A devout follower of Vekalla, Abel is the chief diviner and weatherman of the Harborwatch Spire. He is laconic, only speaking when he must, and preferring to let his reports speak for themselves. When not poring over the day's forecast he can be found on the upper balconies of the tower watching the skies.

### Lore

Named for the masses of wood that would accumulate from the mills to the North-East, the Pine Harbor's dock area initially extended out of Lourd's Depot to accommodate the ever increasing traffic coming into Ordain. Naturally, the accumulating timber was used to build the first sections of the docks.

Over time, the old wooden docks were reinforced with stone and packed earth, the sodden logs replaced with newer, treated wood, and supporting structures erected to facilitate the docking of larger and larger vessels which other docks were ill suited for.

The unionized longshoremen of Pine Harbor (and adjoining districts) came about after disputes between captains, Trading Houses, and local merchants lead to the dock workers suffering unreasonable hours and pay for backbreaking work. Though they are all quite competitive in their pursuit of contracts, at the end of the day they are all dedicated to the collective good of Ordain's vital longshoreman community.
