# Fallbrook

- **District Type:** Residential with some entertainment
- **Population:** 19,606 (Dense population of younger residents supplemented by an active nightlife scene)
- **Lifestyle:** Fun-loving (Young and energetic residents willing to try new things and accept all comers)
- **Services:** Entertainment (Nightclubs, theaters, bars, and the occasional bordello)
- **Influences:** Has the largest Kithil community in Ordain.

### Culture

Fallbrook was once a fairly unremarkable residential neighborhood known mostly for its river views, but after a small community of Kithil settled in the area, they worked to make it more comfortable for their nocturnal lifestyle, designing awnings that generate shadow and darkness so that they could avoid Lantyr’s direct gaze when possible. While the district looks similar to any other at a distance, a walk through its streets leads to unexpected nightfalls and patches of dusky shadow that have been called dangerous, romantic, and “very easy to trip and fall in,” depending on who you ask.

The dim lighting, coupled with a number of overnight businesses that catered to Kithil clients, slowly began to make Fallbrook a destination for nightlife in Ordain. Soon, dance halls and taverns were beginning to crowd the district’s streets, leading locals, with the permission of the Ordinate, to declare that all new ventures had to have some tie to Kithil culture or have a strong nightlife purpose to be in the district. Although there are still some older establishments in the district that predate this rule, most of the popular venues are either run by Kithil or have an agreement with the locals to operate a business with their support.

### Landmarks

**The Bronze Rask Theater**

This long-standing theater came into its name when a visiting noble donated a large bronze statue of a Rask to the theater as thanks for a performance they especially liked. Lacking anywhere else to put it, the statue ended up in the lobby, where it stands to this day. Though small in size and prestige, the local Fallbrook Performers that call this place home welcome all aspiring artists and performers of Ordain. From repertory theater to avant garde musical concerts, the Bronze Rask is a haven for anyone and everyone who is a bard at heart. Owned and operated by Dory "Dangerous" Doss, a former adventurer and mercenary who has rebranded himself as a wealthy, bohemian raconteur

**Moonrise**

The collective name for a series of clubs in one of the waterworks that once brought water from the river into the Ordain, but was cut off by the Big Shake of 20 years earlier. It has been transformed to look like Ember's night sky, with its side passages transformed into small clubs themed after the different moons of Ordain.

**The Shading**

Many buildings in the district feature awnings that generate darkness or cast a shadow, hiding those beneath them from Lantyr's light. The Shading is a path, marked by glowing stones, that takes locals from one shadowed awning to the next so they can avoid sunlight if they want to. At night, when the effects of the awnings are more minimal, it is often used as a walking route through the district in a tradition known as a Shading Stroll.

**The Sunnery**

A lightbathing spa and cafe that uses a series of mirrors and a bit of magic to heighten the brightness of Lantyr's light. Created to help counter the belief that the district is all shadows and dark, it is only rarely used by locals, but has become popular with visitors to Ordain as a place to watch boats go by on the river.

**Tatters**

Billed as a place of "natural debauchery," Tatters is a small park that has turned into a 24-hour club. Planted with a number of thickets and bushes that work together to create an atmosphere of fevered partying, it features thorns that snag clothing but not flesh (giving the area its name), duskberries that create pockets of darkness, a number of Rubita blooms, which are said to give off a slightly euphoric scent (though this has never been proven). At the new year, this is the local site for the traditional and adults-only Kithil welcoming of the New Year.

### Locals

**Ivreain Coravi (true neutral, Kithil keth, they/them)**

Ivreain Coravi (ihv-RAIN core-AH-vee) is the leader of the Kithil community within Ordain and a bit of a stick-in-the-mud. Though he is happy that the district has been able to become a welcoming home for those who feel, as he does, that Lantyr's light is an oppressive force, and enjoys looking for ways to imbue the area with Kithil culture, he does not like that the district has become so synonymous with wild partying, fearing that it is giving visitors the wrong impression about the beliefs and priorities of the Kithil villages.

**Xiantille Luenne (neutral good, Kithil human, she/her)**

Xiantille (ZHAWN-til LOO-enn) was born with another name but changed it, as well as everything else about her, after traveling to the Kithil villages as a teenager and becoming obsessed with Kithil culture and the moons. Determined to make others see the beauty of Ember’s moons in a new way, she returned to Ordain and opened a fortune telling and tattoo business based on an individual’s lunar attunement. Viewed with a bit of suspicion by Kithil who feel that she only has a surface-level understanding of their culture, Xiantille can often be found in whichever of Moonrise's clubs corresponds to the current moon that is brightest in the sky.

**Ellrie Tincer (lawful neutral, Ordani keth, she/her)**

Ellrie has always been curious about what others were up to, spending much of her time even before joining the Hallows hanging out under one of the district's awnings and hoping to be hidden enough by its shadows to listen in to the local conversation. While she aspires to be part of the Veiled Chain, Ellrie likes to show off, dropping bits of whatever knowledge she’s gained into conversation, which makes her a less than ideal operative. Instead, she has been relegated to making sure that the many clubs in the area don’t overcrowd – a position she uses to continue to mine the area for gossip.

### Lore

The reliance on the Kithil villages for some of its food supply has generally made the Ordinate more willing to make deals and concessions to local Kithil in the city who have ties back to their homelands, lest a hard stance create an issue for the Trading Houses or the Ordani people. Some politicians and Ordinite council members like to bring up the bad harvest of 18 years ago (known in the Kithil Villages as Lantyr's Scorn) and the cities' bending of norms to facilitate Kithil practices as an erosion of Ordani cultural dominance, but people don't take these complaints seriously.
