# Highgate

- **District Type:** Residential with some Commercial
- **Population:** 2,570 (Moderate-to-Low, traffic is low after dark when work crews leave.)
- **Lifestyle:** Holdouts (Residents who refuse to give up on their district and plan to rebuild one day.)
- **Services:** Lodging, crafts, trade, food and drink.

Highgate once stood as a proud northern portal to the Redrak Fields, but the earthquake that struck some ten years ago fractured more than stone: it destroyed homes, ruined businesses and scattered much of the population elsewhere.

### Culture

Those who remain in Highgate are its most stubborn or dedicated, those who refuse to abandon their homes or businesses, or simply can't. A small community persists through the hardship even though they often feel like the city gave up on them years ago.

### Landmarks

**Highgate Plaza**

Once the district’s bustling heart, the plaza is now an uneven stretch of cracked stone and collapsed arches that once helped shade market stalls. What few vendors return here sell only to locals, as there is almost no road traffic to speak of.

**The Dryblade**

A battered old pub standing defiantly on the road to the old gate. Despite heavily spackled walls and a sagging roof, it remains open nightly. Its sign, a wooden sword, hangs over a slightly bent door. Inside the ale is cheap and the food simple, but there is always a chair for a vistor.

**The Roadhouses**

A line of inns that once welcomed travelers from Redrak, each one little more than private homes converted to welcome visitors and their coin. Now, most are shuttered and empty, their signs missing or faded to the point of being unreadable. Squatters, ruffians, and refugees have taken up residence in a few of them, making this area especially tricky to navigate.

**The Tayan Worksite**

Encircled by high wooden palisades and locked except when work is being carried out, the construction yard bears no signage or public plans. Wagons bearing stone and wood enter frequently, and laborers under contract seldom speak of their work or stay in the district after sundown.

### Locals

**Harn Veltrin (Lawful Neutral, Ordani Human, he/him)**

An aging mason overseeing the last of the public restoration projects in the plaza. Harn has spent his entire life in Highgate and refuses to leave until the work is finished, though funds have largely dried up.

**Mirro Dane (Chaotic Good, Ordani Human, she/her)**

Owner of the Dryblade, Mirro keeps her pub open more out of pride than profit. Gruff, sharp-eyed, and fiercely loyal, she knows every soul that still walks these streets and looks out for all of them as best she can.

**Tessan Kord (Neutral Good, Arcturian Human, he/him)**

A former caravan guard who now roams the broken streets trying to keep peace. Tessan has taken it upon himself to organize the braver locals to keep ruffians and thieves at bay. Tessan was born and raised in Highgate, and hates the current state of things.

**Aric Talve (Lawful Neutral, Tayan Human, they/them)**

As foreman for the Tayan project, Aric is the first to arrive on-site and the last to leave. Always crisply dressed on arrival, they often leave dusty, showing signs of hard work. When interacting with locals, Aric always chooses a stern silence in lieu of socializing or answering questions.

### Lore

Highgate was once home to thousands of residents and numerous very profitable businesses. As the main road in from Redrak and the lands around it, the district used to see constant traffic and trade. However, once the quake devastated it traffic dwindled rapidly. Once bandits and thieves began to haunt the ruined streets the remaining traffic diverted south to Lastgate, all but sealing Highgate's doom.

In the years since, officials have declared their intent to fully reclaim the district, though little progress has been made. However, the Tayan Empire has purchased several adjacent lots in the fractured district and have begun breaking ground on some new structure, but no public record is available, and workers refuse speak of what they build, if they even know.
