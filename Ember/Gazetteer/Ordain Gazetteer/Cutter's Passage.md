# Cutter's Passage

- **District Type:** Recreational with Commercial and Institutional
- **Population:** 40,407 (Very High. Quarry workers, laborers, et al., with heavy foot traffic).
- **Lifestyle:** Melting Pot (modest-to-comfortable lodging and amenities; sporadic small parks and civic walkways; shops and equipment stores).
- **Services:** Housing, lodging, food & drink, clothes, equipment, misc.

Cutter's Passage is one of the most densely-populated districts in all of Ordain, largely due to the expert planning of its neatly-stacked and phenomenally spacious architecture. The eponymous stone cutters who settled this area took it upon themselves to help further develop the neighborhood as the city continued to grow around it, and the result is a bustling district where the pride of its people can be seen in every brick and on every street corner.

Thanks to the quality of the architecture in Cutter's Passage, several of its structures have taken on landmark status, including The Danwren Building and Mason's tavern. And with so many people occupying its streets, whether full-time residents or visitors, Cutter's Passage is ultimately home to some of the best eateries and taverns in town — not to mention the formidable options in general stores and specialty shops.

### Culture

Cultural touchstones in Cutter's Passage are predictably varied, thanks to the district's extraordinarily dense population. People from all walks of life and all realms of Ember have settled in Cutter's Passage at one time or another, although most of the people in this busy neighborhood consider themselves to be Ordani through and through — no matter their origins. The people of Cutter's Passage tend to be a proud lot, full of the kinds of boundless hopes and dreams one might expect to find in a city as grand and metropolitan as Ordain (even if and when their lives are full of inevitable hardship).

### Landmarks

**The Danwren Building**

Built by the celebrated engineer Horatio Danwren, this famed apartment complex is instantly recognizable by its distinct architecture, which borrows a few aesthetic choices from ancient Shent designs. Throughout the years, several noteworthy Ordani have taken residence in this renowned abode, which continues to rise in prominence thanks to the celebrity of its executive clientele, its ample units for rent, and its storied history as a place where inspiration rubs elbows with everyday people. Some well-known residents of The Danwren include the cynical private investigator **Samwyse Strath** (Chaotic Good, Human Ordani, he/him), the devious girl genius **Brinica Thax** (Lawful Evil, Signborn Ordani, she/her), and the retired bard **Venna Ydorius** (Neutral Good, Human Ordani, she/they) — whose lavish parties tend to invite a rather chaotic element to the Danwren's top floor from time to time.

**Mason's**

This lively tavern is one of the most popular watering holes in Ordain. In fact, its reputation is so upstanding, folks will come from miles around to taste Mason's coveted array of beers, spirits, and bar food. Specialties include the Redgrass Ale, their semi-sweet pan-fried yams, and Colwren's famous Six Bean Stew. The staff of Mason's — including its playful manager **Lynus Colwren** (Chaotic Good, Human Ordani, he/him) — enjoy a light atmosphere, including the jocular and near-constant admission that there is, in fact, no one named "Mason" here, and the name of the place is simply a time-honored pun.

**Hewer's Square**

Hewer's Square is the most traveled intersection among the many wide thoroughfares of Cutter's Passage, a place where eager street vendors, brash news kids, and questionable job postings can be found by the handful. Activity here swells during dawn and dusk, as people are rushing to and from work, and peaks at lunchtime — when countless food carts and corner salesmen gather to tirelessly hawk their wares. There is an organized chaos to this busy place, where the Ordani people come and go like the ceaseless pulse of the city itself.

### Locals

****Venna Ydorius** (Chaotic Neutral, Human Ordani, she/her)**

This aged and retired actress lives in The Danwren. She's gained a reputation for being something of a curmudgeon in her later years, but those who know Venna best understand she's actually a softy at heart. Thanks to the considerable pile of wealth that she saved up throughout her successful years, the incredible Ms. Ydorius has decided to become a benefactor for her more eccentric interests — including research into lost Aterican legends and the collecting of rare Arcturian, Cascilian, and Waerd jewelry and artifacts. Indeed, an intrepid party could likely find themselves benefitting from Venna's patronage from time to time.

****Stessos "Stessy" Thrasher** (Chaotic Good, Human Ordani, he/him)**

With an unkempt mop of raven-colored hair and a messy attitude to match, this brash seventeen-year-old is a stonemason's apprentice by day and a fledgling street vigilante by night (or aims to be, at least). Stessy is convinced that an underbelly of crime and corruption lurks outside the watchful gaze of organizations like the Veiled Chain, and hopes to personally support their efforts with his own local brand of street justice. Unfortunately, no one takes him seriously. (Yet). One thing is certain: young Mr. Thrasher's heart is certainly in the right place, whether his efforts and focus have found their proper footing or not.

****Cora Nylos** (Neutral Good, Kivahr Ordani, she/her)**

Cora Nylos is the headstrong senior bouncer at Mason's, where her smug and impudent personality is a regular hit with the jocular crowd. What most customers don't outright know is that she's also a part-time adventurer and fist-for-hire, if the job is just and the crew are the proper people for it. Covered in elegant tattoos of Arcturian monsters, Cora wears her dark hair in a braided knot, and keeps a sleek dagger in her boot for unexpected combat encounters.

### Lore

The expert stonemasons who settled this area are rumored to have carved a network of secret tunnels that run beneath the district (separate from, but connected to, its subterranean waterways). Of course, the more accessible areas of these tunnels have become host to an assortment of entities of various means and origins — from transients and street urchins, to rogues and the occasional sewer crevit.
