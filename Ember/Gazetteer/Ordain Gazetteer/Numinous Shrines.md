# Numinous Shrines

- **District Type:** Institutional with some Commercial and Residential
- **Population:** 327 (Low. Shrines and temples with heavy-to-moderate foot traffic).
- **Lifestyle:** Reverent (Modest homes for religious professionals and city workers).
- **Services:** Spiritual guidance, healing, religious education and practice.

A central gallery known as the "Mall of the Nineteen" hosts individual shrines that serve each god in the eponymous local pantheon; and a few smaller altars and temples dedicated to more obscure deities can also be found here, erected somewhat haphazardly over the years as newer faiths gained purchase in Ordain. Occasionally, an errant doomsayer or fortune-teller can be found on a cobbled street corner within the Shrines, but these dogmatic opportunists are relatively few and far between. It's also not uncommon to see a few barges moored alongside the district's river channel to the south, where pilgrims have stopped to pay reverence to one or more gods, or passengers await chartered passage to other riverside areas of the city.

The Numinous Shrines are wholly open to the public, and are maintained by locals with assistance from the Hallows when necessary. This district is a popular spot for travelers and locals alike — not only as a venerated site for religious worship, but also as a place of simple respite where they might escape the metropolitan hustle and bustle (if only for a moment).

### Landmarks

**Mall of The Nineteen**

This sprawling three story arcade connects nineteen distinct and individual shrines dedicated to one of the various gods in the revered Aterican pantheon. Most of the shrines to [[The Nineteen]] here are housed within a gazebo or beneath the awning of a walkway, with few exceptions: the Shrine of [[Lantyr]] and the Shrine of [[Raineka]] each sit beneath an open sky, so that the sun and rain might fall upon their respective stone altars without obstruction. Servants of each of the faiths associated with The Nineteen regularly tend to their shrines, while three designated Shrinekeepers maintain a perpetual vigil over the Mall area as a whole. Remarkably, the shrine erected here for the elder god Sockets is a rather modest one compared to others (a direct result of the cultural and spiritual emphasis that's been placed on the Temple of Sockets in [[Temple Ward]] by his adherents).

**Piety Hall**

Facilitated by the Ordinate and managed by a paid Resident Advisor, this large edifice houses dormitories and apartments for short- and long-term lodging here among the Numinous Shrines. Adherents to the various faiths of The Nineteen are afforded preferential treatment and discount rates, although any and all lawful citizens are actually welcome to take residence here. Yet, given Piety Hall's inherent aura of stoicism and selflessness, many find its confines to be a bit droll and lackluster compared to the opportunity and fervor found elsewhere in the city. Nevertheless, the Hall offers local clerics and priests some of the area's most reliable and affordable housing.

**Destine Promenade**

The Promenade is a public thoroughfare along the bank of the Destine in the southeastern edge of the district, where a modest array of market stalls and tents are erected around a small riverside concourse. Locals make use of this landmark as a convenient and valued stop on their way to or from the neighborhood's titular shrines. Destine Promenade isn't merely a public rest area, it provides a few commercial services for the pious — prayer books, holy symbols, and sacred materials are sold here for much more reasonable prices than elsewhere in the city, and information brokers cater to local specifics and the specific needs of Ordain's faithful.

### Locals

****Shrinekeeper Bolara Tol** (Neutral Good, Human Ordani, she/her)**

One of three duly-appointed Shrinekeepers, this kind-hearted cleric of Thoma serves as the senior custodian of the Mall of the Nineteen. The child of two Ordaini priests, she dutifully stepped into her role as Shrinekeeper at the earliest possible age. Treat Bolara Tol as a [[Priest]].

****Norrin Caertaris** (Lawful Neutral, Human Waerd, he/they)**

This scholarly adherent of Sigil works as the Resident Advisor of Piety Hall, and is known to regale visitors with Arcturian legends and capricious tales of "old Ordain" when they aren't buried in the pages of a good book.

****Geb Navik** (Chaotic Good, Keth Kithil, he/him)**

Originally from the Kithil village of Hillhome north of Luen, Geb Navik is a whimsical merchant stationed at the Destine Promenade who caters to devotees of The Nineteen and their specific material needs. And while it remains close to his heart, Geb's nostalgia for his small-town youth is far exceeded by his passion for worldly people and Ordani commerce.

### Lore

Ordani historians have well-traced accounts of the development of the Numinous Shrines and the Mall of the Nineteen that house most of them. However, several mysteries and legends lie at the heart of the district's eponymous landmarks. The most ubiquitous of these tall tales is perhaps the legend of "The Silent Acolyte," which chronicles the story of an apparition who visits the shrines of the four Elder Gods erected here; some say this apparition is a manifestation of [[Ven'avé]], while others maintain the Acolyte is a servant of Sockets who refuses the Soul Cycle for one reason or another. The most wizened of local adherents know the truth: the sightings are real, but the Silent Acolyte's motivations remain inscrutable to this day.
