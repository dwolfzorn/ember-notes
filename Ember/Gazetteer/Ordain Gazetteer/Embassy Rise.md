# Embassy Rise

- **District Type:** Institutional
- **Population:** 2,368, (Low, district is busiest during the day, with frequent with diplomatic functions.)
- **Lifestyle**: Political (Diplomats, clerks, and guards are semi-permanent residents.)
- **Services:** Embassy offices, diplomatic lodgings, interpreters, meeting halls, access to Ordinate.
- **Influences:** Outside cultures are concentrated within embassies, and the locals here are varied.

The district's embassies are imposing but elegant, each designed to showcase the prestige of the nation it represents. These buildings often blend ornate local stone with imported architectural styles and materials, making for a district unlike any other in the city.

High perimeter walls and iron gates are a common sight here, as are quiet courtyards shaded by imported trees and dotted with fountains. The district’s political weight means that only the well-connected — be they residents or visitors —move through it unchallenged.

### Culture

Life in Embassy Rise is one of formality, and ever-present observation, whether by foreign officials or by embedded security. Dignitaries and diplomats keep careful schedules with meetings often running late into the evening, intermixed with banquets and garden receptions, all in service to political aims.

Security is omnipresent but discreet: nearly every embassy employs its own detail of guards, drawn from mercenary bands or the home nation's military, and many properties feature integrated residences for staff and their families.

In addition, the Veiled Chain's presence here is an open secret, and any long standing resident or worker in this district likely knows how to spot a few of the more obvious watchers placed here to keep tabs on things. As a result, there is an air of intrigue that one cannot deny in the district, as though the forces here play games with the delicate balance of power in Ordain.

### Landmarks

**The Council Ascent**

The tallest structure in the district, this multi-storied structure not only grants a commanding view of the district, but is one of two ways into the Ordinate, and the one used by the envoys and ambassadors that live here in Embassy Rise.

**Tayan Embassy**

Regal and imposing, the imperial lines of the Tayan embassy's architecture stand in sharp contrast against the rest of the Ordani structures around it. The grounds are dotted with bubbling fountains and decorative waterfalls meant to remind the occupants of home and make eavesdropping all the more difficult.

**Lumek Embassy**

Distinctly square, and built out of a golden stone, this embassy is distinctly Lumekian in origin, a trait made all the more obvious by the massive brazier of black iron sitting out front, featuring a magical, ever-burning flame. A faint haze of acrid smoke hangs long around the grounds here, and the air is perpetually dry. Unlike most embassies, this one features a small guardhouse and contingent of Flameguard protectors at all times.

**The Blue Lantern**

A refined teahouse built on a steep overlook with a view of the lower waterfront districts, famed as a place for quiet parley and confidential discussion in its back rooms. The lantern that gives the spot its name glows with a striking blue radiance during business hours and is said to be nearly two centuries old.

**Hall of Seals**

An old, unassuming building of white stone, the Hall of Seals is the longest standing structure in the district, and one of great historical significance. Inside it displays the insignias, crests, flags and seals of every recognized delegation to Ordain. In addition it houses rare tomes of law, etiquette, and foreign customs for various nations and cultures. Here, visiting envoys and locals alike can consult texts on embark on knowledge-gathering pursuits in the hushed, marbled reading chambers within, along with any treaties they may hold with the city. While open the public, the documents contained within are accessible by city officials only.

### Locals

**Oathkeeper Orson Malv (Lawful Neutral, Ordani Human, they/them)**

A senior archivist in the Hall of Seals, they track all comings and goings in the building, and personally oversee the small number of personnel tasked with guarding and maintaining the items within. They would die to protect the Hall of Seals.

**Mira Solastis (Neutral Good, Ordani Signborn, she/her)**

Owner of the Blue Lantern teahouse, Mira is quietly trusted by diplomats who seek neutral ground for private meetings. She maintains a collections of rare, imported teas from all over the world.

**Vaeren Crosset (Neutral, Ordani Human, she/her)**

A seasoned interpreter and master of seven language, Vaeren facilitates communication between embassies. She’s respected for her impartiality, though rumors suggest she keeps a private ledger of favors owed and owed to her.

### Lore

In all cases the land here is provided by Ordain and broadly treated as foreign soil, allowing the members of the hill to have some measure of safety and autonomy. However, the Ordinate reserves the right to expel any member of the hill should it feel that the state's presence is no longer beneficial to the city. Though it is rarely necessary, all nations coming to do business in Ordain know they are there at the pleasure of the city alone.

As thinly hidden as the Veiled Chain’s presence is, most consider the surveillance the price of doing business here. The chain ensures peace while quietly cataloguing the ambitions of those who would shape Ordain’s path in the wider world, and those wishing to take advantage of Ordain's wealth and resources know they will be watched.
