# Stonework Hollow

Though it was once a thriving quarry used by House Cevher, a catastrophic earthquake believed to have been tied to the deep drilling happening in the area led to its abandonment by the house. In the years since, the site has mostly gone back to nature, with wildlife taking over the sites that were once used by workers. Stonework Hollow is far enough from the city of Ordain that while it is closely intertwined with its history, its more natural threats pose no real danger to the city. Some enterprising businesses and workers have even taken to scavenging from what is left of the site, despite its potential dangers.

### Environment

Stonework Hollow still shows some of the signs of the quarry it was when the earthquake hit, with carts smashed on the rocky floor and ropes that dangle to nowhere. Some of the fissures in the rock also show the promise of new veins of metal, though House Cevher has very strongly warned against digging any further into the area. Those who do face not only the natural threats that now exist in the area, but the threat of unstable rock outcroppings crushing them.

### Ecology

Since being abandoned, Stonework Hollow has become occupied by several creatures common to rocky environments, including Cave Wasps, Ketral, Crag Raptors, and an occasional Suarrok.

### Lore

While it is widely believed that House Cevher did something wrong in their safety protocols that led to the Stonehollow Quake, there is no evidence that this is actually true. In the days following the quake, some within the trading house argued that they should prove their innocence, but Ralton Cevher believed it would be disrespectful to the victims of the quake to argue about who was to blame, and the belief that House Cevher caused the disaster has now solidified, much to the joy of the Trading House's political foes.
