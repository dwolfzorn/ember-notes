# Lilifeld Stations

- **District Type**: Industrial and Commercial
- **Population**: 2,490 (Moderate-to-High, always busiest during market hours, dawn to dusk.)
- **Lifestyle**: Industrial-Commercial. (Warehouse workers, caravaneers, traders, house Lillifeld associates.)
- **Services**: Agricultural warehousing, milling, produce markets, roadside eateries.

Lilifeld Stations is dominated by sturdy granaries and busy mills, all watched over by the banners of Trading House Lilifeld. These warehouses secure all the city’s grain, flour, and harvested goods coming in from Redrak Fields before they’re sent to other markets or stored for future need. The district’s heart is its celebrated Lilifeld Granary, a landmark of both pride and practical importance.

The surrounding streets are tightly woven with depots and shops, all offering work to haulers, clerks, and barrowmen who ferry foodstuffs through the city. Though House Lilifeld is the district’s namesake, other trading houses — most notably Salva — keep offices and agents here to take advantage of the markets and keep watch over operations.

### Culture

The Station’s culture is professional, neighborly, and fiercely loyal to House Lilifeld which has done its level best to take care of the people here. Everybody is well fed here, partially paid in grain and produce, which helps keep the workforce strong and dedicated. That loyalty even extends to the numerous merchants and restauranteurs that come directly to the Stations to procure their goods, rather than seeking supplies elsewhere.

Lilifeld Stations is less forgiving to smuggling and mischief than other districts in this part of the city: House Lilifeld keeps scrupulous records, and local law is enforced with an even hand by a small army of house officers and guards.

### Landmarks

**Lilifeld Granary**

The city’s largest and most vital warehouse: a colossal, domed structure of pale stone and timber, filled floor to rafters with the stores of Ordain's bread supply. Teams of grain porters and tall grain elevators bustle from dawn till midnight. It is protected day and night by Trading House guards.

**Willow Street Farmer’s Market**

A patchwork sprawl of produce stalls and crate-filled booths, this daily open-air market offers the best vegetables, fruit, and nuts brought in from the eastern fields. Packed during the day by locals trying to get their staples, the prices are reasonable and stocks ample. However it is all but empty by sundown.

**Liliman’s Bar & Grille**

Named after one of the smaller Trading Houses that preceded House Lilifeld, this low, brick-walled tavern is adorned with faded field banners of old houses hung from ancient wooden beams. Liliman's is a working-class establishment that prides itself on being an accessible lunch and dinner spot, and sees heavy traffic from workers and merchants alike. It serves hearty meals and strong spirits at all hours.

**Riverwall Depot**

An old riverside warehouse converted to a bustling transit and weighing station for barges moving between districts. Owned and operated by House Lilifeld, nothing leaves here before paperwork is processed, loads are weighed, and arrival points are settled by posted clerks.

### Locals

**Darissa Liliman (Lawful Good, Ordani Human, she/her)**

The stalwart, matronly barkeep of Liliman’s Bar & Grille, Darissa is trusted by all and knows everyone who calls the Stations home. She often mediates neighborly disputes and is a source of quiet wisdom for newcomers. She is well known for making sure nobody goes hungry in her bar, even if they can't pay.

****Jurlan Savas (Neutral, Ordani Cor'ak, he/him)****

A pragmatic foreman at the Lilifeld Granary, Jurlan oversees shifting crews and storage records. He is known for his tireless energy, sharp memory, and clean-handed honesty in all dealings. As foremen go, he prefers to mix it up with his teams, and can be found moving shipments and goods when not handling paperwork.

**Paela Tern (Chaotic Good, Ordani Signborn, she/her)**

A cheerful market overseer of Willow Street's market, Paela manages the stalls and allots space to merchants that will sell during the daily hours. With a booming laugh and easy manner, she spins stories out of thin air, and constantly jokes that she missed her calling as a bard.

**Matin Folke (Lawful Neutral, Ordani Human, he/him)**

A quiet, meticulous warehouse clerk stationed at the Riverwall Depot, Matin is responsible for keeping all incoming grain accounts balanced. He is known for his patience, discretion, and ability to spot irregularities others might miss.

### Lore

The importance of the Lilifeld Granary cannot be overstated: any threat to its supply or integrity is met with swift response from both the Trading House and the Ordinate. Each year, the Stations’ market and storerooms feed thousands and in lean seasons, have staved off disaster more than once. The district’s order and prosperity remain a quiet backbone to the city’s vibrant daily life.

Additionally, in a bid to leverage this importance, House Lilifeld has begun investing in local restaurants on the waterfront, specifically in Restaurant Row, with intent to influence the markets through food culture, supply and demand, and price fixing. Though some within the House frown on this practice, it has substantially grown the profitability of the district's markets, which has benefitted the house and city alike.
