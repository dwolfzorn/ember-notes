# Restaurant Row

- **District Type:** Commercial (restaurants) with some Residential
- **Population:** 5,605 (Moderate population of service workers, dock workers, and other hands-on professions, along with a high density of visitors)
- **Lifestyle:** Fast-Paced (Diners coming in and out of the area's restaurants create a sense of constant movement)
- **Services:** Food (Restaurants, taverns, cafes)
- **Influences:** Overseen by House Lillifeld, which runs the back-end storage and supplies of most of the restaurants in the district

While the workers of Restaurant Row tend to move together like a well-oiled machine in front of their guests, making sure that everyone feels well taken care of, Restaurant Row is a bundle of sometimes chaotic energy behind the scenes. The many workers who move through the backrooms, undercuts, and side passages that connect the restaurants to House Lilifeld food stores are often only steps or seconds away from disaster as they balance changing supplies, odd customer requests, and House Lilifeld's demands that one ingredient or another be showcased as the fresh new flavor of the moment.

Still, many would say that this level of chaos is what draws them to cooking or serving or preparing food in Restaurant Row over anywhere else in Ordain. In the world behind the cooktops, stakes are high, life can feel like it is on a knife's edge, and those who work (and often) live in the area love the fast pace, even if it means occasional fits of passion, dropped plates, and heightened emotions.

The residential housing in the area, which is tucked away, around corners that the average diner wouldn't explore, is mostly communal, with individual housing groups often focusing on a specific work-related skill or recreational interests. House Lillifeld set these units up initially in the hopes that it would lead to unofficial mentorship and a sense of teamwork, which has largely worked, though there are occasional feuds and fights that show up in the food on the Row in ways that only those who live in the district understand (and often reference in short hand with phrases like "a three fish fight" or "like a barrel of stilling wine").

### Landmarks

**Pancha's Pourhouse**

No one knows who Pancha is or was - general consensus is that she may have never existed other than in some shared inside joke - but the Pourhouse that bears her name is one of the few places in Restaurant Row that exists as both a communal house and a tavern serving the public. Behind the scenes, Pancha's is often the first place that newcomers to Restaurant Row land, known for its late-night conversations and dedication to being able to make everything from a good semi-magical consumable elixir to a Blacksap infused picker-upper, and its small cafe storefront provides drinks to many of the nearby restaurants, as well as those passing through the district.

**Odd Bites**

When House Lillifeld is looking to try something new with one of their ingredients, or a chef has an idea that might or might not be good, it often ends up at Odd Bites, which serves limited portions of experimental food at a deep discount, but requires those who eat there to share their honest thoughts on what they've just consumed. Rumors that there is a truth stone embedded beneath their floor somewhere is just that, though it doesn't stop people from occasionally bringing friends or lovers there to ask the tough questions between bites of new concoctions.

**Shine**

Shine is all about the show, with food preparers who perform a variety of music between courses. The type of music performed varies based on who is running the shift, and it is not unusual to hear a bawdy group round at one time of day and a heartfelt soloist at another.

**Whistler's Tavern**

An inn and tavern that doubles as a Noticery—boards can be found there advertising jobs, leaving messages for people, sharing gossip, and the like, though most patrons use the ink and paper left at their tables to leave notes, thoughts, and even poems, which are attached to the walls, creating an overlapping mosaic of information, news, and silliness.

### Locals

**Pevin Draivel (Neutral, Ordani human, he/him)**

Pevin oversees Whistler's Tavern and enjoys keeping it aa raucous but intimate environment, but he is very protective of the written scraps that he has patrons leave on the walls, and tends to scour over them at the end of the day, as if looking for something. Pevin won't reveal what it is that he is after, but patrons have come up with theories ranging from finding a lost love to avoiding the assassin who for some reason may be trying to kill him.

**Faral the Fearless (Chaotic Good, Arcurian Cor'ak, he/him)**

Faral has lots of stories to tell. A few may even be true, but that's not the point - he's a bit of an institution in the various establishments of Restaurant Row where he tells tales of his time as an adventurer that can somehow never be verified, though that never stops new adventurers from trying to repeat them. Faral is also helpful if there are any disputes in Restaurant Row, always willing to scoop up any angry customers and allow them to fight again "as soon as he finishes his tale." By the time he's done, most of the time, the desire to fight has been replaced by either amusement or incredulity.

**Jemble Kane (Chaotic Good, Ordani human, she/her)**

Though she is technically a server, not a chef, Jemble is always coming up with an idea for a new food fusion or magically imbued drink and often interrupts herself in the middle of serving to rush off and try to whip up something new. Though this can be disconcerting, her concoctions are delicious enough that she has been hired away from her current restaurant at least four times.

**Tidwick Lassinger (Lawful Good, Ordani thornling, they/them)**

As someone who does not eat food themselves, Tidwick is possibly the best choice Lillifeld could have made as their behind-the-scenes food store manager. Tidwick is organized, but more than that, they are fascinated by the idea of eating food and keep track of what Restaurant Row customers are having both by looking at what is in the Lillifeld food stores and by watching diners as they consume ffood. Their stare can be somewhat disconcerting, and they have a habit of occasionally going up to diners to ask why they've chosen a specific food combo, but they are generally thought to be harmless.

### Lore

Once a set of food storage depots used by House Lilifeld, Restaurant Row was repurposed into a series of restaurants that showcase ingredients from the Trading House and its suppliers in the Arctus Plateau in an attempt to bring the House (which has always had less of a visible presence in Ordain than some of its counterparts) a bit of recognition. The ability to use it to try to control supply and demand has been a more recent — if welcome — discovery.
