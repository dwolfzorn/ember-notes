# Throughway

- **District Type:** Commercial and Residential
- **Population:** 14,100 (High. Businesses, depots, and trade centers with extreme foot traffic).
- **Lifestyle:** Working-class (Merchants, moneylenders, logistics professionals).
- **Services:** Warehouse rental, imports and exports, housing, general trade.

Throughway is one of Ordain's most overlooked districts in terms of local color or historical significance, but it nevertheless endures as one of the most valued neighborhoods when it comes to the city's commercial and socio-political affairs. Whether it's Throughway's importance to Ordani trade systems or the reliability of its community-based information network, it's a neighborhood where things are constantly happening.

Because of the district's advantageous proximity to [[Westgate]] and the bounteous trade it constantly brings the city, Throughway has also become a haven for hucksters and profiteers eager to make their coin via the exploitation of Ordain's more impressionable denizens. Verily, every third street corner plays host to some guide or salesperson of questionable repute; and while most of the locals know to steer clear of these unsavory vendors and their dubious wares, the neighborhood has elevated the status of these hawkers to the domain of local legend — tall tales abound about weird and wild happenings that have resulted from a chance encounter with one of Throughway's storied street hustlers.

### Culture

Throughway's local culture is a synthesis of Westgate's diverse trade sector and the inner city's historical eccentricities. The people here — comprised of [[Ordani]] with [[Arcturian]], [[Tayan]], and [[Cascilian]] roots (among others) — tend to focus on the art of commerce and relationships of convenience, due largely to the hustle and bustle brought about by the district's varied goods and services. People often joke that "Throughway" is also a euphemism for the attitude of the opportunistic people who tend to live here, who view each other as assets rather than contemporaries. But this presumption is ultimately a misconception like any other, confirmed by the district's lively atmosphere and gregarious people.

### Landmarks

**Depot Square**

This large public commons hosts a dozen commercial warehouses of various shapes and sizes, primarily used by Ordani businesses and foreign traders for the ongoing import and export of valued goods. Each of Ordain's Trading Houses has ample office and warehouse space here, and a central information kiosk is manned by duly-appointed employees of the [[Ordinate]]. Depot Square is also famed throughout the city for its weekend market, where a score of Ordani vendors congregate to trade their fresh and novel wares.

**The Here & There House**

This extended-stay inn owned by the convivial **Dorna Thassilhaft** (Neutral Good, Human Ordani, she/her) features an adjoining warehouse with rental units for the short term storage of bulk goods. While she tends to be otherwise law-abiding, Dorna is known for looking the other way if a friend or regular needs to move some goods of questionable origin through her warehouses every once in a while. Meanwhile, The Here & There has a sterling reputation for their savory meat pies, which are prized the city over.

**The Hub**

Resembling a warehouse in its own right, The Hub is a spacious tavern where "the ale is never watered down" and the floors are always covered in sawdust — ready to soak up the rough-and-tumble spills of the establishment's rowdy clientele. Owned and operated by the lovably irascible **Logar Halfhelm** (Chaotic Good, Kivahr Ordani, he/him), The Hub is the kind of anything-goes venue where you can either have a spirited homecoming with old acquaintances or break bread (and maybe a few bottles) with someone new.

### Locals

****Zeela Marh** (Chaotic Neutral, Human Ordani, she/her)**

This wily twenty-something cutpurse is a regular patron of The Hub, where she likes to hone her pickpocketing skills as well as her aptitude for meaningless small talk. Always up for fun and lucrative trouble, Zeela most often hires out her thieving abilities to those patrons who manage to entice her with an exciting challenge, a noteworthy reward, or both.

****Gaphne Greyfeld** (Neutral Good, Kiska Ordani, she/they)**

This young hedge wizard is a sedate, yet sharp-minded mage who works as a trusted hireling for several businesses within Throughway and nearby. Where brute strength falls short, Gaphne's spells often rise to the occasion — making her an invaluable asset to clients who might need or want the industrial potential of magic on their side.

****Jonathas Nab** (Neutral Good, Human Arcturian, he/him)**

Truly his own worst enemy, this ornery day laborer never left his rural proclivities behind, despite decades of living in Ordain. Nab's mood swings and penchant for self-destruction are nearly legendary, and he's known among Throughway and neighboring districts for screwing up almost every job he's ever had. As a result, he's worked at nearly every warehouse and shipyard in the city — and has the personal contacts and information network to match. Whether or not his associates have tired of his chaotic tendencies, however, always remains to be seen.

### Lore

It's a little known secret, but a handful of businesses and homes in Throughway play host to a secret fight club, where the anonymous attendees engage in rigorous bouts of bare-knuckle brawling for nothing more than camaraderie and the thrill of it all. Indeed, an agent or two of the [[Veiled Chain]] and several soldiers of the [[Burnished Hand]] are even said to participate in these events, free of the burden of their official duties. But citizens who stumble upon rumors of this macabre pastime can rest assured: the fights in this nameless underground circuit are fair and never, ever fatal.
