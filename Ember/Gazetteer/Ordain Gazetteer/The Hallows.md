# The Hallows

- **District Type:** Institutional and Residential
- **Population:** 18,130 (High. Crowds come and go from dusk till well after dark; busiest during the day. Only a few caretakers and officials keep quarters within the halls. However, extensive housing around the central plazas are home to thousands.)
- **Lifestyle:** Mixed. (While central gathering areas see many lobbyists, barristers, and magistrates, all of Ordain flows through here.)
- **Services:** Mediation, public forums, theaters, guidance for civil affairs.

Here, columns of old Arcturian stone quarried centuries ago meet the new lines of modern Ordani aesthetic, with seams between these architectural movements masked or accented by ever-changing murals contributed by local artists. This bombast of color and pattern creates a visual chaos that somehow binds the ever shifting face of the Hallows into a recognizable if not cohesive identity. While the Hallows remains an architectural tangle, its disparate facades speak to a city ever in negotiation with itself, and a people determined to honor the outcome of that tradition.

### Culture

The Hallows is a district of social and judicial tumult, where law and order are executed in the light of Lantyr for all to see, as much a performance as an affirmation that the systems of the Ordinate work and can be trusted.

Meetings are public; celebrations spring up around verdicts and reconciliations, and debates over current events rage like forest fires across the district, sometimes smoldering for days, never fully leaving the public discourse. Here, anyone may find a soapbox in Speaker’s Square, or a moment to shine in Songhall Theater.

### Landmarks

**Songhall Theater**

This grand, multi-storied theater hosts an array of performances and panels, with a ground floor dedicated to showcasing the upcoming month's events and handling admission to the numerous stages and theaters held on higher levels. While most events are free to the public, it is not uncommon to find private and paid performances as well.

**Speaker's Square**

The open and cacophonous heart of the Hallows where the court of public opinion is held with no regard for order or ceremony. Its pale flagstones have been worn smooth by centuries of foot traffic as crowds shift around orators, singers, and passionate citizens making their presence known. It is said that you can hear a hundred opinions and witness half as many arguments just by walking from one end to the other.

**The Scales**

A venerable structure and vital institution in the city, the Scales is a building wholly dedicated to resolving disputes across the city, whether that is issue of business deals gone awry, crimes committed, or arguments unresolved. The councilors that roost here offer unbiased mediation for minor issues, while teams of barristers and magistrates wait on higher floors to address more major matters worthy of the courts and inspectors.

**The Underfoot**

A maze of stone corridors beneath the Hallows known to be older than the city itself, but with origins few fully known. Lantern-lit, signposted, and open by decree, the Underfoot serves as a shortcut for those in the know. Rumors abound of hidden chambers, forgotten passages, and hushed conspiracies away from the prying eyes above.

### Locals

**Marnis Vell (Lawful Good, Ordani Human, he/him)**

A senior magistrate of the Scales, Marnis is revered for his calm voice and tireless impartiality. He often lingers in Speaker's Square to engage locals in debate, and educate those needing to know the finer points of Ordani law.

**Lisarra Pell (Neutral, Ordani Ashka, she/her)**

A prominent and celebrated muralist whose artworks have become a signature feature of the Hallows’ walls in recent years. Lisarra is well known for creating sprawling pieces that she adds to over time to represent recent events, court rulings, or cultural movements that captured the minds of Ordain.

**Jastor Riven (Chaotic Good, Ordani Altyran, they/them)**

An outspoken advocate and barrister known for energetic speeches in Speaker’s Square and scrappy efforts to fight economic corruption in the city. Jastor only accepts clients they believe are in the right, and is notoriously happy to fight the Trading Houses in court if given a chance.

**Zalend Ark (Lawful Neutral, Ordani Human, he/him)**

A no-nonsense city inspector that haunts the archives of The Scales, looking for inspection records that are out of date. He considers that pursuit of law and order to be the most noble of tasks, but has become a thorn in the side of local merchants and builders who aren't fastidious about their paperwork.

### Lore

The Hallows is not the oldest district in Ordain's eight centuries of history, and was established only after the judicial systems of the city were properly codified by the Ordinate. Before its creation the city handles justice in the streets, often in broad view of the public, with local leaders serving as arbiters and judges.

Though this open-air justice system is formally gone, the Hallows has never stopped being a place where the unruly and vocal mobs of Ordain could gather to make their voices heard, and find out the fate of certain cases that matter to them.

The rumors that the underfoot conceals clandestine meeting halls and operations that the Hallows keeps away from public knowledge is quite true, and those that know the right sequence of turns to take will find themselves in hidden parlors, secret debate spots, and secure bolt holes for the Veiled Chain.
