# Ordain Waterfront

The Ordain Waterfront cuts through the heart of Ordain, offering views of the coursing river, and access to numerous docks and slips where cargo and people can be moved through the grand metropolis. The winding waterfront of Ordain is heavily weighted toward industrial and commercial districts, especially those of shipping and transport, as the mighty Destine was tamed long ago, and turned into a tool for the city's success.

### Environment

Paved with white stones like the rest of Ordain, and accented with sturdy wooden docks, much of the Destine's natural form has vanished with the expansion of the city. The rushing waters flowing from the north been confined by sturdily constructed canals of stone, and directed through underground waterways built centuries ago.

Travel through and across the waterfront is swift, as countless ferries and barges can be found moving cargo, though visitors must be careful where they go. Most of the waterfront is meant for work, and the transit of goods above all else, so navigation requires vigilance, as the traffic of workers and materials creates plenty of hazards for the unwary.

### Ecology

The heavy industrial nature of the waterfront means that little space has been dedicated for greenery and gardens, creating an often unwelcoming landscape for creatures that don't excel in urban and commercial sprawls. As a result, floral and faunal presence is minimal but comprises creatures familiar with dockside activity, such as seabirds, enterprising rodents, and invasive insects taking advantage of the crates, spilled materials, and moving vessels.

### Lore

Significant portions of the waterfront's construction were necessitated by the gradual shifting of the river's flow over the centuries, and city planners worked tirelessly to create the final shape of the Destine's course. It took decades of work, and the plans had to be revised numerous times, including the addition of underwater canals and sewers to facilitate the flow of waters and waste, especially in rainy seasons, to make sure the river didn't overflow into the city.

Most citizens of Ordain won't give the river a second thought, seeing it as an obstacle to travel or just another part of their home. Yet it took many minds and countless hands to tame the swift waters and make it a mundane part of daily life.
