# The Harborage

- **District Type:** Residential with industry (dockwork, shipyards)
- **Population:** 35,957 (Very dense population of newer residents and visitors arriving by sea)
- **Lifestyle:** On The Move (New residents come and go as they get to know the city, and the dock is always open)
- **Services:** Dock work and ships (ship construction and repair), New Resident Assistance (free and low-cost meals, housing boards, etc.)

### Culture

The Harborage is one of the densest districts in Ordain, filled with those who are making their way to Ordain from afar by sea and those whose tireless efforts to build, maintain, and service both local and foreign ships keep trade and travel running smoothly. Ships can arrive at all hours, so there is always is a general hum of noise in the area, and the streets are rarely empty. Work in the dockyard and lights from incoming ships often shed light on the streets, and it is sometimes said by residents that the Harborage never rests.

For those who are new to Ordain, the Harborage can be overwhelming, but the many dock criers and greeters that spend time on the docks attempt to steer newcomers to the resources they need. Most of the time. There are always a few scam artists mixed in with the other criers, often offering a deal on foreign goods or a place to stay at what they claim is a low price but comes with punitive fees. No individual scammer lasts for too long, as the actual criers, who represent everything from the large Dockside Quarters residential complex to nearby restaurants on Restaurant Row the Harborage Healing House, are motivated to get rid of any imposters in their midst.

Between those selling goods, offering tours, and giving directions, the dockworkers of the Harborage come and go, working jobs that are steady but often far from routine. Some focus on ship repairs and others on construction of new sailing vessels, but they are most known for checking ships that come and go from Ordain to ensure that they're seaworthy enough to dock safely and leave Ordain smoothly. Someone must always be working at the docks, in case of an unexpected arrival, and shift workers come and go, often stopping in at the Harborage Seam for gossip and a free meal either before or after their daily shift. While their jobs are ostensibly just about keeping ships afloat, the dock workers also try not to let anyone fall through the cracks in Ordain, running a number of successful new resident programs out of the main Dockside Quarters complex, where decades-long residents and plucky newcomers often live side by side.

### Landmarks

**Dockside Quarters**

This massive residential building in the center of the Harborage provides most of the housing in the district. While its living spaces can vary widely in size, shape, and utilization of magic, the true heart of the Quarters (as most locals call it) is its many communal spaces, where residents can enjoy views of the water, work on projects, and get to know more about the city. Though the Quarters' very active Working Committee is open to anyone who has lived in the Quarters for at least a season, it is generally run by retired Harborage dockworkers, who use their long connections in the city to bring special events and speakers to the Quarters for the residents' enjoyment.

**The Dockyard**

The dockyard is the major employer of the Harborage, and always has at least a partial crew on hand to deal with any unforeseen arrivals or sudden emergencies. Dockworkers start in the dockyard as general workers, learning ships in and out. Once they have proven their proficiency, they generally take a specialization in shipbuilding, ship repair, or inspection. Each of the three attracts a different set of workers — ship repairers often spend some time sailing, or are settling down on land after barely escaping with their lies at sea, while shipbuilders are known for their precision architecture and use of magic, and inspectors are generally the most boisterous, in part to counter the negativity that captains have when their ships are being looked over for seaworthiness.

**The Harborage Seam**

This large open-air plaza provides meals to Harborage workers and residents. The dining tables sit under a number of repurposed sails, which shield it from the worst of any inclement weather, and diners can choose whether to take a free meal, pay for it, or pay extra to provide food for others. The Cindaric Sages provide for any shortfall, and the Seam's chief supplier uses relationships with House Lillifeld and the restaurants of nearby Restaurant Row to procure ingredients that might otherwise go bad and provide a place to work for aspiring chefs who wish to try their hands at providing large quantities of food each day. Many well-known chefs are known to take shifts at the Seam, which helps to keep up its reputation for simple but filling meals. It also is one of the best places in the district to get news, as the large communal tables encourage conversation, welcome, and a lot of gossip.

### Locals

**Divonnay (Lawful Good, Ordani human, she/her)**

Genial ship inspector Divonnay is determined that everyone in the Quarters be enjoying their lives, believing that any individual who is facing emotional distress is as much an issue as a crack in a ship's hull. Though she can be an overbearing busybody at times, determined to set up singles, write letters to bad managers, and otherwise interfere in the life of anyone in the Quarters who looks like they have a problem, the fact that she doesn't mean anything negative by it generally makes her a mild annoyance rather than a major Quarters problem. It doesn't hurt that Divonnay throws a really great party, and has helped to organize the criers on the docks to better direct new arrivals to places where they can get any help they need.

**Elvar Andraad (Neutral Evil, Ordani Soulbound, he/him)**

Elvar has been working on the docks for as long as he can remember — and he can remember a lot. It is said that Elvar has built at least 500 ships, which seems somewhat improbable, but his branded initials can be found on many ships, including those that have no record of being built in Ordain, or having been created recently. In truth, Elvar drowned as an apprentice shipbuilder over a century ago, but has refused to move on, becoming a Soulbound with a ddriving desire to build the perfect ship, at which point he believes he will move on. Unfortunately, over time, he has become increasingly corrupted by his state, and now creates a flaw in each ship he designs so that he can live forever, using enchantments and bribes to hide the truth of who he is from those around him.

**Zale (True Neutral, Bejak Wirrun, they/them)**

Though Zale is far from the only cook working at the Harborage Seam, they definitely have one of the strongest reputations for intriguing flavor combinations, and dockworkers have been known to switch shifts in order to schedule their meals for whenever Zale is in charge of the kitchen. While several Restaurant Row establishments have tried to lure Zale away from their current gig, they refuse, in part because they enjoy the work, and in part because their food includes all of the flavors favored by their sister Ivellia, who was separated from them at sea years ago, and who they hope will one day taste one of their meals and find them for a long-awaited reunion.

### Lore

The Harborage grew fairly organically as the city did — when docks were needed, docks were built; when housing was needed, a warehouse was turned into housing; when a visitor to Ordain was found to be stealing food out of hunger, a place to eat was created. Or at least that's how the Harborage tells it. Nothing was quite as easy as that, often requiring deals to be made, systems to be set up, and information to be shared, but in each case, the dockworkers have been a strong voice, using their influence within the Hallows and importance to a portside city to insist on the things that they believed would make the Harborage a better and stronger community.
