# Westgate

- **District Type:** Commercial (amenities for travel and trade)
- **Population:** 17,554 (High. Businesses, depots, and info centers with extreme foot traffic).
- **Lifestyle:** Audacious (Merchants, moneylenders, information brokers, tourists).
- **Services:** Stables, lodging, food & drink, leisure, equipment sales, accounting.

Although the district of Westgate is named for the actual gate itself, several other structures define this neighborhood as much as the monumental edifice — from various inns, shops, and taverns inside the gate, to the scattered market stalls and stables located without. And while the district’s various shops and other businesses are duly sanctioned by Ordain's Trading Houses, they manage to operate with a relatively larger amount of autonomy than the rest of the city. Locations of note include the Pyrefly Lounge, Westgate Wares, and Traveler’s Rest, in addition to the Great Western Gate that houses the Watchbox and Information Office.

As one of Ordain's primary points of entry, Westgate is sometimes (if rarely) burdened with the need for increased community awareness, if only to defend the local caravans outside the gate from opportunistic thieves or errant natural predators. Since Ordain has stood for centuries without assault from the outside world, its citizens see little need for an overt security presence other than a few city-owned Chessmen constructs; nevertheless, agents of the Burnished Hand and the Veiled Chain are known to occupy the area, along with the scattered array of retired and furloughed adventurers who reside here. Full of opportunity and information, Westgate is a district where nearly anything is possible, and encounters are as diverse as the far-off travelers that walk its streets.

### Culture

Life in Westgate is simultaneously an exemplary and atypical representation of life in Ordain. Despite it being one of the city's most well-traveled points of ingress, it's also a terminus for many travelers who don't manage to venture further into the city — and as a result, the outlying district is a small but potent example of the city's cultural melting pot. The languages of many people are spoken here, and the goods and services offered in Westgate (while often overpriced compared to other areas of the city) are plentiful.

The culture in Westgate has an element of transience to it, held together by the city's array of long-lasting institutions. As one of Ordain's outlying districts, Westgate is in a unique position to attract a wide array of cultural and ancestral backgrounds to its crowded urban confines. And like many of the city's districts, the politics of individuals here are as varied as their respective backgrounds.

### Landmarks

**The Great Western Gate**

Also known as "Adonia's Pass," the Great Western Gate is more than a monument: it's a gatehouse structure that houses multiple smaller facilities with a variety of purposes. The northern Watchbox serves as a shared ready room for members of the Burnished Hand, the Veiled Chain, and the Ordinate who need a temporary base of operations in the area. Meanwhile, the southern Information Office is occupied by an Information Officer (appointed by the Hall of Records in [[Assembly Hills]]), and serves as a 24-hour kiosk for people in need of information about the city.

The very pillars of the structure itself are used as massive notice boards, and are decorated with layers upon layers of parchment posts and hand-scrawled messages of response. The towering stone archway is crowned with an Observation Post, and manned on the ground at all times by one or more [[Chessman]] constructs — owned by the Ordinate and paid for with taxpayer coin.

**Traveler's Rest**

One of many similar healing houses located throughout Ordain and the Plateau, Traveler’s Rest is a soothing place of relaxation and recovery operated by the [[Cindaric Sages]]. This inviting wellness center specializes in healing the body (for physical ailments), the spirit (for magical ailments), and the spark (for ailments emotional and spiritual in nature). A variety of medicinal drugs and herbs are available for sale here, and a bright-eyed Cindaric healer named **Jessa Aritou** (LN, Arcturian Human, she/her) serves the patrons of Traveler’s Rest as the healing house's thoughtful Head Sage.

**Pyrefly Lounge**

The Pyrefly Lounge is a grungy, liquor-soaked tavern where unwitting outlanders often clash with a motley assortment of Ordain’s shadier and shiftier locals. Rumors run rampant here, but their veracity is often as watered down as the cheap wine. Patrons can frequently encounter **Magda Marteth** (Chaotic Good, Human Maziran, she/her), the Pyrefly's shrewd but sultry senior barkeep, who spends most of her time in the tavern — whether she's working or not.

**Westgate Wares**

Owned and operated by the retired adventurer **Nesbit Silverbraid** (Neutral Good, Vrjnhar Ordani, he/him), this busy emporium is often the first market encountered by travelers on their way into the city, and takes advantage of this convenient placement by overpricing its wide assortment of wares (an act considered somewhat garish by many, if not most, Ordani locals). Silverbraid's infamous affability allows Westgate Wares to get away with this opportunism, and everyone who walks out of the store tends to leave with something new in-hand — even if it's one of the emporium's coveted souvenir tokens.

### Locals

****Tolar Galanis** (Lawful Good, Human Ordani, he/him)**

This veteran of the Burnished Hand is a laconic warrior who can be found manning the Watchbox of The Great Western Gate when he isn't drowning his clandestine sorrows in ale at the Pyrefly Lounge. Rumors about about the source of Tolar's epic woes, but nobody has managed to convince the aged solider to share his miserable secrets.

****Phoebe Stodd** (Chaotic Good, Human Ordani, she/her)**

Recognizable by her sleeves of Ordani tattoos and braided top-knot, this sardonic information broker serves as an opportunistic go-between for the caravans parked outside Westgate and the Trading Houses stationed within. For those who seek knowledge about Ordain without drawing the attention of local authorities or enterprise, Phoebe Stodd is happy to oblige (for the right amount of coin).

****Jessa Aritou** (Lawful Neutral, Human Arcturian, she/her)**

Known for the adroitness of her problem-solving and the equanimity of her bedside manner, this thirty-something apothecary is the skilled head sage of the Traveler's Rest healing house. Locals and visitors alike regard Jessa as one of the most caring and considerate people the city has at its disposal, and her Arcturian roots make her an invaluable liaison for the Plateau's more rural patrons who seek succor within the city limits. The qualities people tend to remember most about Jessa are the kindness of her eyes and the wisdom of her words, despite her somewhat younger age.

Treat Jessa Aritou as a [[Cindaric Sage]] equipped with a [[Traveler's Rest Key]] and a [[Traveler's Rest Basement Key]].

****Nesbit Silverbraid** (Neutral Good, Vrjnhar Ordani, he/him)**

The affable general manager of Westgate Wares was an accomplished monster-hunting adventurer in a previous life, a fact that he keeps thinly veiled from his customers. If trouble were to break out in Westgate, you can trust that Nesbit Silverbraid and his handy blades will be the first to arrive — a notion that often serves as an adequate deterrent for would-be thieves and rabble rousers.

Treat Nesbit Silverbraid as an [[Assassin]] armed with an additional [[Shortsword]].

### Lore

Because the construction of the city spread outward from its center over the course of several centuries, Westgate is remarkably one of Ordain's newest neighborhoods, a quality that is evident in the district's architecture, ethos, and people. Some say the name "Westgate" was once ascribed to another district that lies closer to the heart of the city, and that each generation the monumental Great Western Gate is relocated further westward to appease the city's expansion; but this is actually one of many tall tales that do little more than stoke the fires of unrest against aggressive Trading House and Ordinate development.

One legend with more than a shred of truth, however, is the tale concerning Nesbit Silverbraid of Westgate Wares — who is rumored to have slain a chimera in the Rustvar hinterlands during his youth as an adventurer. Indeed, the three skulls of the monstrous creature hang above the cabinet in his arcane trophy room, entrance to which is only permitted to the Vrjnhar merchant's most loyal (or affluent) customers.
