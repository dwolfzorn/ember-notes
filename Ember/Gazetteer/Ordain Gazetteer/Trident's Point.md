# Trident's Point

- **District Type**: Residential
- **Population**: 2,475 (Small population of mainly Cascilian families, traders, and various officials)
- **Lifestyle**: Maritime (Sailors, marines, and diplomats that travel to and from the Casilian Republic)
- **Services**: City Defense, cargo transfer, training

Trident's Points is a recently expanded section of Ordain, a result of the good relations with the distant Cascilian Republic, with the majority of its inhabitants hailing from there. The district is one of the city's newest developments, and construction is still ongoing on certain streets as settlers from the republic arrive each year. Most of the buildings are based on a unique mix of traditional Ordani architecture and Cascilian aesthetics, with the city's usual deep reds replaced by Cascilian ocean blues and cyans.

At first glance, Tridents Point is oddly situated away from the water's edge directly. Instead, it is built upon the same large plateau that houses All-Fable Keep on the southern edge of the city. The Cascillians, however, often build their own cities this way, keeping their citizens safe from coastal predators in their homeland while constructing large lifts, cranes, and platforms to lower goods and people down to their awaiting navy.

### Culture

Tridents Point community is small and tight-knit, with sailors, dock workers, and crane operators often sharing midday meals and swapping gossip near the waterfront cliffside. Cascillian officials are a common sight moving to and from the large Cascilian embassy, and many adventuring types stay at the large Achorside Alehouse while training at the Azor Academy.

While the district is still young, the Cascilians wasted no time since their arrival in constructing a large lighthouse that now towers over the entrance to the city bay. This lighthouse was approved by the Ordinate 30 years ago, and at the time the members believed the lighthouse would be largely symbolic, but the Casicallisn take their duty to protect the city extremely seriously. A number of well-trained Casicallisn Marines are housed in the lighthouse's lower levels, and they often stage mock scenarios in case the city is attacked by outside forces.

### Landmarks

**Azor Academy**

Established by the renowned and now retired Azor "Stormbringer" Dane (Chaotic Good, Human Cascillian, he/him), the academy offers a variety of classes and courses for aspiring weapon fighters. Dane is known to train anyone in the art of "Si-sho," an elegant fighting technique used by the Cascilian Marines on the high seas. It requires a great deal of discipline with a long-bladed weapon, often a spear or, more commonly, a trident. The academy is large, but individual classes are available for those who want to sharpen their skills with other weapons or fighting styles, along with more extensive courses that provide a broader knowledge of sea-fighting techniques.

**Casilian Embassy**

Dominating the cliff edge next to the commanding Farsight Lighthouse, the Cascilian embassy consists of a central building with two long wings, each several stories tall, all containing offices and various bureaucratic stations where many officials write reports, send missives, and trade details back to the republic. The embassy features a large central pond, which is actually a map of the local region, showing the position of all trading vessels of the Republic which is kept off limits from the general public.

**Achorside Alehouse**

An extensive hostel and eatery for those traveling through the district or for those currently enrolled in the nearby Azor Academy, with several open patios filled with tables and chairs, the alehouse is the best spot to find a delicious Casclilian meal, which is often the famous spiced fish dish known as Asa. It's also a great place to chat with locals or fellow adventurers from the Anachraenum who pass through the alehouse or stay within its walls instead of All-Fable Keep itself. The alehouse is currently owned and operated by a married Casclilian couple, Lydia and Mira Lantell.

**Farsight Lighthouse**

The massive Casilian-built lighthouse took roughly 5 years to construct using local stonemasons from House Cevher, but it was almost entirely funded by the Casilian republic after it was approved by the Ordanite in  AS2493. It now stands over the bay and helps ships find their way through the waters late at night by constantly shining a large beam of focused light through a series of complex mirrors at the lighthouse apex. Around fifty Casilian Marines live in the lower floors, with rumors that the Casilian special forces also have an office in the lighthouse and operate clandestinely in Ordain.

### Locals

**Narisa Ress (Lawful Good, Cascilian Ashka, she/her)**

The Cascilian Ambassador is a retired member of the Casilian Navy. Her previous rank is unknown, but she holds a prominent position in the district, with many regarding her as the community's overall leader. She is an avid historian and personally fond of the Anachraenum, maintaining close ties with the organization to foster a healthy partnership on local issues. They are a patient teacher and captivating storyteller who delights in regaling people with her own personal adventures and past while sailing across the world.

**Vice-Admiral Laskin Vix (Lawful Neutral, Cascilian Human/Wirrun, they/them)**

Most are unaware of the Vice-Admiral's presence in Ordain, as they tend to keep to themselves within the Farsight Lighthouse. However, the rugged veteran is a capable commander who was posted to Ordain nearly a decade ago. Laskin takes the protection of the city very seriously but harbors a personal hatred for House Bastilla, and Katerin Bastilla especially, as they consider the trading house to be full of worthless pirates and murderers. They keep these feelings close to their chest, however, and while they are gruff and do not talk much with strangers, they have an impressively strategic mind and are deeply involved in Ordain's inner workings and politics.

**Lydia & Mira Lantell (Chaotic Good, Cascilian Humans, she/her)**

Former crane operators from the capital city of the Casicilian Republic, Alavai, Lydia, and Mira moved to the city twenty years ago and opened an alehouse with a desire to produce traditional Cascilian dishes for the local Ordani population. Since then, their small operation expanded after achieving a huge demand for lodging and food from academy trainees and Anchraenum members, and the construction of the much larger alehouse was finished only 8 years ago. They run the alehouse as a team and are ardently supportive of the mainly martial crowd they cater to, with much of the furnishings designed and built to allow for the "hot tempers" that flare up in such company.

**Azor "Stormbringer" Dane (Chaotic Good, Cascilian Human/Nir'ae, he/him)**

Azor Dane was born into a humble fishing family in the coastal town of Cascadia, in what was then the Cascilian Kingdoms. He lived a peaceful life until a chance encounter with the Shard God Katu while fishing in his late teens. Although he never became a follower of Katu in the traditional sense, many believe that Katu saw something in the young teen and blessed him in some way. When an undead raiding party attacked his village, Dane charged into the fight with his fishing spear, moving with such speed and grace that many in his community claimed he was a whirling blur of iron and wind. He soon left his village, vowing to protect all people from the threat of undeath. Throughout his adventures, he became a near-legendary figure, fighting in some of the most important battles of the last century, including the Battle of the Throat. However, his age has begun to slow him in combat, and he has semi-retired now, having built the Azor Academy in Ordain 10 years ago to help train new adventurers and fighters.

### Lore

Tridents Point is among the newest districts in Ordain and was officially approved by the Ordinate to better align and ally with the Cascilian Republic. It features some of the latest construction in the city and is partly vibrant and fresh, with construction still ongoing in some areas. Now, with the district's large lifts, some goods can bypass longer routes and go directly to the warehouses to the east. It is also the most direct route for many Anachraenums members to enter and leave the city.
