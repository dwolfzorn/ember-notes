# Skycrest

- **District Type**: Residential
- **Population**: 4,620 (Moderate. Private townhomes and apartments rise up around the striking exhibit homes, many of which are lived in. Visitors and locals fill the streets at all hours, and the district has a healthy nightlife.)
- **Lifestyle**: Bohemian. (A mixture of artists, merchants, nobles reside in this visually striking district.)
- **Services**: Food and drink, private clubs, private galleries, architectural tours.

Homes in Skycrest are celebrated for their individuality — each a testament to the owner’s taste and the artisans or architects behind it. Most buildings are built with terraces, large windows and wide balconies that provide sweeping views and a sense of openness.

### Culture

Residents of Skycrest may value aesthetics and refinement more than fortune or pedigree, as this district embraced the ideals of art and innovation as a badge of distinction in Ordain. The community is lively, intellectual, artistically inclined, with a distinct air of competition. Here, artists and patrons vie to set the next great trend.

### Landmarks

**Lantiri Plaza**

A sun-drenched square encircled by columns and trimmed with luminous topiary imported from the Corebright forests. Lantiri Plaza is famous for its open-air recitals, sculpture unveilings, and art shows which draw onlookers from around the city.

**The Skywall Gallery**

A daring, cube of a building whose interior is entirely hollow and consists of numerous platforms and levels suspended from cables or held aloft by enchantment. The inner walls are covered in magically enchanted paint that renders the exterior skyline in a living fresco. Open to the public, admittance is not cheap, but the experience is considered worth the price.

**Iridane House**

Perhaps the most iconic of Skycrest’s private residences, Iridane House is renowned for its seamless blend of prismatic glasswork terrariums fed by fountains that descend through the building's various levels. No longer an active residence, its owner hosts legendary midnight artist salons and private art showings.

**The Riverlight Steps**

A long, winding avenue that runs through several neighborhoods in Skycrest, beginning at its highest terraces and ending at the descent to the waterfront districts below. It is named for the ample lanterns that line the way from start to finish and is a favorite place for contemplative strolls.

### Locals

**Rilan Verdane (Chaotic Neutral, Tayan Human, he/him)**

An architect that fled his homeland to escape mounting debt, he now attempts to convince local patrons to support his next project. He hopes to use light and shadow to create a building with an ever evolving facade. He spends his evenings at the Wind Gallery, always sketching and drinking.

**Myella Tarrie (Neutral, Ordani Human, she/her)**

Hostess of Iridane House, Myella is famed for her exquisite taste in art and her sharp wit. Her midnight salons are the talk of the district, and she quietly champions emerging artists with the ample endowment offered by her position.

**Cavran Ellisar (Lawful Good, Ordani Cor'ak, he/him)**

A master gardener responsible for the design of several gardens and topiary sculptures in the district, and his personal collection of miniature trees. Patient and soft-spoken, Cavran is beloved for his generosity in sharing rare seeds and techniques with others.

**Pelin Dratch (Neutral Good, Lumek Fej, she/her)**

A renowned glass artist with a studio tucked into a hillside along the Riverlight, Pelin’s creations are prized for their vibrant colors and exacting detail. She can often be found working with her doors open so folk passing by can see her latest piece taking shape.

### Lore

Skycrest was once an unremarkable spur of the bluffs until vibrant white stone was found within. In short order numerous quarries were build to supply the growing city, and lasted until the cliff face was reduced to the level it stands at now.

A century after it served the construction of the city, those quarries were torn down and the land taken over by sculptors and masons who wanted easy access to the discarded stone, sand and gravel left in the old quarry sites.

Eventually the studios and brickyards became homes, and those homes became statements by the local artists and architects, stood on the bluffs for all to see. This drew wealthy patrons, their coin fueling growth and development that made Skycrest what it is today.
