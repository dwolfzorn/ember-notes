# Ordain Spires

Ordain's spires form the outer edges of the city, taking advantage of the natural hills in the Arctus Plateau that likely convinced the travelers who first made their way to the inlet to choose it as their home. Though the spires are home to several districts dominated by Ordani government and administrative needs, the term spires is most commonly used to refer to the residential neighborhoods nestled between them, where city life is a little slower paced and often a bit more luxurious.

### Environment

A set of well-maintained paths and interconnected platforms connect many of the spires districts, giving those who live there an easy way to move around the city without needing to cross from one side to the other. For those who work, shop, or enjoy the culture found in the Flats and Docks, most districts have at least one set of stairs or paved road that leads directly into the center of the city.

### Ecology

The Spires have little plant or animal life to speak of outside of curated green spaces created by their residents, with one major exception. Many birds enjoy nesting in nooks and crannies found in the high places in the spires, and can be seen flying in and out of the area at all hours. Some districts in the spires even attempt to attract specific types of birds, seemingly for their beauty, though their ability to carry messages over long distances may add to their appeal.

### Lore

Primarily built during Ordain's first big expansion, after key trading routes and materials were secured, the Spires are said to represent a city deciding how it should govern itself - though there are several residential districts within the Spires, which are often what is most closely associated with the word spires today, it is also the area that holds the Ordinate and Hallows (which make and manage the city's laws}, the Temple Ward and Cindarin Temple districts (which provide spiritual guidance), and All-Fable Keep (which holds many of the city's historical artifacts and knowledge).
