# The Ordinate

- **District Type:** Governmental
- **Population:** 38 (Very light permanent population of Ordinate employees, high traffic while the Ordinate is in session)
- **Lifestyle:** Focused (the Ordinate is everything for those who live there)
- **Services:** Law (citywide policy, development of laws)

### Culture

Few live in the district that holds the Ordinate, but it never feels empty, filled with the many decision facilitators who help the Ordinate to function, including the proxies of important parties who bring petitions to the Ordinate, the clerks who present evidence of the potential impact of a decision (often in private sessions, though any impact reports are usually made public for those who care to read them), the scribes who capture what happens in an Ordinate session, and the Hallows representatives who uphold and enact the Ordinate's laws. On days when the Ordinate is in session, most of these parties are in the Ordinate itself to witness the session, leaving the streets of the district feeling somewhat still unless the Ordinate is making a decision about something that catches the public's fancy and brings more observers to the surrounding streets.

Once the Ordinate adjourns for the week, though, is when the real activity of the Ordinate district begins. The sprawling network of clerks, record keepers, and stewards is somewhat self-managing, kept in balance by a common interest in the continued functioning of the Ordinate and enough competing priorities that anyone who attempts to circumvent the system will have someone reporting on their activities. Tradition also holds a fair amount of weight in the Ordinate, influencing everything from where people sit when the Ordinate is in session to the order in which Presentations are scheduled by the Chief Clerk. While this keeps things moving at a steady hum and ensures that everyone knows what to expect from the Ordinate, there are those who wonder if the reliance on old ways makes the Ordinate unable to respond quickly to any new and unexpected problems.

### Landmarks

**Clerk's Row**

This unofficial term for the stretch of offices outside of the Ordinate holds the few residences in the Ordinate, including a residence for the designee of each current member of the Ordinate and for the Chief Clerk, who deals with the administrative scheduling of Ordinate sessions. Most of these are used primarily as places to live only on the days the Ordinate is in session, but the Chief Clerk generally lives in theirs full-time. Several past Chief Clerks and influential scribes have also built or acquired homes along the row, hoping it will help them stay informed about Ordinate matters after retiring from their posts.

**The Ordinate**

A centerpiece not only of the district but of Ordain proper, the Ordinate is an architectural delight that also contains a myriad of rooms needed to conduct the business of the city, all centered around its magnificent atrium, where the Ordinate members publicly hear petitions and make decrees. Though not always full, the Ordinate usually has some number of observers when it is in session, and tours are given of the structure on its off days, usually by clerks and scribes in training.

**The Underchambers**

For most, the Underchambers are little more than a myth, rumored to be a place where overenthusiastic Ordinate clerks sleep during important debates, or a set of rooms that only members of the Ordinate know about where deals are struck between them in velvet-lined rooms filled with impossible luxuries. In truth, these tunnels beneath the Ordinate's main chamber, though constructed to provide a way out of the Ordinate if needed, have become cluttered, holding everything from gifts for Ordinate members (which they are honor-bound to turn over to the Ordinate for disposal) to supplies for the many scribes who capture each official decree. Some say that there is a way through the mess if you know where you're going, but the popular prank of setting up illusions of skeletons in dusty chairs in the Underchamber corridors makes it clear that most, at least, don't believe it.

### Locals

**Elwin Kirei (Lawful Neutral, Ordani human, he/him)**

A former Chief Clerk of the Ordinate, and father of current chief clerk Winna, Elwin stayed neutral about every issue facing the Ordinate…up until his retirement after 40 years in his role. Now, he makes a living publishing books about his time at the Ordinate that seem calculated to make the role of the clerk one of a heroic public servant saving the Ordinate from their own mistaken ideas, though they are also so drily and confusingly written that most don't care. Elwin believes that his writing is helping his daughter Winna as she makes the role her own, but also enjoys being able to finally speak his mind, and ignores the fact that this has caused tension between them. Instead, he has thrown himself into his work and is hard at work on his next opus, focused on what he believes to be a scintillating discussion about grain pricing and storage from a decade ago.

**Tiravan Elms (Chaotic Neutral, Ordani drakon, they/them)**

Ordinate scribe turned journalist Tiravan keeps their ear to the ground and their quill to the page, reporting on Ordinate business day and night so that the rest of Ordain knows what the Ordinate is doing. Raised by parents who fled the Tayan Empire after falling out of favor, Tiravan tries to be a barrier against corruption in the city, despite having not yet found much of it. They spend much of their time cultivating sources within the Ordinate, enjoying being seen to be among the powerful and influential, but while Tiravan is a dogged pursuer of the truth, they're also easily swayed by the opinions of those they know and trust, and can be manipulated by those who wish their perspective to reported as the absolute truth. Given that few others know the ins and outs of the Ordinate as well as they do, Tiravan's word is often trusted by others without question, even if it is untrue.

**Winna Kirei (True Neutral, Ordani human, she/her)**

The current chief clerk of the Ordinate. Winna has only been in the role a few years, and is well-respected, having overcome those who believed she only got the job due to her father's influence and then those who were insulted in some fashion by her father's ongoing relitigation of former Ordinate business in his dry but detailed books. She's known for her near encyclopedic memory of dates, faces, and incidents from across Ordain, which she uses to determine which items the Ordinate needs to consider in its session and which will either resolve themselves or can be sent to another source of authority, like the Trading Houses or the Hallows. Among the pieces of information that Winna has collected are several incidents and issues that could be used to blackmail various members of the Ordinate, which she holds onto not to use them herself but to be aware of whether or not an Ordinate member is being unduly influenced by someone else. The secrets are meticulously filed and written in a shorthand that Winna developed herself, which is difficult but not impossible to decipher.

### Lore

The ruling body known as the Ordinate came out of the ashes of the Veiled War, but it took time for the massive structure that houses its daily activities to be built. It is said that the building of the structure helped to cement the relationships between the Trading Houses (which provided supplies to construct the building that ranged from stone mined by House Cevher to fabrics created by House Salva to arcane protections carried in by Red Bastilla, founder of House Bastilla) as well as the mutual respect between the Ordinate and the Hallows, which helped supply the labor that turned the raw materials into the building that the Ordinate has now become.
