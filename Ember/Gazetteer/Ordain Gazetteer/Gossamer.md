# Gossamer

- **District Type:** Commercial (fashion) and Residential (fashion industry workers)
- **Population:** 12,851 (Moderately dense population of workers in the fashion industry, along with a steady stream of both commercial and personal customers)
- **Lifestyle:** Competitive (Hard-working fashion workers who like to publicly show off their skills and compete for local glory)
- **Services:** Fashion (Fabrics, Clothing, Thread)
- **Influences:** Closely tied to House Salva, the Trading House responsible for garments

### Culture

Despite most of Gossamer being dedicated to the creation and design of fabrics, and the deep respect for all parts of the craft, there is occasional tension between the "makers" who spool thread and make raw fabric and the "drapers" who turn that fabric into fashion about who is more vital in the creation of the clothes Ordain wears. Both sides, though, have roughly the same values - work hard, try to make beautiful things, and take pride in seeing their work every time they walk through the streets of Ordain.

Gossamer often likes to think of itself as the hardest working district in Ordain, with most living not far from their workplaces and taking every opportunity to show off their skill. While Gossamer isn't about flashing wealth, many who work and live in the neighborhood will wear or carry something that shows how they contribute to the neighborhood, whether it's a pair of spool earrings, a raw-fabric scarf, or a piece of clothing made with fabric they wove.

Though work and fashion are important in Gossamer, they are not the only pastime. Friendly competition - between neighbors, coworkers, and shops - is common. The competitions take different forms, but tend to have some element of ridiculousness or silliness to they m and often use the materials of the district in ways they weren't intended to be used, ranging from tightrope walking on tightly braided threads to the annual "Spin to Win," where two groups compete to move heavy iron spinning wheels as far down the neighborhood's Threadfall Alley as they can without using magic. While most of the competition happens within Gossamer, they have a friendly rivalry with Highgate's Arcturian craftsfolk that shows up in occasional intermural games.

### Landmarks

**The Awlry**

The Awlry specializes in fashion accessories that reflect the cosmos, ornamenting the scarves and belts they sell with small metal beads that are designed to catch the light and resemble stars in the sky and using fabric that changes color to mimic the changing of the moons over time. One of the secrets to this finery is in a hidden garden that can only be accessed by walking through the Awlry, which holds enchanted flowers believed to originate in Rhivan.

**Snips**

A bar where your clothes can be altered by the servers while you wait. Newcomers are not allowed to pick who alters their garment, though they are sometimes asked questions that supposedly connect them to the fitter who will work the best for them. Several of the servers also make clothes for stealth - it is said that if you find their card in your pocket, they've decided that you have promise as a spy or thief and want to attach themselves to you early.

**Threadfall Alley**

A long and narrow street that contains most of the weaving shops in Ordain. They advertise themselves to clients by weaving flags that show some aspect of their work and letting them fly in front of their shops. The use of air and wind spells to make the flags catch the breeze is common, but with so many people attempting the same effect, it can become a bit of a maelstrom. Any attempts to create rules to stop this have been rejected by all involved, who believe that the whipping winds of the alley are part of its charm.

**The Wheel**

A thread-spinning shop whose main spinning wheel (which takes several people to operate) has never slowed to a halt since the shop began. It is strongly believed that stopping the wheel will lead to some citywide calamity, and locals often feel that they are both saving the city through their work with the wheel and being ground down by it, depending on the day.

### Locals

**Vijin Barriq (Lawful Evil, Ordani Human, he/him)**

Vijin spent his career cultivating his reputation as a weaver and has risen steadily over time through House Salva, using his influence to make connections within the district (making sure a certain threadmaker connects with a specific weaver, etc). It is well known within the district that those who gift him some of their goods (or their sewing time) are likely to have more buyers for their product, and he can often be seen wearing an array of fine clothing that doesn’t quite match but looks very expensive.

**Tazhira Dyre (Chaotic Good, Ordani Kiska, she/her)**

Tazhira is a talented cobbler who has been trying to get an official shop on Threadwalk Alley for years, but has yet to catch the attention of any Salva influencers with her “functional but not flashy” footwear. To try to get then to notice her products, which she walks in as she makes her way from her home across the city each day, she has begun adding magical effects to her shoes which range from creating an effect of mist as the wearer walks to leaving footprints that make them easier to track.

**Jintal Aveniri (Chaotic Neutral, Ordani Human, they/them)**

The current three-year champion at Spin to Win, Jintal is somewhat famous in Gossamer for their incredible ingenuity in finding ways to get the iron spinning wheel moving without breaking any of the competition rules. Despite having been approached by a recruiter from the Anachraenum and an administrator from the Hallows about possibly putting their skills to a different use, Jintal has remained in their job collecting scraps that the various fabric shops of Gossamer no longer need, which they're trying to use to create a fabric construct.

### Lore

The competitive nature of Gossamer is no accident - it was encouraged by House Salva to create camaraderie among workplaces, give the locals something to do as an outlet, and (if you ask the right people) prevent the vital workers who create Ordain's clothing from banding together and wanting more relaxed work conditions. The more intense that Gossamer's residents become at all aspects of life, the thinking goes, the more productive they will ultimately be.
