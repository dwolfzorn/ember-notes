# Lastgate

- **District Type:** Commercial with Residential
- **Population:** 20,619 (High. Estates, homes, and shops with heavy foot traffic).
- **Lifestyle:** Modest-to-Comfortable (Merchants, information brokers, retired professionals).
- **Services:** Lodging, stables, secondhand goods, consignment, food & drink, leisure.

Lastgate has affectionately earned its name as the place where many of Ordain's retired and pensioned move to settle down. The district isn't merely a retirement community, however, as much as it overtly caters to a simple, slower lifestyle (which can often be lacking in many of the city's other residential neighborhoods). This is a district of thrift stores, consignment shops, and estate sales; and several of the citizens here live on fixed incomes. Of course, Lastgate also has a thriving community of younger Ordani who like the pace of the wayward district. And more recently, Lastgate provides a convenient point of ingress to the city for soldiers of the Burnished Hand stationed on Bura Island nearby.

As one of Ordain's primary points of entry, Lastgate is sometimes (if very rarely) burdened with the need for increased community awareness, if only to defend the local caravans outside the gate from opportunistic thieves or errant natural predators. Since Ordain has stood for centuries without assault from the outside world, its citizens see little need for an overt security presence other than a few city-owned Chessmen constructs; nevertheless, agents of the Burnished Hand and the Veiled Chain are known to occupy the area, along with the scattered array of retired and furloughed adventurers who reside here.

### Culture

Lastgate's population is a remarkably diverse one, thanks to its proliferation as a commercial thoroughfare and its aged citizenship of assorted retirees. People of all walks of life from all corners of the Arctus Plateau are known to settle in Lastgate, where one's identity as an urbanite is often secondary to one's desire to get along. The shops and assorted business that occupy the district cater to a wide variety of needs and interests, both niche and commonplace; indeed, a robust assortment of Ordani and Arcturian cultural cornerstones can be found throughout the neighborhood's crowded nooks and crannies.

### Landmarks

**The Southern Gatehouse**

This gatehouse structure houses multiple smaller facilities with a variety of purposes. A southern Watchbox serves as a shared ready room for members of the Burnished Hand, the Veiled Chain, and the Ordinate who need a temporary base of operations in the area. Meanwhile, the Information Office to the north is occupied by an **Information Officer** (appointed by the Hall of Records in [[Assembly Hills]]), and serves as a 24-hour kiosk for people in need of information about the city.

The very pillars of the structure itself are used as massive notice boards, and are decorated with layers upon layers of parchment posts and hand-scrawled messages of response. The gate here is manned on the ground at all times by one or more [[Chessman]] constructs — owned by the Ordinate and paid for with taxpayer coin.

**Agatha's Curiosities & Consignments**

Owned and operated by the matronly collector **Agatha Zimm** (NG, Ordani Human, she/her), this thrift shop features a staggering variety of storied curios and secondhand oddities on its dusty and cluttered shelves. Whether or not the tales are true, Agatha has a story for every item in the shop, which she gleefully orates to pressure would-be customers. Indeed, locals find it hard to leave Agatha's curiosity shop each time they visit without a little something to show for it.

**The Rusty Rivet**

This busy repair shop is the pride and joy of **Gordi Grynfeather** (LN, Ordani Nir'ae, he/him), a part-time music teacher who dabbles in artifice. When Gordi can't work the shop himself, he leaves its management to his irreverant son **Brodi** (CG, Ordani Nir'ae, he/him) and his sardonic daughter **Kat** (CG Ordani Nir'ae, she/her), who swap shifts as often as they trade playful insults. A variety of other younger family members are known to help out with smaller tasks around the shop, which is known for handling a wide array of repairs — from musical instruments and miscellaneous equipment to arms and armor. With a welcoming parlor and a convivial all-ages staff, The Rusty Rivet has a warm reputation for being a place where strangers often become fast friends.

**The Rask & The Rider**

A quaint bed and breakfast located near the Southern Gatehouse, The Rask & The Rider is owned and operated by the intrepid young innkeeper **Etheria Matsaros** (NG, Ordani Human, she/her) and her brash husband **Solias** (CG, Ordani Human, he/him). This three-story townhouse features a half-dozen modest-to-comfortable rooms for rent and a luxurious honeymoon suite, which is reserved for special occasions and larger parties when it isn't being rented out to young lovers (which, given the nature of Lastgate's relatively aged community, is more often than not).

### Locals

****Agatha Zimm** (NG, Ordani Human, she/her)**

This elderly collector of curios and antiquities is the proprietor of the aptly-named Agatha's Curiosities & Consignments, a secondhand shop the retired sorceress treats like an extended collection. Her matronly demeanor and penchant for gossip have earned her a reputation among locals as a quidnunc of the highest order; but her curiousness is rarely meddlesome, and her authenticity tends to inspire purchases from those who least expect it.

****Gordi Grynfeather** (LN, Ordani Nir'ae, he/him)**

This part-time music teacher and repair specialist is the owner of The Rusted Rivet, and is considered to be Lastgate's consummate family man. Known for his wide range of skillful abilities, proclivity for artifice, and playful sense of humor, the convivial Mr. Grynfeather has a knack for remembering the name of every customer that enters his shop.

****"The Judge"** (CN, Arcturian Kivahr, he/him)**

Not much is known about this wizened retiree other than his role as a former investigator of the Veiled Chain and his madcap manner of speech. A relative curiosity himself, with a muddled past and a dearth of intimates or relatives, The Judge is an affordable information broker — albeit one known to dispense questionable advice to the desperate and the gullible. The wily octogenarian lives in a rickety townhouse across the street from Agatha Zimm's shop, which he keeps a good eye on for safe measure.

### Lore

Unbeknownst to all, one of the unassuming curios in Agatha Zimm's shop is secretly a powerful Primordis artifact capable of cosmological scrying. Furthermore, a mysterious connection ties this Fey relic to The Judge and his lunatic temperaments — although the specific nature of the connection eludes even the wily Judge himself. Is the former investigator cursed by the strange otherworldly object, is his mind under the control of an unseen Fey entity, or is the supernatural connection a sign of something altogether worse? Perhaps time and fate may tell.
