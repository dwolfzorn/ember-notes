# Palisade Cross

- **District Type:** Residential with some Commercial
- **Population:** 25,939 (High. Historical and redeveloped homes with heavy foot traffic).
- **Lifestyle:** Modest-to-Comfortable (Hirelings, sellswords, laborers, craftspeople, opportunists).
- **Services:** Lodging, housing, grocery, equipment, hirelings.

This rough-and-tumble district was historically one of Ordain's more affluent neighborhoods, but the endless march of time has slowly robbed the area of that status. As time goes by, the once-proud stone buildings sag a bit more than they used to, and the splinters of the timber dwellings become harder and harder to hide. Today, Palisade Cross is much more a representation of the city's transplants than its settlers — a disparate sprawl of people from various places who come together under a united promise of achievement and growth.

Despite its outward looks, Palisade Cross is a rather progressive neighborhood, whose people tend to have a forward-facing approach to their existence here (rather than a painstaking examination of Ordain's past). In fact, many of the denizens of Palisade Cross make their living (and often a good one that) as hirelings for various adventurers, explorers, or profiteers. If you need an extra hand for a profitable escapade, Palisade Cross is a good place to look for one.

### Landmarks

**Fletcher's**

This imposing timber edifice on the western edge of the district is what remains of a once-proud manse known as Fletcher's Community Home for Wayward Youths, a place where Ordain's lost and abandoned adolescents could find the simple pleasures of home, hearth, kith, and kin. But Fletcher's days as an orphanage of warm repute have long fallen by the wayside. Now, this rickety wooden behemoth lies dark and abandoned, and its skeletal eaves of splintered lumber loom above the rest of the district like some monolith of time forgotten. Fletcher's casts a pall over the people of Palisade Cross, who believe various tall tales and legends that surround the place — like the story of the spectral orphans that haunt the mansion's darkest corridors, or the tale of a roguish wizard who sought a place to hide but found his doom instead.

**The Cracked Flagon**

The tower-shaped vestibule of this dingy watering hole resembles a massive stone flagon, embellished with steel and copper fineries; notably, it bears a large crack down the vertical length of its front side, from the roof's edge to the top of the front door jamb. Employees (and a plaque next to the front door) are quick to inform newcomers of the crack's origin during the Great Stonework Quake of 2503, and there is a distinct air of novelty throughout the tavern's musty interior, despite its relative age. The Cracked Flagon known city-wide as a great place to soak up a good story while sipping on a frosty ale.

**The Hayren Arms**

This huge, multi-story apartment building provides ample housing for the locals. Originally built by the Hayren noble house for their personal army of servants and guards, it eventually became a dwelling for rent by the people of Ordain at large. The apartments here are small, and plain, with only room for the basics, but the cost is low. The people of Hayren Arms are good and decent, providing a packed community that looks out for its own.

**Rylo's Rooms**

A large inn partially build into the massive stone pillar found in the district, Rylo's Rooms is the largest inn to be found in Ordain, with several hundred rooms, all offered at low prices. What it lacks in creature comforts and quality it makes up for in affordability. Founded by Erban Rylo several decades ago, it's now run by his children.

### Locals

****Lady Wells** (Chaotic Neutral, Ordani Human, she/her)**

This retired schoolmarm serves as the reclusive caretaker for the estate upon which Fletcher's resides; however, she actually plays very little part in the landmark's preservation or maintenance, and has fallen to the wayside as much as the abandoned estate itself. Considered by many to be a "crazy old hermit," the absentminded Lady Wells is shunned by most of the denizens of Palisade Cross, and rarely seen outside of her own ramshackle apartment (situated in the shadows of Fletcher's, at the end of a row of similar low-income housing).

****Hafton Tradd** (Lawful Neutral, Ordani Human, he/him)**

This congenial patriarch is the owner and head barkeep of The Cracked Flagon, which he almost fully staffs with members of his rather jubilant family. The cousin of a venerated Anachraenum alumnus, Hafton offers steep discounts and complimentary beverages to members of Ordain's famed guild of treasure hunters; as a result, a lot of interesting rumors and legends find their way to the gregarious barkeep's ears (which are always open for more).

### Lore

The eponymous palisades of Palisade Cross were erected during the district's earliest days, and have been all but forgotten as the neighborhood was slowly built upon and around them. Indeed, much of the original palisade serves as the architectural foundation for what became Fletcher's — so west-side denizens of Ordain tend to usually have an eye on the neighborhood's namesake, whether they know it or not.
