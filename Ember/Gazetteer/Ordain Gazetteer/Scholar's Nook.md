# Scholar's Nook

- **District Type:** Commercial with some Residential
- **Population:** 7,662 (Medium. Book stores, shops, and housing with moderate foot traffic).
- **Lifestyle:** Comfortable-to-Wealthy (Scholars, publication professionals, retired citizens).
- **Services:** Book-making, libraries, education, spellcasting, consignment, lodging.
- **Influences:** Early Signaran/Signborn influence.

Binder's Row is one of Ordain's most commercially-minded academic districts, where the private exchange of books and related goods is just as important as the neighborhood's public library and education services. Indeed, important landmarks like the Old City Library draw interested readers here from the entire Plateau over. Furthermore, the name Binder's Row is actually a double entendre that speaks to the district's origins.

A wealthy Signborn conjurer and book collector named **Mordus Kôl** helped settle and develop the district alongside the Cindaric Sages who built Old City Library. It is often said (but rarely known for certain) that Kôl summoned a powerful fiendish entity from Signara to help accumulate his esoteric collection of arcane tomes and manuals, and that this creature lurks beneath the streets of Binder's Row to this very day. Whether locals consider this tale to be rumor or truth, there's no mistaking that a certain legacy of conjuration in Binder's Row has endured.

### Landmarks

**Old City Library**

Also known as Tomehold, this venerable library is maintained by a group of Cindaric Sages known as the Coalescents, and serves as a repository of all modern and historical knowledge regarding the planet Ember — including famous natural and supernatural landmarks, the ecology of Ember's metaphysical resources, and chronicles of the various shard gods. Many initiates of the Cindaric Order begin their training here, where the tenets of the Sages have been recorded since Adonia founded the great city of Ordain.

**The Crooked Spine**

This serpentine alleyway is named for its winding and circuitous streetscape, which is crowded with a variety of specialty bookshops, many of which are dingy havens for dusty, forgotten tomes. Used book stores, smut vendors, and printers of scandal rags are in ready supply here, where nearly two dozen ramshackle business are crammed on top of each other. Of particular note is Ashton's Rare Books, a bookshop that caters to collectors of rare tomes and specializes in the delicate restoration of aged texts; a capricious bookbinder named **Ashton Smythe** (CN, Waerd Human, he/him) owns and operates this sought-after establishment.

**The Secret Shelf**

Those in search of low-level conjuration and divination spellcasting services or rare arcane tomes need look no further than this eccentric magic shop on the northeast edge of the district, owned and operated by **Kastilla Kôl** (LN, Ordani Signborn, she/her) and her younger brother **Yura** (LN, Ordani Signborn, he/him). These capricious Signborn siblings each harbor a bit of their ancestor Mordus Kôl's proclivity for spellcasting — headstrong Kastilla with her conjurations, and apprehensive Yura with his divinations.

**The Gadrick Estate**

A modest but charming public library founded by the late fantasist Erison Gadrick. Maintained by his successors and funded by an array of wealthy Ordani philanthropists, several of which remain anonymous.

**The Inkswell Bookshop**

A bookshop with a small but loyal clientele, run by Darius Cevher. Famed for its selection of rare tomes and the gregariousness of its owner.

**Librarium of Spectra**

This grand temple was erected to honor [[Spectra]], goddess of magic and knowledge, and is well-known throughout the continent for its elaborate display of arcanum and esoterica.

**Scrivener's Den**

A specialty equipment shop that caters to loremasters, cartographers, visual artists, and scribes. Many of the material components and fine inks needed to copy spells can be purchased here, along with a variety of amusing occult curios.

### Locals

****Miles Melfield** (Neutral Good, Ordani Human, he/him)**

This precocious Cindaric Sage serves as the senior Coalescent at Old City Library, and is a generally helpful and attentive fellow despite his penchant for humorous protest. His extensive arcane research has also afforded Miles a small compliment of wizardly spellcasting.

****Elden Jhonda** (Chaotic Neutral, Ordani Human, she/her)**

This aged fortune teller is a purveyor of false hopes and dreams, brought to life by little more than mind games and parlor tricks (with which she is most skilled). Despite an common understanding that Jhonda's prophecies and divinations are the stuff of fantasy, she remains a popular public figure — perhaps due to the flamboyant decor in the window of her shop and her own fun-loving, rambunctious personality.

****Gregorius Prestyn** (Chaotic Good, Arcturian Human, he/him)**

A descendent of the legendary St. Prestyn, this famed Arcturian researcher occupies a humble apartment in Binder's Row, and is known to provide consultation services for organizations that value his extensive acumen (such as the Anachraenum and the Veiled Chain).

### Lore

In addition to the legends concerning the late Mordus Kôl and his so-called fiendish servitor, rumors abound regarding the presence of a dungeon complex below the Old Town Library that's connected to the secret tunnels beneath the Anachraenum and other areas of southeastern Ordain. There is certainly some credence to these stories, but the Coalescents and other Cindaric Sages who attend Tomehold haven't actually used the dungeon passages in nearly a century.
