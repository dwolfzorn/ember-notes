# The Burns

- **District Type:** Commercial (alchemy, consumables) with some Residential
- **Population:** 4,242 (Light population of alchemists, potionmakers, and a few poisoners)
- **Lifestyle:** Experimental (amateur scientists and makers who will do anything to discover something new)
- **Services:** Alchemy and Consumables (potions, poisons, salves, alchemical substances)

### Culture

In the Burns, experimentation is everything. It was experimentation that led the famed alchemist Barbazi to develop his Barbazi balloons, which help to cleanse the air in the district of the smoke from the industrial work in nearby neighborhoods, and attracted many aspiring alchemists to the area. It was experimentation that led these students to stay and build up the district on the edge of the river, iterating on what they'd learned while developing new processes and substances of their own. Where some fear working with unknown substances and trying radical new ideas, especially when there is the potential for them to go wrong in dangerous ways, innovators and risk-takers came together in the Burns to create during an era of rapid-fire new inventions. And though now things have settled, somewhat, as the Burns both innovates and supports itself by selling its existing goods and materials to the rest of Ordain, the district still likes to think of itself as the place where the next genius alchemical idea will be born.

The Burns has always had a slight division between those focused on what can be created through innovation and those who care more about the process of experimentation itself, going back to the days of Barbazi a century ago. Some of Barbazi’s students chose to focus on the end result of his work – the fact that he was able to create something that made a positive impact on the district around him - and felt inspired by targeting their experimentation at specific goals that would help Ordain. Others focused on the innovation of his methods and used them to try out new alchemical concepts, with no end goal other than seeing if they would work at all.

This difference of opinion about the goal of alchemy persists in the Burns to this day. Those who focus on the end product often live closer to the docks, where they believe they can get a better sense of what visitors and locals need, while those who are more interested in the process of creation for its own sake often experiment in warehouses that aren’t currently being used to store products. Both sides respect and generally believe they need each other, though the Dockside alchemists worry about the dangers and frivolousness of the more outlandish experimentation and the Warehouse experimenters complain that their peers have lost their experimental zeal in a desire to meet unnecessary customer needs.

### Landmarks

**The Barbazi Bazaar**

This series of open-air stalls is often called "the Barbazi Bizarre" by locals, as it is the best place to find the rare alchemical materials and oddities, from vessels made of scavenged bones to recipes for only partially tested concoctions. The Bazaar offers stalls on a first-come first-serve basis each day, and many of the sellers work on a trade system, swapping items with fellow sellers that will help them pursue their own goals or experiments. Though the atmosphere is lively, manager/owner **Lizelle Endring** (Chaotic Good, Ordani human, she/her) attempts to keep things relatively safe with her three rules of the Bazaar - all sales are final, all risks must be disclosed, and anyone who gets hurt due to the seller's negligence is banned for life. As a result, warnings about potential death and dismemberment from items in the Bazaar tend to be very thorough, if generally ignored.

**Scorch's**

On the surface, this shop sells hot sauce and "romantic aids" (which the many signs in the shop are careful to warn should not be mixed), but it is also one of the best suppliers of explosive alchemical ingredients in the Burns. Anything dangerous that comes into Scorch's is stored in an empty well in the basement, which is heavily shielded against damage and can be quickly refilled with water in case anything goes wrong. Scorch's purported owner, **Flametongue**, who lives above the shop, likes to tell stories about the fires and explosions created by his development of new hot sauce flavors, which he uses to build up the shop's reputation and hide any accidents from alchemical experimentation, but he has little to do with either. Both the shop and its trade remain under the control of Flametounge's long-time friend **Aevin Sarl** (Chaotic Neutral, Ordani human, he/him), who poses as the store assistant.

**The Syderie**

Drinks at the Syderie are known for never quite being what they seem, with alchemical ingredients that change the nature of a drink — making a hot drink taste cold, for instance — or have an unusual physical effect on whoever drinks them, ranging from making them lighter on their feet to temporarily changing the color of their hair or tongue. **Sydronach Stout** (Neutral Good, Arcturian Keth, she/her), the long-time owner of the wholesaler Syd's Liquids, which supplies commercial fluids like dyes, inks, oils, beverages, chemicals, and potions to businesses across Ordain, opened the to bring more customers into the large direct-to-consumer shop at the front of her warehouse, but its ever-growing popularity of the shop has been more than she expected. With the novelty of each new drink effect wearing off quickly, she wonders how she can either find a new showstopper or tamp down interest in the place.

### Locals

**Flametongue (Neutral Good, Ordani vrjnhar, he/him)**

Flametongue, whose real name is Fazeel, is the face of Scorch's, despite the fact that he has absolutely no skills at alchemy whatsoever. What he does excel at is perfume making, creating scents that seem to capture emotions. To avoid any confusion with his day job, he sells his perfumes under the name Lee Za (created by spelling his name backwards), though he deeply wishes to get the recognition as a perfume maker that he believes he deserves.

**Daurin Vediqili (True Neutral, Ordani human, he/him)**

Daurin has grown in influence over the past few years thanks to the innovative alchemical concoctions he has debuted at the annual Showcase, a social event that also gives all shops in the area an opportunity to show off their best work. His works tend to use cutting-edge techniques that he honed in the Warehouse section of the Burns, but are firmly aimed at giving the Ordani people what they want, making him popular Dockside as well. The attention and popularity he's garnered over the last few years has begun to go to Daurin's head a bit, and he has promised to make his next invention something to rival Barbazi's genius, but he has had no new ideas and is becoming desperate to find some way to save face, even if that means stealing someone else's work and passing it off as his own.

**Ylvo Indrane (Lawful Evil, Ordani human, he/him)**

Ylvo represents the small but influential Poisoner's Guild in the Burns. Though not an officially recognized group, the self-proclaimed experts of the deadly arts meet for drinks at various local establishments (including the Syderie, one of Ylvo's personal faves, though its proprietor Syd really wishes he wouldn't) and go for strolls along the docks as they discuss their work. Ylvo is flamboyant in his appearance and demeanor, wearing a fluttering black cloak with singed edges and a necklace of bones of unknown origins, but this is somewhat affected as a way to draw attention to himself and allow many of his fellow guild members (including those who are never seen publicly with the guild) to operate without drawing attention to them. He personally is one of the least dangerous of the guild, as he operates by what he calls a strict code of ethics, only poisoning those within the guild as a way to maintain his skills and power.

### Lore

Though locals in the Burns often play up the many accidents and conflagrations in the district, in truth there have been very few real accidents, thanks to a tacit agreement between the district guilds and the city that they be allowed to operate without any real oversight as long as they tend to their own safety protocols. Even during the Stonehollow Quake, when tremors shook the district, the alchemists were able to quickly clean up any alchemical spills with the safety guidelines they put in place and help quell fires in some of the nearby industrial districts, preventing disaster from spreading.
