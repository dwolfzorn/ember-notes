# Cindarin Temple

- **District Type:** Institutional
- **Population:** 1,100 Inhabited solely by the Cindaric Sages and their allies).
- **Lifestyle:** Monastic-but-Comfortable (Sages with means living in a lavish, historic temple).
- **Services:** Spiritual guidance, healing, religious education and practice.
- **Influences:** Built and maintained by the Cindaric Sages.

Visible from nearly any point in the city, the spired marble towers and red cupolae of Cindarin Temple are a venerated hallmark of the Ordain skyline, and serve as an inspiring sight for all who gaze upon their splendor. Only from up close can one truly regard the impressive details of this historical structure of the [[Cindaric Sages]], featuring intricate embellishments, precise patterning, and uncanny stonework made possible through potent and artful magic. Huge slabs of stone — far too large and perfect to be quarried by hand — comprise the grand, sweeping lines and arches of this temple's countless walls, towers, and satellite structures. Each building stands as a testament to the measureless skill and cost that was surely required to erect it.

The multiple levels of the Cindarin Temple are spanned by grand stairways of precisely cut, time-worn stone. Each landing grants access to some section of the temple's services, archives, or the Cindaric Sages themselves. Each chamber and tower of this sprawling complex offers some specific service, or provides a dedicated space for a Cindaric ceremony or rite. Cindarin Temple is the tallest manmade structure in the city, rivaled only by the Ordinate to the west and the towering spires of Temple Ward to the north.

### Landmarks

**Emberhome**

This large central spire serves as Cindarin Temple's primary sanctuary, and hosts a majority of the rites and ritual services that the Cindaric Sages participate in themselves (rather than the ones they conduct for the people of Ordain). Still, many of these services are open to the public — in the hopes that someday they'll grow curious or faithful enough to join the ranks of the Cindarics themselves. The massive curves of Emberhome's architecture are laid out in such a way that the voices and footsteps of the people within are reflected about every corner (making the entry hall often feel busier and more full than it actually is).

**Cindarin Dormitories**

Managed by the meddlesome caretaker **Darrisa Sissos** (Neutral Good, Human Ordani, she/her), the Cindarin Dormitories contain a variety of chambers that house the Cindaric Sages who dwell on the Temple grounds (which actually comprise most of the Cindaric Sages who live in Ordain). Treat Darrisa Sissos as a [[Cindaric Adherent]].

Higher levels of the Dormitories house the organization's leaders; and a few of these chambers were constructed with sonic amplification of other chambers in mind, so that the inhabitants might be able to surreptitiously listen in on activity in the Temple's lower areas. But the truth of this particular detail is a rumor of perpetual discussion by Cindaric Sages and temple visitors alike.

**The Murmuring Well**

This stone well built in the rear garden north of Emberhome doesn't draw water, but is actually said to be a conduit that reaches all the way down to the burning heart of Ember, with voices of Ordani ancestors occasionally rising from below. By all accounts, it is a bottomless pit; indeed, no one (including many of the Cindaric Sages themselves) seems to know precisely how far the well reaches or where it terminates. Only the most senior of Sages understands the truth: powerful spells were cast upon the bottom of the well long ago to both mimic the behavior of a "bottomless" chute and emit the phantom sounds of Ordani voices — but things cast into the well are merely annihilated by the old magic, and the voices are simply an assortment of Cindaric tenets. Nevertheless, the Murmuring Well is venerated as a place of reflection, longing, and remorse.

### Locals

**[[Lilla Arien]] (Lawful Good, Signborn Ordani, she/her)**

Also known as the "Second Sage," the affable Signborn known as Lilla Arien serves as the "Keeper of Sages" at Cindarin Temple. This high-ranking position is tasked with overseeing the room, board, and wellbeing of all other sages in the order — duties which Lilla carries out with great respect and dedication, through good times and bad.

**[[Jon Vastil]] (Lawful Good, Human Ordani, he/him)**

Jon Vastil is the Holy Speaker of the Cindaric Sages, and has guided the order from a position of leeadership for nearly 75 years. Ancient in age and infirm of health, the once-commendable Vastil has faced growing criticism in recent years for a perceived lack of compassion towards the commonfolk and the city's mounting problems. An obsession with humanity's spiritual connection to the Heart of Ember is said to guide this aged leader, who battles a curious illness without cause or cure.

**[[Vinarith]] (Lawful Good, Human + Kivahr Ordani, he/him)**

Vinarith is the Steward of the Holy Speaker within the Cindaric order, the right hand to the highest office of the Cindaric Sages. With the Holy Speaker having fallen ill, he now shoulders many responsibilities of leadership alongside Keeper [[Lilla Arien]].

### Lore

When it was constructed during Ordain's formative days by the very first Cindaric Sages under Speaker Adonia, Cindarin Temple was built to literally amplify the voices of the faithful. Indeed, the exquisite stonework of the Temple structures has been crafted to connect and inspire its occupants via sound; chambers built for sonic resonance or suppression, indirect conduits for remote communication, and grand recital halls serve as a testament to this philosophical legacy. And while this curious feature has its detractors, many adherents believe it accurately represents the cacophony of life on Ember (and is thusly close enough to the architect's original artistic and philosophical intents).
