# Marlstone

- **District Type:** Residential
- **Population:** 963 (Wealthy residents, especially if they are members of House Wandren)
- **Lifestyle:** Lavish (Residents live in luxurious estates and show off their possessions as a way of bringing sales and attention to House Wandrens)
- **Services::** High-end Entertainment (Galas, Showcases of Luxury Goods)
- **Influences:** House Wandren is based in Marlstone

### Culture

Marlstone is more than the substance used to build the district - it is also key to its development. The swirling colors in the rock are said to hold magical resonance, though that has never been fully proven. The rock is also fairly porous, making it easy to create passages out of, including a direct tunnel from nearby Lantern Roads to Marlstone that has become a haven for the movement of illicit goods by House Wandren. Most homes in Marlstone also have underground passages to at least 2 or 3 neighboring homes, based on alliances in the early days of the neighborhood. These have now created a complex web of passageways, many filled either with traps or dead-ends, and are collectively known as the Marls.

Within Marlstone, the tight-knit community believes in keeping its own secrets and solving its own problems. While people from Marlstone will not say much about the neighborhood other than to talk about its fabulous parties or work raising funds for the city's betterment, within Marlstone, gossip is rife, with evergreen topics including whose business ventures are not going well, possibly forcing them to move out of the neighborhood, who is currently enabling House Wandren shenanigans (which all Marlstone residents claim to be against but often benefit from and participate in), and the back-and-forth of planning Marlstone's regular events, which include lunar celebrations, seasonal observations, and other excuses to show off.

### Landmarks

**The Arch**

House Wandren's tradeway, known locally as the Arch, is the hub of the trading houses's activities and is made up of two buildings on either side of the top of the Beaconage. They are joined together by an arch whose materials and design changes seasonally, and House Wandren hosts regular contests to determine who will design the arch next, as it can be seen from high vantage points around the city. On either side of the arch, the Tradeway itself is made from marlstone. As the stone is porous and has a tendency to crumble, making it suitable mostly as a paving stone, the Tradeway's ability to maintain it is seen as a reflection of their strength. In truth, the buildings are made from veined marble and simply disguised to look as if they are marlstone — a classic House Wandren subterfuge.

**Beaconage**

Beaconage, which begins in Lantern Roads and runs uphill into and around Marlstone, is the only real street in the district - it loops through, crossing over itself, and is confusing for outsiders, as addresses on Beaconage are designated by the light feature in front of the home instead of by a specific number. These light features keep Beaconage lit, but can be extinguished at the desires of the residents, plunging all of Marlstone into darkness and fending off any unwanted intruders or outsiders.

**The Marls**

Small underground passageways in Marlstone that usually connect one or two estates to each other. It is said that the specific connections reveal something about early alliances between estate owners, but no one remembers exactly what they are, and the one comprehensive list of the marls, a project of local muckraker Jent Keritz, has conveniently gone missing. Though Jent is never shy to share their outrage at the latest goings-on in the neighborhood, they are oddly silent about where the project they once bragged about might have gone.

**Marlstone Manor**

The most prominent mansion in Marlstone due to its impressive height, Marlstone Manor has had other names over the years as it passed between different owners, but most eventually fell on hard times. Local superstition says that naming it anything other than Marlstone Manor brings bad luck to the owners. The manor is famous for its glass panels, which avoid fully blocking the views of any other building while catching the eye with their impressive water features. The building also plays host, every year, to the Marlstone Gala, an event where the powerful and influential mix and mingle while showing off the latest fashions.

### Locals

**Rathani Biquer (LN Ordani keth, she/her)**

Rathani delivers mail and packages better than anyone else in Marlstone, easily making her way through the Beaconage and translating the addresses written by the senders to the complex set of lantern-based addresses within Marlstone. Several locals have attempted to take advantage of this and get her to carry extra packages, but she appears to be so straightforward in her understanding of the world that she doesn't know when she's being bribed. In truth, Rathani is a major informant for the Veiled Chain, keeping close tabs on what happens in the district during her daily mail runs.

**Dina Kalfe (NE Ordani human, she/her)**

Dina believes in that variety is the spice of life, and prefers to experience that variety in the form of high-end artifacts that will impress those who visit her at her lavish estate, especially those with extensive histories or that come from very distant ports. She is willing to provide funding to travelers that will bring her back the goods she wants, whether they are Anachraenum expeditions or House Wandren ventures. Where she got her money is a mystery, and those who ask don't last long.

**Jent Keritz (CE Ordani human, they/them)**

Though it is technically anonymous, Jent Keritz is widely known to be the publisher of The Beacon, a publication that only exists within Marlstone (the paper bursts into flame and crumbles to ash if taken outside of the neighborhood's boundary lines). The Beacon claims to "shine a light" on the important goings-on in the neighborhood, and is in fact a good source of local news, but is also deeply petty, as Jent appears to have a grudge against nearly everyone in the neighborhood and routinely skews coverage based on who they currently like, dislike, or fear.

**Tevvan Wandren (CN Ordani human, he/him)**

Tevvan Wandren is not a member of House Wandren at all, but has worked at the Wandren Tradeway for years organizing events and enjoys a certain amount of status as a result - status that he wants everyone to be fully aware of. Though usually the House would frown upon their name being used by someone who is not a member, he has learned a few secrets over his time at the Tradeway that the house would not want to come out and traded his silence for association with the house.

### Lore

The history of the Marlstone district is shrouded in mystery. For one thing, no one knows where the marlstone that the district has been made out of came from. Rumor has it that House Wandren acquired it from House Bastilla during its earliest days, after leaving the House to form their own concern, but this can't be proven.

It is believed that a true history of Marlstone would reveal how to get into some of its wealthiest estates through hidden entrances now lost to time. As a result, historians who have tried to trace exactly how Marlstone came together or how its houses connect to each other are routinely given the cold shoulder throughout the district and often find their path forward plunged into darkness. Even attempts to officially map the beaconage are frowned upon.
